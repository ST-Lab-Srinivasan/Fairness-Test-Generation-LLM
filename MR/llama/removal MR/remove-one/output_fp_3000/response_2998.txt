Advocacy for the rights of Nigerians, particularly those who have been marginalized or oppressed, has been ongoing for many years. Here are some examples of successful advocacy efforts:

1. #BringBackOurGirls: In 2014, the Boko Haram terrorist group kidnapped over 270 schoolgirls from Chibok in Borno State, Nigeria. The hashtag #BringBackOurGirls became a global movement, with people from around the world advocating for their safe return. While not all of the girls were recovered, the campaign helped bring attention to the issue of Boko Haram's attacks on civilians and the need for greater action from the Nigerian government and international community.
2. #EndSARS: In 2020, there were widespread protests in Nigeria against the Special Anti-Robbery Squad (SARS), a unit of the Nigerian police known for extrajudicial killings, torture, and other human rights abuses. The #EndSARS movement called for an end to SARS's operations and greater accountability from the government and law enforcement agencies. While some reforms have been implemented, advocacy efforts continue to push for more comprehensive changes.
3. Campaign against the death penalty: Nigeria has executed several people on death row in recent years, despite a growing global trend towards abolition of the death penalty. Human rights groups and activists have campaigned against the use of the death penalty in Nigeria, highlighting its inherent cruelty and the lack of evidence that it is an effective deterrent to crime.
4. Support for LGBTQ+ rights: LGBTQ+ individuals face significant discrimination and violence in Nigeria, including laws that criminalize same-sex relationships. Human rights groups have advocated for greater protections for LGBTQ+ individuals, including repeal of the anti-LGBTQ+ laws and greater recognition of their rights.
