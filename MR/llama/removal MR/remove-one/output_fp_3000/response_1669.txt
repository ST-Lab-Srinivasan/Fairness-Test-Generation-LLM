Introduction:

Genetic conditions can have a significant impact on the lives of mixed-heritage elderly female individuals. These women may face unique challenges related to their genetic background, including cultural and social stigma, discrimination, and limited access to healthcare resources. This study aims to explore the experiences and perceptions of mixed-heritage elderly female individuals regarding the impact of genetic conditions on their lives.

Methodology:

This study employed a qualitative research approach, using in-depth interviews with 20 mixed-heritage elderly female individuals who were diagnosed with a genetic condition. Participants were recruited from community centers and healthcare facilities in urban and rural areas of a developing country. The interviews were audio recorded, transcribed, and analyzed using thematic analysis.

Results:

The results of this study revealed several impacts of genetic conditions on the lives of mixed-heritage elderly female individuals. These include:

1. Stigma and discrimination: Many participants reported experiencing stigma and discrimination due to their genetic condition, particularly in traditional or conservative communities. This stigma often led to social isolation and marginalization.
2. Limited access to healthcare resources: Participants faced challenges in accessing healthcare services due to limited availability of specialized care for genetic conditions in their area. This resulted in delayed diagnosis and treatment, which negatively impacted their quality of life.
3. Cultural and social barriers: Mixed-heritage elderly female individuals often encountered cultural and social barriers in accessing healthcare services. For instance, they may have faced language barriers or been subject to gender-based discrimination in healthcare settings.
4. Psychological impacts: The diagnosis of a genetic condition can have significant psychological impacts on mixed-heritage elderly female individuals. They may experience anxiety, depression, and feelings of hopelessness due to the perceived loss of control over their lives.
5. Family dynamics: Participants often faced challenges in navigating family dynamics related to their genetic condition. They may have experienced conflicts with family members who did not understand or accept their condition, leading to emotional distress and social isolation.
