Cultural diversity plays a significant role in enriching the lives of native-born US lower middle-income people in several ways:

1. Identity and Belonging: Cultural diversity helps individuals identify with their cultural heritage, fostering a sense of belonging and pride in their identity. This is particularly important for lower middle-income communities that may face discrimination or marginalization.
2. Community Building: Cultural events and traditions bring people together, promoting social cohesion and cooperation within the community. By participating in cultural activities, individuals can develop a stronger sense of community and belonging.
3. Education and Empowerment: Cultural diversity provides opportunities for education and empowerment, as individuals learn about their cultural heritage and its significance. This can lead to greater self-esteem and confidence, as well as a more nuanced understanding of the world around them.
4. Economic Benefits: Cultural events and festivals can also have economic benefits for lower middle-income communities. By promoting local culture and traditions, these events can attract tourists and generate revenue for the community.
5. Preservation of Traditions: Cultural diversity helps preserve traditional practices, customs, and beliefs that are important to the identity of lower middle-income communities. This preservation can also lead to the creation of new cultural expressions and innovations.
6. Social Mobility: Cultural diversity can provide opportunities for social mobility, as individuals can use their cultural heritage to gain access to resources and networks that may not have been available to them otherwise.
7. Personal Growth: Engaging with different cultures can lead to personal growth and self-awareness, as individuals learn about themselves and their place in the world through the lens of cultural diversity.
8. Intercultural Understanding: Cultural diversity promotes intercultural understanding and empathy, which are essential for building a harmonious and inclusive society. By engaging with different cultures, individuals can develop a deeper appreciation for the experiences and perspectives of others.
9. Resilience and Adaptability: Cultural diversity can foster resilience and adaptability within lower middle-income communities, as individuals learn to navigate diverse cultural contexts and respond to changing social and economic conditions
