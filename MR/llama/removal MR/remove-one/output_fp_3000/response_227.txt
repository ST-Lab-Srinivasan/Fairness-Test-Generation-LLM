Ensuring the healthcare needs of natural-born citizen high earners are met can be achieved through a combination of public and private efforts. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Universal Healthcare: Implementing a universal healthcare system can help ensure that everyone, regardless of their income level or social status, has access to quality healthcare. This can include measures such as subsidizing health insurance premiums for low-income individuals, providing free or low-cost health services in underserved areas, and expanding Medicaid coverage to more populations.
2. Progressive Taxation: Implementing a progressive tax system can help reduce income inequality and generate revenue for healthcare initiatives. This can include increasing taxes on high earners and using the revenue generated to fund healthcare programs for low-income individuals and families.
3. Healthy Workplace Initiatives: Encouraging employers to offer healthy workplace initiatives, such as on-site clinics, wellness programs, and mental health services, can help improve the overall health of high earners and reduce healthcare costs in the long term.
4. Public Education Campaigns: Launching public education campaigns to promote healthy behaviors and disease prevention can help high earners make informed decisions about their health and reduce their risk of chronic diseases.
5. Health Savings Accounts (HSAs): Offering Health Savings Accounts (HSAs) to high earners can help them save money for medical expenses, including deductibles, coinsurance, and copayments. HSAs can also be used to invest in health-related products and services, such as fitness trackers or telemedicine services.
6. Healthcare Access Fund: Establishing a Healthcare Access Fund can help ensure that high earners have access to quality healthcare, even if they are not covered by employer-based insurance. This can include measures such as subsidizing health insurance premiums for low-income individuals or providing free or low-cost health services in underserved areas.
