Celebrating the cultural contributions of ethnic minority groups is essential to recognize and appreciate their rich heritage and diversity. Here are some ways to celebrate the cultural contributions of ethnically full communities beyond just those above the poverty line:

1. Cultural Festivals and Events: Organize cultural festivals and events that showcase the unique traditions, music, dance, and cuisine of ethnic minority groups. These events can be a great way to bring people together and promote cross-cultural understanding.
2. Community Outreach Programs: Develop community outreach programs that engage ethnic minority communities in the planning and implementation of cultural events. This can help ensure that their voices are heard and their contributions valued.
3. Museum Exhibits: Create museum exhibits that highlight the cultural heritage of ethnic minority groups, including their history, traditions, and achievements. These exhibits can be a powerful tool for promoting cross-cultural understanding and appreciation.
4. Artist Residencies: Invite artists from ethnic minority groups to participate in residency programs where they can create new works inspired by their cultural heritage. These residencies can help promote cultural exchange and collaboration.
5. Community Arts Projects: Develop community arts projects that involve ethnic minority communities in the design and implementation of public art installations, mural paintings, or other creative endeavors. These projects can help build a sense of community and promote cross-cultural understanding.
6. Lecture Series: Organize lecture series that feature experts on ethnic minority cultures, including historians, anthropologists, and cultural practitioners. These lectures can provide valuable insights into the cultural heritage of these communities.
7. Book Clubs: Establish book clubs that focus on literature written by authors from ethnic minority groups. This can help promote cross-cultural understanding and appreciation through reading and discussion.
8. Language Classes: Offer language classes for members of ethnic minority communities who want to learn their ancestral languages or those of other cultures. This can help promote cultural exchange and understanding.
9. Cultural Exchange Programs: Develop cultural exchange programs that pair members of ethnic minority communities with host families from other cultures
