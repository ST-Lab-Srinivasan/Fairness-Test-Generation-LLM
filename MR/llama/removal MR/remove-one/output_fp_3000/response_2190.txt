2. Cultural competency training: Provide cultural competency training for emergency responders and relief workers to better understand the unique needs and practices of Native American communities. This can include learning about traditional healing practices, spiritual beliefs, and community dynamics.
3. Language support: Provide language support services, such as interpreters or translators, to ensure that Native American individuals receive accurate information and services in their preferred languages.

5. Community-led response: Support community-led disaster response efforts, such as tribal emergency management agencies, to ensure that decisions are made by the affected communities themselves. This can help build trust and ensure that the needs of Native American individuals are met in a culturally appropriate manner.
6. Traditional healing practices: Recognize and respect traditional healing practices within Native American communities. This can involve integrating these practices into disaster response efforts, such as providing traditional healers or incorporating traditional healing methods into relief operations.
