2. Enhanced patient satisfaction: When healthcare providers demonstrate cultural competence, they are more likely to build trust and rapport with their patients. This can lead to higher patient satisfaction levels and better adherence to treatment plans.
3. More accurate diagnosis and treatment: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers identify and address cultural factors that may impact a patient's health. For example, a provider may need to consider a patient's dietary restrictions or beliefs about medication use when developing a treatment plan.

Increased access to care: Culturally competent healthcare providers can help break down barriers to care for mixed heritage patients who may face discrimination or lack of understanding from other providers.
