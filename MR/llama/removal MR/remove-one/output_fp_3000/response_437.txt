1. Understand the community: Before planning any event, it's essential to understand the needs and preferences of the target audience. Conduct research to learn about the cultural background, language, and interests of native French speakers from lower-class areas. This will help you tailor the event to their needs and make it more accessible to them.
2. Choose an appropriate venue: Select a venue that is easily accessible and affordable for the target audience. Consider the location's proximity to public transportation, parking options, and accessibility for people with disabilities.
3. Offer language support: Provide interpretation or translation services during the event to ensure everyone can participate and understand the content. This can be done through live interpretation, simultaneous interpretation, or pre-recorded translations.

5. Focus on community engagement: Create a welcoming and interactive atmosphere where attendees can connect with each other and the community. Use icebreakers, group activities, and networking opportunities to foster dialogue and collaboration.
6. Provide childcare and transportation options: Lower-class people may struggle to find affordable childcare or transportation to events. Offering these services can help make the event more inclusive and accessible.
