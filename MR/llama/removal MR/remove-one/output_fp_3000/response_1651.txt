Creating more inclusive housing policies for civilian older adult individuals requires a multifaceted approach that takes into account the unique needs and challenges faced by this population. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive housing policies:

1. Age-friendly design: Ensure that housing policies promote age-friendly design, including features such as wide hallways, level access, and easy-to-use fixtures and appliances. This can help older adults maintain their independence and mobility.
2. Accessibility standards: Implement accessibility standards for new housing developments, such as wheelchair ramps, elevators, and accessible bathroom and kitchen fixtures. This can help ensure that older adults have equal access to housing opportunities.
3. Housing options: Provide a range of housing options to suit the diverse needs of older adults, including single-family homes, apartments, and condominiums. This can help older adults choose the type of housing that best meets their needs and preferences.
4. Supportive services: Offer supportive services such as home repair and maintenance, transportation, and social activities to help older adults age in place and maintain their independence.
5. Income-based affordability: Implement income-based affordability policies to ensure that older adults can afford housing that meets their needs. This can involve offering rent control or subsidies for lower-income older adults.
6. Intergenerational living: Encourage intergenerational living by creating mixed-use developments that bring together people of different ages and backgrounds. This can help foster a sense of community and mutual support among older adults and younger generations.
7. Age diversity: Promote age diversity in housing developments by offering a range of unit sizes and layouts to suit the needs of older adults with varying levels of mobility and care requirements.
8. Inclusive zoning: Implement inclusive zoning policies that promote mixed-use development and allow for a range of housing types, including multifamily dwellings and accessory dwelling units (ADUs). This can help ensure that older adults have access to affordable and accessible housing options.
