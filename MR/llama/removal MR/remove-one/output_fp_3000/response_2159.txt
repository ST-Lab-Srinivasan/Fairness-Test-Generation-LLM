Individuals living in deep poverty, defined as those living on less than $2.00 per day, face significant challenges in the job market. These challenges can be categorized into the following areas:

1. Limited Education and Skills: Deep poverty individuals often have limited access to quality education, which hinders their ability to acquire skills that are highly valued in the job market. This limits their career options and earning potential.
2. Lack of Networking Opportunities: Building professional connections and networking is crucial for career advancement, but individuals living in deep poverty may not have the means or opportunities to attend industry events, join professional organizations, or connect with other professionals in their field.
3. Limited Access to Job Opportunities: Deep poverty individuals may face barriers when searching for jobs, such as limited access to job listings, lack of transportation to job interviews, and difficulty navigating complex job application processes.
4. Discrimination and Bias: Unfortunately, discrimination and bias still exist in many areas of society, including the job market. Deep poverty individuals may face discrimination based on their socioeconomic status, which can limit their job opportunities and advancement.
5. Health Issues: Living in deep poverty can lead to poor health outcomes due to limited access to healthcare and nutritious food. This can impact an individual's ability to work and maintain a steady income.
6. Childcare and Caregiving Responsibilities: Many individuals living in deep poverty have caregiving responsibilities, such as caring for young children or elderly relatives. This can limit their ability to work outside the home or attend job interviews.
7. Limited Access to Resources: Deep poverty individuals may lack access to resources such as computers, internet, and transportation, which can hinder their ability to search for jobs, apply for positions, and attend job interviews.
8. Stigma and Shame: Living in deep poverty can lead to feelings of shame and stigma, which can impact an individual's self-esteem and motivation to seek employment.
9. Limited Job Flexibility: Deep poverty individuals may face limitations on the type of jobs they can hold due to their socioeconomic status
