The economic empowerment of infants and young children is crucial for their overall development and well-being. Here are some ways to support their economic empowerment:

1. Provide access to education: Education is a critical factor in determining an individual's economic status later in life. Ensure that infants and young children have access to quality educational resources, including preschool and early childhood programs.
2. Encourage entrepreneurship: Introduce children to entrepreneurship at an early age through activities such as lemonade stands or other small business ventures. This can help them develop skills in leadership, problem-solving, and financial management.
3. Teach money management: Teach children the basics of personal finance, including saving, budgeting, and investing. Encourage them to make smart financial decisions and avoid debt.
4. Promote financial literacy: Provide children with age-appropriate information about personal finance and economics. This can help them understand the value of money, how to make informed financial decisions, and how to navigate the complexities of the economy.
5. Encourage work ethic: Teach children the importance of hard work and dedication in achieving their goals. Encourage them to set goals and work towards achieving them through diligent effort and perseverance.
6. Provide mentorship: Connect children with successful professionals in their desired field, who can serve as role models and provide guidance on how to achieve success.
7. Support skill development: Encourage children to explore their interests and develop skills in areas such as music, art, or sports. This can help them build confidence, discipline, and a sense of purpose.
8. Offer opportunities for community involvement: Encourage children to participate in community service projects, which can help them develop a sense of social responsibility and civic engagement.
9. Teach the value of giving: Instill in children the importance of giving back to their communities through volunteer work or charitable donations. This can help them understand the value of generosity and the impact they can have on others.
