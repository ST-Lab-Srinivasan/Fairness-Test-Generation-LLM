Ensuring the voices of beginner Arabic speaker ethnic minority individuals are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting inclusivity and equity. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Create a safe space: Establish a safe and inclusive environment where beginners feel comfortable sharing their opinions and experiences. This can be achieved by setting ground rules, providing support, and encouraging active listening.
2. Build trust: Develop relationships with the individuals based on mutual respect, empathy, and trust. Encourage open communication and provide opportunities for them to share their thoughts and ideas.
3. Provide language support: Offer language support and resources to help beginners improve their Arabic skills, enabling them to participate more effectively in decision-making processes.
4. Involve them in the decision-making process: Encourage beginners to participate in decision-making activities, such as brainstorming sessions, focus groups, or voting exercises. This will give them a voice and an opportunity to influence decisions that affect their lives.
5. Use visual aids: Utilize visual aids like pictures, diagrams, and videos to help beginners understand complex information and participate more effectively in decision-making processes.
6. Provide cultural context: Offer cultural context and background information to help beginners better understand the issues being discussed during decision-making processes. This can include historical, social, and political contexts.
7. Foster a sense of belonging: Encourage beginners to feel a sense of belonging by promoting diversity, inclusivity, and respect for their cultural backgrounds. This can be achieved through storytelling, cultural events, and community-building activities.
8. Leverage technology: Utilize digital tools like online forums, virtual meetings, and social media platforms to engage beginners in decision-making processes. These tools can help bridge the gap between physical locations and ensure that everyone has an equal opportunity to participate.
