There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of low-income and recently immigrated individuals in the US:

1. Access to affordable financial services: Many low-income and recently immigrated individuals lack access to traditional banking services due to lack of financial resources or credit history. Organizations like the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC) and the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) can provide guidance on how to design and offer affordable financial products, such as check cashing services, money transfer services, and small dollar loans.
2. Financial education and literacy: Many immigrants may not have a strong understanding of personal finance or the US financial system. Providing financial education and literacy programs can help them better manage their finances, make informed decisions about credit and savings, and build credit history.
3. Job training and placement: Low-income and recently immigrated individuals may face significant barriers to finding employment due to a lack of job skills or limited access to job opportunities. Organizations like the US Department of Labor can provide job training and placement services to help individuals gain the skills they need to compete in the job market.
4. Small business development: Many low-income and recently immigrated individuals may be interested in starting their own businesses but lack the resources or knowledge to do so. Organizations like the Small Business Administration (SBA) can provide guidance on how to start and grow a small business, including access to capital, mentorship, and training.

Health insurance and healthcare access: Many low-income and recently immigrated individuals may lack access to affordable healthcare due to lack of health insurance or limited access to healthcare providers. Organizations like the Affordable Care Act (ACA) can provide guidance on how to access affordable healthcare options, including Medicaid expansion and marketplace plans.
6. Housing support: Many low-income and recently immigrated individuals may face significant barriers to finding affordable and safe housing. Organizations like the US Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) can provide guidance on how to access affordable housing options, including rental assistance programs and homeownership programs.
