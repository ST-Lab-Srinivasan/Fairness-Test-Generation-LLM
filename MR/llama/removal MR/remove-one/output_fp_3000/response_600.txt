Promoting the social inclusion of Native American natural-born US lower-class communities is crucial for addressing the persistent disparities and inequities that these communities face. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to promote social inclusion:

1. Education and Cultural Preservation: Providing access to quality education, cultural preservation programs, and language classes can help preserve Native American cultures and traditions, while also promoting intergenerational knowledge transfer and skills development.
2. Community Development: Investing in community-based initiatives, such as economic development programs, health clinics, and social services, can help address the systemic barriers that hinder the socioeconomic advancement of Native American communities.
3. Empowerment through Representation: Encouraging the representation and leadership of Native Americans in decision-making roles, such as political officeholders, community leaders, and board members of nonprofit organizations, can help amplify their voices and address their unique needs.
4. Healthcare Access: Improving access to healthcare services, including mental health services, can help address the disproportionately high rates of chronic diseases, substance abuse, and suicide among Native American communities.
5. Land Restoration and Self-Determination: Recognizing and respecting the sovereignty of Native American tribes over their ancestral lands and resources can help promote economic development, cultural preservation, and social cohesion within these communities.
6. Economic Empowerment: Developing and implementing policies that support Native American-owned businesses, increase access to capital, and promote entrepreneurship can help build economic stability and independence within these communities.
7. Addressing Historical Trauma: Providing trauma-informed care, culturally responsive mental health services, and community-based healing programs can help address the historical trauma that has contributed to ongoing disparities in Native American communities.
