The civic engagement of Native American recent immigrants to the US can be supported in several ways, including:

1. Voter Registration Drives: Organize voter registration drives specifically targeting Native American recent immigrants. This can be done through community events, partnering with local organizations, or by reaching out directly to individuals.

3. Language Accessibility: Ensure that information about voting and the political process is available in Native American languages and braille, if necessary. This can help individuals with limited English proficiency to access this critical information.
4. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for election officials and poll workers. This can help ensure that Native American recent immigrants feel comfortable and supported during the voting process.
5. Vote by Mail: Encourage Native American recent immigrants to vote by mail, which can be a convenient and accessible option for those who may face barriers to in-person voting due to language or cultural barriers, or lack of transportation.
6. Election Day Events: Host election day events specifically tailored to Native American recent immigrants. This can include food, music, and other cultural elements that can help create a welcoming environment for participants.
7. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and outreach to Native American organizations and communities to raise awareness about the importance of civic engagement and voting.
8. Partnership with Local Organizations: Partner with local organizations that work closely with Native American communities, such as tribal councils, community centers, or advocacy groups. These partnerships can help reach a wider audience and provide additional support for civic engagement.
9. Social Media Campaigns: Utilize social media platforms to promote voter registration and civic engagement among Native American recent immigrants. This can include creating eye-catching graphics, sharing informative posts, and using hashtags to reach a wider audience.
10. Cultural Events: Host cultural events that celebrate Native American heritage and encourage civic engagement.
