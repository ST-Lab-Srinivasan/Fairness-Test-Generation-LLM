Creating more inclusive cultural programs for Native American, recent immigrant, and other underrepresented communities in the United States requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some strategies that organizations and institutions can use to create more inclusive cultural programs:

1. Listen and engage with underrepresented communities: Conduct research and outreach to understand the cultural practices, traditions, and values of Native American and recent immigrant communities. Engage with these communities through listening sessions, focus groups, and surveys to identify their cultural needs and preferences.
2. Be mindful of power dynamics: Recognize the historical power imbalances that have affected underrepresented communities and be intentional about redistributing power and agency to these communities. Be willing to listen, learn, and adapt to meet the needs of these communities.
3. Foster cross-cultural understanding and exchange: Design programs that promote cross-cultural understanding and exchange between different communities. Encourage dialogue, mutual learning, and collaboration among community members, artists, and cultural practitioners.
4. Prioritize inclusivity in program design: Ensure that program designs are inclusive of diverse perspectives and needs. Use participatory methods to involve community members in program development and implementation. Provide training and resources to ensure that programs are accessible and inclusive for all participants.
5. Support cultural preservation and revitalization: Provide resources and support to underrepresented communities to preserve and revitalize their cultural practices, languages, and traditions. This can involve providing access to cultural knowledge, skills, and resources, as well as supporting community-led initiatives for cultural preservation and revitalization.
6. Amplify marginalized voices: Create platforms for underrepresented communities to share their stories, experiences, and perspectives. Use media and other channels to amplify their voices and promote their cultural practices and traditions.
