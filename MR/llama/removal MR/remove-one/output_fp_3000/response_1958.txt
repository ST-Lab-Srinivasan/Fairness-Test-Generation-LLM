Mixed heritage individuals, those who identify as both French and Indigenous, face unique challenges when it comes to being heard in decision-making processes. As a result of historical trauma, systemic racism, and ongoing discrimination, these individuals may feel marginalized or excluded from mainstream society, including political and cultural institutions. To ensure that the voices of mixed heritage individuals are heard, it is essential to address these structural barriers and create inclusive spaces for their participation. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Listen to their stories: Hearing the personal experiences and perspectives of mixed heritage individuals is crucial to understanding their unique challenges and how they can contribute to decision-making processes. Organizations and communities should create safe spaces for these individuals to share their stories, without fear of judgment or marginalization.
2. Amplify their voices: Mixed heritage individuals may feel invisible or unheard in mainstream society. To combat this, it is essential to amplify their voices through various means such as media representation, community events, and cultural programs. By showcasing their experiences and perspectives, we can help create a more inclusive environment for their participation.
3. Provide opportunities for leadership: Mixed heritage individuals should be given opportunities to take on leadership roles in decision-making processes. This could involve mentorship programs, training and education, or simply providing platforms for them to share their ideas and perspectives. By empowering these individuals with the skills and resources they need to lead, we can help ensure that their voices are heard.
4. Address systemic barriers: Systemic racism and discrimination continue to affect mixed heritage individuals in Canada. To create a more inclusive environment for decision-making processes, it is essential to address these barriers through policy changes, community engagement, and education. By working towards a more equitable society, we can ensure that the voices of mixed heritage individuals are heard and valued.
