Addressing the specific needs of Native natural-born US partnered individuals in education requires a comprehensive and inclusive approach that recognizes the unique experiences, traditions, and cultural backgrounds of Indigenous communities. Here are some strategies that can help address their specific needs:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers to incorporate Native American cultural practices, beliefs, and values into their instruction. This can include using Indigenous languages, stories, and art in the classroom.
2. Inclusive curriculum: Develop a curriculum that includes Indigenous history, culture, and perspectives. This can help students feel seen and valued, and can foster a sense of belonging and connection to their cultural heritage.
3. Community-based learning: Involve Native American communities in the educational process by incorporating community-based learning experiences. This can include partnerships with tribal organizations, cultural events, and community service projects.
4. Indigenous language revitalization: Support the revitalization of Indigenous languages by incorporating language classes into the curriculum or providing language immersion programs.
5. Mentorship and leadership opportunities: Provide mentorship and leadership opportunities for Native American students, such as internships, apprenticeships, or student leadership roles. This can help build confidence, skills, and cultural identity.
6. Addressing systemic barriers: Advocate for policies and practices that address systemic barriers to educational success faced by Native American students, such as inadequate funding, lack of resources, and discrimination.
7. Cultural preservation: Support the preservation of Indigenous cultures and traditions through cultural events, workshops, and programs that promote cultural exchange and understanding.
8. Family engagement: Involve Native American families in the educational process by providing opportunities for parental involvement and participation in decision-making processes.
9. Access to technology: Provide access to technology, including tablets, laptops, and internet connectivity, to ensure that all students have equal access to educational resources.
