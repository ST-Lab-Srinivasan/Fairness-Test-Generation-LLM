Supporting the social inclusion of deep poor ultra-poor individuals can be a complex and challenging task, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Education and Skills Development: Providing access to education and skills development opportunities can help ultra-poor individuals acquire the knowledge and skills needed to find better-paying jobs or start their own businesses. This can include vocational training, literacy programs, and entrepreneurship training.
2. Access to Financial Services: Many deep poor individuals lack access to basic financial services such as savings accounts, loans, and insurance. Providing access to these services can help them manage their finances more effectively and build assets.
3. Health and Nutrition Programs: Ensuring that ultra-poor individuals have access to quality healthcare and nutritious food can help improve their overall well-being and reduce the risk of illnesses and malnutrition.
4. Community Mobilization: Engaging with local communities and mobilizing them to support the ultra-poor can help create a sense of ownership and responsibility for addressing poverty. This can involve organizing community meetings, training community volunteers, and creating opportunities for social interaction and networking.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocating for policy changes that address the root causes of poverty can help create an enabling environment for ultra-poor individuals to overcome their poverty status. This can involve working with government agencies, civil society organizations, and other stakeholders to influence policies related to education, healthcare, and economic opportunity.
6. Microfinance Programs: Providing microfinance services such as small loans, savings accounts, and insurance products can help ultra-poor individuals build assets and improve their financial stability.
7. Livelihoods Support: Identifying potential livelihood opportunities for ultra-poor individuals and providing support to establish them can help create a pathway out of poverty. This can involve providing training, resources, and technical assistance to help individuals start or expand their businesses.
