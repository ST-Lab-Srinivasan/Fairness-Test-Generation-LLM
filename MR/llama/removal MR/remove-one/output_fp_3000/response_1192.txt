Promoting the artistic expression of nominal beginner English speakers can be a challenging task, but there are several strategies and activities that can help encourage and foster their creativity. Here are some ideas:

1. Provide opportunities for expression: Encourage beginners to express themselves through various forms of art, such as writing, drawing, painting, or even dancing. Offer a variety of materials and tools to help them explore their creativity.
2. Practice with fun activities: Engage beginners in fun and interactive activities that promote creative expression, such as rhyming games, storytelling exercises, or group brainstorming sessions.
3. Encourage self-expression: Allow beginners to express themselves freely without worrying about grammar or syntax mistakes. This will help them build confidence in their abilities and encourage them to explore their creativity further.
4. Provide positive feedback: Offer constructive feedback that highlights the strengths and potential of each individual's artistic expression. Positive reinforcement can go a long way in boosting their confidence and motivation to create.
5. Collaborate with other learners: Encourage beginners to work together on creative projects, such as group storytelling or poetry writing. This will not only foster collaboration and teamwork but also provide an opportunity for them to learn from each other's perspectives and ideas.
6. Celebrate diversity: Embrace the unique styles and perspectives of each beginner, and celebrate the diversity of their artistic expressions. Encourage them to embrace their individuality and express themselves authentically.
7. Provide access to resources: Offer beginners access to a variety of resources, such as books, magazines, or online tutorials, that can help them develop their creative skills and knowledge.
8. Encourage reflection and self-awareness: Encourage beginners to reflect on their artistic expressions and identify what they are passionate about and what they enjoy expressing through art. This will help them tap into their inner selves and create more meaningful and personal works of art.
9. Set achievable goals: Help beginners set realistic and achievable goals for their artistic expression, such as creating a certain number of pieces
