Introduction:
Genetic conditions can have a significant impact on the lives of individuals, especially those in their late 30s. These conditions can affect various aspects of an individual's life, including their physical health, mental well-being, social relationships, and career prospects. In this report, we will explore the impact of genetic conditions on the lives of actively equal late 30s people.

Physical Health:
Individuals with genetic conditions may experience a range of physical symptoms, including pain, fatigue, and mobility issues. These symptoms can significantly affect an individual's quality of life, making it difficult to perform daily tasks and maintain their independence. For example, individuals with inherited muscle disorders may experience muscle weakness and loss of motor function, which can limit their ability to work or participate in social activities.

Mental Well-being:
Genetic conditions can also have a profound impact on an individual's mental well-being. Many individuals with genetic conditions may experience anxiety, depression, and stress related to their diagnosis, the unpredictability of their condition, and the stigma associated with it. These mental health issues can affect an individual's relationships, work performance, and overall quality of life.

Social Relationships:
Individuals with genetic conditions may experience social isolation and stigma due to their diagnosis. They may find it challenging to maintain relationships, especially romantic ones, as their condition can be seen as a burden or a source of insecurity. Friends and family members may struggle to understand the individual's condition, leading to feelings of loneliness and exclusion.

Career Prospects:
Genetic conditions can also impact an individual's career prospects, particularly if their condition affects their physical abilities or cognitive function. Many individuals with genetic conditions may face discrimination in the workplace, limiting their opportunities for advancement and promotions. Additionally, they may experience stress and burnout due to the challenges of managing their condition while working.
