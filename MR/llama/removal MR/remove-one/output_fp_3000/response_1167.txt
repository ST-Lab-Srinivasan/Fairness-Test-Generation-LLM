Creating more inclusive social policies for recent migrants, including those holding temporary visas, requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique challenges and promotes integration. Here are some recommendations:

1. Access to education and training: Offer language classes, vocational training, and educational programs tailored to the needs of temporary visa holders. This will help them acquire the skills and knowledge required for employment in the host country.
2. Workforce integration: Encourage employers to hire temporary visa holders by providing incentives such as tax breaks or subsidies for hiring migrant workers. Create mentorship programs that pair temporary visa holders with local professionals, helping them navigate the job market and build connections within the community.
3. Healthcare access: Ensure that temporary visa holders have access to affordable healthcare by partnering with local healthcare providers or creating specific health insurance plans for migrant workers.
4. Housing support: Provide temporary visa holders with information on affordable housing options, subsidized rent, or assistance in finding accommodation that meets their needs.
5. Social security and benefits: Offer temporary visa holders access to social security programs, such as unemployment insurance, maternity leave, and parental benefits. This will help them feel more connected to the host society and reduce poverty and inequality.
6. Community engagement: Encourage temporary visa holders to participate in local events, volunteer work, or community groups. This will help them build social connections and a sense of belonging within the host country.
7. Legal support: Provide free or low-cost legal services to temporary visa holders, including assistance with obtaining work permits, residence permits, and citizenship applications.
8. Financial inclusion: Offer financial literacy programs, budgeting advice, and access to affordable bank accounts or microfinance options to help temporary visa holders manage their finances effectively.
9. Child protection: Ensure that children of temporary visa holders have access to quality education, healthcare, and child protection services. This will help them feel safe and supported in their new environment.
