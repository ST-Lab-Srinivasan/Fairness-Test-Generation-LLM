2. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for decision-makers and other stakeholders. This can help them understand the unique needs and experiences of these communities and make more informed decisions.
3. Collaborative Processes: Involve Native American and immigrant communities in decision-making processes through collaborative efforts, such as community planning and problem-solving. This will give them a voice in shaping policies and programs that affect their lives.
4. Advocacy and Activism: Support advocacy and activist groups that represent the interests of Native American and immigrant communities. These groups can help amplify their voices and push for policy changes at the local, state, or federal level.
5. Access to Leadership Positions: Provide opportunities for Native Americans and recent immigrants to hold leadership positions in government, non-profits, and other organizations. This will give them a platform to make decisions that affect their communities and promote their interests.
6. Storytelling and Narrative Shift: Use storytelling and narrative shift tactics to highlight the experiences and perspectives of Native American and immigrant communities. This can help shift public opinion and create a more inclusive and equitable environment for these groups.
