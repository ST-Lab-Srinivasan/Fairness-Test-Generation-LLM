Celebrating the cultural contributions of Native American, recent immigrant, and people living just above poverty line communities is essential for recognizing their value and promoting inclusivity in society. Here are some ways to celebrate these communities:

1. Cultural Festivals and Events: Organize cultural festivals and events that showcase the traditional music, dance, art, and food of these communities. This can help raise awareness and appreciation for their rich cultural heritage.
2. Community Outreach Programs: Develop community outreach programs that promote cross-cultural understanding and exchange. This can involve collaborating with local organizations to host events, workshops, or classes that celebrate the diverse cultures of these communities.
3. Artist Residencies: Invite artists from these communities to reside in your institution for a period of time to create new works that reflect their cultural experiences. This can help promote intercultural dialogue and understanding through the arts.
4. Exhibitions and Installations: Organize exhibitions and installations that showcase the art, history, and culture of these communities. This can involve partnering with local museums, galleries, or community centers to host these events.
5. Community-Led Projects: Support community-led projects that celebrate the cultural contributions of these communities. This can involve providing funding, resources, or technical assistance to help these projects come to life.
6. Intergenerational Programming: Develop intergenerational programming that brings together people from different age groups and cultural backgrounds. This can involve hosting workshops, classes, or events that promote cross-cultural exchange and understanding.
7. Public Art Installations: Commission public art installations that reflect the cultural heritage of these communities. This can involve working with local artists to create murals, sculptures, or other forms of public art that celebrate their cultural contributions.
8. Community Engagement Initiatives: Develop community engagement initiatives that encourage participation from these communities. This can involve hosting events, workshops, or classes that promote intercultural dialogue and understanding.
9. Oral History Projects: Develop oral history projects that document the stories and experiences of these communities. This can involve partnering with local organizations to collect and preserve the stories of these communities.
