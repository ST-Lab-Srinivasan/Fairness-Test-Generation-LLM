Creating more inclusive social policies for jobless separated individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the root causes of unemployment and marginalization. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Employment training programs: Provide access to vocational training, skills development, and job readiness programs to help individuals acquire new skills and prepare them for the labor market.
2. Job placement services: Offer job placement services that match jobseekers with employers who are willing to hire individuals from marginalized groups. This can include one-on-one coaching, resume building, and interview preparation.
3. Microfinance programs: Offer microfinance programs that provide small loans or other forms of financial support to help individuals start their own businesses or invest in existing ones. This can help create economic opportunities for individuals who may not have access to traditional funding sources.
4. Mental health support: Provide mental health support services to help individuals deal with the emotional and psychological impacts of unemployment and marginalization. This can include counseling, therapy, and stress management techniques.
5. Childcare support: Offer childcare support services to help parents who are struggling to balance work and family responsibilities. This can include affordable childcare options, parenting classes, and respite care.
6. Education and literacy programs: Provide education and literacy programs that help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge to enhance their employability. This can include basic literacy classes, vocational training, and degree programs.
7. Social enterprise incubators: Establish social enterprise incubators that provide resources, mentorship, and support to individuals who want to start their own social enterprises. This can help create sustainable economic opportunities for marginalized communities.
8. Community-based programs: Develop community-based programs that bring together jobseekers, employers, and local organizations to provide a range of services, including job placement, skills training, and mentorship.
9. Government policies: Advocate for government policies that promote inclusive hiring practices, such as targeted hiring initiatives, diversity and inclusion training, and the elimination of systemic barriers to employment.
