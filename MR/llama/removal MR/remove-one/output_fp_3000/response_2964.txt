Creating more inclusive social policies for deep poverty long-term individuals requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the root causes of poverty and provides sustainable solutions. Here are some strategies that can be employed:

1. Increase access to education: Education is a key factor in breaking the cycle of poverty. Governments can invest in public education, provide scholarships, and offer vocational training programs to help individuals acquire skills and knowledge that can lead to better job opportunities.
2. Job creation and entrepreneurship support: Governments can create jobs by investing in infrastructure projects, promoting small businesses, and providing financial assistance to start-ups. This can help reduce unemployment rates and increase economic mobility.
3. Healthcare and social services: Ensuring access to quality healthcare and social services is essential for reducing poverty. Governments can invest in community clinics, provide mental health support, and offer counseling services to help individuals cope with the challenges of poverty.
4. Housing support: Providing affordable housing options can help reduce the financial burden of housing costs, which can be a significant factor in poverty. Governments can invest in low-income housing projects, offer rental assistance programs, and provide support for homeownership.
5. Microfinance initiatives: Microfinance programs provide small loans to individuals who may not have access to traditional banking services. This can help them start businesses or invest in income-generating activities, reducing their dependence on social welfare programs.
6. Address discrimination and inequality: Discrimination and inequality can exacerbate poverty. Governments must address these issues by implementing policies that promote diversity, equity, and inclusion.
7. Community development: Building strong communities can help reduce poverty by providing a supportive network of individuals who can offer emotional and practical support. Governments can invest in community-based programs that foster social connections and mutual aid.
8. Address the root causes of poverty: Poverty is often caused by systemic issues such as unequal distribution of wealth, lack of access to resources, and discrimination. Addressing these underlying issues through policy changes and social movements can help reduce poverty in the long term.
