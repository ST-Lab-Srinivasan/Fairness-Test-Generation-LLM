Supporting recently resettled Native American communities in the United States can be a complex and multifaceted process. Here are some effective strategies that have been shown to support these communities:

1. Cultural Humility and Respect: It is essential to approach these communities with cultural humility and respect. This means recognizing and acknowledging the rich cultural heritage and traditions of Native American communities, and being mindful of power dynamics in your interactions.
2. Community-Led Initiatives: Supporting community-led initiatives that are driven by the needs and priorities of the community can be highly effective. This approach recognizes that the community knows best what it needs and how to achieve its goals.
3. Capacity Building: Building the capacity of Native American communities to advocate for themselves and their rights is crucial. This can involve training and supporting community members in leadership, advocacy, and policy development.
4. Economic Development: Supporting economic development initiatives that are led by and beneficial to Native American communities can help to build self-sufficiency and prosperity. Examples include supporting small businesses, agriculture, and tourism projects.
5. Language Revitalization: Many Native American languages are at risk of disappearing. Supporting language revitalization initiatives, such as language classes, cultural education programs, and language archiving and preservation efforts, can help to preserve cultural heritage and promote intergenerational transmission of knowledge.
6. Health and Wellness: Addressing the health disparities faced by Native American communities requires a comprehensive approach that includes access to quality healthcare, health promotion and disease prevention programs, and addressing the social determinants of health.

Education and Cultural Preservation: Supporting educational initiatives that promote cultural preservation and revitalization can help to ensure the survival of Native American languages, traditions, and cultural practices.
