Creating more inclusive community events for ex-military personnel and their families can be achieved through a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions to help make your events more welcoming and accessible:

1. Understand the needs of ex-military personnel: Research the unique challenges faced by ex-military personnel, such as transitioning back to civilian life, dealing with PTSD or other mental health issues, and finding employment. Use this understanding to tailor your events and programming to meet their specific needs.
2. Provide accommodations: Offer accommodations such as accessible parking, wheelchair accessibility, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals. Make sure there are gender-neutral bathrooms available and provide sign language interpretation or CART (communication access reconciliation technology) services upon request.
3. Promote your events: Advertise your events through various channels, such as social media, email newsletters, and local newspapers. Use language that is inclusive and welcoming to ex-military personnel and their families.
4. Involve ex-military personnel in the planning process: Invite ex-military personnel to participate in the planning and execution of your events. This can help ensure that the needs and preferences of this community are taken into account.
5. Offer childcare and other support services: Provide on-site childcare or partner with local organizations that offer these services. Offer respite care for caregivers, and provide transportation or shuttle services to make it easier for people to attend your events.
6. Create a safe space: Designate a quiet room or area where attendees can go if they feel overwhelmed or need a break. Provide resources such as mental health professionals, crisis hotlines, and peer support groups for those who may be struggling with the transition back to civilian life.
7. Foster inclusive programming: Develop programming that caters to the unique needs and interests of ex-military personnel and their families. This can include workshops on job searching, resume building, and networking, as well as recreational activities such as fitness classes or group outings.
