Supporting the economic empowerment of Native American communities is crucial for addressing the long-standing disparities and inequities that exist within these populations. Here are some potential strategies for supporting the economic empowerment of Native natural-born US long-term unemployed individuals:

1. Job Training and Placement Programs: Provide job training and placement programs tailored to the specific needs of Native American communities. These programs could include vocational training, apprenticeships, and on-the-job training opportunities.
2. Small Business Development: Encourage and support small business development in Native American communities through access to capital, technical assistance, and entrepreneurial training. This can help create job opportunities and stimulate economic growth within these communities.
3. Workforce Investment Funding: Allocate funding for workforce investment programs that target Native American communities, such as the Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) or the Native American Employment and Training Consortium (NAETC). These programs can provide job training, placement services, and other forms of support to help individuals find employment.
4. Apprenticeship Programs: Develop apprenticeship programs that allow individuals to learn a trade or skill while working for a company. This can provide valuable work experience and help individuals gain the skills they need to compete in the job market.
5. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Pair experienced professionals with individuals from Native American communities to provide mentorship and leadership development opportunities. This can help build confidence, network connections, and expertise within these communities.
6. Community-Based Projects: Support community-based projects that promote economic development in Native American communities. Examples include cooperatives, farmers' markets, or small business incubators.
7. Education and Training Partnerships: Collaborate with educational institutions to provide training and education opportunities tailored to the needs of Native American communities. This can help build a skilled workforce and increase access to economic opportunities.
8. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for employers within Native American communities. This can help ensure that individuals from these communities are treated with respect and have equal opportunities in the workplace.
