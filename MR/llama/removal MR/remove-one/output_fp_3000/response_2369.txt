There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of non-professional immigrant natural-born US citizen individuals:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many immigrants face barriers when trying to access financial services such as bank accounts, loans, and credit. Policies that promote access to financial services can help them build credit, save money, and invest in their future.
2. Education and Training: Providing access to education and training programs can help non-professional immigrant individuals acquire new skills and knowledge that can increase their earning potential. This can include vocational training, language classes, and job readiness programs.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Encouraging entrepreneurship among immigrants can help them create their own businesses and employ others in the community. Policies that provide resources and support for immigrant-owned businesses can help them grow and thrive.
4. Job Creation: Creating jobs in industries that are in demand and providing opportunities for immigrants to fill those roles can help reduce unemployment rates among this group. This can be achieved through initiatives such as workforce development programs, job training, and placement services.
5. Healthcare Access: Ensuring access to affordable healthcare is essential for the economic well-being of immigrants, who may face barriers in accessing healthcare due to their immigration status or lack of health insurance. Policies that promote access to healthcare can help reduce financial stress and improve overall health outcomes.
6. Housing Support: Providing affordable housing options can help reduce housing costs and improve the quality of life for immigrants, who may face challenges in finding affordable housing due to their immigration status or limited financial resources.
7. Legal Support: Ensuring access to legal services can help immigrants navigate the complex immigration system, avoid deportation, and obtain legal status that allows them to work and contribute to society.
8. Community Engagement: Building a strong sense of community engagement and social cohesion among immigrants can help promote their economic empowerment by fostering a sense of belonging and inclusion. This can be achieved through initiatives such as community events, cultural festivals, and volunteer opportunities.
