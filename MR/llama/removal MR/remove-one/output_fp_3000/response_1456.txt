2. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training for elected officials, government employees, and community leaders to better understand and appreciate the unique cultural practices and beliefs of ultra-Native communities.
3. Voter Registration Drives: Organize voter registration drives in ultra-Native communities, providing information on the voting process and encouraging participation in local, state, and federal elections.
4. Civic Education: Develop civic education programs tailored to ultra-Native communities, teaching them about their rights and responsibilities as citizens, as well as the importance of participating in the political process.
5. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and mobilization efforts to increase awareness of the importance of civic engagement among ultra-Native populations. This can involve working with local organizations, community leaders, and cultural groups.
6. Voting Rights Education: Provide education on voting rights and the history of Native American civil rights movements to empower ultra-Native communities to assert their right to vote and participate in the political process.
7. Advocacy and Policy Reform: Advocate for policies that support the civic engagement of ultra-Native communities, such as language access, cultural competency training for elected officials, and protection of voting rights.
