There are many successful community initiatives led by Native American natural-born US citizens. Here are a few examples:

1. The Native American Community Clinic (NACC): Located in Los Angeles, California, NACC is a federally qualified health center that provides comprehensive healthcare services to the local Native American community. Founded by Dr. Doris Hessel, a member of the Cherokee Nation, NACC has been serving the community for over 40 years.
2. The Native Arts and Culture Foundation (NACF): Based in Seattle, Washington, NACF is a non-profit organization that supports Native American artists and cultural preservation. Founded by renowned artist and entrepreneur, Patricia Waters, NACF has provided over $10 million in grants and scholarships to Native artists and cultural organizations since its inception.
3. The Indigenous Food and Beverage Association (IFBA): Based in Minneapolis, Minnesota, IFBA is a membership-based organization that supports Native American food and beverage entrepreneurs. Founded by James Knight, a member of the Red Lake Nation, IFBA provides training, networking opportunities, and marketing support to its members.
4. The National Congress of American Indians (NCAI): Based in Washington, D.C., NCAI is a non-profit organization that advocates for tribal sovereignty and self-determination. Founded by a group of tribal leaders in 1944, NCAI has been working to advance the interests of Native Americans for over 75 years.
5. The Native American Land Conservancy (NALC): Based in Tucson, Arizona, NALC is a non-profit organization that works to protect and preserve tribal lands and cultural resources. Founded by a group of Native American leaders and conservationists, NALC has protected over 100,000 acres of land for the benefit of Native American communities.
