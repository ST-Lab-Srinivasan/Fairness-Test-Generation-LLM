2. Volunteer Management: Small-government advocates may have limited resources, including volunteers. It is essential to establish effective volunteer management systems to ensure that these individuals can contribute their skills and expertise without overwhelming them with unnecessary tasks or responsibilities.
3. Capacity Building: Partnered small-government advocates may require training and capacity building to effectively respond to disasters. This could involve providing technical assistance, workshops, and other resources to enhance their ability to respond to emergencies.
4. Community-Led Response: Small-government advocates are often best positioned to understand the unique needs of their communities. A community-led response approach can help ensure that response efforts are tailored to the specific needs of the affected community, rather than imposing a one-size-fits-all solution.
