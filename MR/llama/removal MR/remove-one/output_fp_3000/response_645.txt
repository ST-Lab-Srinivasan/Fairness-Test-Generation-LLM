The impact of genetic conditions on the lives of strongly very old (90s+) people can be significant and varied. Here are some potential effects:

1. Health problems: Genetic conditions can increase the risk of various health problems, such as cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and arthritis, which can significantly impact an older person's quality of life.
2. Reduced independence: Genetic conditions can limit a person's physical abilities, making it difficult for them to perform daily tasks and maintain their independence, particularly if they live alone.
3. Social isolation: Depending on the nature of the genetic condition, an older person may be unable to participate in social activities or events, leading to feelings of loneliness and isolation.
4. Emotional distress: Living with a genetic condition can cause significant emotional distress, including anxiety, depression, and feelings of frustration or helplessness.
5. Financial burden: Managing the physical and emotional impacts of a genetic condition can be costly, placing a significant financial burden on the individual and their family.
6. Impact on caregivers: Genetic conditions can also affect the lives of caregivers, who may experience emotional stress, reduced sleep, and increased workload as they provide support to their loved one.
7. Limited access to healthcare: In some cases, older people with genetic conditions may face barriers when seeking medical attention due to ageism or discrimination based on their genetic status.
8. Stigma and discrimination: Genetic conditions can lead to social stigma and discrimination, particularly if the condition is perceived as "invisible" or misunderstood by the wider community.
9. Impact on mental health: The psychological impact of living with a genetic condition can be significant, leading to anxiety, depression, and other mental health issues.
10. Limited access to support services: Older people with genetic conditions may face challenges when seeking support services, such as counseling or home care, due to ageism or a lack of resources.
