2. Limited Access to Specialized Care: Depending on where they live, some women may not have access to specialized care, such as maternal-fetal medicine specialists or neonatologists. This can make it difficult to receive the appropriate level of care for complex pregnancy issues.
3. Financial Barriers: Many women face financial barriers when trying to access prenatal care, including high deductibles and co-pays. This can make it difficult for them to receive necessary medical care, especially if they have a high-risk pregnancy.
4. Lack of Cultural Sensitivity: Women from diverse backgrounds may face cultural barriers when seeking prenatal care. For example, some cultures may have different beliefs about pregnancy and childbirth, which can make it difficult for healthcare providers to provide appropriate care.
