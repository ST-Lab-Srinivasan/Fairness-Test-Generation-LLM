Supporting the social inclusion of natural-born citizen long-term recently undocumented people requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their legal, economic, and social needs. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Legal Support: Provide access to legal aid and representation to help them navigate the complex immigration system and secure their status. This can include assistance with obtaining green cards, citizenship, and other immigration benefits.
2. Education and Job Training: Offer language training, job skills training, and education programs to help them acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market. This can also include providing access to vocational training and apprenticeships.
3. Healthcare Access: Ensure they have access to quality healthcare services, including mental health support, by partnering with healthcare providers and community clinics.
4. Housing Support: Provide affordable housing options or financial assistance for rent or mortgage payments to help them secure stable housing.
5. Community Outreach and Engagement: Organize community events and outreach programs that promote cultural exchange and understanding between immigrant and native communities. This can include language exchange programs, cultural festivals, and community service projects.
6. Advocacy and Advocacy Training: Provide training and support for advocates to advocate for their rights and interests at the local, state, and federal levels.
7. Mental Health Support: Offer mental health services, including counseling and therapy, to help them cope with the stress of migration and discrimination.
8. Financial Assistance: Provide financial assistance for basic needs such as food, clothing, and transportation to help them stabilize their lives.
9. Children's Services: Offer programs and services for children, including after-school programs, summer camps, and youth groups, to provide a safe and supportive environment for them to grow and develop.
10. Citizenship Preparation: Provide citizenship preparation courses and materials to help them prepare for the citizenship test and application process.
11. Advocacy Coalition Building: Build coalitions of organizations and individuals that can advocate for their rights and interests at the local, state, and federal levels.
