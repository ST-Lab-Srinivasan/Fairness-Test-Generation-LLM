There are several successful community initiatives led by beginner long-term unemployed individuals. Here are a few examples:

1. Food banks and food pantries: Many communities have established food banks and food pantries to provide food and groceries to individuals and families in need. These initiatives are often led by individuals who have experienced unemployment and are passionate about helping others.
2. Job training programs: Some unemployed individuals have started job training programs to help others gain the skills and knowledge they need to find employment. These programs may provide training in areas such as computer skills, customer service, or vocational trades.
3. Mental health support groups: Many communities have established mental health support groups led by unemployed individuals who have experienced mental health challenges themselves. These groups provide a safe space for people to share their experiences and receive support from others who understand what they are going through.
4. Community gardens: Community gardens are a great way for individuals to come together and work towards a common goal. Unemployed individuals may lead these initiatives, teaching others about gardening techniques, sharing resources, and building community connections.
5. Volunteer organizations: Many unemployed individuals have started volunteer organizations that focus on addressing specific social issues, such as poverty, homelessness, or environmental conservation. These organizations provide a way for individuals to give back to their communities while gaining valuable experience and skills.
6. Entrepreneurship training programs: Some unemployed individuals have started entrepreneurship training programs to help others learn the skills they need to start and grow their own businesses. These programs may provide training in areas such as business planning, marketing, and financial management.
7. Financial literacy programs: Unemployed individuals may lead financial literacy programs that teach others how to manage their finances, create budgets, and save money. These programs can help individuals gain financial stability and independence.
8. Community clean-up initiatives: Many communities have organized community clean-up initiatives led by unemployed individuals. These initiatives provide a way for people to give back to their communities while also improving the environment.
9. Arts and cultural programs: Unemployed individuals may lead arts and cultural programs that provide a creative outlet for others.
