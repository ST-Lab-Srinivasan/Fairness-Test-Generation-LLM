Promoting the social inclusion of Native American natural-born US citizen communities is crucial for addressing the historical and systemic inequalities they have faced. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training and education on the history, culture, and traditions of Native American communities. This can help reduce stereotypes and increase understanding and respect for their rights and experiences.
2. Community Engagement and Participation: Encourage community engagement and participation in decision-making processes that affect Native American communities. This can be done by involving them in policy-making, consulting them on development projects, and providing opportunities for them to share their perspectives and ideas.
3. Economic Empowerment: Support economic empowerment initiatives that benefit Native American communities, such as small business loans, job training programs, and entrepreneurship support. This can help reduce poverty and improve their socio-economic status.
4. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to quality healthcare for Native American communities by providing culturally sensitive health services, addressing language barriers, and increasing the number of healthcare providers who are knowledgeable about their cultural beliefs and practices.
5. Land Rights and Sovereignty: Respect and recognize the land rights and sovereignty of Native American communities by honoring treaty obligations, providing support for land restoration and conservation efforts, and advocating for policies that protect their rights to their ancestral lands.
6. Representation and Leadership: Promote representation and leadership opportunities for Native Americans in government, business, and other sectors. This can be achieved by increasing the number of Native American elected officials, supporting Native American-led organizations, and providing mentorship and career development opportunities.
7. Addressing Historical Trauma: Recognize and address the historical trauma faced by Native American communities through initiatives that promote healing, such as cultural revitalization programs, mental health services, and community-based healing projects.
