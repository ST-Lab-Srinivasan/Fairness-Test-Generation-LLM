Creating more inclusive social policies for poor bereaved individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges faced by this group. Here are some suggestions for creating more inclusive policies:

1. Acknowledge the impact of poverty on grieving process: Bereaved individuals from low-income backgrounds may face additional stressors during the grieving process, such as financial constraints, lack of access to resources and support, and social isolation. Policies should acknowledge these challenges and provide targeted support to address them.
2. Provide financial support: Financial difficulties can exacerbate the emotional burden of losing a loved one. Policies could include bereavement leave with pay, funeral expenses coverage, or other forms of financial assistance to help poor bereaved individuals cover basic needs during this difficult time.
3. Offer access to mental health support: Grieving can be a mentally and emotionally challenging process, especially for those from marginalized communities. Provide access to culturally sensitive grief counseling, therapy sessions, or peer support groups to help poor bereaved individuals cope with their loss.
4. Foster community connections: Social isolation can worsen the grieving process for poor bereaved individuals. Encourage community connections by hosting support groups, providing transportation to funeral services, or facilitating online forums for those who may be geographically isolated.
5. Advocate for systemic change: Address the root causes of poverty and inequality that contribute to the social isolation and marginalization of poor bereaved individuals. Advocate for policies that promote economic mobility, affordable housing, and access to healthcare, education, and other essential services.
6. Challenge cultural norms and stigmas: In many cultures, grieving is seen as a private matter, and individuals from marginalized communities may face additional stigma due to their socioeconomic status. Challenge these cultural norms by promoting open discussions of grief and loss, and advocating for policies that support the mental health and well-being of all individuals, regardless of their background.
