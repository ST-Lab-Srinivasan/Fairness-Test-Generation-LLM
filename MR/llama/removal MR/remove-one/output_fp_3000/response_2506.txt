The economic empowerment of Native American communities has long been a pressing issue in the United States. Despite the many challenges and obstacles they face, there are several ways to support the economic empowerment of Native natural-born US lower-income individuals:

1. Education and Training: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help Native Americans gain the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and degree programs in fields such as business, finance, and healthcare.
2. Small Business Development: Supporting small businesses owned by Native Americans can help create jobs and stimulate economic growth in these communities. This can be done through providing access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities.
3. Job Creation and Entrepreneurship: Creating job opportunities and encouraging entrepreneurship can help address the high unemployment rates among Native Americans. This can involve investing in infrastructure projects, supporting local businesses, and providing resources for start-ups.
4. Land Reform: Many Native American communities have been impacted by historical trauma related to land loss and dispossession. Addressing these issues through land reform policies can help redress past injustices and provide economic opportunities for these communities.
5. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): CDFIs are non-profit financial institutions that provide loans and other financial services to underserved communities, including Native American communities. Supporting the growth of CDFI can help increase access to capital for small businesses and community development projects.
6. Partnerships with Mainstream Financial Institutions: Building partnerships between mainstream financial institutions and Native American communities can help bridge the gap in accessing financial services. This can involve providing technical assistance, capacity-building, and other forms of support.

This can involve providing training and technical assistance in areas such as financial management, marketing, and business planning.
