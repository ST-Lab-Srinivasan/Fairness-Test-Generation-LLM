Native American recent immigrants to the US often face significant barriers to economic empowerment, including systemic racism, discrimination, and a lack of access to resources and opportunities. Here are some strategies that can help support their economic empowerment:

1. Access to Capital: Native American recent immigrants often face challenges in accessing capital for entrepreneurship or small business ventures due to lack of credit history, collateral, or access to traditional banking services. Alternative lending platforms, crowdfunding, and impact investors can provide alternative sources of funding.
2. Education and Skills Development: Many Native American recent immigrants may lack the necessary education or skills to compete in the US job market. Providing access to vocational training, language classes, and higher education can help them acquire the skills they need to succeed.
3. Job Placement and Career Advancement: Partnering with local businesses and organizations to provide job placement services and career advancement opportunities can help Native American recent immigrants access better-paying jobs and advance in their careers.
4. Micro-Enterprise Development: Encouraging and supporting micro-enterprises, such as small food vendors or artisans, can provide a pathway to economic empowerment for Native American recent immigrants who may not have access to traditional employment opportunities.
5. Community Land Trusts: Community land trusts can provide affordable and accessible land for housing and micro-enterprises, which can help reduce the cost of living and increase economic mobility for Native American recent immigrants.
6. Health and Wellness Services: Providing access to health and wellness services, such as mental health counseling, nutrition education, and health insurance enrollment assistance, can help address the social determinants of health that impact economic outcomes.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocating for policies that address the systemic barriers faced by Native American recent immigrants, such as discrimination, language barriers, and lack of access to resources, can lead to policy changes that promote greater economic empowerment.
