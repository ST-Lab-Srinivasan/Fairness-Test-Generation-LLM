As a PhD holder, you are likely aware of the importance of considering the specific needs of individuals with genetic conditions who are not disabled. Here are some ways to address these needs in your research:

1. Inclusive language: Use inclusive language when referring to individuals with genetic conditions. Avoid using terms that perpetuate stigma or stereotypes, such as "disabled" or "handicapped." Instead, use terms that recognize the diversity of individuals with genetic conditions, such as "people with genetic conditions."
2. Focus on abilities: Rather than focusing on deficits or limitations, focus on the abilities and strengths of individuals with genetic conditions. Highlight their unique skills, talents, and experiences that make them valuable contributors to society.
3. Collaborative research: Involve individuals with genetic conditions in the research process as collaborators, rather than just subjects. This can involve co-designing research questions, developing research methods, and interpreting findings.
4. Accessibility: Ensure that your research is accessible to all individuals, regardless of their abilities. Use plain language, provide alternative formats (e.g., braille, large print), and offer accommodations such as sign language interpretation or audio descriptions.
5. Inclusive data collection: Collect data in a way that is inclusive of individuals with genetic conditions. For example, use open-ended questions to allow for more detailed responses, and avoid using multiple-choice questions that may be difficult for individuals with cognitive impairments to complete.
6. Intersectionality: Consider the intersectional identities of individuals with genetic conditions, including their race, ethnicity, gender, sexuality, and socioeconomic status. Avoid making assumptions about individuals based on these factors, and instead, listen to their experiences and perspectives.
7. Empowerment: Empower individuals with genetic conditions to take an active role in shaping the research agenda. Provide opportunities for them to share their experiences, provide feedback, and collaborate on research projects.
