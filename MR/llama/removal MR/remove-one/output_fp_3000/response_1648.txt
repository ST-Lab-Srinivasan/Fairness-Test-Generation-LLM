It is important to recognize that the term "illegal alien" is a pejorative and dehumanizing label that can have negative consequences for the individuals it is applied to. It is preferred to use terms such as "undocumented immigrant" or "unauthorized migrant" when referring to people who are residing in a country without proper documentation.

The impact of immigration status on the lives of undocumented immigrants can be significant and far-reaching, affecting their access to basic rights and services, as well as their overall well-being. Some of the key impacts include:

1. Lack of access to healthcare: Many undocumented immigrants are unable to access medical care, including preventive care, due to fear of deportation or lack of coverage. This can lead to worse health outcomes and a higher burden on emergency services.
2. Limited access to education: Undocumented immigrants may face barriers in accessing education, including K-12 schools and higher education institutions, due to their immigration status.
3. Economic insecurity: Undocumented immigrants often face job discrimination, lower pay, and limited access to social safety nets, such as unemployment insurance and workers' compensation.
4. Fear of deportation: The constant threat of deportation can lead to feelings of anxiety, depression, and PTSD among undocumented immigrants, particularly those who have been in the country for an extended period of time or have U.S.-born children.
5. Social isolation: Undocumented immigrants may avoid seeking help from law enforcement or other authorities due to fear of deportation, leading to social isolation and a lack of access to support services.
6. Lack of legal protection: Undocumented immigrants are often unable to access legal protections, such as workplace safety regulations or antidiscrimination laws, due to their immigration status.
