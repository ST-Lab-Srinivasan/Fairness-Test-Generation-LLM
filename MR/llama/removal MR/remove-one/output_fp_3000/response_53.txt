Supporting the social inclusion of late 30s recent immigrants to Canada can be a complex and multifaceted process. Here are some ways that individuals, communities, and organizations can help promote social inclusion:

1. Language Training: Providing language training programs specifically tailored for late 30s recent immigrants can help them improve their communication skills in English or French, which is essential for integrating into Canadian society.
2. Cultural Orientation: Offering cultural orientation programs that teach about Canadian values, customs, and practices can help newcomers understand the country's social norms and expectations, reducing feelings of isolation and confusion.
3. Job Training and Placement: Providing job training and placement services can help late 30s recent immigrants gain the skills and experience needed to find employment in their field, which is critical for social inclusion.
4. Networking Opportunities: Organizing networking events that bring together newcomers with established professionals in their industry can provide valuable connections and mentorship opportunities, helping them build a professional network in Canada.
5. Community Outreach Programs: Developing community outreach programs that engage late 30s recent immigrants in local events and activities can help them connect with other members of the community, fostering a sense of belonging and inclusion.
6. Access to Education and Healthcare: Ensuring access to quality education and healthcare services can help late 30s recent immigrants navigate the Canadian healthcare system and access educational opportunities that can improve their socio-economic status.
7. Housing Support: Providing affordable and safe housing options can help reduce stress and anxiety associated with finding suitable accommodation, allowing newcomers to focus on building a new life in Canada.
8. Mentorship Programs: Establishing mentorship programs that pair late 30s recent immigrants with experienced professionals or volunteers can provide guidance and support as they navigate their new life in Canada.
9. Volunteer Opportunities: Encouraging late 30s recent immigrants to participate in volunteer activities can help them build connections within the community, develop new skills, and integrate into Canadian society.
