Ensuring the healthcare needs of Native American recent immigrants in the US is crucial, as they face unique challenges due to their cultural and social identity. Here are some ways to ensure that their healthcare needs are met:

1. Culturally sensitive care: Provide culturally sensitive care by hiring healthcare professionals who understand the beliefs, values, and practices of Native American communities. This can help build trust and improve the quality of care.
2. Language support: Offer language support services, such as interpreters or translated materials, to ensure that patients can communicate effectively with healthcare providers.
3. Cultural competency training: Provide cultural competency training for healthcare professionals to help them understand the unique needs and experiences of Native American communities.
4. Access to specialized care: Ensure access to specialized care, such as genetic counseling or mental health services, that may be particularly important for individuals with a genetic condition.
5. Community outreach and engagement: Engage in community outreach and education efforts to raise awareness about the importance of healthcare and how to access it. This can help build trust and increase the likelihood that individuals will seek care when they need it.
6. Collaboration with tribal organizations: Work closely with tribal organizations and community leaders to ensure that healthcare services are tailored to meet the specific needs of Native American communities.
7. Telehealth services: Utilize telehealth services to provide access to healthcare for individuals who may face barriers in accessing care, such as those living in rural areas or those with mobility issues.
8. Addressing historical trauma: Recognize the historical trauma that Native American communities have experienced and work to address its ongoing impacts on their health and well-being. This can involve incorporating traditional healing practices and cultural activities into healthcare services.
9. Health promotion and disease prevention: Develop culturally appropriate health promotion and disease prevention programs that take into account the unique needs and experiences of Native American communities.
10. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies and changes at the local, state, and federal levels to address systemic barriers to healthcare access and quality for Native American communities.
