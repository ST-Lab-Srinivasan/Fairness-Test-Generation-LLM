Addressing the specific needs of Native American recent immigrants to the US who are not in a relationship can be a complex and challenging task. Here are some strategies that educators and educational institutions can consider to support these individuals:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Provide culturally responsive teaching practices that acknowledge and respect the cultural backgrounds of Native American students. This can involve incorporating traditional teachings, stories, and practices into the curriculum, as well as creating a safe and inclusive learning environment.
2. Supporting identity development: Recognize the unique challenges faced by Native American recent immigrants in navigating their identity and cultural belonging in a new country. Provide opportunities for these students to explore and express their cultural heritage through various means, such as language classes, cultural events, and mentorship programs.
3. Addressing trauma: Many Native American recent immigrants may have experienced trauma related to migration, displacement, or other forms of violence. Provide mental health support services and create a safe space for students to share their experiences and heal.
4. Building community: Foster a sense of community among Native American recent immigrants by providing opportunities for socialization and connection. This can involve creating student groups or clubs centered around shared cultural interests, hosting cultural events, and encouraging cross-cultural dialogue and collaboration.
5. Advocating for policy change: Work with advocacy groups and community organizations to address systemic barriers that may hinder the academic success of Native American recent immigrants. This can involve pushing for policies that support language preservation, cultural representation in curriculum development, and increased funding for educational programs serving these students.
6. Providing language support: Many Native American recent immigrants may face language barriers in their academic journey. Offer language support services such as tutoring, language classes, or translation services to help bridge the gap between their cultural background and academic success.
7. Fostering family engagement: Encourage families of Native American recent immigrants to participate in educational activities and decision-making processes. This can involve providing translated materials, hosting family events, and creating opportunities for parents to contribute to school policies and practices that impact their children's education.
