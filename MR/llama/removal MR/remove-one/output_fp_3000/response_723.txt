4. Create a safe and inclusive environment: Train staff and volunteers on how to interact with attendees who may have different abilities or cultural backgrounds. Encourage attendees to respect each other's boundaries and provide a code of conduct for the event.
5. Offer sensory-friendly activities: Provide activities that are tailored to the needs of individuals with sensory processing issues, such as tactile art projects or calm spaces for those who need to take breaks from sensory input.
6. Accommodate dietary restrictions: Offer vegetarian and gluten-free options at events where food is involved, and provide clear labeling of ingredients to help individuals make informed choices.
