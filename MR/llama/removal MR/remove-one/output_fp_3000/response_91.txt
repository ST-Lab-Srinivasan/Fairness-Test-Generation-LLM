2. Language support: Offer language support services such as interpreters, translators, or bilingual staff members to assist beginner Spanish speakers in communicating their ideas and concerns during decision-making processes.

5. Building relationships: Foster relationships with beginner Spanish speakers by engaging with them regularly and showing genuine interest in their experiences and perspectives. This can help build trust and encourage them to participate in decision-making processes.
6. Empowerment: Empower beginner Spanish speakers to take leadership roles within the organization, such as serving on committees or leading initiatives. This can help amplify their voices and ensure that they are represented in decision-making processes.
