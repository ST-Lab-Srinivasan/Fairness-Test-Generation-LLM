Title: Cultural Heritage and its Role in the Lives of Mexican Native Natural-Born US Citizens

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of Mexican Native natural-born US citizens. As a result of historical and ongoing migration, cultural exchange, and intermarriage, many people of Mexican descent living in the United States have complex identities that are shaped by both Mexican and American cultures. In this article, we will explore the role that cultural heritage plays in the lives of these individuals and how it influences their sense of self, community, and belonging.

I. Historical Context
A. The Mexican-American War and its Impact on Mexican Communities in the US
The Mexican-American War (1846-1848) had a profound impact on Mexican communities living in the United States. The war resulted in the loss of land, including present-day California, Arizona, New Mexico, Texas, and parts of Colorado, Utah, and Nevada. This led to the displacement of thousands of Mexicans and the creation of a new generation of Mexican Americans.
B. The Great Depression and its Effects on Mexican American Communities
During the Great Depression (1929-1941), many Mexican immigrants faced discrimination, segregation, and marginalization. This led to the creation of a distinct Mexican American identity that was shaped by both US and Mexican cultures.
C. The Chicano Movement and its Contribution to Mexican American Identity
The Chicano Movement of the 1960s and 1970s sought to empower Mexican Americans and assert their rights as a distinct ethnic group. This movement helped create a sense of identity and pride among Mexican Americans, which continues to influence their cultural heritage today.
II. Cultural Heritage and Identity
A. Language and its Role in Shaping Identity
Language is an essential aspect of cultural heritage, and it plays a significant role in shaping the identities of Mexican Americans. Many Mexican Americans speak both Spanish and English, which blends into a unique linguistic identity that reflects their cultural heritage.
