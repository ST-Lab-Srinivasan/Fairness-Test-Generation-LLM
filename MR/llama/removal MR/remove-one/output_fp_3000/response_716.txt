Creating inclusive community programs for physically able-bodied very old individuals (ages 90 and above) requires careful consideration of their unique needs and abilities. Here are some strategies to create more inclusive programs:

1. Consult with older adults: Engage in consultation processes with older adults, listening to their concerns, interests, and preferences when designing programs. This will help ensure that the program is tailored to their needs and will be more likely to attract and retain participants.
2. Offer a variety of activities: Provide a range of activities that cater to different interests, abilities, and preferences. For example, offer gentle exercise classes, creative workshops, social events, and educational programs.
3. Accommodate mobility challenges: Ensure that the program venue is easily accessible and has amenities that support individuals with mobility challenges, such as wheelchair ramps, elevators, and adaptive seating.
4. Use technology to facilitate participation: Utilize technology like video conferencing or online platforms to enable older adults who may have difficulty traveling or participating in in-person activities. This can help expand the reach of your programs and make them more accessible to a wider audience.
5. Foster intergenerational connections: Encourage interactions between older adults and younger generations through joint activities, mentorship opportunities, or volunteer work. This can help build bridges between different age groups and create a more inclusive community.
6. Provide opportunities for social connection: Older adults often face social isolation, which can have negative effects on their mental and physical health. Create programs that facilitate social interaction and provide opportunities for older adults to connect with others in a supportive environment.
7. Address cultural and language barriers: Ensure that your programs are inclusive of diverse cultures and languages by providing interpreters, translating materials, or hosting events at locations convenient for participants from different backgrounds.
8. Involve community partners: Collaborate with local organizations, such as senior centers, libraries, or religious institutions, to reach a broader audience and create more inclusive programs.
