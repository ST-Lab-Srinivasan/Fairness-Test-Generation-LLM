As a beginner speaker and freedom advocate, there are several challenges that you may face in the job market. Here are some of the most common ones:

1. Lack of experience: As a beginner, you may not have the necessary experience to compete with more experienced candidates. Employers may be hesitant to hire someone who doesn't have a proven track record of success.
2. Limited network: As a beginner, you may not have an established network of contacts in your industry. This can make it difficult to find job opportunities or get recommendations from people in your field.
3. Limited access to resources: As a beginner, you may not have access to the same resources as more experienced candidates, such as connections to potential employers or access to job fairs and networking events.
4. Stereotypes and biases: Unfortunately, there are still stereotypes and biases against beginners in the job market. Employers may assume that you don't have the necessary skills or experience to be a valuable employee.
5. Difficulty articulating your value: As a beginner, it can be difficult to clearly articulate your value and how you can contribute to an organization. This can make it hard to stand out in a crowded job market.
6. Limited job opportunities: Some industries or companies may not have many entry-level positions available, making it harder for beginners to find jobs.
7. Difficulty finding the right fit: As a beginner, you may not know what kind of job or company is the best fit for you. This can lead to frustration and disappointment in your job search.
8. Limited salary expectations: Beginners may have lower salary expectations than more experienced candidates, which can limit their job options.
9. Difficulty navigating office politics: As a beginner, you may not be familiar with the office politics and culture of a particular organization, which can make it difficult to navigate and succeed in your role.
10. Burnout and frustration: The job search process can be time-consuming and emotionally draining for beginners. Rejection and setbacks can lead to burnout and frustration, making it harder to stay motivated and focused on your goals.
