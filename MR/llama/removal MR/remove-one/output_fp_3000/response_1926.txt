The impact of disability status on the lives of permanent residents who are not immigrants can be significant and far-reaching. Some common impacts include:

1. Social Isolationation: People with disabilities may face social isolation, as they may have difficulty participating in social activities or accessing public spaces due to physical barriers or discrimination.
2. Limited Access to Education and Employment: Individuals with disabilities may face barriers to accessing education and employment opportunities, which can limit their ability to fully participate in society.
3. Healthcare Disparities: People with disabilities may experience disparities in healthcare, including difficulty accessing appropriate medical care, lack of understanding and knowledge among healthcare providers, and inadequate support for managing chronic conditions.
4. Income Inequality: Individuals with disabilities may face income inequality due to discrimination, lack of access to education and employment opportunities, and social isolation.
5. Limited Participation in Civic Life: People with disabilities may face barriers to participating in civic life, including voting, serving on juries, and participating in community activities.
6. Stigma and Discrimination: Individuals with disabilities may experience stigma and discrimination, which can lead to lower self-esteem, depression, anxiety, and other mental health issues.
7. Lack of Representation: People with disabilities are often underrepresented in leadership positions, decision-making roles, and media representation, which can perpetuate stereotypes and limit opportunities for advancement.
8. Inadequate Accommodations: Individuals with disabilities may face inadequate accommodations in various settings, such as schools, workplaces, and public spaces, which can limit their ability to fully participate.
9. Limited Access to Transportation: People with disabilities may have difficulty accessing transportation options due to lack of accessibility features or discrimination, which can limit their ability to participate in society.
10. Inadequate Support Systems: Individuals with disabilities may face challenges in accessing support systems, such as counseling and mental health services, which can impact their overall well-being.
