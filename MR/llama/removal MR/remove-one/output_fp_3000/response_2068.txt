Creating more inclusive community events for civilians living in poverty requires a thoughtful and strategic approach. Here are some suggestions to help make your events more accessible and welcoming:

1. Identify the needs of the target audience: Before planning any event, it's essential to understand the needs and challenges faced by the people you want to reach. Conduct surveys or focus groups to gather information about their living conditions, access to resources, and any barriers they face in their daily lives.
2. Choose an accessible location: Select a venue that is easily accessible for people with disabilities and those living in poverty. Consider factors like proximity to public transportation, parking options, and wheelchair accessibility.
3. Offer financial assistance: Provide financial support to attendees who may struggle to cover event-related costs, such as transportation or childcare. You can also offer free or discounted admission prices for those living in poverty.
4. Promote the event widely: Utilize various marketing channels to reach your target audience, including social media, flyers, and local community centers. Consider partnering with organizations that serve low-income populations to help spread the word.
5. Provide accommodations for attendees: Ensure that the event space is inclusive by providing accommodations such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, or braille materials.
6. Offer cultural sensitivity training: Provide cultural sensitivity training to volunteers and staff to help them understand and respect the diverse backgrounds of attendees.
7. Foster a welcoming atmosphere: Create a comfortable and inviting environment by offering refreshments, providing a safe space for attendees to relax, and encouraging attendees to share their experiences and connect with others.
8. Collaborate with local organizations: Partner with local organizations that serve low-income populations to help promote the event and ensure that it is accessible to those who may face barriers to attending.
9. Provide childcare and transportation options: Offer free or discounted childcare and transportation services to attendees who may struggle to access these resources.
