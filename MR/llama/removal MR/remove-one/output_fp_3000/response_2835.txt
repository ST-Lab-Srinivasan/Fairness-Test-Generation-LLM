Section 1: Understanding Cultural Heritage
Cultural heritage refers to the shared knowledge, traditions, and practices that are passed down from one generation to the next within a particular group or community. It encompasses a wide range of elements, including language, religion, customs, beliefs, and art. For openly mildly extreme ultra-liberal individuals, cultural heritage plays a crucial role in shaping their identities by providing a sense of belonging and connection to their community.
