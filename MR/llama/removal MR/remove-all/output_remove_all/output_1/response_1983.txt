Celebrating the cultural contributions of all communities, regardless of their socio-economic status, is crucial for promoting diversity and inclusivity. Here are some ways to recognize and celebrate the cultural contributions of ethnically diverse groups, including those who are not above poverty line:

1. Community Events: Organize community events that showcase the cultural diversity of the local population. Invite artists, musicians, dancers, and food vendors from different ethnic backgrounds to participate. Provide a platform for underrepresented groups to share their talents and traditions with the broader community.
2. Cultural Fairs: Host cultural fairs that highlight the unique customs, beliefs, and practices of diverse ethnic groups. Offer workshops, demonstrations, and performances that showcase traditional crafts, music, and dance. Encourage participants to share their stories and experiences, and provide opportunities for intercultural dialogue and exchange.
3. Art Exhibitions: Organize art exhibitions that feature the works of local artists from diverse ethnic backgrounds. Provide a platform for emerging artists to showcase their talents and gain recognition within the community. Consider collaborating with local galleries, museums, or cultural centers to reach a wider audience.
4. Language Classes: Offer language classes that cater to the needs of diverse ethnic groups. Provide opportunities for individuals to learn each other's languages and explore the rich linguistic heritage of the community. Encourage intercultural communication and understanding through language exchange programs.
5. Food Festivals: Host food festivals that celebrate the culinary diversity of the local population. Invite chefs, restaurateurs, and food vendors from different ethnic backgrounds to showcase their specialties. Provide a platform for underrepresented groups to share their traditional cuisines and cooking techniques with the broader community.
6. Music Festivals: Organize music festivals that feature local musicians from diverse ethnic backgrounds. Provide opportunities for emerging artists to perform and gain recognition within the community. Consider hosting concerts, jam sessions, or workshops that highlight traditional musical styles and instruments.
