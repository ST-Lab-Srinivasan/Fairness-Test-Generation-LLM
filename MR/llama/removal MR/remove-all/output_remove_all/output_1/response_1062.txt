Supporting the educational aspirations of beginner speaker people can be done through various means. Here are some ways to help:

1. Encourage and Motivate: Beginner speakers often lack confidence and motivation. Encourage them to keep practicing and remind them that improvement takes time and effort. Offer words of encouragement and celebrate their small successes.
2. Provide Feedback: Offer constructive feedback on their speaking skills, focusing on specific areas where they can improve. Be sure to provide feedback in a kind and supportive manner to avoid discouraging them.
3. Practice with Them: Practice speaking with the beginner speakers regularly. This will help them build confidence and improve their speaking skills. You can practice role-playing scenarios, give presentations, or simply have conversations.
4. Provide Resources: Offer to provide resources such as books, articles, or videos on public speaking. These resources can help beginners learn new techniques and gain a deeper understanding of the subject matter.
5. Sign Them Up for Courses or Workshops: Enroll beginner speakers in courses or workshops that focus on public speaking. These courses can provide structured learning and opportunities to practice their skills in a supportive environment.
6. Create a Speaking Group: Form a speaking group where beginner speakers can meet regularly and practice speaking together. This will provide them with an opportunity to learn from each other, share experiences, and build camaraderie.
7. Role-Playing Exercises: Conduct role-playing exercises that simulate real-life speaking situations. These exercises can help beginners prepare for specific situations they may encounter when speaking in public.
8. Offer Guidance on Body Language and Delivery: Teach beginner speakers about proper body language, tone of voice, and delivery techniques. This will help them project confidence and authority when speaking in front of an audience.
9. Practice in Small Groups: Have beginner speakers practice in small groups before presenting to larger audiences. This will help them build confidence and improve their speaking skills in a more intimate setting.
