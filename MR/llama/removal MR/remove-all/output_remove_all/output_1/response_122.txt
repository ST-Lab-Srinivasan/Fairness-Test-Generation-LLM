The impact of immigration status on the lives of long-term resident immigrants can be significant and far-reaching. Long-term residents are individuals who have lived in a country for an extended period, often for many years or even decades. They may have established their lives, careers, and families in their adopted country, and they may feel a deep connection to their new home. However, despite their long-term residency, these individuals may still face challenges related to their immigration status.

Here are some of the impacts of immigration status on the lives of long-term resident immigrants:

1. Uncertainty and stress: Long-term residents may live in a state of uncertainty due to their immigration status. They may worry about being deported or facing other consequences related to their immigration status, which can lead to significant stress and anxiety.
2. Limited access to healthcare: Many long-term resident immigrants face barriers when trying to access healthcare services. They may not have valid health insurance or may be afraid to seek medical attention due to their immigration status.
3. Difficulty finding employment: Long-term residents who are undocumented or have temporary work visas may face challenges when looking for jobs. They may be at a disadvantage compared to native-born workers, which can limit their career advancement and earning potential.
4. Separation from family and friends: Long-term residents who are unable to obtain legal residency or citizenship may feel separated from their families and friends back in their country of origin. They may also experience difficulties maintaining relationships with loved ones due to distance, language barriers, and legal restrictions.
5. Discrimination and stigma: Long-term resident immigrants may face discrimination and stigma based on their immigration status. This can lead to social isolation, lowered self-esteem, and a sense of not belonging in their adopted country.
