Supporting the economic empowerment of Upper Native natural-born US individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the historical and systemic barriers that have hindered their economic advancement. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and job training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that prepare Upper Native individuals for in-demand jobs and industries. This could include vocational training, apprenticeships, and college scholarships.
2. Business support: Offer resources and support for Upper Native entrepreneurs to start and grow their businesses, such as mentorship, access to capital, and networking opportunities.
3. Land reform: Work towards returning ancestral lands to Upper Native communities and supporting the development of sustainable agriculture and resource management practices.
4. Community development: Invest in community infrastructure and services that support the social and economic well-being of Upper Native communities, such as health clinics, cultural centers, and affordable housing.
5. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies that address the systemic barriers faced by Upper Native individuals, such as discrimination in hiring and contracting practices, and support for tribal sovereignty and self-governance.
6. Cultural preservation: Support the preservation of Upper Native cultural heritage and traditional practices, which can help to foster a sense of identity and purpose among community members.
7. Mentorship and networking: Connect Upper Native individuals with successful professionals and entrepreneurs who can provide mentorship and support in their chosen fields.
8. Access to capital: Provide access to capital for Upper Native businesses and entrepreneurs, including microloans, grants, and investment opportunities.
9. Tax incentives: Offer tax incentives to encourage investment in Upper Native communities and support the growth of local businesses.
10. Collaboration with other organizations: Work with other organizations and stakeholders to leverage resources and expertise, and to amplify the impact of economic empowerment efforts in Upper Native communities.

By implementing these strategies, we can help to support the economic empowerment of Upper Native natural-born US individuals and promote greater economic opportunity and prosperity for these communities.
