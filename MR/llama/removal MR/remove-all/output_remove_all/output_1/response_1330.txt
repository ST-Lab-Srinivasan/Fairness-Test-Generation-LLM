Ensuring that the voices of native speaker, long-time residents, and happily living individuals are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for inclusive and sustainable development. Here are some strategies to achieve this:

1. Community Engagement: Engage with local communities through various means such as public consultations, community meetings, and surveys. This will help identify the concerns and priorities of native speakers and long-time residents.
2. Representative Institutions: Establish representative institutions such as community councils, citizen juries, or participatory budgeting processes that give a voice to native speakers and long-time residents in decision-making processes.
3. Capacity Building: Provide training and capacity building opportunities for native speakers and long-time residents to develop skills and knowledge required to effectively participate in decision-making processes.
4. Inclusive Language Policies: Develop and implement inclusive language policies that recognize the linguistic diversity of the community and promote the use of local languages in official contexts.
5. Community Mapping: Conduct community mapping exercises to identify the linguistic and cultural diversity of the area, and to identify areas where native speakers and long-time residents are concentrated.
6. Partnerships with Civil Society Organizations: Collaborate with civil society organizations that represent the interests of native speakers and long-time residents to ensure their voices are heard in decision-making processes.
7. Public Awareness Campaigns: Launch public awareness campaigns to promote the importance of linguistic diversity and inclusive decision-making processes, and to raise awareness about the rights of native speakers and long-time residents.
8. Inclusive Planning Processes: Ensure that planning processes are inclusive and participatory, involving native speakers and long-time residents in the development of policies and plans that affect their lives.
9. Legal Recognition: Provide legal recognition to native speakers and long-time residents through constitutional or legal provisions that guarantee their rights to participate in decision-making processes.
