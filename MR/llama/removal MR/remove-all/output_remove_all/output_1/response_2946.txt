Highlighting the achievements of newly arrived Canadians can be a great way to celebrate their contributions to Canadian society. Here are some ways to do so:

1. Host a welcome event: Organize a welcome event for recently arrived Canadians, where they can meet with local community members and share their experiences. This could include a potluck dinner, a language exchange, or a cultural showcase.
2. Create a mentorship program: Pair newly arrived Canadians with experienced local volunteers who can provide guidance on navigating Canadian society and culture. This could involve regular meetings, skill-sharing activities, and social events.
3. Offer language classes: Provide language classes or resources specifically tailored to the needs of recently arrived Canadians. This could include one-on-one tutoring, group classes, or online resources.
4. Connect them with local organizations: Introduce newly arrived Canadians to local organizations that can provide support and resources for their specific needs. For example, you could connect them with job training programs, mental health services, or parenting groups.
5. Celebrate cultural events: Host cultural events that showcase the diversity of Canadian culture and encourage intercultural exchange. This could include festivals, concerts, or workshops on traditional crafts and cooking techniques.
6. Create a community newsletter: Produce a regular newsletter that highlights the achievements and stories of recently arrived Canadians. This could include profiles of successful individuals, local news and events, and resources for settling in Canada.
7. Host a job fair: Organize a job fair specifically targeting recently arrived Canadians, where they can meet with local employers and learn about job opportunities. This could also involve workshops on resume writing, interview skills, and career development.
8. Provide childcare services: Offer childcare services or resources for newly arrived parents, including drop-in centers, playgroups, and parenting classes.
