Supporting the social inclusion of natural-born people with intellectual disabilities or other developmental disabilities can be achieved through various means. Here are some ways to promote their social inclusion:

1. Education and Awareness: Educate the community about the capabilities and potential of individuals with intellectual disabilities. Raise awareness about the importance of inclusivity and the benefits of supporting their social inclusion.
2. Inclusive Employment: Encourage businesses to hire individuals with intellectual disabilities in inclusive work environments. Provide job training and placement services to help them secure employment.
3. Social Events: Organize social events that bring together individuals with intellectual disabilities and the broader community. This can include picnics, game nights, or other activities that promote social interaction and inclusion.
4. Volunteer Opportunities: Offer volunteer opportunities for individuals without intellectual disabilities to interact and support those with intellectual disabilities. This can include helping with daily tasks, providing transportation, or simply being a friend and companion.
5. Advocacy Groups: Support advocacy groups that promote the rights and well-being of individuals with intellectual disabilities. These groups can help amplify their voices and promote their inclusion in society.
6. Healthcare Services: Ensure that healthcare services are inclusive and accessible to individuals with intellectual disabilities. Provide training for healthcare professionals on how to support individuals with intellectual disabilities in a respectful and inclusive manner.
7. Community-Based Programs: Develop community-based programs that provide opportunities for individuals with intellectual disabilities to engage in social and recreational activities with their peers. These programs can help build social connections and promote inclusion.
8. Technology Accessibility: Ensure that technology is accessible and inclusive for individuals with intellectual disabilities. Provide training on the use of assistive technologies, such as screen readers or voice-controlled devices, to help them participate in digital environments.
9. Public Transportation: Advocate for public transportation systems that are accessible and inclusive for individuals with intellectual disabilities. Provide training for transit operators on how to support individuals with intellectual disabilities in a respectful and inclusive manner.
