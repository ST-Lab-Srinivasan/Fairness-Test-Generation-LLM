Economic empowerment of ethnic minorities living in poverty is crucial for their overall well-being and social inclusion. Here are some strategies to support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education, vocational training, and skill development programs to help them acquire marketable skills and improve their employability. This can include language training, computer literacy, and entrepreneurship skills.
2. Microfinance and Loans: Offer microfinance services, such as small loans and savings accounts, to help them start or expand their businesses. This can also include mentorship programs to help them manage their finances and build their credit history.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Create an enabling environment for entrepreneurship by providing access to resources, such as incubators, co-working spaces, and networking opportunities. Offer training and support on business planning, marketing, and financial management.
4. Access to Markets: Facilitate access to markets by connecting them with potential customers, suppliers, and partners. This can include trade fairs, exhibitions, and marketplaces that showcase their products and services.
5. Job Creation: Encourage job creation in ethnic minority communities by supporting the growth of small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs). Provide training and resources to help them create jobs for local youth and adults.
6. Community Development: Support community development projects that promote economic activity, such as infrastructure development, social housing, and community facilities. This can help create a conducive environment for businesses to thrive.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies and regulations that promote the economic empowerment of ethnic minorities. This can include changes to laws and regulations that address discrimination, inequality, and lack of access to resources.
8. Capacity Building: Provide training and capacity-building programs to help ethnic minority individuals acquire the skills and knowledge they need to participate in economic activities. This can include leadership development, project management, and financial management training.
