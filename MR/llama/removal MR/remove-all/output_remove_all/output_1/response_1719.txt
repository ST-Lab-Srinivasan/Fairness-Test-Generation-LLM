Spouse people, also known as caregivers or family members, play a crucial role in the healthcare system. They provide emotional, practical, and financial support to their loved ones who are patients in hospitals, clinics, or undergoing treatments at home. However, caring for a sick family member can be challenging, and spouse people often face various difficulties in the healthcare system. Here are some of the common challenges faced by spouse people:

1. Lack of support: Spouse people often feel isolated and alone in their caregiving journey. They may not have access to adequate emotional or practical support, leading to feelings of burnout, stress, and depression.
2. Limited access to information: Spouse people may struggle to get accurate and timely information about their loved one's condition, treatment options, and prognosis. This lack of information can lead to confusion, anxiety, and uncertainty.
3. Financial strain: Caring for a sick family member can be expensive, leading to financial strain on the family. Spouse people may need to take time off work, incur debt, or use up savings to cover medical bills, medication, and other expenses.
4. Emotional toll: Caring for a loved one can be emotionally draining, leading to feelings of sadness, anger, guilt, and helplessness. Spouse people may struggle to cope with the emotional demands of caregiving while maintaining their own mental health.
5. Lack of respite: Caring for a sick family member can be a 24/7 responsibility, leaving spouse people little time for self-care or personal activities. They may not have access to adequate respite care or support services, leading to feelings of exhaustion and burnout.
6. Limited access to healthcare professionals: Spouse people may face challenges in accessing healthcare professionals, such as doctors, nurses, or social workers, due to limited availability, long wait times, or language barriers.
