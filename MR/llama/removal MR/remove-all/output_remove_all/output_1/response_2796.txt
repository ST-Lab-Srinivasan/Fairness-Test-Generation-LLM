Creating more inclusive social policies for jobless partnered individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the various challenges faced by these individuals. Here are some strategies that policymakers and stakeholders can consider:

1. Addressing the gender pay gap: One of the primary reasons why jobless partnered individuals struggle is due to the gender pay gap. Policymakers can implement policies that promote equal pay for equal work, provide family-friendly benefits, and support women's economic empowerment.
2. Flexible work arrangements: Offering flexible work arrangements can help jobless partnered individuals balance their work and family responsibilities. This can include telecommuting options, part-time work, or flexible hours.
3. Job training and placement programs: Providing job training and placement programs can help jobless partnered individuals gain the skills they need to find employment. These programs can focus on in-demand industries and occupations, such as technology, healthcare, or renewable energy.
4. Financial support: Offering financial support, such as subsidized childcare, transportation assistance, or emergency savings accounts, can help jobless partnered individuals cover their basic needs while they are searching for employment.
5. Addressing systemic barriers: Policymakers must address systemic barriers that prevent jobless partnered individuals from finding employment. This includes addressing discrimination, improving access to education and training, and providing support for marginalized communities.
6. Encouraging entrepreneurship: Encouraging entrepreneurship can provide jobless partnered individuals with an alternative pathway to economic stability. Policymakers can offer resources and support for starting a business, such as mentorship programs, access to funding, or business incubators.
7. Building community connections: Building community connections can help jobless partnered individuals find support and resources beyond what is offered through government programs. This includes connecting them with local non-profits, community organizations, or religious groups that provide financial assistance, childcare, or other forms of support.
