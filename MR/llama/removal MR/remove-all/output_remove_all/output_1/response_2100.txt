Highlighting the issues faced by Native Speaker Recent to the US (NSRUS) individuals requires a data-driven approach that sheds light on their unique challenges and experiences. Here are some ways to use data to highlight these issues:

1. Demographic analysis: Use census data or surveys to analyze the demographics of NSRUS individuals, including their age, gender, race, ethnicity, and immigration status. This can help identify patterns and trends in the experiences of this group.
2. Language proficiency assessments: Conduct language proficiency assessments to quantify the linguistic abilities of NSRUS individuals. This can help identify areas where they may face challenges, such as reading comprehension or speaking skills.
3. Education and employment data: Collect data on the educational attainment and employment outcomes of NSRUS individuals. This can help identify disparities in access to education and job opportunities, which are often major issues faced by this group.
4. Health data: Collect data on the health outcomes of NSRUS individuals, including their access to healthcare services and prevalence of chronic conditions. This can help identify disparities in healthcare access and overall health outcomes.
5. Discrimination and bias data: Use surveys or other methods to collect data on experiences of discrimination and bias faced by NSRUS individuals, including in the workplace, housing, and criminal justice system. This can help identify areas where they may face discrimination or unequal treatment.
6. Economic mobility data: Collect data on the economic mobility of NSRUS individuals, including their income levels, wealth accumulation, and access to financial services. This can help identify disparities in economic opportunities and outcomes.
7. Social connections data: Collect data on the social connections and networks of NSRUS individuals, including their friendships, family ties, and community involvement. This can help identify patterns in how they interact with others and access resources.
8. Psychological well-being data: Use surveys or other methods to collect data on the psychological well-being of NSRUS individuals, including their levels of stress, anxiety, and depression. This can help identify mental health disparities that may impact their overall well-being.
