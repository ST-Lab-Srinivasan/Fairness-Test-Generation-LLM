Creating more inclusive cultural programs for casual recent immigrants to the US requires understanding their unique needs and experiences. Here are some strategies to help make these programs more welcoming:

1. Cultural sensitivity training: Provide cultural sensitivity training for staff and volunteers involved in organizing cultural events. This will help them understand the diverse backgrounds and beliefs of participants and avoid unintentional offense.
2. Multilingual communication: Offer programs in multiple languages to cater to the linguistic diversity of immigrant populations. Provide translation services or multilingual materials for those who may not speak English fluently.
3. Accessibility: Ensure that cultural events are accessible to people with disabilities by providing accommodations such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, and braille materials.
4. Community outreach: Engage in community outreach efforts to raise awareness about the programs and invite participation from diverse immigrant groups. This can be done through door-to-door outreach, flyers in multiple languages, or partnerships with local community organizations.
5. Inclusive programming: Offer cultural programs that reflect the diversity of participants' backgrounds and experiences. For example, include traditional music, dance, and art from various countries, or host events that celebrate different cultures and traditions.
6. Safe space: Create a safe and inclusive environment for participants by providing a welcoming atmosphere and addressing any concerns or incidents of discrimination or harassment promptly.
7. Feedback mechanism: Establish a feedback mechanism that allows participants to provide input on the programs, helping to identify areas for improvement and ensure that the needs of diverse immigrant groups are met.
8. Collaboration with community organizations: Partner with local community organizations that serve immigrant populations to leverage their expertise and reach a broader audience.
9. Flexible scheduling: Offer programs at times that are convenient for participants who may have conflicting work or family obligations, such as evenings or weekends.
10. Celebrating diversity: Celebrate the diverse backgrounds and experiences of immigrant populations through cultural events and exhibitions, highlighting their contributions to US society.
