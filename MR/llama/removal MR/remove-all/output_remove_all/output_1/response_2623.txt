For long-term, recently native speaker individuals, their cultural heritage can help them understand where they come from and who they are, even if they have lived in a different society for a significant portion of their life.

For long-term, recently native speaker individuals, their cultural heritage may provide them with a strong foundation in their birth language and culture, which can make it easier for them to learn other languages and cultures.
4. Cultural practices: Cultural heritage includes not only language but also customs, traditions, and practices. For long-term, recently native speaker individuals, these cultural practices can provide a sense of continuity and connection to their birth culture, even if they have lived in a different society for a significant portion of their life.
