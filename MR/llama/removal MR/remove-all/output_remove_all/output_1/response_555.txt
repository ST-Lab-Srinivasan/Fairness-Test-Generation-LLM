Addressing the needs of "Not Recently Homeless" (NRH) individuals in disaster response is crucial as they are more likely to face challenges during disasters due to their precarious housing situation. Here are some strategies to address their needs:

1. Include NRH populations in disaster planning and response: Ensure that NRH individuals are included in disaster planning and response efforts. This can be done by actively engaging with local homeless populations, community organizations, and social service providers to gather information about their specific needs and concerns.
2. Provide temporary housing options: Offer temporary housing options such as emergency shelters, transitional housing, or safe parking lots for NRH individuals who may not have access to stable housing during disasters. These spaces should be easily accessible, secure, and provide basic necessities like food, water, and hygiene facilities.
3. Address health and mental health needs: Provide medical and mental health services specifically tailored to the needs of NRH populations. This can include mobile clinics, mental health counseling, and substance abuse treatment. Ensure that these services are accessible and culturally sensitive.
4. Facilitate communication and collaboration: Encourage collaboration between emergency responders, social service providers, and community organizations to ensure a coordinated response to NRH individuals' needs during disasters. Establish clear communication channels and protocols for referrals and resource sharing.
5. Advocate for long-term solutions: Disasters often exacerbate existing housing insecurity. Advocate for policies and programs that address the root causes of homelessness, such as affordable housing, rental assistance, and supportive services. Encourage policymakers to prioritize these issues during disaster response and recovery efforts.
6. Foster community resilience: Engage with local communities and encourage their participation in disaster preparedness and response efforts. This can help build resilience and social connections, which are critical for NRH individuals who may be more vulnerable to the impacts of disasters.
