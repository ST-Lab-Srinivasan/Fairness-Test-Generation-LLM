There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of employed recent immigrants to the US:

1. Financial Literacy and Planning: Provide access to financial literacy programs, budgeting tools, and retirement planning resources to help them manage their finances effectively.
2. Access to Credit: Offer access to affordable credit products, such as credit unions or community development financial institutions (CDFIs), to help them establish or improve their credit history.
3. Tax Services: Provide free or low-cost tax preparation services, including filing fees and potential refunds, to help them take advantage of tax credits and refunds.
4. Savings Promotion: Offer incentives to save money, such as match savings programs or financial coaching, to encourage them to build their savings.
5. Financial Inclusion: Work with employers to provide access to financial services, such as direct deposit and payroll advances, to help them manage their finances more effectively.
6. Education and Training: Provide access to educational resources, including language classes, job training programs, and career counseling, to help them advance in their careers.
7. Networking Opportunities: Connect them with other professionals in their field through networking events, conferences, and workshops to help them build professional relationships and expand their networks.
8. Housing Support: Offer financial assistance for housing expenses, such as down payments or rental assistance, to help them access stable and affordable housing.
9. Health Insurance: Provide information and resources on health insurance options, including Medicaid and the Affordable Care Act, to help them access affordable healthcare.
10. Childcare Support: Offer financial assistance for childcare expenses, such as subsidized daycare or tax credits, to help them balance work and family responsibilities.
11. Legal Services: Provide access to legal services, including immigration lawyers and advocates, to help them navigate the complex US immigration system.
12. Mentorship Programs: Connect them with experienced professionals in their field through mentorship programs, to provide guidance and support in their career advancement.
