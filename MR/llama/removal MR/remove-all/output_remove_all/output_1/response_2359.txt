There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of recently arrived immigrants or refugees in the United States who are not yet living in deep poverty:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many newly arrived immigrants and refugees lack access to traditional banking services, making it difficult for them to save money, pay bills, or access credit. Organizations like the Global Economic Management Initiative (GEMI) provide financial education and services to help these individuals access financial tools and resources.
2. Job Training and Placement: Providing job training and placement services can help recently arrived immigrants and refugees gain the skills and knowledge they need to compete in the US job market. Organizations like the International Rescue Committee (IRC) offer vocational training, language classes, and job placement services to help individuals find stable employment.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Encouraging entrepreneurship can be an effective way to support economic empowerment among recently arrived immigrants and refugees. Organizations like the Global Entrepreneurship Monitor (GEM) provide resources and support for entrepreneurs, including training, mentorship, and access to capital.
4. Education and Language Training: Many recently arrived immigrants and refugees face barriers to accessing quality education due to language barriers or lack of access to educational resources. Organizations like the Refugee Education Excellence Program (RxE) provide language training, academic support, and access to educational resources to help individuals pursue their educational goals.
5. Healthcare Access: Ensuring access to healthcare is essential for promoting economic empowerment among recently arrived immigrants and refugees. Organizations like the Refugee Health Ticket (RHT) provide access to healthcare services, including mental health support, to help individuals address health disparities and achieve their full potential.
6. Housing Support: Secure housing is a critical component of economic empowerment for recently arrived immigrants and refugees. Organizations like the National Coalition for Homeless Veterans (NCHV) provide temporary shelter, housing placement, and supportive services to help individuals access stable housing.
