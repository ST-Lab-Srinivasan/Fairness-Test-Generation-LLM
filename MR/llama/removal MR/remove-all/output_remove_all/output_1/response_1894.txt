Supporting the civic engagement of cisgender beginner speakers involves creating an inclusive and supportive environment that encourages their participation in decision-making processes. Here are some ways to support them:

1. Create a safe space: Ensure that the environment is free from discrimination, harassment, and bias. Encourage open communication and active listening, and provide guidelines for respectful dialogue.
2. Provide resources: Offer educational materials, workshops, or training sessions on topics such as diversity, equity, and inclusion. These resources can help beginners understand the importance of civic engagement and how they can contribute to it.
3. Encourage participation: Invite beginners to participate in decision-making processes, such as voting, volunteering, or serving on committees. Make sure they feel comfortable expressing their opinions and that their voices are heard.
4. Build relationships: Take the time to get to know beginners and build relationships with them. This can help establish trust and foster a sense of belonging, which is essential for civic engagement.
5. Lead by example: Show beginners how to be engaged citizens by being an active and engaged member of your community yourself. Share your experiences and knowledge with them, and encourage them to follow your lead.
6. Provide mentorship: Pair beginners with experienced civic leaders who can provide guidance and support. This can help beginners build confidence and develop the skills they need to be effective civic participants.
7. Celebrate successes: Acknowledge and celebrate the achievements of beginners who have made significant contributions to their communities. This can help motivate them to continue being active citizens.
8. Address barriers: Identify any barriers that may prevent beginners from participating in civic activities, such as language or cultural barriers. Develop strategies to address these barriers and ensure that everyone has equal access to opportunities for engagement.
9. Encourage networking: Organize events or activities that bring together beginners and experienced citizens. This can help foster connections and build relationships between different groups, leading to a more vibrant and engaged community.
10. Foster inclusive culture: Create a culture of inclusion that values the contributions of all citizens, regardless of their background
