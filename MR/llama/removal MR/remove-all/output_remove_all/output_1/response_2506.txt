Supporting the economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US lower-income individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the historical and systemic barriers that have hindered their economic opportunities. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and job training programs: Provide access to quality education and job training programs, including vocational training, apprenticeships, and certificate programs. This will help individuals acquire skills that are in demand in the job market and increase their employability.
2. Microfinance initiatives: Establish microfinance programs that provide small loans and other financial resources to individuals who lack access to traditional banking services. This can help them start or expand their businesses, which can generate income and create jobs.
3. Entrepreneurship support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing resources and support for individuals who want to start their own businesses. This can include business plan competitions, mentorship programs, and access to capital.
4. Community development investments: Invest in community development projects that create jobs, improve infrastructure, and promote economic growth in Native American communities.
5. Tax relief and incentives: Provide tax relief and incentives to encourage businesses owned by Native Americans and low-income individuals. This can help them compete with larger corporations and attract investment.
6. Access to capital: Increase access to capital for Native American and low-income individuals through initiatives such as crowdfunding, impact investing, and venture capital.
7. Land reform: Address the historical injustices of land dispossession by advocating for land reform policies that return ancestral lands to their rightful owners. This can help Native American communities regain control over their land and resources.
8. Cultural preservation: Support cultural preservation initiatives that promote the revitalization of Native American languages, customs, and traditions. This can help individuals connect with their heritage and culture, which can have positive effects on their mental and emotional well-being.
9. Healthcare access: Improve access to healthcare for Native American and low-income individuals by investing in community health centers, telemedicine services, and other initiatives that address health disparities.
