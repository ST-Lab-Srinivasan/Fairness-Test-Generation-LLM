Addressing the specific needs of ethnically diverse students, including those who are not recently immigrated, is crucial for creating an inclusive and effective learning environment. Here are some strategies that educators can use to address the unique needs of these students:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide teachers with cultural competency training to help them understand and appreciate the diverse backgrounds and experiences of their students. This can include learning about different cultures, religions, and traditions, as well as understanding the impact of discrimination and bias on student success.
2. Language Support: Offer language support services for students who may be struggling with English proficiency. This can include bilingual or multilingual education programs, as well as language classes for teachers to help them communicate more effectively with students from diverse linguistic backgrounds.
3. Academic Accommodations: Provide academic accommodations for students who may need extra support due to their cultural or linguistic background. This can include things like adapting curriculum materials to be more inclusive, providing extra time to complete assignments, or offering tutoring services in a student's native language.
4. Identity-Based Curriculum: Incorporate identity-based curriculum into the classroom to help students see themselves reflected in their education. This can include teaching about different cultures, histories, and experiences, as well as encouraging students to share their own cultural practices and traditions.
5. Teacher Diversity: Ensure that the teacher workforce is diverse and representative of the student population. Research has shown that students perform better academically when they have teachers who look like them and understand their cultural background.
6. Community Engagement: Build relationships with local community organizations and centers that serve ethnically diverse populations. This can help to create a more inclusive and supportive learning environment for students from these groups.
7. Parent Involvement: Encourage parent involvement in the educational process by providing opportunities for parents to learn about their child's education, participate in decision-making processes, and contribute to school events.
