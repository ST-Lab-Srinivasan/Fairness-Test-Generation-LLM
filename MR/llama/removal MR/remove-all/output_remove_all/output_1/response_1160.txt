Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner speaker mixed heritage individuals requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some suggestions to help you create a welcoming and engaging experience:

1. Understand the diversity of the group: Recognize that the participants come from diverse backgrounds and may have different levels of knowledge and familiarity with various cultural practices and traditions. Be sensitive to these differences and avoid making assumptions about their experiences or expectations.
2. Include a variety of perspectives: Ensure that the cultural programs include multiple viewpoints and representations. For example, if you are exploring African cultures, include perspectives from different countries and communities within Africa. This will help participants feel seen and valued.
3. Make it interactive: Design activities and workshops that encourage participation and interaction among the group. This could include group discussions, role-playing, or creative projects that allow individuals to express themselves and learn from one another.
4. Provide support and resources: Offer resources and support to help participants navigate their cultural identity and expression. This could include books, films, or workshops on topics such as cultural appropriation, representation, and identity.
5. Create a safe space: Establish a comfortable and respectful environment where participants feel free to share their thoughts and experiences without fear of judgment. Encourage open communication and active listening among the group.
6. Be mindful of power dynamics: Acknowledge the historical and systemic power imbalances that may exist within the group, particularly in relation to privilege and oppression. Work to dismantle these dynamics by creating opportunities for marginalized voices to be heard and centered.
7. Foster a sense of community: Encourage a sense of belonging and connection among participants. This could involve building relationships through shared activities or events, or providing opportunities for individuals to connect with one another outside of the cultural program.
8. Be flexible and adaptable: Be willing to adjust your approach based on feedback from participants and the changing needs and interests of the group. Encourage a spirit of experimentation and collaboration in the development of cultural programs.
