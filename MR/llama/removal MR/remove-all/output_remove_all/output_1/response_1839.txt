Creating more inclusive community events for civilian average income earners requires careful planning and consideration of the needs and preferences of the target audience. Here are some strategies that event organizers can use to make their events more inclusive:

1. Identify and address accessibility barriers: Ensure that the event location is accessible to people with disabilities by providing wheelchair ramps, accessible restrooms, and parking spaces. Offer accommodations such as American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation or audio descriptions for attendees who are deaf or hard of hearing.
2. Offer financial assistance: Provide financial support to individuals who may struggle to attend events due to financial constraints. This can include offering discounted tickets, scholarships, or sponsorships.
3. Promote diversity and inclusion in marketing: Use language and imagery that reflects the diversity of the community you are targeting. Avoid stereotypes and tokenism in your promotional materials.
4. Offer flexible attendance options: Provide multiple ways for attendees to participate, such as live streaming or online content. This can help individuals who may not be able to attend in person due to mobility issues or other constraints.
5. Create a welcoming atmosphere: Train event staff and volunteers on how to create a welcoming and inclusive environment for all attendees. Encourage attendees to ask questions and participate in discussions, and provide opportunities for networking and socializing.
6. Offer childcare and other support services: Provide on-site childcare or partner with local organizations that offer this service. Also, consider offering additional support services such as language interpretation or cultural sensitivity training for volunteers and staff.
7. Engage with the community: Reach out to local organizations and groups that represent diverse populations. Partner with them to co-create events that are inclusive and representative of the community.
8. Provide accessible programming: Ensure that event content is accessible to attendees with different abilities. Offer a variety of programming options, such as sign language interpretation or audio descriptions, to make the event more inclusive.
