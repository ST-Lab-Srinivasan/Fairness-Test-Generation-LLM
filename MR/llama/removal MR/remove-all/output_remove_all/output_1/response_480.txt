Ensuring that the healthcare needs of natural-born US citizens are met is a critical aspect of any healthcare system. Here are some ways to achieve this:

1. Universal Healthcare Coverage: Implementing a universal healthcare coverage system can help ensure that everyone has access to quality healthcare, regardless of their income or social status. This can be achieved by providing government-funded health insurance to all citizens, as is the case in many developed countries.
2. Strengthening Primary Care: Investing in primary care can help identify health issues early on and provide preventive care, which can help prevent more severe conditions from developing. This can involve increasing funding for community health centers, rural health clinics, and other primary care facilities.
3. Expanding Medicaid and CHIP: Medicaid and the Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP) provide critical coverage to low-income individuals and families. Expanding these programs can help ensure that more people have access to healthcare, including those who may not qualify for other forms of coverage.
4. Improving Mental Health Services: Mental health issues are common in the US, and ensuring that everyone has access to quality mental health services is essential. This can involve increasing funding for mental health facilities, training more mental health professionals, and reducing stigma around mental illness.
5. Addressing Disparities: Disparities in healthcare exist in many areas, including race, ethnicity, income, and geography. Addressing these disparities can involve identifying and addressing the root causes, such as systemic barriers and biases, and implementing targeted interventions to improve access to care for marginalized populations.
6. Investing in Public Health: Investing in public health initiatives can help prevent illnesses and promote overall health. This can involve increasing funding for programs such as immunization campaigns, disease surveillance, and environmental health initiatives.
