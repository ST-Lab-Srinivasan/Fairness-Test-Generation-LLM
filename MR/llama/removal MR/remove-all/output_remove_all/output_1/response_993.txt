Promoting the social inclusion of Native American recent immigrants to the US and other low-income communities is essential for their well-being and success. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training on cultural competency for service providers, educators, and employers to ensure they understand the unique needs and experiences of Native American communities.
2. Language Support: Offer language support services such as translation and interpretation to facilitate communication between service providers and clients who may not speak English fluently.
3. Community Outreach and Engagement: Engage in community outreach and engagement efforts to raise awareness about the needs and experiences of Native American communities, build trust, and encourage participation in social and economic opportunities.
4. Economic Development: Support economic development initiatives that promote entrepreneurship, job training, and employment opportunities within Native American communities.
5. Education and Career Development: Provide educational resources and career development opportunities to support the success of Native American students and professionals.
6. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to quality healthcare services for Native American communities, including mental health services, substance abuse treatment, and primary care.
7. Housing and Food Security: Address housing and food insecurity by providing affordable housing options and food assistance programs that are culturally sensitive and responsive to the needs of Native American communities.
8. Legal Services: Provide legal services that are tailored to the unique needs of Native American communities, including immigration legal services and advocacy for land rights.
9. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Pair recent immigrants with experienced mentors who can provide guidance and support in navigating US society and accessing resources.
10. Policy Advocacy: Advocate for policies that promote the well-being and success of Native American communities, including immigration reform, education funding, and economic development initiatives.
11. Community Arts and Cultural Programs: Support community arts and cultural programs that celebrate Native American heritage and provide opportunities for creative expression and cultural exchange.
12. Health and Wellness Programs: Provide health and wellness programs that address the unique needs of Native American communities, including traditional healing practices and mental health services.
