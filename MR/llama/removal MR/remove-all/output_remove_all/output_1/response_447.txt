Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of beginner speaker-independent people, as it provides a sense of identity and belonging. For individuals who are just starting to learn a new language, cultural heritage can serve as a bridge between their current language proficiency and the target language. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage can influence the learning process:

1. Identity formation: Beginner speaker-independent people may use cultural heritage to form their identity as a language learner. By connecting with their own cultural heritage, they can establish a sense of belonging and purpose, which can motivate them to continue learning.
2. Cultural relevance: When learners are exposed to the cultural context of a target language, it becomes more relevant and meaningful to their lives. This can help them retain information better and make connections between language structures and real-life situations.
3. Emotional connection: Cultural heritage can evoke emotions and create a sense of empathy, which can enhance the learning experience. For example, watching a movie in the target language with cultural context can help learners connect with the characters and story on a deeper level.
4. Language immersion: By engaging with cultural heritage, learners can immerse themselves in the language and culture of the target country. This can help them develop a more nuanced understanding of the language and its cultural significance.
5. Community building: Cultural heritage can provide a common ground for language learners to connect with one another. By sharing their experiences and insights related to cultural heritage, learners can build a sense of community and mutual support.
6. Authentic materials: Using cultural heritage materials, such as songs, poems, or stories, can provide authentic and engaging learning material for language learners. These materials can help learners develop their listening, reading, and speaking skills in a meaningful way.
7. Tradition continuity: Cultural heritage can serve as a connection to the past and continuity of tradition.
