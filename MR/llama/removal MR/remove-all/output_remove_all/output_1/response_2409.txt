1. Lack of cultural competence: Healthcare providers may not be familiar with the cultural beliefs and practices of non-mixed heritage communities, leading to misunderstandings or misdiagnoses.
2. Stereotyping and stigma: Non-mixed heritage individuals may face stereotypes and biases from healthcare providers based on their race or ethnicity, which can lead to a lack of trust in the healthcare system.

Limited representation in medical research: Many medical research studies have historically been conducted on predominantly white populations, leading to a lack of representation for non-mixed heritage individuals. 4. Language barriers: Non-mixed heritage individuals who do not speak the dominant language in their area may face difficulties communicating with healthcare providers, leading to misunderstandings or missed diagnoses.
5. Limited access to culturally sensitive care: Non-mixed heritage individuals may have limited access to healthcare providers who are knowledgeable about their cultural beliefs and practices, leading to a lack of culturally sensitive care.
6. Distrust of the medical system: Historical abuses and discrimination within the medical system can lead to a lack of trust in the healthcare system among non-mixed heritage individuals.
7. Limited availability of culturally competent providers: Non-mixed heritage communities may have limited access to healthcare providers who are knowledgeable about their cultural beliefs and practices, leading to a lack of culturally sensitive care.
8. Lack of representation in health policy: The voices and experiences of non-mixed heritage individuals may be underrepresented in health policy decisions, leading to a lack of understanding of their health needs and experiences.
