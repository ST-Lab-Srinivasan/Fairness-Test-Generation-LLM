It can influence their sense of self, values, beliefs, and practices, and help them navigate their new surroundings. Recently arrived immigrants may find comfort in connecting with their cultural heritage, especially when they are facing challenges in their new environment.

Recently arrived immigrants may find solace in adhering to their cultural values and beliefs, which can help them maintain a sense of identity during times of transition.
3. Language and communication: Cultural heritage can influence language use and communication styles, which are critical for forming and expressing one's identity. Recently arrived immigrants may find that their cultural heritage provides a framework for understanding and communicating with others in their new environment.
4. Customs and traditions: Cultural heritage encompasses customs and traditions that shape the way individuals interact with others and express themselves. Recently arrived immigrants may find comfort in maintaining their cultural customs and traditions, which can help them feel grounded and connected to their roots.
5. Identity formation: Cultural heritage can play a significant role in identity formation, particularly during times of transition. Recently arrived immigrants may use their cultural heritage as a way to construct their identities, exploring their history, values, beliefs, and practices to create a sense of self.
