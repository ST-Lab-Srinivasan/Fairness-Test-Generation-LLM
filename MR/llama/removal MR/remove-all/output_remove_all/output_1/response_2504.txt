Native American economic empowerment is crucial for improving their socio-economic status and bridging the gap between them and the rest of the population. Here are some ways to support the economic empowerment of Native natural-born US individuals:

1. Education and job training programs: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that cater specifically to Native American communities. This can help them acquire skills and knowledge that are in demand in the job market, enabling them to compete for better-paying jobs.
2. Small business development: Encourage and support Native American entrepreneurs by providing resources and access to capital to start or grow their own businesses. This can create jobs and economic opportunities within their communities.
3. Access to capital: Many Native American communities struggle with limited access to capital, which hinders their ability to invest in their businesses or pursue economic opportunities. Increase access to capital through grants, loans, or other financial instruments that cater specifically to Native American communities.
4. Job placement and career advancement: Work with local organizations and companies to provide job placement and career advancement opportunities for Native Americans. This can help them climb the economic ladder and achieve higher levels of income.

Land and resource management: Ensure that Native American communities have control over their land and resources, which can generate revenue through sustainable resource management practices like timber harvesting, mining, or renewable energy production.
