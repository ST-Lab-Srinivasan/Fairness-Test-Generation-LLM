Economic empowerment is a critical aspect of ensuring the well-being and prosperity of any community, including Native American communities. Here are some ways to support the economic empowerment of Native natural-born US long-term individuals:

1. Provide access to education and job training programs: Many Native American communities face significant barriers to accessing quality education and job training opportunities. Governments and organizations can work towards providing access to these programs, which can help individuals gain the skills and knowledge they need to compete in the job market.
2. Support small business development: Small businesses are the backbone of many Native American communities, and supporting their growth and success can have a significant impact on the local economy. Organizations can provide training, mentorship, and access to capital to help individuals start and grow their own businesses.
3. Invest in infrastructure and community facilities: Many Native American communities lack access to basic infrastructure, such as roads, bridges, and utilities. Investing in these critical infrastructure needs can help improve the quality of life for community members and support economic growth.
4. Promote sustainable resource management: Native American communities have a deep connection to their land and natural resources. Promoting sustainable resource management practices can help ensure the long-term health of these resources and support the economic well-being of the community.
5. Address systemic barriers to economic success: Native American communities face significant systemic barriers to economic success, including discrimination, lack of access to capital, and limited access to markets. Addressing these barriers can help level the playing field and support the economic empowerment of Native individuals.
6. Foster intergenerational wealth transfer: Many Native American communities have experienced generations of economic disadvantage, which has resulted in a significant wealth gap between Native and non-Native communities. Fostering intergenerational wealth transfer through education, mentorship, and other means can help bridge this gap and support the economic empowerment of Native individuals.
7. Support cultural preservation: Cultural preservation is an important aspect of Native American identity and community well-being. Supporting cultural preservation through arts, language, and other initiatives can help foster a sense of pride and belonging among community members and contribute to their overall economic well-being.
