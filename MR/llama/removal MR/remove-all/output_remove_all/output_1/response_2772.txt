To ensure that the educational needs of beginner speakers are met, here are some strategies that can be implemented:

1. Assess their level of proficiency: Conduct a language assessment to determine the beginners' level of proficiency in speaking English. This will help the teacher identify areas where students need more attention and support.
2. Set achievable goals: Work with the students to set realistic and achievable goals for their speaking skills. Break down these goals into smaller, manageable tasks to help them stay motivated and track their progress.
3. Provide opportunities for practice: Offer regular opportunities for beginners to practice speaking English in a supportive environment. This can include pair or group work, role-playing activities, and conversation classes.
4. Use authentic materials: Use real-life materials such as news articles, videos, and songs to help students learn new vocabulary and phrases in context.
5. Foster a supportive learning environment: Create a relaxed and welcoming atmosphere where beginners feel comfortable taking risks and making mistakes. Encourage peer support and collaboration to help build confidence and fluency.
6. Use visual aids: Utilize visual aids such as pictures, flashcards, and videos to help students learn new vocabulary and concepts. This can be particularly helpful for beginners who are still developing their language skills.
7. Provide feedback and support: Offer regular feedback and support to help beginners improve their speaking skills. Encourage them to reflect on their progress and identify areas where they need more work.
8. Use technology: Leverage technology to provide beginners with additional resources and opportunities for practice. This can include online language learning platforms, apps, and games.
9. Involve parents and guardians: Encourage parents and guardians to be involved in their child's language learning journey. They can help reinforce new vocabulary and phrases at home and provide additional support outside of class.
10. Monitor progress: Regularly monitor the progress of beginner speakers to identify areas where they need more attention and support. Adjust teaching strategies accordingly to ensure that their educational needs are being met.
