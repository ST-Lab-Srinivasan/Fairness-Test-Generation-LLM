Mexican people face a number of challenges within the legal system, including:

1. Limited access to justice: Many Mexicans, especially those living in rural areas or from lower socio-economic backgrounds, have limited access to legal services and representation. This can lead to unequal access to justice and a lack of opportunities for redressal of grievances.
2. Corruption: Corruption is a pervasive problem in the Mexican legal system, with instances of bribery, nepotism, and other forms of corruption affecting the fairness and impartiality of the legal process.
3. Lack of resources: The Mexican government has limited resources to invest in the legal system, which can result in underfunded courts, inadequate infrastructure, and a lack of trained personnel.
4. Inefficient court processes: Court proceedings in Mexico can be slow and inefficient, leading to delays in the resolution of cases and a lack of confidence in the legal system.
5. Limited protection for human rights: The Mexican legal system has limited provisions to protect human rights, particularly for marginalized groups such as women, children, and indigenous peoples.
6. Discrimination: Discrimination based on race, gender, sexual orientation, and other factors is still prevalent in the Mexican legal system, leading to unequal treatment under the law.
7. Lack of transparency: The Mexican legal system lacks transparency, with many court proceedings being conducted behind closed doors and limited access to information for the public.
8. Limited access to legal aid: Many Mexicans face challenges in accessing legal aid, particularly those from low-income backgrounds, due to a lack of resources and infrastructure.
9. Lack of specialized courts: Mexico has a lack of specialized courts, which can result in cases being handled by generalist judges who may not have the necessary expertise to handle complex legal issues.
10. Limited enforcement of court orders: Court orders are not always effectively enforced in Mexico, leading to a lack of confidence in the legal system and difficulties in resolving disputes.
