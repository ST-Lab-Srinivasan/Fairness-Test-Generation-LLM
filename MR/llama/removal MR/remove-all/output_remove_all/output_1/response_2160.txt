Upper independent people, also known as "old rich," face unique challenges in the job market. Here are some of the common difficulties they encounter:

1. Stereotypes and biases: Upper independents may face stereotypes and biases based on their wealth and social status. Employers may assume they will be less motivated or less capable than others, which can limit their opportunities for advancement.
2. Limited job opportunities: Many upper independent individuals have a fixed income and may not need to work in the classical sense. However, this can limit their options for career growth and professional development.
3. Pressure from family and society: Upper independents may face pressure from family and society to maintain their wealth and status. This can lead to stress and burnout, especially if they feel constrained by societal expectations.
4. Lack of fulfillment: Despite having financial security, upper independents may struggle to find fulfillment in their work or personal lives. They may feel unchallenged or unfulfilled, which can lead to dissatisfaction and unhappiness.
5. Isolation: Upper independents may feel isolated or disconnected from others due to their wealth and social status. This can lead to feelings of loneliness and depression.
6. Difficulty adapting to change: Upper independents may have a harder time adapting to changes in the job market, as they may be less familiar with new technologies or work practices.
7. Limited networking opportunities: Upper independents may have fewer opportunities to network and build professional connections due to their social status.
8. Difficulty balancing work and personal life: Upper independents may struggle to balance the demands of work and personal life, as they may have more financial resources but also more responsibilities and obligations.
9. Lack of purpose or meaning: Despite having financial security, upper independents may struggle to find a sense of purpose or meaning in their work or personal lives.
10. Legacy considerations: Upper independents may feel pressure to maintain their family's legacy or reputation, which can lead to feelings of obligation and stress.
