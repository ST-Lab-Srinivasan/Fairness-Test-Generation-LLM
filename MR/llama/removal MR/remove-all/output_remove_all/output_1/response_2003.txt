Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner speakers who are recent to the US requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions:

1. Offer language classes: Provide language classes specifically designed for beginner speakers, tailoring the instruction to their needs and levels of proficiency. This will help them develop their language skills and gain confidence in communicating.
2. Provide cultural orientation: Offer cultural orientation programs that introduce the basics of US culture, customs, and etiquette. This can help newcomers navigate everyday situations and feel more comfortable in their new environment.
3. Create intercultural spaces: Establish spaces where people from different cultural backgrounds can come together to share experiences, ask questions, and learn from each other. This can foster cross-cultural understanding and promote a sense of community.
4. Encourage participation: Make sure that newcomers feel included in cultural programs by actively encouraging their participation. This can be done by offering opportunities for them to share their own cultural traditions, practices, or art forms.
5. Foster collaboration: Encourage intercultural collaboration between beginner speakers and more established members of the community. This can help build bridges between different cultural groups and create a sense of mutual understanding and respect.
6. Provide resources: Offer access to resources such as books, films, or online content that showcase diverse cultures and perspectives. This can help newcomers learn about different cultural practices and traditions outside of formal programs.
7. Celebrate diversity: Host events and celebrations that highlight the diversity of cultures in the community. This can include festivals, parades, or other cultural gatherings that promote intercultural exchange and understanding.
8. Create mentorship opportunities: Pair beginner speakers with more experienced members of the community who can act as mentors and provide guidance on navigating US culture.
9. Offer networking opportunities: Provide opportunities for newcomers to connect with other individuals from diverse cultural backgrounds, fostering a sense of community and mutual support.
