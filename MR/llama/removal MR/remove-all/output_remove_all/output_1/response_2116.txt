Addressing the specific needs of ethnically minoritized students who have not had access to quality education for a long time requires a comprehensive and multifaceted approach. Here are some strategies that can help educators address these needs:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers to understand and appreciate the cultural backgrounds of ethnically minoritized students, and how these factors impact their learning experiences. This includes being aware of the historical and contemporary issues affecting these communities, such as systemic racism and discrimination.
2. Language support: Provide language support for students who may be non-native speakers of the dominant language in the school. This can include bilingual or multilingual education programs, language classes, or hiring interpreters and translators to facilitate communication between teachers and students.
3. Identity-based curriculum: Develop a curriculum that reflects the experiences, histories, and cultures of ethnically minoritized communities. This can include incorporating diverse perspectives, texts, and media into the curriculum to help students see themselves represented and valued.
4. Community partnerships: Collaborate with community organizations and cultural centers to provide additional resources and support for ethnically minoritized students. This can include mentorship programs, tutoring services, and extracurricular activities that promote cultural identity and pride.
5. Teacher diversity: Ensure that the teaching staff reflects the diversity of the student population. This can help create a more inclusive learning environment and provide role models for students from underrepresented groups.
6. Student voice and agency: Empower ethnically minoritized students to take an active role in their education by providing opportunities for leadership, advocacy, and decision-making. This can include creating student clubs or organizations that focus on issues affecting these communities.
7. Trauma-informed teaching: Be aware of the historical trauma faced by ethnically minoritized students and their families, and how this may impact their learning experiences. Provide supportive and inclusive classroom environments that recognize and address these challenges.
