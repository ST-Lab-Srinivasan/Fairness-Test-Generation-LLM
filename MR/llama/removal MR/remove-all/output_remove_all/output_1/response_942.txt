1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers to incorporate traditional knowledge and cultural practices into their teaching methods. This can involve incorporating Native languages, stories, and cultural symbols into lessons, as well as creating opportunities for students to share their own cultural experiences and perspectives.
2. Indigenous-centered curriculum: Develop curricula that are grounded in the histories, cultures, and experiences of Native communities. This can involve incorporating traditional knowledge and practices into lessons, as well as highlighting the contributions and achievements of Native peoples throughout history.
3. Community partnerships: Build partnerships with local Native communities to co-design and deliver educational programs that are responsive to their needs. This can involve working with elders, community leaders, and other stakeholders to identify areas of concern and develop solutions that are culturally relevant and effective.

Language revitalization: Support the revitalization of Native languages by incorporating language learning into educational programs and providing opportunities for students to learn their native languages.
