Supporting the social inclusion of professional ultra-runners can be done through various means, including:

1. Awareness and education: Raise awareness about the sport of ultra-running and its participants, highlighting their achievements and the challenges they face. This can be done through media coverage, documentaries, and community events.
2. Accessibility: Make ultra-running events more accessible to a wider audience by offering a range of distances and levels of difficulty, as well as providing accommodations for participants with disabilities.
3. Inclusive language: Use inclusive language when referring to ultra-runners and their achievements, avoiding language that perpetuates stereotypes or discrimination.
4. Representation: Showcase the diversity of ultra-runners in media coverage and promotional materials, including runners of different genders, ages, and backgrounds.
5. Community engagement: Foster a sense of community among ultra-runners by creating opportunities for connection and collaboration, such as through social events or online forums.
6. Advocacy: Advocate for the rights and interests of ultra-runners, including access to public spaces and resources, and protection from discrimination and harassment.
7. Mentorship: Provide mentorship and support to new and aspiring ultra-runners, particularly those from underrepresented groups, to help them navigate the sport and achieve their goals.
8. Sponsorship and funding: Secure sponsorship and funding for ultra-running events and initiatives, which can help to increase visibility and accessibility, as well as provide financial support to participants.
9. Collaboration with other sports organizations: Work with other sports organizations and communities to promote the inclusion of ultra-runners in broader athletic and cultural discourse.
10. Celebrating diversity: Highlight the diverse range of ultra-runners, including those from different cultures, genders, and abilities, and celebrate their achievements and contributions to the sport.

By implementing these strategies, it is possible to support the social inclusion of professional ultra-runners and create a more inclusive and supportive environment for all runners.
