1. The Native American Community Academy (NACA) - Founded by Deborah Parker, a Native American woman from the Lummi Nation, NACA is a K-8 public charter school that provides a culturally responsive education to native students in Seattle, Washington. The school's mission is to empower students to succeed academically, socially, and culturally while fostering a sense of identity, belonging, and pride in their Native American heritage.
2. The Indigenous Education Foundation (IEF) - Based in Australia, IEF was established by an indigenous woman named Leanne Liddle to support the educational and economic development of indigenous communities. The organization provides scholarships, mentorship programs, and community-based initiatives aimed at improving the educational outcomes of indigenous students and promoting cultural preservation.
3. The African Diaspora International Film Festival (ADIFF) - Founded by an African immigrant named Ayanna B. Jennings, ADIFF is a non-profit organization that showcases films by and about people of African descent from around the world. The festival aims to promote cultural exchange, understanding, and representation while providing a platform for underrepresented filmmakers to share their stories.
4. The Asian American Psychological Association (AAPA) - Led by Dr. Hong-An Truong, AAPA is a professional organization dedicated to promoting the mental health and well-being of Asian Americans. The association provides advocacy, research, and education on mental health issues affecting this community while fostering cultural competence in mental health services.
5. The National Association of Latino Independent Producers (NALIP) - Founded by a group of Latinx filmmakers, NALIP is dedicated to promoting diversity and inclusion in the media industry.
