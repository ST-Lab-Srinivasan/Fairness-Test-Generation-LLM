Economic empowerment is a critical aspect of addressing poverty among Native Americans living in the United States. Here are some ways to support their economic empowerment:

1. Job training and placement programs: Provide job training and placement services specifically tailored for Native Americans, including vocational training, entrepreneurship development, and workforce readiness programs.
2. Small business loans and grants: Offer small business loans and grants to help Native Americans start or expand their own businesses. This can help create job opportunities and increase economic mobility within the community.
3. Mentorship and capacity building: Connect Native American entrepreneurs with successful business leaders and provide training and resources to build their capacities in areas such as financial management, marketing, and operations.
4. Access to capital: Address the lack of access to capital for Native American-owned businesses by providing access to venture capital, angel investors, and other funding sources.
5. Microfinance programs: Implement microfinance programs that provide small loans and other financial services to help Native Americans start or grow their own businesses.
6. Entrepreneurship training: Provide training and resources to help Native Americans develop entrepreneurial skills, including developing a business plan, marketing, and financial management.
7. Networking opportunities: Create opportunities for Native American entrepreneurs to connect with potential investors, partners, and mentors through events, conferences, and networking platforms.
8. Community development initiatives: Support community-based economic development initiatives that promote local economic growth and job creation within Native American communities.
9. Capacity building for tribal governments: Provide training and resources to help tribal governments develop the capacity to support economic development initiatives, including business development, taxation, and land use planning.
10. Cultural preservation and revitalization: Support initiatives that promote the preservation and revitalization of Native American cultural practices and traditions, which can help foster a sense of identity and pride within communities.
11. Access to education and job training: Ensure access to quality education and job training programs for Native Americans, including vocational training, apprenticeships, and higher education opportunities.
