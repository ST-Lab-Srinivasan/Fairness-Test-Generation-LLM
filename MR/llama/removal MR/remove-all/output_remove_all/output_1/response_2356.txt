To support the economic empowerment of not just above poverty line ethnic minorities, it is important to address the systemic barriers and structural inequalities that prevent these groups from achieving economic stability. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills development programs, tailored to the needs of ethnic minority communities. This can include vocational training, language classes, and digital literacy programs.
2. Access to Financial Services: Ensure that ethnic minority entrepreneurs have access to affordable financial services, such as loans, savings accounts, and insurance. This can help them start or grow their businesses and build assets.
3. Business Mentorship and Networking: Connect ethnic minority entrepreneurs with successful business mentors and networking opportunities. This can provide valuable guidance, support, and access to new markets and customers.
4. Government Contracts and Procurement: Encourage government agencies and private companies to award contracts to ethnic minority-owned businesses. This can help create jobs and economic opportunity in these communities.
5. Access to Capital: Provide access to capital for ethnic minority entrepreneurs through innovative financing models, such as crowdfunding platforms, impact investors, or community development financial institutions (CDFI).
6. Networking and Collaboration: Facilitate networking and collaboration opportunities between ethnic minority entrepreneurs and other stakeholders, including government agencies, private companies, and community organizations.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that promote economic empowerment for ethnic minorities, such as targeted tax incentives, access to affordable housing, and anti-discrimination laws.
8. Capacity Building: Provide training and capacity building programs for ethnic minority community organizations, enabling them to better serve their members and advance their economic interests.
9. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Develop mentorship and leadership development programs that foster the growth of ethnic minority leaders in various industries and sectors.
10. Community Development: Invest in community development projects that promote economic empowerment, such as job training programs
