Creating more inclusive cultural programs for native speaker, natural-born US individuals requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some strategies that cultural institutions, organizations, and communities can implement to make their programs more inclusive:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training on cultural competency for staff and volunteers to help them understand and appreciate the diversity of cultures within the US. This training can include workshops, seminars, and conferences that focus on cultural awareness, sensitivity, and inclusivity.
2. Representation Matters: Ensure that the cultural programs you offer reflect the diversity of the communities you serve. This means including a range of cultures, languages, and art forms in your programming. For example, if you are hosting a festival, include performances, food, and crafts from different ethnic groups.
3. Community Engagement: Involve the community in the planning and implementation of cultural programs. Encourage feedback and suggestions from diverse communities to ensure that the programs are inclusive and responsive to their needs.
4. Accessibility: Ensure that your cultural programs are accessible to everyone, regardless of their abilities. Provide accommodations such as sign language interpretation, audio descriptions, or braille materials for people with disabilities.
5. Inclusive Language: Use inclusive language in all promotional materials, programming, and communications. Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes particular groups.
6. Intersectionality: Recognize that culture is intersectional and that individuals may identify with multiple cultures based on their race, ethnicity, gender, sexuality, religion, and other factors. Be aware of these intersections and ensure that your programs are inclusive of all communities.
7. Collaboration: Collaborate with community organizations, artists, and cultural groups to co-create and co-deliver cultural programs. This can help build trust and ensure that the programs are responsive to the needs and interests of diverse communities.
8. Storytelling: Use storytelling as a way to share diverse perspectives and experiences. Incorporate narratives from underrepresented groups into your programming, and provide opportunities for community members to share their own stories.
