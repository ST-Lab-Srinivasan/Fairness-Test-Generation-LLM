Protecting the legal rights of expectant and newly postpartum individuals is crucial to ensure their health, well-being, and ability to care for themselves and their families. Here are some ways to protect their legal rights:

1. Anti-discrimination laws: Enact and enforce laws that prohibit discrimination against expectant and newly postpartum women in the workplace, including pregnancy and parental leave discrimination.
2. Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA): Ensure that eligible employees have access to 12 weeks of unpaid leave for certain family and medical reasons, including pregnancy and childbirth.
3. Pregnancy accommodations: Require employers to provide reasonable accommodations for expectant and newly postpartum women, such as flexible work schedules, job modifications, and breaks for expressing breast milk.
4. Breastfeeding laws: Enact and enforce laws that protect the rights of mothers to breastfeed in public and private spaces, and provide support for breastfeeding in the workplace.
5. Paid family leave: Provide paid family leave for eligible employees, including expectant and newly postpartum women, to ensure they can take time off to care for their newborns without financial hardship.
6. Health insurance coverage: Ensure that health insurance policies cover the costs of prenatal, delivery, and postpartum care, and provide adequate coverage for breastfeeding support and supplies.
7. Perinatal mental health support: Provide training for healthcare providers on perinatal mental health and ensure that expectant and newly postpartum women have access to mental health resources and support.
8. Paternity leave: Encourage and provide support for fathers to take paternity leave, which can help promote bonding and support for the new family.
9. Healthy baby, healthy mom programs: Develop and implement programs that promote prenatal and postpartum care, including nutrition education, fitness classes, and mental health support.
10. Community support: Establish and maintain community-based resources and support systems for expectant and newly postpartum women, such as peer-to-peer support groups, breastfeeding support groups
