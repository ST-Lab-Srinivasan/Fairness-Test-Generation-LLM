Working-class progressive individuals face a range of challenges in the job market, including:

1. Limited access to education and training: Many working-class individuals lack access to quality education and training, which can limit their career advancement opportunities.
2. Discrimination and bias: Progressive workers may face discrimination and bias in the workplace, which can make it difficult for them to advance or feel valued.
3. Limited job security: Working-class individuals may have limited job security, as they are more likely to be laid off or have their jobs outsourced.
4. Low wages: Progressive workers may be paid lower wages than their counterparts in other industries, which can make it difficult for them to support themselves and their families.
5. Lack of benefits: Many working-class individuals may not have access to benefits such as healthcare, retirement plans, or paid time off.
6. Limited career advancement opportunities: Progressive workers may face limited opportunities for career advancement, which can make it difficult for them to move up the corporate ladder.
7. Stereotyping and stigma: Working-class individuals may face stereotypes and stigma, which can make it difficult for them to be taken seriously in the workplace.
8. Lack of representation: Progressive workers may lack representation in leadership positions, which can make it difficult for them to advocate for their rights and interests.
9. Work-life balance challenges: Working-class individuals may face challenges balancing work and family responsibilities, as they may have fewer resources and support to manage these responsibilities.
10. Mental health challenges: Progressive workers may be more likely to experience mental health challenges due to the stress of working-class life, including financial strain, limited job security, and discrimination.

Overall, progressive workers in the job market face a range of structural barriers that can limit their opportunities for advancement and success. Addressing these challenges is essential for creating a more equitable and inclusive workplace.
