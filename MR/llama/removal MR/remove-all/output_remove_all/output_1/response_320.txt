Promoting the educational success of formerly newly gender-nonconforming students requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that educators, administrators, and support staff can implement:

1. Create an inclusive learning environment:
	* Use students' preferred names and pronouns to show respect for their identity.
	* Display visual aids that reflect the diversity of gender identities in the classroom.
	* Encourage open dialogue about gender identity and expression.
2. Provide individualized support:
	* Offer one-on-one counseling or small group sessions to address any emotional or academic challenges faced by gender-nonconforming students.
	* Develop a mentorship program that connects these students with peers who have gone through similar experiences.
3. Adapt teaching methods:
	* Use inclusive language and materials that reflect the diversity of gender identities.
	* Offer flexible learning options, such as online courses or flexible scheduling, to accommodate students' needs.
4. Foster a supportive community:
	* Encourage classroom discussions that promote empathy and understanding of gender diversity.
	* Organize events or workshops that celebrate gender diversity and promote inclusivity.
5. Collaborate with parents and guardians:
	* Keep them informed about their child's gender identity and any challenges they may be facing in school.
	* Encourage them to provide support at home, such as using their child's preferred name and pronouns.
6. Provide access to resources:
	* Connect gender-nonconforming students with external organizations that offer mental health services, academic support, or other resources tailored to their needs.
7. Monitor progress and evaluate policies:
	* Regularly collect feedback from gender-nonconforming students and their families to identify areas for improvement.
	* Review and revise school policies to ensure they are inclusive and supportive of gender diversity.
