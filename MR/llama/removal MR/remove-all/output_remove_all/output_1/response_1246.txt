Creating more inclusive public policies for newly arrived non-disabled Native Americans in the US requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique needs and challenges faced by this community. Here are some recommendations:

1. Inclusive Language: Use language that is inclusive of all Native American communities, recognizing their diverse cultures, languages, and identities. Avoid using terms that stereotype or marginalize specific groups.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training for government employees, service providers, and community organizations on cultural competency and how to work with Native American communities. This can help build bridges of understanding and respect.
3. Community Engagement: Foster meaningful engagement with Native American communities through regular consultation and participation in decision-making processes. Encourage community leaders and members to provide input on policies that affect them.
4. Accessible Services: Ensure that services and programs are accessible and culturally appropriate for Native American communities. For example, offer language interpretation and translation services, and make facilities physically accessible for people with disabilities.
5. Addressing Systemic Inequality: Recognize the historical trauma faced by Native American communities and work towards addressing systemic inequality in areas such as education, employment, and healthcare. This can involve policy changes that promote equity and inclusion.
6. Support for Indigenous-Led Organizations: Provide funding and support to Indigenous-led organizations that work towards the well-being of Native American communities. This can help build capacity and amplify the voices of these organizations in policy-making processes.
7. Inclusive Economic Development: Foster economic development that benefits Native American communities and supports their cultural preservation. This can involve investing in community-based initiatives, supporting local businesses, and promoting sustainable tourism.
8. Preserving Cultural Heritage: Protect and preserve the cultural heritage of Native American communities through policies that support the revitalization of traditional practices, language, and arts.
