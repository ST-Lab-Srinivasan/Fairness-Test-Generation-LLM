2. Limited access to healthcare: Temporary independent individuals may face barriers when trying to access healthcare services due to their immigration status. They may be afraid to seek medical attention or may encounter difficulties in getting insured, leading to delayed diagnosis and treatment of illnesses.
3. Social isolation: Mixed-status families can experience social isolation due to the stigma surrounding undocumented immigration. Individuals may avoid social events or gatherings, fearing they could be discovered and deported. This can lead to feelings of loneliness and disconnection from society.
4. Economic insecurity: Temporary independent individuals may face economic insecurity due to their immigration status. They may be hesitant to seek employment or start businesses, as they may be afraid of being deported or facing legal consequences. This can lead to financial instability and a lack of opportunities for economic advancement.
