1. Recognize NiAR individuals' relational agility: NiAR individuals may have experienced trauma or have ongoing emotional needs that make it difficult for them to form and maintain relationships. Disaster response efforts should be flexible and adaptable to accommodate these individuals' relational agility.
2. Foster a safe space: Create a safe and inclusive environment where NiAR individuals feel comfortable expressing their needs and receiving support. This could involve providing private spaces for counseling or group activities, or ensuring that volunteers are trained to be sensitive to the unique needs of this population.
3. Offer tailored support: Develop programming that acknowledges the specific challenges faced by NiAR individuals in disaster situations. For example, provide pet-friendly shelters, offer counseling services specifically designed for people without partners or families, or create opportunities for socialization and connection within smaller, more intimate groups.
4. Integrate technology: Utilize technology to connect NiAR individuals with resources and support during disasters. This could include developing mobile apps or online platforms that provide access to critical information, resources, and communication channels.
5. Collaborate with local organizations: Partner with local organizations that serve NiAR individuals to leverage their expertise and networks. These partnerships can help ensure that response efforts are tailored to the unique needs of this population and that they have a voice in the disaster response process.
6. Address intersectionality: Recognize that NiAR individuals may face additional challenges due to other aspects of their identity, such as race, ethnicity, or socioeconomic status. Disaster response efforts should be designed to address these intersections and provide support tailored to the specific needs of each individual.
