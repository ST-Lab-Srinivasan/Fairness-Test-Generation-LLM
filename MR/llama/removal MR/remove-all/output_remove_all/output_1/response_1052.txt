Ensuring that the educational needs of natural-born cultural individuals are met requires a thoughtful and inclusive approach to education. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural Competence Training for Educators: Provide training on cultural competence for educators, including understanding the cultural backgrounds of students and how to create an inclusive learning environment.
2. Incorporate Cultural Content into Curriculum: Incorporate cultural content and perspectives into the curriculum, including literature, history, and social studies. This can help students see themselves reflected in the material and feel more connected to their heritage.
3. Language Preservation and Promotion: Support language preservation and promotion efforts for indigenous languages and other marginalized languages. This can involve providing language classes or resources for students, as well as promoting language use within the school community.
4. Community Engagement and Partnerships: Build partnerships with local communities and organizations to provide cultural enrichment programs, guest speakers, and mentorship opportunities. These can help students connect with their cultural heritage and develop a sense of identity and purpose.
5. Inclusive Curriculum Design: Design curricula that are inclusive of diverse perspectives and experiences. This can involve incorporating multiple voices and stories into the curriculum, as well as using teaching methods that promote critical thinking and cultural awareness.
6. Teacher Training and Support: Provide ongoing training and support for teachers to help them better understand and address the educational needs of natural-born cultural individuals. This can involve providing resources on culturally responsive teaching practices, as well as creating opportunities for teachers to share knowledge and experiences.
7. Student Voice and Choice: Incorporate student voice and choice into the learning process, allowing students to take an active role in their own education. This can help students develop a sense of agency and ownership over their learning, as well as promote cultural competence and self-awareness.
