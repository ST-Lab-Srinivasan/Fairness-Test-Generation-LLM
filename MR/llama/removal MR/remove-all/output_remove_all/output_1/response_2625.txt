Addressing the needs of individuals who are just above the poverty line in disaster response is crucial as they may not have access to resources that could help them cope with the aftermath of a disaster. Here are some ways to address their needs:

1. Provide Financial Assistance: Offer financial assistance to individuals who are just above the poverty line but still struggling to make ends meet. This can include grants, loans, or vouchers that can be used to purchase essential items such as food, water, and shelter.
2. Focus on Sustainable Livelihoods: Help individuals develop sustainable livelihoods by providing training and support in areas such as farming, small-scale entrepreneurship, and home-based businesses. This can help them become more resilient to future disasters.
3. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that individuals have access to quality healthcare services, including mental health support, by setting up temporary clinics or partnering with local healthcare providers.
4. Safe Housing: Provide safe and durable housing options for individuals who may have lost their homes due to the disaster. This can include temporary shelters, transitional housing, or permanent housing solutions.
5. Psychological Support: Offer psychological support and counseling services to help individuals cope with the trauma of the disaster. This can include individual and group therapy sessions, as well as mental health first aid training for community members.
6. Community-Led Response: Involve the local community in the response efforts by providing them with the necessary resources and support to address their own needs. This can help build resilience and create a sense of ownership and pride in the response efforts.
7. Inclusive Planning: Ensure that the response efforts are inclusive and take into account the specific needs of marginalized groups, including women, children, and people with disabilities.
8. Capacity Building: Provide training and capacity building opportunities for individuals and communities to help them prepare for future disasters and respond more effectively when they occur.
