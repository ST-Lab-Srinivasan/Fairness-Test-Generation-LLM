Supporting the civic engagement of cisgender beginner speakers is crucial for fostering inclusivity and participation in public discourse. Here are some ways to support their engagement:

1. Create safe spaces: Provide a comfortable and welcoming environment where beginners can share their thoughts without fear of judgment or criticism. Encourage respectful listening and open communication.
2. Offer training and resources: Provide workshops, tutorials, or online resources that teach beginners how to communicate effectively, prepare for speaking engagements, and handle questions from the audience.
3. Encourage participation: Actively invite beginners to participate in discussions, debates, or other speaking events. Make sure they feel included and valued by acknowledging their contributions.
4. Provide feedback: Offer constructive feedback on beginners' speaking performances, highlighting areas of improvement and encouraging them to continue practicing.
5. Role-model inclusive behavior: Set a good example by demonstrating respectful communication, active listening, and empathy towards others. Encourage beginners to follow your lead and emulate these behaviors in their own interactions.
6. Networking opportunities: Arrange networking events or introductions between beginners and experienced speakers, allowing them to learn from each other and build connections within the speaking community.
7. Celebrate successes: Recognize and celebrate the achievements of beginner speakers, no matter how small. This will help build their confidence and encourage them to continue participating in civic discourse.
8. Provide mentorship: Pair beginners with experienced speakers who can offer guidance, support, and valuable insights into the art of public speaking.
9. Encourage peer-to-peer learning: Facilitate opportunities for beginners to learn from each other through peer-to-peer discussions or workshops. This can help build a sense of community and mutual support within the beginner speaker group.
10. Foster a growth mindset: Encourage beginners to view public speaking as a skill that can be developed over time with practice and dedication. Emphasize the importance of perseverance, resilience, and continuous learning.
