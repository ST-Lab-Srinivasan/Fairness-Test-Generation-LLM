People with disabilities face significant challenges in their daily lives, including barriers to accessibility, social exclusion, and discrimination. The impact of disability status on the lives of long-term people can be far-reaching and varied, affecting different aspects of their well-being, including:

1. Social isolation: People with disabilities may experience social isolation due to a lack of accessibility in their environment, which can lead to feelings of loneliness and disconnection from society.
2. Limited access to education and employment: Disability can create barriers to accessing education and employment opportunities, leading to lower levels of educational attainment and economic participation.
3. Health disparities: People with disabilities may experience poorer health outcomes due to inadequate access to healthcare services, which can exacerbate existing conditions or lead to new ones.
4. Stigma and discrimination: Disability can lead to social stigma and discrimination, which can negatively impact mental health and overall well-being.
5. Financial constraints: People with disabilities may face financial challenges due to higher costs associated with living with a disability, such as assistive technology, transportation, and home modifications.
6. Limited participation in civic life: Disability can limit participation in civic life, including voting, volunteering, and engaging in community activities.
7. Lack of representation and voice: People with disabilities are often underrepresented in decision-making processes, which can result in policies and practices that do not reflect their needs and priorities.
8. Inadequate support systems: Disability can lead to a lack of access to support systems, such as healthcare, social services, and family support networks, which can exacerbate existing challenges.
9. Mental health concerns: Living with a disability can lead to increased stress, anxiety, and depression, which can impact mental health and overall well-being.
