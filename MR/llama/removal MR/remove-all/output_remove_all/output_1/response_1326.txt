Ensuring that the voices of beginner speakers are heard in decision-making processes is crucial to create a more inclusive and diverse society. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Create safe spaces: Beginner speakers may feel intimidated or uncomfortable expressing their opinions in front of experienced speakers. Create safe spaces where they can share their thoughts without fear of judgment or criticism.
2. Use inclusive language: Avoid using language that may be alienating or exclusive. Use language that is inclusive and welcoming to all participants, regardless of their level of experience.
3. Encourage participation: Make sure that all participants are encouraged to share their thoughts and opinions. Use techniques like round-robin discussions or small group discussions to give everyone a chance to speak.
4. Foster a culture of mutual respect: Encourage all participants to listen actively and show respect for each other's opinions. Create a culture where everyone feels valued and heard.
5. Provide opportunities for training and development: Offer training and development opportunities to help beginner speakers build their confidence and skills. This can include workshops, mentorship programs, or coaching sessions.
6. Celebrate diverse perspectives: Embrace the diversity of opinions and perspectives that beginner speakers bring to the table. Avoid dismissing or marginalizing their views, and instead, create an environment where all voices are valued.
7. Use technology to amplify their voices: Utilize digital platforms to amplify the voices of beginner speakers. This can include creating online communities or forums where they can share their thoughts and ideas without fear of judgment.
8. Create a mentorship program: Pair beginner speakers with experienced speakers who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement. This can help build their confidence and skills over time.
9. Encourage them to take risks: Encourage beginner speakers to take risks and try new things. Provide a safe and supportive environment where they can experiment with different speaking styles and techniques.
10. Celebrate their successes: Celebrate the successes of beginner speakers, no matter how small they may seem. This can help build their confidence and motivation to continue speaking up
