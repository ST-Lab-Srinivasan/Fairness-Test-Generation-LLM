Creating more inclusive cultural events for recently civilian individuals can be achieved by taking into account their unique needs and experiences. Here are some suggestions:

1. Involve the community: Engage with local communities and organizations that represent diverse groups of people, such as LGBTQ+, people of color, people with disabilities, and others. This will help ensure that the event is inclusive and representative of the community.
2. Provide accessibility options: Offer accessible venues, performances, and activities to accommodate individuals with disabilities. This can include sign language interpretation, audio descriptions, and wheelchair-accessible spaces.
3. Promote diversity in programming: Ensure that the event's programming reflects the diversity of the community. This can be achieved by featuring performers and artists from different backgrounds and cultures, and including a range of art forms such as music, dance, theater, and visual arts.
4. Offer multilingual support: Provide interpreters or translators for individuals who may not speak the dominant language of the event. This can include language interpretation during performances, as well as printed materials and signage in multiple languages.
5. Foster a welcoming environment: Train staff and volunteers on how to create an inclusive and welcoming environment for all attendees. This can include providing information on accessibility options, being respectful of individuals' cultural practices and beliefs, and creating a safe space for all.
6. Involve youth: Engage with local schools and youth organizations to involve young people in the planning and execution of the event. This can help ensure that the event is relevant and appealing to a diverse range of ages and backgrounds.
7. Provide sensory-friendly activities: Offer activities that are tailored to individuals with sensory processing needs, such as interactive art installations or calming spaces.
8. Offer financial assistance: Provide financial support to individuals who may not be able to afford the cost of attending the event. This can include scholarships, discounted tickets, or sliding scale pricing.
9. Create a sense of community: Encourage attendees to connect with one another through icebreaker activities, group discussions, and networking opportunities. This can help create a sense of belonging and inclusivity
