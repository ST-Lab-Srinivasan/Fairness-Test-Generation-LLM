Ensuring that the housing needs of mixed-mildly individuals are met requires a comprehensive and inclusive approach that takes into account their unique needs and circumstances. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Inclusive Housing Policies: Develop inclusive housing policies that recognize the rights of mixed-mildly individuals to adequate and affordable housing. These policies should aim to provide a range of housing options, including shared accommodation, co-living spaces, and accessible apartments.
2. Accessible Design: Ensure that housing design is inclusive and accessible to people with mild intellectual disabilities. This can be achieved by incorporating universal design principles, such as open spaces, easy navigation, and adaptable features.
3. Supported Housing: Provide supported housing options for mixed-mildly individuals who may require additional support in their daily lives. This can include shared accommodation with on-site support staff or nearby community resources.
4. Housing Coordination: Establish a housing coordination system that connects mixed-mildly individuals with available housing options and provides ongoing support to help them maintain their tenancies.
5. Financing Options: Explore innovative financing options, such as social impact bonds or community land trusts, to provide affordable and accessible housing for mixed-mildly individuals.
6. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement and participation in housing planning and development to ensure that the needs of mixed-mildly individuals are taken into account.
7. Training and Education: Provide training and education for housing providers, policymakers, and other stakeholders on the unique needs and strengths of mixed-mildly individuals.
8. Inclusive Planning: Ensure that urban planning and development processes are inclusive and take into account the needs of mixed-mildly individuals. This can involve incorporating universal design principles, promoting walkability and bikeability, and creating public spaces that are accessible to all.
