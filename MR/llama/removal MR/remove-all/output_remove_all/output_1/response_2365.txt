Supporting the economic empowerment of marginalized groups, including those who are not multimillionaires, is crucial for creating a more equitable and inclusive society. Here are some ways to support the economic empowerment of individuals from these groups:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills development programs that can help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge to enhance their employability and earning potential. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and vocational education programs.
2. Microfinance and Access to Credit: Offer microfinance options and other forms of access to credit to help individuals start or grow their businesses. This can include small loans, line of credits, and other financial instruments that are tailored to the needs of marginalized groups.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Provide resources and support for entrepreneurs from marginalized groups to start and grow their businesses. This can include mentorship, networking opportunities, and access to funding.
4. Job Training and Placement: Offer job training programs that focus on in-demand skills and industries, such as technology, healthcare, and renewable energy. Additionally, provide placement services to help individuals find employment opportunities that match their skills and experience.
5. Addressing Systemic Barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers that prevent marginalized groups from accessing economic opportunities. This can include advocating for policy changes, challenging discriminatory practices, and promoting diversity and inclusion in hiring processes.
6. Community Development: Invest in community development initiatives that provide a range of services, such as job training, mentorship, and access to healthcare. These initiatives can help build stronger, more resilient communities that support economic empowerment.
7. Addressing Intersectionality: Recognize that individuals from marginalized groups may face multiple forms of discrimination based on their race, gender, sexual orientation, religion, and other aspects of their identity. Addressing intersectionality is crucial for creating a more inclusive and equitable society.
