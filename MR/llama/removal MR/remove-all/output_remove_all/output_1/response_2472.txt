There are several ways to highlight the achievements of new employees, even if they have been with the company for a relatively short period of time:

1. Create a welcome package: Put together a packet of information about the company, including its history, mission, and values. Include any relevant documents, such as employee handbooks or policy manuals. This will help new employees get up to speed quickly and feel more connected to the organization.
2. Provide orientation sessions: Offer formal orientation sessions that cover the company's policies, procedures, and expectations. This can include presentations from key personnel, tours of the facilities, and opportunities for new employees to ask questions.
3. Assign mentors: Match new employees with experienced colleagues who can provide guidance and support. This can help new employees navigate the company culture and get a better understanding of how things work around here.
4. Set clear expectations: Make sure new employees understand what is expected of them in terms of job responsibilities, performance goals, and communication channels. Provide regular feedback and coaching to help them stay on track.
5. Celebrate milestones: Recognize and celebrate the achievements of new employees as they complete milestones and reach important benchmarks. This can help build morale and reinforce the company's commitment to recognizing and rewarding hard work.
6. Offer training and development opportunities: Provide opportunities for new employees to learn and grow within the organization. This can include formal training programs, as well as on-the-job learning and mentoring.
7. Encourage collaboration: Create opportunities for new employees to work with other teams and departments. This can help them build relationships and get a better understanding of how different parts of the company work together.
8. Share successes: Highlight the achievements of new employees in company-wide communications, such as newsletters or all-hands meetings. This can help build morale and reinforce the company's commitment to recognizing and rewarding hard work.
9. Provide feedback and coaching: Regularly provide feedback and coaching to new employees to help them improve their performance and achieve their goals. This can involve regular check-ins, as well as formal evaluations at set intervals.
