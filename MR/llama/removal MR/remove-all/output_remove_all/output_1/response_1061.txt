Supporting the educational aspirations of beginner speaker people involves providing them with opportunities and resources to help them improve their public speaking skills. Here are some ways to do so:

1. Offer training programs: Provide beginner speaker people with structured training programs that teach them the fundamentals of public speaking, such as effective communication, body language, vocal variety, and storytelling.
2. Encourage practice: Encourage beginner speaker people to practice their public speaking skills regularly. This can be done through regular speeches in front of a small group or by recording themselves and watching the videos to identify areas for improvement.
3. Provide feedback: Offer constructive feedback on their public speaking performances to help them identify areas for improvement. Be specific with your feedback, highlighting what they did well and what they can improve on.
4. Connect them with mentors: Pair beginner speaker people with experienced speakers who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement. This can help them learn from others' experiences and gain valuable insights into the world of public speaking.
5. Encourage networking: Encourage beginner speaker people to attend industry events and conferences where they can network with other professionals in the field. This can help them learn from others, gain new ideas, and build their confidence in public speaking.
6. Offer resources: Provide beginner speaker people with access to resources such as books, videos, and online courses that can help them improve their public speaking skills. Encourage them to watch TED Talks, listen to podcasts, or read books on the topic of public speaking.
7. Create a supportive environment: Foster a supportive and inclusive environment where beginner speaker people feel comfortable sharing their ideas and experiences. Encourage collaboration and constructive feedback to help them improve their skills.
8. Recognize and reward progress: Celebrate the progress of beginner speaker people and recognize their achievements along the way. This can help motivate them to continue improving their public speaking skills.
9. Be patient and encouraging: Support beginner speaker people by being patient and encouraging. Help them set realistic goals and provide positive reinforcement as they work towards achieving those goals.
