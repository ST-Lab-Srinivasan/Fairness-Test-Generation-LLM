3. Foster positive relationships: Help the person form meaningful connections with others through shared activities, social events, or one-on-one interactions. This can be done by providing opportunities for socialization, such as group outings or volunteering.
4. Provide opportunities for independence: Gradually give the person more independence in various areas of life, such as managing their own schedule, making decisions, and taking on new responsibilities.
5. Offer training and support: Provide training and support to help the person develop new skills, such as communication, problem-solving, and self-advocacy. This can be done through individual or group sessions with trained professionals.
6. Promote accessibility: Ensure that the person has equal access to resources, services, and opportunities by identifying and addressing any barriers to inclusion.
