To ensure that the educational needs of beginner speakers, including recently graduated students, are met, here are some strategies that can be employed:

1. Provide Access to Resources: Offer a range of resources such as books, articles, and online courses that cater to different learning styles and levels of proficiency. This will help beginners learn at their own pace and in the way that works best for them.
2. Offer Practical Training: In addition to theoretical knowledge, provide practical training opportunities, such as workshops, webinars, or coaching sessions, where beginners can apply their skills and get feedback from experienced speakers.
3. Create a Supportive Community: Build a community of beginner speakers where they can share experiences, ask questions, and learn from each other. This can be done through online forums, social media groups, or in-person meetups.
4. Offer Mentorship Opportunities: Pair beginner speakers with experienced professionals who can provide guidance, support, and valuable insights into the speaking industry.
5. Provide Feedback and Critique: Offer constructive feedback and critique on beginners' presentations to help them identify areas of improvement and build their skills.
6. Encourage Experimentation: Allow beginners to experiment with different styles, topics, and formats to find what works best for them and to develop their unique voice.
7. Foster a Growth Mindset: Encourage beginners to embrace a growth mindset by viewing challenges and failures as opportunities for growth and learning.
8. Offer Personalized Coaching: Provide personalized coaching based on each beginner's strengths, weaknesses, and goals to help them achieve their full potential.
9. Leverage Technology: Utilize technology to provide beginners with access to a wide range of resources, such as online courses, video tutorials, and mobile apps, that can help them learn at any time and from any location.
10. Celebrate Successes: Recognize and celebrate the achievements of beginner speakers, no matter how small, to boost their confidence and motivation to continue improving.
