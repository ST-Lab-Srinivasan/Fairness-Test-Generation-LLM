Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of newly beginner speaker individuals. It refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, and practices that are passed down from one generation to the next within a particular culture or society. For individuals who are learning a new language and culture, their cultural heritage can serve as a foundation for their identity formation. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage can influence newly beginner speaker individuals:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a particular group or community. For instance, a person who is learning a new language and culture may feel more connected to their own cultural heritage, leading to a stronger sense of identity.
2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage influences how individuals perceive themselves in relation to others. For example, someone who is learning a new language and culture may find that their existing identity is challenged or expanded as they learn about new customs and practices.
3. Language acquisition: The process of acquiring a new language can be influenced by an individual's cultural heritage. For instance, someone who is familiar with multiple languages may find it easier to learn a new language due to their existing linguistic knowledge.
4. Interaction with others: Cultural heritage can affect how individuals interact with others in the language learning process. For example, someone from a collectivist culture may be more comfortable communicating in a group setting, while someone from an individualist culture may prefer one-on-one interactions.
5. Learning strategies: An individual's cultural heritage can also influence their preferred learning strategies. For instance, someone who is more visual in their learning style may find it helpful to use images or videos to aid in language acquisition.
6. Attitudes towards language learning: Cultural heritage can shape attitudes towards language learning. For example, someone from a culture that values education and intellectual pursuits may be more motivated to learn a new language.
7. Social identity: Cultural heritage can affect how individuals perceive themselves in relation to others within the language learning context. For instance, someone who is part of a minority group within their new cultural environment may find it challenging to navigate social interactions and language use.
