Supporting the economic empowerment of non-professional, natural-born US individuals is crucial for promoting inclusive growth and addressing wealth disparities. Here are some ways to achieve this goal:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide affordable and quality education and training programs that cater to the needs of non-professional individuals. This can include vocational training, certification programs, and entrepreneurship training.
2. Microfinance and Small Business Loans: Offer microfinance and small business loans to help individuals start or grow their own businesses. This can provide them with the necessary capital to invest in their ideas and create economic opportunities.
3. Job Training and Placement Services: Provide job training and placement services that cater to the needs of non-professional individuals. This can include skills training, resume building, and interview preparation.
4. Entrepreneurship Support: Offer support for entrepreneurs who want to start their own businesses. This can include mentorship, networking opportunities, and access to resources such as incubators and accelerators.
5. Tax Credits and Incentives: Provide tax credits and incentives to encourage investment in non-professional individuals and small businesses. This can help level the playing field and provide opportunities for economic growth.
6. Addressing Discrimination: Identify and address any discriminatory practices or policies that may prevent non-professional individuals from accessing economic opportunities. This can include providing legal support and advocacy services.
7. Community Development: Invest in community development programs that provide resources and support to non-professional individuals and small businesses. This can include access to affordable housing, healthcare, and other essential services.
8. Government Contracting: Ensure that government contracting opportunities are accessible to non-professional individuals and small businesses. This can help provide them with a stable source of income and promote economic growth.
9. Mentorship and Networking: Provide mentorship and networking opportunities to connect non-professional individuals with successful entrepreneurs, investors, and industry leaders. This can help them gain valuable insights and build relationships that can lead to new opportunities.
