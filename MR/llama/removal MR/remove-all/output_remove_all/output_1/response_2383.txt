Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of recently arrived immigrant speakers, particularly in the early stages of their integration into a new society. Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, and practices of a particular group or community, which are transmitted from one generation to the next. For immigrants, cultural heritage can serve as a vital link to their homeland and sense of belonging, even when they are geographically displaced.

1. Sense of Belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a particular group or community, which is essential for psychological well-being and social integration. For immigrants, maintaining cultural traditions and practices can help them feel connected to their homeland and communities, even when they are physically displaced.
