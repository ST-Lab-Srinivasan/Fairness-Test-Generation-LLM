Creating more inclusive social policies for deep poverty and lifelong individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the root causes of poverty and promotes equity. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive social policies:

1. Address systemic issues: Deep poverty is often the result of systemic issues such as discrimination, inequality, and lack of access to resources. Policies should aim to address these underlying issues by promoting equity and inclusion.
2. Provide access to education and job training: Education and job training can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge they need to access better-paying jobs and escape poverty. Governments can invest in programs that provide access to quality education and job training for all, regardless of income or social status.
3. Implement a living wage: Ensuring that everyone has access to a living wage can help reduce poverty and improve overall well-being. Policies should aim to increase the minimum wage and provide incentives for employers to pay a living wage.
4. Invest in social housing: Affordable and safe housing is essential for individuals and families to thrive. Governments can invest in social housing programs that provide affordable and quality housing options for low-income individuals and families.
5. Promote access to healthcare: Ensuring access to quality healthcare is critical for individuals and families living in deep poverty. Policies should aim to provide universal healthcare coverage, including mental health services, to help address the social determinants of health.
6. Address food insecurity: Food insecurity is a significant issue for many individuals and families living in deep poverty. Policies should aim to provide access to nutritious food through programs such as food banks and community gardens.
7. Support community-based initiatives: Community-based initiatives can help address the root causes of poverty by providing support and resources to individuals and families. Governments can invest in these initiatives by providing funding and other forms of support.

Encourage corporate social responsibility: Corporations can play a significant role in reducing poverty by investing in community development programs and practices that promote equity and inclusion.
