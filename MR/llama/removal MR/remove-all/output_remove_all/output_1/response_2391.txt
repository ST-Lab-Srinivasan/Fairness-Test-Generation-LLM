Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of recently arrived immigrant individuals, particularly those who are beginners in the language and culture of their adopted country. Here are some ways cultural heritage can impact the identities of these individuals:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a particular group or community, which is essential for individuals who may be feeling isolated or disconnected from their adopted society. By connecting with their cultural heritage, they can find a sense of belonging and identity within their new community.
2. Cultural practices and traditions: Cultural heritage includes the customs, traditions, and practices that are passed down from one generation to the next. For recently arrived immigrants, these practices can provide a sense of continuity and stability in an unfamiliar environment. They may also help individuals maintain their cultural identity and connect with their roots.
3. Language acquisition: Learning the language and culture of their adopted country is crucial for newly arrived immigrants. Cultural heritage can play a significant role in this process by providing a framework for language acquisition and cultural understanding. For example, learning about customs and traditions associated with a particular holiday or celebration can help individuals better understand the language and culture of their adopted country.
4. Identity formation: Cultural heritage can influence how individuals form their identities in their new surroundings. By connecting with their cultural heritage, they can develop a sense of self that is rooted in their cultural background. This can help them navigate the complexities of their adopted society and build relationships with others from different cultural backgrounds.
5. Intersectionality: Cultural heritage intersects with other aspects of identity, such as race, ethnicity, and socio-economic status. Recently arrived immigrants may find that their cultural heritage influences how they experience these intersections, particularly if they are part of a marginalized group in their adopted society.
6. Power dynamics: Cultural heritage can also influence the power dynamics between individuals and groups within a society. For example, individuals from dominant cultures may hold more power and privilege than those from marginalized cultures, which can impact how cultural heritage is perceived and valued.
