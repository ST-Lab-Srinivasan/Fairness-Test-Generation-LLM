Retired Native American service members often face unique challenges upon returning to civilian life, including cultural disconnection, discrimination, and a lack of support. To address these challenges, several support systems have become increasingly important for combat recently retired Native natural-born US individuals:

1. Veterans Service Organizations (VSOs): VSOs like the American Legion, Disabled American Veterans, and Veterans of Foreign Wars provide a range of services, including counseling, employment assistance, and advocacy for veterans' rights. These organizations often have specific chapters or programs focused on serving Native American veterans.
2. Native American Veterans Organizations: There are several organizations that cater specifically to Native American veterans, such as the National Congress of American Indians (NCAI) and the Native American Vietnam Veterans Memorial Foundation. These organizations provide cultural support, advocacy, and resources for Native American veterans.
3. Mental Health Services: Combat veterans often experience mental health issues like PTSD, depression, and anxiety. Retired Native American service members are no exception. Accessing mental health services through the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) or other organizations can help them manage these challenges effectively.
4. Employment Assistance: Returning to civilian life can be daunting, especially for veterans who face discrimination or struggle to find employment due to cultural barriers. Organizations like the US Department of Labor's Veterans' Employment and Training Service (VETS) and the National Resource Directory provide job training, placement, and other employment resources specifically designed for Native American veterans.
5. Educational Opportunities: Many retired Native American service members may be interested in pursuing higher education or vocational training to enhance their career prospects. Programs like the GI Bill and the VA's Education Benefits Program can help cover the cost of tuition, fees, and other expenses associated with furthering their education.
