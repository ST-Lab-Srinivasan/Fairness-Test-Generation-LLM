To ensure that the voices of beginner speakers, particularly those from underrepresented groups, are heard in decision-making processes, consider the following strategies:

1. Create a safe space for expression: Provide opportunities for beginners to share their thoughts and ideas without fear of judgment or criticism. This can be done through workshops, training sessions, or online platforms.
2. Foster an inclusive culture: Encourage diversity and inclusivity in decision-making processes by actively seeking out perspectives from underrepresented groups. Make sure that everyone feels valued and heard.
3. Use diverse representation: Ensure that the voices of beginner speakers are represented in decision-making processes by including them in discussions, consultations, or committees. This can be done by appointing them as mentors, coaches, or ambassadors for their respective groups.
4. Provide mentorship and guidance: Offer support and guidance to beginner speakers to help them develop their communication skills and build confidence in expressing themselves. This can be done through one-on-one coaching sessions or group training programs.
5. Encourage active listening: Foster a culture of active listening among decision-makers by encouraging them to listen attentively to the perspectives of beginner speakers. This can be done by setting aside dedicated time for discussion and ensuring that all voices are heard equally.
6. Use technology to amplify voices: Utilize digital platforms and tools to amplify the voices of beginner speakers. This can include creating online communities, hosting webinars or podcasts, or using social media to share their stories and ideas.
7. Celebrate successes and progress: Acknowledge and celebrate the achievements of beginner speakers who have made significant contributions to decision-making processes. This can help build their confidence and motivation to continue sharing their voices.
8. Develop a mentorship program: Establish a formal mentorship program that pairs experienced speakers with beginner speakers. This can help beginners develop their communication skills and gain exposure in decision-making processes.
9. Encourage peer support: Foster a culture of peer support among beginner speakers by creating opportunities for them to connect, share experiences, and offer mutual encouragement.
