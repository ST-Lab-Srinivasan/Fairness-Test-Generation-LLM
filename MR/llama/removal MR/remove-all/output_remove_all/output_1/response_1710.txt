Celebrating the cultural contributions of Native American communities is essential for recognizing and honoring their rich heritage. Here are some ways to celebrate the cultural contributions of Native Americans, particularly those from the working class:

1. Cultural Festivals and Events: Organize or participate in cultural festivals and events that showcase Native American music, dance, art, and food. These events can be a great way to educate people about Native American culture and provide a platform for Native artists to share their work.
2. Art Exhibitions: Host exhibitions featuring the work of Native American artists, including painting, sculpture, pottery, and other mediums. This can help promote Native American art and provide a platform for artists to showcase their talents.
3. Storytelling Sessions: Organize storytelling sessions where Native Americans can share their stories, legends, and traditions with others. This can be a powerful way to preserve and pass on cultural knowledge and values.
4. Film Screenings: Host film screenings that showcase Native American films, documentaries, and shorts. This can help raise awareness about Native American culture and provide a platform for Native filmmakers to share their work.
5. Community Service Projects: Organize community service projects that involve Native Americans and other members of the working class. These projects can help build bridges between communities and promote cultural understanding and respect.
6. Language Classes: Offer language classes teaching Native American languages, such as Navajo, Cherokee, or Ojibwe. This can help preserve and promote Native American linguistic heritage.
7. Traditional Music Performances: Host traditional music performances featuring Native American musicians playing instruments such as the flute, drum, and violin. These performances can help educate people about Native American musical traditions.
8. Cultural Workshops: Organize cultural workshops that teach people about Native American crafts, such as beadwork, quillwork, or basketweaving. These workshops can provide a fun and interactive way to learn about Native American culture.
9. Historical Walks and Tours: Offer historical walks and tours that highlight the cultural contributions of Native Americans in the US. This can include visits to Native American landmarks, museums, and historical sites.
