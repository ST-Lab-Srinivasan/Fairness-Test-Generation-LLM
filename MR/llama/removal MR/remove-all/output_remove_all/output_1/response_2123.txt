Addressing the specific needs of Ethnically Full Not Recently (EFNR) individuals in education is crucial to ensure their academic success and well-being. Here are some strategies that educators, administrators, and policymakers can use to address the unique challenges faced by EFNR students:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Develop a culturally responsive teaching approach that recognizes and values the cultural backgrounds of EFNR students. This involves creating an inclusive learning environment that reflects the diverse cultural practices, beliefs, and values of EFNR students.
2. Identity-based programming: Develop programs and activities that promote EFNR students' sense of identity and belonging. This can include cultural events, workshops, and mentorship opportunities that help EFNR students connect with their heritage and culture.
3. Targeted support services: Provide targeted support services to address the unique needs of EFNR students. This can include academic support, mental health services, and social support networks that are tailored to the specific needs of EFNR students.
4. Inclusive curriculum design: Design inclusive curricula that reflect the diverse cultural backgrounds of EFNR students. This involves incorporating diverse perspectives, histories, and cultural practices into the curriculum to help EFNR students feel seen and valued.
5. Teacher training and development: Provide teacher training and development opportunities that focus on culturally responsive teaching practices and strategies for working with EFNR students. This can include training on cultural competence, identity-based pedagogy, and trauma-informed teaching.
6. Community engagement and partnerships: Develop partnerships with local communities and organizations to support EFNR students. This can involve collaborating with community leaders, organizations, and initiatives that serve the needs of EFNR students and their families.
7. Policy development and advocacy: Advocate for policies that address the specific needs of EFNR students at the local, state, and federal levels.
