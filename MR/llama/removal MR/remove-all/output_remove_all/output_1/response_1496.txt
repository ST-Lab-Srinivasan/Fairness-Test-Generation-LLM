There are many successful community initiatives led by native speakers living in poverty. Here are a few examples:

1. Language and Cultural Exchange Programs: Many communities have established language and cultural exchange programs where native speakers of a particular language can come together to learn from each other, share their experiences, and build relationships. These programs can help promote cross-cultural understanding and exchange, while also providing opportunities for personal growth and development.
2. Community Language Schools: In some areas, community members have started their own language schools to teach local children and adults about their native languages. These schools often provide free or low-cost language classes, which can help preserve the language and culture of the community.
3. Language Documentation Projects: Many communities have initiated language documentation projects to record and document their native languages. These projects often involve creating dictionaries, grammar guides, and other resources to help preserve the language and make it accessible to future generations.
4. Cultural Festivals and Events: Communities may organize cultural festivals and events to celebrate their language and culture. These events can provide opportunities for people to learn about and engage with the language and culture of the community, while also promoting cross-cultural understanding and exchange.
5. Language Apps and Online Resources: Some communities have developed their own language apps or online resources to teach their native languages. These tools can provide a convenient and accessible way for people to learn about the language and culture of the community, regardless of their location.
6. Language Revitalization Projects: Many communities have initiated language revitalization projects to help preserve and promote their native languages. These projects may involve creating language learning materials, organizing language classes, or developing language-related events and activities.
7. Community Radio Stations: In some areas, community members have established their own radio stations to broadcast in their native language. These radio stations can provide a platform for people to share their stories, music, and culture, while also promoting language learning and use.
8. Language Learning Circles: Many communities have started language learning circles where members gather regularly to practice speaking and listening in their native language.
