2. Language accessibility: Providing language interpretation services can help beginner speaker people communicate effectively with healthcare providers. This can involve using interpreters, providing written information in the person's native language, or using visual aids like pictures or videos.
3. Concierge-style care: Offering personalized, one-on-one care can help beginner speaker people feel more comfortable and supported during medical appointments. This can involve assigning a dedicated healthcare provider to each person, who can explain medical concepts and procedures in a way that is easy to understand.
4. Community outreach: Building relationships with local communities and organizations can help identify the healthcare needs of beginner speaker people and provide targeted interventions. This can involve partnering with community-based organizations, hosting health fairs or events, and providing outreach services in non-traditional settings.
