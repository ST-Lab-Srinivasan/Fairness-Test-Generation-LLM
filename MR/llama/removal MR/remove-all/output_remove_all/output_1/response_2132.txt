Ensuring that the voices of marginalized or underrepresented groups, including recent immigrants and refugees, are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for creating a more inclusive and equitable society. Here are some strategies that can help ensure the voices of Mandarin speakers are heard:

1. Language Accessibility: Ensure that information and decision-making processes are accessible to Mandarin speakers by providing translation services, interpreters, or bilingual staff. This can include providing written materials in both English and Mandarin, as well as offering language classes for community members.
2. Community Outreach and Engagement: Reach out to Mandarin-speaking communities through community events, workshops, and meetings. Provide opportunities for community members to share their thoughts and opinions on issues that affect them. This can be done through surveys, focus groups, or other forms of engagement.
3. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for decision-makers and staff to better understand the needs and experiences of Mandarin-speaking communities. This can help build bridges between different cultures and improve decision-making processes.
4. Representation in Decision-Making Bodies: Ensure that Mandarin speakers are represented on decision-making bodies, such as community boards, advisory committees, or advocacy groups. This can provide a platform for their voices to be heard and their perspectives to be taken into account.
5. Community Partnerships: Build partnerships with organizations that serve Mandarin-speaking communities, such as community centers, cultural associations, or religious organizations. These partnerships can help amplify the voices of Mandarin speakers and ensure that their needs are being met.
6. Advocacy and Activism: Advocate for policies and practices that promote the inclusion of Mandarin speakers in decision-making processes. This can involve working with advocacy groups, community leaders, or local government officials to raise awareness about the issue and push for change.
