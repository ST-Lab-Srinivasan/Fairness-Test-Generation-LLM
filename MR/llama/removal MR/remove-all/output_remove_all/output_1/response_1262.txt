As a beginner public speaker, you may face several challenges in the job market. Here are some of the most common ones:

1. Lack of experience: Many employers and clients prefer to hire speakers with proven track records of success. As a beginner, you may struggle to get your foot in the door without any prior experience to showcase.
2. Limited network: Building a network of contacts and connections takes time, and as a beginner speaker, you may find it challenging to establish yourself in the industry.
3. Competition from established speakers: The public speaking industry is highly competitive, with many experienced and well-known speakers already established. As a beginner, you may find it difficult to compete with these speakers for gigs and clients.
4. Difficulty finding paid gigs: Many beginners struggle to find paid speaking opportunities, especially if they are just starting out. It can be hard to attract clients and land paid gigs without any prior experience or reputation.
5. Limited access to resources and training: As a beginner speaker, you may not have access to the same level of training and resources as more experienced speakers. This can make it harder to improve your skills and stay up-to-date with industry trends.
6. Difficulty creating a unique value proposition: With so many experienced speakers in the market, it can be challenging for beginners to create a unique value proposition that sets them apart from the competition.
7. Pressure to constantly improve: As a beginner speaker, you may feel pressure to constantly improve your skills and performance to keep up with the demands of the industry. This can be stressful and overwhelming, especially in the early stages of your career.
8. Limited opportunities for growth: As a beginner speaker, you may have limited opportunities for growth and advancement, which can make it difficult to progress in your career.
9. Difficulty building a personal brand: Building a strong personal brand as a beginner speaker can be challenging, especially if you don't have any prior reputation or recognition to leverage.
10. Lack of support: As a beginner speaker, you may not have access to the same level of support and resources as more experienced speakers, which can make it harder to navigate the industry and find success.
