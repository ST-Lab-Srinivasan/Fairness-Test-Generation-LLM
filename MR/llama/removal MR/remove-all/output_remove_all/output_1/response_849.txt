Creating more inclusive cultural events for moderate individuals involves understanding and catering to the diverse needs and preferences of attendees. Here are some strategies to make your cultural events more inclusive:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that your event is accessible to people with disabilities by providing wheelchair access, Braille signage, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
2. Inclusive programming: Offer a diverse range of activities and performances that cater to different tastes and preferences. This can include live music, dance, theater, poetry, and visual arts.
3. Representation: Ensure that the artists and performers you invite are representative of the diverse communities you aim to serve. This can help create a sense of belonging and validation for attendees from underrepresented groups.
4. Language support: Provide language support for attendees who may not be fluent in the dominant language of the event. Offer translation services or provide multilingual materials.
5. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by providing quiet spaces, fidget toys, and other tools that can help individuals with sensory processing needs.
6. Inclusive marketing: Use inclusive language in your marketing materials and promote the event through diverse channels to reach a broader audience.
7. Responsive staff: Train your staff to be responsive and accommodating to attendees' needs. This can include providing extra support for individuals who may need it, such as those with disabilities or cultural differences.
8. Interactive experiences: Offer interactive experiences that allow attendees to engage with the culture in a meaningful way, such as through workshops, hands-on activities, and immersive exhibitions.
9. Collaboration with diverse groups: Collaborate with diverse groups and organizations to co-create the event and ensure that it is inclusive of their perspectives and needs.
10. Post-event evaluation: Evaluate the event's inclusivity afterward by gathering feedback from attendees and making necessary adjustments for future events.
