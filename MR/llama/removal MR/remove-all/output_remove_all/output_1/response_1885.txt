Promoting the inclusion of Native American recent to the US civilian individuals in casual athletics requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses historical and systemic barriers. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Awareness: Raise awareness about the cultural significance of sports and physical activity among Native American communities. Educate the public about the historical and ongoing impacts of colonization, forced assimilation, and discrimination on Native American health and wellness.
2. Community Engagement: Collaborate with local tribal leaders, organizations, and community members to develop inclusive sports programs that cater to the unique needs and interests of Native American youth and adults. Encourage community participation and leadership in the design and implementation of these programs.
3. Scholarships and Financial Support: Provide financial support for Native American athletes to participate in casual athletics, such as scholarships, grants, or sponsorships. This can help alleviate financial barriers that may prevent individuals from participating in sports.
4. Cultural Sensitivity Training: Offer cultural sensitivity training for coaches, trainers, and other sports professionals to ensure they understand the unique needs and experiences of Native American athletes. Encourage them to incorporate traditional Native American practices and values into their coaching methods.
5. Accessibility and Inclusive Facilities: Ensure that sports facilities are accessible and inclusive for Native American athletes. Provide appropriate equipment, uniforms, and other resources that reflect the cultural identity of Native American communities.
6. Mentorship and Role Models: Connect Native American youth with mentors and role models who can inspire and guide them in their athletic journey. Encourage older or more experienced Native American athletes to share their experiences and provide support to younger generations.
7. Inter-Tribal Competitions: Organize inter-tribal sports competitions that promote cultural exchange and understanding between Native American communities. Encourage the sharing of traditional practices, music, and art during these events.
8. Community Events: Host community events that celebrate Native American culture and promote physical activity. These events could include powwows, running races, or other traditional sports competitions that are meaningful to Native American communities.
