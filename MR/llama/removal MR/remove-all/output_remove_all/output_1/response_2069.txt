Creating more inclusive community events for civilians with upper or lower limb differences requires careful planning and consideration. Here are some suggestions to help make your events more accessible and welcoming:

1. Accessibility features: Ensure that the event venue is wheelchair-accessible, has ramps or elevators, and provides adequate parking and restrooms for people with mobility impairments. Offer assistive listening devices or sign language interpretation for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing.
2. Adaptive equipment: Provide adaptive equipment such as wheelchairs, walkers, or prosthetic limbs for participants who may need them. Consider offering loaner devices for those who cannot bring their own.
3. Inclusive programming: Develop event activities that are inclusive and accessible to people of all abilities. This might include adaptive sports, dance classes, or other recreational activities that can be modified to accommodate different abilities.
4. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by reducing noise levels, using dim lighting, and avoiding strong scents or smells that may trigger anxiety or discomfort for some participants.
5. Communication support: Provide communication support such as American Sign Language (ASL) interpreters, visual aids, or written materials in multiple formats (e.g., braille, large print, electronic text) to ensure that all participants can access information and communicate effectively.
6. Volunteer training: Train volunteers on how to assist participants with disabilities, including how to push wheelchairs, help with transfers, or provide additional support as needed.
7. Collaboration with disability organizations: Partner with local disability organizations to promote your event and ensure that you are reaching out to the most diverse group of people possible. These organizations can also provide valuable advice and resources for making your event more inclusive.
8. Accessibility awareness: Educate participants and volunteers about accessibility and inclusion, and encourage them to be mindful of the needs of individuals with disabilities.
9. Feedback mechanism: Establish a feedback mechanism to gather input from participants on how to improve future events for inclusivity and accessibility.
