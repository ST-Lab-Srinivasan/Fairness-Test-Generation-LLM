Having diverse perspectives in leadership roles can bring numerous benefits to an organization. Here are some of the advantages of having beginner speakers, or those with less experience, in leadership positions:

1. Fresh Ideas and Perspectives: Beginner speakers can bring new and innovative ideas to the table, as they haven't been influenced by traditional ways of thinking or decision-making processes. This can lead to more creative solutions and better problem-solving.
2. Diverse Experiences: People with less experience may have had different life experiences, exposing them to various cultures, industries, or sectors. This diversity can enrich the organization's understanding of different markets, customers, or business practices.
3. Outside-the-Box Thinking: Beginner speakers may challenge the status quo and think outside the box due to their limited knowledge of the industry or company culture. This unconventional thinking can lead to groundbreaking ideas and approaches.
4. Adaptability: Those with less experience are more likely to adapt quickly to new situations, challenges, and technologies. They bring a willingness to learn and grow, which is essential in today's fast-paced business environment.
5. Increased Emotional Intelligence: As people gain more experience, they often develop higher emotional intelligence, enabling them to better understand and manage their own emotions, as well as those of others. This can lead to more effective communication and collaboration within the organization.
6. Broader Network: Beginner speakers may have a smaller professional network compared to more experienced leaders, but they often have connections in diverse areas, such as academia, government, or non-profit organizations. This can expand the organization's reach and create new partnership opportunities.
7. Lower Expectations: Sometimes, beginner speakers are less likely to be burdened by preconceived notions or legacy systems, allowing them to approach problems with a clean slate. This can lead to more innovative solutions and faster implementation times.
8. Potential for Growth: By investing in beginner speakers, organizations can develop their leadership skills and watch them grow into more experienced and effective leaders over time.
