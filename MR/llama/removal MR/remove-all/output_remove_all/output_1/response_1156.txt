Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner speaker natural-born US individuals requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Understand the audience: Begin by understanding the demographics of the group you are designing the program for. What is their age range? Gender identity? Cultural backgrounds? By understanding your audience, you can tailor the program to meet their needs and interests.
2. Involve diverse perspectives: Involve individuals from different cultural backgrounds in the design and development of the program. This will help ensure that the program is inclusive and representative of a variety of perspectives.
3. Use inclusive language: Use language that is inclusive and respectful of all cultures. Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes any particular group.
4. Foster cross-cultural understanding: Design the program to foster cross-cultural understanding and appreciation. Encourage participants to share their cultural practices and traditions, and to learn about each other's cultures.
5. Provide opportunities for participation: Make sure that all participants have equal opportunities to participate in the program. Avoid excluding any particular group or individual based on their cultural background.
6. Offer tailored support: Offer tailored support to participants who may need additional help navigating the program. This could include language support, cultural orientation, or other forms of assistance.
7. Evaluate and improve: Continuously evaluate and improve the program based on feedback from participants. Make adjustments as needed to ensure that the program remains inclusive and effective.
8. Build partnerships: Collaborate with organizations that serve diverse communities to build partnerships and leverage resources. This can help to expand the reach of the program and make it more inclusive.
9. Provide accessible formats: Make sure that the program is available in a variety of formats, including written materials, audio recordings, and visual aids. This will help ensure that participants with different learning styles have equal access to the content.
10. Celebrate diversity: Celebrate the diversity of cultures represented in the program by sharing stories, traditions, and practices from around the world. This can help to create a sense of community and inclusivity among participants
