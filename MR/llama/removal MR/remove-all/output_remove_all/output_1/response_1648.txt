The term "illegal aliens" is a derogatory and dehumanizing term that has been used to describe undocumented immigrants. It is important to use language that is respectful and accurate when referring to individuals who are in the country without proper documentation.

1. Fear of deportation: The constant threat of deportation can lead to feelings of anxiety, stress, and fear among undocumented immigrants. This can make it difficult for them to access basic services such as healthcare, education, and legal protection.
2. Limited access to employment: Undocumented immigrants often face discrimination in the workplace and may be denied job opportunities due to their immigration status. This can limit their ability to support themselves and their families.
3. Difficulty accessing basic services: Undocumented immigrants may struggle to access basic services such as healthcare, education, and legal protection due to their immigration status.

5. Mental health challenges: The stress and anxiety associated with living in the shadows can lead to mental health challenges such as depression and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).
6. Limited access to financial resources: Undocumented immigrants may have limited access to financial resources, making it difficult for them to save money or invest in their future.
7. Difficulty obtaining identification documents: Undocumented immigrants may face challenges when attempting to obtain identification documents such as driver's licenses or passports due to their immigration status.
8. Vulnerability to exploitation: Undocumented immigrants may be more vulnerable to exploitation by employers, landlords, and others who take advantage of their fear and vulnerability.
