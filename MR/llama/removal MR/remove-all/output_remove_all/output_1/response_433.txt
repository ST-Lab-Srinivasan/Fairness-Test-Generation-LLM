Creating more inclusive community events for beginner speakers can be achieved by considering the following strategies:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the event is accessible to all, including those with disabilities, by providing accommodations such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, and braille materials.
2. Diverse Programming: Offer a diverse range of programming that caters to different interests and needs, including workshops, panels, and networking opportunities. This will help attract a broader audience and create a more inclusive environment.
3. Volunteer Opportunities: Provide volunteer opportunities for beginners to get involved in the planning and execution of the event. This will give them hands-on experience and allow them to build their skills and confidence.
4. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair experienced speakers with beginner speakers, providing them with guidance and support throughout the event planning process.
5. Inclusive Marketing: Utilize marketing materials and campaigns that are inclusive and representative of diverse voices and perspectives. This will help attract a more diverse audience and create a more inclusive environment.
6. Safe Spaces: Create safe spaces for beginners to practice their speaking skills without fear of judgment or criticism. This can be achieved by providing a supportive environment, offering constructive feedback, and encouraging open communication.
7. Collaboration with Local Organizations: Partner with local organizations that serve diverse communities to help promote the event and ensure that it is inclusive and representative of their needs.
8. Provide Resources: Offer resources such as speaking tips, practice opportunities, and feedback to help beginners improve their skills and feel more confident in their abilities.
9. Create a Sense of Belonging: Foster a sense of belonging among beginner speakers by recognizing their achievements and providing opportunities for them to connect with other speakers and the community.
10. Continuous Improvement: Continuously gather feedback from beginners and make improvements to ensure that the event is inclusive, supportive, and meets the needs of its attendees.
