This sense of belonging is essential for constructing a strong identity.
2. Identity Formation: Cultural heritage influences how ethnically diverse non-individuals form their identities. It provides them with a set of values, beliefs, and practices that shape their understanding of themselves and their place in the world. These cultural norms and values help individuals navigate their social and cultural environments.
3. Self-Expression: Cultural heritage offers ethnically diverse non-individuals a means of self-expression. Through music, art, dance, and other creative forms, they can express their unique experiences, perspectives, and emotions. This form of self-expression is essential for constructing a personal identity that is authentic and meaningful.
4. Resistance and Challenge: Cultural heritage can also be used as a tool for resistance and challenge. Ethnically diverse non-individuals can use their cultural practices and traditions to challenge dominant narratives and power structures. By embracing their cultural heritage, they can subvert oppressive systems and reclaim their agency and autonomy.
