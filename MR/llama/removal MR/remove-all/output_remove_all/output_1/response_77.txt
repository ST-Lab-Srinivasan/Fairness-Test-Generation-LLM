Creating more inclusive social policies for working-class bereaved individuals requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the unique challenges faced by this group. Here are some recommendations for creating more inclusive policies:

1. Acknowledge the economic impact of bereavement: The financial burden of losing a loved one can be particularly acute for working-class individuals who may already be struggling to make ends meet. Policies should provide support for funeral expenses, lost income, and other financial obligations that arise during the grieving process.
2. Provide access to counseling and mental health services: Working-class individuals may face additional barriers in accessing mental health services due to lack of transportation, childcare responsibilities, or limited access to care. Policies should prioritize remote or flexible counseling options and provide resources for finding affordable mental health services.
3. Address social isolation: Bereaved individuals may feel isolated and disconnected from their communities, particularly if they lack social support networks. Policies should encourage community-based initiatives that bring people together and provide opportunities for social connection and emotional support.
4. Offer flexible work arrangements: Losing a loved one can be a significant life event that may require time off from work. Policies should provide flexible work arrangements, such as telecommuting or compressed workweeks, to allow employees to grieve without jeopardizing their jobs or income.
5. Advocate for better benefits and compensation: Working-class individuals may not have access to the same level of benefits and compensation as their higher-income counterparts. Policies should advocate for more equitable benefits packages, including paid bereavement leave, sick leave, and other forms of support.
6. Promote education and awareness: Many people may not understand the impact of bereavement on working-class individuals or how to provide effective support. Policies should prioritize education and awareness campaigns that promote empathy and understanding among colleagues, employers, and communities.
