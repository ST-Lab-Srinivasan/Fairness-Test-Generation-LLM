Addressing the specific needs of ethnically diverse students, including those who are not recently immigrated, requires a comprehensive and inclusive approach to education. Here are some strategies that educators can use to address the unique needs of these students:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training for teachers and staff on cultural competency and how to work with diverse student populations. This can include understanding the cultural backgrounds and experiences of ethnically diverse students, as well as learning how to navigate differences in communication styles and expectations.
2. Language Support: Offer language support services such as bilingual or multilingual education programs, language assessments, and interpretation and translation services for parents who may not speak the dominant language of the school.
3. Academic Accommodations: Provide academic accommodations such as extra time to complete assignments, note-taking assistance, and audio recordings of lectures for students who may need additional support due to language barriers or other factors.
4. Social Integration: Encourage social integration by creating opportunities for ethnically diverse students to connect with each other and the broader school community. This can be done through extracurricular activities, club formation, and mentorship programs.
5. Community Partnerships: Develop partnerships with community organizations that serve ethnic minority groups to provide additional resources and support for these students. This can include mental health services, tutoring programs, and after-school activities.
6. Inclusive Curriculum: Develop an inclusive curriculum that reflects the diversity of the student population and addresses the unique experiences and perspectives of ethnically diverse students. This can include incorporating diverse literature, history, and cultural practices into the curriculum.
7. Teacher Support: Provide teacher support through professional development opportunities and mentorship programs to help teachers understand and address the specific needs of ethnically diverse students.
8. Family Engagement: Encourage family engagement by providing opportunities for parents to participate in school events, volunteer in the classroom, and serve on school committees. This can help build a sense of community and support for ethnically diverse students.
