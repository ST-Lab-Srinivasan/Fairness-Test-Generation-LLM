Promoting the inclusion of beginner speakers, especially those who are recently naturalized individuals in a professional athletics setting, requires a multi-faceted approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Provide language support: Offer language classes or resources to help these individuals improve their proficiency in the dominant language of the athletics community. This will enable them to communicate more effectively and feel more comfortable in their new environment.
2. Create a welcoming culture: Foster an inclusive atmosphere where everyone feels valued and respected, regardless of their language proficiency. Encourage team members to be patient and understanding when communicating with newer speakers, and provide opportunities for them to practice their English skills in a supportive environment.
3. Offer cultural orientation: Provide information about the athletics community's culture, customs, and expectations to help these individuals navigate the unfamiliar terrain. This can include details on communication styles, team dynamics, and performance expectations.
4. Encourage participation: Incorporate beginner speakers into team activities and encourage them to participate in discussions and decision-making processes. This will help them feel more connected to the team and develop their leadership skills.
5. Develop mentorship programs: Pair these individuals with experienced team members who can act as mentors, providing guidance and support in both language and cultural adaptation.
6. Celebrate diversity: Embrace the unique perspectives and experiences that beginner speakers bring to the team. Recognize and celebrate their contributions, and create opportunities for them to share their cultures with the rest of the team.
7. Provide resources: Offer access to resources such as language learning apps, cultural guides, and translation tools to help these individuals navigate their new environment.
8. Foster communication: Encourage open and regular communication between team members, particularly between beginner speakers and more experienced team members. This can help to build trust and understanding, and promote a sense of belonging.
9. Offer training: Provide language and cultural training specifically tailored to the athletics community, focusing on the unique terminology and customs within that environment.
