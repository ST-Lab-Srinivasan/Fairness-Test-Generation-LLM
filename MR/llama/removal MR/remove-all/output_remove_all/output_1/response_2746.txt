There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of Native natural-born US individuals:

1. Provide access to education and job training programs: Many Native American communities lack access to quality education and job training programs, which can limit their employment opportunities. Governments and organizations can work to provide these resources to help individuals acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market.
2. Encourage entrepreneurship and small business development: Native communities often face barriers to starting and growing their own businesses, such as limited access to capital and lack of infrastructure. Programs that provide financial support, mentorship, and networking opportunities can help individuals start and grow successful businesses.
3. Support sustainable economic development: Economic development projects should be designed to benefit the local community and prioritize their needs. This can involve supporting small-scale enterprises, cooperatives, and community-based initiatives that promote economic self-determination.
4. Address systemic barriers: Many Native communities face systemic barriers to economic success, such as discrimination, inequality, and lack of access to resources. Addressing these issues through policy changes and advocacy can help create a more level playing field for Native individuals to succeed.
5. Foster partnerships and collaborations: Collaboration between government agencies, private sector companies, and Native American communities can lead to innovative solutions that promote economic development and job creation. These partnerships can also help to provide access to new markets, technologies, and resources.
6. Invest in infrastructure: Many Native communities lack basic infrastructure such as roads, bridges, and telecommunications networks. Investing in these critical infrastructure elements can help to facilitate economic development and improve the quality of life for community members.
7. Support cultural preservation and revitalization: Native cultures are a valuable part of the US cultural heritage, and supporting their preservation and revitalization can help to promote economic development and job creation.
