Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of ethnically diverse non-individuals. It is the collective memories, customs, beliefs, and practices that are passed down from one generation to another, and they help shape the sense of self and belonging among individuals. Here are some ways cultural heritage influences the identities of ethnically full non-individuals:

1. Sense of Belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a particular group or community. It helps individuals connect with their ancestors and feel a part of something larger than themselves. This sense of belonging is essential for the development of identity.
2. Identity Formation: Cultural heritage influences how individuals form their identities. It shapes their beliefs, values, and practices, which in turn affect how they perceive themselves and their place in the world. For example, a person's cultural heritage may influence their views on gender roles, family dynamics, or spirituality.
3. Shared Experiences: Cultural heritage creates shared experiences among individuals who belong to the same community. Sharing customs, traditions, and rituals helps build a sense of solidarity and belonging, which is essential for identity formation.
4. Resistance and Empowerment: Cultural heritage can also be used as a tool for resistance and empowerment. By embracing their cultural heritage, individuals can challenge dominant narratives and assert their own identities. For example, marginalized communities may use their cultural heritage to reclaim lost histories and challenge oppressive systems.
5. Intersectionality: Cultural heritage intersects with other aspects of identity, such as race, gender, sexuality, and class. The intersection of these identities influences how individuals experience and express their cultural heritage. For example, a person's cultural heritage may be shaped by their experiences as a queer person of color.
6. Performance and Representation: Cultural heritage is often performed and represented in various ways, such as through art, music, dance, or language. These performances and representations can shape how individuals understand themselves and their place in the world. For example, an individual may use traditional clothing or language to express their cultural identity.
