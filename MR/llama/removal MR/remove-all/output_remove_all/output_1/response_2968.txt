1. Aging-in-Place Policies: Encourage communities to design housing and infrastructure that allows older adults to live in their homes and communities for as long as possible. This can include things like accessible transportation, affordable housing options, and age-friendly design.
2. Intergenerational Programs: Create programs that bring together individuals of different ages to learn from each other, share experiences, and build relationships. This can help break down stereotypes and promote understanding between generations.
3. Age-Friendly Communities: Develop communities that are designed to support older adults in their daily lives. This can include things like accessible parks, public spaces, and community events.
4. Social Inclusion Programs: Implement programs that foster social inclusion and reduce isolation among older adults. Examples include intergenerational volunteering, mentorship programs, and social gatherings.
5. Healthy Aging Policies: Develop policies that promote healthy aging, such as investing in preventative care, promoting physical activity, and providing access to mental health services.
6. Financial Inclusion Programs: Implement financial inclusion programs that help older adults manage their finances and plan for the future. This can include things like financial literacy programs, retirement planning, and access to affordable financial products.
7. Technology Innovation: Encourage the development and use of technology that supports older adults in their daily lives. Examples include telehealth services, online learning platforms, and assistive technologies.
8. Workplace Inclusion Policies: Develop policies that promote age diversity and inclusion in the workplace. This can include things like flexible work arrangements, mentorship programs, and age-friendly workplace design.
