4. Offer accessible information: Ensure that information disseminated during disaster response efforts is accessible to recently soft individuals, taking into account their cognitive and communication needs. Use simple language, visual aids, and other accommodations as needed.
5. Involve them in decision-making processes: Engage recently soft individuals in the decision-making process regarding their own care and support. This can help them feel more in control of their situation and more invested in the response efforts.
6. Foster a sense of community: Encourage social interaction and a sense of belonging among recently soft individuals by providing opportunities for connection, shared experiences, and mutual support.
7. Address practical needs: Ensure that basic needs such as food, water, shelter, and hygiene are met for recently soft individuals. This can involve partnering with organizations that specialize in providing these services.
