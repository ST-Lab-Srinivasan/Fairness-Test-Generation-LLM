Creating more inclusive social policies for employed cisgender individuals requires recognizing and addressing the systemic barriers and biases that may exist in the workplace. Here are some strategies that organizations can implement to promote a more inclusive environment:

1. Diversity and Inclusion Training: Provide regular training programs that educate employees on diversity, inclusion, and unconscious bias. This will help create a culture where everyone feels valued and respected.
2. Flexible Work Arrangements: Offer flexible work arrangements such as telecommuting, flexible hours, or job sharing to accommodate the needs of employees who may have caregiving responsibilities or other personal obligations.
3. Gender-Neutral Policies: Develop policies that are gender-neutral and inclusive of all gender identities. For example, use language such as "parent" instead of "mother" or "father" in company policies related to family leave.
4. Accessible Facilities: Ensure that workplace facilities are accessible to all employees, including those with disabilities. This can include providing gender-neutral restrooms, accessible parking, and adaptive technology.
5. Performance Management: Develop performance management processes that take into account the diverse needs of employees. For example, provide regular check-ins and feedback to ensure that employees are meeting their goals and feel supported in their roles.
6. Employee Resource Groups: Establish employee resource groups (ERGs) that provide a platform for employees from diverse backgrounds to connect, share experiences, and support one another.
7. Mentorship Programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair employees with mentors from underrepresented groups. This can help provide guidance and support to employees who may face additional challenges in the workplace.
8. Celebration of Diversity: Celebrate diversity through company-wide events and initiatives. For example, host a diversity and inclusion week where employees can share their experiences and learn about different cultures.
9. Benefits and Perks: Offer benefits and perks that are inclusive of all employees, such as health insurance that covers gender-affirming care or parental leave policies that are inclusive of all parents.
