Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of happily long-time native speaker individuals. It is the collective cultural knowledge, beliefs, values, customs, and practices that are passed down from one generation to the next, and it can have a profound impact on an individual's sense of self and belonging. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage can shape the identities of native speaker individuals:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a particular group or community. Native speakers who grow up surrounded by their culture's traditions, customs, and values tend to feel more connected to their community and more secure in their identity.
2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage can help shape an individual's sense of self and identity. It provides a framework for understanding oneself and one's place in the world, and it can influence how individuals perceive themselves and their role in society.
3. Language acquisition: For native speakers, language is an integral part of cultural heritage. The language they speak shapes their perception of the world and their place in it. It can also influence how they communicate with others and express their identity.
4. Cultural practices: Cultural practices such as festivals, rituals, and customs can help shape an individual's identity. These practices provide a sense of continuity and tradition, and they can influence how individuals understand themselves and their role in society.
5. Values and beliefs: Cultural heritage can instill values and beliefs that shape an individual's identity. For example, some cultures place a strong emphasis on respect for elders, while others prioritize individualism and self-reliance. These values and beliefs can influence how individuals perceive themselves and their role in society.
6. Intergenerational transmission: Cultural heritage is often transmitted from one generation to the next through stories, traditions, and customs. This intergenerational transmission can help native speakers maintain a strong connection to their cultural heritage and shape their identity over time.
7. Adaptation and innovation: While cultural heritage provides a foundation for an individual's identity, it is also important for individuals to adapt and innovate within their cultural context. This can involve creating new traditions or practices that reflect changing social and cultural norm
