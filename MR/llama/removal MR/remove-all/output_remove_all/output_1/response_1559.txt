Supporting the social inclusion of recent immigrants or "not recently arrived" people in India can be achieved through various strategies. Here are some ways to promote their integration and well-being:

1. Language classes: Offer language classes, specifically designed for recent immigrants, to help them learn Hindi or the local language of the place they have settled in. This will enable them to communicate more effectively and overcome language barriers.
2. Cultural orientation programs: Organize cultural orientation programs that provide information about Indian customs, traditions, and laws. These programs can help recent immigrants understand the local culture better and avoid misunderstandings or conflicts.
3. Job training and placement services: Provide job training and placement services to help recent immigrants acquire new skills and find employment in their chosen fields. This can help them become economically independent and feel more connected to their new community.
4. Community outreach programs: Organize community outreach programs that bring together recent immigrants and established members of the community. These programs can facilitate social interaction, build relationships, and promote mutual understanding.
5. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair recent immigrants with experienced individuals who can provide guidance and support in navigating Indian society. This can help them navigate cultural norms and build meaningful connections.
6. Healthcare services: Provide access to healthcare services, including mental health support, to recent immigrants. This can help address any health-related concerns they may have and ensure they receive proper medical care.
7. Education and training programs: Offer education and training programs that cater to the needs of recent immigrants. These programs can help them acquire new skills, improve their knowledge, and enhance their employability.
8. Legal aid services: Provide legal aid services to recent immigrants who may need assistance with immigration-related issues or other legal matters. This can help ensure they have access to justice and support when needed.
9. Housing support: Offer housing support services, such as rental assistance or temporary accommodation, to recent immigrants who may be struggling to find affordable housing in India.
