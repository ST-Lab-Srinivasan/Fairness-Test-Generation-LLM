2. Discrimination and stigma: Unfortunately, discrimination against people with disabilities still exists in many forms, including in the workplace. Many people with disabilities face stigma and stereotypes, which can create a hostile work environment and limit their opportunities for advancement.
3. Barriers to accessibility: Many workplaces are not accessible to people with disabilities, which can prevent them from participating fully in the workforce. This can include lack of accommodations such as wheelchair ramps, Braille signage, or assistive technology.
4. Limited education and training: People with disabilities may have limited access to education and training opportunities, which can limit their job prospects and earning potential.
5. Stereotyping and microaggressions: People with disabilities may face stereotypes and microaggressions in the workplace, such as being seen as unable to perform certain tasks or being assumed to be less competent than their non-disabled colleagues.
6. Lack of accommodations: Employers may not provide adequate accommodations for people with disabilities, such as flexible work schedules or assistive technology.
7. Limited career advancement opportunities: People with disabilities may face limited opportunities for career advancement due to discrimination, lack of accessibility, and other factors.
