2. Limited access to education and employment: Individuals with disabilities may face barriers to accessing educational resources and job opportunities due to discrimination or inadequate accommodations. This can limit their ability to achieve their goals and reach their full potential.
3. Income inequality: People with disabilities are more likely to experience poverty and income inequality compared to the general population. This can be due to limited access to employment opportunities, lack of support from government programs, or discrimination in the workplace.
4. Health disparities: Individuals with disabilities may face health disparities due to inadequate access to healthcare services, lack of knowledge about their condition, or poor quality of care. This can lead to worse health outcomes and reduced life expectancy.
5. Limited independence: Despite the name "independent living," many individuals with disabilities may still face challenges in living independently due to physical barriers, lack of accessibility, or societal attitudes that perpetuate dependence.
6. Emotional and mental health impacts: Living with a disability can have significant emotional and mental health impacts, including increased stress, anxiety, and depression. This can be exacerbated by societal stigma and discrimination.
7. Lack of representation and voice: Individuals with disabilities are often underrepresented in decision-making processes and may not have a voice in shaping policies that affect their lives. This can result in a lack of understanding and support for their needs.
