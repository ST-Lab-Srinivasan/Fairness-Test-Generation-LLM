Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of long-term recently native speaker individuals. Cultural heritage refers to the shared knowledge, beliefs, values, customs, and practices passed down from one generation to the next within a particular culture or society. For individuals who have acquired a new language and culture through migration or other means, their cultural heritage can serve as a vital connection to their past and identity.

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides individuals with a sense of belonging to a particular group or community. For example, someone who has migrated to a new country may feel more connected to their ethnic community than to the dominant culture. This sense of belonging can help individuals navigate their identity and feel more grounded in their new environment.
2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage plays a crucial role in shaping an individual's identity, particularly during periods of transition or change. For example, someone who has recently migrated to a new country may use their cultural heritage as a way to define themselves and differentiate themselves from the dominant culture.
3. Cultural practices: Cultural heritage includes various practices such as language, religion, customs, and traditions. These practices can shape an individual's identity by providing a sense of continuity and connection to their past. For example, someone who has migrated to a new country may continue to practice their traditional rituals and customs as a way to maintain their cultural identity.
4. Intergenerational transmission: Cultural heritage is often transmitted from one generation to the next, providing individuals with a sense of connection to their ancestors and cultural history. This intergenerational transmission can help individuals navigate their identity and feel more grounded in their cultural practices.

For example, someone who has migrated to a new country may use their cultural heritage as a way to resist dominant cultural norms and values that are in conflict with their own identity.
