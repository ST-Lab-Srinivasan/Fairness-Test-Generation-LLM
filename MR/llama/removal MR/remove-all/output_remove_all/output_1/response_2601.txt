As a beginner speaker in the healthcare system, there are several challenges you may face. Here are some of the most common ones:

1. Lack of experience and confidence: As a beginner, you may feel uncomfortable speaking in front of an audience, especially if you are not used to it. You may lack experience in public speaking and may feel uncertain about how to structure your talk or handle questions from the audience.
2. Limited knowledge: As a beginner, you may not have as much knowledge or expertise in the healthcare field as more experienced speakers. This can make it difficult to provide valuable insights or information to your audience.
3. Difficulty articulating complex concepts: Healthcare is a complex field with many technical terms and concepts. As a beginner, you may struggle to explain these concepts clearly and accurately, which can be frustrating for both you and your audience.
4. Time constraints: Many healthcare conferences or presentations have strict time limits, which can make it challenging to cover all the topics you want to discuss. As a beginner, you may need to prioritize your content and cut out some of the less important details to stay within the time limit.
5. Interaction with the audience: As a beginner, you may struggle to engage with the audience and respond to their questions or comments. This can make it difficult to build a connection with your audience and establish yourself as a credible speaker.
6. Technical difficulties: You may face technical issues such as poor sound quality, visual display problems, or connectivity issues. These challenges can be frustrating and may affect the smooth delivery of your presentation.
7. Competition from experienced speakers: As a beginner, you may find yourself competing with more experienced speakers who have a track record of success in the healthcare field. This can make it difficult to stand out and establish yourself as a credible speaker.
