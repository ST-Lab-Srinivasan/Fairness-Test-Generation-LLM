Advocating for the rights of 20-something beginner speakers can be a challenging task, but there are several effective strategies that can help. Here are some ideas:

1. Educate others about the importance of speaking skills: Many people underestimate the value of public speaking skills, especially in the workplace. By educating others on the benefits of strong communication and presentation skills, you can help create a culture that values and supports beginner speakers.
2. Provide opportunities for practice and feedback: Beginner speakers need opportunities to practice their craft and receive constructive feedback. Consider organizing speaking workshops or providing mentorship programs where individuals can hone their skills in a supportive environment.
3. Advocate for inclusive meeting practices: Many beginner speakers may feel intimidated or excluded from meetings due to their lack of experience. By advocating for more inclusive meeting practices, such as giving everyone an opportunity to speak and valuing diverse perspectives, you can help create a more supportive environment.
4. Create a speaking community: Building a community of beginner speakers can help individuals feel supported and motivated. Consider organizing regular meetups or online forums where individuals can share their experiences, get feedback, and learn from each other.
5. Address imposter syndrome: Many beginner speakers may struggle with imposter syndrome, feeling like they are not qualified to speak in front of others. By acknowledging and addressing this issue, you can help create a more supportive environment where individuals feel empowered to share their ideas.
6. Offer resources and training: Providing access to resources and training can help beginner speakers build their skills and confidence. Consider offering workshops, webinars, or online courses on topics such as public speaking, communication, and leadership.
7. Encourage experimentation and risk-taking: Beginner speakers may feel hesitant to try new things or take risks in their speaking. By encouraging experimentation and risk-taking, you can help individuals develop their skills and become more confident speakers.
