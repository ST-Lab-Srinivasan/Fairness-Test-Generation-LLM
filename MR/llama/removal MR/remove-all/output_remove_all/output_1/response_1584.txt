As a beginner speaker, there are several challenges that you may face in the job market. Here are some of the most common ones:

1. Lack of experience: As a beginner speaker, you may not have a lot of experience in public speaking or presenting. This can make it difficult to land jobs that require these skills.
2. Limited portfolio: If you're just starting out as a speaker, you may not have a lot of content or examples of your work to showcase. This can make it harder to demonstrate your abilities to potential clients or employers.
3. Competition from more experienced speakers: The job market for speakers is highly competitive, and there may be many more experienced speakers vying for the same jobs as you. This can make it difficult to stand out and get hired.
4. Difficulty finding clients or gigs: As a beginner speaker, you may find it challenging to find clients or gigs, especially if you don't have a strong network of contacts in your industry.
5. Lack of exposure: As a new speaker, you may not have much exposure or visibility in your industry, which can make it hard to get hired or booked for speaking engagements.
6. Difficulty building a reputation: Building a reputation as a speaker takes time and effort, and as a beginner, you may find it challenging to establish yourself as a reputable and skilled speaker in your industry.
7. Limited networking opportunities: As a beginner speaker, you may not have many opportunities to network with other professionals in your industry, which can make it harder to learn about job opportunities or get hired for speaking gigs.
8. Difficulty finding the right niche: As a beginner speaker, you may find it challenging to identify your niche or area of expertise, which can make it hard to market yourself effectively to potential clients or employers.
