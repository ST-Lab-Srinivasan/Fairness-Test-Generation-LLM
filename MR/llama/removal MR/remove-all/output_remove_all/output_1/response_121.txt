Immigration status can have significant impacts on the lives of recently arrived migrants, particularly those who have been forced to flee their home countries due to conflict, persecution, or natural disasters. Here are some of the common challenges faced by recently arrived migrants:

1. Uncertainty and insecurity: Many newly arrived migrants face an uncertain future, with concerns about their immigration status, access to basic needs such as food, shelter, and healthcare, and the possibility of being deported or separated from their families.
2. Lack of documentation: Without proper documentation, migrants may struggle to access essential services such as healthcare, education, and employment. This can lead to feelings of marginalization and isolation.
3. Discrimination and stigma: Unfortunately, many newly arrived migrants experience discrimination and stigma due to their immigration status. This can lead to social exclusion and mental health problems.
4. Language barriers: Many recently arrived migrants may struggle with language barriers, making it difficult for them to navigate everyday tasks and services.
5. Limited job opportunities: Migrants may face significant challenges in finding employment due to their immigration status, which can lead to financial instability and difficulties supporting themselves and their families.
6. Separation from family: Many migrants are forced to leave behind family members, including spouses, children, and other loved ones. This can lead to emotional distress and feelings of isolation.
7. Trauma and psychological distress: Recently arrived migrants may experience trauma related to their experiences in their home countries or during their migration journey. They may also struggle with anxiety, depression, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).
8. Lack of access to healthcare: Without proper documentation, newly arrived migrants may struggle to access healthcare services, which can lead to delayed treatment and poorer health outcomes.
