Supporting the educational aspirations of Native Speaker people involves recognizing and addressing the unique challenges they face in their academic journey. Here are some ways to provide adequate support:

1. Cultural Sensitivity Training: Provide training for educators on cultural sensitivity, diversity, and inclusivity. This will help them understand the specific needs of Native Speaker students and create an inclusive learning environment.
2. Language Support: Offer language support services such as English language classes or tutoring to help Native Speaker students improve their English proficiency. This can help bridge the gap between their native language and academic language.
3. Academic Support: Provide academic support services such as supplementary instruction, study skills workshops, and peer tutoring. These resources can help Native Speaker students stay on track academically and overcome any challenges they may face.
4. Counseling Services: Offer counseling services to help Native Speaker students navigate the academic and social challenges they may encounter. This can include individual counseling sessions, group therapy, and referral services for more extensive support.
5. Community Outreach: Develop partnerships with local Native American communities and organizations to provide educational resources and opportunities outside of the classroom. This can include cultural events, workshops, and mentorship programs.
6. Collaborative Learning: Encourage collaborative learning experiences between Native Speaker students and their peers. This can help foster a sense of community and mutual understanding, as well as provide opportunities for cross-cultural exchange and learning.
7. Indigenous Curriculum: Incorporate Indigenous knowledge and perspectives into the curriculum to provide a more comprehensive and inclusive education. This can include teaching Indigenous languages, histories, and cultural practices.
8. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair Native Speaker students with successful Indigenous role models. These mentors can provide guidance, support, and inspiration to help Native Speaker students achieve their academic goals.
9. Career Development: Offer career development opportunities and resources specifically tailored to the needs of Native Speaker students. This can include job shadowing, internships, and career counseling services.
