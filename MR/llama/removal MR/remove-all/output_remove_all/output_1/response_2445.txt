1. Limited access to health insurance: Documented non-citizens may not have access to affordable health insurance, especially if they do not qualify for Medicaid or the Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP). They may also face barriers in accessing employer-based insurance due to their immigration status.
2. Language barriers: Many documented non-citizens may face language barriers when communicating with healthcare providers, which can lead to misunderstandings or missed diagnoses.
3. Cultural and social barriers: Documented non-citizens may encounter cultural and social barriers in the healthcare system, such as discrimination or stigma related to their immigration status. This can make it difficult for them to access care or feel comfortable seeking medical attention.
4. Difficulty navigating complex healthcare systems: The US healthcare system can be complex and confusing, especially for those who are not familiar with the language or cultural norms. Documented non-citizens may struggle to navigate the system and access the care they need.
5. Limited access to specialized care: Documented non-citizens may have limited access to specialized care, such as oncology or cardiology, due to the cost of these services or their availability in their area.
6. Difficulty affording copays and deductibles: Even if documented non-citizens have health insurance, they may struggle to afford copays and deductibles, which can lead to delayed or foregone care.
