Supporting the social inclusion of deep poverty ultra-poor individuals can be a complex and multifaceted process. Here are some strategies that have been shown to be effective:

1. Financial Inclusion: Providing access to financial services such as savings accounts, loans, and insurance can help ultra-poor individuals manage their finances and build assets. This can also help them participate in the formal economy and access opportunities for economic mobility.
2. Vocational Training: Offering vocational training programs that teach skills such as tailoring, carpentry, or agriculture can help ultra-poor individuals acquire marketable skills and increase their earning potential.
3. Microfinance: Microfinance programs that provide small loans and other financial services to marginalized communities can help ultra-poor individuals start or expand their businesses, improve their living conditions, and build assets.
4. Community-Based Initiatives: Community-based initiatives such as savings groups, credit unions, and microfinance programs can provide a platform for ultra-poor individuals to pool their resources, share knowledge and skills, and access financial services collectively.
5. Health and Nutrition Programs: Providing health and nutrition programs can help improve the overall well-being of ultra-poor individuals and their families, which can have a positive impact on their social inclusion.
6. Education and Literacy Programs: Educating and empowering ultra-poor individuals through literacy programs can help them access opportunities for economic mobility and improve their social status.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocating for policy changes that address the root causes of poverty and promote inclusive economic growth can help create an enabling environment for ultra-poor individuals to access opportunities for social inclusion.
8. Social Cohesion Programs: Building social cohesion programs that bring together marginalized communities can help promote social inclusion and reduce discrimination against ultra-poor individuals.
9. Livelihoods Development: Developing livelihoods programs that provide training and support for ultra-poor individuals to start or expand their businesses can help them increase their income and improve their social status.
