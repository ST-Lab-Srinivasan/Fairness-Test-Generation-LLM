Meeting the healthcare needs of beginner speaker people requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique challenges and circumstances. Here are some strategies to ensure their healthcare needs are met:

1. Inclusive Healthcare Services: Provide accessible and inclusive healthcare services that cater to the needs of beginner speaker people. This includes training for healthcare providers on how to communicate with and provide care to individuals who use Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) devices or have limited verbal communication abilities.
2. AAC Devices and Technology: Make available a variety of AAC devices and technology that are user-friendly, affordable, and tailored to the individual's needs. This can include picture communication symbols, voice-output devices, and other assistive technologies.
3. Healthcare Providers Trained in AAC: Ensure that healthcare providers are trained in working with individuals who use AAC devices or have limited verbal communication abilities. This includes understanding the different types of AAC devices, how to use them, and how to communicate effectively with individuals who rely on them.
4. Collaboration with AAC Communities: Build partnerships with communities that use AAC devices or have limited verbal communication abilities. This can include working with organizations that provide support and resources for these individuals, such as advocacy groups, support clubs, and user-led organizations.
5. Public Education Campaigns: Launch public education campaigns to raise awareness about the healthcare needs of beginner speaker people. This can include information on how to communicate effectively with individuals who use AAC devices or have limited verbal communication abilities, as well as resources for finding support and services.
6. Inclusive Healthcare Settings: Ensure that healthcare settings are inclusive and accessible for beginner speaker people. This includes designing waiting areas and examination rooms to be wheelchair-friendly, providing adequate lighting and acoustics for individuals who use AAC devices, and training staff on how to provide care in an inclusive manner.
7. Home Visits: Offer home visits for beginner speaker people who may have difficulty accessing healthcare facilities. This can include providing mobile clinics or working with community health workers to deliver healthcare services directly to individuals in their homes.
