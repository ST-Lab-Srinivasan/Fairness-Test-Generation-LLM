Creating more inclusive social policies for long-term unemployed individuals who have experienced multiple periods of unemployment requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the root causes of joblessness and supports those who are struggling to find stable employment. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive social policies:

1. Address systemic barriers: Identify and address the underlying systems, structures, and biases that prevent long-term unemployed individuals from finding decent work. This may involve updating job training programs to reflect changing labor market demands, providing accommodations for individuals with disabilities, or challenging discriminatory hiring practices.
2. Provide holistic support: Offer a range of services beyond job training, such as mental health counseling, financial management assistance, and childcare support. These services can help individuals address the emotional and practical challenges associated with long-term unemployment.
3. Foster collaboration between stakeholders: Bring together government agencies, employers, community organizations, and other stakeholders to develop a coordinated response to long-term unemployment. This can help ensure that individuals receive seamless support throughout their job search journey.
4. Encourage lifelong learning: Develop programs that promote lifelong learning and skill development, enabling individuals to adapt to changing labor market demands. This may involve providing access to continuing education and training courses, as well as financial incentives for workers who pursue ongoing professional development.
5. Invest in infrastructure and technology: Invest in infrastructure and technology that can support the creation of jobs and help individuals access new employment opportunities. This may involve investing in transportation systems, telecommunications networks, or innovative technologies that can enhance productivity and job creation.
6. Promote entrepreneurship and self-employment: Encourage long-term unemployed individuals to explore entrepreneurial opportunities by providing training, mentorship, and financial support. This can help them develop the skills and resources needed to start their own businesses or become self-employed.
