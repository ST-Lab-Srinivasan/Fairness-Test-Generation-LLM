Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of long-time native speaker individuals. It is the collective cultural knowledge, practices, and traditions that are passed down from one generation to the next, and it can greatly influence an individual's sense of self and belonging. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage can shape the identities of long-time native speaker individuals:

1. Sense of Belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a community or group, which is essential for an individual's identity. It gives them a sense of roots and a connection to their past, present, and future.
2. Shared Values and Norms: Cultural heritage encompasses shared values, beliefs, and norms that are passed down from one generation to the next. These shared values and norms can shape an individual's identity by providing a framework for understanding what is right and wrong, good and bad, and what is expected of them within their community.
3. Language: The language spoken in a particular culture or community is an essential aspect of cultural heritage. It can shape an individual's identity by providing a means of communication that is unique to their culture and community. Long-time native speaker individuals may feel a strong connection to their language and the cultural practices associated with it, which can influence their sense of self and belonging.
4. Traditions and Customs: Cultural heritage includes a wide range of traditions and customs that are passed down from one generation to the next. These traditions and customs can shape an individual's identity by providing a sense of continuity and connection to their past and present. For example, religious festivals and celebrations, cultural dress, and traditional foods can all play a role in shaping an individual's identity.
5. Historical Events: The historical events that have shaped a particular culture or community can also influence an individual's identity.
