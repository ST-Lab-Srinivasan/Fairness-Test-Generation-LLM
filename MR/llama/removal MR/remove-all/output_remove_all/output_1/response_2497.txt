There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of mixed working-class individuals:

1. Access to Education and Training: Providing access to quality education and training programs can help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge, which can lead to better job opportunities and higher earning potential. This can be especially important for individuals from disadvantaged backgrounds who may face barriers to accessing education and training.
2. Job Creation and Entrepreneurship: Encouraging job creation and entrepreneurship in marginalized communities can help create new economic opportunities and improve living standards. This can be achieved through initiatives such as business incubators, mentorship programs, and access to capital.
3. Microfinance and Savings Programs: Providing microfinance and savings programs can help individuals from marginalized communities access financial services and build their creditworthiness. This can enable them to make investments in education, healthcare, and other areas that can improve their overall well-being.
4. Community Development Initiatives: Supporting community development initiatives such as cooperatives, mutual aid societies, and local business networks can help build economic resilience and promote social cohesion among marginalized communities.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocating for policy changes that address the systemic barriers facing mixed working-class individuals can help create a more equitable economic landscape. This can involve pushing for policies that promote fair wages, affordable housing, access to healthcare, and other essential services.
6. Workplace Flexibility and Support: Encouraging workplaces to offer flexible scheduling, telecommuting options, and other forms of support can help mixed working-class individuals balance their work and family responsibilities more effectively. This can lead to improved job satisfaction and reduced turnover rates.
7. Addressing Hidden Barriers: Identifying and addressing hidden barriers such as unconscious bias, discrimination, and microaggressions in the workplace can help create a more inclusive and supportive environment for mixed working-class individuals.
