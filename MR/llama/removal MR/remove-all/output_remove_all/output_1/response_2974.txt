Creating more inclusive social policies for long-term happiness requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the various factors influencing an individual's well-being. Here are some strategies that can help create a more inclusive society for long-term happiness:

1. Address income inequality: One of the primary drivers of unhappiness is financial insecurity. Policies that promote economic mobility and reduce income inequality, such as progressive taxation, affordable healthcare, and access to education and job training, can help create a more inclusive society.
2. Foster a sense of community: Social connections are essential for happiness. Communities that prioritize social cohesion and provide opportunities for people to come together can foster a sense of belonging and well-being. This can be achieved through initiatives such as neighborhood potlucks, volunteering programs, or community events.
3. Promote mental health support: Mental health issues are prevalent in many societies and can significantly impact an individual's quality of life. Policies that prioritize mental health support, such as access to therapy, mental health education, and workplace mental health programs, can help create a more inclusive society.
4. Encourage work-life balance: Long working hours and excessive stress can lead to burnout and unhappiness. Policies that promote work-life balance, such as flexible work arrangements, paid parental leave, and mandatory vacation time, can help create a more inclusive society by prioritizing employees' well-being.
5. Address discrimination and prejudice: Discrimination and prejudice can significantly impact an individual's happiness. Policies that promote diversity, equity, and inclusion, such as anti-discrimination laws, cultural sensitivity training, and support for marginalized communities, can help create a more inclusive society.
6. Invest in education: Education is a critical factor in an individual's happiness, as it provides opportunities for personal growth and development. Policies that prioritize access to quality education, including early childhood education, can help create a more inclusive society by providing equal opportunities for all individuals.
