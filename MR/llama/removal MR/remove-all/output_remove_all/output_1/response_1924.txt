Disability can have a significant impact on the lives of older adults, affecting their physical, emotional, and social well-being. Here are some common impacts of disability status on the lives of long-term survivors:

1. Reduced independence: Disability can limit an individual's ability to perform daily tasks and maintain their independence. They may require assistance with activities such as bathing, dressing, and using the bathroom.
2. Social isolation: Disability can lead to social isolation, as individuals may avoid social interactions due to fear of rejection or stigma. This can result in feelings of loneliness and depression.
3. Limited access to healthcare: Older adults with disabilities may face barriers when accessing healthcare services due to physical limitations, transportation issues, or discrimination.
4. Financial strain: Disability can result in increased financial burdens, such as higher healthcare costs, assistive technology expenses, and reduced income due to unemployment or underemployment.
5. Emotional distress: Living with a disability can lead to emotional distress, including anxiety, depression, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).
6. Changes in relationships: Disability can affect an individual's relationships with family members, friends, and romantic partners. They may experience changes in their social support network or feel a sense of loss due to reduced mobility.
7. Stigma and discrimination: Older adults with disabilities may encounter stigma and discrimination from healthcare providers, caregivers, and society at large. This can lead to feelings of shame and low self-esteem.
8. Limited access to leisure activities: Disability can limit an individual's ability to participate in recreational activities, such as travel, sports, or hobbies, which can result in a reduced quality of life.
9. Impact on cognitive functioning: Certain disabilities, such as dementia or stroke, can affect cognitive functioning and lead to memory loss, confusion, and difficulty with problem-solving.
