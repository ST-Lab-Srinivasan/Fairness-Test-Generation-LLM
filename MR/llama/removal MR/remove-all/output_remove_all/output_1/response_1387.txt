As a beginner public speaker, there are several challenges you may face in the job market. Here are some common ones:

1. Lack of experience: Many employers and clients prefer to hire speakers who have a proven track record of success. As a beginner, you may struggle to gain recognition and build your reputation.
2. Limited network: Networking is crucial in the public speaking industry, and having a limited network can make it harder to get opportunities. You may need to put in more effort to connect with other speakers, event organizers, and potential clients.
3. Difficulty finding paid gigs: As a beginner, you may have difficulty finding paid speaking gigs, especially if you don't have a strong network or reputation. Many events and conferences prefer to hire established speakers who can bring a proven track record of success.
4. Competition from experienced speakers: The public speaking industry is highly competitive, and many experienced speakers have a strong reputation and client base. As a beginner, you may struggle to compete with these speakers for gigs and opportunities.
5. Difficulty creating a niche: It can be challenging to create a niche or specialize in a particular area of public speaking, especially as a beginner. You may need to put in more effort to develop your skills and establish yourself as an expert in your chosen area.
6. Limited access to resources: As a beginner, you may have limited access to resources such as training programs, mentorship opportunities, and industry events. These resources can be helpful in developing your skills and building your reputation.
7. Difficulty building confidence: Public speaking can be intimidating, especially for beginners. You may need to work on building your confidence and overcoming stage fright to become a successful public speaker.
8. Limited opportunities for feedback: As a beginner, you may have limited opportunities to get feedback on your speaking skills, which can make it harder to improve and develop your abilities.
9. Difficulty balancing work and personal life: Public speaking can be demanding, especially if you're just starting out. You may need to balance your work and personal life, which can be challenging.
