Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of native English speakers as it provides a sense of identity, belonging, and connection to their community. Here are some ways cultural heritage impacts the lives of independent individuals:

1. Sense of identity: Cultural heritage helps individuals develop a sense of identity and belonging. It shapes their beliefs, values, and behaviors, influencing how they perceive themselves in relation to others. Native English speakers often find comfort in sharing cultural practices and traditions with their peers, fostering a sense of community and connection.
2. Preservation of history: Cultural heritage serves as a link to the past, preserving historical events, customs, and beliefs. It allows native English speakers to learn about their ancestors' experiences, traditions, and values, providing a deeper understanding of their cultural background. By preserving these aspects of their heritage, individuals can feel a sense of pride in their cultural legacy.
3. Cultural expression: Cultural heritage provides a means for native English speakers to express themselves creatively. Through music, art, literature, and other forms of cultural expression, individuals can connect with their heritage and share it with others. This creative outlet helps foster a sense of community and connection among people from different cultural backgrounds.
4. Understanding of diversity: Cultural heritage promotes an understanding of diversity by highlighting the unique customs, beliefs, and practices of different cultures. By embracing this diversity, native English speakers can develop empathy and respect for other cultures, fostering a more inclusive and tolerant society.
5. Connection to their roots: Cultural heritage serves as a connection to one's ancestors and the land where they grew up. It provides a sense of grounding and stability in an ever-changing world, allowing individuals to feel rooted in their cultural traditions and practices. This can be especially important for those who have migrated or been displaced from their homeland.
6. Shared values: Cultural heritage often encompasses shared values, beliefs, and practices that are essential to the identity of a community. By participating in these cultural activities, native English speakers can develop a sense of shared purpose and belongingness with their peers.
