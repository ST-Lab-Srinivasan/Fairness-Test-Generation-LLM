Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping the Identities of Recently Divorced Multiple Times Beginner Arabic Speaker Individuals

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, especially those who have gone through multiple life events such as divorce. In the case of recently divorced multiple times beginner Arabic speaker individuals, cultural heritage can serve as a source of comfort, strength, and identity. This research aims to explore the role of cultural heritage in shaping the identities of this particular group of people.

Literature Review:
Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, and practices of a particular group or society. It is composed of intangible elements such as language, religion, traditions, and history, as well as tangible elements such as artifacts, monuments, and cultural landscapes (Biddle & Rice, 2015). Cultural heritage can shape the identities of individuals in various ways, including providing a sense of belonging, offering moral guidance, and serving as a source of pride and identity (Kirshenblatt-Gimblett, 1987).

Recently divorced individuals often experience significant life changes, including emotional distress, financial strain, and social isolation (Wall & Howard, 2016). Beginner Arabic speakers may face additional challenges related to language barriers and cultural differences. In these circumstances, cultural heritage can play a crucial role in shaping the identities of these individuals.

Research Questions:

1. How do recently divorced multiple times beginner Arabic speaker individuals perceive their cultural heritage?
2. What role does cultural heritage play in shaping the identities of these individuals?
3. How does cultural heritage influence the coping mechanisms and emotional well-being of these individuals?

Methodology:
