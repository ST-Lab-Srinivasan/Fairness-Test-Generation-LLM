As a recently retired combat veteran who is ethnically full Hispanic, there are several support systems that can be important to help you navigate the challenges of civilian life. Here are some of them:

1. Veterans Service Organizations (VSOs): Organizations such as the Veterans of Foreign Wars (VFW), Disabled American Veterans (DAV), and Vietnam Veterans of America (VVA) provide a range of services, including mental health support, employment assistance, and advocacy for veterans' rights. These organizations often have local chapters that can provide more personalized support.
2. Mental Health Professionals: As a combat veteran, you may be at risk for mental health issues such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), depression, and anxiety. Seeking the help of licensed mental health professionals can provide you with the tools and support to manage these challenges. Many VA hospitals and clinics offer free or low-cost mental health services to veterans.
3. Peer Support Groups: Connecting with other veterans who have experienced similar challenges can be incredibly helpful in providing a sense of community and understanding. Many organizations, such as the Wounded Warrior Project (WWP) and the Iraq and Afghanistan Veterans of America (IAVA), offer peer support groups for veterans.
4. Family Support: As a combat veteran, you may be facing challenges that affect not only you but also your loved ones. Building a strong support system within your family can help you navigate these challenges. Consider reaching out to organizations such as the USO or the Military Family Support Group for additional resources and support.
5. Employment Assistance: Transitioning back into civilian life can be challenging, especially when it comes to finding employment. Many organizations, including the VA and the US Department of Labor, offer job placement services and career counseling specifically tailored for veterans.
