As a result, native English speakers in their mid-50s may encounter ageist attitudes from healthcare providers, which can lead to misunderstandings or missed diagnoses.
2. Lack of cultural competence: Healthcare providers may not be familiar with the cultural nuances and values of older adults, leading to miscommunication or mishandling of medical situations. For example, some older adults may prefer to maintain their independence, while others may prioritize comfort and quality of life.

Limited access to healthcare services: Older adults may face barriers in accessing healthcare due to mobility issues, transportation challenges, or lack of availability of healthcare providers in their area. This can result in delayed or missed medical care, which can negatively impact health outcomes.
