Lower-income individuals face numerous challenges in the job market, including:

1. Limited access to education and training: Many lower-income individuals lack the resources to pursue higher education or receive formal training, which can limit their career advancement opportunities.
2. Limited job opportunities: Lower-income areas often have limited job opportunities, particularly in industries with good pay and benefits. This can lead to a cycle of poverty and unemployment.
3. Discrimination: Discrimination based on race, gender, age, or other factors can limit the job opportunities available to lower-income individuals.
4. Limited networking opportunities: Lower-income individuals may have limited opportunities to network and make connections that can lead to job opportunities.
5. Limited access to technology: Many lower-income individuals lack access to technology, such as computers or smartphones, which can limit their ability to apply for jobs or access job training resources.
6. Lack of childcare: Lower-income individuals may have limited access to affordable childcare, making it difficult to balance work and family responsibilities.
7. Limited mobility: Lower-income individuals may have limited options for relocating for job opportunities due to financial constraints or lack of support systems.
8. Skills mismatch: Lower-income individuals may have skills that are not in demand in the job market, making it difficult to find employment.
9. Lack of job security: Lower-income individuals may have limited job security, with limited options for advancement or protection from layoffs.
10. Limited access to benefits: Lower-income individuals may have limited access to benefits such as health insurance, paid time off, or retirement plans.
11. Lack of work-life balance: Lower-income individuals may have difficulty balancing work and family responsibilities due to lack of support systems.
12. Limited social safety net: Lower-income individuals may have limited access to social safety nets such as unemployment insurance or welfare programs, which can leave them vulnerable in times of economic downturn.
13. Lack of representation: Lower-income individuals may have limited access to legal and political representation, which can limit their ability to advocate for their rights in
