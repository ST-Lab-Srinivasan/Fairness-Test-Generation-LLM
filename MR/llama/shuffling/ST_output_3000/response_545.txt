Addressing the Needs of Non-Conservative Individuals in Disaster Response: A Guide for NGOs and Governments

Introduction:
Disaster response is an area where traditional gender roles and expectations are often challenged. In many cases, non-conservative individuals, including those who identify as LGBTQ+, genderqueer, or non-binary, may face additional barriers in accessing disaster relief services. This guide outlines the specific needs of these individuals and provides recommendations for NGOs and governments to address them in their disaster response efforts.

I. Understanding the Needs of Non-Conservative Individuals in Disaster Response
A. Identifying and Addressing Discrimination:
1. Recognize that discrimination based on gender identity, expression, or sex characteristics is a significant issue in disaster response.
2. Develop training programs for responders to recognize and address these forms of discrimination.
B. Access to Safe Accommodation:
1. Ensure that safe accommodations are available for all individuals affected by the disaster, regardless of gender identity or expression.
2. Provide private, secure spaces for individuals who may feel vulnerable in shared accommodations.
C. Healthcare and Medical Services:
1. Train medical professionals to recognize and address the specific health needs of non-conservative individuals.
2. Ensure that medical services are inclusive and respectful of all gender identities.
D. Psychological Support:
1. Provide mental health support services that are tailored to the unique needs of non-conservative individuals.
2. Train mental health professionals to recognize and address the specific psychological needs of these individuals.

2. Provide information in a variety of formats, including visual and audio aids, to ensure accessibility for individuals with diverse learning styles.
