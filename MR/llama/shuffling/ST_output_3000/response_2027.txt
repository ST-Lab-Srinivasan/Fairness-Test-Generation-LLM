Creating more inclusive cultural programs for native English speaker ethnically full African Americans requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some strategies that can help make these programs more inclusive:

1. Involve the Community: Engage with the local African American community by hosting town hall meetings, focus groups, or surveys to gather input on what they would like to see in cultural programs. This will help ensure that the programs are culturally relevant and appealing to the target audience.
2. Diversify Staff and Artists: Ensure that the staff and artists involved in the cultural programs are diverse and representative of the community you are serving. This can include hiring African American staff, artists, and performers to create a more inclusive environment.
3. Offer Cultural Workshops: Provide workshops or classes on African American culture, history, and traditions. These workshops can cover topics such as African music, dance, art, literature, and more. This will help educate the community on the rich cultural heritage of African Americans.
4. Create Interactive Exhibits: Design interactive exhibits that allow visitors to engage with African American culture in a hands-on manner. For example, you could create an exhibit on African American history, with artifacts and interactive displays that allow visitors to learn about the experiences of African Americans throughout history.
5. Host Cultural Events: Organize cultural events such as festivals, concerts, or lectures that showcase African American culture. These events can include performances by African American artists, musical groups, or speakers who are experts on African American history and culture.
6. Collaborate with Local Organizations: Partner with local organizations that serve the African American community to co-host cultural programs. This will help leverage resources and expertise, and ensure that the programs are well-received by the community.
7. Provide Cultural Resources: Offer cultural resources such as books, films, or other media that showcase African American culture. This can include works by African American authors, filmmakers, or artists that highlight the experiences of African Americans.
