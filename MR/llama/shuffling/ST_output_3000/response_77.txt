Creating more inclusive social policies for working-class bereaved individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges and needs of this population. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive policies:

1. Listen to the voices of working-class bereaved individuals: It is essential to listen to the experiences and perspectives of working-class bereaved individuals to understand their needs and challenges. This involves conducting research, surveys, or focus groups to gather data and insights from this group.
2. Address systemic barriers: Working-class bereaved individuals often face systemic barriers such as lack of access to resources, social isolation, and discrimination. Policies should aim to address these barriers by providing equal access to healthcare, education, and financial support.
3. Provide flexible and affordable support services: Working-class bereaved individuals may struggle to access support services due to cost or lack of availability. Policies should prioritize the development of flexible and affordable support services that can be tailored to meet their needs.
4. Promote social inclusion: Social inclusion is crucial for working-class bereaved individuals who may feel marginalized or excluded from society. Policies should aim to promote social inclusion by providing opportunities for social interaction, cultural expression, and community engagement.
5. Address the intersectionality of grief: Grief can be complex and multifaceted, especially for working-class bereaved individuals who may face multiple forms of oppression (e.g., racism, sexism, classism). Policies should recognize the intersectionality of grief and provide support services that address the unique challenges faced by this population.
6. Foster a culture of empathy and compassion: Creating a culture of empathy and compassion is essential for supporting working-class bereaved individuals. This involves promoting emotional expression, validating their experiences, and recognizing the value of their contributions to society.
7. Provide financial support: Financial difficulties can be a significant challenge for working-class bereaved individuals. Policies should prioritize the provision of financial support, such as funeral expense assistance, lost wage compensation, or emergency financial assistance.
