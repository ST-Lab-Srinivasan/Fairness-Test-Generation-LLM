Supporting newly immigrant or refugee families from any racial or ethnic background can be challenging, but there are several effective strategies that have been shown to support the well-being and integration of these families in recent Hispanic communities. Here are some of them:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training for staff and volunteers on cultural competency and diversity issues to help them understand and address the unique needs of each family. This can include learning about different cultural practices, values, and beliefs.
2. Language Support: Offer language classes or interpretation services to help families communicate with service providers and access resources. This can also involve providing bilingual staff or interpreters in healthcare settings.
3. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and engagement activities to connect with immigrant and refugee families and provide information about available resources. This can involve partnering with local organizations, hosting events, and using social media to reach these communities.
4. Legal Services: Provide legal services or referrals to help families navigate the immigration process and access benefits such as citizenship, work permits, and healthcare.
5. Education and Employment Support: Offer education and employment support services, such as job training programs, career counseling, and connections to job opportunities. This can also involve helping families access educational resources for their children.
6. Housing Support: Provide information about affordable housing options and offer financial assistance or referrals to help families access stable and secure housing.
7. Health and Mental Health Services: Offer culturally competent healthcare services, including prenatal care, pediatric services, and mental health counseling. This can also involve providing information about available community resources for these services.
8. Parenting Support: Provide parenting classes or workshops to help families develop positive parenting skills and strategies. This can also involve offering childcare services while parents attend these programs.
