Non-upper-class immigrant recent immigrants to the US face a variety of challenges when accessing healthcare in the US. Some of the key challenges include:

1. Lack of health insurance: Many non-upper-class immigrants may not have access to affordable health insurance, making it difficult for them to receive necessary medical care.
2. Language barriers: Immigrants who do not speak English fluently may face difficulties communicating with healthcare providers, which can lead to misunderstandings or misdiagnoses.
3. Cultural differences: Immigrants may have different cultural beliefs and practices surrounding healthcare, which can lead to conflicts with healthcare providers who may be unfamiliar with these cultural nuances.
4. Limited access to specialized care: Non-upper-class immigrants may face barriers when seeking specialized medical care, such as cancer treatment or organ transplants, due to limited access or lack of knowledge about available resources.
5. Discrimination: Immigrants may experience discrimination in the healthcare system, including bias from healthcare providers or difficulties accessing care due to their immigration status.
6. Limited understanding of US healthcare system: Many immigrants may be unfamiliar with the US healthcare system and its complexities, leading to confusion when trying to navigate it.
7. Difficulty finding culturally competent providers: Immigrants may have difficulty finding healthcare providers who are familiar with their cultural beliefs and practices, leading to misunderstandings or misdiagnoses.
8. Cost of care: Non-upper-class immigrants may face financial barriers when seeking medical care, including high deductibles, co-pays, and other out-of-pocket costs.
9. Lack of transportation: Immigrants may have limited access to transportation, making it difficult for them to reach healthcare facilities or appointments.
10. Limited awareness of available resources: Many immigrants may be unaware of the resources available to them, such as community clinics or sliding-scale fee clinics, which can limit their access to care.
