2. Voter Registration: Collaborate with local election officials and community organizations to register undocumented African Americans to vote. Offer language-appropriate voter registration materials and provide transportation to polling stations, if needed.
3. Civic Education: Organize workshops and training sessions on civic engagement, voting, and advocacy. Focus on topics relevant to their experiences, such as immigration policy, civil rights, and social justice.
4. Leadership Development: Identify and support emerging leaders within the community. Provide training, mentorship, and networking opportunities to help them build skills and confidence in advocating for their rights and interests.
5. Community Building: Facilitate events and activities that promote community building and social cohesion among undocumented African Americans. This can include cultural festivals, language classes, and support groups.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Work with local, state, or federal policymakers to advocate for policies that address the unique needs and challenges of long-term undocumented immigrants. Encourage changes in laws and regulations that impact their lives, such as access to healthcare, education, and employment opportunities.
