Divorce can be a challenging experience for anyone, but it can be particularly difficult for Nigerian individuals who have been divorced multiple times. Social exclusion and stigma can be significant barriers to their social reintegration, making it crucial to develop strategies that support their inclusion. Here are some ways to support the social inclusion of Nigerian recently divorced people:

1. Education and awareness: Organize workshops, seminars, and campaigns to educate the community about the rights and challenges faced by divorcees. Raise awareness about the importance of inclusivity and the need to support these individuals in their social reintegration.
2. Community engagement: Encourage community leaders, religious leaders, and other influential figures to speak out against discrimination and stigmatization of divorcees. Facilitate dialogue and discussions among community members to promote understanding and empathy towards these individuals.
3. Economic empowerment: Provide training and support for divorcees to start or grow their businesses. This can help them become financially independent and reduce their vulnerability to social exclusion.
4. Legal support: Advocate for policy reforms that protect the rights of divorcees, particularly women, who are often disproportionately affected by divorce. Provide legal aid and representation to ensure they have access to fair and just legal proceedings.
5. Mental health support: Offer counseling services and psychological support to help divorcees cope with the emotional challenges of their situation. Encourage them to seek professional help when needed, and provide a safe and non-judgmental space for them to express themselves.
6. Social connections: Facilitate social events and activities that bring together divorcees from different backgrounds. This can help build a sense of community and belonging, reducing feelings of isolation and loneliness.
7. Advocacy and representation: Act as an advocate for the rights of divorcees within the community and society at large. Provide a voice for those who may feel marginalized or silenced, and work towards creating a more inclusive and supportive environment.
