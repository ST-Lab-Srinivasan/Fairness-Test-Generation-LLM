Ensuring that the healthcare needs of lower-class native Arabic speakers are met requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the social, economic, and cultural barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural sensitivity training for healthcare providers: Provide training on cultural competency and linguistic diversity to ensure that healthcare providers are aware of the unique needs and experiences of lower-class native Arabic speakers. This can help reduce language barriers and improve communication between patients and healthcare providers.
2. Language access services: Provide interpretation and translation services for patients who do not speak the dominant language in the area, such as Arabic. This can include on-site interpretation, telephone interpretation, and video remote interpretation.
3. Outreach and community engagement: Engage with lower-class native Arabic speakers through outreach and community events to raise awareness about available healthcare services and how to access them. This can help build trust and improve healthcare literacy among this population.
4. Community health workers: Train and deploy community health workers who are fluent in Arabic and familiar with the cultural nuances of the lower-class native Arabic speaker population. These workers can act as liaisons between patients and healthcare providers, helping to bridge the gap in communication and access to care.
5. Health literacy programs: Develop health literacy programs that cater to the unique needs and experiences of lower-class native Arabic speakers. This can include educational materials, videos, and interactive sessions that are culturally sensitive and tailored to their level of understanding.
6. Patient navigation: Establish patient navigation programs that provide personalized support and guidance to lower-class native Arabic speakers throughout the healthcare system. These navigators can help patients navigate the complex healthcare system, schedule appointments, and communicate with providers in a language they understand.
7. Partnership with local organizations: Collaborate with local organizations that serve lower-class native Arabic speakers to leverage their existing networks and resources.
