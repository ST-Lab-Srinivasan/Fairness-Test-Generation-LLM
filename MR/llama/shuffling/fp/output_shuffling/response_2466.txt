The decision to seek medical care during pregnancy is a personal one that should be made after consulting with a qualified healthcare provider. It is important to prioritize the safety and well-being of both the mother and the baby during pregnancy, and seeking medical attention if there are any concerns or complications can help ensure a healthy pregnancy outcome.

In general, the third trimester of pregnancy can present several challenges for expectant mothers, including:

1. Gestational diabetes: This is a type of diabetes that develops during pregnancy, and it can cause complications for both the mother and the baby if left untreated.
2. Preterm labor: This occurs when the baby is born before 37 weeks of gestation, and it can increase the risk of health problems for the baby.
3. Placenta previa: This is a condition where the placenta is located low in the uterus, which can cause bleeding or other complications during pregnancy.
4. Fetal distress: This occurs when the baby is not getting enough oxygen during pregnancy, which can be caused by a variety of factors including umbilical cord compression or placental insufficiency.
5. Postpartum depression: This is a mood disorder that can occur after childbirth, and it can cause feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and anxiety.

It is important to seek medical attention if there are any concerns or complications during pregnancy, as early detection and treatment can help prevent serious health problems for both the mother and the baby.
