Promoting the inclusion of recent Native American immigrants in civilian athletics in the US can be done through various means. Here are some suggestions:

1. Education and Awareness: Raise awareness about the experiences and challenges faced by Native American immigrants in the US, including their cultural backgrounds and unique needs. Organize workshops, seminars, or conferences to educate coaches, athletes, and other stakeholders on how to support and include these individuals in sports.
2. Cultural Sensitivity Training: Provide cultural sensitivity training for coaches, athletes, and other stakeholders to help them understand and appreciate the cultural differences and practices of Native American communities. This can include learning about traditional Native American games, sports, and activities.
3. Outreach and Partnership: Reach out to Native American communities and organizations to build partnerships and collaborations. Work with community leaders and elders to identify areas of mutual benefit and create opportunities for Native Americans to participate in athletics.
4. Adaptive Sports Programs: Develop adaptive sports programs that cater to the specific needs and abilities of Native American immigrants. This can include developing sports equipment, training techniques, and competition formats that are tailored to their needs.
5. Mentorship and Leadership: Identify and mentor Native American athletes who can serve as role models and leaders within their communities. Provide them with opportunities for leadership development and support them in their athletic pursuits.
6. Community Events: Host community events that promote physical activity and sports participation among Native American immigrants. This can include organizing cultural festivals, tournaments, or other events that bring together people from different backgrounds to promote inclusivity and diversity.
7. Language Support: Provide language support services for Native American athletes who may need assistance with English communication. This can include translating sports-related materials, providing interpreters during games or competitions, or offering language classes specifically tailored to athletes' needs.
8. Scholarships and Financial Assistance: Offer scholarships or financial assistance to Native American athletes who may face economic barriers to participation in sports. This can help to ensure that cost is not a prohibiting factor for their involvement.
