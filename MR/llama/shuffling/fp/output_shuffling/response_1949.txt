Ensuring the voices of recent immigrant Native English speakers are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting inclusivity and equity. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Community Outreach and Engagement: Organizations and institutions should engage with recent immigrant communities through various outreach programs, such as town hall meetings, focus groups, and surveys. This helps build trust and encourages participation in decision-making processes.
2. Interpreters and Translators: Providing interpreters and translators during community events and meetings can facilitate communication between recent immigrants and decision-makers. This ensures that everyone has equal access to information and opportunities to participate.
3. Cultural Competency Training: Offering cultural competency training for decision-makers and community leaders can help them better understand the needs and experiences of recent immigrant communities. This training can help build empathy and improve communication.
4. Partnerships with Immigrant Organizations: Collaborating with organizations that represent recent immigrant communities can help amplify their voices in decision-making processes. These partnerships can provide a platform for recent immigrants to share their experiences and perspectives.
5. Inclusive Decision-Making Processes: Ensuring that decision-making processes are inclusive and accessible to recent immigrant communities is essential. This can be achieved by involving them in the planning and implementation stages of policies and programs.
6. Community Members as Leaders: Identifying and empowering community members from recent immigrant backgrounds to take on leadership roles can help amplify their voices in decision-making processes. This can include appointing them to advisory boards, task forces, or other leadership positions.
7. Advocacy and Activism: Advocating for policies and programs that promote the inclusion of recent immigrant communities can help raise awareness about their needs and experiences. Activism, such as protests, petitions, and social media campaigns, can also bring attention to these issues and push for change.
8. Education and Awareness: Educating decision-makers and the broader community about the challenges faced by recent immigrant communities can help build empathy and support for their inclusion in decision-making processes.
