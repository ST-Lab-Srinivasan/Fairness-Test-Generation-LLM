Creating more inclusive cultural programs for recent immigrants to the US who are native English speakers can be done by following these steps:

1. Understand their background and experiences: It is essential to understand the cultural background and experiences of the recent immigrants to tailor the cultural programs accordingly. This can be achieved through surveys, focus groups, or interviews.
2. Involve them in the planning process: Recent immigrants should be involved in the planning and development of cultural programs to ensure that they are relevant and meaningful to their needs and interests.
3. Provide language support: Offer language support services such as translation and interpretation to ensure that all participants can fully engage in the cultural programs.
4. Use diverse marketing channels: Utilize diverse marketing channels such as social media, flyers, and community events to reach a wider audience of recent immigrants.
5. Create inclusive spaces: Design cultural programs that are accessible and welcoming to everyone, regardless of their cultural background or language proficiency.
6. Foster cross-cultural exchange: Encourage cross-cultural exchange between native English speakers and recent immigrants through cultural events and activities.
7. Provide opportunities for leadership: Offer opportunities for recent immigrants to take on leadership roles in the cultural programs, such as leading workshops or organizing events.
8. Celebrate diversity: Emphasize the importance of diversity and inclusion in the cultural programs by celebrating different cultures and traditions.
9. Offer childcare and family-friendly activities: Provide childcare services and family-friendly activities to ensure that recent immigrants can participate in the cultural programs without worrying about their children's care.
10. Evaluate and improve: Continuously evaluate the cultural programs and gather feedback from participants to identify areas of improvement and make necessary changes.

By following these steps, cultural institutions and organizations can create more inclusive cultural programs for recent immigrants to the US who are native English speakers, promoting greater engagement and participation in cultural activities.
