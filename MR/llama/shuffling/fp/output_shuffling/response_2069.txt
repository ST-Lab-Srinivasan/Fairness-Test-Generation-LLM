Creating more inclusive community events for civilian upper middle to lower-income people requires a thoughtful and strategic approach. Here are some ideas to consider:

1. Identify the needs of the target audience: Conduct surveys or focus groups to understand the interests, preferences, and challenges faced by the target audience. This will help you design events that cater to their specific needs.
2. Offer affordable ticket prices: Make sure the event tickets are priced affordably to accommodate a wide range of income levels. Consider offering discounts for early bird tickets, student tickets, or group tickets.
3. Provide free or low-cost activities: In addition to the main event, consider organizing free or low-cost activities such as workshops, panel discussions, or networking sessions. This will allow attendees to engage with each other and learn new skills without breaking the bank.
4. Create a diverse lineup of speakers: Ensure that the event features a diverse lineup of speakers who are representative of the target audience's background and experiences. This can include individuals from different racial, ethnic, and socioeconomic backgrounds.
5. Offer childcare and transportation support: Provide childcare and transportation support to make it easier for attendees to participate in the event. This can include on-site childcare services or subsidized transportation options.
6. Promote the event through multiple channels: Use a variety of marketing channels to promote the event, including social media, email marketing, and local newspapers. Consider partnering with local organizations and community groups to reach a wider audience.
7. Offer scholarships or financial assistance: Offer scholarships or financial assistance to individuals who may not be able to afford the event's ticket price. This can help ensure that the event is accessible to a diverse range of attendees.
8. Provide accessibility accommodations: Ensure that the event venue and activities are accessible to attendees with disabilities. Provide sign language interpretation, wheelchair accessibility, and other accommodations as needed.
