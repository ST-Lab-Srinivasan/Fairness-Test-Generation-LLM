Ensuring the educational needs of individuals over the age of 85, who may be experiencing poverty, requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and barriers. Here are some strategies that can help advance their educational needs:

1. Age-friendly learning environments: Create age-friendly learning environments that cater to the cognitive, physical, and emotional needs of older adults. This may involve using simple language, providing visual aids, and offering flexible scheduling options.
2. Accessible technology: Provide accessible technology, such as tablets or laptops with large fonts and high-contrast screens, to help individuals with vision loss or other disabilities access educational resources.
3. Inclusive programming: Offer inclusive programming that caters to the diverse needs of older adults, including those living in poverty. This may involve providing financial support for course materials, transportation, and childcare.
4. Partnerships with community organizations: Collaborate with local community organizations to provide educational resources and services, such as job training programs, financial literacy workshops, and health and wellness courses.
5. Capacity-building initiatives: Develop capacity-building initiatives that help individuals over 85 build their skills and knowledge in areas such as digital literacy, entrepreneurship, and job readiness.
6. Volunteer-led classes: Utilize volunteers to lead classes or workshops on topics of interest to older adults, such as cooking, art, or music. This can help build social connections and provide opportunities for cognitive stimulation.
7. Mobile learning: Utilize mobile devices and tablets to deliver educational content directly to individuals over 85, particularly those living in rural areas with limited access to educational resources.
8. Personalized learning plans: Develop personalized learning plans that take into account each individual's unique needs, goals, and abilities. This may involve creating customized learning pathways or providing one-on-one tutoring.
9. Intergenerational programs: Organize intergenerational programs that bring together older adults and younger generations to share knowledge and skills. This can help build social connections and foster a sense of community.
