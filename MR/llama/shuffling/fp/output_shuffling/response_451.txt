Supporting the social inclusion of individuals over 90 years old, or those over 85, is crucial for their well-being and quality of life. Here are some ways to support the social inclusion of natural-born Indian citizens in this age group:

1. Encourage intergenerational relationships: Connect older adults with younger generations through programs such as mentorship, volunteering, or community events. This can help break down age-related stereotypes and create opportunities for socialization and learning.
2. Provide access to technology: Many older adults may feel isolated or disconnected from society due to a lack of access to technology. Providing access to devices such as smartphones, tablets, or computers can help them stay connected with loved ones, access information, and participate in online activities.
3. Support age-friendly communities: Develop age-friendly policies and infrastructure that cater to the needs of older adults. This includes designing public spaces that are accessible and easy to navigate, providing transportation options, and ensuring that essential services are available locally.
4. Promote healthy aging: Encourage older adults to maintain an active lifestyle through exercise, social engagement, and cognitive stimulation. Support initiatives that promote healthy aging, such as fitness classes, community gardening projects, or cultural events.
5. Challenge ageist stereotypes: Dispel negative stereotypes and misconceptions about aging by promoting positive representations of older adults in media and culture. Encourage respectful language and attitudes towards aging, and advocate for policies that support inclusive social environments.
6. Offer financial literacy training: Many older adults may struggle with managing their finances due to cognitive decline or lack of access to financial education. Provide training and resources on budgeting, saving, and investment to help them make informed financial decisions.
7. Support mental health: Older adults may be at a higher risk for mental health issues such as depression, anxiety, and cognitive decline. Offer mental health services, including counseling and therapy, and promote social connections and stress management techniques to support mental well-being.
