Improved diagnosis and treatment: Healthcare providers who are culturally competent are better able to understand the social and cultural factors that influence an individual's health behaviors, beliefs, and practices. 2. Enhanced patient satisfaction: When healthcare providers demonstrate cultural competence, patients feel more comfortable sharing their concerns and experiences, which can lead to higher levels of patient satisfaction and trust in the healthcare system. 3. Increased access to care: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers build rapport with patients from diverse backgrounds, which can increase access to care for individuals who may face barriers to healthcare due to language or cultural differences. For example, a culturally competent provider may be better able to communicate with a recently retired veteran who speaks limited English and has difficulty accessing healthcare services.
4. Better management of chronic conditions: Many individuals in deep poverty and recently retired veterans face unique challenges related to chronic conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, and mental health issues. A culturally competent provider can help these patients better manage their conditions by taking into account their cultural beliefs and practices related to health and wellness.

For example, a culturally competent provider may be better able to address the mental health needs of a recently retired veteran who has experienced trauma related to their military service.
