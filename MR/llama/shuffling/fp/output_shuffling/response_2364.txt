Economic empowerment of recent immigrants to the US who are not low-income individuals can be supported through a variety of means. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to financial services: Many recent immigrants face barriers in accessing financial services such as bank accounts, credit cards, and loans. Organizations can provide financial education and assistance in opening these accounts, which can help them manage their finances and build credit.
2. Job training and placement: Many recent immigrants lack the skills and knowledge to find jobs that match their qualifications and experience. Organizations can offer job training programs that focus on the specific needs of recent immigrants, such as language training, cultural awareness, and industry-specific skills.
3. Entrepreneurship support: Recent immigrants often have unique entrepreneurial ideas and experiences that can contribute to the US economy. Organizations can provide mentorship, networking opportunities, and access to funding to help them start their own businesses.
4. Networking and community building: Building a network of recent immigrants and connecting them with other professionals in their field can help them navigate the job market and build meaningful relationships. This can be done through events, workshops, and online platforms.
5. Advocacy and policy change: Organizations can advocate for policies that support the economic empowerment of recent immigrants, such as providing a pathway to citizenship, protecting DACA recipients, and addressing discrimination in the job market.
6. Education and skills development: Many recent immigrants may lack access to education and training opportunities. Organizations can provide language classes, vocational training, and academic programs to help them develop new skills and advance their careers.
7. Housing support: Recent immigrants may face challenges in finding affordable and safe housing. Organizations can provide rental assistance, emergency financial aid, and information on available housing resources.
