Creating more inclusive community events for low-earning ex-military people requires a thoughtful and strategic approach. Here are some suggestions to help make your events more accessible and welcoming:

1. Outreach and Promotion: Reach out to local organizations that serve low-income veterans, such as Veterans of Foreign Wars (VFW), American Legion, or Disabled American Veterans (DAV). Offer to promote their events or services through your organization's channels.
2. Accessibility: Ensure that the event location is easily accessible for people with disabilities. Provide wheelchair access, Braille signage, and assistive listening devices upon request.
3. Affordable Tickets or Admission Fees: Offer affordable ticket prices or make admission free to ensure that low-earning ex-military individuals can participate without financial burden. You could also consider offering scholarships or sponsorships for those who cannot afford the cost of attendance.
4. Childcare and Transportation Support: Provide childcare services or arrange for transportation to and from the event to help remove logistical barriers for attendees.
5. Inclusive Programming: Develop a program that includes activities and workshops relevant to low-earning ex-military individuals, such as job training, resume building, mental health support, or financial literacy classes.
6. Volunteer Opportunities: Offer volunteer opportunities for attendees to get involved in the planning and execution of the event, which can help build a sense of ownership and community engagement.
7. Food and Beverage Options: Provide a variety of food and beverage options that cater to dietary restrictions and preferences, including vegetarian, gluten-free, and halal options.
8. Comfortable Venue: Choose a venue that is comfortable and welcoming for attendees, with ample seating, adequate lighting, and a non-intimidating atmosphere.
