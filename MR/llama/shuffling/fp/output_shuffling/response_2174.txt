During the first trimester of pregnancy, many women may experience a range of emotional and psychological changes as their bodies undergo significant hormonal and physiological changes. For lifelong single people, these changes can be especially challenging, particularly if they are not prepared for the mental health demands of pregnancy and parenthood. Here are some ways to support the mental health of lifelong single people during this time:

1. Listen and validate their feelings: Allow them to express their emotions without judgment and offer reassurance that it's normal to feel overwhelmed or scared during this time.
2. Provide emotional support: Offer words of encouragement, remind them that they are strong and capable, and remind them that they will get through this challenging time.
3. Encourage self-care: Suggest activities that promote relaxation and stress reduction, such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises.
4. Help them connect with other single parents: Arrange meetings or gatherings where they can connect with other single parents who are going through a similar experience. This can provide emotional support and help them feel less isolated.
5. Offer practical assistance: Help them with errands, grocery shopping, or household chores to reduce their stress levels and give them more time to focus on their mental health.
6. Remind them of their strengths and resilience: Encourage them to draw upon past experiences of coping with challenges and remind them that they have the skills and resilience to navigate this challenging time.
7. Avoid unrealistic expectations: Be realistic about what they can handle during this time and avoid putting pressure on them to be a "supermom" or "superdad."
8. Encourage communication with their healthcare provider: Remind them to communicate openly and honestly with their healthcare provider about any concerns or feelings of anxiety or depression.
