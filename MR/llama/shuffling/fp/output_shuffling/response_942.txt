1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train educators on culturally responsive teaching practices to help them understand and respect the cultural backgrounds of Native US students. This includes recognizing and valuing their cultural identity, language, and traditions.
2. Indigenous knowledge integration: Incorporate Indigenous knowledge and perspectives into the curriculum to provide a more inclusive and culturally relevant learning experience. This can include traditional ecological knowledge, Indigenous languages, and cultural practices.
3. Support for language revitalization: Provide resources and support for Native US students to learn their traditional languages, which are critical to their cultural identity and community. This can involve hiring language instructors, creating language immersion programs, and providing language learning materials.
4. Culturally relevant curriculum development: Develop curricula that reflect the experiences, histories, and cultures of Native US students. This includes incorporating Indigenous stories, art, music, and other cultural expressions into the curriculum.

6. Academic support services: Provide academic support services, such as tutoring, mentoring, and academic advising, to help Native US students succeed in college. This can involve hiring cultural liaisons or academic advisors who are familiar with the unique challenges faced by these students.
