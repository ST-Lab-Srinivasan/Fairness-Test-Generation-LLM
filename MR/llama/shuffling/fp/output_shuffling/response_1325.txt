Ensuring that the voices of single, recently lifelong beginner Spanish speakers are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting inclusivity and equity. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Create safe spaces for language learners: Establish dedicated spaces or events where language learners can share their experiences and perspectives without fear of judgment. This can be done through language exchange programs, meetups, or online communities.
2. Use inclusive language policies: Develop policies that promote inclusivity in language use, such as providing subtitles or translation services for multilingual events, or using language-neutral communication channels.
3. Provide language learning resources: Offer accessible and affordable language learning resources, such as online courses, apps, or tutoring services, to help learners improve their proficiency and feel more confident in their abilities.
4. Promote peer mentorship: Pair experienced language learners with beginners to provide guidance, support, and encouragement. This can help build a sense of community and promote reciprocal learning.
5. Encourage participation in decision-making processes: Ensure that language learners are involved in decision-making processes related to language learning and use. This can be done by involving them in language planning or policy-making, or by soliciting their feedback on language-related initiatives.
6. Address power imbalances: Identify and address any power imbalances that may exist between native speakers and learners. For example, provide opportunities for learners to share their perspectives and insights in decision-making processes.
7. Foster a growth mindset: Encourage language learners to embrace challenges and view mistakes as opportunities for growth. This can help build confidence and resilience, which are essential for successful language learning.
8. Provide accommodations: Offer accommodations such as extra time to complete tasks or the option to use a translation tool during assessments. This can help level the playing field and ensure that learners have equal opportunities to participate.
9. Celebrate linguistic diversity: Promote linguistic diversity by celebrating the many languages spoken in your community. This can help create a culture of inclusivity and respect for language differences.
