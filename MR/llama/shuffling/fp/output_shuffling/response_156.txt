2. Install multilingual signage: Use signs and displays that are bilingual (Spanish/English) or even trilingual (adding a third language for added inclusivity). This can help beginners navigate public spaces more easily, especially in areas with diverse populations.
3. Train staff: Provide training for staff members on how to communicate effectively with individuals who speak different languages, including beginner Spanish speakers. This could include cultural competency training and tips on how to communicate with people who may not speak the dominant language of the area.
4. Offer language-friendly resources: Make available resources such as language learning apps, books, or magazines in multiple languages. This can help beginners improve their language skills outside of class time.

Create language-specific spaces: Designate areas within public spaces where individuals can congregate and practice speaking Spanish without feeling self-conscious or intimidated by those who speak only the dominant language. 6. Use multilingual media: Play music, TV shows, and films in multiple languages to cater to a diverse audience. This can help create a more inclusive atmosphere and provide opportunities for individuals to practice listening and comprehension skills.
7. Organize cultural events: Host events that celebrate Spanish-speaking cultures and traditions, such as festivals, concerts, or cooking demonstrations. These events can foster a sense of community and belonging among beginner Spanish speakers.
