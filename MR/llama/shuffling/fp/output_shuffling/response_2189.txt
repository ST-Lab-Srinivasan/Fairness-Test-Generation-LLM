1. Accessibility: Ensure that all disaster response efforts are accessible to people with physical disabilities, including those who may use wheelchairs or have other mobility impairments. Provide accessible shelter locations, evacuation routes, and emergency communication systems.
2. Cultural Competency: Disability is not a solely medical issue, but also a social and cultural one. Recognize the importance of cultural competency in addressing the needs of individuals with mixed Hispanic heritage, who may have diverse cultural practices and beliefs. Engage community leaders and members from these groups to inform disaster response efforts.

Language Access: Provide language access services, such as interpreters and translated materials, to ensure that individuals with limited English proficiency can access critical information during a disaster. This is particularly important for individuals who may have difficulty communicating in emergency situations due to hearing or speaking impairments.
4. Inclusive Sheltering: Ensure that shelters are accessible and inclusive for people of all abilities. Provide accommodations such as wheelchair ramps, Braille signage, and assistive technologies for individuals who rely on these tools to communicate or navigate their surroundings.
5. Personal Supports: Recognize the importance of personal supports, such as service animals, medical equipment, and other adaptive devices, in ensuring the safety and well-being of individuals with disabilities during a disaster. Make arrangements for the safe storage and transportation of these items during evacuations or sheltering.
6. Disability-Inclusive Emergency Planning: Incorporate disability-inclusive emergency planning into community preparedness efforts. Develop emergency plans that take into account the unique needs of individuals with physical disabilities, including those related to mobility, communication, and access to medical care.
