Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping the Identities of Recently Divorced Multiple Times Individuals of Mandarin Descent

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, particularly those from diverse backgrounds. In the case of recently divorced multiple times individuals of Mandarin descent, cultural heritage can have a profound impact on their sense of self and belonging. This paper aims to explore how cultural heritage influences the identities of this population, and how it shapes their experiences of divorce and remarriage.

Literature Review:
Previous research has shown that cultural heritage can influence an individual's identity in various ways. For instance, cultural values and beliefs can shape an individual's expectations and attitudes towards marriage and family (Kim, 2017). Cultural practices and traditions can also impact an individual's sense of belonging and community ties (Wang, 2018). Furthermore, cultural identity can be a source of strength and resilience during times of crisis, such as divorce (Gill, 2015).

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research design, using in-depth interviews with recently divorced multiple times individuals of Mandarin descent. Participants will be recruited through community organizations and online forums, and will be selected based on their self-identification as Mandarin Chinese and their experience of divorce multiple times. The interviews will be recorded, transcribed, and analyzed using thematic analysis.

Expected Findings:
The findings of this study are expected to reveal the ways in which cultural heritage shapes the identities of recently divorced multiple times individuals of Mandarin descent. Specifically, it is anticipated that participants will discuss how their cultural values and beliefs influence their attitudes towards marriage and family, as well as their experiences of divorce and remarriage. Additionally, the study may uncover the ways in which participants draw on their cultural heritage for emotional support and resilience during times of crisis.
