2. Language access services: Provide language access services such as interpreter services, translated materials, and bilingual staff to assist patients who may not speak French fluently. This can help ensure that patients receive accurate information and can communicate their needs effectively.

4. Financial assistance programs: Offer financial assistance programs such as sliding scale fees, discounts, or payment plans to make healthcare more affordable for lower-class beginner French speakers. This can help reduce financial barriers to healthcare access.
5. Telehealth services: Utilize telehealth services to reach patients who may not have access to healthcare facilities. This can help bridge the gap in healthcare access for those living in rural or underserved areas.
6. Partnership with community organizations: Collaborate with community organizations that serve lower-class beginner French speakers to provide culturally sensitive healthcare services. This can help build trust and increase participation in healthcare services.
7. Addressing systemic barriers: Advocate for policy changes that address systemic barriers to healthcare access, such as lack of transportation or limited hours of operation. This can help ensure that lower-class beginner French speakers have equal access to healthcare services.
