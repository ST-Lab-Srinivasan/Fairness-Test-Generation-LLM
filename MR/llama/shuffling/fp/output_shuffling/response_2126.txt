To address the specific needs of recent immigrants to the US, it is important to consider their unique experiences and challenges. Here are some strategies that educators and policymakers can use to support recent immigrants:

1. Provide language support: Many recent immigrants may struggle with English language proficiency, which can create barriers to academic success. Educators can provide language support services, such as English as a Second Language (ESL) classes or bilingual education programs, to help recent immigrants improve their language skills.
2. Foster a welcoming school culture: Recent immigrants may feel isolated or uncomfortable in a new educational environment. Educators can promote a welcoming and inclusive school culture by celebrating diversity, providing support for cultural events and traditions, and encouraging student participation and engagement.
3. Address trauma and stress: Many recent immigrants may have experienced trauma or stress related to their migration journey. Educators can provide mental health support services, such as counseling or therapy, to help students manage these challenges.
4. Provide academic support: Recent immigrants may need additional academic support due to gaps in their education prior to arriving in the US. Educators can offer individualized learning plans, academic mentoring, or tutoring services to help recent immigrants catch up with their peers.
5. Connect students with resources: Recent immigrants may face challenges related to accessing healthcare, legal aid, or other community resources. Educators can connect students with local organizations that provide these services and support them in navigating the US healthcare and legal systems.
6. Engage families in educational processes: Engaging families in their children's education can help recent immigrants feel more connected to their new community. Educators can involve parents in decision-making processes, offer parenting workshops or classes, and provide opportunities for family engagement in the school.
7. Create bridging programs: Bridging programs can connect recent immigrants with local organizations, businesses, or communities that can help them integrate into the US society. Educators can collaborate with community partners to develop these programs and provide students with valuable networking opportunities.
