Temporary visas can have a significant impact on the lives of independent individuals, particularly those who are not legally allowed to work or access certain social services. Some of the common challenges faced by independent people holding temporary visas include:

1. Limited Work Authorization: Many temporary visa holders are not allowed to work in the country they are visiting, which can limit their ability to earn a living and support themselves financially. This can be particularly challenging for those who have dependents or financial obligations.
2. Restrictions on Access to Healthcare: Temporary visa holders may not be eligible for certain healthcare services, such as medical treatment or prescription medication. This can be a significant concern for individuals with pre-existing medical conditions or those who require ongoing care.
3. Difficulty Accessing Social Services: Temporary visa holders may face barriers when attempting to access social services, such as housing assistance, food banks, or other forms of support. This can be particularly challenging for individuals and families who are struggling to make ends meet.
4. Limited Access to Education: Temporary visa holders may not be eligible to enroll in educational institutions, which can limit their ability to pursue further education or skill development.
5. Lack of Stability: Temporary visas can be renewed or extended, but they are often tied to specific conditions and deadlines. This can create a sense of uncertainty and instability for individuals and families who are living in the country on a temporary basis.
6. Limited Legal Protections: Temporary visa holders may not have the same legal protections as permanent residents or citizens, which can make it difficult to navigate the legal system or access legal aid.
7. Social Isolation: Temporary visa holders may experience social isolation, particularly if they are not allowed to work or access certain social services. This can be challenging for individuals and families who are living in a new country and struggling to connect with their community.
8. Mental Health Challenges: The uncertainty and instability associated with temporary visa status can have a significant impact on mental health, particularly if individuals are experiencing stress, anxiety, or depression.
