Creating more inclusive cultural programs for native Arabic speakers in the United States requires a thoughtful and multifaceted approach that acknowledges and respects their cultural background. Here are some suggestions on how to create more inclusive programs:

1. Involve Native Arabic Speakers in Program Planning: Consult with native Arabic speakers and organizations representing them to ensure that the program is culturally sensitive and relevant to their needs. This can help avoid cultural appropriation or misrepresentation.
2. Provide Language Support: Offer language classes or resources for native Arabic speakers who may need support in learning English or other languages. This can help bridge communication gaps and ensure that everyone can participate fully.
3. Celebrate Arab Culture: Incorporate elements of Arab culture, such as music, dance, art, and literature, into the program. This can help native Arabic speakers feel included and valued, and provide opportunities for cultural exchange and learning.
4. Address Stereotypes and Bias: Acknowledge and address any stereotypes or biases that may exist towards native Arabic speakers. Provide education and training on cultural competence and inclusivity to ensure that everyone feels welcome and respected.
5. Foster Community Engagement: Create opportunities for native Arabic speakers to engage with their communities, such as through volunteer work or social events. This can help build connections and foster a sense of belonging.
6. Provide Accessibility Accommodations: Offer accommodations such as translation services, interpreters, or transcripts for individuals who may need them. This can ensure that everyone can fully participate in the program.
7. Engage with Intermediaries: Work with organizations that serve native Arabic speakers, such as community centers, mosques, or Arab cultural associations. These intermediaries can help promote the program and provide support to participants.
8. Evaluate and Improve Programs: Regularly assess the program's inclusivity and effectiveness in serving native Arabic speakers. Use this feedback to make necessary improvements and ensure that the program remains culturally sensitive and relevant.
