Supporting the economic empowerment of Native American communities is crucial for promoting equity and addressing the historical injustices that have hindered their economic development. Here are some strategies to support the economic empowerment of upper lower middle-income Native natural-born US citizen individuals:

1. Entrepreneurship and Small Business Development: Encourage entrepreneurship and small business development in Native American communities through training programs, access to capital, and mentorship opportunities. This can help create jobs and generate income for community members.
2. Job Training and Placement: Provide job training and placement services to help individuals acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and on-the-job training programs.
3. Capacity Building: Invest in capacity building initiatives that support Native American communities in developing their economic infrastructure. This can include supporting the development of community-based organizations, cooperatives, and other collective enterprises.
4. Access to Financial Services: Improve access to financial services for Native American individuals by establishing bank branches or kiosks on tribal lands, offering microfinance programs, and providing financial literacy training.
5. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): Encourage the establishment of Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI) that provide affordable loans and other financial services to Native American communities. CDFI's can help support small businesses, affordable housing development, and community facilities.
6. Sustainable Economic Development: Support sustainable economic development initiatives that promote long-term economic growth and stability in Native American communities. This can include investing in renewable energy projects, sustainable agriculture practices, and eco-tourism ventures.
7. Capacity Building for Land Use Planning: Provide technical assistance and training to help Native American communities develop land use plans that support economic development while preserving cultural and environmental resources.
8. Support for Traditional Arts and Crafts: Encourage the preservation and revitalization of traditional arts and crafts in Native American communities by providing training, mentorship, and marketing support. This can help create jobs and generate income for community members.
