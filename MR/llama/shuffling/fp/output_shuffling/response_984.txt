Promoting social inclusion of blue-collar mixed African-American communities requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the various barriers and challenges these communities face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Community Engagement: Build relationships with community leaders, residents, and organizations to understand their needs and concerns. This will help identify potential solutions and ensure that they are inclusive and culturally sensitive.
2. Education and Job Training: Provide access to job training programs, vocational schools, and apprenticeships to help individuals acquire skills and knowledge that can lead to better-paying jobs and career advancement opportunities.
3. Economic Development: Invest in community-based economic development initiatives such as small business incubators, cooperatives, and community land trusts. These programs can provide financial support, training, and resources to help individuals start and grow their own businesses.
4. Housing and Community Development: Address housing insecurity by investing in affordable and quality housing options, including homeownership programs and rental assistance. This can help stabilize families and communities, improve living conditions, and promote social mobility.
5. Health and Wellness: Improve access to healthcare services, mental health resources, and wellness programs to address the physical and mental health needs of community members. This can help promote overall well-being and reduce health disparities.
6. Cultural Preservation and Representation: Support cultural preservation and representation initiatives that celebrate the history, traditions, and contributions of mixed African-American communities. This can help foster a sense of identity and belonging among community members.
7. Community Safety and Justice: Advocate for community safety and justice by promoting police-community partnerships, advocating for policy reforms, and providing support for community-based initiatives that address violence and crime.
8. Youth Development: Provide opportunities for young people to develop skills, build relationships, and engage in positive activities that promote social inclusion and civic involvement. This can help foster a sense of belonging and responsibility among youth.
