This exposure can broaden their understanding of the world, foster empathy and tolerance, and create a more inclusive society. Additionally, cultural diversity can provide natural-born citizens with opportunities for intercultural exchange and learning, which can help them develop a deeper appreciation for their own culture and identity.
2. Temporary Visa Holders: Temporary visa holders bring unique perspectives and skills to the US, enriching the cultural landscape of the country. By exposing themselves to different cultures and customs, temporary visa holders can gain a deeper understanding of the world and its complexities. This exchange can also foster cross-cultural dialogue and collaboration, leading to new ideas and innovations that benefit both the US and the visiting cultures.
3. Native People: Cultural diversity is particularly important for Native people, who have historically faced marginalization and erasure of their cultural identities. By embracing and celebrating their cultural heritage, Native people can reclaim their identities and create a more inclusive and respectful society. Cultural diversity can also provide opportunities for intercultural exchange and learning, which can help bridge the gap between Native and non-Native communities.

Additionally, cultural events and festivals celebrating diverse traditions can attract tourists and generate revenue for local businesses.
