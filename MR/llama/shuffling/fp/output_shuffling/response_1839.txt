Creating more inclusive community events for people of average income earning civilians requires careful planning and consideration of their needs and preferences. Here are some strategies to help make your community events more accessible and enjoyable for everyone:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the event location is easily accessible for people with disabilities by providing wheelchair ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms. Offer assistance to those who may need it, such as guide dogs or sign language interpretation.
2. Affordability: Keep costs low by offering free or affordable admission tickets, food, and drinks. Consider partnering with local businesses to offer discounts or special deals for attendees.
3. Inclusive programming: Offer a variety of activities that cater to different interests and abilities. This can include cultural performances, workshops, and interactive exhibits that are inclusive and accessible to everyone.
4. Volunteer opportunities: Encourage volunteers from the local community to get involved in organizing and running the event. This can help create a sense of ownership and belonging among attendees.
5. Food and beverage options: Offer a variety of food and beverage options that cater to different dietary needs and preferences, such as vegetarian, gluten-free, and halal options.
6. Family-friendly activities: Include activities and entertainment that are suitable for all ages, including children. This can help create a sense of inclusivity and belonging among families.
7. Community outreach: Reach out to local community groups, organizations, and social media platforms to promote the event and encourage participation from a diverse range of individuals.
8. Interpretation services: Provide interpretation services, such as live translation or sign language interpretation, to ensure that all attendees can fully participate in the event.
9. Accessibility-focused marketing: Use marketing materials and promotional content that are accessible and inclusive, such as images with alt text, closed captions, and audio descriptions.
10. Post-event follow-up: Follow up with attendees after the event to gather feedback and make improvements for future events. This can help create a more inclusive and welcoming environment for everyone.
