Addressing the needs of beginner Chinese speakers in disaster response can be challenging, especially for those from lower middle-income backgrounds. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Provide language training: Offer language classes or workshops specifically designed for beginners to improve their Chinese language skills. This can help them communicate more effectively with local communities and respond to disaster situations more efficiently.
2. Use visual aids and simple language: When communicating in Chinese, use visual aids such as pictures and diagrams to convey messages. Also, simplify the language to make it easier for beginners to understand.
3. Provide cultural training: Offer cultural training sessions to help beginners understand the local customs, values, and beliefs. This can help them avoid cultural misunderstandings or offenses that could hinder their ability to respond effectively in a disaster situation.
4. Use technology: Utilize technology such as translation apps or electronic devices with pre-loaded Chinese phrases to help beginners communicate during disaster response efforts.
5. Create a supportive environment: Encourage and support beginner Chinese speakers through regular check-ins, mentorship programs, and peer-to-peer learning opportunities. This can help build their confidence and competence in using Chinese during disaster response situations.
6. Collaborate with local organizations: Partner with local organizations that specialize in disaster response and work with beginner Chinese speakers. These organizations can provide valuable insights and support to help them navigate the language barrier and respond effectively to disaster situations.
7. Offer remote language training: For beginners who may not have access to in-person language classes, offer remote language training options such as online courses or language exchange programs.
8. Use storytelling: Utilize storytelling techniques to help beginner Chinese speakers learn and retain important disaster response phrases and vocabulary. Stories can make learning more engaging and memorable.
9. Focus on practical language skills: Teach beginners practical language skills such as how to ask for directions, describe a situation, or express their emotions in Chinese. This can help them communicate more effectively during disaster response efforts.
