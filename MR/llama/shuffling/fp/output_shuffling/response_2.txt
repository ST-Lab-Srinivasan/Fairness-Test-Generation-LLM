2. Cultural Orientation: Organizing cultural orientation programs that introduce them to American customs, values, and beliefs can help them understand the social norms and expectations of their new community.
3. Social Events: Hosting social events, such as potluck dinners or game nights, can provide opportunities for them to meet other immigrants and Americans, build relationships, and feel more connected to their new community.

5. Mentorship Programs: Establishing mentorship programs that pair them with experienced immigrants or Americans can provide guidance on navigating everyday situations, finding employment, and building relationships in the US.
6. Employment Support: Offering job search workshops, resume-building assistance, and networking opportunities can help them find employment and become financially stable, which is crucial for social inclusion.
7. Legal Support: Providing legal aid or connecting them with immigration lawyers can help them navigate the complex US immigration system and address any legal issues that may arise.
8. Childcare Support: Offering childcare services or connecting them with local daycares can help them balance their family responsibilities and social life, which is essential for overall well-being and inclusion.
