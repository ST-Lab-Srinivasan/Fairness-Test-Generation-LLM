Creating more inclusive cultural programs for mixed African-American individuals who are beginner Chinese speakers requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some suggestions:

1. Understand the background and identity of your participants: It's essential to recognize that your participants have a unique experience as mixed race individuals, and they may face different challenges than their monoracial counterparts. Consider including questions about their cultural background, identity, and experiences in the initial assessment or survey.
2. Use culturally responsive teaching methods: Incorporate teaching methods that are sensitive to the participants' cultural backgrounds. This can include using visual aids and examples that reflect their experiences, as well as creating a safe and inclusive learning environment.
3. Offer language learning opportunities tailored to their needs: Mixed African-American individuals may have different learning styles and preferences based on their cultural background. Provide language learning opportunities that cater to these differences, such as offering conversational practice with native Chinese speakers or incorporating cultural elements into the language lessons.
4. Foster a sense of community: Encourage participants to connect with one another and share their experiences. This can help create a supportive environment where they feel comfortable learning and practicing Chinese. Consider organizing group activities or events that celebrate African-American culture and heritage, while also promoting cultural exchange with China.
5. Provide resources and support: Offer participants access to language learning resources, such as textbooks, language learning apps, or online courses. Additionally, provide support for participants who may need additional help outside of class, such as one-on-one tutoring or language exchange programs.
6. Address potential cultural barriers: Be aware of potential cultural barriers that may arise when teaching Chinese to mixed African-American individuals. For example, some participants may have had negative experiences with the Chinese language or culture in the past. Be sensitive to these issues and work to address them in a constructive and respectful manner.
