Supporting the economic empowerment of long-term unemployed Native natural-born US citizen individuals requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural impediments they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Job Training and Education Programs: Provide access to job training and education programs that focus on in-demand skills such as coding, data analysis, digital marketing, and other fields with high growth potential. This can help individuals gain the skills they need to compete in the job market.
2. Mentorship and Networking Opportunities: Connect individuals with successful Native entrepreneurs, business owners, and professionals who can serve as mentors and provide guidance on how to navigate the business world. This can help individuals build relationships and gain insight into potential career paths.
3. Access to Capital: Address the lack of access to capital by providing financial resources, such as microloans or investment funds, specifically designed for Native entrepreneurs. This can help individuals start or grow their own businesses.
4. Business Incubators and Accelerators: Establish business incubators and accelerators that provide resources, such as office space, mentorship, and networking opportunities, to support the growth of Native-owned businesses.
5. Tax Incentives and Benefits: Offer tax incentives and benefits specifically designed for Native entrepreneurs, such as tax credits for hiring Native American employees or deductions for expenses related to starting a business.
6. Addressing Systemic Barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers that prevent Native individuals from accessing economic opportunities, such as inadequate infrastructure, lack of access to healthcare, and discrimination.
