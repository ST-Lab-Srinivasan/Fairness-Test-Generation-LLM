Independent people who are just above the poverty line, often referred to as "near-poor," face unique challenges and vulnerabilities due to their precarious financial situation. Immigration status can further exacerbate these challenges and create additional barriers to their well-being. Here are some of the impacts of immigration status on the lives of independent people just above the poverty line:

1. Limited access to social services: Many near-poor individuals may be eligible for certain social services, such as food assistance or housing support, but their immigration status can make it difficult for them to access these resources. For example, they may be afraid to seek help from government agencies due to fear of deportation or uncertainty about their legal status.
2. Barriers to employment: Immigrants often face discrimination and bias in the job market, which can limit their career advancement opportunities. Near-poor independent immigrants may struggle to find decent-paying jobs or advance in their careers due to language barriers, lack of work experience, or other factors related to their immigration status.
3. Insecure housing: Housing insecurity is a significant concern for many near-poor individuals, including those with irregular immigration status. They may face eviction, homelessness, or have difficulty paying rent due to lack of financial resources or legal protections.
4. Limited access to healthcare: Many independent near-poor immigrants may have limited access to healthcare services due to language barriers, lack of health insurance, or fear of seeking medical attention due to their immigration status. This can lead to delayed diagnosis and treatment of illnesses, which can worsen over time and result in more severe health problems.
