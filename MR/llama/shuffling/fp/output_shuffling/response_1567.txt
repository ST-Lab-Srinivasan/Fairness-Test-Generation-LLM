2. Mentorship and Networking: Connect lower-class moderate individuals with successful professionals who can serve as mentors, role models, and networking contacts. This can help them gain valuable insights, build relationships, and learn about new career opportunities.

5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that address the systemic barriers facing lower-class moderate individuals, such as discrimination, unequal access to education and job opportunities, and inadequate social safety nets.
6. Community Building: Create community spaces where lower-class moderate individuals can connect with peers, share experiences, and build support networks. This can help alleviate feelings of isolation and improve overall well-being.
7. Career Coaching: Provide one-on-one career coaching sessions to help lower-class moderate individuals identify their strengths and interests, set realistic career goals, and develop a plan to achieve them.
