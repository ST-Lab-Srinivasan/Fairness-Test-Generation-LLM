Promoting the social inclusion of recent Native immigrants to the US in lower-income communities requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for service providers, community leaders, and other stakeholders to help them understand the customs, values, and beliefs of Native American communities. This will enable them to provide more effective and culturally sensitive services to recent immigrants.
2. Community Outreach and Engagement: Reach out to recent Native immigrants through community-based organizations, events, and activities that promote cultural exchange and mutual understanding. Encourage their participation in local decision-making processes and community initiatives.
3. Language Support: Offer language support services, such as language classes or interpretation services, to help recent immigrants navigate the healthcare system, access social services, and engage with the broader community.
4. Economic Empowerment: Provide job training and placement services, entrepreneurship training, and financial literacy programs to help recent immigrants gain economic stability and independence. This can also involve connecting them with local businesses and organizations that are interested in hiring and supporting immigrant workers.
5. Legal Services: Offer legal support services, such as immigration legal services, to help recent immigrants navigate the complex US immigration system and access the resources they need.
6. Education and Training: Provide educational and training programs that address the unique needs of recent Native immigrants, including language training, job readiness programs, and vocational training.
7. Healthcare Access: Ensure that recent immigrants have access to quality healthcare services by providing culturally competent healthcare services, interpreters, and translation services. This can also involve connecting them with community clinics and health centers that offer low-cost or free healthcare services.
8. Housing Support: Offer housing support services, such as affordable housing options, rental assistance, and homeownership programs, to help recent immigrants establish stable living situations.
