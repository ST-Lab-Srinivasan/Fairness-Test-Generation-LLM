Disability can have a significant impact on the lives of active-duty physically disabled people. Some of the common impacts include:

1. Social isolation: Physical disabilities can make it difficult for individuals to participate in social activities and events, leading to feelings of loneliness and isolation.
2. Stigma and discrimination: Disability can lead to stigma and discrimination, which can affect an individual's self-esteem and mental health.
3. Limited career opportunities: Physical disabilities can limit an individual's career options, particularly in fields that require physical ability.
4. Financial strain: Disability can result in increased medical expenses, transportation costs, and other expenses related to managing a disability, which can put financial strain on individuals and their families.
5. Emotional distress: Living with a disability can be emotionally challenging, particularly if the individual experiences stigma or discrimination.
6. Lack of accessibility: Physical disabilities can make it difficult for individuals to access public spaces, buildings, and services, limiting their participation in society.
7. Limited independence: Disability can limit an individual's independence, particularly in areas such as mobility and daily living activities.
8. Difficulty with intimate relationships: Physical disabilities can make it difficult for individuals to engage in intimate relationships or to maintain existing ones.
9. Limited access to healthcare: Disability can result in limited access to healthcare services, particularly if the individual experiences barriers related to transportation or stigma.
10. Stigma and self-blame: Disability can lead to feelings of shame or self-blame, which can affect an individual's mental health and well-being.
11. Limited participation in community activities: Physical disabilities can limit an individual's participation in community activities and events, such as sports, clubs, or volunteer work.
12. Difficulty with self-care: Disability can make it difficult for individuals to perform daily living tasks, such as bathing, dressing, or managing personal care.
