Creating more inclusive social policies for permanent resident individuals who are recently widowed requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some recommendations to make society more supportive and inclusive:

1. Acknowledge the loss: Recognize the loss of a spouse as a significant life event that can have a profound impact on an individual's emotional, social, and economic well-being. Provide emotional support and validation to help them cope with their grief.
2. Offer financial assistance: As a recent widow or widower may face financial difficulties due to the loss of their spouse's income, provide financial assistance such as emergency funds, low-interest loans, or grants to help them cover basic needs like housing, food, and healthcare.
3. Ensure access to healthcare: Recent widows/widowers may face challenges in accessing healthcare due to a lack of insurance or limited financial resources. Provide access to affordable healthcare options, including mental health services, to help them maintain their physical and emotional well-being.
4. Create support groups: Facilitate the creation of support groups specifically for recent widows/widowers, where they can connect with others who have gone through similar experiences. These support groups can provide a safe space for sharing experiences, receiving emotional support, and building social connections.
5. Promote social integration: Encourage recent widows/widowers to participate in community activities, volunteer, or pursue hobbies to help them reintegrate into society and build new social connections. This can also help them feel more connected and engaged with their local community.
6. Address age-related concerns: Recent widows/widowers may face additional challenges related to aging, such as declining health or reduced mobility. Ensure that policies address these issues and provide support for older adults, including access to age-appropriate healthcare services, transportation options, and home safety modifications.
