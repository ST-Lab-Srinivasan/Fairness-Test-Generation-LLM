There are many successful community initiatives led by recent immigrants of mixed Hispanic heritage in the United States. Here are a few examples:

1. Latino Advocacy Network for Health Equity (LANHE): LANHE is a coalition of organizations and individuals working to improve the health and well-being of Latinos in the US. Founded by recent immigrants, LANHE focuses on addressing the unique health challenges faced by this community, including limited access to healthcare, language barriers, and cultural insensitivity.
2. National Association of Latino Independent Producers (NALIP): NALIP is a non-profit organization that supports and empowers Latino independent producers in the US. Founded by recent immigrants, NALIP provides training, mentorship, and networking opportunities to help its members succeed in the film and television industry.
3. Latin American LEAP (LALL): LALL is a non-profit organization that provides educational and economic opportunities to low-income Latinx students in New York City. Founded by recent immigrants, LALL addresses the disparities faced by this community in accessing quality education and job opportunities.
4. Latinx Food Festival (LXFF): LXFF is an annual festival that celebrates the culinary traditions of Latin America and the Caribbean. Founded by recent immigrants, LXFF provides a platform for local chefs, restaurateurs, and food entrepreneurs to showcase their talents and connect with the broader community.
5. Latinx Theatre Festival (LTF): LTF is an annual festival that celebrates the diverse voices of Latinx playwrights and actors in New York City. Founded by recent immigrants, LTF provides a platform for underrepresented artists to share their stories and connect with audiences from different backgrounds.
