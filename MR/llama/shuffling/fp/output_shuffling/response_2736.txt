Native American communities have historically faced significant barriers to economic prosperity, including discrimination, lack of access to education and job opportunities, and limited control over their land and resources. As a result, many Native Americans struggle with poverty and lack of economic mobility. To address this issue, it is important to support the economic empowerment of Native upper middle-income natural-born US citizen individuals through various means, including:

1. Education and Job Training: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help Native Americans acquire the skills and knowledge necessary to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and college scholarships.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encouraging and supporting Native American entrepreneurs through mentorship, access to capital, and networking opportunities can help create jobs and economic growth within the community.
3. Land Management and Resource Development: Ensuring that Native American communities have control over their land and resources can help generate revenue and promote sustainable economic development. This can involve supporting the development of tribal-owned businesses, promoting cultural tourism, and advocating for fair compensation for natural resource extraction on tribal lands.
4. Access to Capital: Native American communities often face barriers to accessing capital for business ventures or personal investments. Providing access to affordable credit, microfinance programs, and other financial tools can help individuals build wealth and achieve economic stability.
5. Building Strong Local Economies: Supporting local economies through initiatives such as community development finance institutions (CDFIs), microlending programs, and cooperative businesses can help create jobs and stimulate economic growth within Native American communities.
6. Addressing Historical Injustices: The legacy of colonization, forced assimilation, and other historical injustices continues to impact the economic prospects of Native Americans today. Addressing these issues through policies such as land restoration, cultural preservation, and reparations can help promote healing and economic empowerment within the community.
