Creating more inclusive social policies for financially disadvantaged individuals requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the root causes of poverty and inequality. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to create more inclusive social policies:

1. Address income inequality: Implement progressive taxation policies that reduce the wealth gap between the rich and the poor. This can be achieved through policies such as increasing taxes on the wealthy, implementing a living wage, and providing financial assistance to low-income families.
2. Increase access to education and job training: Provide free or affordable education and job training programs that help individuals acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and entrepreneurship programs.
3. Promote affordable housing: Implement policies that make housing more affordable, such as rent control, subsidized housing, and home ownership programs. This can help reduce the burden of housing costs on low-income individuals and families.
4. Improve access to healthcare: Ensure that everyone has access to quality, affordable healthcare by implementing a universal healthcare system or by providing financial assistance for medical expenses.
5. Address discrimination and inequality: Identify and address the systemic barriers and biases that prevent marginalized communities from accessing resources and opportunities. This can include policies to combat discrimination in employment, housing, and education.
6. Foster community engagement and participation: Encourage community participation in policy-making processes by providing opportunities for public consultation and feedback. This can help ensure that policies are responsive to the needs of marginalized communities.
7. Support social enterprises and microfinance initiatives: Provide financial support and resources to social enterprises and microfinance initiatives that provide financial services and support to low-income individuals and communities.
8. Invest in digital literacy and access: Ensure that everyone has access to digital technologies and the internet by providing low-cost or free internet access, digital literacy training, and device loans.
