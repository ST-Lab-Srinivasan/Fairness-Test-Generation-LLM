Ensuring the healthcare needs of non-disabled people in early pregnancy (1st trimester) are met requires a comprehensive and inclusive approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Accessible Healthcare Facilities: Ensure that healthcare facilities are accessible and welcoming to all, regardless of disability status. This includes wheelchair accessibility, Braille signage, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
2. Trained Healthcare Professionals: Provide training to healthcare professionals on disability awareness and how to provide inclusive care to people with disabilities. This can include understanding the specific needs of people with different types of disabilities, such as mobility, cognitive, or sensory impairments.
3. Adaptive Equipment and Technology: Provide adaptive equipment and technology that can help people with disabilities access healthcare services more easily. For example, audio loops or infrared systems for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing, or adjustable examination tables for individuals who have mobility impairments.
4. Patient-Centered Care: Prioritize patient-centered care, which involves understanding the individual's needs and preferences and involving them in decision-making processes. This can help ensure that the healthcare provided is tailored to the person's specific needs and abilities.
5. Collaboration with Disability Organizations: Work closely with disability organizations to understand their members' needs and how to provide inclusive care. This can involve consulting with organizations that represent people with different types of disabilities, such as the National Disability Rights Network or the Disability Rights Education and Defense Fund.
6. Telehealth Services: Offer telehealth services for individuals who may face barriers to accessing healthcare facilities due to their disability status. This can include video consultations, phone consultations, or online support groups.
7. Disability-Specific Resources: Provide resources that are tailored to the specific needs of people with different types of disabilities. For example, resources for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing, or resources for individuals who have mobility impairments.
8. Advocacy and Support: Advocate for policies and practices that promote inclusive healthcare and support individuals
