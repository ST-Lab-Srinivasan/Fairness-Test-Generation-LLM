Advocating for the rights of older adults in Nigeria can be challenging, but there are several effective ways to promote their well-being and protect their rights. Here are some strategies that can be employed:

1. Raising Awareness: Educate the public about the issues faced by older adults in Nigeria, such as poverty, social isolation, and discrimination. Use media platforms, community events, and cultural gatherings to raise awareness and build support for their rights.
2. Policy Advocacy: Work with government agencies, non-profit organizations, and community groups to advocate for policies that benefit older adults. This can include pushing for the passage of laws that protect their rights, provide social support, and promote their full participation in society.
3. Community Mobilization: Engage local communities to take action and advocate for the rights of older adults. This can involve organizing community events, establishing support groups, and mobilizing community members to speak out against ageism and discrimination.
4. Healthcare Advocacy: Collaborate with healthcare providers and policymakers to improve access to quality healthcare for older adults. This can include advocating for the provision of age-friendly healthcare services, such as geriatric care and palliative care, and pushing for greater investment in healthcare infrastructure.
5. Economic Empowerment: Support initiatives that promote economic empowerment for older adults, such as microfinance programs, entrepreneurship training, and job creation opportunities. This can help to reduce poverty and social isolation among older adults.
6. Social Inclusion: Work to promote social inclusion and combat ageism in Nigerian society. This can involve advocating for greater representation of older adults in decision-making positions, promoting intergenerational dialogue and collaboration, and challenging stereotypes and discrimination against older adults.
7. Legal Support: Provide legal support to older adults who are facing age-related discrimination or exploitation. This can involve advocating for their rights in court, providing legal aid and representation, and pushing for greater enforcement of laws that protect older adults.
