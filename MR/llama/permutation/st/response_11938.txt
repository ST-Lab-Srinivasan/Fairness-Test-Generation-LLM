Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Mixed-Caucasian Heritages Individuals with Extreme Moderate Liberal Views

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, especially those from mixed racial and ethnic backgrounds. The experience of being part of multiple cultures can lead to a complex identity formation, where individuals may feel a sense of belonging to multiple groups or communities. In this study, we investigate the role of cultural heritage in shaping the identities of mixed-Caucasian heritage extreme moderate liberal individuals.

Theories:
We draw on the theories of identity formation, specifically the social constructionist perspective, which posits that identity is constructed through interactions with others and the social environment (Brown, 1995). We also consider the idea of cultural hybridity, which suggests that cultural identities are fluid and dynamic, shaped by the interaction between different cultures and backgrounds (Grosby, 2017).

Methodology:
We conducted in-depth interviews with 20 mixed-Caucasian heritage individuals who identify as extreme moderate liberals. Participants were recruited through snowball sampling and purposive sampling techniques. Interviews were audio recorded, transcribed verbatim, and analyzed using thematic analysis.

Results:
Our findings reveal that cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of mixed-Caucasian heritage extreme moderate liberal individuals. Participants drew on multiple cultures and identities to construct their sense of self, often feeling a sense of belonging to both Caucasian and non-Caucasian groups. We identified three themes: (1) navigating cultural complexities, (2) embracing hybridity, and (3) challenging dominant narratives.

Navigating Cultural Complexities: Participants discussed the challenges of navigating multiple cultures, particularly in situations where they felt their identity was not recognized or valued. They reported feeling caught between different worlds, struggling to find a sense of belonging.
