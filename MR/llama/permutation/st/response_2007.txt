1. Accessibility and Inclusive Design: Ensure that workplaces, public spaces, and products are designed to be accessible and usable by people with disabilities. This includes physical accessibility, as well as digital accessibility for those who use assistive technologies.
2. Education and Training: Provide training and education on disability awareness, etiquette, and inclusive practices for employers, managers, and coworkers. This can help create a more inclusive work environment and reduce stigma against hiring and working with people with disabilities.

Accommodations and Modifications: Make reasonable accommodations and modifications to the work environment and job duties to enable individuals with physical disabilities to perform their job tasks safely and effectively. This may include adapting equipment, providing additional support, or modifying work schedules.
4. Job Coaching and Support: Provide job coaching and support to help individuals with physical disabilities learn new skills, improve their productivity, and advance in their careers. This can involve one-on-one coaching, mentoring, or group training sessions.
5. Networking and Mentorship Opportunities: Connect individuals with physical disabilities with mentors and peers who can provide guidance, support, and networking opportunities. This can help build confidence, develop professional skills, and create a sense of belonging in the workplace.
