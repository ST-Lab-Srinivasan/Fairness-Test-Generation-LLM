This can result in delayed or foregone care, which can exacerbate existing health conditions and lead to poorer health outcomes.
2. Discrimination and bias: Mixed race individuals may experience discrimination and bias within the healthcare system, particularly if they are seen as "not black enough" or "not white enough." This can affect their quality of care and create a distrust of the medical system.
3. Lack of culturally competent care: Lower-class mixed African Americans may encounter healthcare providers who lack understanding of their cultural background and experiences, leading to inadequate care or misdiagnosis.
4. Stigma and shame: Mixed race individuals may internalize the stigma and shame associated with being a "mixed" person, which can affect their mental health and well-being. This can also lead to avoidance of healthcare services due to feelings of embarrassment or shame.
5. Lack of representation in medical research: Lower-class mixed African Americans may be underrepresented in medical research studies, which can result in a lack of understanding of their unique health needs and experiences.
6. Limited access to specialized care: Mixed race individuals may face barriers to accessing specialized care, such as cardiologists or oncologists, due to limited availability in their area or inability to afford the services of these specialists.
