The impact of a genetic condition on an individual's quality of life can be significant, particularly for active-duty military personnel who may face additional challenges due to their profession. Here are some potential impacts of disability status on the lives of carrier of genetic conditions among active-duty people:

1. Stigma and discrimination: Carriers of genetic conditions may face stigma and discrimination within their military unit or wider society, which can lead to social isolation and decreased mental health.
2. Limited career advancement opportunities: Depending on the severity of the condition, carriers may face limitations in their career advancement due to the perceived impact of their disability on their performance. This can lead to feelings of frustration and disappointment.
3. Increased stress and anxiety: The added pressure of managing a genetic condition while serving in the military can lead to increased stress and anxiety, which can affect mental health and overall well-being.
4. Physical limitations: Depending on the nature of the genetic condition, carriers may experience physical limitations that can impact their ability to perform certain military tasks or duties. This can lead to decreased self-esteem and confidence.
5. Financial strain: The added expenses associated with managing a genetic condition, such as medication and medical appointments, can put a significant financial strain on active-duty personnel, particularly if they do not have access to adequate military benefits or insurance coverage.
6. Difficulty in forming personal relationships: Carriers of genetic conditions may struggle to form and maintain personal relationships due to the stigma and discrimination associated with their condition.
7. Increased reliance on family and friends: Due to the added challenges associated with managing a genetic condition, carriers may become more reliant on their family and friends for emotional support and practical help with daily tasks.
8. Decreased sense of purpose: Carriers of genetic conditions may struggle to find meaning and purpose in their military service due to the limitations imposed by their condition.
9. Increased risk of mental health issues: The added stress and stigma associated with a genetic condition can increase the risk of developing mental health issues such as depression and anxiety.
