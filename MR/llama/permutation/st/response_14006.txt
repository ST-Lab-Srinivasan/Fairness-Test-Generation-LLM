2. Networking: Family members often have a vast network of contacts, including friends, colleagues, and acquaintances, which can be leveraged for job opportunities. Nominal Christian individuals may benefit from their family's connections when searching for employment.
3. Practical Assistance: Family members can offer practical assistance, such as childcare, transportation, or household chores, allowing the individual to focus more on their job search. This support can be especially helpful during times of unemployment when the individual may be struggling to balance work and family responsibilities.
