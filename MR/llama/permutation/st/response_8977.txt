The impact of disability status on the lives of permanent resident left-leaning people can vary depending on a range of factors, including the nature and severity of their disability, the level of support and resources available to them, and the broader social and cultural attitudes towards disability. However, some common impacts include:

1. Social isolation: People with disabilities may face discrimination and stigma, leading to social isolation and difficulties forming and maintaining relationships.
2. Limited access to education and employment: Discrimination and barriers in the education system and workplace can limit the opportunities available to people with disabilities, leading to lower rates of employment and education attainment.
3. Health disparities: People with disabilities may experience poorer health outcomes due to lack of access to appropriate healthcare services, inadequate health insurance, or discrimination in the delivery of healthcare.
4. Financial insecurity: People with disabilities may face financial challenges due to lower earning potential, increased healthcare costs, and reduced social security benefits.
5. Lack of representation and voice: People with disabilities are often underrepresented in decision-making processes and lack a platform to advocate for their rights and interests.
6. Stigma and stereotyping: People with disabilities may experience stigma and stereotyping, leading to lower self-esteem and confidence.
7. Limited access to assistive technology: People with disabilities may face barriers in accessing assistive technology, such as wheelchairs, prosthetics, and communication devices, which can limit their ability to participate fully in society.
8. Inadequate transportation options: People with disabilities may face challenges in accessing transportation, leading to reduced mobility and independence.
9. Limited access to recreational and leisure activities: People with disabilities may face barriers in accessing recreational and leisure activities, such as sports, hobbies, and social events, which can lead to feelings of isolation and exclusion.
10. Difficulty navigating the service system: People with disabilities may face challenges in accessing services and supports, such as healthcare, education, and employment services, due to lack of knowledge or resources.
