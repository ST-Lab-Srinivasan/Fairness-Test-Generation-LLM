Creating more inclusive social policies for deep poverty, lifelong singleness, and other marginalized groups requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the root causes of poverty and discrimination. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive social policies:

1. Data collection and analysis: Collect data on the prevalence of deep poverty, lifelong singleness, and other marginalized groups to identify areas of need and inform policy decisions.
2. Consultation with affected communities: Engage with marginalized communities to understand their experiences, challenges, and aspirations. This can help policymakers design policies that are responsive to their needs.
3. Addressing systemic barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers such as discrimination, lack of access to education and job opportunities, and inadequate social safety nets.
4. Promoting affordable housing: Ensure that there are adequate affordable housing options available to low-income individuals, including single people.
5. Improving access to healthcare: Ensure that everyone has access to quality, affordable healthcare, including mental health services.
6. Encouraging diversity and inclusion in the workplace: Promote diversity and inclusion in the workplace to create a more level playing field for marginalized groups.
7. Providing financial support: Provide financial support, such as microfinance programs or basic income guarantees, to help individuals overcome poverty and achieve financial stability.
8. Addressing social isolation: Address social isolation by promoting community-based initiatives that bring people together and provide a sense of belonging.
9. Improving access to education: Ensure that everyone has access to quality, inclusive education that prepares them for the workforce and society.
10. Addressing discrimination: Address discrimination by promoting policies that protect marginalized groups from discrimination and ensure their equal treatment under the law.
11. Encouraging intergenerational mobility: Promote policies that help individuals overcome socio-economic barriers and improve intergenerational mobility.
