Promoting the economic empowerment of permanent resident native English speaker communities can be achieved through a combination of policies and initiatives that support their economic development and well-being. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and training programs that help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and higher education programs.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing resources and support for start-ups and small businesses. This can include access to funding, mentorship, and networking opportunities.
3. Job Creation: Foster job creation in the community through initiatives such as tax incentives for businesses that hire local residents, or programs that provide subsidies for job training and placement services.
4. Community Development: Invest in community development projects that create jobs and opportunities for local residents. This can include infrastructure development, cultural and recreational facilities, and social services.
5. Access to Capital: Provide access to capital through programs such as microfinance initiatives or cooperative banks that cater specifically to the needs of local residents.
6. Mentorship and Networking: Establish mentorship programs that connect local entrepreneurs with successful business leaders and professionals, and provide opportunities for networking and collaboration.
7. Cultural Preservation: Support the preservation of local cultural practices and traditions through initiatives such as cultural festivals, heritage tourism, and community arts programs.
8. Access to Markets: Facilitate access to markets for local businesses by providing resources and support for marketing and branding, and by creating opportunities for local businesses to showcase their products and services.
9. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes at the local, state, or federal level that promote economic empowerment for permanent resident native English speaker communities. This can include policies that address issues such as housing affordability, transportation accessibility, and healthcare availability.
10. Collaboration with Other Groups: Work collaboratively with other organizations and groups that share a commitment to promoting economic empowerment for permanent resident native English speaker communities.
