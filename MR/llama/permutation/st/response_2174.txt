Supporting the mental health of recently early pregnant lifelong single people can be challenging, but there are several ways to help them cope with the emotional changes and stresses associated with pregnancy. Here are some ways to provide support:

1. Listen and validate their feelings: Allow them to express their emotions without judgment, and validate their feelings by acknowledging that it's normal to feel overwhelmed or scared during this time.
2. Offer practical help: Help them with errands, grocery shopping, and household chores to reduce their stress levels and give them more time to focus on their mental health.
3. Encourage self-care: Suggest activities that promote relaxation and stress reduction, such as yoga, meditation, or reading. Encourage them to take breaks and engage in activities that bring them joy and happiness.
4. Connect them with resources: Provide information about prenatal massages, meditation classes, or support groups for pregnant women. These resources can help them manage their mental health during this time.
5. Be a sounding board: Allow them to talk about their fears, worries, and hopes without interrupting or judging. Listen attentively and offer emotional support.
6. Respect their boundaries: Recognize that they may need privacy and alone time during this period, and respect their wishes regarding visitors or social engagements.
7. Offer virtual support: If they prefer to spend time alone, offer virtual support through messaging, video calls, or online forums. This can help them feel less isolated and more connected to others.
8. Encourage open communication: Create a safe space where they feel comfortable expressing their emotions and asking questions about pregnancy and parenthood.
9. Remind them of their strengths: During this time, it's essential to remind them of their resilience and ability to cope with challenges. Encourage them to focus on their strengths and what they can control.
10. Celebrate milestones: Acknowledge and celebrate their progress and achievements during pregnancy, no matter how small they may seem. This can help boost their confidence and self-esteem.
