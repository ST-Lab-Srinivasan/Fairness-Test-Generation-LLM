Ensuring that the healthcare needs of natural-born Indian citizens who are out of work are met is a critical aspect of promoting social justice and inclusive growth in India. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to achieve this goal:

1. Increase access to affordable healthcare: The government can launch initiatives to make healthcare more affordable for the unemployed population, such as subsidizing medical expenses or providing free or low-cost health insurance coverage.
2. Strengthen primary healthcare infrastructure: The government can invest in strengthening the primary healthcare infrastructure, including setting up more health clinics and improving existing ones, to provide better access to basic healthcare services.
3. Provide mental health support: Many unemployed individuals may struggle with mental health issues due to the stress of job searching and financial instability. The government can provide mental health support services, such as counseling and therapy, to help them cope with these challenges.
4. Promote preventive care: Encouraging people to prioritize preventive care can help identify health issues early on and prevent more serious problems from developing. The government can launch public awareness campaigns to promote preventive care and provide resources for individuals to access affordable screenings and check-ups.
5. Offer job-related health support: Many jobs require specific physical or mental abilities, and unemployed individuals may struggle to maintain their health while searching for work. The government can offer job-related health support services, such as occupational therapy or vocational rehabilitation, to help them prepare for potential job opportunities.
6. Foster collaboration between government and NGOs: Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) often have extensive experience in providing healthcare services to marginalized communities. The government can collaborate with these NGOs to leverage their expertise and resources in serving the unemployed population.
