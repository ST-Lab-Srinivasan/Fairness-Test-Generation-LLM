The economic empowerment of deeply lower-class Native natural-born US citizen individuals is a complex and multifaceted issue that requires a comprehensive approach. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and Training: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge they need to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and certification programs.
2. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship: Offering microloans and other financial instruments can help individuals start their own businesses or invest in existing ones. This can provide a pathway to economic mobility and create jobs in the community.
3. Job Creation and Employment Opportunities: Working with local employers to create job opportunities and provide career advancement paths for individuals can help them gain stability and security in their employment.
4. Addressing Systemic Barriers: Addressing systemic barriers such as discrimination, lack of access to healthcare, and limited social services can help level the playing field and create more equitable opportunities for economic empowerment.
5. Community Development: Investing in community development initiatives such as infrastructure, public transportation, and cultural facilities can help improve the quality of life and create a more vibrant economy.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocating for policy changes at the local, state, and federal levels can help address systemic issues that hinder economic empowerment, such as inadequate funding for social services or discriminatory laws.
7. Collaboration with Other Organizations: Collaborating with other organizations, such as non-profits, businesses, and government agencies, can help leverage resources and expertise to support economic empowerment efforts.
8. Capacity Building: Building the capacity of individuals and communities through training, technical assistance, and mentorship can help them develop the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in the economy.
9. Addressing Health Disparities: Addressing health disparities through access to quality healthcare, nutrition education, and mental health services can help improve overall well-being and reduce barriers to economic empowerment.
