Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for late 30s beginner Spanish speakers can be achieved by taking into account their language proficiency level and cultural awareness. Here are some suggestions:

1. Use simple language and avoid complex vocabulary or idioms that may be difficult for beginners to understand.
2. Provide explanations and context for cultural practices and traditions, especially those that are unfamiliar to non-native speakers.
3. Offer visual aids and interactive activities to help learners engage with the culture in a fun and engaging way.
4. Encourage participation from all members of the group, regardless of their language proficiency level. This can be done by assigning roles or tasks that are appropriate for each individual's comfort level.
5. Use cultural examples and anecdotes that are relevant to the learners' interests and experiences. This can help them connect with the culture on a personal level and make it more meaningful.
6. Provide opportunities for learners to share their own cultural practices and traditions, fostering mutual understanding and respect.
7. Offer support and resources for learners who may be struggling with the language or cultural aspects of the celebration. This could include one-on-one tutoring or access to additional learning materials.
8. Encourage feedback and suggestions from learners on how to make the celebration more inclusive and enjoyable for them.
9. Be mindful of cultural sensitivities and avoid stereotyping or appropriating cultural practices without proper understanding or permission.
10. Celebrate diversity and promote cross-cultural understanding, recognizing that each individual brings their unique perspective and experiences to the celebration.

By following these suggestions, you can create a more inclusive and enjoyable cultural celebration for late 30s beginner Spanish speakers.
