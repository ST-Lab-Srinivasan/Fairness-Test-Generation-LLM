Promoting the educational success of recent immigrant to the US physically disabled students requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that educators, policymakers, and parents can adopt:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training for teachers and staff on cultural competency and how to support immigrant students with disabilities in the US education system. This can help them understand the students' backgrounds, experiences, and learning styles.
2. Language Support: Offer language support services such as interpreters, translators, or bilingual educators to help immigrant students with disabilities communicate more effectively with their teachers and peers.
3. Accessible Curriculum: Develop an accessible curriculum that takes into account the diverse needs of immigrant students with disabilities. This can include visual aids, braille materials, or assistive technology.
4. Accommodations and Modifications: Provide individualized accommodations and modifications to support immigrant students with disabilities in their academic work. This can include extra time to complete assignments, note-taking services, or adaptive technology.
5. Peer Support: Encourage peer support programs that pair immigrant students with disabilities with native-born peers who can provide emotional and social support.
6. Parent Engagement: Engage parents in their child's education by providing them with information about available resources, accommodations, and modifications. Encourage parents to participate in school activities and decision-making processes.
7. Community Partnerships: Develop partnerships with community organizations that provide services and support to immigrant students with disabilities, such as health clinics, social service agencies, or advocacy groups.
8. Policy Advocacy: Advocate for policies that support the educational success of immigrant students with disabilities, such as language access laws, accommodations in standardized testing, and funding for special education services.
9. Research and Evaluation: Conduct research and evaluation to better understand the experiences and outcomes of immigrant students with disabilities. This can inform policy decisions and program development.
10. Professional Development: Provide ongoing professional development opportunities for teachers and staff to learn about best practices in supporting immigrant students with disabilities
