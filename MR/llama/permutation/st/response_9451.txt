The organization has helped thousands of individuals navigate the complex immigration system and access vital resources such as healthcare and education.
2. Asian American Legal Defense and Education Fund (AALDEF): AALDEF is a national organization that protects the rights of Asian Americans through legal advocacy, policy work, and community organizing. Founded by a group of Asian American lawyers in 1974, the organization has been instrumental in advancing the civil rights of immigrant communities.
3. Desi Advocacy and Activism Network (DAAWN): DAAWN is a non-profit organization based in New York City that works to empower South Asian immigrants through advocacy, activism, and community organizing. The organization has successfully campaigned for issues such as affordable healthcare, immigrant rights, and social justice.

The organization was founded by a group of local Indian immigrants who wanted to foster a sense of community and connection among their peers.
