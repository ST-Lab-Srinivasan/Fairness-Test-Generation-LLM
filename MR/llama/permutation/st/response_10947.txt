Promoting the financial stability of native French speakers during their third trimester of pregnancy can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some suggestions:

1. Encourage them to save for the future: Many new parents may not have enough savings to cover unexpected medical expenses or time off work. Encourage them to start saving early and regularly, even if it's just a small amount each month.
2. Offer financial planning resources: Provide access to financial planners or resources that can help them create a budget, manage debt, and plan for the future. This can include workshops, webinars, or one-on-one consultations.
3. Help them navigate medical expenses: Pregnancy can be expensive, especially in the third trimester. Offer resources to help them navigate medical expenses, such as health insurance, co-payments, and prescription drug costs.
4. Provide support during maternity leave: Many new parents may struggle to manage their finances during maternity leave. Offer support by providing information on government programs (such as Employment Insurance or parental leave benefits), as well as resources for managing expenses while on leave.
5. Encourage them to take advantage of tax credits: Many new parents may be eligible for tax credits, such as the Quebec Parental Insurance Plan (QPIP) or the Federal Baby Tax Credit. Encourage them to research these programs and take advantage of any benefits they may be eligible for.
6. Offer financial assistance: If possible, offer financial assistance to help new parents cover unexpected expenses during their third trimester. This could include a one-time payment or ongoing support, such as a monthly allowance.
