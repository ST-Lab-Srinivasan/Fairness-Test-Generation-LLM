Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of native Chinese speakers who are recent immigrants to the US. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage shapes their experiences:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging and identity for immigrants. It helps them connect with their roots and feel a part of a larger community. For native Chinese speakers, their cultural heritage is an important aspect of their self-esteem and self-worth.
2. Language preservation: Many recent immigrants to the US are concerned about preserving their language and cultural practices in the face of assimilation. They may feel pressure to conform to the dominant culture or risk losing their cultural identity. Cultural heritage provides a way for them to maintain their linguistic and cultural traditions.
3. Cultural exchange: Recent immigrants may use cultural heritage as a means of exchange with their new community. For example, they may share traditional foods, festivals, or customs with their neighbors and colleagues, which can help build bridges and foster understanding between different cultures.
4. Preserving memories: Cultural heritage is often tied to memories of home and family. Recent immigrants may use cultural heritage as a way to preserve memories of their homeland and share them with their children or other family members in the US. This can help them maintain a sense of continuity and connection to their roots, even after relocating to a new country.
5. Navigating identity: Recent immigrants may use cultural heritage as a way to navigate their identity in the US. By embracing their cultural heritage, they can assert their distinctiveness and resist assimilation into the dominant culture. At the same time, they may also feel pressure to conform to societal expectations, which can lead to feelings of ambivalence or fragmentation.
6. Resistance and activism: Cultural heritage can be a powerful tool for resistance and activism among recent immigrants. By embracing their cultural traditions and practices, they can challenge dominant narratives and power structures in the US. For example, they may use traditional festivals or rituals to raise awareness about social justice issues affecting their community.
