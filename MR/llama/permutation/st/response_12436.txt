Ensuring that the educational needs of mixed African-American students are met requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the complex and multifaceted experiences of this population. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural Competency Training for Educators: Provide training on cultural competency and diversity for all educators to help them understand the unique needs and experiences of mixed African-American students. This training can help educators recognize their biases and limitations, and develop strategies to support students from diverse backgrounds.
2. Inclusive Curriculum: Develop an inclusive curriculum that reflects the diversity of the student population. Incorporate texts, images, and other materials that represent the experiences and perspectives of mixed African-American students. This can help students see themselves reflected in their education and feel more connected to the material.
3. Identity-Based Support Groups: Create support groups for mixed African-American students where they can connect with peers who share similar experiences. These groups can provide a safe space for students to discuss their challenges, share their experiences, and build a sense of community and belonging.
4. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair mixed African-American students with successful professionals from diverse backgrounds. This can help students develop role models and receive guidance on how to navigate various social and academic challenges.
5. Student Advocacy: Appoint student advocates who are responsible for ensuring that the needs of mixed African-American students are being met. These advocates can serve as liaisons between students, educators, and administrators, and help identify and address any issues or concerns that arise.
6. Family Engagement: Encourage family engagement by providing opportunities for parents and guardians to participate in educational activities and events. This can help build a stronger sense of community and support for mixed African-American students.
7. Community Partnerships: Develop partnerships with local organizations that serve diverse communities, such as cultural centers, community groups, and religious organizations. These partnerships can provide additional resources and support for mixed African-American students and their families.
