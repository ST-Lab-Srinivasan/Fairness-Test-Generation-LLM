2. Language Access: Ensure that public services are available in the languages spoken by Native communities. Provide interpreters or translated materials for vital services such as healthcare, education, and legal aid.

4. Accessible Facilities: Ensure that government buildings and facilities are accessible for individuals with disabilities. This includes wheelchair accessibility, Braille signage, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
5. Online Accessibility: Make public services available online, such as digital portals for healthcare, education, and legal aid. Ensure that these online platforms are accessible to individuals with disabilities and provide alternative means of accessing services for those without access to the internet.

7. Healthcare Access: Improve access to healthcare services by partnering with tribal clinics and hospitals, providing mobile health clinics, and addressing language barriers in healthcare settings.
8. Education and Employment Opportunities: Provide education and employment opportunities for Native individuals through partnerships with tribal organizations, vocational training programs, and job placement services.
