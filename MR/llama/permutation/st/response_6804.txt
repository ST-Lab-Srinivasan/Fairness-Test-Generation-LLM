Lower-income temporary visa holders face significant challenges when accessing healthcare services in Australia. Some of the key challenges include:

1. Lack of access to affordable health insurance: Many lower-income temporary visa holders cannot afford private health insurance, which can limit their access to quality healthcare services. As a result, they may rely on public hospitals and clinics, which can have long wait times and limited resources.
2. Language barriers: Temporary visa holders who do not speak English fluently may face difficulties communicating with healthcare providers, which can lead to misdiagnosis or inadequate treatment.
3. Limited knowledge of the healthcare system: Newly arrived temporary visa holders may be unfamiliar with the Australian healthcare system and its complexities, making it challenging for them to navigate the system and access necessary services.
4. Difficulty finding healthcare providers who accept their insurance: Lower-income temporary visa holders may have difficulty finding healthcare providers who accept their insurance, leading to financial hardship or delayed treatment.
5. Fear of deportation: Temporary visa holders may be reluctant to seek medical attention due to fear of being reported to immigration authorities and facing deportation.
6. Difficulty accessing specialist care: Lower-income temporary visa holders may face challenges accessing specialist care, as they may not have the necessary referrals or appointments with specialists.
7. Limited availability of culturally sensitive healthcare services: Temporary visa holders from diverse backgrounds may face barriers in accessing healthcare services that are culturally sensitive and tailored to their specific needs.
8. Difficulty navigating the mental health system: Lower-income temporary visa holders may find it challenging to access mental health services, which can exacerbate existing mental health issues.
