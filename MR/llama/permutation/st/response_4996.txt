This can involve using language and cultural references that are relevant to the patient's culture, as well as being sensitive to nonverbal cues and expressions.
3. Better diagnosis and treatment: By being culturally competent, healthcare providers can better understand the cultural beliefs and practices that influence a patient's health behaviors and choices. This can lead to more accurate diagnoses and effective treatment plans.
4. Increased access to care: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers build relationships with patients from marginalized communities, who may have historically faced barriers to accessing healthcare. This can increase patient engagement and improve health outcomes.
5. Improved population health: By addressing the cultural and social determinants of health, cultural competence in healthcare can lead to better overall health outcomes for marginalized communities.
