5. Retirement Planning: Develop a retirement plan that addresses both of your retirement needs and aspirations. This should include setting aside funds for retirement, considering how you will generate income during retirement, and planning for long-term care if needed.
6. Estate Planning: Create a comprehensive estate plan that includes wills, trusts, powers of attorney, and other legal documents. Ensure that your plans align with your shared values and goals, and that you both have a clear understanding of each other's wishes.
7. Risk Management: Identify potential risks to your financial stability, such as unexpected expenses or job loss, and develop strategies to mitigate them. This could include building an emergency fund, obtaining disability insurance, or developing a contingency plan for unexpected events.
8. Regular Check-Ins: Schedule regular check-ins to review your financial progress, discuss any changes in your circumstances, and adjust your financial plan as needed.
