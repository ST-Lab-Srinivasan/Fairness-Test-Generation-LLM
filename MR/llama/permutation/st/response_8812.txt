Congratulations on your happy marriage and upcoming delivery! During the third trimester of pregnancy, it's essential to prioritize your mental health, especially if you're experiencing stress or anxiety. Here are some ways to support your mental well-being during this time:

1. Communicate with your partner: Share your feelings and concerns with your partner. They can offer emotional support and help with household tasks, allowing you to rest and relax more.
2. Seek professional help: Consult a therapist or counselor if you're experiencing anxiety, depression, or other mental health issues. They can provide guidance on how to manage your emotions during pregnancy.
3. Practice self-care: Engage in activities that help you relax and reduce stress, such as yoga, meditation, or reading a book. Make time for yourself each day to unwind and recharge.
4. Stay active: Regular exercise can help alleviate anxiety and depression during pregnancy. Consult your healthcare provider for safe exercises and activities.
5. Connect with others: Join a support group or online community for pregnant women. Sharing experiences and advice with others who are going through similar situations can be incredibly helpful.
6. Set realistic expectations: Understand that pregnancy, childbirth, and parenthood can be challenging. Set reasonable expectations for yourself and your partner, and be prepared to adapt to unexpected circumstances.
7. Take breaks: Allow yourself time to rest and recharge. Taking short breaks throughout the day can help reduce stress and improve overall well-being.
8. Stay organized: Create a schedule or to-do list to help manage your responsibilities and tasks. This can help reduce stress and make you feel more in control.
9. Practice gratitude: Reflect on the things you're grateful for, such as your health, your partner, and your upcoming baby. Focusing on positivity can help improve your mental state during pregnancy.
10. Get enough sleep: Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep each night to help regulate your mood and reduce stress. Establish a consistent bedtime routine to promote better sleep.
