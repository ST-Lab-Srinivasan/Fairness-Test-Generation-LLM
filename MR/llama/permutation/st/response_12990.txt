1. Language Proficiency Tests: Conduct language proficiency tests to assess the English language skills of beginner learners in India. The test can be administered online or offline, and the results can be analyzed to identify areas where learners struggle the most. For example, a test can be designed to measure listening comprehension, speaking fluency, reading comprehension, and writing skills.
2. Survey Research: Conduct surveys among beginner English learner populations in India to gather data on their language learning experiences, challenges, and motivations. The survey can cover topics such as the reasons for learning English, the resources used for language learning, and the difficulties faced during the learning process.
3. Educational Data: Analyze educational data from schools and universities in India to identify trends and patterns in English language teaching and learning. For example, data can be collected on the number of English language classes offered, the qualifications of teachers, and the resources available for language learning.
4. Social Media Analysis: Analyze social media conversations related to English language learning in India to identify common issues and challenges faced by beginner learners. This can help identify areas where language learners are struggling the most and provide insights into their needs and preferences.
5. Corpus Linguistics: Use corpus linguistics techniques to analyze large datasets of written or spoken English language in India.
