Supporting the social inclusion of Nigerian recently widowed people can be achieved through various means. Here are some suggestions:

1. Create a support group: Form a group of like-minded individuals who can provide emotional and practical support to newly widowed individuals in Nigeria. This group can offer a safe space for sharing experiences, coping strategies, and advice.
2. Provide financial assistance: Offer financial aid to recently widowed individuals, such as loans or grants, to help them cover basic needs like rent, food, and clothing. You could also provide financial management training to help them manage their finances effectively.
3. Advocate for policy change: Work with government officials and non-profit organizations to advocate for policies that support the social inclusion of widows in Nigeria. This can include laws that protect the rights of widows, such as inheritance rights and access to healthcare.
4. Educate the community: Organize workshops and campaigns to educate the local community about the importance of supporting recently widowed individuals. This can help change attitudes towards widows and promote a more inclusive society.
5. Provide access to healthcare: Many newly widowed individuals face health challenges due to neglect or lack of access to healthcare. Providing access to health services, such as check-ups, vaccinations, and mental health support, can help them recover from the loss of their spouse.
6. Offer mentorship opportunities: Pair recently widowed individuals with experienced mentors who can provide guidance on managing daily life, navigating social situations, and building a new identity.
7. Create job opportunities: Provide training and job placement services to help widows develop new skills and find employment, which can help them regain their independence and self-esteem.
8. Promote cultural awareness: Raise awareness about the cultural and social norms that affect widows in Nigeria. This can involve educating community members about the importance of respecting and supporting widows, and promoting positive attitudes towards widowhood.
9. Provide legal aid: Many recently widowed individuals face legal challenges due to lack of knowledge or resources. Offering legal aid services can help them navigate the court system and access their rights.
