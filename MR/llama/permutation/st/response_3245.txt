The impact of immigration status on the lives of low-to-middle income independent individuals can be significant and far-reaching. Here are some potential effects:

1. Access to Healthcare: Many immigrants face barriers when trying to access healthcare services due to their immigration status. They may be eligible for public insurance programs but struggle to navigate the enrollment process or worry about being deported if they seek medical attention.
2. Education: The immigration status of a person can affect their ability to access education. Undocumented immigrants, in particular, may face challenges when trying to enroll in school or university programs due to fears of exposure and potential deportation.
3. Employment: Immigration status can greatly impact an individual's ability to find and keep employment. Discrimination based on immigration status is illegal in many countries, but it still occurs, and some immigrants may face difficulties when looking for work or advancing in their careers.
4. Housing: Immigrants may encounter problems finding affordable and secure housing due to discrimination based on their immigration status. This can lead to overcrowding, poor living conditions, and increased vulnerability to homelessness.
5. Social Isolation: Immigrants, especially those who are undocumented or in a precarious immigration status, may experience social isolation due to fear of interaction with law enforcement or other authorities. This can lead to feelings of loneliness, depression, and anxiety.
6. Fear and Stress: Living with uncertainty about one's immigration status can create significant stress and fear for individuals and their families. This can impact mental health and overall well-being.
7. Lack of Access to Services: Immigrants may encounter barriers when trying to access essential services such as food assistance programs, legal aid, or language classes due to their immigration status.
8. Criminalization: In some cases, undocumented immigrants may be criminalized for their immigration status, which can lead to detention, deportation, and separation from their families.
9. Lack of Economic Mobility: Immigration status can limit an individual's economic mobility, as they may face obstacles when trying to access education, employment
