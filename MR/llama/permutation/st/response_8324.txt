Supporting the social inclusion of Native Arabic speakers who have been separated from their communities can be a complex and multifaceted issue. Here are some ways that can help promote their social inclusion:

1. Language Support: Providing language support and classes specifically designed for Native Arabic speakers can help them improve their language skills, which can lead to greater social integration.
2. Cultural Awareness: Educating the community about the culture, customs, and traditions of Native Arabic speakers can help promote mutual understanding and respect. This can be done through cultural events, workshops, or educational programs.
3. Community Outreach: Building relationships with local organizations that serve Native Arabic speakers can help increase their visibility and access to resources within the community.
4. Advocacy: Advocating for policies and practices that promote social inclusion and address discrimination against Native Arabic speakers can help create a more inclusive environment.
5. Economic Empowerment: Providing economic opportunities and support specifically designed for Native Arabic speakers can help them gain financial stability, which is often an important factor in social integration.
6. Mental Health Support: Providing mental health services tailored to the needs of Native Arabic speakers can help address any trauma or mental health issues that may be impacting their ability to socially integrate.
7. Education and Training: Offering educational programs and training opportunities specifically designed for Native Arabic speakers can help them acquire new skills and knowledge, which can lead to greater social inclusion.
8. Healthcare Access: Ensuring access to quality healthcare services that cater to the needs of Native Arabic speakers can help address any health disparities and improve their overall well-being.
9. Community Building: Creating spaces for Native Arabic speakers to come together and build relationships can help foster a sense of community and belonging, which is essential for social inclusion.
10. Policy Change: Advocating for policy changes that promote the rights and dignity of Native Arabic speakers can help create a more inclusive society and address systemic barriers to social inclusion.
11. Media Representation: Increasing media representation of Native Arabic speakers in a positive light can help challenge stereotypes and promote greater understanding and respect.
