3. Differentiated Instruction: Use differentiated instruction techniques to meet the diverse needs of your students. This can include tailoring lessons to meet the individual needs of students with disabilities, as well as providing extensions and challenges for advanced learners.
4. Assistive Technology: Utilize assistive technology devices and tools to support students with disabilities in accessing the curriculum and completing classroom activities. This can include text-to-speech software, speech-to-text software, and other adaptive devices.
5. Support Services: Provide support services such as academic advising, tutoring, and counseling to help students with disabilities succeed in their academic pursuits.
