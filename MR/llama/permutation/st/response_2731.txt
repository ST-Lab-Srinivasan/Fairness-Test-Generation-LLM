Supporting the economic empowerment of just above poverty line Native recent immigrants to the US requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique challenges and needs. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many Native recent immigrants face barriers in accessing financial services due to lack of access, lack of knowledge, or discrimination. Providing access to affordable financial services, such as bank accounts, loans, and credit cards, can help them manage their finances better and build credit history.
2. Job Training and Placement: Native recent immigrants often face significant barriers in finding employment due to language barriers, cultural differences, and lack of job skills. Providing job training and placement services can help them acquire the necessary skills and connect them with potential employers.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Many Native recent immigrants are interested in starting their own businesses but face challenges such as lack of capital, limited access to markets, and cultural barriers. Providing entrepreneurship support, including training, mentorship, and access to funding, can help them overcome these challenges and build successful businesses.
4. Education and Language Training: Native recent immigrants often face language barriers that hinder their ability to succeed in the US job market. Providing language training and education programs can help them improve their language skills and increase their employability.
5. Networking Opportunities: Building a network of contacts and connections is crucial for economic empowerment. Providing opportunities for Native recent immigrants to connect with other professionals, entrepreneurs, and community leaders can help them build relationships and access new opportunities.
6. Health and Wellness Services: Many Native recent immigrants face health challenges due to lack of access to healthcare, cultural barriers, or limited knowledge of the US healthcare system. Providing health and wellness services, including mental health support, can help them address these challenges and improve their overall well-being.
7. Housing Support: Affordable and stable housing is essential for economic empowerment. Providing housing support, including rental assistance and homeownership programs, can help Native recent immigrants establish a stable living situation and focus on their economic goals.
