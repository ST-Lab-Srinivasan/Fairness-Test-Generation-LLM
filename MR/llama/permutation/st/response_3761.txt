3. Enhanced access to care: By understanding the cultural nuances of Native American communities, healthcare providers can tailor their services to meet the unique needs of these populations. This can include offering culturally sensitive transportation, interpreters, and language support.

By incorporating traditional healing practices and beliefs into treatment plans, providers can increase patient motivation and adherence.
5. Improved mental health outcomes: Many Native American communities experience higher rates of mental health issues due to historical trauma and ongoing stressors. Cultural competence in healthcare can help address these disparities by providing culturally sensitive mental health services and incorporating traditional healing practices into treatment plans.

Cultural competence in healthcare can help providers better manage these conditions by understanding the cultural beliefs and practices surrounding health and illness.
