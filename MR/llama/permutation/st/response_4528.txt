Poor women face significant challenges in accessing and receiving quality healthcare, particularly in developing countries. Some of the common challenges faced by poor woman people in the healthcare system include:

1. Limited access to healthcare facilities: Many poor woman live in rural areas or slums, where access to healthcare facilities is limited or non-existent. They may have to travel long distances to reach a healthcare facility, which can be a significant barrier.
2. Financial constraints: Poor women often struggle to afford medical care and medication, even for basic health problems. They may also face difficulties in paying for transportation costs to reach healthcare facilities.
3. Gender-based discrimination: In many cultures, women are expected to prioritize family responsibilities over their own health needs. This can lead to delays in seeking medical care or ignoring symptoms due to feelings of guilt or shame.
4. Lack of awareness: Many poor woman may not be aware of the importance of preventive healthcare, leading to late detection and treatment of illnesses. They may also be unaware of available health resources and services.
5. Limited access to skilled health workers: In many rural or impoverished areas, there may be a shortage of skilled health workers, such as female doctors, nurses, or midwives, who can provide adequate care for women's health needs.
6. Stigma and social exclusion: Poor woman may face stigma and social exclusion due to their socio-economic status, leading to feelings of isolation and marginalization, which can negatively impact their mental and physical health.
7. Limited access to reproductive healthcare: Many poor woman may lack access to reproductive healthcare services such as contraception, maternal healthcare, and sexual and reproductive health services due to cultural or social barriers.
8. Violence and abuse: Poor women are more susceptible to violence and abuse, including domestic violence, sexual assault, and exploitation. These experiences can have a significant impact on their physical and mental health.
