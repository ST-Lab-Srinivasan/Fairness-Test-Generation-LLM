Supporting the social inclusion of older adults, particularly those over the age of 90, can be challenging due to various factors such as physical limitations, cognitive decline, and social isolation. Here are some ways to support the social inclusion of advanced-age Canadian citizens:

1. Volunteer Opportunities: Organizations that provide volunteer opportunities for older adults can help them stay engaged and connected with their communities. These opportunities can include mentoring, teaching, or assisting with local events.
2. Social Clubs: Creating social clubs specifically designed for older adults can provide a platform for them to interact with others of similar ages and interests. These clubs can offer activities such as card games, book clubs, or art classes.
3. Intergenerational Programs: Initiating intergenerational programs that bring together older adults with younger generations can foster relationships and mutual understanding. Examples include mentorship programs, joint volunteering opportunities, or shared cultural events.
4. Technology Training: Providing technology training sessions specifically designed for older adults can help them stay connected with their communities and access digital resources. This can include training on basic computer skills, smartphones, or tablets.
5. Transportation Services: Offering transportation services to older adults who may no longer drive can greatly improve their mobility and social inclusion. This can include volunteer-led transportation programs or partnerships with local taxi services.
6. Home Visits: Organizing regular home visits by trained volunteers can provide older adults with social interaction and emotional support. These visits can be tailored to the individual's needs, interests, and preferences.
7. Telephone Reach-out Programs: Establishing telephone reach-out programs that pair older adults with volunteer phone companions can help combat loneliness and isolation. Volunteers can engage in conversation, play games, or simply provide emotional support over the phone.
8. Community Outings: Organizing group outings to local events or attractions can provide older adults with opportunities for social interaction and community engagement. Examples include trips to museums, parks, or cultural festivals.
