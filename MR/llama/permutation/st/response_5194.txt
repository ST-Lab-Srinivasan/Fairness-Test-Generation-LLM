Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in the Lives of Temporary Visa Holders as Native English Speakers

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities and experiences of individuals, including those holding temporary visas. As native English speakers, these individuals may have different perspectives on cultural heritage compared to permanent residents or citizens. This study aims to investigate the role of cultural heritage in the lives of temporary visa holders and explore how it influences their daily experiences, interactions, and sense of belonging.

Research Questions:

1. How do temporary visa holders perceive and experience cultural heritage in their daily lives?
2. What role does cultural heritage play in shaping the identities and experiences of temporary visa holders?
3. How do temporary visa holders negotiate their relationships with the host culture and their own cultural heritage?
4. What are the challenges and opportunities faced by temporary visa holders in terms of cultural heritage?

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research approach, utilizing interviews and focus groups to collect data from temporary visa holders in various countries. Participants will be recruited through snowball sampling and purposive sampling techniques. Data will be analyzed thematically, with an emphasis on identifying patterns and trends in the participants' experiences.

Expected Findings:
The study is expected to provide insights into the complex ways in which cultural heritage shapes the lives of temporary visa holders as native English speakers. Some potential findings include:

1. Dual identities: Temporary visa holders may experience tensions between their host culture and their own cultural heritage, leading to the development of dual identities or negotiating strategies.
2. Sense of belonging: Participants may struggle to establish a sense of belonging in their host country due to cultural differences or language barriers, which can impact their overall well-being and experience of life abroad.
