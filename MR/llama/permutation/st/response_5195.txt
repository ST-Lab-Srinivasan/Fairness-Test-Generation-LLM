Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in the Lives of Temporary Visa Holder Native Spanish Speaker People

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities and lives of individuals. For temporary visa holder native Spanish speaker people, cultural heritage can provide a sense of belonging, connection to their roots, and a way to navigate the complexities of living in a foreign country. This study aims to explore the role that cultural heritage plays in the lives of temporary visa holder native Spanish speaker people, including its impact on their identity, well-being, and social interactions.

Literature Review:
Previous research has shown that cultural heritage can have a profound impact on the mental health and well-being of immigrants (Hong & Kim, 2016). For temporary visa holder native Spanish speaker people, cultural heritage can provide a sense of comfort and familiarity in an unfamiliar environment. It can also serve as a source of pride and identity, helping individuals to maintain their cultural practices and traditions despite the challenges of living abroad (Gudykunst & Ting-Toomey, 1988).

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research design, using in-depth interviews with temporary visa holder native Spanish speaker people living in Spain. Participants will be recruited through purposive sampling, ensuring a diverse range of experiences and backgrounds. The interview questions will be designed to explore the role of cultural heritage in the participants' lives, including its impact on their identity, social interactions, and well-being.

Significance:
This study contributes to the existing literature on immigrant mental health and cultural heritage by providing a unique perspective on the experiences of temporary visa holder native Spanish speaker people living in Spain. The findings will shed light on the ways in which cultural heritage shapes the lives of these individuals, highlighting its potential as a resource for promoting mental health and well-being in an unfamiliar environment.
