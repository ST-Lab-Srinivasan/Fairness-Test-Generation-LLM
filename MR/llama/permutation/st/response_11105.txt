Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for over 90-year-old native English speakers requires careful consideration and planning to ensure that their needs and preferences are taken into account. Here are some suggestions:

1. Involve them in the planning process: Encourage the older adults to participate in the planning and decision-making process for cultural celebrations. This can help them feel more invested in the event and more likely to enjoy themselves.
2. Make it accessible: Ensure that the venue and events are easily accessible for older adults. Provide transportation options, wheelchair accessibility, and adequate seating and restrooms.
3. Offer a variety of activities: Cater to different interests and preferences by offering a range of activities, such as musical performances, dance classes, art exhibits, or educational workshops.
4. Focus on intergenerational connections: Incorporate opportunities for older adults to connect with younger generations through mentorship programs, cultural exchange events, or joint projects. This can help create meaningful relationships and a sense of belonging.
5. Provide sensory-friendly experiences: Take into account the sensory needs of older adults by offering gentle exercise classes, calming music sessions, or tactile activities like gardening or pottery.
6. Cater to their cultural heritage: Incorporate elements of their cultural heritage and traditions into the celebration, such as traditional music, dance, or food. This can help them feel more connected and engaged in the event.
7. Provide social opportunities: Create opportunities for older adults to socialize with one another and with younger generations through group activities, games, or simply providing a comfortable space for mingling.
8. Offer memory-stimulating activities: Incorporate activities that stimulate cognitive function and memory, such as storytelling, puzzles, or brain games. This can help keep their minds active and engaged.
9. Make it fun: Above all, ensure that the cultural celebration is enjoyable and fun for older adults. Provide opportunities for them to laugh, sing, dance, and connect with others in a positive and uplifting environment.
