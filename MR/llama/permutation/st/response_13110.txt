There are many successful community initiatives led by Native American recent immigrants to the United States, particularly from Mexico. Here are a few examples:

1. The Indigenous Language Revitalization Program at the University of Arizona: This program was established in 2015 with the goal of revitalizing and preserving indigenous languages spoken by Native American communities in the Southwest. The program offers language classes, cultural workshops, and community events to promote language preservation and cultural exchange between Native American communities and the broader Arizona community.
2. The National Congress of American Indians (NCAI): NCAI is a non-profit organization that advocates for the rights and well-being of Native American communities across the United States. The organization provides technical assistance, training, and policy analysis to support tribal sovereignty and self-determination.
3. The Indigenous Media Freedom Alliance (IMFA): IMFA is a coalition of indigenous media organizations that works to promote media justice and cultural diversity in the United States. The organization provides training, resources, and advocacy support to indigenous media makers and organizations.
4. The Native American Business Association (NABA): NABA is a non-profit organization that supports Native American entrepreneurship and economic development. The organization provides networking opportunities, mentorship programs, and training and resources for Native American business owners and startups.
5. The National Indigenous Women's Resource Center (NIWRC): NIWRC is a non-profit organization that works to promote the rights and well-being of indigenous women and their families across the United States. The organization provides training, resources, and advocacy support to address issues such as violence against women, reproductive justice, and economic empowerment.
6. The Indigenous Farming Project: This project is a collaborative effort between the Native American Agriculture Fund and the University of Arizona that works to promote sustainable agriculture practices and food sovereignty in Native American communities. The project provides training, resources, and support to indigenous farmers and gardeners, and advocates for policies that promote indigenous agricultural knowledge and practices.
