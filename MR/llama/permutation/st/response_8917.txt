Protecting the legal rights of recently single active-duty individuals can be a complex and challenging task, as it involves navigating military laws and regulations, as well as federal and state laws. Here are some steps that can be taken to ensure their legal rights are protected:

1. Familiarize yourself with relevant laws and regulations: It is essential to have a thorough understanding of the Uniform Code of Military Justice (UCMJ), the Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA), and other federal and state laws that apply to military members. These laws provide legal protections for service members, including those who are recently single.
2. Seek legal advice: Consulting with a military lawyer or civilian attorney who specializes in family law can help ensure that the individual's legal rights are protected. They can advise on how to handle divorce proceedings, child custody disputes, and other legal matters while maintaining their military status.
3. Document all legal documents: Keeping accurate records of all legal documents related to the divorce, including court orders, property settlement agreements, and child custody arrangements, is crucial. These documents can help establish a paper trail that protects the individual's rights and interests.
4. Obtain a military protection order: If there are concerns about domestic violence or stalking, obtaining a military protection order can provide legal protection. These orders can restrict the ex-spouse's access to the service member and their family.
5. Seek support from military organizations: Military organizations such as the Army Navy Marine Corps Relief Society, the Air Force Aid Society, or the Coast Guard Mutual Assistance can provide financial assistance, counseling, and other forms of support to recently single active-duty individuals. These organizations can help alleviate some of the stress and burden associated with navigating legal proceedings while maintaining a military career.
