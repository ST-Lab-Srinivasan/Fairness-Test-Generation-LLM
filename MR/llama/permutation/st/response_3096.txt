2. Understand Your Audience: Learn about the cultural backgrounds and experiences of your non-traditionally married guests. Be sensitive to their unique needs and preferences, and tailor your events accordingly.

5. Avoid Stereotypes: Avoid perpetuating stereotypes or cultural appropriation by respectfully incorporating elements of different cultures into your events. Be mindful of the historical and social context of each culture, and seek input from members of those communities.
6. Celebrate Diversity: Embrace the diversity of your guests and celebrate their unique perspectives and experiences. Use this as an opportunity to learn from one another and create a more inclusive environment.
7. Communication is Key: Clearly communicate your inclusivity policies and expectations to all attendees, either through pre-event communications or at the event itself. This can help set the tone for a respectful and welcoming atmosphere.
