1. Entrepreneurship Training and Support: Provide training and support to help individuals start and grow their own businesses. This can include workshops on business planning, financial management, marketing, and other relevant skills.
2. Access to Capital: Connect community members with potential investors or lenders who can provide capital for business ventures. This can be done through partnerships with local banks or alternative lending platforms.
3. Mentorship and Coaching: Pair successful entrepreneurs or business owners from the community with those who are just starting out, to provide mentorship and coaching on how to run a business successfully.
4. Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFI): Support the development of CDFIs, which are organizations that provide financial services to underserved communities. These institutions can provide loans, deposit accounts, and other financial products to help community members access capital and build their wealth.
5. Microfinance: Provide microloans or other financial services to individuals who may not have access to traditional banking services. This can help them start or grow a business, pay for education or healthcare expenses, or cover other basic needs.
6. Business Incubators and Accelerators: Establish incubators or accelerators that provide shared office space, mentorship, and other resources to help entrepreneurs launch and grow their businesses.
7. Job Training and Placement: Offer job training programs that teach marketable skills, such as computer programming, graphic design, or culinary arts. This can help community members gain the skills they need to compete in the job market.
8. Community Land Trusts: Establish community land trusts, which are nonprofit organizations that hold land for the benefit of a community. This can provide affordable housing options and promote economic development through community-led projects.
9. Cooperative Business Models: Support cooperative business models, such as worker cooperatives or consumer cooperatives, which allow community members to own and control their own businesses.
