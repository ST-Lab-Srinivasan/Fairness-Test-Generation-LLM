Supporting non-libertarian Mexican communities requires a thoughtful and respectful approach that acknowledges their cultural and social context. Here are some effective strategies to consider:

1. Listen and learn: Take the time to listen to the concerns and needs of the community, and learn about their cultural practices and beliefs. This will help you understand how best to support them.
2. Build relationships: Develop personal connections with community members, and work to build trust and mutual respect. This can involve spending time with them, sharing meals, and engaging in other activities that promote social bonding.
3. Avoid paternalism: Be mindful of your position of power and avoid making decisions on behalf of the community without their input and consent. Instead, work collaboratively with community members to identify their needs and develop solutions that are tailored to their specific context.
4. Foster capacity-building: Support the development of community members' skills and abilities, such as leadership, organizational, and advocacy skills. This can involve providing training and education, as well as opportunities for practical experience.
5. Advocate for policies and practices that support the community: Work with local government and other stakeholders to advocate for policies and practices that promote the well-being of the community, such as access to healthcare, education, and economic opportunities.
6. Support community-led initiatives: Rather than imposing external solutions, support community-led initiatives that are tailored to the specific needs and aspirations of the community. This can involve providing resources and technical assistance to help community members develop their own solutions.
7. Be aware of power dynamics: Be mindful of the power dynamics at play in the community, and work to address any imbalances or inequalities that may exist. This can involve actively seeking out marginalized voices and perspectives, and working to amplify them.
8. Foster intercultural understanding: Work to promote cross-cultural understanding and respect, and advocate for the value of diversity within the community.
