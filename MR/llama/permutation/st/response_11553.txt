Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for beginners with mental disabilities in China requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to make these celebrations more accessible and enjoyable:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the venue is wheelchair-friendly and has easy access to all areas. Provide clear signage and verbal announcements for visually impaired individuals.
2. Sensory Friendly Environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by reducing loud noises, bright lights, and strong smells. This can be achieved by using sound-absorbing materials, dimming the lights, and eliminating strong scents.
3. Simple Language: Use simple language and clear instructions for individuals with mental disabilities. Avoid using complex sentences or abstract concepts.
4. Visual Supports: Provide visual supports such as pictures, symbols, and videos to help individuals understand the cultural celebration. This can include visual schedules, social stories, and visual aids.
5. Social Narrative: Create a social narrative that explains the cultural celebration and its significance. This can be done through a video or a storybook.
6. Accommodations for Different Needs: Provide accommodations for different needs such as seating areas, break areas, and quiet spaces. This can help individuals with mental disabilities to participate fully in the cultural celebration.
7. Involve Them in the Planning Process: Involve beginners with mental disabilities in the planning process by asking them what they would like to see and experience during the cultural celebration. This can help create a more inclusive and enjoyable experience for everyone.
8. Provide Supports: Provide support staff such as sign language interpreters, communication supports, and job coaches to assist individuals with mental disabilities during the cultural celebration.
