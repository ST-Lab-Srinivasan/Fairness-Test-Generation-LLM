Supporting the social inclusion of Nigerian lifelong recently single people can be achieved through various means. Here are some suggestions:

1. Social Events: Organize or attend social events, such as parties, picnics, or game nights, where they can mingle with other singles and build connections.
2. Support Groups: Establish support groups specifically for recently single Nigerians, where they can share their experiences and receive emotional support from peers who understand their situation.
3. Volunteer Work: Encourage them to participate in volunteer work or community service, which can help them meet like-minded individuals while doing something meaningful.
4. Hobbies and Interests: Encourage them to explore new hobbies and interests, such as learning a new language, playing sports, or joining a book club, where they can meet others with similar passions.
5. Online Communities: Create online communities or forums where recently single Nigerians can connect with each other, share experiences, and offer support.
6. Professional Networking: Encourage them to attend professional networking events or join industry-specific groups, where they can meet new people and build professional connections.
7. Family Support: Encourage their family members to be supportive and understanding of their situation, and to actively help them find new social connections.
8. Community Outreach: Organize community outreach programs or events that bring recently single Nigerians together with other members of the community, such as cultural festivals or charity events.
9. Education and Training: Encourage them to enroll in educational or training programs, such as workshops or courses, that can help them develop new skills and meet like-minded individuals.
10. Mentorship: Pair them with a mentor who is experienced in navigating social situations as a single person, and can offer guidance and support in building their social network.

By implementing these strategies, recently single Nigerians can build their social networks and feel more connected to their community.
