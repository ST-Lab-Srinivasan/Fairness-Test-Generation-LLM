2. Microfinance: Offering microloans or other forms of financial assistance can help individuals start or grow their own businesses, which can be a key pathway to economic mobility.
3. Mentorship and Networking: Connecting individuals with successful professionals and entrepreneurs who can provide guidance and support can help them build networks and gain access to valuable resources and opportunities.
4. Access to Capital: Addressing the systemic barriers that prevent mixed African-American individuals from accessing capital, such as limited access to venture capital or crowdfunding platforms, can help them start or grow their businesses.
5. Addressing Discrimination: Addressing discrimination and bias in the workplace and in society at large can help reduce the disparities faced by mixed African-American individuals and create a more level playing field for economic mobility.

9. Entrepreneurship: Encouraging entrepreneurship and innovation among mixed African-American individuals can help create new businesses and job opportunities, which can drive economic growth and mobility.
