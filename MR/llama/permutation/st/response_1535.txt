2. The Hispanic Alliance for Career Enrichment (HACE) was founded by Dr. Maria S. Sanchez in 1997 with the mission of promoting career advancement and professional development opportunities for Hispanics in higher education. HACE provides mentorship, networking events, and scholarships to help Hispanic students and professionals reach their full potential.
3. The National Council of La Raza (NCLR) was founded in 1968 by a group of Chicanos who recognized the need for a national organization that could advocate for the civil rights and well-being of Hispanics. Today, NCLR is led by President and CEO Janet Murguia and works to advance opportunities for Hispanics in areas such as education, health, and civic engagement.
4. The Hispanic Association of Colleges and Universities (HACU) was founded in 1986 by a group of Hispanic educators who wanted to promote the advancement of Hispanics in higher education. Today, HACU provides leadership development programs, scholarships, and advocacy for Hispanic students and institutions.
5. The National Association of Hispanic Journalists (NAHJ) was founded in 1984 by a group of Hispanic journalists who recognized the need for a professional organization that could promote diversity and inclusion in the media. Today, NAHJ provides training, mentorship, and networking opportunities for Hispanic journalists and works to advance their careers and interests.
