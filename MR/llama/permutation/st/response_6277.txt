Creating more inclusive social policies for long-term unemployed, lifelong recently single individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges faced by this group. Here are some suggestions:

1. Access to education and training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs to help them acquire new skills and improve their employability. This could include vocational training, online courses, or certification programs.
2. Mentorship and coaching: Pair them with successful professionals who can provide mentorship, coaching, and networking opportunities. This can help them gain valuable insights, build confidence, and develop professional connections.
3. Career counseling: Offer career counseling services to help them identify their strengths, interests, and goals. This can help them explore new career paths and make informed decisions about their job search.
4. Job placement services: Provide job placement services to help them find suitable employment opportunities. This could include resume writing workshops, interview preparation, and job fairs.
5. Financial support: Offer financial assistance, such as grants or low-interest loans, to help them cover living expenses while they are looking for work.
6. Childcare support: Provide childcare services or subsidies to help them manage childcare responsibilities while they are searching for employment.
7. Housing support: Offer housing subsidies or support services to help them find affordable housing and maintain stable living arrangements.
8. Legal aid: Provide legal aid services to help them navigate the legal system, including issues related to divorce, child custody, and employment discrimination.
9. Mental health support: Offer mental health counseling services to help them cope with the emotional challenges of unemployment, singlehood, and other life changes.
10. Community building: Organize social events and activities that bring together individuals in similar situations, providing a sense of community and support.
11. Advocacy: Advocate for policies that address the systemic issues that contribute to long-term unemployment and singlehood, such as lack of affordable housing, inadequate education and job training programs, and discrimination.
