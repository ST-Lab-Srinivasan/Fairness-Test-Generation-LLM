Addressing the Needs of Not Disabled Mixed-Caucasian Heritage Individuals in Disaster Response: A Call to Inclusivity and Representation

Introduction:
Disaster responses are often focused on providing aid to individuals with disabilities, but what about those who do not identify as disabled? The needs of mixed-Caucasian heritage individuals, who may face unique challenges in disaster situations, are often overlooked. This essay argues that inclusivity and representation are crucial in disaster response efforts to address the needs of not disabled mixed-Caucasian heritage individuals.

Background:
Disaster responses are typically tailored towards individuals with disabilities, as they are more likely to require additional support during emergencies. However, this focus often neglects the needs of other marginalized groups, including mixed-Caucasian heritage individuals. These individuals may face unique challenges in disaster situations due to factors such as cultural identity, socioeconomic status, and access to resources.
