Supporting the career aspirations of recently divorced, multiple times combat, and recently retired veterans requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique challenges and provides tailored resources. Here are some ways to support these individuals:

1. Career counseling: Offer one-on-one career counseling sessions to help them explore their career options, identify their strengths and interests, and develop a career plan. This can be done through military veterans' organizations or non-profit organizations that specialize in career development for veterans.
2. Job training and placement: Provide job training and placement services to help them gain new skills and find employment in their desired field. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and job fairs specifically designed for veterans.
3. Networking opportunities: Organize networking events and workshops where they can connect with other veterans, entrepreneurs, and industry professionals. This can help them build a professional network, learn about new career opportunities, and gain insights into the civilian job market.
4. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair recently divorced, multiple times combat, and retired veterans with successful professionals in their desired field. This can provide valuable guidance and support as they navigate their career transition.
5. Entrepreneurship training: Offer entrepreneurship training and resources to help them explore business ownership as a viable career option. This can include workshops on business planning, marketing, and financial management, as well as access to startup funding and mentorship.
6. Education and certification programs: Provide access to education and certification programs that can help them acquire new skills and knowledge in their chosen field. This can include online courses, vocational training, and professional certifications.
7. Disability employment services: Connect them with disability employment services that can provide support and resources for individuals with service-related injuries or illnesses. These services can help them find employment that accommodates their disability and provides the necessary support.
