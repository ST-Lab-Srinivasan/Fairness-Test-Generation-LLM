Better disease management: Healthcare providers who are culturally competent are better equipped to manage diseases that are influenced by cultural factors, such as diabetes, hypertension, and mental health disorders. 4. Enhanced patient trust: When patients feel that their healthcare providers understand and respect their cultural background, they are more likely to trust them and adhere to treatment plans. This is particularly important in cultures where patient autonomy is highly valued.
5. More effective use of technology: Healthcare providers who are culturally competent can effectively use technology to deliver care to patients from diverse backgrounds.
