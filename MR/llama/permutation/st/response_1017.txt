Temporary visa holders, also known as non-immigrants or non-permanent residents, are individuals who enter a country on a temporary basis for work, study, or other purposes. They may have various immigration statuses, such as H-1B visa holders, L-1 visa holders, J-1 exchange visitors, or TN (NAFTA) professionals. While they are not permanent residents of the host country, they still contribute to the economy and society in significant ways. However, their immigration status can have various impacts on their lives, including:

1. Limited job opportunities: Temporary visa holders may face restrictions on the types of jobs they can work in or the duration of their employment. They may be limited to working for a specific company or in a particular industry, which can limit their career advancement and earning potential.
2. Insecure living arrangements: Temporary visa holders may face challenges in securing long-term housing or leases, as they are not considered permanent residents. This can lead to instability in their living arrangements and a lack of security for themselves and their families.
3. Limited access to healthcare: Depending on their immigration status, temporary visa holders may have limited access to healthcare services in the host country. They may face barriers in obtaining medical care or prescription medications, which can negatively impact their overall well-being.
4. Separation from family and friends: Temporary visa holders may be separated from their families and friends back in their home country due to the limitations of their immigration status. This can lead to emotional distress and feelings of isolation.
5. Uncertainty about future status: The uncertainty surrounding the duration of their stay in the host country can create anxiety and stress for temporary visa holders. They may worry about their future prospects and the potential disruption to their lives if their immigration status is revoked or expires.
6. Limited social interactions: Temporary visa holders may face challenges in establishing a social network in the host country due to their temporary status. This can lead to feelings of loneliness and disconnection from the local community.
