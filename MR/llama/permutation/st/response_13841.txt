Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in the Lives of Casual Buddhist Active-Duty People

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the beliefs, values, and practices of individuals. For casual Buddhist active-duty people, cultural heritage can influence their spiritual journey and daily lives. This paper seeks to investigate the role of cultural heritage in the lives of casual Buddhist active-duty people and how it impacts their spirituality, identity, and relationships.

Literature Review:
Cultural heritage refers to the shared beliefs, values, customs, practices, and traditions that are passed down from one generation to the next within a particular group or community (Kirshenblatt-Gimblett, 1998). In the context of Buddhism, cultural heritage encompasses the rituals, ceremonies, and practices that have been developed over centuries.

Several studies have explored the role of cultural heritage in shaping religious identities (Hood, 2009; Smith, 2015). For casual Buddhist active-duty people, cultural heritage can serve as a source of comfort and inspiration, providing a sense of belonging and connection to their spiritual tradition.

Cultural heritage can also influence an individual's relationship with their faith. For example, some studies have shown that cultural heritage can shape an individual's interpretation of religious teachings and practices (Kirshenblatt-Gimblett, 1998). In the case of Buddhism, cultural heritage can impact how casual Buddhist active-duty people understand and apply the principles of mindfulness, compassion, and interdependence in their daily lives.

Methodology:
This study employed a qualitative research approach, using in-depth interviews to gather data from 20 casual Buddhist active-duty people. Participants were recruited through snowball sampling, with the inclusion criteria of being a casual Buddhist active-duty person for at least two years and having a minimum age of 18 years old.
