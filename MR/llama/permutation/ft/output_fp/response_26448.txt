Promoting the educational success of mixed Asian heritage non-liberal students requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges and opportunities they face. Here are some strategies that educators, policymakers, and parents can use to support these students:

1. Acknowledge and embrace diversity: Mixed Asian heritage students may face discrimination or invisibility based on their identity. Educators should actively acknowledge and celebrate the diversity of their backgrounds and experiences.
2. Provide inclusive learning environments: Create classroom environments that are inclusive and respectful of all students' cultures and identities. Encourage open communication, active listening, and empathy among students.
3. Address microaggressions: Educate students, teachers, and parents about microaggressions and how they can impact mixed Asian heritage students. Provide strategies for recognizing and addressing these incidents in the classroom and beyond.
4. Foster a growth mindset: Encourage mixed Asian heritage students to embrace challenges and view failures as opportunities for growth. Foster resilience, perseverance, and self-efficacy through explicit teaching and modeling.
5. Provide culturally responsive teaching: Develop lessons that reflect the experiences and cultural backgrounds of mixed Asian heritage students. Incorporate authentic texts, images, and artifacts from diverse Asian cultures to promote cultural competence and understanding.
6. Support student leadership and representation: Encourage mixed Asian heritage students to take on leadership roles or participate in student organizations that reflect their identities. Provide opportunities for them to share their experiences and perspectives with the broader school community.
7. Address language barriers: For mixed Asian heritage students who may be non-proficient in English, provide additional support and resources to help them succeed academically. This could include bilingual education programs, language support groups, or accommodations for linguistically diverse students.
