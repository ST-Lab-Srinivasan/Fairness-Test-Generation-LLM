6. Collaborate with organizations: Partner with organizations that support individuals with genetic conditions to gain insight into their needs and preferences. This can also help you reach a wider audience and provide more comprehensive support.
7. Offer virtual options: Provide virtual recreational activities for individuals who may face barriers to in-person activities due to mobility or accessibility issues. This can include online classes, workshops, or social events.
8. Create accessible spaces: Design recreational spaces that are easily accessible and inclusive for individuals with genetic conditions. For example, provide wheelchair-accessible facilities, adjustable tables and chairs, and adequate lighting.
