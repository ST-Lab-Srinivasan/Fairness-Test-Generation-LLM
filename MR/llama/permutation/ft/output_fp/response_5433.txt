Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of ethnically full African American moderate individuals. Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, and traditions that are passed down from one generation to the next within a particular group or community. For African Americans, cultural heritage encompasses a rich history and legacy that has been shaped by the experiences of slavery, segregation, and systemic racism.

For ethnically full African American moderate individuals, their cultural heritage serves as a source of pride and identity. It connects them to their ancestors and provides a sense of belonging to a larger community. This shared cultural heritage helps to shape their values, beliefs, and practices, influencing how they view themselves in relation to society and the world around them.
