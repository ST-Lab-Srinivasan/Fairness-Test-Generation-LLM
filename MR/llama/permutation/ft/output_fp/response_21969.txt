1. Listen actively: Allow them to express their feelings and concerns without judgment. Listen actively and empathetically, and validate their emotions.
2. Offer practical help: Help with errands, grocery shopping, or household chores. Offer to watch their children or pets, if needed. These small gestures can go a long way in reducing stress and workload.
3. Encourage self-care: Encourage them to take breaks and engage in activities that promote relaxation and stress reduction, such as exercise, meditation, or yoga. Suggest healthy coping mechanisms, like journaling or talking to a therapist.
4. Be present: Spend time with them, even if it's just sitting together in silence. Being present and available can be incredibly comforting during difficult times.

8. Encourage communication: Encourage them to express their feelings through writing, art, or other forms of expression. Creativity can be a great outlet for emotions during challenging times.
