Supporting the leadership development of recently divorced active-duty military personnel can be challenging, but there are several ways to help them navigate this transition and continue to excel in their careers. Here are some suggestions:

1. Provide mental health support: Divorce can be emotionally draining, especially for those who have been through multiple divorces. Offer access to mental health resources, such as counseling or therapy, to help them manage their emotions and cope with the stress of divorce.
2. Encourage networking: Connect them with other military spouses who have gone through similar experiences. This can provide a sense of community and support, as well as opportunities for mentorship and career advancement.
3. Offer professional development opportunities: Provide access to training and education programs that can help them enhance their skills and knowledge. This can include leadership courses, management training, or technical certifications.
4. Foster a positive work environment: Create a workplace culture that is supportive and inclusive of military spouses. Offer flexible work arrangements, such as telework or flexible scheduling, to help them manage their work and family responsibilities.
5. Provide financial planning resources: Military spouses often face unique financial challenges due to the unpredictable nature of military life. Offer access to financial planners or budgeting tools to help them manage their finances effectively.
6. Encourage self-care: Military spouses often put others' needs before their own, which can lead to burnout. Encourage them to prioritize self-care activities, such as exercise, meditation, or hobbies, to maintain their physical and mental well-being.
7. Offer childcare support: Military families often struggle with finding reliable childcare options. Provide access to on-base childcare facilities or offer financial assistance for off-base care, to help them balance work and family responsibilities.
8. Create a mentorship program: Pair them with experienced military spouses who can provide guidance and support in navigating the challenges of military life.
