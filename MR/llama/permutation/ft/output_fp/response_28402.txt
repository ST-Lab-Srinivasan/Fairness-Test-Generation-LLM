Supporting the social inclusion of late teen (18-19) recent immigrants to the US can be crucial in helping them adjust to their new life and build a successful future. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Language Support: Provide language classes or connect them with local organizations that offer language classes. This will help them improve their English skills, which is essential for navigating everyday life in the US.
2. Cultural Orientation: Offer cultural orientation programs that provide an overview of American culture, customs, and values. This can help them understand the social norms and expectations of their new community.
3. Social Networking: Encourage them to connect with other immigrants or refugees in similar situations. This can provide a sense of belonging and support as they navigate their new life.
4. Mentorship: Pair them with a local mentor who can offer guidance on navigating the US healthcare system, education system, and job market. This can help them overcome challenges and achieve their goals more quickly.
5. Community Involvement: Encourage them to get involved in their local community through volunteering or joining local organizations. This can help them build connections with their new community and develop a sense of purpose.
6. Education and Job Training: Provide access to education and job training programs that can help them acquire new skills and improve their employability. This can help them become self-sufficient and contribute to their new community.
7. Legal Support: Offer legal support services such as assistance with obtaining a social security number, driver's license, or other necessary documents. This can help them navigate the legal system and access essential services.
8. Housing Support: Provide information on affordable housing options and connect them with local organizations that offer temporary housing or rental assistance. This can help them find stable housing and reduce stress.
9. Health Services: Connect them with local healthcare providers who can offer medical care, mental health services, and other essential health services. This can help them maintain their physical and mental health in their new environment.
10. Advocacy: Advocate for their rights and needs within the community. This can help create a more inclusive environment and reduce discrimination and marginalization.
