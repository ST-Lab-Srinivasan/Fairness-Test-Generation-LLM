Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of Native and recent immigrant Hindus in the United States. Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, traditions, and practices that are passed down from one generation to the next within a particular community or society. For Hindu individuals who have recently immigrated to the US or who are Native American Hindus, their cultural heritage can serve as a source of comfort, strength, and identity.
