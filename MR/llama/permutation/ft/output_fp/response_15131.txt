Supporting the economic empowerment of lower-class Native natural-born US citizens requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural inequalities they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and job training programs: Provide access to quality education, vocational training, and job readiness programs to equip individuals with the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market.
2. Microfinance initiatives: Offer microloans, savings accounts, and other financial services to help individuals start or expand their own businesses, which can help them achieve economic mobility.
3. Entrepreneurship support: Provide resources, mentorship, and networking opportunities to help individuals start and grow their own businesses, which can create jobs and drive economic growth in Native American communities.
4. Apprenticeships and job placement programs: Connect lower-class Native Americans with apprenticeship and job placement programs that provide on-the-job training and employment opportunities in high-demand fields such as construction, healthcare, and technology.
5. Addressing systemic barriers: Advocate for policy changes that address systemic barriers to economic empowerment, such as discrimination, lack of access to capital, and limited access to education and job training.
6. Community development initiatives: Support community-based initiatives that promote economic development and entrepreneurship in Native American communities, such as cooperatives, incubators, and accelerators.
7. Addressing the digital divide: Provide access to technology and internet connectivity to help lower-class Native Americans participate in the digital economy and access new job opportunities.
8. Building partnerships: Foster partnerships between Native American communities, government agencies, non-profits, and private sector organizations to leverage resources and expertise for economic development.
9. Supporting local economies: Encourage purchasing from and investing in local Native American businesses to help build wealth and create jobs within the community.
