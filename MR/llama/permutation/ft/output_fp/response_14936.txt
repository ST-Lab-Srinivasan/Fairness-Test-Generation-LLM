Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of immigrants, including recent immigrants to the United States. Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, traditions, and practices that are passed down from one generation to the next within a particular group or society. For immigrants, their cultural heritage can serve as a source of comfort and familiarity in a new and often unfamiliar environment. It can also influence how they perceive themselves and their place in the world, as well as their relationships with others.

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a particular group or community. For immigrants, maintaining their cultural heritage can help them feel connected to their country of origin or ancestry, even as they adapt to life in a new country.
