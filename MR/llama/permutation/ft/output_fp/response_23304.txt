Creating more inclusive community events for Nigerian young adults (ages 20s) requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions to help you plan successful and inclusive events:

1. Understand the needs and preferences of your target audience: Conduct research to learn about the interests, hobbies, and cultural practices of young adult Nigerians. This will help you tailor your events to meet their specific needs and preferences.
2. Foster intergenerational connections: Include older and younger generations in the planning process to create a sense of shared ownership and responsibility for the event. This can help bridge the gap between different age groups and create a more inclusive environment.
3. Ensure accessibility: Provide accommodations for people with disabilities, such as wheelchair ramps, accessible restrooms, and sign language interpretation. This will help ensure that everyone can participate fully in the event.
4. Promote diversity and inclusion: Use diverse marketing channels to reach a broad audience, including social media platforms popular among young adult Nigerians, print ads in local newspapers, and outreach to community organizations that serve underrepresented groups.
5. Offer culturally relevant programming: Incorporate activities and entertainment that reflect the cultural heritage of young adult Nigerians, such as traditional music, dance, or food. This can help create a sense of belonging and connection to their cultural identity.
6. Encourage participation: Design events that encourage active participation from attendees, such as workshops, brainstorming sessions, or volunteer opportunities. This will help create a sense of ownership and investment in the event's success.

Provide safe spaces for marginalized communities: Create separate spaces or events within the larger community event that cater specifically to the needs of marginalized groups, such as LGBTQ+ individuals or people living with mental health conditions. This can help ensure their safety and well-being while participating in the event.
8. Collaborate with local organizations: Partner with local organizations that serve young adult Nigerians, such as community centers, youth groups, or non-profits. This can help leverage resources, expertise, and networks to create a more successful and inclusive event.
