Volunteerism plays a significant role in supporting very old (90s+) non-libertarian communities, particularly in the following ways:

1. Social Support: Volunteers can provide social support to elderly individuals who may be experiencing loneliness or isolation. They can visit them, engage in conversations, and help them stay connected with their community.
2. Practical Assistance: Volunteers can assist with practical tasks such as grocery shopping, meal preparation, and light housekeeping. This can help alleviate some of the burden on family members who may be responsible for providing care.
3. Emotional Support: Volunteers can provide emotional support by listening to elderly individuals' concerns and offering words of encouragement. They can also help them cope with challenges such as declining health, loss of loved ones, or feelings of isolation.
4. Transportation: Volunteers can offer transportation services to elderly individuals who may no longer be able to drive, providing them with access to essential services and activities.
5. Cultural Enrichment: Volunteers can help preserve cultural heritage by engaging in activities such as teaching traditional dances, playing musical instruments, or sharing stories from their communities' histories.
6. Healthcare Support: Volunteers can assist with health-related tasks such as monitoring vital signs, administering medication, and providing first aid. They can also help coordinate medical appointments and transportation.
7. Legal Assistance: Volunteers can provide legal assistance by helping elderly individuals navigate complex legal systems, such as estate planning, wills, and powers of attorney.
8. Home Maintenance: Volunteers can assist with home maintenance tasks such as yard work, snow shoveling, and minor repairs to help elderly individuals maintain their independence.
9. Personal Care: Volunteers can provide personal care services such as bathing, dressing, and grooming to help elderly individuals maintain their dignity and independence.
10. Advocacy: Volunteers can advocate for the rights and interests of elderly individuals within their communities, ensuring that their voices are heard and their needs are met.
