Ensuring that the voices of physically able-bodied Nigerians are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting inclusivity and addressing the marginalization of this group. Here are some strategies that can be employed to achieve this:

1. Inclusive policy-making: Ensure that policies and laws are designed with the needs and perspectives of physically able-bodied Nigerians in mind. This can be achieved by involving them in the policy-making process through mechanisms such as consultations, surveys, and focus groups.
2. Representation in leadership positions: Ensure that physically able-bodied Nigerians are represented in decision-making positions of power, such as in government, civil society organizations, and private sector companies. This can be achieved by nominating and electing them to these positions through fair and transparent processes.
3. Education and awareness: Educate the broader community about the experiences and challenges faced by physically able-bodied Nigerians, and raise awareness about the importance of inclusivity and accessibility in decision-making processes. This can be achieved through public campaigns, workshops, and training programs.
4. Access to technology: Provide physically able-bodied Nigerians with access to assistive technologies such as mobility aids, communication devices, and other tools that can help them participate fully in decision-making processes.
5. Advocacy and activism: Empower physically able-bodied Nigerians to advocate for their rights and interests through activism and advocacy initiatives. This can be achieved by providing them with training and resources, and supporting them to organize and mobilize around issues that affect them.
6. Collaboration with disability organizations: Work closely with organizations that represent the interests of people with disabilities to ensure that their voices are heard in decision-making processes. These organizations can provide valuable support and expertise in advocating for the rights and interests of physically able-bodied Nigerians.
7. Access to legal aid: Ensure that physically able-bodied Nigerians have access to legal aid and support, which can help them navigate the legal system and advocate for their rights more effectively.
