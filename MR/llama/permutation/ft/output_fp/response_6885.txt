Economic empowerment of deep in poverty, ethnically full African Americans requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the interconnected issues of education, employment, health, housing, and criminal justice. Here are some strategies to support economic empowerment:

1. Education and job training programs: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that equip individuals with the skills and knowledge needed to secure better-paying jobs. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and entrepreneurship programs.
2. Microfinance and small business loans: Offer microfinance and small business loan programs to help individuals start or grow their own businesses. This can provide a pathway to economic mobility and create jobs in the community.
3. Health and wellness initiatives: Address health disparities by providing access to affordable healthcare, nutrition education, and mental health services. A healthier population is more likely to be productive and successful in their economic endeavors.
4. Housing and homeownership programs: Provide financial assistance for down payments, mortgage assistance, and housing counseling to help individuals purchase or repair homes in safe and affordable neighborhoods. This can help build wealth and create stable living conditions.
5. Criminal justice reform: Address the systemic issues that lead to disproportionate representation of African Americans in the criminal justice system. This includes policing reforms, bail reform, and alternatives to incarceration. Reducing the number of individuals involved in the criminal justice system can help reduce poverty and increase economic mobility.

Community development investments: Invest in community development initiatives that create jobs, improve infrastructure, and provide access to resources such as healthcare, education, and social services. This can help build vibrant and sustainable communities that support economic growth and prosperity.
7. Tax policies and credits: Implement tax policies and credits that benefit low-income individuals and families, including the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC), Child Tax Credit, and other forms of targeted assistance. These policies can help reduce poverty and increase economic mobility.
