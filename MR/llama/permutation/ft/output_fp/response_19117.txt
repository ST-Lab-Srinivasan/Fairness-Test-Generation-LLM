Promoting the financial stability of older adults, particularly those who are native Chinese speakers and aged over 90, is crucial to ensure their well-being and quality of life. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to promote financial stability in this population:

1. Financial Literacy Programs: Implement financial literacy programs specifically designed for older adults. These programs should cover basic financial concepts, such as budgeting, saving, and investing, as well as common financial pitfalls and how to avoid them.
2. Regular Check-ins: Conduct regular check-ins with the older adults to assess their financial situation, provide financial advice, and help them create a comprehensive financial plan tailored to their needs.
3. Assisted Living Facilities: Encourage or facilitate the transition of older adults into assisted living facilities, which can provide a more stable living environment and reduce the burden on family caregivers. These facilities often offer financial planning services and resources specifically designed for older adults.
4. Government Benefits: Help older adults apply for and access government benefits such as Social Security, pension plans, and Medicaid, which can provide a crucial source of income and support.
5. Community-Based Services: Provide community-based services that offer financial planning, budgeting, and money management assistance to older adults. These services can help them manage their finances more effectively and promote financial stability.
6. Tax Counseling: Offer tax counseling services specifically designed for older adults, which can help them navigate complex tax issues and ensure they are taking advantage of all available tax credits and deductions.
7. Investment Planning: Provide investment planning services tailored to the financial goals and risk tolerance of older adults. This can help them grow their savings and prepare for future financial needs.
8. Estate Planning: Encourage older adults to develop an estate plan, which can help them manage their assets, minimize taxes, and ensure that their wishes are carried out after their death.
