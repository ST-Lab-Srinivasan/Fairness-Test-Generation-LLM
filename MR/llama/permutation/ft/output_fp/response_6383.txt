Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for physically able-bodied individuals requires thoughtful planning and consideration of the needs of all participants. Here are some suggestions on how to make cultural celebrations more inclusive:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the venue and activities are accessible to people with disabilities. This can include providing wheelchair access, Braille signage, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
2. Inclusive language: Use inclusive language in all communication materials, avoiding terms that may be offensive or exclusive to certain groups. For example, use "people with disabilities" instead of "disabled individuals."
3. Diverse representation: Ensure that the cultural celebration includes diverse representation of people from different backgrounds and abilities. This can include inviting guests from different cultures, ages, and abilities to participate in the celebration.
4. Adaptive activities: Provide adaptive activities that cater to the needs of individuals with disabilities. For example, provide wheelchair-accessible games or activities that are easy for individuals with mobility impairments to participate in.
5. Sign language interpretation: Provide sign language interpretation for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing. This can be done through live interpretation or pre-recorded videos with sign language.
6. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by reducing sensory stimuli, such as loud noises or bright lights, that may be overwhelming for individuals with sensory processing disorders.
7. Accommodations for different dietary needs: Provide accommodations for individuals with different dietary needs, such as gluten-free or vegan options.
8. Collaboration with disability organizations: Collaborate with disability organizations to ensure that the cultural celebration is inclusive and meets the needs of individuals with disabilities.
9. Education and awareness: Provide education and awareness about disability culture and etiquette, and encourage all participants to be respectful and considerate of others' needs.
10. Feedback mechanism: Establish a feedback mechanism for individuals with disabilities to provide input on how the cultural celebration can be improved for future events.
