Supporting the social inclusion of early teenagers in Nigeria can be achieved through various strategies that promote their well-being, education, and community engagement. Here are some ways to support the social inclusion of early teenagers in Nigeria:

1. Provide Access to Quality Education: Ensure that early teenagers have access to quality education, including formal schooling and vocational training. This will help them develop essential skills, build confidence, and increase their opportunities for future success.
2. Promote Healthy Lifestyles: Encourage early teenagers to adopt healthy lifestyle habits, such as regular exercise, balanced diets, and adequate sleep. This will help them maintain good physical and mental health, which is essential for social inclusion.
3. Foster Community Engagement: Encourage early teenagers to participate in community activities, such as volunteering, sports, or cultural events. This will help them develop a sense of belonging and connection to their communities, which is critical for social inclusion.
4. Address Discrimination and Bullying: Implement strategies to address discrimination and bullying in schools and communities. Early teenagers who experience discrimination or bullying are more likely to feel isolated and excluded, so it's important to create safe and inclusive environments for all.
5. Encourage Leadership and Entrepreneurship: Provide opportunities for early teenagers to develop leadership and entrepreneurial skills through mentorship programs, workshops, or business training. This will help them build confidence, develop problem-solving skills, and increase their employability.
6. Support Mental Health: Address mental health concerns among early teenagers by providing access to counseling services and promoting positive coping mechanisms. Good mental health is essential for social inclusion, as it can help early teenagers manage stress, build relationships, and achieve their goals.
7. Create Safe Spaces: Establish safe spaces for early teenagers to connect with peers and mentors. These spaces can be physical or virtual, and should provide a supportive environment where they can share experiences, ask questions, and receive guidance.
8. Promote Cultural Exchange: Organize cultural exchange programs that bring together early teenagers from different background
