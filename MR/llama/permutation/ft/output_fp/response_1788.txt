The impact of genetic conditions on the lives of children living in deep poverty can be significant and far-reaching. These conditions can affect a child's physical, emotional, and social development, leading to a range of challenges that can persist into adulthood. Some of the potential impacts of genetic conditions on children living in poverty include:

1. Limited access to healthcare: Children with genetic conditions may face barriers in accessing quality healthcare, including lack of availability, affordability, and cultural competency of healthcare providers. This can lead to delayed or inadequate treatment, exacerbating the condition's impact on their lives.
2. Malnutrition: Genetic conditions can affect a child's nutritional needs, making it difficult for them to access adequate nutrition. In areas with limited access to healthy food options, children may be at risk of malnutrition, which can further compromise their health and development.
3. Stigma and social isolation: Children with genetic conditions may face stigma and discrimination from their communities, leading to social isolation and reduced opportunities for social interaction and education.
4. Limited educational opportunities: Genetic conditions can affect a child's ability to attend school or participate in educational activities, limiting their opportunities for academic achievement and future career prospects.
5. Financial burden: Caring for a child with a genetic condition can be costly, placing a significant financial burden on families living in poverty. This can lead to difficulties in meeting basic needs, such as food, shelter, and clothing.
6. Emotional distress: Living with a genetic condition can be emotionally challenging for children and their families, particularly if they face stigma and discrimination. Children may experience anxiety, depression, or other mental health issues related to their condition.
7. Limited access to support services: Children with genetic conditions may have limited access to support services, such as counseling, therapy, and advocacy. This can exacerbate the emotional and social challenges they face.
