Promoting the artistic talents of beginner Chinese speakers in their 90s or older requires a thoughtful and supportive approach. Here are some suggestions:

1. Create a welcoming environment: Ensure that the learning space is comfortable, well-lit, and free from distractions. Provide appropriate seating and equipment for the learners.
2. Use age-appropriate materials: Select language learning materials that are designed specifically for older adults, with simple vocabulary and grammar explanations, and engaging images or videos.
3. Focus on pronunciation: Older adults may have difficulty with the nuances of Chinese pronunciation. Practice pronunciation exercises regularly to help them improve their skills.
4. Encourage storytelling: Encourage learners to share stories from their lives, using both Chinese and their native language. This can help them practice vocabulary and grammar, while also fostering a sense of community.
5. Use music and art: Incorporate music and art into the learning process to make it more engaging and enjoyable for older adults. For example, play traditional Chinese music or use visual aids to help learn vocabulary words.
6. Offer one-on-one instruction: Older adults may prefer or benefit from one-on-one instruction due to hearing or cognitive limitations. Consider pairing them with a dedicated teacher or tutor for more personalized attention.
7. Provide opportunities for cultural exchange: Arrange cultural events or outings that allow older adults to interact with others who share their interests and backgrounds. This can help foster a sense of community and mutual support.
8. Use technology wisely: Utilize digital tools, such as language learning apps or online courses, to supplement traditional classroom instruction. These resources can provide additional practice opportunities and feedback for older adults who may have difficulty attending classes due to mobility issues.
