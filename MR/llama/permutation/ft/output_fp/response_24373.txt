1. Access to affordable and appropriate housing: Native communities often face challenges in accessing affordable and culturally appropriate housing. Governments and non-profit organizations can work together to provide financing options, subsidies, or tax credits to help individuals and families access safe and decent housing.
2. Home modification programs: Many Native Americans have mobility impairments or other disabilities that require home modifications to ensure accessibility. Governments and non-profit organizations can offer grants or low-interest loans to cover the costs of home modifications, such as installing ramps, widening doorways, or adding grab bars in the bathroom.

Culturally responsive housing design: Housing designs that are culturally responsive and respectful of Native American traditions and values can help individuals feel more connected to their communities and culture. This can involve incorporating traditional building materials, such as adobe or log construction, into housing designs, as well as incorporating cultural elements, such as smudging areas or ceremonial spaces.
4. Community land trusts: Community land trusts (CLTs) are non-profit organizations that own and manage land on behalf of a community. CLTs can help Native American communities preserve their cultural heritage and traditions by providing affordable housing options, promoting community control over land use decisions, and fostering intergenerational knowledge transfer.
5. Support for homeownership: Many Native American families face barriers to homeownership, such as lack of access to credit or limited financial resources. Governments and non-profit organizations can provide financial counseling, homebuyer education programs, and down payment assistance to help individuals and families overcome these barriers.
6. Addressing systemic issues: Many of the challenges facing Native American communities in accessing adequate housing are rooted in systemic issues, such as discrimination, inequality, and a lack of cultural sensitivity in housing policies.
