Promoting the cultural preservation of gender-nonconforming Native natural-born US citizen communities requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the social, political, and economic marginalization of these communities. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Centering Community Voices: It is essential to amplify the voices and stories of gender-nonconforming Native individuals and their communities. This involves creating spaces for them to share their experiences, perspectives, and cultural practices without interference or exploitation. Listening to their narratives and acknowledging their expertise can help build trust and legitimacy.
2. Capacity Building: Many gender-nonconforming Native individuals face barriers in accessing education, healthcare, and economic opportunities. Building capacity through training programs, mentorship, and community development initiatives can help address these disparities and promote cultural preservation.
3. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocating for policies that recognize and protect the rights of gender-nonconforming Native individuals is crucial. This involves working with lawmakers to pass legislation that addresses issues such as discrimination, violence, and unequal access to resources.
4. Cultural Resurgence: Encouraging cultural resurgence through revitalization of traditional practices, language, and art can help gender-nonconforming Native individuals reconnect with their heritage and identity. This can also promote intergenerational knowledge transfer and strengthen community bonds.
5. Alliance Building: Fostering alliances between gender-nonconforming Native communities, other marginalized groups, and mainstream organizations can help amplify their voices and create a united front against oppression. This involves building relationships based on mutual respect, trust, and solidarity.
6. Cultural Exchange: Facilitating cultural exchange programs between gender-nonconforming Native communities and other marginalized groups can help promote cross-cultural understanding, empathy, and cooperation. This can also help break down stereotypes and challenge dominant narratives.
