Creating more inclusive public policies for recently retired veterans, long-term undocumented individuals, and other marginalized groups requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the social, economic, and political barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive policies:

1. Listen to their stories: Understanding the experiences and challenges faced by these groups is crucial for creating effective policies. Conduct research, hold focus groups, and gather stories from these communities to identify their needs and priorities.
2. Address systemic barriers: Many of the challenges faced by marginalized communities are rooted in systemic issues such as discrimination, unequal access to education and job opportunities, and lack of political representation. Policies must address these underlying issues to create a more equitable society.
3. Provide pathways to citizenship: Undocumented individuals often face barriers to accessing basic rights and services due to their immigration status. Creating clear paths to citizenship or legal residency can help address this issue and provide greater access to social services, education, and job opportunities.
4. Invest in education and job training: Many marginalized communities lack access to quality education and job training programs. Governments can invest in these areas to provide greater opportunities for economic mobility and stability.
5. Promote community engagement and participation: Creating inclusive policies requires active engagement with marginalized communities. This can involve establishing advisory committees, participating in community events, and providing opportunities for input and feedback.
6. Address housing affordability: Many marginalized communities face significant challenges in affording safe and decent housing. Governments can address this issue by investing in affordable housing programs, subsidies, and rent control measures.
7. Improve healthcare access: Marginalized communities often face barriers to accessing quality healthcare due to systemic issues such as discrimination, lack of cultural competency, and unequal access to healthcare services. Policies must address these issues to ensure equitable access to healthcare.
