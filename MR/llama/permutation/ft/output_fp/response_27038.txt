Addressing the specific needs of Native American recent immigrants to the US in education requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique cultural, social, and historical contexts. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers to incorporate traditional knowledge and practices into their teaching methods, such as using indigenous languages, stories, and art in the classroom. This approach can help Native students feel more connected to their culture and community while also improving academic outcomes.
2. Support for language revitalization: Many Native American communities are working to preserve their languages, which are at risk of disappearing. Educational institutions can provide resources and support for language revitalization efforts, such as hiring language instructors or creating language immersion programs.
3. Culturally relevant curriculum: Develop curricula that reflect the experiences and perspectives of Native American communities, including their histories, traditions, and contributions to society. This can help students feel seen and valued in the educational system.
4. Indigenous mentorship programs: Pair Native American students with indigenous mentors who can provide guidance and support throughout their academic journey. These mentors can serve as role models and connect students with their cultural heritage.
5. Community partnerships: Build partnerships between educational institutions and Native American communities to create a more inclusive and culturally responsive learning environment. This can involve incorporating traditional knowledge and practices into curricula, hosting cultural events and workshops, and providing opportunities for community members to participate in decision-making processes.
6. Addressing systemic barriers: Educational institutions must acknowledge and address systemic barriers that have historically hindered the educational attainment of Native American students, such as inadequate funding, lack of resources, and discrimination. This can involve advocating for policy changes and working to dismantle structural impediments to success.
7. Cultural competency training: Provide cultural competency training for educators to help them understand the unique needs and experiences of Native American students.
