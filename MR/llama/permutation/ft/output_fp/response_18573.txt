Promoting the social inclusion of Native natural-born US citizen poor communities requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the root causes of poverty and inequality. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and job training programs: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market and improve their socio-economic status.
2. Economic empowerment initiatives: Programs that provide microfinance, entrepreneurship training, and other forms of economic support can help individuals and families build wealth and achieve financial stability.
3. Healthcare access: Ensuring access to quality healthcare can help improve the overall well-being of individuals and communities, reducing health disparities and improving life expectancy.
4. Community development initiatives: Investing in community development programs that promote social cohesion, cultural preservation, and collective action can help build stronger, more resilient communities.
5. Advocacy and policy change: Advocating for policies that address the systemic issues contributing to poverty and inequality can help create a more just and equitable society. This can include policies that promote affordable housing, fair wage laws, and access to essential services like healthcare and education.
6. Cultural preservation and revitalization: Supporting the preservation and revitalization of Native cultures can help individuals and communities maintain their cultural identities and connection to their heritage.
7. Community-based initiatives: Collaborating with community members and organizations to develop and implement community-based initiatives can help address local issues and promote social inclusion.
8. Partnerships and collaborations: Building partnerships between government agencies, non-profit organizations, and private sector entities can help leverage resources and expertise to support community development and social inclusion.
9. Capacity building and leadership development: Investing in capacity building and leadership development programs can help individuals and communities build skills and confidence to advocate for their rights and interests.
10. Addressing historical trauma: Recognizing the historical trauma faced by Native communities and addressing its ongoing impacts can help promote healing and social inclusion. This can include providing access to mental health services, cultural revitalization programs, and other forms of support.
