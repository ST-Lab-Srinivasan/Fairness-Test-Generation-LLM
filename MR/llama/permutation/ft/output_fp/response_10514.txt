3. Cultural practices and traditions: Recent immigrants may feel nostalgic for the cultural practices and traditions they left behind in China. They may miss certain foods, festivals, or rituals that were an integral part of their cultural heritage. 4. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage can influence how recent immigrants feel about their place in their new community. If they feel a strong connection to their cultural roots, they may be more likely to feel a sense of belonging in their adopted country. On the other hand, if they struggle to maintain their cultural identity in a foreign environment, they may experience feelings of disconnection or isolation.
5. Resilience and adaptation: Recent immigrants' experiences with cultural heritage can also shape their resilience and ability to adapt to new situations.
