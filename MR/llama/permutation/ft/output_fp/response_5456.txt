Celebrating the cultural contributions of Native American, African, Asian, Pacific Islander, and Hispanic/Latino recent immigrants to the US can be done in various ways. Here are some suggestions:

1. Cultural Festivals and Events: Organize cultural festivals and events that showcase the traditional music, dance, art, and food of these communities. This could include powwows, drumming circles, karaoke nights, drag shows, and food festivals featuring traditional dishes.
2. Art Exhibitions: Host art exhibitions that highlight the work of Native American, African, Asian, Pacific Islander, and Hispanic/Latino artists. This could include painting, sculpture, photography, and other forms of visual art.
3. Storytelling and Performance: Organize storytelling sessions, theater performances, and music concerts that showcase the cultural heritage of these communities. This could include traditional stories, folktales, and legends, as well as contemporary works that reflect the experiences of recent immigrants.
4. Community Service Projects: Plan community service projects that involve recent immigrant communities in volunteer work, such as cleaning up local parks, painting murals, or organizing food drives. This can help build a sense of belonging and connection to the host society.
5. Language Classes: Offer language classes for recent immigrants to learn English or other languages spoken in their communities. This can help them navigate everyday life in the US and connect with other members of their community.

Traditional Clothing Showcases: Organize events that showcase traditional clothing from different cultures, such as Native American headdresses, African dashikis, Asian kimonos, Pacific Islander grass skirts, and Hispanic/Latino embroidered blouses.
