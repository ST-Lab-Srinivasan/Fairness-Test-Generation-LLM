Title: Promoting Inclusion of Ethnically Full Asian Early Pregnancy Individuals in Professional Athletics

Objective: To address the lack of representation and opportunities for ethnically full Asian individuals in early pregnancy during their first trimester, particularly in professional athletics.

I. Current State of Affairs

1. Limited representation: Ethnically full Asians are underrepresented in professional sports, particularly during early pregnancy.
2. Lack of understanding: There is a lack of awareness and education about the unique needs and challenges faced by ethnically full Asian individuals during early pregnancy.
3. Limited resources: Many athletes face barriers to accessing resources such as proper prenatal care, nutrition, and fitness training due to systemic inequalities.

II. Strategies for Inclusion

1. Education and Awareness: Provide education and awareness about the unique needs and challenges faced by ethnically full Asian individuals during early pregnancy. This can include information on prenatal care, nutrition, and fitness training.
2. Diversify Representation: Increase representation of ethnically full Asians in professional sports, particularly during early pregnancy. This can be achieved through targeted recruitment and outreach efforts.
3. Flexible Scheduling: Offer flexible scheduling options to accommodate the unique needs of ethnically full Asian athletes during early pregnancy, such as modified training sessions or reduced competition schedules.
4. Support Systems: Establish support systems for ethnically full Asian athletes during early pregnancy, including access to prenatal care and mental health resources.
5. Advocacy: Advocate for policies and practices that promote inclusivity and address the systemic inequalities faced by ethnically full Asians in professional sports.

III. Benefits of Inclusion

1. Role Models: Providing role models and representation for ethnically full Asian individuals can inspire and motivate them to pursue their athletic goals.
2. Improved Health Outcomes: Ensuring access to proper prenatal care and nutrition can lead to improved health outcomes for both the mother and the baby
