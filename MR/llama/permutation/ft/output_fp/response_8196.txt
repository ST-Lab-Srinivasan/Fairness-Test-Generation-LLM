Ensuring that the educational needs of Native American students, including those who are recent immigrants to the US, are met requires a comprehensive and culturally responsive approach. Here are some strategies that schools and educators can consider:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers in culturally responsive teaching practices, which involve understanding and valuing the cultural backgrounds of students, and creating learning environments that promote cultural competence.
2. Language support: Provide language support services for Native American students who may have limited proficiency in English, such as bilingual education programs or language interpretation services.
3. Cultural sensitivity training: Offer training for teachers and staff on cultural sensitivity and how to work with Native American students, including understanding their cultural practices and traditions.
4. Incorporating Native American culture into the curriculum: Incorporate Native American history, culture, and traditions into the curriculum to help students connect with their heritage and feel more engaged in the learning process.
5. Partnerships with Native American communities: Develop partnerships with local Native American communities to provide opportunities for student internships, job shadowing, and cultural immersion experiences.
6. Recognition of Native American identity: Respect and recognize the Native American identity of students, including their right to self-identify and express their cultural backgrounds.
7. Addressing systemic barriers: Address systemic barriers that may impact the educational outcomes of Native American students, such as inadequate funding, lack of resources, and discrimination.
8. Community involvement: Encourage community involvement in schools by inviting elders and other community members to share their knowledge and cultural practices with students.
9. Cultural celebrations: Celebrate Native American cultural events and traditions in the school, such as powwows, drumming circles, and traditional foods.
10. Professional development: Provide ongoing professional development opportunities for teachers and staff to learn about Native American cultures and how to better serve this population.
11. Creating a safe space: Create a safe and inclusive learning environment that respects and values the cultural backgrounds of all students, including those from Native American communities.
