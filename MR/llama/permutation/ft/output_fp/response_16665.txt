Supporting the mental health of Not Disabled Native Recent Immigrants (NDNRI) to the US can be crucial in helping them navigate the challenges of adjusting to a new culture, language, and society. Here are some ways to support their mental health:

1. Cultural Humility: Understand and respect the cultural beliefs, values, and practices of NDNRI individuals. Avoid making assumptions or stereotypes based on their cultural background.
2. Access to Mental Health Services: Ensure that NDNRI individuals have access to mental health services tailored to their specific needs. This may involve partnering with organizations that provide culturally sensitive mental health services.
3. Community Support: Create a supportive community for NDNRI individuals by connecting them with other immigrants and refugees who share similar experiences. Encourage open dialogue, active listening, and empathy within the group.
4. Language Support: Provide language classes or resources to help NDNRI individuals improve their English skills, which can help reduce feelings of isolation and improve mental health outcomes.
5. Traditional Healing Practices: Respect and incorporate traditional healing practices from NDNRI cultures into mental health services. This may involve partnering with traditional healers or integrating their practices into therapy sessions.
6. Education and Awareness: Educate the broader community about the unique challenges faced by NDNRI individuals and the importance of cultural humility in supporting their mental health. Organize awareness events, workshops, or campaigns to raise awareness and promote understanding.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that support the mental health needs of NDNRI individuals, such as culturally sensitive mental health services, interpreter services, and language support in healthcare settings.
8. Self-Care: Encourage NDNRI individuals to prioritize self-care by engaging in activities that promote relaxation, stress reduction, and overall well-being, such as yoga, meditation, or spending time with loved ones.
