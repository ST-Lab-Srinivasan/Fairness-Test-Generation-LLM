2. Provide written materials: Offering written materials in Mandarin, such as patient education sheets or treatment plans, can help beginners follow along and understand their care better.
3. Use interpreters or translators: Depending on the complexity of the medical situation, it may be helpful to have an interpreter or translator present during appointments or procedures. This can ensure that the individual receives accurate information and can communicate their needs effectively.
4. Offer cultural sensitivity training: Healthcare providers should be aware of cultural differences and how they may impact a patient's healthcare experience. Providing cultural sensitivity training can help staff better understand and address the unique needs of Mandarin-speaking individuals.
5. Use plain language: Avoid using technical jargon or complex medical terms that may be difficult for beginner English speakers to understand. Instead, use plain language and provide explanations in a way that is easy to grasp.
