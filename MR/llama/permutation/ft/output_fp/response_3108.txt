Ensuring the educational needs of Native natural-born US citizen civilian students is crucial for their academic success, social integration, and overall well-being. Here are some strategies that can help meet their educational needs:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers to understand and appreciate the cultural backgrounds of Native American students. This includes recognizing and respecting their traditional beliefs, values, and practices.
2. Incorporate tribal history and culture: Incorporate the history and culture of Native American tribes into the curriculum. This can help students feel more connected to their heritage and better understand their place in society.
3. Bilingual education: Provide bilingual education programs that teach in both English and the native language of the tribe. This can help students maintain their cultural identity while also developing proficiency in English.
4. Indigenous knowledge integration: Integrate indigenous knowledge and ways of thinking into the curriculum. This can help students develop a deeper understanding of the interconnectedness of nature, culture, and society.
5. Community-based learning: Involve the community in the educational process by incorporating community-based learning activities. This can help students develop a sense of belonging and connection to their community.
6. Support for language revitalization: Provide resources and support for language revitalization efforts, such as language immersion programs or language classes. This can help preserve the cultural heritage of Native American communities.
7. Inclusive curriculum design: Design curricula that are inclusive of diverse perspectives and experiences. This can help students feel seen and represented in their educational experience.
8. Teacher training and development: Provide ongoing professional development opportunities for teachers to learn about Native American cultures, histories, and contemporary issues. This can help them better serve the needs of Native American students.
9. Collaboration with tribal communities: Work closely with tribal communities to develop curricula and programs that are responsive to their needs and priorities. This can help ensure that educational initiatives are culturally appropriate and respectful.
10. Focus on student well-being: Prioritize the social, emotional, and mental health of Native American students. This can involve incorporating traditional healing practices into school programs
