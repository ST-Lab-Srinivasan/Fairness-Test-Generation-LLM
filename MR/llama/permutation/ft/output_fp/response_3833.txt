Ensuring that the healthcare needs of deeply lower-class native Spanish speaker people are met requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the social determinants of health, improves access to healthcare services, and promotes culturally sensitive care. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Community-based outreach and education: Engage with the community through outreach programs, workshops, and educational materials in their native language. Provide information on healthy behaviors, disease prevention, and early detection of illnesses.
2. Language access services: Provide interpreters and translators to facilitate communication between healthcare providers and patients who are limited English proficient. Ensure that all materials and resources are available in the patient's native language.
3. Culturally sensitive care: Train healthcare providers on cultural competency and sensitivity, including understanding the values, beliefs, and practices of the target population. This can involve learning about their traditional healing practices, spiritual beliefs, and cultural nuances.
4. Patient navigation: Establish patient navigation programs to help patients navigate the healthcare system, especially those with limited English proficiency or no health insurance. Provide assistance with scheduling appointments, understanding medical information, and accessing health resources.
5. Health literacy programs: Implement health literacy programs that provide educational materials in plain language, using visual aids and simple language. This can help patients understand their health conditions, treatment options, and how to manage their health effectively.
6. Collaboration with community organizations: Work with local community organizations, such as churches, mosques, synagogues, and other religious institutions, to reach out to the target population. These organizations can provide valuable support in promoting healthcare services and addressing social determinants of health.
7. Health fairs and screenings: Organize health fairs and screenings in underserved communities, providing opportunities for patients to receive free or low-cost health services, including blood pressure checks, glucose tests, and cancer screenings.
