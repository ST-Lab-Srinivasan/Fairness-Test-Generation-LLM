Supporting the community engagement of deeply lower-class temporary visa holder individuals requires a multifaceted approach that addresses their social, economic, and cultural needs. Here are some strategies that organizations and communities can consider:

2. Legal Support: Many temporary visa holders face legal barriers that limit their ability to work or access basic services. Providing legal support and advocacy can help them navigate the complex immigration system and access their rights.
3. Health and Wellness Services: Ensure that these individuals have access to quality healthcare and wellness services, including mental health support. This can help address the unique challenges they face and improve their overall well-being.
4. Community Building: Create opportunities for temporary visa holders to connect with each other and build community. This can include organizing events, workshops, and cultural activities that promote socialization and collaboration.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that support the rights and well-being of temporary visa holders, including those in deeply lower-class positions. This can involve working with policymakers, community leaders, and other stakeholders to raise awareness and push for policy changes.
6. Economic Empowerment: Develop programs that support the economic empowerment of temporary visa holders, such as access to affordable housing, financial literacy training, and entrepreneurship opportunities.
7. Access to Basic Services: Ensure that these individuals have access to basic services such as healthcare, education, and social services. This can involve partnering with service providers and advocating for policies that prioritize the needs of temporary visa holders.
8. Mental Health Support: Provide mental health support and resources to help temporary visa holders cope with the unique challenges they face, including discrimination, stigma, and social isolation.
9. Community Outreach and Awareness: Raise awareness about the issues faced by temporary visa holders in deeply lower-class positions and build support within the community.
