Ensuring that the voices of native Arabic speakers, particularly those who are not recent immigrants or single individuals, are heard in decision-making processes can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can help:

1. Representation in leadership positions: Ensure that native Arabic speakers are represented in leadership positions within organizations and institutions, such as government agencies, NGOs, and community groups. This can help ensure that their voices are heard and valued in decision-making processes.
2. Community outreach and engagement: Reach out to native Arabic speaking communities and engage with them through various means, such as public events, surveys, and focus groups. This can help gather input from a diverse range of individuals and ensure that their voices are heard in decision-making processes.
3. Cultural competency training: Provide cultural competency training for decision-makers to help them understand the cultural nuances and values of native Arabic speakers. This can help ensure that decisions are made with sensitivity to these perspectives.
4. Language accessibility: Ensure that language barriers do not prevent native Arabic speakers from participating in decision-making processes by providing translation services, interpretation, or bilingual staff.
5. Fostering collaboration: Encourage collaboration between different communities and organizations to ensure that diverse perspectives are taken into account in decision-making processes.
6. Building trust: Build trust with native Arabic speakers by being responsive to their needs, following through on commitments, and demonstrating a genuine interest in their perspectives.
7. Using technology: Utilize technology such as video conferencing, online surveys, and social media to reach a wider audience of native Arabic speakers and gather input from them.
8. Leveraging existing networks: Leverage existing networks within the native Arabic speaking community, such as community organizations, religious institutions, and cultural groups, to help amplify their voices in decision-making processes.
9. Providing opportunities for leadership development: Offer leadership development opportunities to native Arabic speakers to empower them to take on leadership roles within their communities and beyond.
10. Addressing systemic barriers: Address any systemic barriers that may prevent native Arabic speakers from participating in decision-making processes
