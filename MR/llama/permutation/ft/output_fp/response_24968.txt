5. Offer bilingual patient education materials: Providing patient education materials in both English and the patient's preferred language can help ensure that they understand their condition and treatment options.

7. Hire bilingual staff: Having staff members who are fluent in both English and the patient's preferred language can help ensure that communication is effective and culturally sensitive.
8. Use cultural brokers: Cultural brokers can act as liaisons between healthcare providers and Mandarin native Arabic speakers, helping to facilitate communication and ensure that patients receive culturally appropriate care.
9. Provide tailored support: Developing tailored support programs for Mandarin native Arabic speakers can help address their specific needs and improve their overall health outcomes.
