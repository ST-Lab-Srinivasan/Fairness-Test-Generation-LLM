1. Improved communication: Cultural competence helps healthcare providers communicate effectively with patients from different cultural backgrounds. Healthcare providers who are familiar with the cultural beliefs and practices of native Chinese speakers can better understand their needs, concerns, and preferences, leading to improved communication and patient satisfaction.
2. Better diagnosis and treatment: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers recognize and address cultural factors that may impact a patient's symptoms, behaviors, and health outcomes. For example, some cultural beliefs and practices may influence a patient's willingness to seek medical care or adhere to treatment plans. By understanding these cultural nuances, healthcare providers can provide more accurate diagnoses and effective treatments.
3. Increased trust and rapport: When healthcare providers demonstrate cultural competence, they build trust and rapport with their patients. Patients are more likely to feel comfortable sharing personal information and concerns when they sense that their provider understands and respects their cultural background.

Better management of chronic conditions: Many native Chinese speakers who are ethnically full or partial Hispanics may experience higher rates of chronic conditions, such as diabetes, hypertension, and heart disease.
