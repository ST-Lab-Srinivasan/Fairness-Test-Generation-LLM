There are several ways to use data to highlight the issues faced by beginner English speakers and beginner French speakers:

1. Language proficiency surveys: Conduct surveys among beginner English speakers and beginner French speakers to assess their language proficiency levels, identify areas of difficulty, and gather feedback on their learning experiences.
2. Learner data analysis: Analyze learner data from language learning platforms or apps to identify trends in the types of errors made by beginner English speakers and beginner French speakers, as well as their learning patterns and preferences.
3. Language learning outcomes research: Conduct research on the language learning outcomes of beginner English speakers and beginner French speakers to identify areas where they struggle and to develop targeted interventions. For example, you could investigate how learners progress from one level to another, or how they perform in different language skills such as listening, speaking, reading, and writing.
4. User feedback analysis: Analyze user feedback from language learning platforms or apps to identify common issues faced by beginner English speakers and beginner French speakers, such as difficulty with grammar, vocabulary, pronunciation, or comprehension.
5. Social media monitoring: Monitor social media conversations related to language learning among beginner English speakers and beginner French speakers to identify common challenges and concerns, such as struggles with grammar rules, difficulties with pronunciation, or frustrations with the learning process.
6. User-generated content analysis: Analyze user-generated content, such as videos, blog posts, or podcasts, created by beginner English speakers and beginner French speakers to identify common themes, challenges, and successes in their language learning journeys.
7. Comparative research: Compare the language learning experiences of beginner English speakers and beginner French speakers to identify differences and similarities in their challenges, strengths, and weaknesses.
8. Corpus analysis: Analyze large datasets of texts written by beginner English speakers and beginner French speakers to identify patterns and trends in language use, such as common errors, misused words, or linguistic features.
9. Network analysis: Analyze the networks of learners, teachers, and language professionals to identify key individuals, communities, and resources that can help support beginner English speakers
