Supporting the career aspirations of permanent resident native Spanish speakers involves understanding their unique needs and providing targeted resources and opportunities. Here are some ways to support these individuals:

1. Language Support: Provide language training and resources to help them improve their English proficiency, which can be a significant barrier to career advancement. Offer classes, workshops, or online resources that cater to their language learning needs.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training to help them better understand the cultural norms and values of the host country. This can help them navigate the workplace more effectively and avoid cultural misunderstandings.
3. Networking Opportunities: Organize networking events or connect them with professional organizations that cater to their industry or profession. This can help them build valuable connections and learn about job opportunities from people who understand their background and experiences.
4. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair them with experienced professionals who share similar cultural backgrounds. These mentors can provide guidance, support, and valuable insights into the local professional landscape.
5. Career Counseling: Offer career counseling sessions to help them explore their interests and career options. This can involve assessing their skills, identifying potential career paths, and developing a plan to achieve their career goals.
6. Job Training and Placement: Provide job training and placement services to help them acquire new skills and find employment that matches their qualifications and aspirations. This can include vocational training, job shadowing, or internships.
7. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage and support them in starting their own businesses by providing access to resources such as business planning workshops, mentorship opportunities, and networking events.
8. Education and Training: Offer educational programs and training courses that cater to their needs and interests. This can include formal education, vocational training, or specialized courses in areas such as technology, entrepreneurship, or leadership.
9. Advocacy and Support: Advocate for their rights and interests within the host country's society and institutions. This can involve working with government agencies, community organizations, or advocacy groups to address issues related to discrimination, inequality, or social exclusion.
