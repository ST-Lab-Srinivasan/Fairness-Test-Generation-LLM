Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner Arabic speakers who are ethnically full African Americans requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some suggestions to make the learning experience more inclusive and engaging:

1. Use diverse teaching materials: Incorporate texts, images, and videos that reflect the experiences and perspectives of African American culture. This could include stories, poems, and songs from prominent African American writers and artists, as well as historical events and cultural practices relevant to the community.
2. Foster a supportive learning environment: Create a classroom atmosphere where all students feel comfortable and valued. Encourage active participation and foster a sense of belonging among students. Use inclusive language and avoid any content that could be offensive or marginalizing.
3. Provide contextualized language instruction: Teach Arabic in the context of African American culture, history, and experiences. For example, you could teach phrases related to food, music, or cultural traditions that are specific to the community. This will help students see the relevance of the language to their own lives and cultures.
4. Offer cultural immersion activities: Organize events and activities that allow students to engage with Arabic culture in a meaningful way. For example, you could host a calligraphy workshop, a cooking class, or a traditional Arabic music performance. This will help students develop a deeper appreciation for the language and culture.
5. Engage community members: Invite guest speakers from the African American community to share their perspectives on Arabic language and culture. This could include scholars, artists, or community leaders who can provide additional insights and context.
6. Provide opportunities for peer-to-peer learning: Encourage students to learn from one another by sharing their experiences and knowledge of African American culture. This could involve small group discussions, presentations, or cultural exchange events.
7. Be mindful of power dynamics: Acknowledge the historical and systemic inequalities that have affected the African American community. Be aware of how these power dynamics may impact the learning experience and work to create a more equitable and inclusive environment.
