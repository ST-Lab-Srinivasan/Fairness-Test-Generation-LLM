Ensuring that the healthcare needs of low-income, natural-born US citizens are met is a complex issue that involves addressing various social and economic factors. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Expand Medicaid coverage: Medicaid is a joint federal-state program that provides health insurance to low-income individuals and families. Expanding Medicaid coverage to include more people could help ensure that these individuals have access to necessary healthcare services.
2. Implement a single-payer healthcare system: A single-payer healthcare system, where the government is the sole provider of health insurance, can help ensure that everyone has access to healthcare regardless of their income level. This approach has been shown to be effective in countries like Canada and the United Kingdom.
3. Increase funding for community health centers: Community health centers provide comprehensive primary care services to underserved populations, including low-income individuals and families. Increasing funding for these centers can help ensure that more people have access to affordable healthcare.
4. Provide incentives for providers who serve low-income patients: Many healthcare providers are hesitant to treat low-income patients because they may not be able to pay for services. Providing incentives, such as increased reimbursement rates or tax breaks, can encourage more providers to serve these populations.
5. Address social determinants of health: Many factors outside of the healthcare system, such as poverty, lack of access to healthy food and safe housing, can impact an individual's health. Addressing these social determinants of health can help improve overall health outcomes for low-income populations.
6. Improve access to preventive care: Preventive care, such as regular check-ups and screenings, can help identify health problems early on and prevent more serious conditions from developing. Improving access to preventive care can help ensure that low-income individuals are more likely to receive the care they need to stay healthy.
