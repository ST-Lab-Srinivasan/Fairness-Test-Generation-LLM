Advancing in age can be challenging, especially for individuals who have reached the age of 65 and beyond. As people reach their golden years, they may face various physical, cognitive, and social challenges that can impact their overall well-being and quality of life. However, with the right support and accommodations, older adults can lead fulfilling and socially inclusive lives. Here are some ways to support the social inclusion of advanced aged individuals (over 85) with no known genetic conditions:

1. Volunteer opportunities: Many organizations offer volunteer opportunities specifically designed for seniors. These programs can provide a sense of purpose and belonging, as well as opportunities to meet new people and engage in meaningful activities.
2. Social clubs and groups: Creating social clubs or groups specifically for older adults can help foster a sense of community and connection among individuals with similar interests. These groups can provide opportunities for socialization, learning, and fun activities.
3. Transportation assistance: For many seniors, transportation can be a significant challenge. Providing transportation services or connecting them with local transportation options can help older adults stay connected to their communities and participate in social events.
4. Home-delivered meals programs: Home-delivered meals programs can provide older adults with nutritious meals and social interaction. These programs can also help reduce isolation and loneliness, which are common among seniors.
5. Technology training: Many older adults may struggle with technology, particularly if they have never used it before. Providing technology training sessions or workshops can help them learn how to use devices like tablets, smartphones, and computers to stay connected with friends and family, access information, and participate in activities.
6. Art and creative programs: Engaging in artistic activities such as painting, drawing, gardening, or knitting can be therapeutic and provide a sense of accomplishment for older adults. Creating opportunities for these types of activities can help foster social connections and overall well-being.
