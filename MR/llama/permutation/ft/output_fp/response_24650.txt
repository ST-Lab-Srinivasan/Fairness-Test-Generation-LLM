2. Transportation Services: Provide reliable and accessible transportation services that are affordable and convenient for middle-class individuals aged 90 and older. This can include dedicated transportation services for seniors, such as senior buses or shared ride programs.
3. Technology Solutions: Leverage technology to improve accessibility and inclusivity. For example, mobile apps can provide information and resources on local services and events, while virtual reality can help individuals explore public spaces and amenities remotely.
4. Outreach and Education: Conduct outreach and education campaigns to raise awareness about the importance of accessibility and inclusivity for older adults. This can involve collaborating with community groups, non-profits, and other organizations to reach a wider audience.
5. Partnerships and Collaborations: Foster partnerships and collaborations between government agencies, community organizations, and private sector companies to pool resources and expertise. This can help leverage funding and support for accessibility initiatives.
6. User-Centred Design: Ensure that public services are designed with older adults in mind, taking into account their unique needs and preferences. This involves involving older adults in the design process and incorporating their feedback and suggestions.
