1. Build a support network: Connect with other recent immigrants and create a support network. You can also share resources and information, and support each other in advocating for your rights.
2. Educate yourself: Learn as much as you can about the laws and regulations regarding immigration in India. This will help you understand your rights and responsibilities, and make informed decisions about how to navigate the system.
3. Join advocacy groups: Look for organizations that advocate for the rights of immigrants in India. These groups may provide support and resources, and can help amplify your voice in advocating for change.
4. Engage with local authorities: Meet with local authorities and elected representatives to discuss your concerns and advocate for changes that will improve the lives of recent immigrants. Be respectful and professional, and provide data and evidence to support your arguments.

6. Organize events: Host events such as conferences, workshops, or rallies to raise awareness about the issues faced by recent immigrants. Invite experts and policymakers to speak, and provide opportunities for attendees to engage in discussions and networking.
