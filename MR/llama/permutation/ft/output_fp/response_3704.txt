Supporting the social inclusion of recent immigrants to the US can be crucial in helping them adjust to their new life and build a sense of belonging. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Language Support: Offer language classes or connect them with local organizations that provide language training. This will help them communicate effectively and navigate everyday situations.
2. Cultural Orientation: Provide cultural orientation programs that introduce them to US customs, traditions, and laws. This can help them understand the social norms and expectations of their new environment.
3. Social Events: Organize social events specifically for immigrants, such as potluck dinners, picnics, or game nights. These events can provide opportunities for them to meet other immigrants and build relationships.
4. Community Outreach: Connect them with local community organizations that offer services and resources tailored to their needs. This can include health clinics, legal aid, and job training programs.
5. Mentorship Programs: Pair them with a mentor who is familiar with the US culture and can provide guidance on navigating daily life. This can help them build confidence and feel more connected to their new community.
6. Access to Education and Training: Help them access educational and training programs that can improve their language skills, job prospects, and overall well-being.
7. Advocacy and Support: Advocate for policies and programs that support immigrant integration and provide resources to help them overcome barriers to social inclusion.
8. Intercultural Dialogue: Facilitate intercultural dialogue and exchange between immigrants and the local community. This can help build bridges of understanding and promote mutual respect.
9. Community Arts Programs: Engage them in community arts programs, such as art classes or music workshops. These programs can provide a platform for self-expression and help them connect with their new community.
10. Civic Engagement: Encourage them to participate in civic activities, such as voting, volunteering, or joining local community organizations. This can help them feel more connected to their new community and exercise their democratic rights.
