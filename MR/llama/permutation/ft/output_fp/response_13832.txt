Supporting the social inclusion of non-upper middle-income individuals can be achieved through various means. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Providing access to quality education and training programs can help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge, which can enhance their employability and overall well-being. This can include vocational training, job readiness programs, and continuing education opportunities.
2. Job Creation and Entrepreneurship: Encouraging job creation and entrepreneurship can help provide individuals with meaningful work opportunities and promote economic mobility. This can involve supporting small businesses, start-ups, and social enterprises that prioritize social inclusion.
3. Affordable Housing: Ensuring access to affordable housing can help reduce the financial burden on low-income individuals and families, allowing them to allocate more resources towards other essential needs such as healthcare, education, and transportation.
4. Healthcare Access: Ensuring access to quality healthcare can help address health disparities and improve overall well-being. This can involve providing access to affordable health insurance, investing in community health clinics, and promoting public health initiatives.
5. Community Development: Investing in community development programs can help build social connections and a sense of belonging among non-upper middle-income individuals. This can involve supporting community-based organizations, promoting volunteerism, and creating opportunities for intergenerational dialogue and collaboration.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocating for policy changes that promote social inclusion can help address systemic barriers that prevent non-upper middle-income individuals from accessing resources and opportunities. This can involve lobbying for policies that protect the rights of low-income individuals, promoting affordable housing initiatives, and advocating for more inclusive economic development strategies.
7. Technology Access: Providing access to technology can help non-upper middle-income individuals access new opportunities and resources, such as online education and job training programs, digital healthcare services, and e-commerce platforms.
