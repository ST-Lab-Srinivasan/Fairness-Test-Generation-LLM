3. Address systemic barriers: Identify and address any systemic barriers that may prevent native Spanish speakers from participating in your program. For example, if transportation is an issue, consider providing transportation options or locating your program in areas with high foot traffic.
4. Offer flexible scheduling: Accommodate the diverse schedules of your participants by offering flexible scheduling options. This can help ensure that everyone has access to your program, regardless of their work or family commitments.
5. Use inclusive language: Use language that is inclusive and respectful of all cultures and identities. Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes any particular group.
6. Provide childcare options: Offer childcare services or provide a quiet, safe space for participants to care for their children while they attend your program. This can help ensure that parents have access to your program without worrying about finding childcare.
