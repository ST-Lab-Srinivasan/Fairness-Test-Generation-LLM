Protecting the legal rights of partnered active-duty individuals is crucial to ensuring their well-being and stability while serving in the military. Here are some ways to protect their legal rights:

1. Equal Treatment Under the Law: Ensure that partnered active-duty individuals have the same legal rights as unmarried service members, including access to benefits such as healthcare, housing, and education.
2. Military Family Jurisdiction: Establish a separate military family jurisdiction within the civilian court system to handle cases involving military families, including divorce, child custody, and support disputes. This would help reduce conflicts between military duty and family responsibilities.
3. Service Members Civil Relief Act (SCRCA): Strengthen and expand the SCRCA to provide better legal protections for partnered active-duty individuals, including provisions for spousal support, child custody, and property division in divorce proceedings.
4. Domestic Partnership Recognition: Offer domestic partnership recognition within the military to grant partnered active-duty individuals the same rights and benefits as married service members. This could include access to healthcare, housing, and other benefits.
5. Deployment Support: Provide additional support for partnered active-duty individuals during deployments, including counseling services, financial assistance, and childcare options.
6. Family Readiness Groups (FRGs): Encourage the use of FRGs to provide a platform for partners of deployed service members to connect with each other, share resources, and access support services.
7. Military Spouse Career Fairs: Host annual career fairs specifically for military spouses to help them navigate job searches and find employment opportunities in their desired fields.
8. Education and Training: Offer educational resources and training programs for partnered active-duty individuals, including financial planning, legal assistance, and mental health support.
9. Financial Assistance: Provide financial assistance to partnered active-duty individuals, including emergency financial assistance, education loans, and homeownership assistance.
