To support the economic empowerment of elderly female non-libertarian individuals, consider the following strategies:

1. Financial Inclusion: Ensure access to financial services such as bank accounts, loans, and insurance. This can be done by promoting financial literacy, building infrastructure in underserved areas, and creating partnerships with financial institutions.
2. Skills Development: Provide training and education on skills that are in demand in the job market, such as digital literacy, entrepreneurship, and leadership. This can help them stay competitive and adapt to changing economic conditions.
3. Social Protection: Strengthen social safety nets such as pension plans, health insurance, and housing support. This can help alleviate poverty and financial insecurity, especially in older age.
4. Encourage Entrepreneurship: Support the creation of micro-businesses or home-based enterprises that cater to local markets. This can provide an alternative source of income and help them maintain their independence.
5. Mentorship and Networking: Connect them with successful businesswomen who can act as mentors, providing guidance, support, and opportunities for collaboration.
6. Address Discrimination: Challenge discriminatory attitudes and practices that hinder their economic participation, such as gender-based wage gaps or limited access to education and training.
7. Healthcare Support: Ensure they have access to quality healthcare, including preventive services and age-related medical care. This can help them maintain their physical and mental well-being, which is crucial for economic participation.
8. Housing Support: Provide affordable and secure housing options, particularly for those who may be facing homelessness or struggling to pay for housing expenses.
9. Community Development: Invest in community development programs that provide opportunities for social interaction, skill-building, and access to resources and services. This can help them feel more connected and supported within their communities.
10. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies and regulations that promote gender equality and protect the rights of older women. This can include changes to labor laws, social security systems, and tax policies that benefit low-income women.
