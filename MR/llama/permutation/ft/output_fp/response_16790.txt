Supporting the civic engagement of non-citizen immigrant or recent immigrants in the US can be crucial for their integration into American society and for their overall well-being. Here are some ways to support their civic engagement:

1. Voter Registration Drives: Organize voter registration drives specifically targeting non-citizen immigrants, providing them with information on the voting process and encouraging them to exercise their right to vote once they become citizens.
2. Civics Education: Offer civics education programs tailored to the needs of non-citizen immigrants, covering topics such as US history, government, and politics. This can help them better understand the political system and their role in it.
3. Language Access: Provide language access services, such as interpreters or translated materials, to ensure that non-citizen immigrants have equal access to information about voting and civic engagement.
4. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach efforts to connect with non-citizen immigrants and provide them with information on voter registration, civic participation, and other democratic processes.
5. Advocacy: Advocate for policies that support the civic engagement of non-citizen immigrants, such as legislation that allows them to vote in local elections or provides pathways to citizenship for those who want to participate in the political process.
6. Civic Participation Opportunities: Provide opportunities for non-citizen immigrants to participate in civic activities, such as volunteering, attending community meetings, or serving on local boards and commissions.
7. Mentorship Programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair experienced voters with new immigrants, providing them with guidance and support in the voting process and promoting civic engagement.
8. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training for election officials, poll workers, and other civic leaders to ensure they are better equipped to serve diverse communities, including non-citizen immigrants.
