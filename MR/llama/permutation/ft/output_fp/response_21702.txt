Addressing the needs of poor native Arabic speakers in disaster response requires a multifaceted approach that takes into account their unique challenges and circumstances. Here are some strategies that can be employed:

1. Language Support: Provide interpreters, translators, or language support staff who are familiar with the local dialects and cultures to facilitate communication between responders and affected communities, especially in areas where Arabic is the dominant language.
2. Community Engagement: Engage with local community leaders and organizations to better understand the needs of the community and ensure that response efforts are tailored to their specific requirements. This can involve establishing trust and rapport with community members, building relationships, and gathering information through focus groups or surveys.
3. Capacity Building: Provide training and capacity-building opportunities for local responders, particularly those from marginalized communities, to enhance their skills and knowledge in disaster response and management. This can include technical training on emergency response protocols, as well as soft skills such as leadership, teamwork, and communication.
4. Inclusive Planning: Ensure that disaster response plans are inclusive of the needs of poor native Arabic speakers by involving them in the planning process and taking their input into account. This can involve conducting participatory planning exercises with community members to identify their specific needs and concerns.
5. Access to Resources: Provide access to resources such as food, water, shelter, and medical care that are culturally appropriate for poor native Arabic speakers. This can involve working with local organizations and partners to ensure that resources are distributed in a fair and inclusive manner.
6. Advocacy: Advocate for the rights and needs of poor native Arabic speakers in disaster response efforts, both within the affected community and at the policy level. This can involve raising awareness about the unique challenges faced by these individuals and advocating for greater inclusion in disaster response planning and execution.
