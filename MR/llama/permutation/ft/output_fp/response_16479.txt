Ensuring the educational needs of native Chinese speaker over 90 years old students requires a tailored approach that considers their unique circumstances and challenges. Here are some strategies to help meet their educational needs:

1. Adaptive Learning: Utilize technology-based adaptive learning systems that adjust the difficulty level and content based on the student's performance. This can help older students learn at their own pace and focus on areas where they need improvement.
2. Simplified Language: Use simplified language and reduce complex sentence structures to make it easier for older students to comprehend and retain information.
3. Multisensory Approach: Incorporate multiple sensory stimuli, such as visual, auditory, and kinesthetic activities, to help older students learn and remember new information.
4. Memory Aids: Use memory aids like flashcards, mnemonic devices, and storytelling to help students retain information.
5. Collaborative Learning: Encourage intergenerational learning by pairing older students with younger peers or volunteers. This can help older students stay engaged and motivated while also providing an opportunity for younger students to learn from their experiences.
6. Flexible Delivery: Offer flexible delivery options, such as online or in-person classes, to accommodate the schedules and mobility of older students.
7. Accessibility: Ensure that learning materials and resources are accessible and easy to use for older students with physical limitations or visual impairments.
8. Social Interaction: Provide opportunities for social interaction, such as group discussions or interactive activities, to help older students stay mentally active and engaged.
9. Cognitive Training: Incorporate cognitive training exercises into the curriculum to improve older students' memory, attention, and problem-solving skills.
10. Family Involvement: Encourage family members to participate in the learning process by providing them with educational materials and resources to help reinforce what their loved ones are learning in class.
11. Life Experience Integration: Encourage older students to share their life experiences and insights with younger students, integrating their knowledge and wisdom into the learning process.
