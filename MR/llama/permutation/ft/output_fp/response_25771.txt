Creating more inclusive social policies for individuals who are not deep in poverty but have been widowed for a long time requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the various challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to education and training programs that can help widows acquire new skills and knowledge, enabling them to find better-paying jobs and improve their economic situation. This can include vocational training, entrepreneurship training, and digital literacy programs.
2. Financial Support: Offer financial support through grants, loans, or microfinance programs that can help widows start or grow a business, pay off debts, or cover living expenses. This support can be targeted towards those who have fallen behind financially due to the loss of their spouse.
3. Healthcare and Mental Health Support: Provide access to quality healthcare and mental health services that can help widows manage the emotional and psychological impacts of losing a spouse. This can include counseling, therapy, and support groups.
4. Childcare and Family Support: Offer childcare services or financial support for families with young children, enabling widows to focus on their personal and professional goals without worrying about the care of their children.
5. Housing Support: Provide affordable housing options or financial support for home renovations to help widows who may be struggling to maintain their homes. This can include subsidized rent, home repair grants, or assistance with finding affordable housing.
6. Community Engagement and Social Connections: Encourage community engagement and social connections through events, gatherings, or volunteer opportunities that can help widows build relationships and networks outside of their immediate family. This can help reduce feelings of isolation and loneliness.
7. Legal Support: Offer legal support and advice to help widows navigate the legal system, including issues related to inheritance, property rights, and child custody.
8. Access to Technology: Provide access to technology, such as smartphones or computers, that can help widows stay connected with their social networks, access information and resources, and engage in online learning or entrepreneurship opportunities.
