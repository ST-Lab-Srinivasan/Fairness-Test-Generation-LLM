Promoting the social inclusion of ethnicly full African American communities above the poverty line requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the economic, social, and cultural factors that contribute to inequality. Here are some strategies that can help promote social inclusion:

1. Education and job training: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market and improve their socio-economic status.
2. Economic empowerment: Initiatives that promote economic empowerment, such as microfinance programs, entrepreneurship support, and access to capital, can help individuals start businesses, create jobs, and generate wealth for their communities.
3. Community development: Investing in community development initiatives, such as infrastructure, public services, and social services, can help improve the quality of life in marginalized communities and promote social inclusion.
4. Health and wellness: Providing access to healthcare, mental health services, and wellness programs can help address the health disparities that exist in marginalized communities and promote overall well-being.
5. Cultural preservation and promotion: Supporting cultural expression and heritage can help preserve the identity and sense of belonging of marginalized communities, which is critical for social inclusion.
6. Advocacy and policy change: Advocating for policy changes that address systemic inequality and discrimination can help promote social inclusion by creating a more equitable society.
7. Community-based initiatives: Supporting community-based initiatives, such as mentorship programs, volunteer opportunities, and community events, can help build connections and foster a sense of belonging among marginalized communities.
8. Partnerships and collaborations: Building partnerships and collaborations between marginalized communities, government agencies, non-profit organizations, and private sector entities can help leverage resources, expertise, and support to promote social inclusion.
9. Media representation and diversity: Increasing media representation and diversity can help challenge stereotypes and biases, and promote a more inclusive society.
10. Addressing systemic inequality: Addressing systemic inequality by addressing issues such as discrimination, unequal access to resources and opportunities, and lack of representation in decision-making
