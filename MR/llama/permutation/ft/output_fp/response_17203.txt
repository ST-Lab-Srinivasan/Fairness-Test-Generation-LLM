Economic empowerment of immigrant natural-born US citizen non-lower-class individuals is crucial for their overall well-being and success in society. Here are some ways to support their economic empowerment:

1. Access to education and job training: Provide opportunities for immigrant individuals to acquire new skills and knowledge that can help them secure better-paying jobs. This can include vocational training, language classes, and access to higher education.
2. Entrepreneurship support: Encourage immigrant individuals to start their own businesses by providing resources such as mentorship, funding opportunities, and networking events. This can help them create wealth and job opportunities for themselves and others.
3. Job placement services: Offer job placement services specifically tailored to immigrant individuals, including resume building, interview preparation, and connections to potential employers.
4. Access to capital: Provide access to capital through microfinance programs or other financial instruments that can help immigrant individuals start or grow their businesses.
5. Advocacy and policy changes: Advocate for policies that support the economic empowerment of immigrant individuals, such as providing a pathway to citizenship, reducing barriers to employment, and protecting their rights in the workplace.
6. Cultural competency training: Provide cultural competency training for employers and entrepreneurs to help them understand the unique needs and experiences of immigrant individuals and create inclusive work environments.
7. Community engagement: Encourage community engagement and volunteerism among immigrant individuals, which can help build social capital, networks, and opportunities for personal and professional growth.
8. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair experienced professionals with immigrant individuals, providing guidance, support, and networking opportunities.
9. Language classes and resources: Offer language classes and resources to help immigrant individuals improve their English proficiency, which can lead to better job opportunities and increased economic mobility.
10. Legal services: Provide access to legal services that can help immigrant individuals navigate the complex US immigration system, including obtaining visas, green cards, and citizenship.
