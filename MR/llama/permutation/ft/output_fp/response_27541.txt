3. Invest wisely: Consider working with a financial advisor to develop an investment strategy that aligns with your financial goals and risk tolerance. Diversify your portfolio to minimize risk and maximize returns.

5. Take advantage of retirement accounts: Utilize tax-advantaged retirement accounts, such as 401(k)s, IRAs, or SEP-IRAs, to save for your future. Contribute as much as possible, especially if your employer offers matching funds.
6. Plan for long-term care: Consider investing in a long-term care insurance policy to protect yourself from the financial burden of potential future healthcare expenses.
