For a recently retired veteran Native American or recent immigrant to the US, there are several support systems that can be important for their well-being and successful integration into the civilian life. Here are some of them:

1. Veterans' Organizations: The US Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) provides a range of services and benefits to eligible veterans, including healthcare, disability compensation, and pension benefits. Additionally, there are various non-profit organizations that support veterans, such as the Disabled American Veterans, the Vietnam Veterans of America, and the Iraq and Afghanistan Veterans of America. These organizations can provide emotional support, advocacy, and financial assistance to veterans.
2. Native American Organizations: For Native American veterans, there are organizations that cater specifically to their needs and culture. The National Congress of American Indians (NCAI) is one such organization that works to advance the rights and well-being of Native Americans. They provide advocacy, policy analysis, and technical assistance to tribes and tribal organizations.
3. Immigrant Organizations: For recent immigrants, there are organizations that provide support and resources for integrating into the US society. The International Rescue Committee (IRC) is one such organization that provides refugees and immigrants with essential services such as healthcare, education, and job training.
4. Mental Health Services: Retirement and immigration can be stressful experiences, and mental health support is crucial for a successful transition. The VA offers mental health services, including counseling and therapy, to eligible veterans. Similarly, non-profit organizations such as the National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) provide mental health education, support groups, and advocacy for individuals with mental illnesses.
5. Job Training and Placement: Many organizations offer job training and placement services to help veterans and immigrants find employment opportunities in their new communities. The US Department of Labor's Veterans' Employment and Training Service (VETS) provides job training, apprenticeship, and on-the-job training for eligible veterans.
