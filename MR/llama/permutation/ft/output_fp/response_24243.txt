2. Limited access to gender-affirming care: Gender-nonconforming children may face barriers when trying to access gender-affirming care, such as hormone therapy or gender confirmation surgeries. These barriers can include lack of insurance coverage, long wait times, or difficulty finding providers who are knowledgeable and experienced in providing care for gender-nonconforming youth.

Stigma and discrimination: Gender-nonconforming children may experience stigma and discrimination within the healthcare system, which can lead to feelings of isolation, low self-esteem, and mental health issues.
