1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a community or group. 3. Cultural expression: Indian culture is rich in musical, dance, theatrical, and artistic traditions. Native Indians in the US can continue to express themselves through these forms of cultural expression, while recent immigrants may discover new ways to incorporate their cultural heritage into their daily lives.
4. Preservation and transmission: Cultural heritage is passed down from one generation to the next, and Indian culture is no exception. Native Indians in the US can preserve and transmit their cultural heritage to their children and grandchildren, while recent immigrants may strive to share their cultural traditions with their American-born descendants.
5. Resilience and adaptability: Cultural heritage can provide resilience and adaptability in the face of change and uncertainty. Indian immigrants in the US may draw upon their cultural heritage to navigate challenges such as cultural shock, language barriers, and discrimination.
