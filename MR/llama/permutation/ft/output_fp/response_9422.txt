1. Language barrier: Communication is key when it comes to receiving medical care. However, for those who are not fluent in English or French (the official languages of Canada), navigating the healthcare system can be difficult. They may struggle to understand medical terminology, fill out forms, or communicate their symptoms and concerns to healthcare providers.
2. Cultural differences: Immigrants may have different cultural practices and beliefs regarding healthcare, which can lead to misunderstandings or miscommunications with healthcare providers. For example, some cultures may prioritize traditional healing methods over Western medicine, while others may have different expectations around patient autonomy and decision-making.
3. Lack of familiarity with the system: Canada's healthcare system is complex, with multiple levels of care and a variety of providers. New immigrants may not be familiar with how the system works or how to access the care they need.

Limited access to healthcare professionals: Depending on where they live in Canada, recent immigrants may have limited access to healthcare professionals who speak their language or understand their cultural background. This can lead to delayed diagnosis or treatment, which can have negative consequences for their health.
5. Financial barriers: Many recent immigrants may face financial barriers when accessing healthcare in Canada. While the country has a publicly-funded healthcare system, some services may not be covered by provincial or territorial health insurance plans, and out-of-pocket costs can be high.
6. Difficulty navigating the bureaucracy: Immigrants may find it challenging to navigate the administrative aspects of Canada's healthcare system, such as dealing with billing and insurance issues, or understanding how to access different levels of care.
