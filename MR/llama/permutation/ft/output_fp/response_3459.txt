The term "mixed African-American" is a complex and nuanced identity that encompasses a wide range of experiences and backgrounds. To support the economic empowerment of this group, it's important to recognize and address the systemic barriers and discrimination that they may face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills development programs, particularly in underserved communities. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and higher education opportunities.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage and support entrepreneurial endeavors among mixed African-Americans, through mentorship, access to capital, and networking opportunities.
3. Access to Capital: Address the historical wealth gap by providing access to capital for mixed African-American individuals, including microloans, small business loans, and investment opportunities.
4. Career Advancement Opportunities: Provide career advancement opportunities through training programs, mentorship, and networking events. This can help mixed African-Americans navigate corporate America and reach their full potential.
5. Addressing Discrimination: Implement policies and practices to address discrimination in the workplace, including anti-bias training and diversity initiatives.
6. Community Development: Invest in community development programs that support mixed African-American communities, such as affordable housing, job creation, and infrastructure improvement.
7. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to quality healthcare for mixed African-Americans, including mental health services, by addressing the systemic barriers that limit access to care.
8. Civic Engagement: Encourage civic engagement and political participation among mixed African-Americans, by providing voter registration drives, advocacy training, and opportunities to serve on boards and committees.
9. Cultural Preservation: Support the preservation of cultural heritage and identity among mixed African-Americans, through cultural events, educational programs, and community-based initiatives.
10. Intersectional Advocacy: Advocate for policies that address the unique needs and experiences of mixed African-Americans, including those related to race, ethnicity, class, gender, and sexuality.
