Promoting the accessibility of public services for Nigerian active-duty individuals requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various challenges and barriers. Here are some strategies that can be implemented:

1. Awareness Campaigns: Launch awareness campaigns to educate active-duty personnel, their families, and the wider community about the availability and accessibility of public services. Use various media channels, including radio, television, social media, and print media, to reach a wide audience.
2. Inclusive Policy Frameworks: Develop and implement inclusive policy frameworks that recognize the unique challenges faced by active-duty personnel and their families. These policies should aim to provide equal access to public services for all, regardless of military status.
3. Accessible Facilities: Ensure that public facilities, such as healthcare centers, schools, and recreational spaces, are accessible and easy to navigate for individuals with disabilities. Provide adaptive equipment and assistive technology to support active-duty personnel with disabilities.
4. Accommodative Workplaces: Encourage employers to provide accommodations and flexible work arrangements for active-duty personnel, allowing them to balance their military duties with their civilian jobs. This can include telecommuting options, flexible scheduling, and job assignments that are compatible with military service.
5. Financial Support: Provide financial support to active-duty personnel and their families through various programs, such as scholarships, grants, and low-interest loans. This can help alleviate the financial burden associated with military service and ensure that individuals have access to necessary resources.
6. Advocacy and Support Networks: Establish advocacy and support networks for active-duty personnel, including mental health counseling services, peer support groups, and military family organizations. These networks can provide emotional support, practical assistance, and a sense of community for individuals and their families.
