Ensuring the educational needs of beginner Arabic speakers who are temporary visa holders requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their language learning requirements, cultural integration, and access to resources. Here are some strategies to help meet their educational needs:

1. Language Courses: Offer specialized Arabic language courses tailored to the beginners' level of proficiency. These courses should be designed to teach the basics of the Arabic language, including grammar, vocabulary, and pronunciation. This will help them improve their language skills and communicate effectively in everyday situations.
2. Cultural Immersion: Provide opportunities for beginners to immerse themselves in the Arab culture through activities such as attending cultural events, watching Arabic movies, listening to Arabic music, and participating in cooking classes. This will help them gain a deeper understanding of the culture and customs, which is essential for effective communication.
3. Access to Resources: Make available resources such as language learning apps, online courses, and books that cater to beginners' needs. These resources should be easily accessible and user-friendly, allowing learners to study at their own pace and convenience.
4. Supportive Environment: Create a supportive environment that encourages learners to practice their Arabic skills. This can be achieved by organizing language exchange programs, where beginners can interact with native Arabic speakers and receive feedback on their progress.
5. Personalized Learning: Offer personalized learning plans tailored to each individual's needs, goals, and learning style. This will help learners stay motivated and engaged in the learning process.
6. Access to Qualified Teachers: Ensure that beginner Arabic speakers have access to qualified teachers who are experienced in teaching Arabic as a second language. These teachers should be able to adapt their teaching methods to cater to the learners' needs and abilities.
7. Flexible Scheduling: Offer flexible scheduling options to accommodate the diverse schedules of temporary visa holders. This can include weekend or evening classes, or online courses that can be accessed at any time.
