5. Sensitivity training: Provide sensitivity training for teachers and staff to help them understand the needs and experiences of students with disabilities. This can involve workshops or training sessions on disability awareness and etiquette.
6. Collaboration with disability organizations: Work with local disability organizations to get feedback on your supports and services, and to ensure that they are meeting the needs of not able-bodied students.
7. Flexibility: Be flexible in your teaching approaches and materials to accommodate different learning styles and abilities. For example, provide options for written assignments or use multimedia presentations.
8. Technology: Utilize technology to support not able-bodied students, such as text-to-speech software, speech-to-text software, and other assistive technologies.
9. Peer support: Encourage peer support among students, including those with disabilities. This can involve pairing students with similar needs or experiences for group work or study sessions.
