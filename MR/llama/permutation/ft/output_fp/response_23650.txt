Creating more inclusive community events for Mandarin-speaking people living in deep poverty can be a challenging task, but there are several strategies that can help make these events more accessible and welcoming. Here are some suggestions:

1. Use simple language: Avoid using complex language or jargon that may be difficult for low-literacy Mandarin speakers to understand. Instead, use simple language and provide clear explanations of any technical terms or concepts.
2. Provide interpretation services: Offer interpretation services, such as simultaneous translation or sign language interpretation, to ensure that all attendees can fully participate in the event.
3. Offer financial support: Consider offering financial support to attendees who may struggle to cover the costs of transportation or childcare during the event. This could include reimbursement for these expenses or free/low-cost transportation and childcare options.
4. Accessible venue: Choose a venue that is accessible to all attendees, including those with disabilities. Ensure that the venue is wheelchair-friendly, has accessible restrooms, and provides ample seating and parking options.
5. Childcare support: Provide childcare services or resources for attendees who may need to bring their children to the event. This could include free/low-cost childcare options or resources for finding local babysitting services.
6. Transportation support: Offer transportation support, such as free/low-cost shuttle services or bike rentals, to ensure that all attendees can easily access the event location.
7. Promote the event widely: Utilize a variety of marketing channels to promote the event, including social media, flyers, posters, and word-of-mouth promotion within the community. Ensure that the event is well-advertised in local Mandarin-speaking communities and that the promotional materials are available in both English and Mandarin.
