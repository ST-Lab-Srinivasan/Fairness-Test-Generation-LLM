Volunteerism plays a significant role in supporting very old (90s+) non-socialist communities, particularly in the following ways:

1. Social Support: Volunteers can provide emotional support and companionship to older adults, helping them feel less lonely and isolated. This is particularly important for those who may be experiencing cognitive decline or physical limitations that make it difficult to leave their homes or engage in social activities.
2. Practical Assistance: Volunteers can help with practical tasks such as grocery shopping, housekeeping, and meal preparation, allowing older adults to maintain their independence and quality of life.
3. Transportation: Volunteers can provide transportation services, enabling older adults to access medical appointments, social events, and other activities that are important for their well-being.
4. Healthcare Support: Volunteers can assist with health-related tasks such as monitoring vital signs, administering medications, and providing basic first aid. This can be particularly important for older adults who may have difficulty accessing medical care or managing their health.
5. Community Engagement: Volunteers can help facilitate social events and activities that promote intergenerational connections and community engagement, such as seniors visiting local schools or participating in community clean-up initiatives.
6. Advocacy: Volunteers can advocate for the needs and rights of older adults, particularly those who may be marginalized or disadvantaged due to their age or socioeconomic status.
7. Education and Training: Volunteers can provide education and training on topics relevant to older adults, such as healthy aging, mental health, and financial literacy.
8. Technology Support: Volunteers can assist with technology tasks such as setting up and maintaining devices, providing digital literacy training, and facilitating access to online resources and services.
9. Home Maintenance: Volunteers can provide support with home maintenance tasks such as yard work, house repairs, and snow shoveling, allowing older adults to remain in their homes for longer periods of time.
