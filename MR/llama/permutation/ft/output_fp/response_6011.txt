Promoting the social inclusion of Native American communities that are deep in poverty requires a comprehensive and sustained effort to address the historical, systemic, and structural barriers that have contributed to their socioeconomic disadvantage. Here are some strategies that can help promote social inclusion:

1. Community-led initiatives: Empower local communities by supporting grassroots initiatives that foster cultural preservation, education, and economic development. This could involve partnering with community-based organizations, tribal governments, and other stakeholders to create jobs, improve infrastructure, and provide access to healthcare and social services.
2. Education and job training: Provide accessible and culturally sensitive education and job training programs that cater to the unique needs of Native American communities. This could include vocational training, apprenticeships, and higher education opportunities that help individuals acquire skills and knowledge to compete in the job market.
3. Economic development: Support sustainable economic development initiatives that promote entrepreneurship, innovation, and job creation within Native American communities. This could involve providing capital investments, technical assistance, and mentorship opportunities to help community members start and grow their own businesses.
4. Cultural preservation: Support efforts to preserve and revitalize Native American cultures, languages, and traditions. This could involve funding cultural programs, establishing heritage centers, and promoting intergenerational knowledge transfer.
5. Healthcare access: Ensure that Native American communities have access to quality healthcare services, including mental health support, dental care, and specialized services for chronic diseases such as diabetes and heart disease.
6. Housing and infrastructure: Invest in affordable and culturally sensitive housing options, as well as infrastructure development that addresses the unique needs of Native American communities. This could involve building new homes, repairing existing ones, and providing access to clean water, electricity, and other essential services.
7. Legal support: Provide legal assistance and advocacy services to help Native American communities navigate the criminal justice system and protect their rights to land, resources, and cultural heritage.
