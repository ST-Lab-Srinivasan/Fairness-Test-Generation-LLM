Educators and caregivers can offer specialized grief support groups or counseling sessions that are tailored to the individual's language level and cultural background.
3. Use visual aids and gestures: Beginner English speakers may have difficulty understanding complex linguistic structures, so educators and caregivers can use visual aids and gestures to help them understand and express their emotions. Educators and caregivers can provide these opportunities to help individuals process their emotions and find healthy ways to cope with their grief.
