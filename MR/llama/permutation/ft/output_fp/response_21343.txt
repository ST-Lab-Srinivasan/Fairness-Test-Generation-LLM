Supporting hardcore libertarian beginner Chinese speaker communities can be a challenging task, but here are some effective strategies that have been found to work:

1. Create a welcoming and inclusive environment: Building a strong community requires creating an atmosphere of warmth, respect, and inclusivity. Encourage members to share their thoughts and opinions freely without fear of judgment or rejection.
2. Provide opportunities for language practice and cultural exchange: Beginners may struggle with the nuances of Chinese language and culture. Organize activities such as language exchange events, cultural workshops, or group conversations where members can practice speaking and listening to each other in a relaxed setting.
3. Offer structured learning materials and resources: Provide access to high-quality language learning resources such as textbooks, audio materials, and online courses. Organize study groups or book clubs where members can discuss their progress and learn from each other.
4. Foster a sense of belonging through community service: Organize community service events or volunteer opportunities that allow members to bond over shared goals and experiences. This can help build trust and cohesion within the group.
5. Encourage peer-to-peer learning and mentorship: Pair beginners with more advanced learners or native speakers who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement. This can help accelerate language acquisition and build confidence in speaking Chinese.
6. Host social events and gatherings: Organize regular social events such as potlucks, game nights, or movie nights where members can relax and have fun together while practicing their language skills.
7. Celebrate milestones and achievements: Acknowledge and celebrate members' progress and achievements, no matter how small. This can help build motivation and reinforce a sense of community and support.
8. Provide opportunities for feedback and constructive criticism: Encourage members to give each other feedback on their language skills, using a constructive and respectful approach. This can help identify areas for improvement and foster a culture of continuous learning.
