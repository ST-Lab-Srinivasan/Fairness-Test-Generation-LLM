Lower middle-income Native natural-born US citizen people face a range of challenges in the healthcare system, including:

1. Limited access to care: Many Native communities are located in rural areas with limited access to healthcare facilities and specialized care. This can result in delayed or lack of access to necessary medical care, particularly for chronic conditions.
2. Cultural barriers: The healthcare system often does not take into account the cultural beliefs and practices of Native communities. This can lead to mistrust and resistance to seeking medical care from healthcare providers who do not understand or respect Native cultural values.
3. Language barriers: Many Native Americans speak indigenous languages, which can create a barrier to communication with healthcare providers who may not speak these languages.
4. Limited health insurance coverage: Many Native Americans have limited access to health insurance, which can make it difficult to afford necessary medical care.
5. Historical trauma: The legacy of colonization, forced assimilation, and other forms of oppression has had a lasting impact on the health and well-being of Native communities. This historical trauma can manifest in various ways, including mental health issues, substance abuse, and chronic diseases.
6. Limited access to specialized care: Many Native communities have limited access to specialized medical care, such as cardiology, oncology, and neurology.
7. Mental health disparities: Native Americans experience higher rates of mental illness compared to the general population, including substance abuse, depression, and suicide.
8. Oral health disparities: Native Americans experience higher rates of oral health problems, including tooth decay and gum disease.
9. Chronic diseases: Native Americans are at higher risk for chronic diseases such as diabetes, obesity, and heart disease compared to the general population.
10. Limited access to preventive care: Many Native communities have limited access to preventive healthcare services, such as regular check-ups, screenings, and vaccinations.
