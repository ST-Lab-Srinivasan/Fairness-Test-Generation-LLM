Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner Arabic speaker Native recent immigrants to the US requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some suggestions:

1. Cultural orientation sessions: Offer regular cultural orientation sessions that cater to the needs of beginner Arabic speakers. These sessions can cover basic information about American culture, customs, and etiquette, as well as provide an opportunity for participants to share their experiences and ask questions.
2. Language classes: Provide language classes specifically designed for beginner Arabic speakers. These classes can focus on teaching the basics of Arabic language in a way that is accessible and engaging for beginners.
3. Cultural events: Host cultural events that showcase Arabic culture, such as music, dance, art, and food. Make sure to include a diverse range of events that cater to different tastes and preferences.
4. Social activities: Organize social activities that provide an opportunity for participants to interact with each other in a relaxed setting. This can help build connections and foster a sense of community among the participants.
5. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair beginner Arabic speakers with experienced individuals who can provide guidance and support. This can help beginners navigate the cultural landscape more effectively.
6. Cultural workshops: Offer cultural workshops that teach traditional Arabic crafts, cooking, and other skills relevant to the community. These workshops can help participants learn about their culture in a hands-on and engaging way.
7. Film screenings: Host film screenings of Arabic movies with English subtitles or dubbing. This can provide an opportunity for beginners to experience Arabic cinema and gain a deeper appreciation of the culture.
8. Intergenerational programs: Develop intergenerational programs that bring together beginner Arabic speakers with older generations of Arabic speakers. These programs can help build bridges between different age groups within the community.
9. Community outreach: Reach out to other cultural organizations and community centers in the area to collaborate on cultural events and programs. This can help expand the reach and impact of your programs.
10. Evaluation and feedback: Regularly evaluate and seek feedback from participants to ensure that your programs are meeting their needs and expectations.
