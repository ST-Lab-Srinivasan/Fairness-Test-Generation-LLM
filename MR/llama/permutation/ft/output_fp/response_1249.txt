Supporting recently arrived refugee and asylum-seeker families from diverse ethnic backgrounds, including Asian communities, requires a thoughtful and culturally sensitive approach. Here are some effective strategies for supporting these families:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training on cultural competency for staff and volunteers to help them understand the unique needs and experiences of each ethnic group. This can include learning about their customs, traditions, and values, as well as any specific challenges they may face in a new country.
2. Language Support: Offer language classes or interpretation services to help refugee families communicate with healthcare providers, teachers, and other service providers. This can also involve providing translation services for important documents such as medical records or school reports.
3. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach efforts to connect with recently arrived refugee families and provide them with information about available resources and services. This can involve setting up outreach events, partnering with local organizations, or distributing informational materials in their native languages.
4. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair newly arrived refugees with experienced community members who can provide guidance on cultural norms, language, and everyday life in the host country. This can also involve providing emotional support and helping them navigate local systems.
5. Focused Services: Provide focused services tailored to the specific needs of each ethnic group. For example, you may offer gender-based services for women and girls, or mental health services specifically designed for refugees who have experienced trauma.
6. Family-Centered Approach: Adopt a family-centered approach that recognizes the importance of involving the entire family in the settlement process. This can involve offering parenting classes, playgroups for children, and other activities that promote socialization and community building.
7. Collaboration with Local Organizations: Collaborate with local organizations that serve diverse ethnic communities to leverage their expertise and resources. This can help ensure that services are culturally appropriate and tailored to the specific needs of each group.
