Supporting the professional aspirations of divorced, multiple times unemployed individuals can be challenging, but there are several ways to help them achieve their career goals:

1. Encourage and Believe in Them: Offer emotional support and belief in their abilities, even if they have faced several setbacks in their career journey. Help them see their strengths and potential for success.
2. Provide Resources and Training: Offer to help them access resources such as job training programs, online courses, or workshops that can help them acquire new skills or improve existing ones. This can help them stay competitive in the job market.
3. Networking Opportunities: Introduce them to your professional contacts or provide opportunities for them to connect with other professionals in their industry. This can help them learn about new job opportunities and get leads on potential openings.
4. Career Coaching: Offer to act as a career coach, helping them identify their strengths and passions, and develop a plan to achieve their career goals. This can include help with resume writing, interviewing skills, and job search strategies.
5. Job Leads: Keep an eye out for job openings that may be a good fit for them and share the information with them. You can also offer to help them apply or prepare for interviews.
6. Mentorship: Offer to mentor them, sharing your own professional experiences and providing guidance on how to navigate their career. This can help them gain valuable insights and advice from someone who has been in their shoes.
7. Professional Development Opportunities: Encourage them to attend conferences, workshops, or other professional development opportunities that can help them stay up-to-date in their field and network with other professionals.
8. Flexible Work Arrangements: If possible, offer flexible work arrangements such as remote work, part-time work, or freelancing. This can help them balance their career and personal responsibilities while they get back on their feet.
9. Building Confidence: Help them build their confidence by highlighting their past successes and accomplishments. Encourage them to focus on their strengths and achievements rather than dwelling on their failures.
