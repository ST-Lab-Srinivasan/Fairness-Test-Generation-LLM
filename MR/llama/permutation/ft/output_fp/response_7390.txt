Addressing the Needs of Not Long-Time Married Independent Individuals in Disaster Response:

Introduction:
Disaster responses often prioritize the needs of families and children, overlooking the unique challenges faced by not long-time married independent individuals. However, these individuals may require specific support to ensure their well-being and safety during disaster situations. This article discusses the challenges faced by this group and proposes strategies for addressing their needs in disaster response.

Challenges Faced by Not Long-Time Married Independent Individuals:

1. Lack of Social Support: Not long-time married independent individuals may not have a support system, such as a spouse or family, to rely on during disaster situations. This can lead to feelings of isolation and loneliness, which can exacerbate the psychological impact of the disaster.
2. Limited Financial Resources: Independent individuals may have limited financial resources compared to families, making it challenging to purchase essential items or pay for emergency services.
3. Difficulty Navigating Bureaucratic Systems: Independent individuals may encounter difficulties navigating complex bureaucratic systems, such as applying for government aid or accessing medical services, due to a lack of familiarity with these systems.
4. Limited Access to Information: Independent individuals may have limited access to information about disaster response efforts, emergency services, and available resources, making it challenging to make informed decisions about their safety.
5. Stigma and Discrimination: Not long-time married independent individuals may face stigma and discrimination from their communities or service providers, which can hinder their ability to access support and resources.
