Here are some strategies to ensure their educational needs are met:

1. Assess their language proficiency level: Conduct an initial assessment of the individual's current language proficiency level to determine their strengths, weaknesses, and areas for improvement. This will help create a tailored learning plan that meets their specific needs.
2. Provide access to high-quality resources: Offer a range of Arabic language learning materials, including textbooks, language learning apps, podcasts, and online courses. Ensure these resources are interactive, engaging, and culturally relevant to the individual's interests and goals.
3. Encourage self-directed learning: Facilitate self-directed learning by providing access to Arabic language learning resources and tools that the individual can use independently. This could include language learning apps, online courses, or language exchange programs.
4. Offer interactive and immersive learning experiences: Organize cultural events, workshops, and activities that provide opportunities for the individual to engage with Arabic language and culture in an interactive and immersive environment. Examples could include Arabic language exchange events, cooking classes, or traditional Arabic music and dance workshops.
5. Provide personalized coaching and feedback: Offer one-on-one coaching and feedback sessions to help the individual improve their language skills. This could involve working with a language coach or tutor who can provide tailored guidance and support.
6. Create a supportive learning community: Foster a sense of community among beginner Arabic speaker permanent resident individuals by organizing regular gatherings, events, or online forums where they can share their experiences, ask questions, and learn from one another.
7. Provide opportunities for authentic language use: Encourage the individual to use Arabic in real-life situations by providing opportunities to interact with native speakers, either through language exchange programs or cultural events.
