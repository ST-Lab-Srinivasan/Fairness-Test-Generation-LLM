Promoting the accessibility of public services for Mexican civilian individuals requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various barriers, including physical, financial, and social obstacles. Here are some strategies that can help improve access to public services:

1. Inclusive Design: Ensure that public buildings and facilities are designed with universal design principles in mind, which means they should be accessible to people of all abilities. This includes features such as wheelchair ramps, elevators, wide doorways, and Braille signage.
2. Outreach and Education: Provide information and outreach services to marginalized communities, including those with disabilities, low-income families, and indigenous populations. Educate them about their rights and how to access public services.
3. Financial Inclusion: Implement programs that provide financial support to individuals who cannot afford public services, such as subsidies or scholarships. This can help reduce the financial burden on vulnerable populations.
4. Transportation: Ensure that public transportation is accessible and affordable for all citizens. This includes providing buses with wheelchair ramps, trains with accessible cars, and taxis with specialized vehicles for people with disabilities.
5. Accessible Technology: Promote the use of accessible technology, such as audio or Braille displays on public kiosks, ATMs, and other devices that can help individuals with visual or hearing impairments.
6. Customer Service Training: Provide training to public servants on how to interact with people from diverse backgrounds, including those with disabilities. This can help create a more inclusive environment and improve the quality of service delivery.
7. Community Participation: Encourage community participation in the design and delivery of public services. This can help ensure that services are responsive to local needs and priorities.
8. Accountability Mechanisms: Establish accountability mechanisms, such as complaints and appeals procedures, to ensure that citizens can report issues with accessibility and receive a timely response.
9. Monitoring and Evaluation: Regularly monitor and evaluate the accessibility of public services to identify areas for improvement. This can help ensure that services are continuously improved to meet the needs of all citizens.
