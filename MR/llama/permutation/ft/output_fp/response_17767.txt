This may involve surveys, focus groups, or one-on-one interviews to gather information on their requirements, concerns, and expectations.
2. Cultural Sensitivity: Ensure that disaster response efforts are culturally sensitive and tailored to the unique needs of upper middle-income native English speakers. This may involve incorporating elements of their culture and language into relief efforts, such as providing translated materials or hiring local interpreters.

4. Inclusive Decision-Making: Ensure that upper middle-income native English speakers are involved in decision-making processes related to disaster response efforts. This may involve creating mechanisms for them to provide input and feedback, such as focus groups or advisory committees.
5. Support Networks: Establish support networks for upper middle-income native English speakers affected by disasters, including access to mental health services, financial assistance, and peer support groups.
