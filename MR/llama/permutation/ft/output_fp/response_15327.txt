Promoting the accessibility of public services for individuals aged 90 and above who are still employed is crucial to ensure their continued participation in the workforce and overall well-being. Here are some ways to achieve this:

1. Age-Friendly Workplace Policies: Encourage employers to adopt age-friendly workplace policies, such as flexible working hours, telecommuting options, and phased retirement programs. This can help older workers maintain their physical and mental health while continuing to work.
2. Accessible Public Transportation: Ensure that public transportation is accessible and convenient for older workers, with amenities like wheelchair-accessible buses and trains, priority seating, and audio announcements. This can help reduce transportation barriers and enable older workers to commute independently.
3. Assistive Technologies: Promote the use of assistive technologies, such as voice-to-text software, magnification glasses, and ergonomic workstations, to help older workers manage their workload and reduce the risk of injury.
4. Age-Appropriate Training: Provide age-appropriate training programs that cater to the needs and abilities of older workers. This can include training on how to use new technologies, as well as skills development in areas like time management and communication.
5. Health and Wellness Programs: Offer health and wellness programs that are tailored to the needs of older workers. This can include exercise classes, nutrition counseling, and mental health support.
6. Networking Opportunities: Encourage older workers to connect with their peers and younger colleagues through networking events and mentorship programs. This can help build social connections and foster a sense of community in the workplace.
7. Work-Life Balance: Support older workers in achieving a healthy work-life balance by providing flexible working hours, telecommuting options, and parental leave policies.
