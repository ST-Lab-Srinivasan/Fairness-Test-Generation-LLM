Creating more inclusive cultural programs for long-term unemployed non-disabled individuals requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some strategies that can help make cultural programs more accessible and inclusive:

1. Outreach and Partnerships: Build partnerships with local organizations that serve the long-term unemployed population, such as job training centers, community centers, and social services organizations. Reach out to these organizations to promote your cultural programs and encourage referrals from their clients.
2. Accessible Programming: Develop cultural programs that are accessible to people with various abilities and disabilities. This can include offering subtitles or closed captions for deaf or hard of hearing participants, providing wheelchair accessibility, and ensuring that the program content is relevant and engaging for a diverse audience.
3. Scholarships and Financial Assistance: Offer scholarships or financial assistance to individuals who may not have the means to participate in cultural programs due to unemployment or financial hardship. This can help remove financial barriers and ensure that everyone has an equal opportunity to engage with the arts.
4. Volunteer Opportunities: Provide volunteer opportunities for individuals who are interested in the arts but may not have the financial means to participate. Volunteering can help build skills, network, and gain exposure to various art forms.
5. Inclusive Marketing: Develop marketing strategies that are inclusive of diverse populations. Use language and imagery that reflects the diversity of the community, and avoid stereotypes or assumptions based on race, ethnicity, or disability status.
6. Collaborations and Cross-Pollination: Collaborate with other organizations, artists, and cultural institutions to create more inclusive cultural programs. This can involve co-creating content, sharing resources, and promoting each other's work to reach a broader audience.
