Promoting the educational success of native French speakers who are not disabled can be achieved through various strategies and interventions. Here are some suggestions:

1. Provide a supportive learning environment: Create a warm, inclusive, and stimulating learning environment that fosters a love for learning and encourages students to reach their full potential. Encourage active participation, ask open-ended questions, and provide opportunities for students to share their thoughts and ideas.
2. Develop individualized learning plans: Recognize that each student has unique strengths, needs, and learning styles. Work with teachers, parents, and students to develop individualized learning plans that cater to the student's academic, social, and emotional needs.
3. Foster a growth mindset: Encourage students to embrace challenges, persist through obstacles, and see failures as opportunities for growth. Teach them to be resilient, adaptable, and open to feedback.
4. Improve language instruction: Provide native French speakers with high-quality language instruction that builds on their existing knowledge and skills. This may involve using authentic materials, such as news articles, videos, and podcasts, and incorporating technology and multimedia resources to enhance language learning.
5. Offer extracurricular activities: Encourage students to participate in extracurricular activities that align with their interests and strengths. This can help them develop new skills, build self-esteem, and make meaningful connections with peers and mentors.
6. Foster a sense of belonging: Create an inclusive school community where native French speakers feel valued, respected, and supported. Encourage students to participate in school events, join clubs and organizations, and engage in volunteer work that benefits the local community.
7. Provide additional resources: Offer additional resources, such as tutoring, mentoring, and academic support services, to help native French speakers who may be struggling academically or experiencing difficulties with language proficiency.
8. Encourage parental involvement: Encourage parents to participate in their child's education by attending school events, volunteering in the classroom, and providing emotional support. This can help students feel more connected to their learning environment and more motivated to succeed.
