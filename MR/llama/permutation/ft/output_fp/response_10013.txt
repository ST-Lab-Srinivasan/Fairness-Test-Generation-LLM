Creating more inclusive cultural programs for native French speakers and recent immigrants to the US requires understanding their needs, preferences, and cultural backgrounds. Here are some suggestions on how to create such programs:

1. Inclusive Language: Use inclusive language in all communications, marketing materials, and program descriptions. Avoid using language that may be offensive or marginalizing to any particular group.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for staff and volunteers who will be working with native French speakers and immigrants. This can help them understand the cultural nuances and challenges faced by these groups.
3. Representation Matters: Ensure that the programs and activities are representative of the diverse communities you serve. Use images, videos, and other visuals that reflect the diversity of your participants.
4. Accessibility: Make sure that your programs and activities are accessible to everyone, regardless of their language proficiency or cultural background. Provide interpreters, translators, and other accommodations as needed.
5. Community Engagement: Engage with the local French-speaking community and recent immigrants through outreach and engagement activities. This can help build trust and foster a sense of belonging among participants.
6. Collaborate with Community Organizations: Partner with organizations that serve native French speakers and recent immigrants. These organizations can provide valuable insights and support in creating inclusive cultural programs.
7. Flexibility: Be flexible and adaptable when designing programs and activities. Consider the diverse needs and preferences of your participants, and be willing to make changes as needed.
8. Involve Participants in Program Design: Involve native French speakers and recent immigrants in the design and development of cultural programs. This can help ensure that the programs are relevant and meaningful to these groups.
9. Provide Education and Resources: Offer educational resources and workshops on topics such as cultural competency, diversity, and inclusion. Provide access to resources and services that can help participants navigate their new surroundings.
10. Celebrate Diversity: Celebrate the diversity of your participants by hosting events and activities that showcase different cultures and traditions. This can help build a sense of community and inclusiveness
