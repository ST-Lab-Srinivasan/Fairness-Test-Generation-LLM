1. Inclusive Disaster Response Planning: Develop inclusive disaster response plans that take into account the diverse needs of all individuals, including those with unknown or undiagnosed genetic conditions. 3. Personalized Support: Provide personalized support to individuals with unknown or undiagnosed genetic conditions based on their unique needs. 4. Adaptable Accommodations: Ensure that accommodations are adaptable and can be tailored to meet the diverse needs of individuals with unknown or undiagnosed genetic conditions. For example, provide flexible seating options or adaptive technology to help individuals with mobility impairments navigate evacuation routes.
5. Training for Responders: Provide training for responders on how to recognize and address the unique needs of individuals with unknown or undiagnosed genetic conditions during a disaster response. This can involve learning about the signs and symptoms of various genetic conditions, as well as how to provide appropriate support and accommodations.
