Creating more inclusive public policies for mentally disabled Native natural-born US citizen individuals requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the unique challenges and barriers faced by this community. Here are some key recommendations:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for policymakers, service providers, and other stakeholders to help them understand the specific needs and experiences of Native mentally disabled individuals. This training can help promote culturally responsive policies and practices that are grounded in the values and beliefs of the community.
2. Community-Led Decision Making: Involve Native communities in the decision-making process around policies and services that affect them. This can help ensure that policies are developed in partnership with the community, are responsive to community needs, and reflect community values and priorities.
3. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that Native individuals have access to culturally competent and trauma-informed healthcare services. This can include mental health services, primary care, and specialty care. Provide language and cultural support services to help individuals navigate the healthcare system.
4. Education and Employment: Provide education and employment opportunities that are tailored to the needs of Native mentally disabled individuals. This can include job training programs, vocational rehabilitation services, and education and career counseling.
5. Housing and Infrastructure: Ensure that housing and infrastructure are accessible and inclusive for Native mentally disabled individuals. Provide affordable and accessible housing options, as well as transportation and other support services that can help individuals live independently.
6. Advocacy and Support: Provide advocacy and support services to help Native mentally disabled individuals navigate the system and access the resources they need. This can include legal aid, case management, and peer support groups.
7.
