Title: The Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping the Identities of Immigrant Non-Conservative Individuals in the US

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of immigrants to the United States, particularly for non-conservative individuals. It influences their values, beliefs, and practices, which are often rooted in their country of origin. This paper will explore the impact of cultural heritage on the identities of immigrant non-conservative individuals in the US, highlighting the ways in which it shapes their experiences, behaviors, and interactions with American society.

The Importance of Cultural Heritage:
Cultural heritage refers to the shared knowledge, beliefs, values, customs, and practices that are passed down from one generation to the next within a particular group or community (Haller, 2013). For immigrants, their cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging and identity, helping them navigate unfamiliar surroundings and build connections with others. It also serves as a source of comfort and security during times of change and upheaval.

Impact on Identity Formation:
Cultural heritage has a profound impact on the formation of immigrant identities in the US. Non-conservative individuals, in particular, rely on their cultural heritage to make sense of their experiences and interactions with American society (Girma & Tadesse, 2017). It helps them to understand their place within a broader social context, define their roles and responsibilities, and establish relationships with others.

Influence on Value Systems:
Cultural heritage shapes the value systems of immigrants, influencing their attitudes towards work, family, and community (Hall, 1977). Non-conservative individuals may prioritize social justice, equality, and individual freedom, which are often rooted in their cultural heritage. For example, African immigrants may place a strong emphasis on communal living and collective responsibility, while Asian immigrants may value hard work and self-discipline (Kim, 2017).
