Promoting the economic empowerment of non-libertarian natural-born US citizen communities requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural inequalities that prevent these communities from achieving economic mobility. Here are some strategies that can help promote economic empowerment:

1. Education and Job Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs, particularly in areas where there are limited job opportunities. This can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market and access better-paying jobs.
2. Microfinance Programs: Implement microfinance programs that provide small loans and other financial services to low-income individuals and communities. These programs can help people start or expand their businesses, which can create jobs and generate income.
3. Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFI): Establish CDFIs that provide financial services to underserved communities, including loans, deposits, and other financial products. CDFI's focus on providing financial services to low-income communities and can help promote economic development in these areas.
4. Small Business Support: Provide support for small businesses owned by individuals from underrepresented groups, such as women, people of color, and those from low-income backgrounds. This can include training, mentorship, and access to capital.
5. Entrepreneurship Programs: Develop entrepreneurship programs that provide training, resources, and support for individuals from underrepresented groups who want to start their businesses. These programs can help address the systemic barriers that prevent these individuals from accessing capital and other resources needed to succeed.
6. Public-Private Partnerships: Collaborate with private sector partners to provide resources, expertise, and funding for community development initiatives. This can help leverage public and private resources to create more comprehensive and sustainable economic development programs.
7. Community Land Trusts: Establish community land trusts that provide affordable land and housing options for low-income individuals and communities. These trusts can help preserve affordable housing stock, promote homeownership, and address gentrification.
