Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for temporary visa holders requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some suggestions to make these events more welcoming and enjoyable for progressive individuals:

1. Understand the diversity of your guests: Temporary visa holders come from diverse backgrounds, cultures, and identities. Ensure that your cultural celebration reflects this diversity by including a variety of performances, food, and decorations that cater to different tastes and preferences.
2. Provide interpretation and translation services: Offer interpretation and translation services during the event to ensure that all guests can fully participate and enjoy the festivities. This can include providing headsets or using visual aids for those who may not speak the dominant language in your area.
3. Incorporate cultural elements from different countries: Celebrate the diverse cultures of your temporary visa holders by incorporating elements from their home countries into the event. This can include traditional music, dance, clothing, and decorations that reflect each individual's culture.
4. Foster a sense of community: Encourage guests to mingle and connect with one another during the event. Provide opportunities for people to share their stories, traditions, and cultures through interactive activities or workshops.
5. Address accessibility concerns: Ensure that your cultural celebration is accessible to all guests, regardless of any disabilities they may have. Provide accommodations such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, or Braille materials for those who need them.
6. Offer vegetarian and vegan options: Temporary visa holders may have different dietary preferences or restrictions due to cultural or religious reasons. Offer vegetarian and vegan options at the event to ensure that everyone can enjoy the food without any concerns.
7. Create a safe space: Establish a safe space for temporary visa holders to share their experiences, challenges, and triumphs without fear of judgment or discrimination. This can be done through designated workshops or activities during the event.
8. Foster cross-cultural understanding: Encourage guests to learn about each other's cultures by providing educational materials or interactive activities that promote cultural exchange and mutual respect.
