The impact of disability status on the lives of permanent resident independent people can be significant and far-reaching. Here are some potential effects:

1. Social isolation: People with disabilities may face barriers in accessing public spaces, transportation, and social events, leading to social isolation and reduced opportunities for social interaction.
2. Employment discrimination: Discrimination in the workplace can limit job opportunities and career advancement for people with disabilities, leading to lower earning potential and economic independence.
3. Healthcare disparities: People with disabilities may face barriers in accessing healthcare services, including lack of accessibility in medical facilities, inadequate training for healthcare providers, and inadequate support for self-management of chronic conditions.
4. Stigma and stigmatization: Social stigma and discrimination can lead to feelings of shame, low self-esteem, and mental health problems among people with disabilities.
5. Lack of representation and voice: People with disabilities may be underrepresented in decision-making processes, leading to a lack of understanding and support for their needs and concerns.
6. Limited access to education and training: Inaccessible educational facilities and inadequate support services can limit the ability of people with disabilities to access quality education and training opportunities.
7. Financial stress: The additional costs of living with a disability, such as assistive technology, adaptations to the home, and specialized transportation, can lead to financial strain and stress.
8. Limited independence: People with disabilities may face barriers in achieving independence, including inaccessible housing, transportation, and community services, which can limit their ability to live independently.
9. Lack of autonomy: People with disabilities may have limited control over their own lives, including making decisions about their care and support, due to a lack of access to information and decision-making processes.
10. Limited social participation: People with disabilities may face barriers in participating in social activities, including sports, clubs, and community groups, due to inaccessible facilities or discrimination.
11. Mental health impacts: The experiences of discrimination, stigma, and marginalization can lead to mental health problems, such as depression
