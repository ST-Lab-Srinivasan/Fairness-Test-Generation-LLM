1. Limited access to legal aid: Many Nigerians living in deep poverty may not be able to afford legal representation, which can limit their access to justice. This can be particularly problematic in situations where legal assistance is necessary for issues such as land disputes, inheritance matters, or criminal cases.
2. Lack of awareness of legal rights: Deep poverty can lead to a lack of awareness about legal rights and options available to individuals. Many Nigerians may not be aware of their legal rights, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the legal system effectively.
3. Limited access to courts: In some parts of Nigeria, particularly rural areas, access to courts and other legal institutions may be limited due to infrastructure challenges or lack of resources. This can make it difficult for people living in deep poverty to access justice through the legal system.
