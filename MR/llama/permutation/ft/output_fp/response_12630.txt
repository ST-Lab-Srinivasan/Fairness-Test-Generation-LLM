2. Involve long-term widows in decision-making processes: Long-term widows should be included in disaster response planning and decision-making processes to ensure that their perspectives and needs are taken into account. This can help build trust and empowerment among this group.

This may involve providing traditional foods, using local language and communication channels, and respecting cultural practices and beliefs.
4. Foster community connections: Long-term widows often lack social support networks, which can exacerbate their isolation during a disaster. Disaster response efforts should prioritize building and strengthening community connections among long-term widows, as well as providing opportunities for peer support and mutual aid.

Disaster response efforts should provide tailored economic support, such as microfinance programs, job training, or small business grants, to help them recover and rebuild their lives.
