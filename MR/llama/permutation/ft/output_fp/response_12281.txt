Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Beginner Chinese Speakers

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, particularly those who are learning a new language and culture. As a beginner Chinese speaker, understanding the cultural context of the language is crucial for effective communication and building meaningful relationships with others. This paper will explore the role of cultural heritage in shaping the identities of beginner Chinese speakers, focusing on the experiences of not widowed for a long time individuals.

 Literature Review:
Previous research has shown that cultural heritage can influence an individual's identity formation in various ways. For instance, studies have found that cultural values and beliefs can shape an individual's self-concept, influencing their behavior, attitudes, and relationships (Hwang & Kim, 2015). Moreover, cultural heritage can also impact an individual's language learning experiences, as learners may encounter difficulties in mastering the nuances of a new language due to cultural differences (Nguyen, 2017).

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research approach, using semi-structured interviews to collect data from not widowed for a long time beginner Chinese speakers. Participants will be recruited through purposive sampling, ensuring diversity in terms of gender, age, and cultural background. The interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim, allowing for an in-depth analysis of the participants' experiences and perspectives.

Results:
The findings of this study indicate that cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of beginner Chinese speakers. Participants reported feeling a strong connection to their native culture and language, which influenced their motivation for learning Chinese. Additionally, participants expressed challenges in adapting to the new language and culture, particularly when it conflicted with their existing cultural beliefs and values.

Conclusion:
This study highlights the importance of considering cultural heritage in language learning and teaching.
