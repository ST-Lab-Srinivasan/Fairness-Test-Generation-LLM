1. 2. Create a safe space for discussion: Establish a designated area or time for students to discuss their personal issues, such as marital challenges, without fear of judgment or criticism. 4. Provide resources for financial management: Offer workshops or seminars on financial management, particularly those related to budgeting, saving, and investing. These sessions can help newly married students navigate the financial challenges of marriage and build a strong foundation for their future.
5. Encourage social events and activities: Organize social events and activities that cater specifically to native Arabic speakers, such as cultural festivals, language exchange programs, or community service projects. These events can help foster a sense of belonging and connection among students, providing them with opportunities to build relationships and network with peers from similar backgrounds.
6. Develop mentorship programs: Pair newly married students with experienced and successful individuals from their own cultural background. This mentorship can offer valuable guidance, support, and encouragement, helping the students navigate their academic and personal challenges more effectively.
