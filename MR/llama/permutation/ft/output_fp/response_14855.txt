Volunteerism plays a significant role in supporting non-libertarian communities, both above and below the poverty line. Here are some ways in which volunteerism can make a positive impact:

1. Supplementing government services: In many areas, government resources are limited, and volunteers can help bridge the gap by providing additional support to marginalized communities. For example, volunteers may assist with food banks, after-school programs, or health clinics.
2. Building community connections: Volunteering can help build social connections within a community, fostering a sense of belonging and social cohesion. This can be particularly important in areas where there are high levels of poverty, unemployment, or social isolation.
3. Addressing systemic issues: Volunteers can work to address the root causes of social and economic problems by advocating for policy changes, supporting community organizing efforts, or providing educational resources. For example, volunteers may work with local organizations to promote affordable housing, access to healthcare, or job training programs.
4. Providing emotional support: Volunteering can also provide emotional support to individuals and families who are struggling with poverty or other challenges. By offering a listening ear, a shoulder to cry on, or a smile, volunteers can help people feel less isolated and more connected to their community.
5. Encouraging civic engagement: Volunteering can encourage civic engagement by providing opportunities for people to get involved in their communities and make a positive impact. This can help build a sense of civic responsibility and encourage people to take an active role in shaping their community's future.
6. Developing skills and talents: Volunteering can provide individuals with the opportunity to develop new skills and talents, such as leadership, communication, or problem-solving. These skills can be transferred to other areas of life, including the workplace, and can help individuals advance in their careers.
7. Fostering intergenerational connections: Volunteering can provide opportunities for older adults to connect with younger generations, sharing knowledge, wisdom, and experiences. This can help build bridges between different age groups and create a stronger sense of community.
