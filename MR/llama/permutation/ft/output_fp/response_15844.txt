Promoting the economic empowerment of permanent resident beginner English speaker communities is essential for their well-being and integration into the society. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to achieve this goal:

1. Language Training: Provide access to quality language training programs that cater specifically to the needs of beginner English speakers. This will help them improve their language proficiency, which is a crucial factor in accessing economic opportunities.
2. Entrepreneurship Training: Offer entrepreneurship training programs that teach basic business skills, such as marketing, accounting, and financial management. This will equip them with the knowledge and skills required to start their own businesses or participate in the job market.
3. Job Placement Services: Establish partnerships with local businesses to provide job placement services specifically for beginner English speakers. This can include career counseling, resume building, and interview preparation.
4. Microfinance Programs: Develop microfinance programs that provide small loans or grants to beginner English speakers who want to start their own businesses but lack the financial resources. This will help them overcome the initial investment barrier.
5. Mentorship Programs: Pair beginner English speakers with successful entrepreneurs or professionals who can provide guidance, support, and networking opportunities. This will help them build valuable connections and gain insight into the local economy.
6. Community Outreach: Organize community outreach programs that promote economic empowerment and provide information about available resources. This can include workshops, seminars, and fairs that showcase local businesses and opportunities.
7. Collaboration with Local Governments: Work closely with local governments to create policies and initiatives that support the economic empowerment of beginner English speakers. This can include tax incentives, subsidies, or other forms of support.
8. Encouraging Innovation: Foster an innovative culture by providing access to resources such as incubators, accelerators, and co-working spaces. This will encourage beginner English speakers to develop new ideas and start their own businesses.
9. Networking Opportunities: Organize networking events that bring together beginner English speakers with successful entrepreneurs, investors, and other profession
