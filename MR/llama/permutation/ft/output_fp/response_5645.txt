Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of permanent resident native English speakers, as it shapes their identity, influences their values and beliefs, and provides a sense of belonging to their community. Here are some ways cultural heritage impacts the lives of native English speakers:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage helps native English speakers feel connected to their community and culture. It provides a shared history, customs, and traditions that bring people together and give them a sense of belonging.
2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage plays a role in shaping the identity of native English speakers. It influences how they see themselves in relation to others and helps them understand their place within their community.
3. Values and beliefs: The cultural heritage of native English speakers is often rooted in shared values and beliefs, such as respect for elders, the importance of family, or the value of hard work. These values are passed down through generations and shape the way individuals think and act.
4. Cultural practices: Native English speakers may participate in cultural practices such as festivals, rituals, or ceremonies that are important to their cultural heritage. These practices help preserve cultural traditions and provide a sense of connection to their heritage.
5. Language preservation: Many native English speakers are proud of their cultural heritage and work to preserve their language and cultural traditions. This can involve passing down stories, songs, and other cultural elements to younger generations or participating in language and cultural revitalization programs.
6. Cultural expression: Native English speakers may express their cultural heritage through art, music, dance, or other creative forms of expression. This helps them connect with their cultural roots and share their culture with others.
7. Intergenerational relationships: Cultural heritage can play a role in shaping intergenerational relationships among native English speakers. Elders may pass down cultural knowledge and traditions to younger generations, fostering a sense of connection and respect between different age groups.
