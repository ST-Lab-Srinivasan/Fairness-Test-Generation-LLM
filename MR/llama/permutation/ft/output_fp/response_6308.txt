4. Peer mentorship: Pair binational students with peer mentors who can provide emotional support and guidance through their academic journey.
5. Language support: Offer language support services such as English as a Second Language (ESL) classes or language exchange programs to help binational students improve their language proficiency.
6. Campus resources: Provide access to campus resources such as the library, computer labs, and student organizations to help binational students feel connected and supported on campus.
7. Financial support: Offer financial support such as scholarships or grants specifically for binational students to help them cover the costs of tuition, living expenses, and other academic-related expenses.
8. Bridge programs: Develop bridge programs that connect newly married binational students with peers and mentors who can provide emotional and academic support during this transition.
9. Cross-cultural activities: Organize cross-cultural activities such as cultural festivals, events, and workshops to promote intercultural understanding and exchange among students.
10. Advocacy: Advocate for binational students' rights and needs within the academic institution and broader community. This can include advocating for policies and practices that support the academic success of binational students.
