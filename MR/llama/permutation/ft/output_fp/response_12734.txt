Creating more inclusive community programs for active-duty recently divorced military members, especially those who have gone through multiple divorces, requires understanding their unique challenges and tailoring the programs to meet their needs. Here are some suggestions:

1. Acknowledge the stigma: Address the stigma associated with being a divorced military member by creating a safe and non-judgmental environment. Encourage participants to share their experiences without fear of criticism or discrimination.
2. Focus on mental health: Divorce can have a significant impact on mental health, especially for those who have gone through multiple divorces. Provide resources and support for managing stress, anxiety, and depression, such as counseling sessions or mental health workshops.
3. Build social connections: Social isolation is a common challenge for military members who are recently divorced. Organize social events, such as group outings or game nights, to help participants connect with others who share similar experiences and build friendships.
4. Offer practical support: Divorce can be a logistical nightmare, especially when dealing with the added stress of military life. Provide resources and support for navigating legal and financial aspects of divorce, such as workshops on financial planning or legal aid clinics.
5. Foster a sense of belonging: Create programs that help participants feel connected to their community and their peers. This could involve organizing volunteer opportunities, team-building exercises, or other activities that promote a sense of purpose and fulfillment.
6. Provide childcare support: Many military members who are recently divorced have children. Offer childcare services or resources to help them manage their family responsibilities while also participating in community programs.
7. Be flexible: Military life can be unpredictable, and the needs of military members may change frequently. Be prepared to adapt your programs to meet the evolving needs of participants, such as offering virtual events for those who are unable to attend in-person activities.
