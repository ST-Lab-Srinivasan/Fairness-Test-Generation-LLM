Supporting the social inclusion of Nigerian leftists can involve a variety of strategies and actions. Here are some possible ways to promote social inclusion:

1. Listen and amplify their voices: Create spaces for Nigerian leftists to share their experiences, perspectives, and ideas. Use your platform to amplify their voices and promote their work to a wider audience.
2. Educate and raise awareness: Provide educational resources and workshops on topics such as social justice, inequality, and human rights. Raise awareness about the challenges faced by Nigerian leftists and the importance of inclusive policies.
3. Advocate for policy changes: Work with local organizations and advocacy groups to push for policy changes that promote social inclusion and address the root causes of inequality. This can include advocating for affordable housing, access to education and healthcare, and an end to discrimination based on race, gender, or sexual orientation.
4. Support community-based initiatives: Identify and support local initiatives that promote social inclusion and address the needs of marginalized communities. This can include community gardens, arts programs, or sports teams that bring people together and provide opportunities for social interaction.
5. Build bridges with other groups: Collaborate with other organizations and groups that share similar goals to build a broad coalition of support. This can help to amplify the voices of Nigerian leftists and create a stronger movement for social change.
6. Provide mental health support: Many Nigerian leftists have experienced trauma and stress as a result of their political activism or exposure to violence. Provide mental health support and resources to help them cope with these challenges.
7. Create safe spaces: Establish safe spaces for Nigerian leftists to gather, share ideas, and organize without fear of reprisal. This can include physical spaces such as community centers or online spaces such as social media groups or forums.
8. Encourage dialogue and debate: Facilitate open and respectful dialogue between Nigerian leftists and other groups, encouraging the sharing of perspectives and ideas. This can help to build bridges and promote mutual understanding and respect.
9. Support economic empowerment: Many Nigerian leftists face economic challenges, including unemployment and poverty
