Ensuring the healthcare needs of active-duty very old (90s+) people requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique challenges and requirements. Here are some strategies to help meet their healthcare needs:

1. Early Assessment and Planning: Conduct regular assessments of veterans in their 90s to identify potential health issues and create personalized care plans. This helps ensure timely interventions, reduces the risk of complications, and improves quality of life.
2. Multidisciplinary Care Teams: Assemble a team of healthcare professionals, including gerontologists, primary care physicians, specialists, social workers, and other experts, to provide holistic care. This team can help manage chronic conditions, address functional limitations, and provide emotional support.
3. Home-Based Care: Provide home-based care services whenever possible, as this is often preferable for older veterans who may have mobility or cognitive challenges. Home health aides, hospice care, and palliative care can help manage symptoms, address safety concerns, and provide emotional support.
4. Telehealth Services: Leverage telehealth technologies to remotely monitor and provide care to older veterans, especially those living in remote areas or with mobility challenges. This can include video consultations, remote monitoring of vital signs, and communication with family members or caregivers.
5. Caregiver Support: Offer training and support to family members or caregivers who are providing care for older veterans. This can help them manage the emotional and practical aspects of caregiving, reduce stress, and improve their own health and well-being.
6. Community Resources: Connect older veterans with local resources, such as meal delivery programs, transportation services, and social activities, to support their overall health and well-being.
7. Mental Health Support: Provide mental health services, including counseling and psychotherapy, to help older veterans cope with stress, anxiety, or depression. This can also include support groups for family members or caregivers.
