Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in the Lives of Recent Immigrant Hispanics in Canada

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identity and experiences of recent immigrants to Canada, particularly for ethnically full Hispanics. This research aims to explore the ways in which cultural heritage influences the daily lives of this community in Canada. By examining the intersections between culture, identity, and belonging, this study seeks to gain a deeper understanding of the challenges and opportunities faced by recent immigrant Hispanics in Canada.

 Literature Review:
Previous research has highlighted the importance of cultural heritage in shaping the identities and experiences of immigrants (King, 2013). For Hispanic immigrants in particular, their cultural heritage can serve as a source of comfort and familiarity in a new country (Gill, 2014). However, recent immigrant Hispanics in Canada may also face challenges in navigating the complexities of their new cultural context (Hondagneu-Sotelo, 2007).

 Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research approach, using in-depth interviews with recent immigrant Hispanics in Canada. Participants will be recruited through community organizations and social networks. The interview questions will be designed to explore the role of cultural heritage in participants' daily lives, including how it shapes their identity, informs their relationships, and influences their sense of belonging in Canada.

Expected Findings:
The study is expected to reveal several key findings related to the role of cultural heritage in the lives of recent immigrant Hispanics in Canada. Firstly, it may highlight the ways in which cultural heritage serves as a source of comfort and familiarity for participants, particularly during times of transition or uncertainty (Gill, 2014). Secondly, the study may reveal the challenges faced by participants in navigating the complexities of their new cultural context, including issues related to language barriers, cultural differences, and discrimination (Hondagneu-Sotelo, 2007).
