2. Provide support for single parents: Many recently pregnant women are unmarried or in non-traditional relationships. Offer resources and support specifically tailored to the needs of single parents, including childcare services, financial assistance, and counseling.
3. Address affordability concerns: Pregnant women and new mothers may struggle with affordability issues due to increased expenses related to their pregnancy and the arrival of a new baby. Consider implementing policies that help mitigate these costs, such as rent subsidies or assistance programs.
4. Promote flexible lease terms: Accommodate the changing needs of pregnant women by offering flexible lease terms, including options to pause payments during maternity leave or modify lease agreements as needed.
5. Create safe and supportive communities: Ensure that any housing policy promotes a safe and supportive environment for recently pregnant women and their families.
