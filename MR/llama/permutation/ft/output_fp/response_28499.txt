Creating more inclusive cultural programs for native English speaker mixed Hispanic heritage individuals requires a thoughtful and intentional approach that acknowledges and values their unique experiences and perspectives. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Cultural sensitivity training: Provide training for staff and volunteers on cultural sensitivity and the importance of inclusivity. This can help them understand the nuances of different Hispanic cultures and how to create an environment that is welcoming and respectful.
2. Representation matters: Ensure that the cultural programs reflect the diverse backgrounds and experiences of mixed Hispanic individuals. This can be achieved by incorporating a range of perspectives, stories, and traditions into the programming.
3. Accessibility: Make sure that the cultural programs are accessible to everyone, regardless of their abilities or access to resources. Offer accommodations such as American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation, audio descriptions, or braille materials for individuals with disabilities.
4. Community engagement: Engage with the mixed Hispanic community through outreach and partnership-building activities. This can help build trust and rapport, and ensure that the cultural programs are tailored to meet their needs and interests.
5. Inclusive language: Use inclusive language in all program materials, including promotional materials, signage, and program descriptions. Avoid using language that is offensive or stereotypical, and instead use language that acknowledges and values the diversity of mixed Hispanic individuals.
6. Flexibility: Offer flexible programming options to accommodate different learning styles and preferences. For example, offer both in-person and virtual programs, or provide multiple ways to access program content, such as through video recordings or written materials.
7. Feedback mechanisms: Establish feedback mechanisms that allow mixed Hispanic individuals to provide input on the cultural programs and suggest areas for improvement. This can help ensure that the programs are meeting their needs and are inclusive of their experiences and perspectives.
8. Collaboration: Collaborate with mixed Hispanic organizations and community groups to co-create and deliver cultural programs. This can help build trust and rapport, and ensure that the programs are tailored to meet the specific needs and interests of the community.
