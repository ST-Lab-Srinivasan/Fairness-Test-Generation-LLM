Supporting the economic empowerment of immigrants, regardless of their socioeconomic status or citizenship status, is crucial for building a more inclusive and equitable society. Here are some ways to support the economic empowerment of not just above poverty line immigrant natural-born US citizen individuals:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs that help immigrants acquire new skills and knowledge to improve their employability. This can include language classes, vocational training, and job readiness programs.
2. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship Support: Offer microfinance options and entrepreneurship support to help immigrant-owned businesses thrive. This can include access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities.
3. Job Placement Services: Connect immigrants with job opportunities that match their skills and experience. This can include job fairs, resume building workshops, and interview preparation.
4. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to affordable healthcare for immigrants, including mental health services, medical check-ups, and prescription assistance programs.
5. Legal Support: Provide free or low-cost legal services to help immigrants navigate the complex US immigration system, including citizenship application processes and deportation defense.
6. Advocacy and Community Building: Advocate for policies that support immigrant economic empowerment and build community among immigrants. This can include organizing cultural events, hosting town halls, and creating opportunities for immigrants to connect with one another.
7. Addressing Discrimination and Xenophobia: Work to address discrimination and xenophobia faced by immigrants in the US, including through education and awareness-raising campaigns.
8. Immigration Reform: Advocate for comprehensive immigration reform that addresses the root causes of economic inequality among immigrants, such as providing a pathway to citizenship for undocumented immigrants and addressing the backlog in the legal immigration system.
9. Access to Affordable Housing: Ensure access to affordable housing options for immigrants, including through partnerships with landlords and property owners.
