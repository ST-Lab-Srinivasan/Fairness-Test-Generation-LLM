There are several successful community initiatives led by beginner Arabic speakers from lower middle-income backgrounds. Here are a few examples:

1. Language Exchange Programs: Many beginners have started language exchange programs in their local communities, where they teach Arabic to others in exchange for learning the language themselves. These programs have been successful in promoting language learning and cultural exchange among community members.
2. Cultural Events: Lower middle-income individuals have organized cultural events such as poetry readings, music performances, and film screenings to promote Arabic culture and language. These events have brought together people from different backgrounds and have helped to create a sense of community and shared identity.
3. Language Schools: Some beginners have started their own language schools in their local communities, offering classes in Arabic for adults and children. These schools have been successful in providing high-quality language instruction and promoting language learning in the community.
4. Community Outreach Programs: Lower middle-income individuals have also organized community outreach programs, such as distribution of educational materials, organizing workshops and seminars, and establishing libraries and resource centers. These programs have helped to promote Arabic language and culture in the local community and have provided opportunities for people to learn and engage with the language.
5. Social Media Groups: Many beginners have created social media groups focused on promoting Arabic language and culture, where they share resources, offer language practice sessions, and provide support to one another in learning the language. These groups have been successful in creating a sense of community and connection among learners and have helped to promote language learning and cultural exchange.
6. Language and Culture Camps: Some beginners have organized language and culture camps, where they teach Arabic and provide opportunities for people to immerse themselves in the language and culture. These camps have been successful in promoting language learning and cultural exchange among participants.
