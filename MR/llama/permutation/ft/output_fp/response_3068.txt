Promoting the inclusion of mixed African-American active-duty individuals in casual athletics can be achieved through a combination of cultural sensitivity, education, and policy changes. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural Sensitivity Training: Provide training for coaches, teammates, and other athletes on the importance of inclusion and respecting the identity and experiences of mixed African-American individuals. This can help create a more inclusive and welcoming environment.
2. Education and Awareness: Educate athletes and the broader community about the experiences and challenges faced by mixed African-American individuals, including their historical and contemporary contexts. This can help promote empathy and understanding.
3. Policy Changes: Review and update existing policies to ensure they are inclusive and supportive of mixed African-American athletes. For example, consider implementing policies that address discrimination, harassment, and microaggressions.
4. Representation Matters: Ensure that mixed African-American individuals are represented in leadership positions, coaching staffs, and athlete mentorship programs. This can help create a sense of belonging and role modeling for others.
5. Safe Spaces: Create safe spaces for mixed African-American athletes to share their experiences and feelings without fear of judgment or reprisal. This can help build trust and community.
6. Mentorship Programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair mixed African-American athletes with experienced coaches, athletes, or other role models who understand their experiences and can provide guidance and support.
7. Partner With Community Organizations: Collaborate with community organizations that support mixed African-American individuals to promote inclusion and address any barriers to participation.
8. Focus on Accessibility: Ensure that athletic facilities and programs are accessible and inclusive for mixed African-American athletes, including those with disabilities or other accessibility needs.
9. Celebrate Diversity: Celebrate the diversity of mixed African-American individuals through events, programming, and marketing campaigns that highlight their experiences and achievements.
10. Continuous Improvement: Regularly assess and improve policies, practices, and programs to ensure they are inclusive and supportive of mixed African-American athletes.
