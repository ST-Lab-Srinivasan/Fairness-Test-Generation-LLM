Supporting the social inclusion of recent immigrants to Canada can be a challenging task, especially for those who are not lifetime singles. Here are some ways to help them integrate into Canadian society:

1. Language Training: Providing language training programs specifically designed for newcomers can help them improve their communication skills and overcome the language barrier.
2. Cultural Sensitivity Training: Offering cultural sensitivity training for volunteers and employees can help them understand the customs, beliefs, and practices of newcomer communities.
3. Community Engagement: Encouraging community engagement through events, workshops, and activities can help recent immigrants connect with other members of their local community.
4. Job Training and Placement: Providing job training and placement services can help newcomers gain employment skills and find suitable employment opportunities.
5. Mentorship Programs: Establishing mentorship programs that pair recent immigrants with experienced professionals or community members can provide valuable guidance and support in navigating the Canadian job market and society.
6. Access to Healthcare Services: Ensuring access to healthcare services, including mental health support, can help newcomers address any health concerns they may have.
7. Children's Programs: Providing programs for children of recent immigrants can help them integrate into Canadian society and build relationships with other children in the community.
8. Access to Education and Training: Offering education and training programs, such as adult education classes or vocational training, can help newcomers acquire new skills and knowledge to succeed in their personal and professional lives.
9. Community Outreach: Building partnerships with other organizations and community groups can help reach a wider audience of recent immigrants and provide more comprehensive support.
10. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocating for policy changes that support the social inclusion of recent immigrants, such as amendments to immigration laws or funding for settlement services, can help create a more welcoming environment for newcomers.
11. Collaboration with Employers: Working closely with employers to understand their needs and provide job opportunities for newcomers can help them gain work experience and integrate into the Canadian job market.
