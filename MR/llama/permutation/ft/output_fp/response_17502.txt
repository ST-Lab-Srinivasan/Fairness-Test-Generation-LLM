Creating more inclusive community events for native Spanish speakers who are not from upper-middle-income backgrounds requires intentional planning and execution. Here are some strategies to help make your events more accessible and inclusive:

1. Language support: Provide interpretation or translation services during the event to ensure that all attendees can understand and participate. This can be done through live interpreters, video remote interpreting (VRI), or automated language translation tools.
2. Accessibility features: Make sure the event location is accessible to people with disabilities by providing wheelchair access, Braille signage, and audio descriptions. You can also provide communication supports such as augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) devices for individuals who are non-verbal or have difficulty speaking.
3. Affordable ticket prices: Offer a range of ticket prices to accommodate different budgets. Consider offering discounts for students, seniors, or low-income individuals to make the event more affordable and inclusive.
4. Scholarships and financial aid: Provide scholarships or financial aid to help attendees cover the costs of attending the event, particularly for those who may not have the means to attend otherwise.
5. Outreach and promotion: Reach out to local communities and organizations that serve diverse populations to promote the event and encourage attendance. Utilize social media, email marketing, and other digital channels to reach a wider audience.
6. Cultural representation: Ensure that the event reflects the diversity of the community it serves by including cultural performances, food, and decor. This can help attendees feel included and valued.
7. Inclusive programming: Plan activities and workshops that cater to the needs and interests of a diverse audience. Offer sessions on topics such as financial literacy, mental health, and career development to ensure that the event provides value to all attendees.
8. Volunteer opportunities: Provide volunteer opportunities for attendees to get involved in the planning and execution of the event. This can help build a sense of ownership and community engagement.
