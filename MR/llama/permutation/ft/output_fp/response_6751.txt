Promoting the inclusion of Native natural-born US citizen civilian individuals in casual athletics can be done through several strategies:

1. Education and Awareness: Raise awareness about the importance of inclusivity in sports and the benefits of participation for Native Americans. Provide educational resources and workshops on cultural competency, diversity, and inclusion.
2. Partnerships with Local Communities: Collaborate with local Native American communities to develop initiatives that promote physical activity and sports participation. This can include establishing partnerships with tribal schools, community centers, and organizations.
3. Scholarships and Financial Support: Provide financial support and scholarships to Native American students who want to participate in sports but may face economic barriers. This can help alleviate financial burdens and ensure equal access to opportunities.
4. Cultural Exchange Programs: Organize cultural exchange programs that bring together Native American athletes with mainstream athletes. These programs can foster cross-cultural understanding, build bridges between communities, and promote mutual respect.
5. Mentorship and Leadership Opportunities: Offer mentorship and leadership opportunities to Native American youth in sports. This can help develop their skills, confidence, and sense of belonging within the athletic community.
6. Accessible Facilities and Equipment: Ensure that sports facilities and equipment are accessible and inclusive for Native American athletes. This can involve providing adapted sports equipment or modifying existing facilities to accommodate cultural preferences.
7. Inclusive Representation: Ensure that Native American athletes are represented in leadership positions within sports organizations, such as coaching, refereeing, or organizing events. This can help promote cultural diversity and break down barriers to participation.
8. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and engagement activities to promote physical activity and sports participation among Native American youth. This can involve organizing fun runs, clinics, or other events that cater to diverse needs and interests.
9. Collaboration with Tribal Governments: Work closely with tribal governments to develop initiatives that promote physical activity and sports participation within their communities. This can involve developing customized programs tailored to the specific needs and priorities of each tribe.
