Supporting the social inclusion of temporary visa holders, particularly those from non-English-speaking countries, is essential for creating a harmonious and inclusive society. Here are some ways to promote their social inclusion:

1. Language Support: Offer language classes or workshops specifically tailored for temporary visa holders. This can help them improve their English skills, which can lead to better integration in the community.
2. Cultural Events: Organize cultural events that showcase the diversity of the local community, including food festivals, music concerts, and art exhibitions. This can provide opportunities for temporary visa holders to engage with the local population and learn about each other's cultures.
3. Social Gatherings: Organize social gatherings or potlucks that bring together temporary visa holders and local residents. This can help build connections and friendships among the two groups.
4. Community Service: Encourage temporary visa holders to participate in community service activities, such as volunteering at local non-profit organizations or participating in neighborhood clean-up events. This can help them feel more connected to the community and promote social inclusion.
5. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair temporary visa holders with local residents who can provide guidance and support. This can help temporary visa holders navigate the local culture and build relationships with the community.
6. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that temporary visa holders have access to affordable healthcare services, including medical check-ups and emergency care. This can help them stay healthy and manage any health issues that may arise during their stay in the host country.
7. Housing Support: Provide temporary visa holders with information about affordable housing options, including shared accommodation or rental properties. This can help them find suitable housing and reduce stress related to accommodation.
