Supporting the economic empowerment of ethnically full African American non-upper-class individuals requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural inequalities that hinder their economic mobility. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and job training programs: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that equip individuals with the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and degree programs in high-demand fields such as technology, healthcare, and business.
2. Mentorship and networking: Connect individuals with successful professionals and entrepreneurs who can provide guidance, support, and opportunities for collaboration. This can help individuals build valuable connections and gain access to resources and opportunities that can help them advance in their careers.
3. Access to capital: Address the racial wealth gap by providing access to capital and financial resources that can help individuals start businesses or invest in existing ones. This can include microloans, venture capital, and crowdfunding platforms specifically designed for underrepresented entrepreneurs.
4. Business incubators and accelerators: Offer support services such as office space, mentorship, and networking opportunities to help individuals start and grow their businesses. These programs can provide valuable resources and connections that can help entrepreneurs overcome the challenges of starting a business.
5. Tax incentives and policy reforms: Advocate for policies that address systemic barriers and create more equitable economic opportunities. This can include tax incentives for small businesses owned by underrepresented groups, as well as policy reforms that address discrimination and promote greater access to credit and capital.
6. Community development initiatives: Invest in community development programs that provide resources and support to low-income neighborhoods and communities of color. This can include job training programs, small business incubators, and infrastructure investments that promote economic growth and opportunity.
7. Addressing systemic barriers: Advocate for policies and practices that address systemic barriers to economic empowerment, such as discrimination, bias, and inequality. This can include advocating for fair housing policies, education reform, and criminal justice reform.
