Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for Native English Speaker (NES) individuals requires careful consideration of their needs and preferences. Here are some suggestions to make your cultural celebrations more inclusive:

1. Representation Matters: Ensure that the celebration includes representation from diverse cultures, ethnicities, genders, ages, and abilities. This can be achieved by inviting guests from different backgrounds or incorporating elements from various cultures into the celebration.
2. Accessibility: Make sure the celebration is accessible to everyone, including those with disabilities. Provide accommodations such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, or braille materials.
3. Inclusive Language: Use inclusive language in all communication related to the celebration, avoiding language that may be offensive or exclusive. Be mindful of terminology and phrases that may be culturally sensitive or insensitive.
4. Respectful Appreciation: Show respect for the cultural traditions and practices of NES individuals by learning about their customs and beliefs. Avoid appropriating or exploiting these traditions without proper understanding or permission.
5. Involve NES Individuals in Decision-Making Processes: Involve NES individuals in decision-making processes related to the celebration, such as planning, organizing, or leading cultural activities. This will help create a sense of ownership and inclusivity.
6. Provide Cultural Education: Offer cultural education opportunities during the celebration, providing information about the customs, traditions, and practices of NES individuals. This can be done through workshops, presentations, or interactive activities.
7. Flexibility: Be flexible and accommodating to the needs of NES individuals. Be willing to adjust plans or activities to ensure everyone feels included and comfortable.
8. Collaboration: Collaborate with NES individuals in planning and organizing the celebration. This will help create a sense of ownership and inclusivity, as well as ensure that their needs are met.
9. Cultural Exchange: Encourage cultural exchange between different groups during the celebration. This can be done through interactive activities, such as food sharing, music performances, or dance workshops.
