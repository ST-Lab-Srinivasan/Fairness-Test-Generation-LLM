Divorce can have a significant impact on a person's career and employability, particularly for those who are recently divorced. Here are some of the common challenges that recently divorced civilian people may face in the job market:

1. Lack of confidence: Going through a divorce can be emotionally draining, leading to a lack of confidence and self-doubt. This can make it difficult to perform well in job interviews or at work.
2. Resume gaps: If the divorce resulted in a gap in employment, this can be perceived as a red flag by potential employers. It's essential to have a clear explanation for any resume gaps and to highlight any relevant skills or experiences acquired during that time.
3. Childcare responsibilities: Many recently divorced individuals may have assumed childcare responsibilities, which can impact their ability to work long hours or travel frequently. Employers may view this as a challenge when assessing an individual's suitability for a particular role.
4. Financial strain: Divorce can lead to financial strain, particularly if assets and debts were divided unequally. This can make it difficult to find a job that provides a stable income or to negotiate for better pay and benefits.
5. Stigma: Unfortunately, there may still be a stigma attached to divorce in some workplaces or industries. Recently divorced individuals may face discrimination or bias from colleagues or supervisors, which can impact their career advancement.
6. Career changes: Divorce can lead to a desire for a change in career direction. However, this can be challenging if the individual has invested significant time and energy into their current profession. It may be difficult to transition to a new field or industry without experiencing a loss of income or prestige.
