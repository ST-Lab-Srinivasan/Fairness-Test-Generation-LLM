There are many successful community initiatives led by Native American and Indigenous recent immigrants to the US and Canada. Here are a few examples:

1. Urban Indigenous Education Centre (UIEC): Based in Toronto, Ontario, UIEC is a grassroots organization that provides education and support to urban Indigenous youth. Founded by a group of Indigenous recent immigrants, the organization works to bridge the gap between Indigenous and non-Indigenous communities through cultural exchange programs, mentorship opportunities, and advocacy for Indigenous rights.
2. Native Youth Fitness (NYF): Located in Winnipeg, Manitoba, NYF is a non-profit organization that provides fitness and wellness programs to urban Indigenous youth. Founded by a young Indigenous woman who immigrated to Canada as a child, NYF aims to promote physical and mental health through traditional Indigenous exercises and nutrition education.
3. Red Rising: Based in Vancouver, British Columbia, Red Rising is a community organization that supports the mental health and well-being of Indigenous recent immigrants. Founded by an Indigenous woman who experienced discrimination and marginalization as a newcomer to Canada, Red Rising provides cultural programming, support groups, and advocacy for Indigenous rights.
4. The Native Education College: Located in Winnipeg, Manitoba, The Native Education College is a post-secondary institution that offers Indigenous-focused programs in fields such as education, business, and healthcare. Founded by a group of Indigenous recent immigrants who saw a need for culturally relevant programming, the college provides educational opportunities for Indigenous students and promotes cultural revitalization.
5. The Native Women's Association of Canada (NWAC): With locations across Canada, NWAC is a national organization that advocates for the rights and well-being of Indigenous women and their families. Founded by Indigenous recent immigrants who were committed to addressing the unique challenges faced by Indigenous women in Canada, NWAC provides cultural programming, support services, and policy advocacy.
