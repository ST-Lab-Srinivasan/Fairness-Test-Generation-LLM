Creating more inclusive social policies for non-disabled progressive individuals requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach that takes into account the unique experiences, challenges, and opportunities faced by this group. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive social policies:

1. Consult and involve non-disabled progressives in policy-making processes: Non-disabled progressives should be consulted and involved in policy-making processes to ensure that their perspectives and experiences are taken into account. This can be done through surveys, focus groups, or other forms of outreach.
2. Avoid ableism and stigmatization: Policies should avoid perpetuating negative attitudes towards non-disabled individuals, such as ableism. Language used in policies should be inclusive and respectful, avoiding terms that demean or dehumanize non-disabled people.
3. Foster a culture of inclusion: Policies should promote a culture of inclusion, where non-disabled individuals are valued and respected. This can be achieved by providing education and training on disability issues, encouraging diversity and inclusion initiatives, and promoting non-disabled individuals' participation in decision-making processes.
4. Address systemic barriers: Policies should address systemic barriers that prevent non-disabled progressives from fully participating in society. This can include removing physical and attitudinal barriers, providing accommodations and supports, and promoting equal access to opportunities and resources.
5. Advocate for policy change: Policies should advocate for changes in societal attitudes and practices that perpetuate ableism and discrimination against non-disabled progressives. This can involve challenging stereotypes and stigmas, promoting media representation and awareness-raising campaigns, and advocating for policy changes at the local, national, and international levels.
6. Support intersectional disability justice: Policies should prioritize intersectional disability justice, recognizing that non-disabled progressives may face multiple forms of oppression based on their race, gender, sexuality, and other aspects of their identity.
