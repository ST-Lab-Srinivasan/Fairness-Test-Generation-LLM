2. Limited Legal Knowledge: Many beginner English speakers may not have a thorough understanding of the legal system or the laws that govern it. This lack of knowledge can lead to confusion and frustration when dealing with legal issues.
3. Difficulty Accessing Legal Resources: For those who are not fluent in English, accessing legal resources such as court documents, legal aid organizations, and other support services can be challenging. This is especially true if these resources are only available in English.

5. Limited Access to Legal Representation: For those who are not fluent in English, finding a lawyer who speaks their native language can be difficult. This can limit their access to legal representation and make it harder to navigate the legal system.
6. Difficulty Navigating Court Procedures: The legal system has complex procedures that must be followed in order to navigate it successfully. For beginner English speakers, these procedures can be difficult to understand and follow, leading to confusion and delays.
