Supporting deep in poverty mentally disabled communities requires a comprehensive and multi-faceted approach that addresses the various barriers and challenges faced by these communities. Here are some effective strategies that can help:

1. Capacity Building: Build the capacity of community-based organizations (CBOs) and local governments to provide services and support to people with mental disabilities. This includes training on mental health issues, advocacy skills, and how to access resources and funding.
2. Advocacy and Awareness: Raise awareness about the needs and rights of people with mental disabilities within the community. This can be done through public campaigns, events, and media outreach. Advocate for their rights and involve them in decision-making processes that affect their lives.
3. Inclusive Education: Ensure that children with mental disabilities have access to inclusive education that meets their needs. This includes adapting curricula, providing support staff, and promoting inclusive teaching practices.
4. Vocational Training: Provide vocational training and job placement services for people with mental disabilities. This can help them develop skills and gain independence in their daily lives.
5. Health Services: Improve access to health services, including mental health services, for people with mental disabilities. This can include counseling, psychotherapy, and medication management.
6. Community Based Rehabilitation (CBR): CBR is a community-based approach that addresses the rehabilitation needs of people with disabilities. It involves identifying and mobilizing local resources, capacities, and initiatives to promote the full participation of people with disabilities in society.
7. Social Inclusion: Promote social inclusion by challenging stigmas and discrimination faced by people with mental disabilities. Encourage community members to accept and support people with mental disabilities, and provide opportunities for social interaction and integration.
8. Economic Empowerment: Support economic empowerment of people with mental disabilities through entrepreneurship training, microfinance schemes, and job creation initiatives. This can help them become financially independent and contribute to their communities.
9. Access to Technology: Provide access to assistive technologies, such as communication devices, wheelchairs, and other mobility aids
