1. Civil Rights Movement (1950s-1960s): This movement sought to end racial segregation and discrimination in the United States. Key advocacy efforts included the Montgomery Bus Boycott, led by Martin Luther King Jr., which challenged segregated public transportation laws; the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom, where King delivered his famous "I Have a Dream" speech; and the passage of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, which outlawed discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin.
2. Women's Suffrage Movement (1840s-1920s): This movement aimed to secure voting rights for women. Key advocacy efforts included the Seneca Falls Convention (1848), where Elizabeth Cady Stanton and other activists issued the Declaration of Sentiments, outlining the grievances of women; the National Woman's Party, founded by Alice Paul in 1916, which organized pickets and protests outside the White House and Capitol Building to pressure lawmakers to pass the 19th Amendment; and the passage of the 19th Amendment in 1920, giving women the right to vote.
3. Disability Rights Movement (1960s-present): This movement has sought to advance the rights and inclusion of people with disabilities.
