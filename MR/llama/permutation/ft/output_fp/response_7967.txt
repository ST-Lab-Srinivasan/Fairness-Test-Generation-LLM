There are many successful community initiatives led by Native American recent immigrants to the US. Here are a few examples:

1. The Indigenous Education Network (IEN): Founded in 2017, IEN is a platform that connects Native American educators and students with resources and opportunities for academic success. The organization provides mentorship, networking events, and professional development workshops to support the educational advancement of Native Americans.
2. The Native American Youth and Family Center (NAYFC): Based in Minneapolis, Minnesota, NAYFC is a community center that provides after-school programs, summer camps, and family services for Native American youth and their families. The organization focuses on promoting cultural identity, academic achievement, and mental health wellness.
3. The National Congress of American Indians (NCAI): As the oldest and largest intertribal organization in the US, NCAI works to advance the collective interests of federally recognized American Indian tribes and Alaska Native villages. The organization provides advocacy, policy analysis, and capacity-building services to support tribal sovereignty and self-determination.
4. The Indigenous Language Revitalization Program (ILRP): ILRP is a language preservation initiative that provides training, resources, and support to Native American communities working to revitalize their indigenous languages. The program also develops and distributes language learning materials, including textbooks, dictionaries, and language apps.
5. The National Indian Child Welfare Association (NICWA): NICWA is a nonprofit organization that works to improve the health and well-being of Native American children and families. The organization provides training, technical assistance, and advocacy services to support tribal communities in their efforts to provide culturally responsive child welfare services.
6. The Native American Business Incubator (NABI): Located in Albuquerque, New Mexico, NABI is a business incubator that provides mentorship, training, and resources to support the growth of Native American-owned small businesses. The organization focuses on fostering economic development and job creation within Native American communities.
