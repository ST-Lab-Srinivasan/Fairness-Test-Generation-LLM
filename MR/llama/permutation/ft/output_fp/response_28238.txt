Economic empowerment is a critical aspect of promoting financial stability and prosperity for individuals and communities. Supporting the economic empowerment of mixed African-American individuals just above the poverty line can be achieved through various strategies that focus on education, access to resources, and creating economic opportunities. Here are some ways to support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality educational programs, vocational training, and workshops that help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge. This can include digital literacy training, entrepreneurship courses, and financial management workshops.
2. Access to Capital and Resources: Connect individuals with resources such as microfinance initiatives, small business loans, and grants. These resources can help individuals start or expand their businesses, which can generate income and create jobs.
3. Networking and Mentorship: Pair individuals with successful professionals and entrepreneurs who can provide guidance, support, and mentorship. This can help individuals build connections, gain insights into the industry, and learn from others' experiences.
4. Job Training and Placement: Offer job training programs that equip individuals with the skills and knowledge required for in-demand jobs. Additionally, partner with local businesses to provide placement opportunities and help individuals find employment.
5. Community Development Initiatives: Support community development initiatives such as cooperatives, mutual aid societies, and other collective ownership structures. These can help individuals pool resources, share knowledge, and create economic opportunities together.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that promote economic empowerment for mixed African-American communities. This can include changes to laws and regulations that restrict access to education, credit, or job opportunities.
7. Tax Incentives and Benefits: Offer tax incentives and benefits to individuals who invest in their businesses or start new ones in underserved communities. This can help create jobs and stimulate economic growth.
8. Affordable Housing and Home Ownership: Support affordable housing initiatives and homeownership programs that provide access to decent and affordable housing. This can help individuals build wealth through homeownership and stabilize their living situations.
