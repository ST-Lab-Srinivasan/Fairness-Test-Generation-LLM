Supporting the career aspirations of not recently divorced active-duty individuals requires understanding their unique challenges and providing tailored resources and support. Here are some ways to achieve this:

1. Career counseling: Offer one-on-one career counseling sessions with trained military spouse employment specialists. These sessions can help identify strengths, set career goals, and create a plan for achieving them.
2. Professional development opportunities: Provide access to professional development workshops, conferences, and online training resources. This can include leadership training, skills-building courses, and networking events.
3. Job search support: Offer job search support services such as resume review, interview preparation, and connections to job leads. This can help individuals identify job opportunities that match their skills and interests.
4. Career exploration tools: Provide access to career exploration tools such as career assessments, personality tests, and job market information. These tools can help individuals identify potential career paths and make informed decisions about their future.
5. Networking opportunities: Facilitate networking opportunities through events, conferences, and online platforms. This can help individuals connect with other professionals in their field and learn about new job opportunities.
6. Mentorship programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair individuals with experienced professionals in their desired field. This can provide valuable guidance and support as individuals navigate their career path.
7. Education and training: Offer education and training opportunities to help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge. This can include certification programs, online courses, and degree programs.
8. Work-life balance support: Provide support for work-life balance, including flexible work arrangements, childcare resources, and financial planning services. This can help individuals manage the demands of their career and personal life.
9. Military spouse-friendly employers: Connect individuals with military spouse-friendly employers who understand the unique challenges faced by military families and are committed to providing supportive work environments.
10. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policy changes that address the career challenges faced by military families, such as the Military Spouse Career Advancement Accounts (My Career Advancement Accounts) program in the United States.
