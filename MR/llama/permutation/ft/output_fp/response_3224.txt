Lower-income natural-born Indian citizens face several challenges in the legal system, including:

1. Limited access to legal aid: Lower-income individuals may not have the financial resources to hire a lawyer or access legal aid services, making it difficult for them to navigate the legal system and protect their rights.
2. Language barriers: Many lower-income Indians may not speak the dominant language of the region they live in, which can create a barrier to accessing legal services and understanding court proceedings.
3. Lack of awareness about legal rights: Lower-income individuals may be unaware of their legal rights and obligations, which can lead to them being taken advantage of or exploited by those in positions of power.
4. Discrimination: Lower-income individuals from marginalized communities may face discrimination within the legal system, including biases among legal professionals and judges.
5. Limited access to justice: Lower-income individuals may have limited access to justice due to factors such as distance from courts, lack of transportation, or childcare responsibilities.
6. Lack of representation: Lower-income individuals may not have the means to hire a lawyer, which can lead to them being unrepresented in legal proceedings and at a disadvantage compared to those who are represented.
7. Unfamiliarity with legal procedures: Lower-income individuals may be unfamiliar with legal procedures and protocols, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the legal system.
8. Limited availability of legal remedies: Lower-income individuals may have limited access to legal remedies such as appeals or injunctions, which can limit their ability to seek redressal for grievances.
9. Unfair treatment in court: Lower-income individuals may face unfair treatment in court due to factors such as the perception of their socio-economic status, leading to unequal access to justice.
10. Lack of support from legal aid organizations: Lower-income individuals may not have access to legal aid organizations that can provide them with free or low-cost legal services and advice.
