Celebrating the cultural contributions of Native American, recent immigrants, and those living just above the poverty line can be done in various ways. Here are some suggestions:

1. Cultural Festivals: Organize cultural festivals that showcase the music, dance, art, and food of these communities. Invite local artists, musicians, and chefs to participate and perform traditional pieces.
2. Community Events: Host community events such as potlucks, picnics, or block parties that celebrate the diversity of these communities. Encourage participants to share their cultural traditions and practices.
3. Exhibitions: Organize exhibitions that highlight the cultural contributions of Native American, recent immigrant, and low-income communities. Showcase traditional clothing, art, and crafts, as well as modern works that reflect the diversity of these communities.
4. Storytelling Sessions: Host storytelling sessions where members of these communities can share their personal stories, experiences, and cultural traditions. This can be done through oral histories, video testimonials, or written accounts.
5. Performing Arts: Organize performances that showcase the traditional music, dance, and theater of Native American, recent immigrant, and low-income communities. Invite local performers to participate and collaborate with them on new works that reflect the diversity of these communities.
6. Food Festivals: Host food festivals that celebrate the culinary traditions of these communities. Invite local chefs and restaurateurs to share their recipes, cooking techniques, and cultural practices related to food.
7. Cultural Workshops: Offer workshops that teach traditional crafts, such as beading, weaving, or pottery, from Native American, recent immigrant, and low-income communities. Invite local artisans to share their skills and knowledge with participants.
