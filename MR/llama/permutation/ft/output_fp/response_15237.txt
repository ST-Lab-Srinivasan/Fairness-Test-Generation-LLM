1. The Global Partnership for Education (GPE) - Founded in 2002, GPE is a global partnership of governments, private sector companies, and non-governmental organizations that works to ensure that children around the world have access to quality education. GPE has helped over 50 million children in some of the world's poorest countries gain access to school and improve their learning outcomes.
2. The Malala Fund - Founded by Nobel Prize laureate Malala Yousafzai, the Malala Fund is a non-profit organization that works to provide girls around the world with access to education. The fund has helped over 30 million children gain access to school and has advocated for gender equality in education.
3. The Water Project - Founded in 2004, The Water Project is a non-profit organization that works to provide clean water and sanitation to communities in sub-Saharan Africa. The organization has helped over 1 million people gain access to clean water and has improved the health and well-being of entire communities.
4. The Nature Conservancy - Founded in 1951, The Nature Conservancy is a non-profit environmental organization that works to protect ecologically important lands and waters around the world. The organization has protected over 120 million acres of land and 5,000 miles of rivers, and has helped to preserve biodiversity and mitigate the effects of climate change.
5. The Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation - Founded in 2000, the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation is a philanthropic organization that works to improve global health and education outcomes. The foundation has donated billions of dollars to various causes, including vaccine development, malaria eradication, and support for smallholder farmers in Africa and Asia.
