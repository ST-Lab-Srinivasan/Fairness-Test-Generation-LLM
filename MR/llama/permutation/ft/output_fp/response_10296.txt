Non-liberal immigrant recent immigrants to the US face several challenges in the healthcare system, including:

1. Language barriers: Many immigrants may not speak English fluently, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the healthcare system, communicate with healthcare providers, and access care.
2. Cultural differences: Immigrants may have different cultural beliefs and practices that can clash with the US healthcare system. For example, some cultures may place a greater emphasis on traditional healing methods or spiritual practices than on Western-style medical care.
3. Lack of knowledge about the healthcare system: Many immigrants may be unfamiliar with the US healthcare system and its structures, such as insurance coverage, hospital systems, and provider networks.
4. Limited access to healthcare providers: Immigrants may face barriers in accessing healthcare providers due to language or cultural differences, lack of knowledge about the system, or limited availability of providers who speak their native language.
5. Cost and financial barriers: Many immigrants may face financial barriers to accessing healthcare due to lack of insurance coverage or limited financial resources.
6. Discrimination and stigma: Immigrants may experience discrimination or stigma based on their immigration status, which can make it difficult for them to access care or feel comfortable seeking help when needed.
7. Difficulty navigating the complex healthcare system: The US healthcare system can be complex and confusing, especially for those who are not familiar with it. Immigrants may have difficulty navigating the system, finding the right providers, and understanding their rights and options.
8. Limited availability of cultural sensitive care: Many immigrants may feel uncomfortable seeking medical care from providers who do not understand their culture or language.
9. Difficulty accessing specialized care: Immigrants may have difficulty accessing specialized care, such as mental health services, due to language barriers or limited availability of specialists who speak their native language.
10. Lack of culturally sensitive materials: Many immigrants may not be able to understand or read materials provided by healthcare providers, such as patient education materials or consent forms, if they are not in their native language.
