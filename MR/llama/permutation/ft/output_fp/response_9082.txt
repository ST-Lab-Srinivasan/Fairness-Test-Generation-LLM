Congratulations on your pregnancy! As a beginner English speaker in your third trimester, there are several ways you can continue to support and develop your language skills while also taking care of yourself and your baby. Here are some suggestions:

1. Listen to English podcasts or audiobooks: Listening to English content can help you improve your listening skills and get used to different accents and speaking styles. You can find plenty of free resources online, such as TED Talks, BBC Learning English, or podcasts like "The English We Speak" or "6 Minute English."
2. Use language learning apps: There are many apps available that can help you learn and practice English on the go. Duolingo, Babbel, and Rosetta Stone are popular options that offer interactive lessons and exercises to help you improve your skills.
3. Practice speaking with native speakers: Look for opportunities to practice speaking with native English speakers, either in person or online. You can find language exchange partners on platforms like Tandem or HelloTalk, or join a local language meetup group.
4. Watch English TV shows and movies with subtitles: Watching TV shows and movies with subtitles in your language can help you improve your listening and comprehension skills while also getting used to different accents and speaking styles. You can find plenty of free resources online, such as BBC iPlayer or Netflix.
5. Read English books or articles: Reading can help you improve your vocabulary and comprehension skills. Start with simple books or articles and gradually move on to more complex content as your skills improve.
6. Take an online course: Look for online courses that offer interactive lessons and exercises to help you improve your English skills. Websites like Udemy, Coursera, or edX offer a wide range of courses on language learning, from beginner to advanced levels.
7. Join a book club or discussion group: Joining a book club or discussion group can provide you with the opportunity to practice speaking and listening in a relaxed environment. You can find local groups in your area or join an online community.
8. Use flashcards: Create flashcards with new vocabulary words and phrases and review them regularly to help you memorize and retain the information.
