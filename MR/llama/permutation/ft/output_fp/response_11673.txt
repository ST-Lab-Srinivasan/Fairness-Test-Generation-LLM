As a PhD holder and a therapist, I understand the unique challenges that recently divorced nominal Christians face. Here are some suggestions on how to address their specific needs:

1. Understand their beliefs and values: It is essential to understand the individual's beliefs and values, which may have been shaped by their religious upbringing and experiences. This will help you tailor your approach to meet their emotional and spiritual needs.
2. Foster a safe and non-judgmental space: Create a comfortable and non-judgmental environment where the individual can express themselves without fear of criticism or rejection. This will help them feel secure enough to open up about their feelings and experiences.
3. Explore the impact of divorce on their faith: Discuss how the divorce has affected their beliefs and practices, and how they may be struggling with feelings of guilt, shame, or loss of faith. Help them find ways to reconcile their religious beliefs with their current situation.
4. Address spiritual and emotional needs: Nominal Christians may struggle with feelings of disconnection from God or a higher power after a divorce. Encourage them to engage in spiritual practices such as prayer, meditation, or journaling to help them find comfort and peace. Also, address any emotional issues that may be contributing to their distress, such as grief, anxiety, or depression.
5. Offer support and guidance: Provide emotional support and guidance as the individual navigates this challenging time. Help them identify their strengths and weaknesses, set realistic goals, and develop strategies for coping with the emotional aftermath of divorce.
6. Encourage self-reflection and personal growth: Divorce can be an opportunity for personal growth and reflection. Encourage the individual to explore their interests, values, and goals, and help them identify areas for personal development.
7. Provide resources and referrals: Offer resources and referrals that may be helpful in addressing the individual's specific needs, such as divorce support groups, counseling services, or spiritual direction.
