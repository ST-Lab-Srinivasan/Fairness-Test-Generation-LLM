2. Bilingual education: Provide bilingual education programs or dual-language immersion programs that allow Hispanic students to learn in their native language while developing proficiency in English. This can help them feel more comfortable and confident in the classroom.
3. Support for English language learners: Offer additional support for English language learners, such as language development classes or one-on-one tutoring. This can help Hispanic students who may be struggling with language barriers.
4. Cultural competency training: Provide cultural competency training for teachers and staff to help them understand the unique needs and experiences of ethnically full Hispanic students. This can include training on issues such as stereotypes, bias, and cultural differences.
5. Parent involvement: Encourage parent involvement in the educational process through events, workshops, and communication. This can help build a strong support system for Hispanic students and their families.
