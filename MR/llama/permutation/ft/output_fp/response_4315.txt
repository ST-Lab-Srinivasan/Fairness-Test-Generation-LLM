Promoting the financial stability of over 90-year-old native French speakers requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and needs. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Financial planning and education: Provide older adults with access to financial planners who specialize in working with seniors. These planners can help them create a comprehensive financial plan that takes into account their income, expenses, assets, and goals. Additionally, offer financial education workshops and resources to help them better understand financial concepts and make informed decisions about their money.
2. Income support: Ensure that older adults have access to a stable and adequate income stream, such as a pension or retirement savings. Encourage them to save for retirement and provide incentives for doing so, such as matching contributions or tax credits.
3. Asset protection: Help older adults protect their assets from financial threats, such as nursing home care or medical expenses. This can involve setting up asset protection trusts, transferring assets to family members or friends, or purchasing long-term care insurance.
4. Caregiver support: Provide support and resources for family members or friends who are acting as caregivers for older adults. This can include training on how to manage their loved one's financial affairs, access to legal and financial professionals, and respite care services to give caregivers a break.
5. Healthcare coordination: Ensure that older adults have access to comprehensive healthcare services, including preventive care, chronic disease management, and end-of-life care. Coordinate with healthcare providers to ensure that their financial needs are addressed and that they receive the care they need without facing financial hardship.
6. Housing support: Provide older adults with safe and affordable housing options, such as subsidized rent or homeownership programs. This can help them age in place and avoid costly moves to institutional settings.
7. Transportation assistance: Offer transportation services or resources that enable older adults to maintain their independence and mobility, such as non-emergency medical transportation or ride-sharing programs.
