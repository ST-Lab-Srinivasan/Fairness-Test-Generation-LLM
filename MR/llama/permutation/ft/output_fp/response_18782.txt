Promoting the artistic expression of gender-nonconforming working professional individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the intersections of gender, art, and work. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Create inclusive spaces: Ensure that artistic spaces are inclusive and welcoming for all genders. This can be achieved by actively seeking out and showcasing the work of gender-nonconforming artists, providing mentorship and support, and challenging traditional notions of gender in the art world.
2. Develop programs and initiatives: Design programs and initiatives that specifically cater to the needs and experiences of gender-nonconforming artists. This can include residencies, workshops, and exhibitions tailored to their unique perspectives and challenges.
3. Provide resources and funding: Offer financial support and resources to gender-nonconforming artists, such as grants, scholarships, and crowdfunding platforms. This can help them pursue their artistic passions without facing financial barriers.
4. Engage in advocacy and activism: Advocate for policies and practices that promote gender equality in the art world. This can involve challenging discriminatory practices, advocating for more inclusive hiring practices, and pushing for greater representation of gender-nonconforming artists in galleries and museums.
5. Create alliances and partnerships: Collaborate with organizations that support gender-nonconforming artists, such as LGBTQ+ centers, community groups, and arts organizations. These partnerships can help amplify the voices and work of gender-nonconforming artists and create more opportunities for them to showcase their talents.
6. Educate and raise awareness: Offer educational programs and workshops that teach people about the experiences and challenges faced by gender-nonconforming artists. This can help raise awareness and promote a more inclusive and supportive environment for these individuals.
7. Celebrate and amplify their voices: Highlight and celebrate the achievements of gender-nonconforming artists through various platforms, such as social media, podcasts, and blogs. By amplifying their voices, we can help create a more inclusive and supportive environment for these individuals.
