Cultural competence in healthcare is essential for ensuring that active-duty Native American natural-born US citizens receive quality and culturally sensitive care. Here are some benefits of cultural competence in healthcare for this population:

1. Improved health outcomes: Cultural competence can lead to better health outcomes for Native Americans as it enables healthcare providers to understand and address the unique cultural beliefs, values, and practices that affect their health. For example, traditional healing practices may be incorporated into medical care, leading to improved health outcomes.
2. Increased patient satisfaction: When healthcare providers are culturally competent, they are better able to understand and address the cultural nuances of Native American patients. This can lead to increased patient satisfaction and trust in the healthcare system.
3. Better communication: Cultural competence can facilitate better communication between healthcare providers and Native American patients. Healthcare providers who are knowledgeable about Native American culture can ask culturally appropriate questions, listen actively, and provide explanations that are tailored to the patient's cultural background.
4. Enhanced patient engagement: Cultural competence can encourage greater patient engagement in their healthcare. When patients feel that their cultural beliefs and practices are respected and valued, they are more likely to be engaged in their care and adhere to treatment plans.
5. Reduced health disparities: By addressing cultural barriers to healthcare, cultural competence can help reduce health disparities experienced by Native American populations. This includes disparities in access to care, quality of care, and health outcomes.
6. Improved patient safety: Cultural competence can also lead to improved patient safety. Healthcare providers who are knowledgeable about Native American culture can identify potential safety issues related to traditional healing practices or cultural beliefs and take appropriate measures to mitigate these risks.
7. Enhanced community partnerships: Cultural competence can foster stronger community partnerships between healthcare providers and Native American populations. This can lead to more effective collaboration on healthcare initiatives and improved health outcomes for the community as a whole.
