Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of permanent residents and recent immigrants to India. For many, their cultural heritage is an important part of their identity and sense of self. It can shape their values, beliefs, and practices, and provide a sense of belonging and connection to their ancestral homeland.

Here are some ways that cultural heritage can impact the lives of permanent residents and recent immigrants to India:

1. Sense of identity: Cultural heritage can help individuals define their identity and understand where they come from. For many, their cultural heritage is an important part of their sense of self.
2. Belonging: Connecting with one's cultural heritage can provide a sense of belonging to a larger community and culture. This can be especially important for recent immigrants who may be far away from their ancestral homeland.
3. Practices and traditions: Cultural heritage includes practices and traditions that are passed down through generations. These can include religious rituals, foodways, music, and other cultural expressions. For many, these practices and traditions are an important part of their daily lives.
4. Preservation of history: Cultural heritage helps to preserve the history and traditions of a community or society. This can be especially important for recent immigrants who may be interested in learning more about their ancestral culture and history.
5. Connection to ancestors: For many, cultural heritage provides a connection to their ancestors and the past. This can help individuals feel grounded and connected to something larger than themselves.
6. Community building: Cultural heritage can serve as a unifying force for communities of immigrants. Sharing cultural practices and traditions with others who have similar backgrounds can help build a sense of community and belonging.
7. Preservation of language: Many cultural heritages include languages that are important to preserving the culture. For recent immigrants, learning the language of their ancestral homeland can be an important way to connect with their cultural heritage.
8. Understanding of social norms: Cultural heritage can provide a framework for understanding social norms and expectations within a community or society. This can be especially important for recent immigrants who may be unfamiliar with the customs and traditions of their new home.
