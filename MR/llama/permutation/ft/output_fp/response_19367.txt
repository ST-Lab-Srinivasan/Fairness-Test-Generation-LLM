The success of recently retired veterans with no known genetic conditions can be significantly influenced by their family support. Here are some ways in which family support can impact a veteran's success:

1. Emotional Support: Having a supportive family can provide emotional stability and comfort during the transition from military to civilian life. This can help reduce stress and anxiety, which are common challenges faced by retired veterans.
2. Practical Assistance: Family members can offer practical assistance such as help with household chores, errands, or personal care. This can be especially important for veterans who may have difficulty completing tasks on their own due to physical or cognitive limitations.
3. Social Connection: Having a supportive family can provide a social network for the veteran, which can help combat loneliness and isolation. This is particularly important for veterans who may have difficulty forming new social connections in their civilian life.
4. Encouragement and Motivation: Family members can serve as sources of encouragement and motivation, helping the veteran to stay focused on their goals and aspirations. This can be especially important for veterans who may struggle with self-esteem or confidence issues.
5. Financial Support: In some cases, family members may provide financial support to help the veteran cover living expenses, pay off debts, or invest in education or job training.
6. Advocacy: Family members can act as advocates for the veteran, helping to navigate the complex healthcare and benefit systems available to them. This can include assisting with paperwork, attending appointments, or communicating with medical providers.
7. Coping Strategies: Family members can share coping strategies and techniques with the veteran, helping them to manage stress and anxiety related to their military experience or civilian life.
8. Role Modeling: Family members can serve as role models for the veteran, demonstrating healthy coping mechanisms and behaviors that the veteran can learn from and emulate.
9. Network of Support: A supportive family can provide a network of support that extends beyond the individual veteran. This can include connecting the veteran with other family members, friends, or community resources.
