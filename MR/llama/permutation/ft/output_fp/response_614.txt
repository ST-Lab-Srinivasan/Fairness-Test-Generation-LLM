5. Provide opportunities for interaction: Encourage pair work, group work, and communication with native speakers to help learners practice their language skills in a more immersive setting.
6. Use diverse teaching methods: Vary your teaching methods to accommodate different learning styles, such as visual, auditory, or kinesthetic. This can help keep learners engaged and motivated.

10. Be flexible and adaptable: Be willing to adjust your program based on feedback from learners and the changing needs of the community. This can help ensure that your program remains inclusive and relevant over time.
