Deep poverty refers to a state of income poverty that is particularly challenging to escape due to various barriers such as lack of access to education, job opportunities, and other resources. Native-born US citizens living in deep poverty face significant challenges in the healthcare system, including:

1. Limited access to healthcare services: Deeply poor individuals may struggle to afford health insurance or pay out-of-pocket for medical care, leading to delayed or skipped appointments, inadequate treatment, and worsening health outcomes.
2. Disparities in healthcare quality: Individuals from low-income backgrounds may experience poorer quality of care compared to their more affluent counterparts due to differences in access to resources, facilities, and specialized care.
3. Limited cultural competency: Healthcare providers may not fully understand the unique needs and beliefs of Native American communities, leading to misdiagnosis or inadequate treatment.
4. Lack of trust: Historical trauma and ongoing discrimination have contributed to a breakdown in trust between Native communities and the healthcare system, making it challenging for individuals to seek care or adhere to treatment plans.
5. Mental health concerns: The intersection of poverty, cultural trauma, and marginalization can lead to higher rates of mental health issues such as depression, anxiety, and substance abuse among Native communities.
6. Limited access to specialized care: Individuals living in deep poverty may face difficulties accessing specialized medical care, such as cardiology or oncology services, due to financial constraints or lack of availability in their area.
7. Inadequate preventative care: Deeply poor individuals may struggle to access routine medical care, such as vaccinations and screenings, which can lead to worsening health outcomes over time.
8. Limited health literacy: Individuals with limited access to education may have lower levels of health literacy, making it more challenging for them to navigate the complex healthcare system or make informed decisions about their care.
