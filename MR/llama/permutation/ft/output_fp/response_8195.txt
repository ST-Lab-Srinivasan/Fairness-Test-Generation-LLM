This includes using diverse teaching methods, materials, and assessments that reflect the students' experiences and perspectives.
2. Bilingual education programs: Providing bilingual education programs can help ethnically full Hispanic students who are limited English proficient to succeed academically. 3. Recruitment and retention of bilingual teachers: Ensuring that there are enough bilingual teachers who are fluent in the language spoken by ethnically full Hispanic students can help to bridge the language gap and improve academic outcomes. Schools should also provide opportunities for bilingual teachers to advance their careers and receive professional development.
4. Support for English language learners: Providing additional support for English language learners, such as ESL classes or tutors, can help ethnically full Hispanic students who are struggling academically due to their limited proficiency in English.
5. Addressing systemic barriers: Educational systems should address systemic barriers that prevent ethnically full Hispanic students from accessing quality education. 6. Family engagement: Engaging with families and involving them in their children's educational experience can help to build a supportive learning environment.
