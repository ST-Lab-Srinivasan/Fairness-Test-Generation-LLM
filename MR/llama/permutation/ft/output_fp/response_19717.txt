4. Reduced cultural misunderstandings: Healthcare providers who are culturally competent are better equipped to recognize and address cultural misunderstandings that can arise during medical encounters. This can help prevent adverse events and improve patient safety.
5. Increased access to care: Culturally competent healthcare providers can connect with patients from diverse backgrounds, which can increase access to care for marginalized communities. By building trust and rapport, healthcare providers can help bridge cultural divides and improve health outcomes.
