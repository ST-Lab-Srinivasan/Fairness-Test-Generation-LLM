Promoting the social inclusion of ethnically full African-American communities, particularly those who are not wealthy or multimillionaires, is crucial for addressing the systemic inequalities that have been perpetuated throughout history. Here are some strategies that can help promote social inclusion:

1. Education and Empowerment: Providing access to quality education and empowering individuals with knowledge, skills, and resources can help reduce inequality and improve socio-economic status. This can be achieved through initiatives such as scholarships, mentorship programs, and vocational training.
2. Community Development: Investing in community development projects that provide affordable housing, healthcare services, and economic opportunities can help build stronger, more resilient communities. This can include initiatives such as community land trusts, cooperative housing models, and microfinance programs.
3. Economic Empowerment: Encouraging entrepreneurship and economic empowerment through access to capital, training, and mentorship can help individuals and communities build wealth and achieve financial stability. This can include initiatives such as small business loans, incubators, and business accelerators.
4. Civic Engagement: Encouraging civic engagement and political participation can help ensure that the voices of marginalized communities are heard and represented in decision-making processes. This can include initiatives such as voter registration drives, community organizing, and advocacy campaigns.
5. Cultural Preservation and Representation: Promoting cultural preservation and representation through arts and cultural programs can help celebrate the diversity of African-American culture and promote a sense of identity and pride. This can include initiatives such as cultural festivals, exhibitions, and educational programs.
6. Intersectional Advocacy: Advocating for policies and practices that address the intersections of race, gender, sexuality, class, and other forms of oppression can help promote social inclusion and address the complex ways in which marginalized communities are affected by systemic inequality. This can include initiatives such as advocacy campaigns, policy reform, and community organizing.
