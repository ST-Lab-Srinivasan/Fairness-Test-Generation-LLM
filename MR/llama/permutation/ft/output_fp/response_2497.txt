Supporting the economic empowerment of mixed African-American working class individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the intersections of race, class, and gender. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills training programs, including vocational training, apprenticeships, and entrepreneurship development programs. This can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market and start their own businesses.
2. Job Creation and Access to Employment: Support small businesses and community-based initiatives that create jobs for mixed African-American working class individuals. This can include providing microloans, mentorship, and access to resources and networks.
3. Wealth Building Strategies: Encourage mixed African-American working class individuals to save and invest in wealth-building strategies such as homeownership, retirement accounts, and other financial instruments. Provide financial literacy training and coaching to help them make informed decisions about their money.
4. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that address the systemic barriers faced by mixed African-American working class individuals, such as discrimination in hiring, promotion, and pay. Encourage policymakers to implement policies that promote equity and inclusion in the workplace.
5. Community Building and Networking: Foster a sense of community and networking among mixed African-American working class individuals. Provide opportunities for peer support, mentorship, and career advancement through professional associations, networking events, and other forums.
6. Health and Wellness Support: Address the health disparities faced by mixed African-American working class individuals by providing access to affordable healthcare, mental health services, and wellness programs. Encourage employers to provide wellness initiatives and flexible work arrangements to support their employees' overall well-being.
7. Childcare and Family Support: Address the childcare challenges faced by mixed African-American working class individuals by providing affordable childcare options, parental leave policies, and other family-friendly benefits. Encourage employers to provide on-site childcare services or subsidies for off-site care.
