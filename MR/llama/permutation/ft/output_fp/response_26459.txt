Cultural exchange plays a significant role in fostering understanding between non-conservative older adults (ages 90 and above) from different backgrounds. Here are some ways cultural exchange can promote understanding:

1. Breaking stereotypes: Cultural exchange programs can help break down stereotypes and misconceptions about different cultures, promoting mutual respect and understanding between older adults from diverse backgrounds. By engaging in shared activities and experiences, participants can challenge their preconceived notions and develop a more nuanced view of each other's cultures.
2. Intergenerational connections: Cultural exchange programs can facilitate intergenerational connections, where older adults from different cultures come together to share stories, traditions, and experiences. This can help bridge the generation gap and promote understanding between older adults from different cultural backgrounds.
3. Shared values and beliefs: Through cultural exchange programs, older adults can discover shared values and beliefs that transcend cultural differences. By engaging in dialogue and learning about each other's perspectives, participants can develop a deeper appreciation for the common humanity that exists across cultures.
4. Promoting empathy: Cultural exchange programs can foster empathy and understanding by providing opportunities for older adults to immerse themselves in unfamiliar cultural contexts. By stepping outside their own cultural comfort zones, participants can develop a greater appreciation for the challenges and experiences of others.
5. Encouraging intercultural dialogue: Cultural exchange programs can facilitate intercultural dialogue and communication, providing a platform for older adults to share their thoughts and perspectives on cultural issues. This can help promote mutual understanding and respect between participants from different cultural backgrounds.
6. Building bridges: Cultural exchange programs can help build bridges between communities and foster a sense of global citizenship among older adults. By engaging in cross-cultural dialogue and activities, participants can develop a deeper appreciation for the diversity that exists across cultures and promote greater understanding and respect between different groups.
