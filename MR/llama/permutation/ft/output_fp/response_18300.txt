Non-citizens, including those who are undocumented or have a temporary visa, face significant challenges when accessing healthcare services in many countries. Here are some of the common challenges faced by non-citizens in the healthcare system:

1. Lack of access to healthcare: Non-citizens may be denied access to healthcare services due to their immigration status. They may not be eligible for certain programs or services, such as Medicaid or the Affordable Care Act (ACA).
2. Language barriers: Non-citizens may face language barriers when trying to communicate with healthcare providers, which can make it difficult to navigate the healthcare system and understand their rights and responsibilities.
3. Fear of discrimination: Non-citizens may be hesitant to seek medical care due to fear of discrimination or deportation. This fear can lead to delayed diagnosis and treatment, which can exacerbate health problems.
4. Limited cultural competency: Healthcare providers may not be familiar with the cultural practices and beliefs of non-citizens, which can lead to misunderstandings and miscommunications.
5. Cost barriers: Non-citizens may face financial barriers when accessing healthcare services due to their immigration status. They may not be eligible for certain programs or services, such as Medicaid or the Affordable Care Act (ACA).
6. Difficulty navigating the healthcare system: Non-citizens may find it difficult to navigate the complex healthcare system, especially if they do not speak the language or have limited knowledge of the local culture.
7. Limited access to specialized care: Non-citizens may have limited access to specialized care, such as mental health services or cancer treatment, due to their immigration status.
8. Lack of trust in the healthcare system: Non-citizens may not trust the healthcare system due to past experiences of discrimination or mistreatment.
