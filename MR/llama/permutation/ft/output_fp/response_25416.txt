Promoting the economic empowerment of natural-born citizen beginner English speaker communities is crucial for their overall well-being and development. Here are some strategies that can help achieve this goal:

1. Access to Quality Education: Provide access to quality education that caters to the needs of these communities, including English language training, vocational skills, and basic literacy programs. This will enable them to compete in the job market and improve their socio-economic status.
2. Entrepreneurship Development: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing training, mentorship, and resources to help these communities start and grow their own businesses. This can include microfinance programs, incubators, and accelerators.
3. Job Creation: Create job opportunities in these communities through investment in local businesses, infrastructure development, and creation of employment-generating projects. This can be achieved through public-private partnerships or government initiatives.
4. Microfinance Programs: Provide microfinance programs that offer small loans, savings accounts, and other financial services to help these communities start their own businesses or invest in existing ones.
5. Capacity Building: Organize capacity building programs for community members to enhance their skills and knowledge in areas such as entrepreneurship, management, marketing, and financial literacy. This can be done through workshops, training sessions, and mentorship programs.
6. Community Development: Invest in community development projects that improve the overall quality of life for these communities. This can include infrastructure development, healthcare services, education, and social welfare programs.
7. Access to Markets: Facilitate access to markets for these communities by providing information on market opportunities, connecting them with potential buyers, and offering support in branding and marketing their products.
8. Advocacy and Policy Support: Advocate for policies that promote the economic empowerment of these communities, such as affirmative action programs, tax incentives, and government-backed loan schemes.
9. Collaboration with Civil Society Organizations: Work with civil society organizations that specialize in promoting economic empowerment among marginalized groups to leverage their expertise and resources.
