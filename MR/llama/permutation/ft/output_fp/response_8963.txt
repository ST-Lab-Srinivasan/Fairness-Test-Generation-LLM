Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of natural-born US citizen Native American people, as it is an essential part of their identity and sense of self. Cultural heritage refers to the customs, traditions, beliefs, values, and practices that are passed down from one generation to the next within a particular culture or community. For Native American communities, cultural heritage encompasses their history, language, religion, art, music, and other expressive forms of culture.

Native American cultural heritage is deeply connected to their connection to the land, their ancestors, and their traditional ways of life. Many Native American communities have a deep sense of spirituality and religious beliefs that are rooted in their cultural heritage. For example, the Navajo people have a rich tradition of storytelling, music, and art that is an integral part of their cultural identity. The Cherokee people have a complex system of governance and social organization that is based on their cultural heritage.

Cultural heritage also plays a crucial role in the preservation of Native American cultures and traditions. It provides a sense of continuity and connection to the past, which is essential for maintaining cultural identity and tradition in the face of modernization and cultural assimilation. By preserving their cultural heritage, Native American communities can ensure that their traditions and beliefs are passed down to future generations.

However, cultural heritage can also be a source of conflict and tension between Native American communities and the broader US society. For example, the construction of dams, highways, and other infrastructure has often resulted in the destruction of sacred sites and cultural artifacts, leading to feelings of disrespect and betrayal among Native American communities. Similarly, the legacy of colonization and forced assimilation has had a profound impact on Native American cultural identity, leading to intergenerational trauma and ongoing cultural struggles.
