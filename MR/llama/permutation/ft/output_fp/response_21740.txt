Promoting the financial stability of recently single Native American individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique challenges and circumstances. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Financial Education and Literacy: Provide access to financial education programs that teach Native Americans about budgeting, saving, investing, and managing debt. This can help them make informed financial decisions and avoid common pitfalls.
2. Entrepreneurship and Small Business Development: Encourage entrepreneurship and small business development in Native American communities. Provide training, resources, and access to capital to help individuals start or grow their own businesses. This can create job opportunities and generate income for the community.
3. Employment and Career Advancement: Support employment and career advancement programs that provide job training, placement, and career development opportunities. Help individuals gain skills and experience to compete in the job market and advance in their careers.
4. Homeownership and Housing Stability: Encourage homeownership and housing stability by providing access to affordable mortgage financing, down payment assistance, and home repair loans. This can help individuals purchase homes and build wealth through homeownership.
5. Wealth Transfer and Estate Planning: Provide education and resources on wealth transfer and estate planning to help individuals plan for the distribution of their assets after they pass away. This can ensure that their assets are transferred to future generations in a responsible and sustainable manner.
6. Financial Coaching and Mentoring: Connect individuals with financial coaches or mentors who can provide personalized guidance on managing finances, building credit, and achieving financial goals. This can help individuals navigate the complexities of the financial system and make progress towards financial stability.
7. Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFI): Support the development of CDFIs that provide financial services to underserved communities, including Native American communities. CFDIS are community-based organizations that provide loans, savings accounts, and other financial services to help individuals and businesses access capital and build wealth.
