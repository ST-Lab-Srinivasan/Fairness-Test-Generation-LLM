1. Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that equip individuals with the skills and knowledge required for successful employment. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and college scholarships.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing resources and support for starting and growing businesses owned by ethnically full African Americans. This can include access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities.
3. Job Creation: Foster job creation in underserved communities through initiatives such as community development financial institutions (CDFI), microfinance programs, and job training placements.
4. Wealth Building: Encourage wealth building through homeownership and other investment vehicles. Provide access to affordable housing options, financial education, and wealth-building strategies.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that address systemic inequalities and promote economic empowerment for ethnically full African Americans. This can include changes to tax laws, labor laws, and other policies that affect economic opportunities.
6. Community Development: Support community development initiatives that foster collaboration and investment in underserved communities. This can include community land trusts, cooperative ownership models, and other collective ownership structures.
7. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Provide mentorship and leadership development opportunities to help ethnically full African Americans access positions of authority and influence in their respective industries.
