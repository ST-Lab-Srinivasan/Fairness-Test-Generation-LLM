Promoting social inclusion of employed not early pregnancy (1st trimester) communities requires a multifaceted approach that addresses various factors, including social, cultural, and economic barriers. Here are some strategies that can help promote social inclusion:

1. Education and awareness: Provide education and awareness programs to address any misconceptions or stigmas surrounding pregnancy and work. This can include information on the rights of pregnant women in the workplace, as well as the benefits of breastfeeding and bonding with their infants.
2. Flexible work arrangements: Offer flexible work arrangements such as telecommuting, job sharing, or flexible hours to help pregnant women continue working without compromising their health or well-being.
3. Supportive work environment: Create a supportive work environment that accommodates the needs of pregnant women. This can include providing comfortable and clean breastfeeding areas, as well as policies that promote workplace inclusivity.
4. Networking opportunities: Provide networking opportunities for pregnant women to connect with other professionals in their field and build relationships that can help them advance in their careers.
5. Childcare support: Offer childcare support such as on-site daycare or subsidies for off-site care to help reduce the burden of childcare responsibilities on pregnant women.
6. Financial support: Provide financial support such as maternity leave top-up, parental leave top-up, or emergency funds to help pregnant women cover expenses related to pregnancy and childbirth.
7. Mental health support: Offer mental health support services such as counseling or therapy to help pregnant women manage stress and anxiety related to their pregnancy.
8. Employee resource groups: Establish employee resource groups for pregnant women to connect with each other, share experiences, and provide mutual support.
9. Policy changes: Advocate for policy changes at the organizational or industry level to promote social inclusion of pregnant women. This can include implementing paid parental leave policies, providing accommodations for pregnant women in the workplace, or addressing discrimination based on pregnancy status.
