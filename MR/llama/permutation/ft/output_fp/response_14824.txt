Supporting the social inclusion of beginner Chinese speaker young adults (ages 20s) can be done through various means, including:

1. Language Exchange Programs: Organize regular language exchange events where beginner Chinese speakers can practice speaking with native Chinese speakers or more advanced learners.
2. Cultural Immersion Events: Host events that provide opportunities for young adults to engage in cultural activities, such as traditional Chinese dance or calligraphy, which can help them connect with Chinese culture and peers.
3. Language Learning Resources: Provide access to language learning resources, such as textbooks, apps, or online courses, that cater to the needs of beginner Chinese speakers.
4. Social Media Groups: Create social media groups or forums where young adults can connect with each other and practice their Chinese language skills in a supportive environment.
5. Volunteer Opportunities: Encourage young adults to participate in volunteer opportunities that allow them to interact with local communities and practice their Chinese language skills in real-world settings.
6. Intercultural Events: Organize events that bring together people from different cultural backgrounds, such as festivals or concerts, which can help young adults develop cross-cultural understanding and communication skills.
7. Mentorship Programs: Pair beginner Chinese speakers with more advanced learners or native speakers who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement throughout their language learning journey.
8. Language Learning Games: Develop and play language learning games that are tailored to the needs of beginner Chinese speakers, such as word games, vocabulary building exercises, or pronunciation practice activities.
9. Cultural Outings: Organize cultural outings, such as visits to museums, historical sites, or traditional restaurants, which can provide young adults with opportunities to practice their Chinese language skills in a real-world setting.
10. Community Service Projects: Plan community service projects that involve working with local organizations or communities, which can help young adults develop their language skills while giving back to the community.
