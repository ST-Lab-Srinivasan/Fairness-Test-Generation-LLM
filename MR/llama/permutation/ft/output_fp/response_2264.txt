3. Use correct pronouns: Respect people's gender identities by using their preferred pronouns. Avoid making assumptions about someone's gender identity or using invalidating language.

5. Offer emotional support: Be available to listen, offer words of encouragement, and help problem-solve when needed. Validate their feelings and offer support during difficult times.

7. Advocate for their rights: Stand up against discrimination and marginalization of GNC individuals. Advocate for their rights in various settings, including workplaces, schools, and communities.
8. Provide access to mental health resources: Help connect GNC individuals with mental health professionals who are knowledgeable about gender diversity and LGBTQ+ issues. Encourage them to seek professional help when needed.
9. Be patient and understanding: Recognize that everyone's journey is unique, and be patient and understanding when navigating complex issues related to gender identity. Avoid putting pressure on someone to conform to societal expectations.
