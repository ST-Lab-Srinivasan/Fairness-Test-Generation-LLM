Challenges faced by Native Spanish Speakers:

1. Language barrier: For native Spanish speakers who are not fluent in the local language, communicating with healthcare providers can be difficult. They may struggle to understand medical terminology and instructions, which can lead to misunderstandings or misinterpretations.
2. Limited access to care: Some native Spanish speakers may face barriers to accessing healthcare services due to cultural or linguistic isolation. They may feel uncomfortable seeking care from providers who are not fluent in their language or may have limited access to healthcare facilities that offer bilingual services.
3. Different medical practices: Depending on where they live, native Spanish speakers may encounter different medical practices and terminology than what they are used to in their home country. This can lead to confusion and mistrust of the healthcare system.
4. Cultural differences: Healthcare providers may not be aware of cultural differences that can affect how patients interact with the healthcare system. For example, some native Spanish speakers may have different expectations about who should provide care or how decisions should be made regarding their health.
5. Discrimination: Unfortunately, discrimination based on race, ethnicity, or language proficiency can occur in the healthcare system. Native Spanish speakers may face discrimination from providers or other patients, which can affect their ability to receive appropriate care.
