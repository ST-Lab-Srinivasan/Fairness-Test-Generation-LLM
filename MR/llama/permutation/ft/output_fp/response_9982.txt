Here are some ways to support the educational aspirations of beginner Spanish speakers who are not in a romantic relationship:

1. Language exchange programs: Look for local language exchange programs where you can meet with other beginners and practice speaking with them. This will provide an opportunity to practice speaking and get feedback on your progress.
2. Online language learning communities: Join online communities or forums dedicated to Spanish language learners. Interacting with others who are also learning the language can be a great way to stay motivated and get support.
3. Language learning apps: There are many language learning apps available that offer interactive lessons, quizzes, and games. Some popular options include Duolingo, Babbel, and Rosetta Stone.
4. Tutoring or private lessons: Consider hiring a tutor or taking private lessons from a Spanish teacher. This can provide personalized attention and help you progress faster.
5. Podcasts and audio materials: Listen to podcasts or audio materials that are specifically designed for beginners. This can help you improve your listening skills and get used to the sound of the language.
6. Books and reading materials: Read books, articles, and other materials in Spanish to improve your reading comprehension and vocabulary.
7. Immersion events: Look for local events or workshops that provide an immersive environment where you can practice speaking and listening to Spanish.
8. Language exchange websites: Use websites like iTalki or Tandem to find language exchange partners online.
9. YouTube channels: Watch YouTube channels dedicated to teaching Spanish, such as "SpanishDict" or "Learn Spanish with Carlos."
10. Language learning games: Play language learning games like "Wordscapes" or "Ling Space" on your phone or computer to practice vocabulary and grammar in a fun way.
