Promoting the educational success of immigrant students, particularly those who have recently immigrated to the United States, requires a comprehensive and inclusive approach that addresses their unique challenges and needs. Here are some strategies that educators, policymakers, and community leaders can use to support these students:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers on culturally responsive teaching practices that promote diverse perspectives, cultural competence, and inclusive classroom environments. This can help immigrant students feel more engaged and connected to their academic work.
2. Language support: Provide language support services such as English as a Second Language (ESL) classes, bilingual education programs, or interpreters/translators in the classroom. These resources can help immigrant students navigate the curriculum and communicate with teachers and peers more effectively.
3. Academic and career counseling: Offer personalized academic and career counseling to help immigrant students identify their strengths, set achievable goals, and explore post-secondary education and career options. This can include one-on-one counseling sessions, group workshops, or parental involvement programs.
4. Parent engagement: Engage with immigrant parents through regular communication, workshops, and events that promote academic success and provide support. This can help build a sense of community and empower parents to advocate for their children's educational needs.
5. Mentorship programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair immigrant students with successful professionals or students from similar backgrounds. These mentors can offer guidance, support, and encouragement, helping immigrant students navigate academic and social challenges.
6. College access programs: Provide college access programs that offer financial aid, college application assistance, and academic support to help immigrant students succeed in higher education. These programs can help reduce the financial and logistical barriers that often prevent immigrant students from pursuing post-secondary education.
7. Advocacy and policy changes: Advocate for policies that support immigrant students' educational success, such as providing in-state tuition rates for undocumented students or expanding access to financial aid for Dreamers.
