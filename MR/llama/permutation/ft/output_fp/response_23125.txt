1. Uncertainty and insecurity: Temporary visa holders often face uncertainty about their future in the host country, as their immigration status can change suddenly due to various factors such as changes in government policies or personal circumstances. This can lead to feelings of insecurity and anxiety, which can affect their mental health and well-being.
2. Limited access to employment opportunities: Temporary visa holders may face restrictions on their ability to work legally in the host country, which can limit their earning potential and contribute to financial instability. This can be particularly challenging for those with families to support or other dependents.
3. Difficulty accessing healthcare: Depending on their immigration status, temporary visa holders may have limited access to healthcare services in the host country. This can make it difficult to receive necessary medical care, which can have serious consequences for their health and well-being.
4. Social isolation: Temporary visa holders may experience social isolation due to their immigration status, as they may feel excluded from social events or activities that are only available to permanent residents or citizens. This can lead to feelings of loneliness and disconnection from the host community.
5. Difficulty accessing education: Temporary visa holders may face restrictions on their ability to access educational institutions in the host country, which can limit their opportunities for personal and professional growth.
6. Limited access to social services: Temporary visa holders may have limited access to social services such as housing assistance, food aid, or other forms of support. This can make it difficult for them to meet their basic needs and maintain a stable living situation.
