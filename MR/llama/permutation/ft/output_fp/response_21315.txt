Promoting the educational success of Native American recent immigrants to the US can be challenging due to various factors such as language barriers, cultural differences, and socio-economic disparities. Here are some strategies that educators and administrators can use to support these students' academic achievement:

1. Cultural sensitivity training for teachers and staff: Provide workshops and training sessions on cultural competency and awareness to help teachers understand the unique needs of Native American students. This will enable them to create an inclusive learning environment that values diversity and promotes academic success.
2. Language support: Offer language support services such as interpretation, translation, and bilingual education programs to help Native American students who may not speak English fluently. This can include hiring interpreters or providing translated materials for students who are non-English speakers.
3. Academic support services: Provide academic support services such as tutoring, mentoring, and academic advising to help Native American students succeed academically. This can include individualized attention, small group instruction, or peer-to-peer tutoring programs.
4. Cultural enrichment activities: Organize cultural events and activities that celebrate Native American culture, such as powwows, drumming sessions, and traditional craft-making workshops. These activities can help students connect with their heritage while also fostering a sense of community and belonging in the school setting.
5. Family engagement: Engage with Native American families through various means such as parent-teacher conferences, cultural events, and community outreach programs. This can help build trust and promote collaboration between teachers, students, and families.
6. Collaboration with tribal communities: Work closely with local Native American tribes to provide culturally relevant educational resources and support services. This can include partnering with tribal organizations to offer cultural immersion programs, internships, or job shadowing opportunities for students.
7. Curriculum development: Develop curricula that incorporate Native American history, culture, and contributions to the US society. This can help students feel seen and valued in their academic studies and promote a more inclusive understanding of American identity.
