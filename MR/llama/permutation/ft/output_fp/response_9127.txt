There are several ways to use data to highlight the issues faced by beginner Arabic speakers:

1. Analyze learner data: Collect and analyze data on the language learning behaviors and experiences of beginner Arabic learners, such as their learning goals, motivation levels, and challenges faced during the learning process. This can help identify common issues and areas of improvement.
2. Conduct surveys or questionnaires: Distribute surveys or questionnaires to beginner Arabic learners to gather information on their language learning experiences, such as their level of proficiency, the resources they use, and the challenges they face.
3. Use learner corpora: Analyze data from learner corpora, which are collections of texts written by non-native speakers, to identify common mistakes or areas of difficulty faced by beginner Arabic learners.
4. Conduct interviews: Interview beginner Arabic learners to gather qualitative data on their language learning experiences and challenges. This can provide rich insights into the issues they face and how they can be addressed.
5. Use machine learning algorithms: Apply machine learning algorithms to analyze large datasets of Arabic language learning materials, such as textbooks, online resources, and learner corpora, to identify patterns and trends in the data that can help highlight common issues faced by beginner Arabic learners.
6. Create a community: Create an online community or forum for beginner Arabic learners where they can share their experiences, ask questions, and get support from peers and language learning experts. This can help identify common issues and provide a platform for learners to collaborate and learn from each other.
7. Use sentiment analysis: Analyze learner feedback and reviews of language learning resources, such as textbooks, apps, and online courses, to identify common issues and areas of dissatisfaction among beginner Arabic learners.
