2. Culturally responsive teaching: Teachers should be aware of the cultural backgrounds and experiences of their students, including those who are recent immigrants. This involves incorporating diverse perspectives and practices into the classroom, such as using multicultural materials and promoting cross-cultural understanding.
3. Access to mental health resources: Immigrants may face unique challenges related to adjusting to a new country, culture, and language. Providing access to mental health resources, such as counseling services or peer support groups, can help them manage these challenges and achieve academic success.
4. Bridge programs: Developing bridge programs that connect recent immigrants with local schools and communities can help them navigate the educational system more smoothly. These programs can provide information on school policies, procedures, and expectations, as well as offer support with language acquisition and cultural adjustment.
