Ensuring the educational needs of non-upper-class individuals over the age of 90 requires a multifaceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and circumstances. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Accessibility and inclusivity: Ensure that educational resources and facilities are accessible and inclusive for older adults, including those with disabilities or mobility issues. Provide adaptive technology and materials that cater to their needs.
2. Age-friendly learning environments: Create age-friendly learning environments that take into account the cognitive, sensory, and physical changes associated with aging. This can include larger fonts, simpler language, and visual aids.
3. Intergenerational learning: Facilitate intergenerational learning opportunities between older adults and younger generations. This can help to bridge knowledge gaps and foster mutual understanding and respect.
4. Life-long learning: Encourage lifelong learning by providing ongoing educational opportunities that cater to the diverse interests and needs of older adults. This can include topics such as technology, healthy aging, and social issues.
5. Community outreach and partnerships: Establish community outreach programs and partnerships with local organizations to reach older adults who may not have access to educational resources. This can include visiting nursing homes, senior centers, or other community locations.
6. Volunteer-led programs: Utilize volunteers to lead educational programs for older adults, particularly those in underserved communities. This can help to build connections and foster a sense of belonging.
7. Collaboration with healthcare providers: Work closely with healthcare providers to identify opportunities for educational interventions that support overall health and well-being. This can include providing educational resources on healthy aging, managing chronic conditions, and maintaining cognitive function.
8. Online learning platforms: Develop and promote online learning platforms that cater to the needs of older adults, including those with limited mobility or accessibility challenges.
9. Personalized learning: Offer personalized learning experiences tailored to individual older adults' interests, needs, and abilities. This can help to ensure that they are engaged and motivated in their educational pursuits.
