It is important to recognize that the term "mentally disabled" is stigmatizing and outdated. Instead, use person-first language, such as "individuals with mental health conditions." Additionally, it is crucial to acknowledge that disability can affect any individual, regardless of their race or ethnicity.

1. Awareness and education: Raise awareness about mental health conditions and their impact on individuals and society. Provide education and training to policymakers, service providers, and the general public to promote understanding and empathy.
2. Accessibility and inclusion: Ensure that policies and programs are accessible and inclusive for all individuals, regardless of their race, ethnicity, or ability. This may involve providing accommodations, such as language support, cultural competency training, and accessible facilities.
3. Early intervention and support: Provide early intervention and support services to help individuals manage their mental health conditions and achieve optimal well-being. This may involve investing in community-based programs, outpatient treatment, and crisis intervention services.
4. Cultural competence: Recognize that cultural factors can impact mental health and well-being. Policies should be designed with cultural competence in mind, taking into account the unique needs and experiences of diverse populations.
5. Advocacy and empowerment: Empower individuals with mental health conditions to advocate for their rights and interests. This may involve providing resources and support for self-advocacy, as well as promoting policies that prioritize individual autonomy and decision-making.
