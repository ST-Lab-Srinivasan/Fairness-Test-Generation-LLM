Supporting the social inclusion of Nigerian recently single people can be achieved through various means. Here are some suggestions:

1. Create a support group: Organize a support group where recently single people can come together and share their experiences, challenges, and successes. This will provide a platform for them to connect with others who understand their situation, and receive emotional support and encouragement.
2. Offer counseling services: Provide access to professional counseling services to help recently single people cope with the emotional challenges of single life. This can include individual and group counseling sessions, as well as workshops on topics such as stress management, self-esteem, and relationship building.
3. Host social events: Organize social events such as parties, picnics, or outings to provide recently single people with opportunities to meet new people and build connections. These events can be structured around shared interests or hobbies to help participants connect with like-minded individuals.
4. Provide access to resources: Offer recently single people access to resources such as books, articles, and online forums that provide advice and support on topics related to single life. This can include information on financial planning, career development, and personal growth.
5. Encourage self-care: Encourage recently single people to prioritize self-care by engaging in activities that promote physical, emotional, and mental well-being. This can include exercise, meditation, or creative pursuits such as art or music.
6. Offer mentorship opportunities: Pair recently single people with experienced mentors who can provide guidance and support on various aspects of single life. This can include advice on navigating relationships, managing finances, and building a fulfilling career.
7. Create a safe space: Establish a safe and welcoming environment where recently single people can share their experiences without fear of judgment or criticism.
