Promoting the social inclusion of non-middle-class communities, particularly those in their first trimester of pregnancy, is crucial for ensuring that they receive adequate support and care during this critical period. Here are some strategies that can help promote social inclusion:

1. Education and Awareness: Provide educational resources and workshops on prenatal care, childbirth, and parenting to these communities. This will help them understand the importance of proper care and support during pregnancy and beyond.
2. Community Outreach: Reach out to these communities through community events, health fairs, and door-to-door outreach. Provide information on available resources, such as prenatal care, mental health services, and financial assistance.
3. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that these communities have access to quality healthcare providers who are sensitive to their cultural and socioeconomic needs. This may involve providing language-appropriate materials, culturally competent care, and sliding scale fees based on income.
4. Support Groups: Facilitate support groups for pregnant women from non-middle-class communities. These groups can provide a safe space for sharing experiences, receiving emotional support, and accessing resources and information.
5. Collaboration with Community Organizations: Partner with community organizations that serve these communities, such as churches, mosques, synagogues, and community centers. Leverage their existing networks to reach a wider audience and provide targeted interventions.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that address the social determinants of health, such as affordable housing, access to quality education, and living wage employment opportunities. These changes can help reduce health disparities and promote social inclusion.
7. Media Representation: Promote diverse representation in media, including pregnant women from non-middle-class communities. This can help challenge stereotypes and increase visibility and understanding of these communities.
8. Addressing Systemic Barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers that prevent these communities from accessing healthcare services, such as lack of transportation, childcare, or language barriers.
