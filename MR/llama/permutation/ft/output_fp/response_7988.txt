Creating more inclusive cultural programs for upper lower-middle-income non-disabled individuals requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Consult with diverse groups: Engage in consultations with diverse groups, including community leaders, artists, and cultural organizations, to ensure that the program is inclusive and meets the needs of a diverse audience.
2. Provide accessible venues: Ensure that the cultural programs are held in accessible venues that can accommodate people with disabilities, such as wheelchair-accessible buildings or spaces with audio descriptions or sign language interpretation.
3. Offer flexible pricing options: Offer flexible pricing options to make the program more accessible to a wider range of people, including those on lower incomes. For example, offer Pay-What-You-Can tickets or sliding scale pricing.
4. Incorporate inclusive marketing strategies: Use inclusive language and imagery in marketing materials to ensure that all individuals feel included and welcome.
5. Collaborate with disability organizations: Partner with disability organizations to co-create cultural programs that are tailored to the needs of people with disabilities, such as audio described performances or sign language interpreters.
6. Provide accommodations: Make sure that the program provides accommodations for individuals with disabilities, such as wheelchair accessibility, braille or large print programs, and assistive listening devices.
7. Prioritize accessibility in programming: Ensure that the cultural programs are accessible to people with disabilities by prioritizing accessibility in the programming, such as including diverse perspectives, themes, and content that resonate with a broad audience.
8. Create a safe space: Create a safe and welcoming space for all individuals, regardless of their abilities or disabilities, to participate and enjoy the cultural program.
9. Involve community members: Involve community members in the planning and execution of the cultural programs to ensure that they are responsive to the needs and interests of the local community.
10. Continuously evaluate and improve: Regularly evaluate the accessibility of the cultural programs and make improvements based on feedback from participants, community members, and disability organizations.
