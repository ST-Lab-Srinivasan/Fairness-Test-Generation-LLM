Supporting the community engagement of individuals who are just above the poverty line and are natural-born citizens can be achieved through various means. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Skill Development: Providing access to quality education and skill development opportunities can help individuals acquire the necessary skills to secure better employment opportunities and improve their socio-economic status. Governments, NGOs, and private organizations can collaborate to establish schools, vocational training centers, and other educational institutions that cater to the needs of these individuals.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encouraging entrepreneurship among individuals who are just above the poverty line can help them create sustainable income streams and become economically independent. This can be achieved by providing access to training, mentorship, and funding opportunities. Governments and NGOs can also establish incubators and accelerators to support start-ups and small businesses.
3. Community Empowerment: Building strong, resilient communities is crucial for promoting community engagement. This can be achieved by providing access to social services, infrastructure, and resources that facilitate community development. Community-based organizations (CBOs) can play a significant role in empowering individuals and promoting community engagement.
4. Health and Wellness: Improving the overall health and wellness of individuals can help them contribute more effectively to their communities. Governments, NGOs, and private organizations can collaborate to provide access to quality healthcare services, sanitation facilities, and clean water sources.
5. Leadership Development: Developing leadership skills among individuals who are just above the poverty line can help them assume larger roles in their communities and promote community engagement. This can be achieved through training programs, mentorship opportunities, and other forms of capacity-building.
6. Access to Financial Services: Providing access to financial services such as microfinance loans, savings accounts, and insurance products can help individuals above the poverty line manage their finances more effectively and invest in their communities.
