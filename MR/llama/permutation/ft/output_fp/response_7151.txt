Ensuring that the voices of beginner French speakers, particularly those from ethnic minority backgrounds, are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting inclusivity and equity. Here are some strategies to help amplify their voices:

1. Create a safe space: Establish a comfortable and welcoming environment where individuals feel free to express themselves without fear of judgment or discrimination. This can be done by setting ground rules, providing training on cultural competence, and fostering a culture of inclusivity.
2. Use accessible language: Avoid using technical jargon or complex terminology that may be challenging for beginners to understand. Instead, use simple and clear language that is easy to comprehend. This can help level the playing field and ensure everyone has an equal opportunity to participate.
3. Provide support: Offer language support and resources to help beginners improve their speaking skills. This can include one-on-one tutoring, language exchange programs, or access to language learning materials. By providing these resources, you can help individuals build their confidence and improve their ability to communicate effectively.
4. Encourage participation: Actively seek out the input of beginners in decision-making processes. This can be done by asking for their opinions, seeking their feedback, or inviting them to participate in discussions. By giving them a voice, you can help ensure that their perspectives are taken into account.
5. Foster allyship: Encourage individuals from more privileged positions to use their power and influence to amplify the voices of beginners. This can involve speaking up on their behalf, providing support and resources, or using their platforms to raise awareness about the issues faced by beginner French speakers.
6. Create opportunities for collaboration: Develop partnerships between individuals from different backgrounds and create opportunities for them to work together. By fostering collaboration and mutual understanding, you can help break down barriers and promote inclusivity.
7. Provide mentorship: Pair beginners with more experienced speakers who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement. This can help beginners build their skills and confidence, and also provide them with role models and mentors.
