2. Lack of awareness: Many recent immigrants may be unfamiliar with the Indian legal system and its procedures. This lack of knowledge can make it challenging for them to navigate the legal landscape, especially when dealing with complex issues like inheritance, property rights, or criminal cases.

Limited access to legal resources: As a beginner Arabic speaker, you may have limited access to legal resources in India, such as lawyers who speak your language, legal aid services, or translated versions of important documents. 4. Cultural differences: The Indian legal system is based on a common law framework, which may differ significantly from the legal systems in Arab countries. For example, the concept of "proof beyond reasonable doubt" is not applicable in India, and the burden of proof is on the prosecution rather than the defense. These cultural differences can create confusion and challenges for non-native speakers who are unfamiliar with the legal system.
5. Discrimination: Unfortunately, recent immigrants, especially those from the Arab world, may face discrimination in the Indian legal system. This can manifest in various ways, such as biases among law enforcement officials, judges, or other legal professionals. As a result, you may find it more challenging to access justice or have your rights respected.
