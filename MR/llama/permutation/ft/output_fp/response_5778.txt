Ensuring the educational needs of Native natural-born US citizen civilian students involves addressing the unique challenges and barriers they face in accessing quality education. Here are some strategies to help meet their needs:

1. Cultural relevance and sensitivity: Incorporate Native American culture, traditions, and histories into the curriculum to make it more relevant and engaging for students. This can include incorporating Indigenous knowledge and perspectives, using local flora and fauna as teaching tools, and promoting cultural exchange programs.
2. Language preservation: Many Native American languages are endangered, and language loss is a significant concern. Incorporate Native languages into the curriculum, provide language immersion programs, and support language revitalization efforts in the community.
3. Access to technology: Provide access to technology, including computers, tablets, and internet connectivity, to ensure that students have the necessary tools for success in the digital age. This can help bridge the technology gap between Native American communities and the broader society.
4. Community involvement: Involve parents, elders, and community members in the educational process to promote a sense of ownership and responsibility. This can include collaborating on curriculum development, providing cultural mentorship, and supporting extracurricular activities.
5. Teacher training: Provide professional development opportunities for teachers to learn about Native American cultures, histories, and perspectives. This can help ensure that teachers are better equipped to meet the unique needs of Native American students.
6. Addressing systemic barriers: Advocate for policies and practices that address systemic barriers to educational success, such as inadequate funding, lack of resources, and discrimination. This can include advocating for increased funding for Native American education, supporting the development of culturally responsive curriculum, and promoting policies that promote cultural competency and inclusivity.
7. Support for Indigenous knowledge systems: Recognize and support the unique knowledge systems and ways of knowing that are grounded in Native American cultures.
