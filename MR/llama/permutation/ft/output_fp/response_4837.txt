1. Language Accessibility: Provide interpretation services for patients who do not speak the dominant language of the healthcare system, such as French or Arabic. This can be done through in-person interpretation, video remote interpreting (VRI), or over-the-phone interpretation. Ensure that the interpretation services are culturally and linguistically competent and provide accurate and unbiased translations.

Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for healthcare providers to ensure they understand the beliefs, values, and practices of native French speakers and native Arabic speakers. This can include learning about their traditional healing practices, spiritual beliefs, and communication styles.
3. Health Literacy Materials: Develop health literacy materials that are culturally appropriate for native French speakers and native Arabic speakers. This can include using simple language, avoiding technical jargon, and providing visual aids to help patients understand the information.
4. Patient Navigators: Train patient navigators who are fluent in both French and Arabic to help patients navigate the healthcare system, schedule appointments, and communicate with healthcare providers.
5. Health Disparities Research: Conduct research on health disparities affecting native French speakers and native Arabic speakers to identify areas where they may be experiencing barriers to care. Use this information to develop targeted interventions to address these disparities.
