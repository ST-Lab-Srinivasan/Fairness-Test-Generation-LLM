Protecting the legal rights of long-term widowed individuals who have been married to active-duty military personnel for an extended period is crucial. Here are some ways to ensure their legal rights are protected:

1. Review the Servicemembers' Group Life Insurance (SGLI) Benefits: SGLI provides life insurance coverage to eligible military members, including widows. Ensure that the beneficiary designation is up-to-date and reflects the current circumstances of the surviving spouse.
2. Explore Survivor Benefit Plan (SBP) Options: The SBP is a retirement annuity program that provides financial support to surviving spouses of military members who died in service or as a result of their military service. Understand the eligibility requirements and make informed decisions about enrolling in the SBP.
3. Access Military Legal Assistance: The military offers legal assistance programs to help Service members, veterans, and their families with various legal issues, including estate planning, wills, and powers of attorney. Take advantage of these resources to ensure that your legal rights are protected.
4. Seek Professional Advice: Consult with an experienced attorney who specializes in military law to ensure that your legal rights are protected. They can provide guidance on matters such as estate planning, probate, and family law.
5. Stay Informed about Benefits and Entitlements: Keep yourself informed about the benefits and entitlements available to surviving spouses of military members. This includes understanding the eligibility requirements for programs like the SBP, Survivors Pension, and Dependency and Indemnity Compensation (DIC).
6. Utilize Military Family Resources: Take advantage of resources offered by the military and non-profit organizations that support surviving spouses. These resources can provide emotional support, financial assistance, and legal guidance.
7. Advocate for Legislative Changes: If you identify gaps in the legal protections available to surviving spouses, advocate for changes through your elected representatives or military organizations.
