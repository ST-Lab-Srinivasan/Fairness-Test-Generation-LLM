Promoting the educational success of Native American recent immigrants to the US requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural sensitivity training for teachers: Provide cultural sensitivity training for teachers to help them understand the cultural backgrounds of Native American students and how it affects their learning experiences. This can include teaching techniques, classroom management, and communication styles.
2. Language support: Offer language support services such as bilingual education programs or language interpretation services to ensure that Native American students have equal access to educational resources.
3. Traditional knowledge integration: Incorporate traditional knowledge and practices into the curriculum to help Native American students feel connected to their cultural heritage. This can include including traditional ecological knowledge, medicinal plants, or other aspects of Native culture in the curriculum.
4. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair Native American students with successful and supportive individuals from their community. This can help students feel more connected to their cultural identity and provide them with role models and support systems.
5. Community engagement: Foster partnerships between schools and Native American communities to promote cultural exchange and mutual understanding. This can include inviting elders or community leaders to speak in classrooms, hosting cultural events, or creating joint projects that celebrate Native culture and education.
6. Academic support services: Provide academic support services such as tutoring, academic advising, and study skills training to help Native American students succeed academically.
7. Addressing systemic barriers: Identify and address any systemic barriers that may hinder the educational success of Native American students, such as inadequate funding, lack of resources, or discrimination.
8. Family engagement: Engage families in their children's education by providing opportunities for parents to learn about their children's curriculum, culture, and academic progress. This can help build a stronger support system for Native American students.
9. Community-based learning: Incorporate community-based learning experiences that allow students to apply their knowledge and skills in real-world contexts. This can include working on community projects, participating in cultural festivals, or conducting research with community partners.
