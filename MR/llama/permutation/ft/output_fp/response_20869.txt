Promoting the social inclusion of moderate-income recently expecting communities can be achieved through a variety of strategies. Here are some suggestions:

1. Education and Awareness: Provide educational resources and workshops on prenatal care, childbirth, and parenting to expectant mothers in these communities. This can help them make informed decisions about their health and well-being during pregnancy and beyond.
2. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that these communities have access to quality, affordable healthcare services, including prenatal care, delivery, and postnatal care. This can be achieved through partnerships with local hospitals, clinics, or community health centers.
3. Community Engagement: Organize community events, workshops, and support groups for expectant mothers in these communities. This can provide a safe space for them to connect with one another, share experiences, and receive emotional and practical support during pregnancy and early parenthood.
4. Economic Empowerment: Offer financial literacy training, job skills workshops, and entrepreneurship opportunities to help expectant mothers in these communities build financial stability and independence. This can help them feel more confident and capable of providing for their families.
5. Childcare Support: Provide access to affordable, high-quality childcare options, such as drop-in centers or cooperatives, that can support expectant mothers in these communities who may be balancing work and parenting responsibilities.
