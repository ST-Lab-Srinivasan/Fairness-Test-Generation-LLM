Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of recent immigrants to Canada and the US, particularly for indigenous immigrants. Cultural heritage refers to the customs, traditions, beliefs, and practices passed down from generation to generation within a particular culture or community. For immigrants who have recently arrived in a new country, their cultural heritage can provide a sense of familiarity and comfort in an unfamiliar environment. Here are some ways that cultural heritage can impact the lives of recent immigrants:

1. Sense of identity: Cultural heritage helps individuals identify themselves and connect with their roots. For indigenous immigrants, maintaining cultural practices and traditions can help them preserve their sense of identity and belonging in a new country.
2. Community building: Cultural heritage can serve as a common ground for immigrants to connect with others from the same culture or community. This can help build a sense of community and belonging among recent immigrants, who may feel isolated or disconnected from their new surroundings.
3. Preservation of cultural practices: Maintaining cultural practices and traditions can help immigrants preserve their cultural heritage and pass it down to future generations. This can be particularly important for indigenous immigrants who may face challenges in preserving their cultural practices in a new country.
4. Coping mechanism: Cultural heritage can serve as a coping mechanism for recent immigrants dealing with stress, anxiety, or trauma related to their migration experience. Engaging in cultural practices and traditions can provide a sense of comfort and familiarity during times of uncertainty.
5. Resistance and activism: For some indigenous immigrants, maintaining cultural heritage can be a form of resistance and activism against systemic oppression and marginalization. By preserving their cultural practices and traditions, they can challenge dominant narratives and assert their rights as indigenous peoples.
