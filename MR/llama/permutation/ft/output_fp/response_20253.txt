Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of Native natural-born US citizens, as it is an integral part of their history, traditions, and practices. Cultural heritage refers to the collective cultural expressions and practices of a particular group or society, including customs, beliefs, values, languages, and artifacts. For Native Americans, cultural heritage is closely tied to their connection to the land, their ancestors, and their traditional ways of life.

Here are some ways in which cultural heritage shapes the identities of Native natural-born US citizens:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging and connection to one's community and ancestry. For Native Americans, their cultural heritage is an essential part of their identity and helps them feel connected to their homelands and ancestors.
2. Cultural practices: Native American cultural practices, such as traditional dance, music, and art, are a vital part of their cultural heritage. These practices help shape the identities of individuals within the community and provide a sense of continuity with the past.
3. Language: Many Native American communities have their own languages, which are an essential part of their cultural heritage. These languages reflect the unique history, beliefs, and values of each community and are crucial to preserving cultural traditions.
4. Traditional knowledge: Traditional knowledge refers to the collective wisdom and practices of a particular culture or society. For Native Americans, traditional knowledge includes knowledge about medicinal plants, hunting and gathering techniques, and spiritual beliefs. This knowledge is passed down through generations and is an essential part of their cultural heritage.
5. Resilience: The resilience of Native American communities in the face of colonization, forced assimilation, and other forms of oppression is a testament to their cultural heritage. Their ability to adapt and survive despite historical trauma is a source of strength and pride within these communities.
6. Identity formation: Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of Native natural-born US citizens. It influences how they see themselves, their place in society, and their relationship with the non-Native community.
7. Preservation of cultural practices: Cultural heritage helps preserve traditional practices and beliefs, which are essential for maintaining cultural continuity and identity
