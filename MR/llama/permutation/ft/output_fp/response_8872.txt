1. Cultural identity formation: As young people explore their identities, mentors can help them understand and connect with their cultural heritage. 2. Building self-esteem and confidence: Mentors can provide positive role models and encourage young people to embrace their strengths and abilities. This can help build self-esteem and confidence, which are essential for navigating life's challenges and pursuing goals.

4. Networking opportunities: Mentors can introduce young people to their professional networks, helping them build connections that can lead to future job opportunities or other resources.
