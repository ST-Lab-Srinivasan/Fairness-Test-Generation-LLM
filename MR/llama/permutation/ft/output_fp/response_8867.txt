Supporting strong atheist communities, particularly those with members over the age of 90, requires a thoughtful and compassionate approach. Here are some effective strategies to help build and maintain these communities:

1. Encourage intergenerational connections: Pair younger atheists with older members to foster mentorship and friendship. This can help bridge the gap between different age groups within the community.
2. Provide opportunities for socialization: Organize events, outings, or activities that cater to the interests of older atheists, such as book clubs, movie nights, or gardening groups. This will encourage social interaction and a sense of belonging.
3. Offer support for physical and mental well-being: Arrange for exercise classes, yoga sessions, or meditation groups specifically designed for older atheists. Additionally, provide resources for mental health support, such as counseling services or peer support groups.
4. Foster a sense of purpose: Encourage older atheists to stay engaged in their communities through volunteer work, activism, or other activities that give them a sense of purpose and fulfillment.
5. Create opportunities for intellectual engagement: Host lectures, workshops, or discussions on topics of interest to older atheists, such as science, philosophy, or history. This will help keep their minds active and engaged.
6. Provide access to technology: Offer training sessions or access to devices and software that can help older atheists stay connected with the community and the world outside, such as video conferencing tools or online forums.
7. Address ageism and ableism: Be mindful of the language and attitudes used within the community, avoiding any language or behaviors that might be perceived as discriminatory or disrespectful towards older atheists.
8. Celebrate milestones and achievements: Organize events to celebrate significant birthdays, anniversaries, or other accomplishments of older atheists within the community.
9. Foster a culture of inclusivity and respect: Encourage open-mindedness, empathy, and mutual respect among all members of the community, regardless of age or beliefs.
