Ensuring that the voices of beginner Arabic speakers with mixed Asian heritage are heard in decision-making processes requires intentional efforts to create inclusive environments and practices. Here are some strategies to help achieve this:

1. Create a safe space for self-expression: Establish a supportive environment where individuals feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and opinions without fear of judgment or marginalization. This can be done by promoting respectful communication, actively listening, and avoiding microaggressions.
2. Foster diversity and inclusion training: Offer regular training sessions on cultural competence, unconscious bias, and inclusive leadership. These workshops can help participants recognize their biases and develop strategies to overcome them, leading to more inclusive decision-making processes.
3. Establish mentorship programs: Pair beginner Arabic speakers with experienced professionals who share similar cultural backgrounds or experiences. This mentorship can provide guidance, support, and a platform for mentees to build confidence in their abilities and contribute to decision-making processes.
4. Encourage participation in decision-making meetings: Invite beginner Arabic speakers to participate in meetings and discussions where important decisions are being made. This can be done by actively seeking their input, valuing their opinions, and providing opportunities for them to contribute to the decision-making process.
5. Use inclusive language and practices: Ensure that all communication materials and processes use inclusive language and practices that respect and acknowledge the diversity of participants. This can be achieved by using gender-neutral language, avoiding assumptions based on race or ethnicity, and promoting equal opportunities for participation.
6. Celebrate diverse perspectives: Foster a culture that celebrates and values diverse perspectives and experiences. Encourage participants to share their unique viewpoints and listen actively to create an environment where everyone feels heard and valued.
7. Address power imbalances: Identify and address any power imbalances within the decision-making process, such as unequal representation or dominance by certain groups. This can be achieved by implementing strategies like consensus decision-making, proportional representation, or voting systems that ensure everyone has an equal voice.
