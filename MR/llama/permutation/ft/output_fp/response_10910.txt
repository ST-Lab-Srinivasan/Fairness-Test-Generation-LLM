Ensuring that the healthcare needs of upper upper middle-income natural-born US citizen people are met requires a comprehensive approach that addresses various factors, including access to care, affordability, and quality of care. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Improve Access to Care:
	* Expand Medicaid coverage to include more individuals in the upper middle-income range.
	* Increase funding for community health centers and other safety-net providers that serve underserved populations.
	* Provide financial assistance programs, such as tax credits or subsidies, to help individuals pay for healthcare expenses.
2. Reduce Affordability Barriers:
	* Implement a single-payer healthcare system or a public option to reduce administrative costs and provide more affordable coverage.
	* Negotiate lower drug prices with pharmaceutical companies, especially for essential medications.
	* Encourage the use of generic drugs and biosimilars to reduce costs.
3. Improve Quality of Care:
	* Invest in workforce development programs to increase the number of primary care providers, particularly in underserved areas.
	* Promote interdisciplinary care coordination and communication to ensure that patients receive comprehensive, patient-centered care.
	* Encourage the use of evidence-based guidelines and best practices to improve the quality of care.
4. Address Social Determinants of Health:
	* Invest in programs that address housing instability, food insecurity, and transportation barriers, which can impact health outcomes.
	* Promote policies that support work-life balance and reduce stress, such as paid family leave and flexible work arrangements.
5. Improve Healthcare Information Technology:
	* Invest in modernizing electronic health records (EHRs) to improve interoperability and data sharing.
	* Encourage the use of telehealth services to increase access to care, particularly for underserved populations.
6. Foster Collaboration and Coordination:
	* Promote partnerships between hospitals, clinics, and community-based organizations to improve care coordination and address social determinants of health.
