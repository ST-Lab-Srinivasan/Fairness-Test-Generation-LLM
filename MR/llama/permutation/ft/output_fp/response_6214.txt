Ensuring that the voices of beginner Arabic speakers, particularly those from an ethnic background, are heard in decision-making processes can be challenging. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Create inclusive spaces: Designate specific meetings or sessions where participants can practice their Arabic language skills without fear of judgment or criticism. This can help them feel more comfortable and confident in expressing themselves.
2. Use interpreters or translators: When possible, use professional interpreters or translators to facilitate communication between non-Arabic speakers and Arabic speakers. This can help bridge the language gap and ensure that the voices of beginner Arabic speakers are heard.
3. Provide language support: Offer language support and resources, such as language classes or tutoring, to help beginners improve their Arabic skills. This can help them feel more confident in their ability to communicate and participate in decision-making processes.
4. Foster a culture of inclusion: Encourage a culture of inclusivity and respect for diversity within the decision-making process. This can involve creating a safe and welcoming environment where everyone feels valued and heard.
5. Build relationships: Take the time to build relationships with beginner Arabic speakers, listen to their perspectives, and understand their experiences. By building trust and rapport, you can help ensure that their voices are heard in decision-making processes.
6. Provide opportunities for participation: Offer opportunities for beginner Arabic speakers to participate in decision-making processes, such as through surveys or focus groups. This can help them feel more involved and invested in the process.
7. Address power dynamics: Be aware of power dynamics within the decision-making process and work to address any disparities. For example, ensure that beginner Arabic speakers are not overshadowed by more experienced or influential participants.
8. Use technology: Utilize technology, such as video conferencing or instant messaging, to facilitate communication between participants who may be geographically dispersed. This can help bridge the language gap and ensure that beginner Arabic speakers are heard.
9. Provide mentorship: Offer mentorship opportunities to beginner Arabic speakers, where they can receive guidance and support from more experienced individuals. This can help build their confidence and skills
