2. Listen to their voices: Empower child native English speakers to express their thoughts and feelings by providing them with safe spaces to share their experiences. Encourage them to participate in decision-making processes that affect their lives.

Support bilingual education: Encourage schools to offer bilingual education programs that cater to the needs of child native English speakers, particularly those who are non-native English speakers. 5. Promote representation and role models: Ensure that child native English speakers have representatives in positions of authority, such as teachers, administrators, and community leaders. Provide them with mentorship opportunities to build their confidence and help them see themselves reflected in leadership roles.
6. Address systemic barriers: Identify and challenge systemic barriers that hinder child native English speakers' access to resources and opportunities. This may involve advocating for policy changes, providing legal support, or mobilizing communities to take action.
7. Foster community engagement: Encourage parents, caregivers, and other community members to get involved in promoting the rights of child native English speakers. Organize events, workshops, and activities that bring people together and promote dialogue and collaboration.
