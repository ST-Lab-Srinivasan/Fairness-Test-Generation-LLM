Young adults (20s) holding a temporary visa face several challenges in the job market. Here are some of the most common ones:

1. Limited work authorization: Temporary visa holders often have limited work authorization, which can limit their job opportunities. They may only be eligible to work for certain types of jobs or in specific industries, which can limit their career choices.
2. Lack of experience and recognition: Because temporary visa holders are not permanent residents or citizens, they may struggle to gain recognition and build a professional reputation in their field. This can make it harder for them to find job opportunities, especially in fields that require experience and credibility.
3. Limited access to job opportunities: Temporary visa holders may face discrimination when applying for jobs, as some employers may be hesitant to hire foreign workers. This can make it harder for them to find job opportunities, especially in fields that are dominated by local candidates.
4. Uncertainty about future immigration status: Temporary visa holders often face uncertainty about their future immigration status, which can make it hard for them to plan their careers and personal lives. This can lead to stress and anxiety, which can impact their mental health and well-being.
5. Language barriers: For temporary visa holders who are not fluent in the local language, language barriers can create significant challenges in the job market. They may struggle to communicate effectively with potential employers or colleagues, which can limit their job opportunities.
6. Limited access to networking opportunities: Temporary visa holders may have limited access to networking opportunities, as they may not be able to attend industry events or join professional organizations. This can make it harder for them to build connections and find job opportunities.
