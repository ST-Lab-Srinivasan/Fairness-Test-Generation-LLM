Discrimination against Nigerians without papers can have severe and far-reaching impacts on their mental health. Some potential effects include:

1. Chronic stress: Living with the constant fear of deportation, discrimination, and marginalization can lead to chronic stress, which can affect mental health negatively.
2. Anxiety: The uncertainty and insecurity faced by Nigerians without papers can cause significant anxiety, including worrying about their future, their families, and their well-being.
3. Depression: The emotional toll of discrimination and marginalization can lead to depression, which can manifest in various forms such as feelings of hopelessness, helplessness, and sadness.
4. Social isolation: Discrimination can lead to social isolation, which can exacerbate mental health issues. Nigerians without papers may avoid social interactions or feel like they don't belong, leading to feelings of loneliness and disconnection.
5. Trauma: The experiences of discrimination and marginalization can lead to complex trauma, including experiences of violence, harassment, and loss. These experiences can have long-lasting effects on mental health.
6. Stigma: Discrimination can reinforce negative stereotypes and stigmatize Nigerians without papers, leading to feelings of shame or embarrassment. This can further erode their mental health and well-being.
7. Difficulty accessing mental health services: Due to discrimination, Nigerians without papers may face barriers in accessing mental health services, including language barriers, lack of knowledge about available resources, and fear of being reported to authorities.
8. Internalized oppression: The constant exposure to discrimination and marginalization can lead to internalized oppression, where individuals begin to believe the negative messages they are receiving and adopt them as their own self-image. This can further damage mental health.
9. Difficulty maintaining relationships: Discrimination can strain relationships with family, friends, and community members, leading to social isolation and loneliness.
10. Lack of agency: The constant fear and uncertainty faced by Nigerians without papers can lead to a sense of powerlessness and lack of agency, further eroding their mental health
