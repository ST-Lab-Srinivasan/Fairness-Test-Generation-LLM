Family support plays a significant role in the success of individuals who are unable to perform daily tasks at the age of over 90. Here are some ways in which family support can contribute to their success:

1. Emotional Support: Family members can provide emotional support by offering a listening ear, empathy, and reassurance. This can help alleviate feelings of isolation and loneliness that may arise from being unable to perform daily tasks.
2. Practical Assistance: Family members can offer practical assistance such as helping with daily tasks like bathing, dressing, and grooming. They can also help with managing medications, appointments, and other personal care needs.
3. Social Connection: Family members can provide social connection by taking the person to social events, visiting them regularly, and encouraging them to stay engaged with their community. This can help prevent feelings of loneliness and isolation.
4. Caregiver Support: Family members who are acting as caregivers can benefit from support themselves. This can include emotional support, respite care, and access to resources and services that can help them manage the demands of caregiving.
5. Financial Support: In some cases, family members may need to provide financial support to cover the costs of care, such as hiring a caregiver or paying for medical equipment.
6. Advocacy: Family members can act as advocates for their loved one by advocating for their needs and rights within the healthcare system, social services, and other organizations that provide support to older adults.
7. Legal Planning: Family members can help their loved one with legal planning such as power of attorney, advance directive, and wills. This can ensure that their loved one's wishes are respected and their affairs are in order.
8. Transportation: Family members can provide transportation to appointments, social events, and other activities, which can be especially important for older adults who may no longer drive or have access to reliable transportation.
9. Home Modifications: Family members can help make home modifications to create a safer and more accessible living environment for their loved one, such as installing handrails, removing tripping hazards, and improving lighting.
