Ensuring that the healthcare needs of deep poverty native Spanish speakers are met requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the social, economic, and cultural factors that contribute to health disparities. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Culturally competent care: Provide training to healthcare providers on culturally competent care, which includes understanding the beliefs, values, and practices of the target population. This can help build trust and improve health outcomes.
2. Language access: Ensure that interpreter services are available for patients who do not speak English proficiently. Provide language access services such as translated materials, audio or video remote interpreting, or on-site interpretation.
3. Outreach and education: Develop targeted outreach programs to reach deep poverty native Spanish speakers. Provide educational materials in their native language and use community health workers or promotoras to disseminate information about available healthcare services.
4. Health literacy: Improve health literacy among deep poverty native Spanish speakers by providing clear and concise information about health conditions, treatments, and preventive measures. Use simple language and visual aids to help them understand complex medical concepts.
5. Transportation assistance: Provide transportation assistance to individuals who lack access to transportation, which is a common barrier to healthcare services for deep poverty native Spanish speakers. Partner with local organizations or non-profits that offer transportation services.
6. Oral health services: Address the unique oral health needs of deep poverty native Spanish speakers by providing culturally competent oral health services, including dental screenings and restorative care.
7. Mental health services: Provide mental health services that are tailored to the cultural and linguistic needs of deep poverty native Spanish speakers. Offer counseling, therapy, and psychiatric services in their native language.
8. Community partnerships: Develop partnerships with local community organizations and churches to reach deep poverty native Spanish speakers. These partnerships can help identify healthcare barriers and develop culturally sensitive interventions.
9. Policy changes: Advocate for policy changes that address the social determinants of health, such as affordable housing, education, and employment opportunities.
