1. Offer tailored courses: Design courses that cater to the specific needs and goals of beginner French speakers. For example, offer beginner-friendly courses that focus on basic vocabulary, grammar, and pronunciation.
2. Provide interactive learning opportunities: Incorporate interactive elements into your teaching methods, such as conversation exchanges, language games, and role-playing activities. This will help students practice their speaking skills and gain confidence in using French in real-life situations.
3. Use authentic materials: Utilize authentic materials like news articles, videos, and audio recordings to expose students to real-life French language usage. This will help them learn how to apply the language in practical contexts.
4. Encourage peer learning: Pair beginner French speakers with more advanced students or native speakers for language exchange activities. This will provide an opportunity for beginners to practice speaking and listening to French, while also gaining exposure to different accents and language use patterns.
5. Offer flexible scheduling options: Accommodate the schedules of newly married students by offering classes at different times or online. This will enable them to attend classes without having to sacrifice other responsibilities like work or family obligations.
6. Provide individualized feedback: Offer one-on-one consultations with teachers or language coaches to provide tailored feedback and guidance to beginner French speakers. This will help them identify areas of improvement and track their progress over time.
