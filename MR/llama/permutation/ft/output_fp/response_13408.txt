1. Accessibility: Ensure that public spaces, buildings, and facilities are accessible to people with disabilities by providing ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms.
2. Education and awareness: Educate the public about the experiences and challenges faced by disabled individuals, and raise awareness about the importance of inclusion and accessibility.
3. Employment opportunities: Provide job training and placement services specifically for people with disabilities, and make sure that workplaces are accessible and inclusive.
4. Healthcare access: Ensure that healthcare providers are knowledgeable about the specific needs of disabled individuals and provide accessible care.
5. Social events: Host social events that are inclusive of people with disabilities, such as accessible movie screenings or concerts.
6. Transportation: Provide accessible transportation options, such as wheelchair-accessible buses or trains, to ensure that people with disabilities can easily travel around the community.
7. Inclusive language: Use inclusive language and avoid using terms that are offensive or marginalizing to disabled individuals.
8. Intersectionality: Recognize that disabled individuals may face additional discrimination based on their race, gender, sexuality, or other aspects of their identity, and work to address these intersecting forms of oppression.
9. Advocacy: Advocate for policies and practices that promote inclusion and accessibility, such as mandating accessibility features in new construction or advocating for the rights of disabled individuals in the political sphere.
10. Representation: Ensure that disabled individuals are represented in media and other areas of society, both behind the scenes and in front of the camera, to promote positive representations and challenge ableism.
11. Accommodations in sports and recreation: Provide accommodations for people with disabilities in sports and recreational activities, such as wheelchair basketball or adaptive skiing, to ensure that everyone can participate fully.
12. Accessible housing: Ensure that housing options are accessible to people with disabilities, including wheelchair-accessible units and features like Braille signage and audio signals for visually impaired residents.
