Promoting the accessibility of public services for individuals aged 90 and older who are not wealthy or multimillionaires requires a concerted effort to address the unique challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Age-Friendly Communities: Encourage the development of age-friendly communities that prioritize accessibility and inclusivity for all ages. This involves creating accessible infrastructure, such as sidewalks, crossings, and public transportation, and providing opportunities for social engagement and community participation.
2. Transportation Services: Ensure that public transportation services are accessible and affordable for seniors. This can include wheelchair-accessible vehicles, reduced fares for seniors, and convenient pick-up and drop-off locations.
3. Home Modifications: Assist seniors in making modifications to their homes to make them more accessible, such as installing grab bars, non-slip flooring, and widening doorways. This can help them maintain their independence and prevent accidents.
4. Healthcare Services: Ensure that healthcare services are accessible and responsive to the needs of seniors. This can include telemedicine options, same-day appointments, and caregiver support services.
5. Social Isolation Prevention: Address social isolation by providing opportunities for seniors to connect with others through community programs, volunteering, and intergenerational activities. This can help combat loneliness and depression, which are common among older adults.
6. Financial Assistance: Provide financial assistance to seniors who cannot afford essential services, such as transportation, home modifications, and healthcare. This can include government programs, non-profit organizations, and community fundraisers.
7. Public Awareness Campaigns: Launch public awareness campaigns to educate the public about the challenges faced by seniors and the importance of providing accessible services. This can help build empathy and support for older adults.
8. Advocacy Groups: Support advocacy groups that represent the interests of seniors and work towards promoting their rights and well-being. These groups can provide a voice for seniors and push for policy changes that benefit them.
