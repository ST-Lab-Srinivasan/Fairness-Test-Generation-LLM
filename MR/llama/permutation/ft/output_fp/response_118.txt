Supporting the mental health of Not Disabled Native Recent Immigrants (NDNRI) to the US can be crucial in promoting their well-being and resilience. Here are some ways to support their mental health:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for service providers to understand the unique needs and experiences of NDNRI individuals, including their cultural practices, beliefs, and values.
2. Access to Mental Health Services: Ensure that NDNRI individuals have access to mental health services that are tailored to their specific needs and cultural background. This can include working with traditional healers and practitioners who are knowledgeable about NDNRI cultural practices and beliefs.
3. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement and social connections among NDNRI individuals, as social support is crucial for mental health and well-being. This can involve creating spaces for NDNRI individuals to connect with one another and build relationships.
4. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that promote the mental health and well-being of NDNRI individuals, such as increased funding for mental health services in Native American communities or changes in immigration policies that affect their access to healthcare.
5. Education and Awareness: Educate the broader community about the unique challenges faced by NDNRI individuals and the importance of supporting their mental health. This can involve raising awareness about the cultural significance of mental health and well-being in NDNRI communities.
6. Trauma-Informed Care: Provide trauma-informed care that recognizes the historical trauma experienced by NDNRI individuals and addresses its ongoing impacts on their mental health. This can involve incorporating traditional healing practices and cultural rituals into treatment plans.
7. Support for Families: Offer support to families of NDNRI individuals, including education and resources to help them better understand and support their loved ones' mental health needs.
