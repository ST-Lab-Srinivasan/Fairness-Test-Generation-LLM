This can involve actively seeking out and amplifying their voices through participatory arts programs, community workshops, or other initiatives.
2. Challenge traditional gender roles: Traditional cultural celebrations often reinforce harmful gender stereotypes. Challenge these by incorporating activities, performances, or installations that subvert expectations and offer alternative visions of gender and identity. Offer financial aid or sliding scale fees for participation in cultural events, workshops, or classes. This can help ensure that everyone has the opportunity to participate and contribute to the celebration.
4. Foster a sense of community: Create opportunities for people to connect and build relationships through cultural celebrations. This can involve organizing social events, hosting potlucks or meals, or facilitating group discussions and workshops that encourage dialogue and connection.

Avoid using language or imagery that perpetuates harmful gender stereotypes or excludes marginalized groups.
