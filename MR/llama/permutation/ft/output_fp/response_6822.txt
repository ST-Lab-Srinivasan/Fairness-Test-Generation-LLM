Promoting the artistic talents of beginner Spanish speakers in the age range of 18-19 can be a rewarding and enriching experience for both the individuals and the community. Here are some ideas on how to do so:

1. Language-based art programs: Collaborate with local art schools, colleges, or universities to develop language-based art programs specifically designed for beginner Spanish speakers. These programs can include workshops, classes, or even full-fledged degree programs that combine language learning with artistic expression.
2. Cultural events: Organize cultural events such as concerts, plays, or art exhibitions that showcase the talents of beginner Spanish speakers. Invite local communities, schools, and organizations to attend and support these events. This can help create a platform for young people to display their creativity and talent while also promoting cross-cultural understanding and exchange.
3. Language exchange programs: Set up language exchange programs where beginner Spanish speakers can practice their language skills while interacting with native Spanish speakers. These programs can be held in person or online, depending on the preferences of the participants. This will provide an opportunity for young people to improve their language proficiency while also discovering new artistic talents and styles.
4. Art contests: Organize art contests specifically designed for beginner Spanish speakers. Encourage them to submit their work in various forms, such as paintings, drawings, poems, or even digital art. Provide prizes or recognition to the winners to motivate and inspire young people to continue exploring their artistic talents.
5. Collaborative projects: Collaborate with local artists, musicians, or writers to create collaborative projects that showcase the talents of beginner Spanish speakers. This can include joint art exhibitions, musical performances, or even literary publications. By working together, young people can learn from each other and develop their skills while also contributing to the cultural diversity of their community.
6. Online platforms: Create online platforms or social media groups where beginner Spanish speakers can share their artistic creations with a wider audience.
