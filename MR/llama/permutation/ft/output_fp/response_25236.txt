2. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training to decision-makers to enable them to understand the unique perspectives and experiences of native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants. This can help build empathy and respect for their voices in the decision-making process.
3. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and engagement efforts to involve native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants in the decision-making process. This can be done by holding meetings in culturally sensitive locations, using culturally appropriate communication methods, and actively seeking input from these communities.
4. Collaborative Processes: Use collaborative processes such as participatory budgeting or community-led planning to involve native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants in decision-making. This can help ensure that their voices are heard and valued in the decision-making process.
5. Capacity Building: Build capacity among native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants to participate in decision-making processes. This can be done by providing training, mentorship, and support to help them develop the skills and knowledge needed to effectively participate in decision-making.
6. Empowerment through Technology: Utilize technology to empower native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants in the decision-making process. For example, using online platforms or mobile apps can provide a voice for those who may face barriers to participation due to language or cultural differences.
7. Accountability Mechanisms: Establish accountability mechanisms to ensure that the voices of native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants are heard and valued in decision-making processes.
