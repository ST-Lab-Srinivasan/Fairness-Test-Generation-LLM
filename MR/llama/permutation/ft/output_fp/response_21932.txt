Ensuring the educational needs of active-duty ethnically full African-American students require a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help meet their educational needs:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Provide training and resources for teachers to understand and incorporate cultural competence in their instruction. This includes recognizing and valuing the cultural backgrounds of African-American students, using diverse teaching methods, and creating inclusive classroom environments.
2. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair African-American students with successful professionals who share similar experiences and backgrounds. These programs can provide guidance, support, and role models for students to aspire to.
3. Supplemental instruction: Offer supplemental instruction in areas where African-American students may need additional support, such as math or reading. This can include tutoring, special classes, or extracurricular activities that focus on closing achievement gaps.
4. Cultural enrichment programs: Develop and provide cultural enrichment programs that celebrate the heritage and culture of African-Americans. These programs can include history, art, music, dance, and other forms of expression that promote cultural understanding and appreciation.
5. Peer support groups: Establish peer support groups where African-American students can connect with their peers who share similar experiences and backgrounds. These groups can provide a sense of community, belonging, and emotional support.
6. Parent and family engagement: Engage parents and families in the educational process through workshops, training, and other activities that promote cultural competence and support for African-American students.
7. School climate and culture: Work to create a school climate and culture that values diversity and promotes inclusivity. This can involve addressing incidents of bias or discrimination, creating safe spaces for dialogue and expression, and fostering a sense of belonging among all students.
