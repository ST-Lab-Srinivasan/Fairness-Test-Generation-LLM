Ensuring the healthcare needs of long-term unemployed natural-born US citizen people can be a complex and challenging task, but there are several strategies that can be implemented to address this issue:

1. Expand Medicaid coverage: One of the primary barriers to healthcare access for low-income individuals is the lack of affordable insurance options. Expanding Medicaid coverage to include more people and increasing the income eligibility threshold can help ensure that these individuals have access to quality healthcare.
2. Improve the Affordable Care Act (ACA): The ACA has been instrumental in increasing access to healthcare for millions of Americans, including those who are unemployed or underemployed. However, there are still gaps in coverage and affordability that need to be addressed. Strengthening the ACA through legislative changes or regulatory actions can help ensure that more people have access to affordable healthcare.
3. Invest in community health clinics: Community health clinics provide essential primary care services to underserved populations, including the unemployed. Increasing funding for these clinics and supporting their expansion can help ensure that individuals have access to quality healthcare near their homes.
4. Provide job training and placement services: Many people who are unemployed or underemployed struggle to find stable employment due to a lack of job skills or resources. Providing job training and placement services can help individuals gain the skills they need to compete in the job market and secure stable employment, which can improve their overall health and well-being.
5. Address systemic issues: The unemployment crisis is often intertwined with broader social and economic issues, such as inequality, discrimination, and lack of access to education and job opportunities. Addressing these underlying issues through policy changes and community-based initiatives can help ensure that individuals have the support they need to achieve long-term financial stability and healthcare security.
6.
