Ensuring the voices of native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants to the US are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting equity and inclusion. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Community Engagement: Engage with these communities through community events, town hall meetings, and other forms of outreach. This will provide an opportunity for them to share their thoughts, concerns, and ideas directly with decision-makers.
2. Language Access: Provide language access services such as interpretation and translation to ensure that everyone can fully participate in the decision-making process. This can be especially important for recent immigrants who may not be fluent in English.
3. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for decision-makers and other stakeholders to help them understand the unique needs and experiences of native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants.
4. Advocacy Organizations: Partner with advocacy organizations that represent these communities to amplify their voices and ensure that their perspectives are taken into account in decision-making processes.
5. Inclusive Processes: Ensure that decision-making processes are inclusive and representative of the diverse communities involved. This can be achieved by using consensus-building techniques, participatory budgeting, and other inclusive methods.
6. Data Collection: Collect data on the needs and experiences of native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants to inform decision-making and ensure that their voices are represented in policy and program development.
7. Collaborative Leadership: Foster collaborative leadership structures that bring together representatives from diverse communities to make decisions and drive change.
8. Community-Led Initiatives: Support community-led initiatives that are driven by native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants themselves. This can help ensure that their voices are heard and their needs are met in a culturally appropriate manner.
9. Policy Advocacy: Advocate for policies and programs that specifically address the needs of native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants, such as language access services, cultural competency training for healthcare providers, and inclusive zoning laws.
