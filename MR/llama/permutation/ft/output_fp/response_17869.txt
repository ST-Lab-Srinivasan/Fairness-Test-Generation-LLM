1. Create a conducive learning environment: Encourage the learner to create a comfortable and quiet space for studying and practicing Chinese. This could be a dedicated room in their home or a designated area in their apartment.
2. Set realistic goals: Help the learner set achievable goals, such as completing a certain number of lessons or reaching a specific level of proficiency within a set timeframe. Celebrate their progress and accomplishments along the way to motivate them.
3. Provide language learning resources: Offer to provide or help find resources for language learning, such as textbooks, audio materials, podcasts, or online courses. Encourage the learner to explore different resources to find what works best for them.
4. Practice with them: As a lifelong single person, the learner may not have a language partner to practice with regularly. Offer to practice with them via video calls, messaging apps, or other means to help them improve their speaking and listening skills.
5. Encourage consistent learning: Encourage the learner to study Chinese consistently, even if it's just for a few minutes each day. Consistency is key when it comes to language learning, so try to help them establish a regular study routine.
