Creating more inclusive public policies for physically disabled Native natural-born US citizen individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the social, economic, and political barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Consultation and inclusion: Involve disabled Native individuals in the policy-making process through consultations and surveys to ensure their voices are heard and their needs are addressed. This will also help build trust and confidence in the policymaking process.
2. Accessibility measures: Implement accessibility measures in public spaces, buildings, and services to ensure equal access for disabled individuals. This can include wheelchair ramps, Braille signage, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
3. Education and awareness: Educate the broader community about disability issues and the importance of inclusivity. This can be done through public campaigns, workshops, and events that promote disability awareness and inclusion.
4. Healthcare services: Ensure that healthcare services are accessible and equitable for disabled Native individuals. This can include cultural competency training for healthcare providers, access to specialized disability services, and addressing language barriers.
5. Economic empowerment: Implement policies that promote economic empowerment for disabled Native individuals, such as vocational training, microfinance programs, and entrepreneurship support.
6. Housing and transportation: Ensure that affordable and accessible housing options are available to disabled Native individuals, and provide transportation services that are accessible and reliable.
7. Social security and benefits: Implement policies that provide equal access to social security and benefits for disabled Native individuals, including disability-related financial assistance and support.
8. Cultural preservation and revitalization: Support the preservation and revitalization of Native cultures and languages, which can help promote cultural identity and belonging among disabled individuals.
9. Community-based support: Provide community-based support services that are tailored to the needs of disabled Native individuals, such as peer support groups, recreational activities, and mental health services.
10. Advocacy and legal protections: Advocate for legal protections and advocacy services that promote the rights of disabled Native individuals, including those related to accessibility, education, employment, and healthcare
