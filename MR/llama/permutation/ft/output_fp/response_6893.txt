Supporting the economic empowerment of upper lower middle-income Native natural-born US citizen individuals requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the unique challenges and opportunities facing this population. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills training programs tailored to the needs of Native communities. This can include vocational training, language and cultural preservation, and higher education scholarships.
2. Entrepreneurship and Small Business Support: Encourage entrepreneurship and support small business development through mentorship, access to capital, and networking opportunities. This can help individuals build wealth and create jobs in their communities.
3. Job Training and Placement: Offer job training programs that prepare Native Americans for in-demand industries such as healthcare, technology, and renewable energy. Additionally, provide placement services to connect individuals with job opportunities that match their skills and interests.
4. Economic Development Initiatives: Foster economic development initiatives that promote sustainable business practices, cultural tourism, and community-based entrepreneurship. This can help create jobs and stimulate local economies.
5. Access to Capital: Address the lack of access to capital for Native American-owned businesses by providing access to capital through loan programs, grants, and venture capital.
6. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Provide mentorship opportunities for Native Americans to develop leadership skills, build networks, and gain insights into industry best practices.
7. Cultural Preservation and Revitalization: Support cultural preservation and revitalization efforts that promote the rights and interests of Native American communities. This can include language revitalization, traditional arts and crafts training, and cultural heritage tourism initiatives.
8. Land and Resource Management: Advocate for policies that protect Native American land and resource rights, including the right to self-governance and control over natural resources on tribal lands.
9. Healthcare Access and Delivery: Improve access to healthcare services for Native Americans through telemedicine initiatives, mobile clinics, and culturally sensitive healthcare delivery models.
