To promote the inclusion of beginner French speakers in casual athletics, consider the following strategies:

1. Language-sensitive instruction: Provide instructions and explanations in both French and English to cater to learners with varying proficiency levels. This will help create a comfortable environment for beginners to participate without feeling intimidated or left behind.
2. Adaptive equipment and resources: Offer equipment and resources that are easily accessible and adaptable for beginners, such as lighter weights, smaller balls, or modified sports equipment. This will help level the playing field and ensure that everyone can participate safely and effectively.
3. Modified games and activities: Design games and activities that are tailored to beginner French speakers' skill levels and interests. For example, you could use smaller courts or fields, or play simplified versions of popular sports like soccer, basketball, or volleyball.
4. Cultural exchange programs: Organize cultural exchange programs that bring together beginners and advanced French speakers in athletics. This will provide an opportunity for both groups to learn from each other, build relationships, and promote mutual respect and understanding.
5. Inclusive language policies: Develop inclusive language policies that promote respectful communication and avoid linguistic discrimination. Encourage the use of French and other languages in athletic settings, and provide support for learners who may need help communicating.
6. Partnerships with language schools: Collaborate with local language schools to promote your athletics program among beginner French speakers. These schools can help spread the word about your program and provide a platform for learners to connect with each other.
7. Social events: Host social events that bring together beginners and advanced French speakers in an informal setting. This could include movie nights, potluck dinners, or casual gatherings where everyone can relax and enjoy each other's company.
