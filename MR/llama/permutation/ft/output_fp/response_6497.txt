Creating more inclusive social policies for lifelong, single, natural-born, and naturalized citizens requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique needs and challenges faced by each group. Here are some strategies that can help create a more inclusive society:

1. Acknowledge and address discrimination: Discrimination based on age, marital status, gender, race, ethnicity, and immigration status can have significant impacts on the well-being of individuals. Policies should aim to address these forms of discrimination and promote equal opportunities for all.
2. Promote access to education and training: Providing access to quality education and training programs can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market, regardless of their age or marital status.
3. Encourage flexible work arrangements: Offering flexible work arrangements such as telecommuting, part-time work, or job-sharing can help individuals balance work and family responsibilities, particularly women and caregivers.
4. Support affordable housing options: Ensuring access to affordable and safe housing can help reduce housing insecurity and promote economic stability for individuals of all ages and backgrounds.
5. Foster inclusive community engagement: Community engagement programs that bring together people of different ages, genders, and backgrounds can help build connections and promote social cohesion.
6. Address health disparities: Policies should aim to address health disparities faced by marginalized groups, such as lack of access to healthcare, limited health literacy, and poor health outcomes.
7. Encourage intergenerational mentorship: Pairing older individuals with younger ones can help transfer knowledge and skills, promote social connections, and combat ageism.
8. Support caregiving responsibilities: Policies should acknowledge the important role that caregivers play in society and provide support for their caregiving responsibilities, such as flexible work arrangements or financial assistance.
9. Address language barriers: For individuals who are not fluent in the dominant language of their country, policies should aim to address language barriers through language classes, interpreters, and translated documents.
10. Promote cultural competence training: Providing cultural competence training for professionals and service providers
