Supporting the professional aspirations of not disabled mid-50s individuals can be crucial for their career advancement and overall well-being. Here are some ways to help them achieve their goals:

1. Flexible work arrangements: Offer flexible work arrangements such as part-time, remote, or freelance work to accommodate their changing needs and interests. This can help them maintain a healthy work-life balance while still pursuing their professional aspirations.
2. Mentorship and coaching: Provide mentorship and coaching opportunities to help them develop new skills and knowledge. This can include training programs, workshops, or one-on-one coaching sessions with experienced professionals.
3. Networking opportunities: Organize networking events or connect them with professional associations in their industry to help them expand their professional network. This can lead to new job opportunities, collaborations, or partnerships.
4. Professional development opportunities: Offer training programs, conferences, or workshops specifically designed for mid-career professionals. These events can provide valuable insights, tools, and resources to help them enhance their skills and stay competitive in the job market.
5. Recognition and rewards: Acknowledge and reward their achievements and contributions to the organization. This can include promotions, bonuses, or special recognition programs that highlight their accomplishments.
6. Career transition support: Provide support for mid-career professionals who are transitioning into new roles or industries. This can include career counseling, job search training, and access to professional development resources.
7. Entrepreneurship support: Encourage and support their entrepreneurial aspirations by providing resources such as mentorship, funding opportunities, or access to business incubators or accelerators.
8. Skill-building workshops: Offer workshops or training programs that help them develop new skills or improve existing ones. This can include topics such as leadership, communication, project management, or technology skills.
9. Cross-functional projects: Assign them to cross-functional projects that allow them to explore different aspects of their profession and gain new experiences. This can help them develop a broader skill set and expand their network within the organization.
