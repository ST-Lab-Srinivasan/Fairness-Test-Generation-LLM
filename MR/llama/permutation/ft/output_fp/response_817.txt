Ensuring the educational needs of wealthy and advanced aged individuals, including those over the age of 90, requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique challenges and interests. Here are some strategies to meet their educational needs:

1. Online Learning Platforms: Develop age-friendly online learning platforms that cater to the needs of older adults. These platforms should offer a range of courses, workshops, and lectures on topics that are relevant and interesting to them, such as history, art, literature, music, and science.
2. Customized Learning Paths: Create customized learning paths for each individual based on their interests, knowledge gaps, and learning style. This could involve working with a team of educators and mentors to design a personalized curriculum that meets their unique needs.
3. Intergenerational Learning: Foster intergenerational learning opportunities by partnering with local schools, colleges, and universities. This could involve older adults acting as mentors or teaching assistants to students of different ages, fostering mutual learning and collaboration.
4. Experiential Learning: Provide opportunities for experiential learning through workshops, internships, and volunteer programs. These experiences can help individuals stay engaged, active, and cognitively sharp while also contributing to their community.
5. Accessibility and Inclusivity: Ensure that all educational content and resources are accessible and inclusive of diverse learners. This could involve providing transcripts or subtitles for audio and video content, offering closed captioning, and making sure the website is compatible with assistive technologies.
6. Social Interaction: Facilitate social interaction among older adults by organizing events, workshops, and group activities that promote collaboration and mutual support. This can help combat isolation and loneliness, which are common challenges for this age group.

This could involve creating games or simulations that simulate real-world scenarios, such as financial planning or healthcare decision-making.
