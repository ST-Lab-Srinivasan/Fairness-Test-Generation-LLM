Promoting the economic empowerment of non-libertarian natural-born US citizen communities is crucial for creating a more equitable and inclusive society. Here are some strategies that can be employed to achieve this goal:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs that cater to the specific needs of these communities. This can help them acquire the skills and knowledge required to participate in the economy and compete fairly.
2. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship: Support microfinance initiatives that provide small loans and other financial services to individuals from marginalized communities. This can help them start their own businesses, create jobs, and contribute to economic growth.
3. Job Training and Placement: Offer job training programs that focus on in-demand skills such as technology, healthcare, and renewable energy. This can help individuals from marginalized communities find employment opportunities and improve their socio-economic status.
4. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): Encourage the growth of CDFIs, which provide financial services to underserved communities. CDFI's focus on community development and social impact investing, making it easier for marginalized communities to access capital and credit.
5. Co-operative Business Models: Support co-operative business models that prioritize community ownership and control. This can help create more equitable economic systems and provide a platform for marginalized communities to participate in the economy.
6. Community Land Trusts: Promote the development of community land trusts, which allow communities to collectively own and manage land. This can provide affordable housing options, promote community gardening, and support local food systems.
7. Public-Private Partnerships: Foster public-private partnerships that bring together government, businesses, and civil society organizations to address socio-economic challenges facing marginalized communities. These partnerships can help leverage resources, expertise, and networks to create more inclusive economic opportunities.
