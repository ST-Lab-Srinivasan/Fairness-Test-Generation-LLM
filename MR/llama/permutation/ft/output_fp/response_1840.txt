Creating more inclusive community events for civilian working-class people requires careful consideration of their needs, interests, and preferences. Here are some strategies to help make your events more accessible and enjoyable for this demographic:

1. Identify and address barriers: Assess the potential barriers that may prevent working-class individuals from participating in your event. This could include lack of transportation, childcare responsibilities, or financial constraints. Once you've identified these barriers, develop solutions to mitigate them.
2. Involve working-class communities in the planning process: Work with community members to design and plan events that cater to their needs and interests. This can help ensure that your event is culturally appropriate and resonates with the target audience.
3. Offer affordable tickets or free admission: Make sure that the ticket prices are reasonable and accessible to working-class individuals. Consider offering discounts or free admission for those who cannot afford the full price.
4. Provide family-friendly activities: Many working-class individuals have families and may be unable to attend events that do not cater to their children's needs. Offer activities and entertainment that are suitable for all ages, such as face painting, bounce houses, or interactive workshops.
5. Provide food and drink options: Working-class individuals may have dietary restrictions or preferences that are not catered to by high-end restaurants. Offer a variety of food and drink options that are affordable and appealing to the target audience.
6. Schedule events during non-peak hours: Working-class individuals may have difficulty attending events during peak hours, such as weekends or evenings when childcare responsibilities may be more challenging. Consider hosting events during less busy times, such as weekday afternoons or early mornings.
7. Promote events through local channels: Utilize local newspapers, flyers, and community boards to promote your event. This will help ensure that the event is well-advertised and accessible to working-class individuals who may not have access to social media or online resources.
