Supporting the educational aspirations of beginner English speaker ex-spouses can be a challenging but rewarding experience. Here are some ways you can help:

1. Language classes: Enroll in language classes with your ex-spouse to help them improve their English skills. This will not only help them learn the language but also provide an opportunity for bonding and communication.
2. Practice conversations: Offer to practice conversations with your ex-spouse on a regular basis, using authentic materials such as news articles, videos, or podcasts. This will help them improve their listening and speaking skills in real-life situations.
3. Provide resources: Share online resources, such as language learning apps, podcasts, or YouTube channels, that can help your ex-spouse learn English. You can also recommend books, articles, or videos on various topics to help them improve their vocabulary and comprehension skills.
4. Create a study group: Organize a study group with other beginner English speakers in your community. This will provide a supportive environment where your ex-spouse can practice speaking and listening to English with others who are at a similar level.
5. Offer feedback: Provide constructive feedback on their language use, highlighting areas of improvement and encouraging them to continue practicing. Be sure to do this in a kind and supportive manner to avoid discouraging them.
6. Encourage consistency: Emphasize the importance of consistent practice and encourage your ex-spouse to set aside time each day to study English. This will help them make progress and build their language skills over time.
7. Be patient: Learning a new language can be challenging, so it's essential to be patient and understanding when working with your ex-spouse. Avoid criticizing or making fun of their mistakes, as this can create a negative learning environment.
