Temporary visa holders, also known as non-immigrant visa holders, are individuals who enter a country temporarily for a specific purpose, such as work, study, or tourism. While they are in the host country, these individuals may face various challenges and limitations due to their immigration status. Here are some of the impacts of immigration status on the lives of temporary visa holders:

1. Limited Work Rights: Temporary visa holders often have restrictions on their ability to work in the host country. They may be allowed to work only in specific industries or jobs, or they may not be eligible for certain types of employment. This can limit their career opportunities and earning potential.
2. Limited Access to Healthcare: Temporary visa holders may have limited access to healthcare services in the host country. They may not be eligible for publicly funded healthcare programs, or they may face difficulties in obtaining medical care due to their immigration status.
3. Limited Access to Education: Temporary visa holders may face challenges in accessing education in the host country. They may be restricted from enrolling in certain educational programs or may have difficulty obtaining a student visa.
4. Lack of Social Security Benefits: Temporary visa holders are generally not eligible for social security benefits in the host country. This can make it difficult for them to plan for retirement or unexpected expenses.
5. Lack of Political Rights: Temporary visa holders often do not have the same political rights as citizens of the host country. They may not be able to vote, run for office, or participate in other political activities.
6. Limited Access to Public Benefits: Temporary visa holders may face limitations on their access to public benefits, such as housing assistance, food assistance, or other forms of support.
7. Stigma and Discrimination: Temporary visa holders may face discrimination and stigma due to their immigration status. This can lead to social isolation, mental health issues, and difficulties in finding housing or employment.
