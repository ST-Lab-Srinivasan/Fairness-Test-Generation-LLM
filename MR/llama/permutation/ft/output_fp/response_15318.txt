1. Consult with community members: Before planning any cultural celebration, consult with Indigenous peoples and other marginalized groups to understand their cultural practices and traditions. This will help ensure that the celebration is inclusive and respectful of their cultural heritage.

3. Use inclusive language and imagery: Use language and imagery that is inclusive of all cultures and communities. Avoid using language or symbols that are offensive or stereotypical.
4. Highlight diverse perspectives: Highlight the diverse perspectives and cultural practices of Indigenous peoples and other marginalized groups during the celebration. This can include showcasing traditional music, dance, art, and food.
5. Create a safe space: Create a safe space for Indigenous peoples and other marginalized groups to express themselves and their cultural practices without fear of judgment or discrimination.
6. Educate attendees: Provide education and information about the cultural practices and traditions of Indigenous peoples and other marginalized groups during the celebration. This can include interactive exhibits, workshops, and presentations.
