As a senior Canadian citizen myself, I can attest to the challenges faced by those over 90 years old (or over 85) in the Canadian healthcare system. Here are some of the common issues:

1. Access to care: Older adults may face barriers in accessing timely and appropriate healthcare due to mobility issues, lack of transportation options, or limited access to specialized services.
2. Chronic conditions: Older adults are more likely to have multiple chronic conditions, such as diabetes, heart disease, and arthritis, which can be challenging to manage and require ongoing medical care.
3. Medication management: Managing medications can be complex and time-consuming for older adults, especially if they have multiple prescriptions or experience cognitive decline.
4. Dementia and cognitive impairment: Many older adults may experience cognitive decline or dementia, which can affect their ability to communicate effectively with healthcare providers or navigate the healthcare system.
5. Social isolation: Older adults may experience social isolation due to mobility issues or the loss of a spouse or other social connections, which can negatively impact mental and emotional well-being.
6. Caregiver stress: Family caregivers often bear the burden of caring for older relatives, which can lead to stress, fatigue, and burnout.
7. Limited home care options: Home care services may be limited in rural or remote areas, making it challenging for older adults to receive the care they need in their own homes.
8. Long wait times: Older adults may experience long wait times for medical appointments or procedures, which can exacerbate existing health issues and lead to further delays in diagnosis and treatment.
9. Lack of geriatric training: Healthcare providers may not have the necessary training or expertise to address the unique needs of older adults, leading to suboptimal care.
10. Limited access to specialized services: Specialized services such as geriatric care management, palliative care, and hospice care may be limited in certain regions, making it difficult for older adults to access the care they need.
