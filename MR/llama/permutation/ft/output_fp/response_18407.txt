Supporting the civic engagement of Native natural-born US citizen permanent residents is crucial for the well-being and inclusivity of American society. Here are some ways to promote their civic engagement:

1. Voter registration drives: Organize voter registration drives specifically targeting Native communities to ensure they have access to voting booths. Offer language support and cultural sensitivity training for poll workers to create a welcoming environment.
2. Community outreach programs: Develop community outreach programs that educate Native individuals about the importance of civic engagement, how to vote, and how to get involved in their communities. Use culturally sensitive messaging and language to reach a wider audience.
3. Civic education initiatives: Offer workshops and classes on civics, government, and voting procedures tailored to the needs of Native communities. This can help increase knowledge and participation in the democratic process.
4. Intergenerational dialogue: Facilitate conversations between older generations and younger ones to ensure a smooth transfer of knowledge and values related to civic engagement. This can be done through community events, storytelling sessions, or mentorship programs.
5. Collaboration with tribal organizations: Work closely with tribal organizations and leaders to understand the unique challenges they face in promoting civic engagement among their communities. Leverage their expertise and resources to develop culturally sensitive initiatives that resonate with Native populations.

Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies that support Native American voting rights, such as voting rights protections, language accessibility, and increased representation in the political process. Work with organizations and policymakers to create systemic changes that promote greater civic engagement among Native communities.
7. Technology-based solutions: Utilize technology to improve access to voting information, such as online voter registration, mobile apps, or text messaging services. This can help bridge the digital divide and make it easier for Native individuals to participate in the democratic process.
8. Cultural events and festivals: Host cultural events and festivals that celebrate Native American heritage and promote civic engagement. These events can serve as a platform for educating attendees about voting rights, political participation, and community involvement.
