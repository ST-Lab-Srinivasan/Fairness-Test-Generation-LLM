Creating more inclusive cultural programs for native English speaking mixed African Americans requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions to help you create a more inclusive environment:

1. Understand the diversity of mixed race identities: Mixed race individuals have diverse experiences and backgrounds, and it's essential to recognize and respect this diversity. Avoid making assumptions about someone's identity or experiences based on their race or ethnicity.
2. Include a variety of voices and perspectives: Ensure that the cultural programs include a range of voices and perspectives from different mixed race individuals. This can be done by inviting guest speakers, hosting discussions, or creating opportunities for individuals to share their experiences.
3. Avoid tokenism: Tokenism occurs when a marginalized group is included in a program only as an afterthought or as a way to validate the experiences of other groups. Avoid tokenizing mixed race individuals by ensuring that they are included in meaningful ways and have agency in the cultural programs.
4. Foster a sense of belonging: Create a welcoming environment where mixed race individuals feel valued and included. Encourage participation and engagement, and provide opportunities for individuals to connect with one another.
5. Address microaggressions: Microaggressions are subtle, often unintentional expressions of bias or prejudice. Be aware of these in your cultural programs and address them when they occur. Create a safe space where individuals can speak up without fear of retribution.
6. Provide education and resources: Offer educational resources and workshops that help mixed race individuals understand their identity, culture, and experiences. Provide access to books, films, and other cultural artifacts that reflect the diversity of mixed race identities.
7. Engage in dialogue and reflection: Encourage open dialogue and reflection among participants about their experiences and perspectives. This can help create a deeper understanding of the complexities of mixed race identity and promote empathy and inclusivity.
8. Celebrate diversity: Celebrate the unique cultural practices, traditions, and histories of mixed race individuals. Avoid reducing these identities to a single narrative or experience. Instead, highlight the richness and diversity of mixed race cultures.
