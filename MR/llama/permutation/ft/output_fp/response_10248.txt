2. Lack of accommodations: Courts may not provide adequate accommodations for people with disabilities, such as audio describers for the visually impaired or sign language interpreters for those who are deaf or hard of hearing.
3. Insufficient training: Legal professionals, including judges and lawyers, may not receive adequate training on how to work with people with disabilities, which can lead to misunderstandings or inappropriate treatment.
4. Bias and stereotypes: Unfortunately, there are still many negative attitudes and stereotypes towards people with disabilities within society, including the legal system. This can lead to unfair treatment and discrimination against physically disabled civilian people.
5. Lack of representation: People with disabilities may face barriers when trying to access legal services, including lawyers who are knowledgeable about disability rights and the unique challenges faced by this community.
6. Difficulty navigating legal procedures: Legal processes can be complex and confusing for anyone, let alone people with cognitive or intellectual disabilities. This can lead to difficulties in understanding legal terminology, navigating court proceedings, and accessing justice.
