Promoting the economic empowerment of natural-born US citizens living in disadvantaged or marginalized communities, including those with children, is crucial for reducing poverty and improving overall well-being. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to achieve this goal:

1. Access to Education and Job Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs, tailored to the needs of the community. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and degree programs that lead to in-demand jobs.
2. Small Business Development: Support small businesses and entrepreneurship in these communities by providing access to capital, mentorship, and resources. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities for residents.
3. Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFI): CDFIs provide financial services to underserved communities, including low-income neighborhoods and rural areas. They offer loans, savings accounts, and other financial products that can help individuals and small businesses access capital.
4. Microfinance: Microfinance programs provide small loans and other financial services to low-income individuals, often in developing countries. In the US context, microfinance programs can help residents of disadvantaged communities access capital to start or grow a business.
5. Community Land Trusts (CLTs): CLTs are nonprofit organizations that provide affordable housing and community facilities while promoting community control over land use and development. This can help stabilize housing prices, reduce poverty, and promote economic mobility.
6. Co-operative Businesses: Co-operative businesses are owned and controlled by their members, often in the form of a nonprofit organization. These businesses can provide goods and services to the community while generating income for its members. Examples include worker co-ops, consumer co-ops, and housing co-ops.

Community Investment Funds: Community investment funds are designed to pool capital from individuals, foundations, and other sources to invest in local businesses, real estate, or infrastructure projects that benefit the community.
