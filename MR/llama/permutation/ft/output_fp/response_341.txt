2. Enhanced patient trust: When healthcare providers demonstrate cultural competence, patients are more likely to trust them and feel comfortable sharing personal information. This is particularly important for mixed Hispanic heritage individuals who may have experienced discrimination or marginalization in the past.
3. More accurate diagnosis and treatment: By understanding the patient's cultural beliefs and practices, healthcare providers can make more informed decisions about diagnosis and treatment. For example, a provider may need to take into account a patient's religious beliefs when developing a treatment plan that involves medication or surgery.

Better management of chronic conditions: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers better manage chronic conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, and asthma in patients from diverse backgrounds.
