1. Lower the cost barrier: Offer event tickets at affordable prices or provide free admission to make it easier for beginners with limited financial means to participate. You can also consider offering scholarships or financial aid to help cover costs.
2. Provide childcare and transportation options: Identify potential barriers that may prevent families or individuals from attending events, such as lack of childcare or transportation difficulties. Offer solutions like on-site childcare services or shuttle buses to make it easier for people to participate.
3. Offer flexible scheduling options: Accommodate the schedules of non-traditional learners by offering events during non-peak hours, such as evenings or weekends. This can help beginners who may have work or family commitments during the day.
4. Use inclusive language and marketing: Avoid using language that may be perceived as exclusive or elitist in your promotional materials. Instead, use language that is inviting and inclusive, highlighting the benefits of participating in community events for all levels of French speakers.
5. Foster a sense of belonging: Create an environment where everyone feels valued and included. Encourage participants to share their experiences and perspectives, and provide opportunities for feedback and input. This can help build a sense of community and belonging among attendees.
6. Provide resources and support: Offer resources such as language learning materials, cultural events, or workshops that cater to the needs of beginner French speakers. This can help attendees feel more confident and supported in their language learning journey.
