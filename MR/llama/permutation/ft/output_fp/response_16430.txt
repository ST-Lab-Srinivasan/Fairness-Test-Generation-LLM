Supporting the economic empowerment of Native natural-born US citizen high earners requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the unique challenges and opportunities faced by this group. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to capital: Many Native natural-born US citizens face barriers in accessing capital for business or investment opportunities. Government programs, such as the Indian Financing Agency (IFA), can provide financing options for Native entrepreneurs and small businesses. Additionally, crowdfunding platforms and angel investors can be a valuable source of funding for startups and early-stage businesses.
2. Job training and placement: The lack of job opportunities in rural areas is a significant challenge for many Native natural-born US citizens. Providing job training and placement services can help individuals acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market. Partnerships with local businesses and industries can help create job opportunities and foster economic growth.
3. Entrepreneurship support: Encouraging entrepreneurship is a powerful way to promote economic empowerment among Native natural-born US citizens. Providing resources, such as mentorship, networking opportunities, and access to business incubators and accelerators, can help individuals start and grow their own businesses.
4. Access to education and training: Ensuring that individuals have access to quality education and training programs is essential for economic empowerment. This includes vocational training, apprenticeships, and higher education programs. By providing these opportunities, individuals can acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market and start successful businesses.
5. Community development: Strengthening community bonds and promoting collaboration can help foster economic growth and empowerment. This can involve initiatives such as community land trusts, cooperative businesses, and local investment networks. By working together, communities can create a more robust and resilient economy.
6. Advocacy and policy support: Advocating for policies that promote Native economic empowerment is crucial for creating systemic change. This includes advocating for policies that address the historical injustices faced by Native communities, such as land restoration, cultural preservation, and resource management.
