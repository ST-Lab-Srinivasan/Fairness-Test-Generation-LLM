Supporting the social inclusion of beginner Chinese speaker mid-50s people can be achieved through various means. Here are some suggestions:

1. Language classes: Enroll in a language class specifically designed for seniors or beginners, which can provide a supportive and inclusive environment to learn Chinese.
2. Peer learning groups: Organize peer learning groups where mid-50s people can practice speaking Chinese with each other. This can help build confidence and foster a sense of community.
3. Cultural events: Attend cultural events that showcase Chinese culture, such as traditional music performances, calligraphy demonstrations, or martial arts exhibitions. These events can provide opportunities for socializing and learning about Chinese customs.

5. Online resources: Utilize online resources such as language learning apps, podcasts, or YouTube channels that cater to beginner Chinese speakers. These resources can provide structured lessons and exercises tailored to mid-50s learners' needs.
6. Social activities: Engage in social activities specifically designed for seniors or beginners, such as potluck dinners, game nights, or book clubs. These events can help build connections and foster a sense of community among mid-50s Chinese language learners.
7. Volunteering opportunities: Look for volunteering opportunities that allow mid-50s people to use their language skills, such as teaching Chinese to children or helping at cultural festivals. This can help build confidence and provide opportunities for socializing with people of different ages and backgrounds.
8. Mentorship programs: Participate in mentorship programs where experienced Chinese learners can mentor beginners in their 50s. This can provide guidance, support, and motivation to continue learning.
