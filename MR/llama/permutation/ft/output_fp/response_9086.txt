Congratulations on your pregnancy! As a beginner Arabic speaker, there are several ways you can continue to learn and improve your skills during this time. Here are some suggestions:

1. Online resources: Take advantage of online resources such as language learning apps, YouTube channels, and websites that offer free or affordable Arabic courses specifically designed for pregnant women. These resources often provide gentle exercises and activities that can be done from the comfort of your home.
2. Audio materials: Listen to audio materials such as podcasts, radio shows, or audio books in Arabic. This will help you improve your listening skills and get used to the sound of the language.
3. Practice with a tutor: Consider finding a tutor who can provide one-on-one instruction via video calls. This can be especially helpful during pregnancy when you may have limited mobility or energy levels.
4. Join a community: Look for online communities or language exchange groups where you can connect with other Arabic learners and practice speaking with them.
5. Use visual aids: Utilize visual aids such as flashcards, post-it notes, or whiteboard to help you remember new vocabulary and grammar rules.
6. Keep it simple: As you enter your first trimester, focus on learning basic phrases and vocabulary that you can use in everyday situations. This will help you build confidence and improve your communication skills without feeling overwhelmed.
7. Be consistent: Try to study Arabic at least 10-15 minutes a day, even if it's just for a few days a week. Consistency is key when it comes to language learning, especially during pregnancy when your energy levels may be limited.
8. Use active listening: Listen to native Arabic speakers and try to repeat what they say. This will help you improve your pronunciation and comprehension skills.
9. Read in Arabic: Find simple texts such as children's books, news articles, or short stories to read in Arabic. This will help you improve your reading skills and get used to the way words are written in the language.
