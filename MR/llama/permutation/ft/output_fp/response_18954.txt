Addressing the specific needs of native French speaker Nigerians in healthcare requires a culturally sensitive and linguistically appropriate approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Provide bilingual healthcare services: Offer healthcare services in both French and the local language to ensure that all patients can communicate effectively with healthcare providers. This can involve hiring bilingual staff, providing translated materials and forms, and using interpreters when necessary.
2. Use culturally sensitive communication strategies: Be aware of cultural differences in communication styles and use strategies that are appropriate for each culture. For example, in some Nigerian cultures, it is considered impolite to directly ask about someone's illness or health problems. Instead, start the conversation by asking how the person is feeling or how their family is doing.
3. Provide education and support in both French and local languages: Offer educational materials and support services in both languages to ensure that all patients can access the information they need. This can include brochures, flyers, posters, and videos in both languages.
4. Use culturally relevant visual aids: Use visual aids such as diagrams, pictures, and videos that are relevant to the patient's culture and language to help them understand their health conditions and treatments.
5. Train healthcare providers on cultural competence: Provide training for healthcare providers on how to communicate effectively with patients from diverse cultural backgrounds. This can include learning about different cultural beliefs, values, and practices related to health and illness.
6. Incorporate traditional healing practices: In some Nigerian cultures, traditional healing practices are an important part of healthcare. Healthcare providers should be aware of these practices and work with patients to incorporate them into their treatment plans when appropriate.
7. Foster a supportive and inclusive environment: Create a supportive and inclusive environment in the healthcare setting by promoting cultural awareness and respect for patient's beliefs and values. This can involve training staff on cultural competence, using culturally relevant artwork and decorations in the facility, and providing opportunities for patients to share their cultural practices and beliefs with staff.
