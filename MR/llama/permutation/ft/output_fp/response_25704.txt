There are several ways to support the educational aspirations of Nigerian non-widowed individuals who have been out of school for a long time:

1. Vocational Training: Provide vocational training in skills such as tailoring, hairdressing, and catering. This can help them acquire marketable skills that can earn them a living without the need to attend formal schools.
2. Adult Education Programs: Establish adult education programs that offer basic literacy and numeracy classes, as well as vocational training in areas such as computer literacy, entrepreneurship, and leadership skills.
3. Scholarships and Financial Aid: Provide scholarships and financial aid to help them pursue their educational goals. This can include sponsoring their tuition fees, providing books and other learning materials, and offering stipends for living expenses.
4. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair them with successful individuals in their desired field of study. This can provide them with valuable guidance, support, and networking opportunities.
5. Online Learning Platforms: Utilize online learning platforms to provide access to educational resources such as video lectures, e-books, and interactive quizzes. This can help them learn at their own pace and from the comfort of their homes.
6. Life Skills Training: Offer life skills training in areas such as time management, goal setting, and stress management. This can help them develop the necessary skills to navigate the challenges of education and adulthood.
7. Community Outreach Programs: Organize community outreach programs that provide educational resources and support to marginalized groups. This can include organizing workshops, seminars, and other events that promote education and personal development.
8. Collaboration with Educational Institutions: Collaborate with educational institutions to offer special programs and services tailored to the needs of non-widowed individuals who have been out of school for a long time. This can include partnerships with universities, colleges, and vocational schools to provide them with access to quality education.
