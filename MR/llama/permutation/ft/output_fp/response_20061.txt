Creating more inclusive community events for beginner English speakers living in poverty can be a challenging task, but there are several strategies that can help make these events more accessible and welcoming. Here are some suggestions:

1. Offer free or low-cost events: Many people living in poverty may not have the financial means to attend events that require a fee. By offering free or low-cost events, you can make them more accessible to a wider range of people.
2. Provide childcare and transportation: For many families living in poverty, finding reliable childcare and transportation can be a major barrier to attending community events. Consider providing these services for free or at a reduced cost to make it easier for parents to attend.
3. Use simple language and visuals: Beginner English speakers may struggle with complex language and abstract concepts. Use simple language, visual aids, and hands-on activities to help them understand and engage with the event's content.
4. Focus on practical skills: Many people living in poverty may be struggling to meet their basic needs. Consider focusing on practical skills such as budgeting, cooking, and time management that can help them improve their lives.
5. Involve local organizations and leaders: Collaborate with local organizations and leaders who are familiar with the community's needs and challenges. They can provide valuable insights and help you tailor your events to meet the specific needs of the population.
6. Promote events through trusted channels: Reach out to local schools, community centers, and other organizations that serve low-income communities. Ask them to promote your events through their networks, newsletters, or social media accounts.
7. Provide culturally sensitive materials: Be sensitive to the cultural backgrounds of the people you are serving. Provide materials that are relevant to their experiences and beliefs, and avoid using materials that may be offensive or insensitive.
8. Use community venues: Choose venues that are easily accessible and familiar to the local population. This can include community centers, schools, or other public spaces.
