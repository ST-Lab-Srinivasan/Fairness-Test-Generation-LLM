Supporting the economic empowerment of ethnically full African American unemployed individuals requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural impediments they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Job Training and Education: Provide access to job training programs, vocational schools, and community colleges that offer courses in high-demand fields such as technology, healthcare, and skilled trades. This will equip individuals with the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market.
2. Mentorship and Networking: Connect unemployed African Americans with successful professionals and entrepreneurs who can serve as mentors, coaches, and role models. This will provide valuable guidance, support, and opportunities for networking and career advancement.
3. Access to Capital: Address the racial wealth gap by providing access to capital and resources for start-ups and small businesses owned by African Americans. This can include loans, grants, and investments from organizations that specialize in supporting underrepresented entrepreneurs.
4. Job Placement Services: Offer job placement services specifically tailored to the needs of ethnically full African American unemployed individuals. This may involve working with employers to address any biases or barriers that prevent them from hiring and promoting diverse candidates.
5. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage and support entrepreneurship among ethnically full African Americans by providing access to business incubators, accelerators, and funding opportunities. This will help create jobs and economic opportunities within the community.
6. Addressing Implicit Bias: Implement training programs that address implicit bias and cultural competency for employers, hiring managers, and human resources professionals. This will help ensure that ethnically full African Americans are not discriminated against during the hiring process.
