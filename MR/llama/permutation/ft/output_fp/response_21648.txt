Celebrating the cultural contributions of recent immigrants and naturalized citizens is an important way to recognize their valuable contributions to society. Here are some ways to celebrate the cultural contributions of natural-born or recently naturalized citizen beginner Spanish speakers:

1. Host a cultural festival: Organize a festival that showcases the cultural traditions, music, dance, and food of the immigrant community. This can be a great opportunity for them to share their culture with others and feel proud of their heritage.
2. Create a cultural center: Establish a cultural center or hub where immigrants can gather, learn, and exchange ideas. This can provide a platform for them to showcase their art, music, and literature, and connect with other members of the community.
3. Offer language classes: Provide language classes specifically designed for beginner Spanish speakers. This can help them improve their language skills and become more integrated into the community.
4. Encourage intercultural dialogue: Organize events that bring together people from different cultural backgrounds to share experiences, traditions, and perspectives. This can foster mutual understanding, respect, and empathy between different communities.
5. Support immigrant-owned businesses: Encourage the local community to support immigrant-owned businesses, which can help them feel more connected to the community and provide economic opportunities for their families.
6. Create a mentorship program: Pair beginner Spanish speakers with experienced members of the community who can provide guidance and support in navigating the cultural landscape. This can help them feel more confident and connected to the community.
7. Offer job training and placement: Provide job training and placement services specifically designed for immigrants, which can help them gain employment skills and connect with local employers.
8. Establish a community garden: Create a community garden where immigrants can grow their own fruits and vegetables, providing an opportunity for them to connect with nature and their cultural heritage.
9. Host a storytelling circle: Organize a storytelling circle where immigrants can share their experiences, traditions, and cultures with each other and the local community. This can provide a platform for them to connect with others and feel proud of their heritage.
