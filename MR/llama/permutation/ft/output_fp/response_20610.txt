Supporting the leadership development of non-widowed civilian people can be done through various means. Here are some suggestions:

1. Mentorship programs: Pair them with experienced leaders who can provide guidance, support, and valuable insights into leadership roles. This can help them build confidence, gain practical experience, and develop their skills.
2. Leadership training courses: Offer structured training programs that teach them various aspects of leadership, such as communication, decision-making, problem-solving, and team management. These courses can be conducted in-person or online, depending on the preference and convenience of the participants.
3. Networking opportunities: Encourage them to connect with other leaders in their industry or community, through events, conferences, or social media groups. This can help them build relationships, learn from others, and gain access to valuable resources and information.
4. Coaching and feedback: Provide regular coaching and constructive feedback on their leadership style, actions, and decisions. This can help them identify areas for improvement and develop their skills further.
5. Project-based learning: Assign them to lead real projects or initiatives within the organization, under the guidance of a mentor or supervisor. This hands-on experience can help them apply their knowledge and skills in practical situations.
6. Peer-to-peer learning: Encourage them to learn from their peers by facilitating cross-functional teams or groups that work on specific projects or initiatives. This can foster collaboration, knowledge sharing, and leadership development.
7. Leadership assessment and evaluation: Conduct regular assessments of their leadership style and effectiveness, using validated tools and methods. This can help them identify areas for improvement and track their progress over time.
8. Book clubs or reading groups: Encourage them to read books on leadership and management, and participate in book clubs or reading groups where they can discuss and reflect on their learning.
9. Workshops and seminars: Organize workshops or seminars on various aspects of leadership, such as time management, communication, and goal setting. These events can provide valuable insights and practical skills development opportunities.
