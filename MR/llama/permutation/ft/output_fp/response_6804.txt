They may also be ineligible for Medicaid or other government-funded healthcare programs due to their immigration status.
2. Language Barriers: Many lower-income temporary visa holders may not speak English fluently, which can create a barrier when trying to communicate with healthcare providers or understand medical instructions.
3. Limited Access to Healthcare Providers: Lower-income temporary visa holders may have difficulty finding healthcare providers who are familiar with their immigration status and the specific challenges they face.
