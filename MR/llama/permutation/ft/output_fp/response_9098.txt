1. Lack of Health Insurance: Many temporary visa holders do not have access to employer-based health insurance or government-funded health programs, making it difficult for them to afford medical care.
2. Limited Access to Healthcare Providers: Temporary visa holders may face barriers in accessing healthcare services due to language or cultural barriers, lack of knowledge about the healthcare system, or limited availability of healthcare providers who are familiar with their specific visa status.
3. Difficulty Navigating the Healthcare System: Temporary visa holders may find it challenging to navigate the complex healthcare system, especially if they have limited knowledge of the local language and culture.
4. Limited Access to Specialized Care: Temporary visa holders may face difficulties in accessing specialized care, such as cancer treatment or mental health services, due to limited availability or lack of familiarity with the healthcare system.
5. Fear of Discrimination: Some temporary visa holders may be hesitant to seek medical care due to fear of discrimination or deportation based on their immigration status.
6. Limited Access to Emergency Care: Temporary visa holders may face difficulties in accessing emergency medical care, particularly if they are not eligible for emergency Medicaid or other government-funded health programs.
7. Difficulty in Obtaining Medical Records: Temporary visa holders may find it challenging to obtain their medical records, which can hinder their ability to receive proper care from a new healthcare provider.
