Creating inclusive community events for beginner Spanish speakers in their late 30s can be a rewarding and enriching experience. Here are some suggestions on how to make these events more welcoming and engaging:

1. Provide language support: Offer interpreters or language assistants at the event to help non-native Spanish speakers navigate the event and communicate with other participants. This can be especially helpful for those who may feel uncomfortable speaking in a second language.
2. Use simple language: Avoid using complex vocabulary or idioms that may be difficult for beginners to understand. Instead, use simple language and provide clear explanations of any key terms or concepts.
3. Offer visual aids: Provide visual aids such as pictures, videos, or handouts to help participants learn and practice their Spanish skills. This can also help to make the event more engaging and interactive.
4. Foster a supportive environment: Encourage participants to support and encourage each other throughout the event. This can be done through icebreaker activities or by providing opportunities for participants to share their experiences and progress.
5. Make it fun: Incorporate games, quizzes, or other interactive activities into the event to make it more enjoyable and engaging for participants. This can also help to build confidence and improve language skills.
6. Provide cultural context: Offer insights into Spanish culture and customs to help participants better understand the language and its nuances. This can be done through presentations, discussions, or interactive activities.
7. Use technology: Utilize technology such as language learning apps, digital whiteboards, or online resources to enhance the learning experience and make it more accessible for participants.
8. Offer different levels of difficulty: Cater to participants with varying levels of proficiency by offering different workshops or activities that are tailored to their needs. This can help to ensure that everyone feels comfortable and engaged throughout the event.
