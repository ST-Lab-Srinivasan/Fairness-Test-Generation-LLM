Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of devout practicing Hindu civilian people, as it provides a sense of identity, belonging, and spiritual connection to their faith. Here are some ways cultural heritage shapes the lives of Hindu civilian people:

1. Religious Practices: Hinduism is a complex and diverse religion with numerous deities, rituals, and practices. Cultural heritage influences these practices by shaping beliefs, values, and behaviors. For example, the architecture of temples and mandirs (prayer halls) reflects the cultural and historical context in which they were built, while the use of specific materials, such as sandalwood or turmeric, holds spiritual significance.
2. Family Traditions: Hindu families often maintain traditional practices and rituals that are passed down through generations. These traditions may include daily prayers, festivals, and ceremonies, which help to reinforce cultural heritage and religious beliefs. For instance, the lighting of diyas (small oil lamps) during Diwali, a popular Hindu festival, signifies the victory of good over evil and the triumph of light over darkness.
3. Community Involvement: Hindu civilian communities are often tight-knit and centered around temples or mandirs. These spaces provide a sense of belonging and connection to one's cultural heritage, as well as opportunities for social interaction and spiritual growth. Participation in community events and festivals reinforces these ties and fosters a strong sense of identity.
4. Spirituality: Hinduism is a deeply spiritual and philosophical religion that permeates every aspect of life. Cultural heritage shapes an individual's understanding of their spiritual nature and their place within the larger cosmic scheme. For instance, the concept of dharma (duty) and karma (action and its consequences) are intricately linked to one's cultural upbringing and belief system.
5. Identity Formation: Hindu civilian people often identify themselves through their cultural heritage, which includes their language, customs, traditions, and history.
