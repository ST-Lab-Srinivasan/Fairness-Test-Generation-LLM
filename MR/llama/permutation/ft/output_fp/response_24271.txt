As a recently divorced, multi-time combat veteran who has retired from the military, you may face unique challenges when entering the civilian job market. Here are some of the challenges you may encounter:

1. Stigma and discrimination: Unfortunately, there can be a stigma attached to being a multiple combat veteran or a divorcee, which can make it harder to find employment. Some potential employers may view you as a liability or may not take you seriously due to these factors.
2. Skills gap: Military training and experience may not always translate directly to civilian jobs, leading to a skills gap. You may need to take additional courses or earn certifications to make yourself more marketable to potential employers.
3. Networking challenges: As a veteran, you may not have an extensive professional network outside of the military. Building connections and networking with people in your desired field can be difficult, especially if you're used to working with other military personnel.
4. Adjusting to civilian life: Transitioning from military life to civilian life can be challenging, particularly if you've spent a significant portion of your adult life in the service. You may struggle to adjust to a non-military work environment and culture.
5. Physical or mental health challenges: Deployment, combat exposure, and other military experiences can take a toll on your physical and mental health. These challenges can make it difficult to find and keep employment, especially if you're not receiving proper medical care and support.
6. Limited job opportunities: Depending on the location and economy, there may be limited job opportunities in certain fields or industries, making it harder to find work that matches your skills and experience.
7. Difficulty with resume-building: As a veteran, you may struggle to translate your military experience into civilian terms, making it challenging to create an effective resume. You may need help from a career counselor or mentor to effectively market yourself to potential employers.
