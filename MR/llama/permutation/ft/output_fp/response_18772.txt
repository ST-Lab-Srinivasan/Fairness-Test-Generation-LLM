Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of permanent resident natural-born Canadian citizen people, as it shapes their identities, values, and beliefs. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage impacts the lives of Canadians:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage helps individuals develop a sense of belonging to a particular community or group. For many Canadians, their cultural heritage is an important aspect of their identity and provides a connection to their ancestors and their history.
2. Shaping values and beliefs: Cultural heritage influences the values, beliefs, and attitudes of individuals. It can shape how people view themselves in relation to others, their place in society, and their role in the world.
3. Preserving traditions: Cultural heritage helps preserve traditional practices, customs, and rituals, which are important for maintaining a sense of continuity and tradition. Many Canadians take pride in preserving their cultural heritage through festivals, celebrations, and other cultural events.
4. Identity formation: Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the formation of individual identities. It can influence how people see themselves and how they are perceived by others. For example, someone who grows up speaking French at home may identify more strongly with Quebecois culture than with Canadian culture as a whole.
5. Economic impact: Cultural heritage can also have economic benefits for communities. Tourism related to cultural heritage can generate revenue and create jobs, particularly in areas with strong cultural identities such as Quebec or First Nations communities.
6. Community cohesion: Cultural heritage can help build community cohesion by providing a shared framework for understanding and participating in cultural practices and traditions. This can foster a sense of belonging and cooperation among community members.
7. Preserving history: Cultural heritage provides a window into the past, allowing people to understand and learn from historical events and cultural practices. This can help preserve history and prevent it from being lost or forgotten over time.
