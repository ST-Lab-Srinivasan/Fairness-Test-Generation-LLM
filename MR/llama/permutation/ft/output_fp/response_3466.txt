Supporting the economic empowerment of immigrant recent immigrants to the US, particularly those from non-upper-class backgrounds, requires a comprehensive approach that addresses various challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide language classes, vocational training, and access to higher education to help immigrants acquire new skills and knowledge that can increase their earning potential.
2. Job Placement Services: Offer job placement services that match immigrants with employers who value diversity and provide fair compensation. This can help immigrants find decent-paying jobs more quickly.
3. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship Support: Provide microloans, mentorship, and business incubators to support immigrant entrepreneurs who want to start their own businesses. This can help create jobs for themselves and others in their communities.
4. Tax Credits and Benefits: Advocate for policies that provide tax credits or benefits specifically designed to help immigrants, such as the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) or the Child Tax Credit. These programs can help reduce poverty and increase economic mobility.
5. Workplace Inclusion Initiatives: Encourage employers to adopt policies that promote workplace inclusion, such as providing English language training, flexible work schedules, and anti-discrimination training for employees.
6. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): Support the development of CDFIs, which provide affordable loans and financial services to underserved communities, including immigrant populations.
7. Healthcare Access: Advocate for policies that ensure access to affordable healthcare for all immigrants, regardless of their legal status. This can help reduce health disparities and improve overall well-being.
8. Housing Support: Advocate for policies that provide affordable housing options for low-income immigrant families, including rent control, subsidized housing programs, and supportive housing initiatives.
9. Legal Support: Provide free or low-cost legal services to immigrants who need help with obtaining a green card, citizenship, or navigating the complex immigration system.
