1. The Native American Community Development Institute (NACDI): Founded in 1984, NACDI is a nonprofit organization that provides training, technical assistance, and lending services to support the economic development of Native American communities. They have helped create hundreds of businesses and jobs, and have invested over $50 million in community development projects.
2. The Indigenous Women's Network (IWN): Based in Minneapolis, IWN is a grassroots organization that supports the empowerment of Indigenous women through leadership development, cultural revitalization, and community building. They provide programming for young women, mentorship opportunities, and advocacy for policy change.
3. The Native American Business Alliance (NABA): NABA is a trade association that represents the interests of Native American-owned businesses in the United States. They provide networking opportunities, training programs, and advocacy services to support the growth and success of their member businesses.
4. The Intertribal Council of Michigan (ICM): ICM is a statewide organization that works to improve the health, education, and economic well-being of Native American communities in Michigan. They provide technical assistance, training programs, and advocacy services to support the development of Native American-led initiatives.
5. The Indigenous Leadership Institute (ILI): Based in Arizona, ILI is a nonprofit organization that provides leadership development programs, mentorship opportunities, and community organizing training for Indigenous peoples. They work to build the capacity of Indigenous leaders and organizations to address social, economic, and political issues affecting their communities.
