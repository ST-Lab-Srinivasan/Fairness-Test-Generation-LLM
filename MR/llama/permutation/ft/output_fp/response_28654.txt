Supporting the economic empowerment of older, non-binary individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges they face. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Provide access to financial education and planning tools: Many older adults may not have received adequate financial education or training in their younger years, which can make it difficult for them to navigate retirement planning, investing, and other financial matters. Offer workshops, seminars, or one-on-one coaching sessions that provide guidance on managing finances, creating a budget, and saving for the future.
2. Encourage entrepreneurship and side hustles: Many non-binary individuals may face discrimination in the traditional workforce, which can limit their earning potential. Encourage them to explore entrepreneurial opportunities or side hustles that allow them to monetize their skills and interests. Provide resources and support for starting a business or freelancing.
3. Advocate for age-friendly policies: Advocate for policies that promote age diversity and inclusion in the workforce, such as flexible work arrangements, age-based discrimination protection, and access to affordable healthcare. Encourage employers to adopt policies that support the economic empowerment of older adults.
4. Promote access to technology: Many non-binary individuals may face barriers in accessing or affording technology that can help them reach their full potential. Advocate for programs that provide affordable access to devices, internet, and digital skills training.
5. Foster intergenerational connections: Older adults have a wealth of knowledge and experience that can benefit younger generations. Encourage mentorship opportunities between older adults and younger individuals, which can lead to valuable learning experiences for both parties.
6. Create age-appropriate financial products: Develop financial products that cater to the unique needs of older adults, such as retirement accounts, reverse mortgages, or long-term care insurance. Provide clear information and accessibility features to ensure these products are usable for all individuals.
