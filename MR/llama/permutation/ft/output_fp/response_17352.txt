Ensuring the educational needs of mixed African-American active-duty students can be challenging due to the unique experiences and backgrounds of these students. However, here are some strategies that educational institutions can use to better meet their needs:

1. Cultural Competency Training for Educators: Provide training on cultural competency and sensitivity for educators to recognize and address the diverse needs of mixed African-American students. This could include understanding the historical and contemporary experiences of Black people, as well as the unique challenges faced by mixed-race individuals.
2. Inclusive Curriculum: Develop an inclusive curriculum that reflects the diverse backgrounds and experiences of mixed African-American students. This could include incorporating literature, history, and cultural perspectives that represent a range of racial and ethnic identities.
3. Safe Spaces: Create safe spaces for mixed African-American students to share their experiences and connect with peers who understand their backgrounds. This could include setting aside time in class or creating a designated space on campus for students to discuss issues related to race, identity, and cultural heritage.
4. Mentorship Programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair mixed African-American students with faculty members or peers who can provide guidance and support based on their shared experiences. This could include offering one-on-one mentoring, group mentoring, or peer-to-peer mentoring.
5. Academic Support Services: Provide academic support services tailored to the needs of mixed African-American students. This could include tutoring, study groups, and academic advising to help students navigate the academic environment and achieve their goals.
6. Campus Resources: Offer campus resources that cater to the unique needs of mixed African-American students, such as cultural events, workshops, and support groups. These resources could provide a safe space for students to explore their identity, connect with peers, and access additional support.

Involve Mixed African-American Students in Decision-Making Processes: Encourage mixed African-American students to participate in decision-making processes related to educational policies and practices that affect them directly.
