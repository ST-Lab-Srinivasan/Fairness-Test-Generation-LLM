Promoting the social inclusion of Native American communities, particularly those living in poverty, requires a comprehensive and multifaceted approach that addresses the unique cultural, historical, and socio-economic factors affecting these communities. Here are some strategies that can help promote social inclusion:

1. Community-based initiatives: Develop community-based initiatives that engage Native Americans in decision-making processes and provide opportunities for them to take leadership roles. This can include cultural preservation projects, language revitalization programs, and economic development initiatives that prioritize the needs of Native American communities.
2. Education and job training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that cater to the specific needs of Native American communities. This can help reduce poverty rates and improve socio-economic outcomes for community members.
3. Healthcare services: Ensure access to culturally competent healthcare services that address the unique healthcare needs of Native American communities. This can include traditional healing practices, mental health services, and chronic disease management programs.
4. Land reform: Address the historical injustices of land dispossession by advocating for land reform policies that prioritize the return of ancestral lands to Native American communities. This can help promote economic development and cultural preservation within these communities.
5. Cultural revitalization: Support cultural revitalization initiatives that promote the preservation of traditional practices, languages, and beliefs. This can include language immersion programs, traditional arts and crafts workshops, and community-based cultural festivals.
6. Collaborative governance: Foster collaborative governance structures that involve Native American communities in decision-making processes at the local, state, and federal levels. This can help ensure that policies and programs are designed with the needs and priorities of these communities in mind.
7. Economic development: Support economic development initiatives that benefit Native American communities, such as small business loans, entrepreneurship training, and infrastructure investments. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities within these communities.
