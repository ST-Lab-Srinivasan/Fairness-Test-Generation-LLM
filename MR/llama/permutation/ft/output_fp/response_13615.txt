The impact of immigration status on the lives of temporary visa holders and recent immigrants to India can be significant. Here are some of the common challenges they face:

1. Uncertainty and Insecurity: Temporary visa holders often live with a sense of uncertainty and insecurity, as their legal status can change at any time, and they may face deportation if they overstay their visa or violate immigration rules.
2. Limited Access to Employment Opportunities: Many temporary visa holders are restricted from working in certain industries or for specific employers, which limits their job prospects and earning potential. They may also face discrimination in the workplace due to their immigration status.
3. Difficulty Accessing Basic Services: Temporary visa holders and recent immigrants may face difficulties accessing basic services such as healthcare, education, and social welfare programs, as they are not always eligible for these benefits.
4. Social Isolation: Immigrants may experience social isolation due to language barriers, cultural differences, and limited social connections in their host country. This can lead to feelings of loneliness, depression, and anxiety.
5. Stigma and Discrimination: Immigrants may face discrimination and stigma based on their immigration status, which can affect their mental health and well-being. They may also experience xenophobia and racism, which can make it difficult for them to integrate into society.
6. Financial Insecurity: Temporary visa holders and recent immigrants may face financial insecurity due to limited access to employment opportunities and resources. They may also struggle to afford basic necessities such as housing, food, and healthcare.
7. Limited Access to Legal Protections: Immigrants may have limited access to legal protections, such as labor laws and human rights, which can make it difficult for them to seek redress for any abuses or violations they may experience.
