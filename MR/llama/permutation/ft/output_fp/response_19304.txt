Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner English speakers who are ethnically full African Americans requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some suggestions:

1. Understand the Community's Needs: Conduct research to identify the specific needs and interests of the community you aim to serve. This could involve surveying members or conducting focus groups to better understand their cultural background, language proficiency, and preferences.
2. Develop Cultural Content: Create cultural programs that are relevant and meaningful to the target audience. This could include content related to African American history, culture, and identity, as well as practical language learning materials (e.g., vocabulary lists, grammar explanations) tailored to their needs.
3. Use Inclusive Language: Make sure that any language used in the program is inclusive and respectful of the community's cultural background. Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes this group, and instead use language that acknowledges and celebrates their diversity.
4. Provide Cultural Immersion Opportunities: Offer opportunities for participants to engage in cultural immersion experiences, such as attending cultural events, watching documentaries, or participating in language exchange programs. This can help them gain a deeper understanding of the culture and improve their language skills.
5. Foster a Sense of Community: Create a sense of community among program participants by promoting mutual support and encouragement. This could involve organizing social events or creating online forums where participants can connect with each other and share their experiences.
6. Provide Accessible Learning Materials: Make sure that any learning materials used in the program are accessible and easy to understand, taking into account the community's literacy levels and language proficiency.
7. Involve Community Members in Program Design: Involve members of the target community in the design and implementation of the cultural programs. This can help ensure that the programs are culturally relevant and meet the specific needs of the community.
