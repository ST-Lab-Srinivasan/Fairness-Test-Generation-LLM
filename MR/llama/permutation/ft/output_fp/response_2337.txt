Cultural diversity plays a significant role in enriching the lives of native-born US citizens, particularly those living just above the poverty line. Here are some ways cultural diversity can positively impact their lives:

1. Exposure to new ideas and perspectives: Cultural diversity exposes individuals to unique viewpoints, beliefs, and practices that they may not have encountered otherwise. This broadening of horizons can foster creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills.
2. Enhanced cultural competence: Interacting with people from diverse backgrounds helps individuals develop a deeper understanding and appreciation of different cultures. This can lead to increased cultural competence, which is essential in today's globalized society.
3. Improved social cohesion: Cultural diversity can help bridge social gaps and foster a sense of community among people from different backgrounds. By promoting mutual understanding and respect, cultural diversity can contribute to a more harmonious and inclusive society.
4. Economic benefits: Cultural events and festivals celebrating diverse heritages can attract visitors and generate economic activity for local businesses. This can help boost the local economy and create job opportunities, particularly in areas with high levels of poverty.
5. Preservation of cultural heritage: Cultural diversity helps preserve the unique customs, traditions, and practices of various ethnic groups. By valuing and celebrating these aspects of culture, individuals can help ensure their continued existence for future generations.
6. Increased empathy and understanding: Engaging with people from diverse backgrounds can foster greater empathy and understanding of the experiences and challenges faced by different communities. This can lead to more effective social support networks and a greater sense of social responsibility.
7. Enhanced identity formation: Cultural diversity can provide individuals with a richer sense of self and identity, as they explore and learn about their own cultural heritage while engaging with others.
