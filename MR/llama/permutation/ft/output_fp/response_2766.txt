1. Personalized Learning Plans: Develop personalized learning plans for each NES student, taking into account their prior knowledge, learning styles, and academic goals. This will help tailor the instruction to meet their unique needs and ensure they make progress towards their academic objectives.

Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for teachers to help them understand the cultural backgrounds of NES students and how these may impact their learning experiences. This can include training on cross-cultural communication, diversity, and inclusive teaching practices.
3. Native Speaker Facilitators: Consider hiring facilitators who are native English speakers to provide additional support and guidance to NES students. These facilitators can help bridge the language gap and provide additional academic support, such as reading comprehension, writing skills, and study strategies.
4. Peer Mentoring: Pair NES students with peers who are native English speakers to act as mentors and provide additional support. This can help NES students feel more connected and supported within the school community.
5. Language Support Services: Offer language support services, such as English language learning classes or tutoring, to help NES students improve their language proficiency. This can help them better understand academic content and participate more fully in classroom activities.
6. Academic Adjustment Counseling: Provide academic adjustment counseling to help NES students adapt to the academic demands of the school. This can include counseling on time management, study skills, and test-taking strategies.
7. Social Integration Activities: Organize social integration activities that bring together NES students with other international students. This can help foster a sense of community and belonging among NES students, which is essential for their academic success.
