1. Job Training and Placement Programs: Provide job training and placement programs tailored to the needs of Native communities. These programs could include vocational training, language and cultural sensitivity training, and career counseling.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship in Native communities by providing access to capital, mentorship, and business development resources. This could involve partnering with local organizations to provide small business loans, startup grants, or incubator programs.
3. Apprenticeship Opportunities: Create apprenticeship opportunities in industries such as construction, healthcare, and technology. These programs would allow individuals to learn new skills while earning a paycheck and gaining valuable work experience.
4. Mentorship Programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair experienced professionals with out-of-work Native individuals. This could involve matching mentees with professionals in their desired field, providing guidance on job searches, and offering support in navigating the professional landscape.
5. Addressing Systemic Barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers that prevent Native individuals from finding and keeping employment. This could involve advocating for policy changes, such as improved access to education and job training programs, or challenging discriminatory practices in hiring and promotion processes.
6. Community Development Initiatives: Support community development initiatives that create jobs and economic opportunities within Native communities. This could involve investing in local businesses, supporting cooperative ownership structures, or providing funding for infrastructure projects that create jobs and stimulate economic growth.
