Promoting the educational success of beginner English speaker physically disabled students requires a holistic and inclusive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that educators, policymakers, and parents can implement:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the learning environment is accessible and conducive to physical disability. This includes providing wheelchair accessibility, braille or large print materials for visual impairments, and assistive technology for students with mobility or dexterity challenges.
2. Accommodations: Provide individualized accommodations based on each student's needs. This may include extended time to complete assignments, note-taking services, or assistive technology such as voice-to-text software.
3. Inclusive teaching methods: Use inclusive teaching methods that cater to the diverse learning needs of all students. This includes differentiated instruction, Universal Design for Learning (UDL), and scaffolding techniques that provide support as needed.
4. Peer support: Encourage peer support and collaboration among able-bodied and physically disabled students. This can be done through joint projects, pair programming, or simply encouraging students to work together and learn from each other's strengths and challenges.
5. Mental health support: Provide mental health support and resources for all students, including those with physical disabilities. This can help alleviate stress and anxiety associated with navigating an unfamiliar learning environment.
6. Curriculum modification: Modify the curriculum to meet the needs of physically disabled students. This may involve simplifying complex concepts, breaking down tasks into smaller steps, or providing additional support for students who need it.
7. Technology integration: Integrate technology that can assist physically disabled students in their learning journey. For example, text-to-speech software, voice-controlled devices, or assistive communication apps can help students with mobility or speech challenges.
8. Family involvement: Encourage family involvement and support to help students with physical disabilities succeed academically. This can include providing resources and strategies for parents to use at home, as well as involving them in the educational process.
