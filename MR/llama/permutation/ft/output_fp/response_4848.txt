Creating more inclusive community events for active-duty beginner English speakers can be achieved by following these steps:

1. Identify the needs of the target audience: Understand the challenges and barriers that beginners face when attending community events, such as feeling intimidated or excluded due to their language proficiency.
2. Provide language support: Offer interpretation services, such as simultaneous interpretation or translation assistance, to help beginner English speakers participate in the event. This can be done through volunteers or hiring professional interpreters.
3. Simplify communication: Use simple language and visual aids to make sure that all participants can understand the content being presented. This can include using pictures, videos, or handouts with key phrases written in both English and the local language.
4. Create a welcoming environment: Make sure that beginners feel welcome and included by providing a friendly and supportive atmosphere. This can be done by training event staff and volunteers to be sensitive to the needs of non-native English speakers.
5. Offer cultural activities: Incorporate cultural activities that are relevant to the target audience, such as traditional dances or music performances. This can help create a sense of community and belonging among participants.
6. Provide childcare and other support services: Offering childcare and other support services can help beginners feel more comfortable attending events by reducing their stress levels and allowing them to focus on the event itself.
7. Promote events through multiple channels: Advertise events through various channels, such as social media, flyers, posters, and local newspapers, to reach a wider audience and attract more beginners.
8. Offer language learning opportunities: Provide opportunities for participants to learn English or other languages during the event, such as language exchange programs or language classes.
9. Celebrate diversity: Embrace and celebrate the cultural diversity of the participants by incorporating elements from different cultures into the event. This can include traditional foods, music, and decorations.
10. Evaluate and improve: Continuously evaluate the events and gather feedback from participants to identify areas for improvement. Make adjustments accordingly to ensure that future events are more inclusive and accessible to beginner English speakers.
