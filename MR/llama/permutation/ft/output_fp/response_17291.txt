Supporting the educational aspirations of beginner Arabic speaker civilian people can be done through various means. Here are some ways:

1. Language classes: Offer language classes specifically designed for beginners, covering basic grammar and vocabulary. These classes can be conducted in-person or online, and can include interactive activities and multimedia resources to enhance learning.
2. Language exchange programs: Organize language exchange programs where Arabic speakers can practice speaking with non-native speakers, and vice versa. This can help build confidence and improve communication skills.
3. Cultural events: Host cultural events that showcase Arabic culture, such as food festivals, music performances, or film screenings. These events can provide an opportunity for people to learn about the culture and language in a fun and interactive way.
4. Online resources: Provide access to online resources, such as language learning apps, podcasts, and video tutorials. These resources can be used to supplement classroom learning or for self-study.
5. Mobile apps: Develop mobile apps that provide Arabic language lessons, vocabulary building exercises, and grammar drills. These apps can be downloaded and used on the go.
6. Language immersion programs: Organize language immersion programs where participants can spend time in an Arabic-speaking environment to practice speaking and listening. These programs can be conducted in countries where Arabic is spoken, or at cultural centers or language schools.
7. Personalized coaching: Provide one-on-one coaching sessions with experienced tutors who can help beginners learn the language and culture. This can include tailored lesson plans and feedback on pronunciation and grammar.
8. Gamification: Create games or interactive activities that make learning Arabic fun and engaging. These can be played online or in-person, and can involve role-playing, problem-solving, and other interactive exercises.
9. Community outreach: Reach out to local communities and organizations to promote the importance of learning Arabic. This can involve presentations, workshops, or cultural events that highlight the benefits of learning the language.
10. Scholarships: Offer scholarships or financial assistance to individuals who want to learn Arabic but may face economic barriers. This can help make language classes more accessible
