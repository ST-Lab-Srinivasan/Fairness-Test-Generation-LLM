Non-middle-class mixed Asian heritage people in the healthcare system may face a range of challenges, including:

1. Lack of cultural competency: Healthcare providers may not be familiar with the specific cultural practices and beliefs of mixed Asian heritage individuals, leading to misunderstandings or miscommunications.
2. Stereotyping and stigma: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may face stereotypes and stigma based on their race or ethnicity, which can affect their access to healthcare and the quality of care they receive.
3. Language barriers: Depending on the specific cultural background of the individual, language barriers may exist that can hinder effective communication between the patient and healthcare provider.
4. Limited access to culturally competent providers: Finding healthcare providers who are knowledgeable about mixed Asian heritage individuals' cultural practices and beliefs may be challenging, particularly in rural or underserved areas.
5. Health disparities: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may face health disparities related to their specific cultural background, such as higher rates of certain chronic diseases or lower life expectancy compared to the general population.
6. Lack of representation in medical research: There may be a lack of representation of mixed Asian heritage individuals in medical research studies, which can lead to a lack of understanding of their health needs and preferences.
7. Systemic barriers: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may face systemic barriers in accessing healthcare services, such as discrimination, bias, or lack of access to healthcare facilities.
8. Lack of culturally competent mental health services: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may face challenges in accessing culturally competent mental health services, which can exacerbate existing mental health disparities.
9. Limited availability of cultural resources: Depending on the specific cultural background of the individual, there may be limited availability of cultural resources such as traditional healers, spiritual leaders, or community organizations that provide support and advocacy.
10. Lack of culturally sensitive end-of-life care: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may face challenges in accessing culturally sensitive end-of-life care, which can be particularly important for individuals who hold strong cultural beliefs and practices related to death and dying.
