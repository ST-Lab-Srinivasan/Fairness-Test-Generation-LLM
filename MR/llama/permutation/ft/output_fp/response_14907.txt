Temporary visa holders, including international students and workers, face several challenges when accessing healthcare services in Australia. As lifelong singles, they may have difficulty navigating the healthcare system, especially if they do not have a permanent address or a local doctor. Some of the specific challenges faced by temporary visa holders include:

1. Difficulty finding affordable health insurance: Temporary visa holders are often not eligible for public health insurance, and private health insurance can be expensive. They may struggle to find affordable coverage, which can result in delayed or skipped medical appointments.
2. Limited access to specialist care: Temporary visa holders may have difficulty accessing specialist care, especially if they do not have a referral from a general practitioner. This can be a challenge for people with complex health needs or chronic conditions that require ongoing specialist care.
3. Difficulty obtaining medical records: Temporary visa holders may struggle to obtain their medical records, which can make it difficult to manage their health and access necessary medical care.
4. Language barriers: For temporary visa holders who do not speak English fluently, navigating the healthcare system can be challenging. They may have difficulty communicating with healthcare providers or understanding medical instructions.
5. Difficulty finding a local doctor: Temporary visa holders may have difficulty finding a local doctor who is familiar with their medical history and can provide ongoing care. This can lead to delays in receiving necessary medical care and treatment.
6. Uncertainty about healthcare coverage: Temporary visa holders may be unsure about their eligibility for public or private health insurance, which can make it difficult to plan for medical expenses and access necessary care.
