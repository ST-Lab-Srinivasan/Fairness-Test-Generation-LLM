Creating more inclusive cultural programs for native English speaker mixed African American individuals requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Incorporate diverse perspectives: Ensure that the program's leadership and planning committees reflect the diversity of the community you aim to serve. This can be achieved by actively seeking out input from individuals with different backgrounds, experiences, and identities.
2. Use inclusive language: Avoid using language that assumes a single cultural identity or perpetuates stereotypes. Instead, use language that acknowledges and celebrates the diversity of the community.
3. Highlight intersections: Mixed race individuals often have multiple identities and experiences. Programs should acknowledge these intersections by incorporating topics and perspectives that reflect the complex identities of the community.
4. Create safe spaces: Provide opportunities for mixed race individuals to connect with one another and share their experiences without feeling tokenized or marginalized. Safe spaces can be created through dedicated events, workshops, or online platforms.
5. Offer education and resources: Provide access to educational resources that acknowledge the history and contemporary experiences of mixed race individuals. This can include literature, films, and other media that highlight the diversity of mixed race experiences.
6. Engage in intersectional dialogue: Encourage program participants to engage in open and respectful dialogue about their intersections of identity, including race, ethnicity, gender, sexuality, and more. This can help create a more inclusive environment for all participants.
7. Avoid cultural appropriation: Be mindful of the cultural practices and traditions that you incorporate into your programs. Make sure to acknowledge and respect the cultural heritage of all communities involved.
8. Celebrate mixed race identity: Create programs that celebrate the unique experiences and perspectives of mixed race individuals. This can involve showcasing art, music, dance, or other forms of expression that reflect the diversity of the community.
