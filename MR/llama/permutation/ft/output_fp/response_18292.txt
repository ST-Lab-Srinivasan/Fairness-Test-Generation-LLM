Mentorship can play a significant role in the success of independent, deeply lower-class individuals. Here are some ways in which mentorship can help:

1. Access to knowledge and resources: A mentor can provide guidance and support in accessing educational and career opportunities that may not have been available otherwise. This can include information about job markets, training programs, and scholarships.
2. Networking opportunities: A mentor can introduce the mentee to their professional network, providing access to valuable connections and resources. This can help the mentee build relationships and gain exposure in their industry.
3. Personal development: A mentor can offer advice and support on personal growth and development, helping the mentee to identify and work towards their goals. This can include guidance on time management, communication skills, and emotional intelligence.
4. Confidence building: A mentor can help build the mentee's confidence and self-esteem by providing positive feedback and encouragement. This can be especially important for individuals from lower socio-economic backgrounds who may face additional barriers to success.
5. Role modeling: A mentor can serve as a role model, demonstrating what is possible through hard work and determination. This can inspire the mentee to set goals and work towards achieving them.
6. Accountability: A mentor can provide accountability by checking in with the mentee regularly and tracking their progress. This can help the mentee stay motivated and on track towards their goals.
7. Support system: A mentor can offer emotional support and a sounding board for ideas and concerns. This can be especially important for individuals who may not have access to other forms of support.
8. Advocacy: A mentor can advocate for the mentee's interests and goals within their own network, helping to open doors and create opportunities that may not have been available otherwise.
9. Personalized guidance: A mentor can provide personalized guidance based on their own experiences and knowledge of the industry or field. This can help the mentee navigate unique challenges and make informed decisions about their career path.
