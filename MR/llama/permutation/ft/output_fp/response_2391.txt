Title: The Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Beginner Spanish Speakers Post-Divorce

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, particularly those who have recently gone through a life-altering experience like divorce. This study aims to explore how cultural heritage influences the identities of beginner Spanish speakers post-divorce. By examining the relationship between cultural heritage and identity, this research can help us understand how individuals adapt to new circumstances and form their identities anew.

Research Questions:

1. How does cultural heritage affect the identities of beginner Spanish speakers post-divorce?
2. What are the specific ways in which cultural heritage influences identity formation during this time?
3. How do individuals navigate their cultural heritage and identity after a divorce, and what strategies do they use to maintain a sense of self?

Theoretical Framework:
This study will draw on the theoretical frameworks of identity theory, cultural heritage studies, and language acquisition. Identity theory provides a framework for understanding how individuals form and maintain their identities, while cultural heritage studies examine the role of culture in shaping individual identities. Language acquisition theories can help explain how language use and proficiency impact identity formation.

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research design, using semi-structured interviews with a purposive sample of 20 beginner Spanish speakers who have gone through a divorce. Participants will be recruited from local language learning centers and online communities. Interviews will be audio recorded, transcribed, and analyzed for themes related to cultural heritage and identity.

Expected Findings:
This study anticipates that cultural heritage will play a significant role in shaping the identities of beginner Spanish speakers post-divorce. Specifically, participants are expected to draw on their cultural heritage to make sense of their experiences, emotions, and thoughts. The findings may reveal how cultural heritage influences identity formation through language use, cultural practices, and social interactions.
