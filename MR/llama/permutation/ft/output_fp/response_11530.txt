Creating more inclusive community events for civilian Nigerian people requires thoughtful planning and execution. Here are some suggestions to make your events more welcoming and representative of the local population:

1. Inclusive Planning: Involve a diverse group of people in the planning process, including representatives from different ethnic and religious groups, women, youth, and people with disabilities. This will ensure that the event caters to the needs and interests of everyone in the community.
2. Accessible Venue: Choose a venue that is easily accessible for people with disabilities, has adequate parking and transportation options, and is located in a safe and inclusive environment.
3. Cultural Performances: Incorporate cultural performances that reflect the diversity of Nigerian communities, such as traditional music, dance, and art. This will help showcase the rich cultural heritage of the country and provide an opportunity for people to learn about each other's traditions.
4. Food Festivals: Organize food festivals that highlight the diverse culinary traditions of Nigeria, including local dishes and snacks. This will provide an opportunity for people to taste and learn about different cultures through food.
5. Interactive Activities: Plan interactive activities that promote socialization and community building, such as team-building exercises, group games, and volunteer opportunities. This will help create a sense of belonging among participants.
6. Language Support: Provide interpretation and translation services for people who may not speak the dominant language in the area. This will ensure that everyone can participate and understand the event's activities.
7. Sensory-Friendly Environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by providing comfortable seating, adequate lighting, and reduced noise levels. This will help people with sensory processing issues to enjoy the event without feeling overwhelmed.
