Creating more inclusive community programs for non-disabled young adults (ages 20s) requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions to help you design and implement such programs:

1. Understand the needs and preferences of the target group: Conduct research or surveys to understand what type of activities, events, or workshops would be most appealing to this age group. Consider their interests, hobbies, and lifestyles when designing the program.
2. Involve the community in the planning process: Engage with local organizations, community groups, and individuals to get their input on what types of programs they would like to see for young adults. This will help ensure that the program is relevant and meets the needs of the community.
3. Foster an inclusive environment: Ensure that the program is accessible and welcoming to all participants, regardless of their abilities or disabilities. Provide accommodations such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpreters, or audio describers for visually impaired individuals.
4. Offer a variety of activities: Provide a range of activities that cater to different interests and abilities, such as sports, art classes, music workshops, or volunteer opportunities. This will help ensure that there is something for everyone and that participants can find activities they enjoy and are passionate about.
5. Promote social connections: Young adults with disabilities often face social isolation, so it's important to create opportunities for them to connect with their peers. Consider organizing social events or gatherings where participants can interact with each other and build friendships.
6. Collaborate with local businesses: Partner with local businesses to provide job training and placement opportunities for young adults with disabilities. This can help them develop work skills, gain independence, and become contributing members of their communities.
7. Provide ongoing support: Offer continuous support to program participants, such as counseling or mentoring services, to help them navigate any challenges they may face in their personal and professional lives.
