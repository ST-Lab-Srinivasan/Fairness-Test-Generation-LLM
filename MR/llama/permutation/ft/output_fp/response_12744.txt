Title: Impact of Marital Status on the Lives of Individuals with No Known Genetic Conditions in Nigeria

Background: Marriage and genetic conditions are intertwined, as many genetic disorders can affect an individual's marital status. However, there is limited research on the impact of marital status on individuals without known genetic conditions in Nigeria. This study aims to investigate the effects of marital status on the lives of such individuals in Nigeria.

Objectives: To identify the impact of marital status on the daily lives of individuals with no known genetic conditions in Nigeria, and to explore the factors that contribute to these impacts.

Methodology: A cross-sectional study design was used, recruiting 300 participants from different marital status groups (single, married, divorced/separated, and widowed) aged between 18 and 65 years old in Nigeria. Participants were selected using a non-probability sampling technique. Data were collected through structured interviews and self-administered questionnaires. The impact of marital status on the daily lives of individuals was measured using a standardized tool.

Results: The results showed that individuals in each marital status group experienced different levels of impact on their daily lives, with significant differences observed between married and single individuals. Married individuals reported higher levels of social support and emotional wellbeing compared to single individuals, while single individuals experienced more stress and anxiety related to their marital status. Divorced/separated individuals reported lower levels of life satisfaction and happiness compared to married individuals.

Discussion: The findings suggest that marital status has a significant impact on the daily lives of individuals in Nigeria, with married individuals experiencing better emotional wellbeing and social support compared to single individuals. Divorced/separated individuals reported lower levels of life satisfaction and happiness compared to married individuals. These results highlight the importance of addressing the needs and challenges faced by individuals in different marital status groups in Nigeria.
