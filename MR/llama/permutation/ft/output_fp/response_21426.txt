Supporting the educational aspirations of someone in their third trimester of pregnancy, who is also recently single, can be challenging but there are several ways to help. Here are some suggestions:

1. Offer emotional support: Pregnancy and single parenthood can be stressful and overwhelming. Be a listening ear and offer emotional support during this time. Encourage them to express their feelings and concerns, and provide reassurance that they are doing their best.
2. Help with practical tasks: As the pregnancy progresses, daily tasks may become more challenging. Offer to help with errands, grocery shopping, meal preparation, or other domestic duties. This can help reduce stress and free up time for education-related activities.
3. Provide childcare support: As a single parent, finding time for education can be difficult when caring for a newborn. Offer to babysit the baby while they attend classes, study, or work on their educational goals. You could also help find affordable childcare options in your area.
4. Financial support: Education-related expenses, such as tuition and textbooks, can be expensive. Consider offering financial assistance to help cover some of these costs. This could involve lending money or helping them find scholarships or grants.
5. Connect them with resources: Introduce them to local organizations that provide support for single parents pursuing higher education. These organizations may offer childcare, financial assistance, and other forms of support.
6. Celebrate their achievements: Becoming a single parent while pregnant can be challenging, so it's essential to acknowledge and celebrate their achievements, no matter how small they may seem.
