Ensuring that the educational needs of non-middle-class non-libertarian individuals are met requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the systemic barriers and biases that exist in the current education system. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Inclusive curriculum design: Develop curricula that reflect the diversity of students' backgrounds, experiences, and identities. This includes incorporating perspectives and content that acknowledge and address the systemic inequalities faced by marginalized communities.
2. Teacher training and support: Provide ongoing professional development for teachers to help them understand and address the diverse needs of their students. This can include training on culturally responsive teaching practices, differentiated instruction, and trauma-informed care.
3. Student-centered learning environments: Create learning environments that prioritize student voice, agency, and choice. This can involve student-led projects, collaborative problem-solving, and flexible pacing and sequencing of material.
4. Access to resources and technology: Ensure that all students have access to the resources and technology they need to succeed, regardless of their socioeconomic status or location. This can include providing internet access, laptops or tablets, and digital learning tools.
5. Alternative pathways to credentialing: Offer alternative pathways to credentialing for students who may not be able to complete traditional degree programs due to financial or personal circumstances. This can include microcredentials, online courses, and competency-based assessments.
6. Focusing on social and emotional learning: Prioritize social and emotional learning (SEL) in schools, as it has been shown to have a positive impact on students' academic achievement, mental health, and overall well-being.
7. Community partnerships and mentorship: Develop partnerships with local community organizations, businesses, and individuals to provide mentorship and real-world learning experiences for students. This can help bridge the gap between education and the workforce.
8. Addressing systemic inequalities: Work to address the systemic inequalities that exist within the education system, such as racial and socioeconomic disparities in funding, resources, and opportunities.
