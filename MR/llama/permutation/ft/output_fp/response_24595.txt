2. Use simple language and clear instructions: Avoid using complex vocabulary or convoluted instructions that might be difficult for beginners to follow. Instead, use straightforward language and concise directions that are easy to understand.
3. Offer language assistance: Provide trained interpreters or bilingual staff members who can assist individuals with limited Spanish proficiency during public events, meetings, or in public spaces. This can help ensure that everyone has equal access to information and services.
