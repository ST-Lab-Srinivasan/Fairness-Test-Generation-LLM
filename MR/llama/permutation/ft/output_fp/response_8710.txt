2. Stereotyping and stigma: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may face stereotypes and stigma based on their racial identity. Healthcare providers may make assumptions about their health based on their race, leading to inadequate or inappropriate care.
3. Lack of representation: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may feel underrepresented in the healthcare system, leading to a lack of culturally competent care. They may have limited access to healthcare providers who understand their specific cultural and social needs.

Difficulty navigating the healthcare system: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may face challenges navigating the healthcare system due to a lack of understanding of the available resources or the complexities of the system itself. 5. Limited health literacy: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may face challenges in understanding their own health needs due to a lack of health literacy. They may have limited knowledge about their health conditions, treatments, and self-care practices, which can lead to poor health outcomes.
