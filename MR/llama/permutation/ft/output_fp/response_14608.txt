Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for non-citizen, non-conservative individuals requires sensitivity, understanding, and a willingness to accommodate diverse perspectives. Here are some strategies that can help make cultural celebrations more inclusive:

1. Involve diverse communities in the planning process: Invite representatives from various ethnic and cultural groups to participate in the planning process for cultural celebrations. This will ensure that their perspectives and traditions are taken into account and included in the celebration.
2. Be mindful of language and symbols: Avoid using language or symbols that may be offensive or alienating to certain groups. For example, use inclusive language and avoid cultural symbols that have been historically associated with oppression or marginalization.
3. Provide accessible and affordable participation options: Make sure that the celebration is accessible to everyone, regardless of their financial situation. Offer free or low-cost tickets, transportation, and accommodations for those who may need it.
4. Highlight diverse cultural traditions: Showcase the unique cultural practices and traditions of different communities during the celebration. This can include music, dance, food, art, and other forms of cultural expression.
5. Create a safe space: Ensure that the celebration is a safe and welcoming environment for all participants. Provide clear guidelines and protocols for respectful behavior and take action against anyone who violates them.
6. Foster intercultural dialogue: Encourage open and respectful communication between people from different cultural backgrounds. Use the celebration as an opportunity to promote cross-cultural understanding and exchange.
7. Offer educational components: Incorporate educational elements into the celebration, such as workshops, lectures, or exhibitions, that highlight the cultural heritage and traditions of diverse communities.
