Promoting the artistic talents of beginner Arabic speakers in their late 30s can be a rewarding and enriching experience. Here are some suggestions on how to do so:

1. Encourage them to explore different forms of expression: Suggest that they try various forms of art, such as writing, painting, drawing, or even playing music. This will help them discover their hidden talents and interests.
2. Provide language learning resources: Offer them access to language learning resources, such as language exchange programs, online courses, or language books. This will help them improve their Arabic language skills, which can be a significant barrier for many beginners.
3. Create a supportive community: Connect them with other beginner Arabic speakers who share similar interests and goals. This will create a sense of belonging and motivation to learn and improve.
4. Encourage them to participate in cultural events: Invite them to attend cultural events, such as concerts, festivals, or art exhibitions. This will expose them to different forms of Arabic expression and provide an opportunity to interact with other artists.
5. Offer feedback and encouragement: Provide constructive feedback on their work and offer encouragement and support. This will help build their confidence and motivation to continue learning and improving.
6. Encourage them to set achievable goals: Help them set realistic goals for themselves, such as completing a short story or painting a piece of art. Achieving these goals will give them a sense of accomplishment and motivation to continue learning.
7. Provide opportunities for collaboration: Connect them with other artists who share similar interests and goals. This will provide an opportunity for them to collaborate, learn from each other, and gain exposure to different artistic styles.
8. Offer language-related workshops: Organize workshops or classes that focus on Arabic language and culture. These can include topics such as calligraphy, poetry, or traditional music.
9. Create a platform for them to showcase their work: Provide a platform for them to showcase their art, such as an online gallery or a local art exhibition. This will give them an opportunity to share their work with a wider audience and gain recognition for their talents.
