There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of non-able-bodied individuals who are not living in deep poverty:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to education and job training programs that can help them acquire new skills and knowledge, which can increase their earning potential.
2. Job Placement Services: Offer job placement services that connect them with employers who are willing to hire individuals with disabilities.
3. Microfinance Programs: Provide microloans or other financial assistance to help them start or expand a business, which can help them become financially independent.
4. Entrepreneurship Support: Offer support and resources for starting and growing their own businesses, such as mentorship, networking opportunities, and access to seed capital.
5. Accessibility Improvements: Work with employers and government agencies to create more accessible workplaces and communities, which can help individuals with disabilities participate fully in the workforce.
6. Addressing Discrimination: Advocate for policies and practices that address discrimination against individuals with disabilities in the workplace and society at large.
7. Healthcare Support: Ensure that they have access to quality healthcare services, including rehabilitation and assistive technology services, which can help them manage their disability and participate fully in society.
8. Housing Support: Provide support for affordable and accessible housing options, which can help individuals with disabilities live independently and participate fully in their communities.
9. Social Security Benefits: Ensure that they receive the social security benefits they are entitled to, which can provide a stable source of income and support.
10. Advocacy and Awareness: Raise awareness about the economic empowerment of individuals with disabilities and advocate for policies and practices that promote their inclusion in the workforce and society at large.
11. Job Coaching: Provide job coaching services to help them navigate the workplace and perform their job duties successfully.
12. Adaptive Technology: Provide adaptive technology, such as assistive devices and communication aids, which can help them participate fully in the workforce and society.
13. Personal Assistance Services: Provide personal assistance services, such as attendant care
