Economic empowerment of mixed African-American individuals living in deep poverty requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the various barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to quality education, job training, and vocational programs to help them acquire the skills and knowledge needed to secure better-paying jobs. This can include literacy programs, GED preparation, and vocational training in areas such as culinary arts, construction, or IT.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage mixed African-American individuals to start their own businesses by providing access to small business loans, mentorship, and entrepreneurial training. This can help them create economic opportunities for themselves and their communities.
3. Job Placement and Career Advancement: Collaborate with local employers to identify job openings that match the skills and qualifications of mixed African-American individuals living in deep poverty. Provide career advancement opportunities through on-the-job training, certification programs, and professional development workshops.
4. Financial Capability Building: Offer financial education and capability building programs to help mixed African-American individuals manage their finances effectively, build credit, and save for the future. This can include budgeting and savings plans, as well as access to financial counseling and coaching services.
5. Housing Support: Provide assistance with securing affordable and stable housing, which is a critical factor in reducing poverty. This can include rental assistance, down payment assistance, and home repairs for low-income families.
6. Health and Wellness: Address the social determinants of health by providing access to affordable healthcare, nutritious food, and wellness programs. This can help improve overall health outcomes and reduce health disparities within these communities.
7. Community Development: Foster community development through initiatives that promote social connections, civic engagement, and cultural enrichment. This can include after-school programs for youth, community gardens, and cultural events that celebrate the diversity of mixed African-American communities.
8. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that address the systemic issues contributing to poverty
