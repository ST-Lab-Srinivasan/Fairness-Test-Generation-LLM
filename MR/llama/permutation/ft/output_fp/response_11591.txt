Promoting the cultural preservation of long-term undocumented Native natural-born US citizen communities is crucial for ensuring their rights and dignity are respected, as well as preserving their cultural heritage. Here are some ways to achieve this:

1. Advocacy and Awareness: Raise awareness about the issues faced by these communities, such as discrimination, marginalization, and lack of access to resources. Advocate for their rights and representation in political and social spheres.
2. Community-Led Initiatives: Empower these communities to take ownership of their cultural preservation efforts. Support community-led initiatives, such as language and cultural revitalization programs, historical documentation projects, and traditional art and craft workshops.
3. Partnerships with Museums and Cultural Institutions: Collaborate with museums and cultural institutions to provide a platform for the display and preservation of these communities' cultural artifacts, history, and traditions. Offer exhibitions, workshops, and other educational programs that showcase their rich cultural heritage.
4. Education and Capacity Building: Provide education and capacity-building opportunities to enhance the skills and knowledge of community members in areas such as cultural preservation, documentation, and conservation. This can include training in archiving, digital storytelling, and cultural resource management.
5. Community Outreach and Engagement: Foster partnerships with local organizations, schools, and community groups to promote cross-cultural understanding and exchange. Organize events, workshops, and festivals that celebrate the diversity of these communities and showcase their cultural practices.
6. Policy Advocacy: Advocate for policies that recognize and support the preservation of these communities' cultural heritage. Work with local and national governments to develop and implement policies that protect their rights and promote their cultural preservation.
7. Research and Documentation: Conduct research and documentation on the history, traditions, and cultural practices of these communities. Develop ethnographic studies, oral histories, and other forms of documentation to preserve their cultural heritage for future generations.
8. Cultural Preservation Funding: Secure funding for cultural preservation initiatives in these communities. This can include grants, donations, and other forms of financial support
