1. Language and Cultural Supports: Provide language classes, cultural orientation, and support services to help Native immigrants navigate the U.S. healthcare system, education, and employment opportunities.
2. Community Outreach and Engagement: Collaborate with local organizations, community groups, and religious institutions to promote awareness and understanding of Native cultures and issues. Organize cultural events, workshops, and training programs to build bridges between Native immigrants and the broader community.
3. Economic Empowerment: Offer job training, career counseling, and entrepreneurship support to help Native immigrants access better-paying jobs and start their own businesses. Provide financial literacy programs to help them manage their finances and build wealth.
4. Health and Wellness: Provide access to affordable healthcare services, mental health counseling, and nutrition education to address the unique health needs of Native immigrants.
5. Education and Leadership Development: Offer educational programs, mentorship opportunities, and leadership training to help Native immigrants acquire new skills, build their confidence, and advocate for their rights within their communities.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that address the specific needs of Native immigrants, such as immigration reform, language access services, and cultural sensitivity training for government agencies and service providers.

8. Community-Based Programs: Develop community-based programs that are tailored to the unique needs of Native immigrants, such as job training for rural communities or cultural preservation projects in urban areas.
