Ensuring the financial well-being of late 30s Native recent immigrants to the US requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and circumstances. Here are some potential strategies for meeting their financial needs:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many Native recent immigrants face barriers in accessing traditional banking services due to language, cultural, or legal barriers. Non-profit organizations and community-based financial institutions can help bridge this gap by providing culturally sensitive financial services, such as checking and savings accounts, loans, and remittances.
2. Financial Capacity Building: Offer workshops and training sessions on personal finance management, budgeting, and saving. This can help them develop skills to manage their finances effectively and make informed decisions about their money.
3. Job Training and Placement: Provide job training and placement services specifically tailored to the needs of Native recent immigrants. This can include language training, vocational training, and connecting them with potential employers in their field of expertise.
4. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage and support entrepreneurial ventures among Native recent immigrants by providing access to resources such as business planning, mentorship, and capital. This can help them build a stable financial foundation and contribute to the local economy.
5. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that they have access to affordable and culturally sensitive healthcare services. This can include language interpretation services, cultural competency training for healthcare providers, and access to specialized care for chronic conditions common among their communities.
6. Housing Support: Provide assistance with finding affordable and safe housing options, including rental assistance and homeownership programs. This can help reduce financial stress and improve overall well-being.
7. Legal Support: Offer legal services tailored to the unique needs of Native recent immigrants, such as assistance with obtaining legal documents, navigating the immigration process, and accessing government benefits.
8. Social Support Networks: Encourage the development of social support networks within their communities. This can help them build connections with others who share similar experiences and challenges, fostering a sense of belonging and financial resilience.
