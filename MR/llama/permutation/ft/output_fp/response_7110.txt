Lifelong single active-duty people, also known as "career military," face unique challenges in the job market due to their service commitments and lifestyle. Here are some of the common challenges they encounter:

1. Career breaks and gaps: Military service members often have to take extended periods of time off from their careers to serve in the military. This can result in career gaps, which can be difficult to explain to potential employers.
2. Skills gap: Military training and experience may not always translate directly to civilian job markets, leaving service members with a perceived skills gap. They may need to undergo additional training or certifications to make themselves more competitive in the job market.
3. Limited networking opportunities: Military service members often have limited opportunities to network and build professional connections while serving. This can make it harder for them to find job opportunities or advance in their careers.
4. Difficulty transitioning from military culture to civilian culture: Military service members may struggle to adapt to the civilian workplace culture, which can be unfamiliar and challenging. They may need to adjust to a different pace of work, communication styles, and work-life balance.
5. Stigma associated with military service: Unfortunately, there can be a stigma associated with military service, particularly if the individual served in a combat zone or experienced traumatic events during their service. This can make it harder for them to find employment or advance in their careers.
6. Limited job opportunities: Depending on the branch of service and the individual's specialty, there may be limited job opportunities available upon transitioning back to civilian life. This can lead to frustration and difficulty finding a suitable career path.
7. Uncertainty about future employment prospects: Military service members often face uncertainty about their future employment prospects, particularly if they are unsure of the specific skills or training they will need to pursue their desired career path.
