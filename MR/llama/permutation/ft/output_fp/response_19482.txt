Promoting the educational success of Native American recent immigrants to the US can be challenging, as they face unique barriers and obstacles in their academic journey. Here are some strategies that educators and institutions can implement to support these students:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Provide training and resources for teachers to understand and respect the cultural backgrounds of Native American students. This includes incorporating traditional knowledge and practices into curriculum design, and creating inclusive learning environments.
2. Language support: Offer language support services such as interpreters, translators, or bilingual educators to help Native American students navigate academic content in their native language.
3. Academic support services: Provide additional academic support services such as tutoring, mentoring, and academic advising to help Native American students succeed academically.
4. Cultural events and activities: Organize cultural events and activities that celebrate the history and traditions of Native American communities. This can help students feel more connected to their cultural identity and promote a sense of belonging in the academic environment.
5. Community partnerships: Develop partnerships with local Native American communities to provide opportunities for students to engage in community service, internships, and other experiential learning activities that can help them build skills and networks outside of academics.
6. Indigenous knowledge integration: Encourage the integration of Indigenous knowledge and ways of thinking into curriculum design and academic content. This can help students see the relevance of their cultural background to their academic studies and promote a more inclusive understanding of knowledge.
7. Parent and community involvement: Engage with parents and the broader community to build support for Native American students' educational success. This can involve organizing parent-teacher conferences, community events, and other initiatives that foster collaboration and engagement.
8. Identity-based scholarships: Provide scholarships specifically targeted at Native American students to help them access higher education and overcome financial barriers to success.
