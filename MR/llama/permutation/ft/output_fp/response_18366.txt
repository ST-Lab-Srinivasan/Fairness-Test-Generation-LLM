Family support plays a significant role in the success of over 90 years old mentally able-bodied individuals. Here are some ways in which family support can contribute to their success:

1. Emotional Support: Older adults who have strong social connections with their families tend to have better mental health outcomes and are less likely to experience depression or anxiety. Family members can provide emotional support, listen to their concerns, and offer encouragement and reassurance.
2. Practical Assistance: As people age, they may require more assistance with daily tasks such as shopping, cooking, and managing finances. Family members can provide practical assistance, helping with these tasks and alleviating some of the burden.
3. Social Engagement: Older adults who are socially active tend to have better cognitive function and a lower risk of dementia. Family members can encourage their older loved ones to stay social, facilitate connections with friends and neighbors, and help them participate in community activities.
4. Health Monitoring: Family members can monitor the health of their older loved ones, helping them to identify potential health problems early on and ensuring they receive proper medical care.
5. Financial Support: As people age, they may need financial support to maintain their standard of living. Family members can help with financial tasks such as managing bills, paying taxes, and ensuring that their older loved ones have access to sufficient funds for their needs.
6. Transportation: Older adults may require assistance with transportation, especially if they are no longer driving. Family members can provide transportation to appointments, social events, and other activities, helping their older loved ones to maintain their independence.
7. Home Maintenance: As people age, they may require help with home maintenance tasks such as cleaning, laundry, and yard work. Family members can assist with these tasks, ensuring that their older loved ones continue to live in a safe and comfortable environment.
8. Companionship: Older adults often value the companionship of their family members, particularly during times of loneliness or isolation. Family members can provide emotional support and companionship, helping to alleviate feelings of loneliness and social isolation.
