Temporary visa holders, including recent widows, face several challenges when accessing healthcare services in Australia. Here are some of the common issues they encounter:

1. Lack of access to Medicare: Temporary visa holders are not eligible for Medicare, which means they have to pay out-of-pocket for medical treatment and prescriptions. This can be a significant financial burden, especially if they don't have private health insurance.
2. Limited access to public hospitals: Temporary visa holders may not have access to public hospitals, as they are required to use private hospitals for non-emergency treatment. This can result in higher costs and longer wait times for medical care.
3. Difficulty navigating the healthcare system: The Australian healthcare system can be complex and difficult to navigate, especially for those who are not familiar with it. Temporary visa holders may struggle to find the right healthcare providers, understand their entitlements, or access specialist care.
4. Language barriers: For temporary visa holders who do not speak English fluently, communication with healthcare providers can be challenging. This can lead to misunderstandings, misdiagnosis, or delayed treatment.
5. Lack of continuity of care: Temporary visa holders may have difficulty maintaining a continuous chain of care due to their temporary status. This can result in disrupted treatment plans, missed appointments, and poor health outcomes.
6. Limited access to mental health services: Temporary visa holders may face barriers when seeking mental health services, such as lack of culturally appropriate services or limited access to psychiatrists or psychologists.
7. Difficulty accessing specialist care: Temporary visa holders may have difficulty accessing specialist care, particularly if they are in a remote area or do not have a referral from their general practitioner.
8. Limited access to preventive healthcare: Temporary visa holders may have limited access to preventive healthcare services such as vaccinations, health checks, and cancer screenings.
