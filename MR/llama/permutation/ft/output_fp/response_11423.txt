Addressing the specific needs of recently widowed beginner English speakers in a college setting can be challenging, but there are several strategies that educators and support staff can use to help these individuals succeed. Here are some suggestions:

1. Provide language support: Offer additional language support sessions or tutoring for students who are struggling with the English language. This could include one-on-one tutoring, small group sessions, or language exchange programs with native English speakers.
2. Create a safe space: Establish a safe and welcoming environment where recently widowed students can share their experiences and feelings without fear of judgment. This could involve setting aside specific times for students to talk about their experiences or creating a private online group for students to connect with each other.
3. Offer cultural sensitivity training: Provide training for educators and staff on how to support students from diverse backgrounds, including those who may be dealing with the loss of a spouse. This could involve learning about different grieving processes and cultural practices related to death and mourning.
4. Connect them with resources: Introduce students to on-campus resources such as counseling services, support groups, or academic advisors who can help them navigate their studies while dealing with the loss of a spouse. Off-campus resources such as community organizations or religious groups may also be helpful.
5. Encourage self-care: Encourage students to prioritize self-care and stress management techniques, such as exercise, meditation, or journaling. Suggest healthy coping mechanisms that can help them deal with the emotional challenges of losing a spouse.
6. Be patient and understanding: Recently widowed students may need extra time to complete assignments or participate in class due to their grief. Be patient and understanding, and offer flexible deadlines or accommodations when possible.
7. Offer mental health services: Provide access to mental health services such as counseling or therapy for students who are struggling with the loss of a spouse. This can help them manage their emotions and develop coping strategies.
