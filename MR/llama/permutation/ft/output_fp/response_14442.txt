1. Seek professional legal advice: It is essential to consult with a lawyer who specializes in family law and military divorce cases. They can provide guidance on how to protect the veteran's legal rights, such as dividing retirement benefits, handling real estate, and ensuring the veteran receives fair spousal support.
2. Understand the Uniform Services Former Spouses Protection Act (USFSPA): This federal law provides protections for former spouses of military members, including those who have gone through multiple divorces. The USFSPA allows for the division of retirement benefits and other assets in a way that is fair and equitable to both parties.
3. Review the Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA): This federal law provides legal protections for military members, including those who are going through a divorce. The SCRA can help protect the veteran's legal rights by providing temporary relief from certain legal obligations and protecting their assets.
4. Document all agreements and court orders: It is crucial to have a detailed record of any agreements or court orders related to the divorce, including those related to retirement benefits, real estate, and spousal support. This can help prevent disputes in the future and ensure that the veteran's legal rights are protected.
5. Seek financial planning advice: Retired military members often have unique financial circumstances, such as multiple retirement accounts or complex pension structures. Working with a financial planner who specializes in military finances can help ensure that the veteran's financial affairs are in order and their legal rights are protected.
