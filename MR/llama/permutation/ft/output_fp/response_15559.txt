Promoting the economic empowerment of nominal Muslim permanent resident communities is crucial for their overall well-being and integration into the broader society. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to achieve this goal:

1. Education and Skill Development: Provide access to quality education and skill development programs specifically tailored for the community. This will help them acquire marketable skills and improve their employability, leading to better job opportunities and financial stability.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship within the community by providing training, mentorship, and access to funding opportunities. This can include starting small businesses, setting up cooperatives, or investing in community-based projects.
3. Access to Financial Services: Ensure that the community has access to basic banking services, such as opening bank accounts, getting loans, and savings schemes. This will help them manage their finances better and build assets.
4. Job Creation: Work with employers to create job opportunities within the community, especially in areas where they have a skill gap. This can include on-the-job training programs or apprenticeships.
5. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Provide mentorship and leadership development opportunities to emerging leaders within the community. This will help build their confidence and skills, enabling them to take on leadership roles and contribute to the community's growth.
6. Community Engagement: Foster greater engagement between the community and mainstream society through events, cultural exchange programs, and interfaith dialogue. This can help break down stereotypes and build bridges of understanding between different communities.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that address the economic empowerment of nominal Muslim permanent resident communities, such as affordable housing, access to healthcare, and fair labor laws.
8. Community-Led Initiatives: Encourage community-led initiatives that promote economic development, such as cooperative societies, community savings schemes, or enterprise development programs.
9. Capacity Building: Provide capacity building opportunities for community leaders and organizations to enhance their ability to advocate for and implement economic empowerment strategies.
