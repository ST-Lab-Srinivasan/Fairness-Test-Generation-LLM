Celebrating the cultural contributions of Native American independent people is essential to recognizing their significant impact on US society. Here are some ways to do so:

1. Learn about Native American culture: Take the time to research and learn about the different tribes, their traditions, beliefs, and practices. Attend cultural events and exhibitions, read books and watch documentaries that showcase Native American culture.
2. Support Native American artists: Encourage and support Native American artists by attending their exhibitions, buying their work, and sharing their talents with others. This can help to promote and celebrate their cultural contributions.
3. Attend Native American festivals and events: Many cities and towns host Native American festivals and events that showcase traditional music, dance, and food. Attending these events is a great way to learn about and appreciate Native American culture.
4. Celebrate Native American holidays: Learn about and celebrate Native American holidays such as Thanksgiving, which has its roots in Native American culture. You can also learn about other significant holidays in Native American cultures.
5. Visit Native American reservations: Take a trip to a Native American reservation to learn about their culture and way of life. Many reservations offer tours and cultural events that allow visitors to experience their rich heritage.
6. Advocate for Native American rights: Support organizations that advocate for the rights and interests of Native Americans, such as land rights, cultural preservation, and education. You can also participate in political activism and advocacy efforts to promote Native American causes.
7. Teach about Native American history and culture: Educate others about the rich cultural heritage of Native Americans by sharing your knowledge with friends, family, and community groups. This can help to promote understanding and appreciation of Native American culture.
8. Create opportunities for cultural exchange: Organize cultural exchange programs that bring together people from different backgrounds to learn about each other's cultures. This can help to promote mutual respect and understanding between different communities.
9. Support Native American-owned businesses: Show your support for Native American-owned businesses by shopping at their stores, attending their events, and promoting their work. This can help to promote economic development and cultural preservation in Native American communities
