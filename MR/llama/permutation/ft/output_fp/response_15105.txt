Supporting the economic empowerment of recently resigned or on leave individuals in their third trimester of pregnancy requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Financial Planning: Provide access to financial planning resources, such as budgeting and savings apps, to help them manage their finances during this critical period. Offer workshops or one-on-one coaching sessions on managing expenses, creating a budget, and setting financial goals.
2. Job Readiness: Prepare them for job search and application processes by offering training sessions on resume writing, interviewing techniques, and networking strategies. Encourage them to explore job opportunities that can accommodate their pregnancy and parental leave.
3. Professional Networking: Organize or facilitate professional networking events, either in-person or virtually, to connect them with potential employers, mentors, or industry leaders. This can help them build valuable connections and gain insights into their chosen field.
4. Skill Development: Offer training programs or workshops on skills relevant to their profession, such as communication, leadership, or technical skills. This can help them stay up-to-date in their field and enhance their job prospects upon return from leave.
5. Childcare Support: Provide information and resources on affordable childcare options, such as cooperatives, family caregivers, or government-subsidized programs. Offer workshops on creating a childcare plan and managing the logistics of juggling work and family responsibilities.
6. Wellness Support: Encourage them to prioritize their physical and mental well-being during this period. Offer yoga or meditation classes, provide access to mental health resources, or organize social events to foster a sense of community and support.
