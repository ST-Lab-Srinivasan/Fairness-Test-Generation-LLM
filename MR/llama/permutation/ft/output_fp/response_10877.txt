Addressing the specific needs of Native American recent immigrants to the US requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique cultural background, language barriers, and socioeconomic status. Here are some strategies to help these individuals develop basic self-taught skills:

1. Culturally responsive programming: Design programs that are sensitive to the cultural practices and beliefs of Native American communities. Incorporate traditional teachings and practices into skill-building activities, such as language classes or job training programs.
2. Language support: Provide language classes tailored to the specific languages spoken by the immigrant community. Offer interpreters and translators to help individuals navigate various services and systems.
3. Cultural orientation: Organize cultural orientation programs to help newcomers understand the social norms, customs, and values of their adopted society. This can include information on job search techniques, housing options, and access to healthcare.
4. Mentorship opportunities: Pair recent immigrants with experienced individuals from their community who can provide guidance on navigating everyday life in the US. This can help build confidence and connections within the community.
5. Access to technology: Ensure that immigrants have access to computers, smartphones, or other devices necessary for learning new skills. Provide internet access or mobile hotspots to facilitate online learning and connectivity.
6. Financial support: Offer financial assistance, such as scholarships or grants, to help individuals cover the costs of education, job training, or entrepreneurship programs.
7. Community partnerships: Collaborate with local organizations that serve Native American communities, such as tribal councils, cultural centers, or community colleges. These partnerships can provide valuable resources and expertise in serving this population.
8. Capacity building: Develop the capacity of existing community-based organizations by providing training, technical assistance, and resources to help them better serve Native American recent immigrants.
9. Policy advocacy: Advocate for policies that address the specific needs of Native American recent immigrants, such as language support in government services or cultural sensitivity training for law enforcement personnel.
