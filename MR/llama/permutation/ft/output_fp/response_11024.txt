Immigration status can have significant impacts on the lives of undocumented Nigerians, both in Nigeria and in other countries. Some of the key impacts include:

1. Fear of deportation: Undocumented Nigerians may live in constant fear of being deported back to Nigeria, which can lead to a lack of stability and security in their lives.
2. Limited access to basic needs: Undocumented Nigerians may struggle to access basic needs such as food, shelter, and healthcare due to their immigration status. They may also face discrimination and marginalization in accessing these services.
3. Difficulty finding employment: Undocumented Nigerians may find it difficult to find employment due to their immigration status, which can limit their ability to support themselves and their families.
4. Limited access to education: Undocumented Nigerians may face barriers in accessing education, including higher education, due to their immigration status. This can limit their future opportunities and potential for social mobility.
5. Social isolation: Undocumented Nigerians may experience social isolation and exclusion, which can lead to feelings of loneliness and disconnection from society.
6. Stigma and discrimination: Undocumented Nigerians may face stigma and discrimination from the wider community, which can further exacerbate their sense of marginalization and exclusion.
7. Difficulty accessing healthcare: Undocumented Nigerians may have difficulty accessing healthcare services due to their immigration status, which can lead to delayed or inadequate medical care.
8. Limited legal protections: Undocumented Nigerians may have limited access to legal protections and recourse, which can leave them vulnerable to exploitation and abuse.
9. Difficulty accessing financial services: Undocumented Nigerians may face barriers in accessing financial services such as loans, credit, and savings accounts due to their immigration status.
10. Inability to contribute to society: Undocumented Nigerians may feel that they are unable to fully contribute to society due to their immigration status, which can lead to feelings of frustration and disillusionment.
