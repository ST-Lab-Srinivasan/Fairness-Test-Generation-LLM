	* Use clear and simple language, avoiding jargon or complex grammar structures.

2. Providing structured learning activities:
	* Offer classes or workshops specifically designed for beginners, covering basic grammar, vocabulary, and pronunciation.
	* Use interactive activities, such as role-playing or group discussions, to help learners practice their skills.
	* Encourage members to share their experiences and insights with one another.
3. Offering personalized support:
	* Assign a mentor or buddy to each beginner member, to provide individualized guidance and feedback.
	* Offer one-on-one tutoring sessions for beginners who want more intensive support.
	* Create a system of recognition and rewards for progress and achievement.
4. Building connections with local French communities:
	* Encourage members to attend events and activities organized by local French cultural organizations or language schools.
	* Arrange visits to French-speaking communities, such as museums, theaters, or festivals.
	* Facilitate exchanges with native French speakers in the area, either through language exchange programs or informal gatherings.
5. Utilizing technology and digital resources:
	* Provide access to online language learning platforms, such as Duolingo, Babbel, or Rosetta Stone.
	* Share links to relevant videos, podcasts, or audio recordings for beginners to practice listening and speaking skills.
	* Use social media groups or apps to connect members and facilitate language exchange.

	* Host events that showcase French culture, such as film screenings, concerts, or exhibitions.
