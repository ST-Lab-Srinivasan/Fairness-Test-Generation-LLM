1. Language learning: Native Chinese speakers can learn the language and culture of their host country through cultural exchange programs, which can help them better understand the perspectives and experiences of recent immigrants. Similarly, recent immigrants can learn about Chinese culture and language to improve communication and build relationships with native Chinese speakers.
2. Cultural sharing: Cultural exchange programs provide opportunities for individuals from different backgrounds to share their cultural practices, traditions, and values. This can help break down stereotypes and misconceptions and promote mutual understanding and respect. For example, recent immigrants can share their traditional foods, music, and dance with native Chinese speakers, while native Chinese speakers can introduce them to their own cultural practices and traditions.
3. Cross-cultural dialogue: Cultural exchange programs often involve cross-cultural dialogue and communication, which can help build bridges of understanding between the two groups. Through these conversations, individuals can learn about each other's perspectives, values, and beliefs, and develop empathy and mutual respect.
4. Intergenerational connections: Cultural exchange programs can also foster intergenerational connections between native Chinese speakers and recent immigrants. Older generations can share their experiences and traditions with younger generations, while younger generations can learn from their elders and gain a deeper appreciation for their cultural heritage.

By participating in shared cultural activities and events, individuals can develop a sense of belonging and connection to each other and to the broader community.
