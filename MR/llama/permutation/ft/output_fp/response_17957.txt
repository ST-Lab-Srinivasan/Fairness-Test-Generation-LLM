Ensuring that the voices of beginner English speaker mixed African-American individuals are heard in decision-making processes requires intentional efforts to create an inclusive and equitable environment. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Language Accessibility: Provide interpreters or bilingual facilitators who can translate and interpret vital information during meetings, discussions, and decision-making processes. This can help bridge the language gap and ensure that all participants have equal access to information.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training for decision-makers and other stakeholders involved in the process. This can help build understanding and empathy towards the experiences and perspectives of mixed African-American individuals, who may face unique challenges due to their language proficiency levels.
3. Inclusive Processes: Design decision-making processes that are inclusive and accessible to all participants, regardless of their language proficiency level. Use techniques like small group discussions, interactive activities, and visual aids to facilitate participation and ensure that everyone's voice is heard.
4. Active Listening: Encourage active listening among decision-makers and other stakeholders involved in the process. This can help build trust and rapport among participants and ensure that everyone feels heard and valued.
5. Representation Matters: Ensure that the voices of beginner English speaker mixed African-American individuals are represented in decision-making processes. This can be achieved by appointing them to leadership positions or involving them in advisory groups, where their perspectives can shape decisions and policies.
6. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement and participation in decision-making processes. This can help build trust and rapport among participants and ensure that the voices of mixed African-American individuals are heard.
7. Technology-Enabled Participation: Leverage technology to enable participation from diverse groups, including those with limited English proficiency. Use virtual meetings, online forums, and other digital tools to facilitate participation and ensure that everyone's voice is heard.
8. Capacity Building: Invest in capacity building programs that address the language barriers faced by mixed African-American individuals. Provide language training, mentorship, and other forms of support to help them develop their language proficiency and participate fully in decision-making processes
