2. Connect them with mentors: Pair them with successful professionals who can offer guidance and support. Mentors can provide valuable insights into their field of interest and help them navigate the job market.
3. Offer childcare support: Provide access to affordable childcare options, such as on-site childcare or subsidies for off-site care. This will enable individuals to focus on their career aspirations without worrying about their children's well-being.
4. Provide financial assistance: Offer grants or scholarships that can help individuals cover the costs of education, job training, and other expenses related to pursuing their career goals.
5. Create a supportive community: Build a network of like-minded individuals who are also striving for career advancement. This will provide a sense of belonging and motivation, which can be particularly important during the third trimester of pregnancy when individuals may feel isolated or overwhelmed.
6. Advocate for policies that support working mothers: Encourage policymakers to implement policies that promote work-life balance, such as paid family leave, flexible work arrangements, and protections against discrimination based on pregnancy or parental status.

This can be particularly important for individuals who may not have access to traditional job search channels due to their socioeconomic situation.
