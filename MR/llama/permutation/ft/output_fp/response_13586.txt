Supporting older adults, particularly those over the age of 90, can be challenging but rewarding. Here are some effective strategies for supporting advanced over 90-year-old native English speaker communities:

1. Encourage Social Interaction: Older adults often experience social isolation, which can lead to cognitive decline and depression. Facilitate social interaction through activities such as group games, crafts, or volunteering in a local community organization.
2. Promote Brain Stimulation: Engage older adults in mentally stimulating activities, such as crossword puzzles, Sudoku, or learning a new language, to help maintain cognitive function and delay cognitive decline.
3. Foster Sense of Purpose: Help older adults find meaning and purpose in their lives through volunteering, caregiving, or pursuing hobbies that bring them joy. This can help combat feelings of loneliness and isolation.
4. Support Physical Health: Encourage regular exercise, such as walking, swimming, or yoga, to maintain physical health and mobility. Also, ensure they have access to regular medical check-ups and screenings to detect any health issues early on.
5. Provide Emotional Support: Listen to their concerns, offer emotional support, and validate their feelings. Encourage them to express their emotions through creative outlets like writing, drawing, or painting.
6. Create a Safe Environment: Ensure their living space is safe and conducive to aging, with features such as grab bars, non-slip flooring, and adequate lighting. Also, provide accessibility options like raised toilet seats or shower chairs to maintain independence.
7. Connect Them with Technology: Teach older adults how to use technology, such as smartphones, tablets, or computers, to stay connected with loved ones and access information and entertainment.
8. Encourage Creative Expression: Engage them in creative activities like gardening, cooking, or playing musical instruments to maintain cognitive function and overall well-being.
