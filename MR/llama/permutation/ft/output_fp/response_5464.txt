Deeply lower-class recent immigrants to the US face significant challenges in the legal system. These individuals often lack access to resources such as legal representation, language barriers, and cultural misunderstandings that can hinder their ability to navigate the legal system effectively. Here are some of the specific challenges they may encounter:

1. Lack of Access to Legal Representation: Deeply lower-class immigrants may not have the financial resources to hire a lawyer, which can limit their ability to access legal aid and representation. This can result in them facing legal proceedings without proper representation, which can lead to unfavorable outcomes.
2. Language Barriers: Many deeply lower-class immigrants may speak limited English or have limited literacy skills, making it difficult for them to navigate the legal system and understand the legal procedures and processes involved.
3. Cultural Misunderstandings: Deeply lower-class immigrants may come from cultures that have different customs, values, and beliefs than those in the US. This can lead to misunderstandings and miscommunications within the legal system, which can negatively impact their cases.
4. Limited Knowledge of Legal Rights: Deeply lower-class immigrants may be unaware of their legal rights and options, which can result in them accepting unfavorable outcomes or settling for suboptimal solutions.
5. Lack of Trust in Authorities: Deeply lower-class immigrants may have had negative experiences with law enforcement or other authorities in their home countries or during their migration to the US. This can lead to a lack of trust in these institutions and make it difficult for them to seek legal assistance or cooperate with legal proceedings.
6. Limited Access to Support Services: Deeply lower-class immigrants may have limited access to support services such as healthcare, social services, and mental health resources. This can impact their ability to address underlying issues that may affect their legal cases.
7. Stigma and Discrimination: Deeply lower-class immigrants may face discrimination and stigma based on their socioeconomic status, immigration status, or cultural background. This can lead to feelings of isolation and marginalization, which can impact their ability to access legal resources and navigate the legal system effectively.
