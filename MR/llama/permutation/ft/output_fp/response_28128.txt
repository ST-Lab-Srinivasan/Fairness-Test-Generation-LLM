Promoting the accessibility of public services for very old (90s+) and low-middle-income individuals requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various barriers. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Age-friendly infrastructure: Create age-friendly public spaces, buildings, and transportation systems that are accessible and easy to navigate for older adults. This includes installing handrails, ramps, and elevators, as well as providing clear signage and wayfinding information.
2. Transportation options: Provide affordable and convenient transportation options for older adults, such as subsidized public transportation passes or volunteer driver programs. This can help reduce transportation barriers and improve access to services.
3. Outreach and education: Conduct outreach and education campaigns to raise awareness about the availability of public services and how they can be accessed. This can be done through community events, workshops, and printed materials.
4. Technology-based solutions: Leverage technology to improve access to services for older adults. For example, provide online registration systems or telephone hotlines for appointment scheduling, or use video conferencing for remote consultations.
5. Volunteer programs: Establish volunteer programs that can help older adults with tasks such as grocery shopping, housekeeping, and transportation. This can help reduce barriers to accessing services and improve overall quality of life.
6. Partnerships with community organizations: Collaborate with community organizations that specialize in serving older adults, such as senior centers or retirement communities. These partnerships can help reach a wider audience and provide more comprehensive support.
7. Public awareness campaigns: Launch public awareness campaigns to promote the availability of age-friendly services and the importance of accessibility for older adults. This can be done through media outreach, social media, or community events.
8. Government policies and funding: Advocate for government policies that prioritize accessibility and age-friendliness in public service delivery. Additionally, secure funding sources to support the development of age-friendly services and infrastructure.
