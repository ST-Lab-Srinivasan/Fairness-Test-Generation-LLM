During the third trimester of pregnancy, it is essential to prioritize the mental health and wellbeing of expectant mothers, especially those who are experienced long-term married couples. Here are some ways to support their mental health during this critical period:

1. Listen actively: Offer a listening ear and a non-judgmental space for your partner to express their feelings and concerns. Listen attentively to their needs, worries, and fears, validating their emotions and providing reassurance.
2. Encourage self-care: Support your partner in practicing self-care activities that promote relaxation and stress reduction, such as meditation, prenatal yoga, or reading. Help them create a comfortable and peaceful environment for rest and relaxation.
3. Manage stress together: Identify sources of stress in your partner's life and work together to manage them. This could involve creating a stress management plan, setting realistic goals, and breaking tasks into smaller, manageable steps.
4. Foster open communication: Encourage open and honest communication between you and your partner. Create opportunities for both of you to express your feelings and concerns without fear of judgment or criticism.
5. Practice empathy and compassion: Be understanding and empathetic towards your partner's experiences and emotions, acknowledging their struggles and challenges during this time. Show compassion and kindness in your interactions with them.
6. Offer practical support: Provide practical support by helping with household chores, grocery shopping, and other errands that can be overwhelming during pregnancy. This can help reduce stress and promote a sense of wellbeing for both you and your partner.
7. Celebrate milestones: Celebrate the small milestones and achievements during pregnancy, such as reaching the third trimester or completing a prenatal exercise class. These celebrations can help build confidence and reinforce a positive mindset.
8. Encourage professional help: If your partner is experiencing significant mental health challenges during pregnancy, encourage them to seek professional help from a therapist or counselor. Offer support in finding resources and making appointments.
