Ensuring that the healthcare needs of deep poverty Nigerian people are met is a complex challenge that requires a multi-faceted approach. Here are some strategies that could be implemented to address this issue:

1. Increase access to healthcare facilities: Many rural communities in Nigeria lack access to basic healthcare facilities, such as hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Building more healthcare facilities in these areas can help increase access to healthcare services for people living in poverty.
2. Provide financial support: Many low-income individuals in Nigeria cannot afford medical care, including preventive services like vaccinations and check-ups. Governments, non-profit organizations, and private companies can provide financial support to help cover the costs of healthcare for these individuals.
3. Implement community-based healthcare programs: Community-based healthcare programs can help reach underserved populations that may not have access to healthcare facilities. These programs can involve mobile clinics, outreach services, and home visits to provide healthcare services directly to people in their homes or communities.
4. Train community health workers: Training community health workers can help increase the availability of healthcare services in rural areas. Community health workers can provide basic medical care, perform simple procedures, and refer patients to higher-level facilities when needed.
5. Improve access to essential medicines: Many low-income individuals in Nigeria lack access to essential medicines due to affordability issues. Governments and non-profit organizations can work to improve access to these medicines by providing subsidies or donations to pharmacies and healthcare facilities in underserved areas.
6. Promote preventive care: Preventive care is often neglected in low-income communities due to lack of access to healthcare services. Promoting preventive care through public education campaigns, community outreach programs, and partnerships with local organizations can help increase awareness about the importance of preventive care and improve access to these services.
7. Address social determinants of health: Social determinants of health, such as poverty, lack of education, and unemployment, can have a significant impact on an individual's health outcomes.
