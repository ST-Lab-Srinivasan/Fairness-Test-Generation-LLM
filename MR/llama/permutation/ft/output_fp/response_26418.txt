Supporting the economic empowerment of out-of-work Native recent immigrants to the US requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and provides tailored resources and support. Here are some ways to help:

1. Language and Cultural Support: Provide language classes, cultural orientation, and job training programs specifically designed for Native immigrants. This will help them navigate the US job market and understand the culture and customs of their new community.
2. Access to Job Training and Education: Offer vocational training, GED preparation, and college-level courses in fields such as business, technology, and healthcare. This will help them acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market.
3. Mentorship and Networking: Connect Native immigrants with successful professionals in their field who can serve as mentors and provide guidance on how to navigate the US job market. This will help them build valuable professional connections and gain insights into the industry.
4. Job Placement Services: Provide job placement services specifically tailored to Native recent immigrants. This could include resume building, interview preparation, and job search strategies.
5. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship Support: Offer microfinance opportunities and entrepreneurial training to help Native immigrants start their own businesses or invest in existing ones. This will provide them with financial independence and a sense of control over their economic future.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that support the economic empowerment of Native recent immigrants, such as visa reform, increased access to education and job training programs, and protection against discrimination in the workplace.
7. Community Building: Facilitate community building events and activities that bring together Native recent immigrants and other underrepresented groups. This will help create a sense of belonging and support, which can lead to greater economic empowerment.
8. Access to Healthcare: Provide access to affordable healthcare services, including mental health support, to help Native immigrants prioritize their health and well-being while they work towards economic stability.
