1. Assess their level of proficiency: Before creating a learning plan, assess the student's current level of proficiency in French. This will help you identify areas where they need improvement and tailor your teaching approach accordingly.
2. Provide a supportive learning environment: Offer a safe and supportive learning environment that acknowledges the challenges the student may be facing due to their recent divorce. Encourage open communication and provide resources for coping with stress and emotional challenges.
3. Use relatable content: Use authentic materials such as news articles, videos, or audio recordings that are relevant to the student's interests and experiences. This can help them feel more engaged and motivated in their language learning journey.
4. Foster a sense of community: Encourage collaboration and communication among students who are at similar levels of proficiency. This can help build a sense of belonging and support, which is particularly important for beginner French speakers who may be feeling isolated or struggling to make progress.
5. Offer individualized feedback: Provide regular feedback and guidance tailored to the student's specific needs and goals. This can help them stay motivated and focused on their learning objectives.
6. Be flexible and adaptable: Be willing to adjust your teaching approach based on the student's progress and feedback. Be open to incorporating new materials or techniques that may better meet their needs.

Encourage self-reflection: Encourage the student to reflect on their own language learning journey and identify areas where they feel comfortable and confident, as well as areas where they need improvement.
