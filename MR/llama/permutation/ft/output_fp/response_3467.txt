Supporting the economic empowerment of immigrant natural-born US citizen non-middle-class individuals requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various barriers to their financial stability and prosperity. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs for immigrant youth and adults, including language classes, vocational training, and college scholarships. This will help them acquire the skills needed to compete in the US labor market and advance in their careers.
2. Legal Status: Advocate for policies that provide a clear pathway to legal status for undocumented immigrants, allowing them to access job opportunities and benefits without fear of deportation. This can include providing a roadmap to citizenship or creating temporary worker programs that offer a stable and secure future.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage immigrant entrepreneurs by providing access to resources such as mentorship, funding, and networking opportunities. This can help them start their own businesses, create jobs, and contribute to the US economy.
4. Job Creation: Foster job creation in industries that are aligned with immigrant communities' skills and interests, such as tech, healthcare, and small businesses. This can be achieved through initiatives like business incubators, mentorship programs, and access to capital.
5. Community Development: Invest in community development programs that promote economic growth and stability in immigrant communities. This can include supporting local businesses, creating job opportunities, and addressing social issues such as poverty and inequality.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that support the economic empowerment of immigrant communities, including fair wages, affordable housing, and access to healthcare. Additionally, push for policy changes that address systemic barriers to economic mobility, such as discrimination and unequal access to opportunities.
