Creating more inclusive community events for beginner Arabic speakers who are not high-income professionals requires careful planning and consideration of their needs. Here are some suggestions to make your event more accessible and welcoming:

1. Language support: Provide language support during the event, such as translation services or interpreters, to ensure that everyone can participate and understand the content.
2. Accessible location: Choose a venue that is easily accessible for people with limited mobility or those who may not have transportation. Consider holding the event in a central location with good public transportation options.
3. Affordable ticket prices: Offer affordable ticket prices to make the event more accessible to people on a budget. You could also consider offering scholarships or financial aid to help cover the costs for those who cannot afford it.
4. Childcare and family-friendly activities: Provide childcare services or family-friendly activities to ensure that participants can bring their whole family, including children, without worrying about finding adequate care during the event.
5. Food and refreshments: Serve food and refreshments that cater to a diverse range of dietary needs and preferences, including halal or kosher options, and vegetarian or vegan alternatives.
6. Interactive activities: Incorporate interactive activities and workshops that are engaging and enjoyable for participants of all skill levels. This could include language games, cultural performances, or art activities.
7. Diverse speaker lineup: Ensure that the event features a diverse lineup of speakers who represent different backgrounds, cultures, and perspectives. This will help to create a more inclusive and representative atmosphere.
8. Flexible scheduling: Offer flexible scheduling options to accommodate participants with different availability and schedules. For example, you could hold the event on weekends or in the evening to cater to those who may have work or family commitments during the day.
9. Accessibility features: Ensure that the venue is accessible for people with disabilities by providing features such as wheelchair accessibility, braille signage, and audio descriptions.
