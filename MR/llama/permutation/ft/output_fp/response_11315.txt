Temporary visa holders from Nigeria, like any other foreign national, face various challenges and limitations while residing in a host country. Their immigration status can significantly affect their quality of life, access to opportunities, and overall well-being. Here are some potential impacts of immigration status on the lives of temporary visa holders from Nigeria:

1. Legal uncertainty: Temporary visas often come with strict conditions and limitations, such as work restrictions, time limits, or requirements to leave the country at the end of the visa validity period. This legal uncertainty can create anxiety and stress, making it difficult for individuals to plan their lives or make long-term decisions.
2. Limited access to employment opportunities: Temporary visa holders may face restrictions on their ability to work in certain fields or industries, or they may be eligible only for low-paying or unstable jobs. This can limit their career advancement and earning potential, as well as their ability to save money or support themselves or their families back in Nigeria.
3. Difficulty accessing healthcare: Depending on the type of visa, temporary visa holders may not have access to adequate healthcare services in the host country. This can be particularly challenging if they experience medical emergencies or need ongoing treatment. They may also face barriers when trying to transfer medical records or receive continuing care upon returning to Nigeria.
4. Limited social integration: Temporary visa holders may encounter difficulties forming connections with local communities, especially if their immigration status makes them feel insecure or unwelcome. This can lead to feelings of isolation and loneliness, which can negatively impact their mental health and overall well-being.
