1. Assess their language proficiency level: Before creating a learning plan, assess the student's current level of proficiency in French. This will help you identify areas where they need improvement and tailor your teaching methods accordingly.
2. Use interactive and engaging materials: Beginner learners may find it challenging to stay motivated and engaged in traditional classroom settings. Consider using interactive materials such as language learning apps, videos, podcasts, and online games to make the learning process more enjoyable and accessible.
3. Offer flexible scheduling options: Divorced students may have different schedules or commitments that can interfere with traditional classroom hours. Offering flexible scheduling options, such as online classes or one-on-one tutoring, can help them fit language learning into their busy lives.
4. Provide individualized attention: Beginner learners may feel overwhelmed in a large classroom setting. Consider providing individualized attention through one-on-one tutoring or small group instruction to help them build confidence and improve their language skills.
5. Encourage self-directed learning: Divorced students may need to take responsibility for their own language learning. Encourage them to use language learning apps, watch French movies or TV shows with English subtitles, listen to French podcasts, or read French books with a dictionary to improve their skills.
6. Use authentic materials: Using authentic materials such as news articles, videos, and social media can help beginner learners develop a deeper understanding of the language and culture. This can be especially helpful for divorced students who may be more motivated to learn if they see relevance to their lives.
7. Foster a supportive community: Language learning can be a challenging journey, especially for beginner learners. Create a supportive community where students can encourage and motivate each other, share language learning experiences, and build friendships.
8. Provide opportunities for practical application: Divorced students may want to practice their language skills in real-life situations. Offer opportunities for practical application, such as participating in a language exchange program
