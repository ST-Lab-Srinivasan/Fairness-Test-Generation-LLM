Economic empowerment is a critical aspect of ensuring financial stability and security for any individual or community. Here are some strategies that can support the economic empowerment of not just above poverty line ethnically full African Americans:

1. Education and Training: Providing access to quality education and training programs can help individuals acquire new skills, knowledge, and credentials that can increase their earning potential. This can include vocational training, certificate programs, and degree-granting institutions.
2. Entrepreneurship and Small Business Development: Encouraging entrepreneurship and supporting small business development can create jobs and wealth within the African American community. This can involve providing access to capital, mentorship, and resources for start-ups and existing businesses.
3. Access to Capital: Ensuring access to capital is crucial for economic empowerment. This can involve providing access to traditional banking services, as well as alternative forms of finance such as crowdfunding, angel investors, and venture capital.
4. Job Creation and Economic Growth: Supporting job creation and economic growth within the African American community can help increase employment opportunities and household incomes. This can involve investing in infrastructure, transportation, and other community assets that support business development.
5. Addressing Wealth Inequality: Addressing wealth inequality is critical for ensuring long-term economic stability. This can involve policies such as inheritance taxes, progressive taxation, and social safety nets that help reduce wealth disparities.
6. Supporting Cooperative Enterprises: Cooperative enterprises, such as worker cooperatives, consumer cooperatives, and producer cooperatives, can provide economic opportunities for African Americans while also fostering community control and decision-making.
7. Addressing Discrimination: Discrimination continues to be a major barrier to economic empowerment for many African Americans. Addressing discrimination through policies such as anti-discrimination laws, diversity and inclusion initiatives, and social justice advocacy can help create a more equitable economic environment.
