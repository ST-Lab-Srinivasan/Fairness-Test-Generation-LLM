Promoting the educational success of mixed African-American students can be challenging, as they may face a range of social and cultural barriers that can impact their academic performance. Here are some strategies that educators and parents can use to promote the educational success of mixed African-American moderate students:

1. Create a supportive learning environment: Provide a safe and inclusive learning environment that values diversity and promotes cultural understanding. Encourage open communication and mutual respect among all students, regardless of their racial or ethnic background.
2. Foster a sense of belonging: Help mixed African-American students feel connected to their school community by recognizing and celebrating their unique experiences and perspectives. Provide opportunities for students to share their cultural heritage and traditions with the class, and encourage them to take pride in their identities.
3. Address microaggressions: Educate students, teachers, and parents about the impact of microaggressions on academic performance and mental health. Encourage students to speak up when they experience or witness microaggressions, and provide support for those who are affected.
4. Provide culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers to incorporate culturally responsive teaching practices into their instruction, such as using diverse texts and images, and incorporating students' cultural experiences and backgrounds into lessons.
5. Encourage student voice and agency: Empower mixed African-American students to take an active role in their learning by providing opportunities for them to share their thoughts, opinions, and ideas. Encourage them to ask questions, make connections between their experiences and the curriculum, and advocate for themselves and their peers.
6. Provide mentorship and support: Connect mixed African-American students with caring adults who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement. This can include mentors, coaches, or school staff members who are familiar with the challenges that these students may face.
