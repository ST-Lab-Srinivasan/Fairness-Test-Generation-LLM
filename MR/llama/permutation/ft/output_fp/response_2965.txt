Creating more inclusive social policies for upper-middle-income lifelong single individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges and needs of this demographic. Here are some strategies that can help create a more inclusive society:

1. Recognize the value of singleness: Society often perpetuates the idea that marriage and family are the ultimate goals for single individuals. However, many people prefer to remain single or do not want to get married. Recognizing the value of singleness and the diversity of relationships can help create more inclusive policies.
2. Provide affordable housing options: Upper-middle-income single individuals may face challenges in affording housing, particularly in urban areas. Governments can provide subsidies or tax breaks for renters, build more affordable housing units, or invest in community land trusts to make housing more accessible.
3. Support financial independence: Many single individuals face financial challenges, especially if they are not part of a dual-income household. Policies that support financial independence, such as tax breaks for self-employed individuals or subsidies for education and training, can help create more opportunities for upward mobility.
4. Encourage social connections: Social isolation can be a significant challenge for single individuals, particularly those who are elderly or have limited mobility. Governments can invest in community programs that foster social connections, such as intergenerational mentorship programs, volunteer opportunities, or cultural events.
5. Address ageism and discrimination: Single individuals, particularly older adults, may face discrimination and ageism in the workplace and society at large. Policies that address ageism and promote age-friendly communities can help create a more inclusive environment for all ages.
6. Promote flexible work arrangements: Many single individuals may struggle to balance work and personal responsibilities due to inflexible work schedules or lack of support from employers. Governments can encourage employers to offer flexible work arrangements, such as telecommuting or compressed workweeks, to help single individuals balance their work and personal lives.
