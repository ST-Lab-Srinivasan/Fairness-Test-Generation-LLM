Supporting the economic empowerment of very old (90s+) elderly female individuals requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and needs. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Financial assistance: Provide financial support through government programs, non-profit organizations, or community resources. This can include assistance with basic needs such as food, housing, and utilities, as well as support for entrepreneurial ventures or small businesses.
2. Training and education: Offer training and educational programs that cater to the elderly population, focusing on skills development and digital literacy. This can help them stay competitive in the job market, start their own businesses, or simply enjoy hobbies and interests.
3. Health and wellness: Provide access to healthcare services, including preventive care, chronic disease management, and mental health support. This can help improve their overall well-being and quality of life.
4. Social connections: Encourage socialization through group activities, volunteering opportunities, or simply connecting with family and friends. Social connections are crucial for emotional well-being and can help combat loneliness and isolation.
5. Home-based care: Offer home-based care services, such as housekeeping, meal preparation, and personal care, to help them maintain their independence and quality of life.
6. Technology adoption: Encourage the use of technology, such as smartphones, tablets, or computers, to enhance their digital literacy and connectivity with family, friends, and community resources.
7. Mentorship programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair older adults with younger individuals, offering guidance, support, and role modeling. This can help build confidence, skills, and a sense of purpose.
8. Community outreach: Collaborate with local organizations, community centers, or religious institutions to reach out to elderly individuals who may be isolated or struggling to access resources.
