2. Indigenous knowledge integration: Incorporate Indigenous knowledge and perspectives into the curriculum to help students understand the rich cultural heritage of Native American communities.
3. Community-based learning: Collaborate with Native American communities to design learning experiences that are grounded in their cultural practices and values. This can involve participating in traditional activities, such as sweat lodge ceremonies or powwows, and incorporating these experiences into the classroom.

5. Supportive learning environment: Create a learning environment that is supportive and inclusive of Native American students. This can involve providing resources and support services, such as tutoring or counseling, to help students succeed academically and personally.
6. Language revitalization: Support the revitalization of Native American languages by incorporating language learning opportunities into the curriculum. This can involve teaching Indigenous languages, such as Ojibwe, Dakota, or Navajo, and providing resources for students to learn their heritage languages.
7. Cultural identity development: Help Native American students develop a strong cultural identity by providing opportunities for them to connect with their heritage and culture. This can involve incorporating traditional practices, such as ceremonies or storytelling, into the curriculum.
