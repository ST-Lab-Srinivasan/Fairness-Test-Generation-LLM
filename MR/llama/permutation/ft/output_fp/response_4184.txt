Advocating for the rights of older adults in Nigeria, particularly those who have turned 65 or more, can be challenging due to various factors such as cultural beliefs and societal attitudes towards aging. However, there are several effective ways to advocate for their rights:

1. Educate yourself and others: Learn about the issues faced by older adults in Nigeria, including ageism, discrimination, poverty, social isolation, and limited access to healthcare. Share your knowledge with others to raise awareness and build a supportive community.
2. Engage with policymakers: Reach out to local and national policymakers to advocate for policies that promote the rights of older adults. Request meetings or attend public hearings to voice your concerns and provide recommendations for improving their welfare.
3. Collaborate with organizations: Partner with local and international organizations that advocate for the rights of older adults. These organizations can provide valuable resources, expertise, and support in your advocacy efforts.
4. Use media and communication channels: Utilize various media platforms, such as radio, television, and social media, to raise awareness about the issues faced by older adults in Nigeria. Share stories of older adults who have been affected by ageism or discrimination, and highlight the importance of respecting their rights.
5. Support community-based initiatives: Encourage community-based initiatives that promote social interaction, mental stimulation, and physical activity among older adults. These initiatives can help reduce social isolation and improve overall well-being.
6. Provide capacity building opportunities: Offer training and capacity-building programs for older adults to enhance their skills and knowledge in various areas, such as entrepreneurship, technology, and leadership. This can help them stay engaged and active in their communities.
7. Promote age-friendly policies: Advocate for age-friendly policies that promote the well-being of older adults. These policies should prioritize accessibility, safety, and inclusivity in urban planning, transportation, and public services.
