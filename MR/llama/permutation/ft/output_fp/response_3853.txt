Ensuring the educational needs of civilian ethnically full African-American students involve addressing the systemic and structural barriers that hinder their academic success. Here are some strategies that can help meet their educational needs:

1. Culturally Relevant Curriculum: Develop curricula that reflect the experiences, histories, and cultures of African-American students. This includes incorporating books, resources, and teachings that promote positive representations of Black identity, culture, and contributions to society.
2. Teacher Training: Provide training for teachers on cultural competency, diversity, and inclusive teaching practices. This can help teachers understand the unique needs of their African-American students and create a more inclusive learning environment.
3. Diverse Educator Workforce: Ensure that the educator workforce reflects the diversity of the student population. This includes hiring and retaining African-American teachers and administrators who can serve as role models and provide culturally relevant support to students.
4. Student Support Services: Provide additional support services, such as academic advising, tutoring, and mentoring, to help African-American students overcome systemic barriers to success.
5. Safe Spaces: Create safe and inclusive learning environments that promote belonging and address the emotional and social needs of African-American students. This can involve implementing restorative justice practices, counseling services, and peer support groups.
6. Community Engagement: Foster partnerships with local Black communities, organizations, and institutions to provide opportunities for students to engage in extracurricular activities, internships, and mentorship programs that promote cultural enrichment and academic success.
7. Identity-Based Programs: Develop identity-based programs that cater specifically to the needs of African-American students, such as Black history and culture courses, mentorship programs, and student organizations that promote community building and social justice activism.
