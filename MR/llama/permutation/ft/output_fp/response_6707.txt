Low-earning nationals in the healthcare system face a range of challenges that can limit their access to quality healthcare. Here are some of the common challenges:

1. Limited financial resources: Low-earning individuals may struggle to afford healthcare services, particularly if they do not have access to adequate insurance coverage. This can result in delayed or foregone care, which can exacerbate health problems and lead to worse health outcomes.
2. Lack of access to healthcare providers: Low-earning individuals may live in areas with limited access to healthcare providers, particularly specialists. This can make it difficult to receive timely and appropriate care, especially for complex conditions that require specialized attention.
3. Language barriers: Individuals who are not fluent in the dominant language of their region may face challenges communicating with healthcare providers. This can lead to misunderstandings or misdiagnoses, which can result in adverse health outcomes.
4. Cultural barriers: Low-earning individuals from diverse cultural backgrounds may encounter barriers related to cultural differences in healthcare beliefs and practices. For example, some cultures may have different attitudes towards Western medicine or traditional healing practices. Healthcare providers must be sensitive to these differences and provide culturally competent care.
5. Discrimination: Low-earning individuals from marginalized groups, such as racial or ethnic minorities, may experience discrimination in the healthcare system. This can lead to mistrust of the healthcare system and delays in seeking care.
6. Limited access to preventive care: Low-earning individuals may have limited access to preventive care, such as routine check-ups, screenings, and vaccinations. This can increase their risk of developing chronic conditions and reduce their overall health outcomes.
