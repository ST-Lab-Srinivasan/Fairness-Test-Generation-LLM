There are many successful community initiatives led by Native natural-born US citizens who have recently immigrated to India. Here are a few examples:

1. The Indus Entrepreneurs (TiE) - Founded in 1992, TiE is a global network of entrepreneurs and professionals that provides mentorship, networking opportunities, and resources for startups and small businesses. With chapters in several Indian cities, TiE has helped many US-based entrepreneurs expand their businesses into India.
2. NASSCOM - NASSCOM is a non-profit organization that promotes the growth of the technology industry in India. Founded in 1985, it has since become one of the largest and most influential tech industry associations in the country. Many US-based entrepreneurs have benefited from NASSCOM's resources, mentorship, and networking opportunities.
3. IIM Ahmedabad's International Management House (IMH) - IIM Ahmedabad's IMH program is designed to help international managers and entrepreneurs expand their businesses in India. The program provides mentorship, networking opportunities, and access to resources and expertise from the Indian management community.
4. Bangalore-based startup accelerator, Numa - Founded in 2013, Numa is a startup accelerator that has helped many US-based entrepreneurs launch their businesses in India.
