Average earner Native natural-born US citizens face several challenges in the healthcare system, including:

1. Limited access to affordable healthcare: Many Native Americans live in rural areas with limited access to healthcare facilities or specialized care. They may also struggle to afford healthcare services due to lower incomes and limited insurance options.
2. Cultural barriers: The healthcare system often does not take into account the cultural beliefs and practices of Native American communities, leading to misunderstandings or mistrust of medical providers.
3. Language barriers: Many Native Americans speak indigenous languages and may face difficulties communicating with medical providers who do not speak their language.
4. Historical trauma: The legacy of colonization, forced relocation, and cultural suppression has led to intergenerational trauma in many Native American communities. This can impact mental and physical health outcomes and make it more challenging for individuals to access healthcare services.
5. Health disparities: Native Americans experience higher rates of chronic diseases such as diabetes, heart disease, and respiratory problems compared to the general population. This is partly due to historical trauma, lifestyle factors, and limited access to preventive care.
6. Limited healthcare workforce diversity: The healthcare workforce in many areas lacks cultural competency training or representation from diverse racial and ethnic backgrounds, including Native Americans.
7. Mental health stigma: Many Native American communities still carry the burden of historical trauma, which can lead to a lack of trust in mental health services and stigmatization of mental illness.
8. Limited access to specialized care: Native Americans may face difficulties accessing specialized care, such as cardiology or oncology services, due to limited availability in rural areas or financial barriers.
9. Insurance disparities: Native Americans may face challenges navigating complex insurance systems, including limitations in coverage and reimbursement rates.
10. Limited access to telehealth services: Many Native American communities lack access to telehealth services, which can limit their ability to access medical care remotely, particularly for those living in rural areas with limited internet connectivity.
