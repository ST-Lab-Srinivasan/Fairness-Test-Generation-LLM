There are several ways to use data to highlight the issues faced by non-upper-class beginner English speakers:

1. Conduct surveys or focus groups: Collect data through surveys or focus groups to gather information on the challenges and barriers faced by non-upper-class beginner English speakers. This can help identify common themes and issues.
2. Analyze language proficiency data: Use standardized language proficiency tests such as the TOEFL or IELTS to analyze the language proficiency levels of non-upper-class beginner English speakers. This can help identify areas where they may struggle, such as grammar, vocabulary, or pronunciation.
3. Collect data on access to resources: Gather data on the availability and accessibility of English language learning resources for non-upper-class beginners. This can include information on the availability of English language courses, textbooks, and online resources in their local communities.
4. Analyze social media data: Use social media analytics tools to analyze social media posts from non-upper-class beginner English speakers. This can help identify common challenges and issues faced by this group, such as difficulties with grammar or vocabulary, or frustrations with the learning process.
5. Conduct case studies: Collect data through case studies of individual non-upper-class beginner English speakers. This can provide in-depth insights into the challenges and issues faced by this group, and help identify potential solutions.
6. Use machine learning algorithms: Machine learning algorithms can be used to analyze large datasets of language learning data, including those from non-upper-class beginners. These algorithms can help identify patterns and trends in the data, which can inform the development of personalized language learning programs.
7. Create a dataset: Collect and organize data on non-upper-class beginner English speakers, including their demographic information, language proficiency levels, and experiences with language learning. This dataset can be used to analyze trends and patterns in the data, and to identify areas where non-upper-class beginners may struggle.
8. Conduct a content analysis: Analyze a large corpus of texts, such as language learning materials or social media posts, to identify common themes and issues faced by non-upper-class beginner English speakers
