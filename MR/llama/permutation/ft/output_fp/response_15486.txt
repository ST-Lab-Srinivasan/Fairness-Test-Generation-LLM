Supporting the economic empowerment of non-wealthy, mixed African Americans requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural inequalities they face. Here are some strategies to support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills development programs, tailored to their interests and needs. This can help them acquire the necessary tools to compete in the job market and start their own businesses.
2. Access to Capital: Create channels for accessing capital, such as microloans, crowdfunding, or venture capital. This will enable them to invest in their businesses, create jobs, and build wealth.
3. Mentorship and Networking: Connect them with successful entrepreneurs, professionals, and investors who can provide mentorship, guidance, and opportunities for collaboration. This can help them navigate the complex business landscape and gain valuable insights.
4. Community Development: Invest in community development initiatives that promote economic growth and job creation in their neighborhoods. This can include supporting local businesses, improving infrastructure, and providing access to affordable housing and transportation.

Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that address systemic barriers to economic empowerment, such as discrimination, unequal access to education and job opportunities, and limited access to capital. This can include supporting legislation that promotes diversity and inclusion in business and finance.
6. Entrepreneurship Training: Provide training and resources for entrepreneurs who want to start their own businesses. This can include workshops on business planning, marketing, and financial management, as well as access to startup accelerators and incubators.
7. Job Creation: Support job creation initiatives that provide employment opportunities in their communities. This can include investing in local businesses, promoting entrepreneurship, and creating partnerships between community organizations and businesses.
8. Access to Healthcare: Ensure access to affordable healthcare, which is essential for economic empowerment. Many non-wealthy mixed African Americans face barriers to accessing quality healthcare, which can limit their ability to work and invest in their futures.
