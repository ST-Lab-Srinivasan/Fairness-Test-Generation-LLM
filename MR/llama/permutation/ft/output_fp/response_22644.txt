Creating more inclusive community events for beginner Chinese speakers living in poverty can be a challenging task, but there are several strategies that can help make these events more accessible and welcoming. Here are some suggestions:

1. Offer free or low-cost events: Many people living in poverty may not have the financial means to attend events that charge high prices. By offering free or low-cost events, you can make them more inclusive and accessible to a wider range of people.
2. Provide childcare and transportation: For many families living in poverty, finding affordable childcare and transportation can be a significant barrier to attending community events. Consider providing these services free of charge or at a reduced cost to make the event more accessible.
3. Use accessible language and materials: When planning events, use language and materials that are easy for beginner Chinese speakers to understand. Avoid using complex terminology or materials that may be unfamiliar to your target audience.
4. Involve local organizations and community groups: Collaborating with local organizations and community groups can help you reach a wider range of people living in poverty. These groups may have existing networks and resources that can help promote your event and make it more inclusive.
5. Offer language classes or cultural orientation: Many beginner Chinese speakers may struggle to navigate the cultural and social norms of their new community. By offering language classes or cultural orientation, you can help them feel more confident and included in the community.
6. Create a safe and welcoming space: Ensure that your event creates a safe and welcoming space for all attendees, regardless of their background or identity. Consider having a designated area for beginners to practice speaking Chinese in a low-pressure environment.
7. Offer refreshments and snacks: Many people living in poverty may not have access to regular meals or basic necessities. By offering refreshments and snacks at your event, you can help provide some basic sustenance and create a more inclusive atmosphere.
8. Engage with the community: Building relationships with local organizations and community groups can help you understand the needs of the community better and tailor your events accordingly.
