Creating more inclusive social policies for jobless partnered individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the various challenges and barriers they face. Here are some suggestions for creating more inclusive social policies:

1. Address income inequality: Implement progressive taxation policies to reduce income inequality, which can help jobless partners have access to basic needs such as food, shelter, and healthcare.
2. Provide training and education: Offer vocational training and educational programs specifically designed for jobless partners, focusing on skills that are in demand in the labor market. This can help them acquire new skills and increase their employability.
3. Encourage entrepreneurship: Support jobless partners in starting their own businesses or social enterprises, providing them with access to funding, mentorship, and networking opportunities.
4. Address systemic barriers: Identify and address any systemic barriers that prevent jobless partners from finding employment, such as discrimination, lack of access to transportation, or childcare responsibilities.
5. Foster inclusive work cultures: Encourage employers to adopt inclusive hiring practices, provide flexible work arrangements, and create a supportive work environment for jobless partners who are returning to the workforce.
6. Provide mental health support: Offer mental health services and resources specifically designed for jobless partners, addressing the emotional challenges they may face during this transition.
7. Advocate for policy change: Work with policymakers to advocate for policies that support jobless partners, such as paid family leave, flexible work arrangements, and protections against discrimination based on caregiving status.
8. Build community networks: Establish community networks and support groups specifically designed for jobless partners, providing them with a sense of belonging and connection to others who are going through similar experiences.
9. Address housing insecurity: Develop policies that address housing insecurity, such as rent control or affordable housing programs, to help jobless partners find stable and affordable housing.
10. Promote social safety nets: Strengthen social safety nets, such as unemployment insurance, to provide financial support during periods of joblessness.

By addressing these challenges and barriers, we can create more inclusive social policies
