2. Tutoring: Provide one-on-one tutoring sessions to help them improve their English skills. You could also consider hiring a professional tutor who is experienced in teaching English to beginners.
3. Online resources: There are many online resources available that can provide beginner English speakers with language learning materials, such as grammar guides, vocabulary lists, and practice exercises. Websites like Duolingo, Babbel, and Rosetta Stone offer interactive lessons and exercises that can help them improve their skills.
4. Language exchange programs: Encourage them to participate in language exchange programs where they can practice speaking English with native speakers or other learners. This will provide them with an opportunity to improve their speaking skills and get feedback on their pronunciation and grammar.
5. Cultural events: Attend cultural events with them, such as concerts, plays, or exhibitions, which can help them develop a deeper understanding of English-speaking cultures. These events can also provide opportunities for them to practice their language skills in a social setting.
6. Books and media: Provide them with books, audiobooks, and TV shows/movies in English that they can use to improve their listening and reading skills. This will help them develop their vocabulary and comprehension abilities.
7. Immersion: Encourage them to spend time immersed in an English-speaking environment as much as possible. This could involve spending time with English-speaking friends, family, or colleagues, or even moving to an English-speaking country for a period of time.
