Creating more inclusive public spaces for independent beginner Chinese speakers requires careful consideration of their needs and preferences. Here are some suggestions:

1. Provide clear signage and wayfinding: Many independent beginner Chinese speakers may struggle to navigate unfamiliar environments, especially if they are not familiar with the local language or culture. Providing clear signage and wayfinding can help them locate important areas such as restrooms, food vendors, and seating areas more easily.
2. Offer bilingual support: Train staff and volunteers to provide bilingual support, including both Chinese and English. This can involve providing translated information, such as brochures, maps, and announcements, or offering language assistance during events or programs.
3. Create designated quiet areas: Independent beginner Chinese speakers may prefer quieter spaces for studying or working, especially if they are easily distracted by noise. Consider creating designated quiet areas with comfortable seating, adequate lighting, and minimal background noise.
4. Provide adaptive technology: Many independent beginner Chinese speakers may have difficulty accessing digital resources due to language barriers or lack of access to technology. Providing adaptive technology, such as text-to-speech software or magnification tools, can help level the playing field.

6. Facilitate language exchange programs: Set up language exchange programs that connect independent beginner Chinese speakers with local volunteers who are fluent in English. This can provide an opportunity for both parties to practice their language skills and build cultural understanding.
7. Offer flexible programming options: Design programs and services that cater to the diverse needs of independent beginner Chinese speakers. For example, offering flexible scheduling or online access to resources can help them balance their studies with other responsibilities.
