3. Enhanced communication: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers communicate more effectively with patients from diverse backgrounds, which can lead to better understanding and care.
4. Increased access to care: By being culturally competent, healthcare providers can build trust and rapport with patients from underrepresented communities, which can increase access to care for these populations.
