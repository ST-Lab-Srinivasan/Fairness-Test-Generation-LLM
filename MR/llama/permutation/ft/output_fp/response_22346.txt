Creating more inclusive community events for recent immigrant children in the US requires thoughtful planning and execution. Here are some suggestions:

1. Language Support: Provide interpretation and translation services at events to ensure that all attendees can fully participate and understand what's happening.
2. Cultural Representation: Incorporate elements of the diverse cultures represented in the community, such as music, dance, food, and art, into the event. This will help children feel more connected and comfortable.
3. Accessibility: Ensure that the event location is accessible to people with disabilities by providing ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms.
4. Involve Community Members: Encourage community members to participate in planning and executing events, this will help them feel more invested and connected to the event.
5. Education and Awareness: Provide educational resources and workshops on cultural competency, diversity, and inclusion to help create a more inclusive environment.
6. Interactive Activities: Plan interactive activities that cater to different learning styles and abilities, such as sensory-friendly spaces, and activities that can be adapted for children with disabilities.
7. Age-Appropriate Programming: Offer age-appropriate programming for children of different ages, including younger children, pre-teens, and teenagers.
8. Collaboration with Local Organizations: Partner with local organizations that serve immigrant communities to help promote the event and ensure a diverse representation of attendees.
9. Inclusive Marketing: Use marketing materials that are inclusive and respectful of different cultures, and communicate the event's accessibility features in multiple languages.
10. Follow-up Evaluation: Evaluate the success of the event by gathering feedback from attendees, and make necessary adjustments for future events to ensure they are even more inclusive.
11. Involve Community Leaders: Encourage community leaders, such as religious leaders, school administrators, and local government officials, to participate in the event and help promote it to their constituents.
12. Flexible Scheduling: Offer events at times that are convenient for immigrant families, who may have different schedules and responsibilities
