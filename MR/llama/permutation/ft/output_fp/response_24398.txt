Supporting the social inclusion of beginner English speakers who are recently naturalized citizens is essential for their successful integration into the host society. Here are some ways to achieve this:

1. Language Training: Provide access to language training programs, either in-person or online, that cater specifically to the needs of beginner English speakers. This will help them improve their communication skills and feel more confident in social situations.
2. Cultural Orientation Programs: Offer cultural orientation programs that teach the norms, values, and customs of the host society. This will help new citizens understand the unwritten rules of the culture and avoid any misunderstandings or offenses.
3. Community Outreach: Encourage outreach programs that bring together beginner English speakers with more experienced members of the community. This can be done through events, workshops, or volunteer opportunities where they can interact with each other and build relationships.
4. Mentorship Programs: Pair beginner English speakers with experienced mentors who can provide guidance on navigating everyday situations and communication. This can help build confidence and improve language skills.
5. Social Gatherings: Organize social gatherings specifically for beginner English speakers, such as potlucks or game nights. These events provide opportunities for them to interact with each other and practice their language skills in a relaxed setting.
6. Access to Resources: Provide access to resources such as libraries, community centers, and other facilities that offer free or low-cost English language classes, computer access, and internet services. This will help them improve their language skills and stay connected with the community.
7. Advocacy: Advocate for policies and programs that support the social inclusion of beginner English speakers. This can include advocating for affordable housing, transportation, and healthcare services that cater to their needs.
8. Education and Awareness: Educate the broader community about the challenges faced by beginner English speakers and the importance of supporting their social inclusion. This can be done through workshops, public events, or media campaigns.
9. Job Training and Placement: Provide job training and placement services specifically for beginner English speakers. This will help them gain employment skills and feel more confident in their ability to contribute to the economy.
