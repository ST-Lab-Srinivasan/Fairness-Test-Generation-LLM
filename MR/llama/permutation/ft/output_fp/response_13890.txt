Here are some ways to support the educational aspirations of beginner Arabic speakers who are long-time veterans:

1. Offer language classes: Provide structured language classes that cater to their level and needs, whether online or in-person. Use a variety of teaching methods, such as conversational practice, grammar exercises, and cultural immersion activities.
2. Create a supportive environment: Foster a welcoming and inclusive environment where they feel comfortable learning and practicing Arabic. Encourage peer-to-peer learning, provide feedback, and celebrate small victories to boost their motivation.
3. Provide resources and materials: Offer access to relevant textbooks, language learning apps, and online resources that cater to their level of proficiency. Encourage them to use these resources to supplement their learning and practice outside of class.
4. Practice with them: Engage in conversation and language practice with the veterans, using authentic materials such as news articles, videos, or podcasts. This will help them improve their listening and speaking skills, as well as gain cultural insights.
5. Connect them with native speakers: Arrange for them to interact with native Arabic speakers, either through language exchange programs, conversation practice sessions, or cultural events. This will provide opportunities for them to practice speaking and listening in a more authentic setting.
6. Encourage self-directed learning: Help the veterans discover new learning resources and strategies, such as podcasts, YouTube channels, or online courses, that they can use to continue their language journey at their own pace.

Offer cultural immersion experiences: Organize trips or events that allow them to immerse themselves in Arabic culture, such as attending festivals, visiting historical sites, or participating in traditional activities. This will help them deepen their understanding of the language and its cultural context.
