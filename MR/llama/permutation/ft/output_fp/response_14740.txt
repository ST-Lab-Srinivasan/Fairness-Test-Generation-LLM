Creating more inclusive community events for beginner French speakers from the middle class requires careful planning and consideration of their needs and preferences. Here are some suggestions on how to create such events:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the event is easily accessible to participants, including those who may not be fluent in French. Provide clear instructions and information in multiple languages, and make sure the venue is wheelchair-friendly and has amenities for people with disabilities.
2. Interactive activities: Plan interactive activities that cater to different learning styles and preferences. For example, incorporate games, quizzes, and group discussions to keep participants engaged and motivated. You can also consider cultural immersion experiences, such as cooking classes or wine tastings, to help participants practice their French skills in a fun and relaxed environment.
3. Diverse speaker lineup: Assemble a diverse team of speakers who are experienced in teaching French to beginners. Consider the age, gender, and cultural backgrounds of your target audience when selecting speakers, and ensure that they are passionate about teaching and engaging with their students.
4. Affordable pricing: Middle-class people may have budget constraints, so it's essential to offer affordable pricing options for your events. Consider offering discounts or scholarships to make the events more accessible to a wider range of participants.
5. Social media promotion: Utilize social media platforms to promote your events and reach a broader audience. Create engaging content, such as event trailers or behind-the-scenes peeks, to generate interest and excitement among potential attendees.
6. Inclusive language policies: Ensure that your events are inclusive and respectful of all cultures and backgrounds. Avoid using language that may be offensive or alienating to certain groups, and create a safe and welcoming environment for all participants.
7. Flexible scheduling: Offer flexible scheduling options to accommodate different time zones and schedules. This can include virtual events or pre-recorded content that can be accessed at any time, making it easier for participants from diverse locations to attend.
