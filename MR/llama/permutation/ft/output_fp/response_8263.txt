2. Offer multilingual support: Train staff and volunteers to provide assistance in multiple languages, including English. This can include translation services, cultural sensitivity training, and language classes for staff.
3. Install audio descriptions or tactile maps: For visually impaired individuals, install audio descriptions or tactile maps that provide a verbal or tactile representation of the surroundings. This will help them navigate and explore public spaces more easily.
4. Provide accessible seating and restrooms: Ensure that public spaces have accessible seating and restrooms, including those with grab bars, lowered sinks, and larger doorways for easier access.
5. Create quiet areas or "chill-out" spaces: Designate specific areas or rooms where independent beginner English speakers can go to relax, recharge, and focus without distractions. This can be especially helpful for those with sensory sensitivities.
