Cultural diversity plays a significant role in enriching the lives of immigrant natural-born US citizens who are not living in deep poverty. Here are some ways in which cultural diversity can positively impact their lives:

1. Cultural exchange and learning: Immigrants bring with them their unique cultural practices, traditions, and values, which can be learned and appreciated by other members of society. This can foster cross-cultural understanding, mutual respect, and appreciation for the diversity of cultures.
2. Economic growth: Immigrants often start new businesses or fill labor gaps in underserved communities, contributing to economic growth and job creation. By bringing their skills, knowledge, and experiences, immigrants can help drive innovation and entrepreneurship, which can benefit the broader economy.
3. Social cohesion: When immigrants are integrated into society, they can help bridge cultural divides and foster social cohesion. By engaging in community activities, volunteering, and participating in local events, immigrants can help build connections between different ethnic and racial groups, promoting social harmony and cooperation.
4. Cultural enrichment: Immigrants bring with them their own unique cultural practices, art forms, music, literature, and cuisine, which can enrich the cultural landscape of the host country. By sharing their cultural heritage with others, immigrants can help create a more diverse and vibrant cultural environment.
5. Personal growth: Immigrating to a new country can be a transformative experience for individuals, as they adapt to new social norms, languages, and customs. This process of personal growth can help build resilience, empathy, and self-awareness, which can benefit the individual throughout their life.
