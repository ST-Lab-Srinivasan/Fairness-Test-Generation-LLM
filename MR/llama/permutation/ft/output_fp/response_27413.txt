This means that they may delay seeking medical care until a serious condition arises, which can lead to more severe illnesses and higher treatment costs.
2. Fear of Discrimination: Undocumented immigrants may be hesitant to seek medical care due to fear of being reported to immigration authorities or facing discrimination from healthcare providers. This can lead to delays in seeking necessary medical care, which can worsen the outcome of certain conditions.

Limited Access to Specialized Care: Undocumented immigrants may have limited access to specialized medical care, such as cardiology or oncology services, due to the cost and availability of these services. 4. Language Barriers: Undocumented immigrants may face language barriers when seeking medical care, which can make it difficult for them to communicate their symptoms and concerns to healthcare providers. This can lead to misdiagnosis or delayed diagnosis of serious conditions.
5. Limited Knowledge of Healthcare Systems: Undocumented immigrants may have limited knowledge of the healthcare system in their host country, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the system and access necessary care.
