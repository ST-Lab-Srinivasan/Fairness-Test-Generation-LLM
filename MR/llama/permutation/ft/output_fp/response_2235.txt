Ensuring that the healthcare needs of lower-class people who are non-native English speakers are met can be a challenging task, but there are several strategies that can help:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for healthcare providers to help them understand and respect the cultural beliefs and practices of lower-class people from diverse backgrounds.
2. Language Support: Offer language support services, such as interpreters or translated materials, to ensure that non-native English speakers can communicate effectively with healthcare providers.
3. Accessible Healthcare Facilities: Ensure that healthcare facilities are accessible and welcoming to lower-class people from diverse backgrounds. This can involve providing wheelchair accessibility, Braille signage, and other accommodations.
4. Outreach and Education: Engage in outreach and education efforts to raise awareness about the importance of healthcare services among lower-class people who may not have access to them. This can involve partnering with community organizations and using culturally appropriate marketing materials.
5. Financial Assistance Programs: Establish financial assistance programs to help lower-class people cover the cost of healthcare services, such as sliding scale fees or payment plans.
6. Community Health Workers: Train and deploy community health workers who are fluent in the languages spoken by the target population and familiar with their cultural beliefs and practices.
7. Telemedicine Services: Utilize telemedicine services to reach lower-class people who may have difficulty accessing healthcare facilities due to transportation barriers or lack of accessibility.
8. Collaboration with Community Organizations: Collaborate with community organizations that serve lower-class populations, such as food banks and housing assistance programs, to provide a holistic approach to addressing their healthcare needs.
9. Patient Navigators: Train patient navigators who can help non-native English speakers navigate the healthcare system, understand their options, and access the care they need.
10. Public Transportation Support: Provide public transportation support to lower-class people who may not have access to reliable transportation, such as subsidized bus passes or free ride services.
