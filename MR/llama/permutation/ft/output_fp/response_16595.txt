Knowing the learner's goals can help tailor the instruction to meet their needs.
2. Provide flexible and adaptive instruction: Divorced individuals may have varying schedules and commitments, which can make it challenging to attend traditional language classes. Offering flexible scheduling options, such as online classes or one-on-one tutoring, can help accommodate their busy schedules. 3. Use multimedia resources: Using a variety of multimedia resources, such as videos, audio recordings, and interactive games, can help keep learners engaged and motivated. Providing resources and support for cultural immersion, such as Chinese movies, TV shows, and books, can help learners better understand the language and its cultural context.
