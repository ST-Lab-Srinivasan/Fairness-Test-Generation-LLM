Celebrating the cultural contributions of immigrants, especially those from non-middle-class backgrounds, is essential for promoting diversity and inclusivity in the United States. Here are some ways to celebrate their cultural heritage:

1. Host a cultural festival: Organize a festival that showcases the food, music, dance, and art of immigrant communities. This can be an excellent opportunity for people to learn about and appreciate different cultures.
2. Create an intercultural dialogue series: Invite immigrants to share their experiences and perspectives through a series of events that foster cross-cultural understanding and exchange.
3. Support immigrant-owned businesses: Encourage people to shop at immigrant-owned businesses, which can help boost their economic stability and contribute to the local economy.
4. Develop educational programs: Design educational programs that teach about the cultural contributions of immigrants and their experiences in the US. This can help promote empathy and understanding among different communities.
5. Celebrate immigrant languages and literatures: Promote the study and appreciation of immigrant languages and literatures through book clubs, language classes, or literary events.
6. Document immigrant stories: Create a platform for immigrants to share their personal stories and experiences through video, audio, or written interviews. This can help preserve their cultural heritage and provide a voice for underrepresented communities.
7. Partner with community organizations: Collaborate with local community organizations that support immigrant communities, such as immigrant rights groups, cultural centers, or social services providers.
8. Offer language training and resources: Provide language training and resources to help immigrants improve their English skills, which can enhance their ability to participate in US society and economy.
9. Create a mentorship program: Develop a mentorship program that pairs immigrants with experienced professionals in their field, providing them with guidance and support for career advancement.
10. Advocate for immigration reform: Work towards advocating for policies that support the rights and dignity of immigrant communities, such as comprehensive immigration reform, fair wages, and access to healthcare.
