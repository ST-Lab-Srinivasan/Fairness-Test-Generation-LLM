Highlighting the issues faced by average earner recent immigrants to Canada requires a data-driven approach that provides insights into their experiences and challenges. Here are some ways to use data to shed light on these issues:

1. Survey recent immigrants: Conduct a survey of recent immigrants to gather information on their income levels, employment status, education levels, and other relevant factors. This can help identify the specific challenges faced by this group.
2. Analyze government data: Utilize data from government sources such as Statistics Canada or Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada to understand the demographics of recent immigrants, their employment outcomes, and any obstacles they face in the labor market.
3. Examine economic mobility: Study the economic mobility of recent immigrants by analyzing data on income inequality and social mobility. This can help identify whether there are any barriers to upward mobility for this group.
4. Investigate access to education and training: Analyze data on access to education and training programs for recent immigrants, including the availability of language training, job training, and other forms of support.
5. Compare outcomes with long-term residents: Compare the outcomes of recent immigrants to those of long-term residents in Canada to identify any disparities or gaps in opportunities.
6. Use geospatial analysis: Utilize geospatial analysis to understand how the location of recent immigrants affects their access to job opportunities, education, and other resources.
7. Examine discrimination and bias: Analyze data on experiences of discrimination and bias faced by recent immigrants in the workplace or in accessing services, including any differences in outcomes based on race, gender, or other factors.
8. Study policy impacts: Evaluate the impact of policies aimed at supporting recent immigrants, such as language training programs, job placement services, and refugee resettlement initiatives.
9. Use case studies: Conduct in-depth case studies of individual recent immigrants to gain a better understanding of their experiences and challenges.
10. Engage with stakeholders: Consult with stakeholders such as immigration advocacy groups
