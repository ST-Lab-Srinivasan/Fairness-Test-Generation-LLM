Addressing the specific needs of Native American recent immigrants to the US requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique cultural, historical, and social contexts. Here are some strategies that can help address their specific needs in education:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Native American students often face challenges related to cultural dissonance, identity formation, and language barriers. Teachers can use culturally responsive teaching practices that incorporate traditional knowledge, languages, and cultural beliefs to create a more inclusive learning environment.
2. Identity-based programming: Develop programs that explicitly address the needs of Native American students, such as language revitalization, cultural camps, and mentorship opportunities. These programs can help students develop a positive identity and connection to their culture, which is critical for academic success.
3. Community engagement: Engage with local Native American communities through partnerships, collaborations, and community-based research projects. This can help build trust and mutual understanding between educators and the community, leading to more effective programming and support.
4. Language support: Provide language support services for Native American students, such as language classes or bilingual education programs. This can help address language barriers and improve academic outcomes.
5. Cultural competency training: Provide cultural competency training for educators to help them understand the unique needs of Native American students and how to best support them. This can include training on cultural norms, practices, and beliefs.
6. Indigenous knowledge integration: Incorporate indigenous knowledge and ways of knowing into education curricula and teaching practices. This can help students connect with their cultural heritage and develop a deeper understanding of the world around them.
