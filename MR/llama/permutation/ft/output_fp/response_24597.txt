Creating more inclusive public spaces for moderate beginner Chinese speakers requires careful consideration of their needs and preferences. Here are some suggestions:

2. Offer language classes or workshops: Organize language classes or workshops specifically tailored for moderate beginner Chinese speakers. This can help them improve their language skills and become more confident in using Chinese in public spaces.
3. Train public space staff in Chinese language and culture: Provide training for staff working in public spaces, such as transportation hubs or parks, on basic Chinese language and cultural etiquette to better serve moderate beginner Chinese speakers.
4. Create accessible and inclusive communication materials: Develop communication materials, such as brochures, flyers, or digital displays, that are accessible and inclusive of all language learners. Use simple language and visual aids to help beginners understand the information.
5. Facilitate language exchange programs: Organize language exchange programs where moderate beginner Chinese speakers can practice speaking with native Chinese speakers or more advanced learners. This can help build their confidence and improve their language skills.
6. Offer translation services: Provide translation services for public announcements, signs, and other communication materials to help beginners access important information more easily.
7. Design inclusive public spaces: When designing public spaces, consider the needs of all users, including moderate beginner Chinese speakers. Use universal design principles to create spaces that are accessible and comfortable for everyone.

9. Offer cultural events and activities: Organize cultural events and activities that cater to the interests of moderate beginner Chinese speakers, such as traditional music or dance performances, cultural workshops, or language-related events.
