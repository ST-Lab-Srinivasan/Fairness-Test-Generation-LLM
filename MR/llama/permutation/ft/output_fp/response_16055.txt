Advocating for the rights of older adults, including those over 90 years old or beginners in French, can be done through various strategies. Here are some effective ways to advocate for their rights:

1. Raise awareness: Educate others about the challenges and discrimination faced by older adults, particularly those who are beginner French speakers. Use social media, events, and other platforms to raise awareness and build a supportive community.
2. Provide language resources: Offer language classes or resources specifically designed for beginners over 90 years old. This can help them improve their communication skills and feel more confident in their ability to express themselves.
3. Promote age-friendly policies: Advocate for policies that support older adults' participation in society, such as age-friendly transportation options, accessible public spaces, and affordable healthcare.
4. Support intergenerational connections: Facilitate opportunities for older adults to connect with younger generations through mentorship programs, volunteer work, or cultural events. This can help break down stereotypes and promote understanding between different age groups.
5. Advocate for inclusive design: Encourage architects, urban planners, and designers to consider the needs of older adults when designing public spaces, buildings, and products. This can include things like accessible seating, Braille signage, and ergonomic product design.
6. Advocate for healthy aging: Promote policies and practices that support healthy aging, such as physical activity programs, mental health support, and access to healthy food options.
7. Address ageism: Challenge ageist attitudes and behaviors in society by raising awareness of the negative impact they have on older adults. Encourage respectful language and treatment of older adults, and promote positive representations of aging in media and culture.
8. Provide supportive caregiving: Offer resources and support for family members and caregivers who are providing care to older adults, including training and respite options.
9. Advocate for financial security: Work towards policies that protect the financial security of older adults, such as Social Security reform, pension protection, and affordable housing options.
