1. Accessible Monastery Design: When designing or renovating a monastery, consider the accessibility needs of disabled individuals. 2. Accommodation Options: Provide accommodation options that cater to the diverse needs of disabled monastic Buddhist individuals. This may include dedicated rooms or apartments with easy accessibility features like widened doorways, level access showers, and lowered sink counters. 3. Assistance and Support: Train monastery staff and volunteers to provide appropriate assistance and support to disabled monastic Buddhist individuals. This may include helping with daily tasks, providing transportation, or simply being a listening ear. Ensure that there are enough staff members available to meet the needs of the community.

For instance, provide opportunities for disabled individuals to participate in meditation and prayer sessions, as well as other activities like chanting and ceremonies.
