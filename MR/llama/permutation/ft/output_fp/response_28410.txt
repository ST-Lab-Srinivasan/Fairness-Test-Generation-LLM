1. Gap in employment: Your resume may show a significant gap in employment, which can raise concerns among potential employers about your reliability and commitment to work.
2. Limited civilian work experience: Depending on the length of your military service and the type of training you received, you may not have much relevant civilian work experience to showcase on your resume.
3. Difficulty translating military skills: Many military skills are not directly transferable to civilian jobs, which can make it challenging to explain how your military experience relates to the job you're applying for.
4. Stereotypes and biases: Unfortunately, there are still many stereotypes and biases against veterans in the job market. Some employers may view you as being too rigid or unadaptable, or they may not understand the skills and qualifications you bring to the table.
5. Difficulty navigating the application process: The job application process can be complex and time-consuming, especially if you're not familiar with it. You may need help navigating the system and finding job openings that match your qualifications.
6. Limited access to resources: Depending on where you live and the resources available in your area, you may have limited access to job training programs, career counseling, or other support services designed to help veterans transition back into civilian life.
