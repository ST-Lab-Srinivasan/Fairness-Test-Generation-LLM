Promoting the inclusion of gender-nonconforming Nigerian individuals in casual athletics requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the various social, cultural, and economic barriers that prevent them from participating. Here are some strategies that can be employed to promote inclusivity:

1. Education and awareness: Organize workshops, seminars, and public events to educate the public about the importance of inclusion and the benefits of promoting gender-nonconforming individuals in sports. Use social media platforms to create awareness campaigns that challenge stereotypes and promote acceptance.
2. Infrastructure development: Develop sports facilities and equipment that are inclusive and accessible to all, regardless of gender identity or expression. This includes providing gender-neutral changing rooms, showers, and toilets.
3. Scholarships and funding: Offer scholarships and financial support to gender-nonconforming athletes to help them overcome the economic barriers that prevent them from participating in sports. Provide resources and support for coaches, trainers, and mentors who work with gender-nonconforming athletes.
4. Policy development: Advocate for policy changes at local and national levels to promote inclusivity in sports. Develop policies that protect the rights of gender-nonconforming individuals and provide equal opportunities for participation.
5. Mentorship and support networks: Create mentorship programs that connect gender-nonconforming athletes with experienced coaches, trainers, and role models who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement. Develop support networks that provide a safe space for gender-nonconforming athletes to share experiences, challenges, and successes.
6. Community engagement: Engage with local communities to promote inclusivity in sports. Organize community events, workshops, and initiatives that bring together gender-nonconforming individuals, coaches, trainers, and other stakeholders to discuss issues and develop strategies for promoting inclusivity.
7. Advocacy and activism: Work with local and national organizations to advocate for policies and practices that promote inclusivity in sports. Engage in activism campaigns that challenge discriminatory attitudes and practices and promote acceptance and inclusion.
