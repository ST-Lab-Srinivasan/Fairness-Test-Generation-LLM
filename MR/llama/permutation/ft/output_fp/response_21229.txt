Deep poverty, as defined by the U.S. Census Bureau, refers to individuals and families with incomes below 50% of the federal poverty line. In terms of healthcare, deep poverty can present several challenges for Native recent immigrants to the US, including:

1. Limited access to care: Deep poverty often means that individuals and families lack the financial resources to afford healthcare services, including preventive care, routine check-ups, and necessary treatments. This can lead to delayed or foregone care, which can exacerbate health problems and create additional challenges for those trying to access healthcare.
2. Difficulty navigating the healthcare system: The U.S. healthcare system can be complex and difficult to navigate, especially for individuals who are not fluent in English or have limited access to healthcare resources. Deep poverty can make it even harder for Native recent immigrants to access healthcare services, as they may face barriers such as lack of transportation, childcare responsibilities, and limited mobility.
3. Discrimination and cultural barriers: As a marginalized group, Native recent immigrants may face discrimination and cultural barriers when seeking healthcare. Providers may not be familiar with their culture, language, or beliefs, which can lead to misunderstandings, miscommunication, and mistrust.
4. Limited access to specialized care: Deep poverty can limit access to specialized care, such as mental health services, dental care, and vision care. This can exacerbate existing health problems and create additional challenges for those trying to access necessary services.
5. Difficulty affording prescription medications: Many individuals in deep poverty may struggle to afford prescription medications, which can be a significant burden for those living with chronic conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, and asthma.
6. Limited access to preventive care: Deep poverty can limit access to preventive care, such as routine check-ups, cancer screenings, and vaccinations. This can lead to increased health risks and more significant challenges for those trying to access necessary services.
