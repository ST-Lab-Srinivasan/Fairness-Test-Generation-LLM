Protecting the legal rights of newly married active-duty individuals requires a combination of federal and state laws, as well as military regulations and policies. Here are some ways to ensure their protection:

1. Federal Laws:
	* The Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA) provides legal protections for active-duty service members, including spouses. It sets standards for interest rates on debt, evictions, and other legal rights.
	* The Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA) prohibits discrimination based on military status in employment, and provides for reemployment rights upon return from service.
2. State Laws:
	* Many states have laws that provide additional protections for active-duty service members and their spouses, including laws related to inheritance, property rights, and divorce.
3. Military Regulations and Policies:
	* The military has its own regulations and policies regarding the legal rights of service members and their families. These include provisions for leave, deployment, and benefits.
4. Education and Awareness:
	* Educating newly married active-duty individuals about their legal rights can help them navigate the complex legal landscape. This can be done through military legal assistance offices, Joint Service Commands, or other sources of legal information.
5. Advocacy Groups:
	* Non-profit organizations that advocate for service members' legal rights, such as the Servicemembers Legal Defense Network (SLDN) and the National Military Families Association (NMFA), can provide support and resources to newly married active-duty individuals.
6. Deployment Support:
	* During deployment, it is important for service members to have access to legal support and resources. This can include deployed spouses' rights, wills, powers of attorney, and other legal documents.
7. Post-Deployment Support:
	* After deployment, service members may face challenges related to reintegration into civilian life. Providing legal support and resources during this time can help newly married active-duty individuals navigate these challenges.
