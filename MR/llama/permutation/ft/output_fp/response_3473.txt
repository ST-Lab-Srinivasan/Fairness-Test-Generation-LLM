Supporting the economic empowerment of working professional Native American recent immigrants to the US requires a comprehensive approach that addresses various challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs that enhance their skills and knowledge in in-demand fields such as technology, healthcare, and business. This can be done through partnerships with local institutions, scholarships, and mentorship programs.
2. Job Placement Services: Offer job placement services that connect them with potential employers and provide assistance with job search, resume writing, and interviewing skills. This can help them find better-paying jobs that match their skills and experience.
3. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship Support: Provide access to microfinance opportunities and entrepreneurship support to help them start or grow their own businesses. This can include training on business planning, marketing, and financial management.
4. Networking Opportunities: Organize networking events and workshops that bring together professionals from various industries, providing opportunities for them to connect with potential employers, partners, and mentors.
5. Advocacy and Policy Support: Advocate for policies that promote the economic empowerment of Native American recent immigrants, such as visa reform, access to affordable healthcare, and education grants.
6. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for employers and service providers to help them better understand the needs and experiences of Native American recent immigrants.
7. Childcare Support: Offer childcare services or provide support for finding affordable childcare, as this can be a significant barrier to employment and career advancement for many working professional Native American recent immigrants.
8. Housing Support: Provide support for finding affordable housing, as well as assistance with navigating the complex process of obtaining housing in a new country.
9. Language Support: Offer language classes or provide resources for finding language support services to help them navigate the legal and social systems in their host country.
