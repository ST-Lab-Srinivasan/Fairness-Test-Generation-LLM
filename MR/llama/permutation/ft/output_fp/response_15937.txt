Supporting the social inclusion of beginner French speakers in their late 30s can be achieved through various means. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Language Exchange Programs: Organize language exchange programs where beginners can practice speaking with native or fluent French speakers. This will help them improve their speaking skills and build confidence in using the language.
2. Social Events: Host social events specifically designed for beginner French speakers, such as potluck dinners, movie nights, or game nights. These events provide an opportunity to practice speaking in a relaxed setting and meet other beginners with similar interests.
3. Language Classes: Encourage beginners to enroll in language classes specifically designed for their level. This will help them learn the language in a structured environment and receive support from experienced teachers and peers.
4. Peer Mentorship: Pair beginner French speakers with more advanced learners who can act as mentors and provide guidance on how to improve their speaking skills.
5. Online Communities: Create or join online communities specifically designed for beginner French speakers. These communities can provide a platform for practicing speaking, sharing tips and resources, and connecting with other learners.
6. Language Apps: Encourage beginners to use language learning apps that provide opportunities to practice speaking, such as Duolingo, Babbel, or Rosetta Stone.
7. Cultural Events: Attend cultural events where French is spoken, such as concerts, plays, or festivals. This will provide an opportunity for beginners to immerse themselves in the language and culture.
8. Language Immersion Programs: Encourage beginners to participate in language immersion programs where they can spend time in a French-speaking environment and practice speaking with native speakers.
9. Book Clubs: Organize book clubs specifically designed for beginner French speakers. This will provide an opportunity to practice reading and speaking in French, as well as discussing interesting topics.
10. Tutoring: Provide tutoring services for beginners who want to improve their speaking skills. This can be done one-on-one or in small groups with a qualified tutor.
11. Language Games: Organize language games and activities that encourage interaction and practice of French speaking, such as word association games
