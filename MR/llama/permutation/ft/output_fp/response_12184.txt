Ensuring the educational needs of civilian Native recent immigrants to the US requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique challenges and provides support for their academic success. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training for educators on cultural competency, including an understanding of the traditional beliefs, values, and practices of Native American communities. This will help teachers to create a more inclusive and respectful learning environment.
2. Language Support: Offer language support services such as interpreters, translators, and bilingual education programs to ensure that Native students have equal access to educational resources.
3. Academic Support Services: Provide academic support services such as tutoring, mentoring, and academic advising to help Native students catch up with their peers in terms of academic performance.
4. Cultural Integration: Incorporate traditional Native American cultural practices and knowledge into the curriculum to provide a more culturally responsive education. This can include incorporating traditional ecological knowledge, storytelling, and art into the classroom.
5. Community Partnerships: Develop partnerships with local Native American communities to provide academic and cultural enrichment opportunities for students. This can include internships, job shadowing, and cultural immersion programs.
6. Identity-Based Curriculum: Develop a curriculum that reflects the identities and experiences of Native American students. This can include lessons on Native American history, culture, and contributions to society.
7. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair Native American students with successful professionals from their communities. This can provide role models and support for students as they navigate their academic and career paths.
8. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes at the local, state, and federal levels to better support the educational needs of Native American students. This can include advocating for increased funding for Native American educational programs and policies that promote cultural competency in education.
