1. Consult with experts: Consult with occupational therapists, physiotherapists, or other medical professionals who specialize in working with individuals with genetic conditions. They can provide valuable insights and suggestions on how to create inclusive recreational activities.

3. Use visual aids: Use visual aids such as pictures or videos to demonstrate the activities and help the individuals understand what is expected of them. This can be particularly helpful for beginner Chinese speaker people who may not be familiar with the activities.
4. Provide guidance and support: Provide guidance and support during the activities to ensure that the individuals are comfortable and able to participate fully. This can include providing physical assistance, demonstrating techniques, or simply being present to offer encouragement.
