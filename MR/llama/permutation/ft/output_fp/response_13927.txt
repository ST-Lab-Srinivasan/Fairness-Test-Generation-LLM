1. Involve them in the planning process: Invite young people to participate in the planning and organizing of events. This will give them a sense of ownership and agency, and ensure that their needs and interests are taken into account.
2. Offer diverse activities: Provide a variety of activities that cater to different interests and preferences, such as sports, music, art, dance, or volunteer work. This will help ensure that there is something for everyone, regardless of their hobbies or passions.
3. Make it accessible: Ensure that the event location is easily accessible for people with disabilities by providing wheelchair ramps, Braille signage, and audio descriptions. Additionally, provide accommodations such as ASL interpreters, note-taking services, or audio describers upon request.
4. Provide inclusive language: Use language that is respectful and inclusive of all gender identities, sexual orientations, races, ethnicities, and abilities. Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes any group.
5. Promote cultural diversity: Celebrate the diverse cultures and backgrounds of the young people in your community by incorporating traditional music, dance, art, or food into the event. This will help create a sense of belonging and inclusivity.
6. Offer gender-neutral bathrooms: Provide gender-neutral bathrooms to ensure that all attendees feel comfortable and included.
7. Accommodate dietary restrictions: Offer vegetarian or vegan options for food, and provide information on the ingredients used in case of any allergies or dietary restrictions.
