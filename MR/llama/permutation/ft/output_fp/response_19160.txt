Addressing the specific needs of Native English Speaker Recent Immigrants (NESRIs) in the US healthcare system requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique experiences, challenges, and cultural backgrounds. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Cultural competence training for healthcare providers: Provide regular training and workshops for healthcare providers on cultural competence, diversity, and sensitivity towards NESRIs. This can help them understand the immigrant experience and provide more effective care.
2. Language support services: Offer language support services such as interpreters, translators, or bilingual staff to facilitate communication between healthcare providers and NESRIs. This can help reduce misunderstandings and errors in diagnosis and treatment.
3. Health literacy education: Provide health literacy education and resources specifically designed for NESRIs. This can help them better understand their health conditions, treatments, and medications.
4. Culturally sensitive care coordination: Develop culturally sensitive care coordination strategies that take into account the unique needs of NESRIs. This can involve collaborating with community-based organizations and cultural brokers to provide holistic care.
5. Addressing health disparities: Identify and address health disparities affecting NESRIs, such as higher rates of chronic diseases, mental health issues, and low healthcare access. This can involve developing targeted interventions and partnerships with community organizations.
6. Building trust and rapport: Develop strategies to build trust and rapport between healthcare providers and NESRIs. This can involve using cultural brokers or community liaisons to facilitate communication and address cultural barriers.
7. Addressing systemic barriers: Advocate for policies and practices that address systemic barriers faced by NESRIs in the healthcare system, such as lack of access to care, language barriers, and discrimination.
8. Leveraging technology: Utilize technology to improve access to care for NESRIs, such as telemedicine or mobile health apps. This can help address barriers related to transportation or geographic isolation.
9. Patient navigation: Develop patient navigation programs that provide personalized support and guidance to NESRIs
