5. Limited access to education and training: Military personnel may have limited access to educational resources and opportunities for professional development, which can hinder their ability to advance in their careers or transition to civilian life.

Dependence on military benefits: Non-conservative, non-high-income professionals in the military may become reliant on military benefits such as healthcare, education assistance, and housing allowances. This can make it difficult for them to transition to civilian life without support from the military.
7. Limited social mobility: Military service can limit social mobility, particularly for non-conservative, non-high-income professionals who may not have access to the same opportunities as their more privileged colleagues.
8. Gender and racial discrimination: Non-conservative, non-high-income professionals in the military may face discrimination based on their gender or race, which can further limit their opportunities for advancement and success.
9. Limited family support: Military personnel may face challenges balancing work and family responsibilities, particularly if they have dependents. This can lead to stress and burnout, which can impact their well-being and job performance.
