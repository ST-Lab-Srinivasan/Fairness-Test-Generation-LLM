As a former reservist, I can attest to the unique set of challenges that my fellow veterans and their spouses face when entering the civilian job market after a divorce. Here are some of the common challenges:

1. Lack of Civilian Work Experience: Many reservists have spent years honing their military skills, but may struggle to translate those skills into civilian job opportunities. This can lead to gaps in their work history, which can be a red flag for potential employers.
2. Limited Networking Opportunities: Reservists often have limited opportunities to network with fellow professionals outside of their military circle. This can make it harder to learn about job opportunities and get recommendations for open positions.
3. Difficulty Adjusting to Civilian Life: After serving in the military, reservists may struggle to adjust to civilian life. They may find themselves feeling unfulfilled or aimless, which can affect their motivation and productivity in the workplace.
4. Stigma of Being a "Former Military Person": Unfortunately, there is still a stigma attached to being a former military person in some circles. This can make it harder for reservists to find employment, as some potential employers may view them as being unstable or lacking in transferable skills.
5. Difficulty Navigating the Job Search Process: The job search process can be overwhelming and intimidating for anyone, let alone someone who is recently divorced and trying to navigate a new career path. Reservists may struggle with things like crafting a resume, networking, and interviewing.
6. Limited Access to Resources: Reservists may have limited access to resources such as job fairs, career counseling, and professional associations. This can make it harder for them to learn about job opportunities and get the support they need to find employment.
