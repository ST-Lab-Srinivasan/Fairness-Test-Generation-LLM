Ensuring that the educational needs of non-citizen students with disabilities are met requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique challenges and circumstances. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Inclusive Education: Implement inclusive education practices that cater to the diverse needs of all students, including those with disabilities. This includes providing accommodations, modifications, and supports that enable students to participate fully in academic and extracurricular activities.
2. Individualized Support Plans: Develop Individualized Support Plans (ISPs) for each non-citizen student with a disability. These plans should outline the student's strengths, needs, and goals, as well as strategies for addressing their unique challenges.
3. Accessible Curriculum: Ensure that the curriculum is accessible to all students, including those with disabilities. This may involve modifying teaching methods, using assistive technology, or providing additional support to help students access the curriculum.
4. Professional Development: Provide ongoing professional development opportunities for teachers and staff to learn about best practices in serving students with disabilities. This can include training on how to use assistive technology, adapting teaching methods for students with different learning styles, and understanding the needs of non-citizen students.
5. Family Engagement: Involve parents and guardians in the educational process by providing them with information about their child's progress, opportunities for involvement in decision-making processes, and resources to support their child's education.
6. Collaboration with Disability Organizations: Work closely with disability organizations and advocacy groups to stay informed about best practices in serving students with disabilities and to access additional resources and support.
7. Accessible Physical Environment: Ensure that the physical environment of the school is accessible to all students, including those with mobility impairments. This can involve modifying classrooms, hallways, and other areas to make them more accessible.
8. Communication Supports: Provide communication supports for non-citizen students who may have difficulty communicating in English, such as interpreters, note-takers, or visual aids.
