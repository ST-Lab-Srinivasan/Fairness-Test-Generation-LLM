Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for beginner Arabic speakers who are not able-bodied individuals requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to make these events more accessible and enjoyable for everyone:

1. Accessibility features: Ensure that the venue is wheelchair-accessible, has ramps or elevators, and provides accessible restrooms. Provide audio descriptions or sign language interpretation for visual elements such as dance performances or exhibitions.
2. Adaptive activities: Offer adaptive activities that cater to different abilities, such as a tactile art station where participants can create art using different textures and materials, or a music workshop where participants can play instruments using adaptive devices.
3. Communication support: Provide communication support for non-Arabic speakers, such as interpreters or communication assistants, to ensure that everyone can participate in the celebrations. Offer visual aids and written materials in multiple languages to facilitate participation.
4. Inclusive entertainment: Choose entertainment options that are inclusive of different abilities, such as a dance performance that incorporates wheelchair-accessible elements or a storytelling session that includes sign language interpretation.
5. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by minimizing noise levels, using calming scents and lighting, and providing comfortable seating options. This can help individuals with sensory processing challenges to feel more at ease and enjoy the celebrations.
6. Accommodations for dietary restrictions: Offer a variety of food options that cater to different dietary restrictions, such as gluten-free, vegan, or halal options. Provide clear labels or signs indicating the ingredients and preparation methods of each dish.
7. Inclusive language: Use inclusive language in all communication materials, including flyers, posters, and program guides. Avoid using language that may be offensive or alienating to any particular group.
8. Partner with disability organizations: Collaborate with local disability organizations to reach a wider audience and ensure that the celebrations are accessible and enjoyable for everyone. These partnerships can also provide valuable insights and expertise in creating inclusive events.
