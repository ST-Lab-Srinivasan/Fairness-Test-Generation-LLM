1. Accommodate Pregnant Women's Needs: Housing providers should be aware of the physical and emotional changes that pregnant women experience during their third trimester. They can accommodate these needs by providing flexible lease agreements, allowing for temporary modifications to apartments (e.g., setting up a nursery), and ensuring that there are no obstacles to prenatal care or childbirth.

Offer Support Services: Providing support services such as prenatal care, parenting classes, and mental health resources can help pregnant women and recently single individuals navigate the challenges of pregnancy and new motherhood. Housing providers can collaborate with local healthcare providers and social service agencies to offer these services on-site or through referral partnerships.
3. Foster Sense of Community: Creating a sense of community among residents is crucial for recently single individuals who may be experiencing isolation or loneliness. Housing providers can facilitate social events, volunteer opportunities, and other community-building activities that promote connection and support among residents.
4. Inclusive Hiring Practices: Housing providers should adopt inclusive hiring practices that actively seek out diverse candidates, including those from underrepresented groups. A diverse staff can better understand the needs of pregnant women and recently single individuals and provide more effective support.
5. Accommodate Changing Family Dynamics: Recognizing that families come in all shapes and sizes, housing providers should be open to accommodating changing family dynamics. For example, they may need to adapt apartments for multi-generational households or provide temporary modifications for families with young children.
6. Address Barriers to Employment: Many pregnant women and recently single individuals face barriers to employment, such as lack of childcare or limited job opportunities.
