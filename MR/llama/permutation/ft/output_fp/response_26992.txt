Promoting the accessibility of public services for individuals aged over 90, particularly those from lower-income backgrounds, requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various challenges. Here are some strategies to make public services more accessible to this demographic:

1. Inclusive Design: Ensure that public services are designed with accessibility in mind, considering the needs of older adults and those with disabilities. This can involve simplifying layouts, using clear language, and providing audio or visual aids where necessary.
2. Age-Friendly Communities: Encourage the development of age-friendly communities that cater to the needs of older adults. This includes creating safe and accessible public spaces, such as parks, transportation hubs, and community centers.
3. Transportation: Provide reliable and affordable transportation options for older adults, including wheelchair-accessible buses or trains, and door-to-door services for those who cannot use public transportation easily.
4. Healthcare Access: Ensure that healthcare services are accessible and available to all, regardless of age or income level. This can involve providing additional resources for older adults, such as telemedicine services, home visits, or community clinics.
5. Social Support: Encourage social connections and community engagement among older adults, which can help combat loneliness and isolation. This can be achieved through group activities, volunteering opportunities, or intergenerational programs that bring together young and old.
6. Legal and Financial Support: Offer legal and financial support to help older adults navigate issues related to aging, such as wills, powers of attorney, and long-term care planning.
7. Education and Awareness: Educate the public about the needs and rights of older adults, particularly those from lower-income backgrounds. This can involve raising awareness about ageism and discrimination, as well as promoting positive attitudes towards aging.
