Ensuring that the voices of beginner English speakers, particularly unmarried individuals, are heard in decision-making processes can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can help:

1. Language support: Provide language support and resources to help beginners improve their English skills, such as language classes, language exchange programs, or online resources. This can help them feel more confident and comfortable expressing themselves in English.
2. Inclusive communication channels: Establish inclusive communication channels that cater to the needs of beginner English speakers. For example, provide interpreters or translation services during meetings or decision-making processes, or use visual aids and simple language to help them understand complex concepts.
3. Building trust: Build trust with beginner English speakers by being approachable, understanding, and respectful. This can involve taking the time to listen to their concerns and ideas, and providing support and guidance when needed.
4. Empowerment through education: Provide educational opportunities and resources that cater to the needs of beginner English speakers. This can help them develop the skills and knowledge they need to participate effectively in decision-making processes.
5. Creating safe spaces: Create safe spaces for beginner English speakers to express themselves without fear of judgment or criticism. This can involve setting clear expectations and guidelines, and providing a supportive and inclusive environment.
6. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair beginner English speakers with more experienced individuals who can provide guidance, support, and language practice. This can help build confidence and improve communication skills.
7. Using technology: Utilize technology to facilitate communication and decision-making processes, such as using video conferencing tools or online collaboration platforms. This can help bridge the gap for beginner English speakers who may face barriers in physical participation.
8. Flexible participation options: Offer flexible participation options for beginner English speakers, such as allowing them to participate in decision-making processes through written communication or providing alternative methods of expression, like drawing or gestures.
9. Addressing power dynamics: Acknowledge and address any power dynamics that may affect the ability of beginner English speakers to participate effectively in decision-making processes. This can involve creating a more equitable distribution of power or providing additional support and resources to help them navigate these dynamics.
