Highlighting the achievements of long-time undocumented individuals who have recently gained legal status is crucial to acknowledge their resilience and determination. Here are some ways to recognize their achievements:

1. Share their stories: Create a platform for them to share their experiences, struggles, and triumphs. This can be through written or spoken word, podcasts, videos, or community events. By sharing their stories, you can humanize their journey and bring attention to the challenges they faced.
2. Celebrate their legal status: Highlight the milestone of their recent legal status and the impact it has on their lives. This can be done through events, celebrations, or simply acknowledging their newfound status in a public setting.
3. Provide resources and support: Offer resources and support to help them navigate the legal system, find housing, access healthcare, and pursue education or job opportunities. This can include connecting them with local organizations that provide these services.
4. Advocate for policies that support undocumented individuals: Use their stories to advocate for policies that support undocumented individuals, such as a pathway to citizenship, protection from deportation, and access to social services.
5. Create a mentorship program: Pair them with mentors who can provide guidance and support in navigating their new status and the challenges they may face. This can include connecting them with successful undocumented individuals who have gone through similar experiences.
6. Host community events: Organize community events that celebrate their achievements and bring attention to the issues they face. This can include cultural festivals, art exhibits, or lectures that highlight their stories and experiences.
7. Provide legal aid: Offer free or low-cost legal services to help them navigate the complexities of the immigration system and ensure their rights are protected.
8. Collaborate with media outlets: Work with local media outlets to share their stories and raise awareness about the challenges faced by undocumented individuals. This can include publishing articles, creating documentaries, or hosting radio shows that highlight their experiences.
9. Create a scholarship fund: Establish a scholarship fund specifically for undocumented students who are seeking higher education. This can help them overcome financial barriers and achieve their academic goals.
