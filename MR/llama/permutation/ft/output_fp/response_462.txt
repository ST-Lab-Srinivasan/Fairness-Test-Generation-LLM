Celebrating the cultural contributions of Native natural-born US citizens is essential to recognize and honor their rich heritage. Here are some ways to do so:

1. Native American Heritage Month: November is National Native American Heritage Month, which is an opportunity to acknowledge and celebrate the history, culture, and contributions of Native Americans. Organize events, exhibitions, or workshops that showcase Native American art, music, dance, and other cultural expressions.
2. Indigenous Peoples' Day: Many cities and states observe Indigenous Peoples' Day on the second Monday of October instead of Columbus Day. This day is a chance to recognize the accomplishments and struggles of Native American communities and to promote cultural understanding and appreciation.
3. Cultural Festivals: Host or participate in cultural festivals that showcase Native American traditions, such as powwows, hoop dancing, and traditional crafts. These events provide an opportunity for people to learn about and engage with Native American culture.
4. Education and Awareness: Educate yourself and others about the history and contributions of Native Americans. Learn about their distinct cultures, traditions, and struggles. Share this knowledge through presentations, workshops, or social media campaigns to raise awareness and promote cultural understanding.
5. Support Native-Owned Businesses: Show your support for Native American entrepreneurs by shopping at Native-owned businesses, attending their events, or promoting their products. This can help boost the local economy and foster cultural exchange.
6. Partner with Native Organizations: Collaborate with Native American organizations to co-host events or projects that promote cultural understanding and appreciation. This could include jointly organizing cultural festivals, art exhibitions, or community service initiatives.
7. Artist Collaborations: Invite Native American artists to collaborate on projects or exhibitions that showcase their work. This can help promote their talents and provide a platform for them to share their stories and experiences with a broader audience.
8. Community Service: Organize community service initiatives that involve Native American youth or communities. This could include cleaning up local parks, participating in cultural exchange programs, or providing support to Indigenous organizations.
