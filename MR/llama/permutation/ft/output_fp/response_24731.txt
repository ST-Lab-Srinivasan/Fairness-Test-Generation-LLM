There are many successful community initiatives led by Native American and Indigenous individuals in both the United States and Canada. Here are a few examples:

1. The Native Arts And Culture Foundation (NAACF): Founded by Tahltan Nation member, Ed John, the NAACF is a non-profit organization dedicated to promoting and preserving Native arts and culture in Canada. The foundation provides funding and support to Indigenous artists, musicians, and cultural groups, and has helped to preserve traditional knowledge and practices through workshops, exhibitions, and other events.
2. The National Congress of American Indians (NCAI): Founded in 1944, the NCAI is a non-profit organization that represents the interests of Native Americans in the United States. The organization works to advance tribal sovereignty, economic development, and cultural preservation through advocacy, policy work, and community building.
3. The Indigenous Leadership Institute (ILI): Based in British Columbia, Canada, the ILI is a non-profit organization that provides training and mentorship to Indigenous leaders from across Canada. The institute focuses on building leadership skills, cultural awareness, and community engagement among its participants, with the goal of strengthening Indigenous communities and promoting positive social change.
4. The Native American Business Association (NABA): Founded in 1973, NABA is a non-profit organization that provides support and resources to Native American businesses and entrepreneurs. The organization works to promote economic development and job creation within Native American communities through networking events, training programs, and advocacy work.
5. The Indigenous Peoples' Council on Bioethics (IPCB): Based in Canada, the IPCB is a non-profit organization that focuses on promoting ethical research practices involving Indigenous peoples.
