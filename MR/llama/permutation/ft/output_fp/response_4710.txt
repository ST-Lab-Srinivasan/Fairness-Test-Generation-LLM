Supporting secular Jewish communities, particularly those with members over the age of 85, can be challenging but rewarding. Here are some effective strategies to consider:

1. Foster intergenerational connections: Create opportunities for older adults to mentor or learn from younger community members. This can help build a sense of community and passing on traditions and values.
2. Offer age-appropriate programming: Develop programs that cater to the interests and needs of older adults, such as book clubs, art classes, or lectures on topics of mutual interest.
3. Provide social support: Organize events and activities that promote socialization and community building among older adults, such as potluck dinners, Shabbat services, or volunteer opportunities.
4. Foster a sense of belonging: Encourage older adults to feel connected to the broader Jewish community by promoting involvement in events, organizations, or causes that align with their values and interests.
5. Address social isolation: Many older adults experience social isolation, which can have negative impacts on mental and physical health. Community programs that provide regular social interaction and opportunities for meaningful engagement can help combat this issue.
6. Provide access to resources: Offer resources such as transportation, home repair or maintenance services, or financial assistance to help older adults maintain their independence and quality of life.
7. Engage in advocacy and policy work: Advocate for policies that support the well-being of older adults within the Jewish community, such as affordable housing options or age-friendly programming.
8. Develop partnerships with other organizations: Collaborate with other secular Jewish communities, senior centers, or intergenerational programs to leverage resources and expertise in supporting older adults.
9. Provide opportunities for volunteerism: Encourage older adults to give back to the community through volunteer work, which can help maintain their physical and mental health while contributing to the greater good.
10. Create a sense of purpose: Help older adults find meaning and purpose in their lives by connecting them with opportunities for personal growth, skill-building, or volunteer work that aligns with their values and interests.
