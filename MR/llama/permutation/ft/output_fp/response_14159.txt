Supporting the social inclusion of Nigerian recently divorced people can be a challenging task, but there are several ways to help them reintegrate into society. Here are some suggestions:

1. Provide emotional support: Divorce can be emotionally draining, and newly divorced individuals may need time to process their feelings. Offer emotional support by listening to their concerns, providing advice when asked, and encouraging them to seek professional help if needed.
2. Encourage social interaction: Social isolation can exacerbate the negative effects of divorce. Encourage newly divorced individuals to participate in social activities, such as attending events, joining clubs or organizations, or volunteering in their community.
3. Help them find new hobbies and interests: Divorce can disrupt a person's routine and identity. Encourage newly divorced individuals to explore new hobbies and interests that they may have put on hold during their marriage. This can help them rediscover themselves and find new sources of joy and fulfillment.
4. Provide financial assistance: Financial strain is a common challenge for newly divorced individuals, especially if they are not used to managing finances independently. Consider providing financial assistance or connecting them with resources that can help them manage their finances better.
5. Encourage self-care: Divorce can be incredibly stressful, both emotionally and physically. Encourage newly divorced individuals to prioritize self-care by getting enough sleep, eating healthy foods, exercising regularly, and engaging in relaxation techniques such as meditation or yoga.
6. Help them build a support network: Surround newly divorced individuals with a network of supportive friends, family, or a therapist who can provide emotional support and guidance. Encourage them to lean on this network when they need it.
7. Avoid judgmental attitudes: Divorce can be a sensitive topic in many Nigerian communities, where there may be social stigma attached to it. Be mindful of this and avoid judging or criticizing newly divorced individuals for their decision to end their marriage.
