Supporting the leadership development of elderly men who are not wealthy or multimillionaires can be done through various means. Here are some suggestions:

1. Mentorship programs: Pair them with experienced leaders who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement. This can help them gain valuable insights and build their confidence in leadership roles.
2. Training and workshops: Organize training sessions or workshops on leadership skills, communication, problem-solving, and decision-making. These sessions can be tailored to meet the specific needs of elderly men who may face unique challenges in leadership positions.
3. Networking opportunities: Provide opportunities for them to connect with other leaders from various backgrounds and industries. This can help them build relationships, share experiences, and learn from others.
4. Coaching: Offer one-on-one coaching sessions with experienced coaches who can help them identify their strengths and areas for improvement, set goals, and develop strategies to achieve those goals.
5. Community involvement: Encourage them to get involved in community service or volunteer work. This can help them develop leadership skills by working on projects that benefit others, and also provide opportunities for social interaction and personal growth.
6. Peer-to-peer learning: Organize peer-to-peer learning sessions where elderly men can share their experiences and learn from each other's perspectives. This can help them build relationships and gain new insights into leadership.
7. Storytelling and reflection: Encourage them to share stories of their personal and professional experiences, and reflect on what they have learned along the way. This can help them develop self-awareness and emotional intelligence, which are essential leadership skills.
8. Technology training: Provide training on technology tools and platforms that can help them stay connected with others, access information, and learn new skills. This can help them adapt to changing times and stay engaged in their communities.
9. Cultural events: Organize cultural events or activities that promote diversity and inclusivity. This can help elderly men broaden their perspectives and understand different cultures and backgrounds.
10. Legacy planning: Help them plan for their legacy by documenting their life stories, values, and experiences. This can help them feel fulfilled
