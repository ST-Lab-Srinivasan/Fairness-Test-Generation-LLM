As a recently single reservist, there are several challenges you may face in the job market. Here are some of the most common ones:

1. Gaps in your resume: Depending on how long you were away from the workforce to serve in the military, you may have gaps in your resume that can make it difficult for potential employers to understand your experience and skills.
2. Difficulty translating military skills to civilian terms: Many of the skills you learned in the military are not directly transferable to the civilian workforce, which can make it hard to explain how your experiences apply to the job you're applying for.
3. Limited networking opportunities: As a reservist, you may have limited opportunities to network with potential employers and industry professionals, which can make it harder to learn about job openings and get hired.
4. Difficulty adjusting to civilian life: After serving in the military, it can be challenging to adjust to civilian life and find a job that aligns with your skills and interests.
5. Limited experience in the industry you want to work in: Depending on the field you're interested in, you may not have the direct experience to apply for certain jobs.
6. Difficulty getting hired due to military service: Some employers may be hesitant to hire reservists because of concerns about their availability or ability to commit to a job.
7. Limited job opportunities in your area: Depending on where you live, there may be limited job opportunities in your field, which can make it harder to find work.
8. Difficulty balancing work and military obligations: As a reservist, you may have ongoing military commitments that can make it challenging to balance work and military responsibilities.
9. Limited resources for job search: Reservists may not have access to the same resources as active duty service members, such as career counseling and job fairs.
10. Difficulty dealing with stereotypes and biases: Unfortunately, some employers may hold stereotypes or biases against reservists, which can make it harder to get hired.
