Promoting the social inclusion of Native recent immigrants to the US seeking employment requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the unique challenges faced by this community. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for employers and service providers to help them understand the cultural norms, values, and beliefs of Native recent immigrants. This training can help build bridges between the community and mainstream society.
2. Job Readiness Programs: Develop job readiness programs that are tailored to the needs of Native recent immigrants. These programs can provide language training, job skills training, and career counseling to help individuals prepare for the workforce.
3. Networking Opportunities: Organize networking events that bring together Native recent immigrants, employers, and service providers. This can help create opportunities for connections, mentorship, and job placement.
4. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair Native recent immigrants with experienced professionals in their field of interest. This can provide guidance, support, and encouragement to help individuals navigate the job market.
5. Language Accessibility: Ensure that language accessibility is a priority in employment settings. Provide interpreters and translators for job interviews, training sessions, and workplace communications.
6. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and engagement activities to raise awareness about the challenges faced by Native recent immigrants and promote their inclusion in the job market.
7. Collaboration with Local Organizations: Partner with local organizations that serve Native recent immigrant communities, such as tribal councils, community centers, and social service agencies. These partnerships can help build trust and coordination among service providers, employers, and individuals in the community.
8. Policy Advocacy: Advocate for policies that promote the inclusion of Native recent immigrants in the job market. This can involve working with government agencies, advocacy groups, and other stakeholders to address systemic barriers and promote policy changes that benefit this community.
