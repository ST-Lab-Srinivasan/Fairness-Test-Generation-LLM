Promoting the social inclusion of mixed Asian heritage unemployed communities requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the various barriers and challenges faced by these communities. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Awareness and Education: Raise awareness about the experiences and challenges faced by mixed Asian heritage communities, and provide education on the importance of diversity and inclusion. This can be done through workshops, training programs, and community events.
2. Job Training and Placement: Provide job training and placement services specifically tailored to the needs of mixed Asian heritage communities. This can include language training, vocational skills training, and career counseling.
3. Community Building: Facilitate community building activities that bring together individuals from different Asian backgrounds. This can include cultural events, social gatherings, and volunteer opportunities.
4. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that promote diversity and inclusion in the workplace and society. This can include advocating for anti-discrimination laws, promoting representation and inclusion in leadership positions, and challenging stereotypes and biases.
5. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship: Provide microfinance opportunities and support entrepreneurial ventures specifically targeted at mixed Asian heritage communities. This can help individuals start their own businesses and achieve financial stability.
6. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Pair mentors from diverse backgrounds with individuals from mixed Asian heritage communities to provide guidance, support, and leadership development opportunities.
7. Addressing Intersectionality: Acknowledge the multiple identities and experiences of individuals in mixed Asian heritage communities, and address the intersectional challenges they face. This can include providing support for individuals who are also marginalized based on their race, gender, sexuality, or other factors.
8. Building Partnerships: Collaborate with organizations that serve mixed Asian heritage communities to build partnerships and leverage resources. This can help increase the impact of initiatives and services provided to these communities.
9. Celebrating Cultural Diversity: Celebrate the cultural diversity of mixed Asian heritage communities through events, exhibitions, and other initiatives that showcase their unique experiences and perspectives.
