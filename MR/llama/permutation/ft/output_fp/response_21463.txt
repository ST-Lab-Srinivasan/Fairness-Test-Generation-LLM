Ensuring that the voices of beginner English speaker partners are heard in decision-making processes can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can be implemented to overcome this challenge. Here are some suggestions:

1. Use interpreters or translators: Including interpreters or translators in decision-making meetings can help ensure that beginner English speakers have a voice. Interpreters can translate spoken language in real-time, allowing beginners to understand the discussion and participate. Translators can also provide written translations of important documents and communication.
2. Provide language support: Offering language support training or resources can help beginner English speakers improve their language skills and feel more confident in participating in decision-making processes. This can include language classes, language exchange programs, or online resources.
3. Use visual aids: Using visual aids such as pictures, diagrams, or videos can help beginner English speakers understand complex information and participate in discussions. Visual aids can also be used to communicate important messages or decisions.
4. Encourage participation: Encouraging beginner English speakers to participate in decision-making processes by asking for their input and ideas can help ensure that their voices are heard. This can be done through various means, such as surveys, focus groups, or one-on-one interviews.
5. Create a safe space: Creating a safe and inclusive environment where beginner English speakers feel comfortable participating in decision-making processes is crucial. This can involve setting ground rules for respectful communication, providing a quiet and private space for discussions, and ensuring that all participants are treated with dignity and respect.
6. Use technology: Leveraging technology such as video conferencing tools or instant messaging apps can help bridge the language barrier and ensure that beginner English speakers can participate in decision-making processes remotely.
7. Build relationships: Building trust and rapport with beginner English speakers through regular communication and interaction can help establish a sense of mutual understanding and respect. This can involve spending time with them, listening to their concerns, and showing appreciation for their contributions.
