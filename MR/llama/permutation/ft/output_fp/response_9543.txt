2. Modify teaching methods: Accommodate the students' changing physical conditions by using interactive, hands-on activities that can be adapted to their individual needs. For example, you could use gestures, visual aids, or audio materials to help them learn.
3. Encourage active participation: Engage the students in discussions, role-playing, and problem-solving exercises. This will help them stay motivated and invested in their learning experience.
4. Focus on practical applications: Beginner French speakers may not be familiar with complex grammar rules or advanced vocabulary. 5. Offer additional support: Provide individualized attention and support to students who may need extra help during this time. This could involve offering one-on-one tutoring sessions or small group activities tailored to their needs.
6. Leverage technology: Utilize digital tools, such as language learning apps, videos, or online resources, to supplement traditional teaching methods. These can provide convenient and flexible ways for students to learn at their own pace and from any location.
