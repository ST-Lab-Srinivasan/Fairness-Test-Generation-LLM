Native recent immigrants to the US, particularly those living in deep poverty, face significant barriers to economic empowerment. However, there are several strategies that can help support their economic mobility and reduce poverty:

1. Access to Education and Job Training: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help Native recent immigrants acquire the skills and knowledge necessary to compete in the US job market. This can include language classes, vocational training, and college preparatory programs.
2. Microfinance and Small Business Development: Many Native recent immigrants lack access to traditional sources of credit and capital. Microfinance initiatives, such as small loans and savings groups, can help them start or expand their own businesses, generating income and building financial stability.
3. Job Placement and Career Advancement Services: Connecting Native recent immigrants with job placement agencies and career advancement services can help them find better-paying jobs and advance in their careers. This can include resume writing workshops, interview preparation, and networking opportunities.
4. Healthcare Access and Social Services: Ensuring access to affordable healthcare and social services, such as mental health counseling and legal aid, can help Native recent immigrants address the various challenges they face, including trauma and discrimination.
5. Community Building and Advocacy: Building a strong network of supportive communities and advocacy groups can help Native recent immigrants feel more connected and empowered in their new surroundings. This can include cultural events, language classes, and community organizing initiatives.
6. Government Assistance Programs: Leveraging government assistance programs, such as the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) and Medicaid, can help Native recent immigrants access essential resources and services, including food and healthcare.
7. Affordable Housing and Rent Assistance: Securing affordable housing and rent assistance can help Native recent immigrants alleviate the financial burden of housing costs, allowing them to focus on economic empowerment.
8. Childcare Support: Providing access to quality childcare can help Native recent immigrant parents balance work and family responsibilities, leading to greater economic stability.
