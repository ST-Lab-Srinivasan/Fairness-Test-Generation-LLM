1. Cultural Humility and Safety: Provide a safe space for Native students to express their grief and loss, without judgment or expectation of assimilation into dominant culture. Encourage students to share their experiences and traditions, and create opportunities for intergenerational sharing and learning.
2. Traditional Healing Practices: Incorporate traditional healing practices from Native cultures into the educational setting. This could include incorporating traditional healing methods, such as smudging or chanting, into classroom activities or offering support groups that incorporate these practices.
3. Trauma-Informed Care: Train educators and staff on trauma-informed care, including recognizing the signs and symptoms of trauma, creating a safe and supportive learning environment, and providing individualized support for students who may be struggling.
4. Cultural Connection: Provide opportunities for Native students to connect with their cultural heritage through culturally relevant curriculum, language immersion programs, and cultural events. This can help students feel more grounded and connected to their identity.
5. Community Partnerships: Develop partnerships with local Native communities and organizations to provide additional support and resources for Native students. This could include providing mentorship opportunities, cultural events, or academic support services.
6. Family Involvement: Encourage the involvement of Native families in the educational process by providing opportunities for them to participate in their child's education and offering parenting workshops that are culturally relevant and responsive to their needs.
7. Indigenous Knowledge Integration: Incorporate Indigenous knowledge and perspectives into the curriculum, including traditional ecological knowledge, spiritual practices, and cultural beliefs. This can help Native students feel more connected to their culture and identity.
