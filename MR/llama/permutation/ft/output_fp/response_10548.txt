Governments and healthcare providers can work together to increase accessibility by providing more affordable healthcare options, improving public transportation infrastructure, or offering telemedicine services.
2. Improve health education: Many lower middle-income individuals may not have access to adequate health education, which can lead to a lack of knowledge about healthy behaviors and disease prevention. Governments and healthcare providers can launch public health campaigns or provide educational resources in local languages to improve health literacy.

Governments and healthcare providers can organize community events or partner with local organizations to promote healthy behaviors and build social connections.
4. Provide financial assistance: Many lower middle-income individuals face financial barriers in accessing healthcare services. Governments and healthcare providers can offer financial assistance programs, such as subsidies or sliding scale fees, to make healthcare more affordable.

Improve health infrastructure: Investing in health infrastructure, such as hospitals, clinics, and health facilities, can improve access to quality healthcare services for lower middle-income individuals. Governments and healthcare providers can work together to build new health facilities or upgrade existing ones to meet the needs of these populations.
