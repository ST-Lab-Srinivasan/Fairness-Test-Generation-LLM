Creating more inclusive public policies for physically disabled Native natural-born US citizen individuals requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the unique challenges and barriers faced by this community. Here are some strategies that can help create a more inclusive environment:

1. Inclusive Policymaking: Ensure that the voices of physically disabled Native individuals are heard in policymaking processes. This can be achieved by incorporating their perspectives and experiences into policy discussions and decision-making.
2. Accessibility Improvements: Provide physical accessibility improvements such as wheelchair ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms in public buildings, transportation, and other infrastructure to ensure equal access to services and opportunities.
3. Education and Awareness: Raise awareness about the experiences and challenges faced by physically disabled Native individuals. This can be done through education programs, media campaigns, and community events that promote disability inclusion.
4. Employment Opportunities: Provide employment opportunities for physically disabled Native individuals in government, businesses, and non-profit organizations. This can include job training programs, mentorship, and accommodations in the workplace.
5. Healthcare Access: Ensure that healthcare services are accessible and inclusive for physically disabled Native individuals. This includes providing appropriate medical equipment, staff training, and culturally sensitive care.
6. Cultural Preservation: Support the preservation of Native cultural practices and traditions that are important to physically disabled individuals. This can include initiatives that promote language revitalization, traditional arts and crafts, and cultural events.
7. Representation in Media: Promote representation of physically disabled Native individuals in media such as film, television, and literature. This can help challenge stereotypes and increase visibility and acceptance of disability in the community.
8. Community Support: Build support networks for physically disabled Native individuals through peer-to-peer mentorship programs, support groups, and advocacy organizations.
9. Legal Protections: Strengthen legal protections for physically disabled Native individuals by enforcing anti-discrimination laws and ensuring that policies are in compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and other disability rights legislation.
