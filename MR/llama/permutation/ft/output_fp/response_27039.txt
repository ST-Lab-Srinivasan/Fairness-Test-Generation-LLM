Addressing the specific needs of Native American recent immigrants to the US in education requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique cultural background, historical trauma, and current social and economic challenges. Here are some strategies that educators and policymakers can use to support these individuals:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Provide training for teachers on culturally responsive teaching practices that acknowledge and respect the cultural backgrounds of Native American students. This can include incorporating traditional teachings, stories, and practices into the classroom curriculum.
2. Addressing historical trauma: Recognize the historical trauma faced by Native American communities and provide support for healing and recovery. This can involve incorporating educational materials that address the impact of colonization, forced assimilation, and other forms of oppression on Native American communities.
3. Language preservation: Support the preservation of Native American languages by providing language classes or hiring bilingual teachers who are fluent in Native American languages. This can help students maintain their cultural identity and communicate with their families and communities.
4. Cultural celebrations and events: Host cultural events and celebrations that honor the traditions and practices of Native American communities. This can help create a sense of belonging and inclusivity for Native American students in the educational setting.
5. Partnership with Native American organizations: Develop partnerships with Native American organizations and community groups to provide additional support services for Native American students. This can include mentorship programs, tutoring, and academic enrichment activities.
6. Culturally relevant curriculum: Develop culturally relevant curriculum that reflects the experiences, histories, and cultural practices of Native American communities. This can help students see themselves represented in the educational material and feel more connected to the learning process.
