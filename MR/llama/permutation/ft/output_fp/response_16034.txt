High-earning individuals without papers (HIEWPs) face significant challenges within the healthcare system. These individuals often encounter barriers when accessing medical care, which can compromise their overall wellbeing and quality of life. Some of the common challenges faced by HIEWPs in the healthcare system include:

1. Lack of Health Insurance: HIEWPs may struggle to obtain health insurance due to their immigration status or income level, leaving them exposed to high medical bills and lacking financial protection.
2. Language Barriers: Many HIEWPs face language barriers, which can hinder their ability to communicate effectively with healthcare providers and understand medical instructions.
3. Limited Access to Healthcare Services: HIEWPs may encounter difficulties in accessing healthcare services, particularly specialized care, due to geographic location or lack of transportation options.
4. Discrimination and Stigma: HIEWPs may experience discrimination and stigma based on their immigration status, leading to feelings of isolation and marginalization within the healthcare system.
5. Lack of Cultural Competency: Healthcare providers may lack cultural competency in serving HIEWPs, resulting in inadequate care and misunderstandings about their health needs and preferences.
6. Difficulty Navigating the Healthcare System: HIEWPs may struggle to navigate complex healthcare systems and processes, leading to frustration and confusion.
7. Limited Access to Preventive Care: HIEWPs may have limited access to preventive care, such as routine check-ups and screenings, which can exacerbate health problems and increase the risk of developing chronic conditions.
8. Difficulty Obtaining Medical Records: HIEWPs may face challenges in obtaining their medical records, which can hinder their ability to receive continuity of care or transfer their care to a new provider.
9. Fear of Immigration Enforcement: HIEWPs may fear immigration enforcement while seeking medical care, leading to avoidance of healthcare services and potential worsening of their health conditions.
