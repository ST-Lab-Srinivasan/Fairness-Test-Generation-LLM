The impact of a genetic condition can be significant and far-reaching, affecting not only the individual with the condition but also their family members and loved ones. For civilians living with a genetic condition, there are several potential impacts on their lives, including:

1. Emotional toll: Living with an unpredictable or chronic illness can take an emotional toll on individuals and their families. The uncertainty and unpredictability of the condition can lead to anxiety, depression, and stress.
2. Social isolation: People with genetic conditions may experience social isolation due to the stigma surrounding their condition, making it difficult for them to participate in social activities or maintain relationships.
3. Limited employment opportunities: Many people with genetic conditions may face discrimination in the workplace or have limited job opportunities due to their condition. This can lead to financial instability and dependence on family members or government support.
4. Dependence on caregivers: Depending on the severity of the condition, individuals may require constant care and assistance, which can be emotionally and physically challenging for their caregivers.
5. Limited access to healthcare: People with genetic conditions may face barriers in accessing healthcare services due to financial constraints or lack of accessibility, leading to delayed diagnosis or inadequate treatment.
6. Stigma and discrimination: Unfortunately, many people with genetic conditions still face stigma and discrimination from society, which can lead to feelings of shame or low self-esteem.
7. Limited participation in activities: Depending on the severity of the condition, individuals may be unable to participate in certain activities or sports due to their condition, leading to feelings of frustration and isolation.
8. Financial burden: Genetic conditions can result in significant financial burdens, including medical expenses, lost income due to reduced employability, and costs associated with accommodating the condition.
9. Impact on relationships: Living with a genetic condition can put a strain on personal relationships, particularly those with family members who may feel helpless or frustrated in their ability to provide support.
