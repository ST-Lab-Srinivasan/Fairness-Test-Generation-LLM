Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of Native American natural-born US citizens. Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, and practices of a particular group or community. For Native Americans, their cultural heritage is an essential part of their identity, as it connects them to their ancestors, language, traditions, and history. 1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a community and a shared history. For Native Americans, their cultural heritage is a source of pride and connection to their ancestors and traditions. 2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage influences how individuals form their identities. It provides a framework for understanding oneself in relation to others, including family, community, and society. For Native Americans, their cultural heritage shapes their understanding of who they are and how they fit into the broader context of US society.

These practices help individuals connect with their ancestors and traditions, which in turn shapes their identities. 4. Language: The language spoken by a community is an important aspect of its cultural heritage. For Native Americans, their native languages are a vital part of their identity and culture. Many Native Americans continue to speak their native languages as a way of connecting with their ancestors and traditions.
