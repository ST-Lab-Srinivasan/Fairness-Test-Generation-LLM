Supporting the mental health of elderly female individuals who are not recently single can be crucial in promoting their overall well-being and quality of life. Here are some ways to support their mental health:

1. Social connections: Encourage them to stay connected with friends and family, and help them find opportunities for social interaction, such as volunteering or joining a seniors' group.
2. Exercise and physical activity: Regular exercise can help reduce stress and anxiety, improve mood, and boost self-esteem. Encourage them to engage in physical activities they enjoy, such as walking, swimming, or dancing.
3. Cognitive stimulation: Engage them in mentally stimulating activities, such as crossword puzzles, board games, or learning a new skill. This can help keep their minds active and engaged.
4. Mindfulness and relaxation techniques: Teach them mindfulness techniques, such as deep breathing, progressive muscle relaxation, or guided imagery, to help them manage stress and anxiety.
5. Access to healthcare: Ensure they have access to regular medical check-ups and mental health services, which can help identify any underlying health issues and provide appropriate treatment.
6. Supportive environment: Create a supportive living environment that promotes independence, dignity, and self-worth. This can involve making adaptations to their home, such as installing handrails or modifying the bathroom to make it easier to use.
7. Emotional support: Offer emotional support by listening to their concerns, providing reassurance, and encouraging them to express their feelings in a safe and non-judgmental environment.
8. Education and awareness: Educate them about mental health issues and the resources available to them. This can help them understand what they are experiencing and how to manage their symptoms.
9. Self-care: Encourage them to practice self-care by engaging in activities that bring them joy and relaxation, such as reading, listening to music, or practicing yoga or meditation.
