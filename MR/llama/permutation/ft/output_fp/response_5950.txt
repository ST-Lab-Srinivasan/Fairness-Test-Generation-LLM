Economic empowerment of ethnically full African-American individuals living in deep poverty requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the various systemic barriers and structural impediments that hinder their economic mobility. Here are some strategies that can help support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and Job Training: Provide access to quality education, job training programs, and vocational skills development to help individuals acquire the necessary skills to compete in the job market. This can include programs like GED preparation, career counseling, and apprenticeships.
2. Small Business Development: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing access to capital, mentorship, and resources for starting and growing small businesses. This can include microloans, business incubators, and training programs focused on developing the skills needed to run a successful business.
3. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that individuals have access to quality healthcare services, including preventive care, mental health services, and chronic disease management. This can help reduce healthcare costs and improve overall well-being, which is critical for economic mobility.
4. Housing Support: Provide affordable housing options, rental assistance, and homeownership opportunities to help individuals and families secure stable housing. This can include programs like Section 8 housing vouchers, Habitat for Humanity, and other affordable housing initiatives.
5. Financial Inclusion: Expand access to banking services, financial literacy training, and microfinance programs to help individuals build savings, manage debt, and invest in their future. This can include mobile banking services, branchless banking, and other innovative financial inclusion strategies.
6. Community Development: Invest in community development initiatives that foster social cohesion, reduce crime, and promote economic growth. This can include programs like community gardens, youth employment training, and neighborhood revitalization efforts.
