Promoting the inclusion of beginner Arabic speakers in casual athletics can be achieved through several strategies. Here are some suggestions:

1. Provide language support: Offer language classes or resources for beginners to improve their Arabic skills. This could include hiring a language coach or providing access to online language learning tools.
2. Create a welcoming environment: Ensure that the athletic community is inclusive and welcoming to all individuals, regardless of their language proficiency. Encourage members to communicate in a variety of languages and to make an effort to include those who may be struggling to communicate in Arabic.
3. Offer visual aids: Utilize visual aids such as pictures or videos to help beginners understand instructions or communications during athletic activities. This can be especially helpful for individuals who are not fluent in Arabic but want to participate in the activity.
4. Simplify instructions: Use simple language and avoid complex instructions that may be difficult for beginners to follow. Provide clear and concise explanations of movements or actions, and offer additional support as needed.
5. Foster a growth mindset: Encourage members to view mistakes as opportunities for learning and improvement. Praise effort and progress, rather than language proficiency. This can help create a supportive and inclusive environment for beginners to participate in casual athletics.
6. Provide opportunities for practice: Offer regular opportunities for beginners to practice their Arabic skills through conversation exchanges or language meetups. This can help build confidence and improve language proficiency.
7. Celebrate diversity: Embrace the diversity of languages and cultures within the athletic community. Highlight the benefits of having members from different linguistic and cultural backgrounds and encourage everyone to share their experiences and traditions.
8. Collaborate with language organizations: Partner with local language schools or organizations that support language learning. They may be able to provide additional resources or support for beginner Arabic speakers in the athletic community.
