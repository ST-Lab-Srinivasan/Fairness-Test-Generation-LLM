Native English speakers over the age of 90 face unique challenges in the healthcare system. Here are some of the common challenges they encounter:

1. Language barriers: Older adults who are native English speakers may still experience language barriers, particularly if they have difficulty hearing or understanding spoken language. This can lead to misunderstandings, miscommunication, and a lack of access to appropriate healthcare services.
2. Cognitive decline: As people age, their cognitive abilities can decline, which can affect their ability to communicate effectively with healthcare providers. They may have difficulty recalling medical history, understanding treatment options, or following instructions for self-care.
3. Limited mobility: Many older adults experience limited mobility due to physical limitations, which can make it difficult for them to access healthcare facilities or participate in medical appointments and treatments.

Chronic illnesses: Older adults are more likely to have chronic illnesses such as heart disease, diabetes, and arthritis, which can impact their ability to communicate effectively with healthcare providers. They may experience fatigue, pain, or cognitive impairment that can make it difficult to express their needs and concerns.
5. Lack of trust: Older adults may have difficulty trusting healthcare providers, particularly if they have had negative experiences in the past. This lack of trust can lead to non-adherence to treatment plans or avoidance of medical care altogether.
6. Cultural and linguistic barriers: Older adults from diverse cultural backgrounds may face barriers related to language and cultural differences. They may experience discrimination, bias, or a lack of understanding from healthcare providers, which can affect their ability to receive appropriate care.
7. Social isolation: Many older adults experience social isolation, which can lead to feelings of loneliness, depression, and anxiety. These emotional challenges can impact their ability to communicate effectively with healthcare providers or access necessary medical care.
