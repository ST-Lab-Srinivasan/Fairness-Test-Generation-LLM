2. Reduce Cost-Sharing: Implement cost-sharing mechanisms, such as deductibles and copays, that are fair and reasonable for all income levels. This can help reduce financial barriers to healthcare access for upper-income individuals.
3. Increase Healthcare Workforce: Boost the number of healthcare professionals, particularly in high-demand specialties such as primary care, pediatrics, and geriatrics. This will ensure that there are enough providers to meet the healthcare needs of all Canadians, including those from upper-income households.
4. Improve Access to Specialized Care: Develop a comprehensive network of specialists and subspecialists across Canada, particularly in regions with limited access to these services. This can help ensure that patients have access to the care they need, regardless of their income level or location.
5. Promote Preventive Care: Encourage upper-income individuals to prioritize preventive care by providing education and resources on the importance of early detection and treatment. This can help prevent costly procedures and hospitalizations down the line.
6. Address Mental Health Needs: Provide mental health services that are accessible and affordable for all Canadians, including those from upper-income households. This can help address the growing demand for mental health care and reduce stigma around seeking help.
