Supporting the economic empowerment of physically disabled average earner individuals requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various barriers and challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Accessibility and Inclusive Design: Ensure that workplaces, public spaces, and products are designed to be accessible and usable by people with disabilities. This includes physical accessibility, as well as digital accessibility for online services and platforms.
2. Education and Training: Provide training and education on disability etiquette, inclusion, and diversity to help non-disabled individuals understand the needs and experiences of disabled individuals. This can help create a more inclusive work environment and society.
3. Job Accommodation and Modification: Offer job accommodations and modifications to enable disabled individuals to perform their jobs effectively. This may include adapting equipment, providing assistive technology, or modifying work processes.
4. Mentorship and Networking: Pair disabled individuals with mentors who can provide guidance and support in navigating professional networks and opportunities. This can help build confidence and connections that can lead to economic empowerment.
5. Financial Incentives: Offer financial incentives, such as tax credits or grants, to disabled individuals who start their own businesses or expand existing ones. This can help level the playing field and provide opportunities for economic growth.
6. Health and Wellness: Provide access to health and wellness programs that address the specific health needs of disabled individuals. This may include physical therapy, mental health services, or nutrition counseling.
7. Legal Protections: Ensure that laws and policies are in place to protect the rights of disabled individuals in the workplace and society. This includes anti-discrimination laws, accessibility standards, and support for legal representation.
8. Community Support: Foster a supportive community that embraces and includes disabled individuals. This can involve creating disability-specific support groups, advocacy organizations, or networking events.
9. Access to Capital: Provide access to capital and funding opportunities for disabled entrepreneurs who may face barriers in accessing traditional sources of financing. This can include grants, loans, or investments specifically targeted at supporting disabled-owned businesses.
