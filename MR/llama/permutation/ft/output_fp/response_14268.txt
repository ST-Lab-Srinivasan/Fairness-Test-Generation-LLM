Ensuring the educational needs of physically able-bodied non-citizen students can be achieved through a variety of strategies. Here are some ways to address their unique challenges:

1. Inclusive Education Policies: Develop and implement inclusive education policies that cater to the diverse needs of all students, including those with disabilities. Provide training for teachers and educators on how to include and support non-citizen students with physical disabilities in the classroom.
2. Accommodations and Modifications: Offer accommodations and modifications to help non-citizen students with physical disabilities access the curriculum and participate in learning activities. Examples include providing extra time to complete assignments, using assistive technology, or breaking down complex tasks into smaller steps.
3. Accessible Learning Environments: Ensure that the school building and classrooms are physically accessible for students with mobility impairments. Install ramps, elevators, and widen doorways to enable easy movement around the school. Provide adaptive equipment, such as wheelchairs or standing desks, as needed.
4. Personalized Support: Offer personalized support to non-citizen students with physical disabilities, taking into account their unique needs and learning styles. This can involve one-on-one instruction, small group tutorials, or collaborative projects that cater to diverse abilities.
5. Interpreters and Translators: Provide interpreters and translators for non-citizen students who are deaf or hard of hearing, or who speak a different language at home. This can help ensure equal access to instructional materials, assignments, and assessments.
6. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach efforts to identify and support non-citizen students with physical disabilities who may be facing barriers to education. Offer resources and referrals for families, such as speech therapy, occupational therapy, or mental health services.
