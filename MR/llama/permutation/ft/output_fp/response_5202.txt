1. Increase public investment in healthcare: The Mexican government should prioritize increased investment in the healthcare system, including funding for primary care clinics, hospitals, and other healthcare facilities in underserved areas. 2. Implement a universal healthcare system: Mexico could consider implementing a universal healthcare system, where everyone has access to healthcare regardless of their income or social status. 3. Provide job training and placement services: Offering job training and placement services can help long-term unemployed people gain the skills and resources they need to find employment and become financially stable. This can help reduce their reliance on the healthcare system and improve their overall well-being.

Addressing these social determinants of health can help improve the health outcomes of long-term unemployed people and reduce their reliance on the healthcare system.
5. These models can also help build trust and rapport between patients and healthcare providers.
