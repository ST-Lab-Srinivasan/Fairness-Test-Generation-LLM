3. Provide resources for professional development: Non-traditionally employed individuals may not have access to the same professional development opportunities as traditional employees. Offer to provide resources for professional development, such as training courses, mentorship programs, or networking events.

5. Respect their boundaries: Non-traditionally employed individuals may have different needs and expectations than traditional employees. Respect their boundaries and avoid pressuring them to work long hours or conform to traditional work norms.
6. Offer mental health support: Many non-traditionally employed individuals face unique challenges, such as financial stress or social isolation. Offer mental health support, such as counseling services or employee assistance programs (EAPs), to help them manage these challenges.
