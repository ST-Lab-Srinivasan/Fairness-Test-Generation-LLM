Addressing the specific needs of Native American recent immigrants to the US requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique cultural, historical, and social experiences. Here are some strategies that can be implemented in education to support these individuals:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers on culturally responsive teaching practices that acknowledge and honor Native American students' cultural backgrounds. This can involve incorporating traditional Native American teachings, stories, and practices into the classroom curriculum.
2. Trauma-informed teaching: Recognize that many Native American students have experienced trauma related to historical events such as forced assimilation, cultural suppression, and violence. Train teachers on trauma-informed teaching strategies to create a safe and supportive learning environment.
3. Indigenous language revitalization: Support the revitalization of Native American languages by incorporating language classes into the school curriculum or partnering with language revitalization programs. This can help students connect with their cultural heritage and improve their language proficiency.
4. Cultural programming: Incorporate cultural events and activities into the school calendar that celebrate Native American traditions and cultures. This can include powwows, cultural festivals, and other community-building events.
5. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair Native American students with older, more experienced individuals from their communities. This can provide a sense of belonging and support for students who may be navigating a new cultural environment.
6. Community partnerships: Develop partnerships with local Native American communities to provide academic and career support services. This can involve collaborations with tribal colleges, universities, and other organizations that serve the Native American population.
7. Access to technology: Provide access to technology for Native American students, including laptops, tablets, and internet connectivity. This can help bridge the digital divide and provide equal access to educational resources.
8. Cultural sensitivity training: Offer cultural sensitivity training for all school staff to help them understand and respect the cultural practices and beliefs of Native American students.
9. Support services: Provide support services such as counseling, tutoring, and academic advising to help Native American students succeed academically and navigate the challenges of college life.
