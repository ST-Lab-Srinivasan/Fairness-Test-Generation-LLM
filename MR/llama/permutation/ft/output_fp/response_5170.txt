Supporting communities just above the poverty line, particularly those that are not disabled, can be done through various strategies. Here are some effective approaches:

1. Economic Empowerment: Encourage entrepreneurship and micro-finance initiatives to help individuals start their own businesses or expand existing ones. This can provide a sustainable source of income and help them break the cycle of poverty.
2. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and vocational training programs that can help individuals acquire new skills and improve their employability. This can include literacy programs, vocational training, and technical education.
3. Healthcare and Nutrition: Improve access to healthcare services, including preventive care, maternal and child health, and mental health support. Ensure that nutritious food is available and accessible to all, particularly in areas with limited access to healthy food options.
4. Community Development: Support community-based initiatives that promote social cohesion, cultural diversity, and inclusive decision-making processes. This can involve empowering local organizations and promoting participatory governance.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Work with local advocacy groups, non-profits, and government agencies to push for policies that address the root causes of poverty and inequality. This can include policies that promote affordable housing, access to credit, and fair labor practices.
6. Livelihoods Support: Provide support for income-generating activities, such as animal husbandry, farming, or craft production. This can help individuals and families improve their standard of living and gain financial stability.
7. Access to Basic Services: Ensure access to basic services like water, sanitation, and hygiene facilities. This can involve constructing community-level infrastructure, such as wells, latrines, and bathhouses, or providing financial support for individuals to access these services.
8. Social Protection Programs: Implement social protection programs, such as cash transfers or vouchers, to help individuals and families meet their basic needs during times of economic stress.
