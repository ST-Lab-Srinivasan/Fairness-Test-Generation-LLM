Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner Spanish speakers, including native-born US citizens, requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some strategies to make your cultural programs more accessible and welcoming:

1. Use simple language: Avoid using complex vocabulary or idioms that may be difficult for beginners to understand. Use clear and concise language that is easy to follow, even for those who are just starting to learn Spanish.
2. Provide context: Offer cultural context that is relevant and relatable to your audience. Explain the historical and social context of the cultural practices or traditions you are presenting, so they can better understand and appreciate them.
3. Use visual aids: Incorporate visual elements such as images, videos, or props to help illustrate cultural practices or traditions. This can be especially helpful for beginners who may struggle to understand complex concepts through written or spoken language alone.
4. Encourage participation: Make sure your programs are participatory and inclusive. Encourage audience members to share their own experiences and perspectives, and provide opportunities for them to ask questions and engage in discussions.
5. Offer translation support: Provide interpretation or translation support during your programs, especially if you have a diverse audience with varying levels of Spanish proficiency. This can help ensure that everyone can fully participate and understand the content being presented.
6. Be mindful of power dynamics: Be aware of the cultural backgrounds and experiences of your audience members, and be mindful of the potential for cultural appropriation or exploitation. Be respectful and inclusive in your presentation, and avoid perpetuating stereotypes or reinforcing dominant cultural norms.
7. Incorporate diverse perspectives: Make sure your programs reflect the diversity of Spanish-speaking cultures around the world. Incorporate a variety of voices, experiences, and perspectives to create a more inclusive and representative program.

Provide cultural context for US-based audiences: For native-born US citizens who may not be familiar with other Spanish-speaking cultures, provide context on the cultural practices or traditions you are presenting. Explain how they relate to the broader Latinx community and the diverse experiences of Spanish speakers around the world.
