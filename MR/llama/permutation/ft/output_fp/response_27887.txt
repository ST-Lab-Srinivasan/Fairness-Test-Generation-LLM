Supporting the economic empowerment of moderate-income Native recent immigrants to the US requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and barriers. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many Native recent immigrants face challenges in accessing financial services due to lack of access to banks, credit unions, or other financial institutions. Non-profit organizations or community groups can provide financial literacy training and help them establish a savings culture.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage and support Native recent immigrants to start their own businesses through mentorship programs, training, and access to funding opportunities. This can help them build assets and create jobs for themselves and others in their community.
3. Job Training and Placement: Provide job training and placement services specifically tailored to the needs of Native recent immigrants. This can include language training, cultural sensitivity training, and job readiness programs.
4. Education and Skills Development: Encourage and support Native recent immigrants to pursue further education and skills development opportunities. This can help them advance in their careers or start new businesses.
5. Networking Opportunities: Organize networking events and workshops that bring together successful entrepreneurs, professionals, and community leaders. This can provide valuable connections and mentorship opportunities for Native recent immigrants.
6. Advocacy and Community Engagement: Advocate for policies and programs that support the economic empowerment of Native recent immigrants. Engage with local communities and organizations to raise awareness about their needs and challenges.
7. Access to Healthcare: Ensure access to affordable and quality healthcare services, which can be a significant barrier for many Native recent immigrants. This can include partnering with local clinics or hospitals to provide free or low-cost medical services.
8. Childcare Support: Provide support for childcare services, which can be a significant challenge for many Native recent immigrants who are balancing work and family responsibilities.
9. Language Support: Offer language classes or interpreters to help Native recent immigrants navigate government agencies, healthcare services, and other systems.
