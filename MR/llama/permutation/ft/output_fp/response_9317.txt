Supporting the social inclusion of beginner Arabic speakers as lifelong singletons can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some ways to promote their social integration:

1. Language exchange programs: Organize language exchange events where beginners can practice speaking with more advanced speakers in a relaxed atmosphere. This will give them the opportunity to interact with native speakers and build confidence in their language skills.
2. Cultural events: Encourage beginner Arabic speakers to participate in cultural events, such as festivals, concerts, or traditional celebrations. These events can provide a platform for them to meet other people and make new friends.
3. Language classes: Sign up for language classes specifically designed for beginners. This will give them the opportunity to learn in a supportive environment and connect with others who share their language learning journey.
4. Online communities: Join online forums or groups dedicated to Arabic language learners. These spaces can provide a platform for beginners to connect with others, ask questions, and get feedback on their progress.
5. Social activities: Organize social events specifically designed for beginner Arabic speakers, such as potlucks, game nights, or movie nights. These activities can help build relationships and promote social interaction in a relaxed setting.
6. Mentorship programs: Connect beginners with more advanced speakers who can act as mentors and provide guidance on language learning and cultural immersion.
7. Language exchange apps: Utilize language exchange apps such as Tandem, HelloTalk or iTalki, where beginners can connect with native Arabic speakers for language exchange and cultural exchange.
8. Cultural immersion programs: Encourage beginner Arabic speakers to participate in cultural immersion programs that provide a comprehensive language learning experience. These programs often include homestays, cultural activities, and language classes.
