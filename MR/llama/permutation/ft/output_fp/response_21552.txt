Promoting the educational success of Native American natural-born US citizens, particularly those who are moderately struggling academically, requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses cultural, social, and economic factors. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Culturally Relevant Curriculum: Develop curricula that reflect the history, culture, and experiences of Native American communities. This can help students see themselves in their education and feel more connected to the material being taught.
2. Indigenous Language Immersion Programs: Introduce language immersion programs that focus on teaching Native languages, such as Ojibwe, Navajo, or Cherokee. These programs have been shown to improve academic performance and language proficiency in Native youth.
3. Mentorship and Cultural Support: Provide students with mentors who are knowledgeable about Native culture and can offer guidance and support throughout their educational journey. This can include traditional elders, community leaders, or trained mentors within the school system.
4. Community-Based Learning: Incorporate community-based learning experiences that allow students to apply academic concepts in real-world settings. This can involve partnerships with local organizations, businesses, and community groups.
5. Social Emotional Learning: Foster social emotional learning opportunities that help students develop skills such as empathy, self-awareness, and problem-solving. These skills are critical for success in both academic and personal settings.
6. Early Intervention Programs: Implement early intervention programs that identify struggling students and provide targeted support before they fall behind academically. This can include summer programs, after-school tutoring, or individualized coaching.
7. Family Engagement: Encourage family engagement in the educational process by providing opportunities for parents to learn about their child's academic progress, participate in school activities, and offer support at home.
8. Access to Technology: Provide students with access to technology, such as laptops or tablets, that can help them stay on pace academically and connect with educational resources outside of the classroom.
