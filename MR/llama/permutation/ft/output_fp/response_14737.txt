Creating more inclusive community events for beginner Spanish speakers can be a rewarding and enriching experience for everyone involved. Here are some suggestions on how to make these events more accessible and enjoyable for middle-class people who are learning Spanish:

1. Language-based activities: Incorporate language-based activities that cater to different levels of proficiency in Spanish. For example, you could have beginner-friendly activities like word games, vocabulary building exercises, and pronunciation practice.
2. Cultural immersion: Offer cultural immersion activities that allow attendees to experience the beauty of Spanish culture. This could include cooking classes, traditional music and dance performances, or even a visit to a local art museum.
3. Accessibility: Ensure that the event is accessible to all attendees by providing amenities such as comfortable seating, adequate lighting, and assistive technology (e.g., audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals).
4. Interactive sessions: Organize interactive sessions where attendees can practice their Spanish skills in a fun and engaging environment. For example, you could have a "conversation circle" where participants take turns practicing their speaking skills with each other.
5. Collaborate with local organizations: Partner with local organizations that cater to the Spanish-speaking community to create a more inclusive event. This could include working with language schools, cultural centers, or community groups.
6. Provide resources: Offer attendees access to additional resources such as language learning apps, podcasts, or online courses to help them continue practicing their Spanish skills outside of the event.
7. Diverse speaker lineup: Ensure that the event features a diverse lineup of speakers who are native Spanish speakers or fluent in Spanish. This can include individuals from different countries and backgrounds to provide a more authentic and immersive experience.
