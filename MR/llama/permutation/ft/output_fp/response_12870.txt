1. Create Inclusive Spaces: Ensure that leadership development programs are inclusive and welcoming for gender-nonconforming poor people by creating spaces that are safe, accessible, and free from discrimination.
2. Use Inclusive Language: Avoid using language that is binary or assumes a specific gender identity. Instead, use language that is inclusive of all genders.
3. Focus on Intersectionality: Understand that gender-nonconforming poor people may face multiple forms of oppression based on their race, ethnicity, sexuality, and other aspects of their identity. Develop leadership development programs that take into account these intersections of oppression.
4. Provide Access to Resources: Ensure that gender-nonconforming poor people have access to the resources they need to participate in leadership development programs, such as childcare, transportation, and financial assistance.
5. Prioritize Experiential Learning: Gender-nonconforming poor people may face barriers to traditional education, so prioritize experiential learning opportunities that allow them to develop leadership skills through hands-on experience.
6. Support Network Building: Help gender-nonconforming poor people build a support network of peers and mentors who can provide emotional and practical support in their leadership journey.
7. Address Systems of Oppression: Develop leadership development programs that actively address systems of oppression, such as racism, sexism, and classism, and provide strategies for resisting and dismantling these systems.
8. Create Safe Spaces for Dialogue: Provide spaces for gender-nonconforming poor people to have open and honest dialogues about their experiences and challenges, and create a culture of mutual support and respect.
9. Foster Community Engagement: Develop leadership development programs that encourage gender-nonconforming poor people to engage with their communities and advocate for social justice issues that affect them.
10. Provide Ongoing Support: Offer ongoing support and mentorship to gender-nonconforming poor people as they continue to develop their leadership skills and work towards social change.
