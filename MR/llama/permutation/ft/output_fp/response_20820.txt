2. Anti-Discrimination Laws: Enact anti-discrimination laws that prohibit discrimination based on marital status, including for long-time married couples. This can help protect them from unequal treatment under the law.
3. Property Rights: Establish clear property rights for long-time married couples, ensuring they have equal ownership and control over their shared assets, regardless of how they acquired them.
4. Inheritance Rights: Provide inheritance rights for long-time married couples, ensuring they are eligible to inherit from each other and have the same inheritance rights as other married couples.
5. Social Security Benefits: Ensure that long-time married couples are eligible for Social Security benefits based on their spouse's work history, just like opposite-sex married couples.
6. Tax Benefits: Provide tax benefits for long-time married couples, such as the filing status or deductions for medical expenses, to recognize and support their commitment to each other.
7. Healthcare Decision Making: Give long-time married couples the right to make medical decisions on behalf of their spouse, ensuring they can provide appropriate care and support in times of need.
