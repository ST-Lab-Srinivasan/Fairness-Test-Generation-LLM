Creating more inclusive community programs for active-duty newly married individuals requires understanding their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies to help make these programs more welcoming and supportive:

1. Inclusive Marketing: Use language and imagery that reflects the diverse backgrounds and experiences of military couples. Avoid making assumptions about their identity or experiences based on their race, gender, or sexual orientation.
2. Accessibility: Ensure that program locations are accessible for individuals with disabilities. Provide accommodations such as wheelchair ramps, Braille signage, and audio descriptions for visually impaired participants.
3. Flexible Scheduling: Offer programs at times that work for both partners in a military couple, taking into account their unique schedules and commitments. Be understanding of the challenges of balancing work, school, and family responsibilities.
4. Childcare Support: Provide on-site childcare or partner with local organizations that offer affordable and high-quality childcare services. This will allow couples to focus on their own personal growth and development without worrying about the needs of their children.
5. Financial Assistance: Offer financial aid or scholarships to help military couples cover program costs, such as tuition, transportation, and childcare. This can be especially important for couples with limited financial resources due to deployment, transfer, or other military-related factors.
6. Virtual Programs: Provide online programs or webinars that can be accessed from anywhere in the world. This will allow military couples to participate regardless of their location or duty station.
