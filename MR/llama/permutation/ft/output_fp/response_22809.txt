2. Career Counseling: Offering career counseling services tailored to the needs of casual Buddhists can help them identify their strengths and interests, explore career options, and develop a plan to achieve their professional goals.
3. Networking Opportunities: Creating opportunities for recent immigrants to connect with professionals in their field can help them build valuable networking connections and learn about job opportunities.

5. Mentorship Programs: Establishing mentorship programs that pair recent immigrants with experienced professionals in their field can provide valuable guidance and support as they navigate their careers.
6. Cultural Awareness Training: Providing cultural awareness training can help recent immigrants better understand the expectations and norms of the US workplace, which can help them navigate professional situations more effectively.
7. Education and Certification: Encouraging recent immigrants to pursue further education or certifications in their field can help them gain a competitive edge in the job market.
8. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship Support: Offering microfinance options or supporting entrepreneurial ventures can help recent immigrants start their own businesses or invest in existing ones, which can be a path to economic stability and success.
9. Legal Services: Providing legal services or connecting them with pro bono lawyers can help recent immigrants navigate the complex US immigration system and understand their rights and responsibilities as workers.
10. Community Building: Creating opportunities for recent immigrants to connect with one another and build a sense of community can provide emotional support and help them feel more connected to their new home.
