Individuals who are not deeply in poverty but are recent immigrants to the US may face a number of challenges when accessing the healthcare system. Some of these challenges include:

1. Language barriers: Many recent immigrants may not speak English fluently, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the healthcare system, understand their medical options, and communicate with healthcare providers.
2. Cultural barriers: Different cultures have different beliefs and practices surrounding health and illness. Recent immigrants may have cultural beliefs or practices that are not aligned with US healthcare norms, which can lead to misunderstandings or conflicts with healthcare providers.
3. Lack of familiarity with the US healthcare system: Newly arrived immigrants may be unfamiliar with the US healthcare system and its structures, such as insurance systems, medical billing processes, and hospital layouts. This can lead to confusion and anxiety when seeking medical care.
4. Limited access to healthcare providers: Depending on their immigration status, recent immigrants may face barriers in accessing healthcare services, including limited availability of culturally competent providers or discrimination based on their immigration status.
5. Cost and financial barriers: Even if they have health insurance, recent immigrants may face financial barriers to accessing care due to high deductibles, copays, or other out-of-pocket costs.
