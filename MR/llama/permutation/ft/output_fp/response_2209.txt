1. Voter Registration Drives: Many Native American communities have low voter registration rates. Organizing voter registration drives specifically targeting these communities can help increase their political participation. Partner with local organizations and community leaders to reach out to eligible voters and provide information on the voting process.

3. Language Accessibility: Native American languages are diverse and complex, and not all recent immigrants may speak English fluently. Providing language accessibility services such as interpreters or translated materials can help ensure that everyone has equal access to information and resources.
4. Community Outreach: Build relationships with local community organizations and leaders to better understand the needs and concerns of Native American recent immigrants. Use this knowledge to tailor your outreach efforts and provide culturally appropriate support.
5. Advocacy and Advocacy Training: Many Native American communities face discrimination and marginalization, which can hinder their ability to advocate for their rights. Providing advocacy training and resources can help recent immigrants build their voices and assert their rights.
6. Voter Turnout Campaigns: Organize campaigns specifically targeting Native American communities to increase voter turnout on election days. Use social media, community events, and other outreach methods to encourage voting and build excitement around the political process.
