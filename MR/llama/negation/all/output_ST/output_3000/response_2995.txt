4. Increased trust: When patients feel that you are culturally competent, they are more likely to trust you and your medical advice. This can lead to better adherence to treatment plans and improved health outcomes.
5. Better collaboration with interpreters: As a beginner Chinese speaker reservist, you may work with interpreters to communicate with patients who speak different languages. Cultural competence can help you navigate these interactions more effectively and build trust with the interpreters.
