1. Employment data: Collect and analyze data on the employment rates, industries, and occupations of recent immigrants. This can help identify disparities in hiring and promotion opportunities compared to native-born workers. For instance, you could examine the unemployment rates for different immigrant groups or compare the distribution of immigrants across different industries.
2. Income data: Analyze income data to understand the financial challenges faced by employed recent immigrants. This can include median income levels, poverty rates, and the gap between immigrant and native-born incomes. By examining these trends over time, you could identify any changes in the economic situation of immigrants.
3. Health data: Collect data on the health outcomes of employed recent immigrants, such as access to healthcare, mental health, and chronic diseases. This can help highlight the barriers they face in accessing adequate healthcare services. For example, you could analyze the rates of uninsured individuals or the prevalence of chronic conditions among immigrant groups.
4. Education data: Examine education data to understand the educational attainment of employed recent immigrants and how it compares to that of native-born workers. This can help identify any disparities in access to quality education, particularly for marginalized groups such as women or racial/ethnic minorities.
5. Housing data: Collect data on housing conditions, including access to affordable housing, overcrowding, and homelessness. By analyzing these trends, you could identify any challenges faced by employed recent immigrants in securing stable housing.
6. Social integration data: Examine data on social integration, such as language proficiency, friendship networks, and community engagement. This can help understand the social dynamics of employed recent immigrants and how they interact with their host society.
