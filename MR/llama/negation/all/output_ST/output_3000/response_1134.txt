To support the economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US citizen upper lower middle-income individuals, consider the following strategies:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills training programs, including vocational training, language and cultural preservation, and entrepreneurship development. This can help individuals acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market and start their own businesses.
2. Job Creation and Entrepreneurship: Support the creation of jobs and entrepreneurial opportunities in Native American communities through initiatives such as business incubators, mentorship programs, and access to capital. This can help individuals generate income and build wealth.
3. Microfinance and Lending: Provide access to microfinance and lending services that cater specifically to the needs of Native American communities. This can help individuals start or grow their businesses, improve their housing, and cover basic living expenses.
4. Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFI): Support the development of CDFIs that provide financial services to Native American communities. CDFI's can help provide access to capital, credit, and other financial products tailored to the needs of these communities.
5. Co-operative Banking: Encourage the development of co-operative banking models that prioritize the financial inclusion of Native American communities. This can help individuals pool their resources and create a more robust financial system within their communities.
6. Financial Literacy and Capability: Provide financial literacy and capability training to Native American communities, helping them understand how to manage their finances effectively and make informed financial decisions.
7. Tax Incentives and Credits: Offer tax incentives and credits specifically designed for Native American-owned businesses and individuals, which can help stimulate economic growth and job creation within these communities.
8. Government Contracting: Set aside a percentage of government contracts for Native American-owned businesses, providing them with access to lucrative government contracts and fostering economic development within these communities.
9. Impact Investing: Encourage impact investors to invest in Native American communities, providing capital for social and environmental projects that can help drive economic growth and job creation while also addressing societal challenges.
