Ensuring that the healthcare needs of natural-born Indian citizen moderate-income people are met requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various aspects of healthcare delivery. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Increase access to primary care: Set up more primary healthcare centers, especially in rural and underserved areas, to provide easy access to basic medical services such as check-ups, vaccinations, and management of chronic diseases.
2. Improve infrastructure: Build or upgrade existing hospitals, clinics, and other healthcare facilities to ensure they have the necessary equipment and resources to provide high-quality care.
3. Train more healthcare professionals: Increase the number of doctors, nurses, and other healthcare workers in underserved areas to improve the delivery of healthcare services.
4. Promote preventive care: Implement programs that encourage preventive care and early detection of diseases, such as cancer screenings, blood pressure checks, and other health check-ups.
5. Foster public-private partnerships: Collaborate with private sector organizations to provide financial support for healthcare initiatives, especially in underserved areas.
6. Improve access to telemedicine: Invest in telemedicine infrastructure and services to enable remote consultations, particularly in rural areas where access to healthcare facilities may be limited.
7. Develop a robust health insurance system: Establish a comprehensive health insurance scheme that covers a wide range of medical procedures and treatments, including those related to chronic diseases.
8. Encourage community involvement: Promote community-based initiatives that encourage people to take an active role in maintaining their own health and the health of their neighbors, such as organizing health camps or promoting healthy lifestyles.
9. Foster a culture of prevention: Implement policies that promote preventive care and early detection of diseases, such as cancer screenings, blood pressure checks, and other health check-ups.
10. Address social determinants of health: Address issues such as poverty, education, and sanitation, which can have a significant impact on people's overall health and wellbeing.
