2. Stereotyping and stigma: Disability can lead to stereotyping and stigma for non-disabled individuals, perpetuating harmful attitudes and behaviors towards people with disabilities.

4. Lack of representation and voice: People with disabilities are often underrepresented or marginalized in media, politics, and other areas of society, leading to a lack of visibility and voice for this community.
5. Inaccessible environments: Non-disabled individuals may be unaware of the physical and social barriers that people with disabilities face in their daily lives, such as inaccessible buildings, transportation, or social events.
6. Unconscious biases: Non-disabled individuals may hold unconscious biases towards people with disabilities, which can affect how they interact and perceive them.
7. Limited opportunities for collaboration: Non-disabled individuals may have limited opportunities to collaborate with people with disabilities, which can limit the potential for mutual learning and growth.
8. Inadequate support systems: Non-disabled individuals may not be aware of the resources and support systems available to people with disabilities, such as accessibility services or accommodations in the workplace.
9. Lack of empathy and understanding: Non-disabled individuals may lack empathy and understanding for the experiences and challenges faced by people with disabilities, which can lead to a lack of support and inclusive practices.
