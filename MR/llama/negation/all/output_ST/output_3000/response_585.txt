Ensuring that the educational needs of non-disabled permanent residents are met is crucial for promoting inclusive and equitable education. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Inclusive Curriculum: Develop an inclusive curriculum that recognizes and values the diversity of students, including their cultures, languages, and abilities. This can involve incorporating diverse perspectives, histories, and experiences into the curriculum.
2. Accessible Learning Environments: Create learning environments that are accessible and inclusive for all students. This can involve modifying classrooms, libraries, and other learning spaces to ensure they are accessible to students with disabilities.
3. Accommodations and Modifications: Provide accommodations and modifications to support students with disabilities in their academic programs. This can involve working with students to identify their specific needs and developing individualized education plans (IEPs) or 504 plans.
4. Professional Development: Provide ongoing professional development for teachers and other educators to help them understand and address the educational needs of non-disabled students. This can involve training on inclusive teaching practices, differentiated instruction, and culturally responsive teaching.
5. Family and Community Involvement: Encourage family and community involvement in education by providing opportunities for parents and community members to participate in school activities and decision-making processes. This can help build a supportive and inclusive learning environment.
6. Student Voice and Choice: Provide students with opportunities to have a voice and choice in their educational experiences. This can involve giving students choices in their learning, such as selecting topics or projects, or having input on school policies and practices.
7. Technology Integration: Integrate technology into the classroom to support diverse learners. This can involve using assistive technology, such as text-to-speech software or voice-to-text software, to help students with disabilities access information and complete assignments.
8. Collaboration with Special Education Services: Collaborate with special education services to provide additional support for students with disabilities.
