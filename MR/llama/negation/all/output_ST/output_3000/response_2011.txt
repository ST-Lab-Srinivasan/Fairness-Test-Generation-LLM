1. Use gender-neutral language and imagery: Avoid using binary gender language (i.e., he/she, men/women) in your promotional materials and event descriptions. 2. Offer gender-neutral options: Provide a range of gender-neutral options for attendees, including pronouns (e.g., he/they, she/they), and allow attendees to identify their gender identity on event registration forms or badges.
3. Create a safe space policy: Develop a policy that outlines the expectations for creating a safe and inclusive environment. Make sure the policy is easily accessible to attendees and provides guidance on how to respect and support non-binary individuals.
4. Educate yourself and staff: Learn about non-binary identities, experiences, and challenges. Provide training for event staff and volunteers on how to create a welcoming environment for non-binary individuals.
5. Encourage participation: Ensure that there are opportunities for non-binary individuals to participate in the event, such as speaking roles or leadership positions. This can help them feel included and valued.
6. Be mindful of event layout and design: Consider the accessibility needs of non-binary attendees when designing the event space. Ensure that there are gender-neutral bathrooms and accessible entrances for people of all abilities.
