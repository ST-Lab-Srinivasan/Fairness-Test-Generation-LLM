Supporting ethnically full African-American communities during the third trimester of pregnancy requires a culturally sensitive and tailored approach. Here are some effective strategies that can help:

1. Culturally competent care: Ensure that your healthcare team is aware of and respects the cultural beliefs, values, and practices of African-American communities. This includes understanding their attitudes towards prenatal care, childbirth, and postpartum care. Provide culturally appropriate education and support to pregnant women and their families.
2. Community outreach and engagement: Engage with local African-American communities through outreach programs, partnerships, and collaborations. This can help build trust and increase access to healthcare services. Use community health workers or cultural brokers who are familiar with the community's cultural practices and beliefs to facilitate outreach efforts.
3. Culturally relevant materials: Develop educational materials that reflect the cultural values and experiences of African-American communities. Use images, videos, and language that are relevant to their cultural background. Provide translated materials in languages such as Spanish, Haitian Creole, and Vietnamese, which are commonly spoken in African-American communities.
4. Traditional birth attendants (TBAs): Involve traditional birth attendants (TBAs) in your prenatal care and childbirth services. TBAs are often trusted members of the community who can provide emotional support and cultural guidance to pregnant women and their families. Provide training and education to TBAs on evidence-based practices and how to work collaboratively with healthcare providers.
5. Home visiting programs: Offer home visiting programs that are tailored to the needs of African-American communities. These programs can provide prenatal care, breastfeeding support, and parenting education in a culturally appropriate setting. Use community health workers or cultural brokers who are familiar with the community's cultural practices and beliefs to facilitate home visits.
