Supporting the economic empowerment of just above poverty line Native recent immigrants to the US requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and barriers. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to education and training programs that enhance their skills and knowledge in areas such as English language proficiency, job readiness, and industry-specific skills. This can be done through partnerships with local community colleges, vocational schools, and non-profit organizations.
2. Job Placement Services: Offer job placement services that connect them with employers who are willing to hire and train individuals from diverse backgrounds. This can include career fairs, job training programs, and mentorship opportunities.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing access to resources such as business plan development, financial literacy training, and networking opportunities with potential investors and partners.
4. Financial Inclusion: Ensure access to affordable and responsible financial services, including bank accounts, loans, and credit. This can help them manage their finances effectively, save money, and build credit history.
5. Health and Wellness: Address the health and wellness needs of this population by providing access to affordable healthcare services, mental health support, and nutrition education.
6. Housing Support: Provide access to affordable and safe housing options, including rental assistance and homeownership programs. This can help reduce their housing-related stress and increase their overall well-being.
7. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training for employers and service providers to help them better understand the needs and experiences of this population. This can improve the quality of services provided and increase job placement opportunities.
8. Community Building: Foster a sense of community among recent immigrants by providing opportunities for socialization, cultural expression, and mutual support. This can help reduce isolation and improve overall well-being.
9. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that address the unique needs of this population, such as immigration reform, language access laws, and anti-discrimination measures.
