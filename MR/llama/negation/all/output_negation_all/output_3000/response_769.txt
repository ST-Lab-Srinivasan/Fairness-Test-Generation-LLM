Creating more inclusive cultural events for individuals in their first trimester of pregnancy, particularly those who are not able-bodied, requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to make these events more accessible and enjoyable for all participants:

1. Accessibility features: Ensure that the event venue is wheelchair-accessible, has elevators or ramps, and provides accessible restrooms. Provide audio descriptions or sign language interpretation for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing.
2. Flexible seating: Offer flexible seating options, such as cushions or chairs with armrests, to accommodate different mobility needs. Consider having a designated quiet area for individuals who may need a break from the noise and activity.
3. Accommodating schedules: Be mindful of the first trimester schedule, which can be unpredictable due to pregnancy symptoms. Offer flexible timing or multiple event dates to ensure that everyone can participate.
4. Moderated activities: Plan activities that are moderate in intensity and do not exacerbate pregnancy discomforts, such as light exercise classes or art workshops with gentle stretching. Consider providing modifications or alternatives for individuals who may need them.
5. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by minimizing loud noises, bright lights, and strong smells. Offer fidget toys or other sensory tools for individuals who may benefit from them.
6. Communication support: Provide communication support, such as American Sign Language (ASL) interpreters or written materials in braille, for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing or have other communication needs.
7. Respite areas: Offer respite areas with comfortable seating and adequate space for individuals who may need a break from the event's activities.
8. Partner support: Encourage partners or caregivers to attend the events alongside the expectant mothers, providing them with their own agenda and opportunities for socialization.
9. Private spaces: Provide private spaces for breastfeeding or pumping, if needed, to ensure comfort and convenience for all attendees.
