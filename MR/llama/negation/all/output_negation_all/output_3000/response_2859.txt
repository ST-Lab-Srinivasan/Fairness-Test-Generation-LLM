1. Support tribal sovereignty and self-determination: Recognize the inherent rights of Native American tribes to govern themselves and make decisions about their own cultural preservation. Support policies that uphold tribal sovereignty and self-determination, such as the Indian Reorganization Act of 1934.

3. Encourage cultural exchange: Facilitate cultural exchange programs between Native American communities and other groups, such as schools, community organizations, and cultural institutions. These programs can help promote mutual understanding and respect for each other's cultures.
4. Support Indigenous-led initiatives: Prioritize the involvement of Native American communities in the decision-making process around cultural preservation. Provide resources and support to Indigenous-led organizations and initiatives that are working to preserve their cultural heritage.
5. Address historical trauma: Recognize the impact of historical trauma on Native American communities and work towards healing and reconciliation. This can involve providing mental health services, supporting cultural revitalization programs, and advocating for policies that address the root causes of historical trauma.
6. Protect cultural sites and artifacts: Advocate for the protection of cultural sites and artifacts that are important to Native American communities. This can involve supporting the creation of cultural preservation districts, protecting sacred sites from development, and advocating for the return of cultural artifacts that have been taken without permission.
