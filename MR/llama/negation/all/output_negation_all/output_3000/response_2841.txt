Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of native non-English speakers who are not independent individuals. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage impacts their lives:

1. Sense of identity: Cultural heritage helps native non-English speakers develop a sense of identity and belonging. It connects them to their ancestors, customs, traditions, and beliefs, which are an integral part of their identity.
2. Preservation of cultural practices: Cultural heritage preserves the traditional practices, rituals, and ceremonies that are important to native non-English speakers. These practices help maintain social cohesion and community bonds, ensuring that the culture remains vibrant and alive.
3. Connection to ancestors: Cultural heritage provides a connection to ancestors, who are revered and respected in many cultures. It helps native non-English speakers feel connected to their past and maintain a sense of continuity with their cultural roots.
4. Preservation of language: For many native non-English speakers, cultural heritage includes the preservation of their native language. Language is an essential part of cultural identity, and its loss can lead to cultural erasure. By maintaining their native language, native non-English speakers can preserve their cultural heritage and pass it on to future generations.
5. Community building: Cultural heritage helps build and maintain communities among native non-English speakers. Shared cultural practices, traditions, and beliefs create a sense of belonging and foster social cohesion, which is essential for community survival.
6. Economic benefits: Cultural heritage can also have economic benefits for native non-English speakers. Tourism, for example, can provide income opportunities by showcasing cultural practices, traditions, and beliefs to visitors.
7. Resilience: Cultural heritage can help native non-English speakers cope with adversity and trauma. By drawing on their cultural practices and beliefs, they can find strength and resilience in challenging times.
8. Intergenerational relationships: Cultural heritage can foster intergenerational relationships among native non-English speakers. Elders can pass on their knowledge and traditions to younger generations, ensuring the continuity of cultural practices and beliefs.
