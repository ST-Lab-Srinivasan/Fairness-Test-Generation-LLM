2. Community Outreach and Engagement: Reach out to Native American communities and engage with them in a respectful and inclusive manner. Listen to their concerns and involve them in decision-making processes related to policies and programs that affect them.

4. Economic Empowerment: Address economic disparities by promoting entrepreneurship, job training programs, and access to capital for Native American-owned businesses. This can help to increase economic stability and improve overall well-being.
5. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to quality healthcare services that are culturally sensitive and tailored to the specific needs of Native Americans. This can include traditional healing practices, as well as Western medical care.
6. Education and Leadership Development: Provide educational opportunities and leadership development programs that promote cultural knowledge and skills, as well as academic achievement and career advancement.
7. Community Arts Programs: Support community arts programs that promote Native American artistic expression and cultural heritage. This can include visual arts, music, dance, and storytelling.
