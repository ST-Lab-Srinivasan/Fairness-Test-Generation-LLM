As a society, there are several steps we can take to ensure that the healthcare needs of older adults (those aged 65 and older) are met:

1. Invest in preventive care: Encourage older adults to prioritize preventive care, such as regular check-ups, screenings, and vaccinations. This can help identify health issues early on and prevent more serious problems from developing.
2. Provide access to affordable healthcare: Ensure that older adults have access to affordable healthcare options, including Medicare, Medicaid, and other government-funded programs. Additionally, many organizations offer discounts or free services for older adults.
3. Train healthcare providers in geriatric care: Provide training and resources for healthcare providers on how to diagnose and treat older adults, including those with chronic conditions or disabilities.
4. Promote evidence-based treatment: Encourage the use of evidence-based treatments and interventions that have been proven to be effective in improving the health and well-being of older adults.
5. Address social determinants of health: Identify and address the social determinants of health that can impact older adults, such as poverty, lack of access to transportation, and social isolation.
6. Encourage active aging: Encourage older adults to maintain an active lifestyle through physical activity, cognitive stimulation, and social engagement.
7. Support caregivers: Provide support and resources for caregivers of older adults, including training and respite services, to help them manage the demands of caring for a loved one.
8. Improve accessibility: Ensure that healthcare facilities and services are accessible and easy to navigate for older adults, including those with mobility or sensory impairments.
9. Encourage interdisciplinary care: Encourage healthcare providers to work together in an interdisciplinary approach to provide comprehensive care for older adults, including medical, social, and psychological support.
