Supporting the social inclusion of beginner Arabic speakers who are not independent individuals can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Provide language classes specifically tailored for beginners: Offering language classes that cater to the needs and abilities of beginner Arabic speakers can help them build confidence in their language skills. These classes can provide a supportive environment where learners can practice speaking, listening, reading, and writing Arabic.
2. Encourage socialization through language exchange programs: Language exchange programs pair beginner Arabic speakers with more proficient speakers or native Arabic speakers. This helps beginners practice their language skills in a relaxed setting while also fostering cross-cultural understanding and friendship.
3. Use visual aids and multimedia resources: Utilizing visual aids such as pictures, videos, and flashcards can help beginner Arabic speakers learn new vocabulary and grammar structures. Additionally, using multimedia resources like podcasts, audiobooks, and online courses can provide a variety of learning experiences and make language learning more engaging.
4. Create a supportive community: Building a community of beginners who are learning Arabic can help them feel more connected and motivated to learn. This can be done through regular meetups, social events, or online forums where learners can share their progress, ask questions, and receive feedback from peers and mentors.
5. Offer cultural immersion opportunities: Immersing beginner Arabic speakers in a Arabic-speaking environment can help them improve their language skills more quickly. This could involve participating in homestays with native Arabic speakers, attending cultural events, or volunteering in organizations that promote cross-cultural understanding.
6. Provide opportunities for interactive learning: Interactive learning experiences such as role-playing, group discussions, and problem-solving activities can help beginner Arabic speakers develop their language skills in a more engaging and practical way.
