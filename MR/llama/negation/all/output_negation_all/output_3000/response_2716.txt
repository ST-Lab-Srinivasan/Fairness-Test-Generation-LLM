To ensure that the educational needs of non-elderly, non-female students are met, institutions of learning can take several steps:

1. Inclusive Curriculum: Develop a curriculum that caters to the diverse needs of all students, regardless of their gender or age. This includes incorporating topics and resources that address the unique challenges faced by non-elderly, non-female students.
2. Flexible Learning Options: Offer flexible learning options such as online courses, part-time programs, or evening classes to accommodate the schedules of working students. This can help reduce the burden of juggling work and study responsibilities.
3. Access to Resources: Provide access to resources such as libraries, computer labs, and other facilities that can support their academic success. Offer additional resources such as tutoring services, mentorship programs, or peer support groups to help them succeed academically.
4. Safe Learning Environments: Create safe and inclusive learning environments that promote respect and empathy towards all students, regardless of their gender or age. Encourage open communication and address any incidents of discrimination or harassment promptly.
5. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair non-elderly, non-female students with experienced academics or professionals who can provide guidance, support, and networking opportunities. This can help them navigate their academic journey and build meaningful connections.
6. Career Guidance: Offer career guidance and counseling services to help non-elderly, non-female students explore their career options and develop a plan for their future success. This can include job shadowing, internship opportunities, or workshops on professional development.
7. Accommodations: Provide reasonable accommodations for non-elderly, non-female students with disabilities or other challenges that may impact their ability to succeed academically. Ensure that these accommodations are tailored to meet their individual needs and provide equal opportunities for success.
8. Student Feedback: Regularly solicit feedback from non-elderly, non-female students to understand their unique challenges and preferences. Use this feedback to inform curriculum
