Supporting lower middle-income physically disabled communities requires a comprehensive approach that addresses various aspects of their lives, including accessibility, education, employment, and social inclusion. Here are some effective strategies that can be implemented:

1. Accessibility improvements: Ensure that public spaces, buildings, and transportation are accessible to people with disabilities. This includes installing ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms, as well as improving sidewalks and crossings.
2. Education and training: Provide training and education on disability awareness, inclusion, and accessibility for teachers, students, and employers. This can help create a more inclusive environment and reduce stigma around disabilities.
3. Employment opportunities: Encourage employers to hire people with disabilities and provide reasonable accommodations in the workplace. This can help increase their economic independence and social inclusion.
4. Healthcare services: Ensure that lower middle-income physically disabled communities have access to quality healthcare services, including rehabilitation and mental health support.
5. Social inclusion programs: Implement programs that promote social inclusion, such as sports and recreational activities, cultural events, and community service projects. This can help build relationships and foster a sense of belonging among people with disabilities.
6. Advocacy and advocacy training: Provide training and support to individuals with disabilities to advocate for their rights and interests, both within their communities and at the policy level.
7. Assistive technology: Provide access to assistive technology devices, such as wheelchairs, prosthetics, and communication aids, to help people with disabilities participate fully in society.
8. Financial support: Offer financial assistance, such as grants or scholarships, to individuals with disabilities to help them pursue education or career opportunities.
9. Community-based rehabilitation: Establish community-based rehabilitation programs that provide therapy and support services in a non-clinical setting, such as in the individual's home or community.
10. Disability inclusion policies: Develop and implement disability inclusion policies at the local, national, and international levels to promote equal rights and opportunities for people with disabilities.
