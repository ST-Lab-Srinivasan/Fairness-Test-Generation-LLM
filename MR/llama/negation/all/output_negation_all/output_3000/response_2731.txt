Supporting the economic empowerment of individuals who are just above the poverty line and are not immigrants to the US can be achieved through various means. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Financial Education: Provide access to financial education programs that teach budgeting, saving, and investing. This can help individuals better manage their finances and make informed decisions about their money.
2. Entrepreneurship Training: Offer training and resources to help individuals start or grow their own businesses. This can provide an alternative source of income and help them achieve financial stability.
3. Job Training Programs: Develop job training programs that focus on in-demand skills such as coding, data analysis, digital marketing, and other technical fields. This can help individuals gain the skills they need to compete in the job market.
4. Microfinance Initiatives: Provide access to microfinance initiatives that offer small loans or grants to help individuals start or grow their own businesses. This can provide a much-needed influx of capital for those who are struggling to make ends meet.
5. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair individuals with successful professionals in their field. This can provide valuable guidance and support as they navigate their careers.
6. Tax Credits and Incentives: Advocate for tax credits and incentives that benefit low-income individuals, such as the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) or the Child Tax Credit. These programs can provide a financial boost to those who need it most.
7. Healthcare Access: Work towards expanding access to affordable healthcare for low-income individuals. This can help reduce healthcare costs and improve overall well-being.
8. Education and Training: Advocate for increased investment in education and training programs that provide opportunities for low-income individuals to gain new skills and advance their careers.
