5. Lack of support: The military may not provide adequate support for genderqueer individuals, including training for supervisors and colleagues on how to address genderqueer issues or providing appropriate accommodations.
6. Stigma and discrimination: Genderqueer individuals in the military may face stigma and discrimination from their peers and superiors, which can create a hostile work environment and impact their mental health and well-being.
7. Difficulty navigating bureaucracy: Genderqueer individuals may encounter difficulties when trying to navigate the military's bureaucratic processes, such as accessing benefits or making changes to their military records.
8. Lack of representation: Genderqueer individuals may not have access to legal representation that is knowledgeable about their rights and needs, which can make it difficult for them to access justice in cases of discrimination or harassment.
