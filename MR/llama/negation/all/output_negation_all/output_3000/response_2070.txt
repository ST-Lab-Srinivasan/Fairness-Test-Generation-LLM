Creating more inclusive community events for non-civilian, non-wealthy individuals requires intentional planning and consideration of their needs. Here are some strategies to make your events more accessible and welcoming:

1. Focus on diversity: Make sure your event invitation and promotion materials are inclusive and welcoming to people of all backgrounds, cultures, and abilities. Consider using language that is respectful and inclusive.
2. Offer free or low-cost tickets: To make your event more accessible to a broader range of people, consider offering free or low-cost tickets to those who may not have the financial means to attend. This can include students, seniors, and other groups that may struggle to afford events.
3. Provide accessible venues: Ensure that the venue you choose is wheelchair accessible and has amenities such as gender-neutral restrooms, Braille signage, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
4. Offer accommodations for people with disabilities: Make sure your event provides accommodations for people with disabilities, such as American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation, wheelchair accessibility, and assistive listening devices.
5. Promote your event through multiple channels: Reach a wider audience by promoting your event through multiple channels, including social media, email marketing, local newspapers, and community newsletters.
6. Offer childcare and transportation options: Provide childcare services or transportation assistance to help attendees who may need it. This can include parents with young children or individuals who do not have access to reliable transportation.
7. Involve the local community: Encourage community members to get involved in planning and hosting your event. This can help build a sense of ownership and inclusivity, and ensure that the event reflects the needs and interests of the local community.
8. Provide language support: Offer interpretation or translation services for attendees who may not speak the dominant language of the area. This can include providing translated materials and interpreters for ASL, as well as offering multilingual staff at the event.
