Promoting the economic empowerment of non-independent natural-born US citizen communities is essential for creating a more equitable and inclusive society. Here are some strategies that can help achieve this goal:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills development programs, particularly in underserved communities. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and higher education opportunities.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Offer resources and support for entrepreneurs from underrepresented groups, such as access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities. This can help create jobs and economic growth in these communities.
3. Job Training and Placement: Provide job training and placement services to help individuals from marginalized groups gain employment in high-growth industries. This can include apprenticeships, internships, and on-the-job training programs.
4. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFIs): Support the development of CDFIs, which provide loans and other financial services to underserved communities. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities in these areas.
5. Co-operative Business Models: Promote co-operative business models, where members own and control the business, and profits are shared equitably among members. This can help create a more inclusive and equitable economy.
6. Community Land Trusts: Support the development of community land trusts, which allow communities to retain ownership of land while providing affordable housing and economic opportunities.
7. Public-Private Partnerships: Foster public-private partnerships to provide funding and support for community development initiatives, such as infrastructure projects, small business incubators, and job training programs.
8. Impact Investing: Encourage impact investors to invest in communities with high social and environmental needs, such as low-income neighborhoods, to create jobs and economic opportunities.
