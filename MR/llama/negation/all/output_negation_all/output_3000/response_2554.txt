Disability status can have significant impacts on the lives of non-disabled people as well. Here are some potential effects:

1. Social isolation: Non-disabled people with limited social interactions may experience social isolation, which can lead to feelings of loneliness, depression, and anxiety.
2. Stigma and discrimination: Disability status can perpetuate negative attitudes and stereotypes towards non-disabled people, leading to discrimination and stigma in various areas of life, such as education, employment, and social interactions.
3. Limited access to resources: Non-disabled people may face barriers when accessing resources such as healthcare, education, and employment opportunities due to their disability status.
4. Lack of representation and voice: Disabled individuals may have limited representation and voice in society, leading to a lack of understanding and consideration for their needs and experiences.
5. Internalized oppression: Non-disabled people may internalize societal messages that their disability status is a deficiency or flaw, leading to feelings of shame, inadequacy, and low self-esteem.
6. Health disparities: Disabled individuals may experience health disparities due to limited access to healthcare services, lack of accommodations, and societal factors that contribute to poor health outcomes.
7. Economic inequality: Non-disabled people with limited economic opportunities may face financial insecurity and poverty, which can further exacerbate existing social and economic inequalities.
8. Limited civic participation: Disabled individuals may encounter barriers when participating in civic activities such as voting, volunteering, or advocating for policies that affect their lives.
9. Lack of access to education and employment: Non-disabled people may face discrimination and barriers when accessing education and employment opportunities due to their disability status.
10. Invisible disabilities: Disabled individuals with invisible disabilities, such as chronic illnesses or mental health conditions, may face additional challenges in being recognized and accommodated due to the lack of visibility and understanding of their condition.
