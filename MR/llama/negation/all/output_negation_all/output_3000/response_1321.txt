Ensuring that the voices of native non-English speakers are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for creating a more inclusive and equitable society. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Language Accessibility: Provide language accessibility services such as interpreters, translators, and bilingual staff to ensure that non-English speakers have equal access to information and decision-making processes.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training for decision-makers to better understand the perspectives and needs of diverse communities, including native non-English speakers.
3. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and engagement efforts to connect with native non-English speakers and gather their input on issues that affect them. This can be done through community events, surveys, focus groups, or other forms of outreach.
4. Collaborative Decision-Making: Involve native non-English speakers in decision-making processes through collaborative approaches such as participatory budgeting, community planning, or co-design. This can help ensure that their voices are heard and valued in the decision-making process.
5. Technology Solutions: Leverage technology solutions such as online surveys, social media platforms, or mobile apps to reach a wider audience of native non-English speakers and gather their input on issues that affect them.
6. Partnerships with Community Organizations: Collaborate with community organizations that work with native non-English speakers to gather their input and feedback on decision-making processes. These organizations can help bridge the gap between decision-makers and the communities they serve.
7. Public Education Campaigns: Launch public education campaigns to raise awareness about the importance of including native non-English speakers in decision-making processes and provide resources for individuals who may need language accessibility services.
8. Policy Reforms: Advocate for policy reforms that promote language accessibility and cultural competency in government decision-making processes. This can include policies that require decision-makers to provide language accessibility services or that prioritize the input of diverse communities in decision-making processes.
9. Accountability Mechanisms: Establish accountability mechanisms to ensure that decision-makers are held responsible for including native non-English speakers
