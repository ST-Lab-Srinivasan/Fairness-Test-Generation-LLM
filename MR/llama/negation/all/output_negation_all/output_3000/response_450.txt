For native Arabic speakers, their cultural heritage is an integral part of their identity, and it helps them feel connected to their roots and community.
2. Shaping values and beliefs: Cultural heritage influences the values, beliefs, and practices of individuals. Native Arabic speakers are likely to have a strong sense of loyalty, hospitality, and respect for elders, which are core values in many Arab cultures. These values are shaped by their cultural heritage and are an essential part of their identity.
3. Language and communication: The Arabic language is an essential part of the cultural heritage of native speakers. These traditions are an integral part of their cultural heritage and shape their identities and experiences.
