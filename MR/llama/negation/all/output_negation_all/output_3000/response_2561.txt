Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for non-traditional or non-conservative individuals requires understanding and valuing diversity. Here are some suggestions to make cultural celebrations more inclusive:

1. Involve diverse perspectives: Involve a diverse group of people in the planning process to ensure that everyone's voice is heard. This can include individuals from different racial, ethnic, religious, and cultural backgrounds.
2. Be mindful of language and symbols: Avoid using language or symbols that may be offensive or alienating to certain groups. For example, avoid using gendered language or symbols that reinforce binary gender norms.
3. Foster a sense of belonging: Create an environment where everyone feels welcome and included. Encourage participants to share their experiences and perspectives, and provide opportunities for people to connect with each other.
4. Provide accommodations: Offer accommodations that cater to the needs of diverse individuals, such as sign language interpreters, braille materials, or gender-neutral bathrooms.
5. Avoid cultural appropriation: Be mindful of cultural practices and traditions that are not your own. Avoid using them in a way that is disrespectful or exploitative. Instead, learn from and appreciate the cultural practices of others.
6. Be open to feedback: Be receptive to feedback from participants about how they feel included or excluded during cultural celebrations. Use this feedback to make adjustments and improvements for future events.
7. Celebrate diversity: Embrace the diversity of the individuals involved in the cultural celebration, and highlight the unique perspectives and experiences that each person brings.
8. Foster inclusive spaces: Create physical spaces that are accessible and inclusive to all participants. For example, provide seating options for people with disabilities or create a quiet space for individuals who may need it.
9. Offer diverse food options: Provide a variety of food options that cater to different dietary needs and preferences, such as vegetarian, gluten-free, or halal options.
