1. The Native American Chamber of Commerce: This organization is dedicated to promoting economic development and entrepreneurship in Native American communities. Founded in 2003, the chamber has over 200 member tribes and businesses, and provides training, networking opportunities, and advocacy for its members.
2. The Indigenous Business Association: Based in Seattle, this organization supports indigenous-owned businesses by providing access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities. They also work to promote cultural preservation and economic development in Native American communities.
3. The National Congress of American Indians: This organization is dedicated to advancing the rights and interests of Native Americans through advocacy, policy development, and community building. With over 500 member tribes, the NCAI works to address issues such as tribal sovereignty, education, healthcare, and economic development.

The Native American Youth & Leadership Program: This organization provides leadership development and mentorship opportunities for young Native Americans, with a focus on building cultural identity, personal growth, and community engagement. 5. The Indigenous Roots Institute: Based in Oakland, California, this organization works to promote indigenous-led environmental justice and sustainability initiatives. They provide training, technical assistance, and capacity building for indigenous-led groups working on issues such as climate change, renewable energy, and food sovereignty.
6. The Native American Land Conservancy: This organization works to protect and restore Native American lands and cultural resources through conservation and preservation efforts. They have protected over 100,000 acres of land in the Great Lakes region, and work closely with tribal communities to ensure that their cultural heritage is preserved for future generations.
