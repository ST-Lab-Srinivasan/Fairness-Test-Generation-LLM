Creating more inclusive public policies for non-resident and non-temporary visa holders requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the specific needs and challenges faced by these individuals. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive policies:

1. Consultation and Engagement: Engage with non-resident and non-temporary visa holders through consultations and surveys to understand their experiences, challenges, and suggestions for improving policies. This will help policymakers identify the specific needs of these individuals and tailor policies accordingly.
2. Access to Information: Provide easy access to information and resources on public policies, programs, and services available to non-resident and non-temporary visa holders. This can be achieved through multilingual websites, social media platforms, and community centers.
3. Financial Support: Offer financial support to non-resident and non-temporary visa holders who face economic challenges, such as low-interest loans, grants, or scholarships. This can help them cover living expenses, education, and healthcare costs.
4. Employment Opportunities: Encourage employment opportunities for non-resident and non-temporary visa holders by providing work permits, visas, and access to job training programs. This can help them gain financial stability and integrate into the local economy.
5. Healthcare Access: Ensure that non-resident and non-temporary visa holders have access to affordable healthcare services through public or private providers. This can include subsidies, insurance programs, or referral networks.
6. Education and Training: Provide educational and training opportunities for non-resident and non-temporary visa holders, such as language classes, vocational training, or academic programs. This can help them acquire new skills and knowledge to enhance their employability.
7. Social Inclusion: Encourage social inclusion by promoting cultural events, community activities, and volunteer opportunities for non-resident and non-temporary visa holders. This can help build connections between these individuals and the local community.
