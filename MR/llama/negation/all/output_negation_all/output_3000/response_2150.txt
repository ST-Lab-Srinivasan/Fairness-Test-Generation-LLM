Ensuring the voices of native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting inclusivity and equity. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training to decision-makers and organizations to recognize and value the perspectives of native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants. This training can help them understand the unique experiences, challenges, and opportunities faced by these groups.
2. Community Engagement: Foster community engagement and participation in decision-making processes through mechanisms such as citizen juries, focus groups, and town hall meetings. Encourage native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants to share their thoughts and ideas on policies and programs that affect them directly.
3. Advocacy Groups: Support advocacy groups that represent the interests of native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants. These groups can mobilize communities, provide a platform for voice, and influence policy decisions at local and national levels.
4. Language Accessibility: Ensure language accessibility in decision-making processes by providing translation services, interpreters, and bilingual materials. This can help remove barriers to participation and ensure that everyone has equal opportunities to participate.
5. Education and Awareness: Educate decision-makers and the general public about the experiences and challenges faced by native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants. Raise awareness about the importance of inclusivity and equity in decision-making processes.
6. Leadership Opportunities: Create leadership opportunities for native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants within organizations, community groups, and decision-making bodies. This can help build their confidence and voice in shaping policies and programs that affect them.
7. Collaborative Governance: Adopt collaborative governance models that involve native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants in the decision-making process. This can help create a more inclusive and participatory environment, where diverse perspectives are valued and respected.
