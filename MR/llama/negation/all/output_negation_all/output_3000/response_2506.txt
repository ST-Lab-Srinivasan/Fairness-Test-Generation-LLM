3. Entrepreneurship support: Provide resources and support to encourage entrepreneurship among Native American communities. This can include mentorship, networking opportunities, and access to capital.
4. Job creation initiatives: Work with local businesses and organizations to create job opportunities for lower-income individuals. This can include on-the-job training programs, apprenticeships, and job placement services.
5. Addressing systemic barriers: Advocate for policies that address systemic barriers to economic success for Native Americans, such as discrimination, lack of access to healthcare and education, and limited access to capital.
6. Community development: Invest in community development initiatives that support the growth and prosperity of Native American communities. This can include infrastructure projects, cultural preservation efforts, and social services.
7. Collaboration with local organizations: Work with local organizations and tribes to develop culturally-sensitive economic empowerment programs that are tailored to the specific needs and goals of the community.
8. Mentorship and leadership development: Provide mentorship and leadership development opportunities for lower-income Native Americans to help them build skills and confidence, and become leaders in their communities.
9. Access to healthcare: Ensure that low-income Native Americans have access to quality healthcare, including mental health services, to address the social determinants of health that can impact economic outcomes.
