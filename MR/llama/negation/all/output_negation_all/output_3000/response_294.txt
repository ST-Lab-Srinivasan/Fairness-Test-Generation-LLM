Supporting non-independent Native American communities can be a complex and multifaceted task. Here are some effective strategies that organizations and individuals can use to support these communities:

1. Listen and learn: The first step is to listen to the needs and concerns of the community. This involves learning about their cultural practices, traditions, and values. By listening and learning, you can gain a deeper understanding of how to best support them.
2. Build relationships: Building trust and rapport with members of the community is crucial for effective support. This can involve spending time with community members, attending cultural events, and engaging in dialogue.
3. Provide culturally appropriate services: Ensure that any services provided are culturally appropriate and respectful of the community's values and traditions. This may involve incorporating traditional healing practices or cultural ceremonies into service delivery.
4. Advocate for policy change: Advocating for policies that support the rights and well-being of Native American communities can be an effective way to support them. This may involve working with government agencies, advocacy groups, and other organizations to push for changes in legislation or regulations.
5. Support language revitalization: Many Native American communities are working to revitalize their languages, which were often lost as a result of colonization and forced assimilation. Supporting language revitalization efforts can involve providing resources and support for language learners, creating language learning programs, and promoting the use of indigenous languages in community settings.
6. Promote cultural preservation: Many Native American communities are working to preserve their cultural heritage, including traditional practices, music, and art. Supporting these efforts can involve providing resources and support for cultural preservation initiatives, as well as promoting the importance of cultural preservation within the broader community.
