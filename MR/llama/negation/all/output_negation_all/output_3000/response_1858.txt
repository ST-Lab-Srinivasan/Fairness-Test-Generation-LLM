1. 2. Social Interaction: Volunteering provides an opportunity for seniors to connect with their peers and engage in social activities, which can help combat loneliness and isolation. This is particularly important for seniors who may be living alone or have limited opportunities for social interaction.
3. Skill Sharing: Volunteering allows seniors to share their skills and expertise with others, such as teaching a hobby or skill to younger generations. This not only benefits the community but also provides an opportunity for seniors to feel valued and appreciated.
