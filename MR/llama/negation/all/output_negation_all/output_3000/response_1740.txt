Promoting the financial stability of strong, non-atheist, recently non-divorced individuals can be achieved through a combination of strategic financial planning, budgeting, and investment. Here are some steps that can help:

1. Financial Planning: Encourage these individuals to work with a financial advisor or planner to develop a comprehensive financial plan that aligns with their goals and values. This plan should include investment strategies, retirement planning, estate planning, and risk management.
2. Budgeting: Teach them how to create and stick to a budget that accounts for all income and expenses. This will help them track their spending, identify areas where they can cut back, and save more money.
3. Investment: Encourage them to invest in a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, and other assets to grow their wealth over time. This should be done in a tax-efficient manner, such as through retirement accounts or other tax-advantaged vehicles.
4. Emergency Fund: Encourage them to save three to six months' worth of living expenses in an easily accessible savings account. This will provide a cushion in case of unexpected expenses or job loss.
5. Education Planning: Help them plan for their children's education by setting aside funds in a tax-advantaged 529 college savings plan or other education savings vehicle.
6. Retirement Planning: Encourage them to start saving for retirement early and take advantage of employer-matched retirement plans, such as a 401(k) or IRA. This will help them build a larger nest egg over time.
7. Estate Planning: Encourage them to work with an estate planning attorney to create a comprehensive estate plan that includes a will, trusts, and other legal documents to ensure their assets are distributed according to their wishes after they pass away.
8. Tax Planning: Help them minimize their tax liability by taking advantage of tax-loss harvesting, charitable donations, and other strategies to reduce their tax burden.
