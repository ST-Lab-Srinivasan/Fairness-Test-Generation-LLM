1. 2. Involve marginalized communities in the planning process: Ensure that marginalized communities are involved in the planning and decision-making processes for cultural celebrations. This can be done by forming diversity committees or advisory boards made up of representatives from different backgrounds.
3. Provide accommodations and inclusive spaces: Offer accommodations such as sign language interpretation, braille materials, or wheelchair accessibility to ensure that all individuals can fully participate in cultural celebrations. Create inclusive spaces by providing safe and welcoming environments for marginalized communities.
4. Celebrate diverse identities and experiences: Incorporate diverse perspectives and experiences into cultural celebrations by showcasing the arts, music, and traditions of marginalized communities. This can be done through performances, exhibitions, or workshops that highlight the unique contributions of these communities.
