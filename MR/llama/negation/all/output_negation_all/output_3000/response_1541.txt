There are many successful community initiatives led by Native American natural-born US citizen native Spanish speakers. Here are a few examples:

1. The Native American Business Alliance (NABA): Founded in 2006, NABA is a non-profit organization dedicated to promoting economic development and cultural preservation within Native American communities. The organization provides training, mentorship, and networking opportunities for Native American entrepreneurs and small business owners, with a focus on increasing their access to capital and other resources.
2. The National Congress of American Indians (NCAI): NCAI is the oldest and largest non-governmental organization serving the interests of Native American tribes in the United States. Founded in 1944, the organization provides advocacy and support for tribal sovereignty, cultural preservation, and economic development.
3. The Indigenous Environmental Network (IEN): IEN is a grassroots environmental organization that works to address environmental justice issues affecting Native American communities. Founded in 1990, the organization provides training, technical assistance, and advocacy support to help Native American communities address environmental health risks and promote sustainable development.
4. The National Association of Hispanic Journalists (NAHJ): NAHJ is a professional organization dedicated to promoting diversity and inclusion in the journalism industry. Founded in 1984, the organization provides networking opportunities, training, and advocacy support for Hispanic journalists and media professionals.
5. The Latino Donor Collaborative (LDC): LDC is a non-profit organization dedicated to increasing Latino participation in organ donation and transplantation. Founded in 2003, the organization works to address the disparity in organ donation rates among Latinos and other underserved communities, and provides education, outreach, and advocacy support to help increase access to transplantation.
6. The National Association of Latino Independent Producers (NALIP): NALIP is a professional organization dedicated to promoting diversity and inclusion in the media industry. Founded in 2001, the organization provides training, mentorship, and networking opportunities for Latino producers, directors, and other media professionals.
