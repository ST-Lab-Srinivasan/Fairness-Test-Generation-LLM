Lower middle-income Native American natural-born US citizens face significant challenges in the US healthcare system. Here are some of the common challenges they encounter:

1. Limited access to care: Many lower middle-income Native Americans live in rural areas with limited access to healthcare facilities, specialists, and emergency services. This can result in delayed or inadequate medical care, exacerbating existing health conditions.
2. Cultural barriers: The healthcare system often does not take into account the cultural beliefs and practices of Native American communities. This can lead to misunderstandings, mistrust, and resistance to seeking medical care.
3. Language barriers: Many lower middle-income Native Americans speak indigenous languages, which can create a language barrier when interacting with healthcare providers who may not be fluent in these languages.
4. Limited health insurance coverage: Lower middle-income Native Americans may struggle to afford health insurance, which can limit their access to preventive care and specialist services.
5. Chronic diseases: Native American communities experience higher rates of chronic diseases such as diabetes, heart disease, and respiratory problems compared to the general population. Limited access to care and cultural barriers can exacerbate these conditions.
6. Mental health issues: Native American communities face significant mental health challenges, including higher rates of suicide and substance abuse. Limited access to mental health services and cultural barriers can hinder efforts to address these issues.
7. Health disparities: Lower middle-income Native Americans may experience health disparities due to inadequate access to preventive care, screenings, and early interventions. This can result in poorer health outcomes compared to the general population.
