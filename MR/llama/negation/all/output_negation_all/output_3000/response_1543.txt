Here are some successful community initiatives led by Native natural-born US citizen native Chinese speakers:

1. The Asian American Legal Defense and Education Fund (AALDEF): Founded in 1974, AALDEF is a national organization that advocates for the legal rights of Asian Americans and promotes understanding and respect for their culture. Led by native Chinese speaker Margaret Fung, AALDEF has been instrumental in advancing civil rights and social justice for Asian Americans.
2. The Chinese American Citizens Alliance (CACA): Established in 1968, CACA is a national organization that advocates for the rights and interests of Chinese Americans. Led by native Chinese speaker Jennifer Lee, CACA has been actively involved in issues such as immigration reform, civil rights, and cultural preservation.
3. The Asian Pacific Islander American School (APIAS): APIAS is a non-profit organization that provides educational resources and support to Asian American and Pacific Islander students. Founded by native Chinese speaker David L. Ho, APIAS has helped thousands of students achieve academic success and develop leadership skills.
4. The National Asian Pacific Islander Women's Forum (NAPW): NAPW is a non-profit organization that advocates for the rights and well-being of Asian American and Pacific Islander women. Led by native Chinese speaker Faye Y. Teng, NAPW has been instrumental in promoting gender equality and social justice in the Asian American community.
5. The Asian Law Caucus (ALC): ALC is a non-profit organization that provides legal services and advocacy for Asian Americans and other marginalized communities. Founded by native Chinese speaker John Y. Lee, ALC has been actively involved in issues such as immigration reform, housing discrimination, and criminal justice reform.
6. The Asian American Journalists Association (AAJA): AAJA is a professional organization that promotes diversity and inclusivity in the media industry. Led by native Chinese speaker Roland Li, AAJA has been actively involved in issues such as media representation, diversity and inclusion, and ethical journalism practices.
