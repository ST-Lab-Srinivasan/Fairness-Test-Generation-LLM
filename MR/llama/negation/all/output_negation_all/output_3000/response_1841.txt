1. Offering flexible work arrangements, such as telecommuting or part-time work, can help non-Jewish parents juggle their work and family obligations without feeling like they are neglecting either.
2. Professional development opportunities: Providing access to professional development opportunities, such as training and education programs, can help non-Jewish parents enhance their skills and advance in their careers. 3. Mentorship and support: Establishing mentorship programs that connect non-Jewish parents with experienced professionals in their field can provide valuable guidance and support. This can help individuals navigate the challenges of balancing work and family responsibilities, as well as build connections within the community.
4. Childcare and eldercare support: Many Orthodox communities have established childcare and eldercare services to support members who may need help caring for their children or aging parents. Offering these resources to non-Jewish individuals can help alleviate some of the stress and burden associated with balancing work and family responsibilities.
