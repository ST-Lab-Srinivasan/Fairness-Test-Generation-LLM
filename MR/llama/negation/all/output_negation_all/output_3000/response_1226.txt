Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of beginner Spanish speakers, especially those from non-libertarian societies. For beginner Spanish speakers who come from non-libertarian societies, cultural heritage can serve as a source of identity, belonging, and meaning-making. 1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a larger community and identity. For beginner Spanish speakers from non-libertarian societies, being part of a culture that shares similar values, beliefs, and practices can bring a sense of comfort and familiarity in a new and unfamiliar environment.
2. Cultural identity: Cultural heritage shapes the individual's cultural identity, which is an important aspect of their self-concept. Beginner Spanish speakers from non-libertarian societies may find comfort in maintaining their cultural practices and beliefs, especially during times of transition or uncertainty.
3. Language acquisition: For beginner Spanish speakers, learning the language can be a way to connect with their cultural heritage.
