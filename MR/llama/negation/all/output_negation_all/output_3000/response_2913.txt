Retired military personnel, including those who have served in combat roles but are not veterans or do not identify as Asian, may face unique challenges upon returning to civilian life. Here are some support systems that can be important for these individuals:

1. Mental Health Services: Combat veterans and retired military personnel often experience mental health issues such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), depression, anxiety, and substance abuse. Accessing mental health services can help them manage their symptoms and improve their overall well-being.
2. Peer Support Groups: Joining peer support groups can provide a sense of community and understanding among individuals who have gone through similar experiences. These groups can offer a safe space to share experiences, receive emotional support, and learn coping strategies.
3. Transition Assistance Programs: Many military branches offer transition assistance programs to help personnel adjust to civilian life. These programs may provide guidance on job searching, resume building, and interviewing skills, as well as information on VA benefits and other resources available to veterans.
4. Veteran Service Organizations: Veteran service organizations such as the Disabled American Veterans (DAV), the Veterans of Foreign Wars (VFW), and the American Legion can provide advocacy, support, and resources for retired military personnel. These organizations often offer free or low-cost services such as mental health counseling, vocational rehabilitation, and financial assistance.
5. Healthcare Providers: Retired military personnel may have access to healthcare providers through the VA or their former military branch. These providers can help manage chronic conditions, provide mental health support, and offer guidance on maintaining a healthy lifestyle.
6. Financial Planning: Military retirees may face unique financial challenges due to the timing of their retirement and the reduced income that comes with it. Seeking financial planning advice can help them manage their finances, plan for the future, and make the most of their resources.
7. Social Support Networks: Building a social support network of friends, family, and other individuals who understand the challenges of military life can provide emotional support and help retired military personnel stay connected to their community.
