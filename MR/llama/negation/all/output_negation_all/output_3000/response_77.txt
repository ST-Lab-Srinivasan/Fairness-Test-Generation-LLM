Creating more inclusive social policies for working-class bereaved individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges faced by this group. Here are some recommendations for creating more inclusive social policies:

1. Recognize the value of care work: Working-class bereaved individuals often bear the brunt of care work, such as caring for sick or elderly relatives, while also managing their own lives and work. Policies should recognize the value of this unpaid care work and provide support to help alleviate some of the burden.
2. Provide financial support: Financial difficulties are a common challenge faced by working-class bereaved individuals. Policies should provide financial support, such as funeral expenses assistance or emergency funds, to help ease the financial burden during a time of crisis.
3. Offer mental health support: Grieving is a complex and often lonely process, and working-class bereaved individuals may face additional challenges in accessing mental health support due to lack of resources or stigma. Policies should provide easy access to mental health services, including counseling and therapy, to help them cope with their loss.
4. Foster community connections: Social isolation can exacerbate the challenges faced by working-class bereaved individuals. Policies should promote community connections and social support networks to help individuals navigate their grief and find emotional support during difficult times.
5. Address systemic barriers: Working-class bereaved individuals may face systemic barriers, such as discrimination or lack of access to resources, that can hinder their ability to cope with loss. Policies should address these underlying issues to create a more equitable and inclusive society.
6. Provide flexible work arrangements: Grieving can be an extended process, and working-class bereaved individuals may need flexibility in their work schedules to accommodate their needs. Policies should provide flexible work arrangements, such as paid time off or flexible hours, to help them manage their work and personal lives during this time.
