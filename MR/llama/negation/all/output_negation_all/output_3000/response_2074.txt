Cultural Significance of Marriage in China:
Marriage is a significant institution in Chinese culture, and it has been an integral part of the country's social structure for centuries. Traditionally, marriage was seen as a union between two families rather than just the bride and groom. The choice of a life partner was not solely based on personal preferences but also on factors such as social status, financial security, and family obligations. In Mandarin culture, marriage is considered a vital aspect of an individual's life journey, as it is believed to bring happiness, fulfillment, and social respectability.

Furthermore, the country's one-child policy and subsequent gender imbalance have also contributed to the decline in marriages. As a result, many Mandarin individuals are choosing to remain unmarried for extended periods, as they prioritize their personal goals and aspirations over traditional marriage roles.
