Addressing the needs of individuals below the poverty line in disaster response requires a tailored approach that takes into account their unique circumstances and challenges. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Community-based approaches: Involve local communities in disaster response and recovery efforts, particularly those who are most vulnerable. This can include training community members as volunteers or employees of NGOs, involving them in needs assessments, and empowering them to make decisions about how resources are allocated.
2. Targeted interventions: Develop targeted interventions that address the specific needs of individuals below the poverty line, such as access to shelter, food, and clean water. This can include distributing emergency supplies, providing temporary housing solutions, and establishing community kitchens or food distribution centers.
3. Capacity building: Provide training and capacity-building opportunities for individuals below the poverty line to help them develop skills and knowledge that can help them prepare for and respond to disasters. This can include first aid training, emergency response training, and business skills training.
4. Inclusive decision-making processes: Ensure that individuals below the poverty line are included in decision-making processes related to disaster response and recovery efforts. This can involve establishing community-based committees or forums where their voices can be heard and their needs addressed.
5. Addressing systemic issues: Disasters often expose deep-seated systemic issues, such as inequality, discrimination, and lack of access to basic services. Addressing these underlying issues is crucial to reducing vulnerability among individuals below the poverty line.
6. Collaboration with local organizations: Partner with local organizations that have a presence in the community and can provide targeted support to individuals below the poverty line. This can include community-based NGOs, religious organizations, and cultural groups.
7. Building resilience: Focus on building resilience among individuals below the poverty line by providing opportunities for them to learn new skills, access financial services, and participate in disaster preparedness activities.
