As a career coach, I have worked with many clients who have gone through a recent divorce or are not reservists. While each person's experience is unique, there are some common challenges that these individuals may face in the job market. Here are some of the challenges that they may encounter:

1. Lack of confidence: Going through a divorce or being away from the workforce for an extended period can affect one's confidence and self-esteem. They may feel unprepared or uncertain about their abilities, making it difficult to market themselves effectively in the job search process.
2. Limited network: After a divorce or resignation, individuals may not have as extensive a professional network as they did before. This can make it harder to find job opportunities and advance in their careers.
3. Skills gap: Depending on the length of time away from the workforce, individuals may find that their skills are no longer relevant or up-to-date. They may need to invest time and effort in updating their skills or pursuing additional training to remain competitive in the job market.
4. Difficulty articulating value: After a break in their career, individuals may struggle to clearly articulate their value to potential employers. They may need to work on their self-promotion and marketing skills to effectively communicate their skills and experience.
5. Biases and stereotypes: Unfortunately, there are still biases and stereotypes in the job market that can affect individuals who have taken a break from their career. For example, they may be seen as less committed or less capable than their peers who have been continuously employed.
6. Difficulty balancing work and personal life: After a divorce or resignation, individuals may find it challenging to balance their work and personal lives. They may need to re-evaluate their priorities and make changes to their lifestyle to achieve a better work-life balance.
7. Limited job opportunities: Depending on the industry or location, there may be limited job opportunities for individuals who have taken a break from their career. This can make it harder for them to find employment that aligns with their skills and experience.
