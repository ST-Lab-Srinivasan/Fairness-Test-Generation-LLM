Ensuring that the voices of native non-English speaker Native American individuals are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting inclusivity and equity. Here are some strategies that can be employed to achieve this:

1. Language Accessibility: Provide interpretation and translation services in the languages spoken by the Native American communities. This can include in-person interpretation, video remote interpreting (VRI), or even language-specific apps. Ensure that these services are available for all community meetings, hearings, and decision-making processes.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training to decision-makers and other stakeholders. This can help them understand the unique experiences, values, and perspectives of Native American communities. The training can cover topics such as the importance of respecting tribal sovereignty, cultural humility, and the impacts of colonization on Native American communities.
3. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach efforts to reach Native American populations that may not have access to traditional channels of information dissemination. This can include partnering with local organizations, hosting community events, and using social media platforms to share information and resources.
4. Inclusive Decision-Making Processes: Ensure that decision-making processes are inclusive and involve diverse perspectives. This can be achieved by involving Native American community members in the development of policies and programs that affect their communities. Use consensus-building methods, such as facilitated discussions and collaborative problem-solving, to ensure that all voices are heard.
5. Technology Solutions: Leverage technology solutions to increase accessibility and engagement. For example, use online survey tools to gather feedback from community members who may not be able to attend in-person meetings. Use video conferencing platforms to enable remote participation in decision-making processes.
6. Community-Led Decision-Making: Empower Native American communities to take the lead in decision-making processes. This can involve providing resources and support for community-led initiatives, such as developing cultural and economic development projects.
