The economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US citizens who are long-term unemployed is a critical issue that affects their well-being, self-sufficiency, and overall quality of life. Here are some strategies that can help support their economic empowerment:

1. Job Training and Placement Programs: Provide job training and placement programs that cater to the specific needs of Native American communities. These programs should focus on in-demand industries, such as healthcare, technology, and renewable energy, and provide hands-on training and certification opportunities.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing access to small business loans, mentorship opportunities, and resources for developing business plans. This can help individuals start their own businesses or expand existing ones, creating jobs and economic growth in their communities.
3. Capacity Building: Invest in capacity building programs that focus on developing the skills and knowledge of Native American entrepreneurs and small business owners. This can include training on financial management, marketing, and operations.
4. Access to Capital: Ensure access to capital for Native American entrepreneurs and small business owners through grants, loans, and investments. This can help them scale their businesses and create jobs in their communities.
5. Mentorship and Networking Opportunities: Provide mentorship and networking opportunities for Native American entrepreneurs and small business owners. This can help them connect with potential partners, investors, and customers, and gain insights into the latest industry trends and best practices.
6. Community Development Initiatives: Support community development initiatives that promote economic growth and prosperity in Native American communities. This can include infrastructure development, community facilities, and cultural preservation projects.
7. Education and Training: Implement education and training programs that address the specific needs of Native American communities. These programs can focus on vocational training, job readiness, and academic achievement.
8. Partnerships and Collaborations: Foster partnerships and collaborations between Native American communities, government agencies, non-profit organizations, and private sector companies. This can help leverage resources, expertise, and funding to support economic empowerment in these communities.
