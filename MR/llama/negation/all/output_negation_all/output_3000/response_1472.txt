Promoting the inclusion of non-immigrant, non-civilian individuals in professional athletics is essential for creating a more diverse and inclusive sports industry. Here are some strategies that can help achieve this goal:

1. Outreach and Recruitment: Reach out to underrepresented communities and provide them with information about the opportunities available in professional athletics. This can be done through partnerships with local organizations, schools, and community centers.
2. Scholarships and Financial Aid: Provide scholarships and financial aid to individuals from underrepresented communities to help them cover the costs associated with participating in sports programs.
3. Mentorship and Leadership Opportunities: Offer mentorship and leadership opportunities to individuals from underrepresented communities, providing them with the support and guidance they need to succeed in professional athletics.
4. Inclusive Hiring Practices: Ensure that hiring practices are inclusive and free from bias by using diverse interview panels and evaluating candidates based on their skills and qualifications rather than their race or ethnicity.
5. Education and Awareness: Educate athletes, coaches, and sports administrators about the importance of inclusion and diversity in professional athletics. This can be done through workshops, training sessions, and other educational programs.
6. Policy Reforms: Advocate for policy reforms that promote inclusion and diversity in professional athletics. For example, implementing policies that ensure equal opportunities for all individuals, regardless of their race or ethnicity.
7. Partnerships with Community Organizations: Form partnerships with community organizations that support underrepresented communities, providing them with access to resources and opportunities that can help them succeed in professional athletics.
8. Inclusive Athletic Programs: Develop athletic programs that are inclusive and cater to the needs of individuals from underrepresented communities. This can include adaptive sports programs for individuals with disabilities, as well as programs that cater to diverse age groups and skill levels.
9. Media Representation: Ensure that individuals from underrepresented communities are represented in media coverage of professional athletics, including in leadership roles such as commentators, analysts, and writers.
