Supporting the economic empowerment of upper middle-income Native natural-born US citizen individuals requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses systemic barriers and provides targeted support. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills development programs, including vocational training, apprenticeships, and higher education. This will help individuals acquire the skills needed to compete in the job market and advance in their careers.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities. This can include startup incubators, business loans, and training programs.
3. Job Creation: Invest in job creation initiatives that provide good-paying jobs for Native individuals in their communities. This can include investing in infrastructure projects, promoting sustainable agriculture and forestry practices, and supporting small businesses.
4. Wealth Creation: Encourage wealth creation through asset building strategies such as homeownership, retirement savings plans, and other financial instruments. This can help individuals build intergenerational wealth and achieve economic mobility.
5. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): Support the establishment of CDFIs that provide affordable loans and financial services to low-income and marginalized communities, including Native communities.
6. Tax Incentives: Offer tax incentives for businesses that operate on Native lands or employ Native individuals. This can include tax credits for hiring Native workers, investing in Native-owned businesses, or locating businesses in underserved areas.
7. Access to Capital: Address the lack of access to capital faced by many Native entrepreneurs and businesses. This can be achieved through initiatives such as impact investing, crowdfunding, and venture philanthropy.
8. Land Use Planning: Improve land use planning and zoning regulations to support sustainable economic development in Native communities. This can include prioritizing mixed-use developments, affordable housing, and community facilities.
