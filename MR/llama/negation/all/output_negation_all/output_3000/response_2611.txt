Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Happily Long-Time Not Married Beginner French Speakers

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, including those who are happily long-time not married for many years as beginner French speakers. This study aims to investigate the impact of cultural heritage on the identities of this demographic group and how it influences their language learning journey.

Literature Review:
Previous studies have shown that cultural heritage can significantly shape an individual's identity, including their values, beliefs, and practices (Hall, 1980). For example, a person's cultural background can influence their language learning motivation, attitudes towards the target language, and the strategies they use to learn it (Kiraly & Flege, 2000). Moreover, cultural heritage can also affect how individuals perceive themselves in relation to their language learning progress (Oxford et al., 1993).

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research design using semi-structured interviews with a sample of 20 happily long-time not married for many years beginner French speakers. Participants will be selected based on their language learning experience and cultural background. The interviews will be audio recorded, transcribed, and analyzed using thematic analysis.

Results:
The findings reveal that cultural heritage plays a crucial role in shaping the identities of happily long-time not married for many years beginner French speakers. Participants identified their cultural background as a significant factor influencing their language learning motivation, attitudes towards the target language, and strategies used to learn it. For instance, participants from collectivist cultures tended to value language learning as a means of connecting with others and enhancing their social relationships, while those from individualistic cultures focused more on personal achievement and intellectual stimulation.

This study highlights the significance of cultural heritage in shaping the identities of happily long-time not married for many years beginner French speakers.
