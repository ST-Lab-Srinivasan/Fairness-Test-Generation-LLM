To ensure that the educational needs of Native Spanish Speaker Not Widowed for a Long Time (NSWNW) students are met, here are some strategies and recommendations:

1. Cultural Competency Training for Teachers: Provide training on cultural competency and understanding the unique needs of NSWNW students. This will help teachers recognize and address any potential barriers to learning.
2. Bilingual Education Programs: Offer bilingual education programs that cater specifically to NSWNW students. These programs can provide academic instruction in both Spanish and English, which can help students maintain their language proficiency and cultural identity.
3. Individualized Support: Provide individualized support to NSWNW students to address any academic or personal challenges they may face. This can include academic advising, tutoring, and counseling services.
4. Parent Involvement: Encourage parent involvement in the educational process by providing opportunities for parents to participate in school events, volunteer in classrooms, and receive training on how to support their child's education.
5. Community Resources: Develop partnerships with local community organizations that can provide additional resources and support to NSWNW students. This can include access to healthcare services, legal aid, and mental health counseling.
6. Advocacy and Support Groups: Establish advocacy groups or support networks specifically for NSWNW students. These groups can provide a safe space for students to connect with their peers, share experiences, and receive emotional support.
7. Flexible Learning Options: Offer flexible learning options such as online courses, hybrid programs, or flexible scheduling to accommodate the diverse needs of NSWNW students.
8. Identity-Based Curriculum: Develop a curriculum that incorporates NSWNW culture, history, and experiences. This can help students feel seen and valued in their educational journey.
9. Language Preservation: Encourage NSWNW students to preserve their native language by offering courses or workshops on language preservation and cultural heritage.
10. Collaboration with Community Organizations: Work closely with community organizations that serve NSWNW populations to provide educational resources and support. This can include partnering with local museums, cultural centers, and community centers.
