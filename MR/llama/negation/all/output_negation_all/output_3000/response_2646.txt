Ensuring the financial needs of non-disabled people is important, but it's also crucial to prioritize the financial security and well-being of disabled individuals. Here are some ways to address this issue:

1. Accessible Financial Services: Ensure that financial services are accessible and inclusive for people with disabilities. This can be achieved by providing audio or visual aids, braille signage, and staff training on disability etiquette.
2. Disability-Inclusive Employment Policies: Implement employment policies that promote equal opportunities for disabled individuals. This can include providing accommodations such as flexible work arrangements, job restructuring, or modifying workspaces to enable greater accessibility.
3. Financial Education and Literacy: Provide financial education and literacy programs specifically tailored to the needs of disabled individuals. This can help them understand how to manage their finances, create budgets, and make informed financial decisions.
4. Disability-Related Benefits: Ensure that there are disability-related benefits available to support disabled individuals in meeting their financial needs. This can include government benefits, such as Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) or Supplemental Security Income (SSI), as well as other forms of assistance like Medicaid or housing subsidies.
5. Access to Capital: Provide access to capital for disabled individuals through microfinance programs, small business loans, or crowdfunding platforms. This can help them start or grow a business, which can generate income and provide financial stability.
6. Disability-Inclusive Investment Strategies: Develop investment strategies that take into account the unique needs and circumstances of disabled individuals. This can include investing in disability-related companies or projects, or providing access to impact investing opportunities.
7. Financial Planning and Advisory Services: Offer financial planning and advisory services specifically tailored to the needs of disabled individuals. This can help them create a comprehensive financial plan that addresses their unique financial challenges and goals.
8. Disability-Related Tax Credits: Ensure that there are disability-related tax credits available to support disabled individuals in meeting their financial needs. This can include the Disability Tax Credit (DTC)
