1. Improved communication: Cultural competence helps healthcare providers communicate effectively with patients from diverse backgrounds. Arabic is a complex language with nuances that may not be easily conveyed through translation alone. Healthcare providers who are culturally competent can better understand the emotional and cultural nuances of their patients, leading to more effective communication and care.

Cultural competence can help build trust between healthcare providers and native Arabic speakers, which is essential for providing high-quality care.

For example, healthcare providers who are culturally competent may be more effective in addressing cultural beliefs and practices that influence a patient's health behaviors.
