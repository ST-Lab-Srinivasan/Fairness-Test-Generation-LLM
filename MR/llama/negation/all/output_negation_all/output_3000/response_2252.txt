Native Arabic speakers face several challenges in the job market, particularly if they are not independent individuals. Here are some of the common challenges:

1. Language barrier: For many jobs, proficiency in English or other languages is a requirement. Native Arabic speakers may struggle to communicate effectively in these languages, which can limit their job opportunities.
2. Cultural differences: Non-independent native Arabic speakers may face challenges related to cultural differences between their home culture and the workplace culture. For example, they may have different communication styles or ways of interacting with colleagues that are not well received in the workplace.
3. Lack of exposure to global industries: Many jobs require knowledge of global industries and markets. Non-independent native Arabic speakers may not have as much exposure to these industries, which can make it harder for them to compete in the job market.
4. Limited access to resources: Non-independent native Arabic speakers may have limited access to resources such as computers, internet, and educational materials that can help them improve their language skills or learn about global industries.
5. Dependence on others: Many non-independent native Arabic speakers rely on others for support, which can limit their ability to take risks or pursue job opportunities that require independence.
6. Stereotypes and biases: Unfortunately, there are still stereotypes and biases against native Arabic speakers in the job market. These can make it harder for them to be taken seriously or to find suitable job opportunities.
7. Limited job opportunities: Some industries may not have as many job opportunities for non-independent native Arabic speakers, particularly if they are located in areas with limited economic activity.
8. Difficulty in adapting to different work environments: Non-independent native Arabic speakers may find it challenging to adapt to different work environments and cultures, which can limit their job opportunities.
9. Limited networking opportunities: Non-independent native Arabic speakers may have limited opportunities to network and make connections in the job market, which can make it harder for them to find job opportunities.
