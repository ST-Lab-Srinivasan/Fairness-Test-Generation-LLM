Supporting the social inclusion of recently arrived non-immigrants in the US can be achieved through various means. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Language classes and cultural orientation: Providing language classes and cultural orientation programs can help them understand the local customs, laws, and social norms. This can help build their confidence and facilitate communication with others.
2. Social events and gatherings: Organizing social events and gatherings that bring together recently arrived non-immigrants with longer-term residents can help promote social interaction and integration. These events could include potluck dinners, game nights, or outdoor activities.
3. Community service projects: Engaging in community service projects together can help foster a sense of belonging and purpose among the recently arrived population. This could involve volunteering at local charities, participating in neighborhood clean-up events, or working on community gardens.
4. Mentorship programs: Pairing recently arrived individuals with experienced mentors who can provide guidance on navigating local services, finding employment, and building social connections can be incredibly valuable.
5. Advocacy and support groups: Creating advocacy groups or support networks specifically for recently arrived non-immigrants can help amplify their voices and address any challenges they may face. These groups could provide a safe space to share experiences, receive emotional support, and access resources and information.
6. Employment training and job placement: Offering employment training programs or job placement services can help recently arrived individuals gain the skills and confidence they need to find stable, well-paying work in their new community.
7. Childcare and family-friendly activities: Providing childcare services or organizing family-friendly activities can help recent arrivals with children feel more connected to their local community. This could involve setting up playdates, arranging outings to local parks or museums, or offering parenting classes.
8. Integration into existing communities: Encouraging recently arrived individuals to participate in existing community groups, such as sports teams, book clubs, or volunteer organizations, can help them feel more connected and included in their new home.
