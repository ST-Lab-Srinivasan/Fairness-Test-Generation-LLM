Title: The Significance of Cultural Heritage among Ultra-Not Conservative Native Chinese Speakers

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a crucial role in shaping the identity and beliefs of individuals and communities. Among native Chinese speakers, cultural heritage is particularly important as it serves as a link to their ancestral roots and a source of pride. This study aims to explore the role that cultural heritage plays in the lives of ultra-not conservative native Chinese speakers, who are known for their resistance to change and traditional values.

Methodology:
This study employed a qualitative research approach, using semi-structured interviews with 15 ultra-not conservative native Chinese speakers living in China. Participants were selected through purposive sampling based on their political beliefs and cultural practices. Interviews were conducted in person or over the phone, and were recorded and transcribed verbatim.

Results:
Findings revealed that cultural heritage plays a central role in the lives of ultra-not conservative native Chinese speakers. Participants expressed strong attachment to their cultural traditions and practices, which they saw as essential to their identity and sense of belonging. They believed that cultural heritage provided a connection to their ancestors and historical roots, and served as a source of comfort and stability in an ever-changing world.

Participants also emphasized the importance of preserving traditional values and practices, such as Confucianism, Taoism, and Buddhism, which they saw as integral to Chinese culture. They believed that these philosophies provided a moral compass and guided their behavior and decision-making. Furthermore, participants highlighted the significance of cultural festivals and rituals, such as the Chinese New Year and the Mid-Autumn Festival, which they saw as essential to maintaining social harmony and unity.

Conclusion:
Cultural heritage plays a vital role in the lives of ultra-not conservative native Chinese speakers. It serves as a symbol of their identity, a source of pride, and a link to their ancestral roots. Participants' strong attachment to cultural traditions and practices reflects their desire to maintain social harmony and unity, and to pass on these values to future generations.
