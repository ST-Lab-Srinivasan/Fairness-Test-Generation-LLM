Lifelong migrants, or people who have moved to a new country multiple times throughout their lives, often face unique challenges in the legal system. Here are some of the common challenges faced by lifelong migrants:

1. Lack of legal certainty: Lifelong migrants may find it difficult to establish legal certainty in their residency status due to frequent changes in their immigration status. This can lead to uncertainty and insecurity in their personal and professional lives.
2. Difficulty in building a life: Moving to a new country every few years can make it challenging for lifelong migrants to establish roots and build a life. They may face difficulties in finding stable housing, employment, and education for themselves and their families.
3. Social isolation: Lifelong migrants may experience social isolation due to their frequent moves, which can lead to a lack of social connections and support networks. This can make it difficult for them to access legal services and navigate the legal system.
4. Language barriers: Many lifelong migrants face language barriers when interacting with the legal system, as they may not speak the local language fluently. This can lead to misunderstandings and difficulties in accessing legal services.
5. Lack of trust: Lifelong migrants may lack trust in the legal system due to their experiences with discrimination, racism, or other forms of oppression. This can make it difficult for them to access legal services and advocate for their rights.
6. Complexity of legal procedures: The legal procedures and processes in a new country can be complex and overwhelming for lifelong migrants, especially if they have limited knowledge of the local language and culture.
