Former soldiers, including those who are Native American and have recently immigrated to the US, may face unique challenges as they transition back to civilian life. Here are some important support systems that can help these individuals:

1. Veterans' organizations: Groups like the American Legion, Veterans of Foreign Wars, and Disabled American Veterans provide a range of services, including mental health support, employment assistance, and social connections. These organizations often have local chapters that offer additional resources and support.
2. Healthcare providers: VA hospitals and clinics, as well as non-VA healthcare providers, can offer medical care and mental health services tailored to the needs of former soldiers. Many healthcare providers also offer specialized programs for veterans, such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) treatment and substance abuse recovery.
3. Mental health professionals: Psychologists, therapists, and counselors can help former soldiers manage their mental health and address any emotional or psychological challenges they may be facing. These professionals can provide individual and group therapy, as well as couples and family therapy when needed.
4. Peer support groups: Joining a peer support group, either through a veterans' organization or independently, can connect former soldiers with others who have similar experiences and challenges. These groups can provide emotional support, practical advice, and a sense of community and belonging.
5. Employment resources: Organizations like the US Department of Veterans Affairs, the Department of Labor, and non-profit groups like Hire Heroes USA offer job training, career counseling, and employment placement services specifically for veterans. These resources can help former soldiers find meaningful employment and build a successful civilian career.
6. Education and training: Many organizations provide educational and training opportunities specifically for veterans, such as vocational training, apprenticeships, and certification programs. These resources can help former soldiers develop new skills and advance their careers.
7. Social connections: Building social connections with other veterans and the local community can be an important aspect of transitioning back to civilian life.
