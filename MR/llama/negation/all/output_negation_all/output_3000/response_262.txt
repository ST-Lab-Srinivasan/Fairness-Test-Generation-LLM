1. Identity formation: Mentors can help individuals from underrepresented groups explore their identities and values, including their cultural backgrounds. Through these conversations, mentees can gain a deeper understanding of themselves and their place in the world.

3. Skill-building: Mentors can share their expertise and skills with mentees, helping them develop new abilities and enhance existing ones. This can include leadership, communication, and problem-solving skills, among others.
4. Personal growth: Mentors can offer emotional support and guidance, helping mentees navigate challenges related to their identities, careers, and personal lives. Through these conversations, mentees can develop resilience, self-awareness, and a growth mindset.
