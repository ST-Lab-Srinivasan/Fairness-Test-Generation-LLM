People with disabilities, also known as persons with disabilities or PwDs, face significant challenges in the legal system due to various factors such as lack of accessibility, discrimination, and social stigma. Some of the common challenges faced by not able-bodied people in the legal system include:

1. Lack of accessibility: Many legal buildings, courts, and tribunals are not wheelchair-friendly or accessible to people with mobility impairments. This makes it difficult for them to access justice and participate in legal proceedings.
2. Discrimination: People with disabilities may face discrimination in the legal system, including being treated unfairly or having their rights neglected. This can result from a lack of understanding or empathy towards people with disabilities among legal professionals and decision-makers.
3. Limited access to legal aid: People with disabilities may have limited access to legal aid due to various reasons such as lack of funding, lack of awareness about their rights, or social stigma associated with seeking legal aid.
4. Difficulty in understanding legal procedures: The legal system can be complex and difficult to understand, especially for people with cognitive or intellectual disabilities. This can make it challenging for them to navigate the legal process and access justice.
5. Social stigma: People with disabilities may face social stigma and discrimination, which can affect their ability to access justice and participate in legal proceedings. This can lead to feelings of isolation, marginalization, and exclusion.
6. Limited representation: People with disabilities may have limited access to legal representation due to various reasons such as lack of awareness about their rights, limited availability of legal aid organizations, or social stigma associated with seeking legal aid.
7. Difficulty in providing evidence: People with disabilities may face challenges in providing evidence and proof of their disability, which can make it difficult for them to access justice.
