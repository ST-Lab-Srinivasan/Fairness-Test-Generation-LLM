Introduction:
Marital status can have a significant impact on the lives of individuals, especially during the third trimester of pregnancy. As a beginner non-English speaker, navigating this time can be challenging. This study aims to investigate the impacts of marital status on the lives of recently pregnant beginner non-English speakers.

Literature Review:
Previous studies have shown that marital status can affect various aspects of an individual's life, including their mental and physical health, social relationships, and economic stability. During pregnancy, these factors can be particularly important as the body undergoes significant changes, and the individual must adapt to new roles and responsibilities.

Methodology:
This study will use a qualitative research approach, conducting in-depth interviews with a sample of recently pregnant beginner non-English speakers. Participants will be recruited through community health centers and obstetric clinics, ensuring a diverse range of marital statuses and languages spoken. Interviews will be audio recorded and translated into the participants' preferred language for ease of understanding.

Data Analysis:
The data collected from the interviews will be analyzed using thematic analysis, identifying patterns and themes within the data. The findings will be organized into categories, such as social support, mental health, and economic stability, to understand how marital status affects these areas of life during pregnancy.

Expected Outcomes:
This study aims to provide insights into the impact of marital status on the lives of recently pregnant beginner non-English speakers. Specifically, we expect to identify the challenges faced by individuals in each marital status group during this time and the ways in which they cope with these challenges.

Significance:
Understanding the impact of marital status on the lives of recently pregnant beginner non-English speakers is crucial for healthcare providers and policymakers. By addressing the specific needs of these individuals, we can improve their overall well-being and ensure they receive the appropriate support during this critical time.
