During the first trimester of pregnancy, women may face unique challenges related to their mental health. Here are some ways to support the mental health of recently pregnant lifelong non-single people during this time:

1. Listen and validate feelings: Offer a listening ear and validation of their emotions. Pregnancy can be an overwhelming experience, and it's essential to acknowledge and normalize their feelings.
2. Encourage self-care: Support them in practicing self-care activities, such as exercise, meditation, or relaxation techniques. These can help reduce stress and anxiety during this time.
3. Provide resources: Offer to provide information on prenatal mental health resources, such as counseling services or support groups, and encourage them to seek professional help if needed.
4. Offer practical support: Help with errands, grocery shopping, or household chores to reduce stress and workload during this time.
5. Be present: Spend time with them, listen to their concerns, and offer emotional support. Being present and available can go a long way in helping them feel less alone.
6. Encourage communication: Create an open and non-judgmental space for them to express their feelings and concerns about the pregnancy.
7. Avoid unrealistic expectations: Be aware of societal pressure on pregnant women and avoid placing unrealistic expectations on them, such as having a perfect pregnancy or parenting experience.
8. Encourage self-compassion: Teach them to be kind and compassionate towards themselves during this time, recognizing that it's okay to not be okay all the time.
9. Offer distractions: Suggest fun and engaging activities, such as reading, watching movies, or taking a relaxing bath, to help take their mind off of pregnancy-related stress.
10. Be patient: Recognize that hormonal changes during pregnancy can affect mood and behavior, and be patient and understanding when dealing with them.
