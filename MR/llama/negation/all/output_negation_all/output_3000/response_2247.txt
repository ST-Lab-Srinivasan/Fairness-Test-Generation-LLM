Ensuring that the healthcare needs of lower-class Native Arabic speakers are met requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the social, economic, and cultural barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can be implemented:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training for healthcare providers on cultural competency and how to communicate effectively with patients from diverse backgrounds, including Native Arabic speakers.
2. Language Access: Ensure that interpretations and translations are available for patients who do not speak the dominant language of the healthcare system. This can be done by hiring interpreters or using technology-assisted interpretation services.
3. Outreach and Education: Implement outreach programs to educate lower-class Native Arabic speakers about the importance of preventive care, screenings, and early detection. Promote health literacy by providing written materials in their native language or through bilingual health educators.
4. Community Partnerships: Develop partnerships with community-based organizations that serve Lower class Native Arabic speakers. These partnerships can help increase access to care, improve health outcomes and address social determinants of health.
5. Health Fairs and Screenings: Organize health fairs and screenings in areas with high concentrations of lower-class Native Arabic speakers. Offer free or low-cost health services, including blood pressure, glucose, and cholesterol checks.
6. Patient Navigators: Train patient navigators who are fluent in Native Arabic to assist patients in navigating the healthcare system, scheduling appointments, and accessing care.
7. Telemedicine: Utilize telemedicine services to reach patients in remote areas or those with limited access to healthcare facilities. This can include video consultations, remote monitoring, and store-and-forward imaging.
8. Health Insurance Enrollment: Assist lower-class Native Arabic speakers in enrolling in health insurance programs, such as Medicaid or the Affordable Care Act, to increase access to care.
