Just above poverty line (JAL) individuals in India face several challenges in the legal system, including:

1. Limited access to legal aid: JAL individuals may not have the financial resources to hire a lawyer or access legal aid programs, making it difficult for them to navigate the legal system.
2. Lack of representation: JAL individuals may not be able to afford legal representation, which can result in them being at a disadvantage in court proceedings.
3. Limited knowledge of legal procedures: JAL individuals may not have a thorough understanding of legal procedures and processes, making it difficult for them to navigate the legal system on their own.
4. Language barriers: In India, there are many languages spoken, and JAL individuals who do not speak the language of the court may face challenges in understanding legal proceedings.
5. Discrimination: JAL individuals may face discrimination in the legal system due to their socio-economic status, which can result in unequal treatment under the law.
6. Difficulty in accessing justice: JAL individuals may find it difficult to access justice due to various reasons such as lack of awareness about their rights, lack of resources to pursue legal action, and lack of trust in the legal system.
7. Limited scope of legal aid: Legal aid programs may have limited scope and may not cover all areas of law, making it difficult for JAL individuals to access justice.
8. Lack of legal literacy: JAL individuals may not be aware of their legal rights and obligations, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the legal system.
9. Difficulty in proving poverty: In India, proving poverty can be challenging, especially if the individual does not have proper documentation or witnesses to support their claim.
10. Limited access to alternative dispute resolution mechanisms: JAL individuals may not have access to alternative dispute resolution mechanisms such as mediation and arbitration, which can be less expensive and time-consuming than going to court.
