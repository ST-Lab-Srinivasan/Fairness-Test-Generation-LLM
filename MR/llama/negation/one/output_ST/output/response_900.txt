2. Career guidance: A mentor can offer guidance on how to navigate the workplace and advance in a particular career path. They can provide advice on how to build a professional reputation, manage conflicts, and make strategic decisions that will help individuals achieve their long-term goals.
3. Personal development: A mentor can help individuals identify their strengths and weaknesses, set realistic goals, and develop a plan to achieve those goals. They can also provide emotional support and encouragement, which can be particularly important for lower-class individuals who may face additional challenges such as discrimination or poverty.
4. Role modeling: A mentor can serve as a positive role model, demonstrating what is possible through hard work, perseverance, and dedication. They can show individuals that it is possible to overcome obstacles and achieve success, even in the face of adversity.
5. Access to resources: A mentor may have access to resources such as funding, training, or educational opportunities that can help individuals improve their skills and knowledge. This can be especially important for lower-class individuals who may not have access to these resources otherwise.
6. Conflict resolution: A mentor can provide guidance on how to resolve conflicts and navigate difficult situations. They can offer advice on how to communicate effectively, manage conflict, and make decisions that will benefit the individual and their career.
