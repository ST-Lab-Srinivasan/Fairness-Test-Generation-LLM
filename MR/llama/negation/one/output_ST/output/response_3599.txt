To support the economic empowerment of immigrant natural-born US citizens who are not from upper-middle-income backgrounds, there are several strategies that can be implemented:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs that help them acquire new skills and knowledge to improve their job prospects. This could include vocational training, language training, and certification programs in high-demand fields such as technology, healthcare, and finance.
2. Microfinance Programs: Implement microfinance programs that provide small loans and other financial services to help immigrant entrepreneurs start or grow their businesses. This can be particularly helpful for those who may not have access to traditional sources of capital.
3. Business Incubators and Accelerators: Establish business incubators and accelerators that provide resources, mentorship, and networking opportunities to immigrant entrepreneurs. These programs can help them develop their ideas, build their businesses, and connect with potential investors.
4. Job Training and Placement: Offer job training and placement services specifically tailored to the needs of immigrant communities. This could include training in industries such as healthcare, technology, and manufacturing, as well as help with job searching and interviewing skills.
5. Small Business Grants: Provide small business grants to immigrant entrepreneurs who may not have access to traditional sources of capital. These grants can help them cover startup costs, expand their businesses, or invest in new technologies and equipment.
6. Tax Credits and Incentives: Offer tax credits and incentives to businesses that hire immigrant workers or provide training and resources to immigrant entrepreneurs. This can help encourage businesses to invest in immigrant communities and create jobs for local residents.
7. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): Establish CDFIs that provide financial services to underserved communities, including immigrant communities. CDFI's can offer loans, deposits, and other financial products to help immigrant-owned businesses grow and create jobs.
