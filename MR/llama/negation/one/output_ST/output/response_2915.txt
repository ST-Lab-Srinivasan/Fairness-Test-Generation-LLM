As a recently retired combat veteran who is also a Native American natural-born US citizen, there are several support systems that can be important to help navigate the challenges of readjusting to civilian life. Here are some of them:

1. Veterans Service Organizations (VSOs): VSOs such as the Veterans of Foreign Wars (VFW), Disabled American Veterans (DAV), and American Legion provide a range of services, including financial assistance, employment resources, and mental health support. These organizations understand the unique challenges faced by Native American veterans and can offer culturally sensitive care.
2. Native American Veterans Service Organizations: There are several Native American VSOs that cater specifically to the needs of Native American veterans. These organizations may provide similar services as VSOs but with a focus on cultural sensitivity and understanding. Examples include the Native American Veterans Association and the National Congress of American Indians (NCAI) Veterans Council.
3. Mental Health Services: Combat veterans often experience mental health issues such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), depression, and anxiety. Native American veterans may face additional cultural barriers in seeking mental health care due to historical trauma and ongoing discrimination. Accessing culturally sensitive mental health services from organizations like the Indian Health Service or the National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) can be helpful.
4. Employment Support: Transitioning back to civilian life can be challenging, especially for veterans who face barriers in finding employment due to their military service. Veterans' employment services like the US Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) and non-profit organizations such as Hire Heroes USA can provide job training, resume building, and placement assistance.
5. Peer Support Groups: Connecting with other veterans who share similar experiences can be beneficial for combat veterans. Native American veterans may find it helpful to join peer support groups specifically tailored to their cultural background and experiences, such as the Native American Veterans Association or the National Alliance of Native American Veterans.
