Divorce, especially multiple times, can have a significant impact on an individual's career and job prospects. Here are some of the challenges that recently divorced multiple times active-duty people may face in the job market:

1. Stigma: Unfortunately, there is still a stigma associated with being divorced, especially multiple times. This can make it harder for individuals to be seen as viable candidates for certain positions or promotions.
2. Lack of confidence: Going through multiple divorces can be emotionally draining and may lead to a loss of confidence in one's ability to navigate the job market.
3. Limited education and training: Military spouses often put their own careers on hold to support their partner's military career, which can result in limited education and training opportunities. This can make it harder to compete in the job market.
4. Difficulty with networking: Military spouses may have difficulty building a professional network due to frequent moves and the challenges of balancing work and family responsibilities.
5. Limited work experience: Military spouses may have limited work experience, especially if they have taken time off to raise children or support their partner's military career.
6. Difficulty with resume-building: Military spouses may struggle to create a strong resume due to the lack of concrete work experience or education.
7. Limited job opportunities: Military bases often have limited job opportunities, especially for spouses who do not have a military background.
8. Difficulty with career advancement: Military spouses may face challenges in advancing their careers due to the frequent moves and lack of job security.
9. Limited benefits: Military spouses may not be eligible for certain benefits, such as health insurance or retirement plans, which can make it harder to compete in the job market.
10. Difficulty with work-life balance: Military spouses often have to juggle work and family responsibilities, which can make it challenging to maintain a healthy work-life balance.
