Ensuring the educational needs of civilian Native natural-born US citizen students is crucial for their academic success and social integration. Here are some strategies to address their unique challenges:

1. Culturally responsive curriculum: Develop curricula that reflect the history, culture, and values of Native American communities. Incorporate traditional knowledge, languages, and practices into the educational content.
2. Indigenous language revitalization: Support the revitalization of indigenous languages by incorporating them into the curriculum, hiring fluent speakers as teachers or consultants, and offering language classes for students and community members.
3. Cultural competency training for educators: Provide ongoing cultural competency training for teachers to help them understand the unique needs of Native American students and how to create an inclusive learning environment.
4. Partnerships with Native communities: Establish partnerships with local Native American communities to co-create curricula, provide cultural resources, and involve community members in the educational process.
5. Student-centered learning: Foster a student-centered approach that recognizes the importance of traditional knowledge and practices in the educational process. Encourage students to take an active role in their learning and to explore their cultural heritage.
6. Access to technology: Ensure that Native American students have access to technology, such as computers, tablets, and internet connectivity, to support their educational journey and to bridge the digital divide.
7. Mentorship programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair Native American students with successful role models from their communities or cultures. This can help build confidence, provide guidance, and create a sense of belonging.
8. Community-based learning: Incorporate community-based learning experiences that allow students to engage in hands-on projects and activities that are relevant to their cultural heritage and local context.
9. Support for mental health: Provide support for the mental health and well-being of Native American students, who may be impacted by historical trauma, intergenerational trauma, or ongoing systemic oppression.
