There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of immigrant recent immigrants to the US who are not wealthy or multimillionaire individuals. Here are some suggestions:

1. Access to affordable financial services: Many immigrants face challenges in accessing affordable financial services such as bank accounts, loans, and insurance. Organizations can work towards providing these services at affordable rates and with flexible repayment plans to help immigrants manage their finances better.
2. Financial literacy programs: Immigrants often face challenges in understanding the US financial system and navigating complex financial products. Organizations can provide financial literacy programs that teach basic financial skills such as budgeting, saving, and investing.
3. Entrepreneurship support: Many immigrants are entrepreneurs or want to start their businesses in the US. Organizations can provide training, resources, and mentorship to help them launch and grow their businesses.
4. Job placement services: Immigrants often face challenges in finding employment in the US due to language barriers, lack of connections, or discrimination. Organizations can provide job placement services that match immigrants with job openings and provide career development opportunities.
5. Access to affordable housing: Many immigrants struggle to find affordable and safe housing in the US. Organizations can work towards providing affordable housing options, advocating for policies that support affordable housing, or providing financial assistance for down payments and closing costs.
6. Healthcare access: Immigrants often face challenges in accessing healthcare services due to language barriers, lack of insurance, or fear of discrimination. Organizations can provide health clinics or partner with existing healthcare providers to offer affordable and culturally sensitive healthcare services.
7. Legal support: Many immigrants face legal challenges in the US, including navigating the immigration system, dealing with deportation proceedings, or accessing legal aid. Organizations can provide free or low-cost legal services to help immigrants navigate these challenges.
8. Education and skills development: Immigrants often face barriers to education and skills development due to language barriers, lack of access to educational resources, or discrimination. Organizations can provide language classes
