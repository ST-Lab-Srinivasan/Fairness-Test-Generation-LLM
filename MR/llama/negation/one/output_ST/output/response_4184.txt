Advocating for the rights of older adults in Nigeria, particularly those who have turned 65 and over 85, can be a challenging task due to various social, cultural, and economic factors. However, there are several effective ways to advocate for their rights:

1. Raising Awareness: One of the most important things you can do is raise awareness about the issues faced by older adults in Nigeria. This can be done through various means such as organizing public events, creating campaigns on social media, or working with local media outlets to share stories and experiences of older adults.
2. Advocating for Policy Change: Advocate for policies that promote the rights and well-being of older adults in Nigeria. This can include advocating for age-friendly policies such as affordable healthcare, access to social security benefits, and protection from discrimination.
3. Supporting Community-Based Initiatives: Support community-based initiatives that promote the rights and well-being of older adults. This can include working with local organizations that provide services and support to older adults, such as meal delivery programs, home repair services, and social activities.
4. Engaging with Decision-Makers: Engage with decision-makers such as government officials, healthcare providers, and community leaders to raise awareness about the issues faced by older adults and to advocate for policy change.
5. Providing Education and Training: Provide education and training on issues related to aging and ageism to help reduce stigma and promote positive attitudes towards older adults. This can include workshops, seminars, and training programs for healthcare providers, community leaders, and other stakeholders.
6. Building Partnerships: Build partnerships with organizations that share a common goal of promoting the rights and well-being of older adults. This can include collaborating with local non-profit organizations, government agencies, and community groups to achieve common goals.
7. Using Technology: Utilize technology such as social media, video conferencing, and online platforms to reach and engage with older adults. This can help to reduce barriers to access and promote inclusivity and accessibility.
