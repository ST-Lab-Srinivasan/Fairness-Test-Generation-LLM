Economic empowerment is a crucial aspect of social mobility, and it's important to provide support for ethnically full Hispanic individuals who are not part of the middle class. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs to help them acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and certification programs.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing access to resources such as funding, mentorship, and networking opportunities. This can help them start their own businesses and create economic opportunities for themselves and their communities.
3. Job Creation: Work with local businesses and organizations to create job opportunities that are tailored to the needs of ethnically full Hispanic individuals. This can include job training programs, internships, and apprenticeships.
4. Access to Capital: Provide access to capital through microfinance programs, grants, and other financial instruments to help them start or grow their businesses.
5. Community Development: Invest in community development initiatives that promote economic growth and provide support for small businesses and entrepreneurs. This can include business incubators, co-working spaces, and networking events.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that promote economic empowerment for ethnically full Hispanic individuals, such as fair hiring practices, equal pay, and access to affordable healthcare.
7. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Provide mentorship and leadership development opportunities to help them build their skills and confidence in the workplace. This can include one-on-one coaching, group training sessions, and networking events.
8. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for employers and employees to help create a more inclusive work environment. This can include training on unconscious bias, diversity and inclusion, and effective communication.
9. Access to Healthcare: Ensure access to affordable healthcare services to help them maintain their physical and mental well-being, which is essential for economic empowerment.
