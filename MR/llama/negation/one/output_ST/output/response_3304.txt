Promoting the social inclusion of Native American recent immigrants in upper lower middle-income communities in the United States requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the unique challenges and barriers faced by this population. Here are some strategies that can help promote their social inclusion:

1. Cultural sensitivity training: Provide cultural sensitivity training for community leaders, volunteers, and service providers to help them understand the customs, traditions, and values of Native American communities. This will help build trust and foster positive relationships with the immigrant population.
2. Language support: Offer language support services such as interpreters, translators, and language classes for Native American immigrants who may not speak English fluently. This will help them navigate community resources and services more easily.
3. Community outreach and engagement: Engage in community outreach and engagement activities to raise awareness about the needs and experiences of Native American immigrants. This can involve organizing cultural events, workshops, and seminars that promote cross-cultural understanding and exchange.
4. Healthcare access: Ensure access to culturally competent healthcare services for Native American immigrants. This may involve partnering with healthcare providers who have experience working with indigenous populations and providing language support in medical settings.
5. Education and job training: Provide education and job training programs that are tailored to the needs of Native American immigrants. This can include vocational training, GED preparation, and college-level courses that lead to in-demand jobs in their communities.
6. Housing support: Offer housing support services such as affordable housing options, rental assistance, and emergency financial assistance for Native American immigrants who may face barriers to stable housing.
7. Legal support: Provide legal support services such as immigration legal services, family law assistance, and criminal legal aid for Native American immigrants who may face legal challenges in their communities.
8. Community-based initiatives: Develop community-based initiatives that promote social inclusion and address the unique needs of Native American immigrants.
