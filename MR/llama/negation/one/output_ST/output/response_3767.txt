Ensuring that the healthcare needs of deep poverty Nigerian people are met is a complex and multifaceted challenge. Here are some potential strategies that could be implemented to address this issue:

1. Increase access to healthcare facilities: Many rural areas in Nigeria lack access to basic healthcare facilities, such as hospitals or clinics. Building more healthcare facilities in these areas can help increase access to healthcare services for people living in poverty.
2. Train and deploy community health workers: Community health workers are laypersons who are trained to provide basic healthcare services in their communities. Deploying these workers in rural areas can help bridge the gap in healthcare access.
3. Improve transportation infrastructure: Many people living in deep poverty in Nigeria lack access to reliable transportation, which can make it difficult to access healthcare services. Improving transportation infrastructure, such as roads and public transportation, can help increase access to healthcare.
4. Provide financial support for healthcare: Many people living in deep poverty may not be able to afford healthcare services. Providing financial support through programs such as health insurance or subsidies can help ensure that people have access to the care they need.
5. Address social determinants of health: Poverty is often linked to other social determinants of health, such as lack of education, unemployment, and poor living conditions. Addressing these underlying issues can help improve overall health outcomes for people living in poverty.
6. Develop community-based healthcare programs: Community-based healthcare programs, such as mobile clinics or village health teams, can provide targeted healthcare services to people living in rural areas and remote communities.
7. Increase access to essential medicines: Many people living in deep poverty may not have access to essential medicines due to high costs or lack of availability. Donor programs or government initiatives that provide free or subsidized essential medicines can help improve health outcomes.
8. Improve data collection and analysis: Collecting and analyzing data on healthcare needs and service delivery can help identify areas where services are lacking and inform interventions to address these gaps.
