Creating more inclusive community programs for recently divorced individuals, particularly those who have gone through multiple marriages, requires a thoughtful and compassionate approach. Here are some suggestions to help make these programs more welcoming:

1. Understand the unique needs of this population: Recognize that individuals who have been through multiple divorces may face different challenges than those in their first marriage. They may have emotional scars, financial strains, and social isolation. Consider offering specialized support groups or counseling sessions tailored to their specific needs.
2. Provide flexible programming: Offer programs that are adaptable to the diverse schedules and preferences of this population. For instance, consider holding group meetings at different times or providing online options for those who cannot attend in-person events.
3. Address stigma and discrimination: Sadly, many communities still view divorce as a negative or shameful experience. Work to overcome these attitudes by promoting the idea that divorce is a normal life event and that seeking support is okay. Educate your staff, volunteers, and participants about the importance of creating an inclusive environment for all.
4. Foster a sense of belonging: Help recently divorced individuals connect with others who share similar experiences. Organize social events or activities that encourage mingling and building relationships. You could also consider hosting special events, like potlucks or game nights, where participants can share their interests and hobbies.
5. Provide resources for financial stability: Many individuals going through divorce may face economic challenges. Consider offering budgeting workshops, financial planning assistance, or access to local resources that can help them get back on their feet.
6. Address the needs of LGBTQ+ individuals: Ensure your programs are inclusive of LGBTQ+ individuals by providing support groups specifically for this community or by offering gender-neutral facilities and materials.
7. Be mindful of accessibility: Make sure your programs are accessible to participants with disabilities by providing accommodations such as wheelchair ramps, Braille materials, or sign language interpretation.
