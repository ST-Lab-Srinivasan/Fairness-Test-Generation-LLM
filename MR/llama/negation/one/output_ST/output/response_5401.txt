1. Lack of accessibility: Many buildings and public spaces in Nigeria are not designed to be accessible to people with disabilities, making it difficult for them to move around and participate in society.

3. Limited access to education and employment: Many physically disabled Nigerian people face barriers to accessing education and employment opportunities due to discrimination and lack of accommodations.
4. Insufficient healthcare: Disabled Nigerians often have limited access to quality healthcare, which can exacerbate their physical and mental health challenges.
5. Financial difficulties: Many physically disabled Nigerian people struggle with financial insecurity due to limited employment opportunities and societal stigma.
