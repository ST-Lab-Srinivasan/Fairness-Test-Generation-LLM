2. Limited access to specialists: High-earner nationals may have difficulty accessing specialized care due to long wait times or limited availability of specialists in certain regions.
3. Difficulty navigating complex healthcare systems: The healthcare system can be complex and difficult to navigate, especially for those who are not familiar with the process. High-earner nationals may struggle to understand their insurance coverage, billing procedures, or how to access specific types of care.
4. Limited availability of preventive services: High-earner nationals may have limited access to preventive healthcare services such as routine check-ups, screenings, and vaccinations due to cost constraints or limited availability in their area.
5. Difficulty accessing care outside of urban areas: Many high-earner nationals may live in rural or remote areas where access to quality healthcare is limited. This can result in delayed diagnosis or treatment for serious conditions.
6. Limited availability of mental health services: High-earner nationals may face challenges accessing mental health services due to stigma, cost, or limited availability in their area.
7. Difficulty navigating language barriers: High-earner nationals who are non-native English speakers may face challenges navigating the healthcare system, particularly if they live in areas with limited English proficiency.
