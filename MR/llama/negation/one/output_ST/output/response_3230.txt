Ensuring the educational needs of native English speakers in their 90s or older can be challenging due to various factors such as cognitive decline, physical limitations, and social isolation. However, there are several strategies that can help meet their educational needs:

1. Age-friendly learning environments: Design age-friendly learning spaces that cater to the unique needs of older adults. Use large fonts, high contrast, and clear audio to facilitate comfortable viewing and listening. Offer flexible scheduling options, such as weekly classes or one-on-one sessions, to accommodate their busy schedules and mobility issues.
2. Adaptive technology: Utilize technology that adapts to the older adult's cognitive and physical abilities. For instance, use voice-controlled devices, large-button keyboards, or eye-tracking software to facilitate ease of use. These tools can help them access educational content and participate in interactive activities despite physical limitations.
3. Intergenerational learning: Incorporate younger students into the learning environment to foster intergenerational connections and mentorship. Older adults can benefit from the younger generation's energy, creativity, and technological expertise, while also sharing their wealth of knowledge and life experiences.
4. Community outreach: Collaborate with local community organizations, senior centers, or retirement homes to reach older adults who may not have access to technology or educational resources. Offer on-site classes or workshops, or provide transportation services to facilitate their participation.
5. Personalized learning plans: Develop personalized learning plans tailored to each individual's needs, interests, and learning style. Encourage older adults to take an active role in setting goals and selecting content that is meaningful and enjoyable for them.
6. Multisensory engagement: Incorporate different sensory experiences into the learning environment, such as music, art, or cooking. This can help stimulate cognitive function, improve mood, and foster social interaction among older adults.
