Ensuring the healthcare needs of individuals who are just above the poverty line, particularly those who speak Arabic, can be challenging due to several factors. Here are some potential strategies that could help:

1. Language Accessibility: Provide language accessibility services, such as interpreters or translated materials, to ensure that these individuals have equal access to healthcare information and services. This could involve hiring interpreters who speak the local dialects of the areas where these individuals live.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for healthcare providers to help them understand the unique needs and challenges faced by this population. This training could cover topics such as cultural norms, beliefs, and practices related to healthcare.
3. Outreach and Education: Develop outreach programs that target these individuals and provide education on how to access healthcare services, what services are available, and how to navigate the healthcare system. This could involve partnering with community organizations and religious leaders to reach this population.
4. Partnership with Local Organizations: Partner with local organizations that serve this population, such as community clinics or non-profit organizations, to provide healthcare services and support. This can help ensure that these individuals have access to the care they need.
5. Telemedicine Services: Utilize telemedicine services to provide remote consultations and care to individuals who may face barriers in accessing healthcare facilities. This can be particularly useful for those living in rural or hard-to-reach areas.
6. Insurance Support: Work with insurance providers to ensure that these individuals have access to affordable health insurance options. This can help them access the care they need without facing financial barriers.
7. Community Health Workers: Train community health workers who are fluent in Arabic and familiar with the local culture to provide healthcare support and outreach within the community.
8. Patient Navigators: Train patient navigators who can assist these individuals in navigating the healthcare system, scheduling appointments, and communicating with providers.
