Supporting the economic empowerment of deep poverty Native American communities is crucial for addressing the significant disparities that exist in income, wealth, and access to resources. Here are some ways to support the economic empowerment of Native natural-born US citizens:

1. Provide Access to Education and Training:
Offer culturally appropriate education and job training programs tailored to the needs of Native American communities. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and higher education opportunities.
2. Encourage Entrepreneurship:
Support Native-owned businesses through access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities. Provide resources for entrepreneurs to start and grow their businesses, such as small business loans, incubators, and accelerators.
3. Develop Sustainable Economic Development Strategies:
Collaborate with Native American communities to develop sustainable economic development strategies that prioritize community-driven initiatives and promote economic self-determination. This can involve investing in infrastructure, agriculture, and renewable energy projects that benefit the community.
4. Address Lack of Access to Capital:
Native American communities often face barriers to accessing capital for businesses and community development projects. Provide access to affordable capital through financial instruments such as loan guarantees, grants, and impact investing.
5. Promote Local Food Systems:
Support Native American farmers and food producers by providing access to markets, training, and infrastructure support. This can help build resilience in local food systems and promote economic development that benefits the community.
6. Invest in Cultural Preservation:
Invest in cultural preservation programs that support the revitalization of Native languages, traditional practices, and artistic expression. This can involve supporting language immersion schools, cultural centers, and artist-in-residence programs.
7. Foster Community Partnerships:
Develop partnerships between Native American communities and mainstream organizations to promote collaboration and mutual learning. This can involve capacity building, training, and technical assistance for community-based organizations.
