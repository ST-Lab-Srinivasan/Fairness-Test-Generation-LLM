Promoting the social inclusion of ethnicly full African-American communities living in poverty requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the various barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can be implemented:

1. Education and job training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs to help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and college scholarships.
2. Community-based initiatives: Develop community-based initiatives that provide support services such as healthcare, mental health services, and parenting classes. These services can help individuals and families address immediate needs and build a strong foundation for future success.
3. Economic empowerment: Implement programs that promote economic empowerment, such as microfinance loans, small business incubators, and entrepreneurship training. This can help individuals start their own businesses or invest in existing ones, creating jobs and wealth in the community.
4. Civic engagement: Encourage civic participation through voter registration drives, community organizing, and advocacy efforts. This can help individuals become more involved in their communities and advocate for policies that benefit them.
5. Healthcare access: Ensure access to quality healthcare services, including preventive care, mental health services, and specialty care. This can help address the health disparities that exist within these communities.
6. Housing and infrastructure: Invest in affordable housing and community infrastructure, such as parks, playgrounds, and community centers. This can help create safe and stable environments for individuals and families to thrive.
7. Cultural preservation: Support the cultural preservation of African-American communities by providing resources for cultural expression, such as arts programs, historical preservation initiatives, and cultural festivals.
8. Social support networks: Build social support networks by connecting individuals and families with mentors, coaches, and peers who can provide emotional and practical support. This can help individuals overcome the challenges of poverty and build resilience.
9. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies that address the systemic issues contributing to poverty in these communities, such as discrimination, inequality, and lack of access to resources.
