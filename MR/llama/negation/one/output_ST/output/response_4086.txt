Promoting the educational success of Native American natural-born US citizens is crucial for closing the achievement gap and ensuring that these students have equal opportunities to succeed. Here are some strategies that educators, policymakers, and communities can implement:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Teachers should incorporate traditional Native American teachings, practices, and values into their curriculum to make it more relatable and engaging for students. This can include using storytelling, music, and art from Native cultures to enhance learning.
2. Bilingual education: Offering bilingual education programs in Native languages can help students develop a strong foundation in reading, writing, and math while also preserving their cultural heritage.
3. Indigenous knowledge integration: Incorporating traditional ecological knowledge and practices into science and environmental education can help students connect with their cultural identity and the natural world.
4. Community partnerships: Building partnerships with local Native American communities can provide opportunities for students to learn from elders, participate in cultural events, and gain exposure to traditional ways of life.
5. Mentorship programs: Establishing mentorship programs that pair Native students with successful role models from their community can help build confidence, identity, and academic success.
6. Addressing historical trauma: Recognizing the historical trauma faced by Native American communities and incorporating strategies to address this trauma into educational settings can help students feel safe and supported in their learning journey.
7. Curriculum redesign: Redesigning curricula to include more culturally responsive and inclusive content can help Native students see themselves reflected in their education and feel a sense of belonging.
8. Parent and family engagement: Engaging with parents and families through language and cultural events, workshops, and activities can help build a supportive learning environment for Native American students.
9. Professional development: Providing ongoing professional development opportunities for teachers to learn about Native American cultures, histories, and pedagogies can help them better serve Native students in their classrooms.
