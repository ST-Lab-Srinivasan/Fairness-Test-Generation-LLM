2. Inadequate training: Healthcare professionals may not receive adequate training in disability awareness and accessibility, which can lead to misunderstandings or lack of knowledge about how to provide appropriate care for people with disabilities.
3. Stigma and discrimination: People with disabilities may experience stigma and discrimination from healthcare professionals, which can affect their mental and emotional well-being. This can also lead to delayed or inadequate treatment.
4. Limited access to specialized care: People with complex disabilities may require specialized care and equipment, but these services may not be readily available or accessible within the healthcare system.
5. Financial barriers: People with disabilities may face financial barriers to accessing medical care, including high out-of-pocket costs for prescription medications, medical devices, and other treatments.
6. Lack of advocacy: People with disabilities may not have access to advocacy services or support, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the healthcare system and ensure their needs are met.
7. Limited transportation options: People with disabilities may face challenges in accessing medical care due to limited transportation options, such as lack of wheelchair-accessible vehicles or transportation services that are not adaptable to their needs.
