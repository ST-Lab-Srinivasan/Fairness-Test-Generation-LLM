Creating more inclusive community programs for civilian recently divorced individuals, including those who have been married multiple times, requires a thoughtful and compassionate approach. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Understand the challenges faced by this population: Divorce can be a complex and emotionally draining experience, particularly for those who have gone through it multiple times. It's essential to recognize the unique challenges faced by these individuals, such as financial insecurity, emotional turmoil, and social isolation.
2. Conduct a needs assessment: Engage with members of this population to understand their specific needs and concerns. This can involve surveys, focus groups, or one-on-one interviews. Identify the areas where they need support, such as financial management, mental health counseling, or social networking opportunities.
3. Develop a comprehensive program: Design a program that addresses the identified needs and provides a range of services, including:
	* Financial literacy and management training: Offer workshops or classes on budgeting, debt management, and investment planning to help individuals gain control over their finances.
	* Mental health counseling: Provide access to therapy sessions or support groups for individuals dealing with anxiety, depression, or other mental health issues related to divorce.
	* Social networking opportunities: Organize events or activities that bring together individuals who have gone through similar experiences, providing a safe space for them to connect and build relationships.
	* Childcare and parenting support: Offer resources and services for parents who may be struggling to balance work and family responsibilities, such as on-site childcare or parenting classes.
4. Build partnerships with local organizations: Collaborate with local community organizations, social service agencies, and religious groups to leverage their expertise and resources. This can help reach a wider audience and provide more comprehensive support.
5. Promote the program: Utilize various marketing strategies to raise awareness about the program among the target population. This may involve advertising, social media campaigns, or outreach through local community centers or non-profit organizations.
6. Evaluate and improve the program: Regularly assess the effectiveness of the program and gather feedback from participants
