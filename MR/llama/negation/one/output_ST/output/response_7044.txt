2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage helps define who these individuals are and how they relate to others in their community. It influences their beliefs, values, and practices, shaping their identity and informing their actions.
3. Preservation of traditions: Traditionalist Native Chinese speakers often place great importance on preserving their cultural heritage, including traditional practices, customs, and beliefs. This can involve passing knowledge and skills down through generations, maintaining traditional ceremonies and rituals, and perpetuating the language and culture.
4. Connection to ancestors: Cultural heritage serves as a connection to their ancestors and the past. For these individuals, honoring their ancestors and respecting their traditions is crucial to their identity and well-being.
