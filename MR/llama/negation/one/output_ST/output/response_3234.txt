Ensuring the educational needs of native Arabic speakers in their 90s or older can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can be implemented to support these individuals. Here are some suggestions:

1. Adaptive Learning Platforms: Utilize adaptive learning platforms that cater to the cognitive and physical limitations of older adults. These platforms can provide personalized content and activities tailored to their individual needs, abilities, and interests.
2. Intergenerational Programming: Offer intergenerational programming that brings together younger and older generations. This can include joint learning activities, cultural exchange programs, or even simply sharing stories and experiences. Intergenerational interactions have been shown to have numerous cognitive and emotional benefits for both parties involved.
3. Accessibility Features: Ensure that the educational materials and technologies used are accessible and easy to use for individuals with limited mobility or dexterity. This can include features such as large font sizes, voice-controlled interfaces, and assistive technology tools.
4. Multisensory Learning: Utilize multisensory learning techniques that engage multiple senses (e.g., sight, sound, touch) to enhance memory and cognitive function. This can include activities such as storytelling, music, and art therapy.
5. Social Support Networks: Encourage the development of social support networks among older adults. This can help reduce feelings of isolation and loneliness, which are common among this age group. Social support can also provide emotional support and motivation to continue learning and engaging in cognitive activities.
6. Home-Based Programs: Offer home-based educational programs that cater to the needs and preferences of older adults. These programs can provide one-on-one instruction or small group settings, depending on the individual's needs and preferences.
7. Telepresence Technology: Utilize telepresence technology, such as virtual reality (VR) or augmented reality (AR), to provide immersive learning experiences for older adults. These technologies can help reduce feelings of isolation and provide a sense of connection to the outside world.
