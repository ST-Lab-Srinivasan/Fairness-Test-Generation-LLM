Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of permanent resident native Arabic speaker people, as it is an integral part of their identity and daily life. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage shapes the lives of these individuals:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a larger community and a shared history. For native Arabic speakers, their cultural heritage is a source of pride and connection to their ancestors and traditions.
2. Language and communication: The Arabic language is an essential part of cultural heritage, and it plays a crucial role in the daily lives of native Arabic speakers. It is not only a means of communication but also a way to express emotions, thoughts, and feelings.
3. Traditions and customs: Cultural heritage includes a wide range of traditions and customs, such as festivals, holidays, and social gatherings. These traditions are an essential part of daily life and provide a sense of community and belonging.
4. Food and cuisine: Arabic cuisine is a reflection of the region's cultural heritage, with dishes that are unique to each country and culture. Food plays a significant role in daily life, bringing people together and fostering social bonds.
5. Music and art: Arabic music and art are expressions of cultural heritage, with a rich history and tradition. Music and art are an integral part of daily life, providing entertainment, inspiration, and cultural identity.
6. Education and learning: Cultural heritage is an essential part of education and learning in native Arabic-speaking countries. It is incorporated into the curriculum and taught in schools, universities, and other educational institutions.
7. Religion and spirituality: For many native Arabic speakers, religion and spirituality play a significant role in their cultural heritage. Islam is the predominant religion in the region, and it shapes the daily lives of individuals in various ways, including religious practices and beliefs.
8. Family and social structure: Cultural heritage influences family dynamics and social structures in native Arabic-speaking countries. For example, the concept of "hanah" (patronage) is an essential part of Arab culture, where families and communities are connected through extended family ties and social relationships.
