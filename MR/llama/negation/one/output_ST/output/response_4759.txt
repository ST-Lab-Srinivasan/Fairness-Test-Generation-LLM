Not Lower Middle-Income Mixed Asian Heritage People in the Healthcare System: Challenges and Barriers

Introduction:

Mixed Asian heritage individuals face a unique set of challenges in the healthcare system. They may identify with multiple cultures and ethnicities, which can lead to complex healthcare needs and experiences. This article will explore the specific challenges faced by not lower middle-income mixed Asian heritage people in the healthcare system, including cultural barriers, language barriers, discrimination, and healthcare access issues.

Cultural Barriers:

Mixed Asian heritage individuals may face cultural barriers in accessing healthcare services. They may feel uncomfortable disclosing their cultural identity or may experience discrimination based on their race or ethnicity. For example, a study found that mixed-race individuals were more likely to experience racial discrimination in healthcare settings compared to those who identified with a single race (1).

Language Barriers:

Not lower middle-income mixed Asian heritage people may face language barriers in accessing healthcare services. They may not speak the dominant language of the healthcare system, which can hinder communication and create misunderstandings. For instance, a study found that limited English proficiency was a significant barrier to healthcare access among Asian Americans (2).

Discrimination:

Mixed Asian heritage individuals may experience discrimination in the healthcare system due to their race or ethnicity. They may face stereotyping, stigmatization, and biases from healthcare providers, which can lead to inadequate care. For example, a study found that Asian American patients experienced discrimination in hospital settings, including being treated with less respect than white patients (3).

Healthcare Access Issues:

Not lower middle-income mixed Asian heritage people may face challenges in accessing healthcare services due to systemic barriers. They may have limited access to healthcare providers who are knowledgeable about their cultural and linguistic needs, or they may face financial barriers due to the high cost of healthcare. For instance, a study found that Asian American immigrants faced significant barriers in accessing healthcare services, including language barriers and lack of insurance coverage (4).
