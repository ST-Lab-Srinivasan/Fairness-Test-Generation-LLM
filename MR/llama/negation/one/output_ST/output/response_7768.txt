Ensuring the healthcare needs of long-term unemployed Nigerian people can be challenging due to several factors, including limited access to healthcare facilities, financial constraints, and lack of awareness about available healthcare services. Here are some strategies that can help meet their healthcare needs:

1. Increase access to primary healthcare services: Expanding the network of primary healthcare centers and providing mobile clinics can help reach marginalized communities, including those who are long-term unemployed. These facilities should be equipped with basic medical supplies and staffed with trained healthcare professionals.
2. Provide financial assistance: Implementing programs that offer financial assistance, such as subsidies or free healthcare services, can help reduce the financial burden of seeking healthcare, particularly for those who are unemployed and have limited resources.
3. Promote awareness about available healthcare services: Educating the community about the availability of healthcare services and the importance of seeking medical care can increase utilization rates among marginalized groups. This can be achieved through outreach programs, community engagement, and social mobilization.
4. Foster partnerships with NGOs and CBOs: Collaborating with non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and community-based organizations (CBOs) can help leverage their expertise and resources to provide healthcare services to marginalized communities. These partnerships can also help reach remote or hard-to-reach areas.
5. Develop a comprehensive health insurance scheme: Implementing a nationwide health insurance program can help ensure that the unemployed have access to quality healthcare services without incurring excessive financial costs. The scheme should be designed to cover essential healthcare services, including preventive care, primary care, and specialist consultations.
6. Provide mental health support: Unemployment can lead to psychological distress, anxiety, and depression. Offering mental health support services, such as counseling and therapy, can help address these issues and improve overall well-being.
