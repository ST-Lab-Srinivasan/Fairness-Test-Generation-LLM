Employed mixed Asian heritage people in the healthcare system may face a range of challenges, including:

1. Cultural and identity issues: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may experience cultural and identity conflicts within the healthcare system, which can affect their sense of belonging and self-esteem. They may feel like they don't fit into any one cultural category or that they are not accepted by any particular group.
2. Microaggressions and bias: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may experience microaggressions and bias from healthcare professionals, patients, or colleagues. These experiences can be subtle and may go unnoticed, but they can still have a significant impact on an individual's well-being and sense of belonging.
3. Lack of representation and role models: The lack of representation and role models in leadership positions within the healthcare system can make it difficult for mixed Asian heritage individuals to see themselves in these roles and feel inspired to pursue similar careers.
4. Stereotyping and assumptions: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may be subject to stereotypes and assumptions based on their race or ethnicity, which can affect how they are perceived and treated by healthcare professionals and patients.
5. Language barriers: For mixed Asian heritage individuals who speak multiple languages, there may be language barriers within the healthcare system that can make it difficult to communicate effectively with patients or colleagues.
6. Difficulty navigating institutional racism: The healthcare system can be riddled with institutional racism and discrimination, which can make it challenging for mixed Asian heritage individuals to navigate the system and access the care they need.
7. Limited cultural competence training: Healthcare professionals may not have received adequate cultural competence training, which can result in a lack of understanding and empathy for patients from diverse backgrounds.
8. Invisibility and erasure: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may feel invisible or erased within the healthcare system, particularly if they do not identify with a particular race or ethnicity.
9. Difficulty accessing culturally competent care: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may face challenges accessing culturally competent care due to a lack of cultural understanding and empathy among healthcare professionals.
