Language exchange programs: Many beginners have started language exchange programs in their local communities, where they meet with other learners and native speakers to practice speaking and listening to Arabic. 3. Language learning apps: Some beginners have developed language learning apps that provide interactive lessons and exercises for learners at all levels. These apps can be used to supplement traditional classroom instruction or to provide additional practice outside of class.
4. Tutoring services: Many beginners offer tutoring services to help others learn Arabic. They may charge a small fee for their services or offer them pro bono.
5. Community language classes: Beginners have started community language classes in their local areas, where they teach Arabic to other beginners and non-native speakers. These classes can be held at local libraries, community centers, or even in homes.
