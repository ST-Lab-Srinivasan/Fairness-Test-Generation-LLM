Creating more inclusive community events for ex-military Nigerian people requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions:

1. Understand the needs and preferences of the ex-military community: It is essential to engage with the ex-military community to understand their specific needs and preferences. This can be done through surveys, focus groups, or one-on-one interviews.
2. Provide accessible facilities: Ensure that the event venue is accessible to people with disabilities and has adequate parking, seating, and restrooms.
3. Offer support services: Provide support services such as counseling, job placement, and mental health resources to help ex-military personnel transition smoothly into civilian life.
4. Promote inclusivity: Use promotional materials that reflect the diversity of the community, including images and language that are inclusive of all participants.
5. Train staff and volunteers: Provide training for staff and volunteers on how to interact with ex-military personnel, including cultural sensitivity and understanding their unique needs.
6. Encourage participation: Create opportunities for ex-military personnel to participate in decision-making processes and to have a voice in the planning of events.
7. Collaborate with other organizations: Partner with other organizations that serve the ex-military community to reach a broader audience and provide more comprehensive support.
8. Offer childcare and family-friendly activities: Provide childcare and family-friendly activities to enable spouses and children to participate in events without worrying about care arrangements.
9. Cater to dietary restrictions: Provide options for meals that cater to the diverse dietary needs of participants, including vegetarian, vegan, and gluten-free options.
10. Follow up with attendees: Send a follow-up survey or email to attendees to gather feedback and assess the success of the event in promoting inclusivity and accessibility.

By implementing these strategies, you can create more inclusive community events for ex-military Nigerian people, fostering a sense of belonging and connection within the community.
