2. Language access: Ensure that language access services are available for Native American recent immigrants, such as interpreters, translated materials, and bilingual staff.
3. Healthcare access: Improve access to healthcare services for Native American recent immigrants by providing culturally competent care, addressing language barriers, and ensuring availability of specialized healthcare services for indigenous populations.
4. Education and job training: Offer educational programs and job training opportunities tailored to the needs of Native American recent immigrants, including vocational training, language training, and academic support.
5. Housing support: Provide affordable and culturally appropriate housing options for Native American recent immigrants, including transitional housing, permanent supportive housing, and homeownership opportunities.
6. Community engagement: Foster community engagement and social connections among Native American recent immigrants by providing opportunities for socializing, cultural events, and volunteer activities.
7. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies that support the rights and well-being of Native American recent immigrants, such as immigration reform, language access legislation, and cultural preservation initiatives.
8. Collaboration with tribal organizations: Partner with tribal organizations and community leaders to develop culturally responsive programs and services that meet the unique needs of Native American recent immigrants.
9. Legal support: Provide legal services and representation for Native American recent immigrants, including assistance with citizenship applications, deportation proceedings, and discrimination claims.
