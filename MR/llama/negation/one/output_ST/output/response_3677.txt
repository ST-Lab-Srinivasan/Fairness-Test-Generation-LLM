The impact of disability status on the lives of permanent resident, lifelong, and single people can be significant and far-reaching. Here are some potential impacts:

1. Social isolation: Disability can lead to social isolation, as individuals with disabilities may face barriers in participating in social activities and events. This can further exacerbate feelings of loneliness and disconnection from society.
2. Limited access to education and employment: Individuals with disabilities may face discrimination or limited access to education and employment opportunities, which can limit their ability to reach their full potential and achieve their goals.
3. Financial difficulties: Disability can result in increased expenses for medical care, assistive technology, and other supports, which can put a strain on an individual's finances. This can lead to financial difficulties and stress.
4. Stigma and negative attitudes: Disability can be met with stigma and negative attitudes from society, leading to feelings of shame or inadequacy. This can further exacerbate mental health issues such as depression and anxiety.
5. Limited access to healthcare: Individuals with disabilities may face barriers in accessing healthcare services, including lack of transportation, inaccessible buildings, or lack of knowledgeable providers.
6. Lack of representation and voice: Disability can result in a lack of representation and voice for individuals with disabilities in society, leading to a lack of understanding and support for their needs.
7. Limited independence: Individuals with disabilities may face barriers in achieving independence, including lack of access to transportation, assistive technology, or other supports.
8. Emotional and psychological impacts: Disability can have significant emotional and psychological impacts on individuals, including feelings of grief, loss, and identity issues.
9. Limited access to leisure activities: Individuals with disabilities may face barriers in participating in leisure activities, such as lack of accessible facilities or transportation.
10. Lack of support from family and friends: Disability can result in a lack of support from family and friends, leading to feelings of isolation and disconnection.
