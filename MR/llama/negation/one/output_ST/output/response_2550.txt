2. Recognize prior learning: Recognize the prior learning and experience of veterans and provide opportunities for them to apply their skills and knowledge in a academic setting.
3. Offer mentorship and guidance: Provide mentorship and guidance to help veterans navigate the educational system and access resources that can support their goals.
4. Foster a sense of community: Create a sense of community among veteran students by providing opportunities for them to connect with peers and build relationships with others who share similar experiences.
5. Address mental health needs: Provide mental health support services specifically tailored to the needs of veterans, including counseling and stress management techniques.
6. Offer career guidance: Provide career guidance and job placement services to help veterans navigate their transition into civilian life and identify potential career paths.
7. Provide financial support: Offer financial aid and scholarships specifically designed for veterans to help them cover the costs of education.
8. Create a veteran-friendly environment: Create a learning environment that is welcoming and inclusive of veterans, with resources and supports specifically tailored to their needs.
9. Offer internship and job placement opportunities: Provide internship and job placement opportunities for veterans to gain practical work experience and build their professional networks.
10. Advocate for policies that support veteran education: Advocate for policies at the local, state, and federal levels that support the educational aspirations of veterans, such as tuition waivers, scholarships, and other forms of financial aid.
