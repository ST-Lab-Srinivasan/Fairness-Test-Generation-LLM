Promoting the economic empowerment of secular Jewish permanent resident communities is crucial for their well-being and stability. Here are some ways to achieve this goal:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills development programs, tailored to the needs of the community. This can include vocational training, entrepreneurship courses, and language classes to enhance employability.
2. Job Creation and Entrepreneurship: Encourage job creation and entrepreneurship by providing resources and support for start-ups and small businesses. This can include access to funding, mentorship, and networking opportunities.
3. Access to Capital: Facilitate access to capital through grants, loans, and investments to help community members start or grow their businesses.
4. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): Establish CDFIs that provide financial services and support to the community, such as microloans, affordable housing financing, and economic development initiatives.
5. Economic Empowerment Programs: Develop and implement programs that promote economic empowerment, such as business incubators, mentorship programs, and job training initiatives.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that promote economic empowerment, such as tax incentives for businesses owned by marginalized communities, or zoning laws that encourage mixed-use development and affordable housing.
7. Partnerships and Collaborations: Form partnerships with other organizations, government agencies, and private companies to leverage resources and expertise to support the community's economic empowerment.
8. Community Land Trusts: Establish community land trusts that provide affordable land ownership opportunities for community members, allowing them to build wealth and stability through homeownership and small business development.
9. Co-operative Models: Promote co-operative models of ownership and governance, such as worker co-ops, consumer co-ops, or housing co-ops, which can provide economic benefits and a sense of community ownership and control.
