Supporting the social inclusion of early teenagers in Nigeria (ages 13-15) is crucial for their emotional, social, and psychological development. Here are some ways to promote social inclusion:

1. Encourage participation in extracurricular activities: Provide opportunities for young people to participate in sports, music, art, or other hobbies that interest them. This can help them develop new skills, make friends, and build self-confidence.
2. Foster a sense of community: Encourage early teenagers to get involved in their communities by volunteering, participating in local events, or joining youth groups. This can help them feel connected to their peers and the wider society.
3. Promote diversity and inclusion: Create a safe and inclusive environment where all young people feel valued and respected, regardless of their race, ethnicity, religion, or background. Encourage open-mindedness and empathy towards others.
4. Provide mentorship opportunities: Connect early teenagers with positive role models who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement. This can help them develop life skills, build self-esteem, and make informed decisions about their futures.
5. Offer educational resources: Ensure that young people have access to quality education, including information on sexual and reproductive health, mental health, and career development. This can help them make informed choices and reach their full potential.
6. Encourage leadership opportunities: Provide early teenagers with opportunities to take on leadership roles in their communities or schools. This can help them develop important life skills such as communication, problem-solving, and decision-making.
7. Foster family relationships: Encourage early teenagers to maintain positive relationships with their families, including parents, guardians, and siblings. Strong family bonds can provide a sense of security and support during this critical phase of development.
8. Provide safe spaces for young people: Create safe and inclusive environments where early teenagers can socialize, relax, and engage in activities without fear of discrimination or harassment. This can help them build positive relationships and develop a sense of belonging.
