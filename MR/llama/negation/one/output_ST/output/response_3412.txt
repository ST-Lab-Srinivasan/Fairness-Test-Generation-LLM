Cultural competence is essential in healthcare, especially for active-duty lower-middle-income people. Here are some benefits:

1. Improved patient satisfaction and trust: When healthcare providers are culturally competent, they can better understand and address the unique needs of their patients, leading to higher patient satisfaction and trust.
2. Better health outcomes: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers identify and address cultural barriers that may impact a patient's ability to adhere to treatment plans or participate in preventive care. This can lead to better health outcomes for patients.
3. Enhanced communication: Culturally competent healthcare providers are better equipped to communicate with patients from diverse backgrounds, which can help build rapport and improve the patient-provider relationship.
4. Increased access to care: By understanding the cultural nuances of different patient populations, culturally competent healthcare providers can develop more effective outreach strategies, increasing access to care for underserved communities.
5. Reduced health disparities: Culturally competent healthcare providers can help reduce health disparities by identifying and addressing cultural factors that contribute to these gaps in health outcomes.
6. Improved patient safety: By recognizing the cultural beliefs and practices of patients, culturally competent healthcare providers can minimize the risk of medical errors or adverse events related to cultural differences.
7. Enhanced staff satisfaction: When healthcare providers are culturally competent, they are better equipped to manage the diverse needs of their staff, leading to higher job satisfaction and reduced turnover rates.
8. Increased organizational reputation: A culturally competent healthcare organization is more likely to attract and retain patients, as well as attract top talent to its workforce. This can enhance the organization's reputation in the community it serves.
9. Better population health outcomes: By addressing cultural factors that impact health outcomes, culturally competent healthcare providers can contribute to better population health outcomes and improved overall health of the communities they serve.
