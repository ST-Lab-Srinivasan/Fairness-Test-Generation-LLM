Title: Examining the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Beginner English Speaker Divorcees

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, especially during times of transition and change. When individuals experience life changes such as divorce, their cultural background can provide a sense of comfort, familiarity, and belonging. This study aims to explore the role of cultural heritage in shaping the identities of beginner English speaker divorcees.

Research Questions:

1. How do beginner English speaker divorcees perceive their cultural heritage during the process of divorce?
2. What impact does cultural heritage have on the identities of divorcees, particularly in terms of language use and communication?
3. How can cultural heritage be leveraged to support the language learning and identity formation of beginner English speaker divorcees?

Literature Review:
Previous research has shown that cultural heritage plays a crucial role in shaping individuals' identities, especially during times of change (Hood, 2009). Cultural heritage can provide a sense of belonging and identity to individuals, helping them navigate through difficult situations such as divorce (Gudykunst & Ting-Toomey, 1988). Additionally, language use and communication are closely tied to cultural heritage, with individuals' language choices reflecting their cultural backgrounds (Kirby, 2007).

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research design, using in-depth interviews with beginner English speaker divorcees. Participants will be recruited through local community centers and online Divorce support groups. The interview data will be analyzed using thematic analysis to identify patterns and trends in the participants' experiences.

Significance:
This study contributes to our understanding of how cultural heritage shapes the identities of beginner English speaker divorcees, particularly in terms of language use and communication. Findings from this research can inform language learning and identity formation strategies for this population, helping them navigate through the challenges of divorce.
