Creating more inclusive cultural programs for Native English Speaker Mixed African Americans (NESMAAs) requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some suggestions to help you create programs that are welcoming and inclusive of this diverse group:

1. Understand the NESMAA identity: Start by researching and understanding the experiences, histories, and cultural practices of NESMAAs. This will help you design programs that are relevant and meaningful to them.
2. Involve NESMAAs in program planning: Engage NESMAAs in the planning process to ensure that their perspectives and needs are taken into account. This can involve focus groups, surveys, or consultations with community leaders.
3. Offer a range of cultural experiences: Provide a variety of cultural programs that cater to different interests and preferences among NESMAAs. For example, you could offer cultural events that showcase African American music, art, literature, and history, as well as programs that highlight the diverse cultural practices of other ethnic groups.
4. Foster inclusive spaces: Create spaces where NESMAAs feel comfortable and included. This can involve making sure that your program venues are accessible and welcoming, and that your staff is trained to be culturally sensitive and responsive.
5. Promote cross-cultural exchange: Encourage interaction and dialogue between NESMAAs and other cultural groups. This can help break down stereotypes and promote understanding and empathy between different communities.
6. Address intersectionality: Be mindful of the intersections of race, ethnicity, gender, sexuality, and other identity factors that may impact NESMAAs' experiences. Design programs that are inclusive of these intersections and address the unique needs and challenges faced by this group.
7. Collaborate with community organizations: Partner with local organizations that serve NESMAAs to leverage their expertise and resources. This can help ensure that your programs are grounded in the needs and experiences of this community.
8. Provide education and training: Offer workshops or training sessions on cultural competency, diversity, equity, and inclusion. This can help staff and volunteers better understand and address the unique challenges faced by NESMAAs.
