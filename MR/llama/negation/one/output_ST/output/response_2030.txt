Creating more inclusive cultural programs for native English speakers and recent immigrants to the US requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some strategies that organizations and institutions can use to make their cultural programs more welcoming and inclusive:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training on cultural competency for staff and volunteers involved in developing and delivering cultural programs. This training can help them understand the diverse backgrounds and experiences of the communities they serve, and how to create programs that are sensitive to those differences.
2. Inclusive Programming: Develop cultural programs that are inclusive of all communities, including native English speakers and recent immigrants. This can involve incorporating elements from a variety of cultures, such as music, dance, art, and food. Make sure the programs are accessible to people with different levels of English proficiency.
3. Bilingual Programming: Offer bilingual programming that caters to both native English speakers and recent immigrants. This can include offering programs in multiple languages or providing translation services for those who may not speak English fluently.
4. Partnerships with Immigrant Organizations: Collaborate with organizations that serve immigrant communities to co-create and promote cultural programs. These partnerships can help reach a broader audience and ensure that the programs are tailored to meet the needs of diverse communities.
5. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and engagement activities to raise awareness about cultural programs and make them accessible to a wider audience. This can involve hosting events in underserved communities, partnering with local organizations, or using social media and other digital channels to promote the programs.
6. Feedback Mechanisms: Establish feedback mechanisms that allow participants to provide input on the cultural programs and suggest areas for improvement. This can help ensure that the programs are responsive to the needs of diverse communities and continue to evolve and improve over time.
7. Intersectional Approach: Take an intersectional approach to cultural programming, considering the intersections of race, ethnicity, gender, sexuality, and other factors that impact cultural identity.
