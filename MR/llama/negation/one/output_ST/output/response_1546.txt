1. Right to Education Act (2009): This act recognized the right of children between the ages of 6 and 14 to receive free and compulsory education, including those from Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes. The act also established a framework for the appointment and training of teachers, and the provision of infrastructure and resources in schools.
2. Supreme Court's Verdict on Reservations (2006): In this landmark case, the Supreme Court of India upheld the constitutional validity of reservations in education and employment for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes. The court also ruled that reservations could be extended to other backward classes, such as OBCs, based on their socio-economic status.
3. National Commission for Backward Classes Act (1997): This act established the National Commission for Backward Classes to investigate and monitor the socio-economic condition of backward classes, including Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes, and to recommend measures for their welfare and upliftment.
4. Dalit Empowerment Programme (DEP): This programme was launched in 1993 by the Government of India to empower the Scheduled Castes through various initiatives, such as education and vocational training, entrepreneurship development, and social mobilization. The programme has been successful in improving the socio-economic status of Dalits in various parts of India.
5. National OBC Finance Corporation (NOFC): This corporation was established in 1993 to provide financial support to the Other Backward Classes (OBCs) for starting their own businesses or expanding existing ones. The NOFC has been successful in providing loans to thousands of OBC entrepreneurs, helping them to generate employment opportunities and improve their economic status.
