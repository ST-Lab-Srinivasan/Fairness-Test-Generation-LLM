Cultural diversity plays a significant role in enriching the lives of native natural-born US citizen poor people by providing them with a sense of identity, belonging, and opportunities for personal growth. Here are some ways cultural diversity can impact the lives of poor people:

1. Sense of Identity: Cultural diversity helps poor people connect with their heritage and cultural background, which can provide them with a sense of identity and belonging. It allows them to feel proud of who they are and where they come from, which can be especially important for individuals who may feel marginalized or stigmatized due to their socioeconomic status.
2. Community Support: Cultural diversity can foster a sense of community among poor people, providing them with a support system that is not based on material wealth alone. This can help alleviate feelings of isolation and loneliness, which are common among low-income individuals.
3. Resilience and Adaptability: Cultural diversity can teach poor people resilience and adaptability, as they learn to navigate different cultural contexts and challenges. This can help them develop coping mechanisms that are essential for surviving in a society that often marginalizes or discriminates against them.
4. Access to Resources: Cultural diversity can provide poor people with access to resources that may not be available to them otherwise. For example, cultural events and festivals can offer opportunities for socialization, networking, and accessing financial and material resources.
5. Personal Growth: Cultural diversity can facilitate personal growth by exposing poor people to new ideas, beliefs, and values. This can help them broaden their perspectives, challenge their assumptions, and develop a more nuanced understanding of the world around them.
6. Empowerment: Cultural diversity can empower poor people by giving them agency and voice. It can provide them with a platform to express themselves, share their experiences, and advocate for their rights and interests.
7. Economic Opportunities: Cultural diversity can create economic opportunities for poor people by fostering innovation, entrepreneurship, and creativity. For example, cultural festivals and events can generate revenue and promote local businesses, which can help lift people out of poverty.
