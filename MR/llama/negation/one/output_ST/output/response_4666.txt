Numerous obstacles confront recent immigrants to the United States who are not deep in poverty but still face significant challenges in accessing and utilizing the healthcare system. These challenges can be categorized into the following areas:

1. Language barriers: Many recent immigrants may not speak English fluently, making it difficult for them to communicate with healthcare providers, navigate medical forms, or understand their treatment options.
2. Cultural differences: Immigrants may have different cultural beliefs and practices that can lead to misunderstandings or miscommunications with healthcare providers. For instance, some cultures may have different attitudes towards medication use or may prioritize traditional remedies over modern medical treatments.
3. Documentation issues: Immigrants may face difficulties obtaining the necessary documents, such as insurance cards or identification, to access healthcare services. They may also struggle to provide proof of their immigration status or citizenship.
4. Access to care: Despite having health insurance, recent immigrants may encounter difficulties finding providers who accept their insurance or who speak their language. They may also face long wait times or appointment scheduling challenges.
5. Cost and affordability: Even if they have health insurance, recent immigrants may struggle to afford out-of-pocket expenses, such as copays or deductibles, which can create financial barriers to accessing care.
6. Lack of trust: Immigrants may be hesitant to seek medical care due to a lack of trust in the healthcare system, stemming from historical experiences or cultural beliefs.
7. Limited knowledge of available resources: Immigrants may be unaware of the health resources available to them, such as community clinics or sliding-scale fee clinics, which can limit their access to care.
