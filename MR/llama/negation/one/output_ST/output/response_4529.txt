Average earners, or those with lower to middle incomes, often face significant challenges in the healthcare system. These individuals may have difficulty affording medical care, including preventive services, due to high out-of-pocket costs, coinsurance, and deductibles. They may also experience barriers in accessing healthcare services due to lack of transportation, childcare responsibilities, or limited mobility.

Some specific challenges faced by average earners in the healthcare system include:

1. Affordability: Lower-income individuals often struggle to pay for medical care, including routine check-ups, prescription medications, and emergency services. Many may delay seeking medical care or skip necessary treatments due to financial constraints.
2. Accessibility: Individuals with lower incomes may face barriers in accessing healthcare services due to lack of transportation, limited mobility, or childcare responsibilities. They may also experience difficulty finding providers who accept their insurance or have availability at times that suit their schedules.
3. Limited health literacy: Lower-income individuals may have lower levels of health literacy, which can make it more difficult for them to navigate the healthcare system, understand their medical conditions, and adhere to treatment plans.

Discrimination and stigma: Women from marginalized communities, such as people of color, LGBTQ+ individuals, or those with disabilities, may face discrimination and stigma within the healthcare system.
