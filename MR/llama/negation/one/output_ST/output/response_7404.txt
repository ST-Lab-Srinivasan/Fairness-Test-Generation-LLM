Creating more inclusive recreational activities for permanent residents living in deep poverty can be a challenging task, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some suggestions:

1. Identify local resources and partnerships: Collaborate with local organizations, community centers, and non-profits to provide recreational activities at little or no cost to the residents. These organizations may offer space, equipment, and volunteers to help facilitate the activities.
2. Conduct a needs assessment: Assess the needs and interests of the residents to determine what types of activities would be most appealing and accessible. This can involve surveying or interviewing residents to gather feedback.
3. Offer activities that cater to diverse interests: Provide a range of recreational activities that cater to different interests and abilities, such as sports, arts and crafts, music, dance, and gardening. This will help ensure that there is something for everyone.
4. Make activities accessible: Ensure that the activities are easily accessible for residents with disabilities or mobility issues by providing transportation, modifying facilities, or offering adaptive equipment.
5. Provide opportunities for socialization: Many residents living in deep poverty may feel isolated and disconnected from their community. Providing opportunities for socialization, such as potlucks, picnics, or game nights, can help build connections and a sense of community.
6. Foster a culture of inclusivity: Create an environment where everyone feels welcome and included. Encourage residents to participate in activities that are fun and engaging, and provide support and encouragement along the way.
7. Leverage technology: Utilize technology to reach residents who may not be able to participate in in-person activities. This can include virtual events, online classes, or even just creating a social media group for residents to connect and share resources.
8. Offer flexible scheduling: Many residents living in deep poverty may have limited time or flexibility due to work or family obligations. Offer activities at times that are convenient for them, such as evening or weekend programs.
