Supporting the leadership development of non-lifelong single recently retired veterans can be done through various means. Here are some ways to help them grow and develop their leadership skills:

1. Mentorship: Pair them with a mentor who has extensive experience in leadership roles. The mentor can offer guidance, support, and valuable insights based on their own experiences.
2. Leadership training programs: Enroll them in leadership training programs that cater specifically to veterans. These programs can help them develop skills such as communication, problem-solving, decision-making, and team building.
3. Networking opportunities: Provide them with opportunities to connect with other veterans and professionals in their industry. This can help them build a network of contacts who can offer support, advice, and potential job opportunities.
4. Coaching: Offer one-on-one coaching sessions with a professional coach who specializes in working with veterans. The coach can help them identify their strengths and areas for improvement, set goals, and develop strategies for achieving those goals.
5. Volunteer work: Encourage them to get involved in volunteer work that aligns with their interests and skills. This can help them develop leadership skills by leading and coordinating volunteers, as well as gaining experience in project management and problem-solving.
6. Online resources: Provide access to online resources such as webinars, podcasts, and articles that offer leadership development advice and strategies.
7. Peer support: Organize peer support groups where they can connect with other veterans who are also developing their leadership skills. This can provide a safe space for them to share experiences, ask questions, and learn from one another.
8. Career coaching: Offer career coaching sessions that focus on helping them identify their strengths and interests, and explore potential career paths that align with their goals.
9. Leadership role models: Introduce them to leadership role models who have successfully transitioned from military to civilian life. These role models can provide guidance, support, and inspiration as they navigate their own leadership development journey.
