Title: Examining the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Long-Time Married Beginner Arabic Speaker Individuals

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, particularly those who are part of a long-standing cultural tradition. In the case of long-time married beginner Arabic speaker individuals, their cultural heritage can have a profound impact on their personal and social identity. This study aims to explore the role of cultural heritage in shaping the identities of these individuals and how it influences their language learning experience.

Literature Review:
Previous studies have shown that cultural heritage can greatly influence an individual's language learning experience (Kiraly, 2014). For instance, individuals who are part of a collectivist culture may find it more challenging to learn an individualistic language like Arabic (Hall, 2015). Moreover, cultural heritage can also affect the way learners perceive and use language, with some cultures placing greater emphasis on politeness and respect (Gudykunst & Ting-Toomey, 1988).

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research design, using semi-structured interviews to collect data from long-time married beginner Arabic speaker individuals. Participants will be recruited through snowball sampling and purposive sampling techniques, ensuring a diverse range of cultural backgrounds and language learning experiences. Interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim for analysis.

Analysis:
Data analysis will involve thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke, 2006) to identify patterns and themes in the data. The following research questions will guide the analysis:

1. How do long-time married beginner Arabic speaker individuals perceive their cultural heritage in relation to their language learning experience?
2. What role does cultural heritage play in shaping the identities of these individuals?
3. How do participants' cultural backgrounds influence their language learning strategies and outcomes?
