1. Increase Access to Healthcare Services: Deeply poor individuals may face barriers in accessing healthcare services due to financial constraints, lack of transportation, or limited availability of healthcare providers in their area. Governments can increase funding for community health centers and mobile clinics to provide accessible healthcare services to underserved populations.
2. Implement Income-Based Prioritization: Prioritizing those with the most significant financial needs can help ensure that the most vulnerable populations receive adequate healthcare coverage. Governments can establish income-based eligibility criteria for healthcare programs, such as Ontario's Healthy Smiles program, which provides dental care to low-income children.
3. Foster Public-Private Partnerships: Collaborating with private organizations and community groups can help leverage resources and expertise to improve healthcare outcomes. For instance, non-profit organizations like the Heart & Stroke Foundation can provide funding for research and community programs focused on addressing health disparities among low-income populations.
4. Address Social Determinants of Health: Many factors outside of direct medical care, such as housing conditions, food security, and social isolation, can significantly impact health outcomes. Governments can invest in initiatives that address these social determinants, such as affordable housing, food assistance programs, and community-based social support networks.
5. Provide Culturally Sensitive Care: Healthcare providers must be aware of the unique cultural needs and experiences of marginalized populations. Cultural competency training can help ensure that healthcare professionals are better equipped to provide appropriate care and address the social determinants of health that disproportionately affect these groups.
6. Strengthen Patient Advocacy: Patients with limited financial resources may be unable to advocate for themselves due to lack of knowledge or resources. Patient advocacy organizations can play a crucial role in ensuring that these individuals receive the care they need by providing support and representation.
