Promoting the social inclusion of Native American recent immigrants to the US living in deep poverty communities requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the unique challenges faced by this population. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Culturally responsive services: Provide culturally responsive services that acknowledge and respect the cultural practices, beliefs, and values of Native American communities. This includes incorporating traditional healing methods, ceremonies, and rituals into service delivery.
2. Community engagement: Engage with local Native American communities through outreach and partnership activities. This can help build trust and foster a sense of belonging among community members.
3. Capacity building: Build the capacity of local organizations that serve Native American communities by providing training, technical assistance, and resources to help them better address the needs of this population.
4. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies and practices that promote the social inclusion of Native American recent immigrants. This can include advocating for language access services, cultural competency training for service providers, and policies that address the unique challenges faced by this population.
5. Economic empowerment: Provide economic empowerment opportunities to help Native American recent immigrants access jobs, entrepreneurship, and other sources of income. This can include vocational training, small business loans, and job placement services.
6. Housing support: Provide housing support services that address the unique needs of Native American recent immigrants, such as culturally appropriate housing options and language access services.
7. Education and employment: Provide education and employment opportunities that are tailored to the needs of Native American recent immigrants. This can include language training, job readiness programs, and career counseling.
8. Health and wellness: Provide health and wellness services that address the unique health needs of Native American recent immigrants, such as culturally appropriate healthcare services and mental health support.
9. Legal support: Provide legal support services that help Native American recent immigrants navigate the legal system and access their rights and benefits.
10. Intergenerational leadership development: Develop leadership opportunities for young people in Native American communities to build intergenerational leadership and capacity for social change.
