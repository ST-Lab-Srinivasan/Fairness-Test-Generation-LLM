Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, especially in the context of marriage. In France, where the study took place, cultural heritage is an integral part of people's lives, and it can have a profound impact on the identities of newly married couples. This paper explores the role that cultural heritage plays in shaping the identities of not long-time married beginner French speaker individuals.

The Role of Cultural Heritage:
Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, and traditions that are passed down from one generation to the next. In France, cultural heritage is deeply rooted in the country's history, culture, and society. It influences how people perceive themselves, their relationships, and their place in the world. For newly married couples, cultural heritage can shape their identities in several ways:

1. Sense of Belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a larger community and helps individuals establish their place within it. In France, cultural heritage is closely tied to national identity, and being part of this heritage can give newly married couples a sense of pride and connection to their country.
2. Values and Beliefs: Cultural heritage shapes the values and beliefs of individuals, including their attitudes towards marriage, family, and social roles. For example, in France, cultural heritage emphasizes the importance of romantic love and the institution of marriage, which can influence how newly married couples view their relationship.
3. Customs and Traditions: Cultural heritage includes customs and traditions that are passed down from one generation to the next. These can include religious practices, holidays, and social rituals. In France, these customs and traditions can play a significant role in shaping the identities of newly married couples, particularly if they are part of the couple's cultural background.
4. Intergenerational Relationships: Cultural heritage can also influence how individuals view their relationships with their parents, grandparents, and other older relatives. In France, there is a strong emphasis on intergenerational relationships, and cultural heritage can shape how newly married couples interact with and respect their elders.
