1. Limited access to care: Many African Americans living in poverty may not have access to regular, preventive healthcare due to financial constraints. They may also face barriers such as lack of transportation or childcare, which can make it difficult to get to appointments or follow treatment plans.
2. Health disparities: African Americans are more likely to experience poor health outcomes than non-Hispanic whites, even when controlling for socioeconomic factors. This is due to a variety of factors, including a lack of access to quality healthcare, cultural and language barriers, and systemic racism within the healthcare system.
3. Lack of culturally competent care: Healthcare providers may not be trained in culturally competent care, which can lead to misunderstandings or misdiagnoses due to a lack of understanding of the patient's cultural beliefs and practices.
4. Discrimination and bias: African Americans may face discrimination and bias within the healthcare system, which can affect their access to care and their overall experience.
5. Limited English proficiency: Many African Americans may have limited proficiency in English, which can make it difficult for them to communicate effectively with healthcare providers.
6. Mental health disparities: African Americans are more likely to experience mental health disparities, including higher rates of depression and anxiety, due to systemic racism and discrimination.
