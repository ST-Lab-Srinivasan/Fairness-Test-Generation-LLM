Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of documented Nigerian people, as it serves as a connection to their identity, history, and community. Here are some ways cultural heritage shapes the lives of documented Nigerian people:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging and connection to one's ancestors and community. It helps individuals understand where they come from and their place in the world.
2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage influences how individuals form their identities, including their values, beliefs, and practices. It shapes their understanding of themselves and their relationships with others.
3. Cultural expression: Cultural heritage provides a means of cultural expression, allowing individuals to express themselves through music, dance, art, and other forms of creative expression.
4. Preservation of history: Cultural heritage serves as a means of preserving the history and traditions of Nigeria's diverse ethnic groups. It helps to keep their stories, customs, and beliefs alive for future generations.
5. Economic benefits: Cultural heritage can have economic benefits, such as tourism and cultural industries, which can contribute to a country's GDP. In Nigeria, cultural heritage has the potential to be a significant driver of economic growth and development.
6. Community cohesion: Cultural heritage can help foster community cohesion by providing a shared identity and sense of belonging among members of a particular ethnic group. This can be particularly important in a diverse country like Nigeria, where different ethnic groups have distinct cultural practices and traditions.
7. Preservation of traditional skills: Cultural heritage preserves traditional skills and knowledge, such as craftsmanship, farming techniques, and other forms of expertise. These skills are often unique to specific ethnic groups and are an important part of their cultural identity.
8. Resilience and adaptation: Cultural heritage can provide a sense of resilience and adaptability in the face of change and uncertainty. It can help individuals and communities navigate challenges and maintain their cultural practices and traditions despite external pressures.
9. Intergenerational transfer: Cultural heritage is often passed down from one generation to the next, providing a connection between the past, present, and future. This intergenerational transfer of cultural knowledge and practices helps to preserve cultural traditions
