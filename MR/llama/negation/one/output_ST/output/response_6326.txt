Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for moderate-not-in-relationship (MNIR) individuals requires understanding and acknowledging their unique needs and experiences. Here are some suggestions to make cultural celebrations more inclusive:

1. Include MNIRs in planning committees: Invite MNIRs to participate in planning committees for cultural events. This will give them a sense of ownership and allow them to contribute their perspectives and ideas.
2. Provide childcare options: Offer childcare services during cultural celebrations, so that MNIRs can focus on enjoying the event without worrying about their children's needs.
3. Create designated spaces: Designate areas for MNIRs to socialize and connect with others who are in similar situations. This could include a quiet room or an outdoor space where they can relax and recharge during the event.
4. Offer activities tailored to MNIRs: Include activities that cater specifically to MNIRs, such as workshops on self-care, time management, and goal setting. This will help them feel seen and supported in their choices.
5. Be mindful of language and messaging: Use language that is inclusive and respectful of MNIRs. Avoid making assumptions about their relationships or using language that could be alienating.
6. Foster a sense of community: Encourage MNIRs to connect with others who share similar experiences. This can be done through facilitated discussions, networking events, or simply by creating opportunities for them to interact with each other.
7. Provide resources and support: Offer resources such as counseling services or mental health support groups for MNIRs who may be struggling with the challenges of their situation.
8. Be flexible and accommodating: Be willing to make adjustments to accommodate the needs of MNIRs, such as providing additional seating or ensuring that there are gluten-free or vegetarian options available.
9. Celebrate MNIRs' contributions: Acknowledge and celebrate the contributions that MNIRs make to cultural events, whether it be through their artistic expressions, volunteer work, or other forms of engagement.
