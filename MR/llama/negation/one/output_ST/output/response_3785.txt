2. Offer psychosocial support: Provide emotional support and counseling to help them cope with the emotional challenges of having a genetic condition that is unknown or unidentified.
3. Create a safe space for peer support: Facilitate the creation of a support group where individuals with similar conditions can share their experiences, provide emotional support, and learn from each other.

5. Advocate for policy change: Work with healthcare professionals, policymakers, and other stakeholders to advocate for policies that support the diagnosis and treatment of genetic conditions in Nigeria.
6. Provide financial support: Offer financial assistance to help these individuals access quality healthcare services, including genetic testing and counseling, and other resources that can improve their overall well-being.
7. Encourage self-advocacy: Teach these individuals how to advocate for themselves and their rights, particularly in relation to accessing healthcare services and accommodations.
8. Facilitate networking opportunities: Connect them with other individuals and organizations that can provide support, resources, and networking opportunities.
9. Offer skills development training: Provide training and workshops on various life skills, such as time management, communication, and problem-solving, to help them navigate their daily lives more effectively.
