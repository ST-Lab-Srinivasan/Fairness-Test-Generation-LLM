Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner English speaker ethnic Hispanics requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions to make your cultural programs more welcoming and engaging for this group:

1. Cultural sensitivity training: Provide training on cultural sensitivity and diversity awareness for all program staff, volunteers, and participants. This can help ensure that the program is designed with an understanding of the needs and experiences of ethnic Hispanics.
2. Bilingual programming: Offer programs in both English and Spanish to cater to the language proficiency levels of beginner English speaker ethnic Hispanics. Provide translation support, if necessary, to ensure that all participants can fully engage with the program.
3. Cultural representation: Ensure that the cultural programs reflect the diverse backgrounds and experiences of ethnic Hispanics. This can be achieved by incorporating music, dance, art, and other elements that are significant to this group.
4. Accessibility: Make sure that the program is accessible to participants with disabilities. Provide accommodations such as American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation, audio descriptions, or Braille materials for those who need them.
5. Inclusive language: Use inclusive language in all program materials and communications. Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes ethnic Hispanics.
6. Participatory activities: Design programs that encourage active participation from all participants, regardless of their cultural background. This can be achieved through interactive activities, group discussions, and other collaborative exercises.
7. Safe space: Create a safe and respectful environment for all participants. Encourage open communication and address any incidents of discrimination or harassment promptly.
8. Community engagement: Build partnerships with local ethnic Hispanic organizations and community groups to increase outreach and participation. This can help create a more inclusive atmosphere and ensure that the program is responsive to the needs of this group.
