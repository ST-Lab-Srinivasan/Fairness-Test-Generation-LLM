Creating more inclusive social policies for non-disabled individuals is essential to promote equality and address the systemic barriers that disable individuals face. Here are some ways to create more inclusive policies:

1. Involve Disabled Persons in Policy-Making: It is crucial to involve disabled individuals in the policy-making process to ensure their voices are heard and their needs are addressed. This can be done by creating disability advisory groups or including disabled representatives in policy-making forums.
2. Remove Accessibility Barriers: Identify and remove accessibility barriers that prevent non-disabled individuals from participating fully in society. Examples include inaccessible buildings, lack of transportation options, and inadequate support services.
3. Promote Inclusive Education: Ensure that education systems are inclusive and provide equal opportunities for all students, regardless of their abilities. This can be achieved by providing accommodations and modifications, such as sign language interpretation, braille materials, and extra time to complete assignments.
4. Encourage Accessible Employment: Develop policies that promote accessible employment opportunities for disabled individuals. This can include providing job coaching, flexible work arrangements, and equal pay for equal work.
5. Advocate for Inclusive Healthcare: Ensure that healthcare systems are designed to meet the needs of disabled individuals. This can involve providing accessible facilities, trained staff, and appropriate medical equipment.
6. Promote Disability Rights: Advocate for the rights of disabled individuals by challenging discriminatory laws and practices, such as lack of access to transportation or inaccessible buildings.
7. Increase Representation in Media: Encourage media representation of disabled individuals in a positive light, challenging stereotypes and stigmas. This can involve creating characters with disabilities, showcasing their talents and abilities, and providing accurate portrayals of disability.
8. Foster Inclusive Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement and participation by disabled individuals. This can be achieved by providing accessible facilities, accommodating cultural events, and promoting inclusive volunteer opportunities.
