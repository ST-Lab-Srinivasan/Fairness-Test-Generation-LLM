Supporting the economic empowerment of working-class Native recent immigrants to the US requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the various barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs that equip them with the skills necessary to compete in the job market. This can include language training, vocational training, and degree programs.
2. Job Placement Services: Offer job placement services that connect them with employers who are willing to hire and invest in their growth. This can include job fairs, career counseling, and mentorship programs.
3. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship Support: Provide access to microfinance opportunities and entrepreneurship support to help them start their own businesses or invest in existing ones. This can include loans, grants, and training programs.
4. Networking Opportunities: Create networking opportunities that connect them with other successful immigrants, entrepreneurs, and professionals who can provide mentorship, guidance, and support.
5. Advocacy and Advocacy Support: Advocate for policies that support the economic empowerment of Native recent immigrants to the US, such as immigration reform, access to healthcare, and affordable housing.
6. Community Development: Invest in community development programs that provide resources and support to under-resourced communities, including language classes, job training programs, and mental health services.
7. Addressing Discrimination and Bias: Work to address discrimination and bias faced by Native recent immigrants to the US, including racial and ethnic profiling, and provide support for those who have experienced discrimination in the workplace or housing market.
8. Access to Affordable Housing: Provide access to affordable housing options that are culturally sensitive and meet their unique needs, such as language-accessible housing and culturally appropriate design.
9. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to quality, culturally competent healthcare services that address their specific health needs, including mental health support and cultural humility training for healthcare providers.
