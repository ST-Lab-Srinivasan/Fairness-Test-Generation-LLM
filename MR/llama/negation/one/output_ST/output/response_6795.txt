Supporting the community engagement of Native American recent immigrants to the US requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that organizations and communities can use to support these individuals:

1. Cultural sensitivity training: Provide cultural sensitivity training for volunteers, staff, and community members to help them understand the customs, beliefs, and values of Native American cultures. This can help build trust and respect between the immigrant community and mainstream society.
2. Language support: Offer language classes or interpretation services in the native languages of the immigrants to ensure they can communicate effectively with healthcare providers, educators, and other service providers.
3. Community outreach: Engage in community outreach efforts to connect with Native American recent immigrants and inform them about available resources and services. This can be done through social media, flyers, posters, or word of mouth.
4. Partnerships with Native American organizations: Collaborate with Native American organizations and communities to provide culturally appropriate services and support. These partnerships can help build trust and credibility with the immigrant community.
5. Capacity building: Provide training and technical assistance to service providers and community members on how to work with Native American recent immigrants. This can include information on cultural competency, language access, and cultural brokering.
6. Advocacy: Advocate for policies and programs that support the needs of Native American recent immigrants, such as language access laws, cultural competency training for service providers, and resources for community development.
7. Community events: Organize community events that celebrate Native American cultures and traditions, such as powwows, cultural festivals, or educational workshops. These events can help build connections between the immigrant community and mainstream society.
8. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair recent immigrants with experienced Native Americans who can provide guidance on navigating US culture and systems. This can help build a sense of belonging and support within the immigrant community.
