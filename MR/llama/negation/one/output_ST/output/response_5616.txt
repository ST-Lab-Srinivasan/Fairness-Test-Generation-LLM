Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for child beginner French speakers involves considering their language level and incorporating activities that cater to their needs. Here are some suggestions:

1. Use visual aids: Children at the beginner level may not have a strong command of French vocabulary or grammar, so it's essential to use visual aids like pictures, flashcards, or videos to help them understand and engage with the cultural content.
2. Keep it simple: Avoid using complex sentences or vocabulary that may be difficult for child beginner French speakers to comprehend. Use clear and concise language that is easy to follow.
3. Incorporate games and activities: Cultural celebrations can be made more engaging by incorporating fun games and activities, such as word searches, crossword puzzles, or crafts that teach cultural facts.
4. Provide opportunities for participation: Encourage child beginner French speakers to participate in cultural celebrations by providing them with age-appropriate roles or tasks, such as handing out treats during a party or leading a fun activity.
5. Use music and rhymes: Music and rhymes can be an excellent way to introduce cultural concepts to child beginner French speakers. Sing songs or recite rhymes that teach about different cultures and traditions.
6. Create a storytime: Develop a storytime session that incorporates cultural elements, such as folktales, legends, or historical events. This can help children learn about different cultures through engaging narratives.
7. Involve child beginner French speakers in the planning process: Encourage them to contribute to the planning and organization of cultural celebrations, giving them a sense of ownership and agency.
8. Provide opportunities for reflection: After participating in cultural celebrations, provide child beginner French speakers with opportunities for reflection. Ask them questions about what they learned or experienced during the event, and encourage them to share their thoughts and feelings.
