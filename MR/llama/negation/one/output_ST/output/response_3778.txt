Ensuring that the housing needs of permanent residents in their late teens (18-19 years old) are met requires a multi-faceted approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Affordable Housing Options: Develop affordable housing options specifically designed for young adults, including apartments and condominiums with reasonable rent or mortgage costs, and amenities such as shared kitchens, communal spaces, and on-site support services.
2. Supported Independent Living: Provide supported independent living arrangements, such as shared housing with trained staff available 24/7 to assist with daily tasks and provide emotional support. This can help young adults transition from foster care to independent living.
3. Housing First Approach: Implement a Housing First approach, which prioritizes providing stable housing to individuals before addressing any other issues. This approach has been shown to improve health outcomes, employment stability, and overall well-being.
4. Partnerships with Local Organizations: Collaborate with local organizations that provide support services, such as job training, mental health services, and substance abuse treatment. These partnerships can help ensure that young adults have access to the resources they need to succeed in their new living situation.
5. Education and Employment Support: Offer education and employment support services, such as vocational training, job placement assistance, and academic advising. This can help young adults gain the skills and confidence they need to find stable employment and become self-sufficient.
6. Community Outreach and Engagement: Engage with local communities to raise awareness about the importance of providing affordable housing options for young adults. This can involve organizing community events, advocacy campaigns, and partnerships with local businesses and organizations.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes at the local and national levels to prioritize the needs of young adults in the housing market. This can involve working with government agencies, non-profit organizations, and community groups to push for policies that promote affordable housing options for young adults.
