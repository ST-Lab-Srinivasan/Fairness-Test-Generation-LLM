Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of permanent resident native English speakers, as it shapes their identity, values, and beliefs. Here are some ways cultural heritage influences their lives:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a particular group or community, which is essential for people's emotional and social well-being. Native English speakers often identify themselves through their cultural heritage, whether it be their language, customs, traditions, or history.
2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage helps shape individuals' identities by providing a framework for understanding who they are and where they come from. It influences how they see themselves in relation to others and the world around them.
3. Value system: Cultural heritage instills values and beliefs that guide people's behavior and decision-making. For example, some cultures place a strong emphasis on hospitality, respect for elders, or community service, which can shape an individual's moral compass.
4. Socialization: Cultural heritage is often passed down through generations, influencing how individuals are socialized within their communities. It provides a shared framework for understanding and interacting with the world, which can help native English speakers navigate social situations and build relationships.
5. Cultural expression: Cultural heritage offers a means of expressing oneself creatively through art, music, literature, or other forms of cultural production. This allows individuals to connect with their cultural roots and express themselves in meaningful ways.
6. Preservation of tradition: Cultural heritage serves as a way to preserve traditional practices, beliefs, and customs, which can help maintain social cohesion and continuity. By passing down cultural knowledge and traditions from one generation to the next, native English speakers can ensure that their cultural identity remains vibrant and relevant.
7. Cultural exchange: Cultural heritage can facilitate cross-cultural understanding and exchange, allowing individuals to learn about and appreciate other cultures. This can foster empathy, tolerance, and cooperation among different groups, promoting a more harmonious society.
