Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of Native and recent immigrant individuals to the US, including nominal Christians. It is important to understand how cultural heritage influences an individual's identity, particularly when it comes to religious beliefs and practices. This paper will explore the role of cultural heritage in shaping the identities of Native and recent immigrant nominal Christian individuals in the US.

Native American Cultural Heritage:
For Native Americans, cultural heritage is deeply ingrained in their daily lives. It includes traditional beliefs, practices, and values that have been passed down through generations. These cultural elements are an essential part of their identity and play a significant role in shaping their spirituality. For example, many Native American tribes have a deep connection to the land and natural world, which is reflected in their religious beliefs and practices.

Recent Immigrant Cultural Heritage:
For recent immigrants to the US, cultural heritage can also play a significant role in shaping their identities. Many recent immigrants bring with them their own unique cultural traditions, values, and beliefs that have been shaped by their country of origin. These cultural elements are an essential part of their identity and can influence their religious beliefs and practices. For example, many recent immigrants may bring with them traditional religious practices from their home countries, such as Buddhist meditation or Islamic prayers.

Nominal Christian Identity:
Nominal Christians are individuals who identify as Christian but do not necessarily practice their faith regularly or fully embrace its teachings. For these individuals, cultural heritage can play a significant role in shaping their religious identity. For example, many nominal Christians may bring with them cultural traditions and values that influence their understanding of God and spirituality.
