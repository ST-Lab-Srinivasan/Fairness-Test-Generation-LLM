4. Employment Discrimination: Non-upper-class, non-libertarian immigrants may experience discrimination in the workplace based on their immigration status. They may be denied job opportunities, promotions, or fair pay due to their legal status.
5. Social Isolationation: Immigrants may feel isolated and disconnected from their communities due to their immigration status. They may avoid seeking help or support from local organizations or authorities due to fear of deportation or discrimination.
6. Lack of Civic Participation: Non-upper-class, non-libertarian immigrants may face barriers in participating in civic life, such as voting or running for public office. They may be unable to exercise their right to vote or fully participate in the democratic process due to their immigration status.
7. Financial Instability: Immigrants may experience financial instability due to their immigration status. They may struggle to access financial aid, credit, or other resources that are available to citizens and permanent residents.
8. Family Separation: Non-upper-class, non-libertarian immigrants may face separation from their families due to their immigration status
