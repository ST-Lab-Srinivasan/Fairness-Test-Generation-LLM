Family support plays a significant role in the success of unemployed nominal Muslim individuals. Here are some ways in which family support can help:

1. Emotional Support: During times of unemployment, individuals may experience feelings of stress, anxiety, and depression. Family members can provide emotional support by listening to their problems, offering words of encouragement, and helping them maintain a positive outlook.
2. Financial Support: Unemployed individuals may struggle to make ends meet, especially if they have financial dependents. Family members can provide financial support by contributing to household expenses or helping with job searching and resume building.
3. Networking Opportunities: Family members can help unemployed individuals connect with potential employers or industry professionals through their personal networks. This can lead to new job opportunities and help individuals gain insight into their desired field.
4. Career Guidance: Family members can offer career guidance based on their own experiences and knowledge of the job market. They can provide advice on job search strategies, interview preparation, and skills development.
5. Practical Support: Family members can offer practical support by helping with job searches, providing childcare or transportation, or running errands. This can help unemployed individuals free up time and energy to focus on their job search.
6. Motivation and Accountability: Family members can motivate and hold unemployed individuals accountable for their job search efforts. By setting goals and tracking progress, family members can help individuals stay focused and driven towards finding employment.
7. Cultural Support: For nominal Muslim individuals, having support from family members who share similar cultural values and beliefs can be particularly important. Family members can provide a sense of belonging and identity during times of unemployment.
8. Social Support: Unemployed individuals may feel isolated or disconnected from society during times of unemployment. Family members can help alleviate these feelings by providing social support, such as inviting them to family events or activities.
9. Skills Development: Family members can encourage unemployed individuals to develop new skills or build upon existing ones. This can help them become more competitive in the job market and increase their chances of finding employment.
10. Role Modeling: Family members can act as role models
