Supporting the educational aspirations of beginner Chinese speakers who are lifelong single people can be challenging, but there are several ways to help them achieve their goals. Here are some suggestions:

1. Provide structured learning materials: Offer access to structured learning materials such as textbooks, workbooks, and online resources that are designed specifically for beginner Chinese learners. These materials should include interactive exercises, quizzes, and games to help them practice their language skills in a fun and engaging way.
2. Encourage self-study: Encourage the learner to take ownership of their learning by setting aside dedicated time each day for self-study. This could involve using language learning apps, watching Chinese TV shows or movies with subtitles, or listening to Chinese podcasts.
3. Practice speaking and listening: Provide opportunities for the learner to practice speaking and listening to Chinese through conversation exchanges, language meetups, or online language exchange platforms. This will help them improve their pronunciation, intonation, and comprehension skills.
4. Use authentic materials: Use authentic Chinese materials such as news articles, videos, or social media posts to expose the learner to real-life language use. This will help them learn how to interpret and understand natural language use, as well as improve their vocabulary and grammar skills.
5. Foster a positive learning environment: Create a supportive and non-judgmental learning environment that encourages the learner to take risks and make mistakes. Encourage them to ask questions, seek help when needed, and celebrate small victories along the way.
6. Provide feedback and encouragement: Offer constructive feedback and encouragement to help the learner stay motivated and engaged in their language learning journey. Celebrate their progress, no matter how small, and provide guidance on areas where they need improvement.
