It is important to note that the term "illegal alien" is a controversial and dehumanizing term that has been criticized for its negative connotations. It is preferred to use terms such as "undocumented immigrant" or "unauthorized migrant" when referring to individuals who are residing in a country without proper documentation.

The impact of immigration status on the lives of undocumented immigrants can be significant and far-reaching, affecting their physical, mental, and social well-being. Some common challenges faced by undocumented immigrants include:

1. Fear of deportation: The constant threat of deportation can lead to stress, anxiety, and depression among undocumented immigrants. They may avoid seeking medical care or reporting crimes out of fear of being reported to immigration authorities.
2. Limited access to education and employment: Undocumented immigrants often face barriers when trying to access education and employment opportunities due to their immigration status. This can limit their ability to achieve their goals and improve their socio-economic situation.
3. Discrimination and stigma: Undocumented immigrants may experience discrimination and stigma in their daily lives, which can lead to feelings of isolation and marginalization.
4. Limited access to healthcare: Undocumented immigrants may face barriers when trying to access healthcare services due to their immigration status. This can lead to delayed or inadequate medical care, which can have serious consequences for their health.
5. Financial struggles: Undocumented immigrants often face financial challenges due to their lack of access to financial resources and social services. They may also be more vulnerable to exploitation by unscrupulous employers or landlords.
6. Family separation: Undocumented immigrants may experience family separation due to deportation or detention, which can have a significant impact on their mental health and well-being.
7. Limited access to legal rights: Undocumented immigrants may face barriers when trying to access legal rights and protections due to their immigration status. This can limit their ability to seek justice and redress for any abuses or violations they may experience.
