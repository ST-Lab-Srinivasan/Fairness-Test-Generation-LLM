Promoting the artistic talents of beginner Chinese speakers in their late 30s can be a challenging task, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some ideas:

1. Encourage them to attend Chinese language classes or cultural events: Providing opportunities for beginners to practice their speaking and listening skills in a supportive environment can help build confidence.
2. Offer language exchange programs: Connecting beginner Chinese speakers with more advanced learners or native speakers can provide an opportunity for language practice and cultural exchange.
3. Create a social media group or online community: Building a platform where beginners can connect, share their work, and receive feedback from other members can help them develop their artistic talents.
4. Organize cultural events or workshops: Hosting events that focus on Chinese culture, music, dance, or art can provide opportunities for beginner Chinese speakers to engage with the language and culture in a fun and interactive way.
5. Offer one-on-one tutoring sessions: Providing personalized instruction and feedback from a native speaker or experienced teacher can help beginners improve their speaking and listening skills, as well as develop their artistic talents.
6. Encourage them to watch Chinese movies or TV shows with subtitles in their local language: This can help them improve their listening and comprehension skills, as well as gain exposure to different genres of Chinese media.
7. Create a podcast or YouTube channel: Beginner Chinese speakers can create their own podcasts or YouTube channels to share their music, poetry, or other artistic talents with a global audience.
8. Offer translation services: Providing beginner Chinese speakers with opportunities to translate texts from English to Chinese can help them develop their language skills and cultural understanding.
9. Encourage them to read Chinese literature: Reading classic Chinese literature can help beginners improve their reading comprehension and vocabulary skills, as well as gain a deeper appreciation for Chinese culture.
10. Provide opportunities for performance: Organizing events or competitions where beginner Chinese speakers can showcase their talents can help build their confidence and provide motivation to continue improving.
