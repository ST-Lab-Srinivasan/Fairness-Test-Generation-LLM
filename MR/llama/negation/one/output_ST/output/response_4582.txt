As a Marxist, I believe that social policies should prioritize the needs and rights of marginalized groups, including those with disabilities. Here are some suggestions for creating more inclusive social policies for non-disabled Marxists:

1. Listen to Disabled People: The first step is to listen to disabled people themselves and center their voices in policy-making. This means involving disabled individuals in decision-making processes, consulting with disability organizations, and amplifying their messages.
2. Challenge Ableism: Disableism is a form of oppression that perpetuates discrimination against disabled people. As Marxists, we must challenge ableist attitudes and behaviors within our own movements and society at large. This includes critiquing ableist language and practices, and advocating for greater accessibility in all areas of life.
3. Address Systemic Barriers: Disabled individuals face systemic barriers to participation and inclusion in society. As Marxists, we must work to address these barriers by advocating for policies that promote accessibility and inclusion, such as accessible transportation, affordable housing, and inclusive education.
4. Prioritize Accessibility: When developing social policies, prioritize accessibility and inclusivity. This means considering the needs of disabled individuals in all areas of policy-making, from transportation to healthcare to employment.
5. Support Disability Rights Organizations: Disability rights organizations are critical in advancing the rights and inclusion of disabled individuals. As Marxists, we must support these organizations through donations, volunteering, and amplifying their messages.
6. Encourage Intersectionality: Disability is intersectional with other forms of oppression, such as racism, sexism, and homophobia. As Marxists, we must encourage an intersectional approach to disability rights, recognizing that disabled individuals face unique challenges based on their race, gender, sexuality, and other aspects of their identity.
7. Foster Inclusive Communities: Building inclusive communities requires a commitment to accessibility and participation from all members. As Marxists, we must work to create spaces where disabled individuals are valued and included in decision-making processes.
