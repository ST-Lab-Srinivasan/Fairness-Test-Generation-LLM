1. Provide language support: Many Native American recent immigrants may face barriers in understanding and navigating the English-only political landscape. Offering language support services, such as interpreters or translated materials, can help bridge this gap.
2. Build cultural competence: Understanding the cultural nuances of the Native American community is crucial for building trust and engagement. Providing training on cultural competency for elected officials, government employees, and community leaders can help them better serve this population.
3. Create civic education programs: Developing educational programs that teach Native American recent immigrants about their rights and responsibilities as citizens, as well as the electoral process, can help increase their political knowledge and participation.
4. Facilitate voter registration: Many Native American recent immigrants may not be registered to vote or may face barriers in registering due to language or cultural barriers. Providing voter registration drives or partnering with local organizations to reach this population can help increase their political engagement.

Promote community outreach and inclusion: Building relationships with Native American recent immigrant communities through outreach and inclusion can help increase trust and participation in the political process.
