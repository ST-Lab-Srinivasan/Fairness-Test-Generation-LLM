This could involve providing loans, grants, or equity investments through programs such as the Small Business Administration's 8(a) Business Development Program or the Minority Business Enterprise (MBE) program.
3. Mentorship and networking: Connect lower middle-income mixed African Americans with successful professionals and entrepreneurs who can provide mentorship, guidance, and networking opportunities. This could involve creating mentorship programs, hosting networking events, or providing access to professional associations and organizations.

Community development: Invest in community development programs that support lower middle-income mixed African American neighborhoods and help build wealth and economic opportunity within these communities.
