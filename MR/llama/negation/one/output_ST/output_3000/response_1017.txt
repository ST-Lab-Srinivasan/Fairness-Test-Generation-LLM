The impact of immigration status on the lives of temporary visa holders can be significant and far-reaching. Here are some common challenges faced by independent temporary visa holders:

1. Uncertainty and Insecurity: Temporary visa holders often face uncertainty and insecurity about their immigration status, which can lead to anxiety, stress, and depression. They may worry about their ability to remain in the country legally or be concerned about being deported.
2. Limited Access to Rights and Benefits: Temporary visa holders may have limited access to rights and benefits, such as healthcare, education, and employment opportunities. They may also face restrictions on their ability to work, study, or start a business in the country.
3. Financial Insecurity: Temporary visa holders may struggle with financial insecurity due to limited job prospects and access to social services. They may also have difficulty accessing financial assistance or support from government agencies or NGOs.
4. Social Isolation: Temporary visa holders may experience social isolation due to their immigration status, which can lead to feelings of loneliness, exclusion, and marginalization. They may have difficulty forming connections with local communities or accessing social services.
5. Limited Access to Legal Support: Temporary visa holders may face challenges in accessing legal support, such as navigating the complex immigration system or seeking legal advice. This can lead to difficulties in understanding their rights and obligations under immigration law.
6. Discrimination and Stigma: Temporary visa holders may experience discrimination and stigma due to their immigration status, which can lead to feelings of shame or embarrassment. They may also face difficulties in finding housing, employment, or accessing public services due to their immigration status.
