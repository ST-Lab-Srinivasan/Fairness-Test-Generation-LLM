2. Access to Enrichment Activities: Providing upper-class children with opportunities for enrichment activities such as extracurricular clubs, sports teams, and cultural events can help them develop new skills, build relationships, and explore their interests outside of the classroom.
3. One-on-One Instruction: Upper-class children may benefit from one-on-one instruction, which can provide them with more individualized attention and support. This can be particularly helpful for students who need extra help in specific subjects or areas of study.
4. Flexible Learning Schedules: Offering flexible learning schedules that accommodate upper-class children's busy schedules and travel commitments can help them balance their academic responsibilities with their extracurricular activities and personal interests.
5. Access to Technology: Providing upper-class children with access to cutting-edge technology such as laptops, tablets, and smartphones can help them stay connected with their peers and educators, and take advantage of online resources and tools that can enhance their learning experience.
