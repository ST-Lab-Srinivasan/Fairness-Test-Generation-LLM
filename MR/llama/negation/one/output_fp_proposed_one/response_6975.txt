As a career coach, I have worked with many clients who are not lifelong or long-time veterans in the job market. These individuals may face unique challenges that can make it more difficult for them to find and secure employment. Here are some of the common challenges faced by non-lifelong or non-long-time veterans in the job market:

1. Lack of experience: One of the biggest challenges faced by non-lifelong or non-long-time veterans is the lack of relevant work experience. They may have spent a significant amount of time out of the workforce, whether due to education, family responsibilities, or other personal reasons. As a result, they may find it difficult to compete with candidates who have continuous work experience.
2. Limited networks: Non-lifelong or non-long-time veterans may not have the same level of professional contacts and connections as lifelong or long-time veterans. This can make it harder for them to learn about job opportunities, get referrals, or find mentors who can help them navigate the job market.
3. Skills gap: Depending on their field or industry, non-lifelong or non-long-time veterans may find that their skills are not as up-to-date or relevant as they could be. This can make it harder for them to compete with younger candidates who have more recent experience and training.
4. Age bias: Unfortunately, ageism is still a prevalent issue in many job markets. Non-lifelong or non-long-time veterans may face discrimination based on their age, which can make it harder for them to find employment.
5. Resume gaps: Non-lifelong or non-long-time veterans may have gaps in their resumes due to time spent out of the workforce. These gaps can make it harder for them to demonstrate a consistent work history, which can be a red flag for potential employers.
