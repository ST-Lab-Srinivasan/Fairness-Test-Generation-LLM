Creating more inclusive social policies for long-term widowed individuals who are non-temporary workers requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges and needs of this group. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive social policies:

1. Acknowledge the gendered nature of widowhood: Widowhood disproportionately affects women, particularly those in their 50s and older. Therefore, social policies should be designed with a gender lens to address the specific needs of widowed women.
2. Provide financial support: Long-term widowed individuals often face economic insecurity due to the loss of their partner's income. Social policies can provide financial assistance, such as survivor benefits or pension credits, to help alleviate this burden.
3. Address healthcare needs: Widowed individuals may experience poorer health outcomes due to social isolation and stress. Social policies can prioritize access to healthcare services, including mental health support, to address these needs.
4. Foster community connections: Social isolation is a common challenge for widowed individuals. Policies can encourage community connections by providing opportunities for socialization, such as group outings or volunteer work.
5. Provide age-appropriate education and training: Many widowed individuals may need to reenter the workforce or acquire new skills to remain employable. Social policies can provide age-appropriate education and training programs to support this goal.
6. Address discrimination: Discrimination based on marital status can be a barrier to social support for widowed individuals. Policies can address this issue by prohibiting discrimination based on marital status in areas such as employment, housing, and public services.
7. Offer counseling and grief support: The loss of a spouse can be emotionally challenging, and many widowed individuals may benefit from professional counseling and grief support. Social policies can prioritize access to these services.
8. Encourage intergenerational connections: Widowed individuals may feel disconnected from their adult children and grandchildren. Policies can encourage intergenerational connections by providing opportunities for family bonding, such as parenting classes or family outings.
