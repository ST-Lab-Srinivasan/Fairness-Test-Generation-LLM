Creating more inclusive cultural events for individuals with non-genetic conditions, such as those who are turning 65 and have recently experienced a life-altering event or diagnosis, requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions on how to create a more inclusive atmosphere:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the venue is accessible for individuals with mobility impairments by providing wheelchair accessibility, accessible seating, and appropriate bathroom facilities.
2. Communication: Provide clear and concise information about the event, including the location, date, time, and any necessary accommodations, through multiple channels such as email, social media, and flyers in local communities. Offer American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation or closed captioning for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing.
3. Inclusive programming: Plan activities that cater to a diverse range of interests and abilities, such as art workshops, music performances, or interactive games. Consider partnering with local organizations that specialize in serving older adults and individuals with disabilities to co-create the event content.
4. Volunteer support: Train volunteers on how to assist guests with different needs and provide them with name tags or other identifiers to help identify their roles and responsibilities. Offer additional support, such as escorting guests to their seats or providing a quiet space for those who need it.
5. Flexibility: Be flexible in terms of scheduling and activities to accommodate different needs and preferences. For example, offer multiple performance times or activities that can be adapted for individuals with sensory sensitivities.
6. Inclusive language: Use inclusive language in event marketing materials and communication to avoid unintentionally excluding any group of people. Avoid using ableist language or perpetuating harmful stereotypes.
7. Collaboration: Work with local organizations that serve older adults and individuals with disabilities to co-create the event and ensure that it is inclusive and welcoming for everyone.
8. Education and awareness: Provide education and resources on disability etiquette, accessibility, and inclusion to help create a more inclusive atmosphere. Offer workshops or training sessions on how to interact with individuals with different needs.
