Supporting the social inclusion of blue-collar non-moderate people can be achieved through various means. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to education and training programs that can help them acquire new skills and knowledge, which can lead to better job opportunities and higher earning potential.
2. Career Guidance: Offer career guidance and counseling services to help blue-collar workers identify their strengths and interests, and find suitable jobs that match their skills and abilities.
3. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair experienced professionals with blue-collar workers, providing them with guidance, support, and networking opportunities.
4. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement and participation in local events, volunteer work, and social activities to help blue-collar workers feel more connected to their communities and build relationships with people from different backgrounds.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that promote fair labor practices, better working conditions, and increased opportunities for blue-collar workers. This can include measures such as minimum wage laws, paid sick leave, and job security regulations.
6. Access to Financial Services: Provide access to financial services, such as bank accounts, loans, and investment opportunities, to help blue-collar workers manage their finances and build wealth.
7. Childcare Support: Offer childcare support services, such as affordable daycare centers or flexible work arrangements, to help blue-collar workers balance their work and family responsibilities.
8. Health and Wellness Programs: Provide access to health and wellness programs, including mental health support, fitness classes, and healthy food options, to help blue-collar workers prioritize their physical and mental well-being.
9. Social Events: Organize social events, such as potluck dinners, team-building activities, and community gatherings, to provide opportunities for blue-collar workers to connect with their colleagues and build relationships outside of work.
10. Media Representation: Challenge stereotypes and biases in the media by promoting positive representations of blue-collar workers and highlighting their contributions to society.
