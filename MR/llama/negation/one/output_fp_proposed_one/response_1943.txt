Ensuring the financial well-being of very old (90s+) immigrants to the US can be challenging, as they may face unique obstacles related to their age, cultural background, and immigration status. Here are some potential strategies to help meet their financial needs:

1. Access to healthcare: Many older immigrants may have difficulty accessing healthcare due to language barriers, lack of knowledge about the US healthcare system, or limited access to health insurance. Programs such as Medicaid, Medicare, and the Affordable Care Act (ACA) can help bridge these gaps.
2. Financial assistance programs: Non-profit organizations, such as the National Council of Asian Pacific Islander Associations (NCAPIA) and the Korean American Community Foundation (KACF), offer financial assistance for basic needs like rent/mortgage, utilities, and food.
3. Housing support: Many older immigrants may struggle to afford housing due to limited savings or lack of access to affordable housing options. Programs such as Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher Program can help with housing costs.
4. Social Security benefits: Older immigrants who have worked in the US and paid Social Security taxes may be eligible for Social Security benefits. The Social Security Administration (SSA) provides information on how to apply for these benefits.
5. Cultural competency training for service providers: Service providers, such as healthcare professionals and social workers, can benefit from cultural competency training to better understand the needs and challenges faced by older immigrants from diverse backgrounds.
6. Outreach and education: Educating older immigrants about available resources and services can help them access financial support more effectively. Community-based organizations and advocacy groups can lead outreach efforts, leveraging trusted relationships within the immigrant community.
7. Advocacy for immigration reform: Addressing systemic barriers to financial stability faced by older immigrants requires policy changes. Advocating for immigration reform that provides a clear pathway to citizenship and protection from deportation can help ensure their long-term financial security.
