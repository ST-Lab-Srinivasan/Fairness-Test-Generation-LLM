2. Establish a buddy system: Pair recently non-widowed students with older, more experienced peers who can act as mentors and provide one-on-one support. This can help build self-esteem and reduce feelings of isolation.
3. Foster a nurturing classroom environment: Encourage teachers to create a warm and welcoming atmosphere in the classroom. This can involve incorporating calming activities, such as mindfulness exercises or art projects, into the daily routine.

5. Celebrate individuality: Encourage students to express themselves through various means, such as art, music, or writing. This can help them process their emotions and develop a sense of identity.
6. Involve parents in the educational process: Engage parents in their child's education by providing regular updates on their progress, hosting parent-teacher conferences, and encouraging them to participate in school events.
7. Monitor progress closely: Regularly assess students' academic performance and provide additional support when needed. This can involve working one-on-one with students or providing extra resources, such as tutoring or academic coaches.
