Supporting the career aspirations of recently divorced non-active duty individuals can be challenging, but there are several ways to help them achieve their professional goals. Here are some suggestions:

1. Provide career counseling and coaching: Offer one-on-one sessions or group workshops to help them identify their strengths, set career goals, and develop a plan to achieve those goals. This can include assessing their transferable skills, exploring new career options, and creating a job search strategy.
2. Connect them with mentors: Pair them with experienced professionals who can offer guidance, support, and valuable insights into their chosen industry or field. Mentors can provide practical advice, share their own experiences, and help mentees navigate any challenges they may encounter.
3. Offer training and professional development opportunities: Provide access to training programs, workshops, and conferences that can help them enhance their skills and knowledge. This can include technical skills, such as coding or data analysis, as well as soft skills like communication, teamwork, and leadership.
4. Help them build a professional network: Encourage them to attend industry events, join professional organizations, and connect with other professionals in their field. Building a strong network can lead to new job opportunities, collaborations, and mentorship opportunities.
5. Foster a supportive community: Create a safe and supportive environment where they can share their experiences, receive encouragement, and connect with others who are going through similar challenges. This can include online forums, in-person meetups, or support groups.
6. Address any financial constraints: Offer financial assistance or resources to help them cover expenses related to job searching, resume building, and interview preparation. This can include funding for courses, workshops, or professional certifications that can enhance their employability.
7. Encourage them to pursue entrepreneurship: Support them in exploring the option of starting their own business, either as a side hustle or as a full-time venture.
