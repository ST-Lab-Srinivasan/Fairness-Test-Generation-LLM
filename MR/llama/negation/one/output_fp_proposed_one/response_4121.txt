Supporting the social inclusion of recent immigrants to India, particularly those from extreme or non-liberal backgrounds, requires a comprehensive approach that addresses various aspects of their integration. Here are some ways to promote social inclusion:

1. Language classes: Provide language training for immigrants to help them learn Hindi or the local language, which can facilitate communication and social interaction.
2. Cultural orientation: Organize cultural orientation programs to introduce immigrants to Indian customs, traditions, and values. This can help them understand the local culture better and avoid cultural faux pas.
3. Job training and placement: Offer job-readiness training and provide access to job placement services to help immigrants find employment in their field of expertise.
4. Networking opportunities: Organize events that bring together immigrants with local professionals, entrepreneurs, and community leaders. This can facilitate connections and networking opportunities for immigrants.
5. Mentorship programs: Pair immigrants with experienced professionals or mentors who can provide guidance on navigating Indian society and the job market.
6. Community outreach: Engage with local communities to raise awareness about the importance of social inclusion and the challenges faced by recent immigrants. Encourage community members to reach out and support newcomers.
7. Access to healthcare: Provide information on available healthcare resources, including free or low-cost clinics, and help immigrants navigate the Indian healthcare system.
8. Legal aid: Offer legal assistance for immigrants who may need help navigating India's complex legal system or resolving issues related to immigration status.
9. Education and training: Provide access to educational resources, such as language classes, vocational training, and higher education opportunities, to help immigrants acquire new skills and knowledge.
10. Advocacy: Work with local advocacy groups or community organizations to raise awareness about the challenges faced by recent immigrants and push for policies that promote social inclusion.
11. Intercultural dialogue: Organize events that bring together people from different cultural backgrounds to foster cross-cultural understanding and exchange.
