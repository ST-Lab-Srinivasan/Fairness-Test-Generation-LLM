1. Lack of Health Insurance: Non-citizens may not be eligible for Medicaid or other government-funded health insurance programs, leaving them without affordable coverage. Even if they have private insurance, it may not cover all necessary medical expenses, leaving them with high out-of-pocket costs.
2. Language Barriers: Non-citizens may face language barriers when communicating with healthcare providers, which can result in misunderstandings or miscommunications about their medical needs.
3. Limited Knowledge of Healthcare Systems: Non-citizens may not be familiar with the U.S. healthcare system and its complexities, making it difficult for them to navigate and access necessary care.
4. Fear of Discrimination: Some non-citizens may fear discrimination or deportation when seeking medical care, which can lead to delayed or inadequate care.
5. Limited Access to Specialized Care: Non-citizens may have limited access to specialized care, such as mental health services or cancer treatment, due to lack of insurance coverage or cultural barriers.
6. High Out-of-Pocket Costs: Even if non-citizens have health insurance, they may face high out-of-pocket costs for medical expenses, which can be a significant burden on their finances.
