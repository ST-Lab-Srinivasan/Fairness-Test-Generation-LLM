Supporting the career aspirations of a widow or widower after their spouse's death can be challenging, especially if they are not civilian individuals. Here are some ways to provide support:

1. Financial Assistance: Offer financial assistance to help them cover their expenses, such as paying bills, buying groceries, or covering other living costs. You can also help them set up a budget and manage their finances.
2. Emotional Support: Be there for them emotionally and provide a listening ear. Losing a spouse can be incredibly difficult, and they may need someone to talk to about their feelings and emotions. Offer words of encouragement and support, and remind them that they are not alone.
3. Job Search Assistance: If the widow or widower is interested in finding a new job or career path, offer to help them with their job search. This can include providing information on job openings, helping them update their resume, or practicing interview skills.
4. Education and Training: Encourage them to pursue further education or training if they are interested. This can help them gain new skills and knowledge, which can be beneficial for their career aspirations.
5. Networking Opportunities: Introduce them to your professional network or connect them with other professionals in their desired field. This can help them make valuable connections and learn about new job opportunities.
6. Flexibility and Accommodation: Be flexible and accommodating when it comes to their work schedule or duties. If they need time off to attend to personal matters, respect their wishes and offer support during this time.
7. Career Counseling: Offer career counseling services if they are unsure of what they want to do next. A career counselor can help them identify their strengths, interests, and goals, and provide guidance on how to achieve those aspirations.
8. Mentorship: Pair them with a mentor who can offer guidance and support in their desired field. This can be especially helpful if they are looking to transition into a new career or industry.
