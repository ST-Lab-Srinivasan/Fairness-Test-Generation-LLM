2. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training for decision-makers to help them understand the perspectives and experiences of native non-English speaker and recent immigrant communities. This can include training on topics such as cultural norms, values, and communication styles.
3. Community Engagement: Engage with native non-English speaker and recent immigrant communities through community events, outreach programs, and other activities that promote dialogue and collaboration. 4. Participatory Processes: Use participatory processes, such as participatory budgeting or citizen juries, to involve native non-English speaker and recent immigrant communities in decision-making processes. These processes can help ensure that diverse perspectives are represented and valued.
5. Advocacy and Activism: Support advocacy and activism efforts by native non-English speaker and recent immigrant communities to amplify their voices and push for policy changes that promote inclusivity and equity. This can include advocating for language access measures, cultural competency training for decision-makers, and other initiatives that promote inclusivity.
6. Technology Solutions: Leverage technology solutions, such as online platforms and mobile apps, to provide language support services and enable remote participation in decision-making processes. This can help reach a wider audience and ensure that individuals from diverse backgrounds can participate in decision-making processes.
7. Collaboration with Community Organizations: Collaborate with community organizations that work with native non-English speaker and recent immigrant communities to provide language support services and promote their participation in decision-making processes.
