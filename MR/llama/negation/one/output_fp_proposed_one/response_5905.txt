2. Inadequate staff training: Healthcare providers may not be trained in disability etiquette or how to communicate with disabled patients. This can lead to misunderstandings, miscommunications, and a lack of empathy.
3. Limited medical options: Some medical procedures or treatments may not be suitable for disabled patients due to our unique needs. For example, some surgeries may require me to be in a specific position that is difficult or impossible for me to achieve.
4. Inadequate pain management: Disabled patients may experience more chronic pain than non-disabled individuals due to nerve damage or other conditions. However, healthcare providers may not be adequately trained in managing chronic pain in disabled patients.
5. Discrimination and stigma: Unfortunately, there is still a lot of discrimination and stigma surrounding disability. Healthcare providers may hold biases or make assumptions about my abilities based on my disability, which can lead to subpar care.
6. Lack of representation: Disabled patients are often underrepresented in medical research and clinical trials. This means that the treatments and medications available may not be tailored to our specific needs.
