Genetic conditions can have significant impacts on the lives of children living in poverty. Here are some of the potential effects:

1. Limited access to healthcare: Children with genetic conditions may face barriers in accessing quality healthcare, including diagnosis, treatment, and management of their condition. This can lead to delayed or inadequate care, which can exacerbate the condition and negatively impact their overall health and well-being.
2. Financial burden: Caring for a child with a genetic condition can be expensive, particularly if the condition requires ongoing medical treatment, therapies, or medication. Families living in poverty may struggle to cover these costs, which can lead to financial stress and decreased quality of life.
3. Social isolation: Children with genetic conditions may face social isolation due to discrimination, stigma, or lack of understanding from their peers and community. This can lead to feelings of loneliness, low self-esteem, and depression.
4. Limited access to education: Children with genetic conditions may face challenges in accessing education due to their condition. They may require accommodations or modifications to their learning environment, which can be difficult for schools to provide in areas with limited resources.
5. Emotional toll on families: Caring for a child with a genetic condition can be emotionally taxing for families, particularly if they face social isolation, financial burdens, and limited access to healthcare. This can lead to increased stress, anxiety, and depression within the family unit.
6. Limited opportunities for independent living: Children with genetic conditions may face challenges in achieving independent living due to their condition. They may require ongoing support and care, which can limit their ability to pursue their own goals and aspirations.
