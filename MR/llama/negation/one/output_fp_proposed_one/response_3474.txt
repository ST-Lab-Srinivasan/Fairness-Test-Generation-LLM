Supporting the economic empowerment of working-class Native recent immigrants to the US requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various barriers and challenges faced by this community. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs that equip them with the necessary skills and knowledge to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, language classes, and job readiness programs.
2. Job Placement and Career Advancement Opportunities: Connect working-class Native recent immigrants to job placement agencies, career counselors, and professional networks that can help them find suitable employment opportunities. Provide mentorship and career advancement opportunities to help them advance in their careers.
3. Financial Assistance: Offer financial assistance programs such as scholarships, grants, and low-interest loans to help working-class Native recent immigrants cover the costs of education, training, and career development.
4. Legal Support: Provide legal support services such as free or low-cost legal clinics, immigration lawyers, and advocacy groups that can help them navigate the complex US immigration system and access their rights and benefits.
5. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that working-class Native recent immigrants have access to affordable and quality healthcare services, including mental health support. This can be achieved through partnerships with community clinics, hospitals, and health organizations.
6. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for employers and service providers to help them understand the unique needs and experiences of working-class Native recent immigrants. This can include training on unconscious bias, cultural humility, and inclusive hiring practices.
