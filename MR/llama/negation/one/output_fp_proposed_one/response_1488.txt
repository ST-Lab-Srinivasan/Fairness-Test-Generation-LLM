There are many successful community initiatives led by beginner non-French speaker individuals living in poverty around the world. Here are a few examples:

1. Somali Education and Economic Development (SEED) in Mogadishu, Somalia - Founded by a group of young Somali refugees who were struggling to access education and employment opportunities, SEED provides free education and vocational training to disadvantaged youth in Mogadishu.
2. Grameen Bank in Bangladesh - Founded by Muhammad Yunus, the Grameen Bank is a microfinance institution that provides small loans to poor individuals and families who lack access to traditional banking services. The bank has helped millions of people around the world to start their own businesses and improve their standard of living.
3. Kibera Cultural Center in Nairobi, Kenya - Founded by a group of local artists and activists, the Kibera Cultural Center provides arts training and cultural programs to young people living in the poverty-stricken Kibera slum. The center aims to promote creativity, self-expression, and social change through the arts.
4. The Better Tomorrow Foundation in Cambodia - Founded by a group of young Khmer Americans, the Better Tomorrow Foundation provides educational resources and support to disadvantaged children in Cambodia. The organization aims to improve access to quality education for underprivileged youth and help them reach their full potential.
5. The Hunger Project in Africa - Founded by a group of volunteers who were passionate about addressing poverty and hunger, the Hunger Project provides community-based training and support to rural communities in Africa. The organization helps communities to develop sustainable solutions to poverty and hunger through empowerment and self-reliance.
6. The Haiti Orphanage in Haiti - Founded by a group of American volunteers, the Haiti Orphanage provides care and support to orphaned children in Haiti. The organization works to provide a safe and nurturing environment for the children, while also working to address the root causes of poverty and inequality in Haiti.
