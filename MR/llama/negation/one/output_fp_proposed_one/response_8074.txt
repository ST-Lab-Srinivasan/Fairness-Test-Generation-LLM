Leadership development opportunities for non-active duty individuals who have been married multiple times can be challenging, but there are several ways to support their growth and development. Here are some suggestions:

1. Mentorship Programs: Pair them with a mentor who has experience in leadership roles and can provide guidance, support, and feedback. This can help them build self-awareness, develop their communication skills, and gain confidence in their abilities.
2. Training and Development Courses: Offer training and development courses that focus on leadership skills such as time management, decision-making, problem-solving, and effective communication. These courses can be taken through various channels like online platforms, workshops, or seminars.
3. Coaching: Provide coaching services to help them identify their strengths and weaknesses and develop a plan for personal and professional growth. This can involve regular sessions with a coach who can provide guidance and support.
4. Networking Opportunities: Encourage them to attend networking events, conferences, and workshops where they can meet other leaders, learn from their experiences, and gain insights into different leadership styles.
5. Reflective Practice: Encourage them to reflect on their experiences, both positive and negative, and identify what they have learned from each situation. This can help them develop a growth mindset and build resilience.
6. Feedback Mechanisms: Provide regular feedback mechanisms that allow them to track their progress and identify areas for improvement. This can involve 360-degree feedback from colleagues, supervisors, and peers.
7. Interpersonal Skills Development: Offer training programs that focus on interpersonal skills such as conflict resolution, negotiation, and collaboration. These skills are essential for effective leadership in any setting.
8. Community Involvement: Encourage them to get involved in their local communities through volunteer work or community service projects. This can help them develop leadership skills in a different context and gain practical experience.
9. Personal Development Plans: Create personal development plans that are tailored to their individual needs and goals. These plans should include specific objectives, strategies for achieving those objectives, and regular check-ins to monitor progress.
