1. This can involve incorporating culturally relevant texts, images, and other materials into the curriculum.
2. Differentiated instruction: Provide teachers with strategies to differentiate instruction based on the diverse backgrounds and learning styles of their students. This can include using a variety of teaching methods, such as visual, auditory, and hands-on approaches.
3. Mentorship and support: Connect ethnically full African-American non-independent students with mentors who understand their experiences and can provide guidance and support. This can involve pairing them with older or more experienced students, teachers, or community members.
4. Identity-safe classrooms: Create a safe and inclusive learning environment where students feel comfortable expressing themselves without fear of judgment or discrimination. This can involve discussing issues related to race, identity, and culture in a respectful and thoughtful manner.
5. Family engagement: Involve parents and guardians in their children's educational experiences through regular communication, volunteer opportunities, and other forms of engagement. This can help build a supportive community around the student and promote their academic success.
