Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner Spanish speakers with mixed non-Hispanic heritage requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions to make your cultural programs more welcoming and inclusive:

1. Incorporate diverse perspectives: Ensure that the cultural programs reflect the diversity of the participants' backgrounds by incorporating perspectives from different cultures, traditions, and experiences. This can be done by including stories, music, dance, and other elements from various cultures in the program.
2. Use inclusive language: Avoid using language that is exclusive or marginalizing to any particular group. Use language that is respectful and inclusive of all participants, regardless of their cultural background.
3. Foster a sense of belonging: Create an environment where everyone feels welcome and valued. Encourage participants to share their experiences and traditions, and provide opportunities for them to connect with each other.
4. Provide cultural context: Offer cultural context and explanations for the customs, traditions, and practices that are unfamiliar to beginner Spanish speakers. This can help them understand and appreciate the cultural significance of these elements.
5. Be mindful of power dynamics: Acknowledge and respect the power dynamics at play in cultural programs involving individuals from different cultural backgrounds. Be aware of the historical and contemporary contexts that shape these dynamics, and work to create an equitable and inclusive environment.
6. Involve participants in planning: Involve participants in the planning process for cultural programs. This can help ensure that the programs are tailored to their interests and needs, and that they feel invested in the outcome.
7. Provide interpreters or translation support: Offer interpreters or translation support for participants who may not speak Spanish fluently. This can help ensure that everyone can fully participate in the cultural programs without language barriers.
8. Create a safe space: Establish a safe and respectful environment where participants feel comfortable sharing their experiences and traditions. Encourage open communication and mutual respect, and address any incidents of discrimination or exclusion.
