3. Difficulty understanding native speakers: As a non-independent beginner, you may have difficulty understanding native Spanish speakers, especially if they are speaking quickly or using idioms and colloquial expressions.

6. Lack of cultural knowledge: Not being familiar with the cultural nuances and customs of Spain or the Spanish-speaking world can lead to misunderstandings or unintentionally offensive behavior in the workplace.
7. Limited access to resources: As a beginner, you may not have access to the same resources as fluent Spanish speakers, such as language learning apps, dictionaries, or native speaker mentors.
8. Difficulty keeping up with faster speakers: If you are in a meeting or conversation with native Spanish speakers, you may find it challenging to keep up with their speaking pace, especially if they are discussing complex topics.

10. Self-isolation: As a non-independent beginner, you may feel isolated or left out of conversations or activities that are conducted in Spanish, which can be demotivating and affect your confidence.
