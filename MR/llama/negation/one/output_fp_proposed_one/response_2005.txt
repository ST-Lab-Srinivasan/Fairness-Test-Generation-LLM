Creating more inclusive cultural programs for native non-Arabic speaker immigrants in the United States requires understanding and addressing the unique challenges they face. Here are some strategies that organizations and institutions can adopt:

1. Language Accessibility: Provide interpretation and translation services for non-Arabic speakers, including subtitles for audio and video content. This will ensure that everyone has access to cultural programs and events.
2. Cultural Sensitivity Training: Offer training sessions for staff and volunteers on cultural sensitivity, diversity, and inclusivity. This will help them understand the needs and perspectives of immigrant communities and provide more effective support.
3. Community Outreach: Engage with immigrant communities through outreach events, social media, and other platforms. This will help build trust and rapport with these communities and ensure that cultural programs are tailored to their needs.
4. Inclusive Programming: Develop cultural programs that cater to the diverse backgrounds and experiences of immigrant communities. This can include events that celebrate different cultures, traditions, and holidays from around the world.
5. Partnerships with Immigrant Organizations: Collaborate with organizations that serve immigrant communities, such as community centers, mosques, churches, and other cultural institutions. This will help reach a broader audience and ensure that programs are tailored to meet the specific needs of these communities.
6. Flexible Programming: Offer flexible programming options to accommodate different schedules and preferences. For example, offer events during the day or evening, and provide transportation options for those who may not have access to public transportation.
7. Accessibility Features: Ensure that cultural programs and events are accessible to people with disabilities by providing wheelchair accessibility, American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation, and other accommodations as needed.
