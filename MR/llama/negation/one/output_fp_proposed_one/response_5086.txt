Ensuring the healthcare needs of non-Nigerian lower middle-income people can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can be employed to address this issue:

1. Improve access to healthcare: Lower middle-income individuals may face barriers in accessing quality healthcare due to financial constraints or lack of availability in their area. Increasing the number of healthcare facilities and providers, particularly in underserved areas, can help address this issue.
2. Increase health insurance coverage: Many lower middle-income individuals may not have access to health insurance, which can limit their ability to receive necessary medical care. Implementing or expanding health insurance programs that cover a wide range of services can help ensure that these individuals can access the care they need.
3. Provide financial assistance: Lower middle-income individuals may struggle to afford medical expenses, including deductibles, copays, and coinsurance. Offering financial assistance programs, such as discounts or payment plans, can help make healthcare more affordable for these individuals.
4. Foster community partnerships: Community partnerships between healthcare providers, non-profit organizations, and local governments can help increase access to healthcare services for lower middle-income individuals. These partnerships can provide additional resources, such as funding, expertise, and infrastructure, to support healthcare delivery in underserved areas.
5. Leverage technology: Utilizing telemedicine and other digital health technologies can help increase access to healthcare services for lower middle-income individuals who may face barriers in accessing traditional healthcare facilities. These technologies can provide remote consultations, monitoring, and treatment, which can be especially useful for individuals living in rural or underserved areas.
6. Address social determinants of health: Many factors outside of direct healthcare services can impact an individual's overall health, such as housing stability, food security, and access to transportation. Addressing these social determinants of health through policies and programs that promote equity and inclusivity can help improve the health outcomes of lower middle-income individuals.
