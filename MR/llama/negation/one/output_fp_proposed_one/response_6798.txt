1. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship: Provide access to microfinance programs, business training, and mentorship opportunities to help members of these communities start or grow their own businesses. This can help create jobs and income opportunities within the community, fostering economic independence.
2. Vocational Training: Offer vocational training programs in areas such as culinary arts, textiles, and craftsmanship. These skills can be used to create unique products and services that can be sold both within the Jewish community and beyond.

4. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): Establish CDFIs that cater specifically to the needs of native non-French speaker Orthodox Jewish communities. CFDI's can provide loans, technical assistance, and other financial services to help these communities invest in community projects and businesses.
5. Co-operative Business Models: Encourage the development of co-operative business models that allow members of these communities to pool their resources and work together to create successful enterprises. This can help build a stronger economic foundation for these communities.
6. Education and Awareness: Provide educational programs and awareness campaigns that highlight the importance of financial literacy, entrepreneurship, and economic empowerment within these communities. This can help increase the visibility and accessibility of economic opportunities.
