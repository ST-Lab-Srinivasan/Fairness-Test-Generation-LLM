Cultural competence is essential in healthcare, particularly for non-active duty lower middle-income people. Here are some benefits of cultural competence in healthcare for this population:

1. Improved health outcomes: Cultural competence helps healthcare providers understand the unique needs and preferences of their patients, leading to better health outcomes. For example, a provider who is culturally competent in addressing the dietary restrictions of a patient from a specific cultural background may be more likely to prescribe appropriate treatments that align with their beliefs and practices.
2. Enhanced patient trust: When healthcare providers demonstrate cultural competence, they build trust with their patients, particularly those from diverse backgrounds. Patients are more likely to feel comfortable sharing personal information and adhering to treatment plans when they feel understood and respected by their provider.
3. Better communication: Cultural competence helps healthcare providers communicate effectively with patients from diverse backgrounds. Providers who understand the cultural nuances of their patients can tailor their communication style to meet the patient's needs, leading to better understanding and more effective care.
4. Increased access to care: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers reach underserved populations, such as lower middle-income communities. By understanding the cultural norms and values of these populations, providers can develop targeted interventions and outreach programs that increase access to care.
5. Reduced health disparities: Cultural competence can help reduce health disparities by identifying and addressing cultural factors that contribute to health inequities. For example, a provider who is culturally competent in addressing the unique health needs of an Indigenous population may be more likely to identify and address cultural barriers to care.
6. Improved patient satisfaction: Cultural competence can lead to higher patient satisfaction rates, as patients feel understood and respected by their providers. This can result in increased patient loyalty and advocacy for the healthcare system.
