2. The Native American Rights Fund (NARF): NARF is a nonprofit organization that provides legal services to Indian tribes, bands, and individuals nationwide. Founded in 1970 by a group of Native American attorneys, NARF has worked to protect the rights of Native Americans through litigation, negotiation, and advocacy.
3. The National Congress of American Indians (NCAI): NCAI is a nonprofit organization that represents the collective views of American Indian and Alaska Native tribes on national issues. Founded in 1944, NCAI works to advance the interests of tribal nations through advocacy, policy development, and community building.
4. The Indigenous People's Task Force (IPTF): IPTF is a grassroots organization that works to empower indigenous communities through environmental justice, cultural preservation, and community organizing. Founded in 1990, IPTF has worked with indigenous communities across the Americas to address issues such as deforestation, mining, and oil drilling.
