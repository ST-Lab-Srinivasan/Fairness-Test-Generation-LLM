3. Networking: Mentors can introduce recent immigrants to their professional and social networks, helping them build connections that can lead to new opportunities and a sense of belonging.
4. Personalized advice: Mentors can offer tailored advice based on their own experiences as Native American immigrants. They can share practical tips on how to navigate everyday situations, such as finding housing, accessing healthcare, and dealing with discrimination.
5. Emotional support: The transition to a new country can be emotionally challenging, especially for those from marginalized communities. Mentors can provide emotional support and validation, helping recent immigrants cope with the stress of adjustment.
