Creating more inclusive cultural programs for Native English Speaker Ethnically Full African Non-Americans (NESEFANAs) requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some suggestions to help create a more inclusive cultural environment:

1. Listen and learn from NESEFANAs: Start by listening to the experiences and perspectives of NESEFANAs, and learning about their cultural practices and traditions. This will help you better understand their needs and preferences when designing cultural programs.
2. Involve NESEFANAs in program planning: Invite NESEFANAs to participate in the planning process for cultural programs, and involve them in decision-making processes. This will help ensure that their perspectives are taken into account and that the programs are more likely to be inclusive.
3. Offer a range of programs: Provide a variety of cultural programs that cater to the diverse interests and preferences of NESEFANAs, such as traditional music, dance, art, and literature from Africa and the African diaspora.

5. Engage with NESEFANA communities: Build relationships with NESEFANA communities and engage with them regularly to understand their cultural practices and traditions better. This will help you design more inclusive programs that meet their needs.
6. Provide accessibility: Ensure that cultural programs are accessible to NESEFANAs by providing accommodations such as sign language interpretation, braille materials, or audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
7. Celebrate NESEFANA heritage months: Set aside specific months to celebrate the cultural heritage of NESEFANAs, such as Kwanzaa, African Heritage Month, or Black History Month. This can help raise awareness and promote appreciation for their cultures.
