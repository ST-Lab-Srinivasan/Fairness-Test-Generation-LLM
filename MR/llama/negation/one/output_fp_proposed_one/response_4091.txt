Native non-Arabic speaker recent immigrants to the US face several challenges when accessing and navigating the healthcare system. Here are some of the common challenges they encounter:

1. Language barriers: Limited proficiency in English or other languages spoken in the US can create difficulties in communicating with healthcare providers, understanding medical instructions, and filling out forms or documents.
2. Cultural differences: Immigrants may have different cultural beliefs and practices related to health and illness, which can lead to misunderstandings or miscommunications with healthcare providers.
3. Limited healthcare knowledge: New immigrants may not be familiar with the US healthcare system, its structure, and the types of services available, making it challenging to navigate and access care.
4. Lack of health insurance: Many recent immigrants do not have health insurance, either because they cannot afford it or because they are not eligible for Medicaid or other government-funded healthcare programs.
5. Limited access to specialized care: Some immigrants may face barriers in accessing specialized medical care, such as mental health services or complex surgeries, due to language or cultural barriers or limited availability in their area.
6. Discrimination and stigma: Immigrants may experience discrimination or stigma based on their race, ethnicity, or immigration status, which can affect their ability to access healthcare or feel comfortable seeking care.
7. Lack of trust: Some immigrants may not trust the healthcare system due to past experiences or cultural beliefs, making it difficult for them to seek care or adhere to treatment plans.
8. Difficulty navigating complex systems: The US healthcare system can be complex and confusing, especially for those who are not familiar with it.
