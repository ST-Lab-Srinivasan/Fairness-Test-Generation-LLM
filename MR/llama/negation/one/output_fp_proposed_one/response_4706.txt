2. Education and Awareness Campaigns: Raising awareness about non-genetic conditions and their impact on individuals can help reduce stigma and promote understanding. 3. Employment Support: Many non-genetic conditions can make it challenging for individuals to maintain employment. Governments and organizations can provide job training programs, career counseling, and other forms of support to help individuals with non-genetic conditions enter or remain in the workforce.
4. Access to Rehabilitation Services: Non-genetic conditions often require rehabilitation services to manage symptoms and improve quality of life. Governments and organizations can provide access to physical, occupational, and speech therapy, as well as other forms of rehabilitation support.
5. Home Modifications: For individuals with non-genetic conditions that affect mobility or daily living activities, home modifications can help improve safety and accessibility. This can include installing ramps, widening doorways, and modifying bathrooms to make them more accessible.
