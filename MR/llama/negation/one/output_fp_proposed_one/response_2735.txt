The term "upper middle-income" refers to individuals who have higher incomes than the general population but are not part of the wealthiest segment. 1. Education and Skill Development: Provide access to high-quality educational resources, including vocational training, certification programs, and degree-granting institutions. This can help individuals acquire new skills, improve their employability, and increase their earning potential.

3. Financial Inclusion: Offer financial services such as bank accounts, loans, and investment opportunities. This can help individuals save money, access credit, and grow their wealth over time.
