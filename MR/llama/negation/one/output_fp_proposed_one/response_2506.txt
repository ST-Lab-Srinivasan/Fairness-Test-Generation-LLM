The economic empowerment of Native American non-citizen lower-income individuals is a critical issue that affects their overall well-being and quality of life. Here are some potential strategies for supporting their economic empowerment:

1. Education and job training programs: Providing access to education and job training programs can help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge, which can lead to better job opportunities and higher earning potential. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and certification programs in fields such as healthcare, technology, and sustainability.
2. Microfinance initiatives: Many Native American communities lack access to traditional banking services, making it difficult for individuals to start or grow a business. Microfinance initiatives, such as microlending and crowdfunding, can help provide capital and financial support to entrepreneurs in these communities.
3. Entrepreneurship and small business development: Encouraging and supporting entrepreneurship in Native American communities can help create jobs and economic opportunities. This can involve providing mentorship, training, and resources to help individuals start and grow their own businesses.
4. Community development investments: Investing in community development projects, such as infrastructure improvements, housing developments, and cultural preservation efforts, can help stimulate economic growth and improve living standards in Native American communities.
5. Tax credits and incentives: Offering tax credits and incentives to businesses that operate in or near Native American reservations can help encourage investment and job creation in these areas.
6. Addressing systemic barriers: Many of the economic challenges faced by Native American communities are a result of systemic barriers, such as discrimination, lack of access to resources, and inadequate funding. Addressing these barriers through policy changes and advocacy efforts can help create a more level playing field for economic growth and development.
7. Building partnerships: Collaborating with local organizations, businesses, and government agencies can help identify and address specific economic challenges in Native American communities.
