Early pregnancy, especially in the first trimester, can be a challenging time for many women. It is a period of significant physical and emotional changes, which can impact mental health. Here are some ways to support the mental health of recently early pregnant (1st trimester) non-widowed people:

1. Listen and validate their feelings: Allow them to express their emotions without judgment. Let them know that it's okay to feel overwhelmed, anxious, or scared, and offer support and reassurance.
2. Encourage self-care: Suggest activities that promote relaxation and stress relief, such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises. Encourage them to take breaks and prioritize their well-being during this time.
3. Offer emotional support: Be present for them, listen actively, and offer words of encouragement. Remind them that they are not alone and that you are there to support them through this journey.
4. Provide resources: Share information about prenatal mental health services, postpartum support groups, or hotlines that can provide additional help and guidance. Offer to accompany them to appointments or meetings, if desired.
5. Help with practical tasks: Offer to help with errands, household chores, or childcare duties, which can help reduce stress and promote relaxation.
6. Respect their boundaries: Allow them to set the pace and agenda for your interactions, respecting their need for privacy and alone time. Avoid pushing them to talk or share more than they are comfortable with.
7. Be mindful of language: Use language that is sensitive and inclusive when discussing pregnancy, motherhood, and mental health. Avoid making assumptions or using language that can be stigmatizing or hurtful.
8. Encourage communication: Create a safe space for them to express their thoughts and feelings without fear of judgment. Encourage open communication and active listening.
9. Offer distractions: Suggest activities or hobbies that can help take their mind off pregnancy-related stressors, such as reading, watching movies, or engaging in creative pursuits.
