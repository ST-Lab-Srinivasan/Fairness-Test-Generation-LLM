4. Lack of representation and voice: People with disabilities are often underrepresented in decision-making processes and may have limited opportunities to advocate for their rights and needs. This can result in policies and programs that do not address the specific needs of this population.

6. Limited access to assistive technology: Assistive devices and technology are often not available or affordable for individuals with disabilities in Mexico, which can limit their ability to participate fully in society.
7. Inadequate transportation options: Public transportation in Mexico may not be accessible for individuals with disabilities, making it difficult for them to access healthcare services, education, and employment opportunities.
