Supporting the economic empowerment of ethnically full African non-American long-term unemployed individuals is crucial for promoting inclusive economic growth and reducing poverty in the United States. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Provide Access to Job Training and Education Programs: Many long-term unemployed individuals lack the skills and knowledge required to compete in the job market. Offering job training and education programs tailored to their needs can help them acquire new skills, improve their employability, and gain confidence.
2. Address Systemic Barriers: Discrimination, lack of access to networking opportunities, and limited access to resources are significant barriers that prevent ethnically full African non-American individuals from finding stable employment. Addressing these systemic issues can help level the playing field and create more equitable job opportunities.
3. Foster Community Support: Building a supportive community can help individuals feel less isolated and more motivated to find work. This can be achieved through mentorship programs, networking events, and peer support groups.
4. Encourage Entrepreneurship: Many long-term unemployed individuals may find success as entrepreneurs. Providing resources and support for starting a business can help them become economically independent and contribute to the overall economy.
5. Advocate for Policy Change: Policies that promote job creation, reduce discrimination, and increase access to education and job training can help address the systemic issues faced by ethnically full African non-American long-term unemployed individuals. Advocating for policy change at the local, state, and federal levels can help create a more inclusive economy.
6. Provide Financial Assistance: Offering financial assistance, such as grants or loans, can help individuals cover living expenses while they are actively seeking employment. This can provide peace of mind and allow them to focus on finding a job rather than worrying about how to pay their bills.
