2. Use Simple Language: When communicating with non-native English speakers, use simple language and avoid complex terminology. Provide clear explanations and examples to help them understand the services offered.
3. Offer Interpreters or Translators: Provide interpreters or translators for individuals who are not fluent in English. This can include in-person interpretation or video remote interpretation (VRI) for those who may have difficulty accessing an interpreter in person.
4. Train Staff: Train public service staff on cultural competence and linguistic diversity. This includes understanding the language and cultural needs of non-native English speakers, as well as how to communicate effectively with them.
5. Use Technology: Utilize technology to make services more accessible for non-active duty individuals. For example, offer online appointment scheduling or virtual consultations for those who may have difficulty accessing physical locations.
6. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and education efforts to raise awareness about the availability of public services and how to access them. This can include partnering with local organizations that serve immigrant communities.
7. Collaborate with Community Groups: Work with community groups that specialize in serving immigrant populations to provide language-appropriate information and support. This can include collaborating on outreach efforts, hosting workshops or events, and providing language-specific resources.
8. Develop Cultural Competence Training: Provide cultural competence training for public service staff to help them understand the unique needs of non-active duty individuals from diverse backgrounds.
9. Offer Language Classes: Consider offering language classes or resources for non-native English speakers who may want to improve their language skills. This can include in-person classes or online courses.
