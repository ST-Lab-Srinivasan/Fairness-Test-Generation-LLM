The act was seen as a victory for advocacy groups that had been pushing for greater rights for non-Muslim immigrants in India.
2. 3. National Population Policy (2000): In 2000, the Indian government released the National Population Policy, which aimed to improve the health and well-being of marginalized communities, including immigrants. The policy focused on issues such as access to healthcare, education, and economic opportunities.
4. Right to Education Act (2009): In 2009, the Indian government passed the Right to Education Act, which guarantees free and compulsory education to all children between the ages of 6 and 18. The act was seen as a victory for advocacy groups that had been pushing for greater access to education for marginalized communities, including immigrants.
