Here are a few examples of successful community initiatives led by non-Arabic speaker upper upper-middle-income individuals from different parts of the world:

1. The Big Issue Australia - Founded by Kim Cobb, an Australian entrepreneur who saw the potential for homeless people to sell copies of a magazine they produced. Today, The Big Issue has become a global movement with editions in several countries and has helped thousands of people get back on their feet.
2. War Child Canada - Founded by John Peller, a Canadian musician who was moved by the plight of children affected by war. War Child Canada provides support to children and communities affected by conflict through various programs, including music-based interventions.
3. VSO - Volunteers for Development Overseas is a UK-based organization that partners with local organizations to provide volunteers with skills and expertise to help tackle poverty and inequality in developing countries. Founded by Roy Territt in 1958, VSO has placed over 70,000 volunteers in more than 40 countries around the world.
4. Teach For All - Founded by Wendy Kopp, Teach For All is a global network of independent non-profit organizations that work together to advance educational equity and excellence. The organization was established in 2007 and has since grown to include 29 countries across five continents.
5. The Malala Fund - Founded by Malala Yousafzai, a Pakistani activist for girls' education, the Malala Fund works to ensure that girls around the world have access to quality education. The organization was established in 2013 and has since provided support to girls in countries such as Pakistan, Afghanistan, and Nigeria.
6. The Against Malaria Foundation (AMF) - Founded by a group of philanthropists including Bill Gates, the AMF provides funding for insecticide-treated bed nets to prevent malaria in developing countries. Since its establishment in 2003, the organization has distributed over 100 million nets and saved an estimated 10 million lives.
