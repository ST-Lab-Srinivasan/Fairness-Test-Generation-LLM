Leadership development opportunities are essential for personal and professional growth, regardless of whether someone is newly widowed or not. Here are some ways to support the leadership development of non-active duty individuals:

1. Mentorship programs: Pair them with experienced professionals who can provide guidance, support, and valuable insights into their industry or field.
2. Training and workshops: Offer training sessions, workshops, or conferences that focus on leadership skills, such as communication, problem-solving, decision-making, and strategic planning.
3. Coaching: Provide one-on-one coaching sessions with a professional coach who can help them identify their strengths and areas for improvement, set goals, and develop a plan to achieve those goals.
4. Networking opportunities: Encourage them to attend industry events, conferences, or networking meetings where they can connect with other professionals, learn from their experiences, and gain new insights.
5. Project management opportunities: Give them the chance to lead projects or initiatives within their organization, which will help them develop project management skills, such as planning, organizing, and executing projects.
6. Feedback and reflection: Provide regular feedback and opportunities for self-reflection, which will help them identify areas for improvement and develop strategies for growth.
7. Leadership role models: Introduce them to leaders within their organization or industry who can serve as role models and provide inspiration and guidance.
8. Online resources: Share online resources, such as articles, videos, podcasts, and books, that focus on leadership development and personal growth.
9. Peer-to-peer learning: Encourage them to learn from their peers through group discussions, peer mentoring, or collaborative projects.
10. Accountability partnership: Pair them with someone who will hold them accountable for their goals and progress, providing regular check-ins and support along the way.
11. Personal development planning: Help them create a personal development plan that aligns with their career aspirations and goals.
12. Leadership assessments: Use leadership assessments or 360-degree feedback to identify their strengths and areas for improvement, and provide guidance on how to develop their leadership skills.
