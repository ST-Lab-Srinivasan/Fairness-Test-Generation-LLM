Creating more inclusive recreational activities for non-permanent resident deep in poverty people can be a challenging task, but there are several strategies that can help make these activities more accessible and enjoyable for everyone. Here are some ideas:

1. Partner with local organizations: Collaborate with local organizations that serve low-income communities to reach a wider audience. These partnerships can provide resources, expertise, and networks to help you connect with people who might not have access to recreational activities otherwise.
2. Offer flexible pricing options: Provide affordable pricing options for people on tight budgets. Consider offering discounts or sliding scale fees based on income level to make your activities more accessible.
3. Emphasize community building: Create opportunities for people to connect with each other and build relationships. This can help combat feelings of isolation and loneliness that are often associated with poverty.
4. Incorporate cultural elements: Incorporate aspects of local culture and traditions into your recreational activities to make them more meaningful and enjoyable for participants. This can help people feel more connected to their community and the activities themselves.
5. Provide transportation options: Offer transportation services or provide information on public transportation options to help people get to your activities. This can be especially important for people who may not have access to reliable transportation.
6. Make it fun!: Prioritize making the activities enjoyable and engaging for everyone involved. Use music, art, and other forms of creative expression to make the activities more interesting and fun.
7. Involve the community in planning and decision-making: Engage with the community in the planning and decision-making process around recreational activities. This can help ensure that the activities are responsive to the needs and preferences of the local population.
8. Provide meals or snacks: Offer meals or snacks during the activities to help meet basic needs and make the activities more enjoyable.
9. Be flexible: Be willing to adjust your offerings based on feedback from participants and the community. This can help ensure that the activities are meeting the needs of the people they are intended to serve.
