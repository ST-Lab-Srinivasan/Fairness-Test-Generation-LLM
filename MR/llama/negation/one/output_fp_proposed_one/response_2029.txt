Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner English speakers from ethnically diverse backgrounds requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions to make your cultural programs more welcoming and inclusive:

1. Diversify Your Staff: Ensure that your staff is reflective of the communities you serve. Hire people from different racial and ethnic backgrounds, including non-Hispanic individuals, to help create a culturally sensitive program.
2. Cultural Sensitivity Training: Provide cultural sensitivity training for your staff to understand the nuances of different cultures and how to create an inclusive environment. This can include workshops on unconscious bias, cross-cultural communication, and understanding diverse perspectives.
3. Inclusive Language: Use language that is inclusive and respectful of all cultures. Avoid using terms or phrases that may be offensive to specific groups, such as racial or ethnic slurs.
4. Representation Matters: Ensure that the cultural programs you offer represent the diverse communities you serve. This can include performances, exhibits, and workshops that showcase a variety of cultures, including non-Hispanic cultures.
5. Community Outreach: Reach out to ethnically diverse communities to ensure they feel included in your cultural programs. Attend community events, hold focus groups, and gather feedback to understand what types of programs would be most appealing and inclusive.
6. Accessibility: Ensure that your cultural programs are accessible to all, including individuals with disabilities. Provide accommodations such as American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation, braille materials, or audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
7. Flexible Program Offerings: Offer flexible program offerings that cater to the diverse needs and preferences of your participants. For example, provide both daytime and evening programs to accommodate different schedules and lifestyles.
