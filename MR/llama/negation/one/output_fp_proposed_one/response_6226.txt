Supporting the social inclusion of Native Non-English Speaker (NES) small-government advocates can be crucial for their well-being and success in their advocacy work. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Language Support: Provide language support services such as interpreters, translators, or language classes to help NES advocates communicate effectively with stakeholders. This can include spoken language interpretation, written translation, and language training programs.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training to help NES advocates understand the cultural norms and values of their target audience. This can include training on cultural differences in communication styles, conflict resolution, and decision-making processes.
3. Accessibility Accommodations: Ensure that advocacy events and meetings are accessible to NES advocates with disabilities by providing accommodations such as sign language interpreters, Braille materials, or assistive technology.
4. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair NES advocates with experienced advocates who can provide guidance and support in navigating the advocacy landscape. This can include training on advocacy techniques, networking opportunities, and personalized coaching.
5. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach efforts to connect with NES advocates and their communities. This can involve partnering with local organizations, hosting events in diverse locations, and using multilingual communication channels.
6. Advocacy Skills Training: Offer training programs that teach NES advocates the skills they need to effectively engage in advocacy work, such as public speaking, writing, and lobbying. This can include tailored training sessions that address the unique needs of NES advocates.
7. Networking Opportunities: Provide networking opportunities for NES advocates to connect with other advocates, policymakers, and stakeholders. This can involve organizing conferences, workshops, or roundtable discussions that bring together diverse perspectives and experiences.
8. Advocacy Materials in Multiple Languages: Make available advocacy materials such as pamphlets, brochures, and posters in multiple languages to ensure that NES advocates can access information in their preferred language.
