1. Language Access Services: Provide interpreters and translators for non-Mandarin speakers, especially in hospitals and clinics where language barriers may hinder effective communication. Ensure that these services are available in a timely manner, and that they are accessible to all patients, regardless of their language proficiency.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training for healthcare providers to help them understand the unique needs and beliefs of non-Mandarin speaking Mexican individuals. This can include training on how to communicate with patients who may have different communication styles or cultural practices.
3. Patient Education Materials: Provide patient education materials in the language spoken by the patient, including pamphlets, videos, and other visual aids. This can help ensure that patients understand their health conditions, treatment options, and self-care instructions.

Health Literacy: Improve health literacy among non-Mandarin speaking Mexican individuals by providing clear and concise information about their health conditions, treatments, and preventive measures. Use simple language and avoid technical jargon to ensure that patients can understand the information.
5. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and education efforts to raise awareness about the importance of healthcare access for non-Mandarin speaking Mexican individuals. Partner with local organizations, community centers, and religious institutions to reach a wider audience.
6. Telemedicine: Utilize telemedicine services to provide remote consultations and monitoring for patients who may face barriers in accessing healthcare due to language or cultural differences.
