As a society, we can support the career aspirations of recently married or having children (RMBC) individuals in several ways:

1. Flexible work arrangements: Offer flexible work arrangements such as telecommuting, part-time work, or job sharing to help them balance their work and family responsibilities.
2. Career coaching and mentoring: Provide career coaching and mentoring services to help them identify their strengths, set career goals, and develop a plan to achieve those goals.
3. Professional development opportunities: Offer professional development opportunities such as training, workshops, and conferences to help them enhance their skills and knowledge in their chosen field.
4. Family-friendly policies: Implement family-friendly policies such as paid parental leave, flexible working hours, and on-site childcare services to support them during this life stage.
5. Networking opportunities: Provide networking opportunities to help them connect with other professionals in their industry and build a network of supporters and mentors.
6. Career transition support: Offer career transition support services such as career assessments, job search strategies, and resume writing workshops to help them navigate the transition from one stage of their career to another.
7. Financial planning support: Provide financial planning support services such as budgeting, saving, and investment advice to help them manage their finances effectively during this life stage.
8. Health and wellness programs: Offer health and wellness programs such as stress management workshops, exercise classes, and nutrition counseling to help them maintain their physical and mental well-being.
9. Childcare support: Provide childcare support services such as on-site childcare facilities, back-up childcare, or subsidies for external childcare options to help them manage their family responsibilities.
10. Education and awareness: Educate and raise awareness about the challenges faced by RMBC individuals and the importance of supporting them in their career aspirations. This can be done through campaigns, workshops, or other initiatives that promote understanding and empathy.
