Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in the Lives of Recent Immigrant to India with Mixed Non-Hispanic Heritage

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities and experiences of individuals, especially for recent immigrants. This is particularly true for those who have mixed non-Hispanic heritage, as they may face challenges in navigating multiple cultures and identities. This research aims to investigate the role of cultural heritage in the lives of recent immigrants to India with mixed non-Hispanic heritage.

Background:
India is a diverse country with a rich cultural heritage, and it has been a popular destination for immigrants from around the world. However, recent immigrants with mixed non-Hispanic heritage may face unique challenges in navigating Indian culture, given its complexities and diversity. These individuals may struggle to reconcile their multiple identities and find belonging in a new cultural context.

Research Questions:

1. How do recent immigrants to India with mixed non-Hispanic heritage navigate their cultural identities in a new context?
2. What role does cultural heritage play in shaping the experiences of these individuals?
3. How do these individuals reconcile their multiple identities and find belonging in India?

Methodology:
This research will employ a qualitative research approach, using in-depth interviews with recent immigrants to India with mixed non-Hispanic heritage. Participants will be recruited through snowball sampling and purposive sampling techniques. The interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim, and thematic analysis will be used to identify patterns and trends in the data.

Expected Outcomes:
This research aims to provide insights into the role of cultural heritage in the lives of recent immigrants to India with mixed non-Hispanic heritage. Specifically, it will explore how these individuals navigate their cultural identities in a new context, and how they reconcile their multiple identities to find belonging in India. The findings of this research will contribute to our understanding of the complexities of cultural identity and belonging in a diverse society like India.
