Creating more inclusive social policies requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the complex interplay of factors that contribute to poverty and exclusion. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive social policies for individuals below the poverty line, not just in the short term but also over their lifetimes:

1. Address the root causes of poverty: Poverty is often a result of systemic issues such as discrimination, inequality, and lack of access to resources and opportunities. To create more inclusive social policies, it's essential to address these underlying issues by promoting policies that foster equity and inclusion.
2. Focus on early childhood development: Early interventions can have a significant impact on a child's cognitive, social, and emotional development. Investing in programs that support early childhood development can help break the cycle of poverty and promote more inclusive social policies.
3. Provide access to quality education: Education is a critical factor in reducing poverty and promoting social mobility. Ensuring access to quality education for all, particularly for those from disadvantaged backgrounds, can help create more inclusive social policies.
4. Promote job training and placement: Job training programs can help individuals acquire the skills they need to compete in the labor market. Placement services can also help connect individuals with job opportunities, especially for those from marginalized communities.
5. Support microfinance initiatives: Microfinance programs provide small loans and other financial services to low-income individuals, enabling them to start or expand their businesses. This can help promote economic mobility and create more inclusive social policies.
6. Encourage community involvement: Community-based programs that engage individuals from all walks of life can help promote social cohesion and inclusion. These programs can also provide opportunities for individuals to build relationships, share knowledge, and support one another.
7. Address health disparities: Health disparities can have a significant impact on an individual's ability to participate in society and reach their full potential. Addressing these disparities through policies that promote access to healthcare, health education, and disease prevention can help create more inclusive social policies.
