Here are some strategies that educators and administrators can implement to meet the educational needs of these students:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for teachers and staff to help them understand the diverse backgrounds and experiences of mixed Asian heritage non-civilian students. This training can help educators recognize and address potential biases and barriers that may impact these students' educational experience.
2. Inclusive Curriculum: Develop an inclusive curriculum that reflects the diversity of students' cultural backgrounds. This can include incorporating texts, images, and other materials that represent diverse Asian cultures and experiences. By doing so, students will feel seen and valued in their educational environment.
3. Identity-Based Programming: Develop identity-based programming that acknowledges and supports the unique identities of mixed Asian heritage non-civilian students. This can include creating spaces for these students to connect with peers who share similar experiences, and providing resources and support tailored to their specific needs.
4. Safe Spaces: Create safe spaces for mixed Asian heritage non-civilian students to express themselves without fear of judgment or discrimination. This can include designated areas in schools where these students can retreat for emotional support and validation.
5. Family Engagement: Encourage family engagement and involvement in the educational process. By doing so, mixed Asian heritage non-civilian students will feel more connected to their educational environment and supported in their academic journey.
6. Community Partnerships: Develop partnerships with local Asian American communities and organizations to provide additional resources and support for mixed Asian heritage non-civilian students. 7. Student Voice: Encourage mixed Asian heritage non-civilian students to share their perspectives and experiences through student voice and choice activities.
