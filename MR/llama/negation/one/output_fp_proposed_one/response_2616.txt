Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping the Identities of Recently Divorced Multiple Times Beginner Non-French Speaker Individuals

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, particularly those who have gone through multiple divorces and are beginners in the French language. This study aims to investigate how cultural heritage influences the identities of these individuals and how they navigate their newfound independence.

Background:
Divorce can be a challenging experience, especially for those who have been married multiple times. It requires significant emotional, psychological, and social adjustments. For non-French speakers, the challenge is even more daunting as they must adapt to a new language and culture. Moreover, cultural heritage can significantly impact how individuals perceive themselves and their place in society.

Research Questions:

1. How do recently divorced multiple times beginner non-French speaker individuals perceive their cultural heritage?
2. What role does cultural heritage play in shaping the identities of these individuals?
3. How do these individuals navigate their newfound independence and cultural identity post-divorce?

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research approach, using in-depth interviews with recently divorced multiple times beginner non-French speaker individuals. Participants will be recruited through social media platforms, online forums, and support groups. The interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim. Thematic analysis will be used to identify patterns and themes in the data.

Significance:
This study will contribute to our understanding of how cultural heritage influences the identities of individuals going through divorce, particularly those who are non-French speakers. The findings will provide insights into how these individuals navigate their newfound independence and cultural identity post-divorce. The study's results can inform support services and interventions aimed at helping these individuals cope with the challenges of divorce and cultural adjustment.
