Impact of Genetic Conditions on Lives of Older Adults Over 90 Years Old (Over 85):

1. Increased healthcare costs: Individuals with genetic conditions may require more frequent medical check-ups, treatments, and hospitalizations, leading to increased healthcare costs.
2. Limited mobility and independence: Genetic conditions can cause musculoskeletal problems, neurological disorders, or other physical limitations that can restrict an individual's mobility and independence.
3. Social isolation: Older adults with genetic conditions may experience social isolation due to decreased physical ability, fear of being a burden to family members, or loss of driving privileges.
4. Emotional distress: Living with a genetic condition can cause emotional distress, anxiety, and depression, particularly if the condition is perceived as unpredictable or incurable.
5. Stigma and discrimination: Individuals with genetic conditions may face stigma and discrimination from society, which can further exacerbate their emotional distress.
6. Impact on caregivers: Genetic conditions can significantly impact the lives of caregivers, particularly family members who provide continuous support and care. Caregivers may experience emotional stress, financial burden, and social isolation.
7. Limited access to healthcare: Older adults with genetic conditions may face challenges in accessing timely and appropriate healthcare due to factors such as limited mobility, lack of transportation, or geographic barriers.
8. Cognitive decline: Some genetic conditions can cause cognitive decline or dementia, which can significantly impact an individual's quality of life and independence.
9. Increased risk of comorbidities: Older adults with genetic conditions may be at increased risk of developing other health problems, such as cardiovascular disease, diabetes, or respiratory disorders.
10. Limited opportunities for social engagement and community participation: Genetic conditions can limit an individual's ability to participate in social activities, volunteer, or engage in hobbies, leading to feelings of purposelessness and isolation.
