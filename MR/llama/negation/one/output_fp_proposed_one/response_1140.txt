Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner English speaker mixed non-Hispanic heritage individuals requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Understand the diverse backgrounds of your participants: Recognize that your participants come from different racial, ethnic, and cultural backgrounds. Be aware of the unique experiences and perspectives each individual brings to the table.
2. Incorporate a variety of cultures: Make sure your cultural programs reflect the diversity of your participants by incorporating elements from multiple cultures. This could include music, dance, art, literature, and food from different parts of the world.
3. Use inclusive language: Avoid using language that is exclusive or stereotypical. Instead, use language that is respectful and inclusive of all cultural backgrounds.
4. Encourage participation: Create opportunities for participants to share their experiences and perspectives. This could involve small group discussions, whole-group activities, or individual reflection exercises.
5. Provide resources and support: Offer resources and support to help participants learn more about different cultures and how to engage with them in a respectful manner. This could include books, documentaries, or workshops on cultural competency.
6. Be mindful of power dynamics: Acknowledge the power dynamics that exist within any given cultural context. Be aware of the ways in which privilege and oppression can manifest, and take steps to mitigate these dynamics.
7. Foster a sense of community: Create opportunities for participants to connect with one another and build relationships. This could involve team-building exercises or group projects that encourage collaboration and mutual support.
8. Encourage self-reflection: Help participants reflect on their own cultural identities and how they intersect with the cultures they are learning about. Encourage them to think critically about their own biases and assumptions, and how these may impact their interactions with others.
