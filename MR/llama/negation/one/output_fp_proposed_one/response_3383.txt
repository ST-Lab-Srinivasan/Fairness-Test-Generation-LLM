Title: The Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Lifelong Single Beginner Non-French Speakers

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of lifelong single beginner non-French speakers. It influences their beliefs, values, and behaviors, and helps them develop a sense of self and belonging. This paper will explore the ways in which cultural heritage impacts the identities of these individuals and how it shapes their experiences as learners of French.

The Concept of Cultural Heritage:
Cultural heritage refers to the intangible and tangible elements that make up a group's shared identity, including customs, traditions, language, history, and artifacts. It is passed down from one generation to the next, and it plays a crucial role in shaping individuals' identities and interactions within their communities (Kothari, 2008).

The Influence of Cultural Heritage on Identity Formation:
Cultural heritage has a profound impact on the formation of identities, particularly for lifelong single beginner non-French speakers. It influences how they perceive themselves and their place in the world, and it shapes their attitudes and behaviors towards learning French. For instance, individuals who come from cultures that value education and language acquisition are more likely to be motivated to learn French, while those who come from cultures that prioritize other aspects of identity, such as religion or ethnicity, may be less interested in doing so (Gay, 2000).

Cultural heritage also plays a role in shaping the way individuals interact with each other and their surroundings. For example, individuals from collectivist cultures tend to prioritize group harmony and cohesion over individual achievement, which can impact their approach to learning French (Hofstede, 1980). On the other hand, individuals from individualistic cultures may be more focused on personal achievement and autonomy, which can influence their motivation to learn French (Krueger, 2003).
