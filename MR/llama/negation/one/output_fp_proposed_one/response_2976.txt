Creating more inclusive social policies for long-term unemployed, recently non-widowed individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the root causes of unemployment and promotes inclusive economic growth. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Job Training and Education Programs: Provide access to job training and education programs that equip these individuals with the skills and knowledge required for in-demand jobs. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and certification programs.
2. Mentorship and Networking Opportunities: Connect long-term unemployed individuals with mentors who can provide guidance, support, and networking opportunities. This can help them build relationships and gain insights into different industries and job sectors.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing access to resources such as business loans, incubators, and mentorship programs. This can help create job opportunities for these individuals and promote economic growth.
4. Addressing Discrimination: Identify and address any discriminatory practices or attitudes that may be preventing long-term unemployed individuals from finding employment. This can include providing training on unconscious bias and promoting diversity and inclusion in the workplace.
5. Microfinance Programs: Provide microfinance programs that offer small loans, savings accounts, and other financial services to help these individuals start or grow their own businesses.
6. Housing Support: Address housing affordability issues by providing subsidies or other forms of support for rent or mortgage payments. This can help reduce the financial stress that may be contributing to long-term unemployment.
7. Health and Wellness Services: Provide access to health and wellness services, such as mental health counseling, nutrition education, and fitness programs. This can help improve overall well-being and increase motivation for employment.
8. Childcare Support: Offer childcare support services, such as subsidized daycare or parenting classes, to help these individuals balance work and family responsibilities.
9. Transportation Support: Provide transportation assistance, such as public transportation vouchers or car sharing services, to help these individuals access job opportunities.
