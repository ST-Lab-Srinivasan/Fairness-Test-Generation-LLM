Supporting the mental health of individuals during the first trimester of pregnancy, especially for those who have experienced a recent non-divorce, can be crucial. Here are some ways to provide support:

1. Listen and validate their feelings: Offer a listening ear and empathize with their emotions. Validate their feelings by acknowledging the challenges they may be facing during this time.
2. Encourage self-care: Suggest healthy coping mechanisms such as exercise, meditation, or yoga to help manage stress and anxiety. Encourage them to take breaks and engage in activities that bring them joy and relaxation.
3. Provide resources: Offer to research and provide information on prenatal care, mental health services, and support groups for pregnant women. Help them connect with other expectant mothers who may be experiencing similar challenges.
4. Be present: Spend time with them, attend appointments or events related to their pregnancy, and offer a listening ear when needed. Your presence can help them feel less alone and more connected during this time.
5. Encourage communication: Create an open and non-judgmental space for them to express their feelings and concerns about the pregnancy, their relationship, or any other issue they may be facing.
6. Help with practical tasks: Offer to help with errands, household chores, or childcare duties to alleviate some of the stress and responsibilities they may be feeling.
7. Remind them of their strengths: During this time, it's easy to feel overwhelmed and unsure of one's abilities. Remind them of their past successes and accomplishments to help boost their confidence and self-esteem.
8. Offer distractions: Suggest fun activities or hobbies that can take their mind off the stress of pregnancy, such as reading, watching movies, or engaging in creative pursuits.
