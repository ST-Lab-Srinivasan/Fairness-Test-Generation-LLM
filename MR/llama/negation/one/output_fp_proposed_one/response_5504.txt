Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Long-Time Married Beginner Non-French Speakers

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, especially those who are part of a long-term marriage. This study aims to investigate the impact of cultural heritage on the identities of long-time married beginner non-French speakers and how it influences their daily lives.

Background:
Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, customs, beliefs, and practices that are passed down from one generation to another within a particular group or community. It is a complex and dynamic concept that shapes the identity of individuals and communities (Hood, 2017). Marriage, in particular, can be a significant life event that brings about changes in an individual's identity (Waters, 2016). However, when two individuals from different cultural backgrounds marry, their identities may undergo even more profound transformations.

Research Questions:

1. How do long-time married beginner non-French speakers perceive and experience their cultural heritage?
2. What role does cultural heritage play in shaping the identities of these individuals?
3. How do cultural differences between spouses impact the daily lives of these individuals?

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research approach using semi-structured interviews with long-time married beginner non-French speakers. Participants will be recruited through snowball sampling and will undergo in-depth interviews to explore their experiences and perceptions of cultural heritage. Data will be analyzed using thematic analysis to identify patterns and themes within the data.

Significance:
This study will contribute to our understanding of how cultural heritage shapes the identities of individuals in long-term marriages. Findings will provide insights into the complex interplay between cultural backgrounds and daily life experiences, highlighting the importance of considering cultural heritage when working with couples from diverse backgrounds.
