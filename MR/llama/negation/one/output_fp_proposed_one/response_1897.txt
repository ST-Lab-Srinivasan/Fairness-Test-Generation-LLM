Supporting the civic engagement of cisgender native non-Arabic speakers can be done through various means. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Voter registration drives: Organize voter registration drives specifically targeting cisgender native non-Arabic speakers to ensure they have the necessary information and resources to register to vote.
2. Civic education: Provide educational resources and workshops on civics, government, and voting procedures specifically tailored for cisgender native non-Arabic speakers. This can help them understand their rights and responsibilities as citizens.
3. Language accessibility: Ensure that voting materials, voter information guides, and other resources are translated into languages spoken by cisgender native non-Arabic speakers. This can include providing interpreters at polling stations or online language translation tools.
4. Community outreach: Engage with local communities and organizations that serve cisgender native non-Arabic speakers to build relationships and increase their participation in the civic process.
5. Cultural competency training: Provide cultural competency training for election officials, poll workers, and other stakeholders to ensure they are equipped to interact with diverse communities and provide inclusive services.
6. Accessible voting technology: Utilize accessible voting technology, such as audio ballots or touch-screen devices, to make the voting process more accessible for people with disabilities, including those who may not speak Arabic.
7. Outreach to marginalized communities: Engage in outreach and community organizing efforts specifically targeting marginalized communities, including immigrant communities, refugee communities, and LGBTQ+ communities, to ensure their voices are heard and their rights are protected.
8. Candidate forums and debates: Organize candidate forums and debates that are accessible and inclusive of all candidates, including those who may not speak Arabic. This can help increase civic engagement and participation from marginalized communities.
9. Vote by mail: Implement vote-by-mail systems that allow eligible voters to cast their ballots without having to physically go to the polling station, making it more accessible for people who may not speak Arabic or have other barriers to voting.
