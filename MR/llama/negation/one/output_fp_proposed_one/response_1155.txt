Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner Chinese speakers with mixed non-Hispanic heritage is essential to promote diversity and inclusion. Here are some suggestions to make your cultural programs more welcoming and engaging:

1. Incorporate diverse perspectives: Ensure that the cultural programs include diverse perspectives and representation from different ethnic groups. This can be achieved by incorporating stories, music, and art from various cultures in your lessons.
2. Use inclusive language: Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes particular groups. Instead, use language that is respectful and inclusive of all participants.
3. Foster a sense of belonging: Create an environment where everyone feels valued and included. Encourage participants to share their experiences and perspectives, and provide opportunities for them to connect with one another.
4. Provide cultural context: Offer context about the cultural practices, traditions, and values of the Chinese culture. This can help participants understand the cultural background of the language they are learning.
5. Make it interactive: Incorporate interactive activities that allow participants to engage with the culture in a fun and meaningful way. For example, you could have a traditional Chinese tea ceremony or a calligraphy workshop.
6. Celebrate diversity: Emphasize the diversity of Chinese culture and how it has been influenced by different ethnic groups. Highlight the unique traditions and practices of each group, while also acknowledging their shared cultural heritage.
7. Offer support: Provide support for participants who may be struggling with the language or cultural differences. This can include additional tutoring or cultural orientation sessions.
8. Be mindful of power dynamics: Acknowledge the historical and systemic inequalities that have impacted different ethnic groups differently. Be mindful of the power dynamics at play and work to create a safe and inclusive space for all participants.
9. Involve the community: Collaborate with local community organizations or cultural centers to incorporate their expertise and resources into your cultural programs. This can help create a more immersive and authentic learning experience.
