Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of lifelong non-native English speakers. It is the collective legacy of a community or society, including its customs, traditions, history, language, and artifacts. 1. Sense of belonging: Non-native English speakers may feel a sense of belonging to their cultural heritage, which can provide a sense of identity outside of their English language proficiency. This sense of belonging can help them navigate their interactions with native English speakers and maintain their cultural identity.
2. Cultural values and beliefs: Lifelong non-native English speakers may hold onto their cultural values and beliefs, which can shape their identities and inform their decisions. For example, they may prioritize family obligations or community involvement over individualistic pursuits.
3. Language use: Non-native English speakers may use their native language(s) as a means of identity expression, even in predominantly English-speaking environments. This can help them maintain their cultural heritage and avoid feeling like they are losing touch with their roots.
