Supporting the educational aspirations of non-independent long-time veterans can be challenging, but there are several ways to help them achieve their goals. Here are some suggestions:

1. Financial Assistance: Offer financial assistance to non-independent long-time veterans who want to pursue higher education. This can include scholarships, grants, and other forms of financial aid.
2. Access to Online Resources: Provide access to online resources such as courses, webinars, and tutorials that can help them learn new skills or brush up on existing ones.
3. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair non-independent long-time veterans with experienced professionals in their desired field of study. This can provide valuable guidance and support throughout their educational journey.
4. On-Campus Resources: Many universities have resources specifically designed to support older, non-traditional students. These can include academic advising, tutoring services, and counseling.
5. Flexible Learning Options: Offer flexible learning options such as part-time study, online courses, or hybrid programs that allow them to balance their studies with other responsibilities.
6. Career Counseling: Provide career counseling and job placement services to help non-independent long-time veterans identify their strengths and interests and find suitable employment upon completion of their education.
7. Legal Assistance: Offer legal assistance to help non-independent long-time veterans navigate the complexities of the education system, including issues related to financial aid, scholarships, and veteran benefits.
8. Social Support: Create a supportive community for non-independent long-time veterans, providing opportunities for socialization, networking, and peer support.
9. Accommodations: Provide accommodations such as disability services, childcare assistance, and transportation support to help non-independent long-time veterans overcome barriers to education.
10. Advocacy: Advocate for policies and practices that support the educational aspirations of non-independent long-time veterans, including funding for programs and services that benefit this population.
