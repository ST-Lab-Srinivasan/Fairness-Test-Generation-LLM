2. Inclusive programming: Design community programs that are inclusive and welcoming to all individuals, regardless of their socio-economic status, race, ethnicity, or ability. Ensure that your programs are accessible and affordable for everyone.
3. Partnerships and collaborations: Form partnerships with organizations that serve marginalized communities, such as community centers, non-profits, and social service agencies. Collaborate with these organizations to co-design and deliver programs that are tailored to the needs of these communities.
4. Capacity building: Provide training and capacity-building opportunities for community leaders and members from marginalized groups. This will help them develop the skills and knowledge necessary to design and deliver inclusive programs.

Focus on intersectionality: Acknowledge that individuals may face multiple forms of discrimination (intersectionality) based on their race, gender, sexuality, ability, and other aspects of their identity.
