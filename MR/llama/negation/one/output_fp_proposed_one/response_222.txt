Promoting the economic empowerment of non-disabled upper-middle-income communities is crucial for creating a more equitable and inclusive society. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills development programs, particularly in areas that are in demand in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and certification programs.
2. Access to Financial Services: Ensure that members of these communities have access to affordable financial services, such as bank accounts, loans, and insurance. This can help them save money, invest in their future, and build credit.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Provide resources and support for entrepreneurs from these communities to start and grow their own businesses. This can include training, mentorship, and access to funding.
4. Job Creation: Focus on creating job opportunities in areas that are relevant to the skills and interests of members of these communities. This can include infrastructure development, tourism, and creative industries.
5. Community Development: Invest in community development programs that promote social cohesion and inclusion. This can include initiatives that bring together people from different backgrounds and provide opportunities for them to work together towards common goals.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that promote the economic empowerment of non-disabled upper-middle-income communities. This can include advocating for laws and regulations that promote access to education, employment, and financial services.
7. Social Enterprise: Support social enterprises that are led by members of these communities and focus on addressing social and economic issues. These enterprises can provide jobs, training, and other resources to members of these communities.
8. Mentorship and Role Modeling: Provide mentorship and role modeling opportunities for members of these communities to inspire and guide them towards successful careers and entrepreneurship.
9. Addressing Systemic Barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers that prevent members of these communities from accessing economic opportunities. This can include addressing discrimination, bias, and other forms of oppression.
