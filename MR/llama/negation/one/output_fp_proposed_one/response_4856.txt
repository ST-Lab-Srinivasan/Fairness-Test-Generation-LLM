2. Better diagnosis and treatment: Cultural competence helps healthcare providers recognize the cultural influences on a patient's symptoms, behaviors, and health beliefs. This can lead to more accurate diagnoses and appropriate treatment plans tailored to the patient's cultural background.
3. Enhanced communication: When healthcare providers are culturally competent, they can communicate more effectively with patients from diverse backgrounds. They can use language and terminology that is familiar to the patient, which helps build rapport and improve communication.

Increased access to care: Culturally competent healthcare providers can better address the healthcare needs of underserved populations, such as racial and ethnic minorities, immigrants, and LGBTQ+ individuals. This can lead to increased access to care and improved health outcomes for these populations.
