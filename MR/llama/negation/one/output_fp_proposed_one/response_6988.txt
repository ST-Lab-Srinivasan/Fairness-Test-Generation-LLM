Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for early teen native non-English speaker individuals requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions:

1. Involve them in the planning process: Give young people a voice in determining how they want to participate in cultural events. This can help build their confidence and sense of ownership in the celebration.
2. Provide translation support: Offer interpretation services during cultural events, such as simultaneous interpretation or written translations of important information. This can ensure that all participants can fully engage with the event, regardless of their language proficiency.
3. Use visual aids and multimedia: Incorporate visual aids, such as pictures and videos, into cultural presentations to help non-English speaker individuals understand the context and significance of the celebration.
4. Offer bilingual programming: Provide programming that is available in both English and the dominant language spoken by the local community. This can include bilingual performances, presentations, or workshops.
5. Create a welcoming environment: Make sure that the cultural celebration space is welcoming and inclusive for all participants. This can involve providing comfortable seating areas, accessible restrooms, and other accommodations.
6. Use inclusive language: Avoid using language that could be offensive or alienating to non-English speaker individuals. Instead, use language that is respectful and inclusive of all cultures and backgrounds.
7. Foster cross-cultural connections: Encourage interactions between participants from different cultural backgrounds through activities such as cultural sharing circles or intercultural dialogues.
8. Provide cultural immersion experiences: Offer opportunities for young people to participate in cultural immersion experiences, such as traditional music and dance workshops, language classes, or cultural field trips.
9. Celebrate diverse traditions: Incorporate a variety of cultural traditions and practices into the celebration, recognizing and valuing the diversity of young people's backgrounds.
10. Encourage community involvement: Engage local community members in the planning and execution of the cultural celebration, ensuring that it reflects the diverse cultures and experiences of the local area.
