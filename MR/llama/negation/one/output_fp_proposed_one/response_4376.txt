Creating more inclusive social policies for bereaved non-temporary workers requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges faced by this group. Here are some recommendations for creating more inclusive policies:

1. Acknowledge the impact of grief on work performance: Bereaved individuals may struggle to perform their job duties due to the emotional demands of losing a loved one. Employers should recognize this and provide accommodations to help them manage their grief while still meeting job expectations.
2. Provide paid bereavement leave: Offering paid time off for bereaved individuals can help them take the time they need to grieve without worrying about financial consequences. This can also help prevent burnout and promote overall well-being.
3. Offer counseling and mental health support: Bereaved individuals may benefit from professional counseling and mental health support to help them cope with their loss. Employers can provide access to these resources or offer referrals to external providers.
4. Foster a supportive work environment: Create a workplace culture that supports and understands the needs of bereaved individuals. Encourage colleagues to be empathetic and understanding, and provide opportunities for open communication and discussion about grief.
5. Recognize the impact of grief on daily life: Bereaved individuals may face challenges in their personal lives, such as childcare or household responsibilities, that can affect their ability to work. Employers can offer flexible scheduling or temporary modifications to job duties to help accommodate these needs.
6. Provide resources for long-term support: Bereavement is a complex and ongoing process, and individuals may need ongoing support long after the initial grieving period. Employers can provide access to ongoing counseling, support groups, or other resources to help individuals manage their grief over time.
7. Educate managers and colleagues: Provide training for managers and colleagues on how to support bereaved individuals in the workplace. This can help create a more inclusive and supportive work environment.
