Creating more inclusive social policies for long-term unemployed, long-term non-widowed individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the root causes of their situation. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive policies:

1. Conduct a needs assessment: Conduct a thorough assessment of the needs and challenges faced by long-term unemployed, non-widowed individuals. This will help identify specific areas where policy interventions are needed.
2. Address systemic barriers: Long-term unemployment can be caused by various systemic barriers such as discrimination, lack of access to education and training, and limited job opportunities. Policies should aim to address these barriers and create a more inclusive labor market.
3. Provide job training and placement services: Offer job training programs that are tailored to the needs of long-term unemployed individuals. These programs can help them acquire new skills and knowledge that are relevant to the job market. Additionally, provide placement services to help them find employment opportunities.
4. Encourage entrepreneurship: Encourage long-term unemployed individuals to consider starting their own businesses. Provide them with access to resources such as mentorship, funding, and networking opportunities.
5. Offer financial support: Provide financial assistance to long-term unemployed individuals who are struggling to make ends meet. This can include subsidies for job training, housing assistance, and other forms of support.
6. Address mental health challenges: Long-term unemployment can have a significant impact on an individual's mental health. Provide access to mental health services and support to help them cope with the emotional challenges of long-term unemployment.
7. Foster community connections: Create opportunities for long-term unemployed individuals to connect with their communities. This can include volunteer opportunities, social events, and other forms of community engagement.
8. Address ageism and discrimination: Ageism and discrimination based on gender, race, or other factors can contribute to long-term unemployment. Policies should aim to address these issues and promote a more inclusive labor market.
