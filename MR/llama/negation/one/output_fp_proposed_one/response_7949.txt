Highlighting the issues faced by native non-French speaker recent immigrants to Canada can be done through various methods, including:

1. Data Visualization: Use interactive visualizations to showcase the demographic and socio-economic characteristics of recent immigrants in Canada, such as their country of origin, language proficiency, education level, employment status, and income level. This can help highlight the diverse backgrounds and experiences of these individuals.
2. Survey Research: Conduct surveys to gather data on the experiences and challenges faced by recent immigrants in Canada. The survey could cover topics such as their settlement patterns, access to healthcare and education, language barriers, discrimination, and social integration. This can help identify specific issues that need to be addressed.
3. Case Studies: Conduct in-depth case studies of individual immigrants or small groups to gain a deeper understanding of their experiences and challenges. This can help highlight the unique circumstances and obstacles faced by these individuals.
4. Analyzing Government Data: Analyze data from government sources such as census, labor market statistics, and immigration data to identify trends and patterns in the immigrant population. This can help identify systemic issues that need to be addressed.
5. Collaboration with Immigrant Organizations: Partner with organizations that support and advocate for immigrants to gain insight into their experiences and challenges. This can help ensure that the data is collected and analyzed in a way that is sensitive to the needs of the immigrant community.
6. Focus Groups: Conduct focus groups with recent immigrants to gather qualitative data on their experiences and challenges. This can help identify specific issues and provide insight into the social and cultural barriers faced by these individuals.
7. Literature Review: Conduct a literature review of existing research on immigration to Canada to identify patterns and trends in the data. This can help highlight the key issues faced by recent immigrants and inform policy recommendations.
8. Data from Social Media: Use social media data to understand the experiences and challenges faced by recent immigrants. This can provide insight into the social and cultural barriers they face, as well as their access to information and resources.
