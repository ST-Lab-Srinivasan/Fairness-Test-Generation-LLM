1. The Grameen Bank in Bangladesh: Founded by Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi economist and microfinance pioneer, who started lending small amounts of money to poor villagers in the 1970s. Today, Grameen Bank is one of the largest microfinance institutions in the world, with over 8 million borrowers and operations in 13 countries.
2. The Kibera School for Girls in Kenya: Founded by Nikki Toyama-Bankston, an American entrepreneur who saw the need to provide quality education to girls in the Kibera slum of Nairobi. Today, the school has grown to serve over 100 students and has become a model for community-led education initiatives.
3. The Slum Soccer Project in Rio de Janeiro: Founded by Brazilian entrepreneur Flavio Coelho, who used soccer as a tool to teach life skills and academic concepts to children living in favelas. Today, the project has expanded to reach over 1,000 children and has become a successful model for community development through sports.
