To ensure that the voices of beginner non-Arabic speaker individuals are heard in decision-making processes, here are some strategies that can be employed:

1. Language support: Provide language support and interpretation services to help non-Arabic speakers participate in meetings and discussions. This can include simultaneous interpretation, translation of documents and presentations, and language classes for those who want to improve their Arabic skills.
2. Cultural orientation: Offer cultural orientation programs to help non-Arabic speakers understand the local culture and customs. This can include information on communication styles, social norms, and expectations around decision-making processes.
3. Inclusive decision-making processes: Make sure that decision-making processes are inclusive and accessible to all individuals, regardless of their language proficiency. This can involve using visual aids, providing written materials, and using facilitation techniques that encourage participation from all members of the group.
4. Peer support: Encourage peer support among non-Arabic speakers, where they can help each other navigate the cultural and linguistic barriers. This can be done through social events, language exchange programs, and mentorship opportunities.
5. Leadership development: Provide leadership development programs for non-Arabic speakers to build their confidence and skills in decision-making processes. This can involve training on communication, negotiation, and conflict resolution, as well as providing opportunities for mentorship and coaching.
6. Community outreach: Reach out to the local community to raise awareness about the importance of including non-Arabic speakers in decision-making processes. This can involve collaborating with community organizations, hosting cultural events, and engaging with local media to promote inclusivity and diversity.
7. Technology support: Utilize technology to facilitate communication and participation among non-Arabic speakers. This can include using video conferencing tools, online platforms for discussion and collaboration, and mobile apps for language translation and interpretation.
8. Flexibility and adaptability: Be flexible and adaptable in decision-making processes to accommodate the needs of non-Arabic speakers. This can involve adjusting meeting times and locations, providing additional support and resources, and being open to feedback and suggestions from all members of the group.
