Creating more inclusive social policies for poor bereaved individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges faced by this vulnerable population. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Acknowledge the scope of the problem: The first step is to recognize the scale of the issue and the impact it has on poor bereaved individuals. This involves collecting data and conducting research to understand the prevalence of grief and its effects on different communities.
2. Develop a comprehensive framework: Create a framework that incorporates various aspects of social policy, including healthcare, education, housing, and employment. This framework should be designed to address the specific needs of poor bereaved individuals and provide them with holistic support.
3. Provide access to mental health services: Grief can have a significant impact on an individual's mental health, leading to conditions such as depression, anxiety, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). Ensure that poor bereaved individuals have access to affordable mental health services, including counseling, therapy, and medication.
4. Offer financial support: Financial strain is a common challenge faced by poor bereaved individuals, particularly those who have lost their primary breadwinner. Provide financial assistance, such as funeral expenses, lost income replacement, and ongoing support through social security or other forms of government aid.
5. Advocate for social safety nets: Implement policies that provide a safety net for poor bereaved individuals, including access to affordable housing, food assistance programs, and job training and placement services.
6. Address systemic issues: Grief can be exacerbated by systemic issues such as discrimination, inequality, and social isolation. Address these issues by promoting diversity, equity, and inclusion in all aspects of society.
7. Foster community support: Encourage community members to come together to provide emotional and practical support to poor bereaved individuals. This can involve organizing support groups, volunteering, or simply being a listening ear.
