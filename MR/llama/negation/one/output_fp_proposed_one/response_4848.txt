Creating more inclusive community events for non-active duty beginner English speakers requires careful planning and consideration of their needs. Here are some suggestions to make your events more accessible and welcoming:

1. Provide language support: Offer interpretation or translation services during the event, especially if it's a complex topic. This can be done through remote interpreting services or by hiring on-site interpreters.
2. Use simple language: Avoid using technical jargon or complex terminology that may be difficult for beginners to understand. Instead, use simple language and provide clear explanations and examples.
3. Offer visual aids: Incorporate visual aids such as pictures, videos, or presentations to help illustrate your points. This can make it easier for non-active duty beginner English speakers to follow along and participate in the event.
4. Provide written materials: Offer printed or digital materials such as handouts, flyers, or PowerPoint presentations that attendees can refer to during the event. This can help them stay engaged and follow the discussion more easily.
5. Foster a supportive environment: Encourage attendees to ask questions and participate in discussions. Create a welcoming atmosphere by acknowledging and valuing different perspectives and experiences.
6. Use interactive activities: Incorporate interactive activities such as group discussions, role-playing, or quizzes to keep attendees engaged and involved. This can help them practice their English skills in a fun and dynamic way.
7. Provide opportunities for networking: Offer breaks or social events during the event where attendees can connect with each other and practice their English skills in a more informal setting.
8. Use technology to facilitate communication: Utilize virtual event platforms or collaboration tools to enable attendees to communicate and interact remotely. This can be particularly helpful for those who may have difficulty attending events in person due to location or mobility issues.
