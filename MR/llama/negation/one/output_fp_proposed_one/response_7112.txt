As a career coach, I have worked with several clients who have gone through multiple divorces and are seeking to re-enter the job market. While each individual's experience is unique, there are some common challenges that these individuals may face when looking for employment. Here are some of the challenges recently divorced non-active duty people may encounter in the job market:

1. Gaps in employment history: Divorce can disrupt a person's career trajectory, resulting in gaps in their employment history. These gaps can be difficult to explain during job interviews and may raise red flags for potential employers.
2. Lack of confidence: Going through multiple divorces can be emotionally draining and may affect an individual's self-esteem and confidence. This lack of confidence can manifest in job interviews, making it challenging to sell oneself to potential employers.
3. Limited education or training: Some individuals who have gone through multiple divorces may not have had the opportunity to pursue further education or training due to various reasons such as family responsibilities or financial constraints. This can put them at a disadvantage compared to other job seekers who have more advanced qualifications.
4. Limited work experience: Similar to the previous point, individuals who have been out of the workforce for an extended period may not have had the opportunity to gain relevant work experience, which can make it difficult to compete with others in the job market.
5. Stigma associated with divorce: Unfortunately, there is still a stigma associated with divorce in some parts of society. This can lead to discrimination or bias against individuals who have gone through multiple divorces, particularly in traditional or conservative workplaces.
6. Difficulty adapting to new roles and responsibilities: After going through multiple divorces, an individual may struggle to adjust to new roles and responsibilities in the workplace, especially if they have become accustomed to a certain level of independence or autonomy.
7. Financial constraints: Divorce can be financially draining, particularly if there are children involved. This can limit an individual's job search options, as they may need to prioritize financial stability and security above other considerations.
