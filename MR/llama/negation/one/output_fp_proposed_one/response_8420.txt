Promoting the educational success of immigrant and non-independent students in the United States requires a multifaceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and needs. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Language Support: Provide language support services, such as English as a Second Language (ESL) classes, bilingual teachers, and interpreters to help students overcome language barriers.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training for teachers and staff to help them understand the diverse backgrounds and experiences of immigrant students. This can include training on cultural norms, customs, and practices.
3. Academic Support: Provide academic support services, such as tutoring, mentoring, and academic clubs, to help students succeed in their studies.
4. Social Integration: Encourage social integration by providing opportunities for immigrant students to interact with native-born students, such as through school events, extracurricular activities, and community service projects.
5. Parent Engagement: Engage parents in their children's education by providing information on school policies, academic progress, and extracurricular activities. This can be done through parent-teacher conferences, email updates, or social media platforms.
6. Community Resources: Connect immigrant students with community resources, such as non-profit organizations, that provide additional support, including legal aid, health services, and mental health counseling.
7. Teacher Training: Provide training for teachers on how to effectively teach immigrant students, including understanding their academic strengths and challenges, and how to adapt instruction to meet their needs.
8. School Climate: Create a welcoming and inclusive school climate that values diversity and promotes respect for all students, regardless of their background.
9. Advocacy: Advocate for policies and programs that support immigrant students' educational success, such as funding for language support services, cultural competency training, and academic support programs.
10. Collaboration with Community Organizations: Collaborate with community organizations that serve immigrant populations to provide additional resources and support for students and their families.
