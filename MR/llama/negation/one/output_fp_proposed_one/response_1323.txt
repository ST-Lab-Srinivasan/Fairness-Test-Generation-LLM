Ensuring that the voices of beginner English speakers, particularly those who have gone through multiple divorces and are seeking to rebuild their lives, are heard in decision-making processes can be challenging. However, there are several strategies that can help:

1. Build trust and rapport: Begin by building trust and rapport with these individuals. Listen actively to their stories, show empathy, and validate their experiences. By creating a safe and non-judgmental space, they will be more likely to open up and share their thoughts and feelings.
2. Provide language support: Offer language support and resources to help them improve their English skills. This could include one-on-one tutoring, language classes, or access to language learning materials. Improving their language proficiency can help them navigate complex decision-making processes more effectively.
3. Use visual aids: Visual aids such as pictures, diagrams, and videos can help beginners communicate their ideas more effectively. Provide them with simple language and visuals that they can use to express their thoughts and opinions.
4. Foster a supportive community: Create a supportive community of peers who are also struggling with similar challenges. Encourage them to share their experiences, provide emotional support, and help them build each other's confidence. This can help them feel more empowered to participate in decision-making processes.
5. Use plain language: Avoid using complex language or jargon that may be difficult for beginners to understand. Use plain language and simple terms to communicate ideas and concepts. Provide explanations and examples to help them grasp complex issues.
6. Involve them in small group discussions: Begin by involving them in small group discussions where they can share their thoughts and opinions with a smaller group of people. This can help them build confidence and feel more comfortable participating in larger decision-making processes.
7. Provide opportunities for input: Offer opportunities for these individuals to provide input on decisions that affect them directly. Encourage them to share their ideas and opinions, and make sure they are heard and valued.
8. Celebrate their successes: Celebrate the successes of these individuals, no matter how small they may seem. This can help build their confidence and motivation to continue participating
