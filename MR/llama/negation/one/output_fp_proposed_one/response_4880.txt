Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of ethnically full Asian non-independent individuals. Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, traditions, and practices that are passed down from one generation to the next within a particular culture or community. For individuals who identify as ethnically full Asian, their cultural heritage is an essential part of their identity and can influence how they see themselves in relation to society.

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a larger community and helps individuals understand their place within it. 2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage shapes an individual's identity by providing a framework for understanding who they are and where they come from. It can influence how individuals see themselves in relation to others, including their own family members, community, and society at large.
3. Value system: Cultural heritage influences the values and beliefs of individuals within a particular culture or community. 5. Intergenerational relationships: Cultural heritage can influence intergenerational relationships within families and communities. Ethnically full Asians may feel a strong connection to their parents, grandparents, and other older relatives who are responsible for passing down cultural traditions and values.
