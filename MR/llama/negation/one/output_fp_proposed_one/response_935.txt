Addressing the specific needs of Native American recent immigrants to the US in education requires a comprehensive approach that takes into account their unique cultural background, language barriers, and experiences of trauma. Here are some strategies that educators can use to support these individuals:

1. Cultural Competence Training: Provide training for educators on the cultural practices, values, and beliefs of Native American communities. This can help educators understand the specific needs of Native American students and provide appropriate support.
2. Language Support: Offer language support services such as interpreters, translators, or bilingual education programs to help Native American students who may not speak English fluently.
3. Trauma-Informed Practices: Be aware of the historical trauma that many Native American communities have experienced and provide a safe and supportive learning environment. Use trauma-informed practices such as mindfulness, breathing exercises, and cultural practices to help students manage stress and anxiety.
4. Cultural Celebrations: Incorporate Native American cultural celebrations and events into the school curriculum. This can help students feel more connected to their heritage and culture.
5. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair Native American students with supportive adults who can provide guidance, support, and cultural understanding.
6. Community Partnerships: Build partnerships with local Native American communities to provide additional resources and support for Native American students. This can include internships, job shadowing, and cultural immersion experiences.
7. Education on Native American Culture: Provide education on the history, culture, and traditions of Native American communities. This can help dispel stereotypes and promote understanding and respect.
8. Curriculum Revision: Revise curricula to include more diverse perspectives and histories, including the experiences of Native American communities.
9. Teacher Training: Provide training for teachers on how to incorporate culturally responsive teaching practices into their classrooms.
10. Family Engagement: Encourage family engagement and involvement in the educational process. This can help students feel more supported and connected to their cultural heritage.
