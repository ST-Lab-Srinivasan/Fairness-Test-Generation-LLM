Supporting the economic empowerment of ethnically full African non-Americans living in poverty requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural inequalities they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and skills development: Provide access to quality education and vocational training programs to help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market. This can include technical training, entrepreneurship development, and language proficiency courses.
2. Microfinance and small business support: Offer microloans, savings accounts, and other financial services to help individuals start or grow their own businesses. This can help create jobs and generate income for the individual and their community.
3. Access to healthcare and social services: Ensure that individuals have access to quality healthcare services, including mental health support, maternal and child healthcare, and chronic disease management. Social services such as counseling, job placement, and childcare can also help individuals overcome the challenges of poverty.
4. Addressing discrimination and bias: Implement policies and programs that address discrimination and bias faced by ethnically full African non-Americans, including those related to housing, employment, and criminal justice. This can include anti-discrimination training for businesses and organizations, as well as policy changes to promote equity and inclusion.
5. Community development: Invest in community development programs that bring together individuals from different backgrounds to build relationships, share resources, and work towards common goals. This can help create a sense of belonging and social cohesion, which can be an important factor in overcoming poverty.
6. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies and laws that promote the economic empowerment of ethnically full African non-Americans, including those related to education, employment, and healthcare. This can include advocating for policies that address systemic inequalities and promote greater access to opportunities.
7. Networking and mentorship: Connect individuals with successful professionals and business leaders from their community who can serve as role models and provide guidance on how to navigate the professional world. This can help build confidence and skills, and provide a sense of belonging and connection to a broader community.
