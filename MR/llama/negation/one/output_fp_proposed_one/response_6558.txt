3. Accessibility: Ensure that your event locations are accessible for people with disabilities. Provide accommodations such as wheelchair ramps, Braille signage, and audio descriptions for visually impaired attendees.
4. Flexible scheduling: Offer events at times that are convenient for non-middle-class income individuals who may have limited flexibility in their schedules due to work or family commitments.

7. Interactive activities: Plan activities that are engaging and inclusive for all attendees, regardless of their language proficiency or cultural background. Use visual aids, gestures, and simple language to facilitate participation.
