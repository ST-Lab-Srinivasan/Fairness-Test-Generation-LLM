Promoting the economic empowerment of non-mentally disabled individuals living in deep poverty communities is crucial for their well-being and sustainable development. Here are some strategies that can help achieve this goal:

1. Vocational Training: Provide vocational training programs that teach skills such as tailoring, carpentry, farming, and other income-generating activities. This will enable individuals to earn a decent living and become self-sufficient.
2. Microfinance Programs: Offer microfinance programs that provide small loans and other financial services to help individuals start or expand their businesses. This can help create jobs and increase economic opportunities in the community.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing training, mentorship, and resources for individuals to start their own businesses. This can help create jobs and stimulate economic growth in the community.
4. Access to Markets: Facilitate access to markets for non-mentally disabled individuals by providing them with information on market opportunities, connecting them with buyers, and helping them to develop marketing strategies.
5. Community Development: Support community development initiatives that promote economic growth and social inclusion. This can include initiatives such as cooperatives, community savings schemes, and other collective action programs.
6. Advocacy and Policy Reform: Advocate for policy reforms that address the root causes of poverty and promote inclusive economic growth. This can include advocating for policies that promote access to education, healthcare, and social protection programs.
7. Inclusive Value Chains: Support the development of inclusive value chains that promote economic opportunities for non-mentally disabled individuals in rural and urban areas. This can involve working with businesses to identify and address gaps in their supply chains, and to develop strategies for increasing diversity and inclusion.
8. Technology and Innovation: Leverage technology and innovation to create new economic opportunities for non-mentally disabled individuals.
