Addressing the specific needs of native non-French speaker Nigerians in healthcare requires a comprehensive approach that takes into account the unique cultural, linguistic, and social factors that affect their healthcare experience. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Language support: Provide language interpreters or translators for patients who do not speak French, particularly during medical consultations and procedures. This can help ensure accurate communication and reduce misunderstandings.
2. Cultural sensitivity training: Offer cultural sensitivity training to healthcare providers to help them understand the beliefs, values, and practices of Nigerian culture. This can help providers deliver care that is more responsive to patients' needs and preferences.
3. Patient education materials: Develop patient education materials in local languages and dialects to help non-French speaker Nigerians understand their health conditions and treatment options. This can also help build trust between patients and providers.
4. Community engagement: Engage with local communities and organizations to raise awareness about the importance of healthcare services and how to access them. This can help increase the uptake of healthcare services among Nigerian communities.
5. Collaboration with traditional healers: Collaborate with traditional healers and community leaders to identify ways to integrate traditional healing practices with modern medical care. This can help ensure that patients receive comprehensive and culturally appropriate care.
