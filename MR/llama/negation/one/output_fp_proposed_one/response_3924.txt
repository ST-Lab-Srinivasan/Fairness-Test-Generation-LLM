Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for non-mentally disabled individuals is essential to ensure that everyone feels welcome and valued. Here are some suggestions on how to achieve this:

1. Involve the community: Encourage the participation of people from different backgrounds, cultures, and abilities in the planning and execution of cultural celebrations. This can be done by holding community meetings, surveying the public for input, or establishing a diverse planning committee.
2. Provide accommodations: Offer accommodations such as sign language interpretation, braille materials, and wheelchair accessibility to ensure that everyone can participate fully. This can include providing alternative formats for event programs, flyers, and other materials.
3. Use inclusive language: Avoid using language that is offensive or exclusive to any particular group. Use language that is respectful and inclusive of all individuals, regardless of their abilities.
4. Highlight diverse perspectives: Showcase the diversity of cultures, experiences, and perspectives during cultural celebrations. This can be done by incorporating music, dance, art, and other forms of expression that reflect the community's diversity.
5. Offer sensory-friendly activities: Provide activities that are accessible to individuals with different abilities, such as sensory-friendly art stations, play areas, or interactive exhibits.
6. Create a welcoming environment: Ensure that the venue is wheelchair accessible and has adequate seating and restrooms for all attendees. Provide clear signage and navigation aids to help individuals find their way around the event space.
7. Educate staff and volunteers: Provide training on disability etiquette and cultural sensitivity for staff and volunteers involved in organizing and executing cultural celebrations. This can include understanding how to communicate with individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing, how to provide accommodations for wheelchair users, and how to create a welcoming environment for all attendees.
