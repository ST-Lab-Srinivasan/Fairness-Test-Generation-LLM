During the first trimester of pregnancy, women may experience various emotional and mental health challenges due to hormonal changes, fatigue, and anxiety about the upcoming birth. Here are some ways to support the mental health of early pregnancy (non-widowed) people:

1. Listen and validate their feelings: Offer a listening ear and emotional support without judgment. Validate their feelings and concerns, acknowledging that it's normal to feel overwhelmed or anxious during this time.
2. Encourage self-care: Suggest activities that promote relaxation and stress reduction, such as yoga, meditation, or prenatal massages. Encourage them to prioritize rest and relaxation during the day.
3. Provide education: Offer information on common first trimester symptoms and how to manage them. Share resources on pregnancy-friendly exercise routines, nutrition, and mental health support groups.
4. Encourage communication: Encourage them to express their feelings and concerns with you or a trusted friend/family member. Listen actively and provide emotional support without criticism or judgment.
5. Help them connect with resources: Introduce them to local prenatal yoga classes, pregnancy massages, or other services that can help them manage their mental health during this time.
6. Offer practical help: Offer to help with household chores, grocery shopping, or errands to reduce stress and workload.
7. Be patient and understanding: Remember that they may not be able to do everything they could before pregnancy, so be patient and understanding when it comes to their limitations.
8. Celebrate milestones: Celebrate small accomplishments and milestones during this time, such as completing a prenatal yoga class or reaching the end of the first trimester.
9. Be aware of postpartum depression: Inform them about postpartum depression (PPD) and how it can affect new mothers. Encourage them to seek help if they experience symptoms of PPD, such as persistent sadness, anxiety, or difficulty bonding with their baby.
