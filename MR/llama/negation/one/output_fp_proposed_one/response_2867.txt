2. Comfortable seating: Provide comfortable seating options for expectant mothers, such as cushioned chairs or bean bags, in designated areas. This will allow them to relax and enjoy the event without discomfort.
3. Accommodating bathroom facilities: Ensure that the event venue has clean and well-maintained bathrooms with adequate space for pregnant women to maneuver. Consider providing a designated breastfeeding area or a private area for pumping milk.
4. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by reducing noise levels, using dim lighting, and minimizing strong smells. This will help expectant mothers who may be sensitive to certain stimuli.
5. Modified activities: Offer modified activities that are inclusive of pregnant women, such as yoga or stretching exercises that can be adapted for different stages of pregnancy. Provide options for both low-impact and high-impact activities to cater to various preferences.
6. Support staff: Train event staff to recognize and respond to the needs of expectant mothers. Have a designated support person available to assist with any concerns or issues that may arise.
7. Communication: Provide clear communication channels for expectant mothers, including pregnancy-related announcements, signage, and written materials. Ensure that all information is accessible and easy to understand.
