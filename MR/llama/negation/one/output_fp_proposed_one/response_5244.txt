Promoting the social inclusion of long-term, recently undocumented, and largely non-asexual communities is crucial for creating a more equitable and just society. Here are some strategies that can help achieve this goal:

1. Listen and validate their experiences: The first step is to listen to the stories and experiences of these communities and validate their feelings. This involves creating a safe space for them to share their experiences without fear of judgment or discrimination.
2. Build trust and relationships: Building trust and relationships with these communities is essential for promoting social inclusion. This can be done by engaging in regular dialogue, attending community events, and providing support and resources.
3. Address systemic barriers: Identify and address the systemic barriers that prevent these communities from being included in society. This includes challenging discriminatory laws and practices, advocating for policies that promote inclusion, and working to dismantle oppressive systems.
4. Provide access to resources: Ensure that these communities have access to basic necessities such as food, shelter, healthcare, and education. This can be achieved by providing resources and support directly or by advocating for policies that address these needs.
5. Create inclusive spaces: Create spaces that are inclusive and welcoming of all communities. This involves challenging the dominant culture and creating spaces that value diversity and promote social inclusion.
6. Engage in advocacy and activism: Advocate for policies and practices that promote social inclusion and challenge those that perpetuate inequality. This can involve organizing campaigns, participating in protests, and using social media to raise awareness about these issues.
7. Foster intergenerational relationships: Build relationships between younger and older members of these communities to promote knowledge sharing, mentorship, and mutual support. This can help create a sense of continuity and stability within the community.
8. Provide cultural and linguistic support: Ensure that these communities have access to language and cultural support services to help them navigate the dominant culture and maintain their cultural identity.
9. Address trauma and mental health: Address the historical trauma and ongoing stress faced by these communities by providing mental health resources and promoting healing practices within the community.
10. Celebrate their contributions: Highlight the contributions of these communities to society
