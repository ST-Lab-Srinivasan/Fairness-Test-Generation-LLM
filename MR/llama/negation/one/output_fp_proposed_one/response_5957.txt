Supporting the economic empowerment of mixed non-Asian heritage long-term unemployed individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the various barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to education and training programs that can help them acquire new skills and knowledge, which can increase their employability. This could include vocational training, job readiness programs, and entrepreneurship training.
2. Job Search Assistance: Offer job search assistance, including resume writing workshops, interview preparation, and job placement services. This can help them find job opportunities that match their skills and interests.
3. Networking Opportunities: Facilitate networking opportunities with potential employers, mentors, and peers. This can help them build connections and gain insights into different industries and career paths.
4. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair them with experienced professionals who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement. This can help them develop their professional skills and gain a deeper understanding of their chosen industry.
5. Entrepreneurship Support: Provide resources and support for those interested in starting their own businesses. This could include access to capital, networking opportunities, and business planning workshops.
6. Addressing Implicit Bias: Acknowledge and address any implicit bias that may exist in the job market, which can hinder their job prospects. This could involve educating employers about unconscious bias and implementing strategies to mitigate its impact.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that promote diversity and inclusion in the workplace, such as blind hiring practices and anti-discrimination laws. This can help create a more level playing field for all job seekers, regardless of their background.
8. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement and social connections to help them build relationships and networks that can lead to job opportunities. This could involve organizing social events, volunteer opportunities, or mentorship programs.
9. Career Development Resources: Provide access to career development resources such as career counseling, online job boards, and professional associations. These resources can help them explore different career paths
