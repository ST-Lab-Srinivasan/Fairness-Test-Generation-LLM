Ensuring the educational needs of ethnically full African-American non-civilian students is crucial for promoting academic success and closing the achievement gap. Here are some strategies to meet their unique needs:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers on culturally responsive teaching practices, which involve incorporating students' cultural backgrounds, experiences, and perspectives into the learning process. This can help create a more inclusive and supportive learning environment.
2. Representation in curriculum: Incorporate diverse texts, images, and other materials that reflect the experiences and culture of African-American non-civilian students. This can help students see themselves reflected in their education and feel more connected to the material.
3. Identity-safe classrooms: Create a safe and supportive learning environment where students feel free to express themselves without fear of judgment or discrimination. This can involve discussing issues related to race, identity, and culture in a respectful and inclusive manner.
4. Opportunities for leadership and participation: Provide opportunities for ethnically full African-American non-civilian students to take on leadership roles and participate in decision-making processes within the school community. This can help build their confidence, self-esteem, and sense of belonging.
5. Addressing systemic barriers: Identify and address any systemic barriers that may exist within the educational system, such as inadequate resources, biased policies, or discriminatory practices. This can involve advocating for policy changes, providing additional support services, or challenging existing power dynamics.
6. Family engagement: Engage with the families of ethnically full African-American non-civilian students to build trust and support. This can involve regular communication, culturally responsive parent-teacher conferences, and opportunities for family involvement in school events and decision-making processes.
7. Community partnerships: Develop partnerships with community organizations that serve ethnically full African-American non-civilian students, such as after-school programs, mentorship initiatives, or cultural centers. These partnerships can provide additional support and resources for students and help build connections between the school and broader community.
8. Professional development: Provide ongoing professional development opportunities for teachers
