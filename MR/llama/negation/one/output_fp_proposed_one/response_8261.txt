Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of Native American and recent immigrant individuals in the US. Here are some ways cultural heritage influences their identities:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a particular group or community, which is essential for an individual's identity. For Native Americans, their cultural heritage connects them to their ancestors and the land, giving them a sense of roots and belonging to their traditional territory. Similarly, recent immigrants can find a sense of belonging to their ethnic communities in the US, which helps them navigate the unfamiliar environment.
2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage shapes an individual's identity by providing a framework for understanding themselves and their place in the world. For Native Americans, their cultural heritage includes traditional beliefs, practices, and values that define who they are as a people. Recent immigrants can also use their cultural heritage to form their identities, especially if they feel disconnected from their country of origin due to political or social reasons.
3. Cultural expression: Cultural heritage provides individuals with the means to express themselves culturally, such as through language, art, music, and dance. For Native Americans, cultural expression is an essential aspect of preserving their cultural heritage and maintaining their identities. Recent immigrants can also use cultural expression to maintain their ties to their ethnic communities and to connect with other members of the community.
4. Community connection: Cultural heritage provides a sense of community among individuals who share similar cultural practices, beliefs, and values. For Native Americans, their cultural heritage creates a strong sense of community based on shared cultural practices and traditions. Recent immigrants can also find a sense of community among other members of their ethnic group, which helps them navigate the challenges of living in a new country.
5. Resilience: Cultural heritage provides individuals with the resilience to cope with challenges and hardships. For Native Americans, their cultural heritage has helped them survive centuries of colonization, forced assimilation, and discrimination. Recent immigrants can also draw upon their cultural heritage for strength and resilience when facing challenges in their new environment.
