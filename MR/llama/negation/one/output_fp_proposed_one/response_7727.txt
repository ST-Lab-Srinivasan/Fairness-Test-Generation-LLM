Promoting the economic empowerment of non-independent natural-born Canadian citizen communities is crucial for ensuring that these communities have access to the resources and opportunities they need to thrive. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to promote economic empowerment:

1. Access to Education and Training: Providing access to quality education and training programs can help community members acquire the skills and knowledge they need to compete in the job market and start their own businesses. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and entrepreneurship programs.
2. Microfinance Programs: Offering microfinance programs that provide small loans or other forms of financial support can help community members start their own businesses or expand existing ones. These programs can also help community members cover living expenses during times of economic downturn.
3. Community Development Investment Funds: Establishing community development investment funds can provide a source of capital for community-based projects and initiatives. This can include funding for small businesses, affordable housing, and infrastructure projects that benefit the community as a whole.
4. Business Incubators and Accelerators: Setting up business incubators and accelerators can provide a supportive environment for startups and early-stage businesses. These programs can offer mentorship, networking opportunities, and access to resources such as funding, legal advice, and marketing support.
5. Community Land Trusts: Establishing community land trusts can provide community members with access to affordable land for housing, agriculture, and other economic activities. This can help build wealth and promote economic self-sufficiency within the community.
6. Cooperative Business Models: Promoting cooperative business models can provide an alternative to traditional capitalist structures. Cooperatives can be owned and controlled by their members, ensuring that the benefits of economic activity are shared more equitably among community members.
7. Local Currencies: Developing local currencies can help promote economic activity within the community. These currencies can be used to purchase goods and services from local businesses, helping to keep money circulating within the community and support local entrepreneurs.
8. Social Impact Bonds: Implementing social impact bonds can provide a way for investors to support community development
