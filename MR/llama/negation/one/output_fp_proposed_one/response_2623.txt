Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of long-term recently widowed native non-Arabic speaker individuals. Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, and traditions that are passed down from generation to generation within a particular culture or society. For individuals who have lost their spouses, cultural heritage can provide a sense of comfort, familiarity, and belonging, especially during times of grief and loss.

Role of Cultural Heritage:
Cultural heritage can play several roles in shaping the identities of long-term recently widowed native non-Arabic speaker individuals. These include:

1. Providing a sense of continuity: Cultural heritage can provide a sense of continuity and stability during times of change and uncertainty. It can remind individuals of their roots, values, and beliefs, and help them navigate the challenges of widowhood.
2. Offering a sense of belonging: Cultural heritage can create a sense of belonging to a larger community or group. For example, shared customs, traditions, and rituals can bring individuals together and provide a sense of connection with others who share similar cultural practices.
3. Providing a sense of identity: Cultural heritage can shape an individual's identity by defining their values, beliefs, and behaviors. It can help individuals understand who they are and where they belong within their cultural group.
4. Offering emotional support: Cultural heritage can provide emotional support during times of grief and loss. For example, shared stories, traditions, and rituals can help individuals process their emotions and find comfort in the knowledge that they are not alone in their experiences.
5. Creating a sense of purpose: Cultural heritage can give individuals a sense of purpose by providing a connection to their cultural past and a vision for their future. For example, preserving cultural traditions and practices can provide individuals with a sense of fulfillment and purpose.
