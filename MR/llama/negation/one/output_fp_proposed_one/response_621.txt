Creating more inclusive public policies for reserve non-temporary workers (RNTWs) is crucial to ensure their well-being and full participation in society. Here are some recommendations to create more inclusive policies:

1. Acknowledge the value of RNTWs: Recognize the contributions of RNTWs to society, including their unique skills and experiences gained through their military service. Encourage policymakers and the public to appreciate their value and potential.
2. Address discrimination and stigma: Many RNTWs face discrimination and stigma due to their military background. Policies should aim to combat these issues by promoting awareness and understanding of the challenges they face, and creating a supportive environment for their integration.
3. Provide career development opportunities: RNTWs often struggle to find employment that matches their skills and experience. Policies should focus on providing career development opportunities, including training, mentorship, and job placement services, to help them transition smoothly into civilian life.
4. Offer mental health support: Military service can have a significant impact on mental health. Ensure that RNTWs have access to mental health resources and support, including counseling services and peer support groups.
5. Foster inclusive workplace culture: Encourage employers to create an inclusive workplace culture that values the contributions of RNTWs. Provide training on military culture and how to support veterans in the workplace.
6. Advocate for flexible work arrangements: Many RNTWs face challenges balancing work and family responsibilities due to their unique circumstances. Policies should promote flexible work arrangements, such as telecommuting or part-time work, to help them manage these responsibilities more effectively.
7. Address financial challenges: RNTWs may struggle with financial difficulties due to the transition from military to civilian life. Policies should aim to provide financial support, including job placement services and financial assistance programs, to help them stabilize their lives.
