Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of newly married native non-French speaker individuals. It is essential to understand how cultural heritage influences their sense of self and belonging, especially in a new context like France. This study aims to explore the impact of cultural heritage on the identities of these individuals and how it shapes their experiences in France.

The Role of Cultural Heritage:
Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, customs, beliefs, practices, and traditions that are passed down from one generation to another within a particular group or community. For newly married native non-French speaker individuals, their cultural heritage plays a crucial role in shaping their identities, particularly in a new context like France. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage influences their sense of self and belonging:

1. Sense of Belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a particular group or community. For newly married native non-French speaker individuals, their cultural heritage helps them feel connected to their family, community, and country of origin. This sense of belonging is essential for their well-being and happiness in a new context like France.
2. Identity Formation: Cultural heritage influences how individuals form their identities, including their values, beliefs, and practices. For newly married native non-French speaker individuals, their cultural heritage helps them understand who they are and what they stand for, which is crucial in a new context like France.
3. Adaptation: Cultural heritage can also influence how individuals adapt to a new context like France. For example, if an individual comes from a collectivist culture (e.g., India), they may find it challenging to adjust to the more individualistic culture of France. However, their cultural heritage can provide them with the necessary tools and strategies to navigate this new context.

For newly married native non-French speaker individuals, their cultural heritage may intersect with these other factors in complex ways, influencing how they experience and navigate their new context.
