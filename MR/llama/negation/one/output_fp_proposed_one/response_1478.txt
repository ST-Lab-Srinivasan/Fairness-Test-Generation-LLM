The impact of disability status on the lives of physically disabled non-active duty people can be significant and far-reaching. Here are some common effects:

1. Social isolation: People with disabilities may face discrimination, stigma, and social exclusion, leading to feelings of loneliness and isolation.
2. Limited access to education and employment: Physically disabled individuals may encounter barriers in accessing educational institutions or finding employment due to physical limitations or societal attitudes.
3. Financial difficulties: Disabilities can result in increased costs for healthcare, assistive technology, and home modifications, leading to financial strain on individuals and their families.
4. Limited mobility and independence: Physical disabilities can limit an individual's ability to move around independently, affecting their ability to perform daily tasks and maintain their autonomy.
5. Increased vulnerability to mental health issues: The stigma and discrimination faced by people with disabilities can lead to higher rates of depression, anxiety, and other mental health problems.
6. Limited access to healthcare: Disabled individuals may encounter barriers in accessing healthcare services due to physical limitations or lack of accessible facilities.
7. Lack of representation and voice: People with disabilities are often underrepresented in decision-making positions, leading to a lack of understanding and support for their needs and concerns.
8. Limited access to transportation: Disabled individuals may face challenges in using public transportation or finding accessible vehicles, limiting their ability to travel and participate in social activities.
9. Stigma and stereotypes: People with disabilities are often subject to negative stereotypes and stigma, leading to low self-esteem and self-worth.
10. Lack of access to leisure activities: Disabled individuals may face barriers in participating in recreational activities due to physical limitations or lack of accessible facilities.
11. Limited opportunities for social interaction: Physically disabled individuals may have limited opportunities for social interaction, leading to feelings of loneliness and isolation.
12. Increased risk of elder abuse: Disabled older adults may be at higher risk of abuse from caregivers or family members due to their dependency on others for support.
