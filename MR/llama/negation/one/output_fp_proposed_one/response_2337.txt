Cultural diversity plays a significant role in enriching the lives of Native American, non-citizen individuals living in the United States just above the poverty line. Here are some ways cultural diversity can positively impact their lives:

1. Preservation of Traditions and Culture: Cultural diversity allows these individuals to maintain their traditional practices, beliefs, and values, which are an essential part of their identity. It helps them preserve their cultural heritage and pass it down to future generations.
2. Identity and Sense of Belonging: Being part of a diverse culture provides a sense of belonging and identity for these individuals. They can connect with others who share similar experiences and traditions, fostering a sense of community and inclusivity.
3. Exposure to New Ideas and Perspectives: Cultural diversity exposes these individuals to new ideas, perspectives, and ways of thinking. It broadens their understanding of the world and encourages empathy and tolerance towards others.
4. Creative Expression: Cultural diversity provides a rich environment for creative expression, as individuals can draw inspiration from various cultures to create art, music, literature, and other forms of expression.
5. Economic Benefits: Cultural diversity can also have economic benefits for these individuals. It creates opportunities for cultural exchange, innovation, and entrepreneurship, which can lead to new businesses and job creation.
6. Access to Resources: Cultural diversity provides access to resources such as traditional knowledge, skills, and practices that can be used to address social, economic, and environmental challenges.
7. Resilience and Adaptability: Living in a culturally diverse environment can help these individuals develop resilience and adaptability. They learn to navigate different cultural contexts and communicate effectively across various cultures.
8. Educational Opportunities: Cultural diversity offers educational opportunities for these individuals, as they can learn about different cultures, histories, and traditions. This knowledge can help them better understand themselves and their place in the world.
9. Social Cohesion: Cultural diversity promotes social cohesion by fostering a sense of community and cooperation among individuals from different cultural backgrounds. It encourages collaboration and mutual understanding, which are essential for social harmony.
