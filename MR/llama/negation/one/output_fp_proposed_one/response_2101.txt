Highlighting the issues faced by native non-French speaker recent immigrants to the US requires a comprehensive approach that leverages various data sources and analysis techniques. Here are some ways to use data to shed light on this issue:

1. Survey Research: Conduct surveys among recent immigrant populations to gather information on their experiences, challenges, and barriers. The survey can be administered through various channels, such as online platforms, community centers, or door-to-door outreach. The survey questions should be designed to capture the unique experiences of native non-French speaker immigrants, such as language barriers, cultural differences, and discrimination.
2. Administrative Data: Utilize administrative data sources, such as government records and statistics, to understand the demographic characteristics of recent immigrant populations. This can include data on age, gender, education level, occupation, and country of origin. By analyzing these data, you can identify patterns and trends in the immigrant population, which can help inform policies and interventions aimed at addressing their issues.
3. Social Media Analysis: Analyze social media conversations and posts related to recent immigration issues. This can provide insights into the experiences and concerns of recent immigrants, as well as how they interact with each other and with mainstream society. Using sentiment analysis and topic modeling techniques can help identify patterns in the data that indicate specific issues or challenges faced by recent immigrants.
4. Geographic Information Systems (GIS): Use GIS to analyze the geographic distribution of recent immigrant populations. This can help identify areas with high concentrations of recent immigrants and understand how these populations are distributed across different regions, cities, and neighborhoods. By analyzing the spatial patterns in immigration data, you can develop targeted interventions that address the unique needs of recent immigrant communities.
5. Economic Data: Analyze economic data related to recent immigration, such as employment rates, income levels, and access to financial resources. This can help identify the economic challenges faced by recent immigrants, which are often compounded by language barriers and cultural differences. By understanding the economic dimensions of immigration, you can develop policies that address these issues and promote economic integration.
