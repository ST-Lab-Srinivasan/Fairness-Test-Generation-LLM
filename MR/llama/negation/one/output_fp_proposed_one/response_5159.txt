1. Age-Friendly Housing Design: Design homes and communities that cater to the needs of older adults, such as single-story living, wider doorways, and accessible bathroom and kitchen features. This can make it easier for individuals to age in place and avoid costly and disruptive moves.
2. Income-Based Rent Subsidies: Offer income-based rent subsidies to help older adults afford market-rate rental housing. This can be particularly helpful for those who are struggling to pay their rent due to reduced incomes or other financial challenges.
3. Supportive Housing Programs: Develop supportive housing programs that provide a range of services, including case management, health and wellness programs, and social activities. These programs can help older adults maintain their independence and quality of life while also providing additional support as needed.
4. Intergenerational Housing: Encourage intergenerational housing by creating communities that bring together people of different ages. This can help break down age-based stereotypes and create a more cohesive, supportive community.
5. Affordable Home Ownership Options: Offer affordable home ownership options, such as shared equity schemes or co-housing models, which can provide older adults with greater financial security and a sense of community.
6. Education and Outreach: Provide education and outreach to raise awareness about the housing needs of older adults and the benefits of inclusive housing policies. This can help build support for more inclusive housing options among policymakers, developers, and the general public.
7. Inclusive Zoning Policies: Enact zoning policies that promote mixed-use development, density, and walkability, which can help create more affordable and accessible housing options for older adults.
