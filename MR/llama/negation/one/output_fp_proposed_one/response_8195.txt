Ensuring the educational needs of ethnically full Hispanic non-civilian students can be a complex and multifaceted process. Here are some strategies that schools and educators can use to help meet the educational needs of this population:

This can help them develop proficiency in both Spanish and English, which can be beneficial for their academic success and future career opportunities.
3. Latino-focused Curriculum: Incorporate Latino-focused curriculum into the classroom to help ethnically full Hispanic students see themselves reflected in the material being taught. This can include learning about prominent Hispanic figures, historical events, and cultural practices that are relevant to their heritage.
4. Parent Engagement: Encourage parent engagement by providing opportunities for parents to participate in school activities and decision-making processes. This can help build a stronger relationship between the school and the Hispanic community and ensure that the needs of ethnically full Hispanic students are being met.
5. Recruitment and Retention: Actively recruit and retain teachers and staff who are from diverse backgrounds, including Hispanic culture. This can help create a more inclusive and supportive learning environment for ethnically full Hispanic students.
6. Community Partnerships: Build partnerships with community organizations that serve the Hispanic population to provide additional resources and support for ethnically full Hispanic students. This can include after-school programs, tutoring services, and mentorship opportunities.
