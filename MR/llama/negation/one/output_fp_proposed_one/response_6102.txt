To ensure that the voices of native non-Spanish speakers are heard in decision-making processes, it's important to create an inclusive and participatory environment where everyone feels valued and respected. Here are some strategies you can consider:

1. Provide language support: Offer translation services or interpretations during meetings and events to ensure that non-Spanish speakers can fully participate.
2. Use visual aids: Incorporate visual aids such as pictures, diagrams, and videos to help non-Spanish speakers understand the information being discussed.
3. Encourage participation: Create opportunities for non-Spanish speakers to share their thoughts and ideas through small group discussions or one-on-one interviews.
4. Use technology: Utilize digital tools such as online surveys, polls, or discussion forums to gather input from a diverse group of people.
5. Build relationships: Take the time to build personal connections with non-Spanish speakers and understand their perspectives and concerns.
6. Be mindful of power dynamics: Be aware of the potential for power imbalances within decision-making processes and actively work to address them.
7. Involve community leaders: Engage local leaders or community members who can serve as interpreters or translators, and help facilitate communication between different language groups.
8. Provide cultural context: Be sensitive to the cultural backgrounds of non-Spanish speakers and provide context for decisions that may be unfamiliar to them.
9. Offer training and education: Provide training and educational resources to help non-Spanish speakers develop their communication and decision-making skills.
10. Be proactive: Take the initiative to reach out to non-Spanish speakers and involve them in decision-making processes, rather than waiting for them to come forward.

By implementing these strategies, you can help ensure that the voices of native non-Spanish speaker are heard and valued in decision-making processes.
