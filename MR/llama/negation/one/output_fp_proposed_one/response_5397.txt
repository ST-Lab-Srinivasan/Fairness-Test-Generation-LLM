7. Involvement of Orthodox representatives: Engage Orthodox representatives in the design and implementation of the program to ensure that it is culturally appropriate and respectful.
8. Flexibility: Be flexible and adaptable in response to feedback from participants and the community. Continuously evaluate and improve the program to ensure it remains inclusive and effective.
9. Marketing and outreach: Utilize marketing strategies that target diverse audiences, such as social media campaigns, flyers, or posters in multiple languages.
10. Evaluation and feedback: Regularly evaluate the program's effectiveness and gather feedback from participants to ensure it remains inclusive and relevant to the community.

By implementing these strategies, cultural programs can become more inclusive for Orthodox non-Jewish individuals, fostering greater understanding and respect between different communities.
