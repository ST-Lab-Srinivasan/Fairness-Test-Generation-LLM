Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of Native American, recent immigrant to the US, and devout practicing non-Hindu individuals. Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, customs, beliefs, and practices of a particular group or community. For these individuals, their cultural heritage is deeply intertwined with their identity and plays a crucial role in shaping their sense of self and belonging.

Native American individuals have a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in their history, traditions, and beliefs. Their cultural heritage includes their connection to the land, their ancestors, and their community. This connection is often reflected in their spirituality, art, music, and storytelling. For Native Americans, their cultural heritage is an integral part of who they are and helps them maintain their cultural practices and traditions.

Recent immigrants to the US from non-Hindu cultures also bring their cultural heritage with them, which plays a significant role in shaping their identities. Their cultural heritage influences their language, cuisine, music, and religious practices. For example, recent immigrants from Mexico may maintain traditional Mexican customs and practices such as celebrating Dia de los Muertos or observing Catholicism. Similarly, recent immigrants from the Middle East may observe Islamic practices such as praying five times a day or fasting during Ramadan.

Devout practicing non-Hindu individuals also have a significant cultural heritage that shapes their identities. Their cultural heritage is deeply rooted in their religious beliefs and practices, which often influence their language, customs, and traditions. For example, Buddhists may maintain traditional practices such as meditation or observing the Eightfold Path. Similarly, Sikhs may wear the turban or carry a Kirpan as part of their cultural heritage.

In conclusion, cultural heritage plays a crucial role in shaping the identities of Native American, recent immigrant to the US, and devout practicing non-Hindu individuals. It influences their language, customs, beliefs, and practices, and helps them maintain their cultural practices and traditions. By understanding and respecting the cultural heritage of these groups, we can foster greater appreciation and inclusivity in our society
