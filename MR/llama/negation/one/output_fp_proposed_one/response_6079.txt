Creating more inclusive cultural events for partnered non-active duty individuals requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to make these events more welcoming:

1. Understand the needs of partners: Engage with partners and understand their needs, preferences, and challenges. This will help you tailor the event to their requirements and ensure they feel included.
2. Involve partners in planning: Encourage partners to participate in the planning process by asking for their input and feedback. This will help them feel invested in the event and more likely to attend.
3. Provide childcare options: Offer childcare services or create a safe and engaging space for children during the event. This will allow parents to enjoy the cultural event without worrying about their children's care.
4. Accessibility: Ensure that the event is accessible to all, including those with disabilities. Provide accommodations such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
5. Flexible scheduling: Offer flexible scheduling options to accommodate partners who may have different availability or schedules. For example, hold the event in the evening or on a weekend to accommodate those who cannot attend during regular work hours.
6. Virtual attendance: Consider offering virtual attendance options for partners who may not be able to attend in person. This can include live streaming of events, online exhibitions, or virtual tours.
7. Incorporate diverse programming: Offer a variety of cultural experiences that cater to different interests and preferences. This can include performances, workshops, and exhibitions that reflect the diversity of the community.
8. Provide food options: Offer food options that cater to partners with dietary restrictions or preferences. This can include vegan, gluten-free, or halal options, as well as traditional military spouse meals.
9. Create a comfortable environment: Ensure that the event space is comfortable and welcoming for all attendees. Provide adequate seating, lighting, and temperature control to create an enjoyable experience.
