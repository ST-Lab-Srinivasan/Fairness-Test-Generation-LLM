To promote the educational success of non-binary students, it is important to create an inclusive and supportive learning environment. Here are some strategies that educators can use:

1. Use inclusive language and practices: Avoid using binary language (e.g., "boys" and "girls") in your teaching materials and interactions with students. Instead, use gender-neutral language or ask students how they prefer to be referred to. Provide options for students to identify themselves in course materials, such as a checklist of gender identities.
2. Incorporate diverse perspectives: Include non-binary individuals and experiences in your teaching materials, such as by highlighting the contributions of non-binary scientists, artists, or historical figures. Show images or videos that depict non-binary people in a positive light.
3. Offer gender-neutral accommodations: Provide gender-neutral options for students' preferred names and pronouns on class rosters, exams, and other documents. Allow students to use gender-neutral language when introducing themselves or sharing their identities with the class.
4. Train yourself and your colleagues: Educate yourself and your colleagues about non-binary identities and the challenges they face in educational settings. Encourage your colleagues to be supportive of non-binary students and to use inclusive language and practices.
5. Create a safe space: Foster an environment where all students feel safe and respected, regardless of their gender identity. Encourage non-binary students to share their experiences and perspectives in the classroom, and listen actively when they do.
6. Provide resources and support: Offer non-binary students access to gender-neutral restrooms or other safe spaces where they can feel comfortable expressing themselves. Connect them with support services, such as counseling or academic advising, tailored to their needs.
7. Involve non-binary students in decision-making processes: Encourage non-binary students to participate in class discussions and decisions, and give them opportunities to share their thoughts and ideas on issues that affect them.
