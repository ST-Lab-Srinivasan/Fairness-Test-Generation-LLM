Creating more inclusive community events for native non-English speaker poor people requires thoughtful planning and consideration of their needs. Here are some strategies to help make your events more accessible and inclusive:

1. Provide translation services: Offer interpretation or translation services during events, especially for important announcements or activities. This can include providing headsets with translators or hiring interpreters to assist with communication.
2. Simplify language: Use simple language and avoid jargon or technical terms that may be unfamiliar to non-English speakers. Provide written materials in multiple languages, such as flyers, brochures, or programs.
3. Offer cultural sensitive activities: Incorporate cultural activities that are relevant to the community you are serving. For example, if you are hosting a cultural festival, include traditional music, dance, and food from diverse cultures.
4. Provide childcare and transportation: Offer childcare services during events to ensure that parents can participate without worrying about their children. Also, consider providing transportation options for those who may not have access to reliable transportation.
5. Involve community members in planning: Involve native non-English speaker poor community members in the planning process for events. This can help ensure that the event is tailored to their needs and preferences.
6. Offer financial assistance: Provide financial assistance to individuals who may not have the resources to attend events. This can include scholarships, grants, or subsidies for event registration fees.
7. Create a safe space: Ensure that your events are safe and welcoming spaces for all attendees. This can involve providing gender-neutral restrooms, accessible seating areas, and respectful language usage.
8. Use inclusive marketing: Use marketing materials that reflect the diversity of the community you are serving. Avoid using language or imagery that may be offensive or exclusive to any group.
