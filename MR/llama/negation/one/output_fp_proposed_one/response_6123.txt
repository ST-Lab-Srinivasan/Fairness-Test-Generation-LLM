Just above poverty line (JALP) individuals are those who have a income slightly higher than the poverty line, but still struggle to make ends meet. In India, the poverty line is defined as an annual income of INR 37,000 (approximately $520) for a rural household and INR 49,000 (approximately $680) for an urban household.
