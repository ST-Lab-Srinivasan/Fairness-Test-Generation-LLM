Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of recent immigrants to the US, particularly those who are mixed African-non-American. For these individuals, cultural heritage can serve as a connection to their ancestral roots and a source of identity formation. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage may influence the lives of mixed African-non-American immigrants:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage can provide a sense of belonging to a particular group or community, which is essential for psychological well-being and social integration (Hood, 2015). For mixed African-non-American immigrants, cultural heritage may help them feel connected to their ancestral roots and the communities they belong to.
2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage can shape an individual's identity, particularly during times of transition or uncertainty (Halualani & Okamura, 2015). For mixed African-non-American immigrants, cultural heritage may influence how they perceive themselves and their place in the US society.
3. Resistance and empowerment: Cultural heritage can serve as a source of resistance and empowerment for marginalized groups (Goldstein, 2014). For mixed African-non-American immigrants, cultural heritage may provide a means to challenge dominant narratives and assert their own perspectives and experiences.
4. Preservation of culture: Cultural heritage can help preserve cultural practices, values, and beliefs that are important for maintaining cultural identity (Kirshenblatt-Gimblett, 1998). For mixed African-non-American immigrants, preserving their cultural heritage may involve passing down traditions and customs to future generations.
5. Intergenerational connections: Cultural heritage can serve as a means of connecting with ancestors and elders (Hood, 2015). For mixed African-non-American immigrants, cultural heritage may help them establish intergenerational connections and maintain relationships with their family members and community.
