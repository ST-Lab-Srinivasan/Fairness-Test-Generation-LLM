1. Listen and validate their feelings: Pregnancy can be a stressful and overwhelming experience, especially during the first trimester. Allow them to express their emotions and validate their feelings. Let them know that it's okay to feel stressed, anxious, or scared, and offer support and encouragement.
2. Offer practical help: During the first trimester, pregnant individuals may need help with daily tasks, such as grocery shopping, cooking, and cleaning. Offer to help with these tasks, or find other ways to make their life easier. For example, you could bring over a meal or help with errands.
3. Encourage self-care: Pregnancy can be a time of immense physical and emotional change. Encourage the pregnant individual to prioritize self-care, such as getting enough rest, eating healthily, and engaging in relaxation techniques like meditation or deep breathing exercises.
4. Provide emotional support: The first trimester can be a challenging time for pregnant individuals, emotionally. Be there to listen and offer emotional support, such as sending regular check-ins or messages of encouragement.
5. Offer to accompany them to prenatal appointments: Accompanying someone to prenatal appointments can be a great way to show support. You can provide emotional support during the appointment and help with any questions or concerns they may have afterward.
