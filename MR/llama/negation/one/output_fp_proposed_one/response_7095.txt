Creating more inclusive cultural programs for Native Non-French Speaker Ethnically Full African Americans requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Centering the Voices of Native Communities: It is essential to listen to and center the voices of Native communities in the design and implementation of cultural programs. This means involving Native individuals and organizations in the decision-making process and prioritizing their perspectives and experiences.
2. Acknowledging Historical Trauma: Many Native American communities have experienced historical trauma, including forced assimilation, land theft, and cultural suppression. When designing cultural programs, it is important to acknowledge this history and how it has impacted Native communities. This can involve incorporating healing practices and traditional cultural activities into program offerings.
3. Fostering Cultural Exchange: Cultural exchange programs that bring together Native American and African American communities can help promote mutual understanding and respect. These programs could include cultural workshops, performances, and other interactive activities that celebrate the unique traditions and practices of both communities.
4. Providing Accessibility and Inclusivity: Programs should be designed to be accessible and inclusive for Native Non-French Speaker Ethnically Full African Americans. This means ensuring that program materials are available in multiple languages, providing accommodations for individuals with disabilities, and creating a welcoming environment for all participants.
5. Promoting Intergenerational Knowledge Transfer: Cultural programs can help preserve traditional knowledge and practices by promoting intergenerational knowledge transfer. This involves involving elders and other knowledge holders in the design and implementation of programs, and providing opportunities for younger generations to learn from them.
6. Addressing Systemic Inequality: Many Native American communities face systemic inequality, including discrimination, marginalization, and lack of access to resources. Cultural programs should be designed to address these issues by promoting social justice, equity, and inclusion.
7. Prioritizing Community Engagement: Community engagement is critical for creating inclusive cultural programs. This involves working closely with Native American communities to identify their needs and priorities and ensuring that program offerings reflect their values and practices.
8. Incorporating Traditional Arts and Practices: Traditional arts and practices are an essential part of Native American cultures.
