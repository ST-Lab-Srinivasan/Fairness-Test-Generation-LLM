Ensuring the voices of beginner Arabic speakers, especially those from ethnic minority groups, are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting diversity and inclusivity. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Create a safe space: Establish a welcoming and inclusive environment where individuals feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and opinions. This can be achieved by setting ground rules, being mindful of power dynamics, and actively listening to others.
2. Use interpreters or translators: If the decision-making process is conducted in Arabic, it may be challenging for non-Arabic speakers to participate. Consider hiring interpreters or translators to facilitate communication and ensure that all voices are heard.
3. Offer language training: Provide beginner Arabic speakers with language training opportunities to improve their proficiency in the language. This can help them feel more confident and capable of participating in decision-making processes.
4. Foster a culture of inclusivity: Encourage participants to share their thoughts and ideas, and actively listen to their opinions. Avoid interrupting or dismissing their views, and create opportunities for feedback and constructive criticism.
5. Set clear expectations: Establish clear guidelines and expectations for participation in decision-making processes. Make sure everyone understands the role they play and how their contributions are valued.
6. Provide mentorship opportunities: Offer mentorship opportunities to beginner Arabic speakers, particularly those from ethnic minority groups. This can help them develop leadership skills and feel more confident in their ability to participate in decision-making processes.
7. Celebrate diversity: Embrace the diversity of participants by highlighting the unique perspectives and experiences they bring to the table. Avoid stereotyping or reducing individuals to their ethnic identity, and instead, focus on the value of their contributions.
8. Develop an inclusive agenda: Create an agenda that addresses the needs and concerns of all participants, including beginner Arabic speakers from ethnic minority groups. Make sure the agenda is accessible and actionable, and that it provides a platform for diverse voices to be heard.
