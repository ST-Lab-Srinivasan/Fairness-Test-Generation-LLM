To ensure that the educational needs of beginner English speakers who are not single students are met, here are some strategies and recommendations:

1. Differentiated Instruction: Teachers can use differentiated instruction techniques to cater to the diverse learning needs of beginner English speakers. This involves recognizing that each student has their unique learning style, pace, and abilities, and tailoring the instruction accordingly.
2. Group Work: Encourage group work and collaboration among students. This helps to promote language acquisition through peer-to-peer interaction, sharing ideas, and practicing communication skills.
3. Technology Integration: Utilize technology tools and resources to support language learning. For instance, interactive whiteboards, language learning apps, and online resources can provide engaging and personalized learning experiences for students.
4. Authentic Materials: Incorporate authentic materials such as news articles, videos, podcasts, and books into the curriculum. This helps students develop their language skills in a meaningful and relevant context.
5. Real-Life Situations: Use real-life situations and scenarios to teach English language skills. For example, role-playing exercises, group discussions, and presentations can help students apply their language knowledge in practical situations.
6. Formative Assessments: Regularly assess students' progress throughout the course using formative assessments. This helps teachers identify areas where individual students may need extra support or practice.
7. Flexible Pacing: Allow students to work at their own pace and provide flexible scheduling options. This can help accommodate different learning styles and needs, particularly for those who may require more time to master certain skills.
8. Multilingual Instruction: Incorporate multiple languages into the instructional materials and activities. This can help students who are bilingual or multilingual, as well as those who are learning English as a second language.
9. Guest Speakers: Invite guest speakers from various industries and professions to share their experiences and communicate with students in English. This provides an opportunity for students to learn from experts and practice their communication skills in a real-world setting.
