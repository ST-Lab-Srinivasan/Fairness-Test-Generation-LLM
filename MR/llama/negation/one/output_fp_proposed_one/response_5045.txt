2. Budgeting and Expense Management: Help them create a realistic budget that accounts for their income, expenses, and savings goals. Encourage them to track their expenses and identify areas where they can cut back on non-essential spending.

5. Investment Knowledge: Teach them about various investment options such as 401(k), IRA, and mutual funds. Explain the benefits of diversifying their investments and how to assess risk tolerance.
6. Retirement Planning: Help them understand the importance of starting early to save for retirement. Encourage them to contribute to a retirement account such as a 401(k) or IRA, and explain how compound interest can help their savings grow over time.
