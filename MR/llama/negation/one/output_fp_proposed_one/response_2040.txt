Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner non-French speaker Native recent immigrants to the US requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some strategies that can help make these programs more accessible and engaging:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the program's content, language, and format are accessible to beginner non-French speaker Native recent immigrants. This may involve providing subtitles or translations in the language spoken by the majority of participants, using simple language and avoiding jargon or technical terms, and offering flexible formats such as online classes or small group discussions.
2. Cultural sensitivity: Be aware of the cultural backgrounds and experiences of the participants and tailor the program accordingly. For example, include traditional music, dance, or art from the participants' countries of origin, or incorporate elements that reflect their cultural practices and values.
3. Inclusive language: Use inclusive language and avoid stereotypes or assumptions based on race, ethnicity, or nationality. Avoid using French language or cultural references that may be unfamiliar to non-French speaker Native recent immigrants.
4. Intersectionality: Consider the intersections of race, gender, sexuality, and other factors that may affect participants' experiences and perspectives. Be aware of power dynamics and address them through inclusive practices such as active listening, mutual respect, and shared decision-making.
5. Collaboration: Involve participants in the planning and execution of cultural programs to ensure that they are meaningful and relevant to their needs and interests. Encourage collaboration between different communities and groups to foster cross-cultural understanding and exchange.
6. Education and training: Provide education and training on cultural competence, diversity, and inclusivity to program staff and volunteers. This can help them understand the needs of diverse participants and create more inclusive programs.
7. Community outreach: Reach out to diverse communities and organizations to promote the cultural programs and encourage participation. Use various marketing channels such as social media, flyers, and posters to ensure that the program is visible and accessible to a wide range of audiences.
8. Feedback mechanisms: Establish feedback mechanisms to gather input from participants on the effectiveness and inclusivity of the cultural programs.
