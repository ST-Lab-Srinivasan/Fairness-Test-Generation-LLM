India has a long history of advocating for the rights of marginalized communities, including recent immigrants. Here are some examples of successful advocacy efforts for non-civilian rights in India:

1. Citizenship (Amendment) Act, 2019: This act granted citizenship to non-Muslim immigrants from Afghanistan, Bangladesh, and Pakistan who had entered India before December 2014. The act was passed after years of advocacy by civil society organizations and political parties, highlighting the plight of these immigrants who were living in India without citizenship.
2. National Minorities Commission Act, 1993: This act established the National Minorities Commission to investigate and monitor the social, economic, and educational conditions of minority communities in India. The commission has been instrumental in advocating for the rights of religious minorities, including Muslims, Christians, Sikhs, Buddhists, and Jains.
3. Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (SC/ST) Prevention of Atrocities Act, 1989: This act was enacted to protect the rights of scheduled castes and tribes from discrimination and atrocities. The act has been successful in reducing crimes against these communities and providing them with legal recourse.
4. Right to Information Act, 2005: This act provides a framework for citizens to access information from public authorities, which has helped in exposing corruption and discrimination against marginalized communities. The act has been used by civil society organizations and activists to hold government officials accountable for their actions.
5. NALSA v Union of India (2014): In this landmark judgement, the Supreme Court of India recognized transgender people as a third gender and granted them the right to identify themselves as non-heterosexual. The judgement has been instrumental in advocating for the rights of transgender people in India.
