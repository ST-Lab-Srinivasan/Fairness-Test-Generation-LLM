Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for beginner non-Chinese speakers and individuals with disabilities requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions:

1. Provide Accessibility Information in Advance: Make sure to provide information about the celebration, including its location, date, time, and accessibility features, in a language that is easy for beginner non-Chinese speakers to understand. This can include visual aids, such as pictures or videos, as well as audio descriptions or captions.
2. Offer Multiple Access Points: Provide multiple ways for individuals with disabilities to participate in the celebration, such as sign language interpretation, braille materials, or audio descriptions of the event. This can help ensure that everyone has an equal opportunity to participate and enjoy the celebration.
3. Create a Safe and Inclusive Environment: Make sure the celebration is held in a safe and inclusive environment. This can include providing wheelchair accessibility, using gender-neutral language, and creating a welcoming atmosphere for all participants.
4. Involve Disability Advocates: Involve disability advocates and organizations in the planning process to ensure that the celebration is accessible and inclusive for everyone. This can include seeking feedback from individuals with disabilities and their families, as well as working with organizations that specialize in disability advocacy.
5. Offer Accommodations: Offer accommodations such as wheelchair accessibility, seating areas for individuals who may need to take breaks, and audio descriptions or sign language interpretation for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing.
6. Provide Clear Navigation: Make sure the event space is well-marked and easy to navigate, with clear signs and pathways. This can help reduce confusion and anxiety for individuals with cognitive or mobility impairments.
