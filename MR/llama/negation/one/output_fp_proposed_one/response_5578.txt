Creating inclusive community events for people with non-genetic conditions requires careful planning and consideration. Here are some tips to help make your events more accessible and welcoming:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the event location is wheelchair accessible, has a ramp or elevator, and provides adequate parking for those with mobility issues. Provide audio descriptions or sign language interpretation for deaf or hard of hearing attendees.
2. Inclusive Language: Use inclusive language in promotional materials, such as avoiding terms like "disabled" or "handicapped." Instead, use person-first language, such as "person with a disability" or "person with a non-genetic condition."
3. Representation: Ensure that the event's programming and marketing materials include diverse representations of people with different abilities and conditions. Avoid tokenism by including people from underrepresented groups in leadership roles or on panels.
4. Accommodations: Provide accommodations such as reserved seating, priority registration, or extra time to complete activities for attendees who may need them. Offer a quiet room or space for attendees who are overwhelmed or need a break from the main event.
5. Education and Training: Offer education and training to volunteers, staff, and speakers on disability etiquette, accessibility, and inclusion. This can help create a more welcoming environment for all attendees.
6. Collaboration: Work with organizations that support people with non-genetic conditions to co-host or collaborate on the event. This can provide additional resources, expertise, and support in creating an inclusive atmosphere.
7. Flexibility: Be flexible and willing to accommodate the needs of attendees. This can include adjusting event schedules, providing extra support, or simply being open to feedback and suggestions.
8. Inclusive Programming: Offer programming that is accessible and inclusive for all attendees, such as sign language interpretation, audio descriptions, or braille materials. Provide activities and workshops that are tailored to the needs of people with different abilities and conditions.
