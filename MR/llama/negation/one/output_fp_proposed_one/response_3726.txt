Promoting the economic empowerment of nominal Muslim natural-born non-citizen communities is crucial for their well-being and integration into the society. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to achieve this goal:

1. Education and Skill Development: Providing access to quality education and skill development programs can help individuals from these communities acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, language classes, and other forms of educational support.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encouraging entrepreneurship within these communities can help create jobs and economic opportunities. This can be done through providing access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities.
3. Access to Financial Services: Ensuring that individuals from these communities have access to financial services such as bank accounts, microfinance, and other forms of credit can help them manage their finances and build assets.
4. Job Creation: Governments and private sector actors can work together to create job opportunities within these communities. This can include hiring practices that promote diversity and inclusion, as well as investing in community-based initiatives that provide job training and placement services.
5. Addressing Discrimination: Discrimination based on religion, race, or national origin is a significant barrier to economic empowerment for many individuals from these communities. Addressing this discrimination through policies and practices that promote inclusion can help level the playing field.
6. Community Development: Investing in community development programs that provide infrastructure, social services, and other forms of support can help build stronger, more resilient communities. This can include initiatives that promote cultural exchange, social cohesion, and interfaith dialogue.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocating for policy changes that address the systemic barriers faced by these communities can help create a more inclusive economic environment. This can include advocating for policies that promote equal access to education, employment, and financial services.
8. Collaboration with Civil Society: Working closely with civil society organizations that serve these communities can help identify specific challenges and develop targeted solutions. This can include partnering with community-based organizations, religious groups, and other stakeholders to promote economic empowerment.
