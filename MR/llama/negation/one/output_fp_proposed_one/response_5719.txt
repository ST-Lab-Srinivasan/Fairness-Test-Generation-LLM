Ensuring that the healthcare needs of native non-Arabic speaker poor people are met can be a significant challenge in many parts of the world. However, there are several strategies that can be implemented to address this issue:

1. Language Access Services: Provide language access services such as interpreters and translators to ensure that non-Arabic speakers can communicate effectively with healthcare providers. This can help bridge the language barrier and improve healthcare outcomes.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for healthcare providers to help them understand and address the unique needs of diverse patient populations, including non-Arabic speakers.
3. Health Literacy Programs: Develop health literacy programs that provide information in a language and format that is easily understood by non-Arabic speakers. This can help improve health education and promote better health outcomes.
4. Community Partnerships: Establish partnerships with community-based organizations that serve native non-Arabic speaker populations. These partnerships can help identify healthcare needs and develop culturally appropriate interventions to address them.
5. Telemedicine Services: Utilize telemedicine services to provide remote access to healthcare providers for non-Arabic speakers who may not have access to in-person care. This can help address barriers related to distance and language.
6. Patient Navigators: Train patient navigators who are fluent in the native language of the population being served. These navigators can provide cultural brokering services, helping patients navigate the healthcare system and access needed services.
7. Health Fairs and Outreach: Host health fairs and outreach events that cater to non-Arabic speaker populations. These events can provide an opportunity for people to receive health screenings, ask questions, and connect with healthcare providers in a culturally appropriate manner.
