Lower-class non-independent people, including those from marginalized communities, face significant challenges in the job market. These challenges can be attributed to systemic barriers and structural inequality. Here are some of the common challenges faced by lower-class non-independent people in the job market:

1. Limited access to education and training: Many lower-class non-independent individuals face obstacles in accessing quality education and training opportunities due to financial constraints or lack of access to resources. This limits their ability to acquire the skills and knowledge required for high-paying jobs.
2. Discrimination and bias: Discrimination and bias based on race, gender, sexual orientation, religion, or other factors can prevent lower-class non-independent individuals from being hired or promoted. Research has shown that these biases can be unconscious and often go unaddressed in the workplace.
3. Limited job opportunities: Lower-class non-independent individuals may have limited access to job opportunities, particularly in industries with a strong power dynamic. This can result in fewer career advancement opportunities and lower earning potential.
4. Unstable work arrangements: Many lower-class non-independent individuals face unstable work arrangements, including part-time or temporary employment, which can limit their job security and benefits. This instability can also impact their mental health and well-being.
5. Lack of representation and voice: Lower-class non-independent individuals may feel marginalized or silenced in the workplace due to a lack of representation and voice. This can result in a lack of understanding of their needs and concerns, which can further exacerbate existing disparities.
