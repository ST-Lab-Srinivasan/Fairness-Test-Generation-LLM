Addressing the needs of poor native non-Arabic speaker individuals in disaster response is crucial to ensure that everyone receives appropriate support and aid during emergency situations. Here are some ways to address their needs:

1. Provide multilingual communication: Include multilingual communication channels, such as interpreters or translators, to facilitate communication with non-Arabic speakers. This can be done through various means, including phone hotlines, social media, and in-person assistance.
2. Use visual aids: Rely on visual aids like pictures, diagrams, and videos to convey information, especially for those who may not be proficient in reading or writing Arabic. This can help non-Arabic speakers understand the information better and access critical support services.
3. Simplify forms and documents: Design simplified forms and documents that are easy to fill out for individuals who may not be familiar with Arabic script or have limited literacy skills. Use large fonts, clear instructions, and simple language to make it easier for them to access essential services.
4. Train volunteers and staff: Provide training to volunteers and staff on how to communicate effectively with non-Arabic speakers during disaster response. This can include learning basic phrases in common languages spoken by the affected community, as well as understanding cultural differences and sensitivities.
5. Offer culturally sensitive support: Be aware of cultural differences and tailor support services accordingly. For example, some cultures may have specific rituals or practices that need to be accommodated during disaster response. By being sensitive to these cultural nuances, you can help build trust and rapport with non-Arabic speakers.
6. Collaborate with local organizations: Partner with local organizations that cater to non-Arabic speaking communities. These organizations can provide valuable insights on the specific needs of their communities and help bridge the language barrier during disaster response.
7. Utilize technology: Leverage technology to facilitate communication, such as using translation apps or social media groups dedicated to sharing information in multiple languages. This can help reach a wider audience and ensure that everyone has access to critical information.
