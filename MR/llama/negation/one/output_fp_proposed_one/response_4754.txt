The challenges faced by non-Asian people from ethnic minority backgrounds, regardless of their income level, in the healthcare system can be summarized as follows:

1. Lack of cultural competency: Healthcare providers may not have the necessary training or experience to provide culturally sensitive care to patients from diverse backgrounds. This can lead to miscommunication, misunderstandings, and poor outcomes.
2. Language barriers: Patients who do not speak the dominant language in their area may face difficulties communicating with healthcare providers, which can result in missed diagnoses or inappropriate treatments.
3. Limited access to care: Ethnic minority patients may encounter barriers to accessing healthcare services due to factors such as lack of transportation, childcare responsibilities, or limited availability of healthcare providers who speak their language.
4. Discrimination and bias: Patients from ethnic minority backgrounds may experience discrimination or bias from healthcare providers, which can lead to a lack of trust and poor health outcomes.
5. Limited health literacy: Some patients from ethnic minority backgrounds may have limited health literacy, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the healthcare system, understand their diagnoses, or adhere to treatment plans.
6. Cultural beliefs and practices: Patients' cultural beliefs and practices may conflict with medical advice or treatments, leading to non-adherence or delayed adherence to care.
7. Stigma and shame: Patients from ethnic minority backgrounds may experience stigma or shame associated with their condition, which can lead to avoidance of healthcare services or poor mental health outcomes.
8. Lack of representation in medical research: The majority of medical research focuses on the needs and experiences of white, middle-class populations, leaving ethnic minority patients underrepresented in clinical trials and neglecting their unique health needs.
