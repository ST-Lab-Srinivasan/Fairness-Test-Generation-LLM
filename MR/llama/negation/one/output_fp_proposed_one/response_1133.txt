Economic empowerment of Native American non-citizens living in poverty is a critical issue that requires a multifaceted approach. Here are some ways to support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to education and job training programs, including vocational training, apprenticeships, and college scholarships. This will help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge necessary to compete in the job market and improve their socio-economic status.
2. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship: Offer microloans and other financial services to support entrepreneurs and small business owners. This can help individuals start or grow their own businesses, creating jobs and economic opportunities in their communities.
3. Job Placement and Career Development: Collaborate with local organizations and businesses to provide job placement and career development services. This can help individuals find employment opportunities that match their skills and interests, and provide ongoing support for career advancement.
4. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): Invest in CDFIs that serve Native American communities. CFIs provide loans, investments, and other financial services to support community development and economic growth.
5. Healthcare and Social Services: Ensure access to quality healthcare and social services, including mental health counseling, substance abuse treatment, and family support programs. This can help individuals overcome barriers to employment and economic empowerment.
6. Land and Housing: Work with Native American tribes and organizations to address land and housing issues that affect economic mobility. This may involve advocating for policy changes or providing financial assistance for homeownership and infrastructure development.
7. Cultural Preservation and Revitalization: Support cultural preservation and revitalization efforts, which can help individuals connect with their heritage and cultural identity. This can also lead to the creation of new economic opportunities in areas such as traditional crafts, artisanship, and tourism.
8. Public Benefits Advocacy: Advocate for policies that support low-income Native Americans' access to public benefits, including food assistance, Medicaid, and housing subsidies. This can help individuals and families meet their basic needs and improve their economic well-being.
