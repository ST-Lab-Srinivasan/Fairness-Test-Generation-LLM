Ensuring the educational needs of non-civilian ethnically full African-American students are met requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges and barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Culturally Sensitive Curriculum: Develop a curriculum that reflects the experiences, histories, and cultures of African-American students. Incorporate diverse perspectives, voices, and texts to create an inclusive learning environment.
2. Diverse Teaching Staff: Ensure that the teaching staff is diverse and representative of the student population. This can help increase students' sense of belonging and engagement in the classroom.
3. Identity-Based Support: Provide support groups or clubs for African-American students to connect with their peers and build a sense of community. These groups can address specific issues such as cultural identity, academic achievement, and social justice.
4. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair African-American students with successful professionals or educators who share similar experiences and backgrounds. This can help provide guidance, support, and role models for students.
5. Cultural Events and Activities: Organize cultural events and activities that celebrate African-American history, culture, and contributions. These can include Black History Month events, cultural festivals, and guest speakers from diverse backgrounds.
6. Academic Advising: Provide academic advising that takes into account the unique needs and experiences of African-American students. This can help ensure that they are placed in courses that challenge them academically while also supporting their social and emotional growth.
7. Advocacy and Allyship: Educate teachers, administrators, and parents about the experiences and challenges faced by African-American students. Encourage allyship and advocacy to address systemic barriers and biases that may exist in the educational system.
8. Family Engagement: Foster strong relationships between African-American families and the school through regular communication, events, and activities. This can help build trust and increase parental involvement in their children's education.
9. Community Partnerships: Develop partnerships with local organizations that serve African-American communities. These partnerships can provide additional resources, mentorship, and support for students outside
