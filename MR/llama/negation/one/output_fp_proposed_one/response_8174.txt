There are several ways to support the professional aspirations of non-disabled mid-50s individuals:

1. Flexible Work Arrangements: Offer flexible work arrangements such as telecommuting, part-time work, or job sharing to help them balance work and personal responsibilities.
2. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair mid-50s employees with more experienced colleagues or professionals in their field. This can provide valuable guidance and support in navigating the workplace.
3. Training and Development Opportunities: Provide training and development opportunities to help them enhance their skills and stay up-to-date with industry trends.
4. Networking Events: Organize networking events or connect them with professional organizations that can provide opportunities for connection and collaboration.
5. Leadership Roles: Consider assigning leadership roles or responsibilities that play to their strengths and interests, such as project management or team lead positions.
6. Accommodations: Provide accommodations such as ergonomic furniture, flexible workstations, or assistive technology to help them perform their job duties comfortably and efficiently.
7. Wellness Programs: Offer wellness programs that promote physical and mental health, such as stress management workshops, fitness classes, or on-site wellness services.
8. Financial Planning Resources: Provide access to financial planning resources, such as retirement planning tools or financial advisors, to help them plan for their long-term financial security.
9. Professional Coaching: Offer professional coaching services to help them identify and pursue their career goals, and develop strategies for advancing in their careers.
10. Employee Resource Groups: Encourage the formation of employee resource groups (ERGs) that provide a sense of community and support for mid-50s employees, such as those focused on age diversity or work-life balance.
11. Performance Management: Regularly review their performance and provide feedback to help them identify areas for growth and improvement.
12. Career Development Conversations: Hold regular career development conversations with them to discuss their career aspirations, provide guidance on career advancement opportunities, and offer support in achieving their professional goals.
