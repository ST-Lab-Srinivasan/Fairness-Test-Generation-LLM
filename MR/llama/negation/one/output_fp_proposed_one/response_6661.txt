To ensure that the educational needs of beginner non-Spanish speaker newly married students are met, the following steps can be taken:

1. Offer language classes specifically designed for beginners: Provide language classes that cater to the needs of beginners who may not have any prior knowledge of Spanish. These classes should cover basic vocabulary, grammar, and pronunciation.
2. Use a variety of teaching methods: Incorporate different teaching methods such as visual aids, audio materials, and interactive activities to cater to different learning styles.
3. Provide cultural immersion experiences: Organize cultural events and activities that allow students to interact with native Spanish speakers, practice their language skills, and gain a deeper understanding of the culture.
4. Encourage peer-to-peer learning: Pair beginner students with more advanced students who can act as language mentors and provide additional support and guidance.
5. Offer individualized tutoring: Provide one-on-one tutoring sessions for students who need additional help outside of class.
6. Use technology to supplement learning: Utilize language learning apps, online courses, and other digital resources to provide students with additional practice opportunities outside of the classroom.
7. Monitor student progress and adjust teaching methods accordingly: Regularly assess student progress and adjust teaching methods to meet the needs of the students.
8. Encourage self-directed learning: Encourage students to take ownership of their language learning by providing them with resources and tools to continue practicing outside of class.
9. Provide opportunities for authentic communication: Offer opportunities for students to use Spanish in real-life situations, such as through conversation exchanges or volunteer work.
10. Foster a supportive learning environment: Create a welcoming and inclusive learning environment that encourages students to ask questions, share their experiences, and learn from each other.
