Family support plays a crucial role in the success of Native American recent immigrants to the US, particularly those with non-genetic conditions. Here are some ways in which family support can impact their success:

1. Emotional Support: Having a strong support system of family members who understand and accept them can help recent immigrants navigate the challenges of adapting to a new culture, language, and way of life. They can provide emotional support during difficult times, such as discrimination, cultural shock, or financial struggles.
2. Practical Support: Family members can offer practical assistance, such as helping with everyday tasks, childcare, or household chores. This can be especially important for recent immigrants who may struggle to adjust to the faster pace of life in the US and may need help managing their daily responsibilities.
3. Cultural Connection: Native American recent immigrants may face cultural barriers and challenges in their new environment. Having a supportive family can help them maintain their cultural identity and traditions, which is essential for their mental and emotional well-being. Family members can provide a sense of familiarity and comfort during times of transition and change.
4. Networking Opportunities: Family members may have connections within their community or network, which can be beneficial for recent immigrants looking to establish new relationships or access resources. These connections can help recent immigrants find job opportunities, access healthcare services, or connect with other support systems.
5. Language Support: Many Native American recent immigrants may face language barriers in their new environment. Having family members who speak the local language can provide a vital source of communication and support, especially during critical moments such as medical emergencies or legal issues.
6. Financial Support: Family members can help recent immigrants financially, whether by providing direct financial assistance or by helping them access resources such as government benefits or low-interest loans. This support can be particularly important during periods of economic instability or when facing unexpected expenses.
