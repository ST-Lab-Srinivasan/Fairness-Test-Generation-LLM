Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of mixed African American individuals. The cultural heritage of a person refers to the shared beliefs, values, customs, and practices that are passed down from one generation to the next within a particular group or community. For mixed African Americans, their cultural heritage is often shaped by multiple cultures and experiences, including their African American heritage, as well as their non-African American parentage.

Mixed African Americans may identify with different cultural groups depending on their individual experiences and connections to these groups. For example, they may feel a strong connection to their African American heritage through their ancestry, family history, and cultural traditions, while also feeling a sense of belonging to other cultural groups such as their non-African American parentage.
