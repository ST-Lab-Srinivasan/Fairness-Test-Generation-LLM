The impact of disability status on the lives of non-temporarily workers can be significant, particularly for those who have a long-term or chronic condition. Here are some potential impacts:

1. Reduced work hours or job flexibility: People with disabilities may need to reduce their work hours or request flexible work arrangements to accommodate their needs. This can result in lower earning potential and reduced career advancement opportunities.
2. Limited access to employment opportunities: Discrimination against people with disabilities in the hiring process can limit their access to job opportunities, leading to a lower likelihood of being hired or promoted.
3. Difficulty with transportation: Depending on the nature of their disability, individuals may face challenges with transportation, such as difficulty using public transportation or needing specialized transportation services. This can limit their access to work and other activities.
4. Social isolation: People with disabilities may experience social isolation due to discrimination, stigma, or lack of accessibility in their communities. This can lead to feelings of loneliness, depression, and anxiety.
5. Financial strain: Disability-related expenses, such as medical equipment, home modifications, and assistive technology, can put a significant financial burden on individuals and families.
6. Lack of representation and voice: People with disabilities may feel underrepresented or unheard in their communities and workplaces, leading to a lack of understanding and support for their needs.
7. Stigma and discrimination: Disability-related stigma and discrimination can lead to feelings of shame, low self-esteem, and mental health problems.
8. Limited access to education and training: Depending on the nature of their disability, individuals may face barriers to accessing education and training programs, limiting their career advancement opportunities.
9. Difficulty with daily living tasks: Disabilities can make everyday tasks such as bathing, dressing, and grooming more challenging, leading to feelings of frustration and helplessness.
10. Impact on mental health: The emotional and psychological impacts of disability can be significant, leading to depression, anxiety, and other mental health problems.
