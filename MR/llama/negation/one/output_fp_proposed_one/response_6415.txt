Introduction:
Political affiliation can have a significant impact on the lives of beginner Chinese speakers, particularly those who are non-mentally disabled. In China, the government's stance on political issues can shape the language learning environment and the experiences of learners. This study aims to investigate the impacts of political affiliation on the lives of beginner Chinese speaker non-mentally disabled people in China.

Methodology:
This study will use a qualitative research approach, conducting interviews with 20 beginner Chinese speakers who are non-mentally disabled and have different political affiliations. The participants will be recruited through snowball sampling, selecting individuals who are interested in sharing their experiences and perspectives on this topic. The interviews will be recorded, transcribed, and analyzed using thematic analysis to identify patterns and themes related to the impact of political affiliation on the learners' lives.

Significance:
This study will contribute to the existing literature on language learning motivation and identity by exploring the impacts of political affiliation on beginner Chinese speakers in China. The findings will provide insights into how political beliefs can shape the experiences and motivations of language learners, and how these factors can influence their language learning outcomes.

Potential Outcomes:
The study may reveal several potential outcomes related to the impact of political affiliation on beginner Chinese speakers. These include:

1. Language learning motivation: The participants' political beliefs may affect their motivation to learn Chinese, with individuals who share the same political views as the government being more motivated to learn the language.
2. Identity formation: The learners' political affiliation may influence their identity formation as a language learner, with individuals who identify with a particular political ideology being more likely to adopt linguistic and cultural practices that align with their beliefs.
3. Language learning experience: The participants' political beliefs may shape their language learning experiences, such as the types of language learning materials they use or the ways in which they engage with other learners.
