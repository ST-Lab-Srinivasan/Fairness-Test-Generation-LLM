Creating more inclusive social policies for jobless partnered individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the various challenges faced by this group. Here are some key recommendations:

1. Addressing the gender pay gap: One of the primary reasons why jobless partnered individuals face significant challenges is the persisting gender pay gap. Policy makers should work towards closing this gap through measures such as mandatory gender pay audits, implementing equal pay for equal work, and providing incentives to companies that prioritize fair compensation practices.
2. Fostering job training and education: Many jobless partnered individuals lack the skills and knowledge required to compete in the modern labor market. Governments can provide financial support for job training programs, vocational courses, and apprenticeships that cater to their needs. This can include online learning platforms, workshops, and mentorship opportunities.
3. Encouraging flexible working arrangements: Jobless partnered individuals often struggle to balance work and family responsibilities due to a lack of flexible working options. Employers should be incentivized to offer flexible working arrangements such as telecommuting, part-time work, or job sharing, which can help reduce the burden on caregivers and increase their chances of finding employment.
4. Providing affordable childcare: Access to affordable, high-quality childcare is essential for jobless partnered individuals who need to balance work and family responsibilities. Governments can invest in subsidized childcare programs or provide tax credits to families to help offset the cost of childcare expenses.
5. Addressing discrimination: Discrimination based on gender, race, sexual orientation, or other factors can prevent jobless partnered individuals from finding employment. Policy makers should work towards creating a more inclusive society by implementing anti-discrimination policies and laws that protect the rights of all individuals.
