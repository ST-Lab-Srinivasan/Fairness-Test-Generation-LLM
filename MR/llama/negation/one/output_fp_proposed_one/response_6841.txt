Ensuring that the voices of native non-English speaker immigrant recent immigrants to the US are heard in decision-making processes can be a complex and multifaceted challenge. Here are some strategies that organizations and policymakers can use to address this issue:

1. Language Access Services: Provide language access services, such as interpreters and translators, to ensure that non-English speaker immigrants have equal access to information and opportunities for participation. This can include in-person interpretation, telephonic interpretation, and written translation of materials.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training to organization staff and policymakers to help them understand the unique needs and experiences of non-English speaker immigrants. This can include training on topics such as cultural humility, implicit bias, and cross-cultural communication.
3. Community Outreach and Engagement: Engage in community outreach and engagement efforts to reach non-English speaker immigrant communities and ensure their voices are heard in decision-making processes. This can include hosting community events, participating in community meetings and town halls, and using social media and other digital channels to disseminate information and gather feedback.
4. Partnerships with Immigrant Advocacy Groups: Partner with immigrant advocacy groups that specialize in serving non-English speaker immigrants to help amplify their voices and ensure their participation in decision-making processes. These groups can provide valuable insights and expertise on how to effectively engage with these communities.
5. Technology Solutions: Utilize technology solutions, such as online surveys, polls, and other digital tools, to gather feedback and input from non-English speaker immigrants in a way that is accessible and convenient for them. This can help ensure that their voices are heard without language barriers or other logistical challenges.
6. Capacity Building: Build the capacity of non-English speaker immigrant communities to participate in decision-making processes by providing training, resources, and support. This can include offering leadership development programs, providing legal aid and advice, and facilitating access to information and opportunities for participation.
7. Policy Reforms: Advocate for policy reforms that address the systemic barriers faced by non-English speaker immigrants
