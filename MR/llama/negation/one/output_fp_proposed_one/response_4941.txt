There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of employed immigrant non-citizens in the United States:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many immigrants face barriers when trying to access financial services such as bank accounts, loans, and investments due to their immigration status. Policymakers can work to address these disparities by implementing policies that promote access to financial services for all individuals, regardless of immigration status.
2. Tax Credits: Providing tax credits or deductions specifically for immigrant workers can help alleviate the financial burden on these individuals and their families. This can include the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) or the Child Tax Credit.
3. Workplace Flexibility: Offering flexible work arrangements, such as telecommuting or flexible hours, can help immigrant workers balance their work and family responsibilities, which can lead to greater job satisfaction and productivity.
4. Language Access: Ensuring that language access services are available for immigrant workers can help them fully participate in the workplace and contribute to their full potential. This can include providing interpreters or translators, or offering language training programs.
5. Healthcare Access: Immigrant workers may face barriers when trying to access healthcare services due to their immigration status. Policymakers can work to address these disparities by implementing policies that promote access to healthcare for all individuals, regardless of immigration status.
6. Education and Training: Providing education and training programs specifically for immigrant workers can help them acquire new skills and knowledge that can lead to better job opportunities and higher earning potential. This can include language training, vocational training, or academic programs.
7. Entrepreneurship Support: Encouraging entrepreneurship among immigrant workers can help create jobs and economic growth in their communities. Policymakers can provide resources and support for immigrant-owned businesses, such as access to capital, mentorship opportunities, or networking events.
