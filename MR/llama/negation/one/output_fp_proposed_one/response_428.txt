Supporting the community engagement of Native recent immigrants to the US, especially those who are secular and non-Jewish, requires a thoughtful and inclusive approach. Here are some strategies that organizations and communities can adopt:

1. Cultural orientation programs: Provide cultural orientation programs that help new immigrants understand the social norms, values, and customs of their adopted community. This can include information on local laws, regulations, and cultural practices.
2. Language classes: Offer language classes in the local dialect to help immigrants improve their communication skills and connect with the community.
3. Community events: Organize community events that celebrate diverse cultures and provide opportunities for immigrants to meet and interact with each other. This can include cultural festivals, concerts, and art exhibitions.
4. Mentorship programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair recent immigrants with experienced community members who can provide guidance on navigating the local culture and system.
5. Advocacy and support groups: Establish advocacy and support groups for immigrants, where they can share their experiences, seek advice, and find emotional support.
6. Education and job training programs: Provide access to education and job training programs that help immigrants acquire new skills and improve their employability in the local job market.
7. Health services: Ensure access to quality healthcare services for immigrants, including language-appropriate medical information and cultural sensitivity in healthcare provision.
8. Civic engagement opportunities: Encourage immigrants to participate in civic activities, such as voting, volunteering, and community service, to help them feel more connected to their adopted community.
9. Intercultural dialogue and communication: Facilitate intercultural dialogue and communication between immigrant communities and the wider society, through events, workshops, and media initiatives.
