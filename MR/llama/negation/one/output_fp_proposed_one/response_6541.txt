Supporting the civic engagement of non-moderate immigrant recent immigrants to the US is crucial for their integration and participation in the political process. Here are some ways to support their civic engagement:

1. Language Access: Provide language access services such as interpreters, translators, and bilingual poll workers to ensure that non-English speaking immigrants can fully participate in the electoral process.
2. Voter Registration Drives: Organize voter registration drives specifically targeting immigrant communities. This can be done through partnerships with local organizations, community centers, and places of worship.
3. Civic Education: Offer civic education programs that provide information on the voting process, candidates' positions, and the importance of voting. This can be done through workshops, classes, or online resources.
4. Voting Rights Advocacy: Advocate for policies that protect the voting rights of immigrants, such as automatic voter registration, same-day registration, and the expansion of early voting.
5. Building Community: Foster a sense of community among immigrant groups by organizing cultural events, festivals, and other activities that promote civic engagement and participation.
6. Partnerships with Elected Officials: Build partnerships with elected officials to ensure that the needs and concerns of immigrant communities are represented at the local and national levels.
7. Advocacy for Immigration Reform: Advocate for comprehensive immigration reform that addresses the underlying issues that prevent immigrants from fully participating in the electoral process, such as citizenship pathways and documentation status.
8. Vote by Mail: Encourage non-English speaking immigrants to vote by mail to ensure their voices are heard without facing language barriers at polling stations.
9. Online Resources: Provide online resources and tools that provide information on voting procedures, candidate positions, and issues relevant to immigrant communities.
10. Youth Engagement: Engage youth within immigrant communities by providing opportunities for them to become involved in civic activities, such as volunteering at polling stations or participating in voter registration drives.
11. Community Organizing: Support community organizing efforts that focus on building a more inclusive and equitable society
