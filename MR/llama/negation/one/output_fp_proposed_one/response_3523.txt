3. Advocate for access to care: Individuals with non-known genetic conditions may face barriers in accessing appropriate medical care, such as lack of availability or affordability. Advocate for policies that improve access to care, including financial assistance programs, expanded insurance coverage, and increased funding for research and treatment.

5. Support research and development: Encourage investment in research and development for treatments and diagnostic tools that can help individuals with non-known genetic conditions. This can include funding for basic science research, clinical trials, and the development of new technologies.
6. Create support networks: Establish support groups or online communities where individuals with non-known genetic conditions can connect with each other, share experiences, and find emotional support. These networks can also serve as a platform for advocacy efforts.
