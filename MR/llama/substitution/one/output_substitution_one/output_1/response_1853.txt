2. Limited Networking Opportunities: Reservists may not have the same opportunities to network and build professional connections as their civilian counterparts, which can limit their access to job opportunities.
3. Different Skills and Knowledge: Military training and experience may not always translate directly to civilian jobs, so reservists may need to acquire new skills or knowledge to be competitive in the job market.

5. Unpredictable Work Schedule: Reservists may have unpredictable work schedules due to their military obligations, which can be challenging for employers who require a consistent work schedule.
