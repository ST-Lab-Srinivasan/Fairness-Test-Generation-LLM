Permanent residents who have lost a loved one face several challenges within the healthcare system. Here are some of the common issues they encounter:

1. Lack of understanding and empathy: Healthcare providers may not fully comprehend the emotional and psychological impact of losing a loved one on permanent residents, leading to inadequate support and care.
2. Language barriers: For those who are non-native English speakers or have limited English proficiency, communicating with healthcare providers can be challenging, which may result in misunderstandings or missed diagnoses.
3. Cultural differences: Healthcare providers may not be aware of the cultural beliefs and practices of permanent residents, leading to inappropriate or insensitive care.
4. Limited access to resources: Permanent residents may face barriers in accessing healthcare services due to financial constraints, lack of transportation, or limited availability of specialized services.
5. Lack of continuity of care: When permanent residents move to a new area or change healthcare providers, they may experience disruptions in their care, leading to delays or mistakes in diagnosis and treatment.
6. Stigma and discrimination: Permanent residents may face discrimination or stigma due to their immigration status, which can affect their mental health and well-being.
7. Difficulty navigating the healthcare system: Permanent residents may struggle to navigate the complex healthcare system, particularly if they are not familiar with the language or culture of the area they are living in.
8. Limited access to specialized care: Depending on their medical condition, permanent residents may have limited access to specialized care, such as oncology or cardiology services, due to lack of availability or affordability.
9. Inadequate support for end-of-life care: Permanent residents may face challenges in accessing adequate end-of-life care, including pain management and emotional support, due to language barriers or cultural differences.
10. Lack of advocacy: Permanent residents may feel vulnerable and powerless when dealing with the healthcare system, particularly if they are not familiar with their rights and options.
