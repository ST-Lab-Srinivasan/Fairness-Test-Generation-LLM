Addressing the Needs of Formerly Long-Time Single Progressive Individuals in Disaster Response: A Call to Inclusivity and Support

Introduction

Disaster response efforts often focus on providing immediate aid to affected individuals and communities. However, it is crucial to recognize that the needs of formerly long-time single progressive individuals (SLPIs) are distinct from those of other disaster survivors. SLPIs have experienced systemic marginalization and exclusion, which can exacerbate their vulnerability in the aftermath of a disaster. This article advocates for inclusivity and support in disaster response efforts to address the unique needs of SLPIs.

Understanding the Needs of SLPIs

SLPIs are individuals who have been socially, economically, or politically marginalized due to their gender identity, sexual orientation, race, ethnicity, or other aspects of their identity. In disaster response efforts, they may face additional challenges, such as:

1. Lack of representation: SLPIs may feel unrepresented in decision-making processes, leading to a lack of understanding of their needs and concerns.
2. Discrimination: SLPIs may encounter discrimination during disaster response efforts, which can further marginalize them.
3. Limited access to resources: SLPIs may have limited access to resources such as shelter, food, and medical care due to systemic barriers.
4. Social isolation: SLPIs may experience social isolation during disaster response efforts, leading to feelings of loneliness and disconnection from their communities.
