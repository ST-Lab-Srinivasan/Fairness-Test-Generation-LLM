There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US citizens living in poverty:

1. Access to education and job training: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market and improve their economic situation. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and certification programs.
2. Entrepreneurship and small business development: Encouraging entrepreneurship and supporting small business development can help individuals build wealth and create jobs in their communities. This can involve providing access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities.
3. Job placement and career advancement: Working with local employers to identify job openings and provide training and support to help individuals secure and advance in those positions.
4. Financial literacy and capability-building: Teaching individuals about personal finance, budgeting, and money management can help them make informed decisions about their financial resources and achieve long-term financial stability.
5. Housing and homeownership support: Providing support for affordable housing options and homeownership programs can help individuals build wealth through homeownership and improve their overall quality of life.
6. Healthcare access and mental health support: Ensuring access to affordable, high-quality healthcare and mental health services can help individuals address health disparities and improve their overall well-being.
7. Community development and infrastructure investment: Investing in community development and infrastructure can help create jobs, improve quality of life, and attract new businesses and investment to the area.
8. Cultural preservation and revitalization: Supporting the preservation and revitalization of Native American cultures can help individuals connect with their heritage and identity, which can have positive impacts on mental health and overall well-being.
9. Legal support and advocacy: Providing legal support and advocacy services can help individuals navigate the legal system and address issues related to land rights, resource extraction, and other social and economic justice concerns.
10. Collaboration and partnership-building: Building partnerships with local organizations, government agencies, and businesses can help leverage resources and expertise to support economic empowerment efforts in Native American communities.
