3. Offer language training: Providing language training or resources for beginner Spanish speakers can help them improve their communication skills and feel more confident in participating in decision-making processes.

6. Provide mentorship opportunities: Pairing beginner Spanish speakers with more experienced individuals can help them build confidence and develop the skills they need to participate in decision-making processes.
7. Encourage self-advocacy: Encourage beginner Spanish speakers to advocate for themselves and their ideas. This can involve teaching them how to effectively communicate their thoughts and opinions, and providing support and resources to help them do so.
8. Celebrate diversity: Celebrating the diversity of backgrounds and perspectives can help create a more inclusive environment where beginner Spanish speakers feel valued and heard.
