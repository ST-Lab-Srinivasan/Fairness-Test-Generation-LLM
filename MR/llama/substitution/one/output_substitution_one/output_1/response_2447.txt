Documented not recently single (DNRS) individuals face a range of challenges in the healthcare system, including:

1. Lack of recognition and support: DNRS individuals may face stigma and lack of recognition from healthcare providers, who may assume that they are no longer in relationships or do not have emotional support.
2. Limited access to care: DNRS individuals may face barriers to accessing healthcare services, including lack of transportation, childcare, or financial resources.
3. Inadequate care coordination: DNRS individuals may experience gaps in care due to a lack of communication and coordination between healthcare providers, leading to missed diagnoses or inappropriate treatments.
4. Limited access to mental health services: DNRS individuals may face barriers to accessing mental health services, including stigma, lack of knowledge about available resources, or limited availability of services in their area.
5. Lack of cultural competency: DNRS individuals may experience cultural barriers in accessing healthcare services, including language barriers, religious or cultural beliefs that may affect their willingness to seek care, or a lack of understanding of their cultural practices and values among healthcare providers.
6. Limited access to reproductive health services: DNRS individuals may face barriers to accessing reproductive health services, including lack of knowledge about available resources, limited availability of services in their area, or stigma related to their marital status.
7. Inadequate care for chronic conditions: DNRS individuals may experience gaps in care for chronic conditions due to a lack of coordination between healthcare providers or limited availability of specialized care.
8. Limited access to palliative care: DNRS individuals may face barriers to accessing palliative care, including lack of knowledge about available resources, limited availability of services in their area, or stigma related to their marital status.
