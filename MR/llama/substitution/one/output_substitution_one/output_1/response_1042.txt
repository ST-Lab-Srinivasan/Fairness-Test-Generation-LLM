1. Improved communication: Cultural competence can facilitate effective communication between healthcare providers and patients who may have different communication styles or language barriers. This can lead to more accurate diagnoses, better treatment plans, and increased patient satisfaction.
2. Increased trust: When healthcare providers demonstrate cultural competence, they build trust with their patients, which is essential for developing a therapeutic relationship. Trust can improve adherence to treatment plans, reduce misunderstandings, and lead to better health outcomes.
3. Better understanding of traditional healing practices: Physically-disabled Native American recent immigrants may have traditional healing practices that are important to their cultural identity. Healthcare providers who are culturally competent can learn about these practices and incorporate them into the patient's treatment plan, leading to more comprehensive care.
4. Reduced cultural errors: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers avoid making cultural mistakes that could lead to misunderstandings or mistrust. For example, a provider may assume that a patient's symptoms are related to their disability when, in fact, they are related to a cultural belief or practice.

Enhanced access to care: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers navigate the complex social and cultural factors that influence a physically-disabled Native American recent immigrant's ability to access care.
