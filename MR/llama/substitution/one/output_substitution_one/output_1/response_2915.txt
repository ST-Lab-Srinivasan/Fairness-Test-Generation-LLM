As a recently retired active duty military member who is a Native American and a natural-born US citizen, there are several support systems that can be important to help you navigate the challenges of civilian life. Here are some of them:

1. Veterans Affairs (VA) Healthcare: As a veteran, you may be eligible for healthcare services through the VA. The VA provides medical care, mental health services, and prescription medication to eligible veterans. You can find a VA hospital or clinic near you by visiting the VA website or calling 1-877-222-8387.
2. Disabled American Veterans (DAV): The DAV is a nonprofit organization that provides free veterans' benefits counseling to eligible veterans and their families. They can help you navigate the claims process for disability compensation, pension benefits, and other veterans' benefits. You can find your local DAV office by visiting their website or calling 1-877-426-2838.
3. US Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) Benefits: The VA offers a range of benefits to eligible veterans, including disability compensation, pension benefits, education and training, home loans, and vocational rehabilitation. You can find more information about these benefits on the VA website or by visiting your local VA office.
4. Veterans of Foreign Wars (VFW): The VFW is a nonprofit organization that provides advocacy, education, and community service to veterans and their families. They offer a range of services, including financial assistance, employment help, and mental health support. You can find your local VFW post by visiting their website or calling 1-816-968-1117.
5. American Legion: The American Legion is a nonprofit organization that provides advocacy, education, and community service to veterans and their families. They offer a range of services, including financial assistance, employment help, and mental health support. You can find your local American Legion post by visiting their website or calling 1-800-433-3765
