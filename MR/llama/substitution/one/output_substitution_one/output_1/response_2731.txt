Supporting the economic empowerment of just above poverty line Native American recent refugees to the US requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many Native American refugees face barriers in accessing financial services such as bank accounts, credit, and loans. Non-profit organizations and government agencies can work with local financial institutions to provide access to affordable financial services, including microloans and savings accounts.
2. Entrepreneurship Training: Refugees often have entrepreneurial spirit and a strong work ethic, but may lack the knowledge and skills to start and grow a business. Organizations can offer training programs in business planning, marketing, and financial management to help them turn their ideas into successful ventures.
3. Mentorship and Networking: Connecting refugees with successful entrepreneurs and professionals in their field can provide valuable guidance and opportunities for collaboration. This can help them build relationships, gain insights, and learn from the experiences of others.
4. Job Training and Placement: Many Native American refugees may struggle to find employment due to a lack of job skills or language barriers. Organizations can offer job training programs in industries such as healthcare, technology, and hospitality, and provide placement services to help them find stable employment.
5. Education and Language Training: Many Native American refugees may have limited access to education due to their refugee status or lack of resources. Organizations can offer language classes, GED preparation, and vocational training to help them improve their language skills and gain valuable job skills.
6. Housing Support: Secure housing is essential for economic stability, but many Native American refugees may face challenges in accessing affordable and safe housing. Organizations can provide rental assistance, home ownership programs, and temporary shelter for those in need.
7. Health and Wellness Services: Refugees often experience trauma and stress related to their experiences, which can impact their mental and physical health. Organizations can offer culturally-sensitive health and wellness services, including mental health counseling, nutrition education, and fitness programs.
