Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner Spanish speakers, including native recent refugees to the US, requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training on cultural competency for program staff and volunteers to help them understand and appreciate the diverse backgrounds and experiences of the participants. This can include topics such as cultural norms, customs, and values.
2. Accessible Language Instruction: Offer language instruction that is tailored to the needs and levels of beginner Spanish speakers. Use simple language and visual aids to help learners understand and retain new vocabulary and grammar concepts.
3. Inclusive Activities: Develop cultural programs that include a variety of activities that cater to different interests and abilities. For example, include arts and crafts projects, music and dance classes, and team-building exercises that promote collaboration and mutual respect.
4. Intergenerational Programming: Incorporate intergenerational programming that brings together individuals of different age groups and backgrounds. This can help foster a sense of community and cultural exchange, as well as provide opportunities for mentorship and knowledge sharing.
5. Cultural Field Trips: Organize field trips to cultural events and attractions that are relevant to the participants' experiences and interests. For example, visit local museums, festivals, or cultural centers that showcase the diversity of the US immigrant community.
6. Community Building: Create opportunities for participants to connect with one another and build relationships. This can be done through social events, group outings, or volunteer activities that encourage collaboration and mutual support.
7. Responsive Programming: Be responsive to the needs and preferences of the participants. Collect feedback and make adjustments to programs accordingly. For example, if a participant prefers a particular type of music or dance, incorporate those elements into the program.
8. Partnerships with Community Organizations: Collaborate with community organizations that serve immigrant populations. These partnerships can provide valuable resources, expertise, and networks for program development and implementation.
