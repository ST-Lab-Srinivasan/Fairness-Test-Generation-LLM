Creating more inclusive housing policies for young veterans, specifically those who are 18-19 years old, requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive housing policies:

1. Acknowledge the Unique Needs of Young Veterans: Young veterans face distinct challenges compared to older veterans, such as navigating civilian life, managing mental health issues, and finding employment. Policymakers must recognize these differences and tailor their policies accordingly.
2. Provide Affordable Housing Options: Many young veterans struggle to find affordable housing, especially in high-cost areas. Policymakers can create incentives for developers to build more affordable housing units or provide subsidies for veterans to help them access existing affordable housing options.
3. Offer Supportive Housing Programs: Supportive housing programs provide on-site social services, such as mental health counseling, job training, and childcare, to help young veterans successfully transition into civilian life. Policymakers can invest in these programs to better support young veterans.
4. Encourage Community Involvement: Young veterans often feel disconnected from their communities, which can exacerbate mental health issues. Policymakers can encourage community involvement by creating opportunities for young veterans to participate in local events, volunteer, or join community organizations.
5. Provide Access to Education and Training: Many young veterans lack the skills and knowledge needed to succeed in civilian life. Policymakers can provide access to education and training programs, such as vocational training, apprenticeships, or college courses, to help them acquire new skills and gain confidence.
6. Offer Financial Assistance: Young veterans may struggle with debt from student loans or credit card debt. Policymakers can provide financial assistance through scholarships, grants, or other forms of aid to help young veterans manage their finances and plan for the future.
