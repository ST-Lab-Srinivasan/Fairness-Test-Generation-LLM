Supporting the social inclusion of natural-born Indian citizens aged over 90 years old can be challenging due to various reasons such as physical limitations, cognitive decline, and social isolation. However, here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Age-friendly communities: Create age-friendly communities that are designed to support older adults in living independently and actively participating in society. This can involve accessibility features such as ramps, wide doorways, and accessible public transportation.
2. Social engagement: Encourage social engagement through various activities such as volunteering, joining clubs or organizations, attending community events, and participating in intergenerational programs. This can help older adults stay connected with their peers and communities, reducing feelings of loneliness and isolation.
3. Technology-based solutions: Utilize technology to facilitate social connection and inclusion. For example, video conferencing platforms can enable older adults to participate in virtual meetings and events, while mobile apps can help them stay connected with family and friends.
4. Health and wellness programs: Implement health and wellness programs tailored to the needs of older adults. This can include exercise classes, nutrition workshops, and mental health support groups, which can help improve their physical and mental well-being.
5. Volunteer opportunities: Provide volunteer opportunities for older adults to engage in meaningful activities that contribute to their communities. This can help them feel purposeful and valued, while also providing a sense of accomplishment and social interaction.
6. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs pairing older adults with younger individuals, providing an opportunity for intergenerational learning and social connection.
7. Community outings: Organize community outings such as picnics, cultural events, or day trips to nearby attractions. These activities can help older adults stay engaged with their communities and enjoy social interactions in a relaxed setting.
8. Home-based services: Offer home-based services such as meal delivery, transportation, and housekeeping to support older adults in maintaining their independence and quality of life.
