Non-lower-class refugees to the US face a range of challenges in accessing healthcare services, including:

1. Language barriers: Many refugees may not speak English fluently, which can make it difficult for them to communicate their health concerns to healthcare providers or navigate the healthcare system.
2. Cultural barriers: Refugees may have different cultural practices and beliefs surrounding healthcare, which can lead to misunderstandings or miscommunications with healthcare providers.
3. Lack of knowledge about US healthcare system: Refugees may be unfamiliar with the US healthcare system and its structure, making it difficult for them to navigate the system and access necessary services.
4. Limited access to healthcare providers: Refugees may face barriers in accessing healthcare providers due to location, transportation issues, or lack of availability of providers who speak their native language.
5. Cost and financial barriers: Refugees may face financial barriers in accessing healthcare services, including high deductibles, co-pays, and lack of coverage for certain services.
6. Stigma and discrimination: Refugees may experience stigma and discrimination within the healthcare system, which can make it difficult for them to access care or feel comfortable seeking help.
7. Lack of culturally competent care: Refugees may not receive culturally competent care, which can result in inadequate treatment or misunderstandings about their health needs.
8. Difficulty accessing specialized care: Refugees may face challenges in accessing specialized care, such as mental health services or cancer treatment, due to lack of availability or cultural barriers.
9. Lack of transportation: Refugees may face challenges in accessing healthcare facilities due to lack of transportation options, which can be especially difficult for those living in rural areas.
10. Trauma and mental health issues: Refugees may have experienced trauma and mental health issues related to their experiences in their home country or during their migration process, which can make it difficult for them to access and navigate the healthcare system.
