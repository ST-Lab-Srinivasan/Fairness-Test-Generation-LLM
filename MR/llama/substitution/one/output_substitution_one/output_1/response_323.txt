2. Identity confusion: Mixed-race individuals may struggle with their identity and sense of belonging, which can affect their mental and emotional well-being. They may feel caught between different cultures or communities, leading to feelings of isolation or insecurity.

Limited access to healthcare resources: Depending on where they live, mixed-race individuals may have limited access to healthcare resources, such as specialists, medical equipment, or prescription medications. 4. Language barriers: Mixed-race individuals who speak multiple languages may face language barriers when communicating with healthcare providers. This can result in misunderstandings or missed diagnoses.
5. Stigma and shame: Mixed-race individuals may internalize negative stereotypes and stigma associated with their racial identity, leading to feelings of shame or low self-esteem. This can affect their willingness to seek medical care or disclose personal health information.
6. Difficulty navigating multiple healthcare systems: Depending on their ancestry, mixed-race individuals may have access to different healthcare systems, such as the Veterans Administration (VA) or Indian Health Service (IHS).
