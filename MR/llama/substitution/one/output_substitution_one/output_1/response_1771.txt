There are many successful community initiatives led by Native American natural-born US citizens. Here are a few examples:

1. The Native American Community Clinic (NACC) in Los Angeles, California: Founded by Dr. Loretta Kristof, a Cherokee Nation citizen and USC alumna, the NACC provides comprehensive healthcare services to the underserved Native American population in Los Angeles.
2. The Indigenous Food and Agriculture Initiative (IfAI) at the University of Arkansas: Led by Dr. J.P. Bartlett, a member of the Cherokee Nation, IfAI works to promote food sovereignty and sustainable agriculture practices among Native American communities.
3. The Native American Cultural Exchange Program (NACEP): Founded by Dr. Twila Loarie, a citizen of the Pueblo of Acoma, NACEP provides cultural exchange opportunities for Native American youth to learn about their heritage and connect with other Indigenous cultures around the world.
4. The Indigenous Environmental Network (IEN): Led by a diverse group of Indigenous leaders from across North America, IEN works to protect the environmental rights and self-determination of Indigenous peoples.
5. The National Congress of American Indians (NCAI): As the oldest and largest Native American organization in the country, NCAI advocates for the rights and interests of Native Americans at the federal, state, and local levels. Founded by a group of tribal leaders in 1944, NCAI has played a crucial role in shaping US policy affecting Indigenous peoples.
6. The Native American Land Conservancy (NALC): Founded by Dr. Mark H.D. Covington, a citizen of the Cherokee Nation, NALC works to protect and preserve culturally significant lands for future generations of Native Americans.
7. The Indigenous Language Revitalization Program at the University of Alaska Fairbanks: Led by Dr. Rosemary C. Salamanca, a citizen of the Tlingit Tribe, this program works to revitalize and document endangered Indigenous languages spoken in Alaska.
