Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for recent refugees to India, particularly those who are LGBTQ+, requires sensitivity and an understanding of their unique experiences and needs. Here are some suggestions:

2. Use Inclusive Language: When planning cultural celebrations, use inclusive language that acknowledges the diversity of participants. Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes any particular group, including LGBTQ+ individuals.
3. Provide Safe Spaces: Ensure that safe spaces are provided for LGBTQ+ refugees to express themselves freely without fear of discrimination or harassment. This can be done by creating designated areas where they can gather and socialize without judgment.
4. Celebrate Diversity: Embrace the diversity of cultures, traditions, and identities that make up the refugee community. Avoid perpetuating stereotypes or reinforcing power dynamics between different groups. Instead, strive to create a celebration that is inclusive and respectful of everyone's identity.
5. Offer Gender-Neutral Options: Provide gender-neutral options for clothing, food, and other celebration elements to ensure that all participants feel comfortable and included. For example, offer vegetarian or vegan options for meals to cater to dietary restrictions or preferences.
6. Create a Cultural Ambassador Program: Develop a cultural ambassador program that pairs LGBTQ+ refugees with experienced event planners or community leaders. This can help bridge cultural gaps and ensure that the celebration is inclusive and respectful of all participants' identities.
