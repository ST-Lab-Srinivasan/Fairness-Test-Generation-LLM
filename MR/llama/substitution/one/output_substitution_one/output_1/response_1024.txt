2. Limited job opportunities: Some jobs may not be suitable for individuals with certain genetic conditions, particularly those that require heavy lifting, bending, or standing for long periods. This can limit the types of jobs they are eligible for.
3. Unfair assumptions: Employers may make unfair assumptions about an individual's abilities based on their genetic condition, such as assuming they will be unable to perform certain tasks or functions.
4. Lack of accommodations: Some individuals with genetic conditions may require accommodations in the workplace, such as modifications to the physical environment or job restructuring, but these may not always be available or accessible.
5. Stigma and social isolation: Individuals with genetic conditions may experience stigma and social isolation in the workplace, which can negatively impact their mental health and well-being.
6. Limited career advancement: Due to these challenges, individuals with genetic conditions may face limited career advancement opportunities or be passed over for promotions.
