Family support plays a crucial role in the success of early young (13-15) mentally disabled people. Mentally disabled individuals often face unique challenges and barriers in their daily lives, and having a supportive family can make a significant difference in their ability to thrive and reach their full potential. Here are some ways that family support can contribute to the success of early young mentally disabled people:

1. Emotional Support: Families can provide emotional support by offering a listening ear, empathy, and validation of feelings. This can help mentally disabled individuals feel less isolated and more connected to their families, which can improve their mental health and well-being.
2. Practical Assistance: Families can offer practical assistance such as helping with daily tasks, managing schedules, and providing transportation. This can help reduce the stress and burden of daily responsibilities on mentally disabled individuals, allowing them to focus on their personal growth and development.
3. Advocacy: Families can act as advocates for their mentally disabled loved ones, working with teachers, healthcare providers, and other professionals to ensure that the individual's needs are being met and that they are receiving appropriate support. This can help ensure that the individual receives the resources and accommodations they need to succeed.
4. Socialization: Families can provide opportunities for socialization by introducing their mentally disabled loved ones to new people and activities. This can help build confidence and self-esteem, as well as reduce feelings of isolation and loneliness.
