Addressing the Needs of Non-Traditional Families in Disaster Response: A Call to Inclusivity

Introduction:
Disasters have a way of exposing the weaknesses and inadequacies of our systems, including those designed to respond to them. One such group that is often overlooked in disaster response are non-traditional families, including those composed of individuals who are not lifelong married couples. This group faces unique challenges during disasters, ranging from discrimination and stigma to practical difficulties in accessing aid. In this article, we explore the needs of non-traditional families during disaster response and propose strategies for addressing them.

Understanding Non-Traditional Families:
Non-traditional families are those that do not conform to traditional societal norms of marriage and family structure. This includes single parents, blended families, LGBTQ+ families, and other non-heteronormative configurations. These families may face unique challenges during disaster response due to factors such as:

1. Social stigma: Non-traditional families may face discrimination and social stigma during disaster response, leading to feelings of isolation and marginalization.
2. Practical difficulties: Non-traditional families may encounter practical difficulties in accessing aid and services due to bureaucratic obstacles or lack of inclusive policies.
3. Lack of representation: Non-traditional families may be underrepresented in disaster response efforts, leading to a lack of understanding and support for their specific needs.
