Promoting the cultural preservation of older adults, particularly those who are over 90 years old and considered "extreme elderly," requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement and social interaction among older adults through events, activities, and volunteer opportunities. This can help combat loneliness and isolation, which are common issues among the elderly.
2. Intergenerational Programs: Organize intergenerational programs that bring together older adults with younger generations, such as school children or young adults. These programs can foster mutual understanding, respect, and learning between different age groups.
3. Cultural Heritage Preservation: Support the preservation of cultural heritage and traditions among older adults by documenting their stories, experiences, and traditional practices. This can be done through oral histories, storytelling sessions, and cultural events that celebrate their heritage.
4. Access to Technology: Provide access to technology, such as computers, tablets, and smartphones, to help older adults stay connected with family, friends, and the world around them. This can also provide opportunities for learning and engagement through online resources and services.
5. Health and Wellness Programs: Offer health and wellness programs that cater specifically to the needs of older adults, such as exercise classes, nutrition workshops, and mental health support. These programs can help improve physical and mental health outcomes among this population.
6. Age-Friendly Environments: Advocate for age-friendly environments that are designed to support the needs of older adults. This includes accessibility features such as ramps, elevators, and accessible parking, as well as community spaces that are easy to navigate and use.
7. Volunteer Opportunities: Provide volunteer opportunities for older adults to engage in meaningful activities that contribute to their sense of purpose and well-being. This can include volunteering at local organizations, participating in community events, or mentoring younger generations.
