Creating more inclusive cultural programs for secular Hindus in lower middle-income communities requires a thoughtful and multi-faceted approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Community Outreach: Reach out to the local community through various channels such as social media, flyers, posters, and word of mouth. Make sure to involve local leaders, community organizations, and cultural groups in the planning process.
2. Accessible Venues: Choose venues that are easily accessible and affordable for lower middle-income individuals. This could include community centers, parks, or other public spaces.
3. Free or Low-Cost Events: Offer free or low-cost cultural programs to attract a diverse audience. This could include lectures, workshops, concerts, and other events that showcase Hindu culture and traditions.
4. Collaboration with Local Organizations: Partner with local organizations, such as community centers, schools, and cultural groups, to co-host cultural programs. This can help reach a wider audience and create more inclusive programs.
5. Inclusive Programming: Make sure the cultural programs are inclusive of all Hindu traditions and practices. This could include programs that highlight the diversity of Hindu culture, such as Bhakti, Shakta, and Vaishnavism.
6. Education and Awareness: Offer educational workshops and lectures that teach about Hindu culture and traditions. This can help create a more informed and appreciative audience.
7. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement by involving the audience in the cultural programs. This could include interactive performances, call-and-response sessions, and other participatory activities.
8. Technology: Utilize technology to reach a wider audience. This could include live streaming events, creating online content, and using social media platforms to promote cultural programs.
9. Collaboration with Artists: Collaborate with local artists and performers to create more inclusive cultural programs. This can help showcase the diversity of Hindu culture and traditions through music, dance, art, and other forms of expression.
