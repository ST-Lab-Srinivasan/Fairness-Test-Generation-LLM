Financially disadvantaged immigrant people face numerous challenges when accessing healthcare services. Here are some of the common obstacles they encounter:

1. Language barriers: Many immigrants may not speak the dominant language of their host country, making it difficult to communicate with healthcare providers and navigate the healthcare system.
2. Cultural differences: Immigrants may have different cultural beliefs and practices that can create barriers in accessing healthcare services. For example, some cultures may have specific beliefs about who should provide care or how care should be delivered.
3. Lack of health insurance: Many immigrants are uninsured or underinsured, which can limit their access to healthcare services. They may also face difficulties in navigating the complexities of the healthcare system without adequate financial support.
4. Documentation challenges: Immigrants may have difficulty obtaining the necessary documentation to access healthcare services, such as proof of residency or immigration status.
5. Discrimination: Some immigrants may face discrimination in accessing healthcare services based on their race, ethnicity, or national origin.
6. Limited access to specialized care: Immigrants may have limited access to specialized medical care, such as cardiology or oncology services, due to language barriers or cultural differences.
7. Difficulty in finding culturally competent providers: Immigrants may face challenges in finding healthcare providers who are knowledgeable about their culture and can provide appropriate care.
8. Lack of understanding of the healthcare system: Immigrants may have limited understanding of the healthcare system in their host country, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the system and access necessary services.
9. Cost of healthcare services: Immigrants may face financial barriers in accessing healthcare services due to high out-of-pocket costs or lack of coverage.
10. Fear of deportation: Some immigrants may be hesitant to seek medical care due to fear of being reported to immigration authorities and facing deportation.
