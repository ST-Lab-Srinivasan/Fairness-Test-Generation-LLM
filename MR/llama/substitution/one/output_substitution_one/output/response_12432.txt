Creating more inclusive cultural events for partnered active-duty individuals requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to make these events more welcoming and accessible:

1. Involve the military community: Engage with local military organizations, such as bases, units, or veterans' groups, to reach a wider audience. This will help ensure that the event is tailored to the needs and preferences of active-duty individuals and their partners.
2. Offer flexible attendance options: Consider offering the event at different times or locations to accommodate the diverse schedules of military personnel. For example, hosting the event on a weekend or providing online streaming options for those who cannot attend in person.
3. Provide childcare and family-friendly activities: Active-duty individuals may have limited access to childcare services, so include childcare options during the event. Additionally, offer activities that are enjoyable for both adults and children, such as cultural performances, art exhibitions, or interactive workshops.
4. Cater to diverse tastes: Offer a variety of food and drink options to cater to the diverse tastes and dietary restrictions of attendees. This can include vegetarian, gluten-free, and halal options, as well as refreshments that are culturally significant to different communities.
5. Promote accessibility: Ensure that the event space is wheelchair accessible and provide audio descriptions or American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation for attendees who are deaf or hard of hearing. Provide Braille or large print materials for those with visual impairments.
6. Engage with cultural organizations: Partner with local cultural organizations to co-host the event, which can provide a platform for diverse voices and perspectives. This can also help to build bridges between the military community and local civilian populations.
