There are many successful community initiatives led by beginner French speakers who are not low-income professionals. Here are a few examples:

2. Cultural Events: Beginner French speakers can organize cultural events such as film screenings, concerts, or exhibitions to promote French culture and language. These events can be a great way to meet other French learners and native speakers.
3. Language Classes for Children: Many beginner French speakers volunteer their time to teach French language classes to children in local schools or community centers. This not only helps the children learn a new language but also provides an opportunity for the beginner French speakers to practice their skills.
4. Bilingual Book Clubs: Beginner French speakers can start bilingual book clubs where they read and discuss books in both English and French. These clubs provide an opportunity to improve language skills while enjoying good company and discussing interesting literature.
5. Language and Culture Podcasts: Many beginner French speakers create podcasts that explore French language and culture. These podcasts can cover a wide range of topics, from grammar lessons to cultural insights, and provide an opportunity for listeners to engage with the language and culture.
6. Conversation Cafes: Beginner French speakers can organize conversation cafes where they meet with other learners and native speakers to practice their conversational skills in a relaxed setting. These events provide an opportunity to improve language skills while making new friends.
