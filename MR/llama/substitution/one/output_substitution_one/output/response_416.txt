Ensuring that the voices of permanent residents aged 65 and older are heard in decision-making processes can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can be implemented to promote their participation and inclusion. Here are some suggestions:

1. Create age-friendly communities: Design public spaces and services that cater to the needs of older adults. This includes accessibility features such as ramps, elevators, and wide walkways, as well as programs and activities that are tailored to their interests and abilities.
2. Provide opportunities for socialization: Older adults may feel isolated or disconnected from their communities, which can lead to feelings of loneliness and disengagement. Organize events and activities that bring them together with other older adults, such as senior centers, community clubs, or intergenerational programs.
3. Offer age-appropriate education: Many older adults may feel left behind in the rapidly changing world, particularly when it comes to technology and other areas. Provide age-appropriate educational opportunities that help them stay engaged and informed.
4. Encourage civic engagement: Older adults have a wealth of knowledge and experience that can be valuable in decision-making processes. Encourage them to participate in community events, volunteer opportunities, or advocacy campaigns that align with their interests and values.
5. Use technology to facilitate participation: Older adults may face barriers to participating in decision-making processes due to mobility or accessibility issues. Utilize technology such as video conferencing, online surveys, or mobile apps to enable them to participate more easily.
6. Appoint age-friendly representatives: Consider appointing older adults as representatives on community boards or councils, or as advocates for their rights and interests. This can help ensure that their voices are heard and their needs are represented.
7. Provide transportation options: Many older adults may face challenges in getting to community events or meetings due to mobility issues. Offer transportation options such as wheelchair-accessible vehicles or ride-sharing services to help them participate more easily.
