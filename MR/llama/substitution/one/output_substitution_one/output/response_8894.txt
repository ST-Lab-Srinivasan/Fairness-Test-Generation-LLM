As a beginner English speaker, there can be several challenges that moderate people may face in the job market. Here are some of the common challenges:

1. Language barriers: For many jobs, fluency in English is a requirement, and non-native speakers may struggle to communicate effectively in English. This can make it difficult to secure certain types of jobs or advancement opportunities.
2. Limited vocabulary and grammar: Beginner English speakers may have a limited vocabulary and may not fully understand complex grammatical structures, which can hinder their ability to express themselves effectively in written or spoken communication.
3. Cultural differences: Non-native English speakers may come from different cultural backgrounds, and these cultural differences can lead to misunderstandings or miscommunications in the workplace.
4. Stereotyping and bias: Unfortunately, many employers may hold biases against non-native English speakers, assuming that they are less competent or less committed to their work due to their language skills.
5. Difficulty with accents and pronunciation: Non-native English speakers may have difficulty with the accent and pronunciation of English words, which can make it difficult for others to understand them.
6. Lack of experience in English-speaking work environments: Beginner English speakers may not have much experience working in English-speaking environments, which can make it difficult to navigate workplace dynamics and culture.
7. Difficulty with written communication: Non-native English speakers may struggle with written communication, such as emailing or writing reports, due to differences in grammar and vocabulary usage.
8. Limited access to resources: Beginner English speakers may have limited access to resources such as language learning materials, which can hinder their ability to improve their language skills.
9. Self-doubt and low confidence: Non-native English speakers may experience self-doubt and low confidence in their language abilities, which can affect their performance in the workplace.
10. Lack of support: Beginner English speakers may not have access to adequate support or resources to help them improve their language skills, such as language classes or tutoring.
