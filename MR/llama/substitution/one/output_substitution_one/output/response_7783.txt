There are many successful community initiatives led by beginner French speakers, including non-low-income professionals. Here are a few examples:

1. Conversation Exchange Programs: Many cities have conversation exchange programs where beginners can practice their French with native speakers in a relaxed and informal setting. These programs are often free or low-cost and provide an opportunity for language learners to improve their speaking skills while making new friends.
2. Language Meetups: Meetup.com is a great resource for finding language meetups in your area. These groups usually organize events such as language exchange, movie nights, and cultural outings, providing a fun and interactive way to practice French.
3. Volunteer Work: Many organizations offer volunteer opportunities where you can use your French skills to help others. For example, you could volunteer at a local soup kitchen or community center, helping to communicate with clients who speak French as their second language.
4. Language Podcasts: There are many podcasts dedicated to teaching and learning French. Some popular ones include "Coffee Break French," "French for Fun," and "Learn French with Alexa." These podcasts offer audio and video lessons, as well as opportunities to practice speaking and listening to French.
5. Online Language Communities: Joining online language communities can be a great way to connect with other beginners and learn from each other. Some popular platforms include HelloTalk, Lingbe, and iTalki. These communities often offer language exchange, cultural insights, and support for language learners.
6. Language Books Club: Starting a language books club is a great way to practice reading and conversing in French. You can choose a book, read it together, and discuss it in French. This activity can help improve your comprehension and speaking skills.
7. French Cultural Events: Attending French cultural events such as concerts, plays, or film screenings can be a great way to practice listening and speaking skills. Many cities have French cultural organizations that offer these types of events.
