For example, individualistic native English speakers may be more likely to engage in transactions or interactions with others from different cultural backgrounds, as they value independence and self-reliance.
6. Consumer behavior: Cultural heritage can influence consumer behavior, as individuals may prefer products or services that reflect their cultural values. For instance, individualistic native English speakers may be more likely to purchase goods or services that emphasize personal freedom and autonomy.
7. Education: Cultural heritage can shape the type of education individuals pursue and the value they place on learning. For example, individualistic native English speakers may prioritize self-directed learning and independent research, which are values embedded in their cultural heritage.
