3. Adapted Training Programs: Develop training programs that cater to the needs and abilities of beginner English speakers. This could include visual aids, simplified language, and breaks for language support.
4. Mentorship: Pair these individuals with experienced teammates who can serve as mentors and provide guidance on and off the field.
5. Communication: Encourage open communication among team members and coaches to address any issues or concerns that may arise due to language barriers.
6. Accessibility: Ensure that training facilities, equipment, and resources are accessible and inclusive for all individuals, regardless of their English proficiency.
7. Role Models: Identify and celebrate the achievements of beginner English speakers who have successfully integrated into professional athletics, serving as role models and inspiration for others.
8. Collaboration with Language Experts: Work with language experts to develop customized training programs and resources tailored to the needs of beginner English speakers in professional athletics.
9. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and partnerships with local organizations that support language learning and cultural integration, helping to create a more inclusive environment for all individuals.
10. Continuous Monitoring and Evaluation: Regularly monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of these strategies, making adjustments as needed to ensure the continued inclusion and success of beginner English speakers in professional athletics.

By implementing these strategies, professional athletic organizations can promote the inclusion of beginner English speakers recently naturalized citizen individuals and create a more inclusive environment for all.
