Title: The Significance of Cultural Heritage in Shaping the Identities of Beginner French Speakers

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a vital role in shaping the identities of beginner French speakers, particularly those who are recently married. In this essay, we will explore the various ways in which cultural heritage influences the identities of these individuals and how it impacts their language learning journey.

Body Paragraph 1: Understanding Cultural Heritage
Cultural heritage refers to the shared knowledge, beliefs, values, customs, and practices of a particular group or society. It is shaped by history, religion, traditions, and social structures, and it serves as a vital part of an individual's identity (Hood, 2013). For beginner French speakers who are recently married, their cultural heritage can significantly impact their language learning journey.

Body Paragraph 2: Language Learning and Cultural Identity
Language learning is closely tied to an individual's cultural identity. When learners are exposed to a new language, they are also exposed to a new culture (Kramsch, 1993). Beginner French speakers who are recently married may find it challenging to learn the language due to the conflicting cultural values and beliefs with their own cultural heritage. For instance, they may struggle with the gendered nature of the French language, which can be different from their native language (Bogaards & Schmitt, 2017).

Body Paragraph 3: Challenges in Language Learning
Learning a new language can be a daunting task, especially when it goes against an individual's cultural identity. Beginner French speakers who are recently married may face challenges in learning the language due to the influence of their cultural heritage. For example, they may find it difficult to adopt a new way of speaking, thinking, and behaving that is different from their traditional cultural practices (Kiraly & Szabo, 2017).
