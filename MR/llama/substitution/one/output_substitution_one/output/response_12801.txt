Introduction:
Ethnic minority groups, including Asian populations, are underrepresented in genetic research and are often overlooked when it comes to understanding the impact of genetic conditions on their health. This is particularly true for elderly male individuals from these communities, who may face unique challenges due to their age and cultural background. In this report, we will explore the impacts of genetic conditions on the lives of ethnically full Asian elderly male people, highlighting the specific challenges they face and the importance of tailored approaches to address these issues.

Impacts of genetic conditions:
Genetic conditions can have a significant impact on the lives of elderly male individuals from Asian communities, particularly those with a history of limited access to healthcare resources. Some of the common genetic conditions affecting this population include:

1. Hyperlipidemia: Elderly Asian males are at a higher risk of developing hyperlipidemia, or high levels of cholesterol and triglycerides in the blood. This can increase their risk of heart disease, stroke, and other cardiovascular conditions.
2. Hypertension: Many elderly Asian males experience hypertension, which can lead to an increased risk of heart disease, stroke, and kidney disease.
3. Diabetes: Diabetes is a common condition among elderly Asian males, and it can have serious consequences if left untreated, including blindness, kidney failure, and nerve damage.
4. Osteoporosis: Asian elderly males are at a higher risk of developing osteoporosis, which can lead to bone fractures and decreased mobility.
5. Depression and anxiety: Elderly Asian males may experience depression and anxiety due to the stress of living with a genetic condition, social isolation, or cultural stigma.
