2. Limited vocabulary and grammar: Beginner English speakers may find it challenging to use complex vocabulary and grammatical structures, which can make communication difficult in professional settings.
3. Cultural differences: People from diverse cultural backgrounds may face cultural barriers when communicating with colleagues or clients from different cultures. This can lead to misunderstandings or miscommunications.
4. Fear of speaking: Many beginner English speakers may feel anxious or uncomfortable speaking in a professional setting, especially if they are not confident in their language skills. This can hinder their ability to communicate effectively and make meaningful connections with colleagues.
5. Limited exposure to business English: Beginner English speakers may not have the opportunity to practice using business English in real-world settings, which can make it difficult for them to adapt to professional communication styles.
6. Lack of feedback: Without constructive feedback, beginner English speakers may struggle to identify areas for improvement or develop their language skills effectively.
