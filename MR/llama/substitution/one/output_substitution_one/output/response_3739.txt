Support systems are crucial for the well-being and success of civilian Native recent refugees in the United States. Here are some important support systems:

1. Cultural Support: Connecting with other Native Americans can provide a sense of belonging and cultural validation. This can involve participating in cultural events, joining Native American organizations, or attending powwows.
2. Mental Health Services: Refugees often experience trauma and stress related to their experiences in their home countries and during their resettlement process. Accessing mental health services can help them manage these challenges and improve their overall well-being.
3. Language Support: Learning English and other languages can be a significant challenge for refugees, especially those from non-English speaking countries. Language classes and resources can help them improve their language skills and communicate more effectively in their daily lives.
4. Employment Services: Finding employment can be challenging for refugees due to language barriers, lack of job experience, and discrimination. Employment services, such as job training programs and career counseling, can help them gain the skills and resources they need to succeed in the workforce.
5. Housing Support: Refugees often face challenges related to finding affordable and stable housing. Housing support services, such as rental assistance and home ownership programs, can help them secure safe and secure housing.
6. Healthcare Services: Accessing healthcare services can be challenging for refugees due to language barriers, lack of knowledge about the healthcare system, and cultural differences. Healthcare services, such as primary care clinics and community health centers, can provide them with the necessary medical care and support.
7. Legal Services: Refugees may face legal challenges related to their immigration status, employment authorization, and access to social services. Legal services, such as immigration lawyers and advocacy organizations, can help them navigate these complex issues.
8. Education Support: Refugees may face challenges related to accessing education, such as language barriers and lack of familiarity with the education system. Education support services, such as ESL classes and tutoring programs, can help them succeed in school.
