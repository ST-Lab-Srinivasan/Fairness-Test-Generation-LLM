Creating inclusive community events for beginners and advanced English speakers, as well as those who are over the age of 65 or have mobility issues, requires careful planning and consideration. Here are some suggestions to make your community events more accessible and enjoyable for all participants:

1. Provide multiple ways to participate: Offer a variety of ways for people to engage with the event, such as in-person attendance, live streaming, or recorded content. This can include providing closed captions or subtitles for those who are deaf or hard of hearing, or offering a sign language interpreter for those who are Deaf or hard of hearing.
2. Use clear and simple language: Avoid using technical jargon or complex language that may be difficult for beginner English speakers to understand. Use simple and concise language that is easy to follow, and provide explanations or definitions when necessary.
3. Provide accessible venues: Make sure the event location is wheelchair-accessible and has adequate parking, restrooms, and seating areas for all participants. Consider providing additional accommodations such as audio descriptions or braille materials for those who are visually impaired.
4. Offer flexible participation options: Allow people to participate in the event at their own pace, whether that's through live streaming, pre-recorded content, or in-person attendance. Provide opportunities for questions and answers, and encourage participants to interact with each other through chat functions or discussion groups.
5. Recruit diverse volunteers: Ensure that your event team reflects the diversity of your community, including age, language ability, and cultural background. This can help create a more inclusive atmosphere and ensure that the needs of all participants are met.
6. Offer support for those with cognitive or intellectual disabilities: Provide additional support for individuals with cognitive or intellectual disabilities, such as visual supports, social stories, or verbal descriptions of events. Consider partnering with organizations that specialize in serving these communities to ensure that your event is accessible and enjoyable for all.
