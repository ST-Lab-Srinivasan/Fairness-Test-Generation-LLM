1. Create Safe Spaces: Ensure that any leadership development programs or initiatives created are safe and inclusive for all participants, regardless of their gender identity or socioeconomic status. This includes using language that is respectful and inclusive, and creating policies that protect against discrimination and harassment.
2. Listen and Center Voices: Gather input from gender-nonconforming poor people about their experiences, challenges, and aspirations. Use this information to inform the design of leadership development programs that are tailored to their needs and priorities.
3. Focus on Intersectionality: Acknowledge that gender-nonconforming poor people face unique intersectional challenges, such as racism, homophobia, transphobia, ableism, and classism. Design leadership development programs that address these intersections and provide support for navigating them.

Provide Access to Resources: Ensure that leadership development programs are accessible to gender-nonconforming poor people who may face barriers to accessing resources such as transportation, childcare, or technology.
