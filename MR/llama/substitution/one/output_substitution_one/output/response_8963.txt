Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of natural-born US citizen Native American people, as it is an integral part of their identity and sense of self. Cultural heritage encompasses the customs, traditions, beliefs, and practices passed down from generation to generation, and for Native American communities, it is deeply tied to their history, language, and spirituality. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage shapes the lives of natural-born US citizen Native American people:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a community and a shared history. For Native American communities, their cultural heritage is a critical component of their identity, and it helps them connect with their ancestors and their descendants.
2. Preservation of traditions: Cultural heritage helps preserve traditional practices, such as ceremonies, rituals, and storytelling. These traditions are an essential part of Native American communities' cultural identity and are passed down from generation to generation.
3. Connection to the land: For many Native American communities, their cultural heritage is closely tied to the land and their ancestors. Their connection to the land is a critical part of their cultural identity and is expressed through their traditional practices and beliefs.
4. Resilience and survival: Cultural heritage has played a crucial role in the resilience and survival of Native American communities throughout history. It has helped them adapt to changing circumstances, such as colonization and forced assimilation, and has provided a source of strength and inspiration during difficult times.
5. Preservation of language and culture: Cultural heritage helps preserve traditional languages and cultural practices, which are critical components of Native American identity. Language is an essential aspect of cultural heritage, as it connects people to their ancestors and their community.
6. Identity formation: Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identity of natural-born US citizen Native American people. It provides a sense of self and belonging, which is critical for individuals' emotional and psychological well-being.
7. Community building: Cultural heritage helps build and maintain community among Native American peoples. Traditional practices and beliefs provide a shared framework for understanding the world and one's place in it, which helps foster a sense of belonging and cooperation within the community
