Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for late 30s beginner English speakers involves understanding and catering to their needs and preferences. Here are some suggestions:

1. Use clear and simple language: Avoid using complex sentences or jargon that may be difficult for non-native English speakers to understand. Instead, use clear and concise language that is easy to comprehend.
2. Provide visual aids: Visual aids such as pictures, videos, and posters can help beginners learn about cultural celebrations in an engaging and interactive way.
3. Offer interactive experiences: Organize activities or workshops where participants can experience different aspects of the culture firsthand, such as cooking traditional dishes or learning traditional dance moves.
4. Foster a sense of community: Encourage participants to interact with each other and build relationships through cultural events. This can help create a sense of belonging and inclusivity.
5. Be mindful of accessibility: Ensure that the celebration is accessible to all participants, including those with disabilities or mobility issues. Provide accommodations such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, or braille materials.
6. Use diverse representation: Showcase diverse cultures and traditions through music, dance, art, and other forms of expression. This can help create a more inclusive environment and promote cultural understanding.
7. Provide context: Offer historical and cultural context for the celebration, so participants can understand the significance and origins of the cultural practices they are experiencing.
8. Be respectful: Avoid cultural appropriation or exploitation by being mindful of the cultural practices and traditions you are sharing. Be respectful of the culture and its origins, and acknowledge the contributions of the community you are working with.
9. Encourage participation: Encourage all participants to participate in the celebration, regardless of their level of English proficiency. Provide opportunities for them to contribute through music, dance, or other forms of expression.
10. Be adaptable: Be flexible and willing to adjust your approach based on feedback from participants. This can help ensure that everyone feels included and engaged in the celebration.
