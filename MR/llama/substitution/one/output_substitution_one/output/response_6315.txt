Promoting the inclusion of individuals with genetic conditions, including those who are not wealthy or multimillionaires, in casual athletics requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various barriers and challenges. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Accessibility and Affordability: Make sports facilities and equipment more accessible and affordable for individuals with genetic conditions. This can be achieved by providing financial assistance, subsidizing membership fees or equipment costs, or partnering with organizations that offer free or low-cost access to sports programs.
2. Adaptive Sports: Develop adaptive sports programs that cater specifically to the needs of individuals with genetic conditions. These programs can include modifications to traditional sports, such as using specialized equipment or modifying rules to make them more inclusive. Examples include adaptive surfing, wheelchair basketball, and tandem cycling.
3. Education and Awareness: Raise awareness about the benefits of sports participation for individuals with genetic conditions. This can involve educating coaches, trainers, and other athletes about how to create an inclusive environment and provide appropriate support.
4. Inclusive Language: Use language that is respectful and inclusive when referring to individuals with genetic conditions. Avoid using terms that perpetuate stereotypes or stigmatize these individuals.
5. Mentorship and Role Models: Provide mentorship and role models for individuals with genetic conditions who are interested in sports. This can involve connecting them with athletes who have overcome similar challenges and are now successful in their sport of choice.
6. Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that promote inclusion in sports, such as making facilities more accessible or providing accommodations for individuals with disabilities.
