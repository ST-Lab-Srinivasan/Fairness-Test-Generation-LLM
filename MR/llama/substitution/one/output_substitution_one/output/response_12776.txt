Low-income, recent immigrant Native Americans in the US face significant challenges when accessing and navigating the healthcare system. Here are some of the common challenges they encounter:

1. Language barriers: Many low-income, recent immigrant Native Americans may not speak English fluently, which can create a barrier to accessing healthcare services. They may have difficulty communicating with healthcare providers, understanding medical instructions, and filling out forms and paperwork.
2. Cultural barriers: Healthcare providers may not be familiar with the cultural beliefs and practices of Native American communities, which can lead to misunderstandings or miscommunications about their health needs.
3. Limited access to care: Many low-income, recent immigrant Native Americans live in rural areas with limited access to healthcare services. They may have to travel long distances to see a provider, which can be a significant challenge for those with limited mobility or transportation options.
4. Cost and financial barriers: Low-income individuals may face financial barriers to accessing healthcare services, including high deductibles, co-pays, and other out-of-pocket costs. Recent immigrants may also face challenges related to obtaining health insurance or navigating the complexities of the US healthcare system.
5. Lack of trust: Some low-income, recent immigrant Native Americans may have had negative experiences with the healthcare system in the past, leading to a lack of trust in providers and institutions. This can make it difficult for them to seek medical care when they need it.
6. Limited health literacy: Many low-income, recent immigrant Native Americans may have limited health literacy, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the healthcare system, understand their health needs, and make informed decisions about their care.
7. Discrimination: Low-income, recent immigrant Native Americans may face discrimination in the healthcare system, including bias from providers or institutional barriers to accessing care.
