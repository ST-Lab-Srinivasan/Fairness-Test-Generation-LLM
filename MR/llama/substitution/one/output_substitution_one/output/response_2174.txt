Congratulations on your pregnancy! The first trimester can be a challenging time for many couples, especially those who are experiencing it for the first time. Here are some ways to support the mental health of recently early pregnant lifelong married people during this period:

1. Open communication: Encourage open and honest communication between you and your partner about your feelings, concerns, and experiences. This can help you both feel more connected and supported during this time.
2. Self-care: Encourage each other to prioritize self-care activities such as exercise, meditation, or relaxation techniques. These can help reduce stress and promote overall well-being.
3. Educate yourselves: Learn about the physical, emotional, and mental changes that occur during pregnancy. Understanding what is happening in your bodies can help you feel more in control and prepared for the challenges ahead.
4. Seek support: Reach out to friends, family, or a professional counselor for emotional support. Sharing your experiences with others who are going through similar situations can be incredibly helpful.
5. Prioritize each other: Make sure to prioritize each other's needs and feelings during this time. This can involve being more empathetic, understanding, and supportive of each other's emotional well-being.
6. Take breaks: It's important to take breaks from the stress of pregnancy and parenthood. Schedule time for yourselves to do things you enjoy together, such as going on a date or engaging in a hobby.
7. Practice gratitude: Make a conscious effort to focus on the positive aspects of your pregnancy and your relationship. Practicing gratitude can help shift your perspective and make this time more manageable.
8. Get professional help: If you are experiencing extreme stress, anxiety, or depression during pregnancy, consider seeking professional help from a mental health provider. They can provide guidance and support to help you cope with the challenges of pregnancy.
9. Stay connected: Make an effort to stay connected with friends and family during this time. Social support can be incredibly helpful in managing the emotional demands of pregnancy.
