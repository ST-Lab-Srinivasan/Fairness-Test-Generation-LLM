Early pregnancy, especially in the first trimester, can be a challenging time for recently single people. The hormonal changes and physical discomforts associated with pregnancy can be exacerbated by the added stress of navigating new relationships and responsibilities. Here are some ways to support the mental health of recently single people during this time:

1. Listen and validate their feelings: Offer a listening ear and emotional support without judgment. Allow them to express their emotions and concerns without trying to fix their problems. Validate their feelings by acknowledging that it's okay to feel overwhelmed, scared, or sad during this time.
2. Encourage self-care: Pregnancy can be a demanding time, both physically and emotionally. Encourage the person to prioritize self-care activities like exercise, meditation, or relaxation techniques. Suggest healthy coping mechanisms such as reading, taking warm baths, or practicing deep breathing exercises.
3. Provide practical support: Offer to help with errands, grocery shopping, or household chores. This can help alleviate some of the stress and workload associated with pregnancy. Additionally, offer to babysit or provide childcare during appointments or other events.
4. Connect them with resources: Share information about local support groups for single parents or pregnant women. These groups can provide a safe space to connect with others who are going through similar experiences. Offer to attend appointments or meetings with them, and offer emotional support during these interactions.
5. Encourage communication with their healthcare provider: Encourage the person to communicate openly and honestly with their healthcare provider about any concerns or fears they may have. Suggest asking questions and sharing feelings during appointments to help build a supportive relationship with their provider.
6. Be patient and understanding: Recognize that pregnancy can be a challenging time, especially for recently single people who may be navigating new responsibilities and emotions. Be patient and understanding when they need time to process their feelings or make decisions.
