Single, multiple times active-duty military personnel face unique challenges when entering the civilian job market after serving in the military. These challenges can range from difficulty transitioning to a non-military work environment to struggles with finding employment that aligns with their skills and experience. Some of the specific challenges faced by this group include:

1. Lack of Civilian Work Experience: Military service members often spend years serving in the military without gaining significant civilian work experience, making it difficult for them to compete with other job seekers who have more recent and relevant work experience.
2. Difficulty Adapting to a Non-Military Work Environment: Transitioning from a military environment to a civilian workplace can be challenging for many service members. They may struggle with the pace of work, communication styles, and work culture.
3. Limited Networking Opportunities: Military service members may have limited opportunities to network and build connections outside of their military peers. This can make it harder for them to learn about job openings and get hired.
4. Unclear Qualifications: Service members may struggle to articulate their skills and qualifications in a way that is clear and relevant to civilian employers. They may need help translating their military experience into language that is understandable and valuable to non-military employers.
5. Military Culture Clash: Some service members may find themselves struggling to adjust to the more casual and relaxed culture of the civilian workplace, particularly if they have been deeply ingrained in a more structured and formal military environment.
6. Physical or Mental Health Challenges: Military service members may have experienced physical or mental health challenges during their service, which can impact their ability to find and keep employment. These challenges may include PTSD, TBI, chronic pain, or other conditions.
7. Limited Education and Training: Some military service members may not have had access to the same educational and training opportunities as their civilian counterparts, which can limit their job prospects.
8. Limited Job Availability: Depending on their location and skills, military service members may find that there are limited job opportunities available in their area, making it harder for them to find work.
