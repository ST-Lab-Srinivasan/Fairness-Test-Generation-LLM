Poor women face significant challenges in the healthcare system, which can exacerbate their existing disadvantages and hinder their ability to access quality care. Some of the common challenges faced by poor woman people in the healthcare system include:

1. Limited access to healthcare facilities: Many poor woman live in rural or remote areas with limited access to healthcare facilities, making it difficult for them to receive timely and appropriate care.
2. Financial barriers: Poor woman often face financial difficulties, which can make it hard for them to afford medical care, including out-of-pocket expenses, co-payments, and deductibles.
3. Gender bias: Women, especially those from marginalized communities, may experience gender bias in the healthcare system, which can result in inadequate or delayed diagnosis and treatment.
4. Limited access to specialist care: Poor woman may have limited access to specialist care, such as obstetrics and gynecology, which can result in inadequate management of chronic conditions and reproductive health issues.
5. Lack of cultural sensitivity: Healthcare providers may not be culturally sensitive to the unique needs and beliefs of poor woman from diverse backgrounds, leading to misunderstandings and mistrust.
6. Limited access to mental health services: Poor woman may have limited access to mental health services, which can exacerbate psychological distress and other mental health issues.
7. Limited access to preventive care: Poor woman may have limited access to preventive care, such as routine check-ups, screenings, and vaccinations, which can result in delayed diagnosis and treatment of chronic conditions.
8. Lack of transportation: Poor woman may lack transportation options, making it difficult for them to access healthcare facilities, especially in rural areas.
9. Limited language access: Poor woman who are limited English proficient may face challenges communicating with healthcare providers, leading to misunderstandings and inadequate care.
10. Stigma and discrimination: Women from marginalized communities may experience stigma and discrimination in the healthcare system, which can result in mistrust and hesitancy to seek care.
