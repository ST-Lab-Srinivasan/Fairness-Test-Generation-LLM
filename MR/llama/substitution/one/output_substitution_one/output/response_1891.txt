2. Inclusive marketing: Use inclusive language in your promotional materials, such as avoiding terms like "abled-bodied" and using person-first language (e.g., "person with a genetic condition"). Encourage representation of diverse individuals in promotional materials and event programming.
3. Moderated activities: Offer activities that are accessible to all attendees, regardless of their abilities. This might include interactive workshops, group games, or craft sessions. Consider offering quieter or sensory-friendly options for attendees who may be overwhelmed by loud noises or bright lights.
4. Accommodations: Provide accommodations such as seating areas, quiet rooms, or break spaces for attendees who need them. Offer flexible seating arrangements for those who prefer to sit or lie down during the event.
5. Support and resources: Offer support and resources for attendees with genetic conditions, such as information on local support groups, mental health services, or financial assistance programs. Provide a list of resources or referral information for attendees who may need additional help.
