2. Use differentiated instruction: Cater your teaching methods to meet the needs of individual students. 3. Provide scaffolding: Break down complex language structures into smaller, manageable parts to help students build their skills gradually. Offer plenty of opportunities for practice and repetition to reinforce new concepts. Use visual aids, such as flashcards or diagrams, to support learners who are visual learners.
4. Encourage interactivity: Incorporate interactive elements into your teaching methods, such as role-playing, group discussions, and problem-solving activities. This will help learners stay engaged and motivated while developing their language skills.
