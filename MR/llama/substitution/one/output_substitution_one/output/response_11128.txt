Addressing the specific needs of non-native French speakers in education requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique experiences, challenges, and learning styles. Here are some strategies to help educators address the specific needs of long-time native French speakers:

1. Cultural competency training: Provide educators with cultural competency training to help them understand the non-native French speakers' backgrounds, values, and beliefs. This can include learning about their language acquisition journey, cultural norms, and communication styles.
2. Adaptive teaching methods: Use adaptive teaching methods that cater to the individual needs of each student. For example, provide differentiated instruction based on the students' proficiency levels, learning styles, and goals.
3. Language immersion programs: Offer language immersion programs that allow non-native French speakers to learn the language in an immersive environment. This can help them develop a more natural and spontaneous use of the language.
4. Personalized learning plans: Create personalized learning plans for each student based on their strengths, weaknesses, and learning goals. This can involve setting achievable objectives, providing regular progress updates, and offering additional support when needed.
5. Access to technology: Provide access to technology that can help non-native French speakers learn the language more effectively. For example, use language learning apps, online resources, or multimedia tools to supplement classroom instruction.
6. Peer support: Encourage peer support and collaboration among non-native French speakers. This can help them feel more comfortable and confident in their language learning journey.
7. Ongoing assessment and feedback: Regularly assess students' progress and provide constructive feedback to help them improve. This can involve using formative assessments to identify areas of strength and weakness, as well as summative evaluations to measure overall proficiency.
8. Community involvement: Encourage community involvement and participation in language learning activities. This can help non-native French speakers feel more connected to the language and culture, which can enhance their motivation and engagement.
