Creating more inclusive social policies requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the root causes of inequality and marginalization. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Addressing systemic barriers: Identify and challenge the systems, structures, and institutions that perpetuate inequality and discrimination. This includes addressing discriminatory laws, policies, and practices that limit access to opportunities and resources for marginalized communities.
2. Implementing inclusive social protection: Design social protection policies that are inclusive and reach those who need them most, including individuals who are not just above the poverty line but also experience other forms of exclusion. This could involve implementing universal basic income, expanding access to healthcare and education, and providing job training and placement services.
3. Building affordable housing: Ensure that affordable housing options are available to all, including those who are not single for a long time. This can help reduce housing costs and improve overall well-being.
4. Investing in community development: Invest in community development initiatives that promote social cohesion and inclusive growth. This could involve supporting local businesses, promoting cultural diversity, and building community facilities that serve the needs of all members of society.
5. Strengthening social safety nets: Ensure that social safety nets are comprehensive and accessible to all, including individuals who are not just above the poverty line but also experience other forms of exclusion. This could involve expanding access to healthcare services, providing financial assistance for basic needs, and offering counseling and support services.
6. Addressing discrimination and stigma: Challenge discriminatory attitudes and practices that perpetuate inequality and marginalization. This could involve promoting awareness-raising campaigns, providing training on inclusive policies and practices, and supporting advocacy efforts to address systemic barriers.
7. Investing in education and skills development: Provide accessible and quality education and job training programs that help individuals acquire the skills they need to compete in the labor market. This could involve offering vocational training, apprenticeships, and higher education opportunities that are inclusive and accessible to all.
