Supporting the social inclusion of native French speakers who have recently experienced multiple separations or divorces can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Encourage open communication: Allow them to express their feelings and experiences without judgment. Listen actively and empathetically, and validate their emotions.
2. Offer practical help: Help them with everyday tasks, such as grocery shopping or childcare, if needed. This can help reduce stress and increase their sense of independence.
3. Facilitate social activities: Organize social events or outings that are tailored to their interests and preferences. This can help them meet new people and build connections in a low-pressure environment.
4. Encourage self-care: Remind them to prioritize self-care, such as exercise, meditation, or hobbies. Encourage them to take breaks from socializing when needed and to prioritize their own well-being.
5. Provide emotional support: Be a sounding board for their emotions and concerns. Offer words of encouragement and reassurance, and help them identify resources for additional support if needed.
6. Help them connect with others: Introduce them to other single parents or individuals who have gone through similar experiences. This can help them feel less isolated and more connected to their community.
7. Respect their boundaries: Be mindful of their need for privacy and personal space, especially during times when they may be feeling vulnerable or overwhelmed.
8. Offer virtual support: If they are experiencing difficulties in socializing in person, offer virtual support through video calls, messaging apps, or online communities. This can help them stay connected with friends and family without feeling pressure to socialize in person.
9. Encourage professional help: Suggest that they seek professional help, such as therapy or counseling, if they are struggling to cope with the emotional challenges of separation or divorce.
