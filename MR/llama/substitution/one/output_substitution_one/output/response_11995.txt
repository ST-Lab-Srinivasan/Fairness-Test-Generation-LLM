1. Language Support: Provide language support services such as English language learner (ELL) programs, bilingual education classes, or language immersion programs. These services can help long-term immigrant students develop their English proficiency and academic skills.
2. Cultural Competence Training: Offer cultural competence training for teachers to help them understand the diverse backgrounds and experiences of long-term immigrant students. This can include training on how to teach students from different cultures, how to create an inclusive classroom environment, and how to address potential cultural conflicts.
3. Academic Support: Offer academic support services such as tutoring, mentoring, or academic clubs that cater specifically to the needs of long-term immigrant students. These services can help them catch up on their studies, build confidence, and develop study habits.

Social Integration: Encourage social integration by promoting extracurricular activities, community service projects, and intercultural events that bring together students from different backgrounds. This can help long-term immigrant students connect with their peers and build relationships outside of the classroom.
5. Parent Engagement: Engage parents in their children's education by providing parent training programs, parent-teacher conferences, and opportunities for parents to participate in school activities. This can help long-term immigrant students feel more connected to their school community and receive additional support from their families.
6. Community Partnerships: Develop partnerships with local community organizations that cater specifically to the needs of long-term immigrant populations.
