Native French speakers may find it challenging to adapt to the company's way of working and may need time to understand the unwritten rules and expectations in the workplace.
3. Stereotypes and biases: Unfortunately, there are still stereotypes and biases against French people in some parts of the world. Native French speakers may face discrimination or be seen as arrogant or lazy, which can hinder their career advancement.
4. Difficulty with time management: France has a different attitude towards work and leisure time than many other countries. Native French speakers may struggle to adjust to the faster pace of work in other cultures and may need help managing their time effectively.
5. Lack of international experience: While France is a global player, many native French speakers may not have had the opportunity to work or live abroad, which can limit their exposure to diverse perspectives and experiences. This can make it harder for them to navigate international job markets.
