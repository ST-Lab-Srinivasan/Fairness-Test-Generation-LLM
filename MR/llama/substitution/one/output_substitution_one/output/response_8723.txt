Promoting the educational success of beginner English speaker physically disabled students requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that educators, parents, and caregivers can use to support these students:

1. Accommodations and Modifications: Provide accommodations and modifications in the classroom setting to ensure equal access to education. This may include providing additional time to complete assignments, using assistive technology, or modifying the curriculum to make it more accessible.
2. Individualized Support Plans: Develop individualized support plans that address the student's specific needs and goals. These plans should be developed in collaboration with the student, their parents, and other educators.
3. Accessible Curriculum: Use an accessible curriculum that takes into account the student's physical disability and provides opportunities for them to learn and engage with the material in a way that is comfortable and easy for them.
4. Assistive Technology: Utilize assistive technology such as text-to-speech software, speech-to-text software, and other adaptive devices to help students with physical disabilities access educational materials and complete assignments.
5. Inclusive Classroom Environment: Create an inclusive classroom environment that promotes respect, understanding, and acceptance of all students, including those with physical disabilities. This can be achieved by incorporating diversity and inclusion into the curriculum and encouraging student participation and collaboration.
6. Professional Development: Provide professional development opportunities for educators to learn about best practices in teaching students with physical disabilities, including strategies for accommodating their needs and promoting their success.
7. Family Involvement: Encourage family involvement in the educational process by providing regular updates on the student's progress, involving parents in goal-setting and decision-making, and soliciting their input on how to support their child's educational success.
8. Collaboration with Specialists: Collaborate with specialists such as occupational therapists, speech therapists, and physical therapists to provide additional support and accommodations for students with physical disabilities.
