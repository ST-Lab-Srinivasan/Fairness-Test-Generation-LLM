Highlighting the issues faced by long-term unemployed beginner English speakers requires a comprehensive approach that utilizes various data sources and methods. Here are some ways to leverage data to shed light on this issue:

1. Survey Research: Conduct surveys among long-term unemployed beginner English speakers to gather data on their experiences, challenges, and barriers to employment. Surveys can be administered online or in person, and can include questions on demographics, education level, work history, and job search activities.
2. Job Market Data: Analyze job market data to identify trends and patterns related to the hiring of English language learners. This could involve examining job postings, resumes, and application data to determine which industries or companies are more likely to hire beginner English speakers.
3. Education Data: Examine data on language learning programs, courses, and certifications to understand the types of training and education that can help improve the employability of beginner English speakers. This could involve analyzing enrollment numbers, course completion rates, and job placement outcomes for these programs.
4. Economic Data: Study economic data on unemployment rates, income inequality, and poverty to better understand the broader social and economic context in which long-term unemployed beginner English speakers find themselves. This could involve analyzing data from government agencies, non-profit organizations, or academic research institutions.
5. Social Media Analysis: Utilize social media analytics tools to monitor online conversations and trends related to the experiences of long-term unemployed beginner English speakers. This could involve tracking hashtags, analyzing user-generated content, and identifying patterns in sentiment and topic distribution.

These case studies can provide rich qualitative data on the specific issues faced by these individuals and how they were able to overcome them.
