1. Rights of Refugees: The Indian government has recognized the need to protect the rights of refugees and asylum seekers. In 2017, India adopted the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) guidelines on the treatment of refugees in the country. This led to the establishment of the Protection of Civil Rights Movement (PCRM), which works towards protecting the rights of refugees and asylum seekers in India.
2. Veterans' Rights: The Indian government has also taken steps to address the rights of retired veterans in the country. In 2018, the Ministry of Defence launched the "Veterans' Portal" to provide a platform for veterans to access various benefits and services. This includes access to healthcare facilities, education schemes, and employment opportunities.
3. Advocacy by NGOs: Several non-governmental organizations (NGOs) in India are actively working towards advocating for the rights of refugees and retired veterans. For example, the "Indian Association for Refugee Rights" works towards promoting the rights of refugees and asylum seekers in India, while the "Indian Veterans' Foundation" advocates for the welfare and rights of retired military personnel.

For instance, the "Refugee Awareness Initiative" conducts workshops and events to educate the public about the rights of refugees and asylum seekers.
