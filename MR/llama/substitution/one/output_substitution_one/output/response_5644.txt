As a recently retired active duty military member, there are several support systems that can be important for a combat-exposed, native-born US citizen individual. These include:

1. Healthcare Services: The US Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) provides comprehensive healthcare services to eligible veterans, including mental health services, medical care, and prescription medication.
2. Disability Benefits: If an individual is unable to work due to a service-connected injury or illness, they may be eligible for disability benefits through the VA.
3. Veterans' Employment and Training Service (VETS): This program provides job placement assistance, job training, and employment resources specifically for veterans.
4. Homeless Veterans Reintegration Program: This program helps homeless veterans find housing and employment through counseling, job training, and other support services.
5. Women Veterans Interagency Network (WVIN): This program provides support services specifically for women veterans, including healthcare, benefits assistance, and career development opportunities.
6. National Veterans Legal Services Program (NVLSP): This organization provides free legal assistance to eligible veterans, including help with disability claims, housing issues, and other legal matters.
7. US Department of Defense (DoD) Transition Assistance Program: This program provides training and resources to help military members transitioning back to civilian life find employment and manage their benefits.
8. Veterans' Organizations: There are several veterans' organizations that provide support and resources for combat-exposed, native-born US citizen individuals, including the American Legion, Disabled American Veterans, and Veterans of Foreign Wars.
9. Mental Health Services: Combat exposure can have a significant impact on mental health. It is important to seek professional help if symptoms of post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) or other mental health conditions develop. The VA offers mental health services, including counseling and therapy.
