During the first trimester of pregnancy, many women may experience a range of emotional and psychological changes that can affect their mental health. Here are some ways to support the mental health of early pregnancy (1st trimester) not recently married people:

1. Emotional Support: Offer emotional support and validation to the expectant mother. Listen to her concerns, feelings, and fears without judgment. Encourage her to express her emotions and provide reassurance that she is not alone in this journey.
2. Education and Information: Provide educational resources on pregnancy, childbirth, and parenting. Offer information on prenatal care, labor, and delivery to help the expectant mother feel more prepared and informed.
3. Social Support Network: Create a social support network of other expectant mothers who are going through similar experiences. This can provide a sense of community and connection, which is essential for mental health during this time.
4. Self-Care Activities: Encourage the expectant mother to engage in self-care activities such as exercise, meditation, or yoga. These activities can help reduce stress and promote relaxation, which is crucial during pregnancy.
5. Mindfulness Techniques: Introduce mindfulness techniques such as deep breathing, progressive muscle relaxation, or guided imagery to help the expectant mother manage stress and anxiety.
6. Professional Help: If the expectant mother is experiencing severe emotional distress or suicidal thoughts, it's essential to seek professional help from a mental health provider. Early intervention can help prevent more severe mental health issues later in pregnancy.
7. Prenatal Yoga or Massage: Offer prenatal yoga or massage sessions to help the expectant mother relax, reduce stress, and promote physical well-being during pregnancy.
8. Creative Expression: Encourage the expectant mother to engage in creative expression such as writing, art, or music. This can help her process emotions and cope with the challenges of pregnancy.
