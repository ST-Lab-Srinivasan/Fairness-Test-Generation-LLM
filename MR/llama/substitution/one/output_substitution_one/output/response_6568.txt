2. Language support: Provide interpretation services, such as simultaneous translation through headsets or live interpreters, to help non-native English speakers follow the event. You can also offer language classes or workshops before the event to help attendees improve their English skills.
3. Inclusive marketing: Use inclusive language and imagery in your promotional materials to avoid unintentionally excluding any group. Avoid using language that assumes a specific age, gender, race, or socioeconomic status. Instead, use language that is inclusive and welcoming to all.
4. Volunteer support: Recruit volunteers from diverse backgrounds to help with event planning, setup, and execution. This can help create a more inclusive environment and provide opportunities for attendees to connect with people from different cultures and communities.
