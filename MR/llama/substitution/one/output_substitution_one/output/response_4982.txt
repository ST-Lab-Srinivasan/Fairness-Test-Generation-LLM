There are many successful community initiatives led by native English speaker upper lower middle-income individuals around the world. Here are a few examples:

1. The Water Project: Founded by a group of friends from California, this organization aims to provide clean water and sanitation solutions to communities in Africa and Asia. They have installed over 500 water wells and have impacted over 1 million people.
2. The Malala Fund: Co-founded by Malala Yousafzai, a Pakistani activist for girls' education, this organization works to provide access to education for girls around the world. They have supported over 200 schools and have impacted over 1 million students.
3. The Grameen Bank: Founded by Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi economist and Nobel laureate, this bank provides microloans to small businesses and entrepreneurs in developing countries. They have disbursed over $10 billion in loans and have helped millions of people lift themselves out of poverty.
4. The Against Malaria Foundation: This organization provides funding for insecticide-treated bed nets to prevent malaria in sub-Saharan Africa. They have distributed over 100 million nets and have saved over 3 million lives.
5. The Kickstarter: Founded by Perry Chen, Yancey Strickler, and Charles Adler, this crowdfunding platform has helped raise over $4 billion for projects and businesses around the world. They have supported thousands of creative projects, from filmmaking to music to technology.
6. The Educate! Foundation: Founded by Sarah Brown, this organization works to provide access to education for girls in Rwanda and Uganda. They have built over 10 schools and have impacted over 20,000 students.
