Refugees who have recently resettled in the United States face significant challenges when accessing healthcare services. Here are some of the common challenges employed refugees encounter:

1. Language barriers: Refugees may struggle to communicate effectively with healthcare providers due to language barriers, making it difficult to express their symptoms or understand medical instructions.
2. Lack of cultural competency: Healthcare providers may not be familiar with the cultural beliefs and practices of refugee populations, leading to misunderstandings or misdiagnoses.
3. Limited knowledge of US healthcare system: Refugees may be unfamiliar with the structure and organization of the US healthcare system, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the system and access necessary care.
4. Cost and financial barriers: Refugees may face financial difficulties due to limited financial resources or lack of health insurance, making it hard for them to afford medical care.
5. Difficulty accessing specialized care: Refugees may have difficulty accessing specialized medical care, such as mental health services or complex surgeries, due to a lack of providers with expertise in their specific needs.
6. Trauma and mental health challenges: Many refugees have experienced traumatic events in their home countries or during their migration journey, which can lead to mental health challenges such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), depression, or anxiety. These challenges can make it difficult for them to seek medical care or communicate effectively with providers.
7. Discrimination and stigma: Refugees may face discrimination and stigma from healthcare providers or other members of the community, which can lead to feelings of isolation or mistrust in the healthcare system.
8. Difficulty accessing dental care: Refugees may have difficulty accessing dental care due to a lack of providers who speak their language or understand their cultural beliefs and practices.
9. Limited access to women's health services: Refugee women may face barriers in accessing reproductive healthcare services, including contraception, maternal healthcare, and sexual assault services.
10. Difficulty navigating the complex healthcare system: The US healthcare system can be complex and confusing for anyone, let alone refuge
