Promoting the educational success of Native natural-born US citizen left-leaning students requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the unique challenges and barriers they face. Here are some strategies that educators, policymakers, and communities can implement:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Provide training and resources for teachers to incorporate Native American cultural perspectives and practices into their teaching methods. This can help students feel more connected and engaged in their education.
2. Identity-safe spaces: Create safe and inclusive learning environments where students can express themselves without fear of marginalization or discrimination. This can involve creating culturally responsive curricula, using diverse teaching materials, and promoting student voice and agency.
3. Community partnerships: Develop partnerships with Native American communities and organizations to provide students with opportunities for cultural immersion, mentorship, and community service. These experiences can help students develop a sense of purpose and belonging.
4. Indigenous knowledge integration: Incorporate Indigenous knowledge and practices into curricula and teaching methods. This can involve learning about traditional ecological knowledge, medicinal plants, and cultural practices that promote environmental stewardship and sustainability.
5. Support for mental health and wellness: Provide resources and support for students who may be experiencing trauma or stress related to historical trauma, discrimination, or other systemic issues. This can involve culturally responsive counseling services, mindfulness practices, and community-based healing programs.
6. Access to technology: Ensure that students have access to reliable technology and internet connectivity to support their educational success. This can involve providing laptops or tablets, as well as establishing Wi-Fi networks in schools and communities.
7. Language revitalization: Support efforts to revitalize Native American languages and cultural practices by incorporating language immersion programs, language classes, and cultural events into the curriculum.
8. Early childhood education: Invest in early childhood education programs that are culturally responsive and tailored to the needs of Native American communities. These programs can help build a strong foundation for future educational success.
9. Teacher training and development: Provide ongoing professional development opportunities for teachers to learn about Indigenous cultures
