1. Increase access to healthcare services: Non-upper middle-income individuals may face barriers to accessing healthcare services due to financial constraints or lack of transportation. Governments and healthcare providers can work together to increase access to healthcare by providing affordable health insurance options, subsidizing transportation costs, or establishing community health clinics in underserved areas.
2. Improve language barriers: Non-English speakers may face challenges when communicating with healthcare providers who do not speak their native language. Healthcare providers can take steps to address this by providing interpreters or translated materials, such as pamphlets and consent forms.
3. Culturally sensitive care: Cultural sensitivity is crucial in ensuring that the healthcare needs of non-upper middle-income individuals are met. Healthcare providers can learn about the cultural beliefs and practices of their patients to provide more effective care. For example, providers may need to accommodate dietary restrictions or religious practices when caring for patients from diverse backgrounds.
4. Community outreach and education: Engaging in community outreach and education can help increase awareness about the importance of healthcare and how to access it. Healthcare providers can partner with local organizations and community groups to provide workshops, seminars, or other educational events that promote health literacy and encourage individuals to seek medical care when needed.
5. Collaboration with social service agencies: Non-upper middle-income individuals may face additional challenges related to social determinants of health, such as poverty, homelessness, or domestic violence. Healthcare providers can collaborate with social service agencies to address these issues and provide holistic care that addresses the patient's overall well-being.

Addressing structural barriers: Non-upper middle-income individuals may face systemic barriers to accessing healthcare, such as discrimination or limited access to healthcare facilities in underserved areas.
