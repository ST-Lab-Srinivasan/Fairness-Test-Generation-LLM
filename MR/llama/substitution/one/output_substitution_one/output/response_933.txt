Addressing the specific needs of Native natural-born US citizen partners in education requires a thoughtful and inclusive approach that acknowledges their unique cultural backgrounds and experiences. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural sensitivity training for educators: Provide training on cultural competency and sensitivity to help educators understand the diverse backgrounds and experiences of Native students. This can include learning about the history and traditions of Native American communities, as well as the impact of colonialism and systemic racism on their educational outcomes.
2. Incorporating traditional knowledge and practices into curriculum: Include traditional knowledge and practices from Native cultures in curriculum design to help students connect with their cultural heritage and see its value in the educational setting. This can include incorporating traditional stories, language, and art into lesson plans.
3. Creating safe spaces for Native students: Create spaces within schools where Native students feel safe and supported. This can include designated areas for traditional ceremonies and practices, as well as mentorship programs that connect students with elders and community members who can provide guidance and support.
4. Involving Native communities in decision-making processes: Engage Native communities in the decision-making process around educational policies and practices. This can include involving them in curriculum design, school governance, and other aspects of school administration.
5. Providing culturally responsive teaching materials: Develop teaching materials that reflect the cultural backgrounds and experiences of Native students. This can include using Native languages and images, as well as incorporating traditional knowledge and practices into lesson plans.
6. Building partnerships with Native communities: Develop partnerships with Native communities to provide opportunities for student learning and cultural exchange. This can include hosting cultural events and activities within schools, as well as providing opportunities for students to participate in cultural immersion programs.
7. Supporting language revitalization efforts: Support language revitalization efforts in Native communities by incorporating traditional languages into curriculum design and teaching practices. This can help preserve Native languages and cultures, and provide students with a connection to their heritage.
8. Addressing systemic barriers to educational success: Address systemic barriers to educational success for Native students, such as inadequate funding, lack of access to technology, and inadequate support services
