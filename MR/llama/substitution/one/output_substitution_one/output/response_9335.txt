Creating more inclusive community events for beginner English speakers living in poverty can be a challenging task, but there are several strategies that can help make these events more accessible and welcoming. Here are some suggestions:

1. Provide language support: Offer interpreters or bilingual volunteers who can provide language support during the event. This can include translation services, as well as assistance with navigating the event space and understanding the content being presented.
2. Simplify the event format: Use simple language and avoid technical jargon to make the event more accessible to beginners. Keep the event structure and format straightforward and easy to follow.
3. Offer childcare and transportation: Provide on-site childcare and transportation options to help alleviate some of the logistical barriers that may prevent participants from attending events.
4. Promote the event through multiple channels: Use a variety of marketing channels, such as flyers, posters, social media, and community newsletters, to promote the event and reach a wider audience.
5. Involve the community in planning and decision-making: Engage with local community groups and organizations to get their input on the event format and content. This can help ensure that the event is tailored to the needs and interests of the target audience.
6. Provide free or low-cost refreshments: Offering free or low-cost refreshments can help make the event more accessible and welcoming to participants who may not have the financial means to purchase food or drinks.
7. Use inclusive language and imagery: Use language and imagery that is inclusive and respectful of all cultures and backgrounds. Avoid using language that may be offensive or alienating to any particular group.
8. Offer flexible scheduling options: Schedule the event at a time that is convenient for participants, taking into account any potential barriers such as work or family commitments.
9. Provide accessible venue and materials: Ensure that the venue is wheelchair accessible and that all materials are available in multiple formats, such as braille, large print, and audio versions.
