Creating more inclusive community events for active-duty employed people requires careful planning and consideration of their unique needs and challenges. Here are some suggestions to help make your events more accessible and welcoming:

1. Offer flexible scheduling options: Many active-duty employees have unpredictable work schedules, making it difficult for them to attend events during traditional hours. Consider offering events in the evenings or on weekends when they are more likely to be available.
2. Provide childcare and transportation options: Active-duty employees often have limited access to childcare and transportation, which can make it difficult for them to participate in community events. Offering these services or providing information on local resources can help make your event more inclusive.
3. Be mindful of accessibility: Ensure that your event is accessible to people with disabilities by providing wheelchair accessibility, Braille signage, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
4. Promote the event through multiple channels: Active-duty employees may not always be connected to social media or email, so make sure to promote your event through other channels such as military bases, unit calendars, and local newspapers.
5. Offer virtual participation options: For active-duty employees who are unable to attend the event in person, consider offering virtual participation options such as live streaming or online Q&A sessions.
6. Involve active-duty employees in the planning process: Engage active-duty employees in the planning process by seeking their input and ideas on how to make the event more inclusive and accessible. This can help them feel invested in the event and more likely to participate.
7. Provide information on military benefits and resources: Active-duty employees may be unaware of the resources available to them, such as military benefits or mental health support services. Providing this information at your event can help make it more useful and relevant to them.
8. Offer a variety of activities: Active-duty employees may have limited time outside of work and other commitments, so offer a variety of activities that they can participate in during the event. This could include workshops, training sessions, or social events.
