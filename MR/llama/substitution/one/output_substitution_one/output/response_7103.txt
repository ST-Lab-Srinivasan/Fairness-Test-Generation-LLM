1. Cultural sensitivity training: Provide training for staff and volunteers on culturally sensitive practices, including understanding the unique experiences and challenges faced by recent refugees.
2. Inclusive programming: Develop cultural programs that cater to the diverse needs and interests of native Chinese speakers, such as language classes, traditional music and dance performances, and cultural workshops.
3. Accessibility: Ensure that all cultural programs are accessible to everyone, regardless of their ability level or language proficiency. Offer interpreters or translated materials for those who may not speak English fluently.

5. Collaboration with refugee-serving organizations: Work with organizations that specialize in serving refugees, such as settlement agencies or community centers, to co-host cultural programs and reach a wider audience.
6. Flexible scheduling: Offer flexible scheduling options for cultural programs to accommodate the diverse needs of recent refugees, who may have busy schedules or conflicting commitments.
7. Bilingual programming: Offer bilingual programming that caters to native Chinese speakers, including language classes, workshops, and events. This can help build connections within the community and foster a sense of belonging.
8. Intergenerational programming: Develop cultural programs that bring together people of different ages and backgrounds, such as intergenerational language classes or cultural storytelling sessions. This can help build connections between recent refugees and the broader community.
9. Cultural exchange opportunities: Organize cultural exchange events that bring together native Chinese speakers and other members of the community, providing opportunities for cross-cultural learning and connection.
