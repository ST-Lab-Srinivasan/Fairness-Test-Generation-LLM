2. Disability benefits: Many individuals with genetic conditions are eligible for disability benefits through social security or other government programs. These benefits can provide a critical source of income to help meet financial needs.
3. Employment support: Employers can provide support for employees with genetic conditions by offering flexible work arrangements, accommodations, and disability leave. This can help individuals with genetic conditions continue working without jeopardizing their health or financial well-being.
4. Insurance coverage: Individuals with genetic conditions may require additional insurance coverage to manage the costs of medical treatment and care. Employers can offer supplemental insurance plans or provide resources for finding affordable insurance options.

Education and awareness: Raising awareness about genetic conditions and their financial implications can help individuals with these conditions access the resources they need to manage their finances effectively.
