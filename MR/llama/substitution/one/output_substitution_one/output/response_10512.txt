Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of native English-speaking Native American citizens in the United States. Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, and practices that are passed down from generation to generation within a particular group or society. For Native Americans, their cultural heritage is deeply rooted in their history, traditions, and rituals, which have been shaped by their indigenous cultures, languages, and spiritual beliefs.

Here are some ways that cultural heritage influences the lives of native English-speaking Native American citizens:

1. Identity: Cultural heritage helps define a person's identity and sense of belonging. For Native Americans, their cultural heritage is an important part of who they are as individuals and as members of their communities. It provides a connection to their ancestors and their history, which can help shape their sense of self and purpose in life.
2. Community: Cultural heritage serves as a unifying force within Native American communities. It provides a common thread that brings people together and fosters a sense of belonging and shared identity. Cultural events, traditions, and practices are often the glue that holds these communities together.
3. Tradition: Native Americans place great importance on tradition and cultural heritage. Many traditional practices and beliefs have been passed down from generation to generation, and they continue to play a significant role in contemporary Native American life. These traditions provide a connection to the past and help shape the present and future.
4. Preservation: Native Americans are actively working to preserve their cultural heritage, including their languages, music, art, and traditional practices. This involves documenting and sharing their history, as well as passing down knowledge and skills from one generation to the next.
5. Resilience: Cultural heritage has helped Native Americans navigate the challenges of colonization, forced assimilation, and other forms of oppression. It provides a source of strength and resilience, helping communities endure and thrive in the face of adversity.
6. Economic development: Cultural heritage can also play a role in economic development within Native American communities. Tourism and cultural exchange programs can provide new economic opportunities for communities, while also promoting cultural preservation and understanding.
7. Education: Cultural heritage can serve as a foundation for education within Native American communities.
