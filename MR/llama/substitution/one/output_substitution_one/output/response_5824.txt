Ensuring the educational needs of active-duty Native natural-born US citizen students is crucial to promote academic success and social integration. Here are some strategies that can help meet their educational needs:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Provide training for teachers on culturally responsive teaching practices, which recognize the importance of cultural identity and background in shaping students' learning experiences. This approach helps create a more inclusive and supportive learning environment for Native students.
2. Indigenous knowledge integration: Incorporate Indigenous knowledge and perspectives into the curriculum to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the history, culture, and contributions of Native American communities. This can include including Indigenous language, art, music, and other cultural expressions in the curriculum.
3. Supportive learning environments: Create safe and inclusive learning spaces that recognize and value the diversity of Native students' backgrounds and experiences. This can involve implementing policies and practices that promote cultural humility, mutual respect, and inclusivity.
4. Access to culturally relevant resources: Provide access to culturally relevant books, media, and other resources that reflect the experiences and perspectives of Native American communities. This can help students see themselves represented in the curriculum and feel more connected to their identity and culture.
5. Community partnerships: Develop partnerships with local Indigenous communities to provide opportunities for student engagement and learning. This can include collaborations with elders, traditional healers, and other community members who can share their knowledge and experiences with students.
6. Language revitalization: Support the revitalization of Native American languages by incorporating language learning into the curriculum and providing opportunities for students to learn and practice their native languages. This can help preserve cultural heritage and promote linguistic diversity.
7. Identity-based mentorship: Provide mentorship opportunities that connect Native students with Indigenous role models and mentors who can offer guidance, support, and inspiration. This can help students feel more connected to their identity and culture and provide a positive example of Indigenous success and achievement.
