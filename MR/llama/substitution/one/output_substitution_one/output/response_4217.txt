2. Differentiated instruction: Teachers can use differentiated instruction techniques to tailor their teaching to meet the unique needs and abilities of each student. This can involve providing individualized learning plans or modifying the curriculum to better meet the needs of students with diverse backgrounds and experiences.

4. Addressing systemic barriers: Schools can work to address systemic barriers that may affect the educational outcomes of certain student groups, such as inadequate funding, lack of resources, or biases in school policies and practices.
