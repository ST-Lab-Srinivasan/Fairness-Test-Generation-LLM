2. Language support: Offer language support services to help students navigate the educational system in their new language. This can include bilingual education programs, language classes, or providing interpreters or translators in schools.
3. Trauma-informed care: Recognize that many Native American students have experienced trauma due to displacement and provide trauma-informed care in schools. This includes training educators on how to recognize and respond to students' emotional needs, creating safe and supportive learning environments, and incorporating traditional healing practices into the school curriculum.
4. Community partnerships: Foster partnerships with Native American communities and organizations to provide student support services, such as mentorship programs, tutoring, and academic advising. These partnerships can help bridge the gap between the educational system and the community, ensuring that students feel supported and connected.
5. Indigenous knowledge integration: Incorporate traditional knowledge and practices into the curriculum to promote cultural relevance and understanding. This can include incorporating indigenous perspectives on science, math, and other subjects, as well as teaching traditional skills such as beadwork or basket weaving.
