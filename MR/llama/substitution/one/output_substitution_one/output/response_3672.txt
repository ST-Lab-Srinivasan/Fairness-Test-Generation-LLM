Ensuring that the educational needs of binational students who are living in poverty are met requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the unique challenges these students face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Early Intervention: Identify binational students who are living in poverty early on and provide them with targeted support to address their educational needs. This could include provision of academic support, tutoring, and mentoring.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training for educators on cultural competency to help them understand the experiences and challenges faced by binational students living in poverty. This can help educators to better support these students in their academic and personal lives.
3. Bilingual Education: Offer bilingual education programs that cater to the language needs of binational students. This can help these students to develop proficiency in both languages, which is essential for their academic success.
4. Out-of-School Time Programs: Provide out-of-school time programs that offer enrichment activities, tutoring, and mentoring to binational students living in poverty. These programs can help to bridge the gap between school and home, providing additional support for these students.
5. Family Engagement: Engage with the families of binational students living in poverty to provide them with information and resources that can help to support their child's educational needs. This could include parent-teacher conferences, workshops, and other family engagement activities.
6. Collaboration with Community Organizations: Collaborate with community organizations that serve the needs of binational students living in poverty. These organizations may offer a range of services, including food assistance, healthcare, and mental health support.
7. Advocacy: Advocate for policies and practices that support the educational needs of binational students living in poverty.
