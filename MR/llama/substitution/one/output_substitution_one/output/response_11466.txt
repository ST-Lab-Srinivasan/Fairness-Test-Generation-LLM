Cultural diversity plays a significant role in enriching the lives of Native American natural-born US citizens living in upper low-income communities. Here are some ways:

1. Preservation of Traditions: Cultural diversity helps preserve traditional practices, beliefs, and values of Native American communities. These traditions are an essential part of their identity and contribute to their overall well-being.
2. Sense of Belonging: Cultural diversity provides a sense of belonging to the community for Native Americans. It allows them to connect with their heritage and feel proud of their identity, which is essential for mental health and well-being.
3. Resilience: Cultural diversity helps build resilience in Native American communities. By preserving traditional practices and beliefs, they are better equipped to face challenges and adversities, such as poverty, discrimination, and cultural erasure.
4. Economic Benefits: Cultural tourism and the promotion of Native American arts and crafts can provide economic benefits for these communities. By leveraging their cultural heritage, they can create new revenue streams and improve their standard of living.
5. Intergenerational Relationships: Cultural diversity helps foster intergenerational relationships within Native American communities. Elders can pass down traditional knowledge and practices to younger generations, ensuring the continuation of their cultural heritage.
6. Community Engagement: Cultural events and festivals can bring together members of these communities, fostering a sense of community engagement and social cohesion. This can help address issues related to social isolation and marginalization.
7. Identity Formation: Cultural diversity plays a crucial role in the formation of identity for Native American youth. By being exposed to their cultural heritage, they can develop a strong sense of self and belonging, which is essential for mental health and well-being.
8. Civic Engagement: Cultural diversity can encourage civic engagement among Native Americans. By participating in cultural events and festivals, they can become more involved in their communities and exercise their democratic rights.
9. Health Benefits: Cultural diversity has been shown to have positive health benefits for Native Americans. Research has found that individuals who identify strongly with their cultural heritage tend to have better mental and physical health outcomes.
