Creating more inclusive social policies for long-term unemployed, recently single, multiple times individuals is crucial to address the unique challenges they face. Here are some recommendations for creating more inclusive policies:

1. Recognize the complexity of their situation: Long-term unemployment, multiple marital separations, and single status can lead to a range of emotional, social, and economic challenges. Policies should take into account the complexity of these experiences and provide support tailored to individual needs.
2. Provide access to job training and placement services: Many long-term unemployed individuals struggle to find stable employment due to a lack of relevant skills or experience. Governments can offer job training programs, apprenticeships, and mentorship opportunities to help them acquire the necessary skills and knowledge to re-enter the workforce.
3. Offer financial support: Financial assistance, such as unemployment benefits, can help individuals cover basic needs during periods of long-term unemployment. Governments can also provide incentives for entrepreneurship, such as microloans or business incubators, to help individuals create their own job opportunities.
4. Address mental health concerns: Long-term unemployment and multiple marital separations can lead to increased stress, anxiety, and depression. Mental health services, such as counseling and therapy, can help individuals cope with these challenges.
5. Foster community connections: Social isolation can exacerbate the challenges faced by long-term unemployed and recently single individuals. Governments can encourage community connections through programs that bring people together, such as volunteer opportunities or social events.
6. Advocate for flexible work arrangements: Many long-term unemployed individuals may face barriers to finding stable employment due to inflexible work schedules or lack of remote work options. Governments can advocate for policies that promote flexible work arrangements, such as telecommuting or compressed workweeks.
