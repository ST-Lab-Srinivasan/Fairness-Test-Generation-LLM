Supporting child Native English Speaker (NEST) communities is crucial for their academic success and social integration. Here are some effective strategies that educators, parents, and caregivers can use to support these communities:

1. Create a welcoming environment: Make sure the learning space is inclusive and free from bias. Encourage NESTs to share their cultural practices and traditions with the class, and provide opportunities for them to connect with their peers and build friendships.
2. Develop culturally responsive teaching practices: Incorporate elements of NESTs' culture into lesson plans, such as stories, songs, and games. This can help students feel more connected to the material and develop a sense of belonging.
3. Provide language support: For NESTs who are learning English as a second language, offer additional language support and resources. This can include language classes, tutoring, or individualized support from teachers or tutors.
4. Encourage student leadership: Give NEST students opportunities to take on leadership roles within the classroom or school. This can help build their confidence and provide them with a sense of agency and ownership over their learning experience.
5. Foster cross-cultural understanding: Organize cultural events, such as festivals, concerts, or exhibitions, that showcase the diversity of NEST communities. Encourage students to learn about each other's cultures and traditions through dialogue and shared experiences.
6. Provide academic support: Offer additional academic support to NEST students who may be struggling with their studies. This can include individualized tutoring, study groups, or academic mentorship programs.
7. Engage parents and caregivers: Encourage parents and caregivers to participate in the educational process by attending school events, volunteering in classrooms, or serving on school committees. This can help build a sense of community and support for NEST students.
8. Address microaggressions: Be aware of potential microaggressions that NEST students may face and address them promptly. Provide training for educators and staff on how to recognize and respond to microaggressions in the classroom.
