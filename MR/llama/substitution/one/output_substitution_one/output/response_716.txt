Creating more inclusive community programs for physically able-bodied very young individuals (ages 90 and above) requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some suggestions to create age-appropriate and accessible activities that cater to this demographic:

1. Consult with experts: Engage with occupational therapists, physical therapists, and other disability professionals to gain insights into the specific needs of older adults. They can provide valuable input on how to design programs that are both fun and accessible.
2. Conduct a needs assessment: Assess the needs of the community you're serving. Identify any barriers or challenges that may prevent older adults from participating in community activities. This could include physical limitations, transportation issues, or social isolation.
3. Develop age-appropriate activities: Design activities that are tailored to the interests and abilities of older adults. For example, you could organize art classes, music sessions, or gardening groups. Make sure the activities are fun, engaging, and promote socialization and cognitive stimulation.
4. Provide transportation support: Offer transportation options to ensure that older adults can easily access your community programs. This could include partnering with local transportation services or providing volunteer drivers.
5. Create a welcoming environment: Ensure that your community space is accessible and welcoming for older adults. This could involve modifying the physical space, such as installing grab bars or removing trip hazards, and creating a comfortable and inclusive atmosphere.
6. Foster intergenerational connections: Encourage interactions between older adults and younger generations. This could involve hosting events that bring together people of all ages, such as storytelling sessions or volunteer opportunities.
7. Offer adaptive equipment: Provide adaptive equipment for older adults who may have physical limitations, such as wheelchairs, walkers, or magnifying glasses. This can help them fully participate in your community programs.
8. Develop partnerships: Collaborate with local organizations and businesses to expand your reach and offer more comprehensive services.
