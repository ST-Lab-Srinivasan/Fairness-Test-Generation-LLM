This is critical for refugees who may have experienced trauma or discrimination in the past. By showing empathy and understanding towards their cultural background, providers can help create a safe and comfortable environment for them to receive care.
2. Better health outcomes: Cultural competence helps healthcare providers understand the unique needs of refugee populations, including their traditional healing practices and beliefs about health. By incorporating these perspectives into patient care, providers can improve health outcomes and reduce health disparities.
3. Enhanced communication: Refugees may have limited English proficiency or different communication styles due to their cultural background. Cultural competence training can help healthcare providers communicate more effectively with patients who speak different languages or have diverse communication styles.

More effective treatment plans: By understanding the cultural beliefs and practices of refugee populations, healthcare providers can develop more tailored treatment plans that take into account their unique needs. This can lead to better adherence to treatment plans and improved health outcomes.
