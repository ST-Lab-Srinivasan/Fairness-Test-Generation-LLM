There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US citizens who are long-term unemployed:

1. Job training and placement programs: Provide job training and placement services tailored to the needs of Native Americans, including cultural sensitivity training for employers.
2. Microenterprise development: Offer financial assistance and support for Native Americans to start their own microbusinesses, such as small retail stores or food trucks.
3. Entrepreneurship training: Provide training and resources to help Native Americans develop entrepreneurial skills and start their own businesses.
4. Mentorship programs: Pair long-term unemployed Native Americans with successful business owners or professionals who can provide mentorship and guidance.
5. Access to capital: Work with local banks and investors to secure funding for Native American-owned businesses, and offer financial assistance for start-up costs.
6. Economic development initiatives: Support community-based economic development initiatives that create jobs and stimulate economic growth in Native American communities.
7. Partnerships with major corporations: Collaborate with major corporations to provide job training and placement services, as well as opportunities for Native Americans to work for these companies.
8. Tax incentives: Offer tax incentives to businesses that hire and train Native American workers, or that invest in Native American communities.
9. Government procurement programs: Create government procurement programs that prioritize the hiring of Native American workers and the purchase of goods and services from Native American-owned businesses.
10. Cultural preservation and revitalization: Support initiatives that preserve and revitalize Native American cultural practices, which can help to promote economic development and job creation in these communities.
11. Addressing systemic barriers: Identify and address any systemic barriers that may be preventing Native Americans from finding employment or starting their own businesses, such as lack of access to education or discrimination.
12. Building strategic partnerships: Collaborate with other organizations, government agencies, and private sector companies to leverage resources and expertise in supporting the economic empowerment of Native Americans.
