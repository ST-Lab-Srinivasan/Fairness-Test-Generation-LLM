Promoting the educational success of Native American natural-born US citizens requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the historical and systemic barriers to educational achievement faced by this community. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers in culturally responsive teaching practices, which involve incorporating Native American culture, traditions, and values into the classroom. This can help students feel more connected to their education and see its relevance to their lives.
2. Indigenous language revitalization: Support the revitalization of Indigenous languages by providing language immersion programs, language classes, and resources for language learning. This can help preserve cultural heritage and promote linguistic diversity.
3. Increased representation in education: Ensure that Native American students see themselves represented in their teachers, peers, and curriculum. This can be achieved by hiring more Native American educators, incorporating diverse perspectives into educational materials, and showcasing Native American role models.
4. Addressing systemic barriers: Identify and address the systemic barriers that prevent Native American students from accessing quality education, such as inadequate funding, lack of resources, and cultural insensitivity in the classroom.
5. Community involvement: Engage with Native American communities to understand their unique needs and develop culturally responsive educational programs that reflect their values and beliefs. This can involve partnering with tribal organizations, elders, and community members to co-design educational initiatives.
6. Early intervention: Provide early intervention programs for Native American students who may be struggling in school due to cultural or linguistic barriers. This can help prevent academic disparities and improve overall educational outcomes.
7. Support for mental health: Provide mental health support services tailored to the unique needs of Native American students, including counseling, therapy, and stress management techniques.
8. Access to technology: Ensure that Native American students have access to technology, including tablets, laptops, and internet connectivity, which can help bridge the digital divide and provide equal opportunities for educational success.
