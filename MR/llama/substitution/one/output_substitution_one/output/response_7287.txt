2. Skill development: Mentors can help mentees identify areas of improvement and provide guidance on how to develop new skills. This can be particularly helpful for low-earning individuals who may not have had the opportunity to develop their skills through formal education or training programs.
3. Confidence building: Mentors can provide emotional support and encouragement, helping mentees build confidence in their abilities and feel more empowered to pursue their goals. This is especially important for low-earning individuals who may face additional challenges such as discrimination or marginalization.
4. Role modeling: Mentors can serve as role models, demonstrating what is possible through hard work and determination. This can inspire mentees to set their own goals and work towards achieving them.
