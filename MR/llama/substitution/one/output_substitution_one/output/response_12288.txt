Supporting the social inclusion of recent immigrants to India can be a challenging task, especially when it comes to newly single people. However, here are some ways to help them integrate into Indian society:

1. Language classes: Offer language classes specifically tailored for recent immigrants. This will help them learn the local language and culture, making it easier for them to communicate with others.
2. Cultural orientation programs: Organize cultural orientation programs that provide information about Indian customs, traditions, and way of life. These programs can help newcomers understand the social norms and expectations of Indian society.
3. Social events: Host social events specifically designed for recent immigrants, such as potluck dinners, movie nights, or game nights. This will give them an opportunity to meet other people in similar situations and make new friends.
4. Community groups: Encourage the formation of community groups specifically for recent immigrants. These groups can provide a safe space for individuals to share their experiences, ask questions, and get support from others who are going through similar challenges.
5. Mentorship programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair recent immigrants with experienced Indians who can provide guidance on navigating Indian society. This will help newcomers gain a deeper understanding of the culture and customs of India.
6. Access to resources: Provide access to resources such as healthcare, education, and employment opportunities. This will help recent immigrants settle into their new life in India more easily.
7. Advocacy: Advocate for the rights and needs of recent immigrants within Indian society. This can involve working with government agencies, community organizations, and other stakeholders to ensure that the voices of recent immigrants are heard and their needs are met.
8. Education and awareness: Educate the wider Indian society about the challenges faced by recent immigrants and the importance of inclusivity and diversity. This can involve organizing workshops, seminars, and other educational events that promote cultural understanding and empathy.
