There are several ways to ensure that the healthcare needs of non-upper middle-income native English speakers are met:

1. Cultural competency training for healthcare providers: Provide training on cultural competency and linguistic diversity to healthcare providers to help them understand and address the unique needs of diverse patient populations.
2. Language access services: Ensure that language access services, such as interpreters and translators, are available to patients who do not speak English fluently. This can help bridge the communication gap between healthcare providers and patients.
3. Community outreach and education: Engage in community outreach and education efforts to inform and educate diverse populations about available healthcare resources and how to access them.
4. Health literacy programs: Implement health literacy programs that provide patients with the knowledge and skills needed to navigate the healthcare system, make informed decisions about their care, and communicate effectively with healthcare providers.
5. Patient-centered care: Foster a culture of patient-centered care by prioritizing patients' needs, values, and preferences in the delivery of healthcare services. This can help ensure that patients feel comfortable and supported throughout their care journey.
6. Addressing social determinants of health: Healthcare providers can work to address social determinants of health, such as poverty, education, and housing, which can impact a person's overall health and well-being.
7. Collaboration with community organizations: Collaborate with community organizations that serve diverse populations to leverage their expertise and resources in addressing health disparities.
8. Health equity assessments: Conduct regular health equity assessments to identify and address disparities in healthcare access, quality, and outcomes for non-upper middle-income native English speakers.
9. Policies and regulations: Advocate for policies and regulations that promote diversity, equity, and inclusion in the healthcare system, such as language access laws and cultural competency requirements for healthcare providers.
10. Continuous quality improvement: Foster a culture of continuous quality improvement by monitoring and evaluating the effectiveness of healthcare services provided to non-upper middle-income native English speakers and making adjustments as needed.
