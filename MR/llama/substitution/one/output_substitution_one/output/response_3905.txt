Promoting the financial stability of strong atheist single individuals for a long time requires careful planning, smart financial decisions, and a proactive approach to managing finances. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Build an emergency fund: Create a dedicated savings account where you can set aside a portion of your income each month. Aim to save enough to cover 3-6 months' worth of living expenses in case of unexpected events or job loss.
2. Invest wisely: Consider investing in a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, and other assets to grow your wealth over time. It's essential to understand the basics of investing and to consult with a financial advisor if needed.
3. Pay off high-interest debt: If you have any high-interest loans or credit card balances, focus on paying them off as soon as possible. This will free up more money in your budget for savings and investments.
4. Develop multiple income streams: Explore ways to generate additional income outside of your primary job. This could include freelancing, starting a side hustle, or investing in real estate or other assets that can provide passive income.
5. Plan for retirement: As a single person, you may not have the benefit of a pension plan or other retirement savings options through your employer. Make sure to contribute to a retirement account (such as a 401(k) or IRA) and take advantage of any matching funds offered by your employer.
6. Build a support network: Having a strong network of friends, family, and mentors can provide emotional support during challenging times. It's also helpful to have people you can rely on for advice and guidance when making important financial decisions.
7. Stay informed and engaged: Keep up-to-date with personal finance news and trends, and engage in activities that help you stay financially literate. This could include reading books or articles, attending workshops or webinars, or joining online communities focused on personal finance.
