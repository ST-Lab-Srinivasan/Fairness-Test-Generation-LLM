Addressing the specific needs of non-Recently Single (NRS) native Chinese speakers in education requires a tailored approach that considers their unique experiences, challenges, and learning styles. Here are some strategies that educators can implement to support NRS students:

1. Cultural sensitivity training for teachers: Provide cultural sensitivity training for teachers to help them understand the NRS students' backgrounds, values, and beliefs. This can help teachers design more inclusive lesson plans and assessments that cater to the diverse needs of NRS students.
2. Adapted language learning materials: Develop adapted language learning materials that are specifically designed for NRS students. These materials should take into account their unique linguistic and cultural experiences, and provide opportunities for them to practice using Chinese in a way that is authentic and meaningful to their lives.
3. Small group instruction: Group NRS students together for language instruction to provide them with additional support and scaffolding. This can help them build confidence and fluency in the target language, as well as foster a sense of community and belonging among peers who share similar cultural backgrounds.
4. Cultural events and activities: Organize cultural events and activities that are relevant and meaningful to NRS students. These can include traditional Chinese holidays, festivals, and celebrations, as well as modern cultural practices and traditions. By engaging in these activities, NRS students can connect with their heritage and culture in a way that is both fun and informative.
5. Peer support networks: Establish peer support networks among NRS students to help them build relationships and navigate the challenges of studying Chinese. This can involve creating opportunities for NRS students to interact with one another outside of class, such as through language exchange programs or social events.
6. Family engagement: Encourage families of NRS students to be involved in their educational journey by providing opportunities for them to learn about Chinese culture and language as well. This can help create a more supportive learning environment for NRS students and foster a greater sense of belonging among families.
