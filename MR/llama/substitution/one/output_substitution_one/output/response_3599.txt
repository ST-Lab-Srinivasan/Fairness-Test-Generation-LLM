To support the economic empowerment of refugee natural-born US citizens who are not from upper-middle-income backgrounds, there are several strategies that can be employed. Here are some possible ways to address this issue:

1. Education and Training: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help refugees acquire the skills and knowledge they need to compete in the US job market. This can include vocational training, language classes, and certification programs in high-demand fields such as technology, healthcare, and finance.
2. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship: Many refugees have entrepreneurial aspirations but lack the resources to start or grow a business. Microfinance programs can provide small loans and other financial services to help refugees launch their own businesses or expand existing ones. This can help them generate income and build assets, while also contributing to the US economy.
3. Job Placement and Career Advancement: Refugees often face significant barriers to finding employment in the US due to language differences, cultural misunderstandings, and lack of connections. Organizations that provide job placement services and career advancement opportunities can help refugees find good-paying jobs and advance in their careers.
4. Healthcare Access: Many refugees face health challenges due to violence, persecution, or poor living conditions in their home countries. Providing access to affordable and quality healthcare can help refugees address these challenges and improve their overall well-being.
5. Housing Support: Refugees often struggle to find affordable and safe housing in the US. Organizations that provide rental assistance, housing counseling, and other housing support services can help refugees establish stable living arrangements and reduce stress related to housing insecurity.
6. Mentorship and Networking: Connecting refugees with mentors and networking opportunities can help them build professional connections and gain access to resources and opportunities that can help them advance in their careers.
7. Legal Support: Many refugees face legal challenges related to immigration status, citizenship, and other issues. Providing access to free or low-cost legal services can help refugees navigate the US legal system and address these challenges.
