Supporting the economic empowerment of individuals with genetic conditions who are from lower socio-economic backgrounds can be challenging due to various factors, including discrimination, stigma, and limited access to resources and opportunities. Here are some strategies that can help support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to education and training programs that help individuals develop skills and knowledge in areas such as vocational training, entrepreneurship, and financial literacy. This can help them acquire the skills they need to find employment or start their own businesses.
2. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that individuals have access to quality healthcare services, including genetic counseling and testing, which can help identify the underlying causes of their condition. This can also help them better understand their condition and make informed decisions about their care.
3. Financial Support: Provide financial support in the form of scholarships, grants, or low-interest loans to individuals who want to pursue education or start a business but lack the resources to do so.
4. Networking Opportunities: Create opportunities for individuals to connect with potential employers, investors, or mentors who can provide guidance and support in their career or business endeavors.
5. Advocacy and Awareness: Raise awareness about the challenges faced by individuals with genetic conditions from lower socio-economic backgrounds and advocate for policies that promote their inclusion and access to resources. This can help reduce stigma and discrimination and create a more inclusive society.
6. Community-Based Initiatives: Establish community-based initiatives that provide support and resources to individuals with genetic conditions, including access to healthcare services, education and training programs, and financial assistance.
