Promoting the artistic talents of native English speakers aged 65 and older can be a rewarding and fulfilling experience. Here are some suggestions on how to do it:

1. Showcase their work: Organize exhibitions, performances, or events that showcase their artistic talents. This could include painting, drawing, sculpture, music, dance, poetry, or any other form of art.
2. Encourage intergenerational collaboration: Collaborate with younger artists to create new works or to learn from each other. This can help to bridge the generation gap and provide opportunities for mentorship and learning.
3. Provide resources and support: Offer workshops, classes, or individual instruction to help them develop their skills and knowledge in various art forms.
4. Create a platform: Develop an online platform or social media group where they can share their work, connect with other artists, and receive feedback and support.
5. Celebrate their contributions: Host events or exhibitions that highlight the contributions of senior artists to the community, such as retrospectives, career-spanning exhibitions, or special tributes.
6. Engage with local communities: Collaborate with local organizations, such as nursing homes, retirement communities, or community centers, to bring art programs and activities to seniors in their own communities.
7. Provide opportunities for performance: Organize performances, concerts, or other events that showcase their talents to a wider audience.
8. Offer mentorship programs: Pair experienced senior artists with newer or younger artists to provide guidance and support in developing their skills.
9. Create a network: Establish a network of senior artists who can connect, share ideas, and support each other in their artistic endeavors.
10. Provide recognition: Give them recognition for their contributions by awarding them with certificates, trophies, or other forms of appreciation.

By promoting the artistic talents of native English speakers aged 65 and older, we can help to keep them engaged, active, and connected to their communities, while also preserving and celebrating their cultural heritage for future generations.
