Promoting the artistic talents of native English speakers aged 65 and older can be a rewarding and enriching experience for both the individuals involved and the community at large. Here are some ways to support and showcase their talents:

1. Host an art exhibition or sale: Organize an event where seniors can display and sell their artwork, either in a local gallery or at a community center. This will provide them with exposure and an opportunity to share their creations with a wider audience.
2. Offer workshops and classes: Provide opportunities for seniors to learn new skills and techniques through workshops and classes focused on various art forms, such as painting, drawing, pottery, or photography. This will not only help them develop their talents but also keep their minds active and engaged.
3. Create a senior arts group: Form a group of seniors who share an interest in the arts, providing a platform for them to collaborate, share ideas, and showcase their work. This can be done through regular meetings, exhibitions, or even online platforms.
4. Partner with local organizations: Collaborate with senior centers, retirement communities, or other organizations that cater to older adults. This will help reach a wider audience and provide more opportunities for seniors to showcase their talents.
5. Utilize technology: Leverage digital platforms to showcase seniors' artwork, such as online galleries, social media, or even virtual exhibitions. This will allow them to reach a global audience and gain recognition beyond their local community.
6. Provide mentorship opportunities: Pair experienced artists with novices, providing guidance and support to help them develop their skills and reach their full potential.
7. Offer awards and recognition: Recognize the talents of seniors through awards, certificates, or other forms of appreciation. This will help boost their confidence and motivate them to continue creating.
8. Create a community arts project: Organize a collaborative project that brings together seniors from different backgrounds and skill levels. This can be a fun way for them to work together, learn from each other, and create something meaningful.
