3. Community events: Encouraging recent immigrants to participate in community events such as festivals, cultural performances, and volunteer work can help them connect with like-minded individuals and feel more included in the community.
4. Mentorship programs: Pairing recent immigrants with experienced individuals who can guide them through the challenges of adapting to Indian culture and society can be incredibly helpful. These mentors can provide valuable advice, support, and encouragement during this critical period.
5. Job training and placement: Offering job training and placement services specifically tailored to recent immigrants can help them find employment more easily. This can help them become self-sufficient and feel more confident in their new surroundings.

7. Financial assistance: Offering financial assistance, such as scholarships or small business loans, can help recent immigrants establish themselves financially and feel more secure in their new home.
