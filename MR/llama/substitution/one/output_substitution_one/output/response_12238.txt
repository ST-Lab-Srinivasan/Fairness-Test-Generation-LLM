Supporting the economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US citizens who are low-earning is crucial for addressing the significant wealth gap between them and the general population. Here are some ways to support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that can help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge, enabling them to secure better-paying jobs. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and certificate programs in high-demand fields like healthcare, technology, and business management.
2. Small Business Development: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing resources and support for starting and growing small businesses. This can include access to microloans, mentorship opportunities, and training on business planning and operations.
3. Job Placement Services: Offer job placement services that connect low-earning individuals with employers who are willing to hire and train them. This can help individuals gain work experience and build their careers while also addressing the labor shortages in certain industries.
4. Financial Literacy and Investment Opportunities: Teach financial literacy skills to help individuals make informed decisions about managing their finances, investing, and building wealth. This can include access to financial planning resources, investment seminars, and opportunities to invest in local businesses or real estate.
5. Land Access and Homeownership: Address the historical injustices faced by Native Americans by providing access to affordable land and homeownership opportunities. This can include programs that provide low-interest loans or grants for buying land or building homes, as well as support for community land trusts or cooperative housing models.
6. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to quality healthcare services by providing funding for community clinics, health centers, and telemedicine programs. This can help address the significant health disparities faced by Native American communities and improve overall well-being.
7. Childcare Support: Provide affordable childcare options to support low-earning individuals who may struggle to balance work and family responsibilities. This can include subsidized childcare programs, tax credits for childcare expenses, or investments in early childhood education.
