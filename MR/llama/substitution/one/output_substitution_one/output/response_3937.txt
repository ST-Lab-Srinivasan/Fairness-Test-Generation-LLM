Promoting the educational success of mixed-American heritage independent students requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Cultural Competence Training: Provide training for teachers and staff on cultural competence, inclusivity, and diversity to help them understand and appreciate the experiences and backgrounds of mixed-American heritage students.
2. Identity Development Programs: Offer programs that help students explore their multiracial identity and develop a positive sense of self. This can include workshops, discussions, and activities that promote self-reflection and self-esteem.
3. Academic Support Services: Provide academic support services such as tutoring, mentoring, and academic advising to help students succeed in their studies.
4. Social Emotional Learning: Teach social emotional learning skills such as empathy, communication, and conflict resolution to help students navigate complex social situations and build positive relationships with peers and teachers.
5. Safe Spaces: Create safe spaces for mixed-American heritage students to express themselves without fear of judgment or discrimination. This can include cultural clubs, student organizations, and other extracurricular activities.
6. Teaching Strategies: Use teaching strategies that are inclusive and culturally responsive, such as using diverse texts and materials, incorporating multicultural perspectives, and creating learning environments that reflect the experiences of mixed-American heritage students.
7. Family Engagement: Encourage families to participate in school activities and events, and provide opportunities for parents and guardians to connect with teachers and other families from diverse backgrounds.
8. Community Partnerships: Form partnerships with community organizations that support the educational success of mixed-American heritage students, such as after-school programs, summer camps, and mentorship initiatives.
9. Policy Development: Develop policies that promote inclusivity and cultural competence in education, such as policies on multicultural education, diversity and inclusion, and anti-bias training for teachers and staff.
10. Curriculum Reform: Reform the curriculum to include diverse perspectives and histories, and to promote critical thinking and reflection on issues of identity, culture, and power.
