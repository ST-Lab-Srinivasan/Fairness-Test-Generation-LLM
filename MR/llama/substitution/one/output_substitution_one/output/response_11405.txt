For example, using multimedia presentations, providing written materials, or offering one-on-one support can help ensure that older adults are able to keep up with the coursework.

Educators can offer counseling services or connect students with resources on campus that provide emotional support, such as student mental health services or support groups.
3. Encourage peer support: Peer support can be incredibly beneficial for older adults who are grieving the loss of a spouse. Educators can encourage students to connect with one another and share their experiences in a safe and supportive environment.
4. Offer flexible scheduling options: Older adults may have difficulty adjusting to a traditional class schedule, so educators should consider offering flexible scheduling options, such as online courses or evening classes.
5. Provide additional resources: Educators can provide additional resources to help older adults navigate the educational system, such as information on financial aid, academic advising, and career counseling.

Create a supportive learning environment: Older adults may feel out of place in a traditional classroom setting, so educators should strive to create a supportive learning environment that acknowledges and accommodates their needs.
