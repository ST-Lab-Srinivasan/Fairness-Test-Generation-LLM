Creating more inclusive public policies for non-disabled Native American recent refugees to the US requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique needs and challenges faced by this community. Here are some recommendations:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for government officials, service providers, and community members to help them understand the cultural nuances of Native American communities and the experiences of recent refugees.
2. Language Access: Ensure language accessibility in public policies and services by providing interpretation and translation services, particularly in healthcare, education, and legal settings.
3. Inclusive Hiring Practices: Implement inclusive hiring practices that actively seek out and recruit individuals from underrepresented communities, including Native Americans and recent refugees.
4. Community Engagement: Engage with Native American and refugee communities through participatory governance and community-based decision-making processes to ensure that their voices are heard and valued.
5. Education and Awareness: Develop educational programs and awareness campaigns that promote cultural understanding, empathy, and inclusivity, particularly in schools and universities.
6. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to quality healthcare services for Native American and refugee communities by providing culturally competent care and addressing linguistic and cultural barriers.
7. Economic Empowerment: Develop economic empowerment programs that provide financial literacy training, entrepreneurship support, and job placement services specifically tailored to the needs of Native Americans and recent refugees.
8. Housing Support: Provide affordable and accessible housing options for Native American and refugee communities, including rental assistance, homeownership programs, and homelessness prevention services.
9. Legal Support: Ensure access to legal services by providing culturally competent legal aid and addressing linguistic and cultural barriers in the justice system.
10. Advocacy and Activism: Advocate for policies and programs that promote the rights and well-being of Native Americans and recent refugees, and engage in activism to challenge systemic inequalities and discrimination.
