Creating educational programs that are inclusive of older adult and permanent resident perspectives requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some strategies to help ensure that your programs are accessible and welcoming to this diverse population:

1. Consult with Older Adults and Permanent Residents: Conduct outreach and engagement activities with older adults and permanent residents to understand their needs, preferences, and cultural backgrounds. This will help you design programs that are relevant and meaningful to them.
2. Incorporate Intergenerational Elements: Design educational programs that bring together people of different ages and backgrounds. This can be done through intergenerational mentorship programs, community service projects, or cross-age mentoring.
3. Use Accessible Materials and Format: Use materials and formats that are accessible to older adults and permanent residents, such as large print, audio, or video recordings. Provide multiple ways for them to access the content, including online, in-person, or through community centers.
4. Accommodate Different Learning Styles: Recognize that older adults and permanent residents may have different learning styles, such as visual, auditory, or kinesthetic. Use a variety of teaching methods to accommodate these differences.
5. Address Social Isolation: Many older adults and permanent residents may experience social isolation. Incorporate opportunities for social interaction and community building into your programs to help address this issue.
6. Foster Cultural Competence: Ensure that your educational programs promote cultural competence by incorporating diverse perspectives, experiences, and histories. Provide education on ageism, racism, sexism, and other forms of discrimination to help participants understand and challenge these issues.
7. Offer Support Services: Provide support services such as transportation, childcare, or home-based care to help older adults and permanent residents participate in your programs.
8. Develop Partnerships: Collaborate with community organizations, healthcare providers, and other stakeholders to develop partnerships that can help reach a wider audience and provide more comprehensive support.
