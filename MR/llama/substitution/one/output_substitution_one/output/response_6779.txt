Creating more inclusive social policies for non-wealthy, non-millionaire individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the various barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help create a more equitable society:

1. Progressive taxation: Implementing a progressive tax system where the wealthy are taxed at a higher rate than the poor can help reduce income inequality. This can also generate revenue for social welfare programs that benefit low-income individuals.
2. Increase the minimum wage: Raising the minimum wage can help ensure that low-income workers earn a living wage and have access to basic necessities like food, housing, and healthcare.
3. Strengthen social safety nets: Expanding and improving social safety nets, such as unemployment insurance, food assistance programs, and housing subsidies, can help protect individuals from poverty and economic insecurity.
4. Address discrimination: Discrimination based on race, gender, sexual orientation, and other factors can exacerbate income inequality. Policies that address discrimination, such as anti-discrimination laws and diversity initiatives, can help create a more inclusive society.
5. Invest in education and job training: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help individuals acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market and advance economically.
6. Promote affordable housing: Increasing the availability of affordable housing can help reduce housing costs, which can be a significant burden for low-income individuals and families.
7. Support small businesses and entrepreneurs: Small businesses and entrepreneurs can play a crucial role in creating jobs and economic opportunities for low-income individuals. Policies that support these entities, such as access to capital and mentorship programs, can help them thrive.
8. Encourage corporate social responsibility: Encouraging businesses to adopt socially responsible practices, such as paying a living wage, providing benefits, and supporting local communities, can help reduce income inequality.
