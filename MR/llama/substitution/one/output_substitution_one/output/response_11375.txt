Ensuring that the educational needs of non-upper-class individuals over the age of 90 are met requires a comprehensive and inclusive approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Accessible Learning Environments: Create learning environments that are accessible and inclusive for all, regardless of age or ability. This includes physical spaces, as well as digital platforms and tools.
2. Intergenerational Learning: Encourage intergenerational learning by bringing together individuals of different ages and backgrounds to learn from each other. This can help break down age-based barriers and promote cross-generational understanding.
3. Life-Long Learning: Embrace the concept of life-long learning, which recognizes that learning is a lifelong process. Provide opportunities for individuals over 90 to continue learning and growing, whether through formal education or informal activities such as hobbies or volunteering.
4. Age-Appropriate Content: Develop age-appropriate content that addresses the unique needs and interests of older adults. This can include topics such as healthy aging, social isolation, and mental health.
5. Technology Integration: Leverage technology to enhance learning experiences for older adults. This can include using digital tools to facilitate remote learning, providing access to e-books and other educational resources, or creating interactive games and activities that promote cognitive function.
6. Community Outreach: Collaborate with local communities and organizations to reach out to older adults who may be isolated or marginalized. Provide educational opportunities in these communities, and work to address the systemic barriers that prevent these individuals from accessing education.
7. Flexible Learning Pathways: Offer flexible learning pathways that accommodate the diverse needs and abilities of older adults. This can include providing opportunities for self-directed learning, or offering personalized coaching and mentorship.
8. Interdisciplinary Approach: Take an interdisciplinary approach to education, recognizing that learning is holistic and interconnected.
