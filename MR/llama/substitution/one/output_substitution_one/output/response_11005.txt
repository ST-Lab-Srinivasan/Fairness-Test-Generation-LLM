1. Recognize the unique needs of this population: Deep in poverty, long-term, recently single individuals often face multiple forms of marginalization, including gender-based discrimination, race/ethnicity-based discrimination, and economic inequality. Policies should take into account these intersectionalities and address the specific challenges faced by this population.
2. Implement a comprehensive safety net: Provide a range of social services, including healthcare, housing, food assistance, and job training programs, to help individuals overcome immediate needs and build resilience. This can include targeted funding for organizations that serve this population, as well as partnerships with community-based organizations to reach isolated or marginalized groups.
3. Invest in education and job training: Offer flexible, affordable educational programs that provide skills training and career development opportunities, particularly in fields with high demand for workers. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and certification programs.
4. Support entrepreneurship and small business development: Provide resources and support to help individuals start or grow their own businesses, including low-interest loans, mentorship opportunities, and access to business incubators or co-working spaces.
5. Address systemic barriers: Policies should aim to address the root causes of poverty and marginalization, such as discrimination, inequality, and lack of access to resources and opportunities. This can involve policy changes at multiple levels, including local, state, or federal, to address systemic issues that perpetuate poverty and exclusion.
