Promoting the educational success of ethnically diverse American students, including those who are left-leaning, requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the systemic and structural barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers to recognize and value the cultural backgrounds of their students, and incorporate diverse perspectives and experiences into their lessons. This can help students feel seen and represented in the classroom.
2. Inclusive curriculum: Develop a curriculum that reflects the diversity of American society, including the experiences and contributions of left-leaning individuals and communities. Provide opportunities for students to engage with diverse perspectives and texts.
3. Identity-safe classrooms: Create classroom environments where all students feel safe and valued, regardless of their identity or political beliefs. Encourage open dialogue and respectful debate, rather than silencing or marginalizing certain viewpoints.
4. Support for underrepresented groups: Provide additional support and resources to underrepresented groups, such as funding for scholarships, mentorship programs, and academic enrichment activities. This can help level the playing field and address systemic inequalities.
5. Community partnerships: Collaborate with local community organizations, advocacy groups, and social service agencies to provide students with opportunities to engage in activism and civic engagement. Encourage students to explore their passions and interests outside of the classroom.
6. Family and community involvement: Engage with families and communities to promote academic success and social mobility. Provide resources and support for parents and guardians to help them navigate the educational system and advocate for their children's needs.
7. Addressing implicit bias: Train teachers and staff to recognize and address their own biases, which can impact student outcomes. Encourage a growth mindset and ongoing professional development to ensure that all educators are equipped to provide inclusive and effective instruction.
8. Technology integration: Leverage technology to enhance learning experiences and promote accessibility for all students. Provide equal access to digital resources, devices, and internet connectivity to address the "digital divide."
