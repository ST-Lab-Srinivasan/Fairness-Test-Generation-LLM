Promoting the social inclusion of refugees in the United States is crucial for their well-being and successful integration into society. While many organizations focus on providing basic needs such as food, shelter, and clothing, it is equally important to address the social and cultural aspects of inclusion. Here are some strategies to promote social inclusion of recent refugees above poverty line communities:

1. Language Training and Education: Provide language training and education to help refugees improve their English skills and gain access to job opportunities. This will enable them to communicate effectively, navigate the healthcare system, and participate in civic life.
2. Job Training and Placement: Offer job training and placement services to help refugees develop vocational skills and find employment. This can include workshops on resume writing, interviewing, and networking.
3. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair refugees with experienced professionals in their field of interest. This will provide them with guidance and support in navigating the job market and building a career.
4. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement and socialization by organizing events and activities that bring together refugees and local communities. This can help break down cultural barriers and promote understanding and empathy between the two groups.
5. Advocacy and Awareness: Raise awareness about the challenges faced by refugees and advocate for policies that support their integration into society. This can include advocating for fair wages, access to healthcare, and protection from discrimination.
6. Cultural Adaptation Support: Provide cultural adaptation support to help refugees adjust to the cultural norms and values of their new community. This can include workshops on cultural etiquette, customs, and traditions.
7. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that refugees have access to quality healthcare services, including mental health support. This is crucial for their overall well-being and ability to integrate into society.
8. Childcare and Education Support: Provide childcare and education support to help refugee families access educational opportunities and build a stable future.
9. Housing Assistance: Offer housing assistance, including temporary shelter and permanent housing options, to help refugees establish stability in their living situation.
