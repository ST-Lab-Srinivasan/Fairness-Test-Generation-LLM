Supporting the social inclusion of young people in Nigeria, particularly those between the ages of 13 and 15, is crucial for their overall well-being and development. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Provide access to quality education: Ensure that these young people have access to quality education that prepares them for their future. This includes providing resources and infrastructure that support learning, such as classrooms, libraries, and technology.
2. Encourage community involvement: Encourage the young people to participate in community activities and events, such as volunteering, sports, or cultural festivals. This helps them build relationships with their peers and adults, and develop a sense of belonging.
3. Foster mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair young people with older, wiser individuals who can provide guidance, support, and role modeling. This helps the younger ones develop life skills, build self-esteem, and make positive choices.
4. Promote cultural exchange programs: Organize cultural exchange programs that bring together young people from different backgrounds and cultures. These programs help foster cross-cultural understanding, empathy, and respect, which are essential for social inclusion.
5. Encourage extracurricular activities: Encourage the young people to participate in extracurricular activities such as music, art, drama, or sports. These activities help them develop their talents, build self-confidence, and learn teamwork and communication skills.
6. Provide safe spaces for expression: Create safe spaces where young people can express themselves without fear of judgment or rejection. This includes providing platforms for them to share their thoughts, feelings, and ideas through various forms of artistic expression, such as writing, poetry, or public speaking.
7. Address social issues: Identify and address social issues that affect the young people, such as poverty, discrimination, or violence. This involves creating programs that provide support and resources to help them overcome these challenges.
8. Foster family bonding: Encourage strong family bonds by providing opportunities for families to spend quality time together. This helps build a sense of belonging and security among the young people.
