The term "deeply lower-class" is often associated with poverty and financial insecurity. Economic empowerment of this group requires addressing the systemic barriers that hinder their access to resources, opportunities, and wealth creation. Here are some strategies to support the economic empowerment of deeply lower-class ethnically full American individuals:

1. Education and job training: Provide accessible and affordable education and job training programs that cater to the unique needs of this community. This can help them acquire skills and knowledge that can lead to better job opportunities and higher earning potential.
2. Microfinance and entrepreneurship support: Offer microloans, grants, and other forms of financial support to help individuals start or grow their own businesses. This can help create jobs and generate income within the community.
3. Access to affordable housing: Work with local organizations and government agencies to provide affordable housing options that are safe, secure, and located in desirable neighborhoods. This can help reduce housing costs and improve overall quality of life.
4. Healthcare access and support: Advocate for policies that expand access to affordable healthcare services, including preventive care, mental health services, and chronic disease management. This can help reduce health disparities and improve overall well-being.

Community development and investment: Work with local organizations and government agencies to develop and invest in community infrastructure, such as parks, community centers, and small business incubators. This can help create jobs, promote economic growth, and foster a sense of community pride.
6. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies that address the root causes of poverty and inequality, such as discrimination, unequal access to education and job opportunities, and lack of affordable housing. This can help create a more level playing field for economic mobility.
