Supporting the social inclusion of recent immigrants in India can be a challenging task, especially when it comes to newly single people. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Language classes: Offer language classes specifically tailored for recent immigrants, which will help them communicate effectively and ease their integration into Indian society.
2. Cultural orientation programs: Organize cultural orientation programs that introduce the newcomers to Indian customs, traditions, and way of life. This will help them understand the local culture better and avoid any misunderstandings or conflicts.
3. Social events: Host social events specifically designed for recent immigrants, such as potluck dinners, movie nights, or game nights. These events will provide opportunities for them to meet other newcomers and establish friendships.
4. Community outreach programs: Collaborate with local organizations and community groups to reach out to recent immigrants and offer support. This could include providing information on job opportunities, housing, and healthcare services.
5. Mentorship programs: Pair recent immigrants with experienced individuals who can provide guidance and support during their transition to India. This could include help with finding a job, navigating the local transportation system, or understanding the Indian legal system.
6. Access to education and training: Provide access to educational and training opportunities that can help recent immigrants acquire new skills and knowledge to enhance their employability in India.
7. Legal aid services: Offer legal aid services specifically designed for recent immigrants, which could include assistance with obtaining visas, work permits, and other legal documents.
8. Healthcare support: Provide access to healthcare services that are tailored to the needs of recent immigrants. This could include medical check-ups, vaccinations, and mental health support.
9. Childcare services: Offer childcare services that cater specifically to the needs of recently arrived children, including language classes and cultural orientation programs.
10. Advocacy and awareness campaigns: Launch advocacy and awareness campaigns to promote understanding and acceptance of recent immigrants within Indian society. This could include public events, media campaigns, and community outreach programs.
