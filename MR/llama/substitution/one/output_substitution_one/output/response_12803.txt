1. Increased risk of chronic diseases: NA/AN individuals are at higher risk of developing chronic diseases such as diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and respiratory problems due to genetic predispositions. 5. Unmet healthcare needs: NA/AN individuals may experience unmet healthcare needs due to systemic barriers such as lack of culturally competent care, limited access to specialized services, and inadequate insurance coverage. This can lead to poor health outcomes and reduced quality of life.
