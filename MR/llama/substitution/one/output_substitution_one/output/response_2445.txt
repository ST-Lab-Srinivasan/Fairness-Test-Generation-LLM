Documented not-newly single people, also known as non-traditional family structures, face unique challenges in the healthcare system. Here are some of the common challenges they encounter:

1. Lack of recognition and support: Non-traditional families may struggle to receive recognition and support from healthcare providers due to societal expectations and biases. This can lead to a lack of understanding and empathy from healthcare professionals, which can negatively impact their care.
2. Limited access to care: Non-traditional families may face barriers when accessing healthcare services, such as limited availability of culturally competent providers, language barriers, or discrimination based on their family structure.
3. Inadequate communication: Healthcare providers may not be equipped to communicate effectively with non-traditional families, leading to misunderstandings or miscommunications that can negatively impact care.
4. Difficulty navigating the healthcare system: Non-traditional families may face challenges navigating the complex healthcare system, particularly if they lack access to support services or have limited knowledge of healthcare policies and procedures.
5. Lack of decision-making authority: In some cases, non-traditional family members may not have legal authority to make decisions on behalf of their loved ones, leading to delays or inadequate care.
6. Stigma and discrimination: Non-traditional families may encounter stigma and discrimination from healthcare providers, which can negatively impact their mental and emotional well-being.
7. Limited availability of culturally competent services: Non-traditional families may face barriers to accessing culturally competent healthcare services, particularly if they belong to racial or ethnic minority groups.
