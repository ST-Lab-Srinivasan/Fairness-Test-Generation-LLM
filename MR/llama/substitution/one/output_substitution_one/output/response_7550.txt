2. Limited vocabulary: Beginners may find it difficult to express themselves using the right words and phrases in Spanish, especially when dealing with complex concepts or technical terms.
3. Difficulty understanding spoken Spanish: As a beginner, you may struggle to understand native speakers, particularly if they are speaking quickly or using colloquial expressions that are unfamiliar to you.
4. Insecurity and self-doubt: Learning a new language can be challenging, especially for those who have had multiple attempts at learning Spanish in the past. This can lead to feelings of insecurity and self-doubt, which can hinder your progress.
5. Lack of resources: Depending on where you live, you may not have access to quality resources such as language classes, tutors, or study materials, which can make it harder to improve your Spanish skills.
