1. Understanding and respecting the cultural differences and nuances among patients, families, and communities.
2. Being able to communicate effectively with patients from diverse backgrounds.
3. Recognizing and addressing any biases or stereotypes that may influence patient care.
4. Providing care that is sensitive to the patient's cultural beliefs and practices.
5. Understanding the impact of culture on health outcomes and health behaviors.
6. Being able to adapt treatment plans to meet the needs of patients from diverse backgrounds.
7. Recognizing and addressing any power imbalances that may exist in the patient-provider relationship.
