The impact of disability status on the lives of physically able-bodied people with disabled peers can vary greatly depending on a range of factors, including cultural and societal attitudes towards disability, individual experiences and expectations, and support systems. Here are some possible impacts:

1. Social isolation: People with disabilities may face social isolation due to discrimination, stigma, or lack of accessibility in their environment. This can lead to feelings of loneliness, depression, and low self-esteem.
2. Limited access to education and employment: Disabled individuals may encounter barriers to accessing educational resources and job opportunities, which can limit their career advancement and earning potential.
3. Health disparities: People with disabilities are at a higher risk of experiencing poor health outcomes due to limited access to healthcare services, inadequate health insurance coverage, and societal barriers that hinder their ability to maintain a healthy lifestyle.
4. Financial difficulties: Disabled individuals may face financial challenges due to reduced earning potential, increased expenses related to disability accommodations, and limited access to financial resources and support.
5. Limited social participation: Disabled individuals may experience reduced opportunities for social engagement, recreational activities, and community involvement due to societal barriers, stigma, or lack of accessibility.
6. Internalized oppression: Disabled individuals may internalize societal messages that they are less capable or less valuable than able-bodied individuals, leading to self-esteem issues, lowered expectations, and a decreased sense of self-worth.
7. Caregiver burden: Family members or caregivers of disabled individuals may experience emotional and financial burdens due to the increased caregiving responsibilities, which can impact their own mental and physical health.
8. Lack of representation and voice: Disabled individuals may be underrepresented in decision-making positions, leading to a lack of understanding and empathy from able-bodied individuals and institutions.
9. Inaccessible built environment: Disabled individuals may face barriers to accessing public spaces, transportation, and buildings due to inadequate accessibility features, which can limit their participation in society.
10. Stigma
