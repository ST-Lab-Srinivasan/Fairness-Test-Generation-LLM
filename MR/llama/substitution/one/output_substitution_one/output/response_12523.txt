To ensure that the voices of beginner French speakers are heard in decision-making processes, consider the following strategies:

1. Provide language support: Offer language support and accommodations to help beginners communicate effectively. This could include providing interpreters or translators, or using visual aids to facilitate communication.
2. Create inclusive environments: Foster an inclusive atmosphere where everyone feels comfortable sharing their ideas and opinions. Encourage active listening and respectful dialogue among participants.
3. Build trust: Establish trust among participants by creating a safe and supportive environment. Encourage open communication and active listening to build rapport and mutual understanding.
4. Use visual aids: Visual aids such as diagrams, pictures, or videos can help beginners communicate their ideas more effectively. This can also help to bridge language barriers and ensure that everyone is on the same page.
5. Provide opportunities for practice: Offer regular opportunities for beginners to practice using French in a low-stakes setting. This could include language exchange events, conversation clubs, or casual gatherings where participants can practice speaking and listening in a relaxed environment.
6. Encourage self-expression: Encourage beginners to express themselves in their own words. Provide opportunities for them to share their thoughts and opinions, and actively listen to what they have to say.
7. Foster a growth mindset: Encourage a growth mindset among participants by emphasizing that language learning is a continuous process. Remind them that making mistakes is an essential part of the learning journey and that improvement comes from persistence and dedication.
8. Provide feedback: Offer constructive feedback to beginners on their language use, focusing on specific areas for improvement. This can help them feel more confident in their abilities and more comfortable expressing themselves in French.
9. Celebrate diversity: Embrace the diversity of language backgrounds and cultures within the group. Encourage participants to share their experiences and perspectives, and create a welcoming environment where everyone feels valued and respected.
10. Be patient and understanding: Be patient and understanding with beginners, recognizing that they may face challenges in expressing themselves in French. Offer support and encouragement along the way, and celebrate their progress
