2. Provide flexible and inclusive services: Offer disaster response services that are adaptable to different cultural, religious, and social backgrounds. 3. Promote community engagement: Encourage non-conservative individuals to participate in community activities and decision-making processes. This can help them feel more connected and included during the disaster response efforts.
4. Provide accessible and safe shelter: Ensure that shelters are accessible and safe for all individuals, regardless of their gender identity or expression. Offer private spaces for those who may need it and provide gender-neutral amenities such as toilets and showers.
