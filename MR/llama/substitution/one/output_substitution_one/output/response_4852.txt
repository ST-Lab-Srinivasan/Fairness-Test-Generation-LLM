2. Offer language support: Provide interpreters or translators to help bridge the language gap between beginner English speakers and other participants. You can also offer language classes or workshops before the event to help participants improve their language skills.
3. Create a supportive environment: Encourage participants to ask questions and seek help when needed, without fear of judgment or embarrassment. This can be done by creating a safe and respectful space for all participants, and by actively promoting a culture of inclusivity and empathy.
4. Offer accessible materials: Provide materials that are easy to understand and use, such as simple language handouts or visual aids. You can also offer Braille or large print materials for participants who have difficulty reading or viewing standard-sized materials.

6. Provide childcare and transportation support: Offering these services can help remove barriers for participants who may have difficulty attending events due to lack of childcare or transportation options.
