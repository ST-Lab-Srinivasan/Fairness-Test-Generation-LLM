1. Education and job training programs: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that teach skills relevant to in-demand industries, such as healthcare, technology, and renewable energy. This will help individuals acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market and advance their careers.
2. Microfinance and small business development: Offer microfinance opportunities and support for Native Americans to start or grow their own businesses. This can include loans, grants, and mentorship programs that help individuals build credit, access capital, and develop entrepreneurial skills.
3. Apprenticeships and vocational training: Provide apprenticeship and vocational training opportunities in fields such as construction, manufacturing, and agriculture. This will help individuals gain hands-on experience and skills that can lead to well-paying jobs and careers.
4. Access to capital: Work with community banks and financial institutions to provide access to affordable capital for Native American entrepreneurs and small business owners. This can include loans, grants, and other forms of financing that help individuals start or grow their businesses.
5. Economic development initiatives: Support economic development initiatives that create jobs, stimulate economic growth, and promote entrepreneurship in Native American communities. This can include projects related to renewable energy, sustainable agriculture, and ecotourism, which can provide income-generating opportunities while also promoting cultural preservation and environmental stewardship.

This can include reforms related to land ownership, natural resource management, and tribal sovereignty, which can help to create more equitable economic opportunities and reduce poverty.
