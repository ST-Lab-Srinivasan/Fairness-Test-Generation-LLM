Refugees who have recently resettled in the United States often face significant challenges when trying to access healthcare services. Here are some of the common challenges faced by low-income refugees in the US healthcare system:

1. Language Barriers: Many refugees may not speak English fluently, which can create barriers when trying to communicate with healthcare providers or navigate the healthcare system.
2. Cultural Differences: Refugees may have different cultural beliefs and practices that can affect their healthcare needs and preferences. For example, some cultures may have different attitudes towards mental health or may have traditional healing practices that are not recognized in the US healthcare system.
3. Lack of Knowledge: Refugees may not be familiar with the US healthcare system and its structures, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the system and access the care they need.
4. Cost: Many refugees face financial barriers when trying to access healthcare services in the US. They may not have health insurance or the financial resources to pay for medical care.
5. Limited Access: Refugees may face challenges when trying to access healthcare services due to limited transportation options, childcare responsibilities, or other factors that can limit their ability to seek medical care.
6. Trauma: Many refugees have experienced trauma in their home countries or during their migration journey, which can affect their mental and physical health. They may need specialized care and support to address these issues.
7. Stigma: Refugees may face stigma and discrimination in the US, which can affect their willingness to seek medical care or disclose their medical history.
8. Lack of Trust: Refugees may not trust the healthcare system or healthcare providers due to past experiences or cultural beliefs. Building trust and rapport with refugee patients is essential for providing high-quality care.
