Supporting the social inclusion of newly retired or aged 65 and older individuals can be achieved through various means. Here are some strategies that can help promote their social inclusion:

1. Age-Friendly Communities: Encourage the development of age-friendly communities that cater to the needs of older adults, such as accessible transportation, affordable housing, and social opportunities.
2. Intergenerational Programs: Organize intergenerational programs that bring together younger and older generations, fostering mutual understanding, respect, and learning. Examples include mentorship programs, community service projects, or cultural events.
3. Social Connection: Encourage social connection by facilitating opportunities for older adults to engage in activities they enjoy, such as volunteering, gardening, or joining clubs focused on shared interests.
4. Technology Training: Offer technology training sessions to help older adults learn how to use digital tools and stay connected with loved ones through video calls, messaging apps, or social media platforms.
5. Health and Wellness Programs: Provide access to health and wellness programs that promote physical activity, mental well-being, and overall health. Examples include exercise classes, meditation sessions, or health workshops.
6. Support Groups: Facilitate support groups for older adults who may be experiencing social isolation or loneliness. These groups can provide a safe space to share experiences, offer emotional support, and foster connections with others.
7. Volunteering Opportunities: Encourage older adults to engage in volunteering opportunities within their communities. This not only helps address social issues but also provides opportunities for social interaction, learning, and personal growth.
8. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach efforts to promote social inclusion among older adults. This can involve collaborating with local organizations, community centers, or places of worship to host events or activities that bring people together.
9. Education and Advocacy: Advocate for policies and programs that support the social inclusion of older adults. This can include pushing for age-friendly legislation, funding initiatives, or raising awareness about the importance of intergenerational connections.
