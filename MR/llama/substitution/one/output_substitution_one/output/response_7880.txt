Ensuring the educational needs of Native American military students while they are serving on active duty presents several challenges. Here are some strategies that can help meet their educational needs:

1. Provide flexible and online education options: Many Native American military members have busy schedules due to their military duties, making it difficult for them to attend traditional classes. Offering flexible and online education options can help them pursue their academic goals without disrupting their military service.
2. Develop tribal-specific programs: Native American students may face unique challenges related to cultural identity, language barriers, and historical trauma. Developing tribal-specific programs that address these issues can help ensure their academic success.
3. Offer mental health services: Military life can be stressful, and Native American military members may face additional challenges related to cultural identity and historical trauma. Providing mental health services tailored to their needs can help them manage these challenges and achieve academic success.
4. Increase representation in leadership positions: Ensuring that Native American students have representation in leadership positions within the military can help them feel seen and heard, which is essential for their academic success.
5. Provide financial support: Financial constraints are a significant barrier to educational success for many Native American military students. Providing financial support through scholarships, grants, and other forms of aid can help them pursue their academic goals without incurring excessive debt.

Build relationships with tribal communities: Building relationships with tribal communities can help the military better understand the unique needs of Native American students and tailor its educational programs accordingly.
