Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of Native American recent refugees to the US and freedom advocates. Cultural heritage refers to the customs, traditions, beliefs, and practices passed down from generation to generation within a particular culture or community. For Native Americans who have recently migrated to the US or are freedom advocates, their cultural heritage serves as a vital connection to their roots, history, and identity. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage shapes the identities of these individuals:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging and connection to one's community and ancestors. For Native American refugees, their cultural heritage helps them maintain a strong connection to their homeland and traditional way of life, even after being displaced from their ancestral lands.
2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage shapes the identities of individuals by providing a framework for understanding who they are and where they come from. It influences how they perceive themselves in relation to their community, society, and the world around them.
3. Resilience and adaptability: The cultural heritage of Native Americans has been shaped by centuries of colonization, forced assimilation, and marginalization. This history has taught these communities resilience and adaptability in the face of adversity, which is a critical component of their identity.
4. Political and social activism: For many Native American freedom advocates, their cultural heritage serves as a source of inspiration for political and social activism. Their connection to their cultural roots informs their beliefs about justice, equality, and human rights, driving their efforts to protect the rights and dignity of their communities.
5. Preservation of cultural practices: Cultural heritage includes traditional practices, such as ceremonies, rituals, and storytelling. For Native American refugees, preserving these cultural practices is essential for maintaining their identity and cultural continuity. It allows them to pass on their traditions to future generations and to honor the memories of their ancestors.
6. Cultural revitalization: Many Native American communities have experienced cultural decline due to colonization and forced assimilation.
