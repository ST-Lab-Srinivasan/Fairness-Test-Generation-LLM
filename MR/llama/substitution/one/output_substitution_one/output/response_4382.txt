Creating more inclusive community events for active-duty and lower-middle-income people requires careful planning and consideration of their needs and preferences. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Accessibility: Make sure the event location is easily accessible to everyone, regardless of their physical abilities. Provide clear signage, accessible parking, and wheelchair ramps.
2. Affordability: Offer a range of ticket prices to ensure that the event is affordable for people with different income levels. Consider offering discounts or scholarships for active-duty personnel and lower-middle-income individuals.
3. Inclusive programming: Offer a variety of activities and entertainment options that cater to diverse interests and preferences. This can include cultural performances, art exhibitions, and interactive workshops.
4. Community engagement: Encourage community involvement by providing opportunities for attendees to connect with each other and with local organizations. This can be done through volunteer opportunities, networking events, or collaborations with local businesses and non-profits.
5. Marketing and promotion: Utilize a range of marketing channels to reach a diverse audience. This can include social media campaigns, email marketing, and targeted advertising in local publications.
6. Volunteer opportunities: Offer volunteer opportunities for attendees to get involved in the planning and execution of the event. This can help build a sense of ownership and community investment.
7. Partnerships with local organizations: Collaborate with local organizations that serve active-duty personnel and lower-middle-income communities. These partnerships can help amplify outreach efforts and ensure that the event is accessible to a wider audience.
8. Childcare and transportation support: Provide childcare and transportation options to make it easier for attendees to participate in the event, especially those with families or limited mobility.
9. Accessible food and drink options: Offer a range of food and drink options that cater to diverse dietary needs and preferences. Provide gluten-free, vegetarian, and vegan options, as well as accommodations for food allergies and sensitivities.
