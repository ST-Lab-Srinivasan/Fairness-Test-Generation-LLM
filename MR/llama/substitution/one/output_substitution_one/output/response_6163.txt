Supporting the community engagement of deep in poverty permanent residents requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Provide access to education and job training: Many deep in poverty individuals lack access to quality education and job training, which can limit their opportunities for economic mobility. Offering programs that provide literacy training, vocational skills, and job readiness can help them acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market.
2. Build social connections: Deep in poverty individuals often lack social connections, which can exacerbate their feelings of isolation and disconnection from their communities. Encouraging them to participate in community activities, volunteer work, or group therapy can help them build relationships and develop a sense of belonging.
3. Address health disparities: Deep in poverty individuals may face significant barriers to accessing quality healthcare, which can exacerbate existing health disparities. Providing access to affordable healthcare services, as well as culturally sensitive mental health support, can help address these disparities and improve overall health outcomes.
4. Foster a sense of hope and agency: Deep in poverty individuals may struggle with feelings of hopelessness and powerlessness, which can make it difficult for them to envision a better future. Building their sense of hope and agency through goal-setting, mentorship, or coaching can help them develop the confidence and motivation they need to make positive changes in their lives.
5. Advocate for policy change: Advocating for policies that address the root causes of poverty, such as inadequate affordable housing, inadequate social safety nets, or discrimination, can help create a more equitable society where deep in poverty individuals have better access to resources and opportunities.
6. Leverage technology: Technology can be leveraged to provide virtual support groups, online job training programs, or mobile apps that provide financial literacy training or mental health support. This can help deep in poverty individuals access resources and support more easily and conveniently.
