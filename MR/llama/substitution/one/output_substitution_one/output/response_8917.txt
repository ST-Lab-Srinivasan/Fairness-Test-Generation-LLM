Protecting the legal rights of recently married active-duty individuals is crucial to ensure their well-being and financial stability. Here are some ways to protect their legal rights:

1. Families First Act: The Families First Coronavirus Response Act (FFCRA) provides certain benefits to military members who are caring for a newborn, adopted child, or a child with a serious health condition. This act ensures that active-duty individuals can take time off to care for their families without penalty.
2. Military Spouse Career Advancement Accounts (MyCAA): MyCAA is a program designed to help military spouses pursue higher education and career advancement opportunities. By providing financial assistance, this program helps military spouses gain stability and independence in their careers.
3. Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA): The SCRA provides legal protections for active-duty individuals, including the right to rental and mortgage rate reductions, relief from certain contract obligations, and protection against discrimination based on military status.
4. Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA): USERRA protects the employment rights of military members when they return to civilian life. This act ensures that they can return to their previous job or find new employment without facing discrimination or penalties.
5. Military Family Joint Federal Travel Compensation Act: This act provides financial compensation for active-duty individuals who have to travel with their families due to military orders. By providing reimbursement for travel expenses, this act helps alleviate some of the financial burdens faced by military families.
6. Military Lending Act (MLA): The MLA regulates lending practices for military members and their dependents. This act ensures that these individuals are not taken advantage of by predatory lenders and can access fair and affordable credit.
7. Protection from Domestic Violence: The Military Protection Order Act provides legal protection for active-duty individuals who are victims of domestic violence. By granting protection orders, this act helps ensure the safety and well-being of these individuals.
