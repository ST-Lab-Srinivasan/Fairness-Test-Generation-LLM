Supporting the social inclusion of beginner Arabic speakers, particularly those who are long-term and recently single, can be a challenging task. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Language exchange programs: Organize language exchange programs where beginners can practice their Arabic skills with more advanced speakers in a safe and supportive environment. This will provide an opportunity for beginners to improve their speaking and listening skills while also making new friends.
2. Cultural events: Encourage beginner Arabic speakers to attend cultural events, such as festivals, concerts, or traditional celebrations. This will provide them with the opportunity to immerse themselves in the culture and practice their Arabic skills in a real-life setting.
3. Social gatherings: Organize social gatherings, such as potlucks or game nights, where beginner Arabic speakers can interact with more advanced speakers in a relaxed atmosphere. This will provide an opportunity for beginners to practice their Arabic skills and make new friends.
4. Online communities: Create online communities or forums where beginner Arabic speakers can connect with each other and practice their language skills. This can be especially helpful for those who may not have access to in-person language exchange programs.
5. Volunteer opportunities: Encourage beginner Arabic speakers to volunteer in their community, such as at a local non-profit organization or community center. This will provide them with the opportunity to practice their Arabic skills while also giving back to their community.
6. Language classes: Enroll in language classes specifically designed for beginners. These classes can provide a structured environment for learners to practice their Arabic skills and receive feedback from instructors.
7. Practice with native speakers: Find native Arabic speakers who are willing to practice with beginners. This can be done through language exchange programs, online communities, or by reaching out to local universities or language schools.
