Supporting the economic empowerment of Native American recent refugees to the US who are living in deep poverty requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the unique challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Capacity Building and Training: Provide training and capacity building programs for Native American refugees in areas such as entrepreneurship, small business management, and job readiness. This will help them develop skills that can help them start their own businesses or find better-paying jobs.
2. Access to Financial Services: Many Native American recent refugees face barriers to accessing financial services such as credit, savings, and insurance. Provide access to financial institutions and offer financial literacy programs to help them manage their finances effectively.
3. Mentorship and Networking: Connect them with successful business owners and professionals in their communities who can serve as mentors and provide guidance on how to navigate the economic landscape. This will help them build a network of support and learn from the experiences of others.
4. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that they have access to quality healthcare services, including mental health services, which are critical for their overall well-being and ability to succeed in the workforce.
5. Education and Training: Provide access to education and training programs that help them develop skills relevant to the job market, such as computer literacy, language training, and vocational training.
6. Job Placement Services: Offer job placement services that connect them with employers who are willing to hire and train them, and provide ongoing support to help them succeed in their new jobs.
7. Microfinance and Loans: Provide microfinance options such as small loans or grants to help them start their own businesses or invest in education or job training.
8. Childcare Support: Many Native American recent refugees face challenges related to childcare, which can make it difficult for them to focus on their economic empowerment. Provide support for childcare services or financial assistance to help them cover the costs of childcare.
9. Housing and Shelter: Ensure that they have access to safe and affordable housing, which is critical for their overall well-being and ability to succeed in the workforce.
