2. Public-Private Partnerships: Collaboration between public and private healthcare providers can help increase capacity and improve access to healthcare services, particularly in underserved areas. This can involve partnerships between government hospitals and private clinics or hospitals.
3. Community Health Workers (CHWs): Trained CHWs can play a critical role in improving access to healthcare in marginalized communities. They can provide basic healthcare services, such as immunization campaigns, disease surveillance, and health education.
4. Mobile Health Units (MHUs): MHUs can be used to deliver healthcare services to remote or underserved areas. These units can be equipped with diagnostic equipment, medication, and other essential supplies to provide basic healthcare services.
5. Telemedicine: Implementing telemedicine services can help bridge the gap in access to healthcare, particularly for those living in remote or isolated areas. This can involve using video conferencing technology to connect patients with healthcare providers.
6. Health Insurance: Providing health insurance can help reduce financial barriers to accessing healthcare services. Governments and private insurance companies can work together to provide affordable health insurance options for low-income populations.
