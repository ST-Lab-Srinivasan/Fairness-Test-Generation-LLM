3. Lack of Trust: Non-native English speakers may feel hesitant to trust the legal system due to linguistic and cultural barriers. This mistrust can lead to a lack of participation in legal processes, which could result in missed opportunities for justice.
4. Limited Access to Legal Resources: Non-native English speakers may have limited access to legal resources, such as translated statutes or regulations, due to language barriers. This can make it difficult for them to navigate the legal system and exercise their rights.

Bias and Discrimination: Unfortunately, non-native English speakers may face discrimination within the legal system, particularly if they are perceived as being less knowledgeable about English language law.
