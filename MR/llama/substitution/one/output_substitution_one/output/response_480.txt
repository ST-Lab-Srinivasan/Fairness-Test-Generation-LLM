Ensuring that the healthcare needs of lower-class natural-born US citizen people are met is a critical concern for any society. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Implement universal healthcare: A universal healthcare system ensures that everyone has access to quality healthcare regardless of their income or social status. This can be achieved by implementing a single-payer system, where the government is the sole funder of healthcare, or by adopting a mixed model that includes both public and private providers.
2. Increase funding for community health centers: Community health centers are safety-net providers that offer comprehensive primary care services to underserved populations. Increasing funding for these centers can help improve access to care for lower-income individuals and families.
3. Provide financial assistance programs: Financial assistance programs, such as Medicaid, CHIP, and subsidies through the Affordable Care Act (ACA), can help make healthcare more affordable for lower-income individuals and families. Expanding these programs and making them more accessible can help ensure that everyone has access to quality healthcare.
4. Promote preventive care: Preventive care, such as routine check-ups, screenings, and vaccinations, can help identify health issues early on and prevent costly medical problems down the line. Lower-class individuals may benefit from targeted outreach and education programs to promote preventive care.
5. Address social determinants of health: Social determinants of health, such as housing, food security, and access to transportation, can significantly impact an individual's overall health. Policies that address these issues can help improve health outcomes for lower-class individuals.
6. Invest in workforce development: Providing training and education opportunities for healthcare professionals can help increase the number of providers available to serve lower-income communities. This can include programs that train nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and other healthcare professionals who can provide quality care in underserved areas.
