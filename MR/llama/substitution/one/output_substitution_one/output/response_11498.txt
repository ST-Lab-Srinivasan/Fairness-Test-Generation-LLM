Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of native English-speaking leftists, as it shapes their worldview, values, and beliefs. Here are some ways cultural heritage influences leftist individuals:

1. Critique of dominant culture: Leftists often view the dominant culture as oppressive and exploitative, and see cultural heritage as a way to challenge and subvert this system. By examining their own cultural background and history, leftists can identify and critique the ways in which power operates through cultural institutions.
2. Intersectionality: Leftists recognize that cultural heritage is intersectional, meaning that it is shaped by multiple factors such as race, gender, class, sexuality, and disability. By examining these intersections, leftists can identify how cultural heritage perpetuates inequality and work towards a more inclusive and equitable society.
3. Resistance and protest: Cultural heritage can be used as a tool for resistance and protest against systems of oppression. For example, leftists may use cultural artifacts or symbols to challenge dominant ideologies or to promote alternative perspectives.
4. Community building: Cultural heritage can serve as a means of building community and fostering solidarity among leftists. By sharing cultural experiences and traditions, leftists can create a sense of shared identity and purpose.
5. Personal identity: For many leftists, their cultural heritage is an important part of their personal identity. It shapes their values, beliefs, and worldview, and provides a sense of grounding and connection to their roots.
6. Political education: Cultural heritage can be used as a tool for political education, helping leftists understand the historical and contemporary issues that shape their society. By examining cultural artifacts and practices, leftists can gain insight into the ways in which power operates through cultural institutions.
7. Resistance to neoliberalism: Many leftists see cultural heritage as a way to resist the neoliberal policies that have led to inequality, exploitation, and cultural homogenization. By promoting alternative forms of cultural expression and preservation, leftists can challenge the dominant economic and political systems.
