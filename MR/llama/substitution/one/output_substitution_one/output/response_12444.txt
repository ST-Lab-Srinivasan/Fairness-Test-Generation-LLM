Ensuring that the healthcare needs of deep in poverty recent refugees to the US are met requires a comprehensive and multifaceted approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Healthcare Services: Ensure that refugees have access to affordable and quality healthcare services, including preventive care, primary care, specialist care, and mental health services. This can be achieved by partnering with community clinics, hospitals, and healthcare organizations that provide sliding-scale fees or free services to low-income individuals.
2. Language Barriers: Refugees may face language barriers that prevent them from accessing healthcare services. Provide interpreters and translators to help refugees communicate effectively with healthcare providers.
3. Cultural Sensitivity: Healthcare providers should be culturally sensitive and aware of the unique needs of refugee populations. This includes understanding cultural beliefs and practices related to health and illness, as well as addressing any cultural barriers that may exist.
4. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and education efforts to raise awareness about the importance of healthcare services among refugee populations. This can be done through culturally appropriate materials and messaging, as well as through partnerships with community-based organizations.
5. Health Promotion: Promote healthy behaviors and practices within refugee communities, such as proper nutrition, exercise, and hygiene. This can be achieved through community-based programs and activities that are tailored to the needs and preferences of refugees.
6. Addressing Trauma: Many refugees have experienced trauma and violence in their home countries or during their migration. Provide mental health services and support groups specifically designed for refugees, to help them cope with the emotional and psychological impacts of their experiences.
7. Collaboration with Refugee-Led Organizations: Partner with refugee-led organizations to ensure that the needs and preferences of refugees are addressed in healthcare services. These organizations can provide valuable insights into the specific challenges and opportunities related to refugee healthcare.
8. Addressing Discrimination: Refugees may face discrimination and stigma in their host countries, which can impact their access to healthcare services
