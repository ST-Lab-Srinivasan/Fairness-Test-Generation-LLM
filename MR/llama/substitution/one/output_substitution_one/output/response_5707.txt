Ensuring that the healthcare needs of financially disadvantaged native English speakers are met requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the social, economic, and cultural factors that impact their access to healthcare. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Expand Medicaid and other public insurance programs: Financial disadvantage is often a barrier to accessing healthcare. Expanding Medicaid and other public insurance programs can help provide coverage for low-income individuals, including native English speakers.
2. Provide financial assistance programs: Many hospitals and clinics offer financial assistance programs to help patients cover the cost of care. These programs may provide discounts, payment plans, or even free care to eligible patients.
3. Implement sliding fee scales: Some healthcare providers offer sliding fee scales based on income level. This means that patients who have a lower income pay less for care, while those with higher incomes pay more.
4. Increase funding for community health centers: Community health centers are an important source of primary care for underserved populations. Increasing funding for these centers can help ensure that they have the resources they need to provide high-quality care to native English speakers who may struggle financially.
5. Provide culturally competent care: Native English speakers from diverse backgrounds may have different healthcare needs and preferences. Providers must be aware of these differences and provide care that is culturally competent and sensitive to patients' unique needs.
6. Address language barriers: For native English speakers who are non-English speakers or limited English proficient, language barriers can prevent them from accessing healthcare. Providing interpreters and translators can help address these barriers and ensure that patients receive the care they need.
7. Improve transportation accessibility: Transportation is a significant barrier to healthcare for many financially disadvantaged individuals, including native English speakers. Providing affordable and accessible transportation options can help ensure that patients can get to appointments and receive the care they need.
