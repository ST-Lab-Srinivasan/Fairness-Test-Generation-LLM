Promoting the economic empowerment of Native English Speaker (NES) communities is essential to address the existing disparities in wealth and opportunities. Here are some ways to promote economic empowerment for NES communities:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills training programs that cater specifically to the needs of NES communities. This can help bridge the gap in educational attainment and job market access between NES communities and other marginalized groups.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship among NES communities by providing access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities within these communities.
3. Access to Capital: Address the historical disparities in access to capital by providing financial resources specifically for NES communities. This can help them start businesses, invest in infrastructure, and generate wealth.
4. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): Establish CDFIs that target NES communities with affordable loans, venture capital, and other financial services. This can help create economic opportunities and address the wealth gap between NES communities and other marginalized groups.
5. Public-Private Partnerships: Foster partnerships between government agencies, private sector companies, and NES communities to develop infrastructure, create jobs, and promote economic growth.
6. Capacity Building: Provide training and technical assistance to NES communities to help them build their capacity for economic development. This can include skills training, business planning, and access to market information.
7. Community Land Trusts (CLT): Establish CLTs that provide affordable land ownership options for NES communities. This can help preserve affordable housing stock, promote community control over land use, and address gentrification concerns.
8. Cooperative Ownership: Encourage cooperative ownership models that allow NES communities to collectively own and control economic assets. This can help promote community wealth-building and address issues of inequality.
9. Economic Development Planning: Engage in inclusive economic development planning processes that prioritize the needs and aspirations of NES communities. This can help ensure that economic development initiatives are responsive to local needs and priorities.
