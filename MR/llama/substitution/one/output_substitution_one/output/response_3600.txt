Supporting the economic empowerment of refugees in the United States is crucial for their well-being and integration into society. While it is important to provide assistance to wealthy individuals, it is equally essential to focus on supporting those who are not wealthy or multimillionaire. Here are some ways to support the economic empowerment of recent refugees in the US:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many refugees face challenges in accessing financial services due to their lack of documentation, limited English proficiency, and cultural barriers. Providing access to affordable bank accounts, microfinance loans, and other financial tools can help them manage their finances and build credit history.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Refugees often have valuable skills and experiences that they can leverage into starting their own businesses. Offering training, mentorship, and resources to help them develop entrepreneurial ideas and turn them into successful ventures can create jobs and economic opportunities for themselves and others in their communities.
3. Job Training and Placement: Refugees often need job training and placement services to help them find employment in the US labor market. This can involve language training, vocational skills development, and connecting them with potential employers who are willing to hire refugees.
4. Small Business Grants and Loans: Providing small business grants or loans to recent refugees can help them start or expand their businesses. These funds can be used for various purposes such as purchasing inventory, renting a storefront, or hiring employees.
5. Tax Credits and Incentives: The US government offers various tax credits and incentives that can benefit recent refugees. For example, the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) provides financial assistance to low-income workers, including refugees who are employed or self-employed.
6. Mentorship and Networking: Connecting recent refugees with successful entrepreneurs and professionals in their field can provide valuable mentorship and networking opportunities. This can help them navigate the US business landscape, build relationships, and gain insights into best practices.
