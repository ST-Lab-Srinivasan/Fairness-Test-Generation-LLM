Supporting the economic empowerment of recent refugees to the US who are not deep in poverty can be achieved through a variety of strategies. Here are some potential approaches:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many recent refugees face barriers to accessing financial services such as bank accounts, loans, and insurance. Organizations like the Refugee and Immigrant Center for Education and Legal Services (RAICES) offer financial literacy training and support in accessing financial services.
2. Job Training and Placement: Organizations like the International Rescue Committee (IRC) provide job training and placement services to help refugees develop skills and find employment. This can include language training, computer skills, and industry-specific training.
3. Microfinance Programs: Microfinance programs, such as those offered by the Global Economic Growth Initiative (GEGI), provide small loans and other financial services to help refugees start or expand their businesses.
4. Entrepreneurship Training: Organizations like the Refugee Entrepreneurship Program (REP) offer training and support for refugees who want to start their own businesses. This can include mentorship, networking opportunities, and access to funding.
5. Networking and Mentorship: Connecting recent refugees with successful professionals in their field can help them build relationships and gain valuable insights into the US job market. Organizations like the National Association of Latino Professionals and Business Owners (NAPBO) offer networking opportunities specifically for refugees and immigrants.
6. Education and Training: Providing access to education and training can help recent refugees build skills and qualifications that are in demand in the US job market. Organizations like the International Rescue Committee (IRC) offer literacy and language classes, as well as vocational training in fields like construction, healthcare, and technology.
