2. Cost of healthcare: Healthcare in Nigeria can be expensive, especially for middle-class families who may struggle to afford quality medical care.
3. Lack of qualified healthcare professionals: Nigeria faces a shortage of qualified healthcare professionals, particularly in rural areas, which can result in poor-quality care.
4. Limited availability of essential medications and medical supplies: Some areas of Nigeria have limited access to essential medications and medical supplies, making it difficult for patients to receive proper treatment.
5. Cultural beliefs and practices: In some parts of Nigeria, cultural beliefs and practices can influence healthcare decisions, such as the use of traditional medicine or the avoidance of modern medical care.
6. Lack of trust in the healthcare system: Some Nigerians may lack trust in the healthcare system due to historical trauma, such as the mistreatment of patients in the past.
7. Limited access to specialized care: Nigeria has a shortage of specialists in many areas, making it difficult for patients to receive specialized care.
8. Inadequate infrastructure: Many healthcare facilities in Nigeria lack basic infrastructure, such as electricity and clean water, which can hinder the delivery of quality care.
