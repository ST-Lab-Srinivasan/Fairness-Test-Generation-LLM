Creating more inclusive cultural programs for Native Spanish speaker, ethnically full American individuals involves understanding and valuing their unique experiences and perspectives. Here are some strategies to help make cultural programs more inclusive:

1. Consult with Community Members: Reach out to Native Spanish speakers in the community and gather input on what they would like to see in a cultural program. This will help ensure that the program is culturally sensitive and relevant to their needs.
2. Offer Multilingual Programming: Provide programming that is offered in both English and Spanish to cater to the diverse language needs of the community.
3. Incorporate Indigenous Art and Culture: Showcase the rich cultural heritage of Native American communities through art, music, dance, and other forms of expression. This can help create a sense of belonging and connection to their cultural identity.
4. Provide Accessibility Accommodations: Ensure that all cultural programs are accessible to individuals with disabilities by providing accommodations such as American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation, audio descriptions, or braille materials.
5. Foster Intercultural Dialogue: Encourage intercultural dialogue and exchange between Native Spanish speakers and other communities through cultural events, workshops, and discussions. This can help promote mutual understanding and respect.
6. Create a Welcoming Environment: Designate spaces for Native Spanish speakers to gather and connect with one another without feeling isolated or marginalized. Provide comfortable and accessible seating, adequate lighting, and appropriate temperature settings.
7. Celebrate Indigenous Traditions: Incorporate traditional Native American practices and rituals into cultural programs, such as smudging ceremonies, blessings, or traditional foods. This can help create a sense of belonging and connection to their cultural heritage.
8. Provide Education and Resources: Offer educational resources and workshops on the history, language, and culture of Native American communities. This can help promote cultural competency and understanding among staff and community members.
9. Engage Community Leaders: Collaborate with community leaders and elders to ensure that cultural programs are culturally appropriate and respectful of Native American traditions.
