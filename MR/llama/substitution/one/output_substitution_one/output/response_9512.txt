Introduction:
Marriage is a significant life event that can have a profound impact on an individual's life. When a marriage ends, whether through divorce or death, it can be a challenging and emotional experience for the individual involved. Recent studies have shown that the impact of marital status on the lives of recently single moderate individuals can be significant and far-reaching. This paper will explore these impacts in detail.

Social Isolation:
One of the most significant impacts of marital status on recently single moderate individuals is social isolation. Studies have shown that individuals who are newly single may experience a decline in social connections and a decrease in overall social support (Trawick-Smith, 2016). This can lead to feelings of loneliness and disconnection from society, which can negatively impact mental health and well-being.

Financial Strain:
Financial strain is another common impact of marital status on recently single moderate individuals. Studies have shown that divorce can result in a significant decrease in income, as well as an increase in expenses related to maintaining two households (Ward, 2017). This financial strain can lead to stress and anxiety, which can negatively impact mental health.

Emotional Distress:
The emotional impact of marital status on recently single moderate individuals cannot be overstated. Studies have shown that the loss of a spouse or the end of a marriage can lead to significant emotional distress, including depression, anxiety, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) (Komisaruk & Strauss, 2017). This emotional distress can impact an individual's ability to function in daily life and maintain relationships.

Studies have shown that social support from friends, family, and mental health professionals can significantly improve mental health outcomes in individuals who are newly single (Cohen et al., 2015).
