1. The Refugee and Immigrant Center for Education and Legal Services (RAICES) - Based in San Antonio, Texas, RAICES provides free and low-cost legal services to immigrant and refugee communities, including those from Latin America, Africa, and the Middle East. They also offer education and advocacy programs to support their clients' rights and well-being.
2. The International Rescue Committee (IRC) - With locations throughout the US, the IRC provides a range of services to refugees and immigrants, including emergency assistance, housing, and job training. They also work to advance policy changes that benefit refugee and immigrant communities.
3. The National Immigration Law Center (NILC) - Based in Los Angeles, NILC is a non-profit organization that advocates for the rights of low-income immigrants and their families. They work to advance policies that promote economic and social justice, including those that benefit refugees and asylum seekers.
4. The Asian American Legal Defense and Education Fund (AALDEF) - Based in New York City, AALDEF is a non-profit organization that protects the rights of Asian Americans and other marginalized communities. They provide legal services, advocacy, and education to advance social justice and human rights.
5. The African Community Development Foundation (ACDF) - Based in Minneapolis, Minnesota, ACDF works to empower African immigrant and refugee communities through education, economic development, and community engagement. They provide job training, mentorship, and small business support to help their clients achieve success in the US.
6.
