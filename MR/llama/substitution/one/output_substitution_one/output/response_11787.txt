Addressing the specific needs of bereaved Native English Speaker (NES) individuals in education requires a comprehensive and culturally sensitive approach. Here are some strategies that educators and administrators can implement to support these students:

1. Cultural sensitivity training for teachers: Provide cultural sensitivity training for teachers to help them understand the unique needs of NES students and how to create an inclusive learning environment. This can include understanding the cultural norms, values, and beliefs of NES students and their families.
2. Diverse curriculum: Incorporate diverse perspectives and experiences into the curriculum to help NES students feel seen and represented. This can include including literature, art, music, and other cultural expressions that reflect the diversity of NES communities.
3. Support groups: Establish support groups for NES students where they can connect with their peers and receive emotional support. These groups can be facilitated by trained counselors or teachers who are familiar with the needs of NES students.
4. One-on-one support: Provide one-on-one support to NES students who may be struggling academically or emotionally due to their loss. This can include individualized tutoring, counseling, and mentoring.
5. Family engagement: Engage with the families of NES students to understand their needs and concerns. This can include providing information sessions on how to support their children during this difficult time, as well as involving parents in decision-making processes related to their child's education.
6. Language support: Provide language support services for NES students who may be struggling with the dominant language of instruction in the school. This can include language interpretation and translation services, as well as bilingual or multilingual instructional materials.
7. Academic accommodations: Provide academic accommodations to NES students who are struggling due to their loss. This can include extended time to complete assignments, flexible scheduling, and additional support in the classroom.
8. Mental health resources: Provide mental health resources for NES students who may be experiencing anxiety, depression, or other mental health concerns related to their loss. This can include counseling services and referrals to outside mental health professionals.
