2. Identity-based instruction: Provide opportunities for students to explore and express their multiple identities through various forms of expression, such as writing, art, music, or drama. This can help students connect with their heritage and cultural backgrounds in a meaningful way.
3. 4. Safe spaces for expression: Create safe and inclusive learning environments where students can express themselves without fear of judgment or marginalization. This can involve creating designated spaces for students to share their experiences and cultural practices, such as cultural clubs or affinity groups.
5. Family engagement: Involve parents and guardians in the educational process by providing opportunities for them to learn about their children's cultural backgrounds and how they can support their academic success.
