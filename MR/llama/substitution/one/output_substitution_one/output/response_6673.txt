To ensure that the educational needs of beginner Arabic speakers, including those who are not long-time single students, are met, you can consider the following strategies:

1. Offer a variety of teaching methods: Beginner Arabic learners may have different learning styles and preferences. Therefore, it's essential to offer a range of teaching methods, such as live classes, online courses, podcasts, and language exchange programs. This will cater to different learning styles and make the learning experience more engaging and effective.
2. Provide flexible scheduling options: Beginner Arabic learners may have busy schedules or conflicting commitments. Offering flexible scheduling options, such as weekend classes or online courses with recorded lectures, can help accommodate their needs.
3. Use interactive and engaging materials: Incorporate interactive and engaging materials, such as language games, cultural events, and social activities, into the learning experience. This will help learners stay motivated and engaged in the learning process.
4. Offer personalized feedback: Beginner Arabic learners may need additional support and feedback to improve their language skills. Provide personalized feedback on their progress and offer suggestions for improvement to help them achieve their goals.
5. Encourage peer-to-peer learning: Pair beginner Arabic learners with more advanced speakers or native speakers. This can help facilitate language exchange, cultural sharing, and social interaction, which are essential for language acquisition and retention.
6. Create a supportive community: Building a supportive community of learners and teachers can help create a positive and inclusive learning environment. Encourage collaboration, sharing, and mutual support among learners to foster a sense of belonging and motivation.
7. Offer specialized courses: Beginner Arabic learners may have specific interests or goals, such as studying the Quran or mastering colloquial Arabic. Offer specialized courses that cater to these interests and goals to make the learning experience more meaningful and relevant.
8. Provide access to resources: Make sure beginner Arabic learners have access to a range of resources, including textbooks, language learning apps, and online courses. This will help them supplement their learning and stay motivated outside of class.
