Addressing the specific needs of single, multiple times beginner Arabic speakers in a college graduate program requires a tailored approach that considers their unique challenges and goals. Here are some strategies to help meet their needs:

1. Adaptive Curriculum: Design an adaptive curriculum that caters to the varying levels of proficiency among students. Offer beginner-friendly courses for those who are new to Arabic, while providing more advanced classes for those who have a foundation in the language.
2. Personalized Learning Plans: Create personalized learning plans for each student based on their language proficiency level, learning style, and academic goals. This may involve setting achievable milestones, offering extra support, or challenging advanced students with more complex materials.
3. Interactive Language Learning Tools: Utilize interactive language learning tools such as language learning apps, online games, and multimedia resources to engage students in an immersive learning experience. These tools can help beginners build their foundation in Arabic, while more advanced students can use them to reinforce their knowledge and improve their speaking and listening skills.
4. Cultural Immersion Activities: Organize cultural immersion activities that allow students to interact with native speakers and gain a deeper understanding of the language and culture. This could include attending cultural events, participating in language exchange programs, or engaging in conversations with local Arabic speakers.
5. Peer Support Groups: Establish peer support groups where students can collaborate and learn from each other. Beginners can benefit from the guidance of more advanced students, while both groups can foster a sense of community and mutual support.
6. Flexible Scheduling: Offer flexible scheduling options to accommodate the diverse needs of students. This may involve offering courses at different times, providing online learning opportunities, or allowing students to take classes at their own pace.
7. Access to Language Resources: Provide access to a wide range of language resources, including textbooks, grammar guides, and dictionaries. Make these resources available both in-person and online to ensure that students can access them conveniently.
