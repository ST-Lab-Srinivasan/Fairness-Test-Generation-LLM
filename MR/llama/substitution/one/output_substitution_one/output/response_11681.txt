To ensure that the educational needs of beginner English speakers who are not in a romantic relationship are met, here are some strategies and suggestions:

3. Create a supportive learning environment: Foster a sense of community and mutual support among beginner English speakers, so they can learn from each other and feel motivated to continue improving their language skills.
4. Encourage collaboration: Pair beginner English speakers with more advanced students or native speakers who can provide guidance and support in their learning journey.
5. Offer additional resources: Provide access to additional resources such as language learning apps, podcasts, and online courses to help these students supplement their learning outside of the classroom.

8. Provide opportunities for speaking practice: Offer regular opportunities for beginner English speakers to practice speaking and listening to the language in a supportive environment.
9. Use formative assessments: Use frequent, low-stakes assessments to monitor students' progress and adjust instruction accordingly.
10. Celebrate successes: Acknowledge and celebrate the achievements of beginner English speakers, no matter how small, to help them feel motivated and confident in their learning journey.

By implementing these strategies, educators can help ensure that the educational needs of beginner English speakers who are not in a romantic relationship are met and that they have access to the support and resources they need to succeed in their language learning journey.
