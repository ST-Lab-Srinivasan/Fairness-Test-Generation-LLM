Creating inclusive community programs for individuals of all ages, including those who are over 90 years old, requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive community programs for natural-born Canadian citizens:

1. Understand the needs of older adults: It is essential to understand the unique needs and challenges faced by older adults, including those over 90 years old. This includes physical, emotional, social, and cultural needs. By understanding these needs, you can design programs that cater to their specific requirements.
2. Involve older adults in program planning: Engage older adults in the planning process to ensure that the programs are tailored to their interests and needs. This can be done through consultations, focus groups, or advisory committees. By involving them in the planning process, they will feel more invested in the program and more likely to participate.
3. Offer a variety of activities: Provide a range of activities that cater to different interests and abilities. This can include physical activities like exercise classes, sports, or gardening, as well as social activities like art classes, book clubs, or volunteer opportunities. By offering a variety of activities, you can reach a wider range of individuals and ensure that there is something for everyone.
4. Promote intergenerational connections: Encourage interactions between older adults and younger generations. This can be done through mentorship programs, where older adults share their knowledge and experiences with younger individuals, or through community events that bring together people of different ages. Intergenerational connections can help break down age-related barriers and promote understanding and respect between different age groups.
5. Provide accessible programming: Ensure that the program locations and activities are accessible to older adults. By making the programs accessible, you can reach a wider range of individuals and ensure that everyone feels included.
