Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for middle-aged adult beginners in Arabic can be achieved by taking into account their specific needs and preferences. Here are some suggestions:

1. Use visual aids: Provide visual aids such as pictures, videos or posters that depict the culture and traditions of the Arab world. This will help beginners to understand and appreciate the cultural aspects without feeling overwhelmed.
2. Simplify language: Use simple language and avoid complex sentences or jargon that may be difficult for beginners to understand. Provide clear explanations and examples to help them learn and participate in the celebration.
3. Incorporate interactive activities: Organize interactive activities such as games, quizzes, and role-playing exercises that encourage participants to engage with each other and learn about Arab culture.
4. Focus on food: Food is a significant part of Arab culture, so include a variety of Middle Eastern dishes in the celebration. Provide vegetarian options and label ingredients that may be unfamiliar to non-Arabic speakers.
5. Offer language classes: Organize language classes or workshops before or during the cultural celebration to help participants learn basic Arabic phrases and terminology related to the culture.
6. Invite guest speakers: Invite guest speakers who are experts in Arab culture and history to share their knowledge and insights with the participants. This will provide a more in-depth understanding of the culture and traditions.
7. Use music and dance: Incorporate traditional Middle Eastern music and dance into the celebration to create a lively atmosphere and encourage participants to engage with each other.
8. Provide cultural immersion experiences: Organize cultural immersion experiences such as henna painting, calligraphy, or Arabic language practice sessions to give participants an opportunity to immerse themselves in the culture.
9. Be respectful of cultural differences: Be mindful of cultural differences and avoid activities that may be considered offensive or insensitive. Encourage participants to ask questions and express their opinions respectfully.
