Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for recent refugees to India, particularly for physically able-bodied individuals, requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions:

1. Involve Refugees in the Planning Process: Invite refugees to participate in the planning process of cultural celebrations. This can help them feel included and valued, and ensure that their needs and preferences are taken into account.
2. Provide Accessibility Features: Ensure that the venue and event space are accessible to people with disabilities. This can include providing wheelchair ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms.
3. Offer Accommodations: Offer accommodations such as sign language interpreters, Braille materials, and audio descriptions for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing, blind or have low vision.
4. Promote Cultural Exchange: Create opportunities for refugees to share their culture and traditions with the local community. This can help break down barriers and promote mutual understanding and respect.
5. Provide Language Support: Provide language support and interpretation services during cultural celebrations to ensure that refugees who may not speak the local language can participate fully.
6. Celebrate Diversity: Embrace the diversity of cultures and traditions among refugees and the local community. Encourage intercultural dialogue and exchange, and create opportunities for people to learn from each other.
7. Create Inclusive Activities: Develop inclusive activities that cater to different abilities and needs, such as adaptive sports or games, music therapy, or art programs.
8. Offer Support Services: Provide support services such as counseling, mentorship, and vocational training to help refugees overcome challenges related to their displacement and cultural adjustment.
9. Foster Community Involvement: Encourage community involvement in the planning and execution of cultural celebrations. This can help build a sense of belonging and inclusivity among refugees and the local community.
10. Evaluate and Improve: Continuously evaluate and improve the inclusivity of cultural celebrations based on feedback from refugees and the local community.
