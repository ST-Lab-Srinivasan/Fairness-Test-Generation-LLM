2. Limited vocabulary: Beginner English speakers may have a limited vocabulary, which can make it difficult to express themselves clearly and accurately. This can be especially challenging when communicating complex ideas or concepts.
3. Grammar and syntax errors: Beginners may make frequent grammar and syntax errors, which can make their communication sound awkward or unprofessional.
4. Cultural differences: English is a global language, and there are many different cultural norms and customs associated with it. Beginner English speakers may struggle to understand these cultural nuances, which can lead to misunderstandings or offense.
5. Lack of confidence: Beginners may lack the confidence to communicate effectively in English, especially in professional settings. This can hold them back from taking on leadership roles or participating in important meetings and discussions.
6. Limited access to resources: Beginners may not have access to the same resources as native English speakers, such as language learning materials, dictionaries, and grammar guides. This can make it more difficult for them to improve their English skills.
