Supporting the social inclusion of older adults, particularly those who are 90 years old or older, is crucial for their well-being and quality of life. Here are some ways to support the social inclusion of over 90 years young native English speaker people:

1. Encourage intergenerational interactions: Pair older adults with younger generations, such as children or grandchildren, for activities like reading, games, or crafts. This can help bridge the age gap and create opportunities for socialization and learning.
2. Provide access to community programs: Many communities offer programs specifically designed for older adults, such as senior centers, social clubs, and volunteer opportunities. These programs can provide a sense of belonging and purpose, while also helping to combat loneliness and isolation.
3. Create age-friendly environments: Make public spaces and buildings more accessible and inclusive for older adults by providing things like ramps, elevators, and large print signage. This can help older adults participate more easily in community activities and events.
4. Promote lifelong learning: Encourage older adults to continue learning and growing through classes, workshops, or other educational opportunities. This can help maintain cognitive function and social connections.
5. Support mental and physical health: Ensure that older adults have access to regular health check-ups, exercise programs, and mental health services. This can help them stay physically and mentally active and engaged in their communities.
6. Foster friendships: Encourage older adults to connect with others through social events, volunteer opportunities, or group outings. These social connections can help combat loneliness and isolation.
7. Provide transportation: Offer transportation services or resources to help older adults get to appointments, social events, or other activities. This can be especially important for those who may no longer drive or have limited mobility.
8. Create opportunities for volunteering: Encourage older adults to give back to their communities through volunteer work. This can provide a sense of purpose and fulfillment, while also helping to build social connections and networks.
9. Celebrate cultural diversity: Embrace the diverse backgrounds and experiences of older adults by hosting events that celebrate cultural heritage
