Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of permanent resident native English speaker people, as it shapes their identity, influences their beliefs and values, and provides a sense of belonging to a particular community. In this essay, we will explore the various ways in which cultural heritage impacts the daily lives of native English speakers, including their attitudes towards tradition, their relationships with their ancestors, and their perceptions of their place within society.

1. Tradition and Cultural Practices:
Cultural heritage influences the daily lives of native English speakers through traditional practices and customs. These practices are often rooted in history and are passed down from generation to generation. For example, many families observe holidays and celebrations such as Christmas, Easter, and Thanksgiving, which are deeply rooted in cultural heritage. These events provide a sense of continuity and tradition, connecting individuals to their ancestors and cultural roots.
2. Ancestral Roots:
Cultural heritage also plays a significant role in the daily lives of native English speakers through their relationships with their ancestors. Many people feel a strong connection to their ancestors and seek to honor their memory through cultural practices such as storytelling, music, and dance. These practices help to preserve the cultural heritage of a particular community and provide a sense of belonging for individuals within that community.
3. Sense of Belonging:
Cultural heritage also contributes to the sense of belonging among native English speakers. It provides a shared identity and a common history that unites people within a particular community. This shared identity can be seen in the way that people celebrate cultural events and traditions, and how they express their pride in their cultural heritage through language, art, and music.
4. Influence on Worldview:
Cultural heritage also shapes the worldview of native English speakers. It influences their beliefs, values, and attitudes towards life, providing a framework for understanding the world around them. For example, cultural heritage may influence an individual's beliefs about family, community, and spirituality, shaping their perspectives on these important aspects of life.
