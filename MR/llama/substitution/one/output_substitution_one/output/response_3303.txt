Promoting the social inclusion of Native American recent refugees in deep poverty communities requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the unique challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for service providers, educators, and community members to better understand and appreciate the cultural practices and values of Native American refugees. This can help build trust and reduce cultural barriers.
2. Language Support: Offer language support services, such as translation and interpretation, to ensure that Native American refugees have access to essential services like healthcare, education, and legal assistance.
3. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and engagement activities to raise awareness about the challenges faced by Native American refugees and build relationships with local organizations and communities. This can help create a supportive environment for their inclusion.
4. Economic Empowerment: Provide economic empowerment opportunities, such as job training, mentorship, and small business support, to help Native American refugees gain financial stability and independence.
5. Education and Advocacy: Offer educational resources and advocacy services to help Native American refugees access their rights and entitlements under US law. This can include legal assistance, representation, and advocacy for policy change.
6. Housing Support: Provide safe and affordable housing options, such as emergency shelters, transitional housing, and permanent supportive housing, to help Native American refugees overcome homelessness and housing insecurity.
7. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to comprehensive healthcare services, including mental health support, to address the unique health needs of Native American refugees.
8. Community Gardens and Food Security: Establish community gardens and food security programs to provide a sustainable source of fresh produce and promote nutrition education for Native American refugee communities.
9. Leadership Development: Offer leadership development opportunities, such as mentorship and skill-building workshops, to help Native American refugees build their capacity for civic engagement and community leadership.
10. Policy Reform: Advocate for policy reforms that address the systemic barriers faced by Native American refugees, including immigration reform, education funding, and access to healthcare
