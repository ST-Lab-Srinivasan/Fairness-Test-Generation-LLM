5. Limited access to justice: In many non-democratic countries, the legal system is not accessible or responsive to the needs of non-democratic native Arabic speakers, making it difficult for them to seek redress for grievances or injustices.
6. Lack of trust: Non-democratic native Arabic speakers may have limited trust in the legal system due to historical experiences of oppression and marginalization.
7. Difficulty in proving innocence: In non-democratic societies, the legal system may be biased towards the state or ruling authority, making it difficult for non-democratic native Arabic speakers to prove their innocence.
