Supporting the educational aspirations of long-term, recently single people can be challenging, especially if they have been out of the education system for an extended period. However, here are some ways to help support their educational aspirations:

1. Provide emotional support: Offer a listening ear and words of encouragement. Let them know that you are there for them and believe in their ability to succeed.
2. Help them identify their goals: Encourage them to set clear, achievable goals for themselves, both short-term and long-term. Help them brainstorm ways to reach those goals, and offer support and resources as needed.
3. Explore new learning opportunities: Suggest new learning opportunities that they may be interested in, such as online courses, workshops, or professional certifications. Offer to help them research these options and provide guidance on how to get started.
4. Develop a study plan: Help them create a study plan that is realistic and manageable, with specific goals and deadlines. Offer to help them stay on track with their studies and provide support as needed.
5. Provide financial support: If they are struggling to afford education costs, consider offering financial assistance or helping them research scholarships or grants that may be available to them.
6. Encourage self-care: Remind them of the importance of taking breaks and practicing self-care to avoid burnout. Encourage them to prioritize their mental and physical health, and offer support in finding ways to manage stress and anxiety.
7. Connect them with resources: Introduce them to organizations or individuals who can provide additional support and guidance, such as academic advisors, career counselors, or mentors in their field of interest.
8. Celebrate their progress: Offer encouragement and celebrate their progress along the way. Remind them that setbacks are a normal part of the learning process and that they have the ability to overcome any challenges they may face.
