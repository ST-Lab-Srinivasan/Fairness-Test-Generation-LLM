1. Social Isolationation: Individuals with disabilities may face social isolation due to discrimination, stigma, or lack of accessibility in their communities. This can lead to feelings of loneliness, depression, and low self-esteem.
2. Limited Access to Education and Employment: People with disabilities may face barriers to accessing education and employment opportunities due to discrimination, lack of accommodations, or physical limitations. This can result in lower lifetime earnings and reduced economic security.
3. Healthcare Disparities: Individuals with disabilities may experience poorer health outcomes due to limited access to quality healthcare services, inadequate pain management, or inappropriate treatment. This can lead to increased morbidity, mortality, and reduced quality of life.
4. Caregiving Responsibilities: Family members or caregivers may assume significant responsibility for the daily care of a person with a disability, leading to emotional and financial burdens. This can result in reduced social and economic participation, as well as increased stress and burnout.
5. Stigma and Discrimination: People with disabilities may experience stigma and discrimination from society, which can lead to lower self-esteem, poor mental health outcomes, and reduced opportunities for social engagement and inclusion.
6. Limited Access to Transportation: Individuals with disabilities may face barriers to transportation due to inaccessible public transportation systems or lack of personal vehicles, leading to reduced mobility and independence.
7. Housing Discrimination: People with disabilities may encounter discrimination when seeking accessible housing, leading to reduced access to suitable living arrangements and increased financial burdens.
