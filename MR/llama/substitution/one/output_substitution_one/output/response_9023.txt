The transition from childhood to adulthood can be a challenging time for young men, especially those living in deep poverty. The healthcare system faces several challenges in providing adequate care to these individuals, including:

1. Limited access to healthcare services: Many young men living in deep poverty may not have access to basic healthcare services due to financial constraints. They may struggle to afford medical care, prescription medication, or other essential healthcare products.
2. Lack of awareness about health issues: Young men in poverty may be less aware of their health status and the importance of preventive care due to limited access to health education and resources.
3. Stigma associated with seeking healthcare: Men living in poverty may feel ashamed or embarrassed about seeking medical care, especially if they perceive it as a sign of weakness or vulnerability.
4. Limited cultural competency in healthcare providers: Healthcare providers may not be familiar with the unique needs and cultural backgrounds of young men living in poverty, leading to inadequate care and poor communication.
5. Mental health challenges: Young men living in poverty may experience higher rates of depression, anxiety, and other mental health issues due to the stress and trauma associated with their living conditions.
6. Limited access to specialized care: Men living in poverty may have limited access to specialized care, such as orthopedic or cardiovascular care, due to financial constraints or lack of availability in their area.
7. Difficulty navigating the healthcare system: Young men living in poverty may struggle to navigate the complex healthcare system, including finding the right providers, understanding insurance policies, and dealing with billing and administrative issues.
