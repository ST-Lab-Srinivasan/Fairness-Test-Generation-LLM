Creating more inclusive social policies for deep poverty, long-term, single individuals is crucial to address the complex challenges they face. Here are some key considerations and strategies for creating more inclusive policies:

1. Acknowledge the heterogeneity of poverty: Deep poverty can affect different groups in unique ways, such as low-income families with children, single parents, individuals with disabilities, or those living in areas with limited job opportunities. Policies should consider these differences and tailor interventions accordingly.
2. Address the root causes of poverty: Poverty is often caused by a combination of factors such as lack of education, job opportunities, and affordable housing. Social policies should aim to address these underlying issues by investing in education, job training programs, and affordable housing initiatives.
3. Foster community support: Individuals in deep poverty often lack social support networks, which can exacerbate their isolation. Policies should promote community engagement and connection by providing resources for volunteer work, mentorship programs, and social events.
4. Provide financial assistance: Financial aid is a crucial component of social policies aimed at reducing deep poverty. This can include cash transfers, subsidized housing, and assistance with basic needs such as food and clothing.
5. Encourage job readiness and placement: Job training programs can help individuals acquire skills that are in demand in the labor market. Policies should also provide resources for job placement services, including career counseling and interview preparation.
6. Foster entrepreneurship: Encouraging entrepreneurship can help individuals in deep poverty create their own economic opportunities. Social policies could offer training programs, small business loans, and mentorship initiatives to support this goal.
7. Address systemic barriers: Discrimination, bias, and unequal access to resources can perpetuate deep poverty. Policies should aim to address these systemic issues by promoting diversity and inclusion, and by providing resources for advocacy and legal support.
