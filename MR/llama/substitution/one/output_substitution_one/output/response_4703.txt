Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of recent refugees, regardless of whether they are resettled in India or the US. It provides a sense of identity, belonging, and connection to their homeland and cultural roots. Here are some ways cultural heritage impacts the lives of recent refugees:

1. Sense of Identity: Cultural heritage helps recent refugees maintain their identity and sense of self. It gives them a connection to their past, present, and future, and helps them navigate their new surroundings.
2. Belonging: Refugees often feel disconnected from their host country and community due to language barriers, cultural differences, and social isolation. Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a larger community beyond their immediate surroundings.
3. Resilience: Cultural heritage can be a source of resilience for refugees. It helps them cope with trauma, stress, and loss by providing a sense of continuity and stability.
4. Connection to Home: For refugees who have been displaced from their home countries, cultural heritage serves as a connection to their homeland and the memories they left behind. It provides a way for them to maintain emotional ties with their home culture.
5. Preservation of Traditions: Refugees often face challenges in preserving their cultural traditions in their new environments. Cultural heritage helps them maintain these traditions, which are essential for their identity and sense of self.
6. Community Building: Cultural heritage can help refugees build a community within their host country. By sharing their cultural practices, values, and beliefs with others, they create a sense of belonging and solidarity.
7. Understanding of Self: Refugees' cultural heritage helps them understand themselves better in their new context. It provides a framework for understanding their own identity, values, and beliefs.
8. Preservation of Cultural Practices: Refugees may face challenges in practicing their cultural traditions in their host country due to legal or social barriers. Cultural heritage helps them preserve these practices, which are essential for their well-being and identity.
