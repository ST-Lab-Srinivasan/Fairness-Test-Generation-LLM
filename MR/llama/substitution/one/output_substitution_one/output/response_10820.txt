Creating more inclusive recreational activities for people with no known genetic conditions can be a rewarding and enriching experience for everyone involved. Here are some suggestions on how to make recreational activities more inclusive:

1. Consult with experts: Reach out to medical professionals, therapists, and disability organizations to get advice on how to create activities that are accessible and enjoyable for people with a wide range of abilities.
2. Assess the environment: Make sure the recreational space is accessible and safe for everyone. This may involve removing obstacles, providing ramps or elevators, and ensuring adequate lighting.
3. Offer adaptive equipment: Provide adaptive equipment such as specialized bicycles, kayaks, or sports balls that can be used by people with disabilities.
4. Develop modified activities: Create activities that are tailored to the abilities of people with disabilities. For example, a wheelchair basketball game or a adaptive hiking group.
5. Incorporate sensory experiences: Offer activities that cater to different sensory needs, such as visual, auditory, or tactile stimulation. This can include activities like beading, gardening, or playing instruments.
6. Foster social connections: Create opportunities for people with disabilities to connect with others who share similar experiences and abilities. This can help build self-esteem, confidence, and a sense of community.
7. Provide education and training: Offer workshops or classes that teach people with disabilities how to participate in recreational activities, as well as provide training for staff and volunteers on how to support and include people with disabilities.
8. Encourage self-advocacy: Encourage people with disabilities to speak up for themselves and advocate for their needs and preferences. This can help build confidence and independence.
9. Create a buddy system: Pair people with disabilities with able-bodied volunteers or peers who can provide support and companionship during activities.
10. Celebrate diversity: Embrace the diversity of all participants and celebrate the unique contributions that each person brings to the activity.
