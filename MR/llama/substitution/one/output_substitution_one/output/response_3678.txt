The impact of disability status on the lives of long-time single permanent residents can be significant, as they may face unique challenges and barriers compared to their able-bodied peers. Some of the potential impacts include:

1. Social isolation: People with disabilities may experience social isolation due to physical limitations or societal stigmas, which can lead to feelings of loneliness and depression.
2. Limited access to education and employment: Depending on the nature of their disability, individuals may face barriers in accessing education and employment opportunities, leading to lower socioeconomic status and limited social mobility.
3. Lack of representation and voice: People with disabilities are often underrepresented in decision-making positions, which can result in a lack of understanding and consideration for their needs and experiences.
4. Stigma and discrimination: Disability can lead to social stigma and discrimination, causing individuals to feel marginalized and excluded from society.
5. Health disparities: People with disabilities may experience poorer health outcomes due to inadequate access to healthcare services, lack of knowledge about their condition, or inadequate support for managing their health.
6. Limited access to transportation and mobility: Depending on the nature of their disability, individuals may face barriers in accessing transportation, leading to limited mobility and independence.
7. Financial strain: Disability can result in increased financial burdens due to higher costs for medical care, assistive technology, and home modifications.
8. Emotional toll: Living with a disability can take an emotional toll on individuals and their families, leading to stress, anxiety, and depression.
9. Limited access to recreational activities: Depending on the nature of their disability, individuals may face barriers in accessing leisure activities, leading to reduced quality of life.
10. Negative impact on mental health: The stigma and discrimination associated with disability can lead to negative impacts on mental health, including depression, anxiety, and low self-esteem.
