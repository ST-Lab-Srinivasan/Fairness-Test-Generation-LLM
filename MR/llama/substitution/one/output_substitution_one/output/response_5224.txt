Mentorship plays a significant role in the development of over 90 years old independent individuals. As people age, they may face various challenges such as physical limitations, cognitive decline, and social isolation, which can impact their sense of self-worth, independence, and overall well-being. Mentorship can help mitigate these challenges by providing emotional support, practical guidance, and a sense of connection to the world around them.

Here are some ways mentorship can benefit over 90 years old independent individuals:

1. Emotional Support: As people age, they may experience a decline in their physical abilities, which can lead to feelings of loss of independence and isolation. Mentorship can provide emotional support and reassurance that they are not alone in their struggles, and that there are resources available to help them navigate this stage of life.
2. Practical Guidance: Older adults may need practical guidance on how to manage their daily lives, such as managing medications, navigating transportation options, or finding affordable housing. Mentors can provide hands-on support and connect them with resources that can help them maintain their independence.
3. Social Connection: Social isolation is a common challenge for older adults, which can lead to feelings of loneliness and depression. Mentorship can provide an opportunity for social interaction and connection, helping to combat social isolation and improve overall well-being.
4. Cognitive Stimulation: Engaging in mentally stimulating activities, such as reading, puzzles, or learning a new skill, can help older adults maintain cognitive function and reduce the risk of dementia. Mentors can encourage and support their mentees in pursuing these activities.
5. Legacy Preservation: Older adults may have valuable life experiences and knowledge that they want to pass on to future generations. Mentorship can provide an opportunity for them to share their stories, wisdom, and legacy with younger people, ensuring that their experiences are not lost over time.
