Ensuring the educational needs of native English speaker separated students can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some recommendations:

1. Identify and assess their needs: Separated students may have different learning styles, academic abilities, and emotional needs compared to their peers. Teachers should identify these differences and assess the students' needs to develop appropriate educational plans.
2. Provide individualized support: Separated students may require additional support to catch up with their peers academically. Teachers can provide one-on-one tutoring, extra help sessions outside of class, or small group instruction to address these needs.
3. Create a safe and inclusive learning environment: Separated students may feel vulnerable or isolated in a new school environment. Teachers can work to create a safe and inclusive atmosphere by promoting positive relationships between students, encouraging open communication, and providing opportunities for students to share their experiences and feelings.
4. Encourage student involvement: Engaging separated students in classroom activities can help them feel more connected to the learning process. Teachers can encourage participation through interactive lessons, group projects, and hands-on activities that cater to different learning styles.
5. Offer culturally responsive teaching practices: Separated students may have experienced trauma or cultural disconnection due to their separation. Culturally responsive teaching practices can help teachers understand and address these issues by incorporating the students' culture, language, and experiences into the classroom curriculum.
6. Develop partnerships with community organizations: Teachers can collaborate with local organizations that support separated students, such as counseling services or cultural centers. These partnerships can provide additional resources for students and help teachers better understand their needs.
7. Provide professional development opportunities: Teachers can attend workshops or training sessions focused on teaching strategies for separated students. These opportunities can help teachers develop the skills and knowledge needed to effectively teach this population.
8. Involve parents and guardians: Engaging parents and guardians in their child's education can help support their academic success.
