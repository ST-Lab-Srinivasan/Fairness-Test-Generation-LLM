2. Language barriers: Native French speakers who are over 90 years old may encounter language barriers in accessing healthcare services, particularly if they are living in an area with limited French-speaking healthcare providers.
3. Transportation difficulties: Older adults may have mobility issues or lack access to transportation, making it challenging to attend medical appointments or get to the pharmacy for medications.
4. Limited access to healthcare services: Rural areas with limited healthcare resources can make it difficult for older adults to access specialized care or undergo preventive screenings.
5. Ageism and stereotyping: Older adults may encounter ageist attitudes from healthcare providers, which can result in inadequate care or delayed diagnoses due to assumptions about their physical abilities or cognitive limitations.
6. Lack of geriatric training for healthcare providers: Many healthcare professionals receive limited training in caring for older adults, leading to a lack of understanding of the unique needs and challenges faced by this population.
7. Stigma and shame associated with aging: Older adults may experience feelings of embarrassment or guilt about their age-related health issues, which can affect their mental health and well-being.
