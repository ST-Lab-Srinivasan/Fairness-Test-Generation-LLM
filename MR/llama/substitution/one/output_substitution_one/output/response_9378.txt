1. Expand health insurance coverage: Many long-term unemployed Mexicans may lack access to health insurance, which can limit their ability to receive necessary medical care. Expanding health insurance coverage to include more people, particularly those in vulnerable populations such as the long-term unemployed, could help ensure that they have access to needed healthcare services.
2. Improve access to primary care: Long-term unemployed individuals may face barriers in accessing primary care services due to financial constraints or lack of transportation. Improving access to primary care through initiatives such as telemedicine, community health centers, or mobile clinics could help address these challenges.

Address social determinants of health: Many factors outside of the healthcare system can impact an individual's health, including poverty, lack of access to nutritious food, inadequate housing, and poor living conditions. 4. Provide mental health support: Unemployment can have a significant impact on an individual's mental health, leading to feelings of hopelessness, anxiety, and depression.
