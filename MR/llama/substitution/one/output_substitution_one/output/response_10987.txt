The term "non-democratic" is quite broad and can encompass a wide range of communities, including those based on race, ethnicity, religion, or socioeconomic status. Promoting the economic empowerment of any community requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural inequalities that prevent them from achieving economic stability and growth. Here are some strategies that can help promote the economic empowerment of non-democratic communities in the United States:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs that equip community members with the skills and knowledge necessary for better job opportunities. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and higher education programs.
2. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): Establish CDFIs that provide financial services, such as loans, venture capital, and investments, to underserved communities. These institutions can help channel capital into community development projects, small businesses, and affordable housing initiatives.
3. Small Business Development: Support small businesses owned by community members through training, mentorship, and access to capital. This can help create jobs and stimulate economic growth within the community.
4. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing resources such as incubators, co-working spaces, and networking opportunities. This can help unlock innovation and creativity within the community.
5. Community Land Trusts (CLT): Establish CLTs to provide affordable housing options and promote community control over land use and development. This can help stabilize housing prices and create a more equitable distribution of wealth within the community.
6. Co-operative Business Models: Promote co-operative business models that prioritize member ownership, democratic decision-making, and social and economic benefits for members. Examples include worker co-ops, consumer co-ops, and housing co-ops.
7. Public-Private Partnerships: Collaborate with private sector partners to leverage resources and expertise in supporting community development projects. This can help scale up impactful initiatives and create more opportunities for community members.
