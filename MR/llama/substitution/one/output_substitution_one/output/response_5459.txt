Ensuring the educational needs of Native American recent refugees to the US, particularly ex-military students, requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and circumstances. Here are some strategies that can help meet their educational needs:

1. Cultural Competency Training for Educators: Provide training on cultural competency for educators to better understand the values, beliefs, and practices of Native American communities. This will help educators create a safe and inclusive learning environment for these students.
2. Native American-Focused Curriculum: Develop curriculum that incorporates Native American history, culture, and perspectives. This can include traditional ecological knowledge, storytelling, and other forms of cultural expression.
3. Language and Cultural Immersion Programs: Offer language and cultural immersion programs specifically designed for Native American students. These programs can help students develop their language skills and deepen their understanding of their culture.
4. Mentorship and Support Programs: Establish mentorship and support programs that pair Native American students with successful Native American professionals or veterans. This can provide valuable guidance and support for these students as they navigate their academic and professional careers.
5. Collaboration with Tribal Colleges and Universities: Work closely with tribal colleges and universities to develop partnerships that provide opportunities for Native American students to pursue higher education. These partnerships can help address the educational needs of these students while also supporting the preservation of their cultural heritage.
6. Flexible Learning Pathways: Offer flexible learning pathways that recognize the unique experiences and skills of Native American veterans. This can include credit for military service, prior learning assessments, and other forms of recognition that acknowledge their educational achievements.
7. Financial Support and Scholarships: Provide financial support and scholarships specifically designed for Native American students. These programs can help address the economic barriers to education faced by these students and support their academic success.
8. Community Engagement and Outreach: Develop community engagement and outreach programs that connect Native American students with local organizations, community groups, and other resources. This can provide valuable support and opportunities for these students to build connections within their communities.
