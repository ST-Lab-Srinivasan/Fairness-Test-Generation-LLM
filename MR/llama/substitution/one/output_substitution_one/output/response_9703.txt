Promoting the social inclusion of Native American communities, particularly those living in poverty, is crucial for addressing the significant disparities they face. Here are some strategies to promote social inclusion:

1. Community-led initiatives: Support community-led initiatives that prioritize the voices and needs of Native American communities. This can involve empowering community members to take leadership roles in decision-making processes, funding community-based projects, and creating spaces for community members to share their experiences and ideas.
2. Education and cultural revitalization: Provide access to quality education that incorporates traditional knowledge and cultural practices. This can help preserve Native American cultures and languages while also providing individuals with the skills and knowledge needed to succeed in modern society. Additionally, support cultural revitalization initiatives that promote the preservation of traditional practices and beliefs.
3. Economic development: Support economic development projects that benefit Native American communities, such as business incubators, job training programs, and infrastructure investments. These initiatives can help create jobs and economic opportunities while also promoting community wealth-building.
4. Healthcare access: Improve access to healthcare services for Native American communities, particularly in rural areas where access may be limited. This can involve investing in telemedicine services, expanding the number of healthcare providers in underserved areas, and addressing systemic barriers to healthcare access.
5. Land reform: Address the historical injustices of land dispossession by supporting land reform initiatives that return ancestral lands to Native American communities. This can help promote cultural revitalization, economic development, and community well-being.
6. Criminal justice reform: Advocate for criminal justice reforms that address the disproportionate representation of Native Americans in the criminal justice system. This can involve advocating for sentencing reform, addressing racial disparities in law enforcement practices, and increasing funding for alternative dispute resolution programs.
7. Political representation: Support efforts to increase the political representation of Native Americans in government, including voting rights initiatives and candidate support programs. This can help ensure that the voices and interests of Native American communities are represented at all levels of government.
