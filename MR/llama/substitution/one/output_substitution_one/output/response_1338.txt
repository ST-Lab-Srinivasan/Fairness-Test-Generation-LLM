2. Create opportunities for participation: Provide opportunities for native Arabic speakers to participate in decision-making processes, such as through surveys, focus groups, or community meetings. Make sure that the language barrier is not a hindrance to their participation.
3. Use interpreters and translators: If necessary, use interpreters and translators to facilitate communication between native Arabic speakers and decision-makers. This can help ensure that the voices of native Arabic speakers are heard and considered in the decision-making process.
4. Develop cultural competency: Develop cultural competency among decision-makers by providing training on the culture, values, and beliefs of native Arabic speakers. This can help decision-makers to better understand the perspectives and concerns of native Arabic speakers.
5. Foster partnerships: Foster partnerships between organizations that represent native Arabic speakers and decision-making bodies. These partnerships can help ensure that the voices of native Arabic speakers are heard and considered in the decision-making process.
6. Address power imbalances: Address power imbalances by actively seeking out and involving native Arabic speakers in decision-making processes. This can involve intentionally seeking out their perspectives and opinions, and giving them a platform to express themselves.
7. Create inclusive language policies: Create inclusive language policies that promote the use of native Arabic speakers' languages in official settings. This can help ensure that their voices are heard and considered in decision-making processes.
