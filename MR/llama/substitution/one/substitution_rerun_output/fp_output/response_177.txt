Permanent resident ex-spouses in the United States may face several challenges when accessing healthcare services. Here are some of the common issues they may encounter:

1. Lack of health insurance: Ex-spouses may not have access to health insurance through their former partner's employer or union, especially if they are no longer eligible for coverage due to divorce or separation. This can make it difficult for them to afford medical care, particularly if they have ongoing health issues.
2. Limited coverage: Even if ex-spouses have health insurance, the coverage may be limited or may not provide adequate coverage for their healthcare needs. For example, some policies may have high deductibles, co-payments, or copays that can make it difficult to afford necessary medical services.
3. Difficulty navigating the system: The healthcare system can be complex and confusing, especially for those who are not familiar with it. Ex-spouses may struggle to navigate the system, find the right providers, or understand their benefits and coverage.
4. Lack of continuity of care: When an ex-spouse moves away or loses access to a provider they have been seeing, they may experience disruptions in their care. This can lead to gaps in care, missed diagnoses, or delayed treatments.
5. Emotional challenges: Divorce and separation can be emotionally challenging, and ex-spouses may feel anxious or overwhelmed when dealing with the healthcare system. They may also experience feelings of loss or grief related to their former relationship and the changes that come with it.
6. Language barriers: If an ex-spouse is non-English speaking or has limited English proficiency, they may face additional challenges in accessing healthcare services. This can include difficulties communicating with providers, understanding medical information, or navigating the healthcare system.
