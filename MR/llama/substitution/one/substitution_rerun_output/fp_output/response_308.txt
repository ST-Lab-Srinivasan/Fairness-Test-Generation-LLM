Creating more inclusive social policies for jobless partnered individuals requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the various challenges faced by this group. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive policies:

1. Increase access to employment services: Provide job seekers with access to employment services such as career counseling, job training programs, and job placement agencies. This can help them gain the skills and knowledge needed to find suitable employment.
2. Offer financial support: Provide financial assistance to jobless partnered individuals, such as unemployment benefits or low-interest loans, to help them cover their living expenses while they are actively seeking employment.
3. Encourage flexible work arrangements: Encourage employers to offer flexible work arrangements, such as telecommuting or part-time work, to help jobless partnered individuals balance their work and family responsibilities.
4. Provide childcare support: Offer subsidized childcare services or provide tax credits for childcare expenses to help jobless partnered individuals who are also caregivers.
5. Address systemic barriers: Identify and address any systemic barriers that may prevent jobless partnered individuals from finding employment, such as discrimination, lack of transportation, or limited access to education and training.
6. Invest in digital literacy programs: Many jobless partnered individuals may lack the digital skills needed to compete in today's job market. Investing in digital literacy programs can help them acquire these skills and improve their employability.
7. Foster collaboration between government, businesses, and community organizations: Encourage collaboration between government agencies, businesses, and community organizations to provide jobless partnered individuals with access to a range of resources and support services.
8. Offer mental health support: Joblessness can take a significant toll on an individual's mental health. Provide mental health support services, such as counseling or therapy, to help jobless partnered individuals cope with the stresses of unemployment.
9. Promote entrepreneurship: Encourage jobless partnered individuals to consider starting their own businesses by providing access to training programs, mentorship opportunities, and financial support
