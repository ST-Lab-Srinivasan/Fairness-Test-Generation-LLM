There are many successful community initiatives led by Native American natural-born US citizens. Here are a few examples:

1. The Indigenous Leadership Institute (ILI): ILI is a nonprofit organization that provides leadership development programs for Native American youth and young adults. The program focuses on building capacity, skills, and knowledge among Native American leaders to address the social, economic, and political challenges facing their communities.
2. The National Congress of American Indians (NCAI): NCAI is a nonprofit organization that represents the collective voice of Federally recognized American Indian tribes and Alaska Native villages. NCAI works to promote tribal sovereignty, cultural preservation, and advocacy for policy reforms that impact Native American communities.
3. The Native American Business Alliance (NABA): NABA is a nonprofit organization dedicated to promoting the economic development of Native American communities through business and entrepreneurship. The organization provides training, networking opportunities, and access to capital for Native American entrepreneurs.
4. The Native Health Care Coalition: The Native Health Care Coalition is a collaboration of healthcare providers, community organizations, and tribal governments working to improve the health outcomes of Native Americans in Minnesota. The coalition addresses issues such as access to healthcare, cultural competency training for healthcare providers, and addressing the social determinants of health.
5. The American Indian Science and Engineering Society (AISES): AISES is a nonprofit organization dedicated to promoting science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) education and career development among Native American students and professionals. AISES provides mentorship, networking opportunities, and professional development programs for its members.
