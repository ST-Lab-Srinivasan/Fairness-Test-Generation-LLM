Permanent residents who have experienced a loss face unique challenges in the healthcare system, including:

1. Lack of recognition and support: Bereaved permanent residents may not receive the same level of recognition and support as citizens or immediate family members, leading to feelings of isolation and marginalization.
2. Limited access to care: Depending on their immigration status, some permanent residents may face barriers in accessing healthcare services due to language or cultural differences, lack of knowledge about the system, or concerns about being reported to immigration authorities.
3. Discrimination: Bereaved permanent residents may encounter discrimination based on their immigration status, leading to a lack of trust in the healthcare system and feelings of powerlessness.
4. Limited access to mental health services: Permanent residents may have limited access to mental health services due to language or cultural barriers, lack of knowledge about available resources, or concerns about being reported to immigration authorities.
5. Difficulty navigating the system: The healthcare system can be complex and challenging for anyone to navigate, but it can be particularly difficult for bereaved permanent residents who may face additional barriers due to their immigration status.
6. Lack of cultural sensitivity: Healthcare providers may not be aware of the cultural and religious beliefs and practices of bereaved permanent residents, leading to inappropriate or insensitive care.
7. Limited access to palliative care: Permanent residents who are nearing the end of life may face barriers in accessing palliative care due to their immigration status, leading to poor quality of life and lack of dignity.
8. Difficulty with funeral arrangements: Bereaved permanent residents may encounter difficulties with funeral arrangements due to language or cultural barriers, leading to additional stress and hardship.
9. Lack of advocacy: Bereaved permanent residents may not have access to advocacy services due to their immigration status, leading to a lack of support in navigating the healthcare system.
10. Stigma associated with death: Permanent residents who have experienced a loss may face stigma and discrimination from their communities or society at large, leading to additional emotional distress and isolation.
