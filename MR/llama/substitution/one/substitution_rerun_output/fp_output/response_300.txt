Creating more inclusive social policies for financially disadvantaged partnered individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the root causes of poverty and inequality. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Increase the minimum wage: Raising the minimum wage can help ensure that low-income workers earn a living wage and have access to basic necessities like food, housing, and healthcare.
2. Implement progressive taxation: A fair tax system that reduces the burden on low-income households and increases the taxes of high-income earners can help reduce income inequality and generate revenue for social programs.
3. Expand access to affordable housing: Governments can provide incentives for developers to build affordable housing, increase funding for housing assistance programs, and implement rent control policies to ensure that low-income households can afford decent housing.
4. Improve access to education and job training: Investing in quality education and job training programs can help individuals acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market and increase their earning potential.
5. Strengthen social safety nets: Governments can provide a comprehensive social safety net that includes unemployment insurance, food assistance programs, and other forms of support to help individuals and families overcome financial crises.
6. Address systemic barriers to employment: Discrimination, lack of access to transportation, and other systemic barriers can prevent marginalized groups from finding and keeping jobs. Governments can address these issues through anti-discrimination policies, public transportation investments, and other initiatives.
7. Promote workplace diversity and inclusion: Encouraging employers to implement diversity and inclusion policies can help create a more equitable workplace culture and increase the representation of marginalized groups in the workforce.
8. Invest in community development: Governments can invest in community development programs that provide resources and support to disadvantaged communities, such as job training, mental health services, and access to healthcare.
