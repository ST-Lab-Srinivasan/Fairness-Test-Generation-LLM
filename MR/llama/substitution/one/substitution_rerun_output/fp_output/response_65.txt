Lower middle-income Native natural-born US citizens face unique challenges within the healthcare system. Here are some of the common difficulties they encounter:

1. Limited access to healthcare: Lower middle-income individuals may struggle to afford medical care due to high deductibles, copays, and coinsurance. This can lead to delayed or foregone care, exacerbating existing health conditions.
2. Language barriers: Native Americans often face language barriers when interacting with healthcare providers, which can result in misunderstandings or miscommunications about their medical needs.
3. Cultural barriers: Healthcare providers may not be familiar with the cultural practices and beliefs of Native American communities, leading to inadequate care or misdiagnosis.
4. Distance and transportation issues: Many Native American communities are located in remote areas, making it difficult for residents to access healthcare services. This can be especially challenging during times of emergency or for those with chronic conditions that require ongoing care.
5. Lack of culturally competent providers: The healthcare workforce may not reflect the diversity of Native American communities, resulting in a lack of cultural understanding and empathy among providers.
6. Historical trauma: The legacy of forced relocation, boarding schools, and other policies has had lasting effects on the physical, mental, and emotional well-being of Native Americans. This historical trauma can manifest as chronic stress, anxiety, or depression, making it challenging to access and receive appropriate healthcare.
7. Discrimination: Unfortunately, discrimination based on race or ethnicity still exists in the healthcare system, leading to mistrust and hesitation among Native Americans to seek medical care.
8. Limited specialized care: Depending on where they live, lower middle-income Native natural-born US citizens may not have access to specialized care for conditions specific to their community, such as diabetes or respiratory issues.
9. Difficulty navigating the healthcare system: Navigating insurance claims, medical billing, and other administrative tasks can be a significant challenge for lower middle-income individuals, particularly if they lack support from family members or have limited access to technology.
