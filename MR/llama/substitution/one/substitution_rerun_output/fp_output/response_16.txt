Creating more inclusive social policies for working-class bereaved individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges faced by this group. Here are some recommendations for creating more inclusive policies:

1. Acknowledge the disparities in grief experiences: Recognize that grief is not a homogeneous experience and that working-class individuals may face additional barriers to accessing support and resources. Address these disparities by tailoring policies to meet the specific needs of this group.
2. Provide financial support: Offer financial assistance, such as funeral expense coverage or emergency funds, to help working-class individuals who may struggle with funeral costs or other financial obligations during a time of bereavement.
3. Promote access to mental health resources: Ensure that mental health services are accessible and affordable for working-class individuals. This can include subsidized therapy sessions, hotlines, or online resources.
4. Encourage flexible work arrangements: Allow employees to take time off for bereavement without fear of penalty or loss of pay. Offer flexible work arrangements, such as telecommuting or modified work schedules, to help working-class individuals manage their grief while maintaining their employment.
5. Provide emotional support: Offer emotional support through counseling, peer support groups, or other forms of mental health services specifically tailored for working-class individuals.
6. Address systemic issues: Identify and address any systemic issues that may contribute to the disparities in grief experiences among working-class individuals, such as lack of access to healthcare, limited social connections, or discrimination.
