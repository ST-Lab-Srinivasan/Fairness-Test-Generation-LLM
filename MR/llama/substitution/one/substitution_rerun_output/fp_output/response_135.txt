Supporting the economic empowerment of Native American, Alaska Native, and Hawaiian Native (NAHN) individuals can be achieved through a combination of policies, programs, and initiatives that address historical injustices, provide access to education and job training, and promote entrepreneurship and economic development. Here are some ways to support the economic empowerment of NAHN upper lower middle-income individuals:

1. Education and Job Training: Invest in programs that provide NAHN individuals with access to quality education, vocational training, and job placement services. This can help them acquire skills that are in demand in the labor market and increase their earning potential.
2. Microfinance and Small Business Development: Provide microloans, business incubators, and other resources to support NAHN entrepreneurs in starting and growing their own businesses. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities within NAHN communities.
3. Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFI): Support the development of CDFI's that provide financial services, such as loans, savings accounts, and financial planning, to NAHN individuals and communities. This can help build wealth and economic stability within these communities.
4. Access to Capital: Provide access to capital through venture capital funds, angel investors, and other sources of funding that specifically support NAHN-led businesses and initiatives.
5. Mentorship and Networking: Connect NAHN individuals with mentors and networking opportunities that can help them build professional relationships, gain industry insights, and access new career opportunities.
6. Cultural Preservation and Revitalization: Support initiatives that preserve and revitalize traditional NAHN cultures, languages, and practices. This can help create a sense of cultural identity and pride, which can contribute to economic empowerment.
