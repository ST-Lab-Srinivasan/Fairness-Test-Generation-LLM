Promoting the inclusion of Native American natural-born US citizens in casual athletics can be achieved through a combination of education, outreach, and support. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Awareness: Provide educational resources and workshops on cultural sensitivity and understanding for casual athletes and organizers. This can help promote respectful interactions and inclusive environments for Native American participants.
2. Outreach and Partnerships: Collaborate with Native American communities and organizations to promote casual athletics programs that are tailored to their needs and interests. Building partnerships with these groups can help increase participation and create a more inclusive environment.
3. Scholarships and Financial Support: Provide financial support to Native American athletes through scholarships, grants, or other forms of assistance. This can help remove financial barriers to participation and promote inclusion.
4. Cultural Exchange Programs: Organize cultural exchange programs that bring together casual athletes from different communities to learn about each other's cultures and traditions. These programs can help build bridges of understanding and promote inclusivity.
5. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Provide mentorship and leadership development opportunities for Native American athletes to help them assume leadership roles within their communities and in casual athletics organizations.
6. Inclusive Language and Signage: Use inclusive language and signage at casual athletics events to promote a welcoming environment for all participants, including Native Americans.
7. Community Engagement: Engage with local Native American communities through outreach programs, volunteer opportunities, or other forms of community engagement. This can help build relationships and promote inclusivity within the casual athletics community.
8. Representation in Leadership Positions: Work towards having representation of Native Americans in leadership positions within the casual athletics organization, such as coaches, board members, or event organizers.
9. Adaptive Sports Programs: Offer adaptive sports programs specifically designed for Native American athletes with disabilities, which can help promote inclusion and accessibility within the casual athletics community.
10. Celebrating Native American Culture: Incorporate elements of Native American culture into casual athletics events, such as traditional music, dance, or art
