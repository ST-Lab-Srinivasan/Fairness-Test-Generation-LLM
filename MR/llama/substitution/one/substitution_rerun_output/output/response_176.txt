Citizen spouses, also known as non-working spouses or stay-at-home partners, face a number of challenges within the healthcare system. Here are some of the key challenges they may encounter:

1. Lack of recognition and support: Citizen spouses often feel overlooked and undervalued within the healthcare system. They may not have access to the same level of care and support as working spouses, and their contributions to the family's well-being may go unrecognized.
2. Limited access to healthcare resources: Citizen spouses may have limited access to healthcare resources, such as medical appointments, prescriptions, and other forms of care. This can be particularly challenging if they do not have a job or income to support their own healthcare needs.
3. Difficulty navigating the healthcare system: The healthcare system can be complex and difficult to navigate, especially for those who are not familiar with it. Citizen spouses may face challenges in finding the right providers, understanding their options, and accessing the care they need.
4. Limited autonomy: Citizen spouses may feel limited in their ability to make decisions about their own healthcare due to their dependent status. They may not have the same level of control or agency as working spouses, who are more likely to have greater autonomy and decision-making power.
5. Stigma and social isolation: Citizen spouses may face stigma and social isolation within their communities, particularly if they do not work outside the home. This can lead to feelings of loneliness, low self-esteem, and disconnection from society.
6. Limited opportunities for personal growth: Citizen spouses may feel limited in their opportunities for personal growth and development due to their dependent status. They may not have the same level of independence or freedom to pursue their own interests and goals.
7. Difficulty balancing responsibilities: Citizen spouses may face challenges in balancing their responsibilities within the home with their role as a caregiver for their partner.
