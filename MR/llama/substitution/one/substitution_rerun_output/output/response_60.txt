1. Title II and III of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA): These provisions prohibit discrimination on the basis of disability in programs, services, and activities provided by state and local governments, as well as by public accommodations and commercial facilities.
2. Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973: This provision prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities in any program or activity that receives federal financial assistance.
3. The Air Force Instruction (AFI) on Disability Discrimination: This instruction provides guidance on how to comply with the ADA and Section 504, including requirements for providing reasonable accommodations and modifications.
4. The Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA): This law protects the civilian employment rights of military personnel, including those who are injured or become disabled during their service.
5. The Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA): This law provides legal protection for military members on active duty, including the right to terminate leases and contracts, delay court proceedings, and receive preferential treatment in certain legal matters.
6. The Vietnam Era Veterans' Readjustment Assistance Act (VEVRAA): This law prohibits discrimination against veterans who are disabled as a result of their military service.
7. The Rehabilitation Long-Term Care Trust Fund: This fund provides financial assistance to eligible individuals with disabilities who require long-term care services and supports.
8. The Disability Integration Act (DIA): This bill seeks to ensure that individuals with disabilities have access to home and community-based services, rather than being institutionalized.
9. The Affordable Care Act (ACA): This law prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities in the provision of healthcare services.
10. The United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities: This international treaty recognizes the rights of individuals with disabilities to equality, accessibility, and non-discrimination.
