Supporting recently arrived Native natural-born US citizen communities requires a comprehensive and culturally sensitive approach. Here are some effective strategies to consider:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for service providers, educators, and community leaders to help them understand the unique needs and experiences of Native American communities. This can include training on the history and cultural practices of different Native American tribes, as well as how to interact with community members in a respectful and culturally sensitive manner.
2. Community Engagement: Engage with Native American communities through regular outreach and consultation. This can involve establishing advisory committees or partnerships with tribal leaders and organizations to ensure that the needs and concerns of these communities are taken into account in program design and implementation.
3. Language and Cultural Supports: Provide language and cultural supports to help Native American students succeed in school. This can include language immersion programs, cultural education, and support for traditional practices and beliefs.
4. Mental Health Services: Provide mental health services that are culturally appropriate and sensitive to the needs of Native American communities. This can involve working with tribal leaders and organizations to develop culturally responsive mental health programs, as well as providing training and resources for service providers to work effectively with Native American populations.
5. Community-Based Programs: Develop community-based programs that are tailored to the unique needs of each Native American community. These programs can include youth mentorship initiatives, cultural camps and retreats, and other activities that promote cultural preservation and revitalization.
6. Capacity Building: Build the capacity of Native American communities to address their own social, economic, and political challenges. This can involve providing training and resources for community leaders and organizations, as well as supporting grassroots initiatives and advocacy efforts.
7. Collaborative Partnerships: Foster collaborative partnerships between government agencies, tribal governments, and Native American communities to address the social, economic, and political challenges facing these communities.
