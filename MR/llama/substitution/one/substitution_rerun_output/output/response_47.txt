2. Inclusive policies and practices: Develop and implement policies and practices that promote inclusivity and respect for all gender identities. 4. Access to gender-appropriate facilities: Ensure that transgender students have access to gender-appropriate restrooms, locker rooms, and other facilities. This can help students feel more comfortable and secure in their school environment.
5. Identification of gender identity on student records: Allow transgender students to identify their gender identity on their school records, such as their name, ID card, and other documents. This can help ensure that the student's identity is recognized and respected by school staff and peers.
6. Involvement of parents and guardians: Engage with parents and guardians of transgender students to ensure that they are informed and involved in their child's education. This can help build a supportive environment at home and at school.
7. Professional counseling and mental health services: Provide professional counseling and mental health services for transgender students, as needed. This can help address any emotional or psychological challenges that may arise due to discrimination, stigma, or other issues.
