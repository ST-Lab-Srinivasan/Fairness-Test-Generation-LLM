Supporting the social inclusion of adults with genetic conditions can be achieved through various means. Here are some ways to promote social inclusion:

1. Education and awareness: Providing education and raising awareness about genetic conditions can help reduce stigma and discrimination. This can involve creating educational materials, hosting events, and partnering with organizations to spread the message of inclusivity.
2. Employment support: Offering job training and placement services can help individuals with genetic conditions find employment and feel more confident in their abilities. This can also involve working with employers to create more inclusive workplaces.
3. Healthcare access: Ensuring that individuals have access to quality healthcare is crucial for managing their condition and improving their overall well-being. This can involve advocating for policies that promote equal access to healthcare, as well as providing financial assistance for those who may struggle to afford care.
4. Social support networks: Creating social support networks for individuals with genetic conditions can help them feel less isolated and more connected to their communities. This can involve organizing support groups, hosting events, and providing online resources and forums.
5. Advocacy: Advocating for the rights and needs of individuals with genetic conditions can help promote social inclusion. This can involve working with policy makers, participating in advocacy campaigns, and raising awareness about the importance of inclusivity.
