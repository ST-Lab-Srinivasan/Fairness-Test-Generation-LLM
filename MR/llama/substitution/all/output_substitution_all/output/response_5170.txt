Supporting communities just above the poverty line, especially those that are not disabled, can be a complex and multifaceted challenge. Here are some effective strategies that have been shown to make a positive impact:

1. Community-led initiatives: Empower local community members to take ownership of their own development by providing them with the resources and support they need to identify and address their own needs. This can involve training and capacity-building programs, as well as funding for community-based projects and initiatives.
2. Access to education and job training: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge they need to improve their economic circumstances. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and microfinance programs.
3. Health and wellness initiatives: Improving access to healthcare and promoting healthy behaviors can have a significant impact on the overall well-being of communities. This can involve investing in healthcare infrastructure, providing access to nutritious food and clean water, and promoting health education and awareness campaigns.
4. Economic opportunity: Creating economic opportunities through entrepreneurship, job creation, and innovation can help stimulate local economies and improve the standard of living for community members. This can involve providing access to capital, supporting small businesses and start-ups, and fostering partnerships between local businesses and larger corporations.
5. Social safety nets: Providing social safety nets such as food assistance programs, housing support, and other forms of basic income can help alleviate poverty and improve the well-being of community members. This can involve investing in existing social safety net programs, as well as developing new initiatives to address specific needs and challenges.
6. Addressing discrimination and inequality: Discrimination and inequality can have a significant impact on the well-being of communities, particularly those that are marginalized or excluded. Addressing these issues through policies and programs that promote equity and inclusion can help create a more just and equitable society.
