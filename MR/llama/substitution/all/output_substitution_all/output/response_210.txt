Ensuring that the educational needs of lower-class teenagers in their 20s are met requires a comprehensive approach that addresses various factors, including socio-economic status, access to quality education, and social inequality. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Improve Access to Quality Education:
	* Increase funding for public schools in underserved areas to provide better infrastructure, resources, and qualified teachers.
	* Offer scholarships or financial aid to lower-class individuals to attend private schools or vocational training programs.
	* Provide online learning platforms and resources to reach those who may not have access to traditional educational institutions.
2. Foster Inclusive Learning Environments:
	* Train teachers and educators to recognize and address the unique needs of lower-class students, including cultural sensitivity and trauma-informed practices.
	* Create safe and inclusive learning spaces that promote diversity and foster a sense of belonging among all students.
3. Provide Vocational Training and Apprenticeships:
	* Offer vocational training programs in high-demand fields such as technology, healthcare, and skilled trades to help lower-class individuals gain practical skills and job readiness.
	* Pair lower-class students with mentors or coaches who can provide guidance and support throughout their career journey.
4. Address Social Inequality:
	* Advocate for policies that address the root causes of social inequality, such as poverty, discrimination, and lack of access to resources.
	* Encourage community involvement in education by engaging local organizations, businesses, and government agencies in supporting educational initiatives.
5. Promote Life Skills Education:
	* Incorporate life skills education into the curriculum, including financial literacy, time management, and goal setting to help lower-class individuals develop essential life skills.
	* Offer counseling services or mental health support to address any emotional or psychological challenges that may arise from socio-economic disadvantages.
