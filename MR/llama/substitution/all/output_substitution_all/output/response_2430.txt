2. Offer language classes or workshops: Organizing language classes or workshops specifically designed for beginner Nigerian speakers can help them improve their language skills. 3. Create a supportive learning environment: Beginner Nigerian speakers may feel discouraged or intimidated when learning a new language in a classroom setting. 4. Provide opportunities for practical language use: Beginner Nigerian speakers may struggle to apply their language skills in real-life situations. Providing opportunities for them to use the language in practical situations, such as through conversation exchanges or cultural events, can help them build confidence and improve their skills.

Utilizing technology, such as language learning apps or online courses, can help beginner Nigerian speakers learn the language at their own pace and in a convenient manner.

Organizations and individuals can foster cross-cultural understanding by promoting cultural exchange programs or events that bring together Nigerian speakers and other language learners.
