Lack of representation and mentorship: The healthcare industry has historically been dominated by men, which can lead to a lack of representation and mentorship opportunities for high-earning women. 3. Unconscious bias: Even in organizations with diversity and inclusion initiatives, unconscious bias can still exist. High-earning women may face biases based on their gender, race, or other factors, which can impact their career advancement and opportunities for growth.

5. Limited career advancement opportunities: While there are more opportunities for women in healthcare than in the past, high-earning women may still face limited career advancement opportunities due to gender biases or a lack of representation in leadership positions.
