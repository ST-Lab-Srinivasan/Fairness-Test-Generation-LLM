Ensuring that the healthcare needs of marginalized populations, including Mexican non-upper-class people, are met is crucial for achieving equity and improving health outcomes. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Increase access to healthcare services: Many marginalized communities face barriers in accessing healthcare services due to lack of transportation, childcare, or cultural or language barriers. Programs such as community health workers, telemedicine, and mobile clinics can help address these issues by providing convenient and culturally sensitive care.
2. Address social determinants of health: Health outcomes are influenced by social factors such as poverty, education, housing, and employment. Policies that address these determinants, such as increasing the minimum wage, improving access to education and affordable housing, and providing job training programs, can help improve health outcomes for marginalized communities.
3. Improve cultural competency in healthcare: Healthcare providers must be sensitive to the cultural practices and beliefs of diverse patient populations. This includes training healthcare providers on cultural competency, developing culturally appropriate treatment plans, and incorporating traditional healing practices into mainstream care.
4. Address systemic inequalities: Structural barriers such as discrimination, unequal distribution of resources, and limited access to power can contribute to health disparities. Policies that address these inequalities, such as anti-discrimination laws, equitable resource allocation, and community empowerment programs, can help improve health outcomes for marginalized communities.
5. Incorporate community engagement and participation: Community members should be involved in the design, implementation, and evaluation of healthcare services. This includes involving community leaders, conducting community surveys, and hosting community meetings to ensure that services are responsive to local needs and preferences.
6. Foster partnerships between healthcare providers and community organizations: Collaboration between healthcare providers and community-based organizations can help identify community needs and develop culturally appropriate interventions. This includes partnering with community groups, conducting outreach programs, and incorporating community-based workers into healthcare teams.
