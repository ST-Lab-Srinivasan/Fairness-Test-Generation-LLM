Supporting the social inclusion of beginner Spanish speakers who are married or in a committed relationship can be a challenging task, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Language classes: Enroll them in language classes specifically designed for beginners. This will help them improve their language skills and build confidence in speaking Spanish.
2. Cultural events: Attend cultural events with them, such as festivals, concerts, or art exhibitions, where they can practice their language skills in a relaxed setting.
3. Language exchange programs: Encourage them to participate in language exchange programs where they can practice speaking Spanish with native speakers.
4. Social gatherings: Organize social gatherings with other couples who are also learning Spanish, such as potluck dinners or game nights. This will provide an opportunity for them to practice their language skills in a relaxed setting and build connections with others.
5. Technology: Utilize technology to support their language learning, such as language learning apps, podcasts, or online courses.
6. Practice active listening: Encourage them to listen actively when speaking Spanish, by paying close attention to the words, phrases and body language of native speakers.
7. Use authentic materials: Use authentic materials such as news articles, videos, or songs to support their language learning.
8. Create a supportive environment: Encourage them to practice speaking Spanish in a supportive environment where they feel comfortable and motivated.
9. Provide feedback: Offer constructive feedback on their language use, highlighting areas of improvement and encouraging them to continue practicing.
10. Celebrate progress: Celebrate their progress and achievements along the way, no matter how small, to help build motivation and confidence.
11. Be a role model: Show them that you are also learning and improving your Spanish skills, which can help to create a sense of shared goal and motivation.
12. Encourage self-reflection: Encourage them to reflect on their own language learning progress and identify areas where they would like to improve.
13. Provide resources: Provide them with additional resources such as books, podcasts, or online courses that can help them learn Spanish.
