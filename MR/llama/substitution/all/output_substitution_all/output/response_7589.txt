Ensuring that the housing needs of very young individuals, particularly those in their 90s and beyond, are met requires a comprehensive approach that addresses various factors. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Age-Friendly Housing: Develop age-friendly housing options that cater to the unique needs of older adults. This can include features like wider doorways, lower cabinets, and easier-to-use appliances. Consider incorporating universal design principles to make homes more accessible and comfortable for older adults.
2. Home Modifications: Offer home modifications or adaptations to help individuals maintain their independence and quality of life. This can include installing grab bars, non-slip surfaces, and wheelchair ramps, as well as making adjustments to the layout of the home to accommodate mobility aids.
3. Supportive Housing: Develop supportive housing options that provide on-site care and services tailored to the needs of older adults. This can include assisted living facilities, memory care units, and continuing care retirement communities (CCRCs). Ensure that these facilities are designed with safety features, such as emergency response systems and fall prevention measures, to protect residents.
4. Housing Counseling: Offer housing counseling services specifically tailored to the needs of older adults. This can include assistance with downsizing, finding affordable housing, and navigating the often-complex process of buying or renting a home.
5. Transportation Services: Provide transportation services that cater to the unique needs of older adults, such as wheelchair-accessible vehicles and door-to-door pickup and drop-off. This can help individuals maintain their independence and access essential services.
6. Social Support Networks: Encourage the development of social support networks within the community. This can include volunteer programs, senior centers, and intergenerational initiatives that connect older adults with younger generations. Social connections are crucial for mental and emotional well-being, particularly during later life stages.
