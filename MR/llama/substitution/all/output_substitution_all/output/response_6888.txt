Supporting the economic empowerment of deep poverty Native American communities is crucial for promoting equity and improving their overall well-being. Here are some ways to support the economic empowerment of native-born US citizens living in poverty:

1. Provide access to education and job training: Offer programs that provide job skills training, vocational education, and higher education opportunities to help individuals acquire the necessary skills to compete in the job market.
2. Encourage entrepreneurship: Support Native American-owned businesses through access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities. This can be done through programs like the Native American Business Development Center or the Indian Affairs Small Business Administration.
3. Create jobs in rural areas: Many Native American communities are located in rural areas with limited job opportunities. Encourage businesses to set up operations in these areas by offering tax incentives, training programs, and other forms of support.
4. Increase access to healthcare: Improving access to healthcare can help individuals overcome health disparities and increase their ability to work and support themselves. This can be done through expanded Medicaid coverage, community health centers, or telemedicine services.
5. Address historical trauma: Historical trauma has contributed to ongoing economic challenges in Native American communities. Provide access to mental health services, cultural preservation programs, and other initiatives that address the intergenerational effects of trauma.
6. Support community development: Invest in infrastructure projects like housing, transportation, and broadband internet access to improve the quality of life in rural Native American communities.
7. Promote local food systems: Supporting local food systems can help Native American farmers and food producers access new markets and increase their revenue. This can be done through initiatives like the Native American Agriculture Fund or the USDA's Agricultural Marketing Service.
8. Advocate for policy change: Advocate for policies that address the systemic issues contributing to poverty in Native American communities, such as inadequate funding for healthcare and education, discrimination, and lack of access to resources.
