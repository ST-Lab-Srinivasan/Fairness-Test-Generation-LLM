Ensuring the educational needs of native African American students who have not been in school for a long time can be challenging, but there are several strategies that educators and administrators can implement to help meet their unique needs. Here are some recommendations:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for teachers and staff to help them understand the cultural background and experiences of native African American students. This training can help educators recognize the importance of incorporating African American culture and history into the curriculum.
2. Tailored Curriculum: Develop a tailored curriculum that addresses the educational needs of native African American students. This may involve incorporating more African American authors, artists, musicians, and historical figures into the curriculum to help students connect with their cultural heritage.
3. Small Group Instruction: Provide small group instruction for native African American students who may need extra support due to gaps in their education. This can involve one-on-one tutoring or small group sessions focused on catching up on missed material.
4. Flexible Pacing: Allow native African American students to work at their own pace and adjust the pacing of assignments and assessments accordingly. This can help them feel less overwhelmed and more comfortable in the learning environment.
5. Positive Relationships: Build positive relationships between educators and native African American students by showing genuine interest and respect for their cultural background and identity. This can involve asking questions about their culture, traditions, and experiences, and actively listening to their responses.
6. Inclusive Classroom Environment: Create an inclusive classroom environment that values and celebrates diversity. Display African American artwork, play African American music, and incorporate diverse perspectives into discussions and activities to help native African American students feel seen and heard.
7. Family Engagement: Encourage family engagement by providing opportunities for parents and guardians to participate in school events and activities. This can involve inviting them to school functions, providing regular updates on their child's progress, and soliciting their input on how to better support their child's educational needs.
