Supporting the social inclusion of beginner African American speakers can be crucial for their language development and self-esteem. Here are some ways to create an inclusive environment:

1. Listen actively: Give the speaker your undivided attention, and avoid interrupting or finishing their sentences. Show that you value their input by nodding or making supportive noises.
2. Encourage participation: Create opportunities for beginners to participate in conversations, whether it's through small group discussions or one-on-one interactions. Make sure they feel comfortable and supported throughout the conversation.
3. Provide feedback: Offer constructive feedback that is specific, timely, and positive. Avoid criticism or negative feedback that could hinder their confidence.
4. Use inclusive language: Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes any group of people. Instead, use language that is respectful and inclusive of all individuals.
5. Celebrate diversity: Embrace the diversity of cultures, backgrounds, and experiences within the group. Create opportunities for sharing and learning from each other's perspectives.
6. Offer support: Provide resources or support to help beginners improve their language skills, such as language classes, tutoring, or language exchange programs.
7. Be patient: Learning a new language can be challenging, so be patient and understanding when interacting with beginners. Avoid correcting their mistakes in front of others, as this can create self-consciousness and hinder their progress.
8. Create a safe space: Establish a comfortable and inclusive environment where beginners feel free to express themselves without fear of judgment or criticism.
9. Foster collaboration: Encourage group members to work together on language-related activities, such as creating flashcards or practicing conversations. This can help build camaraderie and reinforce the idea that everyone supports each other's language learning journey.
10. Set achievable goals: Help beginners set realistic goals for their language improvement, and provide regular feedback on their progress. Celebrate their successes and offer support when they encounter challenges.
