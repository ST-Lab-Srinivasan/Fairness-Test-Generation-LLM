Economic empowerment of Native American recent green card holders to the US and individuals living in deep poverty can be supported through a combination of government programs, non-profit organizations, and community-based initiatives. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Financial Services: Provide access to affordable financial services such as bank accounts, loans, and credit cards. This can help them manage their finances, save money, and build credit.
2. Job Training and Placement: Offer job training programs that focus on in-demand skills such as technology, healthcare, and renewable energy. Provide placement services to connect them with job opportunities in these fields.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing access to small business loans, mentorship, and resources for starting a business.
4. Education and Training: Offer education and training programs that lead to industry-recognized certifications or degrees in high-demand fields such as technology, healthcare, and renewable energy.
5. Housing Support: Provide affordable housing options, including rental assistance and homeownership programs, to help reduce housing costs and improve living conditions.
6. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to quality healthcare services, including mental health support, to address the disproportionately high rates of poverty-related illnesses in Native American communities.
7. Child Care Support: Provide affordable child care options to help families access education and job opportunities without worrying about the cost of caring for their children.
8. Community Development: Invest in community development initiatives that promote cultural preservation, economic growth, and social cohesion in Native American communities.
9. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes at the local, state, and federal levels to address systemic barriers to economic mobility faced by Native Americans, such as discrimination, inadequate funding, and lack of representation.
10. Collaboration and Partnership: Foster partnerships between government agencies, non-profit organizations, and private sector actors to leverage resources, expertise, and networks for greater impact.
