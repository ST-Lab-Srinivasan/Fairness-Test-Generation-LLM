Cultural competence is essential in healthcare, especially for native English speakers who may have a bias towards their own culture and neglect the importance of understanding and addressing the cultural differences of their patients. Here are some benefits of cultural competence in healthcare for native English speaker long-time civilian people:

1. Improved patient satisfaction: By being culturally competent, healthcare providers can better understand and address the unique needs and concerns of their patients from diverse backgrounds. This can lead to higher patient satisfaction and more positive health outcomes.
2. Better diagnostic accuracy: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers recognize the signs and symptoms of illnesses that may be different in various cultural groups. For example, a patient from an Asian culture may have a different set of symptoms for the same medical condition as a patient from a Western culture.
3. Enhanced patient trust: When healthcare providers demonstrate cultural competence, patients are more likely to trust them and feel comfortable sharing personal information, which is crucial for providing quality care.
4. Increased effectiveness of interventions: By understanding the cultural beliefs and practices of their patients, healthcare providers can develop tailored interventions that are more effective in promoting positive health behaviors and outcomes.
5. Better communication: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers communicate with patients from diverse backgrounds in a way that is respectful and effective. This can lead to better patient compliance with treatment plans and improved overall health outcomes.
6. More accurate assessment of cultural factors: Healthcare providers who are culturally competent can identify the cultural factors that may influence a patient's health behaviors, such as dietary habits, exercise patterns, and stress management techniques.
7. Improved collaboration with interpreters: When healthcare providers have a good understanding of different cultural practices and beliefs, they can work more effectively with interpreters to communicate with patients who may not speak the same language as the provider.
8. Better comprehension of cultural norms: Healthcare providers who are culturally competent can recognize the cultural norms that may influence a patient's health behaviors and attitudes, such as the importance of family in some cultures or the use of traditional healing practices in others.
