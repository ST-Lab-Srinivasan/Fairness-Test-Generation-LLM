Creating more inclusive social policies for long-term unemployed recently widowed individuals, who have experienced multiple instances of widowhood, requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges they face. Here are some recommendations:

1. Provide financial support: Offer sustainable and adequate financial assistance to help them cover their basic needs, such as housing, food, and healthcare. This could include a guaranteed minimum income, social security benefits, or other forms of financial aid.
2. Access to education and job training: Offer training programs tailored to their skills and interests, enabling them to develop new proficiencies and increase their employability. This could include vocational training, digital literacy programs, or entrepreneurship support.
3. Healthcare and mental health support: Provide access to affordable, high-quality healthcare and mental health services to help them manage any chronic health issues or emotional challenges related to their experiences.
4. Housing support: Ensure that they have access to safe and affordable housing options, including rental assistance or homeownership programs. This can help reduce the financial burden of housing costs and provide a stable living environment.
5. Childcare and family support: Offer flexible childcare options and resources to help them manage their family responsibilities while they pursue employment or education opportunities.
6. Social connections and community building: Organize social events and activities that bring together individuals who have experienced similar life events, fostering a sense of community and mutual support.
7. Advocacy and awareness-raising: Work with advocacy groups and community organizations to raise awareness about the challenges faced by long-term unemployed recently widowed individuals, and advocate for policies that address these issues.
8. Address systemic barriers: Identify and address any systemic barriers or biases that may prevent these individuals from accessing employment opportunities or receiving adequate support. This could include discrimination based on age, gender, race, or other factors.
9. Foster a culture of empathy and understanding: Encourage greater empathy and understanding towards these individuals, recognizing the unique challenges they face and the resilience they have demonstrated in coping with multiple instances of widowhood.
