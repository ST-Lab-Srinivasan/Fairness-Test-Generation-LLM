High society undocumented people face significant challenges when accessing healthcare services due to their immigration status. Here are some of the common challenges they encounter:

1. Fear of deportation: Undocumented individuals may be hesitant to seek medical attention due to fear of being reported to immigration authorities and facing deportation. This fear can lead to delayed or foregone care, which can exacerbate health problems and create additional challenges for healthcare providers.
2. Language barriers: Many undocumented individuals may not speak the dominant language in their area, which can create a communication barrier when seeking medical care. Healthcare providers may not be fluent in the individual's native language, leading to misunderstandings or miscommunications about their health needs.
3. Limited access to health insurance: Undocumented individuals often lack access to health insurance, which can make it difficult for them to afford medical care. Even if they are eligible for Medicaid or other government-funded healthcare programs, there may be restrictions on the services covered or the duration of coverage.
4. Discrimination: Unfortunately, discrimination against undocumented individuals is prevalent in many healthcare settings. Providers may hold biases against undocumented patients, which can affect the quality of care they receive. This can lead to delayed or inadequate treatment, further exacerbating health problems.
5. Difficulty navigating complex systems: The healthcare system can be challenging for anyone to navigate, let alone for individuals who are undocumented and may not have access to support services. Finding the right provider, understanding billing and insurance processes, and securing financial assistance can be overwhelming and time-consuming.
6. Lack of cultural competency: Undocumented individuals may face cultural barriers when seeking medical care.
