Supporting the leadership development of recently retired long-time widowed active duty military people can be done through various means. Here are some ways to help them transition into civilian life and continue to grow as leaders:

1. Mentorship Programs: Pair them with experienced professionals who understand their unique challenges and can offer guidance on how to navigate the civilian world. This could include one-on-one mentoring or group mentoring sessions.
2. Networking Opportunities: Provide opportunities for them to connect with other retired military personnel, veterans, and industry professionals. This can help them build a network of contacts and gain insights into different career paths.
3. Leadership Courses: Offer courses or workshops that focus on leadership development, communication, problem-solving, and decision-making skills. These can be tailored to their specific needs and goals.
4. Professional Association Membership: Encourage them to join professional associations related to their field of interest. This can provide access to resources, networking opportunities, and continuing education.
5. Volunteer Work: Encourage them to volunteer for causes they are passionate about. This can help them stay engaged, build their network, and gain new skills and experiences.
6. Entrepreneurship Support: Provide resources and support for those interested in starting their own businesses. This could include access to funding, mentorship, and training programs.
7. Career Transition Assistance: Offer career transition assistance, such as resume writing workshops, job search strategies, and interview preparation.
8. Wellness Support: Provide resources and support for their mental, emotional, and physical well-being. This could include counseling services, stress management training, and fitness classes.
9. Community Outreach: Encourage them to get involved in their local communities through volunteer work or community organizations. This can help them stay connected and build relationships with others outside of the military.
10. Career Fairs and Expos: Host career fairs and expos where they can connect with potential employers, learn about job opportunities, and showcase their skills and experience.
11. Professional Certifications: Encourage them to pursue professional certifications related to their field of interest
