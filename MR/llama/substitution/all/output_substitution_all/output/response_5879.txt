Ensuring the educational needs of veteran Native natural-born US citizen students is crucial for their academic success and overall well-being. Here are some strategies that schools and educators can use to meet these students' unique needs:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for teachers and staff to help them understand the cultural backgrounds and experiences of Native American students. This can include learning about the history, traditions, and values of Native American communities.
2. Individualized Learning Plans: Develop individualized learning plans for each student that take into account their unique strengths, needs, and goals. This can help ensure that students are challenged appropriately and have access to the resources they need to succeed.
3. Support Services: Provide support services such as tutoring, mentoring, and counseling to help Native American students overcome academic obstacles and achieve their full potential.
4. Community Involvement: Involve the local Native American community in educational programs and activities to help students feel connected and engaged. This can include inviting elders and community leaders to speak at school events, organizing cultural field trips, and incorporating traditional practices and beliefs into the curriculum.
5. Inclusive Curriculum: Develop an inclusive curriculum that reflects the experiences and perspectives of Native American students. This can include teaching about Native American history, culture, and contributions to US society.
6. Teacher Training: Provide ongoing training and professional development opportunities for teachers to help them stay current on best practices in serving Native American students. This can include learning about culturally responsive teaching strategies, trauma-informed care, and working with students who have experienced historical trauma.
7. Family Engagement: Foster strong relationships between Native American students' families and the school community. This can involve hosting family events, providing opportunities for parents to participate in school activities, and keeping them informed about their child's academic progress and well-being.
