Supporting the civic engagement of long-term refugee, Native American, and recently undocumented communities in the US requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and needs. Here are some ways to support their civic engagement:

1. Language Access: Provide language access services such as interpretation, translation, and bilingual voting materials to ensure these communities can fully participate in the democratic process.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training for election officials, poll workers, and community leaders to better understand the needs and experiences of these communities.
3. Voter Registration Drives: Organize voter registration drives specifically targeting these communities to increase their political representation and participation.
4. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and engagement efforts to educate these communities about the importance of voting, the electoral process, and their rights as citizens.
5. Civic Education: Provide civic education programs that cater to the specific needs and experiences of these communities, such as teaching about the role of government, how to participate in the democratic process, and how to advocate for their rights.
6. Advocacy and Policy Reform: Advocate for policies that support the civic engagement of these communities, such as immigration reform, language access laws, and voting rights protections.
7. Collaboration with Community Organizations: Partner with community-based organizations that work specifically with these communities to amplify their voices and increase their political power.
8. Digital Inclusion: Ensure that these communities have access to digital tools and technology to facilitate civic engagement, such as online voter registration, digital voting platforms, and social media outreach.
9. Civic Artivism: Encourage artivistic expression and creative activism as a means of mobilizing these communities and raising awareness about issues that affect them.
10. Community-Led Elections: Support community-led elections, where members of these communities are actively involved in the decision-making process around voting locations, poll worker recruitment, and election materials.
