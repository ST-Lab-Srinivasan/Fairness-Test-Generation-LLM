Creating more inclusive community programs for beginner Arabic speakers, especially newly single individuals, requires a thoughtful and multi-faceted approach. Here are some suggestions to help make these programs more welcoming and supportive:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the program is accessible to all, regardless of their abilities or location. Offer online or virtual options for those who may have difficulty attending in-person events. Provide accommodations such as translation services, audio descriptions, or sign language interpretation for individuals with disabilities.
2. Inclusive marketing: Use inclusive language and imagery in promoting the program to avoid alienating potential participants. Highlight the diversity of the program's target audience and emphasize that everyone is welcome.
3. Cultural sensitivity: Be mindful of cultural differences and tailor the program to be sensitive to the needs and practices of the Arabic-speaking community. This can involve incorporating traditional Arabic customs, music, and art into the program.
4. Supportive environment: Create a safe and supportive environment for participants by promoting mutual respect, empathy, and understanding. Encourage open communication and active listening among participants.
5. Flexible scheduling: Offer flexible scheduling options to accommodate different schedules and needs. This can include offering multiple sessions per week or month, or providing recordings of classes for those who cannot attend in person.
6. Personalized learning: Provide personalized learning experiences tailored to the individual's needs and goals. This can involve assessing each participant's language level and creating a customized learning plan.
7. Community building: Encourage community building through social events, group activities, or peer-to-peer learning. This can help participants feel more connected and supported throughout their learning journey.
8. Access to resources: Provide access to additional resources such as language learning apps, podcasts, or online courses to support participants' learning outside of the program.
