Supporting the social inclusion of late 30s recent refugees to Canada can be achieved through various means. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Language Training and Education: Providing language training and education can help refugees improve their communication skills, which is essential for social integration. Offer classes or workshops on English or French language and culture, as well as access to language learning resources.
2. Job Training and Placement: Refugees often face significant barriers to finding employment due to a lack of job experience, education, or language skills. Offer job training programs that focus on the Canadian job market and provide placement services to help refugees find stable, well-paying jobs.
3. Mentorship Programs: Pairing recent refugees with experienced professionals in their field can provide valuable guidance and support. This can help refugees navigate the job market, build professional networks, and gain confidence in their abilities.
4. Cultural Adaptation Support: Assist refugees in adapting to Canadian culture by providing information on local customs, traditions, and values. Offer cultural orientation programs that cover topics such as etiquette, communication styles, and social norms.
5. Community Engagement: Encourage recent refugees to participate in community events and activities to help them connect with their new surroundings. This can include volunteering opportunities, cultural festivals, or sporting events.
6. Childcare and Family Support: Many recent refugees have children who may face challenges in adjusting to a new environment. Provide childcare services or referrals to family-friendly resources to help support families.
7. Health Services: Refugees often face health challenges due to their experiences, such as trauma, malnutrition, or infectious diseases. Ensure access to primary care and mental health services, including counseling and psychotherapy.
8. Housing Support: Secure affordable and safe housing for recent refugees, especially those who may face challenges in finding adequate accommodation.
