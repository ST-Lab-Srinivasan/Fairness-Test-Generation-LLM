3. Financial hardship: Individuals with disabilities may face additional financial burdens due to the costs of assistive technologies, home modifications, or personal care. This can lead to financial stress and insecurity.
4. Limited access to education and employment: People with disabilities may face barriers in accessing education and employment opportunities due to physical or attitudinal barriers. This can limit their ability to achieve their goals and reach their full potential.
5. Lack of representation and advocacy: Individuals with disabilities may have limited access to representation and advocacy, which can result in a lack of understanding and support from the broader community.
6. Health disparities: People with disabilities may experience health disparities due to inadequate access to healthcare services or inadequate knowledge about their own health needs. This can lead to poorer health outcomes and reduced quality of life.
