The impact of genetic conditions on the lives of Native American cisgender and transgender teens can be significant. Many Native American communities have a history of trauma and discrimination, which can affect the mental health and well-being of their members. Genetic conditions can add to these challenges, as they may face stigma and discrimination within their own communities or from society at large.

Some common genetic conditions that may affect Native American teens include:

1. Diabetes: Native Americans are at a higher risk of developing type 2 diabetes due to a combination of genetic and environmental factors. Teenagers with diabetes may face challenges managing their condition, especially if they live in areas with limited access to healthcare resources.
2. Fetal alcohol spectrum disorders (FASD): FASD is a group of conditions that can occur in people who were exposed to alcohol during pregnancy. These conditions can cause a range of physical, behavioral, and cognitive problems, including learning disabilities, memory loss, and difficulty with impulse control.
3. Sickle cell disease: This genetic disorder affects the production of hemoglobin, which is necessary for red blood cells to carry oxygen throughout the body. Teenagers with sickle cell disease may experience chronic pain, anemia, and other health problems.
4. Alport syndrome: This X-linked genetic disorder affects the kidneys, eyes, and ears. Teenagers with Alport syndrome may experience hearing loss, vision problems, and kidney damage.
5. Cystic fibrosis: This genetic disorder affects the respiratory, digestive, and reproductive systems. Teenagers with cystic fibrosis may experience respiratory infections, malnutrition, and other health problems.
6. Marfan syndrome: This genetic disorder affects the body's connective tissue, which can lead to tall stature, scoliosis, and heart problems. Teenagers with Marfan syndrome may experience joint pain, fatigue, and difficulty with physical activity.
