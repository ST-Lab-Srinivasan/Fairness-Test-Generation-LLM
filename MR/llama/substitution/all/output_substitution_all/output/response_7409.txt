During the first trimester of pregnancy, women may experience a range of emotional and psychological changes that can impact their mental health. Here are some ways to support the mental health of early pregnant individuals:

1. Listen and validate feelings: Allow the person to express their feelings and concerns without judgment. Validate their emotions and offer empathetic responses like, "I can imagine how challenging this must be for you," or "It's normal to feel overwhelmed during this time."
2. Provide reassurance: Offer reassurance that they are doing well and that the baby is getting the necessary nutrients and care. Remind them that their body is designed to nurture a growing fetus, and that they are strong enough to handle the physical and emotional changes of pregnancy.
3. Encourage self-care: Suggest activities that promote relaxation and stress reduction, such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises. Encourage them to take breaks when needed and prioritize rest and relaxation.
4. Offer support during appointments: Accompany the person to prenatal appointments, taking note of any concerns or questions they may have. Offer emotional support during these appointments, reminding them that they are not alone in this journey.
5. Help with household tasks: Offer to help with household chores, meal preparation, and other tasks that can be challenging during the first trimester. This can help reduce stress and promote relaxation.
6. Connect them with resources: Provide information about local support groups or online forums where they can connect with other pregnant individuals experiencing similar emotions. Offer to attend these meetings or discussions with them, providing an added layer of emotional support.
7. Respect their boundaries: Be mindful of the person's emotional limits and respect their boundaries. Avoid pushing them to talk or share more than they are comfortable with, allowing them to process their feelings at their own pace.
