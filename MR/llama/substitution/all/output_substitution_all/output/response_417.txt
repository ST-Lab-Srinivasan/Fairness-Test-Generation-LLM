1. Language Training: Offer language training programs to help refugees improve their English skills, which are essential for career advancement. Many organizations, such as the International Rescue Committee (IRC) and the Refugee Center of Northern Virginia, offer language classes specifically tailored to refugees' needs.
2. Career Counseling: Provide one-on-one career counseling sessions to help refugees identify their strengths, set career goals, and explore job opportunities in their field of interest. Organizations like the Jewish Vocational Service (JVS) offer career counseling services specifically designed for refugees.
3. Job Readiness Training: Offer job readiness training programs that teach refugees the skills they need to succeed in the US workplace, such as resume writing, interviewing techniques, and workplace cultural awareness. The IRC and JVS offer such programs.
4. Networking Opportunities: Arrange networking events or connect refugees with professional mentors who can provide guidance and support in their chosen fields. This can help refugees build valuable connections and gain insights into different industries.
5. Education and Certification: Encourage recent refugees to pursue higher education or certifications that align with their career goals. Many organizations, such as the International Institute of Los Angeles, offer educational programs specifically designed for refugees.

7. Professional Development: Offer workshops or training sessions on topics such as time management, communication skills, and workplace etiquette to help refugees succeed in the US work environment.
