Ensuring that the healthcare needs of low-income or marginalized communities, including those in England, are met is a complex issue that involves various factors and stakeholders. Here are some potential strategies that could be implemented to address this challenge:

1. Increase access to healthcare services: One of the primary barriers to accessing healthcare for low-income individuals is lack of access to healthcare facilities or transportation to get there. Governments and healthcare providers can work together to increase the number of community clinics or health centers in underserved areas, and provide free or affordable transportation to these facilities.
2. Implement a universal healthcare system: A universal healthcare system provides everyone with access to necessary healthcare services without regard to their income or social status. This can help ensure that low-income individuals receive the same level of care as those who are more financially secure.
3. Provide financial assistance: Many low-income individuals struggle to pay for healthcare services, including deductibles, co-pays, and prescription medications. Governments and healthcare providers can offer financial assistance programs, such as sliding fee scales or discounts, to help reduce these costs.
4. Address social determinants of health: Social determinants of health, such as housing, food security, and employment status, can have a significant impact on an individual's overall health. Governments and community organizations can work together to address these issues by providing affordable housing options, food assistance programs, and job training and placement services.
5. Improve cultural competency: Healthcare providers must be aware of the cultural beliefs and practices of the communities they serve. This includes understanding how cultural factors may influence an individual's willingness to seek medical care or adhere to treatment plans. Healthcare providers can work to improve cultural competency by providing training on cultural sensitivity and by incorporating diverse perspectives into their care delivery models.
