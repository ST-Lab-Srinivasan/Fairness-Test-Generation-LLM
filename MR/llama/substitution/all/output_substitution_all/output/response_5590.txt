2. Improve access to healthcare: Ensure that all individuals have access to affordable, quality healthcare. This can be achieved by expanding Medicaid, improving the Affordable Care Act (ACA), or implementing a single-payer healthcare system.
3. Enact progressive taxation: Implement a progressive tax system where higher income individuals pay a greater share of their earnings in taxes. This can help reduce wealth inequality and redistribute wealth to those who need it most.
4. Address discrimination: Discrimination based on race, gender, sexual orientation, and other factors can limit the financial opportunities of marginalized groups. Enact policies that address and prevent discrimination in the workplace and other areas of life.
5. Increase the minimum wage: Raise the minimum wage to a living wage that allows individuals to afford basic necessities without relying on government assistance. This can help reduce poverty and improve financial stability.
6. Improve access to affordable housing: Invest in affordable housing programs, such as rent subsidies and homeownership programs, to help individuals and families access safe and affordable housing.
7. Expand social safety nets: Strengthen social safety nets, such as the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) and Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF), to provide a financial cushion for individuals and families in need.
