Supporting deep in poverty physically disabled communities requires a comprehensive and inclusive approach that addresses the various barriers they face. Here are some effective strategies:

1. Community-based rehabilitation: This approach focuses on empowering individuals with disabilities within their own communities, rather than providing services outside of these communities. It involves training community members to provide rehabilitation services, such as physical therapy and assistive technology provision.
2. Inclusive education: Providing accessible and inclusive educational environments can help individuals with disabilities access knowledge and skills that can improve their socio-economic status. This includes providing accommodations, modifications, and support services to ensure equal access to education.
3. Vocational training: Vocational training programs can provide individuals with disabilities with the skills and knowledge necessary to participate in the workforce. This can include training in areas such as entrepreneurship, agriculture, and craftsmanship.
4. Health and rehabilitation services: Access to health and rehabilitation services is critical for individuals with disabilities, particularly those living in poverty. This includes access to medical care, physical therapy, and occupational therapy.
5. Social and economic empowerment: Empowering individuals with disabilities through social and economic opportunities can help them overcome poverty. This includes providing access to microfinance programs, job training, and entrepreneurship support.
6. Advocacy and awareness-raising: Raising awareness about the needs and rights of individuals with disabilities can help to address social stigmas and discrimination. This includes advocating for policy changes and legal protections that promote inclusivity and accessibility.
7. Inclusive infrastructure: Ensuring that public spaces and buildings are accessible and inclusive can help individuals with disabilities participate fully in society. This includes providing accessible transportation, parks, and other community facilities.
8. Livelihoods support: Providing support for individuals with disabilities to establish and maintain their own livelihoods can help them overcome poverty. This includes access to resources such as start-up capital, mentorship, and business training.
