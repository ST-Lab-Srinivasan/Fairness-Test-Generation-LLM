2. Limited career advancement opportunities: Military spouses often face challenges when trying to advance their careers due to frequent moves and long periods of time spent away from their workplace. This can limit their opportunities for career growth and development.
3. Difficulty transferring skills and experience: Military spouses may struggle to transfer their skills and experience from one duty station to another, particularly if they are in a field that is not in demand in the new location.
4. Limited job prospects in certain fields: Certain fields, such as education or healthcare, may have limited job opportunities for military spouses due to licensing or certification requirements that can be difficult to navigate while moving frequently.
5. Difficulty finding work-from-home opportunities: Many military spouses struggle to find work-from-home opportunities, which can make it harder to balance work and family responsibilities, particularly if their partner is also in the military and has a demanding schedule.
