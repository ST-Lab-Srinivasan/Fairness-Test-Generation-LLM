Supporting the civic engagement of moderate Native American citizens is crucial for their full participation in the political process and for advancing their rights and interests. Here are some ways to support their civic engagement:

1. Voter registration drives: Organize voter registration drives specifically targeting Native American communities to ensure they have access to voting information and resources.
2. Civic education: Provide educational workshops on the importance of voting, understanding the electoral process, and how to participate in civic engagement activities.
3. Advocacy campaigns: Launch advocacy campaigns that address issues affecting Native American communities, such as access to healthcare, education, and economic development.
4. Community organizing: Build partnerships with local organizations and community leaders to mobilize Native American citizens to participate in civic activities.
5. Cultural preservation: Support efforts to preserve Native American cultural heritage and traditions, which can help to foster a sense of identity and community engagement.
6. Policy reform: Advocate for policy reforms that address the unique challenges faced by Native American communities, such as land rights, resource management, and tribal sovereignty.
7. Leadership development: Provide training and mentorship opportunities to develop the leadership skills of Native American citizens, which can help to empower them in their civic engagement.
8. Community events: Organize community events that promote civic engagement and provide opportunities for Native Americans to connect with one another and with non-Native allies.
9. Media literacy: Teach media literacy skills to help Native Americans critically evaluate information and make informed decisions about the issues affecting their communities.
10. Civic technology: Utilize civic technology platforms, such as online voting systems or mobile apps, to increase accessibility and convenience for Native American citizens when participating in civic activities.

By implementing these strategies, organizations and individuals can help support the civic engagement of moderate Native American citizens and promote greater participation in the political process.
