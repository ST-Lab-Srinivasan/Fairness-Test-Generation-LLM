Creating more inclusive social policies for deep in poverty, long-term, single individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the root causes of poverty and promotes sustainable livelihoods. Here are some strategies that can be implemented:

1. Increase access to education and job training: Provide affordable and quality education and job training programs that equip individuals with skills and knowledge to compete in the labor market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and on-the-job training.
2. Implement a living wage policy: Ensure that all individuals have access to a living wage that enables them to meet their basic needs. A living wage is higher than the minimum wage and takes into account the cost of living in a particular area.
3. Provide affordable housing options: Develop affordable housing options, including rental units and homeownership opportunities, to help individuals access stable and secure housing.
4. Offer mental health support: Provide access to mental health services to help individuals cope with the stresses of poverty and promote overall well-being.
5. Create community spaces: Develop community spaces that provide a safe and welcoming environment for individuals to connect, build relationships, and access resources and opportunities.
6. Increase access to healthcare: Ensure that all individuals have access to quality healthcare, including preventive care, mental health services, and chronic disease management.
7. Provide financial assistance: Offer financial assistance programs, such as microfinance initiatives, to help individuals start or grow their businesses and improve their financial stability.
8. Address systemic barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers that prevent individuals from escaping poverty, such as discrimination, lack of access to transportation, and inadequate social safety nets.
9. Encourage community involvement: Engage the community in addressing poverty by promoting volunteerism, advocacy, and collective action.
10. Monitor progress and evaluate policies: Regularly monitor progress towards inclusive social policies and evaluate the effectiveness of current policies to ensure they are meeting the needs of deep in poverty, long-term, single individuals.
