Creating more inclusive community events for beginner English speakers from financially disadvantaged backgrounds requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to help make your events more accessible and inclusive:

1. Language support: Provide interpretation or translation services, such as simultaneous interpretation or live captioning, to help non-native English speakers follow the event. This can be especially helpful for those who may not be fluent in English.
2. Financial assistance: Offer financial aid or scholarships to cover the cost of attendance for individuals from financially disadvantaged backgrounds. This could include waived registration fees, travel grants, or accommodation assistance.
3. Accessibility: Ensure that the event venue is accessible to people with disabilities by providing wheelchair ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms. Offer assistive listening devices or sign language interpretation for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing.
4. Childcare: Provide childcare services or allocate a quiet area for parents to care for their children during the event. This can help alleviate some of the financial burden and allow participants to focus on the event.
5. Food and refreshments: Offer free or low-cost food and refreshments, such as snacks and water, to help reduce the financial burden on attendees. You could also consider offering vegetarian or vegan options to cater to dietary restrictions.
6. Promotion: Utilize affordable marketing strategies, such as social media advertising, flyers in local community centers, and word of mouth, to reach a wider audience. Consider partnering with local organizations that serve financially disadvantaged populations to help promote the event.
7. Volunteer support: Recruit volunteers from the local community to assist with event logistics, such as registration, greeting attendees, and manning information tables. This can help reduce costs associated with hiring staff or contractors.
