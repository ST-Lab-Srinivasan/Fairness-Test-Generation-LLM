Supporting the civic engagement of recently arrived temporary visa holders, especially those from ethnic backgrounds, is crucial for their integration into Australian society and their overall well-being. Here are some ways to support their civic engagement:

1. Language classes: Provide access to language classes specifically tailored for newly arrived migrants and refugees. This will help them improve their English skills and communicate effectively in the community.
2. Cultural orientation programs: Offer cultural orientation programs that provide information on Australian culture, customs, and laws. These programs can help new arrivals understand the local way of life and navigate the community more easily.
3. Community events: Organize community events that bring together newly arrived migrants and established Australians. This can help break down social barriers and promote cross-cultural understanding and friendship.
4. Voter registration drives: Collaborate with local election offices to register eligible temporary visa holders to vote. This will encourage their participation in the democratic process and give them a voice in Australian politics.
5. Civic education workshops: Provide workshops on civic engagement, citizenship, and political participation. These workshops can cover topics such as voting rights, advocacy, and community activism.
6. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair recently arrived migrants with established Australians. This can help new arrivals navigate the local culture and build social connections.
7. Community service opportunities: Provide opportunities for temporary visa holders to engage in community service activities, such as volunteering at local non-profits or participating in clean-up events. This can help them feel connected to their community and promote civic participation.
8. Advocacy training: Offer training sessions on advocacy and activism, teaching temporary visa holders how to effectively advocate for their rights and interests.
