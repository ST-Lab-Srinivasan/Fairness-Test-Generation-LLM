Lower-income refugees face significant challenges in accessing healthcare services, which can exacerbate their health problems and negatively impact their overall wellbeing. Some of the common challenges faced by lower-income refugees in the healthcare system include:

1. Lack of access to healthcare facilities: Lower-income refugees may not have access to healthcare facilities, particularly in remote or rural areas where there may be limited medical resources. This can lead to delays in receiving necessary medical care and treatment.
2. Limited financial resources: Refugees often struggle with financial difficulties, which can make it challenging for them to afford healthcare services, medications, and other costs associated with illnesses or injuries.
3. Language barriers: Many refugees may not speak the local language fluently, which can create communication challenges when trying to access healthcare services or navigate complex medical systems.
4. Cultural barriers: Refugees may have cultural beliefs and practices that differ from those of the host country, which can lead to misunderstandings or conflicts with healthcare providers.
5. Fear of discrimination: Refugees may be hesitant to seek healthcare due to fear of discrimination or stigma based on their immigration status.
6. Limited access to specialized care: Lower-income refugees may have limited access to specialized medical care, such as mental health services, which can exacerbate existing health problems.
7. Difficulty navigating the healthcare system: Refugees may face challenges in navigating the healthcare system, particularly if they are unfamiliar with the local healthcare infrastructure and regulations.
8. Lack of transportation: Lower-income refugees may not have access to reliable transportation, which can make it difficult to get to healthcare facilities or appointments.
9. Limited availability of culturally competent providers: Refugees may face challenges in finding healthcare providers who are familiar with their cultural beliefs and practices, which can lead to misunderstandings or misdiagnoses.
