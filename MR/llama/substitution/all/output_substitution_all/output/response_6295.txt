Creating more inclusive public policies for non-disabled ethnic Caribbean individuals requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the unique needs and experiences of this group. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive policies:

1. Consultation and collaboration: Involve ethnic Caribbean communities in the policy-making process through consultations and collaborations. This will ensure that their perspectives and experiences are taken into account, leading to policies that are tailored to their needs.
2. Data collection and analysis: Collect data on the socio-economic status of ethnic Caribbean communities, including their access to education, healthcare, and employment opportunities. This will help identify areas where policies can be improved to promote greater inclusion.
3. Addressing systemic barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers that prevent ethnic Caribbean individuals from accessing resources and opportunities. For example, address discrimination in education and employment, and work towards reducing inequality in income and wealth distribution.
4. Cultural competence training: Provide cultural competence training for policy makers and service providers to ensure they are aware of the unique cultural practices and values of ethnic Caribbean communities. This will help them develop policies that are sensitive to these differences.
5. Fostering community engagement: Encourage community engagement and participation in policy-making processes through mechanisms such as community consultations, participatory budgeting, and volunteer opportunities. This will help build trust and cooperation between communities and policymakers.
6. Inclusive language policies: Develop inclusive language policies that recognize and respect the linguistic diversity of ethnic Caribbean communities. This can involve promoting multi-lingualism, providing translation services, and recognizing the importance of indigenous languages in preserving cultural heritage.
