Promoting the economic empowerment of permanent resident native Canadian speaker communities is crucial for their overall well-being and prosperity. Here are some strategies that can help achieve this goal:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs that cater to the specific needs of these communities. This can include language and cultural training, as well as vocational training in areas such as entrepreneurship, small business management, and trades.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage and support entrepreneurial activities within these communities by providing access to funding, mentorship, and networking opportunities. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities for community members.
3. Community Development: Invest in community development initiatives that promote economic growth and prosperity within these communities. This can include infrastructure development, such as roads, bridges, and buildings, as well as social programs aimed at improving healthcare, education, and social services.
4. Access to Financial Services: Ensure that members of these communities have access to financial services such as bank accounts, loans, and credit. This can help them manage their finances, start businesses, and invest in their future.
5. Job Creation: Work with local employers to create job opportunities within these communities. This can be done through partnerships, apprenticeships, and other initiatives that promote job creation and economic growth.
6. Collaboration with Other Organizations: Collaborate with other organizations, such as non-profits, government agencies, and private sector companies, to leverage resources and expertise in promoting economic empowerment within these communities.
7. Policy Reforms: Advocate for policy reforms that address the systemic barriers preventing economic empowerment within these communities. This can include policies that promote affordable housing, access to healthcare, and social services.
8. Capacity Building: Build the capacity of community members through training and education programs aimed at developing skills in areas such as business management, financial literacy, and entrepreneurship.
9. Access to Markets: Provide support for community members to access markets and customers, both locally and internationally. This can be done through trade missions, marketing and branding initiatives, and partnerships
