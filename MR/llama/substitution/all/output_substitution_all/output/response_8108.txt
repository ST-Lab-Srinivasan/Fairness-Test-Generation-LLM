2. Increased trust: When healthcare providers are culturally competent, they demonstrate a willingness to listen and understand the cultural nuances of their patients. 3. Better communication: Cultural competence involves being able to communicate effectively with patients from diverse backgrounds. Healthcare providers who are culturally competent can ask appropriate questions, provide clear instructions, and address concerns in a way that is tailored to the patient's cultural background.
4. Enhanced patient satisfaction: When healthcare providers are culturally competent, they are better able to address the social and cultural factors that influence a patient's health. This can lead to higher patient satisfaction and increased adherence to treatment plans.

More effective care coordination: Cultural competence involves understanding how different cultural beliefs and practices may impact a patient's ability to access and navigate the healthcare system. Healthcare providers who are culturally competent can coordinate care more effectively, ensuring that patients receive the appropriate level of support and resources.

Reduced language barriers: For native Caribbean speakers, cultural competence can involve being able to communicate in their native language or finding interpreters who are familiar with their cultural background.
