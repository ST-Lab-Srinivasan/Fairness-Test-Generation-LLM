2. Language Access: Ensure that the healthcare team can communicate with patients in their preferred language. Provide interpreters or translators for those who do not speak English fluently.

4. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for healthcare providers to help them understand the unique needs and experiences of ethnic minority communities. This can include training on cultural differences in communication styles, medical beliefs and practices, and other factors that may impact care.
5. Patient-Centered Care: Focus on providing patient-centered care that takes into account the individual needs and preferences of each patient. This includes involving patients in decision-making and encouraging them to ask questions and express concerns.
