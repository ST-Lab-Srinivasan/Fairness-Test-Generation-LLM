Supporting the economic empowerment of unemployed Native natural-born US citizen individuals is crucial for their well-being and to address the systemic inequalities they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Job training and placement programs: Provide job training and placement programs specifically designed for Native Americans, focusing on in-demand skills and industries such as renewable energy, healthcare, and technology. This will help individuals gain the necessary skills to compete in the job market and secure employment opportunities.
2. Microfinance initiatives: Offer microfinance programs that provide small loans or grants to support Native American entrepreneurs in starting or growing their businesses. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities within Native American communities.
3. Mentorship and networking opportunities: Establish mentorship programs that connect unemployed Native Americans with successful entrepreneurs, executives, and professionals in their field of interest. This can provide valuable guidance and connections to help individuals advance their careers.
4. Tax incentives for hiring Native Americans: Offer tax incentives or deductions to businesses that hire Native American employees, particularly those who are unemployed or underemployed. This can encourage businesses to prioritize hiring from underrepresented groups and create more job opportunities.
5. Support for tribal-owned businesses: Provide financial support, training, and resources to help Native American tribes establish and grow their own businesses. This can help increase economic activity within Native American communities and create jobs that are owned and operated by the community.
6. Addressing systemic barriers: Identify and address any systemic barriers that may prevent unemployed Native Americans from finding and keeping employment, such as discrimination, lack of access to transportation or childcare, or limited job opportunities in their area.
7.
