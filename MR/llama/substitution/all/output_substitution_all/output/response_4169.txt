There are several ways to ensure that the voices of beginner Arabic speakers are heard in decision-making processes:

1. Provide language support: Offer language classes or resources to help beginners improve their Arabic skills, which can enhance their ability to participate in decision-making processes.
2. Create inclusive spaces: Design spaces where everyone feels comfortable and included, regardless of their proficiency in Arabic. This can be achieved by providing interpreters or translators during meetings, events, or discussions.
3. Foster a culture of inclusion: Encourage decision-makers to actively seek out and listen to the perspectives of beginner Arabic speakers. This can involve actively seeking out diverse voices and creating opportunities for them to share their thoughts and ideas.
4. Use accessible communication channels: Utilize multiple communication channels, such as social media, online forums, or community meetings, to ensure that all voices are heard. This can help beginners who may struggle with face-to-face interactions.
5. Foster mentorship relationships: Connect beginner Arabic speakers with more experienced individuals who can provide guidance and support in navigating decision-making processes. This can help build confidence and improve their ability to contribute meaningfully.
6. Address power imbalances: Identify and address any power imbalances that may exist within communities or organizations, which can hinder the voices of beginner Arabic speakers from being heard. This can involve challenging dominant narratives and creating more equitable decision-making processes.
7. Create opportunities for feedback: Provide regular opportunities for beginner Arabic speakers to provide feedback on decision-making processes, ensuring that their voices are heard and valued throughout the process.
8. Celebrate diversity: Embrace and celebrate the diversity of language backgrounds within communities or organizations, recognizing the value of each voice and perspective.
9. Provide resources and support: Offer resources and support to beginner Arabic speakers to help them navigate decision-making processes, such as providing access to information, networking opportunities, or mentorship programs.
10. Lead by example: Encourage decision-makers to lead by example by actively seeking out and valuing the perspectives of beginner Arabic speakers in their decision-making processes
