Supporting the economic empowerment of lower middle-income mixed Nigerians requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various socio-economic challenges. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Financial Inclusion: Expand access to affordable and inclusive financial services, such as microfinance, savings accounts, and insurance products. This can be achieved through partnerships with banks, microfinance institutions, and mobile network operators.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Provide training, mentorship, and capital support to aspiring entrepreneurs, particularly those from marginalized communities. This can help create jobs and stimulate economic growth.
3. Skills Development: Offer vocational training and skill-building programs in areas such as digital literacy, business management, and leadership. This can help individuals acquire the skills needed to compete in the job market and start their own businesses.
4. Access to Markets: Facilitate connections between lower middle-income mixed Nigerians and formal markets, enabling them to access new customers, suppliers, and investors. This can be achieved through trade fairs, expos, and other networking events.
5. Community Development: Invest in community development projects that create jobs, improve infrastructure, and enhance the overall quality of life. This can help stimulate economic growth and reduce poverty.
6. Advocacy and Policy Reform: Advocate for policy reforms that promote economic empowerment, such as tax incentives, reduced bureaucratic barriers, and improved access to markets.
7. Social Protection: Implement social protection programs, such as cash transfers, food subsidies, and health insurance, to help reduce poverty and vulnerability.
8. Education and Healthcare: Invest in education and healthcare infrastructure to improve the human capital of lower middle-income mixed Nigerians, enabling them to compete more effectively in the job market and start their own businesses.
9. ICT Infrastructure: Invest in information and communication technology (ICT) infrastructure, including internet connectivity and digital literacy training, to help individuals access new economic opportunities and improve their productivity.
