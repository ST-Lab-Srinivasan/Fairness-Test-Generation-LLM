There are many successful community initiatives led by beginner French speakers, both above and below the poverty line. Here are some examples:

1. Language exchange programs: Many communities have established language exchange programs where beginners can practice their French skills with more proficient speakers. These programs often involve regular meetings between language learners and native French speakers, fostering cross-cultural understanding and language improvement.
2. Cultural events: Beginner French speakers may organize cultural events such as film screenings, concerts, or food festivals to promote French culture and language. These events provide opportunities for community members to practice their French skills while enjoying a shared cultural experience.
3. Tutoring programs: Some beginner French speakers may establish tutoring programs to help other learners improve their French skills. These programs can be organized in-person or online, providing one-on-one instruction and language practice opportunities for learners of all levels.
4. Language classes: Beginner French speakers may also organize language classes specifically designed for beginners. These classes provide a structured environment for learners to improve their French skills, with instruction on grammar, vocabulary, and pronunciation.

Cultural immersion programs: Some beginner French speakers may organize cultural immersion programs, where participants can immerse themselves in French culture by living with a host family or participating in language and cultural activities. 6. Volunteer work: Beginner French speakers may also organize volunteer work opportunities where they can use their French skills to make a positive impact in their community. Examples include volunteering at local charities, hospitals, or community centers where they can interact with French-speaking individuals and practice their language skills.
