Supporting the economic empowerment of native-born US citizens, particularly those from upper middle-income backgrounds, is crucial for promoting equity and inclusive growth in the United States. Here are some ways to support their economic empowerment:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs that help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge to enhance their career prospects. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and degree programs in high-demand fields such as technology, healthcare, and finance.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing access to resources, mentorship, and funding opportunities. This can help individuals start their own businesses, create jobs, and contribute to economic growth.
3. Job Creation and Infrastructure Development: Invest in job creation and infrastructure development projects that provide good-paying jobs and improve the overall quality of life in communities. This can include investments in transportation, housing, and community facilities.
4. Wealth-Building Opportunities: Provide opportunities for individuals to build wealth through homeownership, retirement savings plans, and other financial instruments. This can help reduce wealth disparities and promote intergenerational economic mobility.
5. Tax Incentives and Investment Programs: Implement tax incentives and investment programs that encourage individuals and businesses to invest in low-income communities. This can include tax credits for small businesses, affordable housing initiatives, and investments in community facilities.
6. Addressing Systemic Barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers that prevent Native-born US citizens from accessing economic opportunities, such as discrimination, lack of access to credit, and limited access to education and training.
7. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): Encourage the growth and development of Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFIs), which provide financial services and investments to low-income communities.
8. Cooperative Business Models: Promote cooperative business models that allow individuals to pool their resources and collectively own and control assets, such as housing, land, and other community assets.
