Promoting the accessibility of public services for Mexican active-duty individuals requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various challenges and barriers. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Awareness campaigns: Launch awareness campaigns to educate active-duty personnel and their families about the available resources and services. This can be done through various channels, such as social media, posters, flyers, and public events.
2. Accessibility infrastructure: Improve the accessibility of public facilities and infrastructure by installing ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms. Provide audio and visual aids for people with disabilities.
3. Transportation: Ensure that public transportation is accessible and affordable for active-duty personnel. This can be achieved by providing discounted or free transportation passes, or by implementing a carpooling program.
4. Online services: Make public services available online to make it easier for active-duty personnel to access them from any location. This can include online portals for bill payment, permit applications, and other services.
5. Mobile units: Establish mobile units that can travel to different military bases and provide on-site access to public services. This can help reduce the burden of traveling to a central location.
6. Flexible scheduling: Offer flexible scheduling options for active-duty personnel, such as evening or weekend appointments, to accommodate their busy schedules.
7. Military liaisons: Appoint military liaisons who can act as a single point of contact for active-duty personnel to access public services. These liaisons can help streamline the process and provide personalized support.
8. Community partnerships: Collaborate with local businesses and organizations to provide additional resources and support for active-duty personnel and their families. This can include discounts, free services, or other forms of support.
