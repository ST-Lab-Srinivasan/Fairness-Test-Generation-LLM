Supporting the social inclusion of native European speaker ex-spouses can be crucial in helping them reintegrate into society after a divorce or separation. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Language Support: Many native European speakers may struggle with language barriers after moving to a new country. Providing language classes or connecting them with language exchange programs can help them improve their language skills and communicate more effectively in their new environment.
2. Social Networking: Encourage the ex-spouses to join social clubs, groups, or organizations that align with their interests. This can provide opportunities for socializing, making new friends, and building a support network.
3. Career Support: Offer career guidance and job placement services to help them find employment in their field of expertise. This can boost their self-esteem and financial stability, enabling them to rebuild their lives.
4. Childcare and Education: If they have children, provide support with childcare and education. This can include access to schools, universities, or extracurricular activities that cater to their needs.
5. Financial Support: Help them navigate the financial aspects of their divorce by providing information on budgeting, debt management, and financial planning. Offer financial assistance programs or connect them with professionals who can help them manage their finances effectively.
6. Legal Aid: Provide legal aid services to help them understand their rights and obligations during the divorce process. This can include advice on property division, spousal support, and child custody arrangements.
7. Mental Health Support: Offer mental health counseling or therapy sessions to help them cope with the emotional challenges of divorce. This can include anxiety, depression, and stress management techniques.
8. Home Support: If they are struggling to adapt to a new living environment, provide support with finding housing, furniture, and other basic necessities. This can help them feel more secure and comfortable in their new home.
9. Community Outreach: Encourage them to participate in community activities, such as volunteering, joining local clubs or organizations, or attending cultural events. This can help them connect with their new community and build meaningful relationships.
