To support the economic empowerment of recently arrived non-citizens in the US, particularly those on temporary visas or recent green card holders, here are some potential strategies:

1. Access to Financial Services: Provide access to affordable and inclusive financial services, such as bank accounts, loans, and insurance products. This can help them manage their finances, build credit, and achieve financial stability.
2. Job Training and Placement: Offer job training programs that cater to the needs of non-citizens, including language training, vocational skills development, and industry-specific knowledge. Connect them with potential employers through job fairs, networking events, and online platforms.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurial spirit by providing resources and support for starting businesses. This can include access to mentorship, seed capital, and networking opportunities.
4. Education and Skills Development: Offer educational programs that help non-citizens acquire new skills or enhance existing ones, such as English language training, vocational training, and degree programs.
5. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to affordable and quality healthcare services, including mental health support, to help them manage their physical and mental well-being.
6. Housing Support: Provide temporary or permanent housing solutions for non-citizens who may be facing housing insecurity or homelessness. This can include rental assistance, mortgage assistance, and emergency shelter services.
7. Legal Support: Offer legal services and resources to help non-citizens navigate the complex US immigration system, including advice on obtaining a green card or citizenship, and representation in court proceedings.
8. Community Building: Facilitate community building activities that bring together non-citizens from diverse backgrounds, helping them connect with one another and build social capital. This can include cultural events, volunteer opportunities, and social gatherings.
9. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that benefit non-citizens, such as reforming the immigration system to provide more pathways to citizenship or addressing discrimination in employment and housing.
