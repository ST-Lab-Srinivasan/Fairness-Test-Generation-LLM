Ensuring the healthcare needs of natural-born US citizen lower middle-income people is crucial for promoting equity and improving their overall well-being. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Expand Medicaid eligibility: One of the most significant barriers to accessing healthcare for lower middle-income individuals is the lack of affordable insurance options. Expanding Medicaid eligibility and making it more accessible can help bridge this gap.
2. Implement a public option: A publicly-funded health insurance option can provide an alternative to private insurance plans, offering lower premiums and more comprehensive coverage for lower middle-income individuals.
3. Increase funding for community health centers: Community health centers are critical in providing primary care services to underserved populations. Increasing funding for these centers can help improve access to care for lower middle-income individuals.
4. Support nurse practitioner and physician assistant programs: Training more nurse practitioners and physician assistants can help increase the number of healthcare providers serving in underserved areas, improving access to care for lower middle-income individuals.
5. Implement a refundable tax credit: A refundable tax credit can help lower middle-income individuals cover out-of-pocket expenses, such as copays and deductibles, making healthcare more affordable.
6. Increase funding for mental health services: Mental health is an essential aspect of overall health, and increasing funding for mental health services can help improve access to care for lower middle-income individuals.
7. Promote telehealth services: Telehealth services can help expand access to healthcare for lower middle-income individuals living in rural or underserved areas, where access to healthcare providers may be limited.
8. Improve data collection and analysis: Better data collection and analysis can help identify healthcare disparities and develop targeted interventions to address them.
9. Increase funding for preventive services: Preventive services, such as screenings and vaccinations, can help lower middle-income individuals avoid costly medical expenses down the line.
