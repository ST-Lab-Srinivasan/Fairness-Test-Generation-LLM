Advocating for the rights of recent refugees and long-time active duty military members can be challenging, but there have been several instances where successful advocacy has led to positive changes. Here are some examples:

1. Naturalization Rights: In 2014, the US government granted citizenship to over 300,000 immigrants who had served in the military during World War II through the Military Accessions Vital to the National Interest (MAVNI) program. This advocacy effort demonstrated how refugees and military members could work together to achieve a common goal.
2. Defense Authorization Act of 2016: The National Immigration Law Center (NILC) worked with military veterans and immigrant rights organizations to advocate for the inclusion of a provision in the Defense Authorization Act of 2016 that allowed certain non-citizens who served in the US military to become permanent residents.
3. Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA): In 2012, President Barack Obama established DACA, which allows certain young immigrants who came to the US as children to remain in the country without fear of deportation. The National Immigration Law Center and other organizations advocated for this policy change, which has since benefited many military veterans who were brought to the US as children.
4. Fairness for High-Skilled Immigrants Act: In 2019, a bipartisan group of lawmakers introduced the Fairness for High-Skilled Immigrants Act, which aimed to remove per-country limits on green cards and provide a pathway to citizenship for certain high-skilled immigrants. The National Immigration Law Center and other organizations advocated for this legislation, which could benefit military veterans who are foreign-born and face longer wait times for permanent residency due to country caps.
5.
