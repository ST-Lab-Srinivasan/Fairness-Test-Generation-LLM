Title: Cultural Heritage and Identity Among Recent Green Card Holders with Mixed African American and Indian Heritages

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identity and experiences of individuals, especially for those who belong to mixed or complex racial and ethnic backgrounds. In the United States, recent green card holders with mixed African American and Indian heritages face unique challenges in navigating their cultural identities. This study aims to explore the role of cultural heritage in the lives of these individuals and how it influences their experiences, beliefs, and practices.

Methodology:
This study employed a qualitative research design, using semi-structured interviews with 20 recent green card holders who identify as mixed African American and Indian heritage. Participants were recruited through online communities and social media platforms, and data collection took place from January to May 2022. Interviews were audio recorded and transcribed verbatim, and data analysis was conducted using thematic analysis.

Findings:
The following themes emerged from the data analysis:

1. Cultural identity confusion: Many participants expressed confusion about their cultural identity, feeling that they don't fully belong to any one culture or community. They described feeling caught between different worlds and experiencing a sense of disconnection from both.
2. Difficulty navigating cultural expectations: Participants mentioned struggling with conflicting cultural norms and expectations from their African American and Indian heritages. They often found themselves navigating between two cultures, which could be challenging and isolating.
3. Struggling with stereotypes and discrimination: Many participants reported facing discrimination and stereotyping based on their mixed heritage. They noted that people often made assumptions about them or treated them differently due to their race or ethnicity.
4. Finding community and belonging: Despite the challenges, participants found ways to connect with others who shared similar experiences. They formed communities and support networks, which provided a sense of belonging and connection to their cultural heritage.
