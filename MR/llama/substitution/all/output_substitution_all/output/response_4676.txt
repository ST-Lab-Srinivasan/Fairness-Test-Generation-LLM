As a recent refugee to the United States on a professional temporary visa, navigating the healthcare system can be particularly challenging. Here are some of the common challenges faced by low-income professional temporary visa holders:

1. Language Barriers: Many refugees and immigrants face language barriers, which can make it difficult to communicate with healthcare providers and understand medical instructions.
2. Lack of Cultural Competency: Healthcare providers may not be familiar with the cultural beliefs and practices of refugees, leading to misunderstandings or miscommunications.
3. Limited Access to Care: Refugees may face barriers in accessing healthcare due to their immigration status, lack of insurance coverage, or limited financial resources.
4. Difficulty Navigating the Healthcare System: The US healthcare system can be complex and confusing for anyone, let alone refugees who may not be familiar with the system or have had negative experiences in the past.
5. Mental Health Challenges: Refugees often experience trauma and stress related to their experiences in their home countries or during their migration journey. These mental health challenges can make it difficult for them to access and navigate the healthcare system.
6. Lack of Trust: Refugees may have had negative experiences with healthcare providers in the past, leading to a lack of trust in the system.
7. Stigma: Refugees may face stigma and discrimination from healthcare providers or other members of society, which can make it difficult for them to access care.
8. Limited Knowledge of Available Resources: Refugees may not be aware of the resources available to them, such as community clinics or sliding-scale fee clinics.
9. Difficulty Finding a Provider Who Speaks Their Language: Many refugees may speak languages other than English, which can make it difficult to find a healthcare provider who speaks their language.
10. Limited Access to Specialized Care: Refugees may face barriers in accessing specialized care, such as mental health services or cancer treatment, due to limited availability or affordability.
