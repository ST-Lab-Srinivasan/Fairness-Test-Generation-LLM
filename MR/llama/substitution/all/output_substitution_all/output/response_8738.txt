Creating more inclusive public policies for deaf, ethnically full English individuals requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the unique needs and challenges faced by this community. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to create more inclusive public policies:

1. Consultation and Engagement: Deaf individuals must be consulted and engaged in the policy-making process through various means, such as focus groups, surveys, or direct engagement with policymakers. This will ensure that their voices are heard and their needs are considered.
2. Accessibility: All public policies should be made accessible to deaf individuals by providing sign language interpreters, braille documents, or other forms of communication that can be understood by this community.
3. Language Accommodation: Policies should accommodate the diverse languages and communication styles used by deaf individuals, including sign language, finger spelling, and cued speech.
4. Education and Awareness: Educating policymakers and the general public about the experiences and needs of deaf individuals can help promote more inclusive policies. Awareness campaigns can be organized to raise understanding and empathy towards this community.
5. Employment Opportunities: Deaf individuals face significant barriers in the job market due to discrimination and lack of accommodations. Policies should aim to provide equal employment opportunities by promoting accessibility in hiring practices, providing sign language interpreters during interviews, and ensuring reasonable accommodations are made for deaf employees.
6. Healthcare Access: Deaf individuals may face challenges when accessing healthcare services due to language barriers. Policies should prioritize the provision of accessible healthcare services, including sign language interpreters and written communication in braille or large print.
7. Social Protection: Deaf individuals are at a higher risk of poverty and social isolation due to discrimination and lack of access to resources.
