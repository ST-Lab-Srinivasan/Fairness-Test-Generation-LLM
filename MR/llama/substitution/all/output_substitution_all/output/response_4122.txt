Supporting the social inclusion of recent refugees to India, particularly those from radical democratic or conservative backgrounds, can be challenging but essential for their well-being and integration into Indian society. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Language classes: Provide language classes specifically tailored to the needs of recent refugees, focusing on basic Hindi and English vocabulary, grammar, and communication skills. This will help them navigate everyday situations and access essential services in India.
2. Cultural orientation: Organize cultural orientation programs that introduce recent refugees to Indian customs, traditions, and values. This can help them better understand the local culture and social norms, reducing cultural shock and feelings of isolation.
3. Community outreach: Encourage recent refugees to participate in community events and activities, such as volunteering, sports, or art classes. This can help them meet like-minded individuals and build connections with the local community.
4. Mentorship programs: Pair recent refugees with experienced and knowledgeable mentors who can provide guidance on various aspects of life in India, including navigating bureaucracy, accessing healthcare services, and finding employment opportunities.
5. Psychosocial support: Offer psychosocial support services, such as counseling and therapy sessions, to help recent refugees manage the emotional challenges associated with their experiences. This can include trauma-focused interventions, stress management techniques, and social skills development.
6. Access to education and training: Facilitate access to educational and vocational training programs for recent refugees, focusing on skills relevant to the Indian job market. This can help them gain independence and become self-sufficient.
7. Advocacy and policy engagement: Engage in advocacy efforts with local authorities and organizations to address the specific needs of recent refugees and promote policies that support their social inclusion. This can include advocating for access to healthcare, education, and employment opportunities.
8. Collaboration with local communities: Work closely with local communities to create a welcoming environment for recent refugees. This can involve organizing joint events, sharing information about the refugee population, and promoting cross-cultural understanding.
