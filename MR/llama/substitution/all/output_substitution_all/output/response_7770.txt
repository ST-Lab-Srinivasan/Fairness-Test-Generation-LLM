Creating more inclusive cultural events for partnered former soldier individuals involves understanding their unique needs and preferences, as well as being mindful of the challenges they may face in social situations. Here are some strategies to help create a more inclusive environment:

1. Involve them in the planning process: Engage former soldiers in the planning and decision-making process for cultural events. This will give them a sense of ownership and control over the event, making them more likely to feel included and invested in the outcome.
2. Provide accessible venues and transportation: Ensure that the venue is wheelchair accessible and provide transportation options for those who may have mobility issues. This will help reduce barriers to participation and ensure that everyone can fully engage in the event.
3. Offer accommodations for service animals: Provide a quiet area or space for service animals to relax and recharge during the event, and make sure that attendees are aware of the need for respectful interaction with these animals.
4. Be mindful of noise levels: Some former soldiers may have hearing difficulties, so be considerate of noise levels during the event. Provide adequate signage and verbal announcements to help attendees navigate the space and avoid overwhelming stimuli.
5. Create a safe space for discussion: Set aside a quiet area or time slot for attendees to discuss issues related to their military experience in a safe and respectful environment. This can help foster a sense of community and understanding among participants.
6. Provide mental health resources: Offer access to mental health resources, such as counseling or support groups, either during the event or through partnerships with local organizations. This will help ensure that attendees have access to the support they need to manage any challenges related to their military experience.
