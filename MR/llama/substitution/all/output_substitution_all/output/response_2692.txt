2. Language barriers: For those who are not fluent in English or French, navigating the legal system can be especially challenging. They may struggle to understand legal terminology, read documents, or communicate with lawyers and judges.
3. Limited knowledge of Canadian laws and procedures: New immigrants may be unfamiliar with Canadian laws and legal procedures, making it difficult for them to navigate the system effectively.
4. Fear of deportation: Many undocumented immigrants may be in a state of constant fear due to the possibility of being deported at any time. This can lead to anxiety, depression, and other mental health issues.
5. Limited access to employment opportunities: Undocumented immigrants may face barriers when trying to find work due to their immigration status. They may be unable to access certain jobs or industries, limiting their ability to support themselves financially.
6. Discrimination and stigma: Lower middle-income undocumented immigrants may face discrimination and stigma due to their immigration status, which can further compound the challenges they face within the legal system.
