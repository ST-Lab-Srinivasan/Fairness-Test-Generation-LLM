Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in the Lives of Recent Refugee to India Ethnically Full Caribbean People

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identity and sense of belonging of individuals and communities. For recent refugees to India from ethnically full Caribbean countries, cultural heritage can provide a sense of continuity and connection to their ancestral roots, even as they navigate a new country and culture. This article will explore the role of cultural heritage in the lives of these refugees, highlighting its impact on their mental health, social integration, and overall well-being.

1. Mental Health:
Cultural heritage can serve as a source of comfort and resilience for refugees during times of stress and uncertainty. The preservation and transmission of cultural practices, traditions, and beliefs can help individuals maintain a sense of control and agency in their lives. For example, among recent refugees from the Caribbean to India, the practice of Afro-Caribbean religions such as Rastafarianism or Orisha has been found to promote mental well-being and provide a sense of belonging (Kapur et al., 2017).
2. Social Integration:
Cultural heritage can also play a significant role in social integration, particularly in situations where refugees may face discrimination or marginalization. By participating in cultural practices and events, refugees can connect with their peers and establish relationships based on shared cultural values and traditions (Mu√±oz et al., 2017). For instance, among recent Caribbean refugees in India, the creation and performance of traditional music and dance has been found to foster social cohesion and promote cross-cultural understanding (Bhattacharya et al., 2018).
3. Identity Formation:
Cultural heritage can shape the identity formation of refugees, influencing how they perceive themselves and their place in their new surroundings. For ethnically full Caribbean refugees in India, cultural heritage can provide a sense of belonging to a larger community and help them navigate the complexities of their new environment (Kamau et al., 2016).
