Creating more inclusive public spaces for non-conservative beginner English speakers requires a thoughtful and intentional approach to design, management, and community engagement. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Accessible Language: Use simple language and avoid idioms or jargon that may be difficult for non-native English speakers to understand. Provide language support through subtitles, translations, or interpreters during events or meetings.
2. Cultural Sensitivity: Be aware of cultural differences and customs when designing public spaces. For example, provide separate areas for prayer or meditation, or offer cultural sensitivity training for staff and volunteers.
3. Inclusive Design: Design public spaces that are accessible and comfortable for people with disabilities. Ensure that the space is easy to navigate, has adequate lighting, and provides seating options for individuals with different needs.
4. Flexible Spaces: Create flexible spaces that can accommodate a variety of activities and events. For example, provide multi-purpose rooms or outdoor spaces that can be adapted for different uses, such as yoga classes, book clubs, or community meetings.
5. Community Outreach: Engage with local communities to understand their needs and preferences. Conduct surveys or focus groups to gather feedback and incorporate it into the design of public spaces.
6. Diverse Programming: Offer a variety of programs and activities that cater to different interests and needs. For example, host events that promote cultural exchange, provide language classes, or offer activities for seniors or children.
7. Inclusive Events: Host events that are inclusive and welcoming to all members of the community. Ensure that events are accessible and comfortable for individuals with disabilities, and provide accommodations as needed.
8. Volunteer Programs: Develop volunteer programs that allow community members to get involved in the design and management of public spaces. This can help build a sense of ownership and belonging among community members.
9. Partnerships: Collaborate with local organizations and community groups to develop and manage public spaces. This can help leverage resources, expertise, and networks to create more inclusive public spaces.
