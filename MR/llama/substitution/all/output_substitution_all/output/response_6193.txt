1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage can provide a sense of belonging to a particular community or group, which is essential for mental and emotional well-being. For recent green card holders and temporary visa holders, who may be experiencing a new environment and culture, their cultural heritage can offer a sense of familiarity and comfort.

Cultural practices: Many individuals from diverse backgrounds observe cultural practices such as religious ceremonies, festivals, and traditional foods, which help them maintain their cultural identity while adapting to the US culture. These practices can also serve as a bridge between their homeland and their new environment, fostering a sense of connection and belonging.
3. Language preservation: For those who speak minority languages or dialects, their cultural heritage may be closely tied to their language. Preserving their native language can help them maintain their cultural identity and feel connected to their community, particularly if they are in the US for an extended period.
4. Cultural exchange: Green card holders and temporary visa holders can share their cultural practices and traditions with their American peers, fostering cross-cultural understanding and exchange. This mutual learning can enrich their experiences in the US and help them feel more connected to their new community.
5. Community building: Cultural heritage can serve as a foundation for building communities among recent immigrants. Sharing cultural practices and traditions can help establish common ground and foster a sense of belonging among individuals from diverse backgrounds.
6. Psychological well-being: Cultural heritage can provide psychological benefits, such as increased self-esteem and confidence, particularly during times of transition or uncertainty. Maintaining cultural practices and traditions can also help individuals cope with stress and anxiety associated with adapting to a new environment.
7. Identity formation: Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of recent immigrants. It can influence how they perceive themselves, their values, and their place within their community and society at large.
