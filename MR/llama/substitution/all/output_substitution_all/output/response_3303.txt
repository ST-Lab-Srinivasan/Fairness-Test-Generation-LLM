1. Language and Cultural Training: Many Native recent temporary visa holders may not speak English fluently, which can create barriers to accessing services and resources. Providing language and cultural training can help them navigate the healthcare, education, and employment systems more effectively.
2. Community Outreach and Engagement: Building trust and rapport with community members is crucial for promoting social inclusion. Organizations can engage in community outreach and engagement activities, such as hosting cultural events, providing information sessions, and offering mentorship programs.
3. Education and Job Training: Native recent temporary visa holders may face significant barriers to employment due to lack of access to job training, education, and work experience. Providing access to job training programs, vocational schools, and apprenticeships can help them acquire new skills and gain confidence in their abilities.
4. Healthcare Access: Native recent temporary visa holders may face challenges accessing healthcare services due to language barriers, lack of knowledge about the healthcare system, or fear of discrimination. Providing access to culturally competent healthcare services, interpreter services, and mental health support can help address these issues.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocating for policy changes at the local and national levels can help address systemic barriers that prevent Native recent temporary visa holders from accessing resources and opportunities. This can include advocating for language access services, cultural competency training for service providers, and policies that promote inclusivity and diversity.
6. Collaboration with Local Organizations: Building partnerships with local organizations that serve low-income communities can help leverage resources, expertise, and networks to promote social inclusion.
