Supporting the civic engagement of long-term refugee, Native American, and recently undocumented immigrants in the US is crucial for their full integration into society. Here are some ways to encourage their participation in the political process:

1. Voter registration drives: Organize voter registration drives specifically targeting these communities. Partner with local organizations and community centers to reach a wider audience.
2. Civic education: Provide educational workshops on civics, voting rights, and the electoral process. This can help increase their understanding of how government works and their role in it.
3. Language accessibility: Offer language interpretation services during voter registration drives, poll stations, and other civic events to ensure equal access to information and participation.
4. Cultural sensitivity: Be aware of the cultural backgrounds and traditions of these communities, and be sensitive to their unique needs and experiences. This can involve adapting outreach strategies to be more inclusive and respectful.
5. Community organizing: Build relationships with local community leaders and organizations to help amplify their voices and mobilize them to participate in the political process.
6. Advocacy: Advocate for policies that support the full civic participation of these communities, such as non-citizen voting rights, language accessibility in elections, and cultural sensitivity in voter registration drives.
7. Legal services: Provide free or low-cost legal services to help these communities navigate the complex immigration system and understand their rights and options.
8. Digital literacy: Offer digital literacy training to help these communities access information and resources online, which can be critical for civic engagement in the digital age.
9. Civic engagement opportunities: Create opportunities for these communities to participate in civic events, such as town halls, rallies, and protests, where they can express their views and advocate for their rights.
