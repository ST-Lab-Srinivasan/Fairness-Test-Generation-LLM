Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Married Multiple Times Beginner English Speakers

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, particularly those who have been married multiple times and are beginner English speakers. This study aims to investigate how cultural heritage influences the identities of this population in terms of their values, beliefs, and practices.

Research Questions:

1. How do married multiple times beginner English speakers perceive their cultural heritage?
2. What role does cultural heritage play in shaping the identities of married multiple times beginner English speakers?
3. How does cultural heritage influence the values, beliefs, and practices of married multiple times beginner English speakers?

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research design using in-depth interviews with married multiple times beginner English speakers. Participants will be recruited through snowball sampling and purposive sampling techniques. Data will be analyzed thematically, examining the ways in which cultural heritage shapes the identities of the participants.

Significance:
This study will contribute to our understanding of how cultural heritage influences the identities of married multiple times beginner English speakers. Findings will provide insights into the role of cultural heritage in shaping values, beliefs, and practices, which can inform strategies for promoting cross-cultural understanding and respect.

Conclusion:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of married multiple times beginner English speakers. Understanding how cultural heritage influences their values, beliefs, and practices can help promote cross-cultural understanding and respect, ultimately leading to more inclusive and diverse communities.
