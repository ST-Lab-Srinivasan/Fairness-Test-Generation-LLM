As a mixed Nigerian heritage person learning Chinese, you may face several challenges in the legal system. Here are some of the common challenges:

3. Limited resources: As a beginner, you may not have access to the same resources as native Chinese speakers, such as language classes or tutors. This can make it more difficult to learn and retain information.
4. Lack of exposure: If you are not exposed to the Chinese language and culture from a young age, you may struggle to pick up the language and understand its intricacies.
5. Stereotypes and bias: Unfortunately, there is often a stigma attached to speaking a second language, particularly in legal settings. You may face stereotypes or bias from other legal professionals who assume that you are not proficient in Chinese.
6. Difficulty with tones: Chinese is a tonal language, which means that the same word can have different meanings based on the tone used. This can be challenging for beginners to master, particularly if they are not used to hearing and producing tones in their native language.
7. Limited access to legal resources: Depending on where you live, you may not have access to the same legal resources as native Chinese speakers. For example, you may not be able to find a lawyer who speaks your native language fluently.
8. Difficulty with legal terminology: Legal terminology can be complex and technical, and it can be challenging for beginners to understand and use correctly.
9. Limited understanding of legal concepts: Depending on your background and experience, you may not be familiar with certain legal concepts or terminology, which can make it difficult to navigate the legal system.
10. Difficulty with navigating the court system: The court system in China can be complex and bureaucratic, particularly for foreigners.
