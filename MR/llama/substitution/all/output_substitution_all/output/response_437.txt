Creating more inclusive community events for Native English speakers from lower-class backgrounds requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the event location is easily accessible by public transportation or carpooling options. Provide clear instructions on how to get there, including any necessary transportation assistance.
2. Affordability: Offer free or low-cost admission tickets to make the event more accessible to lower-income individuals. Consider partnering with local organizations to help promote the event and provide tickets at a discounted rate.
3. Inclusive marketing: Use inclusive language in your marketing materials, such as avoiding gender or racial stereotypes, and highlighting the diversity of the community. Promote the event through social media, flyers, and posters that reflect the local culture.
4. Diverse programming: Offer a diverse range of activities and workshops that cater to different interests and needs. This could include cultural performances, art exhibitions, language classes, and job training programs.
5. Safe space: Ensure that the event creates a safe and welcoming environment for all participants. Provide gender-neutral bathrooms and respectful signage, and have a clear policy on harassment and discrimination.
6. Community engagement: Encourage community members to get involved in the planning and organizing of the event. This can help build trust and ownership among local residents, and ensure that the event reflects their needs and interests.
7. Volunteer opportunities: Offer volunteer opportunities for community members to get involved in the event planning and execution. This can help build a sense of community and ownership among participants.
8. Food and refreshments: Provide food and refreshments that cater to a diverse range of dietary needs and preferences, including vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, and halal options.
9. Accessibility accommodations: Ensure that the event venue is accessible to people with disabilities. Provide accommodations such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, and audio descriptions.
10. Follow-up and evaluation: After the event, follow up with participants to gather feedback and evaluate the success of the event. Use this information to improve future events
