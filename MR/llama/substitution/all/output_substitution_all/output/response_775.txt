Introduction:

Disability can have a significant impact on the lives of individuals, including those with genetic conditions. In China, there is a growing awareness of genetic conditions and their impact on individuals and families. However, there are limited studies on the experiences and challenges faced by carrier individuals, particularly among beginner Chinese speakers. This study aims to explore the impacts of disability status on the lives of carrier individuals with genetic conditions in China.

Methodology:

This study used a qualitative research approach, including semi-structured interviews with 15 carrier individuals with genetic conditions in China. Participants were selected using purposive sampling, and data collection occurred from January to June 2022. Interviews were audio recorded and transcribed verbatim, and thematic analysis was conducted to identify patterns and trends in the data.

Findings:

1. Stigma and social isolation: Many participants reported experiencing stigma and social isolation due to their genetic condition. They felt embarrassed or ashamed about their condition and avoided social interactions to avoid being judged or discriminated against.
2. Emotional challenges: Carrier individuals experienced a range of emotional challenges, including anxiety, depression, and frustration. These emotions were often triggered by the uncertainty of their future health prospects, as well as the impact of their condition on their relationships and daily life.
3. Financial burden: Genetic conditions can be expensive to manage, particularly if they require ongoing medical treatment or specialized care. Participants reported struggling to afford these costs, which could have a significant impact on their financial stability and quality of life.
4. Lack of support: Many carrier individuals reported feeling isolated and lacking emotional support from family members, friends, or community resources. This lack of support could exacerbate the emotional challenges and stigma experienced by participants.
5. Identity formation: Genetic conditions can have a profound impact on an individual's sense of identity, particularly if they are perceived as "different" or "abnormal." Participants reported struggling to reconcile their genetic condition with their personal values, beliefs, and aspirations.
