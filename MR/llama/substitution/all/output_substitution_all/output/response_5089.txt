2. Increased trust: When healthcare providers demonstrate cultural competence, they build trust with their patients. Mixed Australian heritage individuals may feel more comfortable sharing personal and medical information when they perceive their provider as culturally aware and sensitive.

For example, providers who understand the cultural beliefs and practices surrounding mental health may be more effective in treating patients with mental health issues.

5. Reduced health disparities: Cultural competence can help reduce health disparities experienced by mixed Australian heritage individuals. For instance, providers who are aware of the unique health challenges faced by this population (e.g., higher rates of diabetes, heart disease) can develop tailored interventions to address these disparities.

When providers understand and respect the cultural practices and beliefs of this population, patients are more likely to participate in medical decision-making and follow treatment plans.
