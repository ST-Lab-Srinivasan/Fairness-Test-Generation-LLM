1. Community-based care: Provide healthcare services in the community, where people live and work. This can include mobile clinics, telemedicine services, or partnering with local organizations to provide care.
2. Patient navigation: Train patient navigators to assist individuals in accessing healthcare services, including scheduling appointments, interpreting medical information, and providing transportation.
3. Language access: Provide interpretation services, such as telephone interpretation, video remote interpreting, or on-site interpretation, to ensure that patients can communicate effectively with healthcare providers.
4. Cultural competency training: Provide cultural competency training for healthcare providers to help them understand the unique needs and experiences of lower-class native English speakers.
5. Health literacy: Improve health literacy by providing clear, culturally sensitive, and patient-centered information in plain language. This can include using visual aids, such as diagrams or pictures, to help patients understand complex medical concepts.
6. Addressing social determinants of health: Identify and address the social determinants of health that may impact lower-class native English speakers' access to healthcare, such as housing instability, food insecurity, and transportation barriers.
7. Collaboration with community organizations: Partner with local community organizations to provide healthcare services, outreach, and education. This can help increase access to care and improve health outcomes for lower-class native English speakers.
8. Health equity research: Conduct research to identify the specific healthcare needs of lower-class native English speakers and develop targeted interventions to address those needs.
