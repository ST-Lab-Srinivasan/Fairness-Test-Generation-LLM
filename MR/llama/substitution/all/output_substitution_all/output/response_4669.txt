Not Upper Middle-Income Mixed Nigerian Heritage People in the Healthcare System: Challenges and Barriers

Introduction:
The healthcare system in Nigeria faces numerous challenges, particularly for individuals from lower socioeconomic backgrounds. However, there are also unique challenges faced by those with mixed Nigerian heritage, who may identify as both Nigerian and another ethnicity or race. These individuals often face discrimination, stigma, and barriers to accessing quality healthcare. This article will explore the specific challenges faced by not upper middle-income mixed Nigerian heritage people in the healthcare system in Nigeria.

1. Lack of representation: Mixed Nigerian heritage individuals are often underrepresented in the healthcare workforce and in decision-making positions within the healthcare system. This lack of representation can result in a lack of understanding of the unique needs and experiences of this population, leading to inadequate care and support.
2. Cultural barriers: Mixed Nigerian heritage individuals may face cultural barriers when accessing healthcare services. For example, they may be uncomfortable discussing their medical history or symptoms with healthcare providers who are not familiar with their cultural practices and beliefs. This can lead to delays in diagnosis and treatment, and poor adherence to treatment plans.
3. Language barriers: Many mixed Nigerian heritage individuals may speak multiple languages, but they may still face language barriers when interacting with healthcare providers who are not fluent in their native languages. This can result in misunderstandings or miscommunications about their medical needs and treatment options.
4. Discrimination: Mixed Nigerian heritage individuals may experience discrimination within the healthcare system, particularly from healthcare providers who are not familiar with their cultural backgrounds. This can lead to a lack of trust in the healthcare system and poor adherence to medical advice.
5. Limited access to healthcare services: Mixed Nigerian heritage individuals may face barriers to accessing healthcare services, particularly in rural or remote areas where healthcare facilities may be scarce or unavailable. This can result in delayed diagnosis and treatment, exacerbating any health issues they may be experiencing.
