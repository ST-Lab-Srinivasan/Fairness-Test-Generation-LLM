2. Limited access to healthcare: Older adult ex-military people with genetic conditions may face barriers in accessing healthcare services due to factors such as limited mobility, lack of transportation options, or lack of healthcare providers with experience in treating genetic conditions.
3. Social isolation: Genetic conditions can lead to social isolation and loneliness, particularly if older adult ex-military people are unable to participate in activities they enjoy due to their condition.
4. Stigma and discrimination: Older adult ex-military people with genetic conditions may experience stigma and discrimination from family members, friends, and society at large, which can affect their mental health and well-being.
5. Impact on independence: Genetic conditions can impact older adult ex-military people's ability to maintain their independence, particularly if they experience mobility limitations or cognitive decline.
