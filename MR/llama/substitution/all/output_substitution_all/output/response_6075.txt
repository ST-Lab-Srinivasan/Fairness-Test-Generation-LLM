Creating more inclusive cultural programs for native English speaker, natural-born US citizens requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Understand the diverse backgrounds of participants: Recognize that participants come from various racial, ethnic, and cultural backgrounds. Be aware of the different experiences, perspectives, and traditions that they bring to the program.
2. Involve diverse stakeholders in program planning: Engage individuals from diverse backgrounds in the planning process to ensure that the program is inclusive and meets the needs of all participants. This can include community leaders, artists, educators, and other cultural practitioners.
3. Provide language support: Offer language support for participants who may not be fluent in English. This can include interpreted events, translated materials, or bilingual staff.
4. Create inclusive programming: Design programs that are accessible and inclusive to all participants. This can include using diverse formats, such as visual arts, music, dance, and theater, to engage participants and create opportunities for collaboration.
5. Foster a sense of belonging: Encourage participants to share their experiences and perspectives, and create opportunities for them to connect with one another. This can be done through interactive workshops, peer-to-peer mentorship, or group discussions.
6. Address power dynamics: Be aware of the potential power imbalances that may exist within the program, particularly in terms of race and ethnicity. Work to address these imbalances by providing opportunities for marginalized groups to take leadership roles and have a voice in the program's decision-making processes.
7. Provide cultural competency training: Offer training on cultural competency and diversity, equipping staff and volunteers with the skills they need to work effectively with diverse participants.
8. Evaluate and adjust: Regularly evaluate the program's effectiveness in promoting inclusivity and making adjustments as needed. Solicit feedback from participants to identify areas for improvement.
