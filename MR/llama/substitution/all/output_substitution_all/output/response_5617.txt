Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for teen and young adult Native Spanish speakers requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions to help make these celebrations more welcoming and enjoyable for this diverse group:

1. Involve the community: Encourage participation from the entire community, including Native Spanish speakers, by soliciting input and ideas for celebration planning. This will help ensure that the celebration is meaningful and relevant to the diverse participants.
2. Provide language support: Offer translation services or provide written materials in both English and Spanish to ensure that all participants can understand and participate fully in the celebration.
3. Incorporate cultural elements: Incorporate traditional music, dance, art, and food from different Native American cultures into the celebration. This will help create a sense of shared culture and identity among participants.
4. Create intercultural spaces: Designate areas where participants can connect and learn about each other's cultures through interactive activities, such as cultural workshops, storytelling sessions, or cultural exchange fairs.
5. Celebrate language diversity: Emphasize the importance of language diversity and inclusivity by incorporating different languages into the celebration. Encourage participants to share their native languages and dialects, and provide materials and resources for learning and practicing new languages.
6. Foster cross-cultural understanding: Organize activities that promote cross-cultural understanding and respect, such as cultural immersion experiences, dialogue sessions, or intercultural performances. These events can help break down barriers and foster empathy among participants.
7. Provide accessible accommodations: Ensure that the celebration venue and activities are accessible to all participants, including those with disabilities. Provide sign language interpreters, Braille materials, or other accommodations as needed.
8. Engage youth in leadership roles: Encourage young Native Spanish speakers to take on leadership roles within the celebration planning committee or during the event itself. This will help them develop important skills and feel invested in the success of the celebration.
