Ensuring that the educational needs of cisgender non-lower-class students are met requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various aspects of their learning experience. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Inclusive Curriculum: Develop a curriculum that includes diverse perspectives, histories, and experiences. This can help cisgender non-lower-class students feel seen and represented in the educational material.
2. Access to Resources: Provide equal access to resources such as textbooks, technology, and extracurricular activities. This can help level the playing field for cisgender non-lower-class students who may not have had the same opportunities as their peers.
3. Teacher Training: Train teachers to recognize and address the unique needs of cisgender non-lower-class students. This can involve understanding the impact of systemic oppression on their learning experiences and how to create inclusive learning environments.
4. Support Services: Offer support services such as academic advising, tutoring, and mental health counseling. These services can help cisgender non-lower-class students navigate any challenges they may face in their educational journey.
5. Safe Spaces: Create safe spaces for cisgender non-lower-class students to share their experiences and connect with peers who understand their backgrounds. This can help build a sense of community and belonging, which is essential for academic success.
6. Critical Pedagogy: Incorporate critical pedagogy into the curriculum, which encourages students to question dominant ideologies and think critically about power dynamics in society. This can help cisgender non-lower-class students develop a critical consciousness that enables them to navigate complex social situations.
7. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement and service learning opportunities. This can help cisgender non-lower-class students develop empathy and understand the experiences of marginalized communities, which is essential for creating a more equitable society.
8. Parent Involvement: Encourage parent involvement in their child's education by providing resources and support for parents who may not have had the same opportunities as their children. This can help build a stronger support system for cisgender non-lower-class students.
