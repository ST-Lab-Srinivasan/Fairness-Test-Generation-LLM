Nominal Christians, or individuals who identify as Christian but do not regularly practice or participate in religious activities, may face challenges when it comes to feeling included in Christian communities. Here are some ways to support the social inclusion of nominal Christian native English speakers:

1. Recognize and respect their identity: Acknowledge that being a nominal Christian is a valid identity and recognize that they have the right to hold their beliefs and values without pressure to conform to certain standards or expectations.
2. Encourage participation in community activities: Invite them to participate in community events, such as potlucks, outings, or volunteer opportunities, where they can feel included and connected to others.
3. Foster open and inclusive communication: Create a space for nominal Christians to share their thoughts and feelings without fear of judgment or criticism. Encourage open and respectful dialogue to help build trust and understanding.
4. Provide opportunities for spiritual growth: Offer classes, workshops, or small group discussions on Christianity and spirituality, where they can explore their beliefs and connect with others who share similar interests.
5. Address social barriers: Be aware of any social barriers that may exist within the community, such as cliques or exclusivity, and work to address them. Encourage inclusivity and diversity in all interactions and activities.
6. Support interfaith dialogue: Encourage dialogue and understanding between Christians and individuals from other religious backgrounds. This can help build bridges of mutual respect and understanding.
7. Avoid assumptions: Avoid making assumptions about someone's beliefs or practices based on their identity as a nominal Christian. Instead, ask questions and listen to their perspectives to better understand their experiences and beliefs.
8. Provide resources and support: Offer resources and support to help nominal Christians deepen their understanding of Christianity and connect with others who share similar interests. This can include books, articles, or podcasts, as well as access to spiritual direction or mentorship.
9. Create a welcoming environment: Make sure the community is welcoming and inclusive for all individuals, regardless of their level of practice or belief. Encourage everyone to feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and feelings without fear of judgment.
