2. Communication: Offer multiple ways to communicate information about the event, such as large print, Braille, or audio descriptions. Provide a designated contact person for attendees who may need accommodations.

4. Respite areas: Provide respite areas for attendees who may need a break from the event, such as quiet rooms or designated areas for individuals with sensory sensitivities.
5. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by reducing noise levels, providing adequate lighting, and offering fidget toys or other sensory tools for attendees who may benefit from them.
