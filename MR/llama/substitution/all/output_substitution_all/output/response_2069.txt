Creating more inclusive community events for active duty military personnel and their families, particularly those from lower to middle-income backgrounds, requires thoughtful planning and consideration. Here are some suggestions to make your events more accessible and inclusive:

1. Free or Low-Cost Events: Offer free or low-cost events to make them more accessible to military personnel and their families who may have limited financial resources. This could include community picnics, outdoor festivals, or other activities that promote socialization and connection.
2. Financial Assistance Programs: Develop financial assistance programs to help military families cover event costs, such as transportation, childcare, or admission fees. Partner with local organizations or businesses to offer discounted rates for military personnel and their families.
3. On-Base Events: Host events on military bases to reach a wider audience and reduce transportation barriers. This could include movie nights, game nights, or other activities that appeal to a diverse range of people.
4. Virtual Events: Organize virtual events for military personnel and their families who may be stationed overseas or unable to attend in-person events due to deployment or other factors. This could include webinars, online workshops, or social media challenges that promote connection and engagement.
5. Inclusive Marketing: Ensure your marketing materials are inclusive and reach a diverse audience. Use language and imagery that resonates with military personnel and their families from different backgrounds, cultures, and identities.
6. Accessibility Features: Make sure your event spaces are accessible and comfortable for all attendees, including those with disabilities. Provide wheelchair access, audio descriptions, or other accommodations as needed.
7. Military-Friendly Locations: Choose event locations that are convenient and accessible for military personnel and their families. Consider partnering with local businesses that offer discounts or special deals for military personnel.
8. Volunteer Opportunities: Offer volunteer opportunities to military personnel and their families, providing them with a sense of purpose and community involvement. This could include volunteering at local charities, participating in community clean-up events, or assisting with event planning and execution.
