The impact of disability status on the lives of wheelchair-using individuals can be significant and far-reaching. Here are some of the common challenges and barriers faced by wheelchair-using individuals:

1. Social Isolationation: People with mobility impairments may face social isolation, as they may have difficulty accessing public spaces, participating in social events, or engaging in activities with able-bodied peers.
2. Accessibility Barriers: Wheelchair users often encounter physical barriers such as inaccessible buildings, inadequate transportation options, and lack of assistive technology. These barriers can limit their participation in society and restrict their access to education, employment, and healthcare.
3. Stigma and Discrimination: People with disabilities may experience stigma and discrimination, which can lead to lower self-esteem, reduced opportunities, and a lack of social inclusion.
4. Health Problems: Wheelchair users may be at higher risk for various health problems such as pressure sores, joint pain, and respiratory infections due to the physical demands of using a wheelchair.
5. Limited Employment Opportunities: People with disabilities may face significant barriers when trying to enter or advance in the workforce, including lack of accommodations, job discrimination, and limited career advancement opportunities.
6. Financial Hardship: Individuals with mobility impairments may experience financial hardship due to higher costs associated with acquiring and maintaining assistive technology, transportation, and home modifications.
7. Lack of Representation: People with disabilities are often underrepresented in decision-making roles and lack a voice in shaping policies and practices that affect their lives.
8. Limited Access to Education: Wheelchair users may face barriers to accessing education, including inaccessible buildings, lack of accommodations, and discrimination.
