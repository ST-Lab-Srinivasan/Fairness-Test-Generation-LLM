2. Increased trust: When healthcare providers demonstrate cultural competence, they build trust with their patients. Patients are more likely to feel comfortable sharing personal information and seeking medical advice from providers who understand their cultural backgrounds.
3. Better diagnosis and treatment: Cultural competence helps healthcare providers recognize the impact of culture on a patient's symptoms, behaviors, and medical beliefs. This leads to more accurate diagnoses and effective treatments tailored to each patient's unique needs.

Reduced health disparities: Cultural competence can help reduce health disparities among native Arabic speakers, as providers are better equipped to address the unique health needs of this population.
