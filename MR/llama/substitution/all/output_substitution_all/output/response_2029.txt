Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner Canadian speakers of Middle Eastern descent requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some suggestions to help make these programs more welcoming and engaging:

1. Cultural sensitivity training: Provide training on cultural sensitivity and diversity for program staff, volunteers, and instructors. This will help them understand the needs and preferences of Middle Eastern communities and create a more inclusive environment.
2. Inclusive language: Use language that is inclusive and respectful of all cultures. Avoid using terms or phrases that may be offensive or stereotypical, such as "Middle Eastern" alone, which can be seen as reductionist and ignore the diversity within this category. Instead, use terms like "Canadian Arabs," "Canadian Muslims," or "Canadian Iranians," which acknowledge the specific cultural backgrounds of these communities.
3. Representation matters: Ensure that the program's content, marketing materials, and staff reflect the diversity of the communities they serve. This can be achieved by incorporating images, stories, and perspectives from different Middle Eastern cultures in promotional materials, programs, and other communications.
4. Community outreach: Reach out to local Middle Eastern communities through events, workshops, or partnerships. This will help build trust and rapport with these communities, increase participation, and ensure that the program is tailored to their needs and preferences.
5. Accessibility: Ensure that the program is accessible to all, regardless of cultural background or socio-economic status. Provide accommodations such as language support, wheelchair accessibility, and affordable program fees to make the program inclusive for all.
6. Flexible scheduling: Offer flexible scheduling options to cater to different cultural practices and preferences. For example, offer programs at times that are convenient for working parents or individuals observing Ramadan.

Cross-cultural exchange: Organize cross-cultural exchange events, such as cultural festivals, exhibitions, or workshops, where participants can learn about and appreciate different Middle Eastern cultures.
