Promoting the educational success of native English speaker physically disabled students requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies to help ensure their academic success:

1. Inclusive Learning Environments: Create inclusive learning environments that promote equal opportunities for all students, regardless of their abilities. This can be achieved by incorporating Universal Design for Learning (UDL) principles, which provide multiple means of representation, expression, and engagement for learners.
2. Adaptive Technology: Utilize adaptive technology to help physically disabled students access educational materials and complete assignments. For example, text-to-speech software, speech-to-text software, and other assistive devices can help them communicate more effectively.
3. Accommodations and Modifications: Provide accommodations and modifications that cater to the individual needs of each student. These may include extra time to complete assignments, one-on-one support, or modified assessments that take into account their physical limitations.
4. Differentiated Instruction: Offer differentiated instruction that takes into account the diverse learning styles and abilities of physically disabled students. This can involve tailoring teaching methods, materials, and activities to meet their unique needs.
5. Inclusive Curriculum: Develop an inclusive curriculum that incorporates diverse perspectives and experiences. This can help foster a sense of belonging and inclusion among all students, including those with physical disabilities.
6. Teacher Training: Provide teachers with training on how to support physically disabled students in the classroom. This may involve learning about best practices for inclusive education, strategies for adapting curriculum and instruction, and understanding the needs of students with different types of disabilities.
7. Peer Support: Encourage peer support and mentorship programs that pair able-bodied students with physically disabled peers. This can help promote socialization, friendship, and a sense of belonging among all students.
