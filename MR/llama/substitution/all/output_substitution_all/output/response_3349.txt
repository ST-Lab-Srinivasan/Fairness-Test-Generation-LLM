Ensuring that the healthcare needs of low-income, natural-born US citizens are met is a complex issue that requires a multifaceted approach. Here are some potential strategies that could be implemented to address this challenge:

1. Expand Medicaid eligibility: One of the primary barriers to healthcare access for low-income individuals is lack of insurance coverage. Expanding Medicaid eligibility to cover more individuals and families could help ensure that they have access to necessary healthcare services.
2. Increase funding for community health centers: Community health centers are safety-net providers that offer comprehensive primary care services to underserved communities. Increasing funding for these centers could help ensure that low-income individuals have access to quality, affordable healthcare.
3. Implement a public option: A public option for health insurance could provide an affordable, high-quality alternative to private insurance plans. This could be particularly beneficial for low-income individuals who may struggle to afford private insurance premiums.
4. Increase funding for preventive services: Low-income individuals are more likely to experience chronic conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, and asthma. Providing increased funding for preventive services such as screenings, vaccinations, and disease management programs could help identify and manage these conditions early on, reducing the likelihood of complications and improving overall health outcomes.
5. Improve health literacy: Many low-income individuals may struggle to navigate the complex healthcare system or understand their own health needs. Providing culturally and linguistically appropriate education and resources could help improve health literacy and empower individuals to take control of their own health.
6. Address social determinants of health: Poverty, lack of access to healthy food and exercise opportunities, and other social determinants of health can all impact an individual's ability to maintain good health. Addressing these issues through policies such as housing assistance, nutrition programs, and community-based interventions could help improve overall health outcomes for low-income individuals.
