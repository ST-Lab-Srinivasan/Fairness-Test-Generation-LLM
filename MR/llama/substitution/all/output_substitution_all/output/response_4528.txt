Poor women face significant challenges in the healthcare system, which can exacerbate existing health disparities and hinder their ability to access quality care. Some of the challenges faced by poor woman people in the healthcare system include:

1. Limited access to healthcare services: Many poor woman lack access to basic healthcare services due to financial constraints, lack of transportation, or limited availability of healthcare facilities in their area. This can result in delayed or incomplete treatment, which can worsen their health outcomes.
2. Gender-based discrimination: Women, especially those from low-income backgrounds, may face gender-based discrimination in the healthcare system. For example, they may be less likely to receive appropriate cancer screenings or be subjected to unnecessary pelvic exams.
3. Limited cultural competency: Healthcare providers may not be sensitive to the cultural and social norms of poor woman, which can lead to misdiagnosis or inappropriate treatment. For example, a provider may not understand the cultural beliefs surrounding menstruation and may misinterpret symptoms as a result.
4. Lack of reproductive healthcare: Poor woman may have limited access to reproductive healthcare services, including contraception, maternal healthcare, and abortion. This can lead to unintended pregnancies, unsafe abortions, and poor maternal health outcomes.
5. Mental health disparities: Women from low-income backgrounds may experience higher rates of mental health disparities, such as depression and anxiety, due to the stress of poverty and gender-based discrimination. However, they may face barriers to accessing mental health services, including lack of availability, stigma, and cost.
6. Limited health literacy: Poor woman may have limited knowledge about their health and the healthcare system, which can lead to misunderstandings or misuse of medications and treatments.
7. Social isolation: Women from low-income backgrounds may experience social isolation, which can negatively impact their mental and physical health. This can be exacerbated by limited access to support systems and resources.
8. Limited English proficiency: Poor woman who are not fluent in English may face barriers to accessing healthcare services
