1. Language Barrier: The primary challenge faced by beginner Arabic speakers is the language barrier. Legal terminology and procedures can be complex and nuanced, which can make it difficult for non-native speakers to understand and navigate the system.
2. Limited Access to Resources: Many beginner Arabic speakers may not have access to resources such as legal dictionaries, law books, or online legal platforms that are available in Arabic. This can limit their ability to research and prepare for legal proceedings.
3. Lack of Trust and Confidence: Beginner Arabic speakers may struggle to build trust and confidence in the legal system due to language barriers and cultural differences. This can make it difficult for them to communicate effectively with lawyers, judges, and other legal professionals.
4. Limited Understanding of Legal Terms: Non-native speakers of Arabic may find it challenging to understand legal terminology and procedures, which can lead to misunderstandings and mistakes in legal proceedings.
5. Cultural Barriers: The legal system in Arab countries is influenced by Islamic law and cultural traditions, which can create cultural barriers for non-native speakers of Arabic. For example, the concept of "majaz" (a measure of the degree of guilt or innocence) may be unfamiliar to non-Arabic speakers, which can lead to misunderstandings in legal proceedings.
