2. Provide linguistic support: Offer beginner Spanish speakers additional language support, such as one-on-one tutoring or small group sessions, to help them build confidence in their language skills.
3. Emphasize mutual respect: Encourage learners to value and appreciate each other's cultural backgrounds and language proficiency levels. Foster a culture of mutual respect and empathy to promote inclusivity.

5. Encourage peer-to-peer learning: Pair beginner Spanish speakers with more advanced learners or native speakers to facilitate language exchange and cultural exchange. This can help build confidence and improve language skills.
6. Provide opportunities for social interaction: Organize social events, such as language exchange meetings, cultural festivals, or group outings, where beginner Spanish speakers can interact with more advanced learners and native speakers in a relaxed setting.
