Supporting the civic engagement of recently refugee mixed Italian heritage people can be achieved through various means. Here are some strategies that could help:

1. Language Support: Provide language classes, translation services, and interpreters to help them navigate bureaucratic processes, access healthcare and education, and fully participate in the civic life of their host country.
2. Cultural Orientation: Offer cultural orientation programs that introduce them to local customs, laws, and social norms, helping them understand the values and beliefs of their new community.
3. Civic Education: Provide education on the rights and responsibilities of citizenship, including voting processes, political participation, and community engagement. This can be done through workshops, classes, or interactive sessions.
4. Voter Registration Drives: Organize voter registration drives specifically targeting recently arrived refugees to ensure they are registered to vote in local elections.
5. Community Outreach: Engage with local communities and organizations to promote understanding and acceptance of refugees. This can involve interfaith dialogues, community events, and cultural festivals that celebrate diversity.
6. Advocacy and Advocacy Training: Train recently arrived refugees to advocate for their rights and interests within the host country. This can include training on how to navigate bureaucratic systems, how to access resources, and how to build coalitions with other marginalized communities.
7. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair recently arrived refugees with local volunteers who can provide guidance on settling into the host country, navigating bureaucratic systems, and building a new life.
8. Online Resources: Create online resources, such as websites, blogs, or social media groups, that provide information, support, and connection for recently arrived refugees. These resources can help them stay informed about local issues, events, and services.
