Supporting the economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US citizens living in poverty is crucial for improving their quality of life and bridging the wealth gap. Here are some ways to achieve this:

1. Education and job training programs: Provide access to high-quality education and job training programs that cater specifically to the needs of Native American communities. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and certification programs in fields such as construction, technology, and healthcare.
2. Small business development: Support the growth of small businesses owned by Native Americans through access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities. This can help create jobs and stimulate economic growth within these communities.
3. Job placement services: Offer job placement services specifically tailored to the needs of Native American job seekers. This can include resume building, interview preparation, and connections to job openings in their communities.
4. Community development finance institutions (CDFIs): Invest in CDFIs that provide loans and other financial services to Native American-owned businesses and community organizations. This can help provide the necessary capital for these entities to grow and thrive.
5. Entrepreneurship training: Provide entrepreneurship training programs specifically designed for Native Americans, focusing on skills such as business planning, marketing, and financial management. This can help individuals start and grow their own businesses, creating jobs and economic opportunities within their communities.
6. Mentorship and networking opportunities: Connect Native American job seekers with experienced professionals in their field through mentorship and networking opportunities. This can provide valuable guidance and support for career advancement.
7. Addressing systemic barriers: Identify and address any systemic barriers that may hinder the economic empowerment of Native Americans, such as discrimination, lack of access to education and job opportunities, and inadequate infrastructure.
8. Collaboration with tribal governments: Work closely with tribal governments and community leaders to understand their specific needs and develop tailored solutions that address the economic challenges facing Native American communities.
