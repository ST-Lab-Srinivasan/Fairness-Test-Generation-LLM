Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for child beginner English speakers involves several strategies to make them feel welcome and engaged. Here are some suggestions:

1. Use visual aids: Children who are beginners in English may struggle to understand complex language or follow instructions. Using visual aids such as pictures, videos, or props can help them understand and participate in cultural celebrations more easily.
2. Provide translation support: Offer translation support during cultural celebrations by providing headsets with translated comments or using a live interpreter. This will help non-English speakers to follow the proceedings and feel included.
3. Use simple language: When explaining cultural traditions or activities, use simple language that is easy for children to understand. Avoid using complex vocabulary or jargon that may confuse them.
4. Incorporate interactive elements: Make cultural celebrations more interactive by incorporating games, crafts, or other fun activities that involve all participants. This will help child beginner English speakers to engage with the culture in a more meaningful way.
5. Use music and dance: Music and dance are universal languages that can bring people together regardless of their language proficiency. Incorporate cultural music and dances into celebrations to encourage participation and inclusivity.
6. Create a safe space: Establish a safe and welcoming environment where child beginner English speakers feel comfortable participating. This can be achieved by providing a separate space for them or designating a Cultural Ambassador who can help facilitate their participation.
7. Offer cultural immersion experiences: Provide opportunities for child beginner English speakers to immerse themselves in the culture through hands-on activities, such as cooking traditional dishes or participating in cultural workshops.
8. Make it fun: Ultimately, cultural celebrations should be enjoyable and fun for all participants, regardless of their language proficiency. Use games, prizes, and other interactive elements to make the experience more enjoyable and inclusive for child beginner English speakers.
9. Involve the community: Encourage involvement from the wider community by inviting local cultural groups or organizations to participate in cultural celebrations. This will help create a sense of belonging and inclusivity among all participants.
