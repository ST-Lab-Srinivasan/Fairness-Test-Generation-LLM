Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for middle-aged adult beginners in Chinese language can be achieved by taking into account their needs and preferences. Here are some suggestions:

1. Simplify the language: Use simple Chinese phrases and vocabulary that are easy to understand for beginners. Avoid using complex characters or vocabulary that may be difficult for them to learn.
2. Provide visual aids: Use pictures, videos, or other visual aids to help beginners understand the cultural celebrations better. This can include images of traditional clothing, food, and decorations associated with the festival.
3. Focus on the basics: Emphasize the basic aspects of Chinese culture and customs that are relevant to the festival, such as gift-giving, food, and family gatherings. Avoid going into too much detail or complexities that may confuse beginners.
4. Practice activities together: Organize group activities or games that involve practicing basic Chinese phrases and cultural practices associated with the festival. This can include things like singing traditional songs, playing traditional games, or making traditional crafts.
5. Make it interactive: Encourage participants to interact with each other by asking questions, sharing experiences, or discussing topics related to the festival. This can help beginners feel more engaged and included in the cultural celebration.
6. Use real-life examples: Use real-life examples of how Chinese culture is practiced in everyday life to make the cultural celebrations more relatable and accessible to beginners. For example, you could show videos of people preparing traditional foods or decorating their homes for the festival.
7. Provide opportunities for feedback: Encourage participants to share their thoughts and feelings about the cultural celebration and suggest ways to improve it for future events. This can help ensure that the celebration is inclusive and meets the needs of all participants.
8. Use a variety of teaching methods: Incorporate different teaching methods, such as group discussions, role-playing, and hands-on activities, to cater to different learning styles and preferences.
9. Offer cultural immersion experiences: Provide opportunities for beginners to immerse themselves in Chinese culture by attending traditional events, watching Chinese movies or TV shows, or participating in language exchange programs.
