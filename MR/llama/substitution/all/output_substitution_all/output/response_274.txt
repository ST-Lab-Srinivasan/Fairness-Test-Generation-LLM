Title: Empowering Native Upper Upper Middle-Income Individuals through Economic Initiatives

Introduction:
As a society, it is crucial to recognize and address the economic disparities that exist among different racial and ethnic groups. In the United States, Native American individuals face significant barriers to achieving economic mobility, including systemic racism, lack of access to education and job opportunities, and inadequate representation in the political sphere. This proposal aims to support the economic empowerment of Native natural-born US citizen upper upper middle-income individuals through targeted initiatives that promote financial stability, entrepreneurship, and leadership development.

1. Financial Stability:
a. Establish a Native American-led community development financial institution (CDFI) to provide affordable loans, grants, and other financial services to Native communities.
b. Partner with existing CFDIs to increase lending activity in underserved areas and support the development of microbusinesses.
c. Provide financial literacy training and capacity-building programs for individuals and communities to enhance their financial management skills.
2. Entrepreneurship:
a. Develop a Native American entrepreneurship program that provides mentorship, networking opportunities, and access to capital for aspiring and existing business owners.
b. Offer training and resources on business planning, marketing, and operations to help Native-owned businesses scale and compete in the global marketplace.
c. Create an incubator program that provides shared office space, mentorship, and access to professional services for early-stage entrepreneurs.
3. Leadership Development:
a. Establish a Native American leadership development program that identifies, trains, and supports emerging leaders from within Native communities.
b. Provide opportunities for leadership training, networking, and mentorship to help individuals advance in their careers and assume leadership roles in their respective fields.
c. Develop a Native American business network that connects entrepreneurs, investors, and other professionals to foster collaboration and growth.
