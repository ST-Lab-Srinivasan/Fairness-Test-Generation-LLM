Highlighting the issues faced by low-income Mexicans is crucial for addressing the underlying causes of poverty and inequality. Here are some ways to use data to shed light on these issues:

1. Poverty rates: Collect and analyze data on poverty rates among different demographic groups, such as rural vs. urban areas, age, gender, and ethnicity. This can help identify which groups are disproportionately affected by poverty.
2. Income distribution: Analyze income distribution patterns to understand the gap between the rich and poor. This can help identify the extent of income inequality and how it affects different segments of the population.
3. Access to basic services: Collect data on access to basic services such as healthcare, education, and sanitation. This can help identify which groups have limited or no access to these services, highlighting the disparities in service delivery.
4. Employment and labor market conditions: Analyze employment rates, unemployment rates, and labor market conditions to understand the challenges faced by low-income individuals in finding decent work.
5. Housing conditions: Collect data on housing conditions, including access to adequate housing, overcrowding, and lack of basic amenities such as running water and sanitation. This can help identify the extent of housing poverty and its impact on health and well-being.
6. Health outcomes: Analyze health outcomes data to understand the differences in access to healthcare, disease prevalence, and mortality rates among different demographic groups. This can help identify the health disparities faced by low-income individuals.
7. Social exclusion: Collect data on social exclusion indicators such as discrimination, marginalization, and lack of access to social services. This can help understand the barriers faced by low-income individuals in accessing opportunities and resources.
8. Government policies and programs: Analyze government policies and programs aimed at addressing poverty and inequality. This can help identify the effectiveness of these interventions and inform policy decisions to address the issues faced by low-income individuals.
