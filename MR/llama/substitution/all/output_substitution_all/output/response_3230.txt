Ensuring the educational needs of very young Native Nigerian speakers (age 90 and below) can be achieved through a combination of effective teaching methods, culturally responsive practices, and a supportive learning environment. Here are some strategies to meet their educational needs:

1. Language-rich environment: Create a language-rich environment in the classroom by using Nigerian languages in teaching and learning. Encourage students to use their mother tongue during lessons, and provide opportunities for them to interact with each other in their native languages.
2. Adaptive teaching methods: Use adaptive teaching methods that cater to the unique needs of young learners. This includes using visual aids, storytelling, songs, and games to make learning more engaging and enjoyable.
3. Cultural relevance: Incorporate cultural practices and values into the curriculum to make it more relevant and meaningful to students. Encourage students to share their cultural experiences and traditions in the classroom.
4. Bilingual education: Implement bilingual education programs that promote simultaneous exposure to both Nigerian languages and English. This can help students develop fluency in both languages, which is essential for effective communication and academic success.
5. Parental involvement: Encourage parents to be actively involved in their child's education by providing them with resources and support. Organize parent-teacher conferences and workshops to keep them informed of their child's progress and involve them in decision-making processes.
6. Community partnerships: Collaborate with community organizations, cultural centers, and other educational institutions to provide additional learning opportunities for students. This can include field trips, guest speakers, and joint projects that promote intercultural understanding and exchange.
7. Technology integration: Leverage technology to enhance teaching and learning. Use educational apps, games, and online resources to make learning more engaging and accessible for young learners.
8. Differentiated instruction: Offer differentiated instruction to cater to the diverse needs of students. Provide opportunities for students to work at their own pace, explore different learning styles, and receive individualized feedback and support.
