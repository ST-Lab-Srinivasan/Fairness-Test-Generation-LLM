Addressing the needs of non-disabled mixed Japanese individuals in disaster response requires a comprehensive approach that takes into account their unique experiences and challenges. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Inclusive planning: Ensure that disaster response plans are inclusive of diverse communities, including those with disabilities. This can be done by involving representatives from diverse backgrounds in the planning process and taking into account their specific needs.
2. Accessibility considerations: Make sure that disaster shelters and evacuation routes are accessible to people with disabilities. Provide ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms to enable easy access.
3. Communication support: Provide communication supports such as sign language interpreters, CART (communication assistive technology), or written materials in braille for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing.
4. Disability-specific training: Provide disability-specific training for emergency responders and volunteers to help them understand the unique needs of people with disabilities during a disaster.
5. Accessible transportation: Ensure that transportation is accessible for individuals with disabilities by providing wheelchair-accessible vehicles or transporting individuals in their own vehicles with proper safety measures in place.
6. Assistive technology: Provide assistive technology such as augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) devices, voice-controlled devices, or other assistive tools to help individuals with disabilities communicate or perform tasks during a disaster.
7. Healthcare support: Ensure that there are adequate healthcare resources available to meet the unique medical needs of individuals with disabilities during a disaster. This can include providing medical equipment and supplies that cater to their specific needs.
8. Inclusive shelter operations: Ensure that shelters are inclusive and accessible for individuals with disabilities by providing accommodations such as modified living spaces, assistive technology, or other supports.
9. Community engagement: Engage with the community to raise awareness about the unique needs of individuals with disabilities during a disaster and encourage them to take an active role in disaster response efforts.
