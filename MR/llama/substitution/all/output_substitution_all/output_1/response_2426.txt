The impact of disability status on the lives of permanent residents with moderate disabilities can be significant and far-reaching. Some common effects include:

1. Social Isolationation: Individuals with disabilities may face barriers in accessing public spaces, transportation, and social events, leading to social isolation and reduced opportunities for social interaction.
2. Limited Access to Education and Employment: People with disabilities may encounter difficulties in accessing educational institutions and workplaces due to physical or attitudinal barriers, limiting their career advancement and earning potential.
3. Financial Hardship: The additional costs of living with a disability, such as adapted housing or assistive technology, can lead to financial hardship for individuals and their families.
4. Stigma and Discrimination: Disability status can lead to social stigma and discrimination, affecting an individual's self-esteem and mental health.
5. Limited Access to Healthcare: Individuals with disabilities may encounter difficulties in accessing healthcare services due to physical or attitudinal barriers, leading to delayed diagnosis or treatment of medical conditions.
6. Reduced Participation in Community Activities: People with disabilities may face barriers in participating in community activities and events due to physical or attitudinal barriers, limiting their opportunities for social engagement and personal growth.
7. Lack of Representation and Voice: Individuals with disabilities may feel underrepresented or unheard in decision-making processes, leading to a lack of understanding and support for their needs.
8. Limited Access to Information and Communication: People with disabilities may encounter difficulties in accessing information and communication due to physical or attitudinal barriers, limiting their ability to access services and support.
9. Increased Vulnerability to Abuse: Individuals with disabilities may be more vulnerable to abuse, neglect, or exploitation due to their reliance on others for support.
10. Reduced Quality of Life: Living with a disability can lead to reduced quality of life, including limitations in physical functioning, social participation, and emotional well-being.
