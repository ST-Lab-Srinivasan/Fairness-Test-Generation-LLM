4. Providing role models: Introduce gender-nonconforming children to role models who embody the qualities they aspire to have, such as confidence, resilience, and creativity. This will help them see that their identity is valid and achievable.
5. Offering mentorship: Pair gender-nonconforming children with mentors who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement. Mentors can offer valuable insights and help the children develop their leadership skills.
6. Encouraging community involvement: Encourage gender-nonconforming children to participate in community service or volunteer work. This will help them develop a sense of responsibility and compassion for others, which are essential leadership qualities.
