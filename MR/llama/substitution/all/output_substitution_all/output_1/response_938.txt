1. Lack of access to health insurance: Undocumented children are not eligible for provincial or territorial health insurance plans, which means they may have to pay out-of-pocket for medical services. This can be a significant barrier, especially for families living in poverty.
2. Language barriers: Many undocumented children may not speak the dominant language of their host province or territory, which can create difficulties when trying to access healthcare services. They may also face discrimination or lack of cultural sensitivity from healthcare providers.
3. Fear of deportation: Undocumented children may be hesitant to seek medical care due to fear of being reported to immigration authorities and facing deportation. This can lead to delayed or inadequate care, which can exacerbate health problems.
4. Limited access to specialized care: Undocumented children may have limited access to specialized care, such as pediatric services, due to the costs associated with these services. This can result in delays or inadequate treatment for conditions that require specialized care.
