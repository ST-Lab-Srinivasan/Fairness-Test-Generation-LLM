2. Language immersion: Provide opportunities for students to use Italian in the classroom, such as through group discussions, presentations, and role-playing activities. This can help students develop their language skills and foster a sense of belonging.
3. Personalized learning: Get to know each student's strengths, weaknesses, and interests to tailor the educational experience to their individual needs. This can involve setting goals and objectives that are specific to each student and providing regular feedback and support.
4. Incorporating Italian culture: Incorporate Italian culture into the curriculum through activities such as cooking classes, art projects, or visiting cultural events. This can help students develop a deeper appreciation for the language and culture.
5. Collaboration with Italian institutions: Work with Italian institutions, such as universities or cultural centers, to provide students with access to high-quality resources and opportunities for experiential learning.
6. Technology integration: Utilize technology to provide students with a variety of learning tools and resources, such as online language learning platforms, apps, or games. This can help students stay motivated and engaged in the learning process.
