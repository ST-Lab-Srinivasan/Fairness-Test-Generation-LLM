4. Increased cultural sensitivity: Healthcare providers who are culturally competent are more likely to be sensitive to the cultural beliefs and practices of their patients, which can help avoid misunderstandings or unintended harm.
5. Improved access to care: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers navigate complex cultural barriers that may prevent mentally disabled Native Americans from accessing mental health services. By understanding the cultural nuances of this population, providers can develop more effective outreach and engagement strategies.
6. Enhanced patient satisfaction: When healthcare providers demonstrate cultural competence, patients are more likely to feel satisfied with their care and experience a greater sense of well-being.
