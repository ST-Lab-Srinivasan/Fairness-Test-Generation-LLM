Creating more inclusive cultural programs for Native English Speaker Ethnically Full American Individuals requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some suggestions:

1. Understand the Community: Start by understanding the needs, interests, and preferences of the community you are trying to serve. Conduct surveys, focus groups, or interviews to gather information about their cultural background, preferences, and expectations.
2. Involve the Community: Engage with the community throughout the program development process. Involve them in the decision-making process and seek their input on what they would like to see in the program. This will help ensure that the program is culturally relevant and meaningful to the community.
3. Provide Cultural Education: Offer cultural education programs that teach about the history, traditions, and practices of the Native American community. This can include workshops on traditional music, dance, art, and storytelling.
4. Celebrate Traditional Events: Celebrate traditional Native American events such as powwows, sun dances, and cultural festivals. These events provide an opportunity for community members to share their culture and traditions with others.
5. Develop Cultural Programs: Develop programs that are specifically tailored to the needs of the Native American community. This can include language classes, cultural camps, and mentorship programs.
6. Create Inclusive Spaces: Create inclusive spaces where community members feel comfortable sharing their culture and traditions. This can include creating a designated space for traditional practices or events.
7. Collaborate with Community Members: Collaborate with community members to develop programs that are culturally relevant and meaningful. This can include working with elders, community leaders, and other stakeholders to ensure the program is culturally appropriate.
8. Provide Accessibility: Ensure that cultural programs are accessible to all community members, regardless of their ability. This can include providing accommodations such as American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation or audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
