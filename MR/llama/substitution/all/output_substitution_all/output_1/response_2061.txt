It is important to recognize that individuals with disabilities are not defined solely by their disability status. Rather, they are multifaceted individuals with their own unique experiences, personalities, and identities. As such, it is crucial to avoid making assumptions or stereotypes about people based on their disability status.

4. Internalized oppression: Disabled individuals may internalize societal messages that they are not worthy or capable, leading to self-doubt, low self-esteem, and a lack of confidence.
