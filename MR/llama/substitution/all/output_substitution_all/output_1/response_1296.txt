2. Provide opportunities for cultural immersion: Invite them to participate in cultural events, such as concerts, plays, or art exhibits, where they can practice their Spanish skills in a supportive environment.
3. Encourage creative expression: Suggest that they write stories, poems, or songs in Spanish. This can be a fun way for them to express themselves and improve their language skills.
4. Create a language exchange group: Gather a group of beginner Spanish speakers in their late 30s and organize regular language exchange meetings where they can practice speaking with each other.
5. Use technology to their advantage: There are many apps, websites, and online courses available that can help them learn Spanish. Duolingo, Babbel, and Rosetta Stone are popular options.
6. Celebrate their progress: Acknowledge and celebrate their efforts and progress, no matter how small. This can help build their confidence and motivation to continue learning.
7. Provide resources and support: Offer to lend them Spanish language books, movies, or TV shows to help them improve their skills. Also, encourage them to seek out a tutor or mentor who can provide additional support and guidance.
8. Encourage them to practice with native speakers: Arrange for them to practice speaking with native Spanish speakers, either in person or through online language exchange platforms. This can help them improve their pronunciation, grammar, and vocabulary.
9. Create a supportive environment: Encourage them to surround themselves with other beginners who are also learning Spanish. This can create a supportive and motivating environment where they can practice speaking and learning together.
