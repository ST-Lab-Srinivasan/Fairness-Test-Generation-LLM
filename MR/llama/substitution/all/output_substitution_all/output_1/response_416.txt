To ensure that the voices of permanent residents aged 65 and older are heard in decision-making processes, several strategies can be employed. Here are some suggestions:

1. Create age-friendly communities: Design communities that are accessible and inclusive for all ages, including older adults. Provide amenities such as transportation, housing, and community spaces that cater to the needs of this population.
2. Engage in outreach and education: Reach out to older adults through various channels, such as community centers, senior centers, and faith-based organizations. Provide educational materials and workshops on topics relevant to their interests, such as healthcare, social issues, and civic engagement.
3. Establish advisory committees: Set up advisory committees or task forces composed of older adults to provide input on decision-making processes related to age-friendly policies and programs.
4. Utilize technology: Leverage technology to reach older adults who may face barriers to participation, such as mobility issues or lack of access to transportation. Use tools like video conferencing, social media, and online surveys to engage with this population.
5. Foster intergenerational connections: Encourage interactions between older adults and younger generations to foster mutual understanding and respect. Organize events and activities that bring people of different ages together, such as mentorship programs, community service projects, or cultural events.
6. Celebrate their contributions: Acknowledge and celebrate the contributions of older adults in their communities, whether through volunteering, advocacy, or other forms of engagement. This can help build trust and recognition among this population.
7. Provide opportunities for leadership: Offer training and development programs to equip older adults with leadership skills and opportunities to take on leadership roles within their communities.
8. Address systemic barriers: Identify and address any systemic barriers that may hinder the participation of older adults in decision-making processes, such as lack of transportation or limited access to healthcare services.
9. Foster a culture of inclusion: Encourage a culture of inclusion and respect for diversity within communities, recognizing the value of different perspectives and experiences.
