1. Education and Skills Development: Invest in programs that provide access to quality education, job training, and vocational skills development for African-Americans. This can help them acquire the skills needed to compete in the job market and advance in their careers.
2. Job Creation and Entrepreneurship: Encourage entrepreneurship and job creation in underserved communities by providing access to capital, mentorship, and resources. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities for African-Americans.
3. Wealth Building: Implement policies that promote wealth building and financial inclusion, such as affordable housing, homeownership programs, and retirement savings plans. This can help African-Americans build intergenerational wealth and achieve financial stability.
4. Addressing Systemic Inequality: Advocate for policies that address systemic inequality in education, employment, housing, and criminal justice. This can include initiatives such as targeted hiring programs, diversity and inclusion training, and community-based reentry programs for individuals returning from prison.

Corporate Diversity and Inclusion: Encourage corporations to adopt diversity and inclusion strategies that address the underrepresentation of African-Americans in leadership positions and other areas of the workforce.
