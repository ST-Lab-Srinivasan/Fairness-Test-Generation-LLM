Supporting the career aspirations of long-term divorced military spouses can be challenging, but there are several ways to help them achieve their goals:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to educational resources and training programs that can help them acquire new skills and knowledge to pursue their career aspirations. This could include online courses, certification programs, or vocational training.
2. Mentorship: Pair them with experienced professionals in their desired field for mentorship and guidance. This can provide valuable insights and advice on how to navigate the workforce as a military spouse.
3. Networking: Encourage them to attend networking events and conferences where they can meet industry professionals, learn about job opportunities, and build connections that can help them advance their careers.
4. Career Fairs: Host career fairs specifically for military spouses, where they can interact with potential employers and learn about job opportunities. This can be a great way to connect them with potential employers and showcase their skills and qualifications.
5. Job Placement Assistance: Offer job placement assistance, such as resume building, interview preparation, and job search strategies. This can help them identify suitable job opportunities and increase their chances of getting hired.
6. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage and support entrepreneurial pursuits by providing access to resources such as business planning workshops, mentorship programs, and funding opportunities. This can help them start their own businesses or side hustles, which can be a great way to generate income and achieve financial stability.
7. Childcare Support: Offer childcare services or support to help them balance work and family responsibilities. This can include on-base childcare facilities, subsidized childcare programs, or referrals to local providers.
8. Financial Assistance: Provide financial assistance, such as scholarships or grants, to help them cover education and training costs, or to support their entrepreneurial pursuits.
9. Career Counseling: Offer one-on-one career counseling sessions with a trained career coach to help them identify their strengths, set career goals, and develop a plan to achieve those goals.
