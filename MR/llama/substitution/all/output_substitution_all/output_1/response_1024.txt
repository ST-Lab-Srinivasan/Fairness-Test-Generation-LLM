2. Stigma: There is often a stigma associated with genetic conditions, which can lead to social isolation and discrimination. Documented carriers may face social pressures to hide their condition or avoid discussing it at work.
3. Limited job opportunities: Some documented carriers may have limitations in the types of jobs they can perform due to the nature of their condition. For example, someone with a genetic condition that affects their mobility may have limited options for jobs that require physical stamina.
4. Lack of accommodations: Many documented carriers may not receive adequate accommodations from their employers to help them perform their job duties. This can include things like modified work schedules, assistive technology, or additional support staff.
5. Confidentiality concerns: Documented carriers may be concerned about maintaining their privacy and confidentiality in the workplace, especially if their condition is not widely understood or accepted.

7. Career advancement: Documented carriers may face barriers to career advancement due to their condition, such as being passed over for promotions or having their credibility questioned.
