Ensuring that the voices of permanent residents, especially those who have recently turned 65, are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting inclusivity and equity. Here are some strategies to help amplify their voices:

1. Establishment of Age-Friendly Community Committees: Create committees comprising permanent residents aged 65 and above, who can provide input on issues affecting their age group. These committees can advise local government officials on policies, programs, and services that cater to the needs of older adults.
2. Public Consultations and Surveys: Conduct public consultations and surveys specifically targeting permanent residents aged 65 and above. This can help gather their opinions on various issues, such as transportation, housing, healthcare, and social isolation. The results of these consultations can inform decision-making processes at the local level.
3. Community Outreach Programs: Develop outreach programs that target older adults in their communities. These programs can provide opportunities for permanent residents to share their thoughts on issues affecting them and to build connections with other members of their community.
4. Age-Friendly City Accreditation: Apply for age-friendly city accreditation, which recognizes cities that are designed to support older adults. This can create a framework for promoting inclusivity and equity in decision-making processes.
5. Intergenerational Collaboration: Foster intergenerational collaboration between older adults and younger generations. This can help promote mutual understanding, respect, and cooperation, ensuring that the voices of permanent residents are heard and valued.
6. Advocacy Groups: Support advocacy groups specifically focused on promoting the rights and interests of older adults. These groups can mobilize their members to participate in decision-making processes and ensure that their voices are represented.
7. Capacity Building: Provide capacity building opportunities for permanent residents aged 65 and above, enabling them to develop skills and knowledge in areas such as advocacy, leadership, and community organizing. This can empower them to effectively participate in decision-making processes.
