Supporting the economic empowerment of unemployed Native natural-born US citizen individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the systemic barriers and challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Job training and placement programs: Provide job training and placement services specifically tailored to the needs of Native communities. These programs can help individuals develop in-demand skills and connect with potential employers.
2. Entrepreneurship and small business development: Encourage entrepreneurship and support small business development in Native communities. Provide resources, such as training, mentorship, and access to capital, to help individuals start and grow their own businesses.
3. Education and career counseling: Offer education and career counseling services to help individuals identify their strengths and interests, and explore career paths that align with their skills and goals.
4. Mentorship and networking opportunities: Connect unemployed Native individuals with mentors who can provide guidance and support in navigating the job market. Offer networking opportunities to help individuals connect with potential employers and build professional relationships.
5. Access to capital: Provide access to capital, such as loans or grants, to help individuals start or grow their own businesses. This can include microloans, crowdfunding platforms, or other financial tools.
6. Addressing systemic barriers: Advocate for policies and practices that address the systemic barriers facing Native communities, such as discrimination, lack of access to healthcare, and inadequate infrastructure.
7. Cultural preservation and revitalization: Support cultural preservation and revitalization efforts in Native communities, which can help individuals connect with their heritage and identity. This can include language classes, traditional arts and crafts training, and cultural festival and event support.
8. Housing and infrastructure development: Work to address the lack of affordable housing and inadequate infrastructure in Native communities. This can include supporting housing development projects and advocating for infrastructure improvements.
9. Healthcare access and mental health services: Advocate for improved access to healthcare and mental health services in Native communities. This can include support for community health centers, mental health counseling, and substance abuse treatment.
