Genetic conditions can have significant impacts on the lives of children in lower middle-income countries. Here are some of the potential effects:

1. Limited access to healthcare: Children with genetic conditions may not have access to proper medical care, including diagnosis, treatment, and management of their condition. This can lead to delayed or inadequate treatment, which can exacerbate the condition and impact the child's quality of life.
2. Social isolation: Children with genetic conditions may face social stigma and discrimination, leading to social isolation and reduced opportunities for education and social interaction.
3. Economic burden: Caring for a child with a genetic condition can be financially draining on families, particularly in lower middle-income countries where access to healthcare and support services may be limited.
4. Limited access to reproductive healthcare: In some cultures, children with genetic conditions may be less likely to receive appropriate reproductive healthcare, which can lead to unintended pregnancies or inadequate prenatal care.
5. Difficulty with education: Children with genetic conditions may face challenges with education due to learning disabilities or other cognitive impairments. This can limit their opportunities for future success and independence.
6. Limited employment opportunities: Adults with genetic conditions may face discrimination in the workplace, leading to limited employment opportunities and economic stability.
7. Difficulty with social relationships: Children and adults with genetic conditions may experience difficulties with social relationships due to stigma, discrimination, or social isolation.
8. Limited access to mental health services: Children and adults with genetic conditions may have limited access to mental health services, which can exacerbate symptoms of anxiety, depression, or other mental health disorders.
9. Difficulty with daily living activities: Children and adults with genetic conditions may face challenges with daily living activities, such as bathing, dressing, and grooming, due to physical limitations or cognitive impairments.
