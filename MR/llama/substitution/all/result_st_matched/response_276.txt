The impact of disability status on the lives of permanent residents with moderate disabilities can be significant and far-reaching. Here are some of the common impacts:

1. Social Isolationation: People with disabilities may face social isolation, as they may find it difficult to participate in social activities or events due to physical limitations. This can lead to feelings of loneliness and depression.
2. Limited Access to Education and Employment: Individuals with moderate disabilities may face barriers in accessing education and employment opportunities. They may need accommodations or modifications to participate in educational programs or workplaces, which can be costly and time-consuming to implement.
3. Financial Insecurity: People with disabilities may face financial insecurity due to limited job opportunities and higher living expenses. They may require additional financial support to maintain a decent standard of living.
4. Stigma and Discrimination: Individuals with disabilities may experience stigma and discrimination from family members, friends, and society at large. This can lead to feelings of low self-esteem, depression, and anxiety.
5. Lack of Representation: People with disabilities are often underrepresented in decision-making positions, such as in government, businesses, and organizations. This lack of representation can result in policies and practices that do not address the needs and concerns of individuals with disabilities.
6. Limited Access to Healthcare: Individuals with disabilities may face barriers in accessing healthcare services due to physical limitations or discrimination. This can lead to delayed diagnosis and treatment, resulting in poorer health outcomes.
7. Lack of Independent Living: People with disabilities may face challenges in living independently due to physical limitations or lack of accessibility in their living environments. This can result in a dependence on others for daily tasks and activities.
