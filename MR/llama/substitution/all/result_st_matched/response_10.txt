Creating more inclusive social policies for high society bereaved individuals requires a comprehensive and compassionate approach that acknowledges the unique challenges faced by this group. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive policies:

1. Acknowledge the specific needs of high society bereaved individuals: High society bereaved individuals often face additional challenges such as social isolation, loss of status and identity, and pressure to maintain a facade of normalcy. Policies should recognize these challenges and provide targeted support to address them.
2. Provide mental health support: Grieving is a complex and often lengthy process, and high society bereaved individuals may struggle with the added stress of maintaining a public image. Mental health professionals can offer counseling and therapy sessions to help them cope with their loss and manage their emotions in a healthy way.
3. Offer financial assistance: High society bereaved individuals may face significant financial challenges due to the loss of a spouse or partner, including loss of income, investments, or other assets. Policies can provide financial assistance, such as emergency funds or long-term financial planning support, to help them manage their finances during this difficult time.
4. Create support networks: High society bereaved individuals may feel isolated and disconnected from their social circles due to their loss. Creating support networks of peers who have experienced similar losses can help them connect with others who understand their experiences and challenges.
5. Provide access to resources and services: Policies can provide access to resources and services that can help high society bereaved individuals manage their grief and maintain their well-being, such as art therapy, music therapy, or other forms of creative expression.
6. Challenge societal expectations and stigmas: High society bereaved individuals may face societal expectations and stigmas that can exacerbate their grief and isolation. Policies can challenge these expectations and promote a more compassionate and understanding view of grief and loss.
