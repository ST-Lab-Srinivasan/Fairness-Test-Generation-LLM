Cultural diversity plays a significant role in enriching the lives of native-born US citizens, particularly those from lower-income households. Here are some ways cultural diversity can positively impact their lives:

1. Exposure to new ideas and perspectives: Cultural diversity provides an opportunity for people to learn about and appreciate different cultures, beliefs, and values. This exposure can broaden their worldview, foster empathy, and promote inclusivity.
2. Identity formation and expression: Cultural diversity allows individuals to explore and express their own identity, which can be a vital aspect of self-discovery and personal growth. It provides them with the freedom to embrace their cultural heritage and express themselves authentically.
3. Community building and social connections: Cultural events, festivals, and celebrations bring people together, fostering a sense of community and social connections. These interactions can help break down social barriers and create a more cohesive society.
4. Economic benefits: Cultural diversity can also have economic benefits, as it creates opportunities for entrepreneurship, innovation, and job creation in the cultural industries (e.g., music, art, food). This can lead to increased economic growth and opportunities for lower-income households.
5. Increased civic engagement: Cultural diversity can foster greater civic engagement and political participation among marginalized communities. By providing a platform for their voices to be heard, cultural diversity can help promote social justice and democratic values.
6. Personal fulfillment and well-being: Being part of a diverse culture can bring personal fulfillment and well-being, as individuals feel connected to their community and heritage. This sense of belonging can lead to improved mental health and overall quality of life.
