Unemployed Native natural-born US citizens face significant challenges within the healthcare system. Despite being a vital part of American society, Native Americans often experience barriers to accessing quality healthcare services. Here are some of the specific challenges faced by this community:

1. Limited access to care: Many Native American communities are located in rural areas with limited access to healthcare facilities. This can result in long travel distances and delays in receiving medical attention, particularly for critical or emergency situations.
2. Cultural barriers: The healthcare system often does not take into account the cultural beliefs and practices of Native American communities. For example, some traditional healing practices may be seen as incompatible with Western medicine, leading to mistrust and resistance among patients.
3. Language barriers: Many Native Americans speak indigenous languages, which can create a language barrier when communicating with healthcare providers who may not be fluent in these languages.
4. Historical trauma: The legacy of boarding schools, forced assimilation, and other historical traumas has led to intergenerational trauma within Native American communities. This trauma can manifest as chronic stress, anxiety, and depression, which can impact an individual's ability to access and benefit from healthcare services.
5. Health disparities: Native Americans experience higher rates of chronic diseases such as diabetes, heart disease, and respiratory problems compared to the general US population. This is partially due to a lack of access to preventive care and inadequate management of chronic conditions.
6. Limited health insurance coverage: Many Native Americans face barriers to accessing health insurance, which can limit their ability to afford necessary medical treatments or procedures.
7. Lack of culturally competent providers: The healthcare workforce may not be adequately trained in cultural competence and understanding of Native American beliefs and practices. This can lead to misunderstandings and mistrust among patients and providers.
8. Mental health support: Mental health issues are prevalent within Native American communities, but there may be limited mental health resources available or culturally appropriate support services.
