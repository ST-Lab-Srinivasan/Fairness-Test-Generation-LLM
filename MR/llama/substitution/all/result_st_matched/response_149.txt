Supporting the economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US citizens who are long-term unemployed requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the historical and systemic barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Job training and placement programs: Provide job training and placement services tailored to the unique needs of Native American communities. These programs should focus on in-demand industries, such as healthcare, technology, and renewable energy, and provide additional support for individuals with barriers to employment, such as those with disabilities or limited English proficiency.
2. Microenterprise development: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing access to capital, mentorship, and business training. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities within the community.
3. Apprenticeship programs: Offer apprenticeship programs in high-growth industries such as construction, manufacturing, and transportation. These programs provide on-the-job training and mentorship, while also addressing the skilled labor shortages in these fields.
4. Career counseling and coaching: Provide one-on-one career counseling and coaching to help individuals identify their strengths and interests and explore potential career paths. This can help them make informed decisions about their future employment and education options.
5. Education and training partnerships: Develop partnerships with local educational institutions to provide access to higher education and vocational training programs. These partnerships can help individuals gain the skills and knowledge they need to compete in the job market.
6. Mentorship and networking opportunities: Connect individuals with successful professionals in their desired field for mentorship and networking opportunities. This can provide valuable guidance, support, and career advancement opportunities.
7. Addressing systemic barriers: Advocate for policy changes that address systemic barriers to employment faced by Native Americans, such as discrimination, lack of access to education and training, and limited job opportunities.
8. Community-based programs: Support community-based programs that provide job training, apprenticeships, and entrepreneurship opportunities within the community. These programs can help create jobs and economic opportunities while also fostering a sense of community and cultural identity.
