Creating more inclusive social policies for working-class bereaved individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges faced by this group. Here are some suggestions:

1. Acknowledge the stigma: The first step is to acknowledge the stigma attached to being bereaved and the additional challenges faced by working-class individuals who may struggle to access support services due to financial constraints or lack of awareness.
2. Listen to their experiences: Conduct research or gather data on the experiences of working-class bereaved individuals, including their struggles, challenges, and support needs. This will help inform policy development and ensure that policies are tailored to meet their specific needs.
3. Provide financial assistance: Offer financial assistance, such as funeral expense grants or emergency funds, to help working-class bereaved individuals cover funeral costs or other essential expenses during a time of crisis.
4. Develop accessible support services: Ensure that support services, such as counseling, therapy, and peer support groups, are accessible and affordable for working-class individuals. This may involve offering online or telephone-based support services to increase accessibility.
5. Promote workplace support: Encourage employers to provide bereavement leave policies that are inclusive of all employees, regardless of their socioeconomic status. This can help working-class individuals manage their grief without fear of loss of income or job security.
6. Address systemic barriers: Identify and address any systemic barriers that may prevent working-class bereaved individuals from accessing support services or resources. For example, lack of transportation or childcare support can be a significant challenge for some individuals.
7. Raise awareness: Educate the public about the challenges faced by working-class bereaved individuals and the importance of providing inclusive support services. This can help reduce stigma and increase empathy towards this group.
8. Advocate for policy change: Work with policymakers to advocate for changes in policies and laws that impact working-class bereaved individuals.
