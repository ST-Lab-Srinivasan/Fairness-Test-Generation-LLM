Working-class progressive people face a range of challenges in the job market, including:

1. Lack of access to good jobs: Many working-class people lack access to well-paying, stable jobs with good benefits and opportunities for advancement. This can be due to systemic barriers such as discrimination, lack of access to education and training, and limited job opportunities in their communities.
2. Wage stagnation: Despite the economic recovery since the Great Recession, wages have not kept pace with inflation for many working-class people. This means that they are struggling to make ends meet and save for the future.
3. Growing gig economy: The growth of the gig economy has created new opportunities for some workers, but it has also created new challenges for others. Many working-class people are finding it difficult to compete with lower-paid, precarious work that lacks job security and benefits.
4. Lack of job security: The rise of the gig economy and automation has led to a decrease in job security for many workers. This can be particularly challenging for working-class people who may not have the skills or resources to adapt to new economic realities.
5. Discrimination and bias: Working-class people, particularly those from marginalized communities, often face discrimination and bias in the workplace. This can limit their opportunities for advancement and create a hostile work environment.
6. Lack of work-life balance: Many working-class people struggle to balance work and family responsibilities due to lack of support from employers and societal expectations.
7. Limited career advancement opportunities: Working-class people may face limited opportunities for career advancement, particularly in fields that are dominated by upper-class professionals. This can lead to a lack of representation and diversity in leadership positions.
8. Unfair labor practices: Some working-class people may face unfair labor practices, such as unequal pay for the same work, lack of opportunities for advancement, and limited access to benefits and resources.
9. Lack of access to training and education: Many working-class people may not have access to training and education that can help them advance in their careers or transition to new fields.
