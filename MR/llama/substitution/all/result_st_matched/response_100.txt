The impact of immigration status on the lives of permanent resident individuals can be significant and far-reaching. Permanent residents are individuals who have been granted permanent residence in a country, but are not citizens. They may face various challenges and limitations compared to citizens, including:

1. Lack of political rights: Permanent residents may not have the same political rights as citizens, such as the right to vote or run for public office.
2. Limited access to social services: Permanent residents may have limited access to certain social services, such as healthcare or education, compared to citizens.
3. Restrictions on employment: Permanent residents may face restrictions on their ability to work in certain industries or fields, or may be subject to specific regulations or requirements related to their employment.
4. Difficulty accessing government benefits: Permanent residents may have difficulty accessing certain government benefits, such as housing assistance or financial assistance, compared to citizens.
5. Limited social integration: Permanent residents may face challenges when it comes to integrating into the host society, particularly if they are not fluent in the language or do not have a strong social network.
6. Discrimination and stigma: Permanent residents may experience discrimination and stigma based on their immigration status, which can affect their mental health and well-being.
7. Uncertainty about future residency: Permanent residents may face uncertainty about their future residency status, particularly if they are in a situation where their immigration status is temporary or subject to change.
8. Difficulty accessing legal aid: Permanent residents may have difficulty accessing legal aid or representation, particularly if they are not eligible for free legal aid services.
9. Limited access to social security: Permanent residents may have limited access to social security benefits, such as old-age pensions or disability benefits, compared to citizens.
10. Difficulty accessing healthcare: Permanent residents may face difficulties accessing healthcare services, particularly if they do not have a valid health insurance card or are not eligible for certain programs.
