Creating more inclusive social policies for high-income widowed individuals requires a comprehensive approach that takes into account their unique circumstances and needs. Here are some strategies that can be considered:

1. Increase access to financial support: High-income widows may face a loss of income due to the death of their spouse, making it difficult to maintain their standard of living. Policies such as survivor benefits or inheritance tax breaks can help alleviate this financial strain.
2. Provide access to healthcare and other services: Widowed individuals may experience a decline in their physical and mental health due to the loss of their spouse. Ensuring access to quality healthcare, counseling, and other support services can help them cope with their grief and maintain their well-being.
3. Encourage social interaction: Social isolation can be a significant challenge for widowed individuals, particularly those who were heavily reliant on their spouse for social interaction. Policies that promote social connection, such as support groups or volunteer opportunities, can help mitigate this risk.
4. Address discrimination and stigma: Widowed individuals may face discrimination or stigma due to their marital status. Policies that promote awareness and understanding of the challenges faced by widows can help combat these attitudes and create a more inclusive society.
5. Provide opportunities for education and training: Many high-income widowed individuals may have had a significant amount of time dedicated to their spouse's career or personal interests. Policies that provide opportunities for education and training can help them acquire new skills and pursue new career paths, reducing their reliance on their inheritance and fostering greater financial independence.
6. Support intergenerational wealth transfer: High-income widowed individuals may face challenges in transferring wealth to future generations due to complex estate planning or tax issues. Policies that provide guidance on how to navigate these issues can help ensure that their wealth is transferred efficiently and sustainably.
