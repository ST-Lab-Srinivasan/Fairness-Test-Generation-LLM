There are several ways in which data can be used to highlight the issues faced by middle-income immigrant individuals:

1. Analyze demographic trends: Use data to understand the demographics of middle-income immigrants, such as their age, gender, education level, and occupation. This can help identify specific groups within this population that may be facing particular challenges.
2. Examine socioeconomic status: Assess the socioeconomic status of middle-income immigrants, including their income, wealth, and access to social services. This can help identify disparities in access to resources and opportunities compared to the general population.
3. Study integration outcomes: Examine the outcomes of middle-income immigrants in terms of their social, economic, and political integration into the host society. This can include measures such as language proficiency, education attainment, and civic participation.
4. Investigate discrimination and bias: Use data to identify instances of discrimination and bias faced by middle-income immigrants, including in the workplace, housing market, and criminal justice system.
5. Evaluate policy impacts: Assess the impact of policies aimed at supporting middle-income immigrants, such as those related to education, employment, and social welfare programs. This can help identify areas where policy interventions may be needed to address the issues faced by this population.
6. Use statistical modeling: Utilize statistical models to predict the future outcomes of middle-income immigrants based on current trends and policies. This can help identify potential challenges and opportunities for this population in the coming years.
7. Conduct surveys and focus groups: Collect data through surveys and focus groups to gather insights directly from middle-income immigrants themselves. This can provide valuable qualitative information on the experiences and perceptions of this population.
8. Analyze news media coverage: Monitor news media coverage of middle-income immigrants to identify patterns and trends in how they are portrayed and discussed in the public sphere.
