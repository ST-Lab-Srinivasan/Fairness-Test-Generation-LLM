2. Use advance directives: Advance directives are written instructions that individuals can prepare in advance regarding their medical treatment preferences in case they become incapacitated. These directives can help ensure that an individual's wishes are respected and their legal rights protected.

Provide supportive care: Supportive care involves providing assistance with daily tasks, such as bathing, dressing, and managing finances, while still allowing the individual to maintain their independence and autonomy. This can help protect an individual's legal rights by ensuring that they are able to make their own decisions and manage their own affairs.
4. Promote self-advocacy: Encouraging individuals with cognitive impairments to advocate for themselves can help ensure that their legal rights are protected. This can involve providing education and support on how to navigate the legal system, as well as promoting self-advocacy skills.
5. Monitor and report any abuse or neglect: It is essential to monitor an individual's living situation and report any signs of abuse or neglect to the appropriate authorities. This can help protect an individual's legal rights by ensuring that they are safe and their well-being is being maintained.
