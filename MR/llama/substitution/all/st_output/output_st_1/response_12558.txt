Single active-duty personnel face several challenges in the job market, particularly after a recent divorce or separation. Here are some of the common challenges they may encounter:

1. Military background not always valued: Many civilian employers may not understand the unique skills and experiences that military personnel possess. This can make it difficult for them to translate their military experience into civilian job opportunities.
2. Gaps in resume: Military personnel may have gaps in their resume due to deployments, training, or other military obligations. These gaps can make it appear as though they are not committed to their career or lack experience in a particular field.
3. Difficulty adjusting to civilian life: After serving in the military for many years, single active-duty personnel may struggle to adjust to civilian life. This can lead to difficulties in finding and keeping a job.
4. Limited networking opportunities: Military personnel often have limited opportunities to network with potential employers, which can make it harder to find job opportunities.
5. Uncertainty about future deployments: Single active-duty personnel may worry about being deployed again, which can impact their ability to maintain a consistent work schedule or plan for the future.
6. Difficulty balancing work and family responsibilities: Military personnel often have to balance work and family responsibilities, which can be challenging when they are recently single and have to care for children or other dependents.
7. Stigma surrounding military divorce: Unfortunately, there is still a stigma surrounding military divorce in some circles. This can make it harder for single active-duty personnel to find job opportunities or get hired by potential employers.
8. Limited access to career counseling and resources: Military personnel may have limited access to career counseling and resources, which can make it harder for them to identify job opportunities or create a successful job search strategy.
9. Difficulty translating military skills to civilian jobs: Military personnel often have unique skills and experiences that may not be directly applicable to civilian job opportunities. This can make it challenging for them to find work in their field of choice.
