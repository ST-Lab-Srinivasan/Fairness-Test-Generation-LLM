2. Cultural differences: Immigrants may have different cultural beliefs and practices that can affect their healthcare experiences, such as the use of traditional medicine or different attitudes towards healthcare seeking behavior.
3. Limited access to care: Immigrants may face barriers to accessing healthcare due to lack of health insurance, unaffordable copays, or limited availability of healthcare providers who speak their native language.

5. Lack of trust: Immigrants may not trust the healthcare system due to past experiences of mistreatment or a lack of understanding of the system.
6. Limited awareness of resources: Immigrants may be unaware of available healthcare resources, such as community clinics or sliding-scale fee clinics, that can provide affordable and accessible care.
7. Difficulty navigating the system: Immigrants may find it difficult to navigate the complex healthcare system, including understanding insurance options, finding providers who speak their language, and dealing with billing and payment issues.
8. Cost barriers: Many immigrants face cost barriers to accessing healthcare, including high deductibles, copays, and coinsurance.
9. Limited access to specialized care: Immigrants may have limited access to specialized care, such as mental health services or complex medical procedures, due to a lack of providers with expertise in their cultural or linguistic needs.
10. Discrimination based on immigration status: Immigrants may face discrimination based on their immigration status, including being denied care or faced with obstacles to accessing care.
