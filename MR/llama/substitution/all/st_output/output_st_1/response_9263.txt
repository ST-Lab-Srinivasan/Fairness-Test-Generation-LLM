Mentorship plays a significant role in the development of Native natural-born US citizen independent people. Mentorship can provide guidance, support, and encouragement to help individuals navigate various aspects of their lives, including personal, social, and cultural development. Here are some ways mentorship can contribute to the development of Native independent people:

1. Cultural preservation and revitalization: Mentorship can help preserve and revitalize Native cultures by passing down traditional knowledge, practices, and values from elders to younger generations. This can involve teaching traditional crafts, languages, and spiritual practices, as well as sharing stories and histories of the community.
2. Identity formation: Mentorship can help individuals develop a strong sense of identity and belonging within their Native community. By providing guidance and support, mentors can help mentees navigate complex issues related to race, culture, and identity, and build confidence in their cultural heritage.
3. Personal growth and development: Mentorship can facilitate personal growth and development by providing individuals with role models and sources of inspiration. Mentors can offer advice and support on how to set goals, manage challenges, and overcome obstacles, helping mentees develop the skills and confidence they need to succeed in their personal and professional lives.
4. Community involvement: Mentorship can encourage community involvement and participation by providing individuals with opportunities to engage in volunteer work, leadership roles, and other forms of community service. This can help build connections between individuals and the broader community, fostering a sense of belonging and social responsibility.
5. Leadership development: Mentorship can help develop future leaders within Native communities by providing individuals with guidance and support on how to navigate leadership roles and responsibilities. Mentors can offer advice on how to make informed decisions, build coalitions, and communicate effectively, helping mentees develop the skills they need to lead their communities successfully.
6. Economic development: Mentorship can play a role in economic development by providing individuals with opportunities for entrepreneurship, business ownership, and professional growth. Mentors can offer guidance on how to start and run a successful business, manage finances, and access resources and networks that can help mentees achieve their economic goals.
