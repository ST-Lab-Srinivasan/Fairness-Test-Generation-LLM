Ensuring that the healthcare needs of deep poverty Nigerian people are met is a complex and multi-faceted challenge. Here are some potential strategies that could be implemented to address this issue:

1. Increase access to healthcare facilities: Many deep poverty communities in Nigeria lack access to basic healthcare services, including clinics, hospitals, and healthcare professionals. Investing in the construction of new healthcare facilities and the training of healthcare workers can help increase access to care for these communities.
2. Improve financial accessibility: Many deep poverty individuals in Nigeria lack the financial resources to access healthcare services. Programs such as subsidies, tax credits, or other forms of financial assistance can help make healthcare more affordable for these individuals.
3. Increase community involvement: Community-based interventions that involve local leaders and community members in the planning and implementation of healthcare programs can help increase participation and engagement.
4. Address social determinants of health: Deep poverty is often linked to poor living conditions, inadequate housing, lack of access to clean water and sanitation, and other social determinants of health. Addressing these issues through community-based interventions that promote better living conditions can help improve overall health outcomes.
5. Leverage technology: Technology can play a role in improving access to healthcare for deep poverty communities in Nigeria. For example, telemedicine services can provide remote consultations and diagnoses, while mobile health (mHealth) applications can provide access to health information and resources.
6. Foster partnerships: Collaboration between government agencies, NGOs, private sector actors, and community-based organizations is crucial for addressing the healthcare needs of deep poverty communities in Nigeria. These partnerships can help pool resources, share expertise, and leverage each partner's strengths to improve health outcomes.
7. Address cultural and linguistic barriers: Deep poverty communities in Nigeria may have different cultural and linguistic practices that can impact their access to healthcare services. Addressing these barriers through culturally appropriate interventions and language support can help increase participation and engagement.
