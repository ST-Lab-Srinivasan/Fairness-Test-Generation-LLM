Ensuring the educational needs of non-upper-class individuals over the age of 90 is crucial to promote their cognitive and emotional well-being, as well as their ability to navigate the complexities of modern society. Here are some strategies that can help meet the educational needs of this demographic:

1. Age-friendly education: Develop educational programs that cater specifically to the needs and abilities of older adults. This may involve using age-appropriate materials, simplifying language and instructions, and providing opportunities for social interaction and mental stimulation.
2. Community-based learning: Offer educational programs in community settings, such as senior centers, libraries, or other local venues. This can help older adults feel more comfortable and connected to their peers, while also reducing barriers to participation, such as transportation costs or difficulty accessing traditional educational institutions.
3. Technology-based learning: Utilize technology to provide flexible and accessible learning opportunities for older adults. Online courses, mobile apps, and other digital tools can help bridge the gap between generations and provide a platform for intergenerational learning.
4. Collaborative partnerships: Foster partnerships between educational institutions, community organizations, and government agencies to create comprehensive and coordinated approaches to addressing the educational needs of older adults. This can involve sharing resources, expertise, and best practices to ensure that older adults receive the support they need.
5. Capacity-building initiatives: Develop targeted programs to build the capacity of older adults to learn and adapt in a rapidly changing world. This may involve providing training on new technologies, promoting lifelong learning attitudes, and fostering a growth mindset among older adults.
6. Intergenerational learning: Encourage intergenerational learning opportunities between older adults and younger generations. This can help bridge knowledge gaps and promote mutual understanding and respect between different age groups.
7. Personalized support: Provide personalized support to older adults based on their individual needs, interests, and abilities. This may involve tailoring educational programs to address specific cognitive or emotional challenges, as well as providing adaptive equipment or assistive technologies to facilitate learning.
