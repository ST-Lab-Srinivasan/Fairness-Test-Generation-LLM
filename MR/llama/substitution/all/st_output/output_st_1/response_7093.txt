Creating more inclusive cultural programs for native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants to the US requires understanding and acknowledging their diverse backgrounds, experiences, and perspectives. Here are some suggestions for creating more inclusive cultural programs:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training on cultural competency for staff and volunteers involved in the program. This training can help them understand the unique needs and experiences of native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants, and how to provide support and resources that are culturally responsive.
2. Bilingual Programming: Offer programs and services in both English and Spanish to cater to the diverse language proficiency levels within the community. This can include bilingual events, workshops, and classes, as well as providing translation support for important documents and communications.
3. Community Outreach and Engagement: Engage with native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants through community outreach and engagement efforts. This can involve hosting events and activities that cater to their interests and needs, such as cultural festivals, language exchange programs, and support groups.
4. Collaborative Partnerships: Foster partnerships with organizations that serve native Spanish speakers and recent immigrant communities. These partnerships can help to leverage resources, expertise, and networks to create more inclusive cultural programs and services.
5. Inclusive Programming: Develop programming that is inclusive of native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants. This can involve creating opportunities for them to participate in decision-making processes, leading workshops or classes, and having a voice in the development of cultural programs and services.
6. Cultural Representation: Ensure that the cultural programs and services are representative of the diverse backgrounds and experiences of native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants. This can involve incorporating their traditions, customs, and practices into the programming, as well as featuring their art, music, and other forms of cultural expression.
7. Accessibility and Accommodation: Ensure that the cultural programs and services are accessible and accommodating for native Spanish speakers and recent immigrants. This can involve providing interpreters or translators, offering flexible scheduling options, and ensuring that the physical space is accessible to people with disabilities.
