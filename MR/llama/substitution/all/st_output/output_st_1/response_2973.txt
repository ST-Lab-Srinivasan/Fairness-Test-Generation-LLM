Creating more inclusive social policies for long-term unemployed and recently single individuals requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the various challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive policies:

1. Access to education and training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs, including digital skills training, to help individuals acquire new skills and enhance their employability.
2. Job placement services: Offer job placement services that cater specifically to the needs of long-term unemployed and recently single individuals. This can include one-on-one career counseling, job matching, and interview preparation.
3. Income support: Provide income support through programs such as unemployment benefits, job seeker allowances, or welfare payments to help individuals meet their basic needs while they are actively seeking employment.
4. Childcare support: Offer affordable childcare options to help individuals who are recently single or have children, by providing access to quality childcare services that can be flexible and affordable.
5. Housing support: Provide support for housing costs, such as rent subsidies or homeownership assistance, to help individuals who are struggling to make ends meet due to long-term unemployment or recent single status.
6. Mental health support: Offer mental health services and resources to help individuals cope with the emotional challenges of long-term unemployment or recent single status, such as counseling, therapy, or support groups.
7. Networking opportunities: Organize networking events and workshops that bring together individuals from different backgrounds and industries, providing opportunities for mentorship, collaboration, and job opportunities.
8. Work-life balance programs: Offer programs that promote work-life balance, such as flexible working arrangements or compressed workweeks, to help individuals manage their responsibilities and reduce stress.
9. Career development resources: Provide access to career development resources, such as online courses, webinars, or conferences, that can help individuals enhance their skills and knowledge in their chosen field.
10. Advocacy and awareness: Raise awareness about the challenges faced by long-term unemployed and recently single individuals
