Creating more inclusive social policies for individuals who are not just above the poverty line but also for those who have been widowed for a long time requires a multifaceted approach that addresses various aspects of their lives. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive social policies:

1. Address Poverty and Economic Inequality:
	* Implement policies that promote job creation, entrepreneurship, and economic growth to reduce poverty and inequality.
	* Increase access to education and job training programs to enhance employability.
	* Provide financial assistance through social safety nets such as cash transfers or subsidies to help individuals meet their basic needs.
2. Support Widows and Orphans:
	* Provide financial support to widows and orphans through pension plans, scholarships, or other forms of financial assistance.
	* Offer counseling and mental health services to help them cope with the loss of a loved one.
	* Encourage community support and volunteerism to help alleviate their social isolation.
3. Promote Access to Healthcare:
	* Ensure access to quality and affordable healthcare for all individuals, including those who are widowed or living in poverty.
	* Provide health insurance options or subsidies to make healthcare more accessible.
	* Invest in preventive healthcare programs to reduce health disparities.
4. Foster Inclusive Housing Policies:
	* Implement policies that promote affordable and inclusive housing options for low-income individuals, including widows and orphans.
	* Provide subsidies or financial assistance to help individuals access safe and decent housing.
	* Encourage community land trusts or cooperative housing models to promote collective ownership and control of housing.
5. Address Social Isolation:
	* Promote community engagement and social connection through volunteer opportunities, social events, or other forms of community participation.
	* Provide transportation options or subsidies to help individuals access essential services and social activities.
	* Encourage intergenerational connections by providing opportunities for older adults to mentor or interact with younger generations.
