Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of Nigerian recent immigrants to the United States. It provides a sense of identity, belonging, and connection to their roots, which is crucial for maintaining cultural practices and traditions in a new and unfamiliar environment. Here are some ways that cultural heritage influences the lives of Nigerian immigrants:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage helps recent immigrants feel connected to their community and culture, which is essential for building a sense of belonging in a new country. It provides a shared identity and common values that unite people and create a sense of community.
2. Identity formation: Nigerian immigrants' cultural heritage shapes their identity and defines who they are as individuals and as members of their community. It influences their beliefs, values, and behaviors, and helps them navigate the complexities of life in a new country.
3. Preservation of traditions: Cultural heritage provides a framework for preserving traditional practices and customs, such as language, music, dance, and art. These cultural expressions are essential for transmitting knowledge and values from one generation to the next and for maintaining a sense of continuity with their homeland.
4. Coping mechanisms: Cultural heritage can serve as a coping mechanism during times of stress or difficulty. It provides a sense of comfort and security, as well as a way to process and make sense of challenging situations.
5. Integration into the host society: Cultural heritage can also play a role in integrating Nigerian immigrants into the host society. By sharing their cultural practices and traditions with others, they can build bridges of understanding and foster positive relationships with their neighbors and community members.
6. Preservation of cultural knowledge: Cultural heritage helps preserve traditional knowledge and skills that are critical for maintaining cultural practices and traditions. This includes knowledge about farming, food preparation, craftsmanship, and other important aspects of everyday life.
