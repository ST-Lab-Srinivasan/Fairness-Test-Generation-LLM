Supporting the economic empowerment of individuals with deep poverty and no known genetic conditions requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various factors, including education, skills development, access to finance, and social inclusion. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Skills Development:
	* Provide access to quality education and vocational training programs that cater to the individual's interests and strengths.
	* Offer life skills training, such as financial literacy, time management, and goal-setting.
	* Encourage entrepreneurship and innovation through mentorship and networking opportunities.
2. Access to Finance:
	* Provide access to affordable and flexible financial services, such as microfinance loans or savings accounts.
	* Facilitate linkages with local banks or financial institutions for loan availability.
	* Offer financial literacy training to help individuals manage their finances effectively.
3. Social Inclusion:
	* Encourage community engagement and social interaction through cultural events, sports activities, or volunteer opportunities.
	* Provide access to mental health services to address any emotional or psychological challenges that may arise from poverty and isolation.
	* Advocate for policies and programs that promote inclusive growth and reduce inequality.
4. Job Creation and Entrepreneurship:
	* Identify potential entrepreneurs and provide training, mentorship, and resources to help them start their own businesses.
	* Create job opportunities through partnerships with local businesses or social enterprises.
	* Encourage the development of cooperatives or community-based organizations that can generate income for individuals and communities.
5. Livelihood Support:
	* Provide access to essential goods and services, such as food, shelter, and healthcare, through targeted interventions or partnerships with local organizations.
	* Offer skills training in areas like agriculture, animal husbandry, or craft-making to help individuals generate income from these activities.
	* Facilitate access to markets or buyers for these products, either through direct sales or indirectly through intermediaries.
