Creating more inclusive cultural events for non-liberal, non-able-bodied individuals requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to make your event more accessible and enjoyable for everyone:

1. Accessibility features: Ensure that the venue is wheelchair-accessible and has amenities such as ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms. Provide audio descriptions or sign language interpretation for performances or presentations.
2. Accommodate diverse needs: Offer a variety of seating options, including reserved seating for people with mobility impairments, and provide extra seating for those who may need it. Make sure the event is not overwhelmingly loud or bright, as some individuals may be sensitive to sound or light.
3. Inclusive programming: Create a program that includes a diverse range of performers, speakers, or presenters. Consider including artists with disabilities in your lineup, and make sure there are no ableist or discriminatory elements in the content.
4. Accessible communication: Provide multiple ways for attendees to access information, such as large print materials, braille signage, or audio announcements. Offer real-time captioning or translation services for presentations or performances.
5. Sensory-friendly environment: Take steps to reduce sensory overload by minimizing loud noises, bright lights, and strong smells. Allow attendees to take breaks in a quiet area if needed.
6. Involve disabled artists and performers: Collaborate with disabled artists and performers in the planning and execution of the event. Their input will help ensure that the event is more inclusive and accessible to them.
7. Train staff and volunteers: Provide training for staff and volunteers on disability etiquette, accessibility, and inclusion. This will help create a welcoming environment for all attendees.
8. Flexible ticketing options: Offer flexible ticketing options, such as mobile tickets or print-at-home tickets, to accommodate the needs of attendees with disabilities.
9. Accessibility feedback: Solicit feedback from attendees on the accessibility of the event and make improvements for future events.
