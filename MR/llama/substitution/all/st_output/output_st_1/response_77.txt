Creating more inclusive social policies for working-class bereaved individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges and needs of this group. Here are some recommendations:

1. Acknowledge the value of informal caregiving: Working-class individuals often take on an disproportionate amount of unpaid care work, including caring for sick or elderly relatives. Policies should recognize and value this invisible labor, providing support for caregivers in terms of respite, financial assistance, and recognition.
2. Increase access to mental health services: Bereavement can have a significant impact on mental health, particularly for working-class individuals who may lack the resources to access professional help. Policies should prioritize increasing access to affordable mental health services, including counseling, therapy, and support groups.
3. Address poverty and financial insecurity: Bereaved individuals may experience a significant decline in income due to their loss, particularly if they were the primary breadwinner. Policies should aim to address poverty and financial insecurity by providing financial assistance, such as bereavement leave payments or funeral expense assistance.
4. Provide childcare support: Working parents who have experienced the loss of a parent may struggle to balance work and family responsibilities. Policies should provide support for childcare, including flexible work arrangements, subsidized childcare, or emergency backup care.
5. Offer culturally sensitive services: Bereaved individuals from diverse backgrounds may have unique cultural and religious practices that need to be respected. Policies should ensure that services are culturally sensitive and accessible to all communities.
6. Foster a supportive work environment: Employers can play a critical role in supporting bereaved employees by providing a supportive work environment, including flexible work arrangements, counseling services, or employee assistance programs.
7. Advocate for paid leave policies: Paid bereavement leave can help working-class individuals navigate the grieving process without jeopardizing their employment or financial stability.
