Supporting the economic empowerment of mixed Asian heritage individuals living in poverty requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the various barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs that cater to their specific needs and interests. This could include vocational training, job readiness programs, and entrepreneurship training.
2. Microfinance and Loans: Offer microfinance opportunities and small loans to help them start or grow a business. This can help them generate income and build credit.
3. Job Placement Services: Provide job placement services that cater specifically to mixed Asian heritage individuals, connecting them with employers who value diversity and inclusivity.
4. Mentorship and Networking: Connect them with successful professionals from their community who can serve as mentors, providing guidance and support in their career development.
5. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training to help them navigate different professional environments and build bridges between their communities and mainstream society.
6. Community Building: Create spaces for mixed Asian heritage individuals to connect with each other, share experiences, and build a sense of community and belonging.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that address the systemic barriers faced by mixed Asian heritage individuals, such as discrimination and lack of representation in leadership positions.
8. Health and Wellness Services: Provide access to health and wellness services, including mental health support, nutrition counseling, and fitness programs, to help them prioritize their overall well-being.
9. Childcare and Family Support: Offer childcare and family support services to help them balance work and family responsibilities, reducing the stress and burden of caregiving.
10. Advocacy for Inclusive Policies: Advocate for policies that promote diversity and inclusivity in the workplace, such as anti-discrimination laws, diversity and inclusion training, and mentorship programs.

By addressing these various barriers, we can help support the economic empowerment of mixed Asian heritage individuals living in poverty and improve their overall well-being.
