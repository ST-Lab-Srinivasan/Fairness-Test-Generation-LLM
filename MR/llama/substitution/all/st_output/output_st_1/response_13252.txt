As a Buddhist mentor, I understand that providing support and guidance to recently widowed casual Buddhists in high school can be a challenging task. However, here are some strategies that can help address their specific needs:

1. Listen actively: Allow these individuals to share their experiences and feelings without interrupting or judging. Active listening can help them feel heard and validated, which is essential for emotional healing.
2. Offer practical support: High school graduates who have lost a loved one may need help with daily tasks such as grocery shopping, cooking, or childcare. Offer to assist them in these areas, especially during the initial stages of grief.
3. Encourage self-care: Grieving individuals often neglect their own needs while tending to the needs of their loved ones. Encourage them to prioritize self-care activities such as exercise, meditation, or spending time in nature.
4. Provide emotional support: Losing a loved one can be an emotionally draining experience. Offer emotional support by being present and available to talk when needed. Encourage them to express their feelings through journaling, drawing, or talking about their experiences.
5. Help them find meaningful ways to honor their loved one: Losing a loved one can leave individuals feeling lost and uncertain about how to move forward. Encourage them to find meaningful ways to honor their loved one's memory, such as creating a memorial or participating in activities that bring them comfort.
6. Connect them with resources: Loss can be overwhelming, and individuals may need additional support beyond what they can provide on their own. Connect them with local grief support groups, counseling services, or online resources that can offer additional guidance and support.
7. Be patient and understanding: Grieving is a complex and often lengthy process. Be patient and understanding when working with recently widowed casual Buddhists in high school, recognizing that their healing journey will be unique and may take time.
8. Encourage mindfulness practices: Mindfulness practices such as meditation or deep breathing can help individuals manage their emotions and find peace during the grieving process. Encourage them to incorporate these practices into their daily routine.
