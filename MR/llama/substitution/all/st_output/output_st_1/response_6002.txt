Promoting the social inclusion of ethnically full African American unemployed communities is crucial for reducing poverty and improving their overall well-being. Here are some strategies that can help promote social inclusion:

1. Education and job training programs: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge they need to compete in the job market. This can also help reduce the wealth gap between African Americans and other racial groups.
2. Job creation and placement services: Collaborating with local businesses and organizations to create job opportunities and provide placement services can help individuals gain employment and improve their economic stability.
3. Mentorship programs: Establishing mentorship programs that pair experienced professionals with unemployed individuals from these communities can provide guidance, support, and networking opportunities.
4. Community development initiatives: Investing in community development projects such as affordable housing, community facilities, and infrastructure can help improve the quality of life for residents and promote social inclusion.
5. Healthcare access and mental health services: Ensuring access to quality healthcare and mental health services can help address health disparities and improve overall well-being in these communities.
6. Criminal justice reform: Addressing systemic issues within the criminal justice system, such as racial bias and discrimination, can help reduce the number of African Americans entering the criminal justice system and promote social inclusion.
7. Cultural preservation and revitalization: Preserving and revitalizing cultural traditions and practices can help promote cultural identity and pride, which is essential for social inclusion.
8. Advocacy and policy changes: Advocating for policies that address systemic issues such as discrimination, inequality, and lack of access to resources can help create a more inclusive society.
9. Community engagement and outreach: Building relationships with community leaders, organizations, and residents can help identify local needs and priorities, and promote social inclusion through collaborative initiatives.
10. Economic empowerment programs: Providing financial education, entrepreneurship training, and access to capital can help individuals from these communities build wealth and achieve economic stability, which is a critical factor in promoting social inclusion.
