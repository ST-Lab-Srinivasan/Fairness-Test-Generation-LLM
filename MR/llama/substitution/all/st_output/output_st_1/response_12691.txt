Celebrating the cultural contributions of immigrants, including recent immigrants, is an important way to recognize and appreciate their diverse backgrounds and experiences. Here are some ways to celebrate their cultural contributions in a non-libertarian manner:

1. Cultural Festivals: Organize or participate in cultural festivals that showcase the traditions, food, music, and art of immigrant communities. This can include events like Diwali, Chinese New Year, or Day of the Dead.
2. Art Exhibits: Host art exhibits that feature the work of immigrant artists, highlighting their unique perspectives and experiences. This can also include exhibits that showcase traditional crafts and textiles from different cultures.
3. Food Festivals: Organize food festivals that celebrate the culinary traditions of immigrant communities. This can include events like Taste of Ethiopia or Flavors of India.
4. Storytelling Sessions: Host storytelling sessions where immigrants share their personal stories and experiences, highlighting their cultural backgrounds and traditions.
5. Music Performances: Organize music performances that feature traditional instruments and songs from different cultures. This can include events like Latin American music nights or African drumming performances.
6. Language Classes: Offer language classes that teach the languages spoken by immigrant communities, such as Spanish, Mandarin, or Hindi.
7. Cultural Workshops: Host workshops that teach traditional crafts and skills, such as pottery, weaving, or calligraphy, from different cultures.
8. Film Screenings: Organize film screenings that showcase the experiences of immigrants through documentaries or narrative films.
9. Book Clubs: Host book clubs that focus on books written by immigrant authors or books that explore themes related to immigration and cultural identity.
10. Community Service Projects: Organize community service projects that involve immigrant communities, such as cleaning up local parks or volunteering at food banks.
