Creating more inclusive community programs for active-duty newly married individuals requires understanding their unique needs and challenges. Here are some suggestions to make your community programs more welcoming and supportive:

1. Offer flexible scheduling: Many newly married couples have busy schedules with work and military commitments. Provide programs that offer flexible scheduling options, such as evening or weekend sessions, to accommodate their busy lives.
2. Provide childcare options: Newly married couples may struggle to find affordable and quality childcare while they participate in community programs. Consider offering on-site childcare or partnering with local organizations that provide this service.
3. Address financial constraints: Many newly married couples face financial challenges due to the added expenses of starting a new family. Offer financial assistance or scholarships to help cover program costs, making it more accessible for them to participate.
4. Foster a supportive environment: Create a sense of community and belonging by fostering open communication and mutual support among participants. Encourage sharing of experiences and offer mentorship opportunities with more established couples.
5. Incorporate diverse activities: Offer a variety of activities that cater to different interests and hobbies, such as fitness classes, art workshops, or outdoor excursions. This will help ensure that there's something for everyone and make the program more enjoyable for all participants.
6. Provide resources for military-specific challenges: Newly married couples may face unique challenges related to military service, such as deployment, relocation, and stress management. Offer workshops or resources specifically addressing these issues to help them navigate their new life together.
7. Encourage couples to connect with each other: Organize social events or activities that encourage newly married couples to connect with each other, such as potluck dinners, game nights, or outdoor picnics. This will help build a sense of community and support among participants.
