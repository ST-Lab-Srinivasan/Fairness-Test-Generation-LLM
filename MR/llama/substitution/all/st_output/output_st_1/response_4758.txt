The healthcare system faces several challenges that affect not only upper-middle-income mixed African Americans but also other marginalized groups. Some of these challenges include:

1. Limited access to healthcare: Many marginalized communities, including African Americans, face barriers in accessing quality healthcare due to factors such as lack of transportation, long wait times, and limited availability of healthcare providers who understand their cultural backgrounds and linguistic needs.
2. Health disparities: Marginalized communities experience higher rates of chronic diseases, such as diabetes, hypertension, and heart disease, due to factors such as inadequate access to nutritious food, lack of physical activity, and exposure to environmental toxins.
3. Cultural competence: Healthcare providers may not always be aware of the cultural beliefs and practices of marginalized communities, leading to misunderstandings or miscommunications that can result in poor health outcomes.
4. Language barriers: Limited English proficiency can make it difficult for individuals from marginalized communities to communicate effectively with healthcare providers, leading to misunderstandings or missed diagnoses.
5. Cost and financial burden: Many marginalized communities face financial barriers that prevent them from accessing quality healthcare, such as high deductibles, co-pays, and out-of-pocket expenses.
6. Lack of trust: Historical trauma and ongoing discrimination can lead to a lack of trust in the healthcare system among marginalized communities, making it more challenging for them to seek medical care when needed.
7. Limited availability of specialized care: Marginalized communities may have limited access to specialized healthcare services, such as mental health care, reproductive health care, or palliative care, due to factors such as location or availability of providers with the necessary cultural competence.
8. Health literacy: Limited literacy skills can make it difficult for individuals from marginalized communities to navigate the complex healthcare system and make informed decisions about their health.
