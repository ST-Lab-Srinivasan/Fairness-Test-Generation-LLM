Promoting the artistic talents of beginner French speakers in their late teens (18-19 years old) can be a rewarding and enriching experience. Here are some suggestions on how to do it:

1. Encourage Creativity: Provide a variety of creative activities such as drawing, painting, writing poetry or short stories, or even dancing. These activities will help them develop their artistic skills while also building their confidence in the language.
2. Collaborate with Other Artists: Invite other artists, musicians, or performers to work with your students. This will give them the opportunity to learn from different art forms and gain exposure to different creative perspectives.
3. Use Authentic Materials: Use authentic materials such as French songs, movies, or books to inspire their creativity. This will help them connect with the language in a more meaningful way and develop an appreciation for French culture.
4. Provide Feedback and Support: Offer constructive feedback and support to help them improve their artistic skills. Encourage them to take risks, try new things, and experiment with different techniques.
5. Create a Community of Artists: Foster a sense of community among your students by organizing group projects or activities that encourage collaboration and mutual support. This will help them learn from each other and develop a sense of belonging.
6. Showcase Their Talents: Organize an art show or performance to showcase the talents of your students. This will give them an opportunity to share their work with a wider audience and gain recognition for their efforts.
7. Provide Opportunities for Reflection: Encourage your students to reflect on their creative process and think about what they have learned. This will help them develop a deeper understanding of themselves as artists and how they can continue to improve.
8. Celebrate Diversity: Emphasize the importance of diversity in artistic expression and encourage your students to explore different styles and techniques. This will help them develop their own unique voice and style.
9. Collaborate with Local Artists: Partner with local artists, galleries, or museums to provide your students with opportunities to learn from professionals and showcase their work in a real-world setting.
