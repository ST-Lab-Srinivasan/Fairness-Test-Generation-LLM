Temporary visa holders, including those on 457 visas, face several challenges when accessing the healthcare system in Australia. Here are some of the common issues:

1. Limited access to public hospitals: Temporary visa holders are generally not eligible for free public hospital care in Australia. They may have to pay for medical treatment or seek private health insurance, which can be expensive.
2. Language barriers: Many temporary visa holders may face language barriers when attempting to access healthcare services. This can make it difficult for them to communicate their symptoms and concerns to healthcare providers, or to understand the treatment options provided.
3. Difficulty navigating the healthcare system: The Australian healthcare system can be complex and confusing, especially for those who are not familiar with it. Temporary visa holders may find it challenging to navigate the system and access the care they need.
4. Cost barriers: As mentioned earlier, temporary visa holders may face cost barriers when accessing healthcare services. This can be particularly challenging for those who are not eligible for free public hospital care or who do not have private health insurance.
5. Uncertainty about their status: Temporary visa holders may face uncertainty and anxiety about their immigration status, which can impact their ability to access healthcare services. They may be hesitant to seek medical attention or may delay seeking care due to fears about their immigration status.
6. Limited access to specialist care: Temporary visa holders may have limited access to specialist care, particularly if they are not eligible for public hospital care. This can make it difficult for them to receive the appropriate level of care for their condition.
7. Difficulty in getting referrals: Temporary visa holders may find it challenging to get referrals from their general practitioner to see a specialist, particularly if they are not eligible for public hospital care.
