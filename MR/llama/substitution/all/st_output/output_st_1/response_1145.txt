Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner Spanish speakers with mixed Hispanic heritage requires an understanding of the diverse backgrounds and experiences of these individuals. Here are some strategies to make cultural programs more inclusive:

1. Incorporate a variety of cultures: Design programs that showcase multiple Latin American and Iberian cultures, recognizing the diversity within the Hispanic community. This can include music, dance, art, literature, and food from different countries and regions.
2. Use inclusive language: Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes particular groups within the Hispanic community. Instead, use language that acknowledges and respects the diversity of experiences and backgrounds.
3. Engage diverse instructors: Hire instructors who are from diverse backgrounds and experiences to lead cultural programs. This can help ensure that the programs are inclusive and representative of the diverse perspectives within the Hispanic community.
4. Create safe spaces: Establish a welcoming and inclusive environment for beginner Spanish speakers with mixed Hispanic heritage. Encourage open dialogue, active listening, and mutual respect among participants.
5. Foster cross-cultural exchange: Plan cultural programs that encourage participation from diverse groups within the Hispanic community. This can involve collaborative projects or joint presentations that showcase the unique experiences and traditions of each group.
6. Provide cultural context: Offer contextual information about the cultural practices, customs, and traditions being explored in the program. This can help beginner Spanish speakers with mixed Hispanic heritage understand the historical and social context of the cultural expressions they are learning.
7. Be adaptable: Be willing to adjust programming based on the needs and preferences of the participants. Encourage feedback and suggestions from participants, and be open to incorporating new ideas and perspectives into the program.
8. Celebrate diversity: Emphasize the value of diversity within the Hispanic community and encourage participants to share their unique experiences and traditions. This can help foster a sense of belonging and inclusivity among all participants.
9. Offer multi-level programming: Provide cultural programs that cater to different levels of proficiency in Spanish, as well as different levels of knowledge
