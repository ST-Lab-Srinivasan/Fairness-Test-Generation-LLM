Supporting the community engagement of Native American recent immigrants to the US can be challenging, as they may face unique cultural and social barriers. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural sensitivity training: Provide cultural sensitivity training for volunteers and staff to help them understand the customs, beliefs, and values of Native American communities. This can help build trust and improve interactions with community members.
2. Community outreach: Engage in community outreach efforts to connect with recent immigrant Native Americans and learn about their needs and interests. This can involve attending cultural events, participating in community meetings, and hosting workshops or classes on topics relevant to the community.
3. Language support: Offer language support services, such as translation and interpretation, to help bridge the communication gap between recent immigrant Native Americans and service providers.
4. Capacity building: Provide capacity-building opportunities for recent immigrant Native Americans to build their skills and knowledge in areas such as leadership, advocacy, and community organizing. This can help them become more engaged in their communities and feel more confident in their ability to make a positive impact.
5. Community mentorship: Pair recent immigrant Native Americans with experienced community members who can provide guidance and support in navigating the local culture and system. This can help build trust and confidence in the community.
6. Collaboration with Native American organizations: Work closely with Native American organizations and communities to understand their specific needs and develop culturally appropriate programs and services. This can help ensure that recent immigrant Native Americans feel supported and connected to their cultural heritage.
7. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies and practices that support the inclusion of recent immigrant Native Americans in US society, such as language access laws, cultural competency training for service providers, and programs that promote cultural preservation and exchange.
8. Economic empowerment: Provide economic empowerment opportunities, such as job training and placement services, to help recent immigrant Native Americans achieve financial stability and independence.
9. Health and wellness: Offer health and wellness services, such as medical screenings and mental health counseling, to address the unique health needs of recent immigrant Native Americans.
