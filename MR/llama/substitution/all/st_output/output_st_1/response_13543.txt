Supporting the social inclusion of Nigerian partners can be a challenging task, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Education and awareness: Educating family members, friends, and community members about the importance of inclusivity and respect for people with disabilities can go a long way in supporting their social inclusion. Organizing workshops, seminars, and campaigns that promote awareness and understanding of disability issues can be helpful.
2. Accessibility: Ensuring that public spaces, buildings, and infrastructure are accessible to people with disabilities can help them participate fully in society. This includes installing ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms, as well as ensuring that sidewalks and paths are clear of obstacles.
3. Employment opportunities: Providing employment opportunities for Nigerian partners with disabilities can help them become more independent and self-sufficient. This can involve working with employers to create inclusive workplaces or providing job training and placement services.
4. Healthcare access: Ensuring that people with disabilities have access to quality healthcare is crucial for their overall well-being. Providing accessible healthcare facilities, trained healthcare professionals, and appropriate medical equipment can help address the specific health needs of this population.
5. Social activities: Organizing social activities, such as sports tournaments, art exhibitions, or cultural events, that include people with disabilities can help promote their social inclusion. These activities can provide opportunities for people to interact and form connections with others outside of their regular social circles.
6. Advocacy and policy change: Advocating for policies and practices that support the social inclusion of Nigerian partners with disabilities can have a significant impact on their lives. This can involve working with government agencies, civil society organizations, and other stakeholders to push for changes in laws, regulations, and cultural attitudes.
7. Support groups: Creating support groups for people with disabilities can provide a safe space for them to share experiences, receive emotional support, and connect with others who understand their challenges. These groups can also serve as a platform for advocacy and awareness-raising efforts.
