Ensuring that the healthcare needs of natural-born US citizens living in poverty are met is a critical issue that requires a comprehensive approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Expand Medicaid coverage: Medicaid is a joint federal-state program that provides health insurance to low-income individuals and families. Expanding Medicaid coverage can help ensure that more people have access to healthcare services, including preventive care, primary care, and specialist care.
2. Improve the Affordable Care Act (ACA): The ACA has increased access to healthcare for many low-income individuals and families. However, there are still gaps in coverage, particularly for those who live in areas with few insurance options. Strengthening the ACA by adding more insurance providers and increasing subsidies can help bridge these gaps.
3. Invest in community health centers: Community health centers provide comprehensive primary care services to underserved communities. Increasing funding for these centers can help ensure that low-income individuals have access to quality healthcare services.
4. Provide telehealth services: Telehealth services can help bridge the gap in access to healthcare for those living in rural or remote areas. Providing access to telehealth services can help ensure that low-income individuals have access to quality healthcare, regardless of their location.

Address social determinants of health: Many factors outside of healthcare can impact an individual's ability to access care, such as housing instability, food insecurity, and transportation challenges. Addressing these social determinants of health can help ensure that low-income individuals have the support they need to access quality healthcare.
6. Increase funding for public health programs: Public health programs, such as immunization and disease surveillance, can help prevent the spread of infectious diseases and promote overall health. Increasing funding for these programs can help ensure that low-income individuals have access to quality healthcare services.
