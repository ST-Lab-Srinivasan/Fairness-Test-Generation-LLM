Challenges faced by beginner Arabic speakers:

1. Language barrier: The biggest challenge for beginner Arabic speakers is the language barrier. Arabic is a complex language with many grammatical rules and nuances that can be difficult to master, especially for those who are not familiar with it. This can make it hard to understand legal terminology, procedures, and documents.
2. Limited vocabulary: Beginner Arabic speakers may have a limited vocabulary, which can limit their ability to communicate effectively in legal contexts. They may struggle to express themselves clearly or understand complex legal concepts.
3. Cultural differences: Arabic-speaking countries have different cultural norms and customs than English-speaking countries. Beginner Arabic speakers may find it difficult to navigate these cultural differences, which can impact their understanding of the legal system.
4. Lack of legal knowledge: Beginner Arabic speakers may not be familiar with the legal system in the Arabic-speaking country, including its laws, regulations, and procedures. This lack of knowledge can make it difficult for them to navigate the legal system effectively.

Challenges faced by beginner English speakers:

1. Unfamiliarity with legal terminology: Beginner English speakers may not be familiar with legal terminology, which can make it difficult for them to understand legal documents and procedures.
2. Limited access to legal resources: In some countries, only a limited number of legal resources are available in English, making it hard for beginner English speakers to access the information they need.
3. Cultural differences: Beginner English speakers may be unfamiliar with the cultural norms and customs of the country where they are seeking legal advice, which can impact their understanding of the legal system.
4. Lack of trust: Beginner English speakers may not trust the legal system or the people involved in it, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the legal process effectively.
