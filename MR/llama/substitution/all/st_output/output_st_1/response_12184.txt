Ensuring that the educational needs of civilian Native American students in the US are met requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges and barriers faced by this population. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Provide training for teachers on culturally responsive teaching practices, which recognize and value the cultural backgrounds of students. This can involve incorporating traditional Native American teachings and practices into the curriculum.
2. Language support: Offer language support services such as language translation, interpretation, and bilingual education programs to help civilian Native American students who may not speak English fluently.
3. Cultural competency training: Provide cultural competency training for teachers and other school staff to help them understand the cultural nuances of working with Native American students.
4. Indigenous knowledge integration: Incorporate indigenous knowledge and perspectives into the curriculum to help civilian Native American students feel seen and valued in their educational experiences.
5. Community partnerships: Develop partnerships with local Native American communities to provide additional support services, such as tutoring, mentoring, and cultural enrichment programs.
6. Family engagement: Engage with the families of civilian Native American students to build trust and support, and to ensure that their cultural practices and values are respected and valued in the educational setting.
7. Identity-based curriculum: Develop a curriculum that acknowledges and celebrates the identity and experiences of civilian Native American students, including their history, culture, and contributions to society.
8. Access to technology: Provide access to technology such as tablets or laptops for civilian Native American students who may not have access to these resources at home.
9. Address systemic barriers: Address systemic barriers such as funding disparities, inadequate resources, and lack of representation in decision-making processes that can hinder the educational success of civilian Native American students.
10. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies and practices that promote equity and inclusion in education, such as culturally responsive teaching, language support, and indigenous knowledge integration.
