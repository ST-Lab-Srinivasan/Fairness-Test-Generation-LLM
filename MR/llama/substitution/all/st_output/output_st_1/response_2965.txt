Creating more inclusive social policies for upper upper middle-income lifelong single individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges and needs of this group. Here are some potential strategies:

1. Acknowledge the complexity of their situation: Upper upper middle-income single individuals often face discrimination and stigma due to their financial status, age, or social isolation. Policies should recognize and address these challenges by promoting inclusivity and respect for diversity.
2. Provide affordable housing options: Housing costs can be a significant burden for single individuals, especially those with high incomes. Governments can offer subsidized housing programs, rent control measures, or incentives for developers to build more affordable housing units.
3. Encourage flexible work arrangements: Single individuals may face challenges balancing work and personal responsibilities due to their lack of a partner or family. Policies can promote flexible work arrangements, such as telecommuting, part-time work, or compressed workweeks, to help them manage their time more effectively.
4. Offer education and training opportunities: Many single individuals may feel isolated or disconnected from society due to a lack of social connections or educational opportunities. Governments can provide access to continuing education programs, vocational training, or community-based learning initiatives to help them build social capital and acquire new skills.
5. Promote social interaction and connection: Single individuals may face challenges forming and maintaining social connections due to their lack of a partner or family. Policies can encourage social interaction by providing opportunities for singles to connect with others, such as through volunteer work, social events, or support groups.
6. Address ageism in the workplace: Single individuals may face age-related discrimination in the workplace, which can limit their job prospects and earning potential. Policies can promote age diversity and inclusion in the workforce by offering training programs for employers and employees, or by implementing age-blind hiring practices.
7. Support intergenerational connections: Single individuals may have limited opportunities to connect with younger or older generations due to their lack of a family structure.
