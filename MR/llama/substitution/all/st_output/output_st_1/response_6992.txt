Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for early teen native Arabic speakers requires careful consideration of their unique needs and interests. Here are some suggestions:

1. Involve them in the planning process: Give young people a voice in shaping the celebration. Ask them what they would like to see or participate in, and involve them in decision-making processes. This will make them feel valued and included.
2. Use accessible language: Avoid using complex language or jargon that may be difficult for young people to understand. Use clear and simple language that is easy for everyone to follow.
3. Provide interactive activities: Early teens are at an age where they enjoy participating in hands-on activities. Incorporate games, crafts, and other interactive elements into the celebration to keep them engaged.
4. Celebrate their culture: Highlight the unique traditions and customs of their culture during the celebration. This can include music, dance, food, and other cultural elements that are meaningful to them.
5. Foster a sense of community: Encourage young people to connect with one another and build relationships. This can be done through group activities or simply by providing opportunities for them to socialize.
6. Be inclusive of different backgrounds: Make sure the celebration is inclusive of different cultures, religions, and identities. Avoid stereotyping or making assumptions about young people based on their identity.
7. Provide opportunities for reflection: Give young people time to reflect on their culture and traditions during the celebration. This can be done through discussions, writing exercises, or other activities that encourage them to think critically about their heritage.
8. Involve community members: Invite community members who are fluent in Arabic to participate in the celebration. This can include elders, teachers, or other respected individuals who can provide guidance and support.
9. Offer opportunities for creative expression: Encourage young people to express themselves through art, music, or other forms of creative expression. This can be a great way for them to connect with their culture and heritage.
