Creating inclusive community programs for individuals aged 90 and older, including those who are natural-born Canadian citizens, requires a thoughtful and well-planned approach. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive community programs:

1. Consult with Seniors: Involve seniors in the planning process by conducting surveys, focus groups, or one-on-one interviews to understand their needs, preferences, and concerns. This will help ensure that the program meets their expectations and is tailored to their unique requirements.
2. Offer a Variety of Activities: Develop a range of activities that cater to different interests and abilities. For example, offer fitness classes, art programs, book clubs, or cultural events. This will help ensure that there are opportunities for everyone to participate and engage in activities that they enjoy.
3. Provide Accessibility Features: Ensure that the program venue is accessible and has features such as ramps, elevators, and adaptive technology. This will enable individuals with mobility challenges to fully participate in the program.
4. Foster Intergenerational Relationships: Create opportunities for older adults to connect with younger generations through mentorship programs, intergenerational activities, or community service projects. This can help combat loneliness and isolation, while also fostering a sense of community and connection.
5. Encourage Volunteerism: Encourage older adults to volunteer their time and skills within the community. This can help them stay engaged, active, and connected to their community, while also providing valuable services to others.
6. Offer Transportation Support: Provide transportation support to ensure that individuals can easily access the program venue. This may involve partnering with local transportation providers or offering transportation services specifically for older adults.
7. Focus on Social Connection: Create opportunities for social connection and community building, such as potlucks, picnics, or game nights. This can help combat loneliness and isolation, while also fostering a sense of belonging and connection to the community.
