Promoting the economic empowerment of permanent resident native Arabic speaker communities is crucial for their well-being and social inclusion. Here are some strategies that can help achieve this goal:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs that cater to the needs of native Arabic speakers, including language classes, vocational training, and higher education opportunities. This will help them acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market and contribute to the economy.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage and support native Arabic speakers to start their own businesses or take over existing ones. Provide access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities to help them grow and succeed.
3. Job Creation: Work with local businesses and organizations to create job opportunities for native Arabic speakers. This can include hiring them as interpreters, translators, or cultural ambassadors, or providing them with jobs in fields such as healthcare, education, and government.
4. Microfinance Programs: Establish microfinance programs that provide small loans and other financial services to native Arabic speakers who want to start their own businesses or expand existing ones. This can help them overcome financial barriers and achieve economic stability.
5. Community Development: Invest in community development projects that benefit native Arabic speakers, such as building schools, healthcare facilities, and other infrastructure. This can help create jobs, improve living standards, and promote social cohesion.
6. Cultural Exchange Programs: Organize cultural exchange programs that bring together native Arabic speakers and other communities. This can help promote cross-cultural understanding, mutual respect, and economic cooperation.
7. Economic Empowerment through Technology: Leverage technology to empower native Arabic speakers economically. This can include providing them with access to digital tools and platforms that enable them to start their own businesses, sell their products and services online, or access global markets.
8. Government Support: Encourage government policies and programs that promote the economic empowerment of native Arabic speakers. This can include tax incentives, subsidies, and other forms of support that help them start and grow their businesses.
