Supporting the social inclusion of early teenagers in Nigeria can be achieved through various strategies that promote social interaction, self-esteem, and community engagement. Here are some ways to support the social inclusion of 13-15-year-old Nigerian youth:

1. Organize community events: Host events such as sports tournaments, music festivals, or art competitions that bring together teenagers from different backgrounds. These events can help build relationships and foster a sense of belonging among participants.
2. Create safe spaces for dialogue: Establish forums where teenagers can discuss issues that affect them without fear of judgment or discrimination. This can help build self-esteem, confidence, and mutual respect among participants.
3. Encourage mentorship: Pair older, responsible adults with younger teenagers to provide guidance, support, and positive role models. This can help young people develop life skills, navigate challenges, and build relationships with positive influences.
4. Foster cultural exchange programs: Organize exchange programs that bring together teenagers from different ethnic or cultural backgrounds. These programs can promote cross-cultural understanding, empathy, and respect for diversity.
5. Provide access to education and job training: Ensure that young people have access to quality education and job training opportunities. This can help them develop marketable skills, increase their self-esteem, and gain a sense of purpose and accomplishment.
6. Promote sports and recreational activities: Encourage teenagers to participate in sports or other recreational activities that promote teamwork, physical fitness, and social interaction. This can help build relationships and foster a sense of community among participants.
7. Support youth-led initiatives: Empower young people to take ownership of their social inclusion by supporting their initiatives and projects. This can help build their confidence, leadership skills, and sense of purpose.
8. Address discrimination and bullying: Implement strategies to address discrimination and bullying among teenagers. This can include workshops on empathy, conflict resolution, and respect for diversity.
