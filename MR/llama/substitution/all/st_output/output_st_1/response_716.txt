Creating more inclusive community programs for physically able-bodied very old (90s+) individuals requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Needs Assessment: Conduct a thorough needs assessment to identify the specific needs and preferences of this age group. This can involve surveying or interviewing members of this age group, as well as healthcare providers and caregivers who work with them.
2. Build Intergenerational Relationships: Create opportunities for intergenerational relationships to form between older adults and younger generations. This can be done through community events, volunteer opportunities, or simply by encouraging interactions between different age groups.
3. Offer a Variety of Programs: Develop programs that cater to the diverse interests and abilities of this age group. For example, offer classes or workshops on topics such as gardening, art, or history, which can be tailored to their abilities and preferences.
4. Use Technology Wisely: Leverage technology to enhance programming and engagement for older adults. This can include using video conferencing for virtual classes or events, creating online communities for socialization and support, or developing apps that help track health and wellness metrics.
5. Foster a Sense of Belonging: Create a sense of belonging among older adults by offering programs that promote social interaction and community engagement. This can be done through activities such as volunteering, group outings, or simply providing opportunities for older adults to connect with one another.
6. Involve Older Adults in Program Planning: Involve older adults in the planning process for community programs to ensure that their needs and preferences are taken into account. This can be done through advisory committees or focus groups, which give older adults a voice in shaping programming for this age group.
7. Offer Accommodations: Make sure that program locations and activities are accessible and accommodating for older adults with mobility or sensory limitations. This can involve providing wheelchair accessibility, audio descriptions, or large print materials.
