Ensuring that the voices of nominal Christians (those who identify as Christian but do not actively practice or participate in religious activities) are heard in decision-making processes can be challenging, as they may feel marginalized or overlooked within the larger Christian community. Here are some strategies to promote their inclusion:

1. Create inclusive spaces: Design decision-making processes that are accessible and inclusive for all Christians, regardless of their level of practice or participation. Use language and formats that are familiar and comfortable for nominal Christians, and make sure they have equal opportunities to participate.
2. Encourage representation: Ensure that nominal Christians are represented in decision-making bodies, such as church councils, committees, or other leadership positions. This can help ensure their voices are heard and valued within the community.
3. Foster dialogue and collaboration: Facilitate opportunities for nominal Christians to engage in open and respectful dialogue with more actively practicing Christians. Encourage mutual understanding and cooperation, recognizing that both groups have valuable perspectives and insights to offer.
4. Address power imbalances: Acknowledge any existing power imbalances within the Christian community and work to address them. This may involve challenging dominant narratives or structures that marginalize nominal Christians, and promoting more equitable decision-making processes.
5. Provide support and resources: Offer resources and support to nominal Christians who may feel disconnected or disengaged from the Christian community. This could include spiritual guidance, mentorship, or simply being present and available for conversation and connection.
6. Recognize the diversity of Christian identity: Acknowledge that Christian identity encompasses a wide range of experiences, beliefs, and practices. Encourage nominal Christians to share their unique perspectives and contributions, and avoid assumptions or stereotypes based on their level of practice.
7. Foster a culture of inclusion: Cultivate an atmosphere of inclusivity within the Christian community, where all voices are valued and heard. Encourage respectful dialogue, active listening, and openness to diverse perspectives.
