Supporting the social inclusion of beginner English speakers who are solo can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some ways to promote social inclusion:

1. Language exchange programs: Organize language exchange programs where beginner English speakers can practice speaking with more proficient speakers in a welcoming and supportive environment.
2. Social events: Host social events specifically designed for beginner English speakers, such as potlucks, game nights, or outdoor activities. These events can help build confidence and provide opportunities to practice speaking in a relaxed setting.
3. Online communities: Create online communities or forums where beginner English speakers can connect with others who are also learning the language. This can be especially helpful for those who may feel isolated or struggling to find support in their local area.
4. Language classes: Offer language classes specifically designed for beginner English speakers, which can provide a structured environment for learning and practicing the language while also promoting social interaction.
5. Volunteer opportunities: Provide volunteer opportunities that allow beginner English speakers to engage with the local community and practice their language skills in real-world settings, such as working at a local soup kitchen or participating in a community clean-up event.
6. Mentorship programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair beginner English speakers with more proficient speakers who can provide guidance and support in their language learning journey.
7. Cultural events: Organize cultural events that showcase the diversity of languages and cultures in the community, providing an opportunity for beginner English speakers to engage with others and practice their language skills in a fun and inclusive environment.
8. Mobile apps: Develop mobile apps that provide interactive language learning activities and games specifically designed for beginner English speakers, which can be accessed on-the-go and at any time.
9. Gamification: Incorporate gamification elements into language learning activities, such as points, badges, or leaderboards, to make the learning process more engaging and fun for beginner English speakers.
