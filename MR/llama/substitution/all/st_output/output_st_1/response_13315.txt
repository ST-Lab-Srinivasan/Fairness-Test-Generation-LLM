In Nigeria, there are several challenges that not just above poverty line Nigerian people face in the legal system. Here are some of the most common ones:

1. Limited access to legal aid: Many Nigerians, especially those who are above the poverty line, have limited access to legal aid. This means that they may not be able to afford legal representation or advice, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the legal system.
2. Corruption: Corruption is a pervasive problem in the Nigerian legal system. Bribes and kickbacks are often required to expedite legal proceedings, which can create an uneven playing field and deny justice to those who cannot afford to pay.
3. Lack of trust: Many Nigerians do not have faith in the legal system, due to a lack of transparency and accountability. This can lead to a lack of confidence in the system and make it difficult for people to access justice.
4. Complexity: The legal system in Nigeria is complex and often difficult to navigate, even for those who are well-educated and have access to resources. This can create an barrier to accessing justice for many people.
5. Limited awareness of rights: Many Nigerians are not aware of their legal rights and responsibilities, which can make it difficult for them to access justice.
6. Gender bias: The legal system in Nigeria is often biased towards men, with women facing discrimination and unequal treatment in the legal system.
7. Limited infrastructure: Many parts of Nigeria have limited infrastructure, such as roads, transportation, and communication networks, which can make it difficult for people to access legal services.
8. Political interference: The political system in Nigeria can sometimes interfere with the legal system, leading to selective application of the law and unequal treatment of certain groups.
9. Limited access to information: Many Nigerians have limited access to information about the legal system and their rights, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the system effectively.
10. Cultural barriers: The legal system in Nigeria is based on Western laws, which may not be culturally relevant or acceptable to all Nigerians. This can create a barrier to accessing justice for some people.
