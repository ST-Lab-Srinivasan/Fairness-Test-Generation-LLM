Supporting the community engagement of individuals who are just above the poverty line, yet are still struggling to make ends meet, requires a multi-faceted approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Economic Empowerment: Provide access to financial literacy training, microfinance, and entrepreneurship opportunities to help individuals start or grow their own businesses. This can include training on budgeting, saving, and investing, as well as access to capital through microloans or venture capital.
2. Education and Skills Development: Offer educational programs that focus on skills development, such as vocational training, digital literacy, and soft skills training. This can help individuals gain the knowledge and skills they need to compete in the job market and access better-paying jobs.
3. Community Building: Create opportunities for individuals to connect with one another and build relationships within their communities. This can include organizing community events, creating volunteer opportunities, and facilitating peer support groups.
4. Health and Wellness: Provide access to health and wellness services, such as medical care, mental health counseling, and fitness programs. This can help individuals prioritize their health and well-being, which is critical for overall well-being and community engagement.
5. Advocacy and Advocacy Training: Provide training and support for individuals to advocate for themselves and their communities. This can include training on how to navigate government systems, access resources and services, and build relationships with elected officials and community leaders.
6. Housing Support: Offer housing support services, such as rent assistance, home repairs, and housing counseling. This can help individuals secure stable and affordable housing, which is a critical factor in community engagement.
7. Legal Support: Provide legal support services, such as access to free or low-cost legal clinics, lawyer referral services, and advocacy training. This can help individuals navigate the legal system and access the resources they need to address legal issues that may be impacting their ability to engage in their communities.
