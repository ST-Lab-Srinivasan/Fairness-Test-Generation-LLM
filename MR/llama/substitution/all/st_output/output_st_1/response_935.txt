Addressing the specific needs of Native American recent immigrants to the US in education requires a nuanced approach that considers their unique cultural background, experiences, and challenges. Here are some strategies that educators and administrators can use to support these individuals:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Incorporate traditional Native American teachings and practices into the classroom curriculum to make it more relatable and engaging for students. This could include including stories, art, music, and other cultural elements that reflect Native American culture.
2. Building relationships: Foster positive relationships between Native American students and their teachers by acknowledging and respecting their cultural background. Encourage open communication and create a safe space for students to share their experiences and concerns.
3. Cultural sensitivity training: Provide training for educators on cultural sensitivity and how to work with Native American students who may have experienced trauma related to historical events such as forced assimilation, relocation, or cultural suppression.
4. Supporting language and cultural preservation: Encourage the use of Native American languages in the classroom and support language revitalization efforts. Provide resources for students to learn their native language and culture, and incorporate traditional teachings into the curriculum.
5. Incorporating traditional knowledge: Incorporate traditional Native American knowledge and practices into the classroom curriculum, such as traditional ecological knowledge, medicinal plants, or hunting and gathering skills.
6. Providing academic support: Offer academic support services specifically tailored to the needs of Native American students, such as tutoring, mentorship programs, or academic advising.
7. Creating a safe space: Create a safe and inclusive learning environment that acknowledges and respects the cultural background of Native American students. Encourage open communication and provide resources for students who may be struggling with cultural identity or other challenges.
8. Building partnerships: Collaborate with Native American communities, organizations, and tribal schools to develop educational programs that are responsive to their needs and address the specific challenges they face.
9. Providing professional development: Offer professional development opportunities for educators on how to work with Native American students, including cultural sensitivity training, curriculum design, and teaching strategies tailored to Native American learners.
