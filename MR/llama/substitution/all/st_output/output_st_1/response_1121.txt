Supporting the economic empowerment of mixed African-American individuals living in deep poverty requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the various barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs, including vocational training, entrepreneurship training, and digital literacy programs. This will help individuals acquire skills that are in demand in the job market and increase their earning potential.
2. Microfinance and Small Business Development: Offer microloans and other financial services to help individuals start or expand small businesses. This can help create jobs and generate income for the individual and their family.
3. Job Placement and Career Advancement: Provide job placement services and career advancement opportunities to help individuals find better-paying jobs and advance in their careers.
4. Addressing Systemic Barriers: Advocate for policies that address systemic barriers to economic empowerment, such as discrimination, lack of access to healthcare, and limited access to social services.
5. Community Building and Support: Foster a sense of community and support among individuals living in poverty. This can help reduce feelings of isolation and stigma, and provide emotional and practical support during difficult times.
6. Addressing Trauma and Mental Health: Provide mental health services and trauma-informed care to help individuals heal from the effects of poverty, discrimination, and violence.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that address the root causes of poverty and economic inequality, such as inadequate social safety nets, lack of affordable housing, and inadequate access to healthcare.
8. Economic Empowerment Programs: Implement programs that specifically aim to economically empower individuals living in poverty, such as cooperatives, savings clubs, and asset-building programs.
9. Technology Access: Provide access to technology, including smartphones, laptops, and internet connectivity, to help individuals access information, education, and job opportunities.
10. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to quality healthcare services, including preventive care, mental health services, and chronic disease management.
