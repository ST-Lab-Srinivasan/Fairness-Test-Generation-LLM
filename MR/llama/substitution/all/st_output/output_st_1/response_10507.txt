Creating more inclusive public policies for native-born US citizens with disabilities requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the social, cultural, and political barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help create more inclusive policies:

1. Consultation and inclusion of diverse voices: Ensure that the perspectives of individuals with disabilities and their communities are included in the policy-making process. This can be achieved by consulting with disability organizations, advocacy groups, and individuals with disabilities themselves.
2. Adopt a disability rights framework: Frame policies around the principles of disability rights, such as accessibility, equality, non-discrimination, and participation. This will help ensure that policies are designed to promote the full and equal participation of individuals with disabilities in society.
3. Focus on accessibility: Ensure that public spaces, services, and programs are accessible to individuals with disabilities. This includes physical accessibility, such as wheelchair ramps and elevators, as well as communication accessibility, such as Braille signage and audio descriptions.
4. Address systemic barriers: Identify and address the underlying systems and structures that perpetuate inequality and discrimination against individuals with disabilities. This may involve challenging ableist attitudes and practices within institutions and society at large.
5. Invest in inclusive education: Ensure that education is accessible and inclusive for all students, including those with disabilities. This includes providing accommodations and modifications to support individual needs, as well as challenging ableist attitudes and practices within educational institutions.
6. Promote employment opportunities: Create more job opportunities for individuals with disabilities by working with employers to provide reasonable accommodations and modifications in the workplace. This can include providing job coaching, adaptive technology, and flexible work arrangements.
7. Advocate for healthcare access: Ensure that healthcare services are accessible and inclusive for individuals with disabilities. This includes providing accessible waiting areas, examination rooms, and medical equipment, as well as training healthcare providers on disability-related issues.
8. Support independent living: Provide support and resources to help individuals with disabilities live independently in their communities. This may involve creating affordable and accessible housing options, as well as providing home modification and personal assistance
