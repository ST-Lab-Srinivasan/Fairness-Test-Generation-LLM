Creating more inclusive social policies for non-wealthy, non-millionaire, and non-lifelong single individuals requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Address income inequality: Implement progressive taxation policies to reduce wealth disparities and ensure that everyone has access to basic necessities like healthcare, education, and housing.
2. Promote affordable housing: Invest in affordable housing initiatives, such as rent control and subsidized housing programs, to ensure that everyone has a safe and secure place to live.
3. Strengthen social safety nets: Expand and improve unemployment insurance, disability benefits, and other forms of social support to help individuals and families weather financial crises.
4. Improve access to education and job training: Provide accessible and affordable education and job training programs to help individuals acquire new skills and improve their employability.
5. Encourage diversity and inclusion in the workplace: Implement policies that promote diversity, equity, and inclusion in hiring practices, pay scales, and promotion opportunities.
6. Support intergenerational wealth transfer: Introduce policies that facilitate the transfer of wealth from one generation to the next, such as inheritance taxes and trusts.
7. Foster community engagement: Encourage community involvement and volunteerism through incentives like tax credits or volunteer time off.
8. Advocate for better family-friendly policies: Push for policies that support working parents, such as paid family leave, flexible work arrangements, and affordable childcare options.
9. Address ageism: Challenge ageist attitudes and practices in society by promoting age diversity and inclusion in the workplace and beyond.
10. Encourage financial literacy and planning: Provide resources and support for individuals to make informed decisions about their finances, including budgeting, saving, and investing.
