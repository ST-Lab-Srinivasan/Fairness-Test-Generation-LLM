Creating more inclusive community events for native English speaker poor people requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to help make your events more accessible and welcoming:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the event location is wheelchair accessible, has accessible restrooms, and provides accommodations for people with disabilities. Provide clear signage and instructions for attendees who may need assistance.
2. Language support: Provide translation services or interpreters for attendees who may not speak English fluently. This can include language support for popular phrases, signs, and announcements.
3. Affordable tickets or admission fees: Offer affordable ticket prices or make the event free to ensure that everyone can attend, regardless of their financial situation. Consider offering sliding scale pricing or scholarships for those who cannot afford the full price.
4. Food and refreshments: Provide free or low-cost food and refreshments, such as water, juice, and snacks. This can help alleviate any financial burdens attendees may face when attending an event.
5. Childcare and transportation: Offer childcare services or provide information on local transportation options to ensure that attendees can bring their families without worrying about additional expenses.
6. Cultural sensitivity: Be mindful of the cultural backgrounds of the attendees and tailor the event accordingly. This can include incorporating music, art, or other elements from diverse cultures into the event.
7. Involve the community: Engage with local organizations and community groups to help promote the event and ensure that it is inclusive of all members of the community.
8. Accessibility in marketing materials: Ensure that marketing materials are accessible to people with disabilities by providing clear instructions, images, and videos. Use simple language and avoid using jargon or technical terms that may be difficult for some attendees to understand.
