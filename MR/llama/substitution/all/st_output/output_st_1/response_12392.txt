Ensuring that the housing needs of documented over 90-year-old individuals are met requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and circumstances. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Age-friendly design: Develop homes and communities that are accessible, adaptable, and easy to navigate for older adults. This can include features such as wide hallways, single-level living, and grab bars in the bathroom.
2. Housing subsidies: Provide financial assistance to help older adults pay for housing expenses, such as rent or mortgage payments. This can be done through programs like Section 8 housing vouchers or the National Housing Trust Fund.
3. Home repair grants: Offer grants or low-interest loans to help older adults make necessary repairs and modifications to their homes, such as installing ramps or widening doorways.
4. Supportive services: Provide supportive services like home health care, transportation, and meal delivery to help older adults maintain their independence and quality of life.
5. Age-friendly public transportation: Ensure that public transportation is accessible and affordable for older adults, with options like wheelchair-accessible buses and trains.
6. Affordable housing options: Develop and preserve affordable housing options specifically designed for older adults, such as senior apartments or cooperatives.
7. Intergenerational housing: Encourage the development of intergenerational housing communities that bring together people of different age groups, providing opportunities for socialization and mutual support.
8. In-home care: Provide in-home care services to help older adults with daily tasks like bathing, dressing, and managing medications.
9. Technology integration: Integrate technology into home design and daily life to enhance safety and independence, such as smart home devices, medical alert systems, and virtual reality tools for socialization and entertainment.
10. Community outreach: Engage with the community to raise awareness about the housing needs of older adults and to advocate for policies and programs that support their well-being.
