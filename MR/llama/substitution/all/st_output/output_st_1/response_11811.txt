Creating more inclusive social policies for long-term unemployed, recently divorced multiple times individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Employment Support: Provide training and job placement services specifically tailored to their needs. This may include vocational training, career counseling, and job search assistance.
2. Financial Assistance: Offer financial support, such as subsidized employment programs, micro-credit schemes, or one-time grants, to help them cover living expenses while they are searching for stable employment.
3. Housing Support: Provide temporary housing assistance, such as rent subsidies or temporary shelter, to help them get back on their feet. This can be especially important for those who have recently gone through a divorce and may be facing housing insecurity.
4. Mental Health Services: Offer mental health counseling and therapy sessions specifically designed for individuals who are dealing with the emotional challenges of unemployment, divorce, and multiple job losses.
5. Childcare Support: Provide childcare services or subsidies to help them balance work and family responsibilities, which can be particularly challenging when dealing with the stress of unemployment and divorce.
6. Education and Training: Encourage and support their pursuit of further education or training to enhance their employability and increase their earning potential.
7. Networking Opportunities: Facilitate networking events, mentorship opportunities, and professional connections to help them build a strong professional network and gain access to job leads and career advancement opportunities.
8. Legal Aid: Provide free or low-cost legal aid services to help them navigate the complexities of divorce proceedings, child custody arrangements, and other legal issues that may arise during this time.
9. Healthcare Support: Offer access to affordable healthcare services, including mental health counseling, physical therapy, and prescription assistance programs, to help them maintain their overall well-being during this challenging period.
10. Community Outreach: Organize community outreach events and workshops that provide information on available resources, support groups, and other services tailored to their needs
