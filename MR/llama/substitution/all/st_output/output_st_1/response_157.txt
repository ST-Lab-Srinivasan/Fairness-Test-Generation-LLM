As a designer, creating more inclusive public spaces for independent beginner French speakers requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the public space is accessible to people of all abilities by providing wheelchair ramps, Braille signage, and audio descriptions for visual content. This will enable individuals with disabilities to fully participate in the space.
2. Language Support: Provide language support services such as interpreters or language translation tools to help independent beginner French speakers communicate effectively. This can include sign language interpretation, live captioning, or pre-recorded videos with subtitles in multiple languages.
3. Cultural Sensitivity: Be sensitive to the cultural backgrounds and experiences of the individuals using the public space. This can involve incorporating elements of different cultures into the design, such as traditional clothing, music, or art. It's also important to be aware of cultural norms around communication and interaction.
4. Technology Integration: Incorporate technology that facilitates communication and interaction between individuals with different language proficiency levels. For example, video conferencing tools can help connect independent beginner French speakers with native French speakers for language practice and cultural exchange.
5. Gamification: Create interactive and engaging experiences that encourage language practice and cultural exchange. This can include games, quizzes, and other activities that are designed to be fun and accessible to a wide range of users.
6. Community Building: Foster a sense of community among independent beginner French speakers by creating opportunities for social interaction and collaboration. This can involve organizing events, workshops, or language exchange programs where individuals can connect with others who share similar interests and goals.
7. Personalized Feedback: Provide personalized feedback and support to independent beginner French speakers to help them improve their language skills. This can involve using AI-powered language learning tools that provide real-time feedback on pronunciation, grammar, and vocabulary usage.
8. Collaborative Spaces: Create collaborative spaces where individuals can work together on language learning projects. This can involve setting up co-working spaces with shared workstations, whiteboards, or other collaborative tools that facilitate language practice and cultural exchange.
