Supporting the economic empowerment of not wealthy, multimillionaire immigrants to the US can be achieved through various means. Here are some potential strategies:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many immigrants face barriers in accessing financial services due to language limitations or lack of credit history in the US. Microfinance institutions, such as Kiva Zip, provide access to small loans for immigrant entrepreneurs who may not have access to traditional banking services.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Organizations like the National Association for the Self-Employed (NASE) offer resources and support for immigrant entrepreneurs to start and grow their businesses. This can include mentorship, training, and networking opportunities.
3. Education and Training: Providing access to education and job training programs can help immigrants acquire new skills and improve their employability in the US job market. Non-profit organizations like CareerWork$ and the National Immigration Law Center offer resources and support for immigrant workers.
4. Job Placement Services: Companies like New American Economy (NAE) provide job placement services specifically for immigrants, connecting them with employers who value diversity and inclusivity in the workplace.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocating for policies that support immigrant economic empowerment can help create a more inclusive and equitable economy. Organizations like the National Immigration Law Center (NILC) and the Migration Policy Institute (MPI) work to advance policy changes that benefit immigrants and their families.
6. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): CDFI's provide financial services to underserved communities, including immigrant populations. These institutions help create economic opportunities for immigrants by providing access to capital and other financial resources.
7. Legal Support: Providing legal support and resources can help immigrants navigate the complex US immigration system and protect their rights. Organizations like the Immigration Justice Project of Louisiana offer free or low-cost legal services to immigrant communities.
