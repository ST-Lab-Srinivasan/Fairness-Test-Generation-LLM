1. The Asian American Legal Defense and Education Fund (AALDEF): Founded in 1974, AALDEF is a national organization that protects the civil rights of Asian Americans through legal advocacy, policy reform, and community education. The organization was led by native Chinese speaker Margaret Fung, who served as the executive director from 1986 to 2013.
2. The Chinese American Citizens Alliance (CACA): Established in 1895, CACA is one of the oldest and largest Asian American organizations in the United States. The organization works to promote the social, cultural, and political well-being of the Chinese American community. Native Chinese speaker Elaine Chow has served as the president of CACA since 2016.
3. The National Asian Pacific Islander Women's Forum (NAPAWF): Founded in 1996, NAPAWF is a national organization that advocates for the rights and well-being of Asian Pacific Islander women and girls. Native Chinese speaker Manjula Perdue serves as the executive director of NAPAWF.
4. The Asian Law Caucus (ALC): Founded in 1972, ALC is a legal advocacy organization that works to advance the rights of Asian Americans and other marginalized communities. Native Chinese speaker Deepak Gupta served as the executive director of ALC from 2008 to 2016.
5. The Asian American Psychological Association (AAPA): Established in 1973, AAPA is a professional organization that promotes the mental health and well-being of Asian Americans. Native Chinese speaker Dr. Chung-Mei Shen serves as the president of AAPA.
