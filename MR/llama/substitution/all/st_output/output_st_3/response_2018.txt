Supporting the social inclusion of average earners and moderate-income individuals is crucial for building a fair and equitable society. Here are some ways to promote their inclusion:

1. Progressive taxation: Implement tax policies that reduce wealth disparities by increasing taxes on the wealthy and large corporations, and using the revenue generated to fund social programs that benefit lower-income individuals and families.
2. Increase access to education and job training: Provide affordable education and job training opportunities to help average earners acquire new skills and advance in their careers. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and financial support for higher education.
3. Enact rent control policies: Implement rent control measures to prevent skyrocketing housing costs from pricing out moderate-income individuals and families. This can involve setting maximum rent increases each year, offering subsidies or vouchers for affordable housing, and investing in low-income housing projects.
4. Strengthen labor laws: Enact policies that protect workers' rights to fair compensation, paid leave, and safe working conditions. This can include measures like increasing the minimum wage, extending parental leave benefits, and enforcing workplace safety regulations.
5. Encourage community involvement: Foster a sense of community by investing in local initiatives that bring people together, such as parks, recreational facilities, and volunteer opportunities. This can help build social connections and promote a sense of belonging among moderate-income individuals and families.
6. Support cooperative ownership models: Encourage the growth of worker-owned cooperatives, consumer cooperatives, and other collectively owned businesses. These models can provide average earners with greater control over their workplaces and communities, while also promoting social and economic solidarity.
