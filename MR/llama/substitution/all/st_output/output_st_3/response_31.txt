1. Community Outreach and Engagement: Engage with the community members, listen to their stories, and document their history. This will help create a sense of ownership and involvement in the preservation process.
2. Cultural Education and Awareness: Provide educational programs and workshops that teach the community about their cultural heritage, including their music, dance, art, and traditions. This will help raise awareness and appreciation for their cultural practices.
3. Preservation of Historical Sites: Identify and preserve historical sites that are significant to the community, such as homes, churches, schools, and other landmarks. These sites serve as important cultural artifacts that can help tell the story of the community's history and heritage.
4. Support for Artists and Cultural Workers: Provide support for artists and cultural workers who are part of the mixed African-American community. This can include funding, mentorship, and other forms of assistance to help them develop their skills and showcase their work.
5. Community Events and Celebrations: Organize community events and celebrations that promote the cultural heritage of the mixed African-American community. These events can include festivals, parades, and other activities that bring the community together and celebrate their shared identity.

Partnerships with Other Organizations: Collaborate with other organizations, such as museums, historical societies, and cultural centers, to promote the cultural preservation of the mixed African-American community.
