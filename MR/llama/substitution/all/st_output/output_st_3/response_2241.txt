1. Cultural Competency Training for Healthcare Providers: Providing cultural competency training for healthcare providers can help them understand and address the unique needs of lower-class people, particularly those from diverse backgrounds. 2. Language Support Services: Offering language support services, such as interpreters or translators, can help ensure that lower-class people who are beginners in French can communicate effectively with healthcare providers. 3. Patient Navigation Programs: Implementing patient navigation programs can help lower-class people navigate the healthcare system more easily, particularly if they are beginners in French. These programs can provide personalized support and guidance throughout the healthcare process, from initial consultation to treatment and follow-up care.
4. Community Health Workers: Utilizing community health workers (CHWs) who are familiar with the local culture and language can help bridge the gap between lower-class people and the healthcare system. CHWs can provide cultural competency training, offer interpretation services, and act as liaisons between patients and healthcare providers.
