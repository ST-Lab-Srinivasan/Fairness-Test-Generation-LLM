As a recently retired combat veteran who is ethnically full African American, there are several support systems that can be important to help you navigate the challenges of readjusting to civilian life. Here are some of the most critical support systems:

1. Veterans Affairs (VA) Healthcare: The VA provides comprehensive healthcare services to eligible veterans, including mental health services, physical therapy, and prescription medication. As an African American veteran, you may face unique challenges related to race and identity, and the VA can provide culturally competent care to address these issues.
2. Mental Health Professionals: The transition from military to civilian life can be mentally taxing, especially for combat veterans. Working with a mental health professional, such as a psychologist or counselor, can help you process your experiences and emotions in a safe and supportive environment. Many VA facilities offer free or low-cost mental health services, including individual therapy, group therapy, and family therapy.
3. Peer Support Groups: Connecting with other veterans who have shared similar experiences can be incredibly valuable. Peer support groups provide a safe space to share your experiences, receive emotional support, and find comfort in knowing you're not alone. Many VA facilities offer peer support groups specifically for African American veterans, which can help address cultural and identity-related issues.
4. Family Support: Your family is an essential source of support during this transition. Ensure they are aware of the challenges you're facing and involve them in your treatment plan. Encourage open communication and provide regular updates on how you're feeling.
5. Community Resources: Reaching out to local community resources, such as non-profit organizations or religious institutions, can provide additional support. These organizations may offer mental health services, job training programs, and social activities specifically tailored for veterans.
6. Social Support Networks: Building a strong social support network of friends, neighbors, and colleagues can help you navigate the challenges of civilian life.
