Ensuring that the educational needs of infant females from low-middle-income families are met requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various factors, including access to education, quality of education, and socio-economic status. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education: Provide access to quality educational institutions, scholarships, and financial aid programs to ensure that low-income families can afford to send their daughters to school. Governments, NGOs, and private organizations can collaborate to establish schools and provide financial support to disadvantaged communities.
2. Quality of Education: Ensure that educational institutions have well-trained teachers, up-to-date curricula, and adequate infrastructure. This can involve providing ongoing professional development opportunities for teachers, updating teaching methods and materials, and investing in school infrastructure.
3. Socio-Economic Status: Address the socio-economic factors that hinder girls' education, such as poverty, cultural beliefs, and social norms. This can involve empowering communities to recognize the importance of education for girls and providing support systems to help families overcome financial barriers.
4. Gender Equality: Promote gender equality by addressing societal attitudes and practices that hinder girls' access to education. This can involve advocacy campaigns, community engagement programs, and policy reforms to address issues such as early marriage, discrimination, and violence against women and girls.
5. Life Skills Education: Provide girls with life skills education, including health and nutrition, financial literacy, and entrepreneurship training. This can help them develop the knowledge and skills necessary to lead independent lives and contribute to their communities.
6. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that connect girls with successful women in their communities, providing them with role models and support systems. This can involve pairing girls with female mentors who can offer guidance, advice, and encouragement.
7. Menstrual Hygiene Management: Provide girls with accurate information about menstrual hygiene management and provide access to sanitary products. This can help reduce absenteeism and dropout rates among girls due to menstruation-related issues.
