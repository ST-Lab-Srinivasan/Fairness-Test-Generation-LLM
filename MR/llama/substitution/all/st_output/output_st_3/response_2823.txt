Addressing the Needs of Not Disabled Mixed Asian Heritages Individuals in Disaster Response: A Call to Inclusivity

Introduction:
In the aftermath of a disaster, it is essential to recognize that not all individuals are equally affected. Mixed Asian heritage individuals, like any other marginalized group, may face unique challenges during disaster response and recovery. This article aims to highlight the specific needs of not disabled mixed Asian heritage individuals in disaster response and provide recommendations for inclusive practices.

Needs and Challenges:
Mixed Asian heritage individuals may face various challenges during disaster response, including:

1. Cultural stigma: Members of marginalized communities, including mixed Asian heritage individuals, may experience cultural stigma due to their race or ethnicity. This can lead to feelings of isolation and exclusion during disaster response efforts.
2. Language barriers: Limited English proficiency or language barriers can hinder communication between mixed Asian heritage individuals and disaster responders, leading to misunderstandings or missed opportunities for support.
3. Accessibility issues: Inaccessible evacuation routes, shelters, or relief distribution centers can create significant challenges for individuals with mobility impairments or other accessibility needs.
4. Lack of representation: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may feel underrepresented in disaster response efforts, leading to a lack of understanding and empathy from responders.
5. Unique needs: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may have specific needs related to their cultural background or identity, such as access to traditional foods or religious accommodations, which may not be met during disaster response efforts.
