As a retired veteran, there are several support systems that can be important for a combat-exposed, natural-born US citizen. These include:

1. Healthcare Services: Retired veterans may require ongoing medical care due to service-connected disabilities or injuries. The US Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) provides access to healthcare services, including mental health treatment, through its healthcare system.
2. Disability Compensation: Combat-exposed veterans may be eligible for disability compensation through the VA's Disability Compensation program. This program provides financial support to veterans who have experienced service-connected injuries or illnesses.
3. Vocational Rehabilitation: The VA offers vocational rehabilitation programs to help veterans transition back into civilian life. These programs provide training, education, and career counseling to help veterans find employment.
4. Mental Health Services: Combat-exposed veterans may experience mental health issues such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), depression, or anxiety. The VA offers mental health services, including counseling and therapy, to help veterans manage these conditions.
5. Support Groups: Joining a support group can provide a sense of community and connection for combat-exposed veterans. These groups offer a safe space to share experiences, receive emotional support, and connect with others who understand the challenges of military service.
6. Financial Assistance: The VA provides financial assistance to eligible veterans through programs such as the GI Bill, which can help cover education costs or provide a monthly stipend.
7. Housing Support: The VA offers housing support services, including home loans and housing assistance, to help veterans find affordable housing.
8. Employment Resources: The VA provides employment resources, such as job fairs and resume-building workshops, to help veterans find employment opportunities.
9. Legal Services: The VA offers legal services to help veterans with legal issues related to their military service.
