Supporting the civic engagement of immigrant and recently arrived binational people in the US is crucial for their integration and participation in American society. Here are some ways to promote civic engagement among this group:

1. Language classes and citizenship preparation: Offer language classes and citizenship preparation courses specifically tailored for immigrants, focusing on the skills and knowledge required for successful civic engagement.
2. Voter registration drives: Organize voter registration drives within immigrant communities to ensure they have access to the ballot and can exercise their right to vote.
3. Civic education workshops: Host workshops that educate immigrants about the US political system, voting processes, and how to get involved in their local communities.
4. Leadership development programs: Create leadership development programs that provide training and mentorship opportunities for immigrant leaders to build their capacity to engage in civic life.
5. Community events: Organize community events that promote civic engagement, such as town halls, public forums, and cultural festivals, where immigrants can connect with other members of the community and learn about local issues.
6. Advocacy and policy work: Work with immigrant communities to advocate for policies that support their full participation in US society, such as immigration reform, language access, and voting rights.
7. Building partnerships: Collaborate with other organizations and institutions that serve immigrant communities, such as community centers, religious organizations, and schools, to amplify efforts and reach a wider audience.
8. Technology-based interventions: Utilize technology platforms to provide civic engagement resources and opportunities to immigrants, such as online voter registration, virtual town halls, and digital civic education courses.
9. Capacity building for immigrant-led organizations: Provide technical assistance and financial support to immigrant-led organizations to help them build their capacity to engage in civic life and advocate for their communities' interests.
10. Creating safe spaces: Establish safe and inclusive spaces within immigrant communities where individuals can discuss politics, share experiences, and build relationships without fear of discrimination or marginalization.
