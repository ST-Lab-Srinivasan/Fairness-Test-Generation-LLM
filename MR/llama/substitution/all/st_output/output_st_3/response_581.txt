Independent active-duty people often face unique challenges when it comes to pursuing their educational aspirations. Here are some ways that educational institutions and organizations can support these individuals:

1. Flexible learning options: Offer flexible learning options such as online courses, part-time programs, or hybrid models that combine online and in-person learning. This can help independent active-duty people balance their education with their military commitments.
2. Military-friendly admissions processes: Have a streamlined admissions process that takes into account the unique needs of independent active-duty students, such as frequent moves and deployment schedules.
3. Financial aid and scholarships: Provide financial aid and scholarships specifically for independent active-duty students to help them cover the costs of tuition and other expenses associated with higher education.
4. Military training credit: Offer military training credit to independent active-duty students, recognizing the valuable skills and knowledge they have acquired through their military service.
5. Prior learning assessment: Recognize prior learning and experience gained through military service by offering assessments and credit for relevant courses or certifications.
6. Military mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that connect independent active-duty students with experienced military professionals who can offer guidance and support throughout their academic journey.
7. On-campus resources: Provide on-campus resources such as military-specific support services, academic advising, and career counseling to help independent active-duty students navigate the challenges of higher education.
8. Transfer credit policies: Have flexible transfer credit policies that allow independent active-duty students to transfer credits from previous institutions or military training programs, reducing the time it takes to complete their degree.
9. Military spouse and family benefits: Offer benefits specifically for military spouses and families, such as tuition waivers or scholarships, to help them pursue their educational aspirations without facing financial burdens.
10. Outreach and support: Engage in outreach efforts to raise awareness about the educational opportunities available to independent active-duty students and provide ongoing support throughout their academic journey.
