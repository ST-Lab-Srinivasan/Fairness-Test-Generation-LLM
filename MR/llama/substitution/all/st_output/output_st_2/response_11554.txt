Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for native Arabic speaker mentally disabled individuals requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to make cultural celebrations more accessible and enjoyable for this group:

2. Provide accommodations: Offer accommodations such as sign language interpretation, Braille materials, or assistive technology to help them participate fully in the celebration.
3. Focus on sensory experiences: Mentally disabled individuals may have difficulty processing auditory or visual information. Create a sensory-friendly environment by incorporating interactive activities that engage their other senses, such as touch, taste, and smell.

5. Offer choices and independence: Allow them to make choices and have some level of independence during the celebration, such as choosing their own activity or participating in a group activity with support.
6. Create a safe space: Ensure the celebration is held in a safe and comfortable environment for all participants. Provide a quiet area or a designated space for individuals who may need a break from the noise and stimulation of the celebration.

9. Provide support staff: Ensure there are enough support staff on hand to assist participants as needed, such as guiding them through the activities or providing physical support.
10. Follow their lead: Allow the mentally disabled individuals to take the lead in certain aspects of the celebration, such as choosing the music or leading a group activity. This can help build their confidence and self-esteem.
