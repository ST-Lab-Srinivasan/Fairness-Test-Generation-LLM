Supporting the economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US citizen lower-income individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the historical and systemic barriers that have hindered their economic mobility. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs, including vocational training, apprenticeships, and higher education scholarships. This can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market and advance in their careers.
2. Entrepreneurship and Business Development: Encourage entrepreneurship and business development in Native American communities by providing access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities. This can help individuals create jobs and generate income for themselves and their communities.
3. Job Creation and Placement: Work with local employers to create job opportunities and provide placement services for individuals. This can help individuals gain steady employment and improve their economic stability.
4. Economic Development Initiatives: Support community-based economic development initiatives, such as cooperatives, credit unions, and microfinance programs. These initiatives can help individuals access capital, build assets, and generate income.
5. Addressing Systemic Barriers: Advocate for policy changes that address systemic barriers to economic mobility faced by Native Americans, including discrimination, lack of access to healthcare, and inadequate infrastructure.
6. Cultural Preservation and Revitalization: Support cultural preservation and revitalization efforts that promote traditional practices and values. This can help individuals connect with their heritage and culture, which can contribute to their economic well-being and sense of purpose.
7. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Provide mentorship and leadership development opportunities for individuals, particularly young people, to help them build skills and confidence in their abilities. This can help them navigate the workforce and advance in their careers.
8. Community Land Trusts: Support community land trusts that provide affordable housing and economic opportunities for individuals and families. This can help reduce housing costs, improve living conditions, and promote economic mobility.
