Supporting the social inclusion of devout practicing Hindu beginner English speakers involves creating an environment where they feel welcome and valued. Here are some ways to achieve this:

1. Language Support: Provide language support through classes, workshops, or one-on-one sessions to help them improve their English speaking skills.
2. Cultural Sensitivity: Be sensitive to their cultural beliefs and practices, and avoid making assumptions or imposing your own cultural values on them.
3. Inclusive Activities: Organize inclusive activities that cater to their interests and needs, such as religious festivals, cultural events, or community service projects.
4. Access to Resources: Provide access to resources such as books, articles, or websites that can help them learn more about Hinduism and its practices.
5. Mentorship: Offer mentorship opportunities with experienced practitioners who can guide them in their spiritual journey and provide support in navigating cultural differences.
6. Building Communities: Encourage the creation of communities or groups where they can connect with other like-minded individuals, share experiences, and learn from each other.
7. Education and Awareness: Educate the wider community about Hinduism and its practices, dispelling any misconceptions or stereotypes that may exist.
8. Accommodations: Where necessary, accommodate their religious practices in workplaces, schools, or other public spaces to ensure they feel included and respected.
9. Respectful Communication: Communicate with them in a respectful and sensitive manner, avoiding language or behavior that may be offensive or alienating.
10. Celebrations: Celebrate their religious festivals and traditions, and invite the wider community to participate and learn about their cultural practices.

By implementing these strategies, you can help create a more inclusive environment for devout practicing Hindu beginner English speakers in your community.
