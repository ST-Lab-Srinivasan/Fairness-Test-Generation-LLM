As a therapist specializing in couples counseling, I understand that maintaining a healthy and fulfilling marriage takes effort and dedication, especially for binary not long-time married couples. Here are some ways to support the mental health of such couples:

1. Communicate openly and honestly: Encourage your clients to communicate their thoughts, feelings, and needs with each other in an open and honest manner. This can help prevent misunderstandings and resentments that can build up over time.
2. Practice empathy and active listening: Teach your clients the skills of empathy and active listening, which involve understanding each other's perspectives, emotions, and needs. This can help them connect on a deeper level and feel more understood and validated by each other.
3. Prioritize quality time together: Encourage your clients to prioritize spending quality time together, away from distractions and responsibilities. This can help them reconnect and strengthen their bond.
4. Address conflicts head-on: Teach your clients how to address conflicts in a constructive and respectful manner. This can involve identifying the issue, expressing feelings and needs, and working together to find a solution.
5. Encourage individual growth: Encourage each partner to pursue their own interests and hobbies outside of the relationship. This can help them maintain their sense of self and prevent codependency.
6. Practice self-care: Teach your clients the importance of practicing self-care and prioritizing their own emotional well-being. This can involve engaging in activities that bring them joy, managing stress, and setting boundaries.
7. Seek professional help when needed: If a couple is struggling with mental health issues, such as depression or anxiety, it's important to seek professional help from a licensed therapist. A therapist can provide the tools and support needed to manage these issues and improve communication within the relationship.
