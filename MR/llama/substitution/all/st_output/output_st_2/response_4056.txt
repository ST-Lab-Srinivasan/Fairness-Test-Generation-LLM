Having diverse perspectives in leadership roles, including those from right-leaning civilian people, can bring a range of benefits to an organization. Here are some of the advantages of having a diverse group of leaders with different backgrounds and viewpoints:

1. Improved decision-making: When leaders have diverse perspectives, they are more likely to consider a wider range of options and make more informed decisions. This can lead to better outcomes for the organization and its stakeholders.
2. Enhanced creativity: Diverse perspectives can stimulate creativity and innovation, as leaders are exposed to new ideas and approaches. This can help organizations stay ahead of the competition and adapt to changing circumstances.
3. Better representation: Having leaders from diverse backgrounds can ensure that the organization better represents its customers, clients, and stakeholders. This can lead to more effective communication and a greater sense of inclusivity.
4. Increased empathy: When leaders have personal experiences and perspectives different from those of their colleagues, they are more likely to develop empathy and understanding for others. This can lead to better relationships within the organization and with external stakeholders.
5. Broader networks: Leaders with diverse backgrounds may have a wider network of contacts and connections, which can be leveraged to benefit the organization. This can lead to access to new opportunities, resources, and expertise.
6. Improved problem-solving: Diverse perspectives can help leaders approach problems from multiple angles, leading to more effective and efficient problem-solving.
7. Enhanced adaptability: When organizations have leaders with diverse perspectives, they are better equipped to adapt to changing circumstances and stay competitive in their respective industries.
8. Better risk management: Diverse perspectives can help identify potential risks and lead to more effective risk management strategies.
9. Increased credibility: When organizations have leaders with diverse backgrounds, they may be seen as more credible and trustworthy by stakeholders, particularly in industries where diversity is valued.
10. Improved employee morale: When employees see leaders who reflect their own experiences and perspectives, they are more likely to feel valued and engaged, leading to improved morale and productivity.
