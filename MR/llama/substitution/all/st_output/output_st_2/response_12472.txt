2. Limited access to Medicare: While Medicare provides free or low-cost healthcare to eligible Australians, temporary visa holders may not be covered under this system. They may need to pay out-of-pocket for medical expenses, which can be a significant burden for those on a limited budget.

Difficulty navigating the healthcare system: Temporary visa holders may find it challenging to navigate the complex healthcare system in Australia, particularly if they are not familiar with the language or culture. 4. Limited access to specialist care: Temporary visa holders may face difficulty accessing specialist care, as they may not be able to see a specialist without a referral from a general practitioner. This can be particularly challenging if they require ongoing treatment or management of a chronic condition.
