Creating more inclusive public policies for recently immigrated Indigenous people with disabilities in the United States requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the unique challenges and barriers they face. Here are some recommendations for creating more inclusive public policies:

1. Consultation and Collaboration: Involve Indigenous communities and organizations in the policy-making process through consultation and collaboration. This can help ensure that policies are culturally appropriate and responsive to the needs of Indigenous people with disabilities.
2. Disability Inclusion: Ensure that disability inclusion is integrated into all aspects of public policy, including immigration policies, education policies, and healthcare policies. This can be achieved by implementing disability-inclusive language in policy documents, providing accommodations for Indigenous people with disabilities, and ensuring access to accessible facilities and services.
3. Cultural Competence: Develop cultural competence training programs for government employees and service providers to better understand the unique needs and experiences of Indigenous people with disabilities. This can help ensure that policies are developed with a culturally responsive lens.
4. Access to Healthcare: Ensure access to accessible and culturally appropriate healthcare services for Indigenous people with disabilities, including mental health services. This can be achieved by providing culturally competent healthcare providers, accessible facilities, and language-appropriate communication.
5. Education and Employment: Provide inclusive education programs that address the unique needs of Indigenous students with disabilities, including cultural and linguistic accommodations. Additionally, provide job training and placement services that are tailored to the needs of Indigenous people with disabilities.
6. Housing and Transportation: Ensure that affordable and accessible housing and transportation options are available to Indigenous people with disabilities. This can be achieved by providing subsidies for accessible housing, improving public transportation infrastructure, and promoting the use of accessible transportation options such as wheelchair-accessible vehicles.
7. Language Access: Ensure that language access services are available to Indigenous people with disabilities, including sign language interpretation and translation services. This can help ensure that policies are accessible to individuals who may not speak English fluently.
