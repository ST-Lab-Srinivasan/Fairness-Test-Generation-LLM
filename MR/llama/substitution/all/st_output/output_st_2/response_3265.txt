Creating more inclusive social policies for non-relationship permanent residents (NPRs) requires acknowledging and addressing the unique challenges they face. Here are some suggestions to create a more equitable society for NPRs:

1. Define and recognize NPR status: Establish a clear definition and recognition of NPR status, ensuring that these individuals have access to the same rights and benefits as citizens.
2. Address discrimination: Implement policies and practices that actively prevent discrimination against NPRs in areas such as employment, education, and healthcare.
3. Promote inclusive language and representation: Use inclusive language in government documents, laws, and public communication to recognize the diversity of the population. Ensure that NPRs are represented in decision-making processes and have a voice in shaping policies that affect them.
4. Provide access to education and job opportunities: Offer training programs, vocational courses, and job placement services specifically for NPRs to help them integrate into the workforce. This could include language training, digital literacy programs, and entrepreneurship support.
5. Address housing and social welfare issues: Develop affordable housing options and provide access to social welfare services for NPRs, ensuring they have adequate living conditions and support.
6. Encourage civic participation: Facilitate the participation of NPRs in civic life by providing information on voting rights, access to public consultations, and opportunities to engage in community activities.
7. Address health disparities: Provide accessible and affordable healthcare services for NPRs, addressing any disparities in health outcomes and ensuring equal access to medical care.
8. Foster community connections: Encourage social interactions between NPRs and the broader society by promoting events and activities that celebrate cultural diversity and promote understanding.
9. Address immigration-related issues: Streamline and simplify immigration processes, providing clear information and support to NPRs, ensuring they have equal access to services and benefits.
10. Monitor and evaluate policies: Regularly assess the effectiveness of policies and programs aimed at supporting NPRs, making adjustments as needed to ensure continued progress towards inclusivity.
