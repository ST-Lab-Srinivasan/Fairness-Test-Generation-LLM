Supporting the economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US citizens who are long-term unemployed requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Job Training and Placement Programs: Provide job training and placement programs tailored to the needs of Native Americans, including cultural sensitivity training for employers and employees. These programs can help individuals gain the skills and knowledge necessary to compete in the job market.
2. Entrepreneurship and Small Business Development: Encourage entrepreneurship and small business development by providing access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities within Native American communities.
3. Apprenticeships and On-the-Job Training: Offer apprenticeships and on-the-job training programs in various industries, such as construction, healthcare, and technology. These programs can provide individuals with hands-on experience and skills that are in demand in the job market.
4. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Develop mentorship and leadership development programs that pair experienced professionals with Native American individuals who are seeking to advance their careers. This can help build confidence, skills, and networks within the community.
5. Addressing Systemic Barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers that prevent Native Americans from accessing economic opportunities, such as inadequate transportation, limited access to education and job training, and discrimination.
6. Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFI): Support the development of Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs) that provide financing and other financial services to underserved communities, including Native American communities.
7. Tribal-State Partnerships: Foster tribal-state partnerships that promote economic development and job creation within Native American communities. These partnerships can help leverage resources, expertise, and opportunities for the benefit of both tribes and states.
8. Infrastructure Development: Invest in infrastructure development, such as transportation systems, broadband access, and energy infrastructure, to support economic growth and job creation within Native American communities.
