Ensuring the healthcare needs of individuals with extreme or moderate levels of a genetic condition require a comprehensive and multidisciplinary approach. Here are some strategies to ensure their healthcare needs are met:

1. Multidisciplinary team: Assemble a team of healthcare professionals, including geneticists, pediatricians, psychologists, social workers, and special education experts. This team will work together to provide holistic care for the individual.
2. Early intervention: Identify the condition at an early age and begin appropriate interventions as soon as possible. This may include physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy, and mental health services.
3. Ongoing monitoring: Regularly monitor the individual's condition to adjust treatments and interventions as needed. This may involve frequent appointments with healthcare professionals, as well as ongoing communication between team members.
4. Patient advocacy: Ensure that the individual has a voice in their care and that their needs are being met. This may involve involving the patient or their family in decision-making processes and providing emotional support.
5. Community resources: Connect the individual with local organizations and resources that provide support for people with genetic conditions. This may include support groups, advocacy organizations, and recreational activities.
6. Access to specialized care: Ensure that the individual has access to specialized care and services, such as genetic counseling, rehabilitation therapy, and assistive technology.
7. Financial assistance: Provide financial assistance for individuals and their families who may struggle to cover the costs of healthcare and related expenses. This may involve working with insurance providers, non-profit organizations, or government agencies.
8. Education and awareness: Educate the individual and their family about their condition, its management, and available resources. This may involve providing information on genetic testing, treatment options, and support services.
