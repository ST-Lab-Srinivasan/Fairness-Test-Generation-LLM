6. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies that promote the cultural integration of Native American individuals with mental disabilities, such as culturally competent healthcare services and inclusive education systems.
7. Collaboration with tribal organizations: Work closely with tribal organizations to develop and implement programs that address the unique needs of this population.
8. Addressing systemic barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers, such as lack of access to healthcare or discrimination, that can hinder the cultural integration of Native American individuals with mental disabilities.
9. Cultural practices and rituals: Encourage the use of traditional cultural practices and rituals in mental health treatment, where appropriate and respectful, to help individuals maintain their cultural identity.

By implementing these strategies, we can promote the cultural integration of mentally disabled Native recent immigrants to the US and help them achieve better mental health outcomes.
