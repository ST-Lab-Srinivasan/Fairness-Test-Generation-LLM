2. Bilingual Education Programs: Offer bilingual education programs for immigrant students who may not speak English fluently. This can include dual-language programs, where students receive instruction in both English and their native language.
3. ESL (English as a Second Language) Programs: Provide ESL programs for immigrant students who need to improve their English language skills. These programs can be tailored to the students' language proficiency levels and can include additional support services like tutoring and mentoring.
4. Adaptive Curriculum: Develop an adaptive curriculum that takes into account the unique experiences and challenges faced by immigrant students. 5. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair immigrant students with successful and supportive individuals who can provide guidance, advice, and encouragement. This can help build confidence and social connections, which are critical for academic success.
