3. Access to resources: Provide access to resources such as medical professionals, counseling services, and disability accommodations that can help individuals with genetic conditions manage their condition and pursue their professional aspirations.
4. Flexible work arrangements: Offer flexible work arrangements such as telecommuting, flexible hours, or job modifications to help individuals with genetic conditions balance their work and personal life.
5. Accommodation in the workplace: Provide accommodations in the workplace such as wheelchair ramps, elevators, or adaptive technology to ensure equal access to opportunities and resources for individuals with genetic conditions.
