Immigrants, particularly those who are recently arrived in the United States, face numerous challenges when seeking medical care and navigating the healthcare system. Some of the common challenges faced by non-deep in poverty immigrant recent immigrants to the US include:

1. Language barriers: Many immigrants may not speak English fluently, which can create difficulties in communicating with healthcare providers and understanding medical instructions.
2. Cultural differences: Immigrants may have different cultural beliefs and practices that can affect their healthcare experiences, such as preferences for traditional or alternative medicine.
3. Lack of knowledge about the healthcare system: New immigrants may be unfamiliar with the US healthcare system and its structures, making it difficult to navigate and access care.
4. Limited access to health insurance: Many immigrants may not have access to health insurance due to their immigration status or lack of affordability.
5. Cost barriers: Even if they have health insurance, immigrants may face financial barriers to accessing medical care due to high deductibles, co-pays, and other out-of-pocket expenses.
6. Discrimination and stigma: Immigrants may experience discrimination or stigma based on their race, ethnicity, or immigration status, which can affect their willingness to seek medical care.
7. Difficulty finding culturally competent providers: Many immigrants may have difficulty finding healthcare providers who are familiar with their cultural practices and beliefs, leading to mistrust or dissatisfaction with the care they receive.
8. Lack of continuity of care: Immigrants may experience disruptions in their medical care due to changes in their immigration status or difficulty maintaining consistent access to healthcare providers.
9. Limited availability of specialized care: Some immigrant populations may have limited access to specialized medical care, such as cardiologists or oncologists, due to geographic or financial barriers.
10. Mental health challenges: Immigrants may experience stress and mental health challenges related to their migration experiences, including trauma, loss, and separation from loved ones.
