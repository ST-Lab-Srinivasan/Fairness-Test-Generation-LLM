Having diverse perspectives from natural-born US citizen Native American people in leadership roles can bring numerous benefits to society, including:

1. Cultural understanding and sensitivity: Native American leaders can provide a unique perspective on cultural issues, helping to bridge the gap between different communities and promote mutual respect.
2. Representation and inclusion: By having representation in leadership positions, Native Americans can feel seen and heard, and their voices can be amplified to address issues that affect them disproportionately.
3. Historical context and knowledge: Native American leaders can provide insight into the historical experiences of their communities, helping to inform decision-making and policy development.
4. Traditional knowledge and practices: Native American leaders can bring traditional knowledge and practices into leadership roles, contributing to innovative problem-solving and sustainable development.
5. Role modeling and inspiration: Seeing individuals from underrepresented groups in leadership positions can inspire young people from those communities to pursue leadership roles themselves, fostering a more inclusive and diverse leadership pipeline.
6. Broader perspectives and creative problem-solving: A diversity of perspectives can lead to more creative and innovative solutions to complex problems, as leaders are exposed to different ways of thinking and approaching challenges.
7. Enhanced decision-making: When diverse perspectives are brought to the table, decision-making processes can be more robust and well-rounded, leading to better outcomes for everyone involved.
8. Community engagement and trust: Native American leaders who are invested in their communities can help build trust and strengthen relationships between government and community members, leading to more effective public policy and programs.
9. International relations and diplomacy: As the US continues to engage with indigenous peoples globally, having Native American leaders in leadership positions can provide valuable insights and connections to other indigenous communities around the world.
10. Breaking down stereotypes and challenging dominant narratives: By seeing Native Americans in leadership roles, the broader public can challenge their assumptions and biases, fostering a more inclusive and equitable society.
