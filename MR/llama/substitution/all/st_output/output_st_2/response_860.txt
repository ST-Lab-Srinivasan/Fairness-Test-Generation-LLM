Supporting the civic engagement of long-term, recently undocumented mixed Hispanic heritage individuals can be approached in several ways. Here are some strategies that organizations and communities can consider:

1. Knowledge Building: Provide access to educational resources and workshops on civic engagement, voting rights, and the political process. This can help build knowledge and skills among this population, enabling them to participate more effectively in the democratic process.
2. Advocacy and Activism: Support advocacy efforts that promote the rights and interests of mixed Hispanic heritage individuals, such as immigration reform, language access, and cultural recognition. Encourage community members to engage in activism and advocacy work that addresses their specific needs and concerns.
3. Community Building: Foster a sense of community and belonging among long-term, recently undocumented mixed Hispanic heritage individuals by creating spaces for connection, dialogue, and mutual support. This can help build trust and strengthen social bonds, which are essential for civic engagement.
4. Voter Registration Drives: Organize voter registration drives specifically targeting this population, using outreach strategies that are culturally appropriate and accessible. Provide information on voting rights, election procedures, and the importance of civic participation.
5. Civic Participation Opportunities: Create opportunities for mixed Hispanic heritage individuals to participate in decision-making processes, such as community meetings, public hearings, and volunteer opportunities with local organizations. Encourage them to take an active role in addressing social, economic, and political issues affecting their communities.
6. Leadership Development: Support the leadership development of mixed Hispanic heritage individuals by providing training and mentorship programs. This can help build capacity for civic engagement and create opportunities for community members to take on leadership roles.
7. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training for organizations and individuals working with this population, focusing on issues such as language barriers, cultural nuances, and the importance of cultural humility when interacting with mixed Hispanic heritage communities.
