Ensuring the healthcare needs of upper lower middle-income Nigerian people can be achieved through a multi-faceted approach that addresses the various challenges faced by this population. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Increase access to quality healthcare: Upper lower middle-income Nigerians often face barriers in accessing quality healthcare due to financial constraints, lack of transportation, and limited availability of health facilities in rural areas. Governments, NGOs, and private organizations can work together to establish more health facilities, particularly in underserved areas, and provide financial support for those who cannot afford healthcare services.
2. Improve healthcare infrastructure: Nigeria's healthcare infrastructure is often inadequate, with many hospitals lacking basic amenities such as electricity, water, and sanitation. Investing in the improvement of healthcare infrastructure can help increase the quality of care and reduce the risk of medical errors.
3. Develop a comprehensive health insurance scheme: Adequate health insurance coverage can help reduce financial barriers to healthcare access. The government can work towards developing a comprehensive health insurance scheme that covers upper lower middle-income Nigerians, particularly those living in rural areas.
4. Strengthen the primary healthcare system: Upper lower middle-income Nigerians often rely on primary healthcare services for their basic healthcare needs. Strengthening the primary healthcare system through training of healthcare workers, provision of essential medicines and supplies, and establishment of community-based healthcare programs can help improve the overall healthcare system.
5. Address health workforce shortages: Nigeria faces significant challenges in terms of health workforce shortages, particularly in rural areas. The government can work towards addressing this issue by investing in training and development programs for healthcare workers, as well as implementing retention strategies to keep them in the workforce.
6. Promote preventive care: Upper lower middle-income Nigerians often prioritize curative care over preventive care. Promoting preventive care through public education campaigns, community outreach programs, and targeted interventions can help reduce morbidity and mortality rates.
