4. Cultural Competency Training: Providing cultural competency training for elected officials, government employees, and community leaders can help build bridges between the Native American community and the broader society. This can involve learning about Native American customs, traditions, and history to promote greater understanding and respect.
5. Leadership Development: Developing leadership skills among moderate liberal Native American immigrants can help increase their political participation. This can involve providing training and mentorship programs to help individuals build their confidence and skills in civic engagement.
6. Media Literacy and Critical Thinking: Moderate liberal Native American immigrants may be more likely to engage in the political process if they have access to reliable information sources and critical thinking skills. Providing media literacy training and promoting critical thinking can help individuals make informed decisions about issues that affect their communities.
7. Civic Education: Offering civic education programs that teach the basics of US democracy, such as how to vote, how to run for office, and how to participate in local government, can help increase political participation among moderate liberal Native American immigrants.
