6. Establish mentorship programs: Pairing beginner Chinese speakers with more experienced individuals can provide guidance and support, while also creating opportunities for them to share their perspectives and insights.
7. Use visual aids: Utilizing visual aids such as pictures or videos can help beginner Chinese speakers communicate their ideas and opinions more effectively.
8. Encourage active listening: Encouraging listeners to actively engage with the beginner Chinese speakers by asking questions, clarifying points, and summarizing key takeaways can help ensure that their voices are heard and valued.
9. Provide opportunities for feedback: Offering regular opportunities for beginner Chinese speakers to provide feedback on decision-making processes can help them feel more invested in the outcome and increase their participation.
