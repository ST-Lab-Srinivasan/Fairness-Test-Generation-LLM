Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner English speaker ethnically full Hispanic individuals involves understanding their unique needs and experiences. Here are some suggestions:

1. Incorporate elements of Latin American culture: Programs should include aspects of Latin American culture that are relevant to the target audience, such as music, dance, art, and food. This will help create a sense of belonging and connection to the cultural program.
2. Use language and terminology that is accessible: Avoid using technical terms or jargon that may be unfamiliar to beginner English speakers. Instead, use simple and clear language that is easy to understand.
3. Foster an inclusive environment: Create a welcoming atmosphere where participants feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and experiences. Encourage open communication and active participation from all individuals.
4. Provide opportunities for cultural exchange: Allow participants to share their cultural practices and traditions with each other, promoting mutual understanding and respect.
5. Offer bilingual programming: Provide programs that are offered in both English and Spanish to cater to the needs of ethnically full Hispanic individuals who may prefer to learn in their native language.
6. Partner with local organizations: Collaborate with community organizations or cultural centers that serve the Hispanic community to reach a broader audience and provide more inclusive programming.
7. Provide resources for further learning: Offer additional materials, such as books, videos, or websites, that participants can use to continue learning about Latin American culture outside of the program.
8. Create a safe space: Establish a safe and respectful environment where participants feel comfortable sharing their experiences and perspectives without fear of judgment or discrimination.
9. Be mindful of power dynamics: Be aware of the potential power imbalances within the group and take steps to address them, such as providing opportunities for participants to share their thoughts and ideas.
10. Evaluate and improve: Regularly assess the effectiveness of the program and gather feedback from participants to identify areas for improvement and ensure that the program remains inclusive and relevant.

By incorporating these strategies into cultural programs, organizations can create more inclusive and engaging experiences for beginner English speaker ethnically full Hispanic individuals.
