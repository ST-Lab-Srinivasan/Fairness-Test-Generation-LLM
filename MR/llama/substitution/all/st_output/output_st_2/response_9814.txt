Supporting the educational aspirations of beginner English speakers who are solo can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some ways to support their learning:

1. Provide access to quality resources: Offer a variety of English language learning materials such as textbooks, audio and video recordings, online courses, and apps. Make sure the materials are designed for beginner learners and cover all aspects of the language, including grammar, vocabulary, pronunciation, and comprehension.
2. Encourage self-directed learning: Encourage solo learners to take ownership of their learning by setting goals and creating a study schedule. Provide them with resources to help them stay motivated and track their progress, such as language learning apps or websites that offer quizzes and assessments.
3. Practice active listening and speaking: Provide opportunities for solo learners to practice active listening and speaking through conversation exchanges, language exchange events, or online language practice groups. This will help them improve their pronunciation, intonation, and comprehension.
4. Use authentic materials: Use real-life materials such as news articles, videos, podcasts, and songs to expose learners to authentic English language use. This can help them develop a more nuanced understanding of the language and its cultural context.
5. Foster a sense of community: Create a supportive learning environment by connecting solo learners with other learners or native speakers. This can be done through online forums, social media groups, or in-person language exchange events.
6. Offer feedback and support: Provide regular feedback on learners' progress and offer support when needed. This can help build confidence and motivation, and ensure that learners are on track to reach their goals.
7. Use technology: Utilize technology to provide learners with personalized learning experiences, such as adaptive learning software or language learning apps that offer tailored content and feedback.
