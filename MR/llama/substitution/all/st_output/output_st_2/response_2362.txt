Supporting the economic empowerment of Not Lower Middle-Income Mixed Asian Heritages Individuals (NLLMMAHIs) requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges and opportunities faced by this community. Here are some ways to support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and Skills Development: NLLMMAHIs face significant barriers in accessing quality education and skills development opportunities. Governments, organizations, and individuals can invest in programs that provide accessible and affordable education, vocational training, and mentorship opportunities to help them acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Many NLLMMAHIs face challenges in starting and growing their own businesses due to limited access to capital, networks, and resources. Investing in entrepreneurship support programs, such as incubators, accelerators, and mentorship initiatives, can help them overcome these barriers and build successful enterprises.
3. Job Creation: Governments and organizations can work together to create job opportunities for NLLMMAHIs in various sectors, including technology, healthcare, finance, and more. This can be achieved through initiatives such as hiring quotas, mentorship programs, and training and development opportunities.
4. Access to Financial Services: Many NLLMMAHIs lack access to financial services, such as loans, savings accounts, and insurance products. Providing affordable and accessible financial services can help them manage their finances, invest in their businesses, and build wealth over time.
5. Networking Opportunities: Building a network of contacts and connections is crucial for career advancement and business growth. Organizations and individuals can provide opportunities for NLLMMAHIs to connect with other professionals, entrepreneurs, and potential investors to help them build their networks and achieve their goals.
6. Mentorship and Leadership Development: NLLMMAHIs often face challenges in finding mentors and role models who understand their unique experiences and challenges. Investing in mentorship programs and leadership development opportunities can help them build the skills and confidence they need to succeed in their personal and professional lives.
