Promoting the educational success of Native American natural-born US citizens, particularly those who are moderately achieving, requires a comprehensive approach that addresses cultural, social, and academic factors. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Incorporate traditional Native American teachings and practices into the classroom to make learning more relevant and meaningful for students. Provide opportunities for students to share their culture and experiences, and create a safe and inclusive learning environment.
2. Early intervention: Identify struggling students early on and provide targeted support to help them catch up with their peers. This may involve one-on-one tutoring, small group instruction, or additional academic resources.
3. Mentorship programs: Pair moderately achieving Native American students with successful role models who can provide guidance, encouragement, and support. This can help build self-esteem and motivation for academic success.
4. Cultural enrichment activities: Offer extracurricular activities that promote cultural awareness and appreciation, such as traditional dance classes, language clubs, or community service projects. These activities can help students develop a sense of identity and purpose.
5. Family engagement: Involve parents and guardians in the educational process by providing regular updates on their child's progress, soliciting their input on academic goals, and encouraging them to participate in school events and activities.
6. Community partnerships: Develop collaborations with local Native American organizations and communities to provide additional resources and support for students. This can include cultural immersion programs, internships, or job shadowing opportunities.
7. Academic support services: Provide academic support services such as tutoring, study skills training, and academic advising to help students succeed academically.
8. Teacher professional development: Offer ongoing professional development for teachers on how to teach Native American students, including cultural competency training, lesson planning, and classroom management techniques.
9. Curriculum reform: Review and revise the curriculum to better reflect Native American history, culture, and contributions. This can help increase student engagement and motivation.
