Deep poverty refers to a situation where an individual or household has a very low income, often below half of the poverty line. Permanent residents in this context may face unique challenges in accessing healthcare services due to various factors. Here are some of the challenges faced by deep poverty permanent resident people in the healthcare system:

1. Limited financial resources: Deep poverty individuals often have limited financial resources, making it difficult for them to afford healthcare services, medications, and other medical expenses. They may struggle to pay for basic necessities like food, shelter, and clothing before they can think about investing in their health.
2. Lack of health insurance: Many deep poverty individuals do not have access to health insurance, which can limit their ability to receive necessary medical care. Without insurance, they may be unable to afford expensive medical procedures or treatments.
3. Limited access to healthcare providers: Deep poverty residents may live in areas with limited access to healthcare providers, such as rural areas with few doctors or clinics. This can make it difficult for them to receive timely and appropriate medical care.
4. Language barriers: For deep poverty individuals who are non-native English speakers, language barriers can create significant challenges when trying to access healthcare services. They may struggle to communicate their symptoms or concerns to healthcare providers, leading to misdiagnosis or delayed treatment.
5. Stigma and discrimination: Deep poverty individuals may face stigma and discrimination from healthcare providers or society at large due to their socioeconomic status. This can lead to feelings of embarrassment, shame, or isolation, which can further exacerbate health disparities.
6. Poor health literacy: Deep poverty individuals may have limited education and low health literacy, making it difficult for them to navigate the complex healthcare system or understand their medical conditions.
7. Limited mobility: Due to financial constraints, deep poverty individuals may have limited mobility to access healthcare services, leading to delayed or missed appointments.
8. Lack of transportation: Many deep poverty residents lack access to reliable transportation, making it difficult for them to travel to healthcare facilities or appointments.
