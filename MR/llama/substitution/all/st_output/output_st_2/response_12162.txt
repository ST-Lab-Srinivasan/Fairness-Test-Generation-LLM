Ensuring the financial well-being of individuals aged over 90, particularly those from diverse ethnic backgrounds like mixed Hispanic heritage, requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies to help meet their financial needs:

1. Identify potential sources of income:
	* Government benefits: Encourage eligible seniors to apply for Social Security, Supplemental Security Income (SSI), and other government-funded programs.
	* Retirement accounts: Help them access retirement savings from 401(k)s, IRAs, or other employer-sponsored plans.
	* Other income streams: Explore options like rental property income, dividend-paying stocks, or a part-time job.
2. Manage expenses:
	* Assist with budgeting and financial planning to ensure they're making the most of their limited income.
	* Help them navigate costs associated with healthcare, housing, food, transportation, and other essential expenditures.
3. Build a support network:
	* Connect them with local organizations that provide financial counseling, such as the National Council on Aging or the Area Agency on Aging.
	* Encourage them to join peer support groups or online communities where they can share experiences and advice.
4. Consider long-term care options:
	* Explore government-funded long-term care programs, such as Medicaid or Veterans Administration benefits.
	* Help them understand the potential costs of long-term care services, such as assisted living or nursing home care, and plan accordingly.
5. Provide ongoing education and support:
	* Offer regular financial literacy workshops or one-on-one coaching to help them better manage their finances.
	* Encourage them to stay informed about changes in the financial landscape that may impact their well-being.
6. Advocate for policies supporting aging populations:
	* Participate in advocacy efforts aimed at improving access to affordable healthcare, housing, and other essential services for older adults.
