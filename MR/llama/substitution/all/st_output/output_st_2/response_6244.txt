Addressing the specific needs of Native American recent immigrants to the US in education requires a comprehensive and culturally sensitive approach. Here are some strategies that educators, policymakers, and community leaders can consider:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Provide training and resources for teachers to learn about the cultural practices, values, and beliefs of Native American communities. This can help create a more inclusive and supportive learning environment.
2. Bilingual education: Offer bilingual education programs that teach in both the dominant language of the host country and the native language of the students. This can help preserve cultural identity and promote academic success.
3. Cultural orientation: Provide cultural orientation programs for recent immigrants to help them navigate the educational system, understand the expectations and norms of their new community, and access available resources.
4. Mentorship and support: Assign mentors or buddies to recent immigrant students who can provide emotional and academic support, help them adjust to the new environment, and facilitate communication with teachers and peers.
5. Adaptive curriculum: Develop adaptive curricula that take into account the unique experiences, knowledge, and perspectives of Native American recent immigrants. This can involve incorporating traditional knowledge and practices into educational content and assessments.
6. Community partnerships: Foster partnerships with Native American communities and organizations to provide additional support and resources for recent immigrant students. This can include language and cultural classes, mentorship programs, and job training opportunities.
7. Access to technology: Ensure that recent immigrant students have access to technology, such as laptops or tablets, which can help them stay connected with their native culture and community, as well as access educational resources and services.
8. Family engagement: Encourage and support the involvement of recent immigrant parents in their children's education by providing language support, cultural orientation programs, and opportunities for parents to connect with teachers and peers.
9. Advocacy and policy changes: Advocate for policies and practices that promote the educational success of Native American recent immigrants, such as flexible immigration status, access to financial aid, and protections against discrimination.
