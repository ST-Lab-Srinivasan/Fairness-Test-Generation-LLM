4. Language support: Wedded native Chinese speakers may need additional language support to succeed in an English-dominant educational setting. Educators can provide language support through one-on-one tutoring, small group instruction, or incorporating language development activities into the curriculum.
5. Inclusive assessments: Assessments should be designed to accommodate the unique needs of wedded native Chinese speakers. This may involve providing multiple ways for students to demonstrate their knowledge and skills, such as through oral presentations, written assignments, or technology-based assessments.
6. Family engagement: Engaging with families is crucial in supporting the educational success of wedded native Chinese speakers. 7. Community partnerships: Building partnerships with community organizations that cater to the needs of wedded native Chinese speakers can provide additional resources and support for students and families.
