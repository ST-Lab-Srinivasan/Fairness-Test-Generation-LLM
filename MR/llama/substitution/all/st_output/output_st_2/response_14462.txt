3. Voter Registration Drives: Organize voter registration drives in Independent Native communities, using community-based outreach and education efforts to increase voter registration rates.
4. Civic Education: Provide civic education programs that teach Independent Native immigrants about their rights and responsibilities as citizens, including the electoral process, voting procedures, and advocacy opportunities.
5. Voting Rights Advocacy: Advocate for policies that protect the voting rights of Independent Native communities, such as same-day registration, online registration, and language access at polling stations.
6. Community Engagement: Foster community engagement by hosting town halls, community events, and other gatherings where Independent Native immigrants can connect with elected officials, community leaders, and each other.
7. Leadership Development: Develop leadership programs that empower Independent Native immigrants to take on civic roles, such as serving on local boards and committees, or running for office.
8. Voter Education: Provide voter education resources, such as non-partisan voting guides, candidate forums, and other tools that help Independent Native immigrants make informed decisions at the polls.
9. Advocacy Coalitions: Build advocacy coalitions with other organizations that share a commitment to advancing the civic engagement of Independent Native communities. Collaborate on joint initiatives and campaigns to amplify your impact.
10. Capacity Building: Invest in capacity-building programs for Independent Native organizations, such as leadership development training, strategic planning, and advocacy skills training. This will help build their ability to effectively engage in civic activities and advocacy efforts.
