Temporary visa holders in the healthcare system face several challenges that can limit their access to healthcare services. Some of the common challenges include:

1. Lack of Health Insurance: Temporary visa holders may not have access to health insurance, which can make it difficult for them to afford medical care. Without insurance, they may have to pay out-of-pocket for medical services, which can be a significant burden.
2. Limited Access to Healthcare Providers: Temporary visa holders may face barriers in accessing healthcare providers due to language or cultural differences, lack of familiarity with the healthcare system, or limited transportation options.
3. Difficulty Navigating the Healthcare System: The healthcare system can be complex and challenging for temporary visa holders to navigate, especially if they are not familiar with the culture, language, or policies of the host country.
4. Limited Knowledge of Available Services: Temporary visa holders may be unaware of the healthcare services available to them, which can lead to delayed or missed diagnosis and treatment.
5. Stigma and Discrimination: Temporary visa holders may face stigma and discrimination in accessing healthcare services due to their immigration status. This can result in delayed or avoided medical care.
6. Limited Access to Specialized Care: Temporary visa holders may have limited access to specialized healthcare services, such as oncology, cardiology, or mental health services, due to lack of availability or affordability.
7. Difficulty in Building Trust with Healthcare Providers: Temporary visa holders may find it challenging to build trust with healthcare providers due to language barriers, cultural differences, or concerns about their immigration status.
8. Limited Access to Preventive Care: Temporary visa holders may have limited access to preventive care services, such as routine check-ups, screenings, and vaccinations, which can lead to delayed diagnosis and treatment of health problems.
