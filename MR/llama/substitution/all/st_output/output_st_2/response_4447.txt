Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for young adult beginner Arabic speakers involves understanding their needs and preferences, as well as being mindful of the cultural context. Here are some suggestions:

1. Incorporate interactive activities: Provide opportunities for beginners to participate in interactive activities, such as language games, quizzes, or cultural workshops. This will help them feel more engaged and confident in their abilities.
2. Use visual aids: Utilize visual aids, such as pictures, videos, or posters, to help beginner Arabic speakers understand cultural customs and practices. This can also make the celebration more visually interesting and fun.
3. Provide translation support: Offer translation support during the celebration, either through live interpretation or pre-prepared materials with translations. This will help beginners follow along and participate fully in the celebration.
4. Focus on simple language: Use simple language and avoid complex cultural references or terminology that may be difficult for beginner Arabic speakers to understand. This will help them feel more comfortable and included.
5. Involve beginners in planning: Encourage beginner Arabic speakers to participate in planning the celebration, whether it's deciding on activities or helping to create decorations. This will give them a sense of ownership and agency in the event.
6. Create a welcoming atmosphere: Make sure the celebration is held in a comfortable and welcoming environment, with plenty of seating and opportunities for socializing. This will help beginners feel more at ease and enjoy themselves.
7. Offer cultural immersion activities: Provide opportunities for beginner Arabic speakers to engage in cultural immersion activities, such as traditional dress-up, food tastings, or music performances. This will help them gain a deeper appreciation of the culture and feel more connected to it.
