There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US citizens seeking employment:

1. Job training and placement programs: Provide job training and placement services specifically tailored to the needs of Native Americans, including cultural sensitivity training for potential employers.
2. Microenterprise development: Support the creation of microenterprises owned by Native Americans, such as small businesses or entrepreneurial ventures, through financial assistance, mentorship, and technical support.
3. Apprenticeship and internship programs: Offer apprenticeship and internship opportunities in various industries to provide Native American youth with hands-on work experience and exposure to different career paths.
4. Mentorship programs: Pair experienced professionals from the Native American community with individuals seeking employment, providing guidance and support in navigating the job market.
5. Career fairs and networking events: Host regular career fairs and networking events specifically targeting Native American communities, connecting job seekers with potential employers and providing opportunities for professional development.
6. Education and training partnerships: Develop partnerships with educational institutions to provide training programs tailored to the needs of Native Americans, such as vocational training, certification programs, or degree-granting courses.
7. Community-based initiatives: Support community-based initiatives that promote economic development and job creation within Native American communities, such as cooperative businesses or local entrepreneurship projects.
8. Advocacy and policy support: Advocate for policies and regulations that promote the economic empowerment of Native Americans, such as tax incentives for hiring Native American employees or support for Native-owned businesses.
9. Collaboration with tribal governments: Work closely with tribal governments to understand their specific needs and priorities, and develop targeted programs that address these needs.
10. Capacity building: Provide capacity building activities, such as leadership development, strategic planning, and financial management training, to support the growth of Native American-owned businesses and community organizations.
11. Access to capital: Increase access to capital for Native American entrepreneurs through loan programs, grants, or other financial assistance.
