Ensuring that the voices of beginner English speakers, particularly those who have recently gone through a divorce, are heard in decision-making processes can be challenging. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Provide language support: Offer language classes or resources to help these individuals improve their English skills, which will enable them to participate more effectively in decision-making processes.
2. Use interpreters or translators: If the individual does not speak fluent English, consider providing an interpreter or translator during meetings or discussions to ensure they can fully understand and contribute to the conversation.
3. Foster a supportive environment: Encourage a culture of inclusivity and respect, where all individuals feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and opinions. This can be achieved by creating a safe space for dialogue and active listening.
4. Use visual aids: Visual aids such as pictures or diagrams can help beginners communicate their ideas more effectively, especially if they struggle with language barriers.
5. Build relationships: Take the time to build relationships with these individuals, get to know them, and understand their needs and concerns. This will help establish trust and create a sense of belonging, which can increase their participation in decision-making processes.
6. Use accessible communication channels: Make sure that communication channels are accessible and easy to use for all individuals, including those who may have difficulty with language barriers. This could include using plain language, avoiding jargon or technical terms, and providing written materials in addition to verbal communication.
7. Encourage participation: Encourage these individuals to participate in decision-making processes by asking for their input and ideas. Make sure they understand the value of their contributions and that their voices are heard.
8. Be patient and understanding: Recognize that these individuals may face challenges related to language barriers, and be patient and understanding when communicating with them. Avoid making assumptions or jumping to conclusions based on language limitations.
9. Offer resources and support: Provide resources and support to help these individuals navigate the decision-making process, such as providing information in their native language or offering cultural competency training for those involved in decision-making processes.
