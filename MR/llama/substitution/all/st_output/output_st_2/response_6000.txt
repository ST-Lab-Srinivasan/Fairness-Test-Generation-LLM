Promoting the social inclusion of mixed African-American lower middle-income communities requires a multifaceted approach that addresses various aspects of exclusion, including economic, social, and cultural marginalization. Here are some strategies that can help promote social inclusion:

1. Economic Empowerment:
	* Provide access to financial services and capital for entrepreneurship and small businesses in these communities.
	* Support job training and placement programs to increase employability and career advancement opportunities.
	* Encourage investment in community development projects that create jobs, improve infrastructure, and enhance the quality of life.
2. Education and Skills Development:
	* Implement programs to improve access to quality education, including early childhood development, primary school, and vocational training.
	* Provide adult education and literacy programs to help individuals acquire new skills and enhance their employability.
	* Support mentorship and apprenticeship programs that connect individuals with experienced professionals in their chosen fields.
3. Community Building and Social Cohesion:
	* Facilitate community events, cultural festivals, and other activities that promote social interaction and cohesion among diverse groups.
	* Support community-based organizations and initiatives that foster a sense of belonging and shared identity among residents.
	* Encourage intergenerational dialogue and collaboration to build bridges between younger and older generations.
4. Health and Wellness:
	* Provide access to affordable, high-quality healthcare services and promote healthy lifestyle choices through community outreach programs.
	* Support initiatives that address mental health and wellness, including stress management and counseling services.
	* Encourage community gardens and other projects that promote physical activity, nutrition, and environmental sustainability.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change:
	* Advocate for policies that address systemic inequalities and discrimination faced by mixed African-American communities.
	* Support organizations that advocate for social justice and civil rights, and work to influence policy decisions at the local, state, and federal levels.
	* Engage in electoral politics and vote for candidates who prioritize social inclusion and equity.
