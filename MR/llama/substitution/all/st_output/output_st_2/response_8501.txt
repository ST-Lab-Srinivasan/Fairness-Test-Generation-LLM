Ensuring that the voices of beginner Chinese speakers, particularly native recent immigrants to the US, are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting inclusivity and equity. Here are some strategies to achieve this:

1. Language Support: Provide language support services such as interpreters, translators, and bilingual staff members to help beginner Chinese speakers communicate effectively in decision-making processes. This can help bridge the language barrier and ensure their voices are heard.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training for decision-makers to better understand the experiences and perspectives of beginner Chinese speakers. This can help build empathy and create a more inclusive environment.
3. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach efforts to connect with beginner Chinese speakers and listen to their concerns. This can help identify issues and priorities that are important to them, and ensure their voices are heard in decision-making processes.
4. Collaborative Decision-Making: Foster collaborative decision-making processes that involve beginner Chinese speakers in the planning and implementation of decisions. This can help build trust and ensure that their perspectives are incorporated into the decision-making process.
5. Advocacy and Leadership Development: Support advocacy and leadership development initiatives for beginner Chinese speakers, particularly those who are recent immigrants to the US. This can help build their confidence and skills to effectively participate in decision-making processes.
6. Access to Information: Ensure that beginner Chinese speakers have access to information about decision-making processes and opportunities to participate. This can be achieved through various means such as translating important documents, providing accessible language materials, or using technology to facilitate communication.
7. Building Relationships: Foster relationships with beginner Chinese speakers by engaging in regular communication and building trust. This can help create a sense of belonging and increase their participation in decision-making processes.
8. Inclusive Policies: Develop inclusive policies that take into account the unique needs and experiences of beginner Chinese speakers.
