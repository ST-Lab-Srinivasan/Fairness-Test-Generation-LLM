As a social worker, I have worked with many immigrants who have faced significant challenges in accessing healthcare services in the United States. Here are some of the common challenges faced by non-deep poverty immigrant and recent immigrants to the US in the healthcare system:

1. Lack of Health Insurance: Many immigrants, especially those who are undocumented or have a temporary status, may not be eligible for Medicaid or other public health insurance programs. This can make it difficult for them to access medical care, including preventive services and emergency care.
2. Language Barriers: Immigrants may face language barriers when trying to navigate the healthcare system. They may have difficulty communicating with healthcare providers, understanding medical terminology, or accessing written materials in their native language.
3. Cultural Differences: Immigrants may have different cultural beliefs and practices that can impact their healthcare decisions. For example, some cultures may have different attitudes towards seeking medical care or using traditional healing practices.
4. Limited Knowledge of Healthcare System: Many immigrants may be unfamiliar with the US healthcare system and its structures, making it difficult for them to navigate the system effectively.
5. Discrimination and Bias: Unfortunately, discrimination and bias against immigrants and minority groups in the healthcare system is still prevalent. Immigrants may face discrimination from healthcare providers or be denied access to care based on their race, ethnicity, or immigration status.
6. Difficulty Accessing Specialized Care: Immigrants may face challenges accessing specialized care, such as mental health services or complex medical procedures, due to language barriers, lack of knowledge about the system, or discrimination.
7. Lack of Health Education: Many immigrants may have limited access to health education and resources, which can make it difficult for them to understand how to maintain their health and prevent illnesses.
8. Limited Access to Preventive Services: Immigrants may face barriers to accessing preventive services such as routine check-ups, cancer screenings, or vaccinations due to lack of insurance coverage, language barriers, or cultural beliefs.
