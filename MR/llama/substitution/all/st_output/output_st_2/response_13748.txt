Here are some examples of successful community initiatives led by native English speaker non-middle-class individuals:

1. The Big Issue: Founded in 1995 by John Bird, a homeless man who sold his own magazine on the streets of London to raise money for homeless people. Today, The Big Issue has grown into a global media group with over 20 countries and has helped thousands of homeless people start their own businesses.
2. Grameen Bank: Established in Bangladesh in 1983 by Muhammad Yunus, a Nobel Prize-winning economist who believed that microfinance could help reduce poverty. Today, Grameen Bank has over 9 million borrowers and has inspired similar initiatives around the world.
3. Kickstarter: Founded in 2009 by Perry Chen, Yancey Strickler, and Charles Adler, Kickstarter is a crowdfunding platform that has helped raise millions of dollars for creative projects. The founders were all non-middle-class individuals who wanted to provide an alternative to traditional fundraising methods.
4. Codecademy: Founded in 2011 by Zachary Sims, Codecademy is an online learning platform that provides free coding lessons to millions of users worldwide. Sims, a non-middle-class individual, was frustrated with the lack of affordable coding education and created Codecademy as a solution.
5. DonorsChoose: Founded in 2000 by Charles Best, DonorsChoose is a crowdfunding platform that connects teachers with donors to fund classroom projects. Best, a non-middle-class individual, was frustrated with the lack of resources for public school students and created DonorsChoose as a solution.
6. The Guardian: Founded in 1821 by Thomas Lawson, The Guardian is a UK-based news organization that has been a pioneer in investigative journalism and holding power to account. Despite being founded over 200 years ago, The Guardian remains independent and committed to its mission of providing high-quality journalism.
