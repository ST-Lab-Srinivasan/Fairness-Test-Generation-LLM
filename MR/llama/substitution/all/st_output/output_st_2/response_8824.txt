Creating more inclusive cultural programs for Native English Speaker Immigrant Natural-Born US Citizens (NESI-NBUSCs) requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Understand the diversity of NESI-NBUSCs: Recognize that this group encompasses individuals from various countries, cultures, and backgrounds. Acknowledge that each person has their unique identity, experiences, and perspectives.
2. Involve NESI-NBUSCs in the planning process: Engage them in the design and development of cultural programs to ensure their voices are heard and their needs are met. This can be done through focus groups, surveys, or advisory committees.
3. Offer multilingual programming: Provide opportunities for NESI-NBUSCs to engage with culture through their native languages. This could include language classes, cultural workshops, or events that celebrate diverse linguistic heritage.
4. Showcase diverse art forms: Incorporate various art forms, such as music, dance, theater, and visual arts, that reflect the diversity of NESI-NBUSCs. This can help them connect with their cultural roots and appreciate the richness of different cultures.
5. Foster cross-cultural dialogue: Organize events or workshops that encourage interaction between NESI-NBUSCs and other communities. Encourage sharing of experiences, traditions, and perspectives to promote mutual understanding and respect.
6. Provide opportunities for community involvement: Offer ways for NESI-NBUSCs to contribute to their local communities through volunteer work or participating in cultural events. This can help them feel more connected and valued in their new surroundings.
7. Support intergenerational connections: Encourage the participation of elderly NESI-NBUSCs in cultural programs by offering activities that cater to their interests and abilities. This can help preserve cultural heritage and foster connections between different generations.
