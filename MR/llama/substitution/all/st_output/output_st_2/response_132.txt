Addressing the specific needs of Native American recent immigrants to the US and long-time veterans in education requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique experiences, cultural backgrounds, and individual circumstances. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training for educators on cultural competency, including an understanding of Native American customs, traditions, and historical traumas. This will help educators to create a more inclusive learning environment and address the specific needs of these students.
2. Bilingual Education: Offer bilingual education programs that cater to the language needs of Native American students who may speak indigenous languages as their primary language at home. This can include dual-language immersion programs or bilingual education classes.

Traditional Knowledge Integration: Incorporate traditional knowledge and practices into the curriculum, such as including traditional ecological knowledge in science classes or incorporating traditional art forms in visual arts classes. This can help to validate and celebrate Native American cultural practices while also providing a more inclusive learning environment.
4. Indigenous Curriculum: Develop and implement indigenous-based curricula that are grounded in Native American perspectives, histories, and cultures. This can include traditional storytelling, myths, and legends as well as contemporary issues affecting Native American communities.
5. Community Engagement: Build partnerships with Native American communities and organizations to ensure that the educational program is responsive to their needs. This can involve collaborating with elders, community leaders, and other stakeholders to design programs that are culturally relevant and appropriate.
6. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair Native American students with experienced educators who understand the unique challenges and opportunities facing these students. This can help to provide additional support and guidance for these students, particularly during critical transition periods such as middle school or high school.
7. Indigenous Research Methods: Teach indigenous research methods, such as traditional ecological knowledge, participatory observation, and community-based research.
