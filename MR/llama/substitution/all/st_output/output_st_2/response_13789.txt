Highlighting the issues faced by native Chinese speakers who are recent immigrants to India can be done through various methods, including:

1. Surveys and questionnaires: Conduct surveys or questionnaires to gather data on the challenges faced by these individuals. This can include questions on their experiences with language barriers, cultural differences, and social isolation.
2. Interviews: Conduct in-depth interviews with native Chinese speakers who have immigrated to India to gain a deeper understanding of their experiences and challenges.
3. Observational studies: Observe the daily lives of native Chinese speakers in India to identify issues such as language barriers, cultural differences, and social isolation.
4. Analyzing government data: Analyze data from Indian government sources, such as census reports or immigration statistics, to gain insights into the demographic characteristics of recent Chinese immigrants in India.
5. Social media analysis: Analyze social media data to understand the experiences and challenges faced by native Chinese speakers in India, including language barriers, cultural differences, and social isolation.
6. Focus groups: Conduct focus groups with native Chinese speakers who have immigrated to India to gain insights into their experiences and challenges.
7. Case studies: Conduct case studies of individual native Chinese speakers who have immigrated to India to gain a deeper understanding of their experiences and challenges.
8. Comparative analysis: Compare the experiences and challenges faced by native Chinese speakers in India with those faced by other immigrant groups in India, such as Indians living abroad or Rohingya refugees.
9. Mapping: Use geographic information systems (GIS) to map the locations where native Chinese speakers have settled in India, which can help identify patterns of migration and settlement.
10. Time series analysis: Analyze data on the number of native Chinese speakers immigrating to India over time to understand any trends or patterns in migration.

By using these methods, you can gather and analyze data to better understand the issues faced by native Chinese speakers who are recent immigrants to India, and identify potential solutions to address these challenges.
