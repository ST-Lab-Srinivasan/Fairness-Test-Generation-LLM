Addressing the specific needs of non-recently single Native Spanish speakers in education requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique cultural, linguistic, and social experiences. Here are some strategies that educators can use to support these individuals:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training for educators on the cultural practices and beliefs of Native American communities, particularly those related to family structure and relationships. This can help educators understand and respect the unique needs of NRSNS individuals.
2. Language Support: Offer language support services such as bilingual education programs or language translation services to ensure that NRSNS students have equal access to educational materials and resources.
3. Indigenous Curriculum: Incorporate Indigenous knowledge and perspectives into the curriculum to provide a more inclusive and culturally responsive learning environment. This can help NRSNS students feel seen and valued in their educational journey.
4. Mental Health Support: Provide mental health support services specifically tailored for NRSNS individuals, who may face unique challenges related to their cultural background and experiences. This can include counseling services, peer support groups, or mental health workshops.
5. Community Engagement: Foster partnerships with local Indigenous communities to create a more inclusive and culturally responsive learning environment. This can involve collaborating on curriculum development, providing cultural immersion experiences, or hosting community events that celebrate Indigenous culture.
6. Indigenous Role Models: Incorporate Indigenous role models into the educational setting to provide NRSNS students with positive representations of their culture and identity. This can include hiring Indigenous teachers, inviting Indigenous guest speakers, or creating mentorship programs that connect NRSNS students with Indigenous professionals.
7. Student-Centered Approach: Adopt a student-centered approach to education, which recognizes the unique strengths and experiences of NRSNS individuals.
