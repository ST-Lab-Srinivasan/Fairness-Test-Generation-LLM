Ensuring the healthcare needs of natural-born US citizen lower middle-income people is crucial for promoting equity and improving overall health outcomes. Here are some strategies that can help meet their healthcare needs:

1. Expand Medicaid coverage: Lower middle-income individuals often struggle to afford health insurance, especially in states with limited Medicaid expansion. Increasing the eligibility criteria for Medicaid or implementing a more comprehensive Medicaid program can help cover more people.
2. Implement cost-saving measures: Lower middle-income individuals often face high out-of-pocket costs, such as deductibles and copays. Implementing cost-saving measures like subsidies or tax credits for health insurance premiums can help reduce these costs.
3. Increase funding for community health clinics: Community health clinics provide essential primary care services to underserved populations, including lower middle-income individuals. Increasing funding for these clinics can help expand their capacity and improve access to care.
4. Provide financial incentives for providers: Lower middle-income individuals may face challenges finding affordable healthcare due to a lack of providers in their area or limited provider availability during non-traditional hours. Offering financial incentives, such as higher reimbursement rates or additional funding for providers serving underserved populations, can help attract more providers to these areas.
5. Improve transportation access: Lower middle-income individuals may face barriers to accessing healthcare due to lack of transportation options. Providing affordable public transportation or non-emergency medical transportation services can help overcome these challenges.
6. Address cultural and language barriers: Lower middle-income individuals may face cultural or language barriers that hinder their access to healthcare. Implementing cultural competency training for healthcare providers and providing interpretation services can help address these barriers.
7. Promote preventive care: Lower middle-income individuals may delay seeking preventive care due to financial constraints.
