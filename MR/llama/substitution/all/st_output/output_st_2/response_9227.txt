Creating more inclusive community events for civilian native English speaker people requires careful planning and consideration of the needs and preferences of diverse participants. Here are some suggestions to make your events more inclusive:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the event location is easily accessible for people with disabilities by providing wheelchair ramps, elevators, or alternative entrances. Provide clear signage and instructions for attendees with visual impairments.
2. Language support: Offer language support for non-native English speakers by providing interpreters or translating materials into different languages. This can help ensure that everyone can participate and understand the content.
3. Inclusive marketing: Use inclusive language in your promotional materials, such as avoiding stereotypes or assumptions based on race, ethnicity, or nationality. Emphasize the importance of diversity and inclusion in the event's messaging.
4. Representation: Ensure that the event's planning committee and speakers reflect the diversity of the community. This can help attendees feel seen and heard, and can also provide role models for underrepresented groups.
5. Flexibility: Offer flexible seating arrangements to accommodate different learning styles and needs. For example, provide both assigned seating and open seating options to cater to individuals with sensory processing issues or other disabilities.
6. Accommodations for different learning styles: Provide visual aids, such as images or videos, to support attendees with learning disabilities. Offer different formats for the event's content, such as written materials or audio descriptions, to cater to attendees with different preferences or needs.
7. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by providing a quiet room or area for attendees who may need a break from the event's sensory stimuli. Offer fidget toys or other tools to help individuals with sensory processing issues.
8. Respectful communication: Train staff and volunteers on how to communicate respectfully with attendees from diverse backgrounds. Avoid making assumptions based on race, ethnicity, or nationality, and be mindful of the language used in event communications.
