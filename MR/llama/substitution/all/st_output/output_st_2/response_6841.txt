Ensuring that the voices of native English speaker immigrant and recent immigrants to the US are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting inclusivity and equity. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Create inclusive spaces: Establish platforms where immigrant communities can share their experiences, concerns, and ideas without fear of marginalization or tokenization. This could include community meetings, town hall events, or online forums.
2. Build partnerships: Collaborate with organizations that specialize in serving immigrant populations to amplify their voices. These partners can help bridge the gap between immigrant communities and decision-makers by providing language support, cultural competency training, and access to resources.
3. Develop culturally responsive policies: Ensure that policies are tailored to meet the unique needs of immigrant populations. This includes considering the social, economic, and cultural contexts in which they live.
4. Provide language support: Offer interpretation and translation services during decision-making processes to ensure that all participants can fully engage. This can include in-person interpretation, video remote interpreting (VRI), or even automated machine translation tools.
5. Foster leadership opportunities: Encourage immigrant individuals to take on leadership roles within their communities and in decision-making processes. This could involve providing training and resources to help them develop the skills they need to advocate effectively.
6. Use data and research: Utilize data and research to better understand the experiences and needs of immigrant populations. This can help inform decision-making and ensure that policies are evidence-based and tailored to meet their needs.
7. Engage in continuous dialogue: Regularly engage in open and inclusive communication with immigrant communities to ensure that their voices are heard throughout the decision-making process. This can involve regular check-ins, feedback mechanisms, or even establishing a dedicated liaison role within government agencies.
8. Address power imbalances: Recognize and address any power imbalances that may exist between immigrant communities and decision-makers. This could involve building relationships with community leaders, providing training on advocacy and policy-making, or creating spaces for marginalized voices to be heard.
