Promoting the social inclusion of Native natural-born US citizens living in deep poverty communities is essential for addressing the systemic barriers that prevent these individuals from accessing basic rights and opportunities. Here are some strategies that can help promote social inclusion:

1. Community-based initiatives: Develop community-based initiatives that bring together members of the Native American community, local organizations, and government agencies to address poverty and promote social inclusion. These initiatives could include job training programs, mentorship opportunities, and cultural revitalization projects.
2. Education and skills development: Provide access to quality education and skills development programs that can help individuals acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market. This could include vocational training, GED preparation, and college scholarships.
3. Healthcare access: Ensure access to affordable healthcare services, including mental health support, to address the physical and emotional needs of individuals living in poverty. This could involve partnering with local health clinics or establishing mobile health units that can provide on-site medical services.
4. Housing support: Provide access to safe and affordable housing options, including transitional housing and permanent supportive housing. This could involve partnerships with local landlords or the development of community-based housing initiatives.
5. Cultural preservation: Support the cultural preservation of Native American communities by providing resources for language revitalization, cultural education, and traditional arts and crafts programs. This could involve partnering with local organizations or establishing community-based cultural centers.
6. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policy changes at the local, state, and federal levels that can address systemic barriers to social inclusion. This could involve working with advocacy groups, participating in public hearings, and mobilizing community members to push for policy change.
