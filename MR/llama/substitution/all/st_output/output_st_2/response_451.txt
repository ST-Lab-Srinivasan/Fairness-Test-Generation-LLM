Supporting the social inclusion of natural-born Indian citizens aged over 90 years old (or over 85 years old) can be a challenging task, but there are several ways to promote their well-being and involvement in society. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Age-friendly communities: Create age-friendly communities that cater to the needs of older adults. This can involve modifying public spaces, transportation systems, and buildings to be more accessible and easier to navigate for seniors.
2. Social connections: Encourage social connections and community engagement among older adults. This can be done through organized activities such as volunteering, intergenerational programs, and social events that bring together people of different age groups.
3. Health and wellness: Foster a culture of health and wellness among older adults by providing access to preventive healthcare services, promoting physical activity, and offering nutritious food options.
4. Education and training: Offer educational programs and training opportunities that cater to the interests and needs of older adults. This can help them stay mentally active and engaged in society.
5. Civic engagement: Encourage older adults to participate in civic activities such as voting, volunteering, and advocacy. This can help them feel connected to their community and contribute to the well-being of society.
6. Accessible technology: Provide accessible technology such as smartphones, tablets, and computers that are easy to use and can help older adults stay connected with family and friends, access information, and participate in online activities.
7. Support groups: Facilitate support groups for older adults who may be experiencing social isolation or loneliness. These groups can provide emotional support, practical advice, and a sense of belonging.
8. Intergenerational programs: Organize intergenerational programs that bring together people of different age groups to learn from each other, share experiences, and build relationships.
9. Age-appropriate housing: Ensure that older adults have access to age-appropriate housing options that cater to their needs and preferences. This can include independent living apartments, assisted living facilities, and nursing homes.
