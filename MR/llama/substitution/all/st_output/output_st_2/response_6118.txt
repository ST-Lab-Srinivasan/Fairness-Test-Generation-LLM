Supporting late 30s beginner Spanish speaker communities can be an rewarding and challenging task. Here are some effective strategies that you can consider:

1. Creating a supportive environment: Encourage and support each other in their language learning journey. This can be done through regular meetups, language exchange events, or even online groups.
2. Setting realistic goals: Help members set achievable goals, both short-term and long-term, to keep them motivated and track their progress.
3. Providing opportunities for practice: Organize language practice activities such as conversation exchanges, role-playing exercises, or even hosting a Spanish language meetup event.
4. Sharing resources: Share useful language learning resources such as textbooks, podcasts, apps, and videos to help members improve their language skills.
5. Encouraging feedback: Encourage members to give constructive feedback to each other to help improve their speaking and listening skills.
6. Using technology: Utilize online tools and platforms to facilitate communication and practice among members, such as video conferencing software, language learning apps, or online language exchange websites.
7. Offering support: Be available to answer questions, provide advice, and offer support to members who may be struggling with the language.
8. Building a sense of community: Organize social events or activities that are fun and interactive, such as cooking classes, cultural events, or even a Spanish-themed movie night.
9. Encouraging participation: Encourage all members to participate in activities and events, regardless of their level of proficiency.
10. Celebrating progress: Celebrate members' progress and achievements, no matter how small, to keep them motivated and engaged.
11. Providing opportunities for cultural immersion: Organize events or activities that allow members to immerse themselves in the Spanish culture, such as attending a flamenco show or a soccer game.
12. Offering personalized feedback: Provide personalized feedback and corrections to members based on their performance, to help them improve their language skills.
13. Creating a sense of accountability: Encourage members to hold each other accountable for practicing their Spanish skills regularly, by sharing progress and goals with the group.
