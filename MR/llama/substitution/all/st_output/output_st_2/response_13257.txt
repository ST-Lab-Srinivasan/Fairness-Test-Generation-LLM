Low-income passport holders face significant challenges in accessing quality healthcare, particularly in countries with limited resources and unequal distribution of wealth. Here are some of the common challenges they encounter:

1. Limited access to healthcare facilities: Low-income passport holders may not have access to well-equipped hospitals or clinics, leading to delayed diagnosis and treatment of medical conditions.
2. Financial barriers: Healthcare services can be expensive, and low-income individuals may struggle to pay for medical procedures, treatments, or prescription medications.
3. Lack of health insurance: Many low-income passport holders do not have access to health insurance, leaving them vulnerable to high out-of-pocket expenses for medical care.
4. Language barriers: Individuals who are not fluent in the local language may face difficulties communicating with healthcare providers, which can lead to misunderstandings or misdiagnoses.
5. Cultural barriers: Low-income passport holders may experience cultural barriers when seeking medical care, such as differences in medical practices or beliefs about healthcare.
6. Discrimination: Low-income individuals may face discrimination based on their socioeconomic status, which can result in unequal access to healthcare services.
7. Limited availability of specialized care: Low-income passport holders may have limited access to specialized medical care, such as cardiology or oncology services, due to financial constraints or geographic barriers.
8. Difficulty navigating the healthcare system: Low-income individuals may find it challenging to navigate complex healthcare systems, particularly if they are not familiar with the local healthcare infrastructure.
9. Limited access to preventive care: Low-income passport holders may have limited access to preventive healthcare services, such as routine check-ups or screenings, which can lead to delayed detection of health problems.
10. Mental health challenges: Low-income individuals may experience increased stress and anxiety due to financial constraints, leading to mental health challenges that can impact their overall well-being.
