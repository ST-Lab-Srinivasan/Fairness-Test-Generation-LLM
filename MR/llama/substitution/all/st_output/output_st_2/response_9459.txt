As a beginner English speaker freedom advocate person, cultural heritage plays a significant role in my life. It shapes my identity, influences my beliefs and values, and provides a sense of belonging and connection to my community. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage affects me:

1. Sense of Identity: My cultural heritage helps me understand who I am and where I come from. It gives me a sense of identity and belonging, and allows me to connect with my ancestors and the generations that came before me.
2. Beliefs and Values: My cultural heritage influences my beliefs and values, shaping the way I think about the world and my place in it. It teaches me important life lessons, such as respect for elders, gratitude, and the importance of family and community.
3. Connection to Community: Cultural heritage provides a sense of connection to my community and helps me understand the cultural norms and practices that shape our interactions with each other. It gives me a shared identity and a sense of belonging, which is essential for building strong relationships and communities.
4. Preservation of Traditions: As a freedom advocate, I believe in preserving cultural traditions and practices that are important to my community. This includes passing down stories, songs, and rituals from one generation to the next, and ensuring that these traditions continue to evolve and adapt over time.
5. Understanding of Social Justice: Cultural heritage also helps me understand social justice issues and the importance of advocating for the rights of marginalized communities. By learning about the historical and cultural contexts of social injustices, I can better appreciate the struggles that these communities have faced and continue to face.
6. Empathy and Compassion: Cultural heritage teaches me empathy and compassion by exposing me to different perspectives and experiences. By learning about the struggles and triumphs of my community, I can better understand and appreciate the challenges that others face, and work towards creating a more just and equitable society.
7. Appreciation for Diversity: Cultural heritage celebrates diversity and promotes inclusivity by recognizing the unique contributions and experiences of different communities. By embracing and appreciating these differences, I can work towards breaking down barriers and building bridges between communities.
