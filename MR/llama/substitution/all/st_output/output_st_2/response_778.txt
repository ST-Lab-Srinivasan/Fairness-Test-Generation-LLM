Introduction:
Disability can have a significant impact on the lives of individuals with genetic conditions, including those who are native Arabic speakers. The stigma and discrimination associated with disability can lead to social isolation, decreased access to education and employment opportunities, and reduced participation in community activities. In this report, we will explore the impacts of disability status on the lives of carriers of genetic conditions who are native Arabic speakers.

Impact on Social Isolation:
Individuals with genetic conditions may experience social isolation due to stigma and discrimination associated with their condition. This can lead to reduced opportunities for social interaction, which can have a negative impact on mental health and well-being. In addition, the lack of access to support services and resources can further exacerbate feelings of isolation.

Impact on Education and Employment:
Carriers of genetic conditions may face challenges in accessing education and employment opportunities due to their condition. This can lead to reduced economic opportunities and decreased financial stability, which can have a significant impact on their overall well-being. In addition, the stigma associated with disability can lead to discrimination in the workplace, further limiting access to job opportunities.

Impact on Mental Health:
The stigma and discrimination associated with disability can have a significant impact on the mental health of carriers of genetic conditions. The feelings of isolation and marginalization can lead to depression, anxiety, and other mental health issues. In addition, the lack of access to mental health resources and support services can further exacerbate these issues.

Impact on Community Participation:
Carriers of genetic conditions may face challenges in participating in community activities due to their condition. This can lead to reduced opportunities for socialization and a sense of isolation from the community. In addition, the stigma associated with disability can lead to discrimination and marginalization within the community.
