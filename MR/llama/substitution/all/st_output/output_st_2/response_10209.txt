4. Support personnel: Train event staff and volunteers on how to support pregnant individuals, including offering assistance with mobility or providing a quiet space for nursing.
5. Accommodating schedules: Be mindful of the different stages of pregnancy and schedule events accordingly. For example, avoid scheduling events during the first trimester when many pregnant individuals may be experiencing morning sickness.
6. Inclusive programming: Offer a diverse range of cultural activities that cater to various interests and abilities. This could include art exhibitions, music performances, or workshops on topics such as parenting, nutrition, or mental health.
7. Partner with organizations: Collaborate with local organizations that support pregnant individuals, such as prenatal care clinics or postpartum support groups, to reach a wider audience and ensure a more inclusive event.
