Ensuring the financial well-being of very old (90s+) immigrants in the US can be challenging due to various factors, including language barriers, cultural differences, and limited access to resources. Here are some potential strategies that could help meet their financial needs:

1. Language assistance: Providing interpreters or bilingual staff at healthcare facilities, social service agencies, and government offices can help immigrants navigate the complex US healthcare system and access financial resources.
2. Cultural competency training: Training for service providers on cultural competency can help them understand the unique needs and challenges faced by older immigrants, particularly those from non-Western cultures.
3. Financial assistance programs: Developing targeted financial assistance programs for older immigrants, such as subsidized housing, transportation, and home care services, can help alleviate some of the financial burdens they face.
4. Health insurance access: Ensuring access to affordable health insurance options for older immigrants can help them cover medical expenses and maintain their overall health.
5. Asset development: Encouraging older immigrants to save money and invest in assets, such as real estate or stocks, can help build intergenerational wealth and provide a financial safety net.
6. Estate planning: Offering legal services and resources for older immigrants to create wills, advance directives, and other estate planning documents can help ensure their assets are distributed according to their wishes after they pass away.
7. Social connections: Encouraging social connections between older immigrants and their peers, as well as with younger generations, can help build a support network and reduce feelings of isolation and loneliness.
8. Community outreach: Developing community-based programs that reach out to older immigrants and provide financial education, legal assistance, and other resources can help them navigate the complex US financial system.
9. Public benefits access: Assisting older immigrants in accessing public benefits, such as Supplemental Security Income (SSI) or Medicaid, can help them cover basic living expenses and healthcare costs.
