Introduction:
Marriage is a significant life event that can have a profound impact on an individual's life. Beginner English speaker cisgender elderly female individuals, in particular, may face unique challenges in their marital status. This study aims to investigate the impacts of marital status on the lives of beginner English speaker cisgender elderly female individuals.

Literature Review:
Previous studies have shown that marital status can affect various aspects of an individual's life, including their mental and physical health, social relationships, and economic well-being (1, 2). For elderly individuals, the impact of marital status may be particularly significant, as it can affect their sense of purpose and identity, as well as their access to social support and resources (3, 4).

Methodology:
This study will use a qualitative research design, conducting in-depth interviews with beginner English speaker cisgender elderly female individuals. Participants will be recruited through community centers and senior citizen groups, ensuring diversity in terms of age, geographical location, and marital status. Interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim, allowing for a nuanced understanding of participants' experiences.

Results:
Preliminary findings suggest that marital status can have both positive and negative impacts on the lives of beginner English speaker cisgender elderly female individuals. Positive impacts include increased social support and emotional well-being, particularly for those who are married or in long-term relationships (5). Negative impacts include feelings of isolation and disconnection from society, as well as reduced economic security for those who are widowed or divorced (6).

Conclusion:
This study highlights the complex and multifaceted impacts of marital status on the lives of beginner English speaker cisgender elderly female individuals. Findings suggest that marital status can have both positive and negative effects, depending on the individual's circumstances. Further research is needed to explore these issues in greater depth and to develop strategies for supporting this vulnerable population.
