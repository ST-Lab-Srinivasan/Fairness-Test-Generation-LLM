Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of newly married beginner English speakers. It influences their values, beliefs, and behaviors, and helps them establish a sense of belonging and identity within their new cultural context. This paper will explore the role of cultural heritage in shaping the identities of newly married beginner English speakers, focusing on the experiences of individuals from diverse backgrounds.

The Role of Cultural Heritage:
Cultural heritage refers to the shared knowledge, beliefs, values, customs, and practices that are passed down from one generation to the next within a particular group or society. For newly married beginner English speakers, cultural heritage plays a crucial role in shaping their identities by providing them with a sense of belonging and connection to their ancestral culture.

1. Preserving Cultural Traditions:
Cultural heritage helps newly married beginner English speakers preserve their cultural traditions and practices, such as language, music, dance, and food. These traditions serve as a connecting link between the individual and their ancestral culture, providing a sense of identity and belonging. For instance, a newly married couple from India may choose to preserve their cultural traditions by cooking traditional dishes, speaking their native language at home, or participating in cultural events and festivals.
2. Adapting to New Cultural Contexts:
At the same time, cultural heritage also helps newly married beginner English speakers adapt to new cultural contexts. As they navigate their new environment, they rely on their cultural heritage to provide a sense of familiarity and comfort. For example, a newly married couple from China may find solace in practicing their native language or observing traditional customs during special occasions.
3. Identity Formation:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of newly married beginner English speakers by influencing their values, beliefs, and behaviors. It helps them establish a sense of self and belonging within their new cultural context. For instance, a newly married couple from Mexico may identify more strongly with their indigenous heritage, while a couple from Japan may feel more connected to their Buddhist traditions.
