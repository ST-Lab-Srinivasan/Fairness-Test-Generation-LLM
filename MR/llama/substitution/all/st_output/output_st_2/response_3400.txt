Cultural heritage and identity:
Cultural heritage refers to the shared beliefs, values, customs, traditions, and practices of a particular group or society. It is a crucial aspect of identity formation as it provides individuals with a sense of belonging and connection to their community. For Arabic speakers, cultural heritage includes Islamic principles, Arabic language, customs, and social norms. When a person loses their spouse, they may feel a loss of identity, as their role in society is no longer defined by their relationship with their partner. Cultural heritage can play a significant role in shaping the identities of recently widowed beginner Arabic speakers by providing them with a sense of continuity and connection to their community.
