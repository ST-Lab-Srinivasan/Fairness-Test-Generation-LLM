Family support plays a crucial role in the success and well-being of very old (90s+) not able-bodied individuals. Here are some ways in which family support can contribute to their success:

1. Emotional Support: Older adults who are unable to care for themselves due to physical limitations may feel isolated, lonely, and depressed. Family members can provide emotional support by visiting them regularly, listening to their concerns, and offering words of encouragement.
2. Practical Assistance: Family members can help with practical tasks such as grocery shopping, meal preparation, and housekeeping. This can help alleviate some of the burden of daily living and allow the older adult to focus on their physical and mental well-being.
3. Transportation: Older adults may have difficulty getting around, especially if they no longer drive. Family members can provide transportation to medical appointments, social events, and other activities, which can help them maintain their independence and social connections.
4. Financial Support: As older adults face financial challenges due to reduced income or increased expenses, family members can provide financial support by helping with budgeting, managing bills, and covering living expenses.
5. Social Connection: Older adults who are not able-bodied may experience social isolation, which can have negative effects on their mental and emotional well-being. Family members can help facilitate social connections by introducing them to other older adults in similar situations or by organizing social events within the family.
6. Advocacy: Family members can advocate for their loved one's needs, such as arranging for home healthcare services, negotiating with medical providers, and ensuring that they receive appropriate care and support.
7. Respite: Caring for a loved one who is not able-bodied can be emotionally and physically draining for family members. Providing respite by taking on some of the caregiving tasks or finding external resources, such as adult daycare or home healthcare services, can help family members maintain their own physical and mental well-being while continuing to support their loved one.
