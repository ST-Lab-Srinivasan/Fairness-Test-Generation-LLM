1. Understand the needs of young veterans: Conduct research to better understand the specific needs and challenges faced by young combat veterans. This could include understanding the physical, mental, and emotional impacts of their service, as well as the challenges they face when transitioning back to civilian life.
2. Provide affordable and accessible housing: Ensure that there are adequate resources available for young veterans to access affordable and accessible housing. This could include providing financial assistance for down payments or closing costs, as well as ensuring that housing options are located in safe and supportive communities.
3. Offer support services: Provide support services such as mental health counseling, job training programs, and education and career development opportunities to help young veterans overcome the challenges they face when transitioning back to civilian life.
4. Promote peer support: Encourage young veterans to connect with one another through peer support groups or mentorship programs. This can help them feel less isolated and more connected to their community, as well as provide a sense of camaraderie and purpose.
5. Advocate for policy change: Work with policymakers to advocate for policies that support young veterans, such as increased funding for housing and support services, or the development of specialized housing programs for combat veterans in this age range.
