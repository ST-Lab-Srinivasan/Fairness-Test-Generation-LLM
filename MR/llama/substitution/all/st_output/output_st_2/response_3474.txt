Supporting the economic empowerment of working-class Native recent immigrants to the US requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the various challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that cater to the needs of Native recent immigrants. This could include language classes, vocational training, and certification programs in in-demand fields such as technology, healthcare, and manufacturing.
2. Job Placement Services: Offer job placement services that connect Native recent immigrants with employers who are willing to hire them. This could involve working with local businesses to identify job opportunities and providing interview preparation and resume building workshops.
3. Microfinance Programs: Establish microfinance programs that provide small loans or grants to Native recent immigrants who want to start their own businesses. These programs can help them cover startup costs, inventory, and marketing expenses.
4. Mentorship Opportunities: Pair Native recent immigrants with successful entrepreneurs or professionals in their field for mentorship opportunities. This could involve regular meetings, networking events, and access to industry insights and advice.
5. Networking Events: Organize networking events that bring together Native recent immigrants, employers, and community leaders. These events can help build connections and create job opportunities.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that support the economic empowerment of Native recent immigrants, such as changes to immigration laws, tax incentives for hiring them, or increased funding for language and job training programs.
7. Community Development Projects: Support community development projects that create jobs and economic opportunities for Native recent immigrants. This could involve partnerships with local businesses, non-profits, and government agencies to develop affordable housing, infrastructure, and public spaces.
8. Entrepreneurship Support: Provide resources and support for Native recent immigrants who want to start their own businesses. This could involve access to incubators, co-working spaces, and entrepreneurship training programs.
