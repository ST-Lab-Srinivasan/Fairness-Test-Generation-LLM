The impact of disability status on the lives of permanent residents with moderate disabilities can be significant and far-reaching. Here are some of the potential impacts:

1. Social isolation: People with disabilities may face barriers in accessing social activities, events, and spaces, leading to social isolation and a lack of connections with their community.
2. Limited access to education and employment: Individuals with moderate disabilities may encounter difficulties in accessing educational opportunities or finding employment due to physical or attitudinal barriers.
3. Financial strain: The additional costs associated with disability, such as assistive technology, transportation, and home modifications, can put a significant strain on the financial resources of individuals and their families.
4. Health disparities: People with disabilities may experience poorer health outcomes due to inadequate access to healthcare services, lack of representation in health research, and societal attitudes that stigmatize disability.
5. Stigma and discrimination: Disabled individuals may encounter discrimination and stigma in various areas of life, including education, employment, and social interactions, leading to lower self-esteem, mental health issues, and decreased opportunities for personal growth.
6. Limited participation in civic life: Individuals with disabilities may face barriers in participating in civic life, such as voting, volunteering, or advocating for policies that affect their lives.
7. Inaccessible built environment: Many buildings and public spaces are not designed to accommodate people with disabilities, making it difficult or impossible for them to access essential services, such as healthcare, education, and shopping.
8. Lack of representation in media and culture: Disabled individuals are often underrepresented or inaccurately portrayed in media and popular culture, perpetuating negative stereotypes and contributing to a lack of understanding and empathy from the general public.
