The impact of disability status on the lives of natural-born, lifelong single people can be significant and far-reaching. Here are some potential effects:

1. Social isolation: People with disabilities may face social isolation due to discrimination, stigma, or difficulty navigating social situations. This can lead to feelings of loneliness, depression, and anxiety.
2. Limited access to education and employment: Depending on the nature of their disability, individuals may face barriers to accessing education or finding employment. This can result in lower lifetime earnings and reduced opportunities for personal growth.
3. Health disparities: People with disabilities may experience poorer health outcomes due to lack of access to healthcare services, inadequate health insurance coverage, or discrimination in the delivery of healthcare.
4. Financial hardship: Individuals with disabilities may face higher costs for living expenses, transportation, and other necessities due to their disability. This can lead to financial stress and insecurity.
5. Lack of representation and advocacy: People with disabilities are often underrepresented in decision-making roles and may not have a voice in shaping policies and practices that affect their lives.
6. Stigma and discrimination: Disability can lead to stigma and discrimination, which can further marginalize and isolate individuals. This can manifest in various forms, such as ableism or attitudinal barriers.
7. Limited access to assistive technology: Individuals with disabilities may face barriers to accessing assistive technology, such as wheelchairs, prosthetics, or communication devices, which can limit their participation in society.
8. Inadequate transportation options: People with disabilities may encounter difficulties when using public transportation due to inaccessible infrastructure, inadequate training of staff, or lack of accessible vehicles.
