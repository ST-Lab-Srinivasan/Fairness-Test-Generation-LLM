There have been many successful advocacy efforts to protect and advance the civil rights of natural-born US citizens. Here are some examples:

1. Civil Rights Movement (1950s-1960s): The Civil Rights Act of 1964, which outlawed discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin, was a major milestone in the struggle for racial equality. The movement was led by figures such as Martin Luther King Jr., who advocated nonviolent civil disobedience to challenge unjust laws and policies.
2. Women's Suffrage Movement (1840s-1920s): The women's suffrage movement fought for the right of women to vote. The 19th Amendment to the US Constitution, ratified in 1920, granted women the right to vote. Leaders such as Elizabeth Cady Stanton and Susan B. Anthony played a significant role in this advocacy effort.
3. Disability Rights Movement (1960s-present): The disability rights movement has fought for the rights of people with disabilities, including access to education, employment, and healthcare. Landmark legislation such as the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) of 1990 has prohibited discrimination against people with disabilities in various areas of life.
4. LGBTQ+ Rights Movement (1960s-present): The LGBTQ+ rights movement has advocated for the rights of lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and queer individuals, including the right to marry, adopt children, and serve in the military. Major milestones include the Stonewall riots in 1969 and the Supreme Court's ruling in favor of same-sex marriage in 2015.
5. Immigration Rights Movement (1980s-present): The immigration rights movement has advocated for the rights of undocumented immigrants, including a pathway to citizenship and protection from deportation. Groups such as the National Immigration Law Center have worked to promote comprehensive immigration reform and challenge discriminatory policies.
