Documented not recently widowed people, also known as "divorced" or "separated," may face unique challenges in the healthcare system due to their non-traditional family status. Here are some of the challenges they may encounter:

1. Lack of support: Divorced or separated individuals may not have the same level of social support as widows or widowers, who often have a built-in support network of family and friends. In the healthcare system, this lack of support can lead to feelings of isolation and disconnection from caregivers.
2. Limited access to healthcare: Divorced or separated individuals may face financial barriers to accessing healthcare due to their reduced income or limited health insurance coverage. This can result in delayed or foregone care, which can negatively impact their health outcomes.
3. Stigma and discrimination: Unfortunately, there is still a stigma attached to being divorced or separated, particularly among older adults. Healthcare providers may hold biases or assumptions about these individuals based on their marital status, leading to subpar care or inadequate communication.
4. Conflicting care preferences: In the event of a serious illness or injury, divorced or separated individuals may face challenges when it comes to making medical decisions. They may not have a designated partner or family member to make decisions on their behalf, leading to confusion and disagreements among healthcare providers.
5. Lack of advocacy: Without a supportive partner or family member to advocate for them, divorced or separated individuals may find it difficult to navigate the complex healthcare system. They may feel overwhelmed by medical jargon or uncertain about their rights and options.
6. Limited access to palliative care: Palliative care is a type of care that focuses on relieving pain, symptoms, and stress associated with serious illnesses. Divorced or separated individuals may face barriers in accessing this type of care due to financial constraints or lack of understanding among healthcare providers.
