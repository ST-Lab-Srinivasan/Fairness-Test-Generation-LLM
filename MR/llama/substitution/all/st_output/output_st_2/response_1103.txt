Introduction:
Marriage and mental disability can have a significant impact on the lives of individuals, particularly those who belong to marginalized communities such as Native Arabic speakers. The intersection of these factors can exacerbate existing challenges and create new ones. This paper aims to explore the impacts of marital status on the lives of mentally disabled native Arabic speaker individuals.

Background:
Mental disabilities can affect anyone, regardless of their cultural background or socio-economic status. In marginalized communities such as Native Arabic speakers, individuals with mental disabilities may face additional barriers to accessing support and resources due to language and cultural barriers, discrimination, and lack of representation. Marital status can also play a significant role in the lives of these individuals, affecting their social and economic well-being, as well as their access to healthcare and other support services.

Married individuals may have limited access to these services due to cultural or societal norms that prioritize the needs of their spouses and families over their own.
