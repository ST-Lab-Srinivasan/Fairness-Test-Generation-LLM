Supporting the economic empowerment of deep poverty ethnic African Americans requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural impediments that hinder their economic mobility. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and job training programs: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that address the specific needs of deep poverty ethnic African Americans. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and certification programs that lead to in-demand jobs.
2. Microfinance initiatives: Offer microloans and other financial services that cater specifically to the needs of deep poverty ethnic African Americans. This can help them start or expand their own businesses, which is a key driver of economic mobility.
3. Mentorship and networking opportunities: Connect deep poverty ethnic African Americans with successful professionals and entrepreneurs who can provide mentorship, guidance, and support in navigating the professional world. This can help them build networks and gain access to valuable resources and opportunities.
4. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies that address the systemic barriers faced by deep poverty ethnic African Americans, such as discrimination, unequal access to education and job opportunities, and inadequate social safety nets. This can include pushing for policies that promote affordable housing, criminal justice reform, and economic development in underserved communities.
5. Community development initiatives: Invest in community development initiatives that create jobs, improve living conditions, and foster a sense of community and belonging among deep poverty ethnic African Americans. This can include community gardens, small business incubators, and other projects that promote economic growth from the ground up.
6. Healthcare access: Ensure that deep poverty ethnic African Americans have access to quality healthcare services, including preventive care, mental health support, and chronic disease management. Good health is a critical component of overall well-being and can help individuals overcome barriers to economic mobility.
7. Housing assistance: Provide housing assistance programs that address the specific needs of deep poverty ethnic African Americans, such as affordable housing options, rental subsidies, and homeownership programs. Stable housing is essential for economic stability and mobility.
