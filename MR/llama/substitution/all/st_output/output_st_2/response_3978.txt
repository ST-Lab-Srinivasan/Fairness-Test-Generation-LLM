Supporting the civic engagement of long-term undocumented Native American recent immigrants to the US requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some ways to support their civic engagement:

1. Legal Support: Provide legal support for these individuals by connecting them with organizations that offer free or low-cost immigration legal services. This can help them navigate the complex immigration system and access the resources they need to achieve their citizenship goals.
2. Cultural Humility: Be mindful of the cultural differences and histories of the Native American communities they are serving. Avoid cultural appropriation and be respectful of their traditions, beliefs, and practices.
3. Language Support: Offer language support services such as interpreters or translators to help them communicate effectively with organizations and government agencies.
4. Community Building: Foster a sense of community among long-term undocumented Native American immigrants by providing safe spaces for socializing, cultural events, and advocacy activities.
5. Advocacy: Advocate for policies that support the rights and well-being of these individuals, such as immigration reform, healthcare access, and education equity.
6. Civic Education: Provide civic education workshops to help them understand their rights and responsibilities as citizens, including how to vote, run for office, and participate in the democratic process.
7. Health Services: Connect them with healthcare services that cater to their specific needs, such as culturally-sensitive mental health services, and provide resources for chronic disease management.
8. Economic Empowerment: Offer financial literacy training, job training programs, and access to capital to help them build economic stability and independence.
9. Education and Leadership Development: Provide educational opportunities, leadership development programs, and mentorship to help them become leaders in their communities and beyond.
10. Building Alliances: Collaborate with other organizations, community groups, and government agencies to build alliances that support the civic engagement of long-term undocumented Native American immigrants.

By addressing the unique needs and challenges faced by these individuals, these strategies can help promote their full civic engagement and participation in US society.
