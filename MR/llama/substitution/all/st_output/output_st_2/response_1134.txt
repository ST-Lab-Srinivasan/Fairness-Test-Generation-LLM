Supporting the economic empowerment of Native natural-born US citizen upper lower middle-income individuals requires a multifaceted approach that addresses various factors impacting their financial stability and growth. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Financial Services: Provide access to affordable bank accounts, loans, and other financial services to help individuals save money, invest in businesses or education, and build credit. This can be achieved through partnerships with financial institutions, government programs, or non-profit organizations.
2. Job Training and Placement: Offer job training programs that teach skills relevant to the modern economy, such as digital literacy, coding, and entrepreneurship. Provide career counseling and placement services to help individuals find well-paying jobs in their communities or beyond.
3. Microenterprise Development: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing training, resources, and funding opportunities for individuals to start small businesses. This can include microloans, incubators, and mentorship programs.
4. Education and Skills Development: Invest in education and skills development programs that address the needs of Native communities. This can include language and cultural sensitivity training for educators, as well as programs that provide academic and vocational training.
5. Community Development Finance Institutions (CDFI): Support the establishment of CDFI organizations that provide financial services to underserved communities, including Native communities. CFDI organizations prioritize lending to low-income individuals and communities, and can help stimulate local economies.
6. Tax Credits and Incentives: Offer tax credits or incentives for businesses that operate in Native communities or hire locally. This can encourage investment and job creation in these areas.
7. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to quality, affordable healthcare for Native individuals by investing in programs that provide medical care, dental services, and mental health support.
8. Housing and Infrastructure: Provide support for housing and infrastructure development in Native communities. This can include funding for housing rehabilitation, new construction, and community facilities such as schools, hospitals, and community centers.
9. Cultural Preservation: Support cultural preservation efforts by providing funding for language revitalization
