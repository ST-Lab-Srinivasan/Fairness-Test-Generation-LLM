Supporting the social inclusion of recently single people in Nigeria can be a challenging task, but there are several ways to help them navigate this transition and integrate back into society. Here are some suggestions:

1. Social events: Organize or attend social events where singles can meet and mingle with other singles in a safe and welcoming environment. This could include speed dating, singles mixers, or game nights.
2. Support groups: Create or join support groups specifically for recently single people. These groups can provide a safe space for individuals to share their experiences, receive emotional support, and connect with others who are going through similar situations.
3. Online communities: Develop or participate in online communities specifically for singles in Nigeria. These could include social media groups, forums, or chat rooms where individuals can connect with others, share their experiences, and seek advice.
4. Volunteer work: Encourage recently single people to get involved in volunteer work, either individually or as a group. This can help them meet new people, build connections, and gain a sense of purpose and fulfillment.
5. Networking: Encourage recently single people to attend networking events, such as professional conferences or social gatherings. These events can provide opportunities to meet new people, build connections, and advance their careers.
6. Education and personal development: Offer workshops, classes, or seminars on topics such as communication skills, self-esteem, and stress management. These can help recently single people improve their confidence and emotional well-being.
7. Wellness activities: Encourage recently single people to engage in wellness activities, such as exercise, meditation, or yoga. These can help them manage stress, improve their mental health, and feel more balanced and grounded.
8. Mentorship: Pair recently single people with experienced mentors who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement. This can help them navigate the challenges of being single and build a stronger sense of self.
9. Financial planning: Offer financial planning workshops or one-on-one consultations to help recently single people manage their finances, create budgets, and plan for their financial futures.
10. Legal support: Provide legal resources and support to help recently single people
