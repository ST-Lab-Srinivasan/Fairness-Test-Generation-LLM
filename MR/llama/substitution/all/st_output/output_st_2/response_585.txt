To ensure that the educational needs of non-disabled permanent residents are met, schools and educators can take several steps:

1. Inclusive Education: Provide inclusive education by creating a supportive learning environment where all students feel valued and included. This can be achieved by implementing differentiated instruction, providing additional resources and supports, and promoting positive relationships between teachers, students, and peers.
2. Accommodations and Modifications: Offer accommodations and modifications to support the academic success of non-disabled students with identified learning needs. This may include providing extra time to complete assignments or tests, offering different formats for materials (e.g., braille or audio), or providing additional support during class.
3. Universal Design for Learning (UDL): Implement UDL principles to design curriculum and instruction that meet the diverse learning needs of all students. This involves using a variety of teaching strategies, materials, and technologies to engage students and help them access and demonstrate knowledge.
4. Differentiated Instruction: Use differentiated instruction to tailor the educational experience to the unique needs and abilities of each student. This may involve modifying the curriculum, instructional strategies, or assessments to better meet the needs of non-disabled students.
5. Parental Involvement: Encourage parental involvement in their child's education by providing opportunities for parents to learn about and support their child's academic success. This may involve offering workshops or training sessions on topics such as how to support your child's learning, how to advocate for their needs, and how to navigate the educational system.
6. Collaboration with Other Agencies: Collaborate with other agencies and organizations that serve non-disabled students, such as special education cooperatives or community organizations, to provide additional support and resources.
