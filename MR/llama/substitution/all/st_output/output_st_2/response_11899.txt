Supporting the economic empowerment of deeply lower-class mixed African Americans requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural inequalities they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs, particularly in high-demand fields such as technology, healthcare, and finance. This will help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market and advance their careers.
2. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship: Offer microloans and other financial resources to help individuals start or grow their own businesses. This can provide a pathway to economic mobility and create jobs in underserved communities.
3. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that individuals have access to affordable, quality healthcare services. This is critical for maintaining good health and well-being, which is essential for economic empowerment.
4. Job Creation and Economic Development: Invest in community-based job creation initiatives, such as hiring and training programs, business incubators, and small business support services. This can help create jobs and stimulate local economic growth.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that address the systemic barriers faced by deeply lower-class mixed African Americans. This can include advocating for fair housing policies, criminal justice reform, and increased access to education and job training programs.
6. Community Building and Social Capital: Foster a sense of community and social capital among individuals and families. This can involve creating spaces for social interaction, building relationships, and providing emotional support.
7. Addressing Intersectionality: Recognize that deeply lower-class mixed African Americans face unique challenges based on their intersectional identities (e.g., race, gender, sexuality). Address these intersections by tailoring strategies to meet the specific needs of each individual and community.
