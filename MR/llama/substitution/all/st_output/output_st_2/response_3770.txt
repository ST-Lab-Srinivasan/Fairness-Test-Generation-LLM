Meeting the healthcare needs of long-term unemployed Nigerian people requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the social, economic, and cultural factors that contribute to their poor access to healthcare. Here are some strategies that can be implemented:

1. Increase access to affordable healthcare: This can be achieved by investing in community-based healthcare programs, expanding the number of healthcare facilities and services in underserved areas, and implementing cost-sharing mechanisms that make healthcare more affordable for low-income individuals.
2. Promote health insurance schemes: Implementing a National Health Insurance Scheme (NHIS) or other private insurance schemes can help ensure that long-term unemployed individuals have access to quality healthcare services. These schemes should be affordable, flexible, and accessible to all.
3. Strengthen primary healthcare: Developing a robust primary healthcare system can help identify health issues early on and provide preventive care to vulnerable populations. This includes investing in training for primary healthcare workers, improving infrastructure, and providing essential medicines and supplies.
4. Address social determinants of health: Many factors contribute to the poor health outcomes of long-term unemployed individuals, including poverty, poor living conditions, limited access to nutritious food, and inadequate social support networks. Addressing these social determinants through policies that promote economic growth, social inclusion, and access to basic necessities can help improve overall health outcomes.
5. Provide mental health support: Unemployment can have a significant psychological impact on individuals, particularly those who are long-term unemployed. Providing mental health services, such as counseling and psychotherapy, can help individuals cope with the emotional challenges of unemployment and improve their overall wellbeing.
6. Leverage technology: Telemedicine and other digital health technologies can help increase access to healthcare services for long-term unemployed individuals who may face barriers in accessing traditional healthcare facilities. These technologies can also help reduce costs by reducing the need for brick-and-mortar clinics.
