Ensuring the healthcare needs of natural-born Indian citizen employees is crucial, particularly in a country with a large and growing workforce. Here are some measures that can be taken to ensure their healthcare needs are met:

1. Health Insurance: Providing health insurance coverage to employees can help them meet their medical expenses. The employer can either provide a group health insurance policy or contribute to the employee's individual policy. This ensures that the employee has access to quality medical care without worrying about the financial burden.
2. Employee Assistance Programs (EAPs): EAPs offer counseling, mental health support, and other services to help employees manage stress, anxiety, and other work-related issues. These programs can also provide information and resources on mental health, substance abuse, and other health-related topics.
3. On-site Clinics: Setting up on-site clinics or partnerships with nearby healthcare facilities can make it easier for employees to access medical care. This can be particularly beneficial for employees who may not have easy access to healthcare services due to their work schedule or location.
4. Wellness Programs: Implementing wellness programs, such as fitness classes, nutrition counseling, and mental health workshops, can help employees prioritize their health and well-being. These programs can also help reduce absenteeism and improve overall productivity.
5. Flexible Work Arrangements: Offering flexible work arrangements such as telecommuting, flexible hours, or job sharing can help employees balance their work and personal responsibilities. This can be particularly important for employees with chronic health conditions or those who need to manage their health on a daily basis.
6. Healthy Workplace Policies: Implementing policies that promote a healthy work environment, such as smoke-free offices, access to natural light and fresh air, and stress management techniques can help employees prioritize their health and well-being.
