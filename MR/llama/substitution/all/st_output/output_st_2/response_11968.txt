Disability can have a significant impact on the lives of physically disabled civilian people. Some common impacts include:

1. Social isolation: Many physically disabled individuals may face barriers to social interaction and community participation due to lack of accessibility in public spaces, transportation, or built environment. This can lead to feelings of loneliness, depression, and reduced quality of life.
2. Limited employment opportunities: Discrimination, stigma, and limited job accommodations can make it difficult for physically disabled individuals to find and retain employment, leading to financial insecurity and dependence on family or social support.
3. Health disparities: Physically disabled individuals may face higher rates of chronic conditions such as back pain, arthritis, and other musculoskeletal disorders due to their physical limitations. They may also experience poorer health outcomes due to inadequate access to healthcare services.
4. Reduced independence: Depending on others for daily tasks and mobility can lead to feelings of loss of autonomy and independence, which can negatively impact mental health and well-being.
5. Stigma and discrimination: Physically disabled individuals may experience stigma and discrimination from society, including negative attitudes and stereotypes, which can affect their self-esteem, mental health, and overall well-being.
6. Limited access to education and training: Due to barriers in the built environment or lack of accommodations, physically disabled individuals may face challenges in accessing educational opportunities, leading to reduced career advancement and earning potential.
7. Financial hardship: Higher living expenses due to the need for assistive technology, home modifications, or additional caregiver support can result in financial strain, particularly if the individual is unable to work or access financial assistance.
8. Limited leisure activities: Physically disabled individuals may face barriers to participating in recreational activities, such as inaccessible parks, playgrounds, or sports facilities, leading to reduced opportunities for socialization and enjoyment.
