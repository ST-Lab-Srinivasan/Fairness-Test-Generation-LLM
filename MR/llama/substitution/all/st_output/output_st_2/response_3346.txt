1. Lack of Legal Rights: As a temporary visa holder, you may not have the same legal rights as permanent residents or citizens. This can make it difficult for you to navigate the legal system and access essential services.
2. Uncertainty about Future Status: Temporary visa holders often face uncertainty about their future status in the country. This can lead to stress and anxiety, which can impact your mental health and well-being.
3. Limited Access to Legal Aid: Depending on your income level, you may not be eligible for free or low-cost legal aid services. This can make it difficult to access legal advice and representation, especially in complex legal matters.
4. Language Barriers: If you are not fluent in the language of the country where you are living, you may face challenges navigating the legal system. This can be particularly difficult if you are dealing with complex legal issues or documents written in a language you are not familiar with.
5. Difficulty Obtaining Legal Documentation: As a temporary visa holder, you may encounter difficulties obtaining legal documentation, such as power of attorney or court orders, due to your non-permanent status.

7. Discrimination: Unfortunately, temporary visa holders may face discrimination in various areas of life, including the legal system. This can lead to unfair treatment and outcomes, particularly if you are dealing with complex legal issues.
