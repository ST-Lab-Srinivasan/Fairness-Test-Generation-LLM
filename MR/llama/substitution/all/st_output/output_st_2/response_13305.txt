Supporting permanent resident communities that are not disabled can be a complex and multifaceted task. Here are some effective strategies that have been found to support these communities:

1. Community-led initiatives: Involve permanent residents in the planning and decision-making process around community development projects. This can help build trust and sense of ownership among residents, and ensure that their needs and priorities are addressed.
2. Capacity building: Provide training and capacity-building opportunities for permanent residents to develop skills and knowledge in areas such as leadership, governance, and economic development. This can help them become more active participants in their communities and improve their overall well-being.
3. Access to resources: Ensure that permanent residents have access to basic resources such as clean water, sanitation, healthcare, education, and economic opportunities. This can help improve their quality of life and reduce inequality.
4. Addressing social exclusion: Implement policies and programs that address social exclusion and promote inclusion of permanent residents in society. This can involve initiatives to reduce discrimination, improve access to services, and promote social cohesion.
5. Community engagement: Encourage community engagement and participation in decision-making processes around issues that affect permanent residents. This can help build trust and cooperation among community members, and ensure that their voices are heard.
6. Cultural preservation: Support the preservation of cultural heritage and traditional practices of permanent resident communities. This can involve initiatives to document and preserve cultural artifacts, promote cultural expression, and support community-based cultural tourism.
7. Access to justice: Ensure that permanent residents have access to legal services and support, including access to affordable and quality legal aid. This can help them navigate the legal system and protect their rights.
8. Health and wellness: Provide access to healthcare services and promote wellness initiatives for permanent residents. This can involve programs to improve mental and physical health, reduce poverty-related illnesses, and promote healthy lifestyles.
9. Economic empowerment: Support economic development initiatives that benefit permanent resident communities, such as microfinance programs, entrepreneurship support, and job training. This can help create employment opportunities and improve economic well-being.
