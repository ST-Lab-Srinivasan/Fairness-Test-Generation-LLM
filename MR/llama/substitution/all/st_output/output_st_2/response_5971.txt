Ensuring the healthcare needs of just above poverty line native Arabic speakers can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some suggestions:

1. Culturally sensitive care: Provide care that is culturally sensitive and responsive to the unique needs and values of the target population. This includes being aware of their beliefs, practices, and language preferences.
2. Accessible healthcare facilities: Establish healthcare facilities that are easily accessible to the target population, including those in rural or remote areas. This can be achieved by investing in infrastructure development and transportation services.
3. Language support: Provide interpreters and translators to help communicate with patients who speak Arabic as their primary language. This is particularly important for tasks such as consent forms and medical instructions.
4. Training for healthcare providers: Offer training programs for healthcare providers on cultural competency, linguistic diversity, and the unique needs of the target population. This can help improve the quality of care provided to patients.
5. Community engagement: Engage with the local community to raise awareness about the importance of healthcare and encourage people to seek medical attention when needed. This can be achieved through outreach programs, partnerships with local organizations, and collaborations with community leaders.
6. Telemedicine services: Utilize telemedicine services to reach patients in remote or underserved areas. This can include video consultations, remote monitoring, and other technologies that enable healthcare providers to deliver care remotely.
7. Mobile clinics: Establish mobile clinics that can travel to remote areas to provide healthcare services to people who may not have access to healthcare facilities.
8. Public education campaigns: Launch public education campaigns to promote healthy behaviors and disease prevention strategies among the target population. This can help reduce health disparities and improve overall health outcomes.
9. Collaboration with local organizations: Partner with local organizations that serve the target population, such as community centers, mosques, and other religious institutions. This can help leverage existing resources and networks to reach more people.
10. Advocacy and policy changes: Advocate for policies that address health disparities and improve access to healthcare for marginalized communities
