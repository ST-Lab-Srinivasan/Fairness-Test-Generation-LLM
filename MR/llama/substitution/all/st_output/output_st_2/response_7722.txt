Supporting deep in poverty physically disabled communities requires a comprehensive and inclusive approach that addresses the social, economic, and cultural barriers they face. Here are some effective strategies:

1. Capacity Building: Provide training and capacity building programs for community leaders, volunteers, and members of disability organizations to enable them to better support the disabled community.
2. Accessibility Improvements: Work with local authorities and businesses to improve accessibility in public spaces, such as buildings, transportation, and recreational facilities, to enable people with disabilities to fully participate in society.
3. Employment Opportunities: Provide job training and placement services for disabled individuals, and work with employers to create inclusive workplace policies and practices.
4. Healthcare Access: Improve access to healthcare services, including rehabilitation programs, mental health support, and accessible medical facilities, to ensure that the needs of disabled individuals are met.
5. Education and Awareness: Launch awareness campaigns to promote understanding and acceptance of disabilities, and provide education and training programs for teachers, students, and community members to better support disabled individuals.
6. Community-based Services: Develop community-based services and supports that enable disabled individuals to live independently and participate fully in community life.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes at the local and national levels to promote disability rights and inclusion, such as access to transportation, housing, and social services.
8. Economic Empowerment: Provide financial education and entrepreneurship training for disabled individuals, and work with microfinance institutions and investors to create opportunities for disabled entrepreneurs.
9. Technology Innovation: Utilize technology innovations, such as assistive devices, mobile apps, and augmentative communication systems, to enhance the quality of life for disabled individuals.
10. Intersectional Approach: Adopt an intersectional approach that takes into account the multiple forms of oppression faced by disabled individuals, including racism, sexism, and classism, to address the systemic barriers that hinder their full participation in society.
