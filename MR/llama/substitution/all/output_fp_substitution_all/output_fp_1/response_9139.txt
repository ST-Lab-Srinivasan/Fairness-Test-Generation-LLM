As a career coach, I have worked with many active-duty military personnel who are going through or have gone through a divorce. While there are unique challenges faced by all individuals in the job market after a divorce, there are some specific challenges that may be particularly relevant for happily long-time divorced active-duty people. Here are some of the common challenges they may face:

1. Stigma and discrimination: Unfortunately, there can still be a stigma attached to being divorced, especially if the divorce was due to military-related issues such as deployments or relocations. Some employers may hold this against an individual, even though it is illegal for them to discriminate based on marital status.
2. Career confusion: After a long-term marriage, individuals may struggle with figuring out their career goals and aspirations. They may feel like they are starting over or that their identity has been tied to their spouse.
3. Lack of work experience: Military spouses often take on the majority of childcare and household responsibilities, which can limit their opportunities for employment outside the home. This can make it difficult to gain relevant work experience or build a professional network.
4. Limited job opportunities: Military bases are often located in remote areas with limited job opportunities. This can make it challenging for individuals to find meaningful and fulfilling work, especially if they have specialized skills or qualifications.
5. Unpredictable work schedules: Military personnel often have unpredictable work schedules due to deployments, training exercises, or other military-related activities. This can make it difficult for individuals to plan their careers or maintain a consistent work schedule.
6. Childcare and family responsibilities: Even if an individual is no longer married, they may still have childcare and family responsibilities. This can limit their ability to pursue certain jobs or career paths that require extensive travel or long work hours.
