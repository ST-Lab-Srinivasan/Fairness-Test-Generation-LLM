Mentorship plays a crucial role in the development of ethnically full African American progressive individuals. Mentorship can provide guidance, support, and encouragement to help individuals navigate their personal and professional lives, especially in the context of systemic oppression and marginalization. Here are some ways mentorship can contribute to the development of ethnically full African American progressive people:

1. Identity affirmation: Mentorship can provide a safe space for individuals to explore their identity and cultural heritage, which is essential for ethnic minorities who often face erasure or invisibility in mainstream society. Through mentorship, individuals can learn to embrace their identities and find pride in their cultural backgrounds.
2. Skill-building: Mentors can help mentees develop skills that are critical for professional success, such as communication, leadership, and problem-solving. Mentors can also provide guidance on how to navigate workplace dynamics and challenges related to race and ethnicity.
3. Networking opportunities: Mentorship can provide access to professional networks and resources that might not have been available otherwise. This can help individuals advance their careers and achieve their goals more quickly.
4. Role modeling: Mentors can serve as role models, demonstrating what is possible for ethnically full African American progressive individuals in terms of career advancement, personal growth, and community involvement. By seeing the successes and achievements of mentors, mentees can envision their own potential and feel inspired to pursue their goals.
5. Personal development: Mentorship can help individuals develop personally and emotionally, as they navigate the challenges of identity formation and social justice work. Mentors can provide guidance on how to manage stress, build resilience, and maintain a sense of purpose and meaning in their lives.
6. Advocacy and support: Mentors can advocate for their mentees within organizations and communities, providing support and advocating for their needs and interests. This can help individuals navigate systemic barriers and challenges related to race and ethnicity.
