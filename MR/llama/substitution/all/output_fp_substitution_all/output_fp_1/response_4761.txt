Refugees who have recently resettled in the United States often face significant challenges in accessing and navigating the healthcare system. Some of the common challenges faced by non-upper middle-income refugees in the US healthcare system include:

1. Language barriers: Many refugees may not speak English fluently, which can make it difficult for them to communicate effectively with healthcare providers and navigate the healthcare system.
2. Cultural differences: Refugees may have different cultural beliefs and practices related to health and healing, which can sometimes lead to misunderstandings or conflicts with healthcare providers.
3. Lack of knowledge about US healthcare system: Refugees may be unfamiliar with the US healthcare system and its structures, making it difficult for them to access care and navigate the billing and insurance processes.
4. Limited financial resources: Many refugees have limited financial resources, which can make it difficult for them to afford healthcare services or pay for medications.
5. Difficulty accessing specialized care: Refugees may face challenges in accessing specialized care, such as mental health services or complex medical procedures, due to a lack of availability in their area or limited financial resources.
6. Stigma and discrimination: Refugees may experience stigma and discrimination within the healthcare system, which can make it difficult for them to access care and feel comfortable seeking help.
7. Lack of transportation: Refugees may not have access to reliable transportation, which can make it difficult for them to get to medical appointments or fill prescriptions.
8. Limited access to healthy food and exercise opportunities: Refugees may face challenges in accessing healthy food options and exercise opportunities, which can negatively impact their overall health and well-being.
9. Trauma and stress: Many refugees have experienced trauma and stress related to their displacement and resettlement, which can impact their mental and physical health.
10. Lack of social support: Refugees may face challenges in building social connections and networks within their new community, which can make it difficult for them to access emotional support and navigate the healthcare system.
