2. Cultural sensitivity: Be sensitive to the cultural norms and practices of the local community, especially when it comes to gender segregation and dress codes. Provide separate areas or times for men and women to use the public space, and ensure that both genders feel comfortable and included.
3. Language support: Offer language support for non-Arabic speakers, such as translation services or multilingual signage. This can help bridge the language barrier and make the public space more inclusive.
4. Inclusive programming: Develop programming that caters to a diverse range of interests and needs, including cultural events, workshops, and activities that promote social inclusion.
5. Partnerships with community groups: Collaborate with local community groups and organizations that support marginalized or underrepresented communities, such as women's rights groups or LGBTQ+ organizations. This can help create a more inclusive atmosphere and ensure that the public space is welcoming to everyone.

7. Feedback mechanisms: Establish feedback mechanisms that allow users to provide input on their experiences in the public space. This can help identify areas for improvement and ensure that the space is meeting the needs of all users.
8. Flexible spaces: Create flexible spaces that can be adapted to suit different user groups, such as a multi-purpose room that can be used for events or activities. This can help foster a sense of community and inclusivity among users.
9. Access to technology: Provide access to technology, such as Wi-Fi or digital tools, that can help non-Arabic speakers navigate the public space and access information.
