Advocating for the rights of jobless teenagers (20s) can be a challenging task, but there are several effective ways to promote their interests and ensure their well-being. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Educate and raise awareness: Many people are not aware of the struggles faced by young adults who are unemployed or underemployed. Educating them about the issue can help build support for advocacy efforts. Use social media, write articles, attend community events, and participate in local politics to raise awareness.
2. Engage with policymakers: Reach out to elected officials and policymakers to advocate for policies that benefit young adults. Attend town hall meetings, write letters to your representatives, and participate in public hearings to make your voice heard. Encourage others to do the same to create a collective impact.
3. Organize protests and demonstrations: Organizing peaceful protests or demonstrations can raise awareness about the issue and put pressure on policymakers to take action. Use social media to promote the event, and invite other advocacy groups to join you in solidarity.
4. Create a petition or campaign: Start a petition or campaign to gather support for your cause. Use social media platforms like Change.org or Petition2Congress to create an online petition, or use paper petitions at local events. Share the petition with your network and encourage others to sign and share it as well.
5. Partner with businesses and organizations: Collaborate with local businesses, non-profits, and community organizations to amplify your advocacy efforts. They may be able to provide resources, support, or even host events to help raise awareness about the issue.
6. Provide job training and placement services: Many young adults lack the skills and experience needed to compete in the job market. Offer job training and placement services to help them gain valuable work experience and build their professional networks.
7. Advocate for policies that benefit young adults: Work with policymakers to implement policies that support young adults, such as affordable housing, student loan forgiveness programs, and expanded access to healthcare.
