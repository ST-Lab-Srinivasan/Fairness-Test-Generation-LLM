Ensuring the educational needs of long-time civilian ethnic African American students are met requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers to incorporate culturally responsive teaching practices into their instruction. This includes understanding the cultural backgrounds of students, using diverse materials and activities, and creating an inclusive learning environment.
2. Identity-based education: Incorporate lessons that help students understand and celebrate their identities, including their ethnicity, culture, and history. This can help students feel seen and valued in the educational setting.
3. Support systems: Establish support systems for African American students, such as mentorship programs, academic clubs, and peer-to-peer tutoring. These systems can provide additional academic and social support to help students succeed.
4. Addressing systemic barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers that may hinder the educational success of African American students, such as inadequate resources, biases, and stereotypes. This includes advocating for policies and practices that promote equity and inclusion.
5. Community partnerships: Develop partnerships with local African American communities to provide additional support and resources to students. This can include mentorship programs, cultural events, and internship opportunities.
6. Teacher diversification: Increase the diversity of teachers in schools to better reflect the demographics of students. Research has shown that students learn better from teachers who look like them or share similar experiences.
7. Addressing trauma: Recognize the impact of systemic racism and trauma on African American students' educational outcomes. Provide training for teachers on how to address trauma and mental health in the classroom.
8. Parental involvement: Encourage and support parental involvement in schools, particularly from African American parents who may face additional barriers to engagement. This can include language and cultural sensitivity training for teachers and staff.
9. Curriculum reform: Review and revise curricula to ensure they are inclusive and culturally responsive. This includes incorporating diverse perspectives, histories, and cultures into the curriculum.
