Addressing the specific needs of Native American recent temporary visa holders to the US for mental health conditions in education requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique cultural background, historical trauma, and current challenges. Here are some strategies that educators and mental health professionals can implement:

1. Cultural Humility and Safety:
	* Provide training on cultural humility and safety for educators to ensure they can create a safe and inclusive learning environment for Native American students.
	* Encourage educators to listen actively and empathetically to the experiences of Native American students, without interrupting or judging their stories.
2. Incorporate Cultural Teachings and Traditions:
	* Incorporate traditional practices and teachings from Native American cultures into the educational curriculum.
	* Invite Native American elders, leaders, or cultural practitioners to share their knowledge and traditions with students.
3. Address Historical Trauma:
	* Provide education on the historical trauma faced by Native American communities, including colonization, forced assimilation, and boarding schools.
	* Encourage students to express their feelings and experiences regarding these historical events through art, writing, or other forms of expression.
4. Foster a Sense of Belonging:
	* Create opportunities for Native American students to connect with their peers and feel a sense of belonging within the school community.
	* Encourage mentorship programs that pair Native American students with older or more experienced students who can provide support and guidance.
5. Provide Access to Mental Health Services:
	* Offer mental health services specifically tailored for Native American students, including traditional healing practices and Western-style counseling services.
	* Collaborate with local tribes or organizations to provide culturally responsive mental health services that address the unique needs of Native American communities.
6. Involve Families and Communities:
	* Engage families and communities in the educational process by providing opportunities for them to learn about the cultural backgrounds of their children and how they can support their academic success.
