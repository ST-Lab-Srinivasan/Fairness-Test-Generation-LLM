Creating inclusive community programs for Nigerian recently single individuals who have gone through multiple divorces or separations can be challenging, but there are several steps that can be taken to make these programs more welcoming and supportive. Here are some suggestions:

1. Understand the needs of the target group: It is essential to understand the unique needs and challenges faced by recently single individuals who have gone through multiple divorces or separations. Conduct research or surveys to gather information on their experiences, struggles, and aspirations.
2. Provide a safe space: Create a safe and non-judgmental environment where participants can share their experiences without fear of criticism or discrimination. This can be achieved by setting clear ground rules for the program, providing confidentiality, and ensuring that all interactions are respectful and empathetic.
3. Offer counseling and therapy: Provide access to professional counseling and therapy services to help participants deal with the emotional challenges of going through multiple divorces or separations. This can include individual and group sessions, as well as workshops on managing stress, anxiety, and depression.
4. Foster a sense of community: Encourage participants to connect with each other through various activities, such as group discussions, social events, and volunteer opportunities. This can help build a sense of belonging and support, which is essential for mental well-being.
5. Address financial challenges: Many recently single individuals face significant financial challenges after going through multiple divorces or separations. Provide access to financial literacy workshops, budgeting assistance, and job training programs to help participants gain financial stability.
6. Promote healthy relationships: Encourage participants to develop healthy relationship skills through workshops, group discussions, and one-on-one coaching sessions. This can include learning how to communicate effectively, set boundaries, and recognize red flags in relationships.
7. Provide access to resources: Connect participants with local resources such as legal aid services, job placement agencies, and educational institutions that offer programs for single parents or individuals going through transition.
8. Celebrate diversity: Acknowledge the diversity of experiences among participants and create a program that is inclusive of all backgrounds, cultures, and identities.
