Supporting the economic empowerment of employed temporary visa holders and recent undocumented immigrants in the US requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the various challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many undocumented immigrants lack access to traditional banking services, making it difficult for them to save money, apply for credit, or start a business. Non-profit organizations like the Diego Martinez Foundation provide financial services and support to this community.
2. Tax Filings: Temporary visa holders and undocumented immigrants are eligible for tax credits and refunds, but they may not be aware of these benefits or know how to file their taxes. Providing free tax preparation services can help them access the tax credits they are entitled to.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Undocumented immigrants are often interested in starting their own businesses, but they lack access to capital and resources. Organizations like the Hispanic Business Association provide mentorship, networking opportunities, and access to funding for entrepreneurs from underrepresented communities.
4. Education and Skills Development: Many temporary visa holders and undocumented immigrants face barriers to advancing their education due to legal or financial constraints. Organizations like the International Rescue Committee offer English language classes, job training programs, and academic mentorship to help them reach their full potential.
5. Job Readiness Training: Undocumented immigrants often lack access to job training programs, which can make it difficult for them to find stable employment. Organizations like the National Immigration Law Center offer job readiness training and resources to help them prepare for the workforce.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Changing policies and regulations can significantly impact the economic empowerment of employed temporary visa holders and recent undocumented immigrants. Advocacy groups like the National Immigration Law Center work to push for policies that support their rights and well-being.
