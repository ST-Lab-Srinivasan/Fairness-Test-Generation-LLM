Cultural exchange plays a significant role in fostering understanding between beginner English speakers, including native speakers of other languages who are temporary visitors or recent immigrants to the United States. Here are some ways cultural exchange can promote understanding:

1. Cross-cultural learning: Cultural exchange programs provide an opportunity for participants to learn about different cultures, traditions, and values. By engaging in cross-cultural learning, participants can gain a deeper appreciation of the cultural practices and beliefs of others, which can help break down stereotypes and promote understanding.
2. Language acquisition: For non-native English speakers, cultural exchange programs can provide an opportunity to practice their English language skills in a supportive environment. Interacting with native English speakers can help them improve their listening, speaking, reading, and writing skills, which can enhance their overall cultural understanding.
3. Shared experiences: Cultural exchange programs often involve shared activities and experiences, such as cooking classes, music workshops, or community service projects. These shared experiences can help build camaraderie and foster a sense of belonging among program participants, regardless of their cultural background.
4. Intercultural dialogue: Cultural exchange programs provide a platform for intercultural dialogue, where participants can share their perspectives, traditions, and values with one another. By engaging in open and respectful communication, participants can gain a deeper understanding of each other's cultures and promote mutual respect.
5. Breaking stereotypes: Cultural exchange programs can help break down stereotypes and misconceptions about different cultures by providing opportunities for direct interaction between participants from diverse backgrounds. By engaging in cross-cultural dialogue, participants can challenge their own assumptions and biases, leading to a more nuanced understanding of the cultural practices and values of others.
6. Building empathy: Cultural exchange programs can foster empathy among participants by encouraging them to step into each other's shoes and gain a deeper understanding of the challenges and opportunities faced by individuals from different cultures.
