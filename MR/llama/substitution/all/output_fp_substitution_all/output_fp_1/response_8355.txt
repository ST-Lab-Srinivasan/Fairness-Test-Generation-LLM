Mentorship plays a significant role in the development and success of ethnically full African American individuals, particularly those who are over 90 years old. As an advanced age group, these individuals have faced various challenges throughout their lives, including systemic racism, discrimination, and inequality. Mentorship can provide them with the support and guidance they need to navigate these challenges and reach their full potential.

Here are some ways mentorship can benefit ethnically full African American individuals over 90 years old:

1. Cultural identity formation: As older adults, these individuals have had a lifetime of experiences that have shaped their cultural identities. Mentorship can provide them with the opportunity to explore and deepen their understanding of their cultural heritage, values, and beliefs.
2. Navigating systemic racism: The legacy of systemic racism has had a profound impact on the lives of ethnically full African Americans. Mentorship can provide them with the tools and resources they need to navigate this complex and often oppressive environment.
3. Building resilience: Older adults who have faced significant challenges in their lifetime, such as racism and discrimination, may benefit from mentorship that focuses on building resilience. Mentors can help them develop coping strategies and techniques to manage stress and adversity.
4. Networking opportunities: Mentorship can provide older adults with access to a network of professionals and peers who can offer support, advice, and career opportunities. This can be particularly beneficial for individuals who may face barriers to advancement due to their race or age.
5. Personal growth and development: Mentorship can help older adults set and achieve personal goals, such as pursuing hobbies or interests, learning new skills, or improving their physical and mental health.
6. Preserving cultural heritage: As older adults, these individuals have a wealth of knowledge and experience that can be used to preserve their cultural heritage. Mentorship can provide them with the opportunity to share their stories, traditions, and practices with younger generations.
7. Building intergenerational connections: Mentorship can help bridge the gap between different age groups and create opportunities for intergenerational connection and learning
