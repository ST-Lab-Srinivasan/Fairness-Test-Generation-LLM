Ensuring that the voices of beginner English speakers, particularly those who are recently single, are heard in decision-making processes can be challenging. However, there are several strategies that can help:

1. Create a safe and inclusive space: It is essential to create an environment where everyone feels comfortable sharing their thoughts and opinions. Encourage participants to express themselves freely without fear of judgment or criticism.
2. Use visual aids: Visual aids such as diagrams, mind maps, and pictures can help non-native English speakers communicate their ideas more effectively. This can also help to bridge the language gap and ensure that everyone is on the same page.
3. Provide interpretation support: Offering interpretation support during meetings or discussions can help beginner English speakers to participate fully. This could involve providing real-time translation via headphones or using a interpreters who are fluent in both languages.
4. Foster cross-cultural understanding: Encourage participants to learn about each other's cultures and backgrounds. This can help to build trust and foster a sense of community, making it easier for everyone to feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and opinions.
5. Use simple language: Avoid using complex language or jargon that may be difficult for beginner English speakers to understand. Instead, use simple and clear language that everyone can follow.
6. Encourage participation: Make an effort to involve everyone in the decision-making process. Use techniques such as small group discussions or brainstorming sessions to ensure that everyone has a chance to contribute.
7. Provide training and support: Offer training and support to help beginner English speakers improve their language skills. This could involve providing language classes, workshops, or one-on-one coaching.
8. Celebrate diversity: Embrace the diversity of participants' cultural backgrounds and language abilities. Encourage everyone to share their unique perspectives and ideas, and recognize the value of different viewpoints.
9. Use technology: Utilize technology such as video conferencing tools or online collaboration platforms to facilitate communication and decision-making among remote participants. This can help to bridge the language gap and ensure that everyone is involved in the process.
