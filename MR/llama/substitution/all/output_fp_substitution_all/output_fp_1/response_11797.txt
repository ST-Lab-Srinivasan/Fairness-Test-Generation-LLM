1. Identify and address systemic barriers: Deep poverty, lifelong poverty, and divorce often result from systemic barriers such as discrimination, lack of access to education and job opportunities, and limited social safety nets. Policies should aim to address these underlying issues to create a more level playing field.
2. Increase access to education and job training: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help individuals acquire the skills they need to secure better-paying jobs and escape poverty. This could include vocational training, apprenticeships, and college scholarships.
3. Implement a living wage: Ensuring that everyone has access to a living wage can help alleviate poverty. A living wage is the amount of money needed to meet basic needs such as housing, food, and healthcare. Policies could aim to increase the minimum wage or provide incentives for employers to pay their employees a living wage.
4. Provide affordable housing: Lack of affordable housing can exacerbate poverty. Policies could focus on increasing the supply of affordable housing, providing rental assistance, and offering support for homebuyers.
5. Strengthen social safety nets: Social safety nets such as unemployment insurance, food assistance programs, and healthcare can provide a critical layer of support for individuals facing poverty. Policies could aim to expand and improve these programs to better address the needs of deep poverty, lifelong poverty, and divorced individuals.
