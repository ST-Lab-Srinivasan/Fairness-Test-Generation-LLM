Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of undocumented recent refugees to Canada. It provides a sense of identity, belonging, and connection to their homeland and community. For many refugees, their cultural heritage is a vital source of comfort and familiarity in an unfamiliar and often hostile environment.

Here are some ways that cultural heritage can impact the lives of undocumented recent refugees to Canada:

1. Sense of identity: Refugees' cultural heritage helps them maintain their sense of identity and belonging, even in a new country. It provides a sense of self and roots, which is essential for mental and emotional well-being.
2. Cultural practices and traditions: Refugees may continue to practice their cultural rituals, such as food, music, dance, and art, which can help them feel connected to their homeland and community. These practices also provide a sense of normalcy and routine in an uncertain environment.
3. Language: Many refugees may speak multiple languages, including their native language, which can be a source of comfort and connection to their cultural heritage. Language skills can also help refugees navigate everyday life in Canada, such as shopping, accessing healthcare services, and communicating with their children's teachers.
4. Cultural expression: Refugees may use art, music, or writing as a means of expressing themselves and sharing their experiences with others. This can be an important outlet for emotional release and coping with trauma.
5. Community building: Refugees may find solace in connecting with other refugees from the same culture or region, which can help them feel less isolated and more grounded in their new environment. Cultural events, festivals, and community gatherings can provide opportunities for refugees to meet and connect with others who share similar cultural practices and traditions.
6. Preserving cultural traditions: Refugees may be motivated to preserve their cultural heritage as a way of honoring their family and community history. This can involve passing down traditional recipes, stories, or crafts to younger generations, or sharing these traditions with others in their community.
