2. Depression: Discrimination can lead to feelings of isolation, low self-esteem, and a loss of purpose, which can develop into clinical depression if not addressed.
3. Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD): The trauma of experiencing discrimination, particularly in the context of migration, can lead to symptoms of PTSD, such as flashbacks, nightmares, and avoidance behaviors.
4. Identity confusion: Discrimination can lead to feelings of not belonging or being rejected by one's own community, leading to confusion about one's identity and sense of self.
5. Social isolation: Fear of discrimination can lead to social isolation, which can exacerbate mental health problems and limit access to social support networks.
6. Stigma: Discrimination can perpetuate negative stereotypes and stigma against marginalized groups, leading to internalized oppression and self-blame.
7. Lack of trust: Discrimination can erode trust in institutions, community leaders, and government agencies, making it difficult for individuals to access support services or advocate for their rights.
