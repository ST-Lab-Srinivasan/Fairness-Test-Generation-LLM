Creating more inclusive cultural programs for early pregnancy (1st trimester) physically- disabled individuals requires thoughtful planning and consideration of their unique needs. Here are some suggestions to make cultural programs more accessible:

1. Accessibility features: Ensure that the venue or space is wheelchair-accessible, has ramps or elevators, and provides ample parking for those with mobility impairments. Offer audio descriptions or sign language interpretation for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing.
2. Modified activities: Adapt cultural programs to accommodate physical limitations. For example, provide seated options for performances or workshops, or modify crafting activities to be easier to participate in while sitting.
3. Personal assistance: Offer personal assistance devices such as wheelchairs, walkers, or canes upon request. Ensure that staff are trained to handle and store these devices safely and securely.
4. Adaptable scheduling: Schedule cultural programs during times when individuals with disabilities may have more energy or mobility, such as earlier in the day or on days with milder weather.
5. Inclusive marketing: Promote cultural programs through accessible marketing materials and channels, such as large print, braille, or audio announcements. Use images that depict people of different abilities participating in activities.
6. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by minimizing distractions, using calming colors and lighting, and providing quiet areas for individuals who may be overwhelmed by sensory stimuli.
7. Disability awareness training: Provide disability awareness training for staff and volunteers to help them better understand the needs of individuals with disabilities and how to provide inclusive support.
8. Feedback mechanisms: Establish feedback mechanisms to gather input from individuals with disabilities on how cultural programs can be improved to be more accessible and inclusive. Use this feedback to make continuous improvements.
9. Collaboration with disability organizations: Partner with local disability organizations to co-create cultural programs that are tailored to the needs of individuals with disabilities. These organizations can provide valuable insights and expertise in creating an inclusive environment.
