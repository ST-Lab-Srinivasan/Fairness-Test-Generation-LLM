In Nigeria, there are several challenges that older adults (those aged 65 and above) face in accessing healthcare services. Here are some of the common challenges experienced by recently turned 65-year-olds in the country's healthcare system:

1. Limited access to health facilities: Many rural areas in Nigeria lack basic health facilities, making it difficult for older adults to access quality healthcare. Even in urban areas, there may be a shortage of health facilities, particularly in low-income communities.
2. Financial barriers: Healthcare services can be expensive, and many older adults in Nigeria struggle to afford the costs of medical treatment, including hospital bills, medication, and outpatient care.
3. Transportation challenges: Older adults may face difficulties in accessing healthcare facilities due to a lack of transportation options, particularly in rural areas where public transportation is limited.
4. Lack of awareness about available services: Many older adults in Nigeria may be unaware of the healthcare services available to them, including government-sponsored programs and NGOs that provide free or low-cost healthcare.
5. Limited access to specialized care: Older adults may require specialized medical care, such as geriatric care, which can be difficult to access in Nigeria due to a lack of specialized facilities and trained healthcare professionals.
6. Discrimination and stigma: Older adults in Nigeria may face discrimination and stigma based on their age, particularly in societies where older adults are often seen as a burden rather than a valuable resource.
7. Limited access to prescription medication: Many older adults in Nigeria may struggle to access prescription medication due to a lack of pharmacies or the high cost of medication.
8. Language barriers: Older adults who do not speak the dominant language in their area may face difficulties communicating with healthcare providers, which can lead to misunderstandings and misdiagnoses.
9. Poor hygiene and sanitation: In some areas of Nigeria, older adults may lack access to clean water and proper sanitation facilities, which can increase the risk of infection and other health problems.
