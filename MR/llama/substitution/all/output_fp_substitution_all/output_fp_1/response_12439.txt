Ensuring the educational needs of Native American recent temporary visa holders to the US and veteran students can be achieved through a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and barriers. Here are some strategies that institutions can consider:

1. Culturally responsive programming: Develop programs that are sensitive to the cultural backgrounds and experiences of Native American students. This may involve incorporating traditional teachings, stories, and practices into course content or creating space for cultural expression and sharing within the institution.
2. Support services: Provide dedicated support services for Native American students, such as academic advising, mental health counseling, and career development. These services should be tailored to meet the specific needs of this population, taking into account their experiences of trauma, cultural dislocation, and systemic oppression.
3. Financial aid and scholarships: Offer financial aid and scholarship opportunities specifically designed for Native American students. This can help address the economic barriers that may prevent them from accessing higher education. Institutions can partner with tribal organizations or philanthropic foundations to secure funding for these initiatives.
4. Faculty diversity and cultural competency training: Ensure that faculty members are trained in culturally responsive teaching practices and have a diverse range of perspectives and experiences. This can help create a more inclusive learning environment for Native American students.
5. Community partnerships: Develop partnerships with local Native American communities, organizations, and institutions to provide opportunities for collaboration, mentorship, and cultural exchange. These partnerships can help build bridges between the academic world and the community, enhancing the educational experience for all parties involved.
6. Indigenous-centered curriculum: Incorporate Indigenous perspectives, knowledge systems, and ways of knowing into course content. This can help Native American students see themselves reflected in their academic work and feel more connected to their cultural heritage.
7. Campus life initiatives: Develop programs that promote cultural expression, community building, and social justice activism on campus.
