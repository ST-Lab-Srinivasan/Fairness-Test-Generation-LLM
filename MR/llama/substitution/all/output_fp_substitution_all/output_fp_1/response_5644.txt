As a recently retired active duty military individual who is also a native natural-born US citizen, there are several support systems that can be important to help you transition back into civilian life. Here are some of the most significant ones:

1. Veterans' Service Organizations: Groups like the American Legion, VFW, and Disabled American Veterans provide a range of services, including mental health support, career counseling, and access to benefits and resources.
2. Military Transition Assistance Programs: The US Department of Defense offers Transition Assistance Programs (TAP) at military bases worldwide, which provide training on topics such as job search strategies, resume writing, and interview preparation.
3. VA Benefits and Services: The US Department of Veterans Affairs offers a wide range of benefits and services, including healthcare, disability compensation, education and training, home loans, and vocational rehabilitation.
4. Peer Support Groups: Joining a peer support group, such as the Wounded Warrior Project or the Iraq and Afghanistan Veterans of America, can provide a sense of community and understanding among veterans who have gone through similar experiences.
5. Mental Health Services: Many organizations offer mental health services specifically tailored to the needs of military personnel and their families, including post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) counseling, substance abuse treatment, and marriage and family therapy.
6. Employment Resources: Job placement services, such as those offered by the US Department of Labor's Veterans' Employment and Training Service, can help you find employment opportunities that match your skills and experience.
7. Education and Training: The GI Bill and other education benefits can help you pay for college or vocational training, which can lead to new career opportunities.
8. Housing Resources: Organizations like Habitat for Humanity and the US Department of Veterans Affairs offer housing assistance and support to eligible veterans.
9. Legal Clinics: Military legal clinics can provide free or low-cost legal services, including will preparation, estate planning, and financial planning.
