Ensuring that the voices of physically able-bodied Nigerians are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting inclusivity and equality. Here are some strategies that can be employed to achieve this:

1. Inclusive representation: Ensure that people with disabilities are represented in decision-making bodies, such as government agencies, community organizations, and advocacy groups. This can be achieved by setting aside seats for people with disabilities or by actively seeking out and appointing individuals with disabilities to these positions.
2. Accessible participation: Make sure that decision-making processes are accessible to people with disabilities. This can be done by providing accommodations such as sign language interpreters, Braille materials, and assistive technology.
3. Disability awareness training: Provide disability awareness training for decision-makers and other stakeholders to help them understand the experiences and challenges faced by people with disabilities. This can help reduce stigmatization and promote more inclusive decision-making.
4. Inclusive policy development: Involve people with disabilities in the development of policies that affect their lives. This can be done through participatory processes such as focus groups, consultations, and surveys.
5. Collaboration with disability organizations: Work with disability organizations to amplify the voices of people with disabilities and ensure that their perspectives are represented in decision-making processes.
6. Advocacy and activism: Use advocacy and activism to raise awareness about the needs and experiences of people with disabilities and to push for more inclusive decision-making processes.
7. Mainstreaming disability inclusion: Integrate disability inclusion into existing policies, programs, and initiatives to ensure that people with disabilities are not marginalized or excluded from decision-making processes.
8. Building inclusive communities: Work towards building inclusive communities where people with disabilities are valued and included in all aspects of society, including decision-making processes.
9. Addressing systemic barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers that prevent people with disabilities from participating in decision-making processes, such as lack of accessibility, discrimination, and stigma.
