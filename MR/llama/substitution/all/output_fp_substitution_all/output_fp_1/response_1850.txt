Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for Native American, recent refugees to the US, and secular Jewish individuals requires understanding, respect, and acknowledgment of their cultural practices and beliefs. Here are some suggestions:

1. Inclusive Planning: When planning cultural celebrations, ensure that there is a diverse group of people involved in the planning process. This can include members from different cultural backgrounds, age groups, and abilities. Make sure to listen to their input and perspectives when deciding on traditions and activities.
2. Cultural Awareness Training: Provide training for staff and volunteers involved in organizing cultural celebrations. This can include workshops on cultural sensitivity, respect for diverse beliefs and practices, and how to create inclusive environments.
3. Representation Matters: Ensure that the cultural celebrations reflect the diversity of the community you are serving. Incorporate traditions, music, dance, food, and other elements from different cultures. This can help create a sense of belonging and inclusion for all participants.
4. Accessibility and Accommodation: Make sure that the cultural celebrations are accessible to everyone, regardless of their abilities. Provide accommodations such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
5. Respect and Acknowledgment: Show respect for the cultural practices and beliefs of all communities involved in the celebration. Avoid appropriating or exploiting cultural traditions without proper understanding or permission. Instead, seek to learn from and honor these cultures.
6. Cross-Cultural Collaborations: Encourage collaboration between different cultural groups. This can involve jointly planning events, sharing traditions, and learning from each other's practices. This can help build bridges of understanding and foster a sense of community.
7. Education and Storytelling: Provide educational opportunities for participants to learn about the cultures represented at the celebration. Share stories, histories, and traditions through workshops, presentations, or interactive activities.
8. Inclusive Language: Use inclusive language in promotional materials, program descriptions, and event signage. Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes any cultural group.
