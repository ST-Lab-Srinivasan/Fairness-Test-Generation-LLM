1. Lack of access to legal resources: Upper-class Mexicans may have greater financial and social resources at their disposal, but this does not necessarily translate into better access to legal resources such as quality legal representation.
2. Bias and discrimination: Unfortunately, there is a long history of bias and discrimination against upper-class Mexicans within the legal system, particularly in relation to issues such as property rights and business dealings.

4. Limited representation: Upper-class Mexicans may have limited access to legal representation, particularly in rural or impoverished areas, where legal resources are scarce.
5. Lack of understanding: Upper-class Mexicans may not fully understand the legal system and their rights within it, leading to a lack of awareness and participation in legal proceedings.
6. Inadequate legal protection: Upper-class Mexicans may not have adequate legal protection due to weaknesses in the legal framework or a lack of resources to pursue legal remedies.
7. Corruption: Unfortunately, corruption within the legal system can also affect upper-class Mexicans, particularly in relation to issues such as property rights and business dealings.
8. Lack of trust: Upper-class Mexicans may not trust the legal system due to past experiences of discrimination or a lack of faith in the fairness and impartiality of the courts.
9. Limited access to alternative dispute resolution mechanisms: Upper-class Mexicans may have limited access to alternative dispute resolution mechanisms such as mediation or arbitration, which can be more efficient and cost-effective than going to court.
10. Lack of cultural competency: Upper-class Mexicans may face challenges in navigating a legal system that does not always understand their cultural background or practices, leading to misunderstandings and miscommunications.
