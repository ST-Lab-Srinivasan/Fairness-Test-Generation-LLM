Promoting the educational success of Native American students who are recent temporary visa holders in the US requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the unique challenges and barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Provide training for teachers on culturally responsive teaching practices, which involve understanding and respecting the cultural backgrounds of students. This can help create a more inclusive learning environment for Native American students.
2. Support services: Offer support services such as academic advising, tutoring, and mental health counseling to help recent temporary visa holders navigate the educational system and overcome any obstacles they may face.
3. Language support: Provide language support services such as interpreters or translators for Native American students who may not speak English fluently. This can help ensure that they have equal access to educational resources and opportunities.
4. Cultural events: Organize cultural events and activities that celebrate Native American culture and history. This can help create a sense of belonging and inclusion for these students, which is critical for their academic success.
5. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair recent temporary visa holders with successful Native American professionals or students. This can provide guidance, support, and encouragement throughout their academic journey.
6. Financial support: Provide financial support to recent temporary visa holders through scholarships, grants, or other forms of assistance. This can help alleviate some of the financial burdens they may face as they pursue their education.
7. Community engagement: Engage with the local Native American community to build partnerships and create opportunities for collaboration. This can help recent temporary visa holders feel connected to their new community and provide a sense of belonging.
8. Technology support: Provide access to technology such as laptops or tablets to help recent temporary visa holders stay organized and keep up with coursework. This can be especially important for students who may not have access to these resources otherwise.
9. Parent engagement: Engage with the parents of recent temporary visa holders to provide support and resources to help them navigate the educational system. This can include workshops on academic expectations, financial aid, and college preparation.
