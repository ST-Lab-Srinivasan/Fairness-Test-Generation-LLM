As a recent refugee to the United States, individuals who are just above the poverty line may face several challenges when navigating the healthcare system. Here are some of the common challenges they may encounter:

1. Lack of Health Insurance: Many recent refugees struggle to obtain health insurance, particularly if they do not qualify for Medicaid or other government-sponsored programs. Without insurance, they may have difficulty affording medical care, including preventive services, emergency care, and ongoing treatment for chronic conditions.
2. Language Barriers: Refugees may face language barriers that can make it difficult to communicate with healthcare providers, understand medical terminology, and complete paperwork. This can lead to misunderstandings or misdiagnoses, which can have serious consequences for their health.
3. Cultural Differences: Refugees may come from different cultural backgrounds than the healthcare providers they interact with. This can result in misunderstandings or conflicts related to cultural beliefs and practices around healthcare. For example, some refugees may be hesitant to seek medical care due to cultural beliefs about illness and healing.
4. Limited Knowledge of Healthcare System: Refugees may be unfamiliar with the US healthcare system and its structures, such as hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. This can make it difficult for them to navigate the system and access the care they need.
5. Trauma and Mental Health Issues: Many refugees have experienced trauma and stress related to their experiences in their home countries or during their migration journey. They may struggle with mental health issues, such as depression, anxiety, or post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), which can impact their ability to manage their health and access medical care.
6. Discrimination and Bias: Refugees may face discrimination and bias from healthcare providers, which can lead to feelings of mistrust and isolation. This can result in delayed or avoided medical care, which can have serious consequences for their health.
