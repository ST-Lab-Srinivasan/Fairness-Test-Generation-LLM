2. Lack of Legal Representation: Undocumented immigrants often face difficulties accessing legal representation due to their immigration status. Many lawyers are hesitant to take on undocumented clients, and those who do may charge higher fees or have limited knowledge of immigration law.

4. Limited Access to Legal Resources: Undocumented immigrants may have limited access to legal resources such as courts, legal aid organizations, and government agencies. This can make it difficult for them to navigate the legal system and seek the help they need.

7. Lack of Knowledge about Legal Rights: Undocumented immigrants may have limited knowledge about their legal rights and options, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the legal system effectively.
8. Limited Access to Government Services: Undocumented immigrants may have limited access to government services such as social security, education, and healthcare due to their immigration status. This can further limit their ability to access legal resources and support.
