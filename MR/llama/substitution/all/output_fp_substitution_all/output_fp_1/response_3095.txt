2. Offer closed captions: Make sure that any video or audio content played during the event is closed-captioned. This will help hearing-impaired individuals follow along with the content without relying on auditory alone.
3. Use tactile graphics: Provide tactile graphics or materials for hearing-impaired individuals to explore and interact with. This can include 3D models, touch-friendly exhibits, or interactive displays that use raised lines or Braille labels.
4. Provide audio descriptions: Offer audio descriptions of any visual content displayed during the event. This can include live performances, video presentations, or art exhibitions.
