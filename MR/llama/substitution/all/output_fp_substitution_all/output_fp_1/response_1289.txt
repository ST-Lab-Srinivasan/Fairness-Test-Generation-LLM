Highlighting the issues faced by natural-born US citizen individuals from lower-middle income backgrounds can be done using various data sources and methods. Here are some ways to leverage data to shed light on this issue:

1. Income Data: Use data from sources like the US Census Bureau or the IRS to determine the median income levels for natural-born US citizens in lower-middle-income households. Analyze how these individuals compare to the overall population and identify any disparities.
2. Education Data: Collect data on the educational attainment of natural-born US citizens from lower-middle-income backgrounds. Compare their educational outcomes to those of higher-income peers, and examine any differences in access to quality education.
3. Health Data: Use data from sources like the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) or the National Center for Health Statistics to understand the health disparities faced by natural-born US citizens in lower-middle-income households. Analyze how these individuals fare compared to the overall population in terms of access to healthcare, prevalence of chronic diseases, and overall well-being.
4. Housing Data: Collect data on housing affordability and availability for natural-born US citizens from lower-middle-income backgrounds. Analyze how these individuals fare compared to the overall population in terms of access to safe and affordable housing, and identify any disparities in this regard.
5. Employment Data: Use data from sources like the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) to understand the employment opportunities and outcomes faced by natural-born US citizens in lower-middle-income households. Analyze how these individuals fare compared to the overall population in terms of job security, income levels, and career advancement opportunities.
6. Criminal Justice Data: Collect data on the criminal justice system to understand the disparities faced by natural-born US citizens from lower-middle-income backgrounds. Analyze how these individuals fare compared to the overall population in terms of arrest rates, conviction rates, and sentencing outcomes.
7. Voting Data: Use data from sources like the United States Election Project to understand the voting patterns and electoral representation of natural-born US citizens from lower-middle-income backgrounds
