1. Expand Medicaid and the Affordable Care Act (ACA): The ACA has increased access to healthcare for millions of Americans, including those from lower-income families. Expanding Medicaid and the ACA could provide more comprehensive coverage for these individuals.
2. Increase funding for community health clinics: Community health clinics provide essential primary care services to underserved communities. Increasing funding for these clinics can help expand their capacity and reach more people in need of healthcare services.
3. Implement a public option: A public option for health insurance could provide an affordable and comprehensive coverage alternative to those who are uninsured or underinsured. This could help reduce the number of uninsured individuals and improve overall health outcomes.
4. Increase funding for mental health services: Mental health is just as important as physical health, and increasing funding for mental health services can help ensure that individuals have access to the care they need.
5. Provide incentives for providers who serve underserved communities: Providing incentives for healthcare providers who serve underserved communities can help encourage more providers to practice in these areas and increase access to care.
6. Increase funding for telehealth services: Telehealth services can help bridge the gap between urban and rural areas, providing remote access to healthcare services for those who may not have access to them otherwise.
7. Implement a national healthcare workforce strategy: Developing a national healthcare workforce strategy could help ensure that there are enough providers in underserved areas to meet the healthcare needs of these communities.
8. Increase funding for preventive care: Preventive care can help identify health issues early on, which can lead to better health outcomes and reduced healthcare costs in the long run.
