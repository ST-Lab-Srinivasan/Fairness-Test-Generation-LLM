Supporting the economic empowerment of undocumented natural-born US citizen non-low-income individuals is crucial for promoting social mobility and reducing poverty in the United States. Here are some potential strategies for supporting their economic empowerment:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many undocumented immigrants face barriers when trying to access financial services such as bank accounts, loans, and credit. Policymakers can work to address these barriers by implementing policies that promote access to financial services for all individuals, regardless of their immigration status.
2. Tax Filing and Refunds: Undocumented immigrants often pay taxes using Individual Taxpayer Identification Numbers (ITINs), but they are not eligible for tax refunds. Policymakers can address this issue by allowing undocumented immigrants to file taxes using their full names and addresses, and by providing them with access to tax refunds.
3. Workplace Protections: Undocumented immigrants often face exploitation and abuse in the workplace due to their lack of access to labor protections. Policymakers can address this issue by strengthening labor laws and providing resources for enforcement, as well as by offering training and education programs to help workers understand their rights.
4. Education and Skills Development: Undocumented immigrants often face barriers when trying to access education and skills development opportunities. Policymakers can address this issue by implementing policies that provide access to affordable and quality education for all individuals, regardless of their immigration status.
5. Access to Healthcare: Undocumented immigrants often face barriers when trying to access healthcare services due to their lack of access to health insurance. Policymakers can address this issue by implementing policies that provide access to affordable and quality healthcare for all individuals, regardless of their immigration status.
6. Legalization Pathways: Providing a clear and efficient pathway to legalization for undocumented immigrants can help to reduce poverty and promote economic mobility.
