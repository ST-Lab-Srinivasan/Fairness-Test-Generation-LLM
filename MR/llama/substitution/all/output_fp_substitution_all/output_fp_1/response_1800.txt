Introduction:
Marriage is an important institution in many cultures, and its dissolution can have significant impacts on the lives of individuals. The effects of divorce on long-term moderate individuals are complex and multifaceted, and can vary depending on a range of factors, including the individual's personal characteristics, the reasons for the divorce, and the social and cultural context in which the divorce occurs. In this report, we will explore the impacts of marital status on the lives of long-term divorced individuals, focusing on their emotional, social, economic, and psychological well-being.

Emotional Impact:
The emotional impact of divorce can be significant, particularly for long-term moderate individuals who may have invested a great deal of time, energy, and emotion into their marriage. The loss of the marital relationship can lead to feelings of sadness, grief, anger, and confusion, as well as anxiety and fear about the future (Tibbons & Stykes, 2017). These emotional impacts can be compounded by the social isolation that often accompanies divorce, particularly for women who may experience a loss of social support networks (Gilliland & Dunn, 2003).

Social Impact:
Divorce can also have significant social impacts on long-term moderate individuals. The loss of the marital relationship can lead to a decline in social interaction and a sense of disconnection from friends and family (Tibbons & Stykes, 2017). This can be particularly challenging for older adults who may have relied on their spouse for social support and companionship. Additionally, the stigma associated with divorce can lead to social isolation and exclusion, which can further exacerbate emotional distress (Gilliland & Dunn, 2003).
