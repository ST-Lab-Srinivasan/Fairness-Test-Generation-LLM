Ensuring that the healthcare needs of Nigerian lower middle-income people are met is a complex challenge that requires a multi-faceted approach. Here are some potential strategies that could be implemented to address this issue:

1. Increase access to healthcare services: Lower middle-income individuals may face barriers in accessing quality healthcare due to financial constraints, lack of infrastructure, or limited availability of healthcare providers. Increasing access to healthcare services through expansion of existing facilities, development of new ones, and provision of outreach programs can help bridge this gap.
2. Improve health insurance coverage: Many Nigerians do not have access to health insurance, which can make it difficult for them to afford quality healthcare services. Developing and implementing a national health insurance scheme or expanding existing ones could help increase coverage and reduce financial barriers to healthcare.
3. Promote preventive care: Lower middle-income individuals may be more susceptible to chronic diseases due to lack of access to preventive care. Implementing programs that focus on prevention, early detection, and management of chronic diseases can help reduce the burden on the healthcare system and improve overall health outcomes.
4. Strengthen primary healthcare: Primary healthcare is often the first point of contact for individuals seeking medical care. Strengthening primary healthcare through training of healthcare providers, improvement of infrastructure, and expansion of services can help ensure that lower middle-income individuals receive quality care closer to home.
5. Develop digital health solutions: Digital health technologies such as telemedicine, mobile health applications, and online consultations can help increase access to healthcare services for lower middle-income individuals who may have limited access to traditional healthcare facilities.
6. Address social determinants of health: Many chronic diseases are linked to social determinants of health such as poverty, poor housing, and lack of access to clean water and sanitation. Addressing these underlying factors through policies and programs that promote social equity can help improve overall health outcomes for lower middle-income individuals.
