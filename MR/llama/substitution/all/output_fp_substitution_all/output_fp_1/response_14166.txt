2. Adaptive devices: Provide adaptive devices such as grab bars, benches, and transfer stations to help individuals with mobility impairments navigate public spaces safely.
3. Signage and wayfinding: Provide clear and consistent signage and wayfinding throughout public spaces to help individuals with cognitive or visual impairments navigate.
4. Audio descriptions: Provide audio descriptions of exhibits, displays, and performances to help individuals who are blind or have low vision fully engage with public spaces.
5. Sign language interpretation: Provide sign language interpretation for events, presentations, and performances to ensure that individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing can participate fully.
6. Braille and tactile signage: Provide Braille and tactile signage to help individuals who are visually impaired navigate public spaces.
7. Assistive technology: Provide assistive technology such as audio describers, closed captioning, and tactile graphics to help individuals with disabilities fully engage with public spaces.
8. Sensory-friendly environments: Create sensory-friendly environments by reducing noise levels, providing comfortable seating areas, and offering sensory-friendly materials for individuals with sensory processing disorders.
9. Inclusive programming: Develop inclusive programming that caters to the needs of individuals with disabilities, such as adaptive sports, music therapy, and art programs.
10. Staff training: Provide staff with disability awareness training to help them better serve individuals with disabilities and create a more inclusive environment.
11. Collaboration with disability organizations: Work with disability organizations to ensure that public spaces are designed and operated in an inclusive manner.
