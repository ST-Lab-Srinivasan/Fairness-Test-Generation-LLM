Introduction:

1. Lack of Representation: Long-time divorced non-conservative individuals may feel marginalized or excluded from disaster response efforts due to their social identity. This can lead to a lack of representation in decision-making processes and limited access to resources.

4. Lack of Trust: Due to past experiences of exclusion or discrimination, long-time divorced non-conservative individuals may lack trust in disaster response efforts and organizations.
5. Unique Needs: Long-time divorced non-conservative individuals may have unique needs during disaster response, such as culturally sensitive support services or access to alternative forms of communication.
