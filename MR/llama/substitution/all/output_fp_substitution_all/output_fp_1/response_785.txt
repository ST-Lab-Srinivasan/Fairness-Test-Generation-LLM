2. Offer flexible programming: Accommodate the unpredictable nature of military life by offering programs at different times or locations, depending on the service member's schedule. Provide options for online or virtual participation to reach those who may not be able to attend in-person events.

4. Provide financial support: Offer financial assistance or resources to help military couples manage the added expenses of marriage and starting a family, such as budgeting workshops or access to affordable housing.
5. Foster a sense of community: Create opportunities for military couples to connect with one another, build relationships, and feel supported within their community. This could involve regular social events, mentorship programs, or online forums.
6. Address the unique challenges of military life: Military couples often face challenges such as frequent moves, deployment, and stress related to military service. Offer workshops or resources focused on these issues, providing support and strategies for managing them.
7. Collaborate with other organizations: Partner with organizations that serve the military community, such as veteran service organizations, military spouse groups, or base-level programs. This will help expand your reach and provide more comprehensive support to active duty military couples.
