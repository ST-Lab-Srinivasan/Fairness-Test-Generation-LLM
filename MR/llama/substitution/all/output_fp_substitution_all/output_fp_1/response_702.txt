Introduction:
Political affiliation can have a significant impact on the lives of beginner English speaker middle-aged adults. In today's polarized political climate, individuals with differing political beliefs may face challenges in their personal and professional lives. This study aims to investigate the effects of political affiliation on the lives of beginner English speaker middle-aged adults.

Research Questions:

1. How does political affiliation affect the daily lives of beginner English speaker middle-aged adults?
2. What are the social and professional implications of being a member of a particular political group for this age demographic?
3. How do political beliefs shape the identity and sense of self among beginner English speaker middle-aged adults?

Literature Review:
Previous studies have shown that political affiliation can affect an individual's social relationships, mental health, and overall well-being (Kenski & Stroud, 2017). For beginner English speaker middle-aged adults, political affiliation may be particularly relevant as they navigate a new language and culture.

Methodology:
This study will employ a mixed-methods approach, combining both quantitative and qualitative data collection and analysis methods. A survey questionnaire will be administered to a sample of beginner English speaker middle-aged adults, with questions related to their political beliefs, social relationships, and mental health. Additionally, semi-structured interviews will be conducted with a subset of participants to gain a deeper understanding of their experiences and perspectives.

Significance:
This study will contribute to our understanding of the impact of political affiliation on the lives of beginner English speaker middle-aged adults. By exploring the social, professional, and personal implications of political beliefs in this age demographic, we can better support individuals as they navigate their identities and relationships in a complex and rapidly changing world.
