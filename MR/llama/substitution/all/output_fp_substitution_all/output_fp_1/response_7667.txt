Supporting the social inclusion of beginner French speakers who are non-conservative can be a challenging task, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some ways to promote social inclusion:

1. Language classes: Offer language classes specifically designed for beginner French speakers who are non-conservative. These classes can provide a safe and supportive environment for learners to practice their language skills and build confidence.
2. Cultural events: Organize cultural events that celebrate the diversity of French-speaking communities, such as festivals, concerts, or exhibitions. These events can help promote cross-cultural understanding and inclusion.
3. Language exchange programs: Set up language exchange programs where beginner French speakers can practice speaking with native French speakers in a relaxed setting. This can help build relationships and foster a sense of community.
4. Online resources: Create online resources, such as videos, podcasts, or social media groups, that provide beginner French speakers with language learning materials and opportunities to connect with other learners.
5. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs where non-conservative beginner French speakers can be paired with more advanced learners who can provide guidance and support.
6. Social gatherings: Organize social gatherings, such as potlucks or game nights, that bring together beginner French speakers from diverse backgrounds. These events can help promote cross-cultural understanding and friendship.
7. Inclusive language learning spaces: Create inclusive language learning spaces where beginner French speakers can feel comfortable practicing their language skills without fear of judgment or discrimination.
8. Focus on mutual respect: Emphasize the importance of mutual respect in promoting social inclusion. Encourage learners to appreciate and value each other's cultural backgrounds and language learning experiences.
9. Celebrate diversity: Celebrate the diversity of French-speaking communities by highlighting the unique cultural practices, traditions, and languages of each group.
10. Provide support: Offer support to beginner French speakers who may be struggling with language learning or feeling isolated in their learning journey. This can involve providing additional resources, such as language learning materials or academic support, or simply being a listening ear.
