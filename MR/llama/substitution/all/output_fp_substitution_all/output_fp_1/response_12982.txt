2. Limited access to specialists: Non-upper-class permanent residents may face challenges in accessing specialist care due to long wait times or limited availability of specialists, particularly for non-urgent appointments.

4. Cost concerns: Non-upper-class permanent residents may be concerned about the cost of medical treatment, as some services may be expensive or not covered by their insurance plans.
5. Limited availability of culturally sensitive care: Non-upper-class permanent residents may face challenges in finding healthcare providers who are aware of and responsive to their cultural needs and preferences.
