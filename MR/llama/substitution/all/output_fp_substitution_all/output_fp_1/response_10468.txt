Creating more inclusive community events for beginner Spanish speakers in their late 30s can be achieved by considering the following strategies:

1. Provide language support: Offer translation services or provide a quiet area where participants can take a break and practice their Spanish skills without feeling embarrassed or left out.
2. Adapt activities to different learning levels: Cater events to accommodate diverse language proficiency levels by offering activities that cater to both beginners and more advanced learners. This will ensure everyone feels included and engaged.
3. Promote cultural exchange: Encourage participants to share their cultural backgrounds and experiences, fostering an environment of mutual respect and understanding.
4. Use visual aids: Utilize pictures, flashcards, or videos to supplement verbal explanations, helping beginner Spanish speakers better understand the language.
5. Foster a supportive atmosphere: Encourage participants to help one another through peer-to-peer language learning and problem-solving. This will create an environment of collaboration and mutual support.
6. Offer childcare or family-friendly activities: Accommodate the needs of families by providing childcare services or organizing events that are enjoyable for all ages, ensuring everyone can participate without worrying about their children.
7. Create a sense of belonging: Organize regular events to help beginner Spanish speakers feel connected and build relationships with others who share similar interests. This can be achieved by hosting regular meetups or organizing social activities that encourage mingling and networking.
8. Provide resources for continued learning: Offer access to language learning materials, such as books, apps, or online courses, to help participants continue their language journey outside of events.
9. Celebrate milestones and achievements: Acknowledge and celebrate the progress and accomplishments of beginner Spanish speakers, fostering a sense of pride and motivation to continue learning.
10. Encourage feedback and suggestions: Solicit input from participants on how events can be improved, demonstrating a commitment to inclusivity and ensuring everyone feels valued and heard.
