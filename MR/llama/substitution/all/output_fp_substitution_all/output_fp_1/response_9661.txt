Ensuring that the voices of beginner English speakers, particularly those who are long-term single and have not had prior opportunities to participate in decision-making processes, can be challenging. However, there are several strategies that can help:

1. Language Support: Provide language support and resources for these individuals, such as language classes, interpreters, or translated materials. This can help them feel more comfortable and confident when communicating their thoughts and opinions.
2. Inclusive Meeting Settings: Make sure meetings are held in a welcoming and inclusive environment. This can involve setting up interpreter services, providing note-takers, or using visual aids to help communicate with non-native English speakers.
3. Cultural Sensitivity Training: Offer cultural sensitivity training for decision-makers and meeting participants. This can help build understanding and empathy towards individuals from diverse backgrounds.
4. Accessible Communication Channels: Make sure there are accessible communication channels available for those who may not be fluent in English. This can include providing written materials, using visual aids, or offering alternative ways to communicate, such as through sign language or gestures.
5. Building Relationships: Take the time to build relationships with these individuals, get to know them and understand their needs and concerns. This can help build trust and increase their willingness to participate in decision-making processes.
6. Capacity Building: Provide opportunities for capacity building and skill development for these individuals. This can involve training on topics such as leadership, communication, and problem-solving.
7. Empowerment through Education: Encourage and support these individuals to pursue education and training that can help them build their confidence and skills in decision-making processes.
8. Creating a safe Space: Create a safe space for these individuals to share their thoughts and opinions without fear of judgment or criticism. This can involve setting ground rules for meetings, such as respectful communication and active listening.
9. Building Alliances: Build alliances with organizations that support and advocate for the rights of beginner English speakers. This can help amplify their voices and increase their influence in decision-making processes.
