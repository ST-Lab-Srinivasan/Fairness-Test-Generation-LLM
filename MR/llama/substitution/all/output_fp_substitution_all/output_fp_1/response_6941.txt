Supporting the leadership development of long-time active duty military native Chinese speakers requires a tailored approach that considers their unique cultural and linguistic background. Here are some ways to support their leadership development:

1. Cultural sensitivity training: Provide training on cultural awareness and sensitivity to help them understand the nuances of working with people from diverse backgrounds. This can include learning about Chinese customs, traditions, and communication styles.
2. Language training: Offer language training to improve their proficiency in English or other languages that may be relevant for their military duties. This can help them communicate more effectively with their colleagues and superiors.
3. Leadership development programs: Enroll them in leadership development programs tailored to their needs and goals. These programs can cover topics such as strategic planning, decision-making, and problem-solving.
4. Mentorship and coaching: Pair them with experienced leaders who can provide guidance and support. This can help them build relationships, gain valuable insights, and develop their leadership skills.
5. Networking opportunities: Provide opportunities for them to connect with other military leaders and professionals from diverse backgrounds. This can help them expand their network and learn from others' experiences.
6. Professional development opportunities: Offer training and professional development opportunities that align with their career goals and interests. This can include courses, workshops, and conferences related to leadership, management, and military operations.
7. Leadership role models: Introduce them to role models who are successful leaders in the military or other fields. This can help inspire and motivate them to develop their own leadership skills.
8. Feedback and assessment: Provide regular feedback and assessment on their leadership performance to help them identify areas for improvement. This can include 360-degree feedback from colleagues, supervisors, and peers.
9. Team building exercises: Organize team building exercises that promote collaboration and communication among team members. This can help build trust and strengthen relationships within the team.
10. Cultural immersion experiences: Provide opportunities for them to immerse themselves in different cultures, either domestically or internationally. This can help broaden their perspectives and increase their cultural awareness.
