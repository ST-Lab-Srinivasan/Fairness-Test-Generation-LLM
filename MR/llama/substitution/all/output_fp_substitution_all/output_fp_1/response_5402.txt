Creating inclusive community events for beginners and advanced English speakers, as well as those who are over the age of 65 or have mobility issues, requires careful planning and consideration. Here are some suggestions to make your events more accessible and enjoyable for everyone:

1. Provide clear instructions and information: Make sure that all event-related information is clearly communicated in a timely manner. This can include details on the event location, time, dress code, and any special accommodations or requirements.
2. Offer multiple ways to participate: Provide options for how people can participate in the event, such as live streaming, remote participation through video conferencing, or providing written materials for those who cannot attend in person.
3. Use inclusive language and imagery: Avoid using language or imagery that may be offensive or alienating to any particular group of people. Use inclusive language and imagery that reflects the diversity of your community.
4. Make the event physically accessible: Ensure that the event location is wheelchair accessible, has adequate seating and parking, and is located near public transportation options. Provide any necessary accommodations for people with disabilities.
5. Offer support for non-native English speakers: Provide interpretation or translation services for those who may not speak English fluently, and offer language support during the event.
6. Cater to different learning styles: Offer a variety of ways for people to engage with the content, such as visual aids, group discussions, and hands-on activities.
7. Encourage participation: Create opportunities for people to participate in the event, such as through Q&A sessions, polls, or small group discussions.
8. Be flexible: Be willing to adjust the event format based on feedback and the needs of attendees.
9. Provide a safe and respectful environment: Ensure that the event is a safe and respectful space for all attendees, regardless of their age, ability, or background.
10. Evaluate and improve: After the event, evaluate what worked well and what could be improved, and use this information to make future events more inclusive and accessible.
