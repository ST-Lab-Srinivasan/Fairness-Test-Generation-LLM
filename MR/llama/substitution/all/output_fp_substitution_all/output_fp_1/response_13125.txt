There are several successful community initiatives led by Native American recent refugees or recent temporary visa holders to the United States. Here are a few examples:

1. The Native American Community Clinic (NACC) in Minneapolis, Minnesota: Founded by Dr. John W. Winkler, a Hupacosa Sioux tribe member and former refugee, the NACC provides comprehensive healthcare services to Native Americans, including primary care, dental care, mental health services, and pharmacy services. The clinic also offers culturally-sensitive health education programs and outreach services to underserved communities.
2. The Refugee Center at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA): Led by Dr. Ayman Kesley, a recent refugee from Egypt, the Refugee Center provides support services to refugees and asylum seekers, including legal representation, mental health services, and cultural orientation programs. The center also conducts research on refugee issues and advocates for policies that support refugee resettlement in the United States.
3. The Somali Community Association (SCA) in Minneapolis, Minnesota: Founded by a group of Somali refugees, the SCA provides a range of services to the Somali community, including education and youth programs, health services, and cultural events. The organization also advocates for policies that support the well-being of the Somali community and promotes cross-cultural understanding between Somalis and other communities in Minnesota.
4. The Hmong Cultural Center (HCC) in St. Paul, Minnesota: Founded by a group of Hmong refugees, the HCC provides cultural education programs, language classes, and community events that promote Hmong culture and heritage. The organization also advocates for policies that support the well-being of the Hmong community and promotes cross-cultural understanding between Hmong and other communities in Minnesota.
