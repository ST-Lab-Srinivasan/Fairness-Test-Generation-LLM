Creating more inclusive housing policies for active duty military older adult individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that can be implemented:

1. Age-Friendly Housing Policies: Develop age-friendly housing policies that cater to the specific needs of older adult military personnel, such as accessibility features, senior-friendly living environments, and support services.
2. Military Housing Options: Offer a range of housing options specifically designed for older adult military personnel, including on-base housing, off-base housing, and retirement communities. These options should be tailored to their needs and preferences.
3. Financial Incentives: Provide financial incentives to encourage older adult military personnel to downsize or move to more affordable housing options, such as mortgage assistance programs, home equity loan programs, or financial assistance for relocation costs.
4. Assistive Technologies: Make assistive technologies available to older adult military personnel, such as grab bars, walkers, and wheelchairs, to help them maintain their independence and quality of life.
5. Transportation Services: Provide transportation services specifically designed for older adult military personnel, such as door-to-door transportation or transportation assistance programs, to help them access healthcare services, shopping centers, and other essential amenities.
6. Social Engagement: Encourage social engagement among older adult military personnel through community events, social activities, and support groups, which can help alleviate feelings of isolation and loneliness.
7. Health Services: Offer health services tailored to the specific needs of older adult military personnel, including routine check-ups, chronic disease management, and mental health support.
8. Home Modifications: Provide home modifications that enhance safety and accessibility, such as installing grab bars, non-slip flooring, and widening doorways, to help older adult military personnel maintain their independence in their homes.
9. Caregiver Support: Offer caregiver support services specifically designed for older adult military personnel who are caring for a spouse or partner with physical or cognitive limitations, such as respite care, counseling, and financial assistance.
