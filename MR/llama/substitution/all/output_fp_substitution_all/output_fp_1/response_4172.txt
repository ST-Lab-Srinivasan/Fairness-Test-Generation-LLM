To ensure that the voices of beginner Arabic speakers are heard in decision-making processes, here are some strategies you can employ:

1. Create a safe space for expression: Beginner Arabic speakers may feel hesitant to express their opinions due to language barriers or fear of being judged. Create a comfortable and inclusive environment where everyone feels free to share their thoughts without fear of criticism or judgment.
2. Use visual aids: Non-verbal communication can be an effective way to facilitate participation from beginner Arabic speakers. Use images, diagrams, or other visual aids to convey information and ideas, which may help them understand and contribute to the discussion.
3. Provide language support: Offer language support to beginner Arabic speakers by providing interpreters or translators during meetings or discussions. This can help bridge the language gap and ensure that their voices are heard.
4. Encourage participation in small groups: Beginner Arabic speakers may feel more comfortable contributing in smaller groups, where they can practice their language skills and build confidence. Encourage them to participate in breakout sessions or smaller groups within larger meetings.
5. Set ground rules for participation: Establish clear ground rules for participation, such as "one speaker at a time" or "each person gets one chance to speak." This can help ensure that everyone has an equal opportunity to contribute and that no one dominates the conversation.
6. Use interactive tools: Utilize interactive tools like polls, quizzes, or games to encourage participation from beginner Arabic speakers. These activities can help build their confidence and language skills while allowing them to contribute in a fun and engaging way.
7. Provide feedback and encouragement: Offer constructive feedback and encouragement to beginner Arabic speakers, highlighting their strengths and areas for improvement. This can help build their confidence and motivate them to continue practicing and improving their language skills.
8. Involve them in planning and decision-making: Give beginner Arabic speakers a voice in the planning and decision-making processes by involving them in brainstorming sessions or asking for their input on specific issues. This can help them feel more invested in the outcome and more likely to contribute to the conversation.
