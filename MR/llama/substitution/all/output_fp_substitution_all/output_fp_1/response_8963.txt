Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of Native American natural-born US citizens. For many Native Americans, their cultural heritage is an integral part of their identity and sense of self. It connects them to their ancestors, their communities, and their traditions. Here are some ways that cultural heritage shapes the experiences and perspectives of Native American natural-born US citizens:

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging and connection to one's community and ancestors. For many Native Americans, their cultural heritage is a source of pride and identity, and it helps them feel connected to their roots and history.
2. Traditions and practices: Cultural heritage includes traditional practices and rituals that are passed down from generation to generation. These traditions help maintain cultural continuity and provide a sense of stability and security in an ever-changing world.
3. Language: Many Native American communities have their own distinct languages, which are an essential part of their cultural heritage. Speaking one's native language is a way of connecting with one's culture and community, and it helps preserve the cultural identity of the group.
4. Spirituality: Cultural heritage includes spiritual practices and beliefs that are unique to each Native American community. These spiritual practices help provide a sense of meaning and purpose in life, and they connect individuals to their communities and ancestors.
5. Resilience: The resilience of Native American communities is rooted in their cultural heritage. The ability to adapt and survive despite historical trauma and ongoing oppression is a testament to the strength and resourcefulness of Native American cultures.
6. Community engagement: Cultural heritage fosters community engagement and participation. Traditional practices and rituals provide opportunities for individuals to come together and contribute to the well-being of their communities.
7. Preservation of cultural knowledge: Cultural heritage helps preserve traditional knowledge and skills, such as storytelling, music, and craftsmanship. This preservation ensures that these important aspects of Native American cultures are not lost over time.
8. Intergenerational connections: Cultural heritage creates intergenerational connections between elders and youth. Traditional practices and rituals provide a way for younger generations to learn from their elders and maintain cultural continuity.
