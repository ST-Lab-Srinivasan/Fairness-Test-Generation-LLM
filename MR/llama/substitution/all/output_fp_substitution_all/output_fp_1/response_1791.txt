The impact of genetic conditions on the lives of children can be significant and far-reaching, affecting various aspects of their well-being, including physical, emotional, social, and economic. Some of the common impacts of genetic conditions on child unemployed people include:

1. Social isolation: Children with genetic conditions may experience social isolation due to limitations in their physical abilities or appearance, leading to difficulties in forming and maintaining relationships.
2. Limited access to education: Genetic conditions can impact a child's ability to learn and participate in educational activities, leading to limited academic achievement and social exclusion.
3. Stigma and discrimination: Children with genetic conditions may face stigma and discrimination from their peers, family members, and society at large, leading to low self-esteem, depression, and anxiety.
4. Limited employment opportunities: Many genetic conditions can significantly impact a person's ability to work or participate in certain activities, limiting their career choices and earning potential.
5. Financial hardship: The costs of managing a genetic condition can be significant, leading to financial difficulties and stress for families.
6. Impact on family dynamics: Genetic conditions can strain family relationships, particularly between siblings or parents and children, due to the emotional and practical challenges involved in caring for a loved one with a genetic condition.
7. Limited access to healthcare: Children with genetic conditions may face barriers in accessing quality healthcare due to limited resources, leading to delayed diagnosis and treatment, and exacerbating their condition.
8. Emotional distress: Living with a genetic condition can cause significant emotional distress, including anxiety, depression, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).
9. Limited participation in extracurricular activities: Children with genetic conditions may face limitations in participating in sports, clubs, or other extracurricular activities due to their condition, impacting their social and personal development.
10. Impact on overall well-being: Genetic conditions can have a significant impact on a child's overall well-being, including their physical health, mental health, and quality of life.
