Promoting the economic empowerment of permanent resident native English speaker communities is essential for their overall well-being and prosperity. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to achieve this goal:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills development programs to help individuals acquire the necessary knowledge and skills to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and higher education.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing resources, mentorship, and funding opportunities to help individuals start their own businesses. This can include business incubators, accelerators, and loan programs.
3. Job Creation: Foster job creation in the community by investing in industries that are relevant to the local economy and provide good-paying jobs for native English speakers. This can include manufacturing, tourism, and technology.
4. Economic Diversification: Encourage economic diversification by supporting the development of new industries and sectors that can provide job opportunities for native English speakers. This can include innovation hubs, coworking spaces, and business accelerators.
5. Access to Capital: Ensure that native English speaker communities have access to capital to start or grow their businesses. This can include loans, grants, and investments from venture capital firms, angel investors, and crowdfunding platforms.
6. Networking Opportunities: Provide networking opportunities for native English speakers to connect with potential employers, partners, and investors. This can include industry events, conferences, and trade shows.
7. Addressing Discrimination: Address any forms of discrimination that may exist in the community, including systemic barriers and biases, to ensure that native English speakers have equal opportunities for economic empowerment.
8. Community Development: Invest in community development programs that support the overall well-being of native English speaker communities. This can include initiatives that promote social cohesion, mental health, and physical health.
9. Advocacy and Policy Reform: Advocate for policy reforms that promote the economic empowerment of native English speakers, including changes to labor laws, tax policies, and access to public services.
