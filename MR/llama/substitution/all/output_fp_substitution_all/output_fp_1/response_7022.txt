As a lawyer who has worked with many non-long-time single permanent residents (NLTSPRs) in the legal system, I have observed several challenges that they face. Here are some of the most common ones:

1. Lack of Legal Representation: NLTSPRs may not have access to legal representation due to various reasons such as lack of resources, language barriers, or unfamiliarity with the legal system. This can lead to a disadvantage in navigating the legal process and defending their rights.
2. Complex Immigration Laws: The immigration laws and procedures are complex and often change frequently, making it difficult for NLTSPRs to keep up with the requirements and deadlines. Without proper guidance, they may face difficulties in maintaining their legal status or facing deportation proceedings.
3. Discrimination and Bias: Unfortunately, NLTSPRs can face discrimination and bias from some members of society, including government officials and service providers. This can result in unequal treatment and denial of rights, leading to a lack of trust in the legal system.
4. Limited Access to Social Services: NLTSPRs may encounter difficulties when accessing social services such as healthcare, education, and employment due to their immigration status. This can lead to a lack of support and resources for their well-being and integration into society.
5. Fear of Deportation: The threat of deportation is a constant fear for many NLTSPRs, which can lead to anxiety, depression, and other mental health issues. This fear can also make it difficult for them to access legal services or speak out against injustices without fear of reprisal.
6. Lack of Community Support: NLTSPRs may face challenges when building a support network within their local communities due to language barriers, cultural differences, and discrimination. This can lead to feelings of isolation and disconnection from society.
7. Identity Issues: Adjusting to life in a new country and navigating the legal system can be emotionally challenging for NLTSPRs. They may struggle with their identity and sense of belonging, leading to issues such as homesickness, loneliness, and cultural shock.
