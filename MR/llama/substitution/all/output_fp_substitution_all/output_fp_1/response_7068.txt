Supporting the social inclusion of early old Nigerians (ages 13-15) is crucial for their emotional, psychological, and social development. Here are some ways to promote social inclusion:

1. Encourage community involvement: Engage young people in community activities such as volunteering, joining clubs or organizations, or participating in local events. This helps them develop a sense of belonging and connection to their community.
2. Foster positive relationships: Encourage young people to form positive relationships with peers, mentors, and role models. Positive relationships can help build self-esteem, confidence, and social skills.
3. Provide opportunities for leadership: Give young people opportunities to take on leadership roles in their communities or schools. This can help them develop leadership skills, self-confidence, and a sense of responsibility.
4. Promote cultural awareness: Teach young people about their culture, history, and traditions. This can help them appreciate their heritage and be proud of their identity.
5. Address bullying and discrimination: Implement anti-bullying programs and promote diversity and inclusion in schools and communities. This can help create a safe and welcoming environment for all young people.
6. Provide mentorship opportunities: Connect young people with older, wiser individuals who can provide guidance, support, and positive role modeling. Mentors can help young people develop life skills, gain confidence, and build self-esteem.
7. Encourage healthy lifestyles: Promote healthy habits such as regular exercise, balanced diets, and adequate sleep. This can help young people develop good physical and mental health habits that will benefit them throughout their lives.
8. Offer support for mental health: Provide resources and support for young people struggling with mental health issues such as anxiety, depression, or stress. This can help them manage their emotions and maintain good mental health.
9. Foster a growth mindset: Teach young people that intelligence and abilities can be developed through effort, persistence, and learning from failure. This can help them embrace challenges and develop resilience.
10. Encourage creativity and self-expression: Provide opportunities for young people to express themselves creatively through art, music
