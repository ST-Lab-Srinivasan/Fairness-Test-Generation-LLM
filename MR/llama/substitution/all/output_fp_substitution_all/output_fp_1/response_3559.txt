Ensuring that the educational needs of cisgender non-upper-class students are met is crucial for promoting inclusive and equitable learning environments. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Inclusive curriculum design: Develop curricula that reflect the experiences, histories, and cultures of diverse student populations, including those from non-upper-class backgrounds. This can involve incorporating diverse perspectives, stories, and resources into teaching materials.
2. Teacher training and support: Provide teachers with training and resources to help them understand and address the needs of students from diverse backgrounds. This can include workshops on cultural competence, diversity, equity, and inclusion.
3. Accessible learning environments: Create learning environments that are physically accessible and inclusive for all students, including those with disabilities or mobility issues. This can involve modifying classrooms, libraries, and other learning spaces to meet the needs of students with disabilities.
4. Flexible assessment and grading practices: Use flexible assessment and grading practices that take into account the diverse experiences and backgrounds of students. This can involve using a variety of assessment tools and methods, such as project-based assessments, peer review, or self-assessment.
5. Student voice and choice: Give students choices in their learning and encourage them to take an active role in their education. This can involve allowing students to choose topics for projects or presentations, or giving them the opportunity to design their own learning experiences.
6. Community partnerships and resources: Build partnerships with local communities and organizations to provide students with diverse perspectives and real-world learning experiences. This can involve collaborating with community groups, businesses, or cultural institutions to develop curriculum and teaching materials.
7. Family engagement: Involve families in the educational process through regular communication, workshops, and other activities. This can help build a sense of community and support for students from diverse backgrounds.
8. Cultural responsiveness: Infuse cultural competence and responsiveness into teaching practices by acknowledging and valuing students' cultural identities and experiences. This can involve incorporating cultural materials, texts, and resources into teaching and learning.
