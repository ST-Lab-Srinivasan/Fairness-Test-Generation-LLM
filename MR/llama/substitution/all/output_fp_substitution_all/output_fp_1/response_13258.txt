Low-income individuals face numerous challenges in accessing quality healthcare, particularly in countries with underfunded and overburdened healthcare systems. Some of the common challenges faced by low-income nationals in the healthcare system include:

1. Limited access to healthcare facilities: Low-income individuals often live in areas with limited access to healthcare facilities, making it difficult for them to receive timely medical care.
2. Financial barriers: Many low-income individuals struggle to afford the costs of healthcare, including copays, deductibles, and prescription medications.
3. Long wait times: Low-income individuals may face long wait times for appointments and procedures due to a shortage of healthcare providers and facilities.
4. Limited provider choice: In some areas, low-income individuals may have limited access to healthcare providers, particularly specialists, due to a lack of providers in their network or area.
5. Language barriers: Low-income individuals who are non-native English speakers may face challenges communicating with healthcare providers and understanding complex medical information.
6. Limited access to preventive care: Low-income individuals may have limited access to preventive care, such as routine check-ups and screenings, which can lead to delayed diagnosis and treatment of health issues.
7. Mental health challenges: Low-income individuals may face increased stress and mental health challenges due to financial strain and lack of access to mental health services.
8. Limited access to specialized care: Low-income individuals may have limited access to specialized care, such as cancer treatment or cardiovascular care, due to a lack of specialists in their area or network.
9. Lack of cultural competence: Low-income individuals from diverse backgrounds may face challenges in accessing healthcare that meets their cultural and linguistic needs.
10. Limited access to telehealth services: Low-income individuals may have limited access to telehealth services, which can limit their ability to access medical care remotely.
11. Limited transportation options: Low-income individuals may face challenges in accessing healthcare facilities due to a lack of transportation options or affordable transportation costs.
