Ensuring the educational needs of native Arabic speakers, particularly long-time widowed students, require a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique challenges and circumstances. Here are some strategies to help meet their educational needs:

1. Cultural sensitivity: Provide education in an environment that is culturally sensitive and respectful of their background. This can be achieved by incorporating Arabic language and culture into the curriculum, using local resources and materials, and involving the community in the educational process.
2. Accessible learning materials: Offer learning materials that are accessible to students with limited literacy skills or visual impairments, such as large print materials, audio books, and Braille.
3. Flexible scheduling: Offer flexible scheduling options to accommodate the schedules of long-time widowed students who may have caregiving responsibilities or other obligations. This can include part-time or online courses, as well as flexible examination schedules.
4. Supportive learning environment: Create a supportive and inclusive learning environment that recognizes the unique challenges faced by long-time widowed students. This can involve providing additional academic and personal support, such as peer mentoring, counseling services, and academic advising.
5. Collaboration with community organizations: Collaborate with local community organizations, such as mosques, cultural centers, or senior citizen groups, to provide educational opportunities for long-time widowed students who may face barriers to accessing education.
6. Arabic language and culture courses: Offer courses that focus on the Arabic language and culture, which can help long-time widowed students to connect with their heritage and maintain their cultural identity.
7. Intergenerational learning: Encourage intergenerational learning by involving older students in mentorship programs, where they can share their knowledge and experiences with younger generations.
8. Inclusive assessments: Use inclusive assessment methods that take into account the diverse needs of long-time widowed students, such as alternative formats for exams or assignments, or providing additional support for students who may need it.
