Title: The Impact of Political Affiliation on the Lives of Native Arabic Speaker Elderly Adults (20s) in the Middle East

Introduction:
Political affiliation can have a significant impact on the lives of elderly adults, particularly in regions where politics and identity are deeply intertwined. In the Middle East, for instance, political affiliation can affect an individual's social status, access to resources, and overall well-being. This study aims to investigate the impact of political affiliation on the lives of native Arabic speaker elderly adults (20s) in the region.

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research design, using in-depth interviews with native Arabic speaker elderly adults (20s) living in the Middle East. Purposeful sampling will be used to select participants who are representative of different political affiliations. The interviews will be recorded, transcribed, and analyzed using thematic analysis.

Significance:
Understanding the impact of political affiliation on the lives of elderly adults can help policymakers and healthcare providers develop targeted interventions to improve their well-being. This study will contribute to the limited existing literature on this topic, providing valuable insights into the experiences of native Arabic speaker elderly adults in the region.

Research Questions:

1. How do native Arabic speaker elderly adults (20s) perceive the impact of political affiliation on their lives in the Middle East?
2. What are the specific challenges faced by elderly adults with different political affiliations in the region?
3. How do cultural and social factors interact with political affiliation to shape the experiences of elderly adults in the Middle East?
