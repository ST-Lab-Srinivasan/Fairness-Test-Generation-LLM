Supporting the professional aspirations of non-lower-class, non-married individuals can be crucial in helping them achieve their career goals and reach their full potential. Here are some ways to support these individuals:

1. Encouragement and motivation: Provide emotional support and encouragement to help them stay motivated and focused on their goals. Listen to their concerns and aspirations, and offer words of encouragement.
2. Networking opportunities: Introduce them to your professional contacts and help them connect with potential mentors or role models in their field. This can provide valuable insights and advice on how to navigate their career.
3. Access to resources: Offer access to educational resources such as books, articles, and online courses that can help them develop new skills and knowledge. You could also provide financial support for professional development opportunities such as conferences or workshops.
4. Flexible work arrangements: Be open to flexible work arrangements such as telecommuting or flexible hours, which can help them balance their work and personal life without sacrificing one for the other.
5. Mentorship: Pair them with a mentor who can provide guidance and support in their career. This can be especially helpful in providing a different perspective and helping them navigate challenges they may face.
6. Professional development opportunities: Provide opportunities for professional development such as training, workshops, or conferences. These can help them gain new skills and knowledge that can help them advance in their career.
7. Work-life balance support: Encourage and support a healthy work-life balance by offering flexible scheduling, telecommuting options, and ensuring they are not overworked.
8. Financial support: Consider providing financial support for professional development opportunities such as courses, certifications, or conferences. This can help them gain new skills and knowledge that can help them advance in their career.
9. Advocacy: Be an advocate for their professional aspirations within your organization. Work with your HR department to ensure they have access to the resources and support they need to succeed.
