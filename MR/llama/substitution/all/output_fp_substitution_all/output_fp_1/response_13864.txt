Protecting the legal rights of individuals living in deep poverty, particularly those who may be agnostic or have limited access to education and resources, is a complex challenge. However, there are several strategies that can be implemented to ensure their legal rights are protected:

1. Community-based legal clinics: Establishing community-based legal clinics in areas with high poverty rates can provide free or low-cost legal services to individuals who may not have access to traditional legal aid organizations. These clinics can offer basic legal advice, representation, and education on legal rights and responsibilities.
2. Legal aid partnerships: Collaborating with local organizations, community groups, and religious institutions can help identify individuals in need of legal assistance and provide them with the necessary resources. These partnerships can also help raise awareness about legal rights and responsibilities among marginalized communities.
3. Technology-based solutions: Utilizing technology, such as online legal platforms or mobile apps, can provide easy access to legal information and services for individuals living in poverty. These solutions can offer basic legal advice, connect individuals with local legal aid organizations, and provide resources on legal rights and responsibilities.
4. Advocacy and policy reform: Advocating for policy reforms that address the root causes of poverty and inequality can help protect the legal rights of marginalized communities. This can involve campaigning for policies that promote affordable housing, access to education, and job training programs, which can help individuals escape poverty.
5. Public awareness campaigns: Launching public awareness campaigns to educate individuals about their legal rights and responsibilities can help empower them to assert their rights and seek legal assistance when needed. These campaigns can be carried out through various media channels, including radio, television, and social media.
6. Legal literacy programs: Implementing legal literacy programs that provide basic legal education can help individuals living in poverty understand their legal rights and responsibilities. These programs can cover topics such as contract law, family law, and consumer protection laws.
