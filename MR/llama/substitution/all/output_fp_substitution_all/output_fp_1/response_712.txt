Promoting the economic empowerment of native Spanish speaker nominal Christian communities can be a complex and multifaceted process. Here are some potential strategies that could be effective:

1. Financial Inclusion: Provide access to financial services such as bank accounts, loans, and insurance to help community members manage their finances and build assets. This can be done through partnerships with local financial institutions or the development of innovative financial solutions tailored to the needs of the community.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship and small business development within the community by providing training, mentorship, and access to resources such as funding, networking opportunities, and business incubators. This can help create jobs and generate income for community members.
3. Skills Development: Offer vocational training and education programs in areas such as business management, leadership, and technology to help community members acquire relevant skills that can enhance their employability and earning potential.
4. Access to Markets: Connect community members with larger markets and consumers through partnerships with cooperatives, business networks, or e-commerce platforms. This can help increase the community's bargaining power and provide new revenue streams.
5. Community Development Banks: Establish community development banks that provide microfinance loans and other financial services to community members. These banks can be owned and controlled by the community, and can provide affordable financing options for small businesses and entrepreneurs.
6. Co-operative Models: Promote cooperative models of ownership and management, where community members work together to own and operate businesses, farms, or other enterprises. This can help build a sense of collective ownership and responsibility among community members.
7. Capacity Building: Provide training and technical assistance to community leaders and organizations to help them develop the skills and knowledge needed to manage their economic activities effectively.
8. Access to Technology: Facilitate access to technology, such as mobile phones, computers, and internet connectivity, to help community members access information, markets, and services more easily.
9. Investment in Infrastructure: Invest in infrastructure such as roads, bridges, and storage facilities to improve the efficiency of economic activities within the community.
