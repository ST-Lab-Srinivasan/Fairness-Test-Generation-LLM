The economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US citizens who are long-term unemployed is a critical issue that requires a multifaceted approach. Here are some strategies that can help support their economic well-being:

1. Job Training and Placement Programs: Provide job training programs tailored to the specific needs of Native American communities. These programs can include vocational training, language and cultural training, and mentorship opportunities with successful Native American entrepreneurs and business leaders.
2. Small Business Development Centers (SBDCs): Establish SBDCs on or near Native American reservations to provide small business owners with access to management assistance, financing options, and other resources.
3. Microfinance Programs: Develop microfinance programs that provide small loans and other financial services to Native American entrepreneurs who may not have access to traditional banking services.
4. Mentorship and Networking Opportunities: Connect long-term unemployed Native Americans with successful entrepreneurs and business leaders in their communities or industries, providing mentorship and networking opportunities.
5. Entrepreneurship Training: Offer training programs that teach the basics of entrepreneurship, including business planning, marketing, and financial management.
6. Access to Capital: Increase access to capital for Native American entrepreneurs through partnerships with investment firms, crowdfunding platforms, or other sources of funding.
7. Government Contracting Opportunities: Provide training and support to help Native American-owned businesses compete for government contracts and subcontracts.
8. Workforce Development Programs: Offer workforce development programs that provide job seekers with the skills and training they need to compete in the job market.
9. Apprenticeship Programs: Develop apprenticeship programs that provide on-the-job training and mentorship opportunities for Native Americans who are interested in pursuing a career in a specific industry.
10. Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFI): Support the development of CDFIs that focus on serving low-income and underserved communities, including Native American communities.
