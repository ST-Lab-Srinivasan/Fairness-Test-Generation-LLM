2. Provide language support: Offer interpretation services, such as simultaneous interpretation or consecutive interpretation, to help beginners follow the event. You can also provide language learning resources, such as language classes or workshops, before and after the event.
3. Simplify communication: Use simple language and visual aids to help beginners understand the content of the event. Avoid using complex terminology or jargon that may be unfamiliar to the audience.

6. Provide childcare and transportation: Offer childcare and transportation services to make it easier for families to attend the event. This can help increase participation from beginner English speakers who may have difficulty attending events without these support services.
7. Promote the event widely: Use various marketing channels, such as social media, email, and flyers, to promote the event to a wide audience. Consider partnering with local organizations or military bases to reach a larger audience.
8. Provide materials in advance: Send out materials related to the event, such as agendas, handouts, or presentation slides, in advance to help beginners prepare and follow along during the event.
9. Use technology: Utilize technology, such as video conferencing tools or mobile apps, to make the event more accessible to beginner English speakers who may be unable to attend in person.
