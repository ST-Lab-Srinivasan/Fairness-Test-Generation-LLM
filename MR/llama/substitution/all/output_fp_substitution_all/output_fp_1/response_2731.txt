Supporting the economic empowerment of Just-above-Poverty Line (JPL) Native recent temporary visa holders to the US requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and barriers. Here are some strategies that could be implemented:

1. Access to Financial Services: JPL individuals often lack access to basic banking services, credit, and other financial tools that can help them build credit history, save money, and invest in their future. Financial institutions and government agencies can work together to provide affordable financial services, such as checking and savings accounts, low-interest loans, and microloans.
2. Job Training and Placement: Many JPL individuals face significant barriers to finding employment due to a lack of job skills, language barriers, and discrimination. Providing job training programs that cater to their specific needs can help them acquire the skills they need to compete in the US job market. Employers can also be educated on the benefits of hiring JPL individuals and how to provide supportive work environments.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Encouraging entrepreneurship among JPL individuals can help them build their own economic stability. Providing resources such as business training, mentorship, and access to capital can help them start their own businesses or grow existing ones. Government agencies, non-profits, and private organizations can work together to create a supportive entrepreneurial ecosystem.
4. Access to Education and Training: JPL individuals often have limited access to quality education and training opportunities. Governments, educational institutions, and private organizations can provide scholarships, vocational training programs, and language classes to help them acquire new skills and knowledge.
5. Healthcare Support: Many JPL individuals face significant barriers to accessing healthcare due to lack of insurance, cultural and language barriers, and discrimination. Providing affordable healthcare options, culturally sensitive healthcare services, and advocacy support can help them access quality healthcare.
