Promoting the economic empowerment of non-conservative natural-born US citizen communities is crucial for ensuring that all individuals have equal opportunities to succeed and reach their full potential. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to promote economic empowerment in these communities:

1. Access to Education and Training: Providing access to quality education and training programs can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge they need to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and college education.
2. Microfinance and Small Business Development: Microfinance initiatives and small business development programs can provide capital and support to individuals looking to start or grow their own businesses. This can help create jobs and generate economic activity in these communities.
3. Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFI): CDFI's are specialized financial institutions that provide financial services to underserved communities. They can offer loans, venture capital, and other financial products to help individuals start or grow their businesses.
4. Job Training and Placement: Providing job training and placement services can help individuals acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market. This can include on-the-job training, apprenticeships, and job placement services.
5. Entrepreneurship Support: Encouraging entrepreneurship and providing support to start-ups can create jobs and stimulate economic growth in these communities. This can include access to incubators, accelerators, and other resources.
6. Access to Healthcare: Ensuring that individuals have access to quality healthcare can help them be more productive and successful in their personal and professional lives.
7. Community Land Trusts: Community land trusts (CLT's) are non-profit organizations that hold land for the benefit of a community. They can provide affordable housing, commercial space, and other resources to promote economic development.
8. Cooperative Business Models: Cooperative business models can help individuals work together to start and grow their businesses. This can include worker cooperatives, consumer cooperatives, and producer cooperatives.
