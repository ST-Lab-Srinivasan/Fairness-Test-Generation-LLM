Creating more inclusive social policies for long-time single permanent residents (LTPR) is crucial to ensure that they are not left behind in the social and economic fabric of society. Here are some ways to create more inclusive social policies for LTPR individuals:

1. Acknowledge their contributions: LTPR individuals have contributed to society through their work, taxes, and community involvement. Acknowledging their contributions and recognizing their value can help create a more inclusive environment.
2. Address discrimination: Discrimination against LTPR individuals based on their immigration status can be addressed by implementing policies that promote equal treatment under the law. This includes providing access to education, employment, and healthcare for all residents, regardless of their immigration status.
3. Provide pathways to citizenship: Offering clear paths to citizenship for LTPR individuals can help them feel more connected to their communities and society as a whole. This can be achieved through programs such as the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) or the Green Card Lottery.
4. Address language barriers: Many LTPR individuals face language barriers that can limit their access to services and opportunities. Providing language support services, such as interpretation and translation, can help level the playing field.
5. Foster community engagement: Encouraging LTPR individuals to participate in community activities and organizations can help them feel more connected to society. This can be achieved through events, workshops, and volunteer opportunities.
6. Provide affordable housing: Many LTPR individuals face challenges in finding affordable and stable housing. Policies that prioritize affordable housing options can help address this issue.
7. Address health disparities: LTPR individuals may face health disparities due to lack of access to healthcare or cultural barriers. Providing culturally competent healthcare services and addressing language barriers can help improve their health outcomes.
8. Promote education and job training: Offering education and job training programs tailored to the needs of LTPR individuals can help them acquire new skills and find better-paying jobs. This can also help reduce poverty and inequality.
