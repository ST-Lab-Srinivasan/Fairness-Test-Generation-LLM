To ensure that the educational needs of Native Spanish Speaker (NSS) single-multiple times students are met, here are some strategies and recommendations:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Design curricula and instructional practices that reflect the cultural backgrounds and experiences of NSS students. This can involve incorporating authentic materials, such as books, videos, and music, that reflect the students' culture.
2. Bilingual education: Provide bilingual education programs or support for NSS students who are English language learners. This can include dual-language immersion programs, where students learn in both English and Spanish, or transitional bilingual programs, which provide additional support for students as they acquire English.
3. Home language support: Encourage NSS families to continue speaking Spanish at home, and provide resources and support for them to do so. This can include providing parents with Spanish-language materials, such as books and videos, and offering parent-teacher conferences in both English and Spanish.
4. Language scaffolding: Provide language scaffolds, such as visual aids or graphic organizers, to support NSS students' language development. This can help them to better understand and express themselves in both languages.
5. Cultural events and celebrations: Host cultural events and celebrations that reflect the experiences and traditions of NSS students. This can help to create a sense of belonging and connection to school, and provide opportunities for students to share their culture with their peers.
6. Partnerships with community organizations: Develop partnerships with community organizations that serve NSS populations. These partnerships can provide additional resources and support for students, such as language classes or cultural events.
7. Professional development: Provide professional development opportunities for teachers to learn about the educational needs of NSS students and how to best support them. This can include training on culturally responsive teaching practices, bilingual education, and language scaffolding.
8. Parent engagement: Encourage NSS parents to participate in school events and decision-making processes. This can help to build trust and collaboration between home and school, and provide opportunities for parents to learn about their child's educational experience.
