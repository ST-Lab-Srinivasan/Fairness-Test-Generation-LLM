Addressing the needs of individuals who are just above the poverty line in a disaster response requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique challenges and circumstances. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Socio-economic profiling: Conduct a thorough analysis of the affected population, including their socio-economic status, income level, education, and occupation. This will help identify specific needs and tailor response efforts accordingly.
2. Accessibility and inclusivity: Ensure that disaster response efforts are accessible and inclusive for all individuals, regardless of their socio-economic status. This can be achieved by providing accessible communication channels, adapting relief items to suit different dietary requirements, and ensuring that relief distributions are carried out in a fair and transparent manner.
3. Financial assistance: Provide financial assistance to help individuals who are just above the poverty line cope with the aftermath of a disaster. This can include emergency cash transfers, micro-finance schemes, or other forms of financial support that help bridge the gap until normal livelihoods can be restored.
4. Capacity building: Offer capacity-building programs to help individuals develop new skills and knowledge that can help them recover from a disaster more quickly. This can include training on basic first aid, construction techniques, or small business management.
5. Community mobilization: Encourage community mobilization by engaging local leaders, volunteers, and other stakeholders in the response efforts. This can help build trust and social cohesion, which are critical for a successful recovery.
