Supporting the mental health of physically disabled Native recent refugees to the US requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique challenges and needs. Here are some ways to provide support:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training on cultural competency for healthcare providers, educators, and community members to better understand the cultural practices and beliefs of Native American refugees.
2. Access to Health Services: Ensure access to quality medical care, mental health services, and other health-related resources that cater to their specific needs.
3. Language Support: Provide language interpretation and translation services for those who may not speak English fluently, as well as language-specific materials and resources.
4. Community Engagement: Facilitate community engagement and socialization opportunities for refugees to connect with other members of their cultural group and build a sense of belonging in the US.
5. Legal Support: Offer legal assistance and representation to help navigate the complex immigration process, as well as provide information on their rights and responsibilities as immigrants.
6. Education and Employment Support: Provide education and employment resources tailored to the needs of refugees, including job training programs, language training, and career counseling.
7. Trauma-Informed Care: Offer trauma-informed care and mental health services that are sensitive to the unique experiences and traumas faced by Native American refugees.
8. Traditional Healing: Incorporate traditional healing practices and ceremonies into mental health treatment plans, where appropriate and culturally sensitive.
9. Family Support: Provide support for refugee families, including parenting classes, family therapy, and child development resources.
10. Advocacy: Advocate for policies and programs that support the mental health and well-being of Native American refugees, both locally and nationally.
