Supporting the economic empowerment of non-wealthy, mixed African American individuals requires addressing the systemic barriers and structural inequalities that hinder their financial stability. Here are some strategies to help achieve this goal:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs, focusing on skills relevant to in-demand industries. This can help individuals acquire the necessary knowledge and competencies to secure better-paying jobs and advance their careers.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing resources, such as mentorship, access to capital, and networking opportunities. This can help individuals create their own economic opportunities and build wealth.
3. Financial Inclusion: Ensure equal access to financial services, such as bank accounts, loans, and investment opportunities. This can help individuals save money, invest in their future, and avoid predatory lending practices.
4. Wealth-Building Strategies: Implement strategies to help individuals build wealth, such as retirement savings plans, homeownership programs, and investment opportunities.
5. Advocacy and Policy Reform: Advocate for policy reforms that address systemic inequalities and promote economic empowerment, such as progressive taxation, affordable housing initiatives, and anti-discrimination laws.
6. Community Development: Invest in community development programs that provide resources and support to disadvantaged communities, such as small business incubators, job training programs, and community-based financial services.
7. Mentorship and Networking: Connect individuals with successful professionals and entrepreneurs who can serve as role models and provide guidance on career development and business growth.
8. Financial Literacy: Improve financial literacy among individuals by providing education on personal finance, budgeting, and investing. This can help them make informed decisions about their money and avoid predatory practices.
9. Access to Capital: Ensure that individuals have access to capital for business startup and growth, including microfinance options and crowdfunding platforms.
