2. Individualized Education Plans (IEPs): Create an IEP for each student that outlines specific goals, objectives, and strategies for achieving academic success. Regularly review and update the IEP to ensure it remains relevant and effective.

4. Supportive Learning Environments: Create a supportive learning environment that promotes independence, self-advocacy, and socialization. This may involve modifying the physical classroom layout, providing additional support from teachers or peers, and encouraging students to participate in extracurricular activities.
5. Collaboration with Parents: Engage parents in the educational process by providing regular updates on their child's progress, inviting them to school events, and seeking their input on how to best support their child's academic success.
