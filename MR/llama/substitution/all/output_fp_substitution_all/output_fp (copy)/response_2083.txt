Addressing the Needs of Unmarried Traditionalist Individuals in Disaster Response: A Holistic Approach

Introduction

Disaster response and recovery efforts often prioritize the needs of families and married couples, overlooking the unique challenges faced by unmarried traditionalist individuals. However, these individuals, such as single parents, elders living alone, or those who do not identify with contemporary marriage norms, require special attention to ensure their safety and well-being during disasters. This article proposes a holistic approach to address the needs of unmarried traditionalist individuals in disaster response, focusing on their specific challenges and requirements.

I. Identifying Unmarried Traditionalist Individuals

A. Demographic characteristics:

1. Age: Older adults, particularly those aged 65 and above, are more susceptible to injury or illness during disasters due to declining physical abilities.
2. Gender: Women, especially those who identify as female, may face additional challenges in disaster situations, such as gender-based violence or unequal access to resources.
3. Socioeconomic status: Low-income unmarried traditionalist individuals may struggle with limited financial resources and access to basic necessities, making them more vulnerable in disaster situations.
4. Cultural background: Different cultural practices and beliefs can affect how unmarried traditionalist individuals respond to disasters and access support services.

B. Identifying their unique needs:

1. Accessibility: Unmarried traditionalist individuals may face barriers in accessing emergency shelters or other resources due to mobility issues, childcare responsibilities, or lack of transportation options.
2. Emotional support: Disasters can exacerbate feelings of loneliness and isolation among unmarried traditionalist individuals, particularly those who do not have a strong support system.
3. Spiritual needs: Cultural or religious beliefs may play a significant role in how unmarried traditionalist individuals cope with disasters; providing spiritual support can help address these needs.
