Promoting the artistic talents of native Arabic speakers, particularly those in the 13-15 age range, requires a supportive environment and opportunities for growth. Here are some strategies to help foster their artistic development:

1. Encourage and support: Provide a nurturing environment that encourages and supports their artistic pursuits. Encourage them to explore different forms of art, such as writing, painting, music, or dance. Offer constructive feedback and guidance without being overly critical or demanding.
2. Foster creativity through play: Engage them in creative activities that promote imagination and self-expression. Playdough, coloring books, and other arts and crafts projects can help develop their creative skills.
3. Provide access to art resources: Offer access to art supplies, books, and online resources that can help them explore different art forms. This could include museum visits, attending concerts or theater performances, or participating in art workshops or classes.
4. Collaborate with other artists: Arrange for them to collaborate with other artists, either locally or through online platforms. This can help them learn new techniques, gain exposure, and build a network of peers who share similar interests.
5. Showcase their talents: Organize exhibitions, performances, or readings that showcase their artistic abilities. This can help them gain confidence and recognition within their community.
6. Encourage interdisciplinary exploration: Encourage them to explore different forms of art and express themselves through multiple mediums. For example, they could write a story to accompany a piece of art or compose music to complement a dance performance.
7. Foster self-expression: Encourage them to use their art as a means of self-expression and personal growth. Help them understand that art is a way to communicate emotions, ideas, and experiences, and that it can be a powerful tool for personal development.
8. Celebrate their achievements: Acknowledge and celebrate their accomplishments, no matter how small they may seem. This can help build their confidence and motivation to continue exploring their artistic talents.
