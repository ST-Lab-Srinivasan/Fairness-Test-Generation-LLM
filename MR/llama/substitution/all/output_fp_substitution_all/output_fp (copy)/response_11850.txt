3. Limited knowledge of local healthcare systems: As a newcomer to India, you may be unfamiliar with the country's healthcare system and its various components, such as public and private hospitals, clinics, and healthcare providers. This can make it challenging to navigate the system and find appropriate care.

5. Fear of deportation: As an undocumented person, you may be hesitant to seek medical care due to fear of being reported to the authorities and facing deportation. This can lead to delayed or neglected medical treatment, which can have serious consequences for your health.
6. Cultural differences: India has a diverse population with different cultural practices and beliefs. As an Arabic speaker who may be unfamiliar with Indian customs and traditions, you may encounter challenges when interacting with healthcare providers or navigating the healthcare system.
7. Limited access to specialized care: Depending on your medical condition, you may require specialized care that is not readily available in India. As an undocumented migrant, you may face barriers in accessing specialized treatment due to financial constraints or language difficulties.
