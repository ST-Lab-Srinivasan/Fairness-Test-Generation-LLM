Supporting the economic empowerment of non-able-bodied and lower-middle-income individuals is crucial for promoting inclusivity and reducing poverty. Here are some ways to support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to education and training programs that can help them acquire new skills and knowledge, enabling them to compete in the job market. This could include vocational training, digital literacy programs, and entrepreneurship training.
2. Job Creation: Support the creation of jobs in areas that are accessible to people with disabilities. This could involve providing funding or resources for businesses that employ people with disabilities or creating jobs that can be done from home or in a flexible work environment.
3. Access to Financial Services: Ensure that individuals with disabilities have access to financial services, such as bank accounts and loans, which can help them start or grow their own businesses. This could involve partnering with financial institutions to create accessible banking channels or providing financial literacy training.
4. Inclusive Hiring Practices: Encourage businesses to adopt inclusive hiring practices by providing training on disability etiquette and the benefits of employing people with disabilities. This could also involve providing job placement services for individuals with disabilities.
5. Mental Health Support: Provide mental health support services to help individuals with disabilities manage stress and anxiety related to their disability or financial situation. This could involve partnering with mental health professionals to provide counseling services or creating a mental health hotline.
6. Access to Affordable Housing: Ensure that individuals with disabilities have access to affordable and accessible housing options, which can help them save money on housing costs and invest in their future. This could involve partnering with housing providers to create accessible housing options or providing financial assistance for home modifications.
7. Healthcare Support: Provide healthcare support services to help individuals with disabilities manage their medical needs. This could involve partnering with healthcare professionals to provide telemedicine services or creating a healthcare hotline.
8. Social Inclusion: Encourage social inclusion by providing opportunities for individuals with disabilities to participate in community activities and events. This could involve partnering with local organizations to create inclusive spaces
