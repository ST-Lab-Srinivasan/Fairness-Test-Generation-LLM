Supporting the economic empowerment of recently early pregnant (1st trimester) active duty military individuals is crucial for their overall well-being and financial stability. Here are some ways to achieve this goal:

1. Financial Assistance Programs: Offer financial assistance programs, such as supplemental nutrition assistance or emergency loans, to help military members cover expenses related to pregnancy, childbirth, and caring for a newborn.
2. Maternity Leave Policies: Develop and implement comprehensive maternity leave policies that provide adequate paid leave for military members during the first trimester of pregnancy. This will help them focus on their health and well-being without worrying about lost income.
3. On-Base Resources: Provide on-base resources, such as lactation rooms, baby care services, and parenting classes, to support military members during the early stages of pregnancy and after childbirth.
4. Military Discounts: Offer military discounts on essential items, such as diapers, formula, and baby clothes, to help military members save money during this time.
5. Career Counseling: Provide career counseling and job placement services specifically for military members who are pregnant or have recently given birth. This will help them navigate their career options and ensure they can continue serving in the military while balancing family responsibilities.
6. Childcare Services: Offer on-base childcare services, such as drop-in care or part-time slots, to give military members flexibility in their schedules during the first trimester of pregnancy.
7. Financial Planning Workshops: Host financial planning workshops specifically for military members who are pregnant or have recently given birth. These workshops can help them budget, save, and plan for their future while managing the financial responsibilities of a new baby.
8. Dependent Care Assistance Programs: Develop and implement dependent care assistance programs that provide financial support for military members to cover childcare expenses during the first trimester of pregnancy.
