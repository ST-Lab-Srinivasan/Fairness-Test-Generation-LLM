Promoting the educational success of non-independent students with disabilities requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their individual needs and provides supportive environments. Here are some strategies to help achieve this goal:

1. Inclusive Classrooms: Create an inclusive learning environment where all students feel valued and supported. Use differentiated instruction techniques, flexible grouping, and cooperative learning strategies to accommodate diverse learning styles and abilities.
2. Accommodations and Modifications: Provide individualized accommodations and modifications based on each student's needs. This may include visual aids, assistive technology, sign language interpretation, or extra time to complete assignments.
3. Speech and Language Therapy: Offer speech and language therapy sessions to help students improve their communication skills, such as articulation, fluency, and comprehension.
4. Occupational Therapy: Provide occupational therapy to help students develop fine motor, visual perception, and sensory integration skills.
5. Physical Therapy: Offer physical therapy sessions to help students improve their gross motor skills, balance, coordination, and mobility.
6. Individualized Education Programs (IEPs): Develop and implement IEPs for each non-independent student. These plans should outline specific goals, accommodations, and modifications tailored to the individual's needs.
7. Parental Involvement: Encourage parents to take an active role in their child's education by attending IEP meetings, providing emotional support, and helping with homework.
8. Teacher Training: Offer regular training and professional development opportunities for teachers on how to effectively teach students with diverse learning needs.
9. Collaboration with Specialists: Work closely with special educators, speech therapists, occupational therapists, and other professionals to ensure that students receive comprehensive support.
10. Technology Integration: Leverage technology to provide adaptive materials, assistive communication devices, and other tools to support non-independent students in their academic endeavors.
