1. Financial Strain: Military service members often face unique financial challenges due to deployments, training exercises, and other military-related activities. Long-term divorce can add to these financial strains, especially if one partner is not employed or has a lower income.
2. Emotional Stress: Divorce can be emotionally taxing, especially for those in their late 30s who may be dealing with the added stress of military life. The constant moves, separations, and deployments can take an emotional toll on both partners, making it difficult to maintain a healthy relationship.
3. Child Custody and Visitation: If there are children involved, the divorce process can become even more complex, especially if one partner is a military service member. Military regulations may impact child custody and visitation arrangements, which can add an extra layer of stress to the already challenging divorce process.
4. Career Advancement: Military spouses often face career challenges due to frequent moves and deployments. These challenges can be particularly difficult for long-term, divorced individuals in their late 30s who may be trying to advance their careers or establish a new one.
5. Social Isolation: Divorce can lead to social isolation, especially if one partner is a military service member and the other is not part of the military community. This can make it difficult to maintain social connections and build a new support network.
6. Stigma and Judgment: Unfortunately, there may be a stigma attached to divorce, particularly in military communities. Long-term, divorced individuals in their late 30s may face judgment or criticism from friends, family, and even fellow service members, which can add to the emotional strain of the situation.
