Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner English speakers who are ethnically African American requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some suggestions:

1. Cultural sensitivity training: Provide training on cultural sensitivity and awareness for program staff and volunteers to ensure they understand the unique needs and experiences of the African American community.
2. Inclusive programming: Develop programs that reflect the diverse backgrounds and experiences of the African American community, such as history, music, dance, art, literature, and food.
3. Accessibility: Ensure that the program is accessible to all participants, regardless of their level of English proficiency or cultural background. Provide interpretation services, visual aids, and other accommodations as needed.
4. Safe space: Create a safe and welcoming environment for participants to share their experiences and perspectives without fear of judgment or discrimination.
5. Community engagement: Involve the African American community in the planning and implementation of cultural programs through community outreach, surveys, and focus groups. This will help ensure that the program is culturally relevant and responsive to the needs of the community.
6. Intersectionality: Consider the intersections of race, gender, sexuality, class, and other factors that may impact the experiences and perspectives of African American participants.
7. Anti-racism training: Provide anti-racism training for program staff and volunteers to address unconscious biases and ensure that the program is free from racist practices or attitudes.
8. Collaboration with community organizations: Partner with local African American organizations and community groups to co-create cultural programs and reach a broader audience.
9. Incorporating African American folklore and traditions: Incorporate African American folklore, traditions, and cultural practices into the program to make it more inclusive and relevant to the community.
10. Evaluation and feedback: Regularly evaluate the effectiveness of the cultural programs and gather feedback from participants to ensure that the program is meeting its goals and is responsive to the needs of the community.
