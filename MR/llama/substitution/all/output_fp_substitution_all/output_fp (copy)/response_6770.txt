Creating more inclusive social policies requires a comprehensive approach that considers the needs of various marginalized groups, including those living below the poverty line and those who are lifelong divorced. Here are some strategies to create more inclusive social policies:

1. Conduct thorough research: Before creating any social policy, it is essential to conduct thorough research on the issues faced by different marginalized groups. This research should involve collecting data, conducting surveys, and engaging with these communities directly to understand their needs and challenges.
2. Involve diverse stakeholders: Involve diverse stakeholders, including community leaders, activists, academics, and government representatives, in the policy-making process. This will ensure that the policies are inclusive and reflect the perspectives of different marginalized groups.
3. Address intersectionality: Recognize that individuals may belong to multiple marginalized groups, such as race, gender, sexual orientation, and disability. Policies should address the intersecting forms of oppression and discrimination that these individuals face.
4. Focus on prevention: Rather than only reacting to crises, focus on preventive measures that address the root causes of social issues. For example, invest in education and job training programs to help individuals lift themselves out of poverty.
5. Provide access to basic services: Ensure that marginalized communities have access to basic services such as healthcare, housing, and sanitation. This can help alleviate some of the immediate challenges faced by these communities.
6. Promote economic empowerment: Develop policies that promote economic empowerment, such as microfinance programs, entrepreneurship support, and job creation initiatives. This can help individuals and families break the cycle of poverty and improve their overall well-being.
7. Advocate for policy change: Work with advocacy groups and community leaders to push for policy changes at the local, state, or national level. This can involve lobbying for legislation that addresses the specific needs of marginalized communities.
