Ensuring that the voices of Native English Speaking Mixed African Americans (NESMAA) are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting equity and inclusion. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Representation Matters: Ensure that NESMAA individuals are represented in leadership positions, decision-making tables, and community organizations. This can be achieved by implementing targeted recruitment strategies, providing mentorship opportunities, and creating safe spaces for NESMAA voices to be heard.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for leaders and decision-makers to help them understand the unique experiences and challenges faced by NESMAA individuals. This can include workshops, training sessions, and panel discussions featuring NESMAA experts and community members.
3. Community Engagement: Foster community engagement and participation in decision-making processes by creating opportunities for NESMAA individuals to share their perspectives and ideas. This can be achieved through town hall meetings, focus groups, and community surveys that prioritize NESMAA voices.
4. Intersectionality: Recognize the intersectional identities of NESMAA individuals, including race, ethnicity, gender, sexuality, class, and disability. Address the unique challenges faced by these individuals by incorporating an intersectional lens in decision-making processes.
5. Accountability Mechanisms: Establish accountability mechanisms to ensure that NESMAA voices are heard and valued in decision-making processes. This can include tracking participation metrics, conducting regular check-ins with NESMAA community members, and monitoring progress towards inclusivity goals.
6. Community Partnerships: Build partnerships with organizations that serve NESMAA communities to amplify their voices and ensure that they are included in decision-making processes. This can include collaborating with community-based organizations, advocacy groups, and social service providers.
7. Public Education Campaigns: Launch public education campaigns to raise awareness about the experiences and challenges faced by NESMAA individuals. This can include advertising campaigns, social media initiatives, and community outreach programs that highlight the importance of inclusivity and representation.
