Ensuring that the voices of beginner Spanish speakers, particularly those who are newly single or previously excluded from decision-making processes, is crucial for promoting inclusivity and equity. Here are some strategies to amplify their voices:

1. Language-accessible communication: Use simple language, visual aids, and interpretive services to facilitate participation. This can include providing written materials in the target language or using visual aids like pictures or diagrams to help them understand complex concepts.
2. Cultural humility: Be mindful of power dynamics and cultural differences when engaging with beginner Spanish speakers. Listen actively, ask open-ended questions, and avoid making assumptions based on stereotypes or biases.
3. Capacity building: Provide training and capacity-building opportunities to help them develop the skills and knowledge necessary for meaningful participation in decision-making processes. This can include workshops on communication, leadership, and advocacy.
4. Safe spaces: Create safe and inclusive spaces where beginner Spanish speakers feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and opinions without fear of judgment or marginalization. Encourage active listening and respectful dialogue to foster trust and mutual understanding.
5. Mentorship programs: Pair beginner Spanish speakers with more experienced individuals who can provide guidance, support, and advocacy. This can help build their confidence and skills in navigating decision-making processes.
6. Collaborative decision-making: Involve beginner Spanish speakers in collaborative decision-making processes through participatory techniques like consensus-building, brainstorming, or nominal group technique. This can help ensure their voices are heard and valued in the decision-making process.
7. Access to technology: Provide access to digital tools and platforms that enable beginner Spanish speakers to participate in decision-making processes remotely. This can include virtual meetings, online surveys, or social media engagement.
8. Building alliances: Foster partnerships with organizations that support beginner Spanish speakers, such as community centers, cultural institutions, or advocacy groups. These alliances can help amplify their voices and provide additional support in decision-making processes.
