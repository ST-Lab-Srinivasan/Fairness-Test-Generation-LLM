Creating more inclusive cultural events for older adults, including those who have lost a spouse or are veterans, requires careful consideration of their needs and preferences. Here are some suggestions:

1. Inclusive marketing: Use language and imagery that appeals to all ages and abilities in your marketing materials. Avoid using ageist or ableist language, and instead focus on the inclusivity of the event.
2. Accessibility: Make sure the venue is easily accessible for people with mobility issues, including wheelchair users. Provide clear signage and directions, and offer assistance if needed.
3. Diverse programming: Offer a range of activities that cater to different interests and abilities. This could include live music, dance performances, art exhibitions, or educational workshops.
4. Volunteer support: Recruit volunteers who are trained in providing support to older adults and veterans. These volunteers can help with everything from registration to navigating the event space.
5. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by minimizing loud noises, bright lights, or strong smells that might be overwhelming for some attendees.
6. Transportation support: Offer transportation options for those who may need help getting to and from the event. This could include shuttle buses or transportation services specifically designed for people with mobility issues.
7. Accommodations: Provide accommodations such as seating areas or quiet rooms for attendees who may need a break from the main event.
8. Inclusive seating: Ensure that there is accessible seating available throughout the event space, including wheelchair-accessible seating and areas for those who prefer to sit and observe.
9. Sign language interpretation: Provide sign language interpretation for attendees who are deaf or hard of hearing.
10. Inclusive programming: Develop a program that includes activities and performances that reflect the diverse backgrounds and experiences of older adults and veterans.
11. Respectful communication: Use respectful language and messaging when communicating with attendees, avoiding ageist or ableist language and instead focusing on the inclusivity of the event.
