Here are some successful community initiatives led by native French speakers in various fields:

1. Education:
	* "Education for All": A program launched by the French government in 2004 to provide quality education to disadvantaged children and young adults, particularly those from immigrant backgrounds. The program has led to significant improvements in educational outcomes for these groups.
	* "Au Fil de l'Eau": A non-profit organization that provides access to water and sanitation facilities in rural communities in Africa, Asia, and Latin America. Founded by a French couple, the organization has helped over 1 million people gain access to clean water and sanitation.
2. Healthcare:
	* "Médecins Sans Frontières" (Doctors Without Borders): A humanitarian organization that provides medical care to people affected by conflict, epidemics, disasters, or exclusion from healthcare. Founded in France in 1971, the organization has operations in over 70 countries around the world.
	* "La Ligue Contre le Cancer": A French non-profit organization that funds cancer research and provides support to cancer patients and their families. The organization has helped improve cancer treatment outcomes and has also launched awareness campaigns to reduce the stigma surrounding cancer.
3. Environment:
	* "The Nature Conservancy": A global environmental organization that works to protect ecosystems and preserve biodiversity. Founded in France in 1948, the organization has protected over 120 million acres of land and 5,000 miles of rivers around the world.
	* "Earth Observatory": A French non-profit organization that monitors and analyzes environmental data to inform policy decisions and raise awareness about environmental issues. The organization has developed innovative technologies such as satellite imaging and machine learning algorithms to track environmental changes.
