5. Improve Access to Healthcare: Improve access to healthcare services, particularly for underserved populations, by developing policies that prioritize healthcare financing, infrastructure development, and workforce capacity building.
6. Promote Early Childhood Development: Promote early childhood development by investing in programs that support the cognitive, emotional, social, and physical development of young children. This can be achieved through initiatives such as parenting skills training, early childhood education, and community-based interventions.
7. Support Small Businesses: Support small businesses and entrepreneurship by providing access to financing, mentorship, and other forms of support. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities for Nigerian infant males.
8. Address Discrimination: Address discrimination against Nigerian infant males based on their gender or socio-economic status. Develop policies that promote non-discriminatory practices and provide protections against discrimination in education, employment, and other areas.
