Ensuring the healthcare needs of recent temporary visa holders in India, particularly those from the working class, is crucial for their overall well-being and ability to contribute to society. Here are some steps that can be taken to address this issue:

1. Access to affordable healthcare: Recent temporary visa holders often face financial challenges, which can make it difficult for them to access quality healthcare. Governments and private organizations can work together to provide affordable healthcare options, such as subsidized clinics or hospitals, specifically designed for this population.
2. Health education and awareness: Many recent temporary visa holders may not be aware of the available healthcare resources or may not know how to access them. Governments and NGOs can launch public health education campaigns to inform this population about their rights and options, as well as provide information on preventive care and early detection of diseases.
3. Language barriers: Many recent temporary visa holders may face language barriers that hinder their ability to communicate with healthcare providers. Healthcare facilities can provide interpreters or translators to help bridge this gap and ensure that patients receive the appropriate care.
4. Culturally sensitive care: Recent temporary visa holders come from diverse cultural backgrounds, and healthcare providers must be culturally sensitive when providing care. This includes being aware of traditional healing practices and beliefs, as well as respecting patients' privacy and dignity.
5. Community outreach: Healthcare providers can work with community leaders and organizations to reach out to recent temporary visa holders and provide information on available healthcare services. Outreach programs can also help build trust between the healthcare system and this population.
6. Regular check-ups: Regular check-ups can help identify health issues early, which is crucial for this population. Healthcare providers can offer free or low-cost check-up camps specifically designed for recent temporary visa holders.
7. Mental health support: Recent temporary visa holders often face stress and anxiety related to their immigration status, which can impact their mental health. Healthcare providers can offer counseling services and mental health support groups specifically designed for this population.
