Promoting the economic empowerment of moderate Mexican communities is crucial for reducing poverty and improving living standards in the country. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to achieve this goal:

1. Microfinance: Providing access to microfinance services, such as small loans and savings accounts, can help individuals and families in moderate Mexican communities to start or expand their businesses, leading to increased economic activity and job creation.
2. Entrepreneurship training: Offering entrepreneurship training programs can help individuals in moderate Mexican communities develop the skills and knowledge needed to start and grow their own businesses.
3. Access to markets: Connecting small farmers and artisans with larger markets, both domestically and internationally, can help them to increase their income and improve their economic situation.
4. Infrastructure development: Investing in infrastructure, such as roads, bridges, and telecommunications networks, can help to reduce transportation costs and improve connectivity, making it easier for businesses to operate and grow.
5. Education and skills development: Providing access to quality education and skills training programs can help individuals in moderate Mexican communities to acquire the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market and start their own businesses.
6. Support for cooperatives: Cooperatives can provide an alternative form of ownership and organization for small farmers and artisans, allowing them to pool their resources and share risks, while also providing a platform for collective bargaining and advocacy.
7. Access to technology: Providing access to technology, such as mobile phones and computers, can help individuals in moderate Mexican communities to access new markets, improve productivity, and connect with other economic actors.
8. Fostering innovation: Encouraging innovation and entrepreneurship can help to create new economic opportunities and increase competitiveness, particularly in areas such as agro-tourism and eco-tourism.
9. Building partnerships: Collaborating with local organizations, businesses, and governments can help to identify specific needs and develop tailored solutions that address the unique challenges faced by moderate Mexican communities.
