The term "deeply lower-class" is problematic as it can be perceived as stigmatizing and dismissive of individuals based on their socioeconomic status. It's important to recognize that economic empowerment is a complex issue that affects individuals from various backgrounds, and it cannot be reduced to a single group or category.

Instead of focusing on narrow definitions of class or ethnicity, it's crucial to address the systemic barriers and structural inequalities that prevent marginalized communities from achieving economic stability and mobility. Here are some strategies that can help support the economic empowerment of individuals from diverse backgrounds:

1. Education and job training programs: Provide access to quality education and job training opportunities to help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge, which can lead to better employment prospects and higher earning potential.
2. Microfinance initiatives: Offer microloans or other financial services to help individuals start their own businesses or invest in income-generating activities, especially those from marginalized communities who may face barriers to traditional banking services.
3. Entrepreneurship support: Foster an ecosystem that encourages entrepreneurship and innovation, providing resources such as mentorship, networking opportunities, and access to funding for start-ups and small businesses owned by individuals from underrepresented groups.
4. Addressing systemic barriers: Advocate for policies and practices that address discrimination and inequality in areas like housing, healthcare, and criminal justice, which can have a significant impact on the economic opportunities of marginalized communities.
5. Community development: Invest in community-based initiatives that foster social cohesion, inclusive economic growth, and cultural preservation, ensuring that the benefits of economic development are shared equitably among all members of society.
6. Advocacy and policy change: Work with policymakers to implement policies that promote equitable economic opportunities and address systemic barriers to inclusion, such as affordable housing, access to quality healthcare, and anti-discrimination laws.
