Ensuring the healthcare needs of Nigerian long-term unemployed people can be challenging due to several factors, including limited access to healthcare facilities, high out-of-pocket expenses, and a lack of awareness about available healthcare services. Here are some potential strategies that could be implemented to address this issue:

1. Increase access to primary healthcare services: Establishing more primary healthcare centers in underserved areas can improve access to basic healthcare services, including preventive care and management of chronic conditions.
2. Implement an insurance scheme: Introducing a national health insurance program or partnering with private insurance companies can help reduce out-of-pocket expenses for unemployed individuals, making healthcare more affordable and accessible.
3. Promote community-based healthcare: Community-based healthcare programs can help address the social determinants of health, such as poor living conditions, inadequate nutrition, and lack of physical activity, which are common among long-term unemployed individuals.
4. Provide mental health support: Unemployment can have a significant psychological impact on individuals, including increased stress, anxiety, and depression. Offering mental health services, such as counseling and therapy, can help address these issues.
5. Leverage technology: Telemedicine and mobile health technologies can help bridge the gap between unemployed individuals and healthcare services, particularly in underserved areas.
6. Collaborate with NGOs and CBOs: Partnering with non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and community-based organizations (CBOs) can help reach vulnerable populations and provide targeted interventions to address their specific healthcare needs.
7. Provide education and training: Offering health-related education and training programs can empower unemployed individuals to take control of their health, making informed decisions about their healthcare and managing chronic conditions more effectively.
8. Encourage preventive care: Implementing public health campaigns and promoting preventive care can help identify health issues early on, reducing the risk of chronic diseases and improving overall health outcomes.
