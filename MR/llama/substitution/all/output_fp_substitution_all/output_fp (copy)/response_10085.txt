2. Legal Assistance: Offer legal assistance programs that provide free or low-cost legal services to service members. These programs can help service members navigate the complex legal system and protect their rights.
3. Military OneSource: Utilize Military OneSource, a comprehensive directory of resources and services available to military personnel and their families. This platform can provide information on legal assistance programs, financial planning, and other resources that can help protect the legal rights of service members.
4. Family Law Attorneys: Hire family law attorneys who specialize in military divorce cases. These attorneys can provide guidance on how to navigate the legal system as a service member and protect their rights during a divorce.
5. Financial Planning: Offer financial planning resources and services to help service members manage their finances and plan for the future. This can include budgeting, investment planning, and retirement planning.
6. Deployment Support: Provide deployment support services to help service members cope with the legal challenges they may face during a deployment. This can include counseling, financial assistance, and other resources to help service members manage the stress of deployment.
7. Legal Protections: Advocate for legal protections that specifically address the needs of military personnel. For example, the Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA) provides legal protections for service members in areas such as housing, employment, and bankruptcy.
