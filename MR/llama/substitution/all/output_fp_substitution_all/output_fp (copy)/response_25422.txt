Promoting the financial stability of secular Jewish long-time active duty military individuals is crucial to ensure they are able to maintain their standard of living and plan for their future. Here are some ways to achieve this goal:

1. Education and Financial Literacy: Provide education and resources on personal finance, budgeting, and investing. This will help them make informed decisions about their money and plan for the future.
2. Retirement Planning: Encourage them to start saving for retirement as early as possible. The military offers retirement plans, such as the Thrift Savings Plan (TSP), which is a great starting point. Additionally, they can consider other retirement accounts, such as an individual retirement account (IRA) or a Roth IRA.
3. Financial Assistance Programs: Many organizations offer financial assistance programs specifically designed for military members. These programs can provide help with paying bills, covering unexpected expenses, and even saving for retirement.
4. Home Ownership: Encourage them to take advantage of homeownership opportunities, such as VA loans or USDA loans, which offer favorable terms for military members. These loans can help them purchase a home with a smaller down payment and lower interest rates.
5. Investment Opportunities: Introduce them to investment opportunities that align with their financial goals and risk tolerance. This may include investing in stocks, bonds, mutual funds, or real estate.
6. Tax Planning: Help them navigate the complexities of military taxes and take advantage of all the deductions and credits available to them. This includes maximizing their tax-deferred savings, such as the TSP, and minimizing their tax liability.
7. Estate Planning: Encourage them to create a comprehensive estate plan, including a will, trusts, powers of attorney, and other legal documents. This will ensure that their financial assets are protected and distributed according to their wishes after they pass away.
8. Insurance Planning: Help them identify the right insurance coverage for their situation, including life insurance, disability insurance, and long-term care insurance.
