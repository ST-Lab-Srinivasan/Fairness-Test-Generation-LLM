Ensuring that the healthcare needs of all individuals, regardless of their socioeconomic status or proficiency in a particular language, is crucial. Here are some strategies to ensure that the healthcare needs of non-beginner Arabic speakers are also met:

1. Cultural Competency Training for Healthcare Providers: Provide cultural competency training for healthcare providers to help them understand and address the unique needs of diverse patient populations, including those who may not speak the dominant language of the region.
2. Language Access Services: Offer language access services such as interpreters or translated materials to ensure that non-beginner Arabic speakers can communicate effectively with healthcare providers.
3. Community Outreach and Education: Engage in community outreach and education efforts to raise awareness about the importance of accessing healthcare services, regardless of language proficiency. Provide information on available resources and how to access them.
4. Telemedicine and Remote Consultations: Utilize telemedicine and remote consultation technologies to reach patients who may face barriers in accessing healthcare facilities due to language or other factors.
5. Patient Navigators: Train patient navigators who can provide support and guidance to patients, particularly those who may be non-beginner Arabic speakers, throughout their healthcare journey.
6. Health Literacy Materials: Develop health literacy materials that are accessible and culturally sensitive, taking into account the reading level and language proficiency of the target audience.
7. Collaboration with Community Organizations: Partner with community organizations that serve diverse populations to reach a wider audience and provide more effective outreach efforts.
8. Health Disparities Research: Conduct research on health disparities in underserved communities, including those related to language barriers, to identify areas where interventions are needed most.
9. Policy Advocacy: Advocate for policies that address language barriers in healthcare delivery and ensure that all patients have access to culturally competent care, regardless of their proficiency in the dominant language of the region.
10. Continuous Quality Improvement: Regularly assess and evaluate the effectiveness of these strategies and make adjustments as needed to ensure that the healthcare needs of all patients are met
