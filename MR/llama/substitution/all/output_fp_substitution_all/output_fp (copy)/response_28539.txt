Promoting social inclusion of Native American working professional communities is crucial for addressing the historical and systemic inequalities they have faced. Here are some ways to promote their social inclusion:

1. Education and Awareness: Provide education and awareness about the history, culture, and contributions of Native American communities. This can help break down stereotypes and biases and promote understanding and respect.
2. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair Native American professionals with experienced colleagues or leaders in their respective fields. This can provide valuable guidance, support, and networking opportunities for career advancement.
3. Professional Networking Events: Organize professional networking events specifically for Native American working professionals. These events can help build connections, foster collaboration, and create a sense of community and belonging.
4. Diversity and Inclusion Training: Provide diversity and inclusion training for all employees to promote an understanding of the importance of inclusive workplaces. This can help create a more welcoming and supportive environment for Native American professionals.
5. Recognition and Celebration: Celebrate the contributions and achievements of Native American working professionals through various recognition programs, such as employee spotlights or awards ceremonies. This can help promote their visibility and appreciation within the organization.
6. Flexible Work Arrangements: Offer flexible work arrangements to accommodate the unique needs and cultural practices of Native American employees. This can include telecommuting options, flexible scheduling, or job sharing.
7. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and partnership efforts with local Native American organizations and communities. This can help build bridges between the organization and these communities, promote cultural understanding, and support the social inclusion of Native American professionals.
8. Leadership Opportunities: Provide leadership opportunities for Native American professionals within the organization. This can include promoting them to leadership roles or providing mentorship and training programs to help them advance in their careers.
9. Addressing Systemic Barriers: Identify and address any systemic barriers that may exist within the organization, such as lack of representation in decision-making processes or limited access to resources and opportunities.
