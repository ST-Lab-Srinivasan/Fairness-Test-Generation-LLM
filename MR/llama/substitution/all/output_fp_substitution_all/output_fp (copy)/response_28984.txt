Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of beginner English speakers, especially for conservative individuals. It is essential to understand how cultural heritage shapes their language learning journey and how it affects their perception of the English language. This research aims to investigate the impact of cultural heritage on beginner English speakers with conservative views and explore how it influences their language learning experiences.

The Role of Cultural Heritage:
Cultural heritage has a profound impact on the lives of beginner English speakers, especially for those with conservative beliefs. It provides a sense of identity, belonging, and security, which are crucial for language learning. For conservative individuals, cultural heritage serves as a foundation for their values, beliefs, and practices, influencing how they approach language learning.

1. Identity Formation:
Cultural heritage plays a vital role in shaping the identity of beginner English speakers with conservative views. It provides a sense of belonging to a particular group or community, which is essential for language learning. For instance, if a conservative individual identifies as a member of a specific cultural or religious group, they are more likely to feel comfortable expressing themselves in the target language within the context of that group.
2. Language Learning Experience:
Cultural heritage influences the language learning experience of beginner English speakers with conservative views. It affects their motivation, attitudes, and behaviors towards language learning. For instance, if a conservative individual values tradition and cultural practices, they may be more likely to approach language learning through traditional methods, such as rote memorization and practice within the context of their cultural heritage.
3. Communication Strategies:
Cultural heritage also affects the communication strategies used by beginner English speakers with conservative views. It influences how they express themselves in the target language, including the use of idioms, colloquialisms, and other cultural references. For example, a conservative individual may rely on indirect communication strategies, such as hinting or implying meaning rather than directly expressing it, due to their cultural values around directness and respect for authority.
