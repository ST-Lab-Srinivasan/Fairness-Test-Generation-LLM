Highlighting the issues faced by native Spanish speakers and native Arabic speakers can be done through various methods, including:

1. Data analysis: Analyze datasets of native Spanish and Arabic speakers to identify patterns and trends in language use, such as grammar, vocabulary, and pronunciation. This can help identify specific issues faced by these groups, such as difficulty with certain sounds or grammatical structures.
2. Surveys: Conduct surveys among native Spanish and Arabic speakers to gather information on their experiences and challenges related to language use. For example, you could ask about their level of proficiency in the language, the contexts in which they use it, and any difficulties they face.
3. Case studies: Conduct in-depth case studies of individual native Spanish and Arabic speakers to gather detailed information on their language use and experiences. This can help identify specific issues and challenges faced by these individuals, such as discrimination or cultural identity.
4. Observational studies: Observe native Spanish and Arabic speakers in naturalistic settings, such as homes, workplaces, or communities, to gather data on their language use and interactions. This can help identify subtle but important issues faced by these groups, such as social dynamics or power dynamics.
5. Machine learning: Use machine learning algorithms to analyze large datasets of text, speech, or other forms of language use by native Spanish and Arabic speakers. This can help identify patterns and trends that may not be immediately apparent through manual analysis.
6. Collaborative research: Collaborate with native Spanish and Arabic speakers and organizations that support these communities to gather data and insights on the issues they face. This can help ensure that the research is culturally sensitive and responsive to the needs of these groups.
7. Focus groups: Conduct focus groups with native Spanish and Arabic speakers to gather qualitative data on their experiences and challenges related to language use. This can help identify specific issues and concerns faced by these groups, as well as potential solutions.
8. Ethnographic research: Conduct ethnographic research among native Spanish and Arabic speakers to gather in-depth data on their language use and cultural practices. This can help identify the social and cultural contexts that shape their language use and experiences.
