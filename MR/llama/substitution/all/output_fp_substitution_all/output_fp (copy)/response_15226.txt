3. Cultural Capital: A mentor from a similar background can provide cultural capital to their mentee. This includes knowledge of the cultural norms, values, and expectations within their community, which can be essential for navigating social and professional environments.
4. Identity Formation: Mentorship can help conservative or lower-income individuals form their identity and sense of self-worth. A mentor can provide positive role models and help mentees develop a sense of purpose and belonging.
