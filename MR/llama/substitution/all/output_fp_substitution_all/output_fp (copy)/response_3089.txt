Promoting the economic empowerment of ultra-liberal Nigerian communities requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the social, economic, and political factors that hinder their development. Here are some strategies that can be employed:

1. Capacity Building: Provide training and capacity building programs for community members in areas such as entrepreneurship, financial management, and business skills. This will enable them to start and grow their own businesses, which will create jobs and generate income.
2. Access to Finance: Facilitate access to finance by providing microfinance loans, grants, or other forms of financial support. This will help community members to start or expand their businesses, and improve their overall economic well-being.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Create an enabling environment for entrepreneurship by providing resources such as incubation spaces, mentorship opportunities, and networking events. This will encourage innovation and creativity, and help to create successful businesses that can provide sustainable income streams.
4. Community Development: Invest in community development projects that promote social cohesion, improve infrastructure, and enhance the overall quality of life. This will create a conducive environment for economic growth and prosperity.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that promote the economic empowerment of ultra-liberal communities. This can include changes to laws and regulations that hinder their development, such as restrictions on access to finance or limited opportunities for entrepreneurship.
6. Partnerships and Collaborations: Foster partnerships and collaborations between ultra-liberal communities and other stakeholders, including government agencies, private sector companies, and civil society organizations. This will help to leverage resources, expertise, and networks to promote economic empowerment.
7. Skills Development: Provide training and development opportunities for community members in areas such as digital literacy, coding, and other skills that are in demand in the job market. This will enable them to compete effectively in the global economy and access better-paying jobs.
