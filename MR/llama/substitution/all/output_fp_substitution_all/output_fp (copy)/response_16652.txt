Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for permanent resident non-independent individuals involves recognizing and valuing their contributions to society, while also accommodating their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that can help create a more inclusive environment:

1. Involve them in the planning process: Invite permanent residents with disabilities or mental health conditions to participate in the planning and decision-making process for cultural celebrations. This will give them a sense of ownership and agency, and ensure that their needs are taken into account.
2. Provide accessible venues and activities: Make sure that the venue and activities are accessible to people with disabilities. This can include providing wheelchair accessibility, braille signage, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
3. Offer accommodations and support: Provide accommodations such as sign language interpreters, communication support workers, or service animals to help individuals with disabilities or mental health conditions participate fully in the celebration.
4. Use inclusive language and imagery: Avoid using language or imagery that can be offensive or alienating to certain groups. Use inclusive language and imagery that reflects the diversity of the community, including individuals with disabilities and mental health conditions.
5. Foster a culture of inclusion: Encourage a culture of inclusion and respect for all individuals, regardless of their abilities or challenges. This can involve providing education and training on disability etiquette and cultural sensitivity, and promoting a culture of empathy and understanding.
6. Provide respite and support: Offer respite and support services to individuals with disabilities or mental health conditions who may need additional help during the celebration. This can include providing one-on-one support, relaxation areas, or other forms of support.
7. Engage in advance planning: Plan ahead and engage with individuals with disabilities or mental health conditions well in advance of the celebration. This will give them the opportunity to provide input and feedback on how they can participate fully in the event.
