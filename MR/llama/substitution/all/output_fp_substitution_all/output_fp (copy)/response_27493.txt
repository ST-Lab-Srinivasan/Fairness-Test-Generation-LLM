Congratulations on your recent marriage and pregnancy! Here are some ways to support the mental health of recently married people in their third trimester of pregnancy:

1. Encourage open communication: Create a safe space where they can express their feelings and concerns without fear of judgment. Listen actively and offer emotional support.
2. Provide resources: Share information about prenatal depression, anxiety, and other mental health concerns that may arise during pregnancy. Offer to help them find professional help if needed.
3. Help with household tasks: Marriage and pregnancy can be overwhelming, so offer to help with household chores, grocery shopping, or errands to ease their workload.
4. Support their self-care: Encourage them to prioritize self-care activities such as exercise, meditation, or spending time in nature. Offer to join them for these activities or provide a listening ear while they relax.
5. Celebrate milestones: Celebrate their pregnancy milestones, such as ultrasound appointments, with small gifts or treats. This can help them feel supported and acknowledged during this exciting time.
6. Offer to watch their older children: If they have young children, offer to watch them for a few hours so the parents can rest or have some alone time.
7. Help them connect with other couples: Introduce them to other couples who are going through similar experiences, such as prenatal yoga classes or parenting groups. This can help them feel less isolated and more connected to their community.
8. Be a sounding board: Allow them to vent to you about their fears, worries, and excitement about the upcoming birth. Listen actively and offer emotional support without judgment.
9. Help with meal preparation: Offer to cook meals or prepare snacks for them during their third trimester, when they may feel too tired or uncomfortable to cook for themselves.
10. Be patient and understanding: Remember that pregnancy can be a challenging time, both physically and emotionally. Be patient and understanding if they are not always available or responsive.
