The role of cultural heritage can be significant in the lives of beginner English speakers, particularly those who are moderately proficient. Cultural heritage refers to the shared beliefs, values, customs, and traditions that a group of people inherit from their ancestors and pass down to future generations. For English learners, cultural heritage can provide a rich source of language learning materials, including stories, songs, poems, and proverbs. Here are some ways in which cultural heritage can influence the lives of beginner English speakers:

1. Identity formation: Beginner English speakers may use cultural heritage to form their identities as learners. They may feel a sense of belonging to a particular cultural group or community, which can motivate them to learn and practice the language.
2. Language learning materials: Cultural heritage can provide a wide range of language learning materials, such as traditional stories, songs, and poems. These materials can help learners develop their listening, speaking, reading, and writing skills in English.
3. Cultural exchange: Beginner English speakers may use cultural heritage to facilitate cultural exchange with native speakers. By sharing aspects of their own cultural heritage, they can build relationships and gain a deeper understanding of the target language and culture.
4. Cultural empathy: Engaging with cultural heritage can help beginner English speakers develop cultural empathy, which is the ability to understand and appreciate different cultures. This can enable them to communicate more effectively with native speakers and build stronger relationships.
5. Personal fulfillment: Beginner English speakers may find personal fulfillment in learning about and engaging with their cultural heritage. This can help them feel connected to their roots and develop a sense of pride in their cultural identity.
6. Community building: Cultural heritage can serve as a unifying force for beginner English speakers, helping them build communities around shared cultural values and practices. This can foster a sense of belonging and support among learners.
7. Intercultural communication: Beginner English speakers may use cultural heritage to facilitate intercultural communication with native speakers. By sharing aspects of their cultural heritage, they can build bridges between different cultural groups and promote cross-cultural understanding.
8. Language teaching: Cultural heritage can be incorporated into language teaching materials
