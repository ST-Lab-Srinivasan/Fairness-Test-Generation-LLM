Promoting the educational success of mixed African American students can be challenging, but there are several strategies that educators and parents can use to support these students. Here are some effective ways to promote their educational success:

1. Cultural Competence Training: Provide cultural competence training for teachers and staff to help them understand the unique experiences and challenges faced by mixed race students. This training can help teachers recognize and address any biases or stereotypes that may affect these students' educational experience.
2. Identity Affirmation: Encourage mixed race students to explore and affirm their identities through discussions, activities, and events that celebrate their cultural heritage. This can help them feel more connected to their identity and confident in their academic abilities.
3. Inclusive Classroom Culture: Foster an inclusive classroom culture that values diversity and promotes respect for all students. Encourage teachers to use diverse teaching methods and materials that reflect the experiences of mixed race students and promote a sense of belonging.
4. Support Networks: Create support networks for mixed race students, such as peer mentoring programs or clubs that focus on cultural identity and academic success. These networks can provide a sense of community and belonging, which can help students feel more motivated to succeed academically.
5. Addressing Microaggressions: Educate teachers and staff on how to recognize and address microaggressions that may affect mixed race students. This can include training on implicit bias and cultural responsiveness, as well as strategies for addressing subtle forms of racism and discrimination.
6. Family Engagement: Engage with the families of mixed race students to provide support and resources for their academic success. This can include parent-teacher conferences, family workshops, and communication through email or phone calls.
7. Academic Support: Provide academic support services, such as tutoring, academic clubs, and mentoring programs, to help mixed race students succeed academically. These services can help address any gaps in knowledge or skills that may exist due to their diverse backgrounds.
