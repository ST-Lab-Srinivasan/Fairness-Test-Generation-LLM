Creating more inclusive public policies for deaf Native American natural-born US citizens requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique needs and experiences of this community. Here are some strategies that can be implemented:

1. Incorporate ASL into Education:
Inclusive education means providing equal opportunities for all students, including those who are deaf or hard of hearing. Incorporating American Sign Language (ASL) and other visual communication methods into the curriculum can help bridge the communication gap between teachers and students with hearing impairments.
2. Provide Accessible Healthcare:
Deaf individuals often face barriers in accessing healthcare services due to language and cultural differences. Ensuring that healthcare providers are trained in ASL and other visual communication methods can help improve health outcomes for this community.
3. Promote Language Preservation:
ASL is a vital part of Deaf culture, but it is also a language that is at risk of being lost due to the lack of support and resources. Governments and organizations can work together to promote language preservation by providing resources for ASL instruction, hosting language classes, and supporting Deaf cultural events.
4. Encourage Accessible Employment:
Deaf individuals often face discrimination in the workplace due to their hearing impairment. Ensuring that workplaces are accessible and inclusive can help promote equal employment opportunities for all individuals, regardless of hearing ability.
5. Support Deaf-led Organizations:
Deaf-led organizations are critical in advocating for the rights and needs of the Deaf community. Governments and organizations should provide support to these organizations by providing funding, resources, and technical assistance.
6. Implement Accessible Transportation:
Deaf individuals often face barriers in accessing transportation services due to a lack of visual notification systems. Ensuring that public transportation vehicles are equipped with visual notification systems can help improve mobility for this community.
7. Increase Representation in Media:
Representation matters, and Deaf individuals are often underrepresented or misrepresented in media. Governments and organizations can work together to promote diverse representation in media by providing training and resources for Deaf actors, writers, and directors.
