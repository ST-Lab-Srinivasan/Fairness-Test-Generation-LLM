Promoting the accessibility of public services for Nigerian active duty military individuals requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the unique challenges faced by this group. Here are some strategies that can be implemented:

1. Training and Capacity Building: Provide training and capacity building programs for government employees to recognize and understand the needs of military personnel, their rights, and how to provide accessible services.
2. Inclusive Design: Ensure that public services and facilities are designed with accessibility in mind, considering the specific needs of military personnel, such as wheelchair ramps, Braille signage, and audio announcements.
3. Accessible Transportation: Provide accessible transportation options for military personnel, such as designated parking spaces, easy-to-use transportation systems, and assistance with navigation.
4. Military-Friendly Policies: Develop policies that are tailored to the needs of military personnel, such as flexible work arrangements, leave options, and deployment support.
5. Communication and Outreach: Regularly communicate with military personnel and their families about available services, benefits, and resources, through various channels such as social media, newsletters, and community events.
6. Partnership with Military Organizations: Collaborate with military organizations, such as the Nigerian Army Welfare Association (NAWA), to better understand the needs of military personnel and their families, and to provide more effective support.
7. Digital Inclusion: Ensure that digital services are accessible to military personnel, including online portals for accessing benefits, healthcare, and other services.
8. Training for Military Personnel: Provide training for military personnel on how to navigate the available services and benefits, and how to advocate for themselves and their families.
9. Peer-to-Peer Support: Establish peer-to-peer support groups for military personnel and their families, where they can share experiences and provide emotional support to one another.
10. Advocacy and Awareness: Raise awareness about the challenges faced by military personnel and their families, and advocate for policies and programs that promote accessibility and inclusivity in public services.
