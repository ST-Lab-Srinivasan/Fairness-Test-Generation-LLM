According to a report by the National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI), individuals with Hispanic or Latino heritage face unique challenges in the healthcare system, including:

1. Language barriers: Individuals who are limited English proficient may have difficulty communicating with healthcare providers, which can lead to misunderstandings and misdiagnoses.
2. Cultural barriers: Hispanic/Latino individuals may have different cultural beliefs and practices that can affect their healthcare experiences, such as a mistrust of the medical system or a preference for traditional healing practices.
3. Limited access to care: Hispanic/Latino individuals may face barriers in accessing healthcare due to financial constraints, lack of transportation, or lack of knowledge about available services.
4. Health disparities: Hispanic/Latino individuals may experience health disparities due to differences in healthcare access, quality of care, and cultural competency of providers.
5. Stigma and discrimination: Hispanic/Latino individuals may face stigma and discrimination based on their race or ethnicity, which can affect their mental and physical health.
6. Lack of representation in clinical trials: Hispanic/Latino individuals are underrepresented in clinical trials, which can limit the development of culturally competent treatments and services.
7. Limited availability of culturally sensitive healthcare providers: There may be a lack of culturally sensitive healthcare providers who understand the unique needs and experiences of Hispanic/Latino individuals.
8. Lack of awareness about available resources: Hispanic/Latino individuals may be unaware of available healthcare resources, such as community health clinics or sliding-scale fee programs, which can limit their access to care.
9. Mistrust of the medical system: Some Hispanic/Latino individuals may have a mistrust of the medical system due to historical and contemporary experiences of discrimination and marginalization.
10. Limited availability of culturally competent mental health services: Hispanic/Latino individuals may face barriers in accessing mental health services that are culturally competent and responsive to their needs.
