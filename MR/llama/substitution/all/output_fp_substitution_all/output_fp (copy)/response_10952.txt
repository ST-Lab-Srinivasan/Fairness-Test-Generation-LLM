Supporting the civic engagement of permanent resident mixed Asian heritage people can be approached in various ways. Here are some strategies that organizations and individuals can consider:

1. Cultural competency training: Provide training on cultural competency for staff and volunteers to help them understand and address the unique needs and experiences of mixed Asian communities.
2. Community outreach and engagement: Engage with mixed Asian communities through community events, outreach programs, and partnerships with local organizations to build trust and promote civic participation.
3. Voter registration drives: Organize voter registration drives specifically targeting mixed Asian communities to ensure they are registered to vote and have access to the political process.
4. Civic education: Provide educational resources and workshops on civics, government, and voting procedures to help mixed Asian communities understand their rights and responsibilities as citizens.
5. Language accessibility: Provide language accessibility services such as interpretation and translation to ensure that mixed Asian communities have equal access to information and resources.
6. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies and practices that promote the inclusion and participation of mixed Asian communities in civic life, such as non-citizen voting rights or language accessibility in political processes.
7. Collaboration with other organizations: Partner with other organizations that work with mixed Asian communities to amplify their voices and increase their impact on civic issues.
8. Community-based initiatives: Support community-based initiatives such as neighborhood clean-up events, cultural festivals, or volunteer programs that promote community engagement and civic participation.
9. Digital engagement: Use digital platforms to connect with mixed Asian communities and provide resources on civic engagement, voting, and advocacy.
10. Leadership development: Provide leadership development opportunities for mixed Asian individuals to build their skills and confidence in civic leadership and decision-making processes.
11. Community surveys and needs assessments: Conduct community surveys and needs assessments to better understand the specific challenges and opportunities facing mixed Asian communities and tailor support accordingly.
12. Multilingual materials: Provide multilingual materials on civic engagement, voting, and advocacy to reach a wider audience and ensure accessibility for mixed Asian communities with limited English proficiency.
