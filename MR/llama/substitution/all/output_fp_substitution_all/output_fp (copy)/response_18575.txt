Promoting the social inclusion of Native American recent undocumented immigrants in affluent communities requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the unique challenges and barriers faced by this population. Here are some strategies that can help:

2. Language Access: Provide language access services such as interpreters, translators, and bilingual staff in healthcare and social service settings to ensure that Native American recent undocumented immigrants can fully participate in these services.
3. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for healthcare and social service providers to help them understand the unique needs and experiences of Native American recent undocumented immigrants.
4. Community Engagement: Encourage affluent communities to engage with Native American recent undocumented immigrants through volunteer opportunities, cultural events, and community activities. This can help to build relationships and foster a sense of belonging.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that support the inclusion and well-being of Native American recent undocumented immigrants, such as access to healthcare, education, and legal protection.
6. Economic Empowerment: Provide economic empowerment opportunities such as job training, microfinance, and entrepreneurship support to help Native American recent undocumented immigrants achieve financial stability and independence.
7. Legal Support: Provide free or low-cost legal services to Native American recent undocumented immigrants to help them navigate the complex immigration system and access available resources.
8. Community Health Workers: Train community health workers from affluent communities to work with Native American recent undocumented immigrants in their own languages and cultural contexts, which can help to build trust and improve health outcomes.
