Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner French speakers who are ethnically African American requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions to make the program more welcoming and inclusive:

1. Representation Matters: Ensure that the cultural program features diverse content, including works by African American artists, writers, and musicians. This will help learners connect with the material on a personal level and feel seen and represented in the program.
2. Inclusive Language: Use language that is inclusive and respectful of all cultures. Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes any particular group. Instead, use language that celebrates diversity and promotes understanding and empathy.
3. Cultural Context: Provide cultural context for the materials being taught. This can include information about the history, traditions, and customs of African American culture. By providing this context, learners will gain a deeper appreciation and understanding of the material.
4. Collaboration with Community Members: Involve members of the African American community in the development and delivery of the cultural program. This can include collaborating with artists, writers, musicians, and other cultural practitioners to create content that is authentic and meaningful to the community.
5. Safe Spaces: Create safe spaces for learners to share their experiences and perspectives. This can be done through interactive activities, discussions, and other methods that promote mutual respect and understanding.
6. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training on cultural competency for instructors and staff involved in the program. This can help ensure that everyone is aware of the needs and experiences of learners from diverse backgrounds and can provide appropriate support.
