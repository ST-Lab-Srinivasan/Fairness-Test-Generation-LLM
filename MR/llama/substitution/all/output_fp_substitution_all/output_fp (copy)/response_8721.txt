1. Recognize the military background: Active-duty refugees have served their country and protected its interests. Recognizing this service and providing support for their military experience can go a long way in creating a welcoming environment. This can include providing access to military resources, such as career counseling and transition assistance programs.
2. Address language barriers: Many active-duty refugees may face language barriers due to cultural or linguistic differences. Providing language training and support can help them navigate the social services system and access necessary resources.
3. Offer job training and placement: Active-duty refugees often face significant challenges when it comes to finding employment. Providing job training and placement services can help them gain the skills and experience they need to succeed in the civilian workforce.
4. Provide housing support: Many active-duty refugees may struggle with finding affordable and safe housing. Offering rental assistance or providing information on available housing resources can help address this challenge.
5. Address healthcare needs: Active-duty refugees may have unique healthcare needs due to their military service. Providing access to medical care and mental health services can help them address these needs and maintain their overall well-being.
6. Offer cultural orientation: Moving to a new country and culture can be challenging for anyone, including active-duty refugees.
