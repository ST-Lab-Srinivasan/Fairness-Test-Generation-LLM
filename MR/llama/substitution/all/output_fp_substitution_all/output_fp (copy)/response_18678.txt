Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of natural-born US citizen ethnically African American people. It shapes their identity, influences their beliefs and practices, and provides a sense of belonging and connection to their community. Here are some ways cultural heritage affects the lives of ethnically African American people:

1. Sense of Belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging and connection to one's community. Ethnically African American people can trace their roots back to Africa, and their cultural practices, beliefs, and traditions are deeply rooted in African cultures. This shared history and culture creates a strong sense of belonging among members of the community.
2. Identity Formation: Cultural heritage helps shape the identity of ethnically African American people. It influences how they perceive themselves and their place in society, including their values, beliefs, and practices. Their cultural heritage informs their understanding of themselves and their role in the world.
3. Resilience and Adaptability: Cultural heritage has enabled ethnically African American people to adapt and respond to challenges throughout history. The resilience and resourcefulness of African Americans can be attributed, in part, to their cultural heritage, which has allowed them to maintain their identity and traditions despite oppression and discrimination.
4. Connection to Ancestors: Cultural heritage provides a connection to ancestors and the past. Ethnically African American people can honor and learn from their ancestors through cultural practices such as storytelling, music, dance, and art. This connection helps them feel grounded and rooted in their history and culture.
5. Preservation of Culture: Cultural heritage is essential for preserving the unique customs, beliefs, and practices of ethnically African American people. It allows them to pass down their traditions and values to future generations, ensuring the continuation of their cultural identity.
6. Pride and Identity Expression: Cultural heritage provides a sense of pride and identity expression for ethnically African American people. They can express their cultural identity through various means, such as music, art, literature, and fashion, which helps them assert their presence in society.
