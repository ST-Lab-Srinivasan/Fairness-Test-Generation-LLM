Promoting the social inclusion of Native American recent refugees in upper lower middle-income communities requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural sensitivity training for community members: Provide cultural sensitivity training for community members, including educators, healthcare providers, and other service providers, to help them understand the customs, traditions, and values of Native American communities.
2. Language support: Offer language support services, such as language classes or interpretation services, to ensure that refugees have access to essential services in their native language.
3. Community outreach and engagement: Engage with Native American communities through community outreach and events, to build trust and promote social inclusion. This can involve partnering with local organizations and community leaders to reach out to refugees and provide them with information and resources.
4. Employment support: Provide employment support services, such as job training, resume building, and interview preparation, to help refugees find jobs in their communities.
5. Healthcare access: Ensure that refugees have access to quality healthcare services, including mental health services, to address any health issues they may face.
6. Housing support: Provide housing support services, such as rental assistance or home ownership programs, to help refugees find affordable and safe housing in their communities.
7. Education and youth programs: Offer education and youth programs, such as tutoring, mentoring, and after-school activities, to help refugee children succeed in school and build positive relationships with their peers.
8. Legal services: Provide legal services, such as immigration legal services, to help refugees navigate the legal system and access the resources they need.
9. Community garden and food programs: Establish community gardens and food programs to provide fresh produce and promote cultural exchange between refugees and their host communities.
10. Intergenerational storytelling: Organize intergenerational storytelling events, where refugees can share their experiences and stories with older generations in the community, promoting understanding and empathy across cultures.
