Supporting the civic engagement of progressive mixed African American people requires recognizing and addressing the unique challenges and barriers they face. Here are some ways to support their civic engagement:

1. Listen and amplify their voices: Hear their stories, listen to their concerns, and amplify their voices in your networks. Create spaces for them to share their experiences and perspectives.
2. Educate yourself: Learn about the history and current state of mixed race identity, the challenges they face, and the ways they are pushing for change. This will help you better understand how to support them.
3. Advocate for policy changes: Support policies that promote equity and inclusion for mixed race individuals, such as anti-discrimination laws and measures to address systemic racism.
4. Engage in voter registration and get-out-the-vote efforts: Register mixed race individuals to vote and encourage them to participate in elections. Provide transportation or childcare for those who may face barriers to voting.
5. Support mixed race-led organizations: Financial support, volunteering, and networking can help build the capacity of organizations led by mixed race individuals, such as community groups, advocacy organizations, and political campaigns.
6. Create inclusive spaces: Make sure that all voices are heard in decision-making processes by creating spaces that are inclusive and welcoming to all. Encourage diverse perspectives and create opportunities for mixed race individuals to lead and participate.
7. Address internalized racism: Internalized racism can be a significant barrier to civic engagement for mixed race individuals. Create spaces where they can process their experiences and work through these issues.
8. Provide mentorship and leadership opportunities: Connect mixed race individuals with mentors and leadership opportunities to help them build skills and confidence in their civic engagement.
9. Build coalitions: Collaborate with other communities of color and allies to address the unique challenges faced by mixed race individuals and promote collective action.
10. Celebrate and amplify mixed race identity: Highlight the contributions and experiences of mixed race individuals in your communities, organizations, and networks. Amplify their voices and celebrate their identities to promote a more inclusive and equitable society
