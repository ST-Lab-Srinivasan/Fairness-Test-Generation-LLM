3. Language Support: Provide language support services such as translation services, interpreters, or bilingual tutors to assist Native Spanish speaker students who may need extra help with language-related tasks.

6. Teaching Strategies: Use teaching strategies that are culturally responsive and linguistically appropriate for Native Spanish speakers, such as using visual aids, hands-on activities, and authentic materials from their culture.
7. Assessment and Evaluation: Use assessment and evaluation tools that are culturally sensitive and linguistically appropriate to measure the academic progress of Native Spanish speaker students.
8. Professional Development: Provide ongoing professional development opportunities for teachers to learn about the cultural backgrounds and language needs of Native Spanish speakers, as well as strategies for teaching and supporting these students.
9. Resources and Materials: Provide resources and materials that reflect the cultural diversity of Native Spanish speakers, such as books, videos, and other multimedia resources that represent their culture and experiences.
