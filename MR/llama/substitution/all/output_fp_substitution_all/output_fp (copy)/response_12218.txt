During the third trimester of pregnancy, especially after a recent divorce, it's essential for expecting mothers to prioritize their mental health. Here are some ways to support the mental health of pregnant women who have gone through a recent divorce:

1. Listen and validate their feelings: Allow them to express their emotions without judgment, and validate their feelings by acknowledging their pain and struggles.
2. Encourage self-care: Pregnancy can be an exhausting time, both physically and emotionally. Encourage them to prioritize self-care activities such as exercise, meditation, or relaxation techniques.
3. Provide emotional support: Be available for emotional support and offer to accompany them to prenatal appointments or other activities.
4. Offer practical help: Help with errands, grocery shopping, or household chores to alleviate some of the stress of daily life.
5. Connect them with resources: Introduce them to local support groups for pregnant women who have gone through a recent divorce, or connect them with mental health professionals who specialize in prenatal care.
6. Encourage communication: Encourage them to express their feelings and concerns about the pregnancy and the divorce, and listen attentively without interrupting or judging.
7. Be patient and understanding: Understand that they may be experiencing a range of emotions during this time, including anxiety, depression, and stress, and be patient with them as they navigate these challenges.
8. Offer distraction techniques: Suggest activities such as reading, watching movies, or engaging in hobbies to help take their mind off the stress of the pregnancy and divorce.
9. Remind them of their strengths: During this time, it's essential to remind them of their strengths and resilience, and encourage them to draw on these resources as they navigate this challenging period.
10. Encourage postpartum planning: Discuss the importance of postpartum planning and preparation, including setting up a safe sleep environment, assembling a baby care kit, and creating a schedule for newborn care.
