Family support plays a crucial role in the success of over 90 years old physically disabled people. Here are some ways in which family support can contribute to their success:

1. Emotional Support: Family members can provide emotional support by being there for the elderly person, listening to their concerns, and offering words of encouragement. This can help alleviate feelings of loneliness and isolation that may be experienced by the elderly person.
2. Practical Assistance: Family members can assist with practical tasks such as grocery shopping, housekeeping, and personal care. This can help reduce the burden on the elderly person and allow them to focus on other aspects of their life.
3. Social Engagement: Family support can help the elderly person stay socially engaged by facilitating social interactions with other family members and friends. This can help prevent feelings of isolation and loneliness, which are common among the elderly.
4. Financial Support: Family members may provide financial support to help cover the costs of living, such as housing, food, and medical expenses. This can help reduce financial stress and allow the elderly person to focus on other aspects of their life.
5. Advocacy: Family members can act as advocates for the elderly person by speaking with healthcare providers, government agencies, and other organizations to ensure that their needs are met. This can help ensure that the elderly person receives the appropriate level of care and support.
6. Sense of Belonging: Family support can provide a sense of belonging and connection to others, which is important for overall well-being and happiness. Being part of a family unit can give the elderly person a sense of purpose and meaning in their life.
7. Coping with Challenges: Family support can help the elderly person cope with challenges such as illness, disability, or loss by providing emotional and practical support. This can help them navigate difficult times and maintain their quality of life.
8. Preserving Identity: Family support can help preserve the elderly person's identity and sense of self by providing opportunities for them to share their experiences and stories with others. This can help them feel connected to their past and present, and maintain a sense of purpose and meaning in their life.
