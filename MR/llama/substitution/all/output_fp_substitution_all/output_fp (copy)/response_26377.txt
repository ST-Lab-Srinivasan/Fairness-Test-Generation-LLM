1. Language barriers: Many refugees arrive in Canada with limited English or French proficiency, which can make it difficult for them to navigate the legal system and access necessary services.
2. Lack of familiarity with Canadian laws and policies: Refugees may be unfamiliar with Canadian laws and regulations, which can lead to misunderstandings and missteps during the immigration process.
3. Discrimination and bias: Unfortunately, discrimination and bias against Muslims and other marginalized groups still exist in Canada. Refugees may face prejudice and racism within the legal system, which can affect their ability to access justice.
4. Difficulty accessing legal aid: Many refugees may not have the financial means to hire a lawyer or access legal aid services, which can limit their ability to navigate the legal system effectively.
5. Complex immigration procedures: The Canadian immigration process can be complex and confusing, with many rules and regulations that are difficult to understand. Refugees may struggle to navigate this process without proper guidance and support.
6. Detention and removal threats: In some cases, refugees may be detained by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) pending their immigration hearing. They may also face removal from Canada if their application is denied.
7. Lack of cultural sensitivity: The Canadian legal system may not always take into account the cultural and religious practices of refugees, which can lead to misunderstandings and conflicts.
8. Limited access to mental health services: Refugees often experience trauma and stress during their journey to Canada, and may struggle to access mental health services that are tailored to their needs.
9. Difficulty finding employment: Refugees may face challenges finding work in Canada due to language barriers, lack of job experience, and discrimination. This can make it difficult for them to support themselves and their families.
