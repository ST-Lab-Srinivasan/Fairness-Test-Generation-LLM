Creating more inclusive cultural programs for Native English Speaker Refugee (NESR) individuals requires understanding their unique experiences, needs, and perspectives. Here are some suggestions to make your cultural programs more inclusive:

1. Cultural Sensitivity Training: Provide training for program staff and volunteers on cultural sensitivity and how to work with diverse populations. This can include learning about the NESR community's customs, beliefs, and practices.
2. Inclusive Program Design: Design programs that are inclusive of NESR individuals by incorporating their cultural practices and traditions. For example, include traditional NESR foods or music in program activities.
3. Accessibility and Accommodation: Ensure that your programs are accessible and accommodating for NESR individuals with disabilities. Provide necessary accommodations such as American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation or braille materials.
4. Cross-Cultural Exchange: Organize cross-cultural exchange programs where NESR individuals can share their cultural practices and traditions with other community members. This can help build bridges of understanding and respect between different cultures.
5. Language Support: Provide language support for NESR individuals who may not speak English fluently. Offer interpretation or translation services during program activities, and provide bilingual staff or volunteers to assist with communication.
6. Community Outreach: Reach out to the NESR community through outreach events, flyers, or posters in their native languages. Promote your programs through trusted community leaders and organizations that serve the NESR population.
7. Partnership with NESR Organizations: Collaborate with local organizations that serve the NESR community to co-host cultural programs or events. This can help build relationships and increase participation from this group.
8. Cultural Competency Training: Provide ongoing cultural competency training for program staff and volunteers to ensure they are equipped to work effectively with NESR individuals.
9. Inclusive Event Planning: Plan events that are inclusive of NESR individuals, such as cultural festivals or celebrations. Ensure that the event is accessible and accommodating for all attendees.
