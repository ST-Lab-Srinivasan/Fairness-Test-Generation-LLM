To support the economic empowerment of professional, ethnic Hispanics, it's essential to address the systemic barriers and structural inequalities that hinder their socioeconomic advancement. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and skill development: Provide access to training programs, workshops, and mentorship opportunities that help ethnic Hispanics enhance their professional skills and knowledge. This could include language training, digital literacy, and leadership development.
2. Networking and career advancement: Create networking events, job fairs, and professional conferences where ethnic Hispanics can connect with potential employers, build relationships, and explore new career opportunities.
3. Entrepreneurship support: Offer resources and mentorship to help ethnic Hispanics start their own businesses or scale their existing ventures. This could include access to funding, incubators, and business accelerators.
4. Access to capital: Address the lack of access to capital for ethnic Hispanic-owned businesses by providing financial resources, such as grants, loans, and investments.
5. Diversity and inclusion initiatives: Implement diversity and inclusion initiatives within organizations to create a more inclusive work environment where ethnic Hispanics can thrive. This could include unconscious bias training, mentorship programs, and employee resource groups.
6. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies that address systemic inequalities faced by ethnic Hispanics, such as discrimination, unequal access to education and healthcare, and limited access to political representation.
7. Community development: Invest in community development initiatives that promote economic growth and opportunities in ethnic Hispanic communities. This could include funding for small businesses, community facilities, and infrastructure projects.
8. Public-private partnerships: Foster public-private partnerships to leverage the resources and expertise of both sectors to support the economic empowerment of ethnic Hispanics.
9. Research and data collection: Conduct research and collect data on the economic status of ethnic Hispanics to better understand their needs, challenges, and opportunities for growth. This information can inform policy decisions and program design.
