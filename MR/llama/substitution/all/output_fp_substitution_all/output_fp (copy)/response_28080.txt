Creating more inclusive community programs for beginner English speakers who are not recently single individuals requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions to help you design such programs:

1. Identify the target audience: Start by identifying the group of people you want to include in your program. Are they immigrants, refugees, or international students? Are they from diverse cultural backgrounds? Understanding your target audience will help you tailor your program to their needs.
2. Assess language proficiency: Not everyone may have the same level of English proficiency. Consider offering different levels of classes based on language proficiency to ensure that everyone can participate and learn at their own pace.
3. Offer flexible scheduling options: Many beginners may have busy schedules or conflicting responsibilities. Offering flexible scheduling options, such as evening or weekend classes, can help accommodate different time constraints.
4. Provide childcare and transportation support: For parents or caregivers who are beginner English speakers, providing childcare and transportation support can be a significant barrier to participation. Consider offering on-site childcare or partnering with local organizations that provide transportation services.
5. Create a safe and inclusive learning environment: Beginner English speakers may feel nervous or anxious about participating in a language program. Creating a safe and inclusive learning environment can help them feel more comfortable and confident. Use visual aids, gestures, and simple language to help learners understand complex concepts.
6. Incorporate cultural activities: Language learning is not just about grammar and vocabulary; it's also about understanding the culture associated with the language. Consider incorporating cultural activities, such as food tastings or traditional dance, into your program to help learners connect with the language and its associated culture.
7. Offer one-on-one tutoring: Some beginner English speakers may benefit from personalized attention and guidance. Offer one-on-one tutoring sessions to help learners overcome specific challenges or improve their language skills in areas of interest.
