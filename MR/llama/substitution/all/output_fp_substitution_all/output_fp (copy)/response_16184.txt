Here are some successful community initiatives led by mixed African American/Hispanic individuals:

1. The National Association of Latino Elected and Appointed Officials (NALEO) Education Fund: Founded in 1981, NALEO is a non-partisan organization that aims to promote the political participation of Latinos in the United States. The organization provides leadership development programs, advocacy training, and research on issues affecting the Latino community.
2. The National Council of La Raza (NCLR): Founded in 1968, NCLR is a non-partisan organization that aims to advance the civil rights and well-being of Hispanic Americans. The organization provides advocacy training, policy analysis, and research on issues affecting the Latino community.
3. The Latino Justice PRLA (formerly known as the Puerto Rican Legal Defense and Education Fund): Founded in 1972, Latino Justice is a non-profit organization that provides legal advocacy services to the Latino community. The organization focuses on issues such as immigration, education, housing, and healthcare.
4. The Mexican American Legal Defense and Educational Fund (MALDEF): Founded in 1968, MALDEF is a non-profit organization that provides legal advocacy services to the Latino community. The organization focuses on issues such as immigration, voting rights, education, and employment discrimination.
5. The National Hispanic Media Coalition (NHMC): Founded in 1986, NHMC is a non-profit organization that aims to promote the representation and inclusion of Latinos in media. The organization provides training and mentorship programs for Latino media professionals and advocates for greater diversity and inclusion in media.
6. The Hispanic Association of Colleges and Universities (HACU): Founded in 1985, HACU is a non-profit organization that aims to promote the development and success of Hispanic-serving institutions (HSIs) and the advancement of Hispanics in higher education.
