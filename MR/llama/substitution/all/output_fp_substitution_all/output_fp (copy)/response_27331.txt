Ensuring the voices of native Arabic speakers, particularly those who are recent undocumented immigrants to the US, are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting inclusivity and equity. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Language Access: Provide language access services such as interpreters, translators, and bilingual staff in healthcare settings, schools, and government agencies. This will ensure that these individuals have equal access to information and services.
2. Community Engagement: Engage with Arabic-speaking communities through community outreach and events. This can help build trust and facilitate communication between these communities and decision-makers.
3. Advocacy Groups: Partner with advocacy groups that represent the interests of Arabic-speaking immigrants. These groups can provide a platform for individuals to voice their concerns and opinions on policy issues affecting them.
4. Public Education Campaigns: Launch public education campaigns in Arabic to raise awareness about issues affecting these communities, such as access to healthcare, education, and legal services.
5. Collaborative Decision-Making: Involve Arabic-speaking immigrants in decision-making processes through collaborative approaches that encourage their participation. This can include participatory budgeting, community planning, and other forms of co-creation.
6. Capacity Building: Provide capacity-building opportunities for Arabic-speaking immigrants to develop leadership skills and knowledge of policy issues affecting them. This can help increase their representation in decision-making processes.
7. Media Outreach: Utilize media outlets that cater to Arabic-speaking communities to disseminate information and engage with these individuals. This can help ensure that their voices are heard and their concerns are addressed.
8. Policy Reforms: Advocate for policy reforms that address the specific needs and challenges faced by Arabic-speaking immigrants, such as access to healthcare, education, and legal services.
9. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for decision-makers and service providers to better understand the needs and experiences of Arabic-speaking immigrant communities.
