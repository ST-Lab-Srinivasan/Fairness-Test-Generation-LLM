Title: The Impact of Political Affiliation on the Lives of Native French Speaker Cognitive Impaired People

Introduction:
Cognitive impairment refers to a decline in cognitive functioning, such as memory loss, difficulty with concentration, and problem-solving skills. In France, it is estimated that approximately 1.5 million people suffer from some form of cognitive impairment. This number is expected to increase as the population ages, making it crucial to understand how political affiliation affects the lives of these individuals. This study aims to investigate the impact of political affiliation on the daily lives of native French speaker cognitively impaired people in France.

Methodology:
A mixed-methods approach was employed, combining both qualitative and quantitative data collection and analysis methods. A survey was administered to 100 cognitively impaired individuals living in France, with 50 respondents identifying as right-wing politically affiliated and 50 as left-wing. Semi-structured interviews were conducted with a subset of participants to gather more in-depth information on their experiences.

Results:
The survey results revealed that cognitively impaired individuals who identified as right-wing experienced fewer difficulties in their daily lives compared to those who identified as left-wing. These differences were primarily attributed to the social and economic support networks established by right-wing individuals, which provided a sense of security and stability. In contrast, left-wing individuals reported experiencing more discrimination and stigma due to their cognitive impairment, leading to feelings of isolation and marginalization.

The qualitative interviews highlighted several key themes:

1. Social exclusion: Left-wing individuals experienced social exclusion due to their cognitive impairment, leading to feelings of isolation and stigma.
2. Discrimination: Both right-wing and left-wing individuals reported experiencing discrimination based on their political affiliation, with left-wing individuals facing more significant discrimination.
3. Access to resources: Right-wing individuals had better access to resources such as healthcare and social services, which helped mitigate the impact of their cognitive impairment.
