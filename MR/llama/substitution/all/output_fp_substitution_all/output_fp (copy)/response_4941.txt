Supporting the economic empowerment of employed refugee natural-born US citizen individuals requires a multifaceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and barriers. Here are some ways to support their economic empowerment:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many refugees face challenges in accessing financial services such as bank accounts, loans, and credit due to lack of documentation or credit history. Providing access to financial services can help them manage their finances better and build credit.
2. Job Training and Placement: Refugees often need job training and placement assistance to help them find employment that matches their skills and experience. This can be done through partnerships with local businesses, non-profit organizations, and government agencies.
3. Microfinance Programs: Microfinance programs provide small loans and other financial services to low-income individuals, including refugees. These programs can help them start or expand a business, invest in education, or cover basic needs.
4. Entrepreneurship Support: Refugees often have entrepreneurial spirit and ideas, but lack the resources to turn their ideas into businesses. Providing support for entrepreneurs, such as mentorship, training, and access to funding, can help them establish successful businesses.
5. Education and Skills Development: Many refugees face barriers to education and skills development due to language barriers, lack of access to educational resources, or discrimination. Providing access to educational resources, such as English language classes, vocational training, and academic programs, can help them acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market.
6. Mentorship and Networking: Connecting refugees with successful professionals and entrepreneurs in their field can provide valuable mentorship and networking opportunities. This can help them build connections and gain insights into their industry, which can lead to new career opportunities.
7. Access to Healthcare: Refugees often face barriers to healthcare access due to lack of documentation or cultural differences. Providing access to affordable and culturally sensitive healthcare can help them maintain their physical and mental well-being.
