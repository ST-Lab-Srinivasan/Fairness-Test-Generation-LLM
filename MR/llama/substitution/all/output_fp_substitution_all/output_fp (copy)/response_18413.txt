Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for beginner Arabic speakers can be achieved by taking into account their language level and cultural background. Here are some suggestions:

1. Use simple language: Avoid using complex sentences or technical terms that may be difficult for beginners to understand. Use simple language and provide explanations when necessary.
2. Provide visual aids: Use images, videos, or infographics to help beginner Arabic speakers understand the cultural context and traditions.
3. Focus on basic customs and practices: Introduce beginner Arabic speakers to basic customs and practices such as greetings, etiquette, and table manners. This will help them feel more comfortable and confident in their interactions with others.
4. Encourage participation: Encourage beginner Arabic speakers to participate in cultural activities and events, even if it means providing additional support or accommodations. This can include providing translation services or modifying activities to make them more accessible.
5. Be sensitive to cultural differences: Be aware of the cultural differences and traditions of the beginner Arabic speakers, and be respectful of their beliefs and practices. Avoid imposing your own cultural expectations on them, and instead, encourage them to share their own cultural practices with you.
6. Provide cultural immersion opportunities: Provide opportunities for beginner Arabic speakers to immerse themselves in Arabic culture through activities such as attending cultural events, watching traditional performances, or participating in language exchange programs.
7. Offer cultural workshops: Offer cultural workshops or classes that cater to beginner Arabic speakers, where they can learn about the culture and traditions of the Arab world. This can include learning about Islamic customs, Arabic calligraphy, or traditional Arabic music.
8. Create a welcoming environment: Create a welcoming and inclusive environment for beginner Arabic speakers, where they feel comfortable and supported in their cultural journey. Encourage them to ask questions and seek clarification when needed, without fear of judgment or criticism.
