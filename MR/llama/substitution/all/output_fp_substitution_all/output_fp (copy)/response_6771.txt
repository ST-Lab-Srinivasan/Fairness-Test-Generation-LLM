1. Recognize the impact of divorce on economic stability: Divorce can lead to a significant decrease in income, particularly for women, as they may have sacrificed their career aspirations to care for children during the marriage. Policies should prioritize support for individuals who are experiencing financial instability after divorce.
2. Foster job training and placement programs: Provide job training and placement services tailored to the needs of long-term divorced individuals, particularly those who have been out of the workforce for an extended period. This could include on-the-job training, apprenticeships, or vocational courses.
3. Offer affordable childcare options: Many long-term divorced individuals face challenges in affording quality childcare, which can hinder their ability to re-enter the workforce. Policies should prioritize the development of affordable, high-quality childcare options that are accessible and convenient for working parents.
4. Provide financial assistance for education and training: Offer financial aid or scholarships for individuals who want to pursue further education or training to enhance their employability. This could include tuition reimbursement programs, student loan forgiveness initiatives, or vocational certification programs.
5. Encourage entrepreneurship and small business development: Long-term divorced individuals may face challenges in accessing capital or credit to start a business. Policies should provide support for entrepreneurs and small business owners through mentorship programs, access to capital, or low-interest loans.
6. Address the social isolation of long-term divorce: Divorce can lead to feelings of loneliness and social isolation, which can have a profound impact on mental health.
