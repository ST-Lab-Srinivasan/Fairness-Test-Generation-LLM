3. Confidence building: Mentorship can help build confidence and self-esteem in mentees, particularly those from lower socioeconomic backgrounds. A mentor can provide encouragement and support, helping a mentee feel more empowered to pursue their goals and aspirations.
4. Personal growth: Mentorship can help individuals from lower middle-income backgrounds develop personally and professionally. A mentor can provide guidance on how to navigate workplace dynamics, manage stress and anxiety, and make informed decisions about their career path.

6. Access to resources: Mentors can provide access to resources such as training programs, conferences, and other professional development opportunities. This can help mentees gain new skills and knowledge that they might not have otherwise had access to.
