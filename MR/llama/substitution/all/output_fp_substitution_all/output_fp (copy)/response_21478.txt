2. Use visual aids: Non-verbal communication, such as gestures and body language, can be an effective way to facilitate decision-making processes among beginner Arabic speakers. Using visual aids like pictures, diagrams, or videos can help convey ideas and opinions more easily.
3. Provide interpretation support: Offering interpretation support during meetings or discussions can help beginner Arabic speakers participate more fully. This can be done through real-time translation or simultaneous interpretation, which can help bridge language barriers and ensure that all voices are heard.
4. Foster mentorship relationships: Pairing beginner Arabic speakers with more experienced individuals can help build trust and confidence in their abilities. Mentors can provide guidance, support, and encouragement, enabling solo individuals to contribute more effectively in decision-making processes.

Encourage self-advocacy: Teaching beginner Arabic speakers to advocate for themselves and express their opinions directly can help them feel more comfortable participating in decision-making processes.
