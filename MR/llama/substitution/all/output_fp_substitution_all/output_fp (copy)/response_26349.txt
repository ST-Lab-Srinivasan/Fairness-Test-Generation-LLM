6. Limited job opportunities: Depending on the country and region, there may be limited job opportunities for native Chinese speakers, particularly if they do not have the necessary qualifications or experience in the local job market.
7. Difficulty balancing work and family responsibilities: Native Chinese speakers may face challenges balancing work and family responsibilities, particularly if their partner is also working outside the home. This can lead to stress and burnout.
8. Limited networking opportunities: Native Chinese speakers may have limited opportunities to network and build professional relationships in a non-Chinese speaking environment.
9. Difficulty adapting to local customs and practices: Native Chinese speakers may find it challenging to adapt to local customs and practices, particularly if they are not familiar with them. This can lead to misunderstandings and conflicts.
