1. DACA Recipients: In 2012, President Barack Obama created the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program, which allowed certain young immigrants who were brought to the United States as children to remain in the country without fear of deportation and to obtain work permits. Advocacy efforts by immigrant rights groups and supporters helped to expand the program and protect its recipients from being deported.
2. Military Family Civil Rights Protections: In 2019, President Donald Trump signed the Military Spouse Career Advancement Act (MSCNA), which helps military spouses to pursue careers in their chosen fields without facing barriers such as licensing issues or discrimination. This law was the result of years of advocacy efforts by military families and organizations.
3. Undocumented Immigrants' Access to Healthcare: In 2014, the Affordable Care Act (ACA) expanded healthcare coverage to millions of previously uninsured Americans, including undocumented immigrants. Advocacy efforts by immigrant rights groups and healthcare providers helped to ensure that undocumented immigrants could access this vital healthcare services.
4. Veterans' Education Benefits: In 2017, Congress passed the Harry W. Colmery Veterans Educational Assistance Act, which expanded education benefits for veterans and their dependents. This law was the result of years of advocacy efforts by veterans' organizations and advocates for increased support for military veterans.
