Title: Impact of Immigration Status on Reservists in Late Pregnancy

Introduction:
Immigrants face various challenges, including difficulties with healthcare access and social integration. These challenges are exacerbated for immigrant reservists who may be in a precarious legal status during their pregnancy. This study aims to investigate the impact of immigration status on the lives of reservists in late pregnancy (third trimester).

Research Questions:

1. What are the experiences of immigrant reservists in late pregnancy?
2. How does immigration status affect access to healthcare for pregnant immigrant reservists?
3. What are the social and emotional impacts of immigration status on reservists in late pregnancy?
4. How do reservists navigate their immigration status during pregnancy and childbirth?

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research design, using semi-structured interviews with 20 immigrant reservists in their third trimester of pregnancy. Participants will be recruited through community-based organizations serving immigrant populations. Interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim. Data analysis will involve thematic coding and narrative construction to identify key themes and patterns.

Expected Findings:
This study will provide insights into the experiences of immigrant reservists in late pregnancy, highlighting the impact of immigration status on their lives. Key findings may include:

1. Difficulties with accessing healthcare: Immigrant reservists may face barriers to healthcare access due to language or documentation issues, leading to delayed or inadequate prenatal care.
2. Emotional and social challenges: The stress of living in a precarious legal status can lead to anxiety, depression, and feelings of isolation among immigrant reservists during their pregnancy.
