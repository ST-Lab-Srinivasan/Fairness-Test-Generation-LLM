3. Enhanced patient engagement: Culturally competent healthcare providers are better able to engage patients in their care by understanding their values, beliefs, and practices. This can lead to more effective treatment plans and better health outcomes.
4. Reduced health disparities: By being culturally competent, healthcare providers can identify and address the social determinants of health that contribute to health disparities among African American patients.
5. Improved patient safety: Culturally competent healthcare providers are less likely to make assumptions about patients based on their race or ethnicity, which can lead to adverse events.
