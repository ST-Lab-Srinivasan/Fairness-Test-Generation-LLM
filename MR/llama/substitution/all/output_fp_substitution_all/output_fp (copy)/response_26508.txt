2. Increased trust: When healthcare providers demonstrate cultural competence, they build trust with their patients, which is essential for providing high-quality care. African American patients may be more likely to seek medical care and adhere to treatment plans when they feel comfortable and confident in their healthcare providers.

Better management of health disparities: African Americans experience higher rates of chronic diseases, such as diabetes and heart disease, due to systemic barriers and biases in the healthcare system. 4. Enhanced patient satisfaction: When healthcare providers are culturally competent, they are more likely to provide care that is sensitive to the unique needs and experiences of African American patients. This can lead to higher levels of patient satisfaction and loyalty.
5. Improved adherence to treatment plans: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers develop treatment plans that take into account the cultural beliefs and practices of African American patients, leading to better adherence and health outcomes.
6. Reduced likelihood of medical errors: When healthcare providers are culturally competent, they are less likely to make assumptions about their patients based on stereotypes or biases, reducing the likelihood of medical errors.
