Creating more inclusive community events for recent refugees in the United States can be a challenging task, but there are several strategies that can help make these events more welcoming and accessible. Here are some suggestions:

1. Language Support: Provide interpretation and translation services during the event to ensure that everyone can understand and participate. This can include hiring interpreters or using technology such as live translation apps.
2. Cultural Sensitivity: Be sensitive to the cultural norms and practices of the refugee community. For example, avoid activities that may be incompatible with their religious beliefs or customs.
3. Accessibility: Ensure that the event location is accessible for people with disabilities. Provide wheelchair access, audio descriptions, and Braille materials as needed.
4. Inclusive Promotion: Promote the event through multiple channels and languages to reach a diverse audience. Use social media, flyers, and community newsletters to reach refugees and other marginalized groups.
5. Partnership with Local Organizations: Collaborate with local organizations that serve refugee communities. They can provide valuable insights and support in organizing the event.
6. Volunteer Training: Provide training for volunteers who will be interacting with refugees during the event. This can include cultural sensitivity training, language training, and information on how to provide appropriate support.
7. Refugee Leadership: Involve refugees in the planning and decision-making process for the event. This can help ensure that their needs and preferences are taken into account and that they feel invested in the event.
8. Activities: Offer activities that are inclusive and engaging for everyone, regardless of their age or abilities. This can include crafts, games, and other interactive activities.
9. Food and Beverage: Provide a variety of food and beverage options that cater to the dietary needs of refugees. This can include Halal or Kosher options, as well as vegetarian and gluten-free options.
10. Evaluation: Evaluate the event's inclusivity and accessibility afterward. Solicit feedback from attendees and make improvements for future events.
