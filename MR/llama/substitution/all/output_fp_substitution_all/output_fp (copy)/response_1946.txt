Ensuring the voices of Native English Speaker Ethnically Full African Americans (NESEFAAs) are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting equity and inclusion. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Create safe spaces: Establish regular gatherings or meetings where NESEFAAs can share their experiences, opinions, and ideas without fear of judgment or discrimination. These spaces should be free from dominant cultural influences and promote mutual understanding and respect.
2. Foster inclusive leadership: Encourage NESEFAAs to take on leadership roles within organizations or communities. This could involve setting up mentorship programs, providing training and resources, and actively seeking out diverse perspectives in decision-making processes.
3. Use participatory methods: Incorporate participatory techniques, such as brainstorming, facilitated discussions, and co-creation, into decision-making processes. This can help NESEFAAs contribute their unique viewpoints and experiences to the table.
4. Provide education and training: Offer educational workshops or training sessions on topics like leadership, advocacy, and community organizing. This can help NESEFAAs build skills and confidence in their ability to participate in decision-making processes.
5. Use digital platforms: Utilize digital tools and social media platforms to amplify the voices of NESEFAAs. Create online forums or discussion groups where they can share their thoughts and ideas without being marginalized or ignored.
6. Address power dynamics: Be mindful of the power dynamics at play in decision-making processes, particularly when it comes to issues of race and ethnicity. Take steps to address any biases or disparities that may exist, such as actively seeking out diverse perspectives and actively challenging dominant narratives.
