Supporting the social inclusion of recent temporary visa holders in Canada, particularly those from non-English speaking backgrounds, is crucial for their successful integration into Canadian society. Here are some ways to promote social inclusion:

1. Language Training: Offer language training programs specifically designed for recent immigrants, including English as a Second Language (ESL) classes and cultural orientation programs. This will help them develop the necessary language skills to communicate effectively in their new environment.
2. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement by organizing events and activities that promote intercultural exchange and understanding. This could include cultural festivals, volunteer opportunities, and social gatherings.
3. Access to Healthcare: Ensure access to healthcare services for recent immigrants by providing information on available resources, such as health clinics and hospitals, and offering translation services for those who do not speak English.
4. Education and Training: Provide educational resources and training programs to help recent immigrants acquire the skills and knowledge needed to succeed in their new environment. This could include job training, computer skills training, and academic programs.
5. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair recent immigrants with experienced professionals or community members who can provide guidance on settling into Canadian society.
6. Housing Support: Offer housing support services, such as information on affordable housing options and rental agreements, to help recent immigrants navigate the Canadian housing market.
7. Legal Services: Provide legal services and advice to help recent immigrants understand their rights and responsibilities under Canadian law, including issues related to employment, housing, and family reunification.
8. Economic Empowerment: Support recent immigrants in finding employment and starting their own businesses by providing access to job training, entrepreneurship workshops, and networking opportunities.
9. Children's Programs: Offer programs and services that support the integration of children of recent immigrants into Canadian schools and society, such as tutoring, mentorship, and extracurricular activities.
10. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies and practices that promote social inclusion and address systemic barriers faced by recent immigrants, including those related to employment
