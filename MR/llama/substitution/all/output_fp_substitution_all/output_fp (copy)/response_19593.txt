Introduction:
Marital status can have a significant impact on an individual's life, especially for those who have been through a long and challenging divorce process. Long-time divorced liberal individuals may face unique challenges and opportunities that are shaped by their marital history. This study aims to explore the impact of marital status on the lives of long-time divorced liberal individuals, including their emotional well-being, social connections, financial stability, and personal growth.

Literature Review:
Previous research has shown that divorce can have both positive and negative effects on an individual's life. On one hand, divorce can provide an opportunity for personal growth, increased autonomy, and improved emotional well-being (Komisaruk & Witt, 2007). On the other hand, it can also lead to feelings of loss, grief, and social isolation (Amato, 1994).

For long-time divorced liberal individuals, the impact of marital status may be particularly pronounced. These individuals may have experienced a longer duration of marriage, which can lead to deeper emotional investments and a greater sense of loss upon divorce. Additionally, they may have stronger social connections and community ties that are impacted by their marital status (Hill & Hoath, 2013).

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research design, using in-depth interviews with long-time divorced liberal individuals. Participants will be recruited through community-based organizations and online platforms, and will be selected based on their length of time since divorce and political affiliation. The interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim, allowing for a rich analysis of the participants' experiences and perspectives.
