Lower-income natural-born Indian citizen people face significant challenges in the legal system due to various factors such as lack of access to justice, limited financial resources, and societal biases. Here are some of the common challenges faced by this group:

1. Lack of access to legal aid: Lower-income individuals often struggle to afford legal representation, which can result in a disadvantage in court proceedings. This can lead to missed opportunities for justice and an increased risk of unfavorable outcomes.
2. Limited financial resources: Legal procedures can be expensive, especially when it comes to hiring lawyers or filing appeals. Lower-income individuals may not have the financial means to pursue legal action or defend themselves in court.
3. Societal biases: The legal system can be biased towards those with more resources and social status. Lower-income individuals may face discrimination or prejudice from judges, lawyers, or other legal actors, which can affect their ability to access justice.
4. Limited understanding of legal procedures: Legal terminology and procedures can be complex and difficult to understand for those without a legal background. Lower-income individuals may struggle to navigate the legal system without proper guidance or support.
5. Limited access to information: Lower-income individuals may not have access to information about their legal rights and options. This lack of awareness can lead to missed opportunities for justice and increased vulnerability to exploitation.
6. Power imbalances: The legal system can be hierarchical, with more powerful actors such as lawyers and judges holding significant influence over the outcome of cases. Lower-income individuals may feel disempowered in their dealings with these actors, leading to a lack of agency in seeking justice.
7. Limited access to legal resources: Lower-income individuals may not have access to essential legal resources such as law libraries or online legal databases. This can limit their ability to research and prepare for legal proceedings.
8. Discrimination based on socio-economic status: The legal system can perpetuate discrimination against lower-income individuals based on their socio-economic status. This can result in unequal treatment under the law and a lack of access to justice.
