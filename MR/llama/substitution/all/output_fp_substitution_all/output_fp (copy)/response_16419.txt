Supporting the economic empowerment of mixed African American individuals who are jobless can be achieved through various strategies that address the systemic and structural barriers they face. Here are some suggestions:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to education and skills development programs, including vocational training, apprenticeships, and certification courses. This can help individuals acquire marketable skills and improve their employability.
2. Job Placement Services: Offer job placement services that cater specifically to the needs of mixed African American individuals. This can include resume building, interview preparation, and networking opportunities with potential employers.
3. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship Support: Provide access to microfinance loans and entrepreneurship support to help individuals start their own businesses or invest in existing ones. This can help create economic opportunities for individuals who may not have access to traditional forms of capital.
4. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Pair jobless mixed African American individuals with successful professionals and entrepreneurs who can serve as mentors and role models. This can provide valuable guidance, support, and networking opportunities.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that address systemic barriers to economic empowerment faced by mixed African Americans. This can include advocating for anti-discrimination laws, housing and education reform, and increased access to healthcare and social services.
6. Community Development and Capacity Building: Invest in community development programs that build the capacity of mixed African American communities to drive economic growth and prosperity. This can include initiatives to support small businesses, create affordable housing, and provide cultural and artistic opportunities.
7. Access to Capital: Provide access to capital through crowdfunding platforms, angel investors, and venture capital firms that specifically target mixed African American entrepreneurs. This can help individuals start or grow their businesses and create wealth-generating opportunities.
8. Industry-Specific Training and Resources: Offer industry-specific training and resources to help mixed African Americans navigate specific industries and job markets. For example, providing legal resources for those interested in law or medical resources for those interested in healthcare.
