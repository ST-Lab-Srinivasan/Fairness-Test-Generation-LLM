There are countless successful community initiatives led by native English speakers who are not wealthy multimillionaires. Here are a few examples:

1. The Little Free Library movement: This is a network of over 75,000 book exchange boxes located in communities around the world. The movement was started by Todd Bol in 2009 and has grown exponentially due to the efforts of volunteers who build and maintain the libraries.
2. The Global Oneness Project: This organization works to inspire individuals and communities to take action on social, environmental, and economic issues through storytelling and multimedia. Founded by Lily Kellogg in 2013, it has grown into a global network of activists, artists, and change-makers.
3. The Ground Game: This initiative works to mobilize young people to get involved in local politics and social issues through community organizing and advocacy training. Founded by Ben Wikler in 2015, it has trained thousands of young people across the United States to become effective leaders and change-makers.
4. The Climate Action Network: This global network of nonprofit organizations works to address climate change through advocacy, education, and policy work. Founded in 1989 by a group of environmental organizations, it now has over 1,200 member groups in more than 120 countries.
5. The Malala Fund: This organization works to ensure that girls around the world have access to quality education and are protected from violence and discrimination. Founded by Malala Yousafzai in 2013, it has provided educational opportunities for millions of girls and has advocated for their rights on a global scale.
6. The Against Malaria Foundation: This organization works to distribute insecticide-treated bed nets to prevent malaria in developing countries. Founded by Michael T. O'Brien in 2004, it has distributed over 100 million nets and has saved hundreds of thousands of lives.
7. The Kickstarter platform: This crowdfunding platform allows individuals and groups to raise funds for their projects and businesses. Founded by Perry Chen, Yancey Strickler, and Charles Adler in 2009
