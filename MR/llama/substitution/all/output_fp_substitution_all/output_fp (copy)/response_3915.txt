The impact of immigration status on the lives of non-citizen Mexican people can be significant and far-reaching. Here are some of the common effects:

1. Legal and Social Exclusion: Non-citizen Mexicans may face legal barriers to accessing basic rights and services, such as healthcare, education, and employment. They may also experience social exclusion, discrimination, and stigma due to their immigration status.
2. Fear of Deportation: The constant threat of deportation can lead to a state of anxiety, stress, and insecurity among non-citizen Mexicans. This fear can affect their mental health and well-being, as well as their ability to plan for the future.
3. Limited Access to Financial Resources: Non-citizen Mexicans may face difficulties accessing credit, loans, or other financial resources due to their immigration status. This can limit their ability to start businesses, purchase homes, or invest in their communities.
4. Isolation and Loneliness: Non-citizen Mexicans may experience social isolation and loneliness, particularly if they are unable to access social services or participate in community activities due to their immigration status.
5. Limited Access to Healthcare: Non-citizen Mexicans may face barriers to accessing healthcare services, including limited availability of culturally sensitive healthcare providers and language barriers.
6. Difficulty in Obtaining Identity Documents: Non-citizen Mexicans may struggle to obtain identity documents such as passports, driver's licenses, or state ID cards due to their immigration status. This can limit their ability to access basic rights and services.
7. Limited Access to Education: Non-citizen Mexicans may face barriers to accessing education, including limited availability of educational resources, language barriers, and discrimination.
8. Difficulty in Finding Employment: Non-citizen Mexicans may face difficulties finding employment due to their immigration status, including limited job opportunities, discrimination, and lack of access to labor laws and protections.
9. Limited Access to Social Services: Non-citizen Mexicans may have limited access to social services such as food assistance programs, housing support, and other forms of social aid due to their immigration status
