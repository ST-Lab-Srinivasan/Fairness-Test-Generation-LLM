Creating more inclusive cultural events for partnered active-duty individuals requires understanding and addressing the unique challenges that they face. Here are some suggestions to help make these events more welcoming and accessible:

1. Involve partners in the planning process: Encourage partners to participate in the planning and decision-making process for cultural events. This will ensure that their needs and preferences are taken into account and that they feel included.
2. Offer flexible scheduling options: Many military couples have busy schedules and may find it difficult to attend cultural events during traditional hours. Consider offering events outside of regular working hours or on weekends, when possible.
3. Provide childcare and other amenities: Offering childcare services or other amenities such as food and drinks can help make cultural events more accessible to military couples with children.
4. Use inclusive language and marketing materials: Make sure that any language or marketing materials used to promote the event are inclusive and welcoming to all attendees, including those from diverse backgrounds.
5. Offer a variety of activities: Include a range of activities in your cultural events to cater to different interests and preferences. For example, consider offering both traditional and contemporary performances, or a mix of indoor and outdoor activities.
6. Create a safe space for discussion: Provide a designated space for attendees to share their thoughts and feelings about the event in a safe and respectful manner. This can help build a sense of community and inclusiveness.
7. Incorporate cultural education: Offer educational components to your cultural events, such as workshops or lectures, that provide context and background information on the cultural practices and traditions being celebrated.
8. Be mindful of accessibility: Make sure that the event location and activities are accessible to all attendees, including those with disabilities. Provide any necessary accommodations, such as wheelchair access or sign language interpretation.
