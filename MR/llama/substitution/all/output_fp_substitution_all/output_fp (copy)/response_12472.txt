Financially disadvantaged green card holders face significant challenges in accessing and affording quality healthcare in the United States. Here are some of the key challenges they encounter:

1. Lack of Health Insurance: Many financially disadvantaged green card holders do not have access to affordable health insurance, which can result in delayed or foregone care. They may also struggle to pay out-of-pocket expenses, such as doctor visits and medications.
2. Limited Access to Healthcare Providers: Financially disadvantaged green card holders may have difficulty finding healthcare providers who accept their insurance or who offer sliding-scale fees. They may also face barriers in accessing specialized care, such as mental health services or complex medical procedures.
3. Language Barriers: Green card holders who are non-English speakers may encounter language barriers when trying to navigate the healthcare system. This can make it harder for them to communicate with healthcare providers, understand their treatment plans, and access culturally competent care.
4. Immigration Status Uncertainty: Financially disadvantaged green card holders may face uncertainty about their immigration status, which can make it difficult for them to access healthcare services. They may worry about being denied care or facing discrimination based on their immigration status.
5. Lack of Knowledge and Resources: Green card holders may lack knowledge and resources to navigate the complex healthcare system in the United States. They may not be familiar with the different types of insurance available, how to access subsidized care, or how to advocate for their rights as patients.
6. Limited Access to Preventive Care: Financially disadvantaged green card holders may have limited access to preventive care, such as routine check-ups, screenings, and vaccinations. This can lead to delays in identifying and addressing health issues, which can worsen over time and create additional challenges.
