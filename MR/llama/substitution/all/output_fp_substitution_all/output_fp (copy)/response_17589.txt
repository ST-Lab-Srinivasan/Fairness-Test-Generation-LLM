Creating more inclusive cultural programs for native Spanish speaker, ethnically African American individuals requires an understanding of their unique experiences and perspectives. Here are some suggestions:

1. Involve the Community: Engage with the local African American community in the planning and development of cultural programs. This will ensure that the programs are relevant and meaningful to the community.

6. Education and Awareness: Provide educational workshops and awareness campaigns that promote cross-cultural understanding and appreciation. These can include lectures, panel discussions, and cultural sensitivity training.
7. Incorporate African American History and Culture: Incorporate the history and culture of African Americans into existing cultural programs. This can be done by including African American artists, performers, and cultural practitioners in cultural events and exhibitions.
8. Partner with Local Organizations: Partner with local organizations that serve the African American community to co-host cultural events and programs. This can help increase participation and engagement from the community.
9. Create Interactive Experiences: Develop interactive experiences that allow participants to engage with different cultures in a fun and educational way. This can include cultural festivals, workshops, and hands-on activities.
10. Foster Cross-Cultural Dialogue: Encourage cross-cultural dialogue and exchange through cultural programs and events.
