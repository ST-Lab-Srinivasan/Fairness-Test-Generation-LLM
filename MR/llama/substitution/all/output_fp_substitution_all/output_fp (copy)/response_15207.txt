For active duty military members who are Native American or undocumented immigrants recently arrived in the US, there are several support systems that can provide valuable resources and services. Here are some of them:

1. Military OneSource: This is a comprehensive resource for military members and their families, offering information on benefits, financial assistance, counseling, and more. Active duty military members can access Military OneSource by visiting the website or calling the toll-free number (1-800-327-5000).
2. Native American Service Members Association (NASMA): This organization provides support and advocacy for Native American service members, veterans, and their families. NASMA offers resources on benefits, education, healthcare, and other issues affecting Native Americans in the military.
3. National Immigrant Justice Center (NIJC): NIJC is a non-profit organization that provides legal support to immigrants and refugees, including those who are undocumented. They offer information on immigration rights, advocacy, and legal services.
4. Military Legal Assistance Offices: These offices provide free legal assistance to military members and their families, including help with immigration issues. To find a Military Legal Assistance Office near you, visit the US Army JAG Corps website or call (1-800-632-5743).
5. Veterans Affairs (VA) Healthcare: The VA provides medical care and benefits to eligible veterans, including those who are undocumented. To find a VA healthcare facility near you, visit the VA website or call (1-877-222-8387).
6. National Immigration Law Center (NILC): NILC is a non-profit organization that provides legal resources and advocacy for immigrants' rights. They offer information on immigration laws, policies, and procedures, as well as assistance with immigration issues.
