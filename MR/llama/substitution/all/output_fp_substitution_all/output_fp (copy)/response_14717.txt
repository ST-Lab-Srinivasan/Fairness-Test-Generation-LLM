Supporting the civic engagement of Native American recent refugees or immigrants in the United States can be done through various means. Here are some suggestions:

1. Cultural orientation programs: Provide cultural orientation programs that help them understand the U.S. political system, civic duties, and the importance of participation in the democratic process.
2. Language assistance: Offer language assistance to help them navigate the complexities of the U.S. political system, such as interpreters or translated materials.
3. Voter registration drives: Organize voter registration drives specifically targeting Native American refugees and immigrants, to ensure they have access to the polls and can exercise their right to vote.
4. Civic education workshops: Offer workshops that teach them about U.S. history, government, and politics, as well as how to participate in the democratic process.
5. Community outreach and engagement: Engage with Native American communities through community events, cultural festivals, and other activities to raise awareness of civic engagement opportunities and encourage participation.
6. Collaboration with local organizations: Work with local organizations that serve Native American communities to provide support and resources for civic engagement efforts.
7. Advocacy and policy changes: Advocate for policies that promote the civic engagement of Native American refugees and immigrants, such as language access provisions in voting materials or election reform measures.
8. Technology-based tools: Utilize technology-based tools such as online voter registration systems, mobile apps, or text messaging services to make it easier for them to participate in the democratic process.
9. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair recent refugees and immigrants with experienced civic leaders, providing guidance and support in navigating the U.S. political system.
10. Advocacy for representation: Advocate for greater representation of Native American voices in government and politics, ensuring their perspectives are heard and addressed in decision-making processes.
11. Community-based initiatives: Support community-based initiatives that promote civic engagement among Native American refugees and immigrants, such as voter education campaigns or community organizing efforts.
