3. Connect them with resources: Newly widowed agnostic individuals in college may need help navigating the college system, finding resources for mental health support, and accessing financial aid. Offer workshops or one-on-one counseling sessions specifically designed to address these needs, and connect them with on-campus resources such as academic advisors, financial aid officers, and mental health professionals.

Consider creating a support group specifically for this population, where they can connect with others who share similar experiences and emotions. This could involve offering training for faculty and staff on how to support students from diverse religious backgrounds, creating spaces for interfaith dialogue and discussion, and promoting events and activities that celebrate diversity and inclusivity.
