Ensuring that the voices of Native English Speaker Mixed Hispanic Heritage Individuals (NEMSHIs) are heard in decision-making processes requires a deliberate and inclusive approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Representation Matters: Make sure NEMSHIs are represented in decision-making bodies, such as boards of directors, committees, or task forces. This can be done by actively seeking out and recruiting NEMSHIs to participate.
2. Create Safe Spaces: Provide NEMSHIs with safe spaces where they feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and opinions without fear of marginalization or discrimination. This can be achieved through cultural sensitivity training for decision-makers and creating a culture of inclusivity.
3. Amplify Their Voices: Use various channels to amplify the voices of NEMSHIs, such as social media campaigns, public events, and media outreach. This can help raise their visibility and ensure that their perspectives are heard beyond their immediate communities.
4. Foster Cross-Cultural Understanding: Promote cross-cultural understanding and empathy among decision-makers to create an environment where NEMSHIs feel valued and respected. This can be achieved through cultural competence training, intercultural dialogue, and community events that celebrate diversity.
5. Empowerment Through Education: Provide NEMSHIs with education and training opportunities that equip them with the skills and knowledge to effectively participate in decision-making processes. This can include leadership development programs, mentorship opportunities, and access to educational resources.
6. Create Inclusive Decision-Making Processes: Design decision-making processes that are inclusive and accessible to NEMSHIs. This can involve using language and formats that are familiar to them, providing accommodations for those who may need them, and ensuring that all perspectives are given equal weight.
7. Build Networks and Alliances: Connect NEMSHIs with other marginalized communities and allies who can provide support and amplify their voices. This can help build a stronger collective voice and create a more united front in advocating for their rights and interests.
