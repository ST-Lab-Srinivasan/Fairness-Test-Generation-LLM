Poor women face significant challenges in accessing and utilizing healthcare services due to various socio-economic and cultural factors. Some of the common challenges faced by poor women in the healthcare system include:

1. Limited access to healthcare facilities: Many poor women live in remote or rural areas, far away from healthcare facilities. They may not have access to basic medical care, let alone specialized services like obstetrics and gynecology.
2. Financial barriers: Poor women often face financial barriers when seeking healthcare services. They may not be able to afford the costs of medical care, including out-of-pocket expenses, co-pays, and deductibles.
3. Lack of transportation: Many poor women rely on public transportation or non-motorized modes of transportation, such as walking or cycling, to access healthcare facilities. This can be time-consuming and may lead to missed appointments or delayed care.
4. Cultural barriers: In some cultures, women may face cultural barriers that prevent them from seeking medical care. For example, they may be discouraged from seeking medical attention for certain conditions or may be expected to bear the burden of illness without seeking outside help.
5. Gender-based discrimination: Poor women may face gender-based discrimination in healthcare settings, which can lead to inadequate care or delays in receiving needed services.
6. Limited education and awareness: Many poor women may have limited access to health education and awareness programs, which can lead to a lack of understanding about their health needs and how to access care.
7. Stigma and shame: Poor women may face stigma and shame associated with seeking medical care, particularly for issues related to sexual and reproductive health.
8. Limited availability of specialized services: In many areas, there may be limited availability of specialized services such as maternal and child healthcare, which can make it difficult for poor women to access the care they need.
