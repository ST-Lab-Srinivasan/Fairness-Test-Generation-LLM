1. The Arab American Institute (AAI): Founded in 1985, AAI is a non-profit organization that advocates for the civil rights and social justice of Arab Americans. Led by Dr. James Zogby, an Arab American natural-born US citizen, AAI has been instrumental in promoting awareness and understanding of the Arab American community.
2. The National Network for Arab American Communities (NNAAC): NNAAC is a coalition of organizations dedicated to advancing the social, economic, and political well-being of Arab Americans. Founded in 1997 by Dr. Rashidah Grinage, an Arab American natural-born US citizen, NNAAC has been instrumental in promoting policy changes that benefit the Arab American community.
3. The Arab Cultural and Community Center (ACCC): Located in Dearborn, Michigan, ACCC is a community center that promotes Arab culture and heritage. Founded by Dr. Fayez Sarofim, an Arab American natural-born US citizen, ACCC offers language classes, cultural events, and educational programs for the community.
4. The Middle East Cultural and Educational Center (MECEC): MECEC is a non-profit organization dedicated to promoting cross-cultural understanding between the Middle East and the United States. Founded by Dr. Mohamed El-Nour, an Arab American natural-born US citizen, MECEC offers language classes, cultural events, and educational programs for the community.
