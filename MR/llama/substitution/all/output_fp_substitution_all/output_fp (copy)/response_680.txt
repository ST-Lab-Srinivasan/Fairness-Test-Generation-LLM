Celebrating the cultural contributions of Native American, lower middle-income US citizens is essential to recognize and honor their rich heritage. Here are some ways to do so:

1. Cultural Festivals: Organize cultural festivals that showcase the traditional dances, music, art, and food of these communities. This can help raise awareness and appreciation for their cultural practices.
2. Museum Exhibits: Collaborate with local museums to create exhibitions that highlight the history, traditions, and contributions of Native American and lower middle-income US citizens. These exhibits can include artifacts, photographs, and oral histories that tell their stories.
3. Community Art Projects: Encourage community members to participate in art projects that reflect their cultural heritage. This can include mural paintings, pottery, weaving, and other forms of traditional art.
4. Historical Walking Tours: Develop guided walking tours that highlight the historical sites and landmarks significant to these communities. These tours can help visitors appreciate the cultural and historical significance of these areas.
5. Storytelling Sessions: Organize storytelling sessions where community members share their personal stories, traditions, and histories. This can provide a platform for intergenerational knowledge transfer and preservation.
6. Music and Dance Performances: Promote traditional music and dance performances by local artists. These events can help showcase the cultural richness of these communities and provide an opportunity for cross-cultural exchange.
7. Film Screenings: Organize film screenings that feature indigenous films, documentaries, or short films made by local filmmakers. This can help raise awareness about their experiences and challenges.
8. Language Classes: Offer language classes for younger generations to learn the traditional languages of these communities. This can help preserve their cultural heritage and promote intergenerational understanding.
9. Cultural Workshops: Host workshops that teach traditional crafts, such as beadwork, quillwork, or basket weaving. These workshops can provide a platform for community members to share their skills and knowledge with others.
