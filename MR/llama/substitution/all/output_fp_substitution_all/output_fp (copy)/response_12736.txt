Deep poverty refers to a state of income poverty that is chronic and prolonged, often resulting in severe deprivation and limited access to basic necessities such as food, shelter, and healthcare. Permanent residents who are living in deep poverty may face significant challenges in accessing quality healthcare due to various reasons. Here are some of the common challenges faced by deep poverty permanent resident people in the healthcare system:

1. Limited access to healthcare services: Deep poverty permanent residents may not have the financial resources to afford basic medical care, let alone specialized or complex treatments. They may also face barriers in accessing healthcare facilities due to geographical constraints, lack of transportation options, or long wait times.
2. Poor health outcomes: Living in deep poverty can lead to poor health outcomes due to limited access to nutritious food, inadequate housing conditions, and lack of access to preventive care. This can result in higher rates of chronic diseases such as diabetes, hypertension, and respiratory problems.
3. Limited understanding of health issues: Deep poverty permanent residents may have limited knowledge about their health issues due to a lack of access to health education materials or resources. They may also face cultural barriers in seeking medical care, which can hinder their ability to navigate the healthcare system.
4. Discrimination and stigma: People living in deep poverty may face discrimination and stigma from healthcare providers due to their socio-economic status. This can lead to feelings of shame or embarrassment, which can further hinder their ability to seek medical care.
5. Language barriers: Deep poverty permanent residents who are not fluent in the dominant language of the region may face challenges in communicating with healthcare providers, leading to misunderstandings or misdiagnoses.
6. Lack of transportation options: Many deep poverty permanent residents rely on public transportation or non-motorized modes of transportation, such as walking or cycling. This can make it difficult to access healthcare facilities, especially those located in distant areas.
