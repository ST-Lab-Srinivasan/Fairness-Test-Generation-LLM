1. Access to Education and Job Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs specifically designed for Native Americans. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and certification programs that lead to well-paying jobs in high-demand fields such as technology, healthcare, and renewable energy.
2. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship: Offer microloans and other financial tools to help individuals start or grow their own businesses. This can include mentorship programs, business incubators, and access to capital through partnerships with local banks and investors.
3. Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFI): Support the development of CDFIs that provide affordable loans, financial counseling, and other financial services tailored to the needs of low-income Native American communities.
4. Healthcare Access: Improve access to quality healthcare services, including mental health support, through partnerships with local clinics, hospitals, and telemedicine providers.
5. Housing and Infrastructure: Work with local governments and non-profits to provide affordable housing options, including home repair and rehabilitation programs, and improve access to basic infrastructure such as clean water, sanitation, and reliable transportation.
6. Cultural Preservation and Revitalization: Support the preservation and revitalization of Native American languages, cultural practices, and traditional arts through partnerships with local cultural organizations and educational institutions.
7. Collaborative Governance: Foster collaboration between local governments, non-profits, and community members to address the root causes of poverty and improve decision-making processes that affect the community.
