Creating more inclusive public spaces for moderate Native Arabic speakers requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach that takes into account the unique needs and preferences of this population. Here are some suggestions on how to create more inclusive public spaces for moderate Native Arabic speakers:

1. Provide Multilingual Signage: Moderate Native Arabic speakers may feel excluded or uncomfortable when encountering signage that is only in the dominant language of the region. Providing multilingual signage can help make public spaces more inclusive and accessible to this group.
2. Offer Cultural Sensitivity Training: Provide cultural sensitivity training for public space staff, including police officers, librarians, and park rangers. This training can help staff understand the needs and preferences of moderate Native Arabic speakers and provide more effective customer service.
3. Add Quranic Verses or Arabic Language Materials: Including Quranic verses or Arabic language materials in public spaces can help create a sense of inclusivity for moderate Native Arabic speakers. This can be done through display cases, interactive exhibits, or even temporary installations.
4. Design Public Spaces with Moderate Native Arabic Speakers in Mind: When designing public spaces, consider the needs and preferences of moderate Native Arabic speakers. For example, provide seating areas where they can comfortably rest during their visit.
5. Host Cultural Events: Host cultural events that celebrate the diversity of the region and its different language groups. This can help create a sense of inclusivity and belonging among moderate Native Arabic speakers.
6. Provide Interpretation Services: Provide interpretation services, such as live translation or audio descriptions, for public events or exhibitions that may be of interest to moderate Native Arabic speakers.
