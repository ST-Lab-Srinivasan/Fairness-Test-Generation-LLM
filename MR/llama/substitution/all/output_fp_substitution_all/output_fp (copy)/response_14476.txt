Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for permanent resident non-independent individuals requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to make cultural celebrations more inclusive:

1. Involve Them in the Planning Process: Invite non-independent residents to participate in the planning process of cultural celebrations. This can be done by including them in brainstorming sessions, asking for their input on what they would like to see happen during the celebration, and involving them in decision-making processes.
2. Provide Accessibility Options: Make sure that the cultural celebration is accessible to all residents, regardless of their abilities. This can be done by providing wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, and large print materials for those with visual impairments.
3. Offer Multi-Sensory Experiences: Incorporate different sensory experiences into the cultural celebration, such as music, dance, art, and food. This can help to engage residents who may have different learning styles or abilities.
4. Create Interactive Elements: Incorporate interactive elements into the cultural celebration, such as craft stations, games, and puzzles. This can help to keep residents engaged and entertained throughout the event.
5. Provide Support for Caregivers: Recognize that caregivers may need support during cultural celebrations. Offer resources such as childcare services or respite areas where caregivers can take a break.
6. Incorporate Cultural Diversity: Celebrate the diverse cultures of the residents by incorporating different traditions, customs, and foods into the celebration. This can help to create a sense of community and inclusivity.
7. Offer Accommodations for Dietary Needs: Provide options for residents with dietary restrictions or preferences. This can include vegetarian or vegan options, gluten-free meals, and cultural foods that meet specific dietary needs.
8. Encourage Resident Leadership: Encourage non-independent residents to take on leadership roles within the cultural celebration. This can help to build confidence and self-esteem, while also providing opportunities for them to contribute to the community.
