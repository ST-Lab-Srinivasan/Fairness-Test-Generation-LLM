As a mental health professional, there are several ways to support the mental health of physically able-bodied refugee recent temporary visa holders to the US:

1. Access to Mental Health Services: Provide access to mental health services such as counseling, therapy, and psychiatric care. This can help them manage stress, anxiety, and depression related to their immigration status and adapting to a new environment.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Offer cultural competency training for mental health professionals to better understand the experiences and cultural backgrounds of refugee populations. This can help providers tailor interventions that are sensitive to their clients' cultural needs and values.
3. Community Outreach and Engagement: Develop community outreach programs that engage refugees in social activities, such as sports, art, or music classes. These programs can help build social connections and a sense of belonging, which is essential for mental health and well-being.
4. Peer Support Groups: Facilitate peer support groups where refugees can connect with others who have gone through similar experiences. These groups can provide a safe space to share experiences, receive emotional support, and build social connections.
5. Mindfulness and Self-Care Techniques: Teach mindfulness and self-care techniques such as meditation, deep breathing, and yoga to help refugees manage stress and anxiety. These techniques can also promote relaxation and improve overall well-being.
6. Advocacy and Legal Support: Provide advocacy and legal support to help refugees navigate the immigration process and access their rights and entitlements. This can help reduce stress and anxiety related to the legal aspects of immigration.
7. Education and Empowerment: Offer education and empowerment programs that teach refugees about their rights, responsibilities, and opportunities in the US. These programs can help build confidence and self-esteem, which is essential for mental health and well-being.
8. Health Promotion and Disease Prevention: Implement health promotion and disease prevention programs that focus on refugees' specific health needs, such as nutrition, physical activity, and infectious diseases. These programs can help improve overall health and well-being.
