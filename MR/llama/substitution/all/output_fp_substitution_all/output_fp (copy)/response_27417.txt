1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for faculty and staff to better understand the cultural backgrounds of Native American students and how to support them in their academic journey.
2. Indigenous-Led Programs: Develop programs and courses that are led by Indigenous peoples, for Indigenous peoples. This can help create a sense of belonging and connection to one's cultural heritage.
3. Financial Support: Offer financial support in the form of scholarships, grants, and other forms of aid to help Native American students cover the costs of tuition and living expenses.
4. Campus Resources: Provide access to campus resources such as academic advising, mental health services, and tutoring programs specifically tailored to the needs of Native American students.
5. Community Engagement: Foster a sense of community among Native American students by providing opportunities for socialization, cultural events, and volunteer work that are meaningful and relevant to their experiences.
6. Partnerships with Tribal Colleges: Establish partnerships with tribal colleges and universities to provide academic credit transfer agreements, dual-enrollment programs, and other collaborative initiatives that support the educational goals of Native American students.
7. Curriculum Development: Develop curricula that incorporate Indigenous knowledge and perspectives, and provide opportunities for Native American students to share their experiences and cultural practices with non-Native students.
8. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair Native American students with successful Indigenous professionals or community leaders who can provide guidance, support, and role models.
9. Language and Cultural Preservation: Offer language classes and cultural preservation initiatives that help Native American students preserve their cultural heritage and language.
