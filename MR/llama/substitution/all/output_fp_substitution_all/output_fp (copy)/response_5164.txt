The Impact of Disability Status on the Lives of People with No Known Genetic Conditions in Nigeria:

Introduction:
Disability can have a significant impact on the lives of individuals, regardless of the cause. In Nigeria, little is known about the experiences and challenges faced by people with no known genetic conditions, who are often overlooked in discussions around disability. This study aims to explore the impact of disability status on the lives of people with no known genetic conditions in Nigeria.

Methodology:
This study used a qualitative research approach, using in-depth interviews and focus groups to collect data from 20 participants with no known genetic conditions. The participants were recruited through purposive sampling, and included individuals who self-identified as having disabilities. The interviews and focus groups were conducted in English or the local language, depending on the participant's preference.

Results:
The results of this study indicate that people with no known genetic conditions in Nigeria face significant challenges and discrimination in various aspects of their lives. These include:

1. Social isolation: Many participants reported feeling isolated and excluded from social events and activities, due to the stigma associated with disability in Nigerian society.
2. Limited access to education and employment: Participants faced barriers to accessing education and employment opportunities, due to a lack of support and resources for people with disabilities in Nigeria.
3. Financial difficulties: Many participants reported struggling to make ends meet, due to limited job opportunities and inadequate financial support from family and government.
4. Lack of representation and advocacy: Participants felt that their voices were not being heard, and that there was a lack of representation and advocacy for people with disabilities in Nigeria.
5. Stigma and discrimination: Participants experienced stigma and discrimination from family members, friends, and community leaders, which further exacerbated their challenges.

Conclusion:
This study highlights the significant impact of disability status on the lives of people with no known genetic conditions in Nigeria.
