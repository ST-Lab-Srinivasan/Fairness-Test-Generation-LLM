Creating more inclusive community events for recently pregnant or parenting women can be achieved by taking into consideration their specific needs and preferences. Here are some suggestions:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the event location is easily accessible for women with mobility issues, including those who may be experiencing discomfort during pregnancy or recovering from childbirth. Provide clear signage, wheelchair accessibility, and gender-neutral bathrooms.
2. Flexible Scheduling: Offer event times that cater to the varying schedules of pregnant women and parents, including those who may have limited energy or mobility during certain stages of pregnancy.
3. Childcare: Provide on-site childcare services or recommend local options for parents who need to attend the event. This can include age-appropriate activities for infants, toddlers, and school-age children.
4. Comfortable Spaces: Create comfortable spaces for breastfeeding or pumping, as well as areas for mothers to rest and relax during the event.
5. Inclusive Marketing: Use inclusive language in marketing materials and promote the event through various channels to reach a diverse audience, including social media groups focused on pregnancy, parenting, and family-friendly activities.
6. Sensory-Friendly Environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by minimizing loud noises, bright lights, or strong smells that may be overwhelming for some individuals. Offer quiet areas or fidget toys for those who may need them.
7. Accommodations for Different Abilities: Provide accommodations for women with different abilities, such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, or braille materials.
8. Gender-Neutral Language: Use gender-neutral language and imagery in event promotion and programming to create a welcoming environment for all genders.
9. Inclusive Programming: Offer inclusive programming that caters to the diverse needs and interests of pregnant women and parents, such as prenatal yoga, postpartum support groups, or parenting workshops.
