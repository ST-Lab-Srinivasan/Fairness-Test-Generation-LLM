Ensuring that the educational needs of recently single not disabled students are met can be achieved through a variety of strategies. Here are some suggestions:

1. Assess their needs: Conduct an assessment of the student's educational needs, including their academic strengths, weaknesses, and interests. This will help identify any areas where the student may need additional support or accommodations.
2. Provide individualized support: Offer individualized support to the student, such as extra academic support or counseling services, to help them adjust to their new situation.
3. Foster a positive school environment: Create a supportive and inclusive school environment that promotes a sense of belonging among all students, including those who have recently experienced a change in family status.
4. Offer extracurricular activities: Encourage the student to participate in extracurricular activities or clubs that align with their interests, which can help them meet new friends and develop new skills.
5. Provide access to technology: Ensure that the student has access to technology, such as a laptop or tablet, to help them keep up with their schoolwork and stay connected with peers and teachers.
6. Encourage open communication: Encourage open communication between the student, their teachers, and other school staff to ensure that everyone is aware of the student's needs and can provide appropriate support.
7. Offer flexible scheduling: Consider offering flexible scheduling options, such as part-time enrollment or online courses, to help the student manage their academic workload while also dealing with personal issues related to their family change.
8. Connect them with mentors: Pair the student with a mentor who can provide guidance and support, both academically and personally.
9. Provide additional resources: Offer additional resources, such as tutoring or study skills classes, to help the student improve their academic performance if needed.
10. Monitor progress: Regularly monitor the student's progress and adjust support strategies as needed to ensure that their educational needs are being met.

By implementing these strategies, schools can help ensure that recently single not disabled students receive the support they need to succeed academically and personally during this challenging time.
