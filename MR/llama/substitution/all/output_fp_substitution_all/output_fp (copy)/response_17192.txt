Supporting the economic empowerment of ethnically full African-American non-upper-class individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural inequalities that hinder their economic advancement. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education and skills training programs that address the needs of the target population, such as vocational training, job readiness programs, and entrepreneurship development.
2. Job Creation and Employment Opportunities: Support small businesses and start-ups owned by African Americans, particularly those in underserved communities. This can be achieved through access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities.
3. Wealth Building Strategies: Encourage saving and investing through financial literacy programs, matched savings initiatives, and homeownership programs. This will help build intergenerational wealth and reduce the racial wealth gap.
4. Advocacy and Policy Reforms: Advocate for policies that address systemic inequalities in education, housing, healthcare, and criminal justice. These reforms can include addressing discrimination in hiring practices, expanding affordable housing options, improving access to quality healthcare, and reducing the number of people incarcerated on minor offenses.
5. Community Development Initiatives: Support community-based initiatives that promote economic development, such as cooperative businesses, community land trusts, and local investment programs. These initiatives can help create jobs, increase access to affordable housing, and build wealth within the community.

Mentorship and Networking Opportunities: Connect ethnically full African-American non-upper-class individuals with successful professionals and entrepreneurs who can provide mentorship, guidance, and networking opportunities. This can help them gain access to valuable resources, such as business connections, career advice, and investment opportunities.
7. Access to Capital: Provide access to capital for small businesses and start-ups owned by African Americans, including loans, grants, and other forms of financial assistance. This will help them grow their businesses and create jobs within the community.
