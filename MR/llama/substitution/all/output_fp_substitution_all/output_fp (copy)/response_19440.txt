Introduction:

India has a significant number of people living with genetic conditions, and many of them are undocumented. These individuals face numerous challenges in their daily lives, including social stigma, limited access to healthcare, and economic hardship. This study aims to explore the impacts of disability status on the lives of carriers of genetic conditions who have recently migrated to India.

Methods:

This study will employ a qualitative research approach using in-depth interviews with 15 carriers of genetic conditions who have recently migrated to India. The participants will be recruited through snowball sampling, and data will be collected from their homes or community settings. The interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim.

Results:

The findings of this study indicate that disability status has a significant impact on the lives of carriers of genetic conditions who have recently migrated to India. Some of the key themes that emerged from the data include:

1. Social stigma and discrimination: Many participants reported experiencing social stigma and discrimination due to their disability status. They felt isolated and excluded from society, which affected their mental health and overall well-being.
2. Limited access to healthcare: Participants faced challenges in accessing healthcare services due to a lack of awareness about their condition, limited availability of specialized care, and financial constraints.
3. Economic hardship: Many carriers of genetic conditions struggled with poverty and economic instability due to their inability to work or support themselves financially.
4. Lack of social support: Participants reported feeling unsupported by their families and communities, which further exacerbated their sense of isolation and marginalization.
5. Stigma and discrimination in education: Children with genetic conditions faced discrimination and stigma in schools, leading to lower academic achievement and social isolation.
6. Limited access to assistive devices: Participants reported difficulties in accessing assistive devices such as wheelchairs, crutches, and hearing aids due to limited availability and affordability.
