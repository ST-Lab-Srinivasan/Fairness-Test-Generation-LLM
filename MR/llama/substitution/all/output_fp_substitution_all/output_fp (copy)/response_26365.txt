Ensuring that the educational needs of newly single, non-disabled students are met can be achieved through various strategies and practices. Here are some key considerations:

1. Identify and assess their needs: Conduct a thorough assessment of the student's academic, social, and emotional needs. This can involve interviews with the student, parents, or guardians, as well as academic evaluations and psychological assessments.
2. Develop an individualized education plan (IEP): Based on the assessment findings, develop a personalized IEP that outlines specific goals and objectives for the student. The IEP should be reviewed and updated regularly to ensure it remains relevant and effective.
3. Provide academic support: Offer additional academic support and resources to help the student succeed in their studies. This can include one-on-one tutoring, study skills training, and access to specialized learning programs.
4. Foster a positive school culture: Create a welcoming and inclusive school environment that promotes socialization and collaboration among students. Encourage the student to participate in extracurricular activities and clubs that align with their interests.
5. Address emotional and psychological needs: Provide access to counseling services or mental health resources to help the student manage any emotional or psychological challenges related to the divorce. This can include individual therapy sessions, group therapy, or family therapy.
6. Involve parents and guardians: Keep parents and guardians informed of their child's progress and involve them in decision-making processes regarding their education. Encourage open communication and collaboration between home and school to ensure the student's needs are being met.
7. Leverage technology: Utilize digital tools and resources to support the student's learning, such as online learning platforms, educational apps, or virtual classrooms. This can help bridge any gaps in educational opportunities and provide flexibility in their learning schedule.
8. Provide additional resources: Offer access to external resources such as books, educational software, or online courses that align with the student's interests and needs. This can help supplement their learning experience and address any areas of weakness.
