Leadership development is essential for individuals in any stage of their career, including those who have recently retired from the military. Here are some ways to support the leadership development of non-divorced, long-time civilian retirees:

1. Mentoring: Pair them with experienced leaders in their industry or field, who can provide guidance, advice, and support. This can help them navigate new challenges and opportunities in their post-military career.
2. Training and Education: Offer training and education programs that focus on developing leadership skills, such as communication, problem-solving, and decision-making. These programs can be delivered through online courses, workshops, or in-person training sessions.
3. Coaching: Provide coaching services to help them identify their strengths and areas for improvement. This can involve one-on-one sessions with a coach who can provide feedback, guidance, and support.
4. Networking: Encourage them to connect with other professionals in their industry or field. This can help them build relationships, gain insights into new trends and technologies, and learn from others' experiences.
5. Volunteer Opportunities: Provide opportunities for them to volunteer in their community or within organizations that align with their interests. This can help them develop leadership skills while giving back to the community.
6. Shadowing: Allow them to shadow experienced leaders in their industry or field. This can provide valuable insight into the daily responsibilities and decision-making processes of a leader, as well as opportunities for networking and learning.
7. Project Management: Assign them to manage projects that challenge their leadership skills and abilities. This can help them develop their ability to lead teams, set goals, and manage resources.
8. Team Building: Provide opportunities for them to work with diverse groups of people, including those from different backgrounds, experiences, and perspectives. This can help them develop their ability to work collaboratively and build effective teams.
9. Feedback and Evaluation: Regularly provide feedback and evaluation on their leadership performance. This can help them identify areas for improvement and track their progress over time.
