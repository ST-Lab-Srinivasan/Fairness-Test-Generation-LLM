Meeting the educational needs of physically disabled beginner Arabic speakers requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique challenges and requirements. Here are some strategies to ensure that their educational needs are met:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the learning environment is accessible and conducive to individuals with physical disabilities. This may involve providing wheelchair access, audio descriptions, braille materials, or sign language interpretation.
2. Accommodations: Provide individualized accommodations based on the student's specific needs, such as extra time to complete assignments, use of assistive technology, or one-on-one support.
3. Customized learning plans: Develop customized learning plans that take into account the individual's learning style and abilities. This may involve breaking down complex concepts into simpler terms, providing visual aids, or using multimedia resources.
4. Supportive instructors: Train instructors to be sensitive to the needs of physically disabled students and provide them with the necessary support and resources to help them succeed.
5. Inclusive classrooms: Create inclusive classrooms where all students feel valued and supported. This may involve incorporating diverse perspectives, using inclusive language, or providing opportunities for students to share their experiences and insights.
6. Technology integration: Utilize technology to support learning, such as text-to-speech software, speech-to-text software, or assistive listening devices.
7. Collaboration with disability services: Collaborate with disability services on campus to provide additional support and resources for physically disabled students. This may involve referring students to counseling services, providing adaptive equipment, or facilitating peer support groups.
8. Flexibility in scheduling: Offer flexible scheduling options to accommodate the unique needs of physically disabled students, such as adjustable class times or flexible exam schedules.
9. Professional development: Provide ongoing professional development opportunities for instructors to learn about best practices in teaching physically disabled students and how to create an inclusive learning environment.
10. Student advocacy: Advocate for physically disabled students by providing a voice for their needs and concerns, and working to address systemic barriers to access and inclusion.
