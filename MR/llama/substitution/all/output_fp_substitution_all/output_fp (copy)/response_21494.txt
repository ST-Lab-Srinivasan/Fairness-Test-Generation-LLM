1. Cultural Competency Training for Educators: Provide training for educators on cultural competency and how to work with Native American students who may have experienced trauma, loss, and dislocation. This training should include understanding the cultural values, beliefs, and practices of Native American communities.
2. Access to Native American Curriculum and Resources: Ensure that schools have access to Native American curriculum and resources, such as textbooks, videos, and other educational materials that reflect the cultural and historical experiences of Native American communities.
3. Recruitment and Retention of Native American Educators: Attract and retain Native American educators who can provide role models and mentors for Native American students. This can be done by offering professional development opportunities, providing competitive salaries and benefits, and creating a supportive work environment.
4. Native Language Instruction: Provide language instruction in the Native American languages spoken by the refugee population, such as Ojibwe, Navajo, or Cherokee. This can help students maintain their cultural identity and connect with their heritage.
5. Support Services for Refugee Students: Provide support services, such as tutoring, counseling, and mentoring, to help refugee students adjust to the US educational system and address any academic or social challenges they may face.
6. Community Partnerships: Develop partnerships with Native American communities and organizations to provide additional resources and support for Native American students. This can include cultural events, field trips, and guest speakers who can share their experiences and perspectives with students.

This can include advocating for more funding for Native American education, changes in curriculum and assessment policies, and greater support for language and cultural programs.
