Advocating for the rights of individuals with mental health conditions, particularly those who are natural-born Indian citizens, can be a challenging but important endeavor. Here are some examples of successful advocacy efforts:

1. The Mental Health Act, 1987: This act provides for the detention of mentally ill persons in certain circumstances, and lays down guidelines for their treatment and care. It also established a system of mental health review tribunals to hear appeals against detention orders.
2. The National Mental Health Policy, 2017: This policy aims to promote mental health and well-being, reduce stigma associated with mental illness, and improve access to mental health services. It emphasizes the need for a comprehensive approach to mental health care that includes prevention, early intervention, and integrated treatment.
3. The Right to Mental Health Campaign: This campaign was launched in 2017 by the National Alliance of Mental Illness (NAMI) India to raise awareness about mental health issues and advocate for better access to care. The campaign has resulted in increased media coverage of mental health issues, as well as changes in government policies and programs.
4. The Mental Health Action Plan for India: This plan was launched in 2016 by the World Health Organization (WHO) to address the mental health needs of the Indian population. It includes strategies for improving access to mental health services, reducing stigma associated with mental illness, and promoting mental health in the workplace.
5. The Mental Health Advocacy Network: This network was established in 2018 by a group of civil society organizations to advocate for better mental health policies and programs in India. The network has been successful in mobilizing public support for mental health issues and influencing government policy decisions.
6. The Indian Psychiatric Society's Advocacy Efforts: The Indian Psychiatric Society (IPS) has been actively advocating for better mental health policies and programs in India. The society has been successful in lobbying for increased funding for mental health services, as well as promoting awareness about mental illnesses among the general public.
