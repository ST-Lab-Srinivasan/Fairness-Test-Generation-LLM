The economic empowerment of poor Native natural-born US citizen individuals is a critical issue that requires a multifaceted approach. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs that equip individuals with the necessary skills to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and college scholarships.
2. Job Creation and Entrepreneurship: Support the creation of jobs and entrepreneurial opportunities in Native American communities. This can be done through initiatives such as business incubators, loan programs, and mentorship opportunities.
3. Economic Development Initiatives: Implement economic development initiatives that promote sustainable economic growth and job creation in Native American communities. Examples include infrastructure projects, renewable energy investments, and support for local businesses.
4. Land Management Reform: Address the historical injustices of land management by reforming policies that have dispossessed Native Americans of their ancestral lands. This can involve returning land to tribal ownership, promoting sustainable agriculture practices, and supporting community-based economic development initiatives.

Cultural Preservation and Revitalization: Support the preservation and revitalization of Native American cultures and traditions, which are critical to the economic empowerment of these communities. This can involve initiatives such as language revitalization programs, cultural festivals, and the establishment of cultural centers.
6. Access to Capital: Increase access to capital for Native American-owned businesses by providing grants, loans, and other financial resources. This can help entrepreneurs start and grow their businesses, creating jobs and economic opportunities in their communities.
7. Healthcare Access: Improve access to healthcare services in Native American communities, which is critical for the overall well-being of these communities. This can involve initiatives such as health clinics, telemedicine services, and mental health support.
8. Community Development: Invest in community development initiatives that promote social cohesion, economic growth, and cultural revitalization in Native American communities. Examples include community gardens, youth programs, and intergenerational mentorship opportunities.
