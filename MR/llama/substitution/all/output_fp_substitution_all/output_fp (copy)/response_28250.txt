Supporting the economic empowerment of Native American recent refugees to the US, particularly those living just above the poverty line, requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the unique challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that cater to the specific needs of Native American refugees. This can include language classes, cultural sensitivity training, and vocational training in high-demand fields such as healthcare, technology, and renewable energy.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Offer resources and support for Native American refugees to start their own businesses or social enterprises. This can include access to microloans, mentorship opportunities, and networking events with potential investors and partners.
3. Job Placement Services: Collaborate with local employers to provide job placement services specifically tailored for Native American refugees. This can include resume building workshops, interview preparation, and connecting refugees with job openings in their field of expertise.
4. Financial Literacy and Capability-building: Offer financial literacy training and capability-building programs to help Native American refugees better manage their finances, create budgets, and save for the future.
5. Community-based Initiatives: Foster community-based initiatives that bring together Native American refugees with local business owners, entrepreneurs, and mentors. This can include networking events, peer-to-peer mentorship programs, and collaborative project development.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies and practices that address the unique needs of Native American refugees and promote their economic empowerment. This can include working with local government agencies, non-profits, and community organizations to create more inclusive and supportive environments.
7. Cultural Preservation and Revitalization: Support initiatives that preserve and revitalize Native American cultures, languages, and traditions. This can include cultural festivals, language classes, and community events that promote cross-cultural understanding and exchange.
8. Access to Healthcare: Ensure access to quality healthcare services for Native American refugees, including mental health support and primary care services.
