Ensuring that the voices of beginner Chinese speakers, particularly those from ethnic minority groups, are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for promoting inclusivity and equity. Here are some strategies to help amplify their voices:

1. Create a safe space: Establish an environment where individuals feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and opinions without fear of judgment or marginalization. This can be done by setting ground rules, providing support, and actively listening to their concerns.
2. Foster inclusive language: Use language that is inclusive and respectful of all identities, including those from ethnic minority groups. Avoid using jargon or technical terms that may be unfamiliar to some participants. Instead, use clear and simple language that everyone can understand.
3. Provide resources and support: Offer resources and support to help beginners improve their Chinese language skills. This could include providing access to language classes, language exchange programs, or other opportunities for practice and improvement.
4. Encourage participation: Encourage all participants, including those from ethnic minority groups, to actively participate in decision-making processes. This can be done by assigning roles or tasks that allow them to contribute their unique perspectives and experiences.
5. Foster a culture of mutual respect: Encourage a culture of mutual respect among all participants, regardless of their ethnic background. Promote active listening, empathy, and open-mindedness to help build trust and understanding.
6. Use visual aids and multimedia: Utilize visual aids and multimedia tools to help beginner Chinese speakers communicate their ideas and opinions more effectively. This could include using images, videos, or other visual materials to illustrate their points.
7. Provide feedback and support: Offer constructive feedback and support to help beginner Chinese speakers improve their communication skills and confidence. Encourage them to take risks and try new things in a safe and supportive environment.
8. Encourage peer-to-peer learning: Pair beginner Chinese speakers with more advanced learners or native speakers who can provide guidance and support. This can help beginners build their skills and confidence more quickly, while also promoting intercultural understanding and exchange.
