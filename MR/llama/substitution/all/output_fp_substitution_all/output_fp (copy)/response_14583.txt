Addressing the specific needs of Native American natural-born US citizens in education requires a comprehensive approach that considers the unique cultural, social, and historical contexts of these communities. Here are some strategies that can help address the educational needs of Native American students:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Incorporate traditional Native American teachings, stories, and cultural practices into the curriculum to make it more relevant and meaningful to students. This can involve incorporating Indigenous knowledge systems and perspectives into the learning process.
2. Community-based education: Engage with local Native American communities to develop educational programs that are tailored to their needs and interests. This can involve partnering with tribal governments, community organizations, and elders to design curriculum and programs that reflect the cultural values and beliefs of these communities.
3. Early childhood education: Provide early childhood education programs that are culturally responsive and tailored to the needs of Native American children. This can involve developing age-appropriate curricula that incorporate traditional games, stories, and activities that promote language and cultural preservation.
4. Language revitalization: Support language revitalization efforts by providing language classes, cultural camps, and other programs that help preserve and promote Native American languages. This can involve collaborating with local language speakers and language experts to develop culturally relevant language learning materials.
5. Mentorship and role models: Provide mentorship and role model opportunities for Native American students to connect with successful Indigenous professionals and community leaders. This can help inspire and motivate students to pursue their educational and career goals.
6. Cultural immersion programs: Offer cultural immersion programs that provide students with opportunities to learn about Native American cultures, traditions, and ways of life. These programs can involve participating in traditional ceremonies, learning traditional skills such as beading or basket weaving, and engaging in other cultural activities.
7. Collaboration with tribal communities: Develop partnerships with local tribal communities to provide educational programs that are tailored to their needs and priorities. This can involve working closely with tribal leaders, educators, and community members to develop culturally responsive curricula and programs.
