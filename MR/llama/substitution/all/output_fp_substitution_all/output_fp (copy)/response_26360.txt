Discrimination against recent and long-term temporary visa holders in India can have significant negative impacts on their mental health. Some common mental health effects of discrimination include:

1. Anxiety and stress: The constant fear of being deported, the uncertainty of one's status, and the lack of control over one's life can lead to chronic anxiety and stress, which can have serious consequences for mental health.
2. Depression: The emotional toll of discrimination can lead to depression, including feelings of hopelessness, helplessness, and isolation.
3. Low self-esteem: Discrimination can erode a person's sense of self-worth and identity, leading to low self-esteem and a loss of confidence.
4. Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD): Experiencing or witnessing discriminatory incidents can lead to PTSD, including flashbacks, nightmares, and avoidance behaviors.
5. Social isolation: Discrimination can lead to social isolation, as individuals may avoid social interactions to avoid experiencing further discrimination.
6. Stigma and shame: Discrimination can lead to feelings of shame and stigma, which can further erode a person's mental health and well-being.
7. Difficulty concentrating: The constant stress and anxiety caused by discrimination can make it difficult for individuals to focus and concentrate, leading to problems with memory, attention, and cognitive function.
8. Sleep disturbances: Discrimination can lead to sleep disturbances, including insomnia and nightmares, which can further affect mental health.
9. Substance abuse: In an effort to cope with the stress and anxiety caused by discrimination, some individuals may turn to substance abuse, which can have serious consequences for their mental health.
10. Suicidal ideation: The emotional toll of discrimination can lead to suicidal ideation and attempts, particularly among marginalized groups who face compounding forms of discrimination.
