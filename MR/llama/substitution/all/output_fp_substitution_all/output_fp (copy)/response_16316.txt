There are several ways to support the leadership development of active-duty natural-born US citizen people:

1. Mentorship programs: Pair them with experienced leaders who can provide guidance, support, and feedback on their leadership skills.
2. Leadership training courses: Offer training courses that focus on developing leadership skills such as communication, decision-making, problem-solving, and strategic thinking.
3. Leadership role models: Provide opportunities for them to observe and learn from senior leaders who embody the values and ethics of the military.
4. Professional development opportunities: Offer training and education in areas such as strategy, tactics, and operations, as well as personal and professional development workshops.
5. Leadership assessments: Use validated leadership assessments to identify their strengths and areas for improvement, and provide feedback on how they can continue to develop their leadership skills.
6. Leadership coaching: Provide one-on-one coaching sessions with experienced leaders who can help them refine their leadership style and abilities.
7. Practical experiences: Provide opportunities for them to lead teams and projects, and give them feedback on their performance.
8. Feedback and evaluation: Regularly provide feedback and evaluate their performance as a leader, both formally and informally, to help them understand their strengths and areas for improvement.
9. Leadership networking: Encourage them to network with other leaders in the military and civilian sectors to gain exposure to different leadership styles and best practices.
10. Personal and professional development: Offer opportunities for them to develop personally and professionally, such as through training in areas like time management, communication, and conflict resolution.
11. Leadership simulations: Use simulated scenarios to help them practice and apply their leadership skills in a controlled environment.
12. Team building exercises: Provide opportunities for them to work with other leaders and teams to build trust and collaboration skills.
13. Leadership circles: Facilitate regular meetings of small groups of leaders to share experiences, provide support, and offer feedback on each other's leadership development.
