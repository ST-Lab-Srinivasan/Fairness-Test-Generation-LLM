During the third trimester of pregnancy, it is essential to prioritize the mental health and well-being of expectant mothers. Here are some ways to support the mental health of non-late pregnant individuals who have not recently gotten married:

1. Social Support: Provide a listening ear, emotional support, and encouragement. Offer to accompany them to prenatal appointments or classes, and help with light household chores to reduce stress.
2. Mindfulness Practices: Introduce mindfulness techniques such as deep breathing exercises, meditation, or yoga to help manage stress and anxiety during pregnancy.
3. Self-Care: Encourage them to engage in activities that promote relaxation and stress reduction, such as taking a warm bath, reading a book, or practicing gentle stretching exercises.
4. Educational Resources: Offer access to educational resources on pregnancy, childbirth, and parenting to help them feel more informed and prepared for the upcoming changes in their life.
5. Financial Planning: Help them plan for the financial aspects of pregnancy and parenthood, such as creating a budget, setting aside funds for baby-related expenses, or exploring financial assistance programs available during this time.
6. Emotional Check-ins: Regularly check in with them to offer emotional support and validate their feelings, acknowledging the challenges they may be facing during pregnancy.
7. Activity Planning: Help them plan fun activities and outings that promote relaxation and enjoyment during pregnancy, such as going for walks, trying prenatal yoga classes, or engaging in creative hobbies.
8. Meal Preparation: Offer to prepare healthy meals or snacks that are safe for pregnant women to eat, or help them plan meals and grocery shopping.
9. Household Assistance: Provide support with light household tasks such as laundry, cleaning, or organizing, which can help reduce stress during pregnancy.
