Creating more inclusive social policies for non-low-income, non-recently single individuals requires a thoughtful and comprehensive approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Address intergenerational poverty: Many individuals face barriers to economic mobility due to systemic issues such as discrimination, lack of access to education and job opportunities, and limited social safety nets. Addressing these underlying issues through policies such as affirmative action, mentorship programs, and targeted investments in marginalized communities can help create more inclusive social policies.
2. Focus on early childhood development: Investing in early childhood development programs can help ensure that all children have access to quality education, healthcare, and other essential services. This can help reduce inequality and promote economic mobility across generations.
3. Implement progressive taxation: Progressive taxation policies can help redistribute wealth and reduce income inequality. This can include policies such as increasing taxes on the wealthy, closing loopholes that benefit the rich, and implementing a more progressive tax code.
4. Strengthen social safety nets: Social safety nets such as unemployment insurance, food assistance programs, and housing support can help individuals and families during times of crisis. Expanding and strengthening these programs can help create a more inclusive society.
5. Promote access to affordable healthcare: Ensuring that everyone has access to affordable healthcare can help reduce financial barriers to healthcare and promote overall well-being. This can be achieved through policies such as expanding Medicaid, creating state-based exchanges, and implementing a public option.
6. Encourage diversity and inclusion in the workplace: Policies that promote diversity and inclusion in the workplace can help create a more inclusive society. This can include anti-discrimination policies, mentorship programs, and targeted hiring initiatives.
7. Invest in education and job training: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help individuals acquire the skills they need to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and degree programs that lead to high-paying jobs.
