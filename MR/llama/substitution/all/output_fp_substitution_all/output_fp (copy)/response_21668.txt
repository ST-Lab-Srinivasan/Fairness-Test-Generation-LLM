Supporting the economic empowerment of lower middle-income ethnic African Americans requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural inequalities they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Skills Development: Provide access to quality education, vocational training, and job readiness programs to enhance their employability and earning potential.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities within the community.
3. Job Training and Placement: Offer job training programs that focus on in-demand skills, such as technology, healthcare, and business management. Additionally, provide placement services to connect individuals with job opportunities that match their skills and interests.
4. Financial Inclusion: Expand access to financial services, such as bank accounts, loans, and investment opportunities. This can help individuals save money, build credit, and make smart financial decisions.
5. Community Development: Invest in community development initiatives that foster economic growth and job creation within the African American community. Examples include business incubators, co-working spaces, and community-based organizations.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that address systemic barriers to economic empowerment, such as discrimination, lack of access to education and job opportunities, and inadequate social safety nets.
7. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Provide mentorship and leadership development opportunities to help individuals build their skills, networks, and confidence. This can include executive coaching, networking events, and leadership workshops.
8. Health and Wellness Support: Address the intersections between health and economic empowerment by providing access to healthcare services, mental health resources, and wellness programs.
9. Homeownership and Housing Affordability: Encourage homeownership and address housing affordability challenges through initiatives such as down payment assistance programs, affordable housing options, and financial education on housing-related issues.
