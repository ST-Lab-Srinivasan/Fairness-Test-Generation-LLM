Introduction

Mixed race individuals, particularly those with African American ancestry, face unique challenges in the healthcare system. These challenges are not only related to access and quality of care but also to the complexities of navigating multiple identities and experiences. This paper will explore the specific challenges faced by not deep in poverty mixed African American people in the healthcare system, including barriers to accessing care, cultural competency issues, and the need for inclusive policies and practices.

Barriers to Accessing Care

1. Lack of culturally sensitive providers: Mixed race individuals may face challenges in finding providers who are knowledgeable about their specific cultural backgrounds and experiences. This can lead to misdiagnosis or inadequate care.
2. Language barriers: Individuals with limited English proficiency may encounter difficulties communicating with healthcare providers, leading to misunderstandings or missed diagnoses.
3. Limited access to specialized care: Mixed race individuals may have reduced access to specialized care, such as mental health services or cancer treatment, due to systemic barriers and biases.
4. Financial barriers: Despite having a higher socioeconomic status than some other racial and ethnic groups, mixed race individuals may still face financial barriers to accessing healthcare services.

Cultural Competency Issues

1. Stereotyping and stigma: Mixed race individuals may encounter stereotypes and stigma based on their racial and ethnic identities, leading to negative experiences in the healthcare setting.
2. Lack of understanding of cultural practices and beliefs: Healthcare providers may be unfamiliar with the cultural practices and beliefs of mixed race individuals, resulting in inadequate care or misunderstandings.
3. Inadequate representation: The lack of representation of mixed race individuals in healthcare leadership positions can result in a lack of understanding of their needs and experiences.
4. Tokenization: Mixed race individuals may be tokenized or stereotyped based on their racial and ethnic identities, leading to feelings of isolation and marginalization.
