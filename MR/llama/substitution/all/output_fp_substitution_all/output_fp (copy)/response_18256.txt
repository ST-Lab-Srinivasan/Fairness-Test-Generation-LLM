Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of Mexican Native natural-born US citizen people. However, despite these challenges, cultural heritage remains an important part of their identity and sense of self.

It provides a sense of belonging and identity, especially in the face of discrimination and marginalization. Cultural heritage also serves as a source of pride and a means of resistance against oppressive systems that seek to erase their identities.

In recent years, there has been a resurgence of interest in Mexican Native cultural heritage, particularly among younger generations. This revitalization is driven by a desire to reclaim and reassert indigenous identities, as well as to preserve cultural traditions for future generations. Many Mexican Natives are working to document and preserve their cultural heritage through various means, such as language documentation projects, cultural centers, and museums.
