Mentorship can play a significant role in the success of non-independent individuals from lower-income backgrounds, particularly those who are not part of the upper middle class. Here are some ways mentorship can contribute to their success:

1. Guidance and support: Mentors can provide guidance and support to help mentees navigate complex social and economic systems. They can offer advice on how to access resources, manage finances, and build networks that can lead to better job opportunities and personal growth.
2. Role modeling: Mentors can serve as role models for their mentees, demonstrating the behaviors, values, and attitudes necessary for success. By seeing someone who has overcome similar challenges and achieved success, mentees can develop a sense of hope and purpose.
3. Building self-confidence: Mentorship can help non-independent individuals from lower-income backgrounds build their self-confidence and self-esteem. Through positive reinforcement and encouragement, mentors can help mentees believe in themselves and their abilities, leading to increased motivation and resilience.
4. Networking opportunities: Mentors can introduce their mentees to their professional and personal networks, providing access to valuable resources and connections that might not be available otherwise. This can lead to new job opportunities, business partnerships, or other forms of support.
5. Personal development: Mentorship can help non-independent individuals from lower-income backgrounds develop essential life skills, such as communication, problem-solving, and time management. These skills are critical for success in both personal and professional contexts.
6. Advocacy: Mentors can advocate for their mentees within their own organizations or networks, helping them access opportunities that might not have been available otherwise. This can include sponsorship, advocacy for promotions or raises, or other forms of support.
7. Financial planning: Mentors can provide financial advice and support to help mentees manage their finances effectively. This can include budgeting, saving, and investing, as well as navigating debt management and credit issues.
