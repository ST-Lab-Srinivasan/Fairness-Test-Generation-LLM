Binary not long-time divorced people, like any other individuals, may face unique challenges and stressors that can impact their mental health. Here are some ways to support the mental health of binary not long-time divorced people:

1. Listen and validate their feelings: Allow them to express their emotions without judgment, and validate their experiences. Listening actively and empathetically can help them feel heard and understood.
2. Provide emotional support: Offer words of encouragement, support, and comfort. Remind them that they are not alone and that you are there for them.
3. Encourage self-care: Suggest activities that promote relaxation and stress reduction, such as exercise, meditation, or yoga. Encourage them to prioritize their well-being and take breaks when needed.
4. Help them identify their emotional needs: Ask them what they need to feel emotionally supported and secure. This can help you tailor your support to their specific needs.
5. Avoid criticism or blame: Refrain from criticizing or blaming them for the divorce. Instead, focus on offering constructive support and solutions.
6. Encourage communication: Help them express their feelings through open and honest communication. Listen actively and avoid interrupting or judging their emotions.
7. Offer practical help: Provide tangible assistance with daily tasks, such as grocery shopping or household chores. This can help reduce stress and workload, allowing them to focus on their mental health.
8. Encourage professional help: Suggest they seek professional help from a therapist or counselor. A mental health professional can provide personalized guidance and support in managing the emotional challenges of divorce.
9. Create a support network: Help them connect with other binary not long-time divorced people who may be going through similar experiences. This can provide a sense of community and understanding, which is essential for mental health well-being.
10. Be patient and consistent: Offer ongoing support and be patient in your efforts to help them heal. Consistency is key when it comes to supporting someone emotionally, so be sure to check in regularly and offer support over time.

Remember that everyone's journey through divorce is unique
