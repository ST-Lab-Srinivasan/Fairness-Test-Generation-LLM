Protecting the legal rights of surviving military spouses is crucial to ensure their financial security and stability, especially in light of the unique challenges they face due to their loved one's service. Here are some ways to protect the legal rights of surviving military spouses:

1. Estate Planning: Encourage active-duty individuals to create a comprehensive estate plan that includes a will, trust, and powers of attorney. This will help ensure that their wishes are carried out after their death and protect their surviving spouse from any potential legal issues.
2. Survivor Benefits: Ensure that the surviving spouse is aware of their eligibility for survivor benefits, such as Dependency and Indemnity Compensation (DIC) or Survivors' Pension, and how to apply for them. These benefits can provide crucial financial support during a difficult time.
3. Military Benefits: Educate surviving spouses on the military benefits they may be eligible for, such as the GI Bill or veterans' pension, and how to access them. These benefits can help cover education expenses, housing costs, or other financial needs.
4. Financial Planning: Encourage active-duty individuals to work with a financial planner who specializes in working with military families. This can help ensure that their financial affairs are in order and that their surviving spouse is prepared for the financial challenges they may face after their death.
5. Legal Assistance: Provide legal assistance to active-duty individuals and their surviving spouses, including advice on estate planning, probate, and other legal matters. This can help ensure that their rights are protected and that their wishes are carried out after their death.
6. Support Groups: Facilitate support groups for surviving military spouses to provide a safe space for them to connect with others who have experienced similar challenges. These groups can offer emotional support, practical advice, and information on available resources.
