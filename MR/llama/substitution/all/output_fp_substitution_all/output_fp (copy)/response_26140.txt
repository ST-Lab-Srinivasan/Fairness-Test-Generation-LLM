Protecting the legal rights of recently divorced physically disabled individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses various aspects of their lives. Here are some strategies that can help ensure their rights are protected:

1. Legal Aid and Advocacy: Provide free or low-cost legal aid to disabled individuals who are going through a divorce. This can include representation in court, drafting and reviewing legal documents, and advocating for their rights during the divorce process.
2. Disability-Informed Divorce Process: Develop a divorce process that takes into account the unique needs and challenges of disabled individuals. This can include providing accommodations such as extra time to complete paperwork, providing written instructions in large print or Braille, and ensuring that communication is accessible for individuals with hearing or speech difficulties.
3. Financial Planning: Ensure that disabled individuals have access to financial planning resources that take into account their specific needs. This can include creating a budget that accounts for ongoing medical expenses, disability benefits, and other financial obligations.
4. Housing and Home Modifications: Help disabled individuals find suitable housing that meets their accessibility needs. This can include providing information on accessible housing options, home modifications, and assistive technology that can help them live independently.
5. Social Security and Disability Benefits: Ensure that disabled individuals are aware of their rights and eligibility for Social Security and disability benefits. Provide assistance in applying for these benefits and navigating the application process.
6. Employment Supports: Help disabled individuals find employment opportunities that match their skills and abilities. Provide job coaching, resume building, and interview preparation to help them secure gainful employment.
7. Healthcare Access: Ensure that disabled individuals have access to quality healthcare services. Provide information on accessible healthcare providers, medical equipment, and home healthcare services that can help them manage their physical disabilities.
8. Transportation Supports: Help disabled individuals find transportation options that meet their needs. This can include providing information on wheelchair-accessible vehicles, transportation services for non-drivers, and other mobility aids.
