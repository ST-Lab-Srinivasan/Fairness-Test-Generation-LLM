1. Emotional Support: Volunteers can offer emotional support to pregnant servicewomen by providing a listening ear, validating their feelings, and offering words of encouragement. This can be especially important during the challenging third trimester when physical discomforts and anxieties about childbirth are common.
2. Practical Assistance: Volunteers can help with practical tasks such as grocery shopping, meal preparation, and housekeeping. This can help alleviate some of the stress associated with pregnancy and allow servicewomen to focus on their health and well-being.

Social Connection: Volunteers can provide social connection and a sense of community for pregnant servicewomen who may be feeling isolated or disconnected from their peers due to military deployments or station moves. This can help alleviate feelings of loneliness and improve overall mental health.
4. Childcare Support: Volunteers can offer childcare support, such as babysitting or storytime sessions, which can be especially helpful during the third trimester when pregnant servicewomen may need extra support with caring for their children.
5. Resource Connection: Volunteers can connect pregnant servicewomen with relevant resources and services, such as prenatal care, parenting classes, and baby supplies. This can help ensure that they receive the necessary support and care during this critical time.
6. Morale Boosting: Volunteers can organize morale-boosting events, such as movie nights or game days, which can help distract servicewomen from pregnancy discomforts and provide a much-needed break.
