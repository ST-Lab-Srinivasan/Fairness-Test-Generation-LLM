Supporting the economic empowerment of deep poverty Native natural-born US citizens requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural inequalities they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and job training programs: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that equip individuals with the skills and knowledge needed to secure better-paying jobs. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and college scholarships.
2. Microfinance initiatives: Offer microfinance options such as small business loans, grants, and savings accounts to help individuals start or expand their own businesses. This can help them generate income and build assets.
3. Entrepreneurship support: Provide resources and mentorship to support entrepreneurs from Native communities in starting and growing their businesses. This can include access to capital, networking opportunities, and business incubators.
4. Access to healthcare: Ensure that individuals have access to quality healthcare services, including mental health services, to address the physical and mental health challenges they may face.
5. Housing support: Provide affordable housing options and support services to help individuals secure stable housing and avoid homelessness.
6. Child care support: Offer affordable child care services to help individuals who are raising children while also working to improve their economic situation.
7. Legal services: Provide legal services and advocacy to help individuals navigate the legal system and address issues related to land rights, identity documents, and other legal barriers.
8. Community development: Invest in community development projects that create jobs, improve infrastructure, and promote economic growth in Native communities.
9. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies that address the systemic inequalities faced by Native individuals, including those related to land rights, resource extraction, and cultural preservation.
10. Cultural revitalization: Support efforts to revitalize Native languages, cultures, and traditions, which can help individuals connect with their heritage and promote cultural pride and identity.
11. Mentorship and leadership development: Provide mentorship and leadership development opportunities to help individuals from Native communities build their skills and confidence, and take on leadership roles in their communities.
