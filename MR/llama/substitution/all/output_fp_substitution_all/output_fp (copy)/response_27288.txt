As a society, there are several ways we can support the career aspirations of non- newly single wheelchair-using individuals:

1. Accessibility in the Workplace: Ensure that workplaces are accessible and inclusive for all employees, including those with disabilities. Provide reasonable accommodations and modifications to facilitate their participation in the workforce.
2. Education and Training: Offer training and education programs specifically designed for individuals with disabilities, to help them acquire the skills and knowledge required for their chosen career paths.
3. Mentorship Programs: Pair individuals with disabilities with mentors who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement in their professional development.
4. Disability-Inclusive Hiring Practices: Ensure that hiring practices are inclusive and free from bias, and that job postings are accessible to individuals with disabilities.
5. Flexible Work Arrangements: Offer flexible work arrangements such as telecommuting, flexible hours, or job sharing, to help individuals with disabilities balance their work and personal responsibilities.
6. Disability-Specific Resources: Provide resources and support specifically designed for individuals with disabilities, such as disability-specific networking groups or career fairs.
7. Accommodating Work Environment: Ensure that the work environment is accommodating and inclusive of employees with disabilities, including providing accessible equipment and technology.
8. Disability Inclusion in Leadership: Encourage and support individuals with disabilities to take on leadership roles within their organizations, to promote diversity and inclusion in leadership positions.
9. Disability-Aware Supervisors: Train supervisors and managers to be aware of the challenges faced by employees with disabilities, and to provide appropriate support and accommodations.
10. Continuous Improvement: Regularly assess and evaluate the effectiveness of these initiatives and make necessary adjustments to ensure that they continue to meet the evolving needs of individuals with disabilities in the workplace.

By implementing these strategies, organizations can create a more inclusive and supportive environment for non- newly single wheelchair-using individuals, helping them to reach their full potential in their chosen careers.
