1. 2. Offer language support: Provide interpretation services during program activities, such as Spanish translation for newsletters, announcements, or educational materials. You can also offer language classes or workshops to help participants improve their English skills.

4. Engage with local organizations: Partner with local Hispanic/Latino organizations to co-host programs or events. This will help you reach a wider audience and build trust within the community.
5. Provide accessible transportation: Ensure that your programs are easily accessible for older adults who may have limited mobility. Offer transportation services or arrange for pick-up and drop-off at local transportation hubs, such as bus stops or senior centers.
6. Involve community leaders: Invite local Hispanic/Latino leaders to participate in program planning and implementation. This will help you better understand the needs of the community and build trust among participants.
