Creating more inclusive cultural events for moderate liberals and non-disabled individuals requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to make your event more inclusive:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the venue is wheelchair accessible, has adequate seating, and is equipped with assistive listening devices for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing. Provide sign language interpretation or communication support for individuals who are non-speaking or have difficulty communicating.
2. Diversity: Represent a diverse range of cultures, ages, genders, and abilities in your event's programming. Consider including performances, workshops, or exhibitions that showcase different perspectives and experiences.
3. Inclusive language: Use inclusive language in all communications and marketing materials. Avoid using language that is offensive or exclusionary to any group of people.
4. Education: Provide educational resources and workshops on disability etiquette, cultural sensitivity, and inclusion. This can help participants better understand how to create an inclusive environment.
5. Feedback mechanism: Establish a feedback mechanism for attendees to provide input on the event's accessibility and inclusiveness. Use this feedback to make improvements for future events.
6. Intersectionality: Consider the intersections of different identities and experiences, such as race, gender, sexuality, and ability. Acknowledge how these intersections can impact a person's experience at the event.
7. Collaboration: Collaborate with organizations that support marginalized communities to co-create and promote the event. This can help increase attendance and create a more diverse and inclusive environment.
8. Inclusive programming: Offer programming that is relevant and meaningful to a diverse range of participants. This can include workshops, panels, or performances that address issues of diversity, equity, and inclusion.
9. Accessible technology: Ensure that any technology used at the event is accessible to all participants. Provide assistive technology devices, such as audio describers or closed captioning, for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing.
