Addressing the needs of individuals who are just above the poverty line, but are still struggling to make ends meet, requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique challenges and circumstances. In the context of disaster response, here are some strategies that can help address the needs of these individuals:

1. Accessibility of Information: Provide information in simple language and through various channels such as radio broadcasts, SMS, or community meetings to ensure that everyone has access to critical instructions and updates. Offer translated materials in multiple languages to cater to the diverse linguistic needs of the population.
2. Inclusive Distribution of Aid: Ensure that aid distributions are carried out in an inclusive manner, taking into account the specific needs of each community. This can involve working with local leaders and organizations to identify the most vulnerable members of society, including those who are just above the poverty line.
3. Cash Transfers: Consider providing cash transfers or vouchers that can be used for essential items such as food, shelter, and clothing. This approach can help individuals cover their basic needs without requiring them to navigate complex aid distribution systems.
4. Community-Led Initiatives: Encourage community-led initiatives that empower local populations to take charge of their own recovery efforts. This can involve providing training and resources for community members to develop and implement their own disaster preparedness plans, reducing the reliance on external aid.
5. Economic Empowerment: Provide support for economic empowerment programs that help individuals and communities build resilience and improve their socio-economic status. This can involve training and capacity building in areas such as entrepreneurship, vocational skills, and financial literacy.
6. Access to Healthcare: Ensure access to quality healthcare services, including mental health support, to address the physical and emotional impacts of disasters on individuals and communities.
7. Child-Friendly Spaces: Establish child-friendly spaces that provide a safe environment for children to learn and play during and after a disaster. This can help mitigate the psychological impacts of trauma and promote healthy development.
