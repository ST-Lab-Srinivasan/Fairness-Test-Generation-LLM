Economic empowerment of lower-class ethnic Hispanics requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural inequalities they face. Here are some strategies to support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and job training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs tailored to the needs of lower-class ethnic Hispanics. This can help them acquire skills and knowledge that can lead to better job opportunities and higher earning potential.
2. Microfinance and small business support: Offer microloans, financial literacy programs, and technical assistance to help lower-class ethnic Hispanics start or expand their own businesses. This can help them generate income and build wealth.
3. Access to affordable housing: Address the lack of affordable housing options in underserved communities by providing subsidies, tax credits, or other incentives to developers who build affordable housing units. This can help lower-class ethnic Hispanics access safe and affordable housing.
4. Healthcare access: Improve access to quality healthcare services, particularly for those living in underserved communities. This can help reduce health disparities and improve overall well-being, which is essential for economic empowerment.
5. Community development: Invest in community development initiatives that promote social cohesion, cultural identity, and economic growth. This can help build a sense of belonging and pride among lower-class ethnic Hispanics, which can lead to greater economic empowerment.
