Promoting the educational success of Native American recent undocumented students in the US requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the unique challenges and barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers to incorporate cultural values, beliefs, and practices into their instruction to create a more inclusive and supportive learning environment.
2. Bilingual education: Provide bilingual education programs that cater to the language needs of Native American students who may not be proficient in English.
3. Indigenous knowledge integration: Incorporate traditional knowledge and practices into curriculum design to make it more relevant and meaningful to students' lives.
4. Mental health support: Provide mental health services and resources to help students cope with the trauma associated with migration and displacement.
5. Financial support: Offer financial aid and scholarships specifically designed for Native American students to help them overcome economic barriers to education.
6. Community partnerships: Develop partnerships with local Native American communities to provide additional support services, such as tutoring, mentorship, and cultural activities.
7. Advocacy and policy change: Advocate for policies that address the systemic inequalities faced by Native American students, such as inadequate funding, lack of representation, and discrimination.
8. Parent and family engagement: Involve parents and families in the educational process through workshops, events, and communication to build a supportive community around student success.
9. Teacher training: Provide ongoing professional development opportunities for teachers to learn about Native American culture, history, and pedagogy to better serve this population.
10. Cultural celebrations and events: Organize cultural events and celebrations that highlight the contributions and traditions of Native American communities to promote a sense of belonging and inclusivity on campus.
11. Student organizations: Support the creation of student organizations that promote cultural identity, social justice, and academic success among Native American students.
