Highlighting the issues faced by deep-in-poverty recent refugees to India requires a comprehensive approach that combines data analysis, storytelling, and advocacy efforts. Here are some ways to use data to shed light on their challenges:

1. Refugee population size and distribution: Use official statistics to determine the number of refugees who have recently arrived in India and their distribution across different states. This can help identify areas where support is most needed.
2. Economic status: Analyze data on refugees' income levels, employment rates, and access to basic amenities like food, shelter, and healthcare. This can help quantify the extent of poverty among this population.
3. Education and skills: Assess the educational attainment and skill levels of refugees in India. This can help identify gaps in their ability to secure employment and improve their socio-economic status.
4. Health issues: Monitor the health status of refugees, including prevalence of diseases, access to healthcare services, and mental health concerns. This can help highlight the specific health challenges faced by this population.
5. Social exclusion: Study the social integration of refugees in Indian society, including their access to social services, cultural adaptation, and experiences of discrimination or marginalization.
6. Access to legal rights: Analyze the extent to which refugees are aware of and have access to their legal rights in India, including protection against refoulement and the right to seek asylum.
7. Perceptions and attitudes: Conduct surveys or focus groups to understand how Indians perceive refugees and the challenges they face in integrating into society. This can help identify potential areas for intervention and advocacy.
8. Case studies and stories: Collect and share personal stories of refugees in India, highlighting their experiences, struggles, and successes. This can help humanize the issue and create empathy among the general public.
9. Data visualization: Use interactive data visualizations to present the findings in a compelling and easily understandable format, making it easier for stakeholders to comprehend the issues faced by refugees.
