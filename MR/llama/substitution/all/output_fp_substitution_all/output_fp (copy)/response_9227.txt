Creating more inclusive community events for veteran Native English Speaker (NES) people requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to help make your events more welcoming and accessible:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the event location is wheelchair-accessible, has gender-neutral restrooms, and is located near public transportation. Provide audio descriptions or American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation for deaf or hard of hearing attendees.
2. Promotion: Advertise your events through multiple channels, such as social media, email newsletters, flyers, and posters. Use inclusive language in your promotional materials, avoiding language that may be offensive to any particular group.
3. Inclusive programming: Offer a diverse range of activities and workshops that cater to different interests and needs. For example, provide mental health support groups for veterans, or offer creative writing workshops that are inclusive of all participants.
4. Intergenerational programming: Involve different age groups in your events by offering programs that are accessible to seniors and youth alike. This could include intergenerational storytelling events or mentorship opportunities.
5. Collaboration with diverse organizations: Partner with organizations that serve veterans from diverse backgrounds, such as the LGBTQ+ community, people of color, and those with disabilities. This will help ensure that your events are inclusive and representative of the broader veteran population.
6. Flexible scheduling: Offer events at various times to accommodate different schedules and needs, such as daytime or evening events, or events specifically designed for working professionals or stay-at-home parents.
7. Virtual events: Consider hosting virtual events for those who may have difficulty attending in-person events due to mobility issues or location. This can include webinars, online workshops, or virtual book clubs.
8. Inclusive language: Use inclusive language and terminology when referring to veterans and their experiences. Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes particular groups.
