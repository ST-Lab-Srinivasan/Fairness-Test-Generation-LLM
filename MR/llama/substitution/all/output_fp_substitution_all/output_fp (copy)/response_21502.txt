Creating more inclusive cultural programs for non-citizen individuals requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to make your cultural programs more welcoming and inclusive:

1. Inclusive language: Use language that is inclusive and respectful of all cultures. Avoid using terms that stereotype or marginalize specific groups, such as "illegal immigrants" or "non-citizens." Instead, use language that acknowledges the diversity of your audience, such as "individuals from diverse backgrounds" or "people with varied cultural experiences."
2. Representation matters: Ensure that your cultural programs feature a diverse range of individuals and stories. This can include artists, musicians, writers, and other cultural practitioners who are non-citizens or belong to marginalized communities. Use this opportunity to showcase their talents and experiences, and give them a platform to share their perspectives.
3. Accessibility: Make sure that your cultural programs are accessible to everyone, regardless of their abilities. Provide accommodations such as sign language interpretation, audio descriptions, or braille materials for individuals with disabilities. This will help create a more inclusive environment and ensure that everyone can participate fully.
4. Cultural sensitivity training: Provide cultural sensitivity training for staff and volunteers involved in the program. This can include workshops on cultural competency, diversity, and inclusion. This will help them understand and appreciate the diverse backgrounds of your audience and provide a more inclusive experience.
5. Feedback mechanisms: Establish feedback mechanisms to gather input from participants on how to improve the program's inclusivity. This can include surveys, focus groups, or one-on-one interviews. Use this feedback to make adjustments and improvements to create a more welcoming and inclusive environment.

These organizations can provide valuable insights and help you reach a wider audience. Work together to co-create programs that are tailored to the needs and interests of these communities.
