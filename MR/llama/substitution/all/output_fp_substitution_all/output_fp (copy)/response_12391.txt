There are several successful community initiatives led by physically able-bodied carriers of genetic conditions. These initiatives aim to raise awareness, promote understanding, and provide support for individuals with genetic conditions and their families. Here are some examples:

1. The Genetic Alliance: This organization is a network of patient advocacy groups that works to improve the health and well-being of individuals affected by genetic conditions. Founded in 2001, The Genetic Alliance has over 40 member organizations, including the National Down Syndrome Congress, the Cystic Fibrosis Foundation, and the Muscular Dystrophy Association.
2. The Arc: This organization provides advocacy and support for individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities, including those caused by genetic mutations. The Arc has over 700 chapters across the United States and works to promote inclusive communities and advocate for policies that support the rights of individuals with disabilities.
3. The National Organization for Rare Disorders (NORD): This organization provides information, education, and advocacy for individuals with rare genetic disorders. NORD has a database of over 1,000 rare diseases and conditions, and works to promote research and develop treatments for these diseases.

The Down Syndrome Diagnosis Network (DSDN): This organization provides support and resources for families affected by Down syndrome, including information on prenatal testing, early intervention services, and advocacy for individuals with Down syndrome. DSDN also works to promote awareness and understanding of Down syndrome in the general public.
5. The Cystic Fibrosis Foundation (CFF): This organization is dedicated to finding a cure for cystic fibrosis, a genetic disorder that affects the respiratory and digestive systems.
