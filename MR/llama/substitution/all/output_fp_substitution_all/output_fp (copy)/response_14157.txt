Nigeria has a diverse population with a high density of widows, particularly in rural areas. Widowhood can be challenging for women who have lost their spouses, as they often face social isolation and economic hardship. To support the social inclusion of Nigerian recently widowed people, here are some ways to consider:

1. Provide financial assistance: Offer financial support to help them meet their basic needs, such as food, shelter, clothing, and medical care. This can be done through donations or by providing access to microfinance loans.
2. Create a support group: Form a group of volunteers who can provide emotional support, counseling, and advice on how to navigate the challenges of widowhood. This can help them feel less isolated and more connected to their community.
3. Offer training and education: Provide training and education on various skills such as entrepreneurship, vocational skills, and digital literacy. This can help them earn a steady income and become financially independent.
4. Advocate for policy change: Work with local government agencies and advocacy groups to address the social and economic challenges faced by widows in Nigeria. This can include pushing for policies that promote gender equality, protect their rights, and provide them with access to resources and opportunities.
5. Promote cultural changes: Challenge traditional beliefs and practices that discriminate against widows, such as the practice of "wife inheritance" or the stigma associated with being a widow. Encourage communities to treat widows with respect and dignity, and to recognize their contributions to society.
6. Provide healthcare support: Many widows in Nigeria face challenges accessing healthcare due to financial constraints or lack of access to health facilities. Provide financial support for healthcare services, including access to maternal and child healthcare, and mental health services.
7. Facilitate access to credit and loans: Widows often face challenges accessing credit and loans due to their gender and socio-economic status. Work with microfinance institutions and banks to provide them with access to affordable credit and loan facilities.
