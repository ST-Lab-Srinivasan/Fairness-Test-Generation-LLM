Promoting the artistic talents of elderly Arabic speakers, particularly those in their 90s, can be a challenging task due to various factors such as age-related physical and cognitive decline, limited access to resources and opportunities, and societal attitudes towards aging. However, there are several strategies that can help promote the artistic talents of these individuals:

1. Encourage and facilitate their participation in art classes or workshops: Many community centers, art schools, and non-profit organizations offer classes or workshops specifically designed for elderly individuals. These programs can provide a supportive environment where they can explore their creativity and develop new skills.
2. Provide access to technology and digital platforms: With the increasing use of digital technologies, it's essential to provide elderly Arabic speakers with access to tools that can help them express their artistic talents. This could include tablets or computers equipped with software for painting, drawing, or music composition.
3. Organize cultural events and exhibitions: Hosting cultural events and exhibitions can be an excellent way to showcase the artistic talents of elderly Arabic speakers. These events can provide a platform for them to display their work and gain recognition from a wider audience.
4. Collaborate with local institutions and organizations: Partnering with local museums, galleries, or cultural centers can help promote the artistic talents of elderly Arabic speakers. These institutions can provide valuable resources, expertise, and exposure to a larger audience.
5. Offer mentorship opportunities: Pairing older artists with younger generations can be an effective way to transfer knowledge and skills. This mentorship can help pass on the artistic traditions and techniques of elderly Arabic speakers to a new generation.
