Creating more inclusive public spaces for moderate Native Spanish speakers requires a thoughtful and intentional approach to design, planning, and management. Here are some strategies that can help create more welcoming environments:

1. Language Accessibility: Provide language support and interpretation services, such as translations of signage, brochures, and other materials. Offer bilingual or multilingual staff to assist with inquiries and provide guidance.
2. Cultural Competency Training: Provide training for staff and volunteers on the cultural practices and values of Native American communities. This can help build understanding and empathy when interacting with visitors.
3. Accessibility Features: Ensure that public spaces are accessible to people with disabilities, including wheelchair accessibility, Braille signage, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
4. Inclusive Programming: Develop programming that is inclusive of Native American communities, such as cultural events, workshops, and exhibits that showcase the history and traditions of these communities.
5. Community Engagement: Work with local Native American communities to co-create and co-manage public spaces. This can help build trust and ensure that the space is responsive to their needs and preferences.
6. Education and Awareness: Provide educational materials and exhibits that highlight the history and contributions of Native American communities. This can help increase awareness and appreciation for these cultures.
7. Respectful Naming and Branding: Ensure that public spaces are named and branded in a respectful manner that acknowledges and honors the cultural heritage of Native American communities.
8. Partnerships and Collaborations: Foster partnerships and collaborations with local Native American organizations, schools, and community groups to promote mutual understanding and support.
9. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and engagement activities that actively involve Native American communities in the planning and management of public spaces.
10. Inclusive Design Principles: Apply inclusive design principles when designing and managing public spaces, such as incorporating universal design elements, providing adaptable spaces, and ensuring that the space is comfortable and welcoming for all users.
