Supporting the leadership development of gender-nonconforming poor people requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the intersectional nature of their experiences. Here are some ways to do so:

1. Safe Spaces: Create safe and inclusive spaces for gender-nonconforming poor people to share their experiences, build relationships, and develop leadership skills without fear of judgment or discrimination.
2. Identity-Based Programming: Develop programming that acknowledges and respects the gender identity of participants, recognizing that their experiences are shaped by multiple forms of oppression.
3. Inclusive Funding: Ensure that funding opportunities are accessible to gender-nonconforming poor people, regardless of their gender identity or socioeconomic status.
4. Capacity Building: Provide training and resources to help gender-nonconforming poor people build skills in areas such as leadership, advocacy, and community organizing.
5. Mentorship Programs: Pair gender-nonconforming poor people with experienced leaders and mentors who understand their unique challenges and can provide guidance and support.
6. Community Engagement: Encourage gender-nonconforming poor people to engage with their communities, fostering a sense of belonging and collective power.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies and practices that address the systemic barriers facing gender-nonconforming poor people, such as discrimination in employment, housing, and healthcare.
8. Intersectional Approach: Take an intersectional approach to leadership development, recognizing that gender-nonconforming poor people may face multiple forms of oppression (e.g., racism, homophobia, transphobia) and addressing these intersections in programming and advocacy efforts.
9. Cultural Humility: Practice cultural humility by acknowledging the diversity of gender-nonconforming poor people's experiences and perspectives, and being mindful of power dynamics when engaging with them.
10. Collaboration and Partnership: Build partnerships with organizations that serve gender-nonconforming poor people, working together to address the root causes of inequality and promote inclusive leadership development.
