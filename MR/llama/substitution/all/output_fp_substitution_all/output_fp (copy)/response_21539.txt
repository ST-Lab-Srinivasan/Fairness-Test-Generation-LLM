The economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US citizens who are long-term unemployed is a critical issue that requires a multifaceted approach. Here are some potential strategies that could be implemented to support their economic empowerment:

1. Job Training and Placement Programs: Provide job training and placement programs specifically tailored to the needs of Native American communities. These programs can help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge to enhance their employability.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship among Native Americans by providing access to resources such as business loans, mentorship, and training. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities within the community.
3. Apprenticeships and Internships: Offer apprenticeships and internships in various fields such as construction, healthcare, and technology. These opportunities can provide valuable work experience and skills development for individuals looking to enter the job market.
4. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that connect experienced professionals with unemployed Native Americans. This can provide guidance, support, and networking opportunities for individuals looking to advance their careers.
5. Infrastructure Development: Invest in infrastructure development projects such as road construction, renewable energy installations, and telecommunications services. These initiatives can create jobs and stimulate economic growth within Native American communities.
6. Education and Training Partnerships: Develop partnerships between educational institutions and Native American communities to provide access to education and training programs. This can help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge that are in demand by local employers.
7. Community Development Grants: Provide grants for community development projects that support economic growth and job creation within Native American communities. These grants can fund initiatives such as small business incubators, cooperatives, and other community-based enterprises.
8. Government Contracting Opportunities: Encourage the federal government to contract with Native American-owned businesses for goods and services. This can provide economic opportunities for Native American entrepreneurs and help address the disparity in contracting opportunities.
