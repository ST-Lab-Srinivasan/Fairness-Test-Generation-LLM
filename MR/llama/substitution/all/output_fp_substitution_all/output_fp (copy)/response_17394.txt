Promoting the economic empowerment of Nigerian not-with-child communities is crucial for their overall well-being and development. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to achieve this goal:

1. Access to Finance: Provide access to financial services such as microfinance, savings accounts, and loans to help community members start or grow their businesses. This can be done through partnerships with local banks, microfinance institutions, or non-profit organizations.
2. Entrepreneurship Training: Offer training programs that teach community members how to start and grow a business. This can include topics such as market research, financial planning, and business management.
3. Capacity Building: Provide training and capacity building opportunities in various skills such as vocational skills, leadership, and governance. This can help community members develop the necessary skills to manage and grow their businesses.
4. Market Access: Facilitate access to markets for community members by connecting them with potential buyers, suppliers, and partners. This can be done through partnerships with local businesses, organizations, or government agencies.
5. Technology Transfer: Transfer technology and innovation to community members to help them improve their productivity and competitiveness. This can include training on the use of technology such as mobile phones, computers, and software.
6. Social Enterprise: Support social enterprises that focus on addressing specific social and economic challenges in the community. These enterprises can provide employment opportunities and generate income for the community while also addressing broader societal issues.
7. Community Mapping: Conduct a mapping exercise to identify the strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats of the community. This can help identify potential areas for economic development and investment.
8. Participatory Planning: Engage community members in participatory planning processes to identify their needs and priorities. This can help ensure that any interventions are responsive to the needs of the community and reflect their priorities.
9. Public-Private Partnerships: Facilitate partnerships between public and private sector actors to leverage resources, expertise, and influence for economic development in the community.
