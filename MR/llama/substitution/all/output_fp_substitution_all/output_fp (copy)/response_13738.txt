Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Recently Married Multiple Times Multiple Times Native Spanish Speaker Individuals

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, especially when it comes to recently married multiple times multiple times native Spanish speaker individuals. This study aims to investigate how cultural heritage influences the identities of these individuals and how they navigate their different cultural backgrounds.

Literature Review:
Previous research has shown that cultural heritage can have a profound impact on an individual's identity, particularly in the context of intercultural relationships. Kittler and Pahl (2015) found that individuals who marry outside of their cultural background may experience a sense of dislocation or loss of identity, as they navigate between different cultural worlds. This can be especially challenging when it comes to preserving cultural traditions and practices within a blended family context.

Methodology:
This study will use a qualitative research approach, conducting in-depth interviews with recently married multiple times multiple times native Spanish speaker individuals. Participants will be recruited through snowball sampling, reaching out to local communities and networks to identify potential participants. Interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim, allowing for a detailed analysis of participants' experiences and perspectives.

Analysis:
Data analysis will involve thematic coding, identifying patterns and themes within the data. The following subthemes will be explored: (1) Cultural heritage and identity, (2) Blended family dynamics, (3) Navigating cultural differences, (4) Adapting to new cultural contexts, and (5) Preserving cultural traditions.

Expected Results:
This study aims to provide an in-depth understanding of how cultural heritage shapes the identities of recently married multiple times multiple times native Spanish speaker individuals. Findings will contribute to the literature on intercultural relationships and blended families, highlighting the unique challenges and opportunities faced by these individuals.
