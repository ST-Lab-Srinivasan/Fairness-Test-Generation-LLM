Supporting the leadership development of non-traditional leaders, such as those who are not lifetime divorcees or recently retired civilian employees, can be challenging but crucial for organizations that want to tap into their unique perspectives and talents. Here are some ways to support the leadership development of these individuals:

1. Identify areas of interest and strengths: Encourage non-traditional leaders to explore their areas of passion and expertise, and provide opportunities for them to develop their skills in these areas. This could involve offering training programs, mentorship, or shadowing opportunities.
2. Provide flexible pathways to leadership: Offer alternative pathways to leadership positions, such as lateral moves or specialized roles, that can help non-traditional leaders gain leadership experience without necessarily following the traditional career progression.
3. Foster a culture of inclusion: Create a work environment that values diversity and promotes inclusivity, where non-traditional leaders feel comfortable bringing their whole selves to work. This could involve creating employee resource groups or hosting events that celebrate diversity.
4. Leverage technology for remote leadership development: With the rise of remote work, there are now more opportunities than ever before to provide leadership development programs and resources to non-traditional leaders who may not be able to attend in-person programs. Consider offering virtual training sessions, webinars, or online courses that can be accessed at any time and from any location.
5. Encourage informal mentorship: Pair non-traditional leaders with more experienced colleagues who can provide guidance and support outside of formal mentorship programs. This could involve setting up regular check-ins or offering informal feedback and coaching opportunities.
6. Provide opportunities for community involvement: Encourage non-traditional leaders to get involved in their local communities, either through volunteer work or board service. This can help them develop leadership skills and build relationships with others outside of the workplace.
7. Offer coaching and feedback: Provide regular coaching and feedback sessions to help non-traditional leaders identify areas for improvement and track their progress over time.
