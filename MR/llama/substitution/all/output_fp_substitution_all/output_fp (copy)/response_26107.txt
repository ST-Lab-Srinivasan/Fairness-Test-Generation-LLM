Title: Examining the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Unmarried Beginner Arabic Speaker Individuals

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, particularly in the context of unmarried beginner Arabic speakers. This study aims to explore how cultural heritage influences the identities of these individuals and how it impacts their language learning journey.

 Literature Review:
Previous research has shown that cultural heritage can have a profound impact on an individual's identity (Hall, 1996). For unmarried beginner Arabic speakers, cultural heritage can shape their sense of self and their place in the world. It can also influence their attitudes towards learning a new language, as well as their motivations for doing so.

One of the key factors that influence an individual's identity is their upbringing and socialization (Bouchard & Sam, 2013). For unmarried beginner Arabic speakers, their cultural heritage can shape their beliefs, values, and attitudes towards language learning. For example, if they grow up in a culture that places a high value on language learning, they may be more motivated to learn Arabic. On the other hand, if they come from a culture that does not prioritize language learning, they may face additional challenges in their language learning journey.

Another factor that can influence an individual's identity is their social environment (Tajfel & Turner, 1979). For unmarried beginner Arabic speakers, the people they surround themselves with can play a significant role in shaping their identities. If they are surrounded by supportive and encouraging individuals who share their language learning goals, they may be more likely to succeed in their language learning journey. On the other hand, if they are surrounded by negative influences that discourage them from learning Arabic, it can impact their self-esteem and motivation.
