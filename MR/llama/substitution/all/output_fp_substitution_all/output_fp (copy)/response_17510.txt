2. Provide childcare and transportation options: For many families, finding reliable childcare and transportation can be a significant barrier to attending community events. Consider providing these services, either for free or at a reduced cost, to make it easier for people to attend.

Use language accessibility: Make sure that event materials, such as flyers, posters, and programs, are available in multiple languages, including simplified Chinese, Traditional Chinese, and other languages spoken by native Chinese speakers in your community. This can help ensure that everyone has access to the information they need.
4. Cultural sensitivity: Be mindful of cultural sensitivities and customs when planning events. For example, some cultures may have specific dietary restrictions or preferences, so it's important to provide options that cater to these needs.
5. Involve diverse community members in the planning process: Involve a diverse group of people from different backgrounds and perspectives in the planning process. This can help ensure that events are inclusive and meet the needs of a wide range of people.
6. Offer interpreters or translation services: If you're hosting a large event with a lot of speakers or presentations, consider hiring interpreters to provide real-time translations for native Chinese speakers who may not speak fluent English.
