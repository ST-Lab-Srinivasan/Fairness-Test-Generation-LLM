Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of natural-born US citizen Native American people. It is an essential part of their identity, culture, and community. Here are some ways cultural heritage impacts the lives of natural-born US citizen Native Americans:

1. Sense of Belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a larger community and a connection to their ancestors. For many Native American tribes, their cultural heritage is closely tied to their land and their ancestral territories, which are often still important to them today.
2. Cultural Practices and Traditions: Cultural heritage encompasses traditional practices, such as ceremonies, rituals, and customs, which are passed down from generation to generation. These practices and traditions are an integral part of daily life for many Native American communities.
3. Language and Literature: Many Native American tribes have their own languages and literatures, which are an essential part of their cultural heritage. Learning and preserving these languages and literatures is crucial to maintaining cultural continuity and ensuring the survival of their cultures.
4. Art and Music: Native American art and music are highly distinctive and varied, reflecting the diverse cultures and traditions of the many tribes. These forms of cultural expression are not only important for preserving cultural heritage but also play a significant role in shaping identity and community.
5. Foodways and Cuisine: The foods and cuisines of Native American communities are an essential part of their cultural heritage. Traditional foods, such as corn, beans, and wild game, are often central to cultural celebrations and ceremonies.
6. Spirituality and Religion: Many Native American tribes have their own spiritual beliefs and practices, which are closely tied to their cultural heritage. These beliefs and practices are an essential part of daily life for many Native Americans and play a significant role in shaping identity and community.
