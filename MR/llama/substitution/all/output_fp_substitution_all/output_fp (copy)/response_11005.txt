Creating more inclusive social policies for deep poverty long-term recently single individuals requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the various challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Education and Job Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that cater to the needs of this population, including adult basic education, vocational training, and job placement services. This can help them acquire new skills and knowledge to improve their employability.
2. Income Support: Implement a comprehensive income support system that provides financial assistance to individuals who are struggling to make ends meet. This can include cash transfers, subsidies for housing and food, and other forms of financial assistance.
3. Healthcare Access: Ensure access to affordable healthcare services, including mental health support, to address the physical and mental well-being of this population.
4. Childcare Support: Provide affordable childcare options to help individuals who are single parents or have other caregiving responsibilities. This can include subsidies for childcare costs, parenting classes, and other forms of support.
5. Housing Support: Ensure access to affordable and safe housing options, including rent subsidies, homeownership programs, and supportive housing services.
6. Legal Support: Provide legal aid services to help individuals navigate the justice system and address issues related to family law, employment law, and other areas of law that may be relevant to this population.
7. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement and social connections among individuals who are long-term single and in poverty. This can include volunteer opportunities, social events, and other forms of community building.
8. Advocacy and Awareness: Raise awareness about the issues faced by this population and advocate for policies that address their needs. This can include working with media outlets, policymakers, and other stakeholders to amplify their voices and push for systemic change.
9. Collaboration with Other Organizations: Collaborate with other organizations that serve similar populations, such as food banks, homeless shelters, and other social service agencies. This can help leverage resources, expertise, and funding to better address the needs of this population.
