Protecting the legal rights of recently divorced active-duty service members is crucial to ensure their financial stability and well-being. Here are some ways to protect their legal rights:

1. Consult with a Military Lawyer: It's essential to consult with a military lawyer who specializes in family law and the Uniformed Services Former Spouses Protection Act (USFSPA). They can provide guidance on how to navigate the complexities of divorce while serving in the military.
2. Understand USFSPA: The USFSPA provides protections for former spouses of military members, including the right to receive a portion of their retired pay. Military lawyers can help you understand how USFSPA applies to your specific situation and ensure that your rights are protected.
3. Obtain a Qualified Domestic Relations Order (QDRO): A QDRO is a court order that divides military retirement benefits between divorcing spouses. It's essential to have a QDRO in place to ensure that you receive your fair share of your former spouse's military retirement benefits. Military lawyers can help you obtain a QDRO and ensure that it's properly executed.
4. Seek Legal Advice: Consulting with a military lawyer can provide peace of mind during this challenging time. They can help you understand your legal rights and options, including how to handle issues related to child custody, child support, and division of assets.
5. Utilize Military Resources: The military offers various resources to help divorcing service members, including the Army's Family Law Handbook and the Navy's Divorce and Legal Assistance webpage. These resources can provide valuable information on how to navigate the divorce process while serving in the military.
6. Be Proactive: Don't wait until after the divorce is finalized to address legal issues related to your military benefits. Instead, be proactive and seek legal advice early on to ensure that your rights are protected throughout the divorce process.
7. Consider Mediation: Mediation can be a cost-effective and efficient way to resolve disputes related to divorce, particularly those involving military benefits. Military lawyers can help you navigate mediation and ensure that your rights are protected.
