Moderate-income refugees often face significant challenges when accessing healthcare services. Some of the common challenges include:

1. Lack of awareness: Many refugees may not be aware of the available healthcare services or may not know how to access them, especially if they are not familiar with the language or culture of the host country.
2. Limited financial resources: Moderate-income refugees may struggle to pay for healthcare services, especially if they do not have access to financial assistance programs or cannot afford the costs of healthcare in the host country.
3. Barriers to care: Refugees may face barriers to accessing healthcare services due to discrimination, stigma, or lack of cultural sensitivity among healthcare providers. They may also encounter difficulties in navigating complex healthcare systems or finding healthcare providers who speak their language and understand their culture.

Health disparities: Refugees may experience health disparities due to limited access to preventive care, delayed diagnosis and treatment of health conditions, and poor health outcomes due to underlying health issues that were not addressed in their home country or during their migration journey.
5. Mental health challenges: Refugees often experience significant mental health challenges, including post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), depression, anxiety, and substance abuse. These challenges can be exacerbated by the trauma they have experienced and the difficulties they face in adapting to their new environment.
6. Limited access to specialized care: Refugees may have limited access to specialized healthcare services, such as oncology, cardiology, or neurology, due to a lack of availability or affordability in the host country.
7. Cultural and language barriers: Refugees may face cultural and language barriers that can hinder their ability to communicate effectively with healthcare providers, leading to misunderstandings or misdiagnoses.
