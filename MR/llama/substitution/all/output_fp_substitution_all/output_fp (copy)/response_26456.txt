Promoting the educational success of green card holders and recent temporary visa holders in the US can be challenging, especially for non-conservative students. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Provide language support: Many green card holders and temporary visa holders may struggle with English proficiency, which can hinder their academic performance. Offer language support services such as English language courses or tutoring to help them improve their language skills.
2. Offer cultural orientation: Green card holders and recent temporary visa holders may face cultural barriers in the US education system. Provide cultural orientation programs that help them understand the expectations and norms of US schools, as well as the importance of academic integrity and time management.
3. Connect them with resources: Introduce green card holders and recent temporary visa holders to resources on campus, such as tutoring services, academic advisors, and mental health counselors. Encourage them to take advantage of these resources to help them succeed academically.
4. Foster a sense of community: Create opportunities for green card holders and recent temporary visa holders to connect with other students who may be facing similar challenges. This can help them feel less isolated and more supported in their academic journey.
5. Provide financial support: Many green card holders and recent temporary visa holders may face financial barriers that can hinder their academic success. Consider offering scholarships or financial aid specifically for these students to help them cover the costs of tuition, books, and living expenses.
6. Advocate on their behalf: Work with university administrators and faculty to ensure that green card holders and recent temporary visa holders are treated fairly and have access to the same opportunities as other students. This may involve advocating for policies that support these students, such as extended deadlines or flexible grading systems.
7. Offer mentorship: Pair green card holders and recent temporary visa holders with experienced student mentors who can provide guidance and support in navigating the US education system.
8. Celebrate diversity: Embrace the cultural diversity of these students and create an inclusive environment where they feel valued and respected. This can help them feel more comfortable and confident in their academic journey.
