Supporting the economic empowerment of unemployed ethnically full African Americans requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the systemic barriers and structural inequalities they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to job training programs, vocational schools, and entrepreneurship courses that cater to the needs of African Americans. This can include skills development in high-demand fields such as technology, healthcare, and finance.
2. Job Placement Services: Offer job placement services that connect unemployed African Americans with job openings at companies that are committed to diversity and inclusion. This can include mentorship programs, networking events, and career fairs.
3. Small Business Development: Encourage and support the growth of small businesses owned by ethnically full African Americans through access to capital, training, and resources. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities in underserved communities.
4. Entrepreneurship Support: Provide resources and support for entrepreneurs who are ethnically full African Americans, including access to funding, mentorship, and networking opportunities.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies that address systemic barriers to economic empowerment faced by African Americans, such as discrimination, unequal access to education and job training, and limited access to capital. This can include advocating for policies that promote diversity and inclusion in hiring practices, contracting, and procurement.
6. Community Development: Invest in community development initiatives that create jobs, improve infrastructure, and promote economic growth in underserved communities. This can include funding for community-based organizations, microfinance programs, and economic development projects.
7. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Provide mentorship and leadership development opportunities for unemployed ethnically full African Americans, including training and support for career advancement and entrepreneurial ventures.
8. Addressing Criminal Justice Issues: Address the disproportionate representation of African Americans in the criminal justice system by advocating for policy changes that reduce recidivism and improve re-entry outcomes. This can include job training programs, mental health services, and support for re-entry housing initiatives
