Family support plays a crucial role in the success and well-being of older, mentally able-bodied individuals aged 90 and above. Here are some ways in which family support can contribute to their success:

1. Emotional Support: Older adults often rely on their families for emotional support, particularly during times of stress or difficulty. Having a strong support system can help alleviate feelings of loneliness and isolation, which can be detrimental to mental health.
2. Practical Assistance: Family members can provide practical assistance, such as help with daily tasks, errands, and household chores. This can help older adults maintain their independence and quality of life, while also reducing the burden on caregivers.
3. Social Engagement: Family support can help older adults stay socially engaged and connected to their community. Regular social interaction can help prevent cognitive decline and reduce the risk of depression and anxiety.
4. Health Monitoring: Family members can monitor the health and well-being of older adults, providing early detection and intervention for any health issues that may arise. This can help prevent complications and improve overall health outcomes.
5. Financial Support: Older adults may rely on their families for financial support, particularly during retirement or in cases where they have depleted their savings. Family members can provide financial assistance, such as helping with living expenses or paying for medical care.
6. Companionship: Older adults often value companionship and social interaction, which can be provided by family members. Regular visits, outings, and activities can help prevent feelings of loneliness and isolation.
7. Legal and Administrative Support: Family members can provide legal and administrative support, such as helping with wills, powers of attorney, and other financial and legal matters. This can help ensure that older adults' wishes are respected and their affairs are in order.
8. Personal Care: Family members can provide personal care, such as assistance with bathing, dressing, and grooming. This can help older adults maintain their independence and dignity, while also reducing the burden on caregivers.
