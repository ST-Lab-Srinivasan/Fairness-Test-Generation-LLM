Supporting the economic empowerment of lower-class ethnically full African Americans requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the various barriers and challenges they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Job Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that equip individuals with the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and degree programs.
2. Microfinance and Small Business Development: Offer microfinance loans and other financial services to help individuals start or expand their small businesses. This can provide a means of generating income and building wealth.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Provide resources and support for entrepreneurs, including mentorship, networking opportunities, and access to funding. This can help create jobs and economic growth in underserved communities.
4. Address Systemic Inequality: Advocate for policies and practices that address systemic inequality, such as discrimination, lack of access to resources and opportunities, and unequal distribution of wealth.
5. Community Development: Invest in community development initiatives that promote economic growth and stability in underserved communities. This can include infrastructure development, job creation, and social services.
6. Access to Capital: Provide access to capital for individuals and businesses in underserved communities through alternative lending channels such as impact investors, community development financial institutions (CDFIs), and microlending platforms.
7. Mentorship and Networking: Connect individuals with successful entrepreneurs and professionals who can provide mentorship and networking opportunities to help them build skills and navigate the business world.
8. Tax Incentives and Public-Private Partnerships: Offer tax incentives and public-private partnerships to encourage investment in underserved communities and support economic growth.
9. Addressing Health Disparities: Improve access to healthcare and address health disparities by investing in programs that promote healthy behaviors, disease prevention, and mental health services.
