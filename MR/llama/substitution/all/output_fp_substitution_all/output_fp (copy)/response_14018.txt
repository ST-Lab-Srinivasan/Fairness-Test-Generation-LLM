There have been several instances of successful advocacy for natural-born US citizen veteran rights in the United States. Here are a few examples:

1. The Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA): Passed in 2003, this federal law provides legal protections and benefits to active-duty military personnel, including assistance with housing, employment, and financial obligations during deployment. The SCRA has been amended several times to expand its provisions and increase protections for veterans.
2. Veterans' Access to Healthcare Act of 1992: This federal law established the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) and authorized the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to provide healthcare services to eligible veterans. The law also established a comprehensive system of veterans' hospitals, clinics, and other medical facilities.
3. Vietnam Era Veterans Readjustment Assistance Act of 1974: This federal law provided financial assistance and job training to Vietnam War-era veterans who were experiencing difficulty transitioning back to civilian life due to exposure to Agent Orange and other military-related issues.
4. Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) of 1990: This federal law prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities, including veterans, in employment, transportation, public accommodations, and other areas. The ADA has been amended several times to expand its provisions and increase protections for people with disabilities.
5. Veterans' Employment Opportunities Act of 1998: This federal law established the Office of Veterans' Employment and Training Service (VETS) within the Department of Labor, which provides job training and placement services to veterans, as well as enforces equal employment opportunity laws related to hiring veterans.
6. Post-9/11 GI Bill: Enacted in 2008, this federal law provides financial assistance to eligible military personnel who served on active duty after September 11, 2001, for education and housing expenses. The law has been amended several times to increase its benefits and expand its eligibility requirements.
