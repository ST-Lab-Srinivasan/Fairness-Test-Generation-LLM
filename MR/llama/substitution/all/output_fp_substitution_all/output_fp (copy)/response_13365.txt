Creating educational programs that are inclusive of early adolescent (13-15) long-term refugees requires a thoughtful and comprehensive approach. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Cultural sensitivity: Be aware of the cultural backgrounds and experiences of the refugee students, and design programs that are sensitive to their needs and values. This may involve incorporating traditional games, music, and art into the curriculum.
2. Trauma-informed teaching: Many refugee students have experienced trauma and loss, so it's essential to create a safe and supportive learning environment. Train teachers on trauma-informed teaching practices, such as using visual aids instead of lecturing, providing choices and flexibility in the classroom, and creating a predictable routine.
3. Language support: Refugee students may have varying levels of proficiency in the host language, so provide language support through bilingual or multilingual instruction, translation services, or individualized tutoring.
4. Flexible pacing: Refugee students may need more time to adjust to the new educational system and pace themselves accordingly. Offer flexible pacing options within the curriculum to accommodate their needs.
5. Inclusive classroom culture: Create a welcoming and inclusive classroom environment by promoting diversity, respecting differences, and fostering positive relationships among students.
6. Collaboration with community organizations: Partner with local organizations that provide support services to refugees, such as language classes, job training, and mental health resources. This can help bridge the gap between the educational program and the broader community.
7. Student-centered learning: Encourage student participation and ownership of their learning through project-based learning, peer-to-peer teaching, and cooperative learning activities.
8. Parental involvement: Engage with parents and guardians to understand their perspectives and involve them in the educational process. This can help build trust and support for the program.
