Supporting the social inclusion of recent temporary visa holders to the US, particularly those who are newly divorced, can be challenging due to cultural and language barriers. However, here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Language classes: Providing language classes or connecting them with local language schools can help them improve their English skills, which is essential for social integration.
2. Cultural orientation: Organizing cultural orientation programs or workshops can help them understand the local customs and values, making it easier to navigate everyday situations.
3. Social events: Hosting social events, such as potluck dinners, movie nights, or game nights, can provide opportunities for them to meet new people and build relationships.
4. Volunteer work: Encouraging them to volunteer in their communities can help them connect with local residents and build a sense of purpose.
5. Job training programs: Offering job training programs or connecting them with local businesses can help them gain employment skills and find jobs, which can improve their overall well-being and social inclusion.
6. Legal support: Providing legal support, such as assistance with obtaining a Social Security number or driver's license, can help them navigate the complex US legal system.
7. Childcare support: Offering childcare services or connecting them with local daycares can help them manage their childcare needs while they focus on finding employment or building social connections.
8. Mental health support: Providing mental health resources, such as counseling or therapy sessions, can help them cope with the stress of relocation and build resilience.
9. Community outings: Organizing community outings, such as trips to local parks or beaches, can provide opportunities for them to explore their surroundings and build social connections.
10. Peer support groups: Facilitating peer support groups, either through local organizations or online communities, can provide a safe space for them to connect with others who are going through similar experiences.
11. Financial assistance: Offering financial assistance, such as help with rent or utility bills, can help alleviate some of the financial stress they may face during their transition to the US.
