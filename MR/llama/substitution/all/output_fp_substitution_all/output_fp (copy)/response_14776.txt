Promoting the financial stability of very elderly (90s+) beginner English speakers requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Financial Literacy Programs: Offer free or low-cost financial literacy programs specifically designed for this age group. These programs can help them understand basic financial concepts, such as budgeting, saving, and investing.
2. Investment Planning: Encourage these individuals to work with a financial advisor to create an investment plan that aligns with their financial goals and risk tolerance. This may include investing in income-generating assets, such as dividend-paying stocks or rental properties, to supplement their retirement income.
3. Social Security Planning: Help them maximize their Social Security benefits by understanding the complex rules surrounding claiming strategies. Encourage them to delay claiming Social Security until they reach their full retirement age, when possible, to increase their monthly benefits.
4. Insurance Planning: Ensure they have adequate insurance coverage, including life insurance, long-term care insurance, and disability income insurance. These policies can help protect their financial well-being in the event of unexpected events or illnesses.
5. Housing Planning: Help them assess their housing needs and explore options for downsizing or right-sizing their living situation. This may involve selling their current home and purchasing a smaller, more affordable property, or exploring alternative housing arrangements such as assisted living or shared housing.
6. Income Stream Planning: Encourage them to create multiple income streams to replace their retirement income. This may include starting a side hustle, investing in dividend-paying stocks, or pursuing other income-generating activities.
7. Estate Planning: Help them navigate the complexities of estate planning, including wills, trusts, and probate. Ensure they understand how to protect their assets and provide for their loved ones after they pass away.
