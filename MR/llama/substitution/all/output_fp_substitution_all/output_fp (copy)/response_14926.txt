Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of mixed Asian heritage non-independent individuals. Cultural heritage refers to the shared beliefs, values, customs, and traditions that are passed down from generation to generation within a particular cultural or ethnic group. For individuals with mixed Asian heritage, their cultural heritage is likely to be complex and multifaceted, comprising elements from various cultures and backgrounds.

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a particular group or community. For mixed Asian heritage individuals, their cultural heritage may help them feel connected to one or more cultural groups, which can be an important aspect of their identity.
2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage influences how individuals form their identities. Mixed Asian heritage individuals may draw on elements from their various cultural backgrounds to construct their sense of self. For example, they may identify as both Asian and American, or they may feel connected to a specific ethnic group within the Asian diaspora.
3. Cultural practices: Participation in cultural practices such as festivals, rituals, and traditions can help individuals with mixed Asian heritage connect with their cultural roots. These practices can also provide a sense of belonging and identity formation.
4. Intersectionality: For mixed Asian heritage individuals, their cultural heritage may intersect with other aspects of their identity, such as race, gender, or sexuality. This intersectionality can influence how they navigate their identities and how they are perceived by others.
5. Internalized oppression: Mixed Asian heritage individuals may internalize oppressive attitudes and behaviors from the dominant culture, which can impact their sense of identity and well-being.
