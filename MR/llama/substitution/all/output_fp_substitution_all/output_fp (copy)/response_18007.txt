Creating more inclusive cultural programs for expectant parents who are not disabled requires thoughtful planning and consideration. Here are some strategies to make your cultural programs more accessible and welcoming:

1. Accessibility Features: Ensure that the cultural space is wheelchair-accessible, has accessible bathrooms, and is free of physical barriers. Provide audio descriptions or Braille signage for visually impaired individuals.
2. Accommodating Expectant Parents: Offer flexible seating options, such as cushions or pillows, for expectant parents who may need extra comfort during the program. Provide nursing areas or designated break spaces for feeding and changing babies.
3. Inclusive Marketing: Use language in your marketing materials that is inclusive and welcoming to all audiences, including those with disabilities. Ensure that your website and social media platforms are accessible and easy to navigate.
4. Sensory-Friendly Environments: Create a sensory-friendly environment by reducing noise levels, using dim lighting, and providing fidget toys or other sensory tools for individuals who may benefit from them.
5. Sign Language Interpretation: Provide sign language interpretation for Deaf or hard of hearing individuals. Ensure that your staff is trained in basic sign language to facilitate communication.
6. Communication Supports: Offer communication supports, such as Large Print programs, braille materials, or audio recordings, for individuals with sensory processing difficulties or learning disabilities.
7. Adaptive Technology: Provide adaptive technology, such as accessible touch screens or assistive devices, to support individuals with mobility or dexterity impairments.
8. Inclusive Programming: Develop programs that are inclusive and welcoming to all participants, including those with disabilities. Ensure that your programs are age-appropriate and tailored to the needs of expectant parents.
9. Partnering with Disability Organizations: Collaborate with local disability organizations to reach a wider audience and ensure that your cultural programs are accessible and inclusive for all.
