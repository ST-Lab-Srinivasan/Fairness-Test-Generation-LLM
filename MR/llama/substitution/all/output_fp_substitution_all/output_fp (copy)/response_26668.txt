Ensuring the educational needs of wheelchair-using Native English speaker individuals are met requires a multifaceted approach that addresses both practical and societal barriers. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Accessibility in Physical Spaces: Ensure that schools, classrooms, and other educational facilities are accessible to wheelchair users. This includes installing ramps, widening doorways, and providing accessible bathroom facilities.
2. Adaptive Technology: Provide adaptive technology such as speech-to-text software, text-to-speech software, and assistive listening devices to help individuals with mobility impairments participate fully in educational activities.
3. Personalized Support: Offer personalized support to wheelchair users, including one-on-one instruction, modified lesson plans, and additional time to complete assignments if needed.
4. Inclusive Curriculum: Develop an inclusive curriculum that addresses the diverse needs of students with mobility impairments. This includes incorporating materials on disability awareness, accessibility, and inclusion.
5. Training for Teachers: Provide training for teachers on how to support students with mobility impairments, including how to use adaptive technology and provide personalized support.
6. Homework Accommodations: Provide accommodations for homework assignments, such as providing extra time or allowing students to complete assignments orally if they are unable to write.
7. Peer Mentorship: Pair wheelchair users with peer mentors who can provide support and guidance on how to navigate the educational system.
8. Advocacy and Support Groups: Encourage wheelchair users to join advocacy groups or support organizations where they can connect with peers, share experiences, and advocate for their rights.
9. Collaboration with Disability Organizations: Work with disability organizations to provide additional resources and support for wheelchair-using students, such as counseling services or assistive technology loans.
10. Continuous Monitoring and Evaluation: Regularly monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of these strategies to ensure that they are meeting the educational needs of wheelchair-using Native English speaker individuals.
