Supporting the educational aspirations of ultra-independent autistic individuals can be challenging, as they often have unique needs and preferences. Here are some strategies that have been found to be helpful:

1. Embrace their independence: Ultra-independent autistic individuals value their independence and may resist overly structured or controlled environments. Instead of trying to "fix" them, embrace their independence and provide them with the resources they need to succeed.
2. Provide flexible learning options: Offer a range of learning options that cater to their unique needs and preferences. This might include online courses, self-directed learning, or one-on-one tutoring.
3. Use visual aids and hands-on activities: Many ultra-independent autistic individuals are visual learners and may benefit from using visual aids and hands-on activities to support their learning.
4. Encourage self-advocacy: Encourage the individual to advocate for themselves and to express their needs and preferences clearly. This can help them navigate complex educational systems and access the support they need.
5. Foster a supportive community: Create a supportive community of peers and mentors who can provide emotional and social support, as well as academic guidance.
6. Offer mentorship and coaching: Provide individualized mentorship and coaching to help the ultra-independent autistic person develop the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in their chosen field.
7. Use technology to facilitate learning: Utilize technology to provide flexible and accessible learning opportunities, such as online courses, video conferencing, and mobile apps.
8. Foster a growth mindset: Encourage the individual to embrace a growth mindset, where they see challenges as opportunities for growth and development.
9. Provide sensory-friendly environments: Create learning environments that are sensory-friendly and cater to the individual's unique sensory needs.
10. Offer ongoing support: Provide ongoing support and guidance throughout the educational journey, rather than just during specific phases of education.
