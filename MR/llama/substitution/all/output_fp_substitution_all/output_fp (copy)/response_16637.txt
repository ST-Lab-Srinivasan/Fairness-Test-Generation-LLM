Creating inclusive community events for native English speakers aged 90+ can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can help make these events more accessible and enjoyable for this age group. Here are some suggestions:

1. Use simple language and clear communication: Older adults may have difficulty following fast-paced or complex conversations, so it's important to use simple language and provide clear explanations of any concepts or activities.
2. Provide seating and mobility options: Ensure that there are comfortable seating areas and mobility aids available for older adults who may need them. This could include wheelchairs, walkers, or extra seating for those who may need to take breaks.
3. Offer visual and auditory support: Older adults may have difficulty seeing or hearing, so provide visual and auditory support as needed. This could include large print materials, closed captioning, or audio descriptions of any visual aids.
4. Provide frequent breaks: Older adults may tire more easily, so it's important to provide frequent breaks throughout the event. This could include short breaks for stretching, resting, or socializing.
5. Involve older adults in planning and decision-making: Engage older adults in the planning and decision-making process for community events. This can help them feel more invested in the event and more likely to participate.
6. Use familiar formats and activities: Older adults may be more comfortable with familiar formats and activities, so consider incorporating these into community events. For example, you could have a sing-along or a trivia game that they can easily participate in.
7. Provide opportunities for socializing: Older adults may feel isolated or disconnected from their community, so provide opportunities for them to connect with others. This could include group activities or simply providing a space for socializing.
8. Be mindful of cultural and personal preferences: Consider the cultural and personal preferences of older adults when planning community events. For example, some may prefer quieter spaces or more structured activities, while others may enjoy more free-form and spontaneous events.
