Creating more inclusive cultural events for non-married, wheelchair-using individuals requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to help make your events more accessible and enjoyable for all attendees:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the event location is wheelchair-accessible and has sufficient parking and transportation options for those who may need them. Provide wheelchair-accessible seating and bathroom facilities, and make sure there are no physical barriers to movement or participation.
2. Inclusive Marketing: Use language in your marketing materials that is inclusive and respectful of all attendees, regardless of their marital status or abilities. Avoid using language that implies exclusivity or discrimination.
3. Accommodations: Offer accommodations such as sign language interpretation, audio descriptions, or braille programs for those who are deaf or hard of hearing, or provide large print materials for those with visual impairments.
4. Interactive Programming: Incorporate interactive programming that is inclusive and engaging for all attendees, regardless of their abilities. This could include tactile art activities, sensory-friendly performances, or adaptive sports demonstrations.
5. Volunteer Training: Provide training for volunteers on how to interact with and support attendees with disabilities, including wheelchair users. Make sure they are aware of the accessible features of the event location and can assist attendees as needed.
6. Flexible Seating: Offer flexible seating options that accommodate different abilities and preferences. This could include designated quiet spaces for those who may need a break from the main event, or adaptive seating options for those with mobility impairments.
7. Accessibility Feedback: Solicit feedback from attendees on the accessibility of the event, including suggestions for improvement. Use this feedback to make adjustments and improvements for future events.
