2. Emotional challenges: Living with a genetic condition can be emotionally draining, particularly when the condition affects one's appearance, cognitive abilities, or overall quality of life. Elderly adults may experience anxiety, depression, or feelings of isolation, which can impact their mental health and well-being.

Financial strain: Genetic conditions can result in significant medical expenses, including hospitalizations, medications, and therapies, which can put a strain on an individual's finances, particularly if they are no longer working. This can lead to financial difficulties and stress for the elderly adult and their family members.
4. Social isolation: Due to physical limitations or emotional challenges, elderly adults with genetic conditions may find it difficult to participate in social activities or maintain social connections, leading to feelings of loneliness and isolation.
5. Stigma and discrimination: Unfortunately, there can be a stigma attached to some genetic conditions, which can lead to social exclusion, discrimination, and marginalization of elderly adults. This can further erode their self-esteem and mental health.
