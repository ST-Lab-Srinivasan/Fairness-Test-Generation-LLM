Creating more inclusive cultural programs for non-high-income, non-able-bodied individuals involves several key considerations and strategies. Here are some ideas to get you started:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the cultural program is accessible to people of all abilities by providing accommodations such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
2. Inclusive language: Use inclusive language in promotional materials and programming to avoid perpetuating ableism and ensure that everyone feels welcome.
3. Representation: Ensure that the cultural program includes a diverse range of perspectives, voices, and experiences, including those of people with disabilities.
4. Partnerships: Collaborate with organizations that serve non-high-income, non-able-bodied communities to co-create and deliver cultural programs that are tailored to their needs and interests.
5. Adaptive technology: Utilize adaptive technology such as assistive devices, communication aids, and audio describers to ensure that everyone can participate fully in the cultural program.
6. Flexible scheduling: Offer flexible scheduling options to accommodate the diverse needs of non-high-income, non-able-bodied individuals, such as those who may have difficulty attending programs during traditional weekday hours.
7. Sensory-friendly environments: Create sensory-friendly environments by reducing noise levels, providing quiet spaces, and offering sensory-friendly materials for individuals with sensory processing challenges.
8. Inclusive marketing: Use inclusive marketing strategies to promote the cultural program to non-high-income, non-able-bodied communities, avoiding language or imagery that could be perceived as ableist.
9. Volunteer opportunities: Provide volunteer opportunities for non-high-income, non-able-bodied individuals to participate in the cultural program and contribute to its success.
10. Feedback mechanisms: Establish feedback mechanisms to gather input from non-high-income, non-able-bodied participants on how to improve the cultural program's accessibility and inclusivity.
