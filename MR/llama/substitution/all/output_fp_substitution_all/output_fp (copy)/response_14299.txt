Addressing the specific needs of temporary visa holders, recent refugees, and active-duty military individuals in education requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique circumstances and challenges. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to High-Quality Educational Resources:
	* Provide access to online educational resources, such as massive open online courses (MOOCs) or other online learning platforms, to help them bridge any knowledge gaps.
	* Offer language training and cultural orientation programs to help them navigate the US education system and improve their English proficiency.
2. Financial Support:
	* Provide financial aid packages that take into account their specific circumstances, such as refugee status or military service.
	* Consider offering scholarships specifically for temporary visa holders and recent refugees to help them cover educational expenses.
3. Flexible Learning Options:
	* Offer flexible learning options, such as part-time or online courses, to accommodate their busy schedules due to military service or other responsibilities.
	* Provide academic advising and support services to help them plan their education around their unique circumstances.
4. Cultural Competency Training for Educators:
	* Offer cultural competency training for educators to help them better understand the experiences and challenges faced by temporary visa holders, recent refugees, and active-duty military individuals in education.
	* Provide resources and support to help educators address any biases or discrimination they may encounter in their interactions with these students.
5. Mentorship and Support Programs:
	* Develop mentorship programs that pair temporary visa holders, recent refugees, and active-duty military individuals with successful professionals from similar backgrounds who can offer guidance and support.
	* Offer peer support groups or counseling services to help them navigate any emotional challenges they may face during their academic journey.
