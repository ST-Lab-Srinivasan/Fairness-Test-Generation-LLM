Supporting the economic empowerment of undocumented natural-born US citizen non-low-income individuals requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the various barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Financial Services: Undocumented immigrants often lack access to traditional banking services, which can limit their ability to save money, take out loans, or start a business. Community development financial institutions (CDFIs) and credit unions can provide alternative financial services that cater specifically to the needs of this population.
2. Financial Capacity Building: Undocumented immigrants may lack knowledge about personal finance, budgeting, and saving. Financial capacity building programs can help them develop these skills and make informed financial decisions.
3. Job Training and Placement: Many undocumented immigrants face barriers to employment due to their immigration status. Job training and placement programs can help them acquire the necessary skills to compete in the job market.
4. Small Business Development: Undocumented immigrants who cannot access traditional banking services may benefit from small business development programs that provide capital, mentorship, and technical assistance.
5. Healthcare Access: Ensuring access to affordable healthcare is crucial for undocumented immigrants, many of whom lack access to medical care due to their immigration status. Community clinics and non-profit organizations can help bridge this gap.
6. Education and Training: Providing access to education and training programs can help undocumented immigrants improve their skills and qualify for better-paying jobs. This can include vocational training, language classes, and higher education opportunities.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocating for policy changes that address the root causes of poverty and economic inequality among undocumented immigrants can help create a more inclusive and supportive environment. This can involve pushing for a clear pathway to citizenship, protecting DREAMers, and addressing systemic barriers to employment and education.
