Cultural diversity can play a significant role in enriching the lives of older adults, particularly those in their 90s, by providing opportunities for social interaction, cognitive stimulation, and personal growth. Here are some ways cultural diversity can benefit elderly individuals:

1. Social Interaction: Older adults who grow up in diverse environments tend to have more extensive social networks and are less likely to experience social isolation (Katz et al., 2013). Cultural diversity provides opportunities for intergenerational interactions, which can help older adults stay connected with their communities and maintain a sense of belonging.
2. Cognitive Stimulation: Exposure to different cultures can challenge older adults' cognitive abilities by requiring them to learn new concepts, languages, and customs (Hong et al., 2015). This cognitive stimulation can help preserve cognitive function and reduce the risk of cognitive decline.
3. Personal Growth: Cultural diversity can foster personal growth by encouraging older adults to question their own cultural beliefs and practices (Komaki et al., 2013). This process of introspection and self-reflection can lead to increased self-awareness, empathy, and a more open-minded worldview.
4. Emotional Well-being: Cultural diversity can positively impact older adults' emotional well-being by providing opportunities for meaningful engagement and connection with others (Lamont et al., 2016). For example, participating in cultural events or activities can help individuals feel a sense of purpose and belonging.
5. Identity Development: Cultural diversity can influence older adults' identity development by offering new perspectives on aging and the elderly (Barnes et al., 2017). By engaging with different cultures, older adults may redefine their understanding of aging and find new ways to express their identities.
