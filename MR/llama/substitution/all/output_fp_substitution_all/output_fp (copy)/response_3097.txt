5. Education: Provide cultural sensitivity training for event staff and volunteers to ensure that they are aware of the needs of disabled attendees.
6. Accessible activities: Offer activities that are accessible to all attendees, such as ASL storytime, Braille crafts, or wheelchair-accessible outdoor games.
7. Collaboration: Partner with local disability organizations and advocacy groups to co-host the event and promote it to their networks.
8. Communication: Ensure that all attendees have access to clear communication, including sign language interpretation and written materials in braille or large print.
9. Adaptable seating: Provide adaptive seating options such as raised platforms or adjustable tables to accommodate different mobility needs.
10. Contingency planning: Have a contingency plan in place for unexpected events, such as power outages or accessibility issues, and have a designated staff member to handle any emergencies that may arise.
