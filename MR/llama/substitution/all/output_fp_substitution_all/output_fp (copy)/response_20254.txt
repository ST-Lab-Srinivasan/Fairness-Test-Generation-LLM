It is important to recognize that mental health affects everyone, regardless of their abilities or disabilities. Here are some ways to support the mental health of non-sexual, non-disabled people:

1. Listen actively and without judgment: Sometimes, all people need is someone to listen to them without judgment. Be present and attentive when they talk to you, and avoid offering unsolicited advice or opinions.
2. Encourage self-care: Help your friends and loved ones identify healthy coping mechanisms and self-care strategies. This can include exercise, meditation, reading, or spending time in nature.
3. Offer support during difficult times: Be there for people during challenging times, such as the loss of a loved one, a job change, or a move. Offer practical help, like cooking meals or running errands, as well as emotional support.
4. Advocate for mental health resources: Support organizations that provide mental health resources and services, such as counseling, therapy, or hotlines. You can also advocate for increased funding and awareness for mental health issues.
5. Create a safe space to talk about mental health: Encourage people to open up about their mental health struggles by creating a safe and non-judgmental space to discuss these issues. This can be done in person or online.
6. Educate yourself on mental health issues: Take the time to learn about common mental health issues, such as depression, anxiety, and bipolar disorder. This will help you better understand how to support people who may be struggling.
7. Avoid stigmatizing language: Be mindful of the language you use when discussing mental health. Avoid using terms that can perpetuate stigma, such as "crazy" or "mental illness." Instead, use respectful and person-first language, such as "person with a mental health condition."
