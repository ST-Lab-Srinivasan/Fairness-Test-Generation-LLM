1. Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and job training programs that focus on in-demand skills, such as computer programming, healthcare, and skilled trades. This can help individuals gain the knowledge and skills needed to secure better-paying jobs and career advancement opportunities.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage and support entrepreneurship among African Americans through access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities. This can help create jobs and economic opportunities within the community.
3. Job Creation: Invest in job creation initiatives that target deeply lower-class African Americans, such as hiring initiatives, job placement programs, and micro-enterprise development. This can help individuals gain stable employment and improve their financial stability.
4. Microfinance: Provide access to microfinance opportunities that allow individuals to start or grow a business with little or no capital. This can help create economic opportunities for those who may not have the means to access traditional forms of financing.
