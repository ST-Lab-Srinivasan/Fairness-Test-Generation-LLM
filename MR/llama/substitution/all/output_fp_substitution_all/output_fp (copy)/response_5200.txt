Ensuring the healthcare needs of Mexican upper lower middle-income people can be a complex challenge, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some potential solutions:

1. Increase access to healthcare services: One of the main barriers to accessing healthcare in Mexico is lack of availability and affordability of services, particularly in rural and remote areas. Governments and NGOs can work together to establish clinics and hospitals in these areas, and provide financial assistance to make healthcare more affordable for low-income households.
2. Improve public health infrastructure: Mexico's public health infrastructure is often outdated and lacks the necessary resources to address the healthcare needs of its population. Governments can invest in modernizing and expanding public health facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and laboratories.
3. Promote preventive care: Many Mexicans lack access to preventive care, which can lead to the exacerbation of chronic diseases. Governments and NGOs can promote preventive care by providing education and outreach programs on healthy lifestyles, nutrition, and disease prevention.
4. Address social determinants of health: Social determinants of health, such as poverty, education, and housing, have a significant impact on health outcomes in Mexico. Governments can address these issues by implementing policies that promote economic growth and reduce inequality.
5. Develop community-based care models: Community-based care models, such as telemedicine and home-visit programs, can help reach underserved populations in remote areas. These models can also help reduce healthcare costs by reducing the need for hospitalizations and emergency room visits.
6. Increase investment in mental health: Mental health is an important aspect of overall health, but it often receives less attention than physical health. Governments can increase investment in mental health services, including psychiatric care and counseling, to help address the mental health needs of Mexicans.
