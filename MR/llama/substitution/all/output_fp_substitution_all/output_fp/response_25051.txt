The impact of immigration status on the lives of permanent residents can be significant and far-reaching. Here are some of the potential impacts:

1. Limited access to healthcare: Permanent residents may face barriers in accessing healthcare services, including delays in receiving medical treatment or being unable to afford it due to high out-of-pocket costs.
2. Difficulty obtaining employment: Permanent residents may encounter challenges when looking for work, such as discrimination based on their immigration status, difficulties in obtaining a social insurance number, or restrictions on the types of jobs they can hold.
3. Limited access to education: Permanent residents may face obstacles when pursuing higher education, including difficulty accessing financial aid or scholarships due to their immigration status.
4. Social isolation: Permanent residents may experience social isolation due to their limited access to social services and networks, which can impact their mental health and overall well-being.
5. Lack of political representation: Permanent residents may have limited political representation and voice in the decision-making processes that affect their lives.
6. Limited access to government services: Permanent residents may encounter barriers when accessing government services, such as difficulty obtaining a driver's license or registering to vote due to their immigration status.
7. Discrimination and stigma: Permanent residents may experience discrimination and stigma based on their immigration status, which can impact their mental health and overall well-being.
8. Limited economic opportunities: Permanent residents may face limitations in their ability to start a business or invest in property due to their immigration status.
9. Lack of security: Permanent residents may experience uncertainty and insecurity due to the potential for changes in immigration policies and laws that could impact their status.
10. Limited social mobility: Permanent residents may face challenges when trying to move up the social ladder, such as difficulty accessing better-paying jobs or obtaining access to education and training opportunities.

It is important to note that the specific impacts of immigration status on permanent residents can vary depending on their individual circumstances and the laws and policies in place in their country of residence.
