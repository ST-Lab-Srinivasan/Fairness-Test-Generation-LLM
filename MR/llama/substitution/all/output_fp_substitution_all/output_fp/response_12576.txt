Ensuring the legal rights of active duty military physically disabled individuals is crucial to provide them with equal opportunities and protections under the law. Here are some ways to protect their legal rights:

1. Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA): This provision prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities, including those in the military, in employment, transportation, public accommodations, and other areas.
2. Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973: This law prohibits federal agencies from excluding or denying individuals with disabilities participation in programs or activities that receive federal financial assistance.
3. The Military Ready Reserve Act: This act provides for the reemployment of military personnel who are injured while on active duty and requires federal agencies to provide reasonable accommodations for employees with disabilities.
4. The Vietnam Era Veterans' Readjustment Assistance Act (VEVRAA): This law prohibits federal agencies from discriminating against veterans based on their status as a veteran, including those who are disabled as a result of their military service.
5. The Affordable Care Act (ACA): This law requires health insurance plans to provide coverage for individuals with disabilities, including those related to military service.
6. Military and Veterans' Health Services: The Department of Defense (DoD) and the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) provide a range of healthcare services to active duty military personnel and veterans, including those who are physically disabled.
7. Disability Rights Education and Protection Act (DREPA): This law provides for the protection and advocacy of the rights of individuals with disabilities, including those in the military.
8. The Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA): This law protects the employment rights of members of the uniformed services, including those who are physically disabled, by providing for reemployment and protection against discrimination based on military status.
9. The Equal Access to Justice Act: This law provides for the payment of attorney's fees and other expenses incurred in connection with legal proceedings related to disability discrimination, including those involving active duty military personnel.
