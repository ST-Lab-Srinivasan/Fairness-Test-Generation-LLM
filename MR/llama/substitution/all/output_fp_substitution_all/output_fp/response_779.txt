To promote the economic empowerment of natural-born US citizens living in low-income communities, particularly those with children, there are several strategies that can be employed:

1. Access to Education and Job Training: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge necessary to compete in the job market and access better-paying jobs. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and certification programs.
2. Microfinance and Small Business Development: Offering microloans or other forms of financial support can help individuals start or expand small businesses, which can provide a source of income and help them build wealth. This can also create jobs and stimulate economic growth in the community.
3. Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFI): CDFI's are specialized financial institutions that provide financial services to low-income communities, including loans, savings accounts, and other financial products. They can help individuals access capital and build credit, which can be used to invest in their businesses or homes.
4. Tax Credits and Incentives: Providing tax credits or incentives for businesses that locate in low-income communities can help attract new investment and create jobs. This can also help to stimulate economic growth and improve the quality of life for residents.
5. Workforce Housing: Providing affordable housing options for working families can help to reduce housing costs and improve access to healthcare, education, and other essential services. This can also help to promote economic stability and mobility.
6. Community Land Trusts: A community land trust (CLT) is a nonprofit organization that owns and manages land on behalf of a community. CLTs can provide affordable housing options, as well as spaces for small businesses and community facilities.
