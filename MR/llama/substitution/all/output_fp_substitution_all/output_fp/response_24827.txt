2. 4. Lack of representation and voice: Non-independent individuals with disabilities may feel underrepresented or voiceless in society, as they may be absent from decision-making processes or lack opportunities for advocacy.
5. Inadequate healthcare: Disabled individuals may encounter difficulties when seeking medical care, as they may face barriers such as inaccessible facilities or a lack of knowledge or understanding among healthcare providers.
6. Limited financial resources: Non-independent individuals with disabilities may face financial challenges due to higher costs associated with disability, such as assistive technology or adaptive clothing, leading to decreased economic security and independence.
7. Lack of personal autonomy: Disabled individuals may feel constrained by societal expectations and stereotypes, leading to a lack of personal autonomy and agency in their daily lives.
