Long-term military spouses, who have been in a support role for their partner's service, often face unique challenges when transitioning back to civilian life. These individuals possess valuable leadership skills honed during their time supporting their partner's career, but may need additional support to fully integrate into civilian society. Here are some ways to support the leadership development of long-term recently married combat retirees and civilians:

1. Leadership training programs: Offer structured leadership training courses specifically tailored for military spouses, focusing on adaptability, communication, problem-solving, and strategic thinking. These programs can help them build on their existing skills and gain confidence in their ability to lead.
2. Mentorship opportunities: Pair long-term military spouses with successful civilian professionals who have navigated similar transitions. This mentorship can provide guidance, support, and valuable insights into the challenges of civilian life and help them build a professional network.
3. Professional certification programs: Encourage long-term military spouses to pursue professional certifications in their chosen field. This will not only enhance their skillset but also demonstrate their commitment to personal and professional growth.
4. Networking events: Organize regular networking events, either in-person or virtual, that bring together long-term military spouses with successful professionals from various industries. These events can provide valuable opportunities for connections, learning, and collaboration.
5. Job shadowing and internships: Offer job shadowing and internship opportunities within organizations to give long-term military spouses a taste of the civilian workforce. This will help them gain practical experience and build connections within their desired field.
6. Entrepreneurship support: Provide resources and mentorship for long-term military spouses who are interested in starting their own businesses. This can include access to funding, business planning guidance, and networking opportunities with successful entrepreneurs.
7. Volunteer work: Encourage long-term military spouses to engage in volunteer work within their communities. This will not only provide an opportunity to give back but also help them develop new skills, build relationships, and gain visibility within their chosen field.
