Supporting the social inclusion of Nigerian non-independent people requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the various barriers and challenges they face. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Education and Empowerment: Provide access to quality education, vocational training, and life skills development programs to help them acquire the knowledge and skills needed to participate fully in society.
2. Healthcare and Rehabilitation: Ensure that they have access to quality healthcare services, including rehabilitation programs for those with disabilities.
3. Economic Empowerment: Provide opportunities for them to engage in income-generating activities, such as microfinance loans, entrepreneurship training, and job placement services.
4. Advocacy and Awareness: Raise awareness about the issues faced by non-independent Nigerians and advocate for their rights through various channels, including media campaigns, community outreach programs, and policy engagement.
5. Inclusive Governance: Work with government agencies and civil society organizations to promote policies and practices that support the social inclusion of non-independent Nigerians, such as inclusive voting systems and representative governance structures.
6. Community Building: Facilitate community building initiatives that bring together non-independent Nigerians with their peers and other members of society, fostering mutual understanding, empathy, and social connection.
7. Access to Technology: Provide access to technology, such as smartphones, laptops, and internet connectivity, to help them stay connected with their communities, access information, and participate in the digital economy.
8. Mentorship and Leadership Development: Pair them with mentors who can provide guidance, support, and role models for leadership and personal development.
9. Addressing Stigma and Discrimination: Work to address stigmatization and discrimination against non-independent Nigerians through awareness campaigns, community engagement, and advocacy efforts.
10. Collaboration with Other Organizations: Collaborate with other organizations, including government agencies, civil society groups, and private sector companies, to leverage resources, expertise, and networks in support of social inclusion initiatives.
