Celebrating the cultural contributions of recent Native American refugees to the US can be done in various ways. Here are some suggestions:

1. Cultural Festivals and Events: Organize cultural festivals and events that showcase the traditional music, dance, art, and food of the Native American community. This will provide a platform for them to share their culture with the broader society.
2. Exhibitions and Galleries: Host exhibitions and galleries that display the artwork and craftsmanship of Native American refugees. This can help raise awareness about their cultural heritage and provide a space for them to sell their art.
3. Storytelling and Oral History: Record and share the stories and oral histories of Native American refugees. This can help preserve their culture and traditions, and provide a window into their experiences.
4. Language Preservation: Support the preservation of Native American languages by providing language classes, dictionaries, and other resources. This can help ensure that the cultural heritage of these communities is passed down to future generations.
5. Community Outreach and Involvement: Engage with local communities and involve them in cultural events and activities. This can help build bridges between different cultures and promote cross-cultural understanding and respect.
6. Education and Workshops: Offer educational workshops and classes that teach traditional Native American skills, such as beading, weaving, and pottery making. This can provide an opportunity for refugees to learn new skills and earn a living while preserving their cultural heritage.
7. Collaborations with Other Organizations: Partner with other organizations and institutions to promote the cultural contributions of Native American refugees. This can help amplify their voices and increase awareness about their experiences.
8. Documentation and Research: Conduct research and document the cultural contributions of Native American refugees. This can help preserve their history and provide a valuable resource for future generations.
9. Artist Residencies: Provide artist residencies for Native American refugees, which can give them an opportunity to focus on their artistic practices and share their work with a broader audience.
10. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair experienced Native American artists and cultural practitioners with newer arrivals.
