5. Emotional support: Mentors can offer emotional support during challenging times, providing a listening ear and words of encouragement. This can be particularly important for young people from marginalized communities who may face unique obstacles related to their race or cultural background.
6. Exposure to new perspectives: A mentor from a different cultural background can provide a fresh perspective on the world, helping their mentee broaden their understanding of diverse cultures and experiences. This exposure can foster empathy and tolerance, essential for creating a more inclusive society.
