Undocumented immigrants, also known as unauthorized or illegal immigrants, are individuals who reside in a country without proper documentation or without being recognized as refugees or asylum seekers. The impact of immigration status on the lives of undocumented native English speaker people can be significant and far-reaching, affecting various aspects of their well-being, including:

1. Fear of deportation: The constant threat of deportation can cause significant stress, anxiety, and fear among undocumented immigrants, particularly those with families and loved ones who are also undocumented. This fear can lead to feelings of isolation and disconnection from society.
2. Limited access to healthcare: Undocumented immigrants often face barriers when seeking medical care due to their immigration status. They may be reluctant to seek necessary medical attention, leading to delayed diagnosis and treatment of illnesses or injuries.
3. Difficulty finding employment: Undocumented immigrants are often excluded from many job opportunities due to their immigration status. This can lead to limited career advancement prospects and lower earning potential compared to their documented counterparts.
4. Inability to access financial services: Many banks and financial institutions may be hesitant to provide services to undocumented immigrants, making it challenging for them to open bank accounts, obtain loans, or send money to family members.
5. Limited access to education: Undocumented immigrants may face challenges when pursuing higher education due to their immigration status. They may be ineligible for financial aid, scholarships, or other forms of support.
6. Social isolation: The stigma surrounding undocumented immigration can lead to social isolation and a lack of connection with the broader community. This can exacerbate feelings of loneliness and disconnection from society.
7. Mental health concerns: The stress and uncertainty associated with living without legal status can take a significant toll on mental health, leading to conditions such as depression, anxiety, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).
