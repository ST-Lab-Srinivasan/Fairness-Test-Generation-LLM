1. Language classes and cultural orientation: Provide language classes and cultural orientation programs to help them learn the local language and customs, which can help them connect with the community better.
2. Community events and activities: Organize community events and activities that promote interaction between immigrants and the local population, such as festivals, workshops, and volunteer opportunities.
3. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair deep in poverty immigrant individuals with local volunteers who can provide guidance and support in navigating the host society.
4. Access to resources and services: Ensure access to essential resources and services, such as healthcare, education, and legal aid, which can help them overcome barriers to community engagement.

7. Economic empowerment programs: Provide economic empowerment programs, such as microfinance initiatives, job training, and entrepreneurship support, to help them gain financial stability and independence.
8. Building intergenerational connections: Encourage intergenerational connections between immigrant communities and the local population by organizing events and activities that bring together people of different ages and backgrounds.
