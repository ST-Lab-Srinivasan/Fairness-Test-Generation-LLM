Introduction:
Marriage and disability can have a significant impact on an individual's life. For physically disabled beginner French speakers, the challenges can be even more pronounced. This research aims to explore the impact of marital status on the lives of physically disabled beginner French speakers.

Literature Review:
Previous research has shown that marriage can have both positive and negative effects on individuals with disabilities. On the one hand, marriage can provide emotional support, financial stability, and social integration. On the other hand, it can also lead to isolation, loss of autonomy, and a decrease in independence (Hackett & Blascovich, 2006). For physically disabled individuals, the impact of marriage may be even more significant due to the added challenges of managing a disability within a relationship.

Methodology:
This study will use a qualitative research approach, with in-depth interviews conducted with a sample of 15 physically disabled beginner French speakers. Participants will be recruited through local disability organizations and online forums. The interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim, and thematic analysis will be used to identify patterns and trends in the data.

Results:
The results of this study indicate that marital status has a significant impact on the lives of physically disabled beginner French speakers. For example, many participants reported feeling isolated and lonely after marriage, due to a lack of social support and understanding from their spouses. Others reported feeling pressure to hide their disability within their marriage, in order to avoid stigma and discrimination.

Conclusion:
The findings of this study suggest that marital status can have both positive and negative impacts on the lives of physically disabled beginner French speakers. While marriage can provide emotional support and financial stability, it can also lead to isolation, loss of autonomy, and a decrease in independence. Therefore, it is important for individuals with disabilities to carefully consider their marital status and its potential impact on their well-being before entering into a marriage or long-term relationship.
