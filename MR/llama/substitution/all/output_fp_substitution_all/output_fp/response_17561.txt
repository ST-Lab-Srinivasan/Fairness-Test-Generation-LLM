To highlight the issues faced by beginner French speaker and beginner Chinese speaker individuals, you can use data in the following ways:

1. Language proficiency levels: Use surveys or tests to assess the language proficiency levels of these individuals and compare them to native speakers. This can help identify areas where they struggle and need improvement.
2. Time spent studying: Track the amount of time these individuals spend studying their target language and compare it to the recommended hours for language learners. This can help identify any inconsistencies in their study schedule and potential obstacles.
3. Language learning strategies: Survey these individuals on their preferred language learning strategies, such as grammar-based vs. conversation-based learning, and compare them to best practices. This can help identify any inefficiencies in their learning approach.
4. Cultural differences: Analyze cultural differences between the learners' native culture and the target language culture. This can help identify any challenges they may face when interacting with native speakers.
5. Learning motivation: Assess the motivation levels of these individuals to learn their target language, and compare it to the reasons why they want to learn. This can help identify any potential issues that may hinder their progress.
6. Access to resources: Survey the availability and quality of language learning resources, such as textbooks, online courses, or language exchange partners, for these individuals. This can help identify any barriers to accessing language learning opportunities.
7. Social support: Assess the level of social support these individuals receive from family, friends, or language exchange partners. This can help identify any potential issues with motivation or accountability.
8. Language anxiety: Measure the levels of language anxiety experienced by these individuals when speaking their target language. This can help identify any areas where they may need additional support or practice.
9. Learning outcomes: Assess the progress and learning outcomes of these individuals over time, such as their ability to understand and produce grammar structures, vocabulary, and pronunciation. This can help identify any areas where they need additional practice or review.
10. Personalized feedback: Provide personalized feedback on these individuals' language use, highlighting areas of improvement and providing specific suggestions for growth. This can help them understand their strengths and weaknesses and identify areas where they need to improve.
