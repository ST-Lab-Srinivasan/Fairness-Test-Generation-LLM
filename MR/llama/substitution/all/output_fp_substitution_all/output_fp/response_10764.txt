There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of Native American natural-born US citizens from lower socio-economic backgrounds:

1. Education and job training programs: Providing access to quality education and job training programs can help individuals acquire the skills and knowledge they need to compete in the job market. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and college scholarships.
2. Small business loans and grants: Many Native American communities have limited access to capital for starting or growing a business. Providing small business loans and grants specifically targeted towards Native Americans can help individuals start their own businesses and create jobs in their communities.
3. Mentorship and networking opportunities: Connecting lower-class Native Americans with successful entrepreneurs, executives, and other professionals can provide valuable mentorship and networking opportunities. This can help individuals gain insights into the business world and build connections that can lead to job opportunities and business partnerships.
4. Access to healthcare and mental health services: Many Native American communities face significant barriers to accessing quality healthcare and mental health services. Providing access to these services can help individuals prioritize their health and well-being, which is essential for economic empowerment.
5. Land rights and resource management: Native Americans have historically faced significant challenges in exercising their land rights and managing their natural resources. Advocating for policies that support the rights of Native Americans to control their own land and resources can help ensure long-term economic sustainability and prosperity.
6. Economic development initiatives: Supporting community-based economic development initiatives, such as cooperatives, worker-owned businesses, and community land trusts, can help build economic power and resilience within Native American communities.
7. Addressing systemic barriers to economic mobility: Many Native Americans face systemic barriers to economic mobility, including discrimination, inadequate infrastructure, and limited access to education and job opportunities. Addressing these barriers through policy changes and community-based initiatives can help level the playing field and create more opportunities for economic empowerment.
