Ensuring that the educational needs of non-relationship physically disabled students are met requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the individual's unique challenges and provides appropriate accommodations. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Identify and assess the student's needs: Conduct a thorough assessment of the student's abilities, strengths, and challenges to determine the accommodations they require. This may involve consulting with parents, teachers, and other educational professionals.
2. Develop an Individualized Education Plan (IEP): Based on the assessment results, create a tailored IEP that outlines the student's learning goals, objectives, and strategies for success. The IEP should also include accommodations and modifications to help the student access the curriculum.
3. Provide adaptive technology: Utilize assistive technology devices such as text-to-speech software, speech-to-text software, and other adaptive tools that can help the student navigate the digital environment and complete assignments.
4. Offer modified assignments and assessments: Modify assignments and assessments to make them more accessible for the student, such as providing extra time to complete tasks or using visual aids to support learning.
5. Provide additional support: Offer additional support services, such as occupational therapy or speech therapy, to help the student develop the skills they need to succeed in school.
6. Foster an inclusive classroom environment: Create a welcoming and inclusive classroom environment that promotes respect and understanding among all students. Encourage peer-to-peer support and collaboration to help the non-relationship physically disabled student feel included and valued.
7. Offer extracurricular opportunities: Provide extracurricular activities and clubs that cater to the student's interests and abilities, such as adaptive sports teams or creative arts groups. This can help the student develop social skills and build relationships with peers who share similar experiences.
8. Encourage self-advocacy: Teach the student how to advocate for themselves by expressing their needs and preferences clearly and respectfully. Encourage them to take ownership of their learning and to seek help when needed.
