2. Engage with Elected Officials: Meet with local, state, and federal elected officials to advocate for policies that benefit lower-class seniors. Provide them with personal stories and data to illustrate the challenges they face and the impact of policy changes on their lives.
3. Build Partnerships: Collaborate with other organizations, such as non-profits, community groups, and religious organizations, to amplify your message and reach a wider audience. Work together to organize events, campaigns, and advocacy efforts.
4. Utilize Media: Leverage media outlets, including local newspapers, radio stations, and television networks, to raise awareness about the issues faced by lower-class seniors. Use press releases, op-eds, and interviews to bring attention to their plight.
5. Develop a Social Media Campaign: Utilize social media platforms, such as Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram, to amplify your message and reach a wider audience. Create engaging content, including infographics, videos, and personal stories, to illustrate the challenges faced by lower-class seniors.

Provide Education and Resources: Offer educational resources, such as workshops, webinars, and informational materials, to help lower-class seniors understand their rights and how to access the resources they need. Provide practical support, such as transportation services, meal delivery, and home repair assistance, to help them navigate the challenges of aging.
7. Advocate for Systemic Change: Work towards systemic change by advocating for policies that address the root causes of poverty and inequality among seniors.
