Promoting the artistic talents of beginner Chinese speakers in their 90s or older can be a rewarding and fulfilling experience. Here are some suggestions on how to do so:

1. Encourage them to explore different forms of creative expression: Offer a variety of art forms such as painting, drawing, writing, music, dance, or even video production. This will help them find their passion and strengths, and provide an outlet for self-expression.
2. Provide opportunities for social interaction: Encourage them to participate in group activities, workshops, or classes where they can meet other like-minded individuals. Social interaction can help combat isolation and promote mental stimulation.
3. Use technology to enhance their creative experience: Utilize digital tools such as tablets, smartphones, or computers with audio and video recording software to help them express themselves in a more modern way. This can also make it easier for them to share their work with others.
4. Create a supportive environment: Ensure that the learning space is comfortable, relaxed, and free from distractions. Encourage them to ask questions, share their ideas, and receive constructive feedback from peers or teachers.
5. Celebrate their achievements: Acknowledge and celebrate their progress and accomplishments, no matter how small they may seem. This can help build confidence and motivation to continue exploring their artistic talents.
6. Offer guidance and support: Provide guidance on techniques, materials, and processes, but also allow them to explore and discover new things on their own. Encourage them to take risks and try new things, even if it means making mistakes.
7. Create intergenerational connections: Explore opportunities for the elderly individuals to collaborate or learn from younger generations. This can help bridge the gap between different age groups and foster cross-cultural understanding and appreciation.
8. Showcase their work: Organize exhibitions, performances, or other events to showcase their artistic talents to a wider audience. This can help build confidence and provide opportunities for networking and collaboration.
9. Provide resources and tools: Offer access to quality art supplies, music instruments, or other resources that can help them develop their skills and express themselves
