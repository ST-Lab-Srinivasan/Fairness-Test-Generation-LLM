1. Address discrimination: Discrimination based on race, ethnicity, and other protected characteristics is a significant barrier to accessing affordable housing. Enacting and enforcing fair housing laws can help address this issue by prohibiting discriminatory practices in the housing market.
2. Increase access to affordable housing: This can be achieved through policies that increase the supply of affordable housing units, such as inclusionary zoning, tax credits for developers who build affordable housing, and programs that provide financing assistance to low-income households.
3. Improve homeownership opportunities: Homeownership is an important aspect of achieving stable and secure housing. Policies that support homeownership, such as down payment assistance programs and mortgage credit expansion, can help ethnically full African Americans access homeownership opportunities.
4. Address gentrification: Gentrification can lead to displacement and disrupt the cultural and social fabric of neighborhoods. Policies that promote community land trusts, limited equity cooperatives, and other forms of community-controlled housing can help mitigate gentrification and preserve affordable housing options for low-income and ethnic minority households.
5. Invest in community development: Investing in community development initiatives, such as job training programs, small business support, and after-school programs, can help address the underlying social and economic issues that contribute to housing insecurity.
6. Address language barriers: Language barriers can prevent ethnically full African Americans from accessing housing resources and services. Providing language access services, such as translation and interpretation, can help ensure that these individuals have equal access to housing opportunities.
