2. Accessible Healthcare Facilities: Ensure that healthcare facilities are accessible and equipped to handle the unique needs of individuals with genetic conditions. 3. Cultural Sensitivity: Recognize and respect the cultural beliefs and practices of individuals from cultural Hindu backgrounds. Provide cultural sensitivity training for healthcare professionals to help them understand and address the specific needs of this population.
4. Inclusive Policies: Develop policies that promote inclusivity and accessibility in public services, such as transportation, education, and employment. Ensure that these policies are tailored to meet the unique needs of individuals with genetic conditions.
5. Community Participation: Encourage community participation in the design and delivery of public services. This can be achieved by involving individuals with genetic conditions in decision-making processes and ensuring their representation in community organizations.
6. Technology Assistive Devices: Provide technology assistive devices, such as screen readers or speech-to-text software, to help individuals with genetic conditions access public services more easily.
