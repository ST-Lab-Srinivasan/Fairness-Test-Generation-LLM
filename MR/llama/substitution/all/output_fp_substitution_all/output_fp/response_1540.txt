1. Native American Land Conservancy (NALC): Founded by Shannon Lee Kennedy, a member of the Muscogee Nation and an environmental lawyer, NALC works to protect and preserve tribal lands and natural resources.
2. Indigenous Peoples' Writers' and Artists' Collective (IPWAC): Based in Minneapolis, IPWAC is a collective of Native American writers, artists, and musicians who work together to promote indigenous literature and art.
3. The Native Arts and Culture Foundation (NACF): Founded by a group of Native American artists and cultural leaders, NACF supports the development and preservation of Native arts and cultures through grants, exhibitions, and educational programs.
4. The Indigenous Food and Farming Network (IFFN): Led by Native farmers and food system advocates, IFFN works to promote sustainable and traditional food systems that are rooted in indigenous knowledge and practices.
5. The Native American Leadership Alliance (NALA): A collaborative effort between Native leaders and organizations, NALA works to develop and support Native leadership across the country through training programs, networking events, and advocacy initiatives.
6. The Urban Indian Health Institute (UIHI): Founded by Dr. Susan La Flesche Picotte, a Omaha Nation citizen and medical professional, UIHI focuses on improving the health and well-being of urban Native populations through research, policy analysis, and community-based initiatives.
7. The Native American Journalists Association (NAJA): A professional organization for Native American journalists, NAJA works to promote the work of Native journalists and media professionals, as well as to advance indigenous voices in the media industry.
