Promoting the financial stability of very elderly (90s+) native Spanish speakers requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique needs and challenges. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Financial Planning: Provide access to financial planners who specialize in working with elderly clients, helping them create a personalized financial plan that accounts for their income, expenses, assets, and liabilities. This plan should also consider their long-term care needs and potential sources of support.
2. Social Security Benefits: Ensure that they understand how to maximize their Social Security benefits, including when to claim benefits, how to coordinate benefits with a spouse or partner, and how to use the "file and suspend" strategy.
3. Retirement Accounts: Encourage them to take advantage of retirement accounts such as 401(k)s, IRAs, and annuities, which can provide tax-deferred growth and income in retirement.
4. Investment Strategies: Help them develop an investment strategy that aligns with their risk tolerance and financial goals, including diversifying their portfolio to minimize losses during market downturns.
5. Long-Term Care Planning: Assist them in planning for long-term care expenses, such as nursing home or in-home care, by exploring options like long-term care insurance, Medicaid, and veterans' benefits.
6. Estate Planning: Ensure that they have a comprehensive estate plan in place, including a will, power of attorney, and advance directive, to ensure their assets are protected and distributed according to their wishes after death.
7. Healthcare Navigation: Help them navigate the complex healthcare system by identifying potential sources of support, such as Medicare, Medicaid, and Veterans Administration benefits, and ensuring that they receive appropriate care and services.
8. Home Modifications: Encourage them to make modifications to their home to improve accessibility and safety, such as installing grab bars, non-slip flooring, and wheelchair ramps, which can help them age in place more comfortably.
