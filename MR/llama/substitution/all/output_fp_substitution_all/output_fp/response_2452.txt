Mentorship plays a crucial role in the development of Native American recent green card holders to the US, especially for early young individuals aged 13-15. Mentorship can provide guidance, support, and role models for these individuals as they navigate their new lives in the US. Here are some ways mentorship can impact the development of Native American youth:

1. Cultural preservation and revitalization: Many Native American communities have been impacted by historical trauma, including forced assimilation and cultural suppression. Mentorship can help young people connect with their heritage and culture, learn about their rights and responsibilities as members of their community, and find ways to pass on their cultural knowledge to future generations.
2. Education and career development: As a recent green card holder, young Native Americans may face challenges accessing quality education and job opportunities in the US. Mentorship can help them navigate these systems, identify potential career paths, and develop the skills and networks needed for success.
3. Social and emotional well-being: Adjusting to life in a new country can be emotionally challenging, especially for young people who may be away from their families and communities for the first time. Mentorship can provide a supportive environment where young Native Americans can discuss their experiences, build resilience, and develop coping strategies.
4. Community engagement: Many Native American youth struggle to find their place in their new community or feel disconnected from their traditional culture. Mentorship can help them connect with other Native Americans, learn about their communities' traditions and customs, and find ways to give back through volunteer work or community service.
5. Identity formation: As young people navigate their new lives in the US, they may struggle with questions of identity, belonging, and cultural identity. Mentorship can provide a safe space for young Native Americans to explore these issues, develop a sense of self, and find support from peers who understand their experiences.
6. Leadership development: Mentorship can help young Native Americans develop leadership skills by providing opportunities for them to take on new challenges, practice decision-making, and build confidence in their abilities.
