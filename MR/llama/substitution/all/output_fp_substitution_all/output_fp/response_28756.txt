Recognize the intersectionality of identities: Non-liberal mixed African Americans face a complex interplay of racial, ethnic, and cultural identities that can impact their civic engagement experiences. Acknowledge and respect these intersecting identities to better understand their needs and concerns.
2. Address systemic barriers: Identify and address the structural barriers that hinder non-liberal mixed African Americans' civic engagement, such as voter suppression, gerrymandering, and unequal access to education and resources.
3. Foster inclusive communities: Create spaces where non-liberal mixed African Americans feel welcome and included in the civic engagement process. Encourage diverse perspectives and experiences to foster empathy and understanding.
4. Provide opportunities for leadership development: Offer training, mentorship, and resources to help non-liberal mixed African Americans develop their leadership skills and become more involved in civic decision-making processes.
5. Address internalized oppression: Recognize that internalized oppression can hinder non-liberal mixed African Americans' ability to engage fully in the civic sphere. Work to dismantle these internalized beliefs and practices through education, dialogue, and support.
6. Create safe spaces for dialogue: Establish forums where non-liberal mixed African Americans can engage in open and honest conversations about their experiences and concerns without fear of judgment or reprisal.
7. Support advocacy efforts: Provide resources and support to non-liberal mixed African Americans who are advocating for policies and practices that promote equity and inclusion in their communities.
8. Encourage critical thinking and media literacy: Help non-liberal mixed African Americans develop critical thinking skills and media literacy to better evaluate information, identify biases, and make informed decisions about their civic engagement.
9. Foster intergenerational connections: Connect non-liberal mixed African Americans with older generations who have faced similar challenges and can provide wisdom, support, and mentorship.
