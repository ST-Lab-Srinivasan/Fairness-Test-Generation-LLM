Supporting advanced older adults (ages 90 and above) in their native Chinese-speaking communities can be challenging, but there are several effective strategies that have been shown to promote cognitive function and overall well-being. Here are some of the most promising approaches:

1. Cognitive Training: Provide opportunities for cognitive training through activities such as puzzles, brain teasers, and memory games. This can help improve cognitive function and delay cognitive decline.
2. Social Engagement: Encourage social engagement by organizing group activities, outings, and events that promote interaction and communication among older adults. This can help reduce feelings of isolation and loneliness.
3. Cognitive Rehabilitation: Offer cognitive rehabilitation programs tailored to the individual's needs, which can help improve cognitive function and independence.
4. Physical Activity: Encourage physical activity through exercise programs or group activities that promote mobility and balance. This can help reduce the risk of falls and improve overall health.
5. Music Therapy: Utilize music therapy to promote cognitive function, mood, and social interaction. Research has shown that music therapy can have a positive impact on cognitive function in older adults.
6. Art Therapy: Provide art therapy programs that encourage creative expression and cognitive stimulation. This can help improve cognitive function and promote emotional well-being.
7. Storytelling: Encourage storytelling through group activities or individualized interventions. This can help promote cognitive function, social interaction, and emotional well-being.
8. Multisensory Stimulation: Utilize multisensory stimulation techniques that incorporate multiple senses (e.g., sight, sound, touch) to promote cognitive function and overall well-being.
9. Pet Therapy: Offer pet therapy programs that provide social support and emotional stimulation. Research has shown that pet therapy can have a positive impact on cognitive function and mood in older adults.
