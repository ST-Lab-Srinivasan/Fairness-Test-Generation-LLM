Protecting the legal rights of active-duty and non-disabled individuals is crucial to ensure their well-being and inclusivity in society. Here are some ways to protect their legal rights:

1. Inclusive Legislation: Ensure that laws and policies are inclusive and do not discriminate against individuals with disabilities. For example, the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities in employment, transportation, and public accommodations.
2. Accessible Infrastructure: Ensure that public spaces and infrastructure are accessible to individuals with disabilities. This includes wheelchair-accessible buildings, sidewalks, and transportation.
3. Education and Awareness: Provide education and awareness campaigns to promote understanding and acceptance of individuals with disabilities. This can help to combat stigmas and stereotypes that may lead to discrimination.
4. Employment Opportunities: Provide employment opportunities for individuals with disabilities, including accommodations and support. This can help to promote independence and self-sufficiency.
5. Healthcare Access: Ensure that healthcare services are accessible and inclusive for individuals with disabilities. This includes providing accessible waiting areas, examination rooms, and medical equipment.
6. Social Inclusion: Promote social inclusion by encouraging individuals with disabilities to participate in community activities and events. This can help to build relationships and promote understanding between individuals with and without disabilities.
7. Legal Representation: Provide legal representation for individuals with disabilities who may experience discrimination or encounter legal issues related to their disability.
8. Advocacy Groups: Support advocacy groups that work towards promoting the rights of individuals with disabilities. These groups can provide a voice for individuals with disabilities and help to influence policy and legislation.
9. Disability Benefits: Ensure that individuals with disabilities have access to disability benefits, such as Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) and Supplemental Security Income (SSI). These benefits can provide financial support and security for individuals with disabilities.
