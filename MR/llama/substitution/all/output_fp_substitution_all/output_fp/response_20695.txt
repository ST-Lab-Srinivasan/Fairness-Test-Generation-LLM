The concept of cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of mixed Asian heritage non-independent individuals. Cultural heritage refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, and practices of a particular group or society, which are passed down from one generation to the next. For individuals with mixed Asian heritage, their cultural heritage is often complex and multifaceted, comprising of different cultures, languages, religions, and traditions from their ancestral backgrounds.

1. Sense of belonging: Cultural heritage provides a sense of belonging to a particular group or community, which is essential for individual identity formation. For mixed Asian heritage individuals, their cultural heritage helps them connect with their ancestral roots and feel a part of a larger social unit.
2. Identity formation: Cultural heritage influences how individuals perceive themselves and their place in the world. Mixed Asian heritage individuals may find themselves navigating multiple cultural identities and experiencing a sense of cultural dissonance, as they try to reconcile their diverse ancestral backgrounds with their own identity.
3. Intersectionality: Cultural heritage intersects with other aspects of identity, such as race, ethnicity, gender, and sexuality, to shape the identities of mixed Asian heritage individuals. For example, a mixed Asian individual may identify as both Asian and Black, or as a woman of color, which informs their cultural heritage and how they navigate the world.
4. Cultural expression: Cultural heritage provides a means of expressing oneself and one's community through art, music, dance, and other forms of cultural expression. Mixed Asian heritage individuals may use these forms of expression to connect with their ancestral cultures and to express their unique identities.
