2. Language learning: As beginner Chinese speakers learn each other's languages, they gain a deeper understanding of each other's cultural practices and values. This language learning can facilitate communication and build bridges between the groups.
3. Performance arts: Cultural exchange programs often involve performance arts such as dance, music, and theater. These activities promote cross-cultural understanding by allowing participants to experience and appreciate each other's artistic expressions.
4. Food and cooking: Sharing food and cooking traditions can be a powerful tool for fostering understanding between ethnically full Asian groups. Participants can learn about each other's culinary practices, ingredients, and cooking techniques, promoting mutual respect and appreciation.

6. Intercultural dialogue: Regular intercultural dialogue can facilitate mutual understanding and respect between the groups. By engaging in open and honest discussions about their cultural practices and values, participants can challenge stereotypes and misconceptions, promoting greater empathy and cooperation.
