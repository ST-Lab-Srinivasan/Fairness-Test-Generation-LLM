2. Interpretation and Translation Services: Offer interpretation and translation services during decision-making processes to ensure that beginner Spanish speakers can fully participate. This can involve providing real-time interpretation through video remote interpreting (VRI) or on-site interpretation, as well as translating important documents and materials into the language spoken by the target population.
3. Language Access Plans: Develop language access plans that outline strategies for ensuring that beginner Spanish speakers have equal access to decision-making processes. These plans can include provisions for interpreters and translators, as well as strategies for outreach and education.
4. Community Engagement: Engage with the community in a culturally sensitive manner to ensure that beginner Spanish speakers feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and opinions. This can involve holding community events, participating in community meetings, and using social media platforms to reach a wider audience.
5. Capacity Building: Build the capacity of beginner Spanish speakers to participate in decision-making processes by providing training and support. This can involve offering language classes, computer skills training, and other forms of capacity building that help individuals feel more confident and prepared to participate.
6. Collaborative Leadership: Foster collaborative leadership structures that involve beginner Spanish speakers in decision-making processes.
