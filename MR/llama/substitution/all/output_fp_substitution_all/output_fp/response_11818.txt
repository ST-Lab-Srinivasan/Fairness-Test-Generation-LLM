Supporting the social inclusion of Nominal Christian Native Chinese Speaker (NCNCS) people requires addressing the systemic and societal barriers that they face. Here are some ways to promote their social inclusion:

1. Education and Awareness: Provide education and awareness programs to help break down stereotypes and biases towards NCNCS people. This can include workshops, seminars, and community events that promote understanding, empathy, and inclusivity.
2. Language Support: Offer language support services, such as language classes or interpretation services, to help NCNCS people communicate more effectively in their daily lives.
3. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement and participation by organizing events and activities that bring together people from different backgrounds. This can help build bridges between communities and promote social inclusion.
4. Employment Opportunities: Provide job training and placement services to help NCNCS people access employment opportunities in their communities. This can include vocational training, resume building, and interview preparation.
5. Mental Health Support: Offer mental health support services, such as counseling and therapy, to help NCNCS people manage the emotional challenges of discrimination and marginalization.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that promote the rights and inclusion of NCNCS people. This can include advocating for anti-discrimination laws, cultural representation in media and education, and access to healthcare services.
7. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and grassroots organizing to build relationships with NCNCS communities and promote social inclusion. This can involve participating in community events, hosting cultural festivals, and providing resources and support to local organizations.
8. Collaboration with Other Groups: Collaborate with other groups and organizations that work towards promoting the rights and inclusion of marginalized communities. This can include working with indigenous organizations, LGBTQ+ organizations, and racial justice groups.
9. Media Representation: Work towards increasing media representation of NCNCS people in a positive and respectful manner. This can involve collaborating with media outlets to produce content that showcases the experiences and perspectives of NCNCS people.
