Creating more inclusive cultural events for non-long-time single wheelchair-using individuals requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions to make your event more accessible and enjoyable for everyone:

1. Accessibility features: Ensure that the venue is wheelchair-friendly and has easy access to seating, bathrooms, and food and beverage areas. Provide wheelchair-accessible seating and viewing areas, and consider hiring sign language interpreters or providing closed captioning for deaf or hard of hearing attendees.
2. Diverse programming: Offer a range of cultural events that cater to different tastes and preferences, such as music, dance, theater, art exhibitions, and film screenings. Consider showcasing diverse perspectives and experiences through the events you host.
3. Inclusive marketing: Use inclusive language in your marketing materials and promotional campaigns to ensure that everyone feels welcome. Highlight the accessibility features of your event and encourage people with disabilities to attend.
4. Volunteer training: Provide volunteers with training on how to assist attendees with disabilities, such as helping them navigate the venue or providing additional support during the event. This will help create a more welcoming and inclusive environment.
5. Communication: Ensure that communication is accessible to all attendees by providing braille or large print materials for those who are visually impaired, or offering American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation for those who are deaf or hard of hearing.
6. Flexibility: Offer flexible seating options and viewing areas to accommodate different access needs. Consider providing additional seating options, such as cushions or pillows, for attendees who may need extra support.
7. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by reducing sensory stimuli, such as loud noises or bright lights, that could be overwhelming for some attendees. Offer quiet areas or fidget toys for those who may need them.
