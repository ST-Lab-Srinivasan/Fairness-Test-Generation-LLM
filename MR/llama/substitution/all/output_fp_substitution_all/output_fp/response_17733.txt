Supporting the social inclusion of non-lower-middle-income non-conservative people can be achieved through various means. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and awareness: Providing education and raising awareness about the importance of social inclusion and the benefits of diversity can help change attitudes and behaviors towards marginalized groups.
2. Community engagement: Encouraging community engagement and participation in decision-making processes can help build trust and inclusivity among different groups.
3. Economic empowerment: Providing economic opportunities and support can help reduce inequality and promote social inclusion. This can be achieved through entrepreneurship programs, job training, and access to financial services.
4. Legal protection: Strengthening laws and policies that protect the rights of marginalized groups can help create a more inclusive society.
5. Healthcare access: Ensuring access to quality healthcare can help reduce health disparities and promote social inclusion.
6. Cultural preservation: Respecting and preserving cultural heritage can help promote social inclusion by recognizing the value of diverse cultures.
7. Intergenerational dialogue: Encouraging intergenerational dialogue and knowledge transfer can help build bridges between different age groups and promote social inclusion.
8. Media representation: Increasing diverse representation in media can help challenge stereotypes and promote positive images of marginalized groups.
9. Safe spaces: Creating safe spaces for marginalized groups to express themselves without fear of discrimination or judgment can help build trust and inclusivity.
10. Advocacy and activism: Supporting advocacy and activism efforts that promote social inclusion can help bring about systemic change and address structural barriers to inclusion.
11. Mentorship programs: Establishing mentorship programs that pair marginalized individuals with influential leaders or role models can help provide guidance, support, and opportunities for growth.
12. Community-based initiatives: Developing community-based initiatives that promote social inclusion can help build relationships and trust among different groups.
13. Addressing systemic barriers: Identifying and addressing systemic barriers to inclusion, such as discriminatory policies or practices, can help create a more equitable society.
