Disability can have significant impacts on the lives of permanent residents with disabilities, particularly in terms of their social and economic participation. Some of the common impacts include:

1. Social Isolationation: People with disabilities may face barriers to social interaction and connection, leading to feelings of isolation and loneliness.
2. Discrimination: Disability can lead to discrimination in various areas of life, including education, employment, and healthcare, limiting the opportunities and resources available to people with disabilities.
3. Economic Impacts: Disability can have a significant impact on the economic well-being of individuals, particularly in terms of income and employment opportunities. People with disabilities may face barriers to finding and keeping employment, leading to lower earning potential and increased reliance on social safety nets.
4. Limited Access to Healthcare: People with disabilities may face barriers to accessing healthcare services, including lack of accessibility in healthcare facilities, discrimination by healthcare providers, and limited knowledge and understanding of disability issues among healthcare professionals.
5. Inadequate Support Systems: Disability can lead to inadequate support systems, such as lack of access to assistive technology, transportation, or home modification, which can limit the ability of people with disabilities to live independently and participate fully in society.
6. Stigma and Stereotypes: People with disabilities may face stigma and stereotypes, which can lead to social exclusion, marginalization, and discrimination.
7. Limited Participation in Decision-Making: Disability can limit the ability of people with disabilities to participate fully in decision-making processes, such as political participation, community involvement, or cultural expression.
8. Inadequate Education and Training: People with disabilities may face barriers to accessing education and training, including lack of accessibility in educational facilities, discrimination by educators, and limited knowledge and understanding of disability issues among educators.
9. Limited Access to Leisure Activities: Disability can limit the ability of people with disabilities to participate fully in leisure activities, such as sports, recreation, or cultural events, due to lack of accessibility or discrimination.
