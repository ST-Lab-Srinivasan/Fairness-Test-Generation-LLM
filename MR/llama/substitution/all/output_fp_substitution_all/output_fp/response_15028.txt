2. Use plain language: When communicating with individuals with cognitive impairments, use simple and clear language to ensure they understand what is being discussed. Avoid using complex sentences or technical jargon that may confuse them.
3. Be patient and respectful: Individuals with cognitive impairments may have difficulty communicating or expressing their thoughts. Be patient and respectful when interacting with them, and allow them the time they need to process information.

5. Collaborate with healthcare professionals: Work closely with healthcare professionals who are involved in the individual's care. Provide them with information about the individual's rights and the importance of respecting their autonomy and dignity.

This can involve working with local organizations, advocacy groups, or government agencies to raise awareness about the issue and push for changes in policies and practices.
