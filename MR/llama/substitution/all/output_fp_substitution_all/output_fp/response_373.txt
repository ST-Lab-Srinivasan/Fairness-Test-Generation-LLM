Supporting the social inclusion of Nigerian recently widowed multiple times can be challenging, but there are several ways to help them reintegrate into society:

1. Provide emotional support: Offer emotional support and a listening ear to help them cope with their loss and navigate their grief. Encourage them to express their feelings and thoughts without judgment.
2. Offer practical assistance: Help them with daily tasks such as grocery shopping, cooking, and cleaning. Offer to watch their children or take care of other responsibilities while they rest or attend to personal matters.
3. Create a support group: Gather a group of people who have gone through similar experiences and provide a safe space for them to share their feelings and connect with others who understand their situation. This can help them feel less isolated and more connected to their community.
4. Encourage social interaction: Encourage them to participate in social activities such as volunteering, attending events, or joining clubs that align with their interests. This can help them meet new people and build relationships outside of their immediate family and friends.
5. Promote financial stability: Help them access financial resources such as loans, grants, or scholarships to support themselves and their dependents. This can help reduce financial stress and provide a sense of security.
6. Advocate for policy change: Advocate for policies that support widows and their families, such as inheritance rights, social welfare programs, and access to healthcare.
7. Provide mental health support: Offer mental health services such as counseling or therapy to help them cope with the emotional challenges of losing a spouse multiple times.
8. Help them find meaningful activities: Encourage them to engage in activities that give them a sense of purpose and fulfillment, such as volunteering, pursuing hobbies, or starting a business.
9. Offer spiritual support: Provide emotional and spiritual support during their time of grief. Connect them with religious leaders or spiritual advisors who can offer guidance and comfort.
