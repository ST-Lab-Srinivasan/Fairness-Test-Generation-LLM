Promoting the financial stability of non-single, long-term temporary visa holders and recent green card holders in the US requires a multi-faceted approach. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Financial Literacy Programs: Provide access to financial literacy programs that teach budgeting, saving, investing, and managing debt. This can help them make informed decisions about their finances and avoid financial pitfalls.
2. Access to Bank Accounts: Encourage temporary visa holders and green card holders to open bank accounts in the US. This will give them access to basic banking services and enable them to manage their finances more effectively.
3. Financial Planning Tools: Offer financial planning tools and resources, such as budgeting apps and financial planning websites, to help them track their expenses, create a budget, and set financial goals.
4. Tax Support: Provide tax support and education to help them understand the US tax system and how it affects their financial situation. This can include information on tax filing requirements, tax credits and deductions, and how to navigate the US tax system.
5. Networking Opportunities: Organize networking events and workshops that bring together temporary visa holders and green card holders with successful professionals in their field. This can help them build connections and learn from others' experiences.
6. Professional Development: Offer professional development opportunities, such as language training, skills workshops, and career counseling, to help them improve their employability and advance in their careers.
7. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that match experienced professionals with temporary visa holders and green card holders who are looking for guidance and support. This can provide valuable insights and advice on how to navigate the US job market.
8. Entrepreneurial Support: Encourage and support entrepreneurial activities among temporary visa holders and green card holders by providing access to resources, such as business incubators, funding opportunities, and networking events.
9. Health Insurance Support: Offer health insurance support and resources to help them navigate the complex US healthcare system and secure affordable health insurance coverage.
