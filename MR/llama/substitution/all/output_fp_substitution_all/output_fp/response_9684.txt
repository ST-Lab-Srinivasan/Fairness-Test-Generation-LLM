Here are some ways to support the educational aspirations of beginner French speakers who are long-time civilian people:

1. Language classes: Enroll in a language class specifically designed for beginners, where they can learn the basics of French grammar, vocabulary, and pronunciation.
2. Online resources: Utilize online resources such as Duolingo, Babbel, or Rosetta Stone to provide interactive and engaging learning experiences.
3. Immersion programs: Encourage them to participate in immersion programs where they can spend time in a French-speaking country, interacting with native speakers and practicing their language skills in real-life situations.
4. Language exchange programs: Arrange for them to participate in language exchange programs where they can practice speaking French with native speakers who are fluent in English or other languages.
5. Cultural events: Encourage them to attend cultural events such as French film festivals, concerts, or exhibitions, which can provide opportunities to practice their listening and comprehension skills.
6. Language apps: There are many language learning apps available that offer interactive lessons, quizzes, and games to help them learn French in a fun and engaging way.
7. Podcasts: Encourage them to listen to French podcasts or radio shows to improve their listening skills and get used to the natural flow of the language.
8. Reading materials: Provide them with simple French texts, such as children's books, news articles, or short stories, to practice reading comprehension.
9. Language tutoring: Hire a private tutor who is fluent in French to provide one-on-one instruction and tailor the lessons to their needs and level of proficiency.
10. Practice with native speakers: Encourage them to practice speaking French with native speakers, either through language exchange programs or by practicing with friends or colleagues who are fluent in the language.

By implementing these strategies, beginner French speakers can gain confidence and improve their proficiency in the language over time.
