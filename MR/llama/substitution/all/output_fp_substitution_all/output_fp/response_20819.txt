1. Equal Access to Benefits: Ensure that widowed veterans have equal access to benefits such as pension, healthcare, and home loans. The Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) should provide clear guidelines and procedures for the distribution of benefits among surviving spouses.

Protect Survivor Benefits: Enact laws that protect survivor benefits for widowed veterans, including the Survivors Pension program, which provides financial assistance to eligible surviving spouses and children. 3. Estate Planning: Encourage widowed veterans to seek professional estate planning advice to ensure their assets are protected and distributed according to their wishes after death. This can include wills, trusts, and powers of attorney.
4. Education and Awareness: Provide educational resources and awareness campaigns to inform widowed veterans about their legal rights and benefits. This can include workshops, webinars, and printed materials that explain the VA's surviving spouse benefits and how to access them.
5. Advocacy Groups: Support advocacy groups that represent the interests of widowed veterans. These organizations can help lobby for legislative changes and provide support to individuals who may be struggling to navigate the legal system.
