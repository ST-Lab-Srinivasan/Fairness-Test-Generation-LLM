Creating more inclusive recreational activities for permanent residents average earners (PRAEs) can be achieved by focusing on accessibility, affordability, and tailored programming. Here are some strategies to make recreation more inclusive:

1. Accessible facilities and programs: Ensure that recreational facilities and programs are accessible to PRAEs, including those with disabilities. Provide adaptive equipment or modifications to existing facilities to make them more inclusive. Offer programs in diverse locations to reach a wider audience.
2. Financial assistance: Offer financial aid or scholarships to PRAEs who might not be able to afford recreational activities. Partner with local organizations to provide funding for community members who cannot afford participation fees.
3. Tailored programming: Develop programs that cater to the unique needs and interests of PRAEs. For example, offer classes or workshops focused on specific hobbies or skills, such as cooking, art, or sports. Encourage collaboration between different communities to create cross-cultural programming.
4. Inclusive marketing: Use marketing strategies that reach a diverse audience, including PRAEs. Highlight the benefits of recreational activities for mental and physical health, social connections, and overall well-being. Emphasize the inclusivity of your programs to attract a wider range of participants.
5. Community outreach: Engage with local communities, including PRAEs, through outreach events, workshops, or partnerships. Provide information about recreational activities and their benefits, as well as opportunities for involvement and participation.
6. Volunteer opportunities: Offer volunteer opportunities within recreational programs to engage PRAEs in the planning and delivery of activities. This can help build a sense of ownership and community involvement, while also providing valuable skills development and social connections.
7. Flexible scheduling: Accommodate the varied schedules of PRAEs by offering programs at different times or days of the week. Provide online or virtual options for those who may find it challenging to attend in-person activities.
8. Intergenerational programming: Develop activities that bring together people of different ages and backgrounds, fostering intergenerational relationships and social connections. This can help create a sense of community and belonging
