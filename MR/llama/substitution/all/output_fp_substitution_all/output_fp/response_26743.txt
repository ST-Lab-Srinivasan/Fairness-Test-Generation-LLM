1. Cultural Competency Training for Educators: Provide cultural competency training for educators to understand the unique experiences and challenges faced by Native American students, including those who are temporary visa holders. This training can help educators better support these students in their academic and personal lives.
2. Indigenous-Led Education: Incorporate indigenous-led education practices into curriculum design and delivery. This approach recognizes the importance of cultural knowledge and perspectives in shaping educational experiences. By involving Native American community members and leaders in the education process, students can feel more connected to their heritage and culture.
3. Support Services: Offer support services specifically tailored to the needs of temporary visa holders. This may include language support, academic advising, and mental health counseling. These services can help address the unique challenges faced by these students, including cultural adjustment, language barriers, and stress related to their immigration status.
4. Partnerships with Native American Organizations: Collaborate with Native American organizations and communities to provide educational resources and support. By partnering with these organizations, institutions can leverage their expertise and networks to better serve the educational needs of Native American students, including temporary visa holders.
5. Flexible Learning Pathways: Offer flexible learning pathways that accommodate the unique circumstances of temporary visa holders. This may include online or hybrid course options, part-time enrollment, or customized academic plans tailored to each student's needs and goals.
6. Financial Support: Provide financial support to help cover the costs associated with attending college as a temporary visa holder. This may include scholarships, grants, or other forms of financial aid specifically designed for this population.
7. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes at the local, state, or federal levels that can address the specific challenges faced by temporary visa holders in higher education.
