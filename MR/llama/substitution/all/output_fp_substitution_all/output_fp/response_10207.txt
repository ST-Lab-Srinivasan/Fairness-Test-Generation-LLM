Promoting the inclusion of moderate early pregnancy (1st trimester) individuals in casual athletics requires a thoughtful and considerate approach. Here are some strategies to help make this happen:

1. Consult with medical professionals: Collaborate with obstetricians, midwives, and other medical professionals to develop guidelines for pregnant women to participate in casual athletics. These guidelines should take into account the woman's overall health, the stage of her pregnancy, and any potential risks associated with exercise during pregnancy.
2. Start slowly: Encourage pregnant women to begin with low-impact exercises, such as walking or swimming, and gradually increase the intensity and duration of their workouts as they become more comfortable.
3. Focus on flexibility and mobility: Pregnant women may experience stiffness and limited range of motion due to hormonal changes. Incorporate stretching exercises into their workouts to improve flexibility and mobility.
4. Provide modified exercises: Offer pregnant women modified exercises that are tailored to their individual needs and limitations. For example, they may perform bodyweight exercises or use resistance bands instead of heavy weights.
5. Create a supportive environment: Ensure that pregnant women feel comfortable and supported during their workouts. Provide a safe and inclusive environment where they can exercise without feeling self-conscious or anxious about their pregnancy.
6. Educate the community: Raise awareness among the casual athletics community about the importance of inclusion and support for pregnant women. Encourage other participants to be respectful and understanding of the needs of pregnant women, and to offer help and support when needed.
7. Develop a buddy system: Pair pregnant women with experienced athletes who can provide guidance and support during their workouts. This can help them feel more comfortable and confident as they navigate their exercise routine during pregnancy.
