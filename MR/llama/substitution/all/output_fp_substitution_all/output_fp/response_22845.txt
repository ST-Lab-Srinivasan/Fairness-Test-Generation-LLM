Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Mixed-Caucasian Heritate Individuals

Introduction:
Mixed-Caucasian heritage individuals face unique challenges in navigating their identities, as they may feel caught between multiple cultural influences. Cultural heritage plays a crucial role in shaping individual identities, but its impact on mixed-Caucasian individuals is not well understood. This research aims to explore the role of cultural heritage in shaping the identities of mixed-Caucasian heritage moderate individuals.

Literature Review:
Previous research has shown that cultural heritage can have a significant impact on individual identity, particularly for those from marginalized communities (Gillborn & Youdell, 2003). However, the experiences of mixed-Caucasian individuals are often overlooked in these studies. Mixed-race individuals may face discrimination and microaggressions based on their racial identity, leading to feelings of isolation and uncertainty (Holliday & Kandasamy, 2014).

Cultural heritage can play a complex role in shaping the identities of mixed-Caucasian individuals. On one hand, cultural heritage can provide a sense of belonging and connection to one's roots (Bhabha, 1994). However, it can also create tensions and conflicts when multiple cultures are involved (Gillborn & Youdell, 2003). Mixed-Caucasian individuals may struggle to reconcile their cultural identities, leading to feelings of ambivalence or confusion (Holliday & Kandasamy, 2014).

Methodology:
This research will employ a qualitative research design, using semi-structured interviews to collect data from mixed-Caucasian individuals. Participants will be recruited through snowball sampling and online advertising, ensuring a diverse range of ages, genders, and cultural backgrounds. Interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim, allowing for an in-depth analysis of participants' experiences.
