2. Complex Immigration Laws: The U.S. immigration laws are complex and constantly changing, making it difficult for green card holders to navigate the system without professional help. Even simple errors or omissions in filing paperwork can lead to denial of benefits or even deportation.
3. Eligibility Requirements: Green card holders may face eligibility requirements that are difficult to meet, such as language proficiency, income requirements, or proof of residency. These requirements can change frequently and may be difficult to satisfy, especially for those who have been in the United States for a short period of time.
4. Paperwork and Documentation: Green card holders must maintain accurate and up-to-date records of their immigration status, including proof of residency, employment authorization, and travel documentation. Maintaining these documents can be a challenge, especially if you are not familiar with the legal requirements or do not have access to professional help.
