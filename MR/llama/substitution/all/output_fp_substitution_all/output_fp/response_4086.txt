Promoting the educational success of Native American natural-born US citizens can be a complex and multifaceted issue, as it involves addressing systemic barriers and disparities that have historically hindered the academic achievement of these students. Here are some strategies that can help promote their educational success:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers to incorporate Native American cultural practices, beliefs, and values into the classroom curriculum. This can help students feel more connected to their heritage and culture, leading to increased engagement and motivation in their academic studies.
2. Identity-based education: Provide opportunities for students to learn about and connect with their Native American identity, including their cultural practices, history, and traditions. This can help students develop a positive sense of self and feel more confident in their ability to succeed academically.
3. Community involvement: Involve the local Native American community in education by providing opportunities for community members to share their knowledge, skills, and experiences with students. This can help foster a sense of belonging and support among students, as well as provide role models and mentors.
4. Indigenous language revitalization: Support efforts to revive and preserve Native American languages, which have been historically marginalized in US education. This can involve incorporating language learning into the curriculum, providing language immersion programs, and supporting community-based language revitalization initiatives.
5. Cultural field trips: Organize cultural field trips to local Native American communities or cultural sites, such as reservations, museums, or historical landmarks. These trips can help students develop a deeper appreciation for Native American culture and history, while also providing opportunities for intercultural learning and exchange.
