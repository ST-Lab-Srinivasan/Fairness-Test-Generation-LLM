4. Use interactive activities: Engage attendees through interactive activities, such as language games, quizzes, or role-playing exercises. This will help them practice their Arabic skills in a fun and engaging way.
5. Provide opportunities for socialization: Create opportunities for attendees to connect with each other, whether it's through small group discussions or networking sessions. This will help build a sense of community and encourage attendees to support and learn from each other.
6. Offer childcare and transportation support: Many beginners in their mid-50s may have family responsibilities or mobility issues, which can make it difficult to attend events. Consider offering childcare services or transportation support to make the event more accessible.
