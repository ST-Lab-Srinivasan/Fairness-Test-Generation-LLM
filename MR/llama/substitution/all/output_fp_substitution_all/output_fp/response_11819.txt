Supporting the social inclusion of Nominal Christian Beginner Arabic Speaker (NCBS) individuals is crucial for their well-being and integration into society. Here are some ways to promote their social inclusion:

1. Language classes: Offer Arabic language classes specifically designed for NCBS individuals, tailoring the teaching methods and materials to meet their needs and abilities. This will help them communicate effectively in Arabic and navigate everyday situations.
2. Cultural orientation: Provide cultural orientation programs that introduce NCBS individuals to the customs, traditions, and values of the host society. This can include information on local etiquette, social norms, and historical context.
3. Social events: Organize social events specifically for NCBS individuals, such as potlucks, picnics, or game nights. These events provide opportunities for them to connect with other people, practice their Arabic skills, and build relationships in a relaxed setting.
4. Mentorship programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair NCBS individuals with local volunteers who can provide guidance on cultural norms, language, and everyday life in the host country. This can help them navigate any challenges they may encounter and feel more supported in their new environment.
5. Community outreach: Engage in community outreach initiatives that promote intercultural understanding and dialogue between NCBS individuals and local communities. This can involve organizing workshops, seminars, or cultural events that bring people together and foster mutual respect and empathy.
6. Access to resources: Provide access to resources such as libraries, cultural centers, or community organizations that offer support services for NCBS individuals. This can include language classes, job training programs, or counseling services.
7. Advocacy: Advocate for the rights and needs of NCBS individuals within the host society. This can involve lobbying for policies that promote social inclusion, advocating for access to education and employment opportunities, and raising awareness about the challenges faced by this group.
