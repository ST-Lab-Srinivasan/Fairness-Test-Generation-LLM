Non-independent individuals, such as those who are sponsored by an employer or have a dependent visa, may face unique challenges and impacts related to their immigration status. These can include:

1. Limited job mobility: Non-independent individuals may be restricted from seeking new employment opportunities without permission from their sponsor or employer, limiting their career advancement and job mobility.
2. Dependence on a single employer: Many non-independent individuals are tied to a single employer, which can make them vulnerable to layoffs, job insecurity, and limited opportunities for career growth.
3. Limited access to benefits: Non-independent individuals may not be eligible for certain benefits, such as health insurance or retirement plans, due to their immigration status.
4. Social isolation: Non-independent individuals may face challenges in building a social network and establishing connections within their community due to language barriers, cultural differences, and limited access to social events and activities.
5. Limited access to education and training: Non-independent individuals may face restrictions on accessing educational resources and training opportunities, which can limit their ability to develop new skills and advance in their careers.
6. Financial instability: Non-independent individuals may be more vulnerable to financial instability due to limited access to credit, loans, or other financial resources.
7. Fear of deportation: The constant threat of deportation can lead to anxiety, stress, and a lack of stability for non-independent individuals, impacting their overall well-being and quality of life.

9. Limited social integration: Non-independent individuals may face challenges in integrating into their host society due to cultural and language barriers, limiting their opportunities for social and economic advancement.
