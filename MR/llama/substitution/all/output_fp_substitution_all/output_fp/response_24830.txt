Supporting the educational aspirations of Native English Speaker (NES) active-duty military personnel requires acknowledging their unique challenges and providing tailored solutions. Here are some ways to support NES active-duty people in pursuing their educational goals:

1. Financial Support: Offer financial assistance, such as scholarships or grants, specifically designed for NES active-duty personnel. This could help them cover the costs of tuition, books, and other expenses associated with pursuing higher education.
2. Flexible Online Programs: Provide access to flexible online programs that can accommodate their busy schedules. Many universities offer online degree programs or courses that can be completed at a pace convenient for military personnel.
3. Prioritized Registration: Offer priority registration for NES active-duty personnel, allowing them to enroll in classes before general registration opens. This ensures that they can take advantage of available spots without facing long waitlists or delays.
4. Military-Friendly Transfer Credits: Establish partnerships with other universities and colleges to ensure that military training and experience are recognized and counted towards transfer credits. This helps NES active-duty personnel avoid unnecessary coursework and reduces the time it takes to complete their degree.
5. Career Counseling: Offer career counseling and job placement services specifically tailored for NES active-duty personnel. This could help them explore career options that align with their military experience and skills, as well as provide support during transitions back to civilian life.
6. Networking Opportunities: Organize networking events and workshops that bring together NES active-duty personnel with professionals from various industries. This can help them build valuable connections and gain insights into different career paths.
7. On-Campus Resources: Provide on-campus resources, such as tutoring services, academic advising, and mental health counseling, that are specifically designed for NES active-duty personnel. These resources can help them succeed in their studies while also addressing any challenges they may face due to their military service.
8. Military Culture Awareness: Train faculty and staff on how to support NES active-duty students
