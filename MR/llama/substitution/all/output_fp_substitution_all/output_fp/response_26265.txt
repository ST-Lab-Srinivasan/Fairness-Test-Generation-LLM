Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner English speakers who are ethnically African American requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some strategies that can help make the program more welcoming and inclusive:

1. Cultural sensitivity training: Provide training on cultural sensitivity and diversity for all program staff and volunteers. This can include workshops, webinars, or other training sessions that help them understand the needs and experiences of African American communities.
2. Inclusive language: Use inclusive language in all program materials and communications. Avoid using language that stereotypes or marginalizes any particular group, including African Americans.
3. Representation matters: Ensure that the cultural programs reflect the diversity of the community. Hire instructors and performers who are from diverse backgrounds, including African American culture.
4. Accessibility: Make sure the program is accessible to everyone, regardless of their race or ethnicity. Provide accommodations such as American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation, Braille materials, or audio descriptions for participants with disabilities.
5. Involve the community: Involve the African American community in the planning and execution of the cultural programs. This can include consulting with community leaders, engaging in outreach and marketing efforts that target the community, and incorporating input from community members into the program design.
6. Focus on mutual respect: Create a program environment where everyone feels valued and respected. Encourage participants to share their experiences and perspectives, and provide opportunities for dialogue and understanding between different cultures.
7. Intersectionality: Recognize that cultural identity is intersectional and that individuals may identify with multiple cultures. Be mindful of the intersections of race, ethnicity, gender, sexuality, ability, and other aspects of identity when designing the program.
8. Inclusive marketing: Market the program in a way that is inclusive and respectful of all cultures. Avoid using language or imagery that stereotypes or marginalizes any particular group.
9. Feedback mechanisms: Establish feedback mechanisms to gather input from participants on how to improve the program's inclusivity. Use this feedback to make adjustments and improvements over time.
