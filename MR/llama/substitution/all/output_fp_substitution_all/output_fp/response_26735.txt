Creating more inclusive cultural events for non-citizen, non-independent individuals requires careful consideration and planning. Here are some suggestions on how to make your events more accessible and welcoming:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the event location is wheelchair accessible, has accessible restrooms, and is located near public transportation. Provide audio descriptions or sign language interpretation for visual or auditory impairments.
2. Language support: Offer translation services in multiple languages to cater to a diverse audience. Provide language support materials such as translated programs, maps, and signs.
3. Inclusive programming: Incorporate activities that are inclusive of all participants, regardless of their abilities. Examples include tactile art exhibits for individuals with visual impairments, or performances by artists with disabilities.
4. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by reducing noise levels, using dim lighting, and providing sensory-friendly materials such as fidget toys or stress balls.
5. Representation: Ensure that the event representation reflects the diversity of the community. Use images and symbols that are inclusive of all cultures and backgrounds.
6. Involve the community: Engage with local organizations that support non-citizen, non-independent individuals to get involved in the planning process or serve as volunteers at the event. This can help build trust and create a sense of belonging.
7. Education and awareness: Provide educational materials and resources that raise awareness about the experiences and challenges faced by non-citizen, non-independent individuals. Use these materials to promote empathy and understanding.
8. Collaborate with diverse artists: Invite artists from diverse backgrounds to showcase their work at the event. This can help create a more inclusive atmosphere and provide opportunities for underrepresented groups to share their talents.
9. Flexible participation options: Offer flexible participation options such as remote or virtual attendance for individuals who may face barriers to attending in-person events.
