1. Early Childhood Education Programs: Implement early childhood education programs that provide quality education to infants and toddlers from low-income families. These programs can help bridge the gap in cognitive development between children from low-income families and their peers from more affluent backgrounds.
2. Scholarships and Financial Aid: Provide scholarships and financial aid to low-income families to cover the costs of early childhood education programs, including tuition fees, books, and other educational materials.
3. Parent Engagement: Encourage parents to participate in their child's education by providing them with information about their child's development, learning strategies, and how they can support their child's education at home.
4. Family-Friendly Learning Environments: Create family-friendly learning environments that are inclusive and welcoming to all families, regardless of their socioeconomic status. This can involve creating safe and comfortable spaces for children to learn and play.

Professional Development for Teachers: Provide professional development opportunities for teachers to enhance their knowledge and skills in teaching young children, particularly those from low-income families.
