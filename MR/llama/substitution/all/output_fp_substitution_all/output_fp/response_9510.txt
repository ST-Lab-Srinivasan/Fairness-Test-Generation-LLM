The impact of marital status on the lives of long-time widowed individuals can be significant and far-reaching. Here are some of the potential impacts:

1. Emotional and Psychological Impact: Losing a spouse can lead to intense grief, sadness, and emotional distress. The process of mourning can be prolonged and challenging, especially for those who have been married for many years. Widowed individuals may experience depression, anxiety, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).
2. Social Isolation: After the loss of a spouse, widowed individuals may feel socially isolated. They may find it difficult to connect with others, especially if they have been married for many years and their social network consists mainly of their spouse's friends and family.
3. Financial Impact: Widows or widowers may face financial challenges, particularly if their spouse was the primary breadwinner. They may need to navigate managing finances alone, which can be a significant burden, especially during retirement.
4. Health Impact: Studies have shown that widowed individuals are at a higher risk of developing chronic health conditions, such as hypertension, cardiovascular disease, and depression. This may be due to the emotional stress of losing a spouse, as well as the loss of social support.
5. Loss of Identity: Marriage can provide a sense of identity and purpose for many individuals. Losing a spouse can lead to a crisis of identity, making it challenging for widowed individuals to find their footing in life.
6. Stigma and Social Exclusion: Unfortunately, there is still a stigma attached to being a widow or widower in some cultures. Widowed individuals may experience social exclusion, particularly if they are seen as "being over the hill" or "past their prime."
