Title: The Significance of Cultural Heritage in Shaping the Identities of Devout Christian Native Temporary Visa Holders to the US

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a crucial role in shaping the identities of individuals, including those who are recent temporary visa holders to the United States. For devout Christian Native individuals, their cultural heritage is deeply intertwined with their religious beliefs and practices. This paper will explore the role that cultural heritage plays in shaping the identities of these individuals and how it influences their experiences as temporary visitors to the US.

The Importance of Cultural Heritage:
Cultural heritage refers to the shared knowledge, traditions, and practices that are passed down from one generation to the next within a particular group or community. For Native individuals who hold devout Christian beliefs, their cultural heritage is closely tied to their spirituality and religious practices. Their cultural identity is shaped by their connection to the land, their ancestors, and their traditions.

In the case of recent temporary visa holders to the US, their cultural heritage can have a profound impact on their experiences in the country. For example, they may find comfort in maintaining their cultural traditions and practices while abroad, such as observing holidays and rituals that are significant to their community. At the same time, they may also face challenges in navigating a new cultural context that may be unfamiliar or even hostile to their beliefs and practices.

The Influence of Cultural Heritage on Identity:
Cultural heritage can shape an individual's identity in several ways. Firstly, it can provide a sense of belonging and connection to a larger community. For Native devout Christians, their cultural heritage is a source of pride and identity, connecting them to their ancestors and the land. This sense of belonging can help individuals navigate unfamiliar or challenging situations, as they draw on the collective wisdom and support of their community.

Secondly, cultural heritage can influence an individual's values and beliefs.
