3. Encourage participation: Make an effort to involve beginner English speakers in decision-making processes by asking for their input and ideas. This can be done through surveys, focus groups, or other forms of active engagement.

6. Build trust: Establish trust with beginner English speakers by being approachable, transparent, and consistent in your communication. This will help them feel more comfortable participating in decision-making processes.
7. Offer mentorship: Pair beginner English speakers with experienced colleagues who can provide guidance and support in navigating the decision-making process.
8. Use technology: Utilize digital tools or platforms that cater to the language needs of beginner English speakers, such as translation software or mobile apps. This can help level the playing field and ensure their voices are heard.
