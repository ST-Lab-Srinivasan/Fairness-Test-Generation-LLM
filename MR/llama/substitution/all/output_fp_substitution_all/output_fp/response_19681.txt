Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner English speakers, including recent temporary visa holders to the US, requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions to make these programs more accessible and inclusive:

1. Assess the needs of the participants: Conduct a needs assessment to understand the language proficiency, cultural background, and interests of the participants. This will help tailor the program to meet their specific needs.
2. Offer a variety of activities: Provide a range of activities that cater to different learning styles and interests. For example, include group discussions, film screenings, theater performances, art exhibitions, and workshops on cultural etiquette and customs.
3. Use inclusive language: Use language that is respectful and inclusive of diverse cultures. Avoid using idioms or phrases that may be offensive or confusing to non-native speakers.
4. Provide interpretation and translation services: Offer interpretation and translation services for participants who may not speak English fluently. This can include live interpretation, video remote interpreting, or pre-recorded translations.
5. Create a safe and welcoming environment: Ensure that the program creates a safe and welcoming environment for all participants. Encourage respectful dialogue, listen actively, and avoid making assumptions based on cultural backgrounds.
6. Involve community members: Invite community members from diverse backgrounds to participate in the program as facilitators, performers, or speakers. This can help build bridges between different cultures and create a sense of belonging.
7. Offer flexible scheduling options: Accommodate participants with varying availability by offering flexible scheduling options, such as online courses or weekend workshops.
8. Develop partnerships with cultural organizations: Collaborate with local cultural organizations to host events, workshops, or exhibitions that cater to the diverse needs of participants.
9. Provide resources for further learning: Offer access to additional resources, such as language learning apps, cultural publications, or online courses, to support participants in their continued learning journey.
