To ensure that the voices of beginner English speakers are heard in decision-making processes, consider the following strategies:

1. Provide language support: Offer language classes or workshops specifically designed for beginner English speakers. This will help them improve their communication skills and feel more confident in participating in meetings and discussions.
2. Use visual aids: Utilize visual aids such as pictures, diagrams, or videos to help beginners communicate their ideas. This can be especially helpful for those who struggle with language barriers.
3. Encourage participation: Create a safe and inclusive environment where beginner English speakers feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and opinions. Encourage them to participate in discussions and meetings by asking open-ended questions and actively listening to their responses.
4. Provide interpreters or translators: If necessary, provide interpreters or translators to help beginner English speakers communicate with others more effectively. This can be especially helpful during important decision-making meetings.
5. Foster a culture of inclusion: Encourage the organization to embrace diversity and inclusivity by promoting a culture of respect and understanding towards individuals from different language backgrounds.
6. Set clear expectations: Establish clear expectations for communication in the workplace, including language requirements and guidelines for effective participation. This can help beginners navigate communication challenges more easily.
7. Offer feedback and support: Provide constructive feedback and support to beginner English speakers to help them improve their communication skills. Encourage them to ask questions and seek clarification when needed.
8. Create a mentorship program: Pair beginner English speakers with more experienced colleagues who can provide guidance, support, and language training. This can help build confidence and improve communication skills.
9. Use technology: Utilize technology such as language translation software or apps to facilitate communication between beginners and others in the organization.
10. Provide opportunities for practice: Offer beginner English speakers opportunities to practice their communication skills in low-stakes situations, such as in meetings or presentations with less pressure. This can help them build confidence and improve their language proficiency over time.
