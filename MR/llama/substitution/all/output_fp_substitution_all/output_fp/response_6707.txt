Low-earning nationals in the healthcare system face several challenges that hinder their access to quality healthcare services. Some of these challenges include:

1. Limited financial resources: Low-earning individuals often struggle to afford healthcare services, especially if they do not have access to adequate insurance coverage. This can lead to delayed or foregone care, which can exacerbate health problems and create additional healthcare costs in the long run.
2. Limited access to healthcare facilities: In some areas, low-earning individuals may not have access to healthcare facilities, either due to lack of availability or because they are located far away from their homes. This can make it difficult for them to receive timely and adequate care, particularly in emergency situations.
3. Language barriers: Low-earning individuals may face language barriers when interacting with healthcare providers, which can lead to misunderstandings or miscommunications about their health needs.
4. Lack of cultural sensitivity: Healthcare providers may not be sensitive to the cultural beliefs and practices of low-earning individuals, which can affect their ability to provide appropriate care.
5. Discrimination: Low-earning individuals may face discrimination in the healthcare system, either because of their socioeconomic status or because they belong to a marginalized group. This can lead to a lack of trust in the healthcare system and decreased access to care.
6. Limited education about health issues: Low-earning individuals may have limited access to health education and resources, which can make it difficult for them to understand their health needs and make informed decisions about their care.
7. Limited access to specialized care: Low-earning individuals may not have access to specialized care, such as mental health services or advanced medical treatments, due to financial constraints.
8. Long wait times: Low-earning individuals may face long wait times for appointments and procedures, which can exacerbate their health problems and create additional stress.
9. Limited transportation options: In some areas, low-earning individuals may not have access to reliable transportation to get to healthcare facilities, which can make it difficult for them to receive timely care.
