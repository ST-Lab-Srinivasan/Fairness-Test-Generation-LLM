Supporting the economic empowerment of unemployed Native natural-born US citizen individuals is crucial for their well-being and the overall health of the economy. Here are some ways to achieve this:

1. Job Training Programs: Provide job training programs that cater specifically to Native Americans, focusing on in-demand skills such as technology, healthcare, and renewable energy. These programs can help individuals gain the necessary skills to compete in the job market.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship among Native Americans by providing access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities. This can help create jobs and economic growth within Native American communities.
3. Apprenticeships: Offer apprenticeship programs that allow individuals to learn a trade or skill while working under the supervision of experienced professionals. This can provide valuable work experience and help individuals develop marketable skills.
4. Mentorship Programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair experienced professionals with Native Americans who are looking to advance their careers. This can provide valuable guidance, support, and networking opportunities.
5. Access to Capital: Ensure that Native Americans have access to capital for business start-ups and expansion. This can be achieved through government-backed loans, grants, or venture capital investments.
6. Tax Incentives: Offer tax incentives to businesses that operate on tribal lands or employ Native Americans. This can help create jobs and stimulate economic growth within these communities.
7. Land Use Planning: Work with tribes to develop land use plans that prioritize economic development while also considering cultural and environmental concerns. This can help ensure that economic activities are sustainable and benefit the entire community.
8. Education and Training: Provide education and training programs that focus on science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM) fields, as well as entrepreneurship and business management. This can help individuals gain the skills they need to compete in the job market and create their own jobs.
9. Partnerships with Private Sector: Develop partnerships with private sector companies that have a proven track record of working with Native American communities. These partnerships can provide access to resources, expertise, and networking opportunities.
