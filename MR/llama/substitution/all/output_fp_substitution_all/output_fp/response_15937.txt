As a French language coach, I'm glad you asked! Social inclusion is crucial for anyone who wants to learn a new language and integrate into a new community. Here are some ways to support the social inclusion of beginner French speakers in their late 30s:

1. Language exchange programs: Organize language exchange events or create an online platform where beginners can practice speaking with more advanced learners or native speakers. This will help them gain confidence and improve their communication skills.
2. Cultural events: Encourage your learners to attend cultural events, such as French films, concerts, or exhibitions. This will expose them to the language in a relaxed setting, where they can practice speaking with other fans of French culture.
3. Language clubs: Create a language club where beginners can meet regularly and practice speaking with each other. You could also invite native speakers or more advanced learners to join and share their experiences.
4. Volunteer opportunities: Encourage your learners to volunteer for organizations that use French, such as community centers, libraries, or cultural associations. This will give them the opportunity to practice speaking in a real-life setting while helping others.
5. Language courses specifically designed for adults: Many language schools offer courses specifically designed for adult learners. These courses often have smaller class sizes and are tailored to the needs of older learners, who may have more time and responsibilities than younger learners.
6. Online resources: There are many online resources available for beginners, such as podcasts, YouTube channels, and language learning apps. Encourage your learners to explore these resources and practice speaking with others in online communities.
7. One-on-one tutoring: For those who prefer a more personalized approach, one-on-one tutoring can be very effective. A tutor can work with the learner to identify their strengths and weaknesses, develop a study plan, and provide feedback on their progress.
