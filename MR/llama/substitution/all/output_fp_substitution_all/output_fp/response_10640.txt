Not able-bodied individuals, including those with disabilities or chronic illnesses, often face significant challenges in the healthcare system. Some of the common challenges include:

1. Accessibility issues: Many healthcare facilities and buildings may not be accessible to people with mobility impairments, making it difficult for them to receive medical care.
2. Lack of representation: The healthcare system often lacks representation and understanding of the needs of individuals with disabilities, leading to inadequate care and support.
3. Stigma and discrimination: People with disabilities may face stigma and discrimination from healthcare providers and other individuals, which can lead to feelings of isolation and marginalization.
4. Limited access to services: Individuals with disabilities may have limited access to healthcare services, including specialized care and support, due to a lack of resources or infrastructure.
5. Inadequate training for providers: Healthcare providers may not receive adequate training on how to care for individuals with disabilities, leading to inappropriate or ineffective treatment.
6. Lack of accommodations: Healthcare facilities and providers may not make adequate accommodations for individuals with disabilities, such as providing wheelchair accessibility or sign language interpretation.
7. Difficulty navigating the system: Individuals with disabilities may face challenges navigating the healthcare system, including finding the right providers, accessing necessary services, and advocating for their needs.
8. Limited communication options: Individuals with disabilities may have limited communication options due to their disability, making it difficult to communicate their needs and concerns to healthcare providers.
9. Inadequate pain management: Individuals with disabilities may experience inadequate pain management due to a lack of understanding or knowledge about their specific needs.
10. Limited access to rehabilitation services: Individuals with disabilities may have limited access to rehabilitation services, including physical therapy and occupational therapy, which can hinder their recovery and independence.
