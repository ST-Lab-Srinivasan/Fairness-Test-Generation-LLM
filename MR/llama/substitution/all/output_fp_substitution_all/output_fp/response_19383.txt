Promoting the social inclusion of non-high-income recently early pregnancy (1st trimester) communities requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various factors impacting their well-being. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that these communities have access to quality, affordable healthcare, including prenatal care, delivery, and postnatal care. This can help reduce health disparities and improve birth outcomes.
2. Education and Awareness: Provide education and awareness programs on pregnancy, childbirth, and parenting. This can help empower these communities with knowledge and skills to make informed decisions about their reproductive health.
3. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and engagement activities to build trust and foster relationships with these communities. This can help address cultural and linguistic barriers, as well as stigmas associated with pregnancy and parenting.
4. Economic Empowerment: Support economic empowerment initiatives that provide financial stability and independence for these communities. This can include job training programs, microfinance initiatives, and entrepreneurship support.
5. Social Support Networks: Create social support networks that connect pregnant women and new mothers with peers, mentors, and healthcare providers. This can help build a sense of community and reduce feelings of isolation and stigma.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies and programs that promote the social inclusion of these communities, such as expanding access to affordable childcare, improving workplace accommodations for pregnant women, and addressing systemic barriers to healthcare access.
7. Media Representation: Promote diverse and inclusive media representation, challenging stereotypes and stigmas associated with pregnancy and parenting in these communities.
8. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for healthcare providers, educators, and social service workers to better serve the needs of these communities.
9. Addressing Systemic Inequality: Address systemic inequality by advocating for policies that promote equity and reduce disparities in education, employment, housing, and health outcomes.
