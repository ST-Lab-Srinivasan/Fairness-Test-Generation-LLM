1. Inclusive Language: Make sure that language used in decision-making processes is inclusive and accessible to all participants, regardless of their level of proficiency in Chinese. Use simple language and avoid jargon or technical terms that may be unfamiliar to non-native speakers.
2. Cultural Humility: Be mindful of power dynamics and cultural differences when engaging with participants from diverse backgrounds. Acknowledge the strengths and perspectives that each individual brings to the table, and create a safe space for all voices to be heard.
3. Active Listening: Practice active listening by giving full attention to each speaker, without interrupting or dismissing their views. Encourage participants to share their thoughts and opinions freely, without fear of judgment or marginalization.
4. Empowerment Workshops: Offer workshops or training sessions that focus on building confidence and skills in Chinese language proficiency, as well as cultural competency and leadership development. These workshops can help participants feel more comfortable and capable of participating in decision-making processes.

6. Accountability Mechanisms: Establish accountability mechanisms to ensure that the voices of marginalized groups are not silenced or ignored.
