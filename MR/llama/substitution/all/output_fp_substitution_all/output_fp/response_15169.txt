Addressing the specific needs of native English speaker recent refugees to the US in healthcare requires a comprehensive approach that considers their unique experiences, cultural backgrounds, and language barriers. Here are some strategies that healthcare providers can use to better serve this population:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cross-cultural training for healthcare staff on the specific needs and practices of recent refugees, including their cultural beliefs, values, and communication styles.
2. Language Accessibility: Ensure that interpretation services are available in a variety of languages, including Arabic, Dari, Farsi, and Somali, to facilitate effective communication between healthcare providers and patients.
3. Health Literacy Materials: Develop patient education materials in multiple languages, using clear and concise language, to help refugees understand their health conditions, treatments, and self-care instructions.
4. Refugee-Specific Services: Provide specialized services for refugees, such as mental health counseling, women's health care, and pediatric services, tailored to their unique needs and experiences.
5. Community Partnerships: Collaborate with local organizations that serve refugee populations, such as resettlement agencies, to leverage their expertise and resources in addressing the healthcare needs of recent refugees.
6. Patient Navigators: Train patient navigators who are fluent in the languages spoken by refugee populations and can help guide them through the healthcare system, answering questions and providing emotional support.
7. Technology-Based Solutions: Utilize technology, such as telemedicine and mobile health applications, to bridge language barriers and improve access to healthcare services for refugees who may face transportation or mobility challenges.
8. Health Promotion Programs: Develop targeted health promotion programs that address the specific health needs of recent refugees, such as nutrition education, stress management, and injury prevention.
