Ensuring that the housing needs of documented over 90-year-old individuals are met requires a comprehensive approach that addresses their unique challenges and requirements. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Aging-in-Place: Encourage and support older adults to age in place by providing home modifications and repair services, such as installing grab bars, non-slip surfaces, and widening doorways, to make their homes more accessible and safe.
2. Affordable Housing Options: Develop and maintain affordable housing options specifically designed for older adults, such as senior apartments or condominiums with amenities like on-site healthcare services, transportation, and social activities.
3. Home Sharing: Facilitate home sharing programs where older adults can live in their own homes while receiving supportive services from younger, able-bodied individuals. This can help to alleviate the financial burden of housing costs and provide a sense of community and social interaction.
4. Community Land Trusts: Establish community land trusts that allow older adults to purchase or rent homes at below-market rates while retaining ownership of the land. This can provide affordable and stable housing options for older adults.
5. Rent Subsidies: Offer rent subsidies or vouchers to help older adults pay for housing costs, particularly those living in market-rate apartments or condominiums.
6. Supportive Housing Programs: Develop and fund supportive housing programs that provide on-site services, such as case management, healthcare, and social activities, to help older adults maintain their independence and well-being.
7. Housing Counseling: Offer housing counseling services specifically designed for older adults, including financial planning, budgeting, and downsizing assistance.
8. Public-Private Partnerships: Collaborate with private sector partners to develop and manage affordable housing options specifically tailored to the needs of older adults.
9. Technical Assistance: Provide technical assistance and capacity building for nonprofit organizations that serve older adults, including training on best practices in aging-in-place, home modification, and supportive housing programs.
