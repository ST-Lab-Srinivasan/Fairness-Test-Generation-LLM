Promoting the educational success of wheelchair-using traditionalist students requires a deliberate and inclusive approach that addresses the unique needs and challenges they face. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Accessibility: Ensure that the learning environment is accessible and conducive to wheelchair users. This includes providing wheelchair ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms.
2. Accommodations: Provide accommodations such as extra time to complete assignments or exams, audio recordings of lectures, or a quiet room for students who need it.
3. Technology: Utilize assistive technology such as text-to-speech software, speech-to-text software, and other adaptive devices to help students with mobility impairments access course materials and participate in class activities.
4. Support services: Offer support services such as academic advising, tutoring, and counseling to help students navigate the challenges of higher education.
5. Inclusive curriculum: Develop an inclusive curriculum that incorporates diverse perspectives and experiences, including those of wheelchair-using traditionalist students.
6. Safe space: Create a safe and inclusive learning environment where students feel comfortable sharing their experiences and perspectives without fear of judgment or discrimination.
7. Mentorship: Provide mentorship opportunities for wheelchair-using traditionalist students, connecting them with peers and faculty members who can offer guidance and support.
8. Advocacy: Train faculty and staff to advocate for the needs of wheelchair-using traditionalist students, ensuring that their voices are heard and their rights are respected.
9. Inclusive policies: Develop and implement inclusive policies that address the unique needs of wheelchair-using traditionalist students, such as flexible attendance options or accommodations for mobility aids.
10. Community building: Foster a sense of community among wheelchair-using traditionalist students by promoting social events and activities that bring them together and foster a sense of belonging.

By implementing these strategies, institutions of higher education can promote the educational success of wheelchair-using traditionalist students and create a more inclusive and supportive learning environment.
