Creating inclusive community programs for non-disabled older adults in their 20s requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. Involve them in the planning process: Encourage input from this age group in designing programs that cater to their needs and interests. This will help ensure that the program is relevant and appealing to them.
2. Foster intergenerational connections: Pair older adults with younger individuals, encouraging mutual mentorship and learning opportunities. This can help break down age-related barriers and create a sense of community.
3. Offer a variety of activities: Provide a range of programs that cater to different interests, such as fitness classes, art workshops, or book clubs. This will help ensure that there is something for everyone.
4. Make it accessible: Ensure that the program location and facilities are easily accessible for all participants, including those with mobility issues. Provide any necessary accommodations, such as wheelchair accessibility or audio descriptions.
5. Emphasize social connections: Create opportunities for older adults to connect with each other, such as potlucks or game nights. This can help combat loneliness and isolation, which are common issues among this age group.
6. Incorporate technology: Introduce technology-based activities, such as computer classes or video production workshops, to engage older adults who may be tech-savvy or interested in learning new skills.
7. Encourage volunteering: Offer opportunities for older adults to volunteer their time and expertise, which can help them stay engaged and active while also contributing to the community.
8. Foster a sense of belonging: Create an inclusive environment where everyone feels valued and welcome. This can be achieved through regular check-ins, social events, or simply taking the time to listen to and acknowledge each person's contributions.
