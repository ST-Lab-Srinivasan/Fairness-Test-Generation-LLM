To support the economic empowerment of Native natural-born US citizen high earners, there are several strategies that can be implemented:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to educational and training programs that help high earners develop new skills and knowledge, such as entrepreneurship, financial planning, and leadership. This can help them grow their businesses or advance in their careers.
2. Mentorship and Networking: Connect high earners with successful business leaders and professionals who can provide guidance, support, and opportunities for collaboration. This can help them build valuable connections and expand their professional networks.
3. Access to Capital: Provide access to capital through grants, loans, or investments that can help high earners scale their businesses or pursue new ventures. This can be especially important for Native-owned businesses, which often face challenges accessing capital from traditional sources.
4. Tax Incentives: Offer tax incentives to encourage high earners to invest in their businesses or communities. For example, providing tax credits for hiring local talent or investing in community development projects can help stimulate economic growth.
5. Entrepreneurship Support: Provide resources and support for entrepreneurs, such as incubators, accelerators, and co-working spaces. This can help high earners turn their ideas into successful businesses and create jobs in their communities.
6. Community Development: Invest in community development projects that provide opportunities for high earners to give back and make a positive impact on their communities. This can include initiatives such as mentorship programs, scholarships, and infrastructure projects.
7. Government Contracting: Provide opportunities for Native-owned businesses to compete for government contracts, which can help them scale their operations and create jobs.
8. Workforce Development: Invest in workforce development programs that provide training and job placement services for high earners in Native communities. This can help address skill gaps and ensure that high earners have access to good-paying jobs.
9. Microfinance: Provide microfinance opportunities for high earners who may not have access to traditional sources of capital. This can include small loans or grants to help them start or grow their businesses.
