Promoting the artistic talents of beginner Chinese speakers in their late 30s can be a rewarding and enriching experience. Here are some suggestions on how to do so:

1. Encourage them to participate in language exchange events or activities: Organize or attend events where they can practice speaking Chinese with other beginners or native speakers. This will help them gain confidence in using the language and improve their communication skills.
2. Provide opportunities for creative expression: Encourage them to express themselves through various forms of art, such as writing, painting, drawing, or playing music. This can be done through workshops, classes, or simply by providing a platform for them to showcase their talents.
3. Collaborate with other organizations: Partner with local cultural institutions, language schools, or community centers to offer joint events or programs that promote artistic expression and Chinese language learning.
4. Create a supportive environment: Foster a sense of community among the individuals by providing a supportive and inclusive environment where they can learn and grow together. This can be done through regular meetups, social events, or online forums.
5. Offer structured learning opportunities: Provide structured learning opportunities, such as classes or workshops, that cater to their needs and interests. This can include topics such as Chinese culture, history, and literature, as well as practical language skills like listening, speaking, reading, and writing.
6. Encourage intergenerational exchange: Pair the beginner Chinese speakers with more experienced learners or native speakers who can share their knowledge and experiences. This can help create a mutually beneficial learning environment and foster cross-cultural understanding.
7. Provide resources and materials: Offer access to relevant resources and materials, such as language learning apps, podcasts, books, and videos, that can help them improve their Chinese language skills and cultural knowledge.
8. Celebrate their progress: Recognize and celebrate the individuals' progress and achievements, no matter how small, to boost their morale and motivation. This can be done through regular feedback sessions or special events like language proficiency tests or cultural presentations.
9. Encourage collaboration: Encourage the beginner Chinese speakers to collaborate with each other
