1. Culturally responsive teaching: Incorporate African American cultural references, histories, and perspectives into lesson plans to create a more inclusive learning environment. 2. Identity-based mentorship: Pair EFAA students with mentors who share their identity and experiences. These mentors can provide guidance and support based on their own experiences and cultural background, helping students navigate academic and social challenges specific to their community.
3. Cultural events and celebrations: Incorporate EFAA-specific cultural events and celebrations into the curriculum or school calendar. 4. Peer support networks: Create peer support networks specifically for EFAA students. These networks can provide emotional support and validation, as well as academic support through group studying or tutoring sessions.
