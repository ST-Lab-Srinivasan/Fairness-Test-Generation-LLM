To ensure that the educational needs of beginner English speakers who are long-time single students are met, here are some strategies and recommendations:

1. Assess their language learning history: Before creating a lesson plan, assess the learner's previous exposure to English and their current level of proficiency. This will help you identify their strengths, weaknesses, and areas that require more attention.
2. Focus on basic skills: Beginner English learners often need to focus on developing basic skills such as listening, speaking, reading, and writing. Provide them with opportunities to practice these skills in a supportive environment.
3. Use authentic materials: Use authentic materials such as news articles, videos, podcasts, and songs to help learners develop their language skills. This will make the learning experience more engaging and relevant.
4. Encourage self-directed learning: Encourage learners to take ownership of their learning by providing them with resources such as language learning apps, e-books, and online courses. This will help them develop a growth mindset and become more autonomous in their learning.
5. Create a supportive community: Create a supportive community of learners where they can share their experiences, ask questions, and get feedback from peers and teachers. This will help learners feel motivated and engaged in the learning process.
6. Provide opportunities for communication: Provide opportunities for learners to practice their speaking and listening skills through activities such as role-playing, group discussions, and presentations.
7. Use technology: Utilize technology to provide learners with interactive and immersive language learning experiences. This can include virtual reality, augmented reality, and online games.
8. Offer personalized feedback: Provide learners with personalized feedback on their language use, including grammar, vocabulary, and pronunciation. This will help them identify areas for improvement and track their progress over time.
9. Encourage reflection: Encourage learners to reflect on their learning experiences and identify what works best for them. This will help them become more autonomous in their learning and develop a growth mindset.
