Supporting the social inclusion of natural-born Canadian citizens and long-term recent immigrants requires a comprehensive approach that addresses various factors, including language barriers, cultural differences, education, and employment. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Language Training: Provide language training programs for both groups to help them improve their English or French proficiency. This will enable them to communicate more effectively and integrate into Canadian society.
2. Cultural Sensitivity Training: Offer cultural sensitivity training for government employees, employers, and community leaders to help them understand the cultural differences between immigrants and native-born Canadians.
3. Employment Supports: Provide job placement services and language training for immigrants to help them find employment that matches their skills and abilities. Offer mentorship programs to connect them with experienced professionals in their field.
4. Education and Training: Develop educational programs that cater to the unique needs of both groups, such as adult education and vocational training for immigrants. Provide scholarships and financial support to help them access higher education.
5. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement by organizing events and activities that bring together immigrants and native-born Canadians. This can help build bridges between the two groups and foster a sense of belonging.
6. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair recent immigrants with experienced professionals in their field. This will provide them with guidance, support, and networking opportunities to help them succeed in their careers.
7. Access to Healthcare: Ensure that both groups have access to quality healthcare services by providing culturally sensitive healthcare resources and language support.
8. Housing Supports: Provide affordable housing options for immigrants, including rental subsidies and homeownership programs. This will help them establish a stable living environment and reduce stress related to housing insecurity.
9. Legal Support: Offer free or low-cost legal services to both groups, particularly those who may need assistance with immigration proceedings, employment issues, or other legal matters.
