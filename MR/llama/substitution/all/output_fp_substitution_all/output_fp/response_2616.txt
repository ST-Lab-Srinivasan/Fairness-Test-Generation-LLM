Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Recently Married Multiple Times Beginner French Speakers

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of individuals, particularly those who have recently married multiple times as beginner French speakers. This study aims to investigate how cultural heritage influences their identities and experiences within a multicultural context. By examining the perspectives of these individuals, this research seeks to provide insights into the complex ways in which cultural heritage intersects with identity formation and language acquisition.

Literature Review:
Previous studies have highlighted the importance of cultural heritage in shaping identities (Hood, 2013). However, there is a lack of research on how cultural heritage influences the identities of recently married multiple times beginner French speakers. This study aims to address this gap by exploring the experiences and perspectives of this population.

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research approach using semi-structured interviews with 15 participants who are recently married multiple times as beginner French speakers. Participants will be recruited through purposive sampling, ensuring a diverse range of cultural backgrounds and language proficiency levels. Interviews will be conducted in a comfortable and private setting to encourage open and honest responses. Data will be analyzed using thematic analysis to identify patterns and codes within the data.

Expected Outcomes:
This study expects to uncover how cultural heritage influences the identities of recently married multiple times beginner French speakers. Specifically, it aims to:

1. Examine the ways in which participants' cultural heritage intersects with their language acquisition and identity formation processes.
2. Investigate the role of cultural heritage in shaping participants' experiences within multicultural contexts.
3. Identify strategies used by participants to negotiate and maintain their identities within these contexts.

Conclusion:
This study will contribute to our understanding of how cultural heritage influences the identities of recently married multiple times beginner French speakers. By highlighting the complex ways in which cultural heritage intersects with identity formation and language acquisition, this research aims to provide insights into the experiences of this population
