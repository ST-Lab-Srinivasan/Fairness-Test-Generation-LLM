Supporting the community engagement of low-earning immigrant individuals is crucial for their well-being and integration into society. Here are some ways to support them:

1. Language classes and cultural orientation: Provide access to language classes and cultural orientation programs to help them navigate their new environment and connect with the community.
2. Community centers and hubs: Establish community centers or hubs that provide a safe and inclusive space for immigrants to gather, socialize, and access resources.
3. Job training and placement services: Offer job training and placement services to help them acquire new skills and find employment opportunities.
4. Microfinance programs: Introduce microfinance programs that provide small loans or grants to help them start their own businesses or invest in education or housing.
5. Health and wellness services: Provide access to health and wellness services, including mental health support, nutrition counseling, and fitness classes.
6. Advocacy and mentorship: Match them with local advocates or mentors who can provide guidance on navigating the legal and social systems in their host country.
7. Intercultural dialogue and exchange programs: Organize intercultural dialogue and exchange programs to promote mutual understanding and respect between immigrant and host communities.
8. Access to education and training: Ensure they have access to quality education and training opportunities, including vocational training, apprenticeships, or higher education.
9. Childcare support: Provide childcare services or support to help them balance work and family responsibilities.
10. Social events and gatherings: Organize social events and gatherings that bring together immigrants and local communities, fostering a sense of belonging and community.
11. Public awareness campaigns: Launch public awareness campaigns to promote cultural diversity and inclusion, challenging stereotypes and prejudices.
