In terms of the challenges faced by undocumented immigrants in the job market, there are several factors at play:

2. Limited access to education and training: Undocumented immigrants may face barriers to accessing educational resources and training programs that can help them develop the skills and knowledge needed to compete in the job market.
3. Language barriers: Many undocumented immigrants may face language barriers that can make it difficult for them to communicate effectively with potential employers or colleagues.
4. High levels of poverty and economic insecurity: Undocumented immigrants often live in poverty and may face financial insecurity, which can make it difficult for them to find stable and well-paying jobs.
5. Fear of deportation: The constant threat of deportation can create a culture of fear and uncertainty among undocumented immigrants, which can impact their ability to seek employment or access support services.
