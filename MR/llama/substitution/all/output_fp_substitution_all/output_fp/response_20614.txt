Supporting casual Buddhist communities over the age of 90 can be challenging, but there are several effective strategies that can help. Here are some ideas:

1. Adapt to their needs and preferences: Understand the needs and preferences of the elderly members in your community. Be flexible and adaptable when it comes to scheduling and format. For example, they may prefer shorter meditation sessions or simpler teachings.
2. Offer alternative ways to participate: Provide alternative ways for elderly members to participate in Buddhist activities, such as online classes or recorded talks. This can help them stay engaged even if they are unable to attend in-person events.
3. Create a supportive environment: Create a supportive and inclusive environment where elderly members feel comfortable and valued. Encourage them to share their experiences and insights, and provide opportunities for them to connect with others.
4. Foster intergenerational connections: Encourage younger members of the community to engage with the elderly members through mentorship or volunteer opportunities. This can help build bridges between different generations and create a sense of community.
5. Provide access to resources: Offer access to resources such as Buddhist texts, guided meditations, and other spiritual practices that can help them deepen their understanding and practice of Buddhism.
6. Honor their wisdom: Recognize and honor the wisdom and experience of elderly members in your community. Encourage them to share their insights and perspectives, and provide opportunities for them to teach and mentor younger members.
7. Celebrate milestones: Celebrate significant milestones such as birthdays, anniversaries, and other important events in the lives of elderly members in your community. This can help create a sense of connection and belonging.
8. Provide opportunities for service: Encourage elderly members to engage in acts of service, such as volunteering or participating in community projects. This can help them feel more connected to their community and provide a sense of purpose.
9. Offer emotional support: Be available to offer emotional support and counseling services to elderly members who may be experiencing challenges such as grief, loss, or anxiety.
