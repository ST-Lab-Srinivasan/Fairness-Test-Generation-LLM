Ensuring that the voices of beginner Spanish speakers, particularly those from an ethnically full African American background, are heard in decision-making processes requires intentional efforts to create inclusive environments and provide opportunities for their participation. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Language-appropriate communication: Make sure that information is shared in a language that is accessible and comfortable for beginner Spanish speakers. Use simple language, avoid jargon, and provide visual aids when necessary.
2. Inclusive decision-making processes: Design decision-making processes that actively involve beginner Spanish speakers. This can be done by creating opportunities for them to share their thoughts and opinions, providing training on effective participation, and ensuring that their voices are heard and valued.
3. Cultural competency training: Offer cultural competency training for all decision-makers to help them understand the experiences and perspectives of beginner Spanish speakers. This can help build empathy and create a more inclusive environment.
4. Peer support networks: Establish peer support networks within the beginner Spanish speaker community. These networks can provide emotional support, practical advice, and a sense of belonging, which can help individuals feel more confident in their ability to participate in decision-making processes.
5. Mentorship programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair beginner Spanish speakers with experienced professionals who can provide guidance and support in navigating decision-making processes. This can help build confidence and provide valuable insights into the decision-making process.
6. Interpreters and translators: Provide interpreters or translators to facilitate communication between beginner Spanish speakers and decision-makers. This can help ensure that information is conveyed accurately and in a language that is comfortable for all parties involved.
7. Flexible participation options: Offer flexible participation options, such as virtual meetings or online forums, to accommodate the schedules and preferences of beginner Spanish speakers. This can help increase their participation in decision-making processes.
8. Building trust: Take steps to build trust among beginner Spanish speakers, such as being transparent about decision-making processes, providing regular updates, and showing respect for their opinions and perspectives.
9. Leadership opportunities: Provide leadership opportunities for beginner Spanish speakers within the community
