Supporting the social inclusion of physically able-bodied binary people involves creating an inclusive environment where they feel valued, respected, and supported. Here are some ways to achieve this:

1. Education and awareness: Provide education and awareness programs that promote understanding, empathy, and acceptance of diverse body types and abilities. Encourage people to recognize and challenge their own biases and stereotypes.
2. Accessibility and accommodations: Ensure that public spaces, workplaces, and other social environments are accessible and inclusive for people of all abilities. Provide accommodations such as wheelchair ramps, Braille signage, and assistive technology to support those with physical disabilities.
3. Inclusive language and communication: Use language and communicate in a way that is respectful and inclusive of all people, regardless of their abilities. Avoid using terms that perpetuate ableism or stigmatize certain body types.
4. Representation and role models: Provide representation and role models for physically able-bodied binary people in media, entertainment, and other areas of society. This can help promote visibility and acceptance of diverse body types and abilities.
5. Intersectionality and allyship: Recognize that social inclusion is intersectional and involves addressing the intersections of ableism with other forms of oppression such as racism, sexism, and homophobia. Be an ally to marginalized groups by listening, learning, and advocating for their rights and inclusion.
6. Community engagement and outreach: Engage with local communities and organizations to promote social inclusion and address ableism. Collaborate with disability organizations, community centers, and other stakeholders to create inclusive environments and programs.
7. Policy and advocacy: Advocate for policies and laws that promote accessibility, inclusion, and social equity for physically able-bodied binary people. Support disability rights organizations in their advocacy efforts to challenge discriminatory practices and promote inclusive policies.
8. Mental health support: Provide mental health support services specifically tailored to the needs of physically able-bodied binary people, recognizing that they may face unique challenges related to societal expectations and stigma
