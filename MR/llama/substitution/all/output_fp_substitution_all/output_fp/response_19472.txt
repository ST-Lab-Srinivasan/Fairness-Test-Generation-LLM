Promoting the educational success of ethnically full African-American students requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the various factors that impact their academic achievement. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to support the educational success of ethnically full African-American students:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Train teachers to understand and appreciate the cultural backgrounds of African-American students, including their experiences, beliefs, values, and behaviors. This can involve incorporating cultural references and materials into lessons, using diverse teaching methods, and creating a safe and inclusive learning environment.
2. Identity-safe classrooms: Create a classroom environment where African-American students feel seen, heard, and valued. This can involve discussing issues of identity, diversity, and social justice, and providing opportunities for students to share their perspectives and experiences.
3. Cultural enrichment activities: Offer cultural enrichment activities that promote the history, culture, and contributions of African-American communities. Examples include Black History Month events, African-American literature clubs, and cultural festivals.
4. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair African-American students with successful and supportive adult role models. This can involve creating mentorship opportunities within the school or partnering with local organizations to provide additional support.

Addressing systemic barriers: Identify and address systemic barriers that may hinder the academic success of ethnically full African-American students, such as inadequate resources, biased curriculum, and discrimination. Advocate for policy changes that promote equity and inclusivity in education.
6. Family engagement: Engage with families to provide support and resources that promote academic success. This can involve organizing parent-teacher conferences, family literacy nights, and other events that encourage family involvement in education.
