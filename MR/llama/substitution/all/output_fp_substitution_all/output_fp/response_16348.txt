Financially disadvantaged Native natural-born US citizen individuals face several challenges when navigating the healthcare system. These challenges can result from a combination of factors, including socioeconomic status, cultural barriers, and discrimination. Some of the specific challenges faced by this population include:

1. Limited access to healthcare services: Financially disadvantaged Native individuals may struggle to afford healthcare services, including preventive care, medical treatments, and prescription medications. This can lead to delayed or forgone care, which can exacerbate existing health conditions and create new ones.
2. Language barriers: Many Native communities have unique languages and cultural practices that can create barriers to accessing healthcare services. Healthcare providers may not be familiar with these cultures, leading to misunderstandings or miscommunications that can affect care quality.
3. Cultural barriers: Native individuals may face cultural barriers when seeking healthcare services. For example, some may feel uncomfortable discussing personal health issues with providers who are not knowledgeable about their culture and beliefs. Others may experience discrimination or stigma based on their race or ethnicity.
4. Lack of trust: Financially disadvantaged Native individuals may have had negative experiences with the healthcare system in the past, leading to a lack of trust in providers and the healthcare system as a whole. This can make it difficult for them to seek care when they need it.
5. Limited health insurance coverage: Many Native individuals face barriers to obtaining affordable health insurance, which can limit their access to healthcare services. This can be particularly challenging in rural areas where healthcare resources may be scarce.
6. Health disparities: Financially disadvantaged Native populations may experience worse health outcomes than other populations due to a range of factors, including limited access to healthy foods and exercise opportunities, exposure to environmental hazards, and inadequate healthcare services.
