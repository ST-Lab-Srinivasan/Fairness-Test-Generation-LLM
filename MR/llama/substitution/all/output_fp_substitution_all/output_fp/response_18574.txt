Promoting the social inclusion of Native American recent refugees in low-income communities in the United States requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses the unique challenges and barriers faced by this population. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training to service providers, educators, and community leaders to help them understand the customs, traditions, and values of Native American refugees. This will enable them to provide more effective support and services tailored to the needs of this population.
2. Language Support: Offer language support services such as interpreters, translators, and language classes to help Native American refugees navigate the healthcare, education, and social service systems.
3. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and engagement activities to raise awareness about the challenges faced by Native American refugees and build bridges between this population and other low-income communities. This can be done through events, workshops, and initiatives that promote cross-cultural understanding and dialogue.
4. Economic Empowerment: Provide economic empowerment opportunities such as job training, entrepreneurship support, and access to small business loans to help Native American refugees start their own businesses or find employment in the local economy.
5. Housing Support: Offer housing support services such as emergency shelter, transitional housing, and permanent housing to help Native American refugees secure stable housing and address any housing-related challenges they may face.
6. Health Services: Provide access to culturally competent healthcare services, including mental health support, to help Native American refugees address any health-related issues they may encounter.
7. Education Support: Offer educational support services such as tutoring, mentorship, and access to educational resources to help Native American refugee children succeed in school.
8. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that benefit Native American refugees, such as increased funding for refugee resettlement programs, and legal protections against discrimination and xenophobia.
