Promoting economic empowerment in any community, including those that are considered "extreme" or "soft" socialist, requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses various factors. Here are some strategies that can be implemented to promote economic empowerment in these communities:

1. Capacity Building and Skills Development: Provide training and capacity building programs for community members on various skills such as entrepreneurship, financial management, and business planning. This will help them develop the necessary skills to start and grow their own businesses.
2. Access to Finance: Ensure that community members have access to affordable and responsible financial services, including loans, savings accounts, and insurance. This can be achieved through partnerships with microfinance institutions or banks that specialize in serving marginalized communities.
3. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage and support entrepreneurship by providing mentorship, training, and resources to help community members start and grow their own businesses. This can include access to incubators, accelerators, and co-working spaces.
4. Job Creation: Work with local organizations and government agencies to create job opportunities in the community. This can include initiatives such as job training programs, apprenticeships, and employment placement services.
5. Community Development: Invest in community development projects that promote economic growth and improve living standards. Examples of such projects include infrastructure development, agricultural projects, and small-scale industrialization.
6. Social Enterprise Support: Encourage and support social enterprises that address local issues and provide goods and services that benefit the community. This can include funding, mentorship, and access to markets.
7. Policy Advocacy: Advocate for policies that promote economic empowerment in the community, such as tax incentives, subsidies, and other forms of support.
8. Collaboration with Local Government: Work closely with local government agencies to identify opportunities for collaboration and joint initiatives that can benefit the community.
9. Access to Markets: Provide access to markets for community members to sell their products and services, either through physical marketplaces or online platforms.
