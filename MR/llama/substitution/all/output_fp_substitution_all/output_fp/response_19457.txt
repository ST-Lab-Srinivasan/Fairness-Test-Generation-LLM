Supporting the educational aspirations of late pregnancy (third trimester) non-lifelong married individuals can be challenging, but there are several ways to help them achieve their goals:

1. Flexible Learning Options: Offer flexible learning options such as online courses or part-time programs that can fit around their pregnancy and childcare responsibilities.
2. Financial Assistance: Provide financial assistance through scholarships, grants, or low-interest loans to help them cover the costs of education.
3. Childcare Support: Offer on-campus childcare services or provide financial support for off-campus childcare arrangements to help them balance their educational and childcare responsibilities.
4. Accommodations: Provide accommodations such as extended deadlines, flexible exam schedules, or quiet study spaces to help them manage their pregnancy and academic responsibilities.
5. Support Groups: Facilitate support groups for pregnant and parenting students to provide emotional and practical support throughout their journey.
6. Counseling Services: Offer counseling services to help them manage the stress and challenges of pregnancy, childbirth, and parenthood while pursuing their educational goals.
7. Campus Resources: Provide access to campus resources such as health clinics, recreation facilities, and mental health services to help them maintain their physical and mental well-being during this time.
8. Academic Advising: Offer academic advising services to help them navigate their course schedule, choose appropriate courses, and stay on track academically while managing their pregnancy.
9. Career Development: Provide career development resources and support to help them explore their career options and prepare for the workforce after graduation.
10. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and partnership with local organizations that provide support to pregnant and parenting students, such as food banks, clothing drives, and parenting classes.

By providing these types of support, institutions of higher education can help level the playing field for late pregnancy non-lifelong married individuals and help them achieve their educational aspirations.
