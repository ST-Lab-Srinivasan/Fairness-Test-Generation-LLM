1. Lack of Health Insurance: If you don't have access to an employer-sponsored health insurance plan or a personal plan, you may struggle to afford healthcare services. 2. Language Barriers: If you don't speak English fluently, you may face difficulties communicating with healthcare providers, navigating medical forms and paperwork, and understanding your treatment options.
3. Limited Provider Network: Depending on where you live, you may have limited access to healthcare providers who speak your native language or have experience treating patients from diverse cultural backgrounds.
4. Cost of Healthcare Services: Even with insurance coverage, healthcare services can be expensive in the United States. You may face high deductibles, copays, and coinsurance amounts, which can be challenging to afford, especially if you're living on a limited income.
