Promoting social inclusion of ethnic minority communities, including those living in poverty, is crucial for creating a fair and just society. Here are some strategies that can help promote the social inclusion of ethnically full Asian deep poverty communities:

1. Education and Awareness: Providing access to quality education and raising awareness about the issues faced by these communities can help reduce stigma and discrimination. This can be done through community-based programs, workshops, and public campaigns.
2. Economic Empowerment: Encouraging entrepreneurship and providing access to microfinance opportunities can help these communities break the cycle of poverty. Governments and organizations can provide training, mentorship, and funding to support economic empowerment.
3. Access to Healthcare: Ensuring access to quality healthcare is essential for promoting social inclusion. Governments and organizations can work towards providing affordable healthcare services, especially in underserved areas.
4. Community Development: Investing in community development projects that promote inclusive growth can help reduce poverty and improve living standards. This can include infrastructure development, vocational training, and support for local businesses.
5. Addressing Systemic Issues: Policymakers must address systemic issues such as discrimination, inequality, and lack of access to resources that perpetuate poverty in these communities. This can involve policy changes, legal reforms, and advocacy initiatives.
6. Building Partnerships: Collaboration between government agencies, civil society organizations, and private sector actors is crucial for promoting social inclusion. These partnerships can help leverage resources, expertise, and influence to address the complex issues faced by these communities.
7. Advocacy and Activism: Advocacy and activism can help raise awareness about the issues faced by ethnic minority communities and pressure policymakers to take action. This can involve campaigns, protests, and lobbying efforts to push for policy changes and legal reforms.
8. Celebrating Cultural Diversity: Promoting cultural diversity and celebrating the unique contributions of ethnic minority communities can help reduce stigma and promote social inclusion. This can involve cultural events, festivals, and initiatives that showcase the richness of these communities' cultures
