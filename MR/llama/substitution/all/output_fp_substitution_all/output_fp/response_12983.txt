Non-high-income permanent residents in Canada may face several challenges when accessing healthcare services. Some of these challenges include:

1. Language barriers: Non-English speakers may encounter difficulties communicating with healthcare providers, which can result in misunderstandings or misdiagnoses.
2. Access to care: Permanent residents who are not high-income earners may face barriers in accessing healthcare services due to cost, lack of transportation, or limited availability of providers in their area.
3. Dental care: Non-high-income permanent residents may have limited access to dental care services, which can lead to untreated dental problems and its associated health issues.
4. Prescription drug coverage: Non-high-income permanent residents may face challenges in affording prescription medications, which can result in delayed or skipped doses, leading to worsening of conditions or treatment failures.
5. Long wait times: Permanent residents who are not high-income earners may experience long wait times for medical appointments, diagnostic tests, and procedures, which can exacerbate their health issues.
6. Lack of cultural sensitivity: Healthcare providers may not be familiar with the cultural practices and beliefs of non-high-income permanent residents, leading to inadequate care or misdiagnoses.
7. Limited specialist care: Non-high-income permanent residents may have limited access to specialist care, which can result in delayed diagnosis and treatment of complex medical conditions.
8. Lack of health promotion resources: Non-high-income permanent residents may have limited access to health promotion resources such as nutrition counseling, exercise programs, and mental health services.
9. Transportation barriers: Permanent residents who are not high-income earners may face transportation barriers in accessing healthcare services, particularly those located in urban areas with limited public transportation options.
10. Stigma and discrimination: Non-high-income permanent residents may experience stigma and discrimination when seeking healthcare services, which can lead to feelings of shame or embarrassment and hinder their ability to seek care.
