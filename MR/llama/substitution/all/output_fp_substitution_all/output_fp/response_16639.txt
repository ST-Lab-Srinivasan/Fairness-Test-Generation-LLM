Creating more inclusive community events for native French speakers, particularly those in the 90s and older age group, requires careful consideration of their needs and preferences. Here are some suggestions to make your events more accessible and enjoyable for this demographic:

1. Provide clear communication: Ensure that all event information is provided in a clear and simple manner. Use large font sizes, high contrast colors, and clear language to make it easy for older attendees to read and understand.
2. Offer audio descriptions or sign language interpretation: For those who are deaf or hard of hearing, provide audio descriptions or sign language interpretation during the event. This will allow them to fully participate in the activities and enjoy the experience without any barriers.
3. Accommodate mobility needs: Ensure that the event location is easily accessible for people with mobility issues. Provide adequate seating, wheelchair accessibility, and clear paths for those who may need assistance.
4. Create a quiet space: For those who are sensitive to noise or overstimulation, provide a quiet space where they can retreat if needed. This could be a designated area with comfortable seating or a quieter part of the event location.
5. Offer gentle exercise activities: Older adults may have limited mobility or energy levels, so offer gentle exercise activities that are low-impact and enjoyable for them. Examples could include chair yoga, tai chi, or walking groups.
6. Incorporate intergenerational activities: Pair older attendees with younger generations to facilitate communication and connection. This could involve joint crafting or art projects, playing games together, or simply sharing stories and experiences.
7. Provide nutritious food options: Older adults may have specific dietary needs or restrictions, so ensure that the event provides a variety of nutritious food options that cater to these requirements. Avoid serving heavy or rich foods that could be difficult for them to digest.
8. Offer sensory-friendly activities: Some older adults may have sensory processing issues, so incorporate activities that are calming and enjoyable for them. Examples could include gentle music performances, aromatherapy, or tactile crafting projects.
