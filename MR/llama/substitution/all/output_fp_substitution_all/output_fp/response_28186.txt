There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of lower-income natural-born US citizens who are not upper middle-income:

1. Access to Education and Training: Providing access to quality education and training programs can help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge, which can lead to better job opportunities and higher earning potential. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and degree programs in fields such as technology, healthcare, and business.
2. Microfinance and Entrepreneurship: Providing access to microfinance and entrepreneurship opportunities can help individuals start their own businesses, which can lead to greater economic independence and mobility. This can include loans, grants, and mentorship programs.
3. Job Creation and Retraining: Governments and private sector organizations can work together to create job opportunities and provide training for workers who are displaced or underemployed due to automation or other factors. This can include on-the-job training, apprenticeships, and vocational training.
4. Progressive Taxation: Implementing a progressive tax system where the wealthy pay a higher percentage of their income than the lower-income individuals can help reduce income inequality and generate revenue for social welfare programs that support economic empowerment.
5. Social Safety Nets: Providing social safety nets such as unemployment insurance, food assistance programs, and housing subsidies can help individuals and families who are struggling financially.
6. Addressing Discrimination and Inequality: Addressing systemic discrimination and inequality in the economy can help ensure that everyone has an equal opportunity to succeed. This can include policies to address discrimination based on race, gender, sexual orientation, and other factors.
7. Investment in Infrastructure and Public Services: Investing in infrastructure and public services such as transportation, energy, water, and telecommunications can help create jobs and improve living standards, particularly in disadvantaged communities.
8. Encouraging Corporate Social Responsibility: Encouraging businesses to adopt corporate social responsibility practices such as providing fair wages, offering benefits, and supporting community development can help promote economic empowerment.
