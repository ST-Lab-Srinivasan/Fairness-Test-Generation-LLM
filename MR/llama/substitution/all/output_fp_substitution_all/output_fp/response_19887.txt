Promoting the educational success of Native English Speaker (NES) students in the first trimester of pregnancy can be challenging, but there are several strategies that educators and parents can use to support these students. Here are some suggestions:

1. Create a safe and inclusive learning environment: NES students may feel self-conscious or embarrassed about their pregnancy, so it's essential to create an atmosphere where they feel comfortable and supported. Educators can encourage open discussions about pregnancy and parenthood without judgment, and provide resources for students who need extra support.
2. Provide individualized learning plans: Pregnancy can affect a student's ability to focus or attend classes, so it's important to offer flexible learning options. Educators can work with NES students to develop individualized learning plans that accommodate their needs and allow them to progress at their own pace.
3. Offer additional resources: Many NES students may need extra support during pregnancy, such as tutoring or counseling services. Educators can connect these students with on-campus resources or community organizations that provide these services.
4. Encourage self-care: Pregnancy can be a challenging time for students, both physically and emotionally. Educators can encourage NES students to prioritize self-care by practicing stress-reducing techniques, such as meditation or deep breathing exercises.
5. Foster a sense of community: Pregnancy can be isolating for some students, so it's important to foster a sense of community and connection among NES students. Educators can facilitate group discussions or activities that promote bonding and mutual support.
6. Address any accommodations needed: Depending on the individual needs of the student, educators may need to provide accommodations such as extra time to complete assignments or a quiet space for studying. It's important to work with the student to identify their specific needs and provide appropriate accommodations.
7. Provide pregnancy-related resources: Educators can provide NES students with resources related to pregnancy, such as books, websites, or support groups. This can help students feel more informed and empowered during this time.
