Supporting the social inclusion of non-upper middle-income, non-independent individuals can be achieved through various means. Here are some ways to promote their social inclusion:

1. Education and Training: Providing access to education and training programs can help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge, which can enhance their employability and social status. This can include vocational training, job readiness programs, and entrepreneurship development initiatives.
2. Employment Opportunities: Creating job opportunities in various sectors, such as manufacturing, agriculture, and services, can help reduce unemployment and poverty rates among non-upper middle-income individuals. This can be achieved through public-private partnerships, job fairs, and entrepreneurship support programs.
3. Microfinance: Providing microloans and other financial services can help individuals start or grow their businesses, which can improve their economic status and social inclusion. This can be done through microfinance institutions, cooperatives, and government-supported initiatives.
4. Healthcare Access: Ensuring access to quality healthcare can improve the overall well-being of individuals and communities, reducing health disparities and improving social inclusion. This can be achieved through public health programs, community health workers, and partnerships with NGOs and private organizations.
5. Social Housing: Providing affordable housing options can help reduce housing poverty and improve social inclusion. This can be achieved through government-subsidized housing programs, non-profit organizations, and public-private partnerships.
6. Community Development: Investing in community development projects, such as infrastructure improvements, cultural events, and social services, can help build strong, cohesive communities that promote social inclusion. This can be achieved through community-led initiatives, government programs, and partnerships with NGOs and private organizations.
7. Advocacy and Awareness: Raising awareness about the challenges faced by non-upper middle-income individuals and advocating for their rights can help promote social inclusion. This can be achieved through media campaigns, public events, and partnerships with civil society organizations.
