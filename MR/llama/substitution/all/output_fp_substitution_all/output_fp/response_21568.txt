Promoting the economic empowerment of very young (90s+) communities, particularly those who are at risk of early pregnancy, is crucial for their overall well-being and development. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Education and Life Skills Training: Provide access to quality education, vocational training, and life skills programs that equip young people with the knowledge and skills needed to succeed in the workforce. This can include literacy and numeracy classes, vocational training, and entrepreneurship training.
2. Access to Financial Services: Facilitate access to financial services such as savings accounts, microfinance loans, and insurance products. This can help young people to manage their finances effectively, start small businesses, and protect themselves against economic shocks.
3. Job Creation and Entrepreneurship Support: Create job opportunities and provide support for young entrepreneurs to start and grow their businesses. This can include mentorship programs, training and capacity-building activities, and access to capital and resources.
4. Community Development: Invest in community development projects that create jobs, improve living standards, and promote social cohesion. This can include infrastructure development, healthcare services, and social welfare programs.
5. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policies and regulations that support the economic empowerment of young people. This can include changes to laws related to education, employment, and financial inclusion.
6. Community Mobilization: Mobilize communities to take action and advocate for policies and practices that promote the economic empowerment of young people. This can include community-based campaigns, awareness-raising activities, and grassroots movements.
7. Partnerships and Collaborations: Foster partnerships and collaborations between government agencies, private sector organizations, civil society groups, and community-based organizations to create a comprehensive approach to promoting economic empowerment.
8. Technology Innovation: Leverage technology innovation to provide access to financial services, job opportunities, and educational resources for young people. This can include mobile banking, online marketplaces, and digital learning platforms.
