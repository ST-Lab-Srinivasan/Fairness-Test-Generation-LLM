Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of long-term refugees, including native Arabic speakers. It provides a sense of identity, belonging, and connection to their homeland and culture. In this answer, we will explore the role of cultural heritage in the lives of long-term refugee native Arabic speakers and its impact on their mental and emotional well-being.

1. Sense of Identity:
Cultural heritage helps long-term refugees maintain a sense of identity and belonging. It provides a connection to their homeland, culture, and community, which is essential for psychological well-being. Refugees often face challenges adapting to a new environment, language barriers, discrimination, and social isolation. Cultural heritage helps them maintain a sense of self and belonging, which can mitigate the negative effects of displacement.
2. Emotional Support:
Cultural heritage serves as an emotional support system for long-term refugees. It provides a sense of comfort, security, and familiarity in an unfamiliar environment. Refugees often experience trauma, anxiety, and depression due to displacement. Cultural heritage can help them cope with these emotions by providing a sense of continuity and stability.
3. Preservation of Traditions:
Cultural heritage preserves traditional practices, customs, and beliefs that are vital for long-term refugees' mental and emotional well-being. These traditions provide a connection to their past and cultural identity, which can help them navigate the challenges of displacement. For example, refugees may preserve traditional music, dance, or art as a way to express themselves and connect with their heritage.
4. Community Building:
Cultural heritage serves as a foundation for community building among long-term refugees. It provides a common language, shared values, and cultural practices that can help build social connections and networks. Refugees can come together to celebrate cultural events, share stories, and maintain their cultural traditions. This collective heritage can foster a sense of belonging and social support, which is crucial for mental health and well-being.
