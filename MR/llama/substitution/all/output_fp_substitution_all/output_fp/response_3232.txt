Use group discussions, think-pair-share activities, and other methods to encourage participation from all students, including those who may be less vocal.
3. Offer differentiated instruction: Recognize that each student has unique strengths, needs, and learning styles. Provide multiple learning paths and activities to cater to different learning needs, such as visual, auditory, and kinesthetic learners.
4. Incorporate authentic materials: Use real-life examples and authentic materials (e.g., news articles, videos, podcasts) to make the learning experience more engaging and relevant for native French speakers. This can help them connect with the content and develop a deeper understanding of the language.
