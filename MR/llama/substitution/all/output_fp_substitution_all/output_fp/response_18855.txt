Creating more inclusive cultural events for moderately abled individuals requires thoughtful planning and consideration of their needs. Here are some suggestions to make your event more accessible and enjoyable for this group:

1. Provide accommodations: Offer accommodations such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, or audio descriptions for attendees who may have difficulty navigating the venue or understanding visual content.
2. Involve diverse talent: Showcase artists and performers with disabilities in your event to highlight their talents and contributions. This can help create a more inclusive atmosphere and encourage greater representation of marginalized communities.
3. Accessible programming: Design the event's programming to be accessible to all attendees, including workshops, lectures, or performances that cater to different abilities. Offer activities that can be participated in by individuals with a range of abilities, such as interactive storytelling or art-making.
4. Communication accessibility: Ensure that all event communications, such as flyers, posters, and announcements, are accessible to attendees with visual or hearing impairments. Use clear language and provide necessary information in multiple formats (e.g., braille, large print, audio).
5. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by minimizing potential triggers, such as loud noises or bright lights. Offer quiet areas or fidget toys for attendees who may feel overwhelmed.
6. Support staff: Hire support staff, such as event coordinators or cultural interpreters, who can provide additional assistance and support to attendees with disabilities.
7. Inclusive marketing: Promote the event through inclusive marketing materials that highlight the accessibility features of the event. Use language that is welcoming and inclusive, and feature images or videos of diverse individuals participating in the event.
8. Accessible seating: Provide accessible seating options, such as wheelchair-accessible seating areas or removable seats for attendees who may need extra space.
