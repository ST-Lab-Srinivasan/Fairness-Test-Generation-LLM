Ensuring the voices of beginner Chinese speakers, particularly those who have been long-term divorced and isolated from social and cultural norms, is crucial in decision-making processes. Here are some strategies to help amplify their voices:

1. Create inclusive spaces: Establish regular gatherings or events where these individuals can come together and feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and opinions. This could be a book club, a language exchange group, or even an online community.
2. Use plain language: When communicating with these individuals, use simple language and avoid jargon or technical terms that may be unfamiliar to them. Be patient and allow for extra time to communicate complex ideas.
3. Empathize and validate feelings: Acknowledge the challenges they have faced and the barriers they have encountered in their journey. Listen actively and show empathy towards their experiences, which can help build trust and rapport.
4. Offer support and resources: Provide access to language learning materials, cultural events, or other opportunities that can help them feel more connected and engaged. This could include language classes, cultural workshops, or even financial assistance for education or training.
5. Involve them in decision-making processes: Encourage these individuals to participate in decision-making processes related to their own lives, such as choosing activities or events they would like to attend. By involving them in the decision-making process, they feel more invested and empowered in their choices.
6. Use visual aids: For those who may have difficulty expressing themselves verbally, use visual aids such as pictures, diagrams, or even gestures to help communicate ideas. This can help bridge the gap between communication styles and ensure that their voices are heard.
7. Provide opportunities for leadership: Encourage these individuals to take on leadership roles within the community or group. This could be in organizing events, leading discussions, or simply being a mentor to others. By providing opportunities for leadership, they can develop their confidence and skills, while also feeling more connected to the group.
8. Foster peer-to-peer support: Encourage mutual support among these individuals through peer-to-peer interactions. This could involve pairing them with a language partner or cultural mentor who shares similar experiences and challenges
