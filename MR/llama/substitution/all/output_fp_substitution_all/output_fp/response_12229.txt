There are several ways to support the economic empowerment of mixed African American individuals who are unemployed or underemployed:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to vocational training, job readiness programs, and educational opportunities that can help them acquire new skills and knowledge to enhance their employability.
2. Mentorship and Networking: Connect them with successful professionals in their desired field who can provide guidance, mentorship, and valuable connections.
3. Access to Capital: Provide access to funding or financial resources that can help them start a business or invest in a new career opportunity.
4. Job Placement Services: Offer job placement services that cater specifically to mixed African Americans, helping them find employment opportunities that match their skills and interests.
5. Advocacy and Advocacy Training: Provide training and resources to help them advocate for themselves and their communities in the workplace and beyond.
6. Business Development: Offer support and resources to help them start or grow their own businesses, such as entrepreneurship training, access to capital, and mentorship.
7. Housing Support: Provide assistance with housing costs, such as rent or mortgage payments, to help reduce financial stress and increase stability.
8. Healthcare Support: Offer healthcare services and resources to help them access quality medical care and mental health support.
9. Childcare Support: Provide childcare services or resources to help them balance work and family responsibilities.
10. Community Building: Create opportunities for social connection and community building, such as cultural events, networking receptions, and volunteer opportunities.
11. Advocacy for Inclusive Policies: Advocate for policies that promote diversity, equity, and inclusion in the workplace and society at large.
12. Leadership Development: Provide training and resources to help them develop leadership skills and confidence, which can help them advance in their careers and contribute to their communities.
13. Legal Support: Offer legal services and resources to help them navigate the legal system and protect their rights.
