Title: Exploring the Impacts of Political Affiliation on the Lives of Native Chinese Speaker Elderly Adults in Their 20s

Introduction:
The political landscape of China has undergone significant changes in recent years, with the Communist Party of China (CPC) maintaining its grip on power while also introducing reforms to address growing social and economic challenges. As a result, the lives of elderly adults in their 20s are being shaped by their political affiliation, which can have both positive and negative impacts. This study aims to investigate these impacts and explore how they affect the daily lives of native Chinese speaker elderly adults.

Background:
Political affiliation has long been an important factor in shaping the lives of elderly adults in China. The CPC has been the dominant political force since 1949, and its ideology has influenced various aspects of society, including education, healthcare, and social welfare. However, recent years have seen growing dissatisfaction among some groups, particularly the elderly, due to issues such as corruption, economic inequality, and lack of political freedom.

Research Questions:

1. How do native Chinese speaker elderly adults in their 20s perceive their political affiliation?
2. What are the impacts of political affiliation on the daily lives of these elderly adults?
3. How does political affiliation affect their social and economic well-being, as well as their mental and physical health?

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research design, using in-depth interviews to collect data from 20 native Chinese speaker elderly adults in their 20s. Participants will be selected purposively based on their political affiliation, with equal numbers of CPC members and non-CPC members included in the sample. Interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim, with thematic analysis used to identify patterns and trends in the data.
