Economic empowerment of Native American recent green card holders and upper lower middle-income individuals in the US can be supported through various means. Here are some potential strategies:

1. Access to Capital: Many Native American communities face barriers to accessing capital for businesses or investments, due to a lack of traditional banking infrastructure or limited access to credit. Organizations such as Native American Community Development Institute (NACDI) and the Inter tribal Council of Michigan provide financing and technical assistance to support Native American entrepreneurs.
2. Business Development Programs: Offer training and resources to help Native Americans start or grow their businesses, including mentorship opportunities with successful Native American entrepreneurs. Organizations such as the National Center for American Indian Enterprise Development (NCAIED) provide these types of programs.
3. Job Training and Placement: Provide job training and placement services specifically tailored to the needs of Native Americans, including cultural competency training for employers. Organizations such as the Native American Career Developments (NACD) offer these types of programs.
4. Homeownership Programs: Offer financial assistance and support for Native Americans to purchase or repair homes in their communities, which can help build wealth and stability. Organizations such as the Native American Housing Assistance and Self-Determination Act (NAHASDA) provide these types of programs.
5. Education and Training: Provide educational resources and training opportunities specifically tailored to the needs of Native Americans, including language and cultural competency training for educators. Organizations such as the Bureau of Indian Affairs (BIA) offer these types of programs.
6. Microfinance Programs: Offer microfinance loans and other financial services specifically designed to support the economic empowerment of Native American communities, including savings accounts, credit building programs, and entrepreneurship training. Organizations such as the Native American Microfinance Program (NAMFP) provide these types of programs.
7. Community Development Initiatives: Support community development initiatives that promote economic growth and stability in Native American communities, such as cooperative businesses, community land trusts, and cultural preservation projects. Organizations such as the Native American Rights Fund (NARF) provide these types of initiatives.
