The impact of disability status on the lives of wheelchair-using individuals can be significant and far-reaching. Here are some of the common challenges and experiences faced by wheelchair-using individuals:

1. Social isolation: People with disabilities, including those who use wheelchairs, may experience social isolation due to discrimination, stigma, or lack of accessibility in public spaces. This can lead to feelings of loneliness, depression, and anxiety.
2. Limited accessibility: Inaccessible buildings, transportation, and public spaces can limit the opportunities and experiences available to wheelchair-using individuals. This can include difficulty accessing education, employment, healthcare, and other essential services.
3. Stigma and discrimination: People with disabilities, including those who use wheelchairs, may face stigma and discrimination in various aspects of life, including education, employment, and social interactions. This can lead to lower self-esteem, self-confidence, and overall well-being.
4. Health disparities: Wheelchair-using individuals may experience health disparities due to lack of access to appropriate healthcare services, inadequate health education, and limited mobility. This can result in higher rates of morbidity and mortality compared to the able-bodied population.
5. Financial challenges: Disability can lead to increased financial burdens, including higher costs for assistive technology, home modifications, and healthcare expenses. Additionally, wheelchair-using individuals may face discrimination in employment and education, leading to lower earning potential and economic stability.
6. Limited participation in society: Wheelchair-using individuals may face barriers to participating in social, cultural, and political activities due to inaccessible environments and lack of accommodations. This can lead to feelings of disconnection and marginalization from society.
7. Emotional impact: Living with a disability can have a significant emotional impact on an individual's self-esteem, self-worth, and overall mental health. The constant struggle to navigate inaccessible environments and the stigma associated with disability can lead to depression, anxiety, and other mental health issues.
