Deep in poverty long-term refugees face significant challenges in accessing quality healthcare. Here are some of the common challenges they face:

1. Limited access to healthcare services: Refugees often have limited access to healthcare services due to language barriers, lack of transportation, and cultural differences. They may also face discrimination from healthcare providers, which can make it difficult for them to seek medical care.
2. Lack of cultural competency: Healthcare providers may not be familiar with the refugees' culture, beliefs, and practices, which can lead to misunderstandings and mistrust. Refugees may also have different expectations and needs than other patients, which can create challenges in providing adequate care.
3. Limited health insurance coverage: Many refugees do not have access to health insurance, which can make it difficult for them to afford medical care. Even if they have insurance, the coverage may be limited or of poor quality, leaving them vulnerable to financial burdens.
4. Trauma and mental health issues: Refugees often experience trauma and mental health issues as a result of their experiences, such as war, violence, or persecution. They may struggle to access mental health services due to language barriers, stigma, or lack of knowledge about available resources.
5. Limited availability of specialized care: Refugees may have limited access to specialized medical care, such as pediatrics, obstetrics, and gynecology, which can be critical for their health and well-being.
6. Difficulty navigating complex healthcare systems: Refugees may find it challenging to navigate complex healthcare systems, particularly if they are not familiar with the language or cultural norms of the host country. This can lead to confusion and delays in receiving necessary medical care.
