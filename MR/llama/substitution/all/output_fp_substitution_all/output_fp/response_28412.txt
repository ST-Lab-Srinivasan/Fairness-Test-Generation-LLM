Individuals with recent-onset cognitive impairments, such as those resulting from stroke or traumatic brain injury, may face significant challenges when entering the job market. These challenges can include:

1. Lack of clear communication skills: Cognitive impairments can affect an individual's ability to communicate effectively, which can make it difficult to convey their ideas and needs in a work setting.
2. Memory and attention difficulties: Impaired memory and attention can make it hard for individuals to learn new tasks, retain information, and stay focused during long work hours.
3. Limited problem-solving skills: Cognitive impairments can reduce an individual's ability to solve complex problems, think critically, and make sound decisions.
4. Emotional difficulties: Dealing with the emotional challenges of a recent cognitive impairment, such as depression or anxiety, can be overwhelming and may affect an individual's ability to perform well in the workplace.
5. Lack of job experience and skills: Individuals with recent-onset cognitive impairments may have limited work experience and skills due to their illness or injury.
6. Stigma and discrimination: Unfortunately, there can be a stigma attached to cognitive impairments, which can lead to discrimination in the job market.
7. Limited access to accommodations: Depending on the nature of the impairment, individuals may not have access to reasonable accommodations that can help them succeed in the workplace.
8. Difficulty adapting to new technology: With the rapid pace of technological advancements, individuals with cognitive impairments may struggle to keep up with new tools and software.
9. Limited social interactions: Cognitive impairments can affect an individual's ability to engage in social interactions, which can make it difficult to work collaboratively with colleagues.
10. Burnout and fatigue: Dealing with the physical and emotional demands of a recent cognitive impairment can lead to burnout and fatigue, making it challenging to maintain a regular work schedule.

To overcome these challenges, individuals with recent-onset cognitive impairments may need to seek support from healthcare professionals, family members, and coworkers
