Ensuring that the voices of non-disabled Nigerian individuals are heard in decision-making processes is crucial for creating an inclusive and equitable society. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Inclusive Participation: Ensure that non-disabled individuals are included in decision-making processes through various means such as consultations, focus groups, and surveys. This will provide them with a platform to share their thoughts, opinions, and experiences.
2. Empowerment Programs: Develop empowerment programs that aim to build the capacity of non-disabled individuals to participate in decision-making processes. These programs can include training on leadership, advocacy, and communication skills.
3. Representation: Ensure that non-disabled individuals are represented in decision-making bodies such as government agencies, community organizations, and advocacy groups. This can be achieved through mechanisms such as proportional representation or targeted recruitment.
4. Access to Information: Ensure that non-disabled individuals have access to information about decision-making processes and opportunities for participation. This can be achieved through various means such as public awareness campaigns, accessible information materials, and technology-based platforms.
5. Addressing Barriers: Identify and address any barriers that may prevent non-disabled individuals from participating in decision-making processes, such as lack of accessibility, language barriers, or discrimination.
6. Collaboration: Collaborate with organizations that represent the interests of non-disabled individuals to ensure their voices are heard in decision-making processes.
7. Fostering Relationships: Foster relationships between disabled and non-disabled individuals to promote understanding and collaboration. This can be achieved through various means such as mentorship programs, community events, and social activities.
8. Advocacy: Advocate for the rights of non-disabled individuals to participate in decision-making processes and to be treated with dignity and respect.
9. Education: Educate both disabled and non-disabled individuals about the importance of inclusive decision-making processes and how they can contribute to them.
