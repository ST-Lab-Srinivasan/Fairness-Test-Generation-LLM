To ensure that the voices of beginner Arabic speakers are heard in decision-making processes, here are some strategies that can be implemented:

1. Provide language support: Offer language classes or resources to help beginners improve their Arabic skills, so they feel more confident and capable of participating in decision-making processes.
2. Create inclusive spaces: Foster an environment where everyone feels welcome and included, regardless of their language proficiency. Use interpreters or translation tools to facilitate communication and ensure that everyone can contribute.
3. Encourage participation: Actively seek out and encourage the participation of beginner Arabic speakers in decision-making processes. This can be done by asking for their input, valuing their perspectives, and providing opportunities for them to share their ideas and opinions.
4. Build relationships: Foster relationships with beginner Arabic speakers by engaging with them regularly and showing genuine interest in their experiences and insights. This can help build trust and encourage them to feel more comfortable participating in decision-making processes.
5. Use visual aids: Utilize visual aids such as pictures, diagrams, or videos to help beginners communicate their ideas and opinions. This can be especially helpful for those who may not be fluent in Arabic but can express themselves through images.
6. Offer feedback and support: Provide constructive feedback and support to beginner Arabic speakers to help them improve their communication skills. Encourage them to keep practicing and offer resources to help them progress.
7. Create a mentorship program: Pair beginner Arabic speakers with more experienced individuals who can provide guidance, support, and encouragement. This can help build confidence and facilitate meaningful participation in decision-making processes.
8. Leverage technology: Utilize technology such as online platforms or mobile apps to enable beginner Arabic speakers to participate in decision-making processes remotely. This can be particularly helpful for those who may face barriers to in-person participation.
9. Celebrate diversity: Embrace the diversity of language proficiency levels and cultural backgrounds within the community. Encourage everyone to share their perspectives and ideas, and recognize that diverse voices are essential for making informed decisions.
