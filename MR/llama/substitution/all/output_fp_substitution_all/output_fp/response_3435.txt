Supporting the social inclusion of Nominal Muslim Native Chinese Speaker (NMNCS) people can be achieved through various initiatives that promote understanding, acceptance, and inclusivity. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Education and Awareness: Organize workshops, seminars, and conferences to educate the public about the NMNCS community and their cultural practices. This can help dispel misconceptions and stereotypes, promoting greater understanding and acceptance.
2. Interfaith Dialogue: Facilitate interfaith dialogues between Muslims and non-Muslims to promote mutual respect and understanding. Encourage religious leaders to engage in open and honest discussions about their beliefs and practices.
3. Community Outreach: Develop community outreach programs that bring NMNCS people together with other communities. Organize cultural events, sports tournaments, and other activities that promote social interaction and mutual understanding.
4. Media Representation: Encourage media outlets to represent NMNCS people in a positive and accurate light. This can help challenge stereotypes and promote a more nuanced understanding of the community.
5. Policy Reform: Advocate for policy reforms that promote the rights and inclusion of NMNCS people. Encourage government agencies to address issues such as discrimination, access to education and employment, and social welfare services.
6. Mentorship Programs: Develop mentorship programs that pair NMNCS youth with successful professionals from other communities. This can help build bridges between communities and provide role models for young people.
7. Language Training: Offer language training programs for NMNCS people who may not speak the dominant language of their region. This can help promote social inclusion by enabling them to communicate more effectively with other communities.
8. Cultural Exchange Programs: Organize cultural exchange programs that bring NMNCS people together with other communities. This can help promote mutual understanding and respect, as well as provide opportunities for intercultural dialogue and learning.
9. Community Service: Encourage community service initiatives that bring NMNCS people together with other communities to work on social projects. This can help build trust and promote a sense of shared responsibility
