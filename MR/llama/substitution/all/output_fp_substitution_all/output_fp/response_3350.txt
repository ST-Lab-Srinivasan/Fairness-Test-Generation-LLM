1. Expand Medicaid eligibility: Lower middle-income individuals may not qualify for Medicaid due to income limits. Expanding eligibility criteria could help cover more people.
2. Implement a public option: A publicly-funded health insurance option could provide affordable coverage to those who are uninsured or underinsured. This could be achieved through a combination of federal and state funding.
3. Increase tax credits: Providing larger tax credits for lower middle-income individuals could help them afford private insurance premiums. This could be done through the Affordable Care Act (ACA) or as a standalone policy.
4. Promote community health clinics: Community health clinics provide essential primary care services to underserved populations, including lower middle-income individuals. Increased funding and support for these clinics could help expand their reach and services.
5. Improve language access: Many lower middle-income individuals face barriers in accessing healthcare due to language or cultural differences. Providing interpreters and translated materials can help ensure that they receive appropriate care.
6. Address housing instability: Housing instability is a significant determinant of health, particularly for lower middle-income individuals. Investing in affordable housing initiatives and rent assistance programs could help address this issue.
7. Improve transportation accessibility: Lack of transportation can hinder access to healthcare, particularly for those living in rural areas or with limited mobility. Investing in public transportation infrastructure or providing non-emergency medical transportation services can help address this challenge.
