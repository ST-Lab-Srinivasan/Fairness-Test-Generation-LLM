Supporting the economic empowerment of individuals with non-high-income no known genetic conditions (NHIC) requires a multifaceted approach that addresses various factors affecting their well-being and opportunities. Here are some ways to support their economic empowerment:

1. Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs, including vocational skills and entrepreneurship training, to help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge that can enhance their employability and earning potential.
2. Job Creation and Support: Encourage the creation of job opportunities in the local community, particularly for individuals with NHIC. This can be achieved by providing resources and support for small businesses, start-ups, and social enterprises that focus on hiring and empowering individuals with NHIC.
3. Financial Inclusion: Ensure access to financial services, such as savings accounts, loans, and insurance, to help individuals with NHIC manage their finances effectively and make the most of their earnings.
4. Mentorship and Networking: Connect individuals with NHIC with successful entrepreneurs, professionals, and community leaders who can provide guidance, support, and networking opportunities. This can help build confidence, skills, and connections that can lead to better economic outcomes.
5. Health and Wellness: Address the health and wellness needs of individuals with NHIC by providing access to affordable healthcare services, promoting healthy lifestyles, and addressing social determinants of health that can impact their overall well-being and productivity.
6. Community Development: Invest in community development initiatives that promote economic growth, social inclusion, and cultural preservation. This can involve supporting local businesses, promoting tourism, and creating job opportunities in the creative and cultural industries.
