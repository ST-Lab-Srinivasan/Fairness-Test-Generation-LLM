Supporting newly pregnant Hispanic women in their third trimester can be challenging, but there are several effective strategies that healthcare providers and community organizations can use to promote positive health outcomes. Here are some strategies that have been shown to be effective:

1. Culturally sensitive care: Provide care that is sensitive to the cultural beliefs and practices of the Hispanic community. This includes using interpreters or translators, providing education materials in Spanish, and being aware of traditional healing practices and beliefs.
2. Access to prenatal care: Ensure that women have access to regular prenatal care, including early and frequent check-ups, laboratory tests, and ultrasound examinations. This can help identify potential complications early on and provide appropriate interventions.
3. Health education: Provide culturally appropriate health education to women, focusing on topics such as proper nutrition, exercise, and weight management during pregnancy. This can help promote positive health outcomes for both the mother and baby.
4. Community outreach: Engage with local Hispanic communities through outreach programs, events, and partnerships. This can help increase awareness of the importance of prenatal care and promote a sense of belonging among community members.
5. Language access: Provide language access services, such as interpretation and translation, to ensure that women have equal access to healthcare services regardless of their language proficiency.
6. Addressing cultural beliefs and practices: Be aware of traditional healing practices and beliefs within the Hispanic community and work with them to address any potential conflicts with medical advice or interventions.
7. Building trust: Build trust with the women in the community by being sensitive to their needs, providing emotional support, and being responsive to their concerns.
