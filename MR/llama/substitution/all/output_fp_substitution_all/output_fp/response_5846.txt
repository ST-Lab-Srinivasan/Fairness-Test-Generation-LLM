To ensure that the educational needs of beginner French speakers who are newly married are met, here are some suggestions:

1. Offer tailored courses: Design courses that cater to the unique needs and goals of this demographic. For example, you could offer a "French for Couples" course that focuses on practical language skills for everyday situations, such as grocery shopping, restaurant visits, and home living.
2. Use relatable content: Incorporate content that is relevant to their lives, such as talking about household chores, meal preparation, and family planning. This will help them connect with the language and feel more motivated to learn.
3. Emphasize communication skills: Beginner French speakers may struggle with basic communication tasks, such as ordering food at a restaurant or asking for directions. Focus on developing their communication skills through role-playing exercises, group discussions, and interactive activities.
4. Provide flexible scheduling options: Many newly married students may have busy schedules with work, family, and other commitments. Offer classes at different times or locations to accommodate their needs, such as online courses or evening classes.
5. Create a supportive community: Encourage collaboration and feedback among students to foster a sense of community and accountability. This can be done through group projects, language exchange events, or creating a private Facebook group for students to share tips and ask questions.
6. Offer cultural immersion opportunities: Expose students to French culture through activities such as movie nights, wine tastings, or visits to local museums and landmarks. This will help them better understand the language and its nuances.
7. Provide resources for learning at home: Send students digital resources, such as apps, videos, and podcasts, that they can use to continue practicing their French skills outside of class.
8. Encourage pairing with a native speaker: Arrange for beginner French speakers to practice speaking with a native speaker, either through a language exchange program or by connecting them with a language tutor. This will help them improve their pronunciation and comprehension.
