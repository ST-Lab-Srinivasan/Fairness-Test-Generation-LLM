Creating more inclusive community events for beginner English speakers who are not from upper-middle-income backgrounds requires careful planning and consideration. Here are some strategies to help make your event more accessible and welcoming:

1. Language Support: Provide language support during the event, such as interpreters or translation services, to ensure that all attendees can fully participate and understand the content.
2. Accessible Venue: Choose a venue that is easily accessible for people with disabilities. Make sure the space is wheelchair-friendly, has enough seating, and is well-lit.
3. Affordable Ticket Prices: Offer affordable ticket prices to ensure that the event is accessible to people of all income levels. Consider offering discounts for students, seniors, or those on a tight budget.
4. Scholarships: Consider offering scholarships or financial aid to individuals who may not be able to afford the ticket price. This can help ensure that the event is inclusive and accessible to a diverse range of people.
5. Outreach and Promotion: Reach out to community organizations, schools, and local businesses to promote the event. Share flyers or posters in public areas where the target audience is likely to see them.
6. Volunteer Translators: Train volunteer translators who can assist attendees during the event. This can help ensure that everyone can participate fully, regardless of their language proficiency.
7. Simultaneous Interpretation: Provide simultaneous interpretation during the event to make it easier for non-English speakers to follow the presentations and discussions.
8. Childcare: Offer childcare services or provide a quiet space for parents to take care of their children during the event. This can help ensure that families can participate together without worrying about childcare arrangements.
9. Food and Beverage Options: Provide food and beverage options that cater to diverse dietary needs, such as vegetarian, gluten-free, or halal. This can help ensure that everyone feels included and comfortable during the event.
