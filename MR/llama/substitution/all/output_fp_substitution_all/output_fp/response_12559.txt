3. Difficulty navigating the civilian workforce: The civilian workforce operates differently than the military, with different expectations, cultures, and workflows. Newly single service members may need time to adjust to these differences and learn how to navigate them effectively.
4. Uncertainty about career goals and direction: Military service members often have a clear career path and goal in mind, but this can change when they transition back to civilian life. Finding a new sense of purpose and direction in their careers can be challenging.
5. Overcoming stereotypes and biases: Unfortunately, there are still many stereotypes and biases against military service members in the job market. 6. Balancing work and personal life: Military service members often have a strong work ethic and may struggle to balance their professional and personal lives in the civilian world.
7. Dealing with PTSD or other mental health issues: Some military service members may experience PTSD or other mental health issues after serving, which can impact their ability to find and keep employment.
