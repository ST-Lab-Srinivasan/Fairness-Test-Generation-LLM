Promoting the financial stability of just-turned 65-year-old permanent residents is crucial to ensure they can enjoy their retirement years without financial stress. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Educate them on personal finance: Many seniors may not have had the opportunity to learn about personal finance and investing when they were younger. Provide workshops or classes on topics such as budgeting, saving, and investing to help them make informed decisions about their money.
2. Encourage retirement savings: Encourage permanent residents to save for retirement by offering tax incentives or other financial benefits. For example, many countries have retirement savings plans that allow individuals to save a portion of their income on a tax-free basis.
3. Provide access to affordable healthcare: As people age, they may experience increased medical expenses. Ensure that permanent residents have access to affordable healthcare options, including preventive care and chronic disease management.
4. Offer financial planning resources: Help permanent residents create a comprehensive financial plan that takes into account their income, expenses, assets, and goals. This can include working with a financial advisor or using online tools and resources to help them map out their financial future.
5. Encourage entrepreneurship: Many seniors have valuable skills and experience that could be leveraged to start a business or pursue other entrepreneurial ventures. Provide resources and support to help them explore this option, such as training programs, mentorship opportunities, and access to funding.
6. Promote financial inclusion: Ensure that permanent residents have access to financial services, including bank accounts, loans, and insurance products. This can help them manage their finances more effectively and avoid financial exclusion.
7. Address elder financial abuse: Unfortunately, seniors are often targeted by scammers and fraudsters. Educate permanent residents on how to protect themselves from financial abuse and provide resources for reporting suspicious activity.
