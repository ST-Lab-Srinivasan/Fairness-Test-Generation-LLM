2. Networking Difficulties: Building a network of contacts and connections is crucial in the job market, but as a beginner English speaker, it may be challenging to connect with people in your industry who speak the language fluently. This can limit your access to job opportunities and make it harder to advance in your career.
3. Limited Vocabulary: As a beginner English speaker, you may have a limited vocabulary, which can make it difficult to understand complex job-related concepts or communicate effectively with colleagues and clients.
4. Cultural Differences: If you are from a non-English speaking country and recently single, there may be cultural differences that affect your job search and work style. For example, some cultures may place a higher value on teamwork and collaboration, while others may prioritize individual achievement.
