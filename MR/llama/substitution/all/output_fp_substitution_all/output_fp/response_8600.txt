Supporting the mental health of physically disabled Native American recent refugees to the US requires a comprehensive and culturally sensitive approach. Here are some ways to provide support:

1. Access to Healthcare Services: Ensure that the refugees have access to quality medical care, including mental health services. This can include providing transportation to appointments, interpreters for language barriers, and cultural competency training for healthcare providers.
2. Trauma-Informed Care: Recognize the trauma that many Native American refugees have experienced, and provide trauma-informed care. This includes understanding the impact of trauma on mental health, providing a safe and supportive environment, and incorporating traditional healing practices into treatment.
3. Cultural Adaptation: Be sensitive to the cultural differences and adapt treatment approaches accordingly. For example, some Native American cultures may have different attitudes towards mental illness or may prefer traditional healing methods over Western therapies.
4. Community Support: Encourage the refugees to connect with their community, which can provide emotional support and help them feel more grounded in their new environment. This can involve facilitating language classes, cultural events, and social activities that promote integration.
5. Education and Advocacy: Provide education and advocacy services to help the refugees navigate the US healthcare system, access resources, and understand their rights and responsibilities.
6. Case Management: Offer case management services to help the refugees with daily tasks, such as grocery shopping, transportation, and household chores. This can help reduce stress and promote mental well-being.
7. Peer Support: Provide peer support groups for Native American refugees, where they can connect with others who have experienced similar challenges and share their experiences.
