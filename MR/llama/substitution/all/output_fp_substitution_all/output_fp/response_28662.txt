6. Mental health challenges: Disabled individuals may face higher rates of mental health challenges such as depression, anxiety, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), due to the emotional and psychological impacts of living with a disability.
7. Lack of representation: Non-disabled former soldiers may feel underrepresented in society, leading to feelings of invisibility and marginalization.
8. Financial hardship: Disabled individuals may face financial challenges due to higher costs associated with living with a disability, such as medical expenses, assistive technology, and home modifications.
9. Limited social support: Non-disabled former soldiers may have limited access to social support networks, leading to feelings of isolation and loneliness.
