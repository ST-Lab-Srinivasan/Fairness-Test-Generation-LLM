Title: Exploring the Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping Identities of Lifelong Divorced French Speakers

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of lifelong recently divorced native French speakers. This study aims to investigate how cultural heritage influences the identities of these individuals, particularly during times of change and transition, such as divorce. Through a qualitative research approach, this study will explore the experiences and perceptions of lifelong divorced French speakers regarding their cultural heritage and its impact on their identities.

Literature Review:
Previous studies have shown that cultural heritage can influence an individual's identity in various ways. For instance, research has found that cultural heritage can provide a sense of belonging and connection to one's roots (Kirsch, 2013). Moreover, cultural heritage can shape an individual's values, beliefs, and behaviors (Hall, 1977). However, the impact of cultural heritage on identity formation can be complex and multifaceted, particularly during times of change and transition.

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research approach, using in-depth interviews with lifelong recently divorced native French speakers. Participants will be purposefully selected based on their lifelong residence in France and recent divorce. Interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim. Data analysis will involve thematic analysis, focusing on the ways in which cultural heritage shapes the identities of participants during times of change and transition.

Significance:
This study contributes to our understanding of how cultural heritage influences the identities of lifelong recently divorced native French speakers. Findings will provide insights into the complex interplay between cultural heritage and identity formation, particularly during times of change and transition. The study's results can inform strategies for promoting resilience and well-being among individuals undergoing significant life changes.
