Creating more inclusive community events for active-duty military personnel who are living in poverty requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions on how to organize such events:

1. Understand the needs of the community: It is essential to understand the unique challenges faced by active-duty military personnel living in poverty, including financial struggles, lack of access to resources, and social isolation. Conduct research or gather input from local organizations serving this population to identify their specific needs.
2. Collaborate with local organizations: Partner with local non-profits, community centers, and religious organizations that serve the military population. These organizations can provide valuable resources, expertise, and networks to help reach a broader audience.
3. Identify potential venues: Look for venues that are easily accessible and affordable for the target audience. Consider renting space at local community centers, parks, or other public spaces.
4. Offer free or low-cost events: To make the event more inclusive, offer free or low-cost admission to active-duty military personnel living in poverty. This can include food, drinks, and activities.
5. Provide childcare and transportation: Military families often struggle with finding affordable childcare and transportation. Offer on-site childcare and transportation options to make the event more convenient and accessible.
6. Promote the event through social media and word of mouth: Utilize social media platforms, such as Facebook and Twitter, to promote the event. Encourage active-duty military personnel living in poverty to spread the word within their networks.
7. Incorporate cultural and entertainment activities: Offer activities that cater to the diverse interests of the military population, including live music, dance performances, and cultural events.
8. Provide resources and support: In addition to entertainment, provide resources and support to help military personnel living in poverty, such as job training, financial assistance, and mental health services.
9. Make it a recurring event: To build a sense of community and continuity, make the event a regular occurrence, such as monthly or quarterly. This can help create a sense of anticipation and excitement among the military population.
