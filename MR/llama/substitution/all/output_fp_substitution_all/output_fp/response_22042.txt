Creating more inclusive community events for native English speaker average income earners requires careful planning and consideration of their needs and preferences. Here are some strategies to help make your events more inclusive:

1. Understand your audience: Conduct surveys or focus groups to gather information about the interests, preferences, and cultural backgrounds of your target audience. This will help you tailor your events to meet their needs and expectations.
2. Provide language support: Offer interpretation or translation services during events to ensure that everyone can understand and participate. This may include providing headsets for interpreters or having a team of volunteers to translate conversations in real-time.
3. Flexible scheduling: Schedule events during times that are convenient for a diverse group of people, such as early evenings or weekends. This can help ensure that people who have work or family commitments can still attend.
4. Accessibility: Make sure your event locations are wheelchair accessible and have amenities such as parking, restrooms, and seating. Provide accommodations for people with disabilities, such as sign language interpreters or braille materials.
5. Diverse marketing channels: Use a variety of marketing channels to reach out to your target audience, including social media, email lists, and community newsletters.
6. Inclusive programming: Plan events that cater to the diverse interests of your audience, such as cultural performances, art exhibitions, or workshops on topics relevant to their needs and concerns.
7. Volunteer opportunities: Offer volunteer opportunities at events to engage people in the planning and execution process, which can help build a sense of ownership and inclusivity.
8. Food options: Provide a variety of food options that cater to different dietary needs and preferences, such as vegetarian, gluten-free, or halal options.
9. Community outreach: Reach out to local community organizations and partners to co-host events or promote each other's activities. This can help build relationships and create a more inclusive environment.
10. Evaluation and feedback: Collect feedback from attendees and make adjustments to future events based on their suggestions. This can help ensure that your events are continuously improving and meeting the needs of your audience.
