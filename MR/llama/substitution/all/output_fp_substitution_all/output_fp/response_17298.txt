1. Provide Language Access Services: Offer interpreters, translators, and bilingual staff to assist with communication between military personnel and public service providers. This can include in-person interpretation, video remote interpretation, or language translation services.
2. Develop Multilingual Resources: Create resources that are available in multiple languages, including Spanish. This can include brochures, flyers, posters, and website content. Provide information on how to access these resources and make them easily accessible to military personnel.
3. Offer Cultural Sensitivity Training: Provide training for public service providers on cultural sensitivity and how to interact with people from diverse backgrounds. This can help build trust and understanding between military personnel and service providers.

Foster Partnerships: Collaborate with organizations that cater specifically to the needs of native Spanish-speaking active duty military individuals, such as the Hispanic Military Veterans Association or the National Association of Latino Elected and Appointed Officials. These partnerships can help provide additional support and resources for military personnel.
5. Leverage Technology: Utilize technology to improve accessibility, such as online appointment scheduling systems with language options, telemedicine services, and mobile apps that provide information and resources in multiple languages.
6. Conduct Outreach and Awareness Campaigns: Organize outreach and awareness campaigns to inform military personnel about the resources available to them. This can be done through social media, community events, and on-base promotions.
