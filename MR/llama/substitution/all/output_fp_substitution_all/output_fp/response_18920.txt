Supporting the social inclusion of Nominal Christian Native French Speaker (NCFS) people involves addressing the systemic and institutional barriers that prevent them from fully participating in society. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Education and Awareness: Provide educational workshops, training sessions, and awareness campaigns to promote understanding and acceptance of NCFS people. This can help reduce stigma and discrimination, and create a more inclusive environment.
2. Employment Opportunities: Encourage businesses and organizations to provide employment opportunities for NCFS people, and ensure that they are not discriminated against in the workplace.
3. Language Support: Offer language support services, such as language classes or interpretation services, to help NCFS people communicate effectively in French and other languages.
4. Healthcare Access: Ensure that NCFS people have access to quality healthcare services, including mental health services, and provide culturally sensitive care.
5. Community Outreach: Engage in community outreach and engagement activities to build relationships and trust between NCFS people and other communities. This can help break down barriers and promote social inclusion.
6. Advocacy and Policy Change: Advocate for policy changes that promote the rights and well-being of NCFS people, such as equal access to education, employment, and healthcare.
7. Cultural Preservation: Support the preservation of NCFS cultural practices, traditions, and beliefs, and ensure that they are not lost or marginalized.
8. Inclusive Spaces: Create inclusive spaces where NCFS people can gather, socialize, and feel safe and respected. This can include community centers, cultural events, and other gathering places.
