2. Limited work experience:
	* Strategy: Highlight any relevant volunteer work, caregiving experiences, or other activities that demonstrate your skills and dedication. Emphasize transferable skills like communication, problem-solving, and time management.
3. Lack of social connections:
	* Strategy: Join local networking groups, attend events related to your industry, and connect with professionals in your desired field. These opportunities can help you build valuable relationships and learn about job openings.
4. Limited access to resources:
	* Strategy: Utilize online resources like job boards, career websites, and professional networks like LinkedIn. Many organizations offer free or low-cost training and mentorship programs specifically for underrepresented groups, so research and take advantage of these opportunities.
5. Ageism:
	* Strategy: Focus on highlighting your transferable skills, adaptability, and enthusiasm for learning. Emphasize how your life experience can bring value to a workplace, and showcase any relevant training or education you've completed recently.
6. Gender bias:
	* Strategy: Avoid making assumptions about potential employers' biases based on your gender. Instead, focus on your skills, experiences, and achievements, and demonstrate how they align with the position and company culture.

	* Strategy: Be open-minded and flexible when entering a new workplace. Observe and learn from colleagues, ask questions, and be willing to adapt your approach as needed.
