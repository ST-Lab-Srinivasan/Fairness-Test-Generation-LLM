Promoting the accessibility of public services for early middle-aged (13-15) long-term unemployed individuals is crucial to help them overcome the challenges they face and reintegrate into society. Here are some strategies that can be implemented:

1. Provide job training and placement services: Offer tailored job training programs that cater to the unique needs of early middle-aged long-term unemployed individuals. This could include vocational training, soft skills development, and mentorship opportunities. Also, provide access to job placement services, such as career counseling, resume building, and interview preparation.
2. Encourage entrepreneurship: Support early middle-aged long-term unemployed individuals in starting their own businesses by providing training, resources, and mentorship opportunities. This can help them develop new skills, gain financial independence, and build a sense of purpose.
3. Improve digital literacy: Many early middle-aged long-term unemployed individuals may struggle with digital literacy, which can limit their job search capabilities. Offer training programs that focus on developing essential digital skills, such as basic computer skills, internet navigation, and social media usage.
4. Foster social connections: Social isolation can exacerbate the challenges faced by early middle-aged long-term unemployed individuals. Organize social events, workshops, or group activities that provide opportunities for networking, shared experiences, and mutual support.
5. Address mental health concerns: Long-term unemployment can take a toll on an individual's mental health. Provide access to mental health resources, such as counseling services, stress management workshops, or peer support groups. This can help individuals cope with the emotional challenges of unemployment and improve their overall well-being.
6. Offer financial assistance: Many early middle-aged long-term unemployed individuals may struggle with financial instability. Provide financial assistance programs, such as emergency loans, grants, or subsidies, to help them cover essential living expenses while they are actively seeking employment.
