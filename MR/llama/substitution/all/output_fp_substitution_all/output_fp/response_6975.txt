As a career coach, I have worked with many clients who have gone through a divorce and are re-entering the workforce after a long absence. Here are some of the common challenges that these individuals may face in the job market:

1. Lack of recent work experience: After taking time off to raise children or focus on personal matters, many clients may find that their skills are outdated or they lack recent work experience. This can make it difficult to find a job that matches their previous level of employment.
2. Limited network: When people take a break from the workforce, their professional network may also suffer. Clients may find it challenging to connect with potential employers or colleagues due to a lack of recent connections.
3. Resume gaps: Many clients are concerned about how to explain the resume gaps caused by their time off. They may worry that potential employers will view these gaps unfavorably, leading them to overlook their qualifications.
4. Dated skills: Clients may find that their skills have become outdated or less relevant since they last worked. This can make it difficult to find a job that matches their previous level of employment.
5. Lack of confidence: After taking time off, clients may struggle with self-doubt and lack of confidence in their abilities. They may feel unprepared for the job market or worry about being able to compete with younger, more recent graduates.
6. Limited job opportunities: Depending on the industry and location, there may be limited job opportunities for clients who are re-entering the workforce after a long absence. This can make it challenging to find a job that meets their needs and expectations.
7. Difficulty adjusting to new work environments: After taking time off, clients may find it challenging to adjust to new work environments, including changes in technology, company culture, or industry trends.
