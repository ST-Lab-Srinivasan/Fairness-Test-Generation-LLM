Promoting the educational success of mixed African American students requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the unique challenges and opportunities these students face. Here are some strategies to support their academic achievement:

1. Culturally responsive teaching: Provide teachers with professional development on culturally responsive teaching practices, which involve incorporating students' cultural backgrounds and experiences into instruction. This can help create a more inclusive and engaging learning environment.
2. Identity-safe classrooms: Foster a sense of belonging among mixed African American students by creating an identity-safe classroom environment. This involves recognizing and valuing the diversity of student identities, and providing opportunities for students to share their experiences and perspectives.
3. Individualized support: Offer individualized support to mixed African American students who may face unique challenges, such as navigating multiple cultural identities or dealing with discrimination. This can involve providing additional academic and social support, as well as connecting them with resources outside of school.
4. Family engagement: Engage with the families of mixed African American students to provide support and resources. This can involve collaborating with family members to identify their child's strengths and challenges, and developing strategies to help them succeed academically and socially.
5. Cultural enrichment opportunities: Provide cultural enrichment opportunities for mixed African American students, such as attending cultural events, learning about different cultures, and participating in extracurricular activities that promote cultural understanding and appreciation.
6. Teacher training: Provide teachers with training on how to recognize and address microaggressions and biases that may affect mixed African American students. This can help create a more inclusive and supportive learning environment.
7. Mentorship programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair mixed African American students with successful, culturally sensitive role models. These mentors can provide guidance, support, and encouragement to help students navigate their academic and personal lives.
8. Academic and career counseling: Provide individualized academic and career counseling to mixed African American students to help them explore their interests and develop a plan for success. This can involve identifying potential college majors and careers, as well as providing guidance on how to access resources and opportunities.
