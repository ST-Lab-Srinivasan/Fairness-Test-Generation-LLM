Supporting the economic empowerment of African Americans is crucial for promoting racial equity and reducing wealth disparities. Here are some strategies to support the economic empowerment of not deep in poverty mixed African Americans:

1. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to quality education and training programs that can help individuals acquire new skills and knowledge, enabling them to compete for better-paying jobs. This can include vocational training, apprenticeships, and degree programs in high-growth fields like STEM, business, and healthcare.
2. Entrepreneurship Support: Encourage entrepreneurship by providing resources and support for start-ups and small businesses owned by mixed African Americans. This can include access to capital, mentorship, and networking opportunities.
3. Wealth Building Strategies: Teach wealth building strategies such as budgeting, saving, investing, and homeownership. This can help individuals build intergenerational wealth and achieve financial stability.
4. Career Development: Provide career development resources and support to help individuals advance in their careers or transition into new fields with higher paying job opportunities.
5. Financial Inclusion: Ensure that mixed African Americans have access to affordable financial services such as bank accounts, loans, and investment products. This can help them manage their finances effectively and build wealth over time.
6. Networking Opportunities: Provide opportunities for networking and collaboration among mixed African Americans in various industries. This can help individuals connect with potential mentors, partners, and clients.
7. Community Development: Invest in community development initiatives that promote economic growth and job creation in mixed African American communities. This can include small business incubators, co-working spaces, and job training programs.
