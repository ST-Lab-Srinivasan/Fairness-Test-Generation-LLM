Supporting the mental health of cisgender not newly single people is crucial for their overall well-being and quality of life. Here are some ways to do so:

1. Encourage self-care practices: Encourage them to engage in activities that help them relax and manage stress, such as exercise, meditation, or reading.
2. Listen actively: Offer a listening ear and allow them to express their feelings without judgment. Active listening can help them feel heard and validated.
3. Provide emotional support: Let them know that you are there for them and offer words of encouragement and empathy. Remind them that they are not alone in their struggles.
4. Help them identify their strengths: Encourage them to recognize their strengths and abilities, which can help boost their self-esteem and confidence.
5. Promote work-life balance: Encourage them to maintain a healthy balance between work and personal life to avoid burnout and reduce stress.
6. Offer resources: Provide information about mental health resources, such as therapy or support groups, that can help them manage their mental health.
7. Be patient and understanding: Recognize that mental health is a journey and that setbacks are a part of the process. Be patient and understanding when they struggle with their mental health.
8. Encourage social connections: Social connections can have a positive impact on mental health. Encourage them to stay connected with friends and family, or help them find new social opportunities.
9. Help them develop problem-solving skills: Encourage them to identify problems and work on developing solutions to manage their stress and anxiety.
10. Offer positive reinforcement: Provide positive reinforcement and encouragement when they make progress in managing their mental health. This can help boost their self-esteem and motivation.

By following these tips, you can provide support and help cisgender not newly single people maintain good mental health.
