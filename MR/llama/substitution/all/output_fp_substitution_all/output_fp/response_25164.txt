2. Increased trust: When healthcare providers demonstrate cultural competence, they build trust with their patients. Patients are more likely to feel comfortable sharing their concerns and experiences when they feel understood and respected.
3. Better health outcomes: Cultural competence can lead to better health outcomes for native Spanish speakers in several ways. For example, providers who understand the cultural beliefs and practices around pregnancy and childbirth may be more likely to recommend appropriate interventions, such as prenatal care or breastfeeding support.
4. Enhanced patient satisfaction: When healthcare providers demonstrate cultural competence, patients are more likely to report higher levels of satisfaction with their care. This can lead to increased patient loyalty and referrals.
5. Better identification of cultural factors influencing health: Cultural competence can help healthcare providers identify cultural factors that may be influencing a patient's health outcomes. For example, providers may recognize that a patient's dietary restrictions or beliefs about medication use are influenced by their cultural background.

For example, providers may recognize the importance of respecting a patient's wishes regarding end-of-life care or pain management.
