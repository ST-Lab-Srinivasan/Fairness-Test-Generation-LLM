Promoting the cultural integration of long-term refugees, particularly those with ultra-liberal political views, requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses their unique challenges and needs. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Language Training: Provide language training to help them improve their proficiency in the local language, which is essential for integrating into the host society.
2. Cultural Orientation: Offer cultural orientation programs that introduce them to the local customs, traditions, and values. This can help them understand and respect the norms of their new home.
3. Social Inclusion: Encourage social interaction between refugees and local communities through events, workshops, and volunteer opportunities. This can help break down barriers and foster mutual understanding.
4. Access to Education and Training: Provide access to education and training programs that help them acquire new skills and knowledge. This can help them become more employable and self-sufficient in their host country.
5. Psychological Support: Offer psychological support services to help them deal with the trauma and stress associated with refugee status. This can help them manage their emotions and behaviors more effectively.
6. Advocacy and Advocacy Training: Provide advocacy training to help them assertively express their needs and rights within their host society. This can help them navigate complex bureaucratic systems and access necessary services.
7. Community Engagement: Encourage community engagement and participation in local events and initiatives. This can help them build social connections and feel more connected to their new home.
8. Access to Healthcare: Ensure they have access to quality healthcare services, including mental health support, to address any medical or psychological needs they may have.
9. Child-Friendly Activities: Provide child-friendly activities and programs to help them integrate into the local community and reduce feelings of isolation.
10. Leadership Opportunities: Offer leadership opportunities and mentorship programs to help them develop their skills and become role models within their host society.
11. Intercultural Dialogue: Facilitate intercultural dialogue and exchange programs to promote mutual understanding and respect between refugees and local communities.
