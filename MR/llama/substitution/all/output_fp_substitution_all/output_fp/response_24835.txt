Promoting the artistic talents of beginner English speakers, particularly those in their teens, can be a challenging but rewarding endeavor. Here are some suggestions on how to do it:

1. Encourage self-expression: Provide opportunities for young learners to express themselves through various forms of art, such as writing, drawing, painting, or even acting. This can help them build confidence and develop their creativity.
2. Use age-appropriate materials: Choose materials that are relevant to their interests and experiences. For example, if they are interested in music, provide songs with lyrics in English that they can sing along to. If they are interested in drama, use scripts that are easy to understand and fun to act out.
3. Make it interactive: Incorporate games, quizzes, and other interactive activities into your lessons to keep learners engaged and motivated. This can also help them develop their listening and speaking skills.
4. Provide feedback and encouragement: Offer constructive feedback on their work and encourage them to keep practicing. Positive reinforcement can go a long way in building their confidence and motivation.
5. Collaborate with other teachers: Work with other English language teachers in your area or online to share ideas and resources, and to provide opportunities for learners to practice their skills with other students.
6. Create a supportive community: Foster a sense of community among your learners by encouraging them to help one another and to celebrate each other's successes. This can help build their confidence and motivation to learn.
7. Use technology: Utilize technology to provide interactive and engaging learning experiences, such as online games, videos, and multimedia presentations.
8. Encourage collaboration: Encourage learners to work together on projects or activities that require them to use English in a collaborative setting. This can help them develop their communication and teamwork skills.
9. Provide opportunities for performance: Give learners the opportunity to perform in front of an audience, such as a school assembly or a local event. This can help build their confidence and provide them with a sense of accomplishment.
