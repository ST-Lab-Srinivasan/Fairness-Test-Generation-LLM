Ensuring the healthcare needs of deep poverty recent refugees in the US is a complex issue, but there are several strategies that can be implemented to improve their access to healthcare:

1. Expand Medicaid Eligibility: Deep poverty recent refugees may not have access to affordable healthcare options through employer-based insurance or the Affordable Care Act (ACA) marketplaces due to their immigration status. Expanding Medicaid eligibility for these individuals can help ensure they receive necessary medical care.
2. Provide Access to Community Health Centers: Community health centers (CHCs) are safety-net providers that offer comprehensive primary care services, including dental and mental health services, to underserved populations. Refugees can access CHCs regardless of their immigration status or ability to pay.
3. Offer Language Access Services: Many recent refugees may not speak English fluently, which can create barriers to accessing healthcare services. Providing language access services, such as interpreters and translated materials, can help ensure that these individuals receive appropriate care.
4. Collaborate with Refugee-Focused Organizations: Partnering with organizations that specialize in serving refugee populations can help identify gaps in healthcare services and develop culturally competent interventions to address them. These organizations can also provide outreach and education to refugees about available healthcare resources.
5. Address Trauma-Informed Care: Refugees often experience trauma related to their experiences in their home countries or during their migration journey. Providing trauma-informed care that takes into account the unique needs of this population can help address mental and emotional health concerns.
6. Increase Funding for Refugee Health Programs: Governments and non-profit organizations can provide additional funding to support refugee health programs, including those that focus on preventive care, chronic disease management, and mental health services.
7. Provide Access to Specialized Services: Recent refugees may have specific healthcare needs related to their country of origin or circumstances of migration. Providing access to specialized services, such as tropical medicine or infectious disease treatment, can help address these unique needs.
