Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for recent temporary visa holders in India requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some suggestions:

1. Inclusive Planning: Ensure that the planning process involves diverse stakeholders, including the visa holder community, local organizations, and government agencies. This will help ensure that the celebrations are inclusive of the needs and preferences of all participants.
2. Accessible Venues: Choose venues that are easily accessible for people with disabilities. Ensure that there are ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms. Provide sign language interpreters, braille signage, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
3. Accommodating Food Options: Offer a variety of food options that cater to different dietary requirements, such as vegan, gluten-free, and halal. Provide nutrition information in braille or large print for individuals with visual impairments.
4. Sign Language Interpreters: Provide sign language interpreters for visually impaired individuals during cultural performances, lectures, or workshops. This will enable them to fully participate and enjoy the celebrations.
5. Assistive Technology: Provide assistive technology devices such as audio describers, closed captioning, and tactile graphics for individuals with visual or cognitive impairments.
6. Inclusive Marketing: Develop marketing materials that are accessible to all, including visually impaired individuals. Use clear and simple language, and provide alternative formats such as braille or large print upon request.
7. Accessible Activities: Offer a range of activities that are inclusive of all participants, such as cultural workshops, dance classes, or art exhibitions. Ensure that the activities are adaptable for individuals with different abilities.
8. Sensory-Friendly Environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by reducing noise levels, using calming scents, and providing comfortable seating areas for individuals with sensory processing challenges.
