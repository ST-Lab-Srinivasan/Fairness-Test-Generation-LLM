Protecting the legal rights of recently single active duty military individuals can be challenging, but there are several steps that can be taken to ensure their rights are protected. Here are some recommendations:

1. Educate yourself and others: Learn about the laws and regulations related to military divorce and the legal rights of service members. Share this information with other military members, family members, and legal professionals to raise awareness and promote understanding.
2. Seek legal advice: Consult with a military lawyer or civilian attorney who specializes in military family law to understand your legal options and protect your rights. They can help you navigate the complex legal system and ensure that your interests are represented.
3. Use Military OneSource resources: The Military OneSource website provides information and resources on a variety of topics, including divorce, separation, and legal assistance. Take advantage of these resources to get the support and guidance you need.
4. Join a military family organization: Many organizations, such as Military Families United, provide support and resources for military families going through a divorce or separation. These organizations can offer emotional support, legal advice, and other forms of assistance.
5. Seek financial planning help: Divorce can have significant financial implications, especially for service members who may face unique financial challenges. Consider consulting with a financial planner or advisor who specializes in working with military clients to develop a financial plan that protects your rights and interests.
6. Advocate for change: If you identify gaps or inconsistencies in the legal system that can impact recently single active duty military individuals, consider advocating for changes through your chain of command or through appropriate military and civilian channels.
7. Seek support from peers: Connect with other recently single service members to share experiences, provide emotional support, and offer advice on how to navigate the legal system. This can help you feel less isolated and more connected to a community that understands your challenges.
8. Be proactive: Don't wait for problems to arise; be proactive in protecting your legal rights. Stay informed about changes in military divorce laws and regulations, and take steps to ensure that your rights are protected throughout the divorce process.
