Introduction:
Marriage is a significant life event that can have a profound impact on an individual's life. However, the effects of marriage may be more pronounced for ultra-conservative individuals who hold strong beliefs and values that are often at odds with mainstream society. This study aims to explore the impacts of marital status on the lives of recently married ultra-conservative individuals.

Theoretical Framework:
The theoretical framework for this study is based on the social constructionist perspective, which posits that an individual's identity and behaviors are shaped by their social context and interactions with others. Within this framework, marital status can have both direct and indirect impacts on ultra-conservative individuals, influencing their personal, social, and cultural lives.

Direct Impacts:

1. Social Isolationation: Ultra-conservative individuals may experience increased social isolation after marriage, as they may feel pressure to conform to societal norms and expectations around monogamy and romantic love. This can lead to a decrease in their sense of belonging and connection to their community.
2. Gender Roles: Marriage can reinforce traditional gender roles, with the husband expected to be the breadwinner and provider, while the wife is expected to be the caregiver and homemaker. Ultra-conservative individuals may find themselves in these roles, leading to a loss of autonomy and agency.
3. Cultural Conflict: Marriage can also create cultural conflicts for ultra-conservative individuals who may struggle with blending their traditional beliefs and practices with those of their spouse. This can lead to feelings of disorientation and identity crisis.
