1. Provide Language Support: Offer language classes or provide materials in their native language to help them improve their English proficiency. This will enable them to better understand and participate in civic activities.
2. Culturally Sensitive Materials: Develop culturally sensitive educational materials that cater to their cultural background and values. This can include pictures, videos, or other visual aids that show diverse people participating in civic activities.
3. Access to Information: Provide them with access to information about local government processes, voting, and community involvement opportunities. This can be done through various channels such as websites, social media, or community events.

5. Mentorship Programs: Establish mentorship programs that pair them with experienced and engaged members of their community. This can help them build confidence and develop skills in civic participation.
6. Youth-Friendly Resources: Develop youth-friendly resources such as interactive websites, apps, or social media groups that provide them with information and opportunities for civic engagement.
7. Intergenerational Dialogue: Organize intergenerational dialogues between infant female beginner English speakers and experienced community members. This can help them learn from each other's experiences and perspectives.
