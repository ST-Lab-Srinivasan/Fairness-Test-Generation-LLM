Physically disabled active-duty personnel face a variety of challenges within the legal system. Some of the common challenges include:

1. Lack of accommodations: Many courthouses and legal offices are not accessible to people with physical disabilities, making it difficult for them to access justice.
2. Limited access to legal resources: Disabled active-duty personnel may face barriers in accessing legal resources such as law libraries, which can hinder their ability to prepare and present their cases.
3. Stereotyping and bias: Disabled individuals may face stereotypes and biases from judges, lawyers, and other legal professionals, which can impact the outcome of their cases.
4. Difficulty navigating the legal system: The legal system can be complex and overwhelming for anyone, let alone those with physical disabilities. Disabled active-duty personnel may find it difficult to navigate the legal system without proper support and accommodations.
5. Lack of representation: Disabled active-duty personnel may have difficulty finding lawyers who are knowledgeable about their rights and needs, or who can provide adequate representation due to a lack of experience with disability issues.
6. Limited understanding of disability laws: Disabled active-duty personnel may encounter legal professionals who are not familiar with disability laws and regulations, which can result in a misinterpretation of their rights and needs.
7. Difficulty proving disability discrimination: Disabled active-duty personnel may face challenges in proving that they have been subject to disability discrimination within the legal system.
8. Limited access to mental health resources: Disabled active-duty personnel may struggle to access mental health resources and support due to barriers such as stigma, lack of availability, or limited coverage by insurance.
9. Difficulty balancing legal proceedings with military obligations: Disabled active-duty personnel may face challenges in balancing their legal proceedings with their military obligations, which can impact their ability to participate fully in the legal process.
