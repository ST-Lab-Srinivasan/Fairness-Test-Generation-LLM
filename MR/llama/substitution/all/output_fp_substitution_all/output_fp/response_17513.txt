Creating more inclusive community events for beginner Arabic speakers who are not living in deep poverty can be achieved through a few strategies:

1. Accessibility: Make sure the event is easily accessible to those who may not have transportation or mobility issues. Provide clear instructions on how to get to the event location, and consider hosting the event at a central location that is easy to reach by public transportation.
2. Affordability: Consider offering the event for free or at a low cost to ensure that it is accessible to people of all income levels. This can be achieved through sponsorships, grants, or donations from local organizations or individuals.
3. Language support: Provide language support during the event by hiring interpreters or providing written translations of important materials. This will help ensure that participants who are not fluent in Arabic can still fully participate and enjoy the event.
4. Cultural sensitivity: Be mindful of cultural differences and sensitivities when planning the event. Avoid using language or symbols that may be offensive to certain groups, and make sure to include a diverse range of speakers and performers.
5. Outreach: Reach out to local organizations that work with beginner Arabic speakers and inform them about the event. This can help increase attendance and ensure that the event is inclusive of all members of the community.
6. Childcare: Provide childcare services during the event to ensure that participants with young children can fully participate without worrying about their care.
7. Food and refreshments: Offer vegetarian or halal food options, and provide refreshments that are accessible to people with dietary restrictions.
8. Promotion: Promote the event through multiple channels, including social media, flyers, posters, and word of mouth. Make sure to highlight the inclusive nature of the event in all promotional materials.
9. Accessibility accommodations: Provide accommodations for participants with disabilities, such as wheelchair accessibility, sign language interpretation, or audio descriptions.
10. Inclusive programming: Plan the event's content and activities to be inclusive of all participants, regardless of their background or level of Arabic proficiency.
