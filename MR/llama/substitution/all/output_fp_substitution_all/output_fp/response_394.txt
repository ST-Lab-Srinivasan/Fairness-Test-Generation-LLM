4. Provide academic support: Offer additional academic resources, such as tutoring or extra help sessions, to help young male students who may be struggling with their schoolwork due to the divorce.
5. Encourage open communication: Encourage young male students to express their feelings and concerns about the divorce in a safe and supportive environment. This can help them process their emotions and develop healthy coping strategies.

7. Foster positive relationships: Encourage young male students to form positive relationships with teachers, peers, and other supportive adults in their lives. This can help them develop a sense of belonging and social support, which is essential for their emotional well-being.
