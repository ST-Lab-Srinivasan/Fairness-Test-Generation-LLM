Discrimination can have a significant impact on the mental health of Native English Speaker Passport Holders (NEPS). Discrimination can take many forms, including racism, xenophobia, and nationalism. The effects of discrimination on NEPS' mental health can be both immediate and long-term.

Immediate effects:

1. Emotional distress: Being subjected to discrimination can cause significant emotional distress, including feelings of anger, frustration, sadness, and fear.
2. Anxiety: Discrimination can lead to increased anxiety levels, particularly in situations where the individual feels vulnerable or powerless.
3. Trauma: Experiencing discrimination can trigger memories of past traumatic events, leading to feelings of hypervigilance and an exaggerated startle response.

Long-term effects:

1. Internalized oppression: NEPS may internalize the negative messages they receive from society, leading to self-doubt, low self-esteem, and a loss of identity.
2. Mental health problems: Chronic exposure to discrimination can increase the risk of developing mental health problems such as depression, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), and anxiety disorders.
3. Social isolation: Discrimination can lead to social isolation, which can further exacerbate mental health problems.
4. Stigma: The stigma associated with discrimination can lead to feelings of shame and humiliation, which can have a profound impact on an individual's mental health.
5. Difficulty forming relationships: NEPS may struggle to form healthy relationships due to the emotional burden of experiencing discrimination.
6. Lack of trust: Discrimination can lead to a lack of trust in oneself and others, making it difficult to form and maintain healthy relationships.
7. Difficulty with anger management: The frustration and anger caused by discrimination can make it challenging for NEPS to manage their emotions and behaviors.
8. Substance abuse: Discrimination can lead to increased substance abuse as a coping mechanism, which can have serious consequences for mental health.
