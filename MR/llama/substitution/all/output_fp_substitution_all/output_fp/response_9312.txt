Supporting the social inclusion of beginner French speakers in a long-term marriage can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can help. Here are some ways to support their social inclusion:

1. Encourage them to practice speaking French regularly: Provide them with opportunities to use their language skills in real-life situations, such as watching French movies together, cooking French meals, or participating in French cultural events.
2. Offer language learning resources: Provide them with access to language learning resources such as language learning apps, books, and online courses. This can help them improve their language skills and build confidence in using French.
3. Create a supportive environment: Encourage them to practice speaking French in a supportive and non-judgmental environment. Avoid correcting their mistakes or making them feel uncomfortable about their language abilities.
4. Foster a positive attitude towards learning: Encourage them to view language learning as a fun and rewarding experience. Praise their efforts and progress, and help them to stay motivated and engaged in the learning process.
5. Provide opportunities for social interaction: Arrange social events or activities where they can practice speaking French with other beginners or native speakers. This can help them build confidence and improve their language skills in a supportive and interactive environment.
6. Use visual aids and props: Use visual aids and props to help them learn new vocabulary and grammar structures. For example, you can use flashcards, pictures, or objects to teach new words and phrases.
7. Practice active listening: Encourage them to listen actively when speaking French, by paying attention to the speaker, asking questions, and summarizing what was said. This can help them improve their comprehension skills and build confidence in using the language.
8. Foster a sense of community: Create a community of beginners who are learning French together. This can provide a sense of support and motivation, as well as opportunities for social interaction and language practice.
9. Provide feedback and encouragement: Offer constructive feedback and encouragement to help them improve their language skills. Be sure to focus on their progress and achievements, rather than their mistakes.
