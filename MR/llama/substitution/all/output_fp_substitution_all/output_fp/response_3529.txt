Creating more inclusive community events for older immigrant adults (ages 90 and above) requires a thoughtful and deliberate approach. Here are some strategies to help make these events more accessible and welcoming:

1. Assess accessibility needs: Conduct an assessment of the event location and activities to identify potential barriers for older immigrant adults. Consider factors such as transportation, parking, seating, lighting, and acoustics. Make necessary adjustments to ensure the space is safe and comfortable for all attendees.
2. Promote events through multiple channels: Use various communication channels to promote events, such as social media, flyers, posters, community newsletters, and word of mouth. Reach out to local senior centers, ethnic organizations, and community groups to help spread the word.
3. Offer language support: Provide interpretation services or bilingual staff at events to assist older immigrant adults who may not speak the dominant language of the host country. This can include translated materials, such as flyers, programs, and signs.
4. Make activities age-appropriate: Design activities that cater to the interests and abilities of older immigrant adults. This could include cultural dance classes, storytelling sessions, or interactive games that are simple and easy to follow.
5. Provide transportation options: Arrange for transportation services or shuttle buses to pick up older immigrant adults from their homes or senior centers. Offer priority seating at events to ensure they can easily access the venue.
6. Foster intergenerational connections: Encourage younger generations to attend events and interact with older immigrant adults. This can help break down social isolation and create a sense of community across age groups.
7. Cater food options: Offer culturally appropriate food options that cater to the dietary restrictions and preferences of older immigrant adults. Consider labeling food items in multiple languages to ensure everyone knows what they are eating.
