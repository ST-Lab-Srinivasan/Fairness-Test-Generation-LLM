Long-term, divorced individuals who have been out of the workforce for an extended period of time, particularly those who have served in the military, may face several challenges when entering the job market. These challenges can include:

1. Lack of recent work experience: Military personnel often take a break from their careers to serve their country, which means they may not have recent work experience to showcase to potential employers.
2. Skills gap: Military training and experience may not always translate directly to civilian job roles, resulting in a skills gap that can make it difficult to find suitable employment.
3. Limited networking opportunities: Military personnel often have limited opportunities to network and build professional connections while serving, which can make it harder to find job opportunities upon return to civilian life.
4. Difficulty adapting to a civilian work environment: Military personnel are used to a structured and hierarchical work environment, which can be different from the more relaxed and informal atmosphere of many civilian workplaces.
5. Stigma associated with military service: Unfortunately, there is still a stigma attached to military service in some parts of society, which can make it harder for veterans to find employment.
6. Challenges in translating military skills and experience to civilian jobs: Military personnel often have specialized skills and experience that may not be immediately applicable to civilian job roles.
7. Limited access to resources: Veterans may face barriers in accessing resources such as job fairs, networking events, and career counseling services.
8. Difficulty in demonstrating soft skills: Military personnel often have a strong technical background but may struggle to demonstrate their soft skills, such as communication and teamwork, which are highly valued by civilian employers.
9. Ageism: Older veterans may face age-related discrimination when seeking employment, particularly if they have been out of the workforce for an extended period of time.
10. Limited job opportunities in their desired field: Veterans may find that there are limited job opportunities in their desired field, or that the job market has changed significantly since they last worked.
