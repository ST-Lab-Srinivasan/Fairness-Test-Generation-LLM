Cultural heritage plays a significant role in the lives of recent African American refugees to the United States. It provides a sense of identity, belonging, and connection to their ancestors and community. Here are some ways cultural heritage impacts the lives of these individuals:

1. Sense of identity: Refugees often face challenges adapting to a new culture and society. Their cultural heritage serves as a constant reminder of their roots and identity, helping them maintain a sense of self.
2. Connection to ancestors: African American refugees' cultural heritage connects them to their ancestors and the rich cultural traditions of Africa. This connection helps them navigate the challenges of their new environment.
3. Community building: Cultural heritage serves as a unifying force for refugees, helping them build a sense of community with others who share similar experiences and backgrounds.
4. Preservation of history: Refugees may be forced to leave behind their homeland and cultural artifacts due to conflict or persecution. In the US, they can work to preserve their cultural heritage through storytelling, music, art, and other forms of expression.
5. Resilience: Cultural heritage provides refugees with a source of resilience in the face of adversity. It gives them hope and strength to overcome challenges and build a new life in their adopted country.
6. Coping mechanism: Refugees often experience trauma and stress related to their experiences. Engaging in cultural activities can serve as a coping mechanism, providing a healthy outlet for emotions and promoting mental well-being.
7. Cultural expression: Refugees can express themselves through cultural practices, such as language, music, dance, or visual arts. This helps them maintain their identity and connect with others who share similar experiences.
