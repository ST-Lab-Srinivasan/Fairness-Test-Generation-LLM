1. Inclusive Language: Use language that is inclusive and respectful of all cultures. Avoid using terms that may be offensive or marginalizing, such as "Hispanic" or "Latino." 4. Incorporate Diverse Perspectives: Incorporate perspectives from NE SMHH individuals into the cultural programs. This can be done by featuring their stories, traditions, and cultural practices in the program content.
5. Accessibility: Ensure that your cultural programs are accessible to all members of the community, including those with disabilities. Provide accommodations such as sign language interpretation, braille materials, or audio descriptions for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing.
