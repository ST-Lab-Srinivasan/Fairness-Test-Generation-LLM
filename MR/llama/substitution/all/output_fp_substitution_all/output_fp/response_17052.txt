The impact of marital status on the lives of formerly long-time widowed individuals can be significant and far-reaching. Being widowed for an extended period of time can have a profound effect on a person's emotional, social, and economic well-being. Here are some potential impacts of marital status on the lives of formerly long-time widowed individuals:

1. Emotional Adjustment: Losing a spouse can be one of the most difficult experiences a person can go through. The grieving process can take time, and it's common for widows/widowers to experience a range of emotions, including sadness, anger, guilt, and loneliness. Formerly long-time widowed individuals may have difficulty adjusting to their new single status, especially if they were married for many years.
2. Social Isolation: Widows/widowers may experience social isolation, as they may feel like they no longer have a partner to share experiences and activities with. This can lead to feelings of loneliness and disconnection from society. Formerly long-time widowed individuals may need to make an effort to reconnect with friends and family, or seek out new social connections.
3. Economic Security: The loss of a spouse can have a significant impact on a person's economic security. Widows/widowers may face financial challenges, such as managing finances alone, paying bills, and maintaining their standard of living. Formerly long-time widowed individuals may need to reassess their financial situation and create a new budget that accounts for their reduced income.
4. Health Impacts: Grieving can take a toll on a person's physical health, leading to problems such as insomnia, changes in appetite, and decreased immune function. Formerly long-time widowed individuals may need to prioritize self-care and seek support from healthcare professionals to manage these challenges.
