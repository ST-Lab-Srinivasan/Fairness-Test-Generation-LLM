Addressing the specific needs of carriers of genetic conditions who are physically disabled can be a complex and multifaceted process. Here are some ways that bachelor graduates in fields such as occupational therapy, physical therapy, and rehabilitation sciences can address these needs:

1. Assistive Technology: Provide individuals with assistive technology devices such as wheelchairs, walkers, and other mobility aids to help them move around easily.
2. Home Modifications: Make modifications to the individual's home environment to improve accessibility and safety, such as installing ramps, widening doorways, and creating a more accessible bathroom.
3. Occupational Therapy: Work with individuals to develop strategies for managing their daily activities, such as dressing, grooming, and feeding themselves, despite physical limitations.
4. Physical Therapy: Provide exercises and stretches to help individuals maintain strength and flexibility despite physical limitations, and also provide pain management techniques.
5. Adaptive Sports and Recreation: Encourage individuals to participate in sports and recreational activities that are adapted for their abilities, such as wheelchair basketball or adaptive skiing.
6. Social Support: Provide emotional support and counseling to help individuals cope with the emotional challenges of living with a genetic condition and its physical limitations.
7. Advocacy: Advocate for policies and programs that promote accessibility and inclusion, such as accessible transportation and employment opportunities.
8. Education and Training: Provide education and training to individuals and their families on how to manage their condition, how to use assistive devices, and how to navigate the healthcare system.
9. Collaboration with Other Professionals: Work with other professionals such as doctors, nurses, and psychologists to provide comprehensive care and support to individuals with genetic conditions.
10. Self-Advocacy: Encourage individuals to advocate for themselves and their needs, and to speak up about the challenges they face in their daily lives.
