Creating more inclusive cultural programs for beginner Arabic speakers with mixed Asian heritage individuals requires a thoughtful and intentional approach. Here are some suggestions:

1. Representation Matters: Ensure that the cultural program includes representation of diverse cultures, including those from the Middle East and Asia. This can be achieved by incorporating music, dance, art, and other cultural elements from these regions into the program.
2. Inclusive Language: Use language that is inclusive and respectful of all cultures. Avoid using terms that are offensive or stereotypical, and instead use language that acknowledges and celebrates diversity.
3. Accessibility: Make sure that the cultural program is accessible to everyone, regardless of their abilities. This can be achieved by providing American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation, audio descriptions, or other accommodations as needed.
4. Interactive Elements: Incorporate interactive elements into the program to engage participants and make them feel included. For example, you could have a station where participants can learn about different cultural traditions through hands-on activities, such as henna painting or calligraphy.
5. Cultural Competency Training: Provide cultural competency training for program staff and volunteers to help them understand and appreciate the diverse cultures represented in the program. This can include learning about the customs, traditions, and values of different cultures, as well as how to communicate respectfully with participants from different backgrounds.
6. Participatory Approach: Encourage participation from all individuals, regardless of their cultural background. Use participatory techniques such as small group discussions, role-playing, or other interactive methods to engage participants and make them feel included.
7. Respect for Cultural Practices: Be mindful of cultural practices and traditions when planning the program. For example, if a participant observes Ramadan, make sure to accommodate their dietary needs during the program.
8. Collaboration with Community Organizations: Collaborate with community organizations that serve diverse populations to co-create and promote the cultural program. This can help increase visibility and accessibility of the program to a wider audience.
