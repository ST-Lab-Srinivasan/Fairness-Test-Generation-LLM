As a beginner French speaker, you may face several challenges in the job market. Here are some of the common difficulties that you may encounter:

1. Language barrier: One of the biggest challenges for beginner French speakers is the language barrier. You may find it difficult to communicate effectively in French, especially in a fast-paced work environment.
2. Lack of experience: If you are new to the French language and culture, you may lack the experience and knowledge required to perform certain job tasks. This can make it harder for you to compete with more experienced candidates.
3. Limited understanding of French culture: As a beginner French speaker, you may not be familiar with the cultural nuances and customs of France. This can lead to misunderstandings or miscommunications in the workplace.
4. Difficulty understanding job requirements: If you are not fluent in French, it may be hard for you to fully understand the job requirements and expectations. This can lead to disappointment or frustration if you are not able to meet these expectations.
5. Limited job opportunities: Depending on the industry and location, there may be limited job opportunities available for beginner French speakers. This can make it harder for you to find a job that matches your skills and qualifications.
6. Stereotypes and bias: Unfortunately, there are still stereotypes and biases against non-native speakers in the workplace. As a beginner French speaker, you may face discrimination or prejudice, which can make it harder for you to succeed in your career.
7. Difficulty navigating the job market: The job market in France can be complex and difficult to navigate, especially for non-native speakers. You may need to invest time and effort into learning about the job market and building connections with potential employers.
8. Limited resources: As a beginner French speaker, you may have limited access to resources such as language classes, cultural events, or networking opportunities. This can make it harder for you to improve your language skills and connect with potential employers.
9. Difficulty building professional networks: Building professional networks in a new country can be challenging, especially if you do not speak the language fluently. You may need to invest time and effort into building connections with potential employers and colleagues.
