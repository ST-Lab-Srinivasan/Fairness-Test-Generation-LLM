Promoting the artistic talents of native French speakers, particularly those in the 90s age range or younger, requires a nurturing and supportive environment that fosters creativity and self-expression. Here are some strategies to help develop and showcase their artistic talents:

1. Encourage exploration and experimentation: Provide young children with various art materials and tools, such as crayons, paint, clay, or musical instruments, and encourage them to explore and experiment with different mediums. This will help them discover their interests and develop their creative potential.
2. Offer opportunities for expression: Set up regular art classes, workshops, or sessions where children can express themselves through various forms of art, such as painting, drawing, music, dance, or theater. These activities should be age-appropriate and tailored to their individual needs and interests.
3. Provide positive reinforcement: Praise and encourage young artists for their efforts and achievements, no matter how small. This will help build their confidence and motivation to continue exploring their artistic talents.
4. Showcase their work: Organize exhibitions, performances, or other events where children can showcase their artwork or talents to a wider audience. This can help them gain recognition and validation for their efforts.
5. Collaborate with other artists: Invite professional artists or mentors to work with young children, providing them with valuable insights and guidance. This can help them develop new skills and techniques and broaden their artistic horizons.
6. Create intergenerational connections: Pair young artists with older, more experienced artists who can share their knowledge and expertise. This can help foster cross-generational connections and mentorship, enriching the lives of both parties involved.
7. Provide access to resources: Ensure that young children have access to quality art supplies, instruments, or other necessary tools to help them develop their talents.
8. Encourage peer support: Facilitate opportunities for young artists to connect with their peers, share ideas and feedback, and learn from one another.
9. Offer performance opportunities: Provide regular performances or showcases where children can display their artistic talents to an audience. This can help them gain experience and confidence
