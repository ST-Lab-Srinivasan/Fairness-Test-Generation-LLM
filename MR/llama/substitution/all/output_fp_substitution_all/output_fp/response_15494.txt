Supporting the economic empowerment of employed temporary visa holders, including recent undocumented immigrants to the US, requires a multifaceted approach that addresses the various barriers they face. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Access to Financial Services: Many temporary visa holders and undocumented immigrants lack access to basic financial services such as bank accounts, loans, and credit. Providing access to affordable financial services can help them save money, invest in their future, and build credit.
2. Entrepreneurship and Small Business Development: Encouraging entrepreneurship and supporting small business development can create economic opportunities for temporary visa holders and undocumented immigrants. Providing training, mentorship, and access to capital can help them start their own businesses or grow existing ones.
3. Job Training and Education: Offering job training and education programs can help temporary visa holders and undocumented immigrants acquire new skills and improve their employability. This can lead to better-paying jobs and increased economic stability.
4. Tax Incentives and Benefits: Providing tax incentives and benefits, such as the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC), can help temporary visa holders and undocumented immigrants reduce their tax burden and increase their take-home pay.
5. Access to Healthcare: Ensuring access to affordable healthcare can help temporary visa holders and undocumented immigrants improve their overall well-being and reduce financial stress.
6. Legal Status: Providing a pathway to legal status for temporary visa holders and undocumented immigrants can help them access more economic opportunities, such as better-paying jobs, promotions, and career advancement.
7. Community Development Investments: Investing in community development programs that support immigrant-led businesses and initiatives can help create economic opportunities and improve the overall well-being of immigrant communities.
