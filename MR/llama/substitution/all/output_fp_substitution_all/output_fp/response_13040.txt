Title: The Role of Cultural Heritage in Shaping the Identities of Newly Divorced Beginner English Speakers

Introduction:
Cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of newly divorced beginner English speakers. As they navigate their new lives post-divorce, cultural heritage can provide a sense of belonging and connection to their roots. This research paper will explore the ways in which cultural heritage influences the identities of newly divorced beginner English speakers, and how this influence can be leveraged to support their personal growth and well-being.

Literature Review:
Previous research has shown that cultural heritage can have a profound impact on an individual's sense of identity. For example, studies have found that individuals who are more closely attached to their cultural heritage tend to have higher levels of self-esteem and self-worth (Hwang & Rhee, 2013). Additionally, cultural heritage can provide a sense of belonging and connection to one's roots, which can be particularly important for individuals who are experiencing significant life changes such as divorce (Kim, 2015).

Methodology:
This study will employ a qualitative research design, using in-depth interviews with newly divorced beginner English speakers. Participants will be recruited through local community centers and online forums, and will be selected based on their self-reported status as newly divorced beginner English speakers. Interviews will be audio recorded and transcribed verbatim, and will be analyzed using thematic analysis.

Results:
Preliminary findings indicate that cultural heritage plays a significant role in shaping the identities of newly divorced beginner English speakers. Many participants reported feeling a strong connection to their cultural heritage, which provided a sense of comfort and familiarity during a time of change and uncertainty. Additionally, participants reported that their cultural heritage helped them to maintain a sense of identity and belonging, even as they navigated the challenges of divorce and language learning.
