Creating more inclusive cultural celebrations for recently arrived refugees requires understanding their unique experiences and needs. Here are some suggestions:

1. Involve them in the planning process: Refugees should be consulted on what they would like to see happen during cultural events. By involving them in the planning process, you can ensure that the event is tailored to their interests and preferences.
2. Provide language support: Offer interpretation or translation services during cultural events to help refugees who may not speak the dominant language of the host country. This will enable them to fully participate and enjoy the celebration.
3. Focus on accessibility: Ensure that the event venue is accessible for people with disabilities, including wheelchair accessibility, Braille signage, and audio descriptions for visually impaired individuals.
4. Offer cultural orientation: Provide cultural orientation sessions before the event to help refugees understand the customs and traditions of the host country. This will enable them to feel more comfortable and confident during the celebration.
5. Incorporate diverse food options: Offer a variety of food options that cater to different dietary needs and preferences, including halal or kosher options for Muslim or Jewish refugees.
6. Create a safe space: Designate a quiet area where refugees can take breaks or seek support if needed during the event. This will provide them with a sense of safety and security.
7. Use visual aids: Utilize visual aids such as pictures, videos, or interactive displays to help refugees understand the cultural significance of the event.
8. Involve refugee leaders: Invite refugee leaders or representatives to speak during the event, highlighting their experiences and perspectives. This will create a sense of ownership and inclusivity.
9. Provide childcare: Offer childcare services during the event to ensure that refugees can participate without worrying about their children's needs.
10. Celebrate diversity: Embrace the diverse backgrounds and experiences of refugees by incorporating elements of their cultures into the celebration. This could include traditional music, dance, or art.
