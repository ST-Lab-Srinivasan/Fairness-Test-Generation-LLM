Creating more inclusive community events for non-binary and genderfluid individuals requires careful planning and consideration. Here are some steps you can take to make your events more welcoming:

1. Use inclusive language: When promoting your event, use language that is inclusive of all genders. Instead of using binary terms like "men" and "women," use terms like "everyone," "all genders," or "gender-diverse."
2. Provide gender-neutral bathrooms: Ensure that there are gender-neutral bathrooms available at the event location. This can help non-binary individuals feel more comfortable and included.
3. Offer gender-neutral options: When planning food and drinks for the event, consider offering gender-neutral options like "vegan" or "gluten-free" instead of binary terms like "men's" or "women's."
4. Be mindful of dress codes: Avoid having strict dress code requirements that may make non-binary individuals feel uncomfortable or excluded. Instead, encourage attendees to dress in a way that makes them feel comfortable and confident.
5. Create a safe space policy: Develop a safe space policy that outlines how you will handle any incidents of harassment or discrimination at the event. Make sure the policy is clearly communicated to all attendees before the event.
6. Involve non-binary individuals in planning: Reach out to non-binary individuals in your community and involve them in the planning process. This can help ensure that the event is inclusive and meets the needs of non-binary attendees.
7. Be respectful of pronouns: When introducing speakers or announcing events, be mindful of using the correct pronouns for each individual. If you're unsure of someone's pronouns, it's okay to ask politely and respect their wishes.
