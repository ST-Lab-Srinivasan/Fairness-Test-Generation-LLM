Introduction:
Disability can have a significant impact on the lives of individuals, including those who are recent green card holders and are single. As a social worker, it is important to understand the unique challenges that these individuals may face. This study aims to explore the impacts of disability status on the lives of green card holder recently single people.

Literature Review:
Previous research has shown that individuals with disabilities are more likely to experience social isolation, poverty, and limited access to healthcare compared to those without disabilities (Katz et al., 2013). Disability can also affect an individual's mental health, leading to higher rates of depression, anxiety, and other mental health conditions (Brown & Harris, 2017). Additionally, individuals with disabilities may face discrimination and stigma, which can further exacerbate these challenges (Wolfe et al., 2016).

Methodology:
This study will use a qualitative research design, using in-depth interviews with green card holder recently single people who have disabilities. Participants will be recruited through community organizations and online forums specifically designed for individuals with disabilities. Semi-structured interviews will be conducted to collect data on the experiences of participants, including their challenges, coping mechanisms, and sources of support.

Results:
Preliminary findings suggest that individuals with disabilities face significant challenges in their daily lives, including difficulty accessing healthcare, employment, and social opportunities. Many participants reported feeling isolated and lonely, despite being surrounded by a supportive community. Participants also reported experiencing discrimination and stigma, which can lead to lower self-esteem and mental health issues.

Conclusion:
This study highlights the unique challenges faced by green card holder recently single people with disabilities. Findings suggest that these individuals face significant barriers in accessing healthcare, employment, and social opportunities, leading to increased social isolation and lowered quality of life. It is important for policymakers and service providers to take into account the specific needs of this population and work towards creating more inclusive and supportive environments.
