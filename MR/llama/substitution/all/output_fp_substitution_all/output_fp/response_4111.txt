Creating more inclusive community events for people with non-long-term single-no-known-genetic conditions (SNGC) requires thoughtful planning and execution. Here are some suggestions to make your events more accessible and welcoming:

1. Inclusive language: Use language that is respectful and inclusive of all individuals, regardless of their abilities or disabilities. Avoid using terms like "disabled" or "handicapped," which can be seen as stigmatizing. Instead, use language like "people with disabilities" or "individuals with SNGC."
2. Accessibility: Ensure that the event space is wheelchair-accessible and has enough seating for people who may need it. Provide assistance devices like audio describers or sign language interpreters upon request. Consider hiring attendants to help guests with mobility issues navigate the event.
3. Sensory-friendly environment: Create a sensory-friendly environment by reducing noise levels, using dim lighting, and providing quiet areas for individuals who may be overwhelmed by stimuli. Offer earplugs or headphones upon request.
4. Inclusive programming: Develop a program that includes activities and entertainment that are accessible to all participants. This could include sign language, audio descriptions, or braille materials for attendees who may have difficulty with visual or auditory information.
5. Diverse representation: Ensure that the event features diverse representation, including people of different abilities, cultures, and backgrounds. Use images and marketing materials that reflect this diversity to help create a more inclusive atmosphere.
6. Accessible communication: Provide multiple ways for attendees to communicate, such as American Sign Language (ASL) interpreters, CART (communication access real-time translation) services, or written materials in braille or large print. Make sure to provide information in a timely manner and through various channels to accommodate different communication needs.
