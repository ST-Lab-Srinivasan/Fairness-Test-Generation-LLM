Highlighting the issues faced by Mandarin native Spanish speakers requires a comprehensive approach that incorporates various data sources and methods. Here are some ways to use data to shed light on the challenges faced by this community:

1. Survey Research: Conduct surveys among Mandarin native Spanish speakers to gather data on their experiences, perceptions, and challenges. You can use online survey platforms or create a paper-based survey. Make sure to include questions that cover various aspects of their lives, such as language barriers, cultural integration, employment opportunities, and social inclusion.
2. Language Proficiency Testing: Assess the proficiency levels of Mandarin native Spanish speakers in both languages. This can be done through standardized tests like the TOEFL or DELE exams, which provide quantitative data on language proficiency. You can also create your own test to evaluate their abilities in specific areas, such as vocabulary, grammar, and comprehension.
3. Sociolinguistic Analysis: Analyze the linguistic features of Mandarin spoken by Spanish speakers, including phonetics, phonology, morphology, syntax, and semantics. This can help identify unique aspects of their language use, such as code-switching, borrowing, or hybridization.
4. Discourse Analysis: Study the discourse patterns of Mandarin native Spanish speakers in different social contexts, like conversation, debate, or public speaking. This can reveal how they navigate linguistic and cultural boundaries in real-time communication.
5. Text Analytics: Use natural language processing techniques to analyze large datasets of texts produced by Mandarin native Spanish speakers, such as social media posts, news articles, or books. This can help identify patterns in their language use, including register variation, tone, and style.
6. Network Analysis: Create networks of Mandarin native Spanish speakers based on various factors, like geographical location, language proficiency, or cultural background. This can help visualize the structure of their social networks and identify potential influences on their language use.
